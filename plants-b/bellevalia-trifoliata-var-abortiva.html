Bellevalia trifoliata var. abortiva

Bellevalia trifoliata var. abortiva, commonly known as the aborted trifoliate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is known for its short stature and compact growth habit, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The variety 'abortiva' is distinguished by its reduced flower development, giving it a unique appearance compared to other varieties of Bellevalia.
