Bunium kandaharicum

Bunium kandaharicum, commonly known as the Kandahar fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, dry fruits. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Bunium kandaharicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
