Bursa sinuosa

Bursa sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous bur, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called achenes, which are enclosed in a pappus, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Bursa sinuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
