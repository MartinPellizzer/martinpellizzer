Brachypodium distachyon var. velutinum

Brachypodium distachyon var. velutinum, commonly known as velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, silky texture due to the presence of short, soft hairs on its leaves and stems. This variant is often used in ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics research. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its compact growth habit and rapid life cycle make it a valuable resource for studying plant responses to environmental stress.
