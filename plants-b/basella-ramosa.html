Basella ramosa

Basella ramosa, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and fleshy, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Basella ramosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
