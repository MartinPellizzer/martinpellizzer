Bubon glaucum

Bubon glaucum, commonly known as the bluebottle or sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Native to coastal regions of southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along the shoreline. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in summer, and its seeds are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
