Bambusa purpurovagian

Bambusa purpurovagian, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking purple-colored shoots and culms. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth texture, while its culms are hollow and segmented.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
