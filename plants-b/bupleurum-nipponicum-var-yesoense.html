Bupleurum nipponicum var. yesoense

Bupleurum nipponicum var. yesoense, commonly known as the Yeso bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small, white or pale blue, arranged in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in East Asian herbal medicine.
