Bulbophyllum stocksii

Bulbophyllum stocksii, commonly known as the Stocks' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Bulbophyllum stocksii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
