Barleria crassa subsp. crassa

Barleria crassa subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the rough barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
