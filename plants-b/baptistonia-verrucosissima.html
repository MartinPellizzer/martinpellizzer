Baptistonia verrucosissima

Baptistonia verrucosissima, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, green, bladder-like structures that are used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Baptistonia verrucosissima plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web through its prey-trapping mechanisms.
