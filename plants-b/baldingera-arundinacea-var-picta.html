Baldingera arundinacea var. picta

Baldingera arundinacea var. picta, commonly known as the variegated reed, is a striking plant characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a unique coloration that includes shades of green, white, and sometimes yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in wet or moist environments.
