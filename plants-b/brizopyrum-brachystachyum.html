Brizopyrum brachystachyum

Brizopyrum brachystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bullock's wheat, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Brizopyrum brachystachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
