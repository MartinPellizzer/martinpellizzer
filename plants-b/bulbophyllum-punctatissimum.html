Bulbophyllum punctatissimum

Bulbophyllum punctatissimum, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with distinctive markings. Its rhizomatous structure allows it to spread and form clumps over time.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
