Begonia crenatiflora

Begonia crenatiflora, commonly known as the serrated-flowered begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green and silver tones.

Begonia crenatiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
