Bupleurum semicompositum var. glaucum

Bupleurum semicompositum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue-flowered bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, with lanceolate leaves that are usually blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
