Boechera microphylla var. macounii

Boechera microphylla var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, pinnately divided leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local flora's biodiversity.
