Bischofia javanica var. oblongifolia

Bischofia javanica var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bishop's candle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and composed of several elliptical to oblong leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
