Boronia artemisiifolia var. wilsonii

Boronia artemisiifolia var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, often hairy stems.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in local habitats.
