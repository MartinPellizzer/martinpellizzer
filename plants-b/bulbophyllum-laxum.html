Bulbophyllum laxum

Bulbophyllum laxum, commonly known as the loose-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, lip-like structure that aids in pollination.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
