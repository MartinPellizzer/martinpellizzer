Barringtonia inclyta

Barringtonia inclyta, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy surface.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the edible fruit it produces.
