Bursera excelsa var. acutidens

Bursera excelsa var. acutidens, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing single seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in tropical dry forests and scrublands.
