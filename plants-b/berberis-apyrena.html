Berberis apyrena

Berberis apyrena, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
