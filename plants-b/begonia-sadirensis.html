Begonia sadirensis

Begonia sadirensis, commonly known as the Sadirensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
