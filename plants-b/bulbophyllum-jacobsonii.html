Bulbophyllum jacobsonii

Bulbophyllum jacobsonii, commonly known as the Jacobson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its intricate floral structure makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
