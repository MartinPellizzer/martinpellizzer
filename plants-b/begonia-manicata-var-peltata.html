Begonia manicata var. peltata

Begonia manicata var. peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The foliage is typically dark green with a glossy texture, and the plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in indoor gardening.
