Botrychium rutaceum var. tripartitum

Botrychium rutaceum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed moonwort, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate fronds that resemble a three-lobed structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in a unique, umbrella-like arrangement on the underside of the fronds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is of interest to botanists due to its ancient lineage and unique morphology.
