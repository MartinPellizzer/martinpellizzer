Betula × dutillyi

Betula × dutillyi, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a deciduous tree resulting from the cross between white birch (Betula pendula) and silver birch (Betula pendula var. jacquemontii).

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellow or pinkish inner layer. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated in various parts of the world for its ornamental value. Betula × dutillyi typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
