Begonia garuvae

Begonia garuvae, commonly known as the Maluku begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to deep purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia garuvae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
