Banisteriopsis lyrata

Banisteriopsis lyrata, commonly known as the Amazonian trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
