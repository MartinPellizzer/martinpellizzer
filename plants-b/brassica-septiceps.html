Brassica septiceps

Brassica septiceps, commonly known as the seven-branched mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces tall, branching stems with small, yellow flowers. Its fruits are slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Brassica septiceps is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
