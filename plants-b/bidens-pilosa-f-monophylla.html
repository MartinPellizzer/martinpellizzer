Bidens pilosa f. monophylla

Bidens pilosa f. monophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features serrated, rough-textured leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into spiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
