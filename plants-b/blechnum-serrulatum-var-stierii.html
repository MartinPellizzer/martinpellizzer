Blechnum serrulatum var. stierii

Blechnum serrulatum var. stierii, commonly known as the Stier's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance that gives it an elegant look.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in shaded forest floors.
