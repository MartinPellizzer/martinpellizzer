Baccharis lycopodioides

Baccharis lycopodioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
