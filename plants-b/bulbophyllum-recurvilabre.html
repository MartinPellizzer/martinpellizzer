Bulbophyllum recurvilabre

Bulbophyllum recurvilabre, commonly known as the recurved lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive recurved lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, slender pseudobulbs and narrow, leathery leaves.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a showy, three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.
