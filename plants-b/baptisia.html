Baptisia

Baptisia, commonly known as false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their showy, spike-like clusters of blue or yellow flowers. Baptisia species are typically perennial, herbaceous plants that thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. They have compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produce pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
