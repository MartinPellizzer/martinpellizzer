Bombacopsis speciosa

Bombacopsis speciosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking, lantern-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fibrous, making it resistant to pests and diseases.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
