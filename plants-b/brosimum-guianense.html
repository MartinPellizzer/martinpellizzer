Brosimum guianense

Brosimum guianense, commonly known as the Guianese breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in countries like Guyana, Venezuela, and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit that is edible and has a starchy, bread-like texture when cooked.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important plant in local ecosystems and traditional diets.
