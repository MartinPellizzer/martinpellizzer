Babiana pauciflora

Babiana pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered babiana, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vivid orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that help it survive in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Babiana pauciflora is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
