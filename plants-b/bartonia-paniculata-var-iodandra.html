Bartonia paniculata var. iodandra

Bartonia paniculata var. iodandra, commonly known as the silverleaf bartronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The variety 'iodandra' is distinguished by its more pronounced silver foliage compared to other forms of Bartonia paniculata.
