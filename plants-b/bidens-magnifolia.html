Bidens magnifolia

Bidens magnifolia, commonly known as the showy beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, sticky seeds that are dispersed by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.

Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
