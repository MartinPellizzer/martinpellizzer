Begonia stigmosa

Begonia stigmosa, commonly known as the pink begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often hairy and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Begonia stigmosa prefers shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
