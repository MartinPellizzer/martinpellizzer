Bromus sewerzowii var. subglaber

Bromus sewerzowii var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with short hairs. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is often subtended by a pair of bracts. This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid environments.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a pioneer species in disturbed soils and its potential use in restoration ecology.
