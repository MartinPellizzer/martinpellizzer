Bulliarda vaillantii

Bulliarda vaillantii, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Bulliarda vaillantii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
