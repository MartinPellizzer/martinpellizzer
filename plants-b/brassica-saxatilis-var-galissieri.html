Brassica saxatilis var. galissieri

Brassica saxatilis var. galissieri, commonly known as the Galician wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
