Berlinia kerstingii

Berlinia kerstingii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in traditional medicine.
