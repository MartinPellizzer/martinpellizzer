Bignonia linearis

Bignonia linearis, commonly known as the slender trumpetvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to 15 meters in length, often twining around supports. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Bignonia linearis is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
