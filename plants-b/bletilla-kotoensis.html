Bletilla kotoensis

Bletilla kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, erect stems that bear a single, large, white or pinkish flower with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Bletilla kotoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
