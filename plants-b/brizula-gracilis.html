Brizula gracilis

Brizula gracilis, commonly known as the slender brizula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Brizula gracilis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and unique flowering structure.
