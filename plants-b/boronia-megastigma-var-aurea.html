Boronia megastigma var. aurea

Boronia megastigma var. aurea, commonly known as the golden boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is often found in heathlands and sand dunes. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
