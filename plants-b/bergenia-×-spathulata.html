Bergenia × spathulata

Bergenia × spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf bergenia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and rosettes of broad, waxy, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Bergenia × spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance groundcovers.
