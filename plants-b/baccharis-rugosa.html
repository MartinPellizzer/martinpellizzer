Baccharis rugosa

Baccharis rugosa, commonly known as rough baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to gray seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Baccharis rugosa is an important plant in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
