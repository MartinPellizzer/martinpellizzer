Begonia menglianensis

Begonia menglianensis, commonly known as the Menglian begonia, is a species native to China, specifically Yunnan province.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within its native habitat. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
