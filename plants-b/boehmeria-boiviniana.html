Boehmeria boiviniana

Boehmeria boiviniana, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fibrous stems are often used in traditional crafts and textiles due to their strength and flexibility.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in local remedies.
