Barringtonia acutangula subsp. spicata

Barringtonia acutangula subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant produces a unique fruit that is a fibrous, spiny capsule containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
