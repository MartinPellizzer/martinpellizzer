Bignonia squalus

Bignonia squalus, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous flat, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist, open habitats.

Bignonia squalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
