Bellia

Bellia, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which are typically blue, violet, or white in color. Bellia species are often found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They are perennial herbs that produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The genus includes around 50 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
