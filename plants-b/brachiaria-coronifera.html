Brachiaria coronifera

Brachiaria coronifera, commonly known as the crowned grass or velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive flowering structures that resemble small crowns, giving it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Brachiaria coronifera is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in certain environments.
