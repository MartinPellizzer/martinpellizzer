Billbergia schimperiana

Billbergia schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, often blue-green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically red or orange, emerging from the leaf rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia schimperiana is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
