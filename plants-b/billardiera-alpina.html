Billardiera alpina

Billardiera alpina, commonly known as the alpine devil's apron, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment.

Billardiera alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
