Barleria thunbergiiflora

Barleria thunbergiiflora, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
