Bignonia neouliginosa

Bignonia neouliginosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small showers of light. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a rough texture with a prominent midrib.
