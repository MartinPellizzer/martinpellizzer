Betula fruticosa var. paishanensis

Betula fruticosa var. paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan willow, is a shrub-like species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
