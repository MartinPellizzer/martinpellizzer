Barleria prionitis subsp. induta

Barleria prionitis subsp.

induta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
