Ballota hirsuta var. vellerea

Ballota hirsuta var. vellerea, commonly known as the velvet ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
