Biota tatarica

Biota tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Biota tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
