Bidens andicola var. cosmantha

Bidens andicola var. cosmantha, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations above 3,000 meters.
