Buddleja vetula var. major

Buddleja vetula var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pale blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and showy blooms contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
