Baccharis angulata var. gracilis

Baccharis angulata var. gracilis, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
