Borbonia humilis

Borbonia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf borbonia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
