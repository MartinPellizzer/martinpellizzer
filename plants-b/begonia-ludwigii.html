Begonia ludwigii

Begonia ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a vibrant red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia ludwigii is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
