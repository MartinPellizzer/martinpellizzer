Borbonia indica

Borbonia indica, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
