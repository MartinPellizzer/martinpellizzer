Begonia plebeja

Begonia plebeja, commonly known as the common begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
