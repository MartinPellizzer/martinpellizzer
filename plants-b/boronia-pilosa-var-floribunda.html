Boronia pilosa var. floribunda

Boronia pilosa var. floribunda, commonly known as the coastal boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
