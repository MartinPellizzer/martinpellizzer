Bryonia fimbristipula

Bryonia fimbristipula, commonly known as the fringed-tip bryony, is a member of the genus Bryonia in the family Bryaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves with fringed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Bryonia fimbristipula is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
