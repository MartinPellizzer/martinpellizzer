Bryonia haussknechtiana

Bryonia haussknechtiana, commonly known as the Hausknecht's bryony, is a rare and striking member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, orange-colored fruits. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
