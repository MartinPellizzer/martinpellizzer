Blechnum chambersii

Blechnum chambersii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Blechnum chambersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
