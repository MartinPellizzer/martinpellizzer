Bursera submoniliformis

Bursera submoniliformis, commonly known as the Mexican birch, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that resembles birch trees, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain three seeds.
