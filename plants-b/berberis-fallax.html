Berberis fallax

Berberis fallax, commonly known as the variable barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Berberis fallax is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide structure and color in landscaping.
