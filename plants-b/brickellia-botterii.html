Brickellia botterii

Brickellia botterii, commonly known as desert brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
