Bletia bracteosa

Bletia bracteosa, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within rainforests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that attracts pollinators.

Bletia bracteosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.
