Bromus macranthos var. setifolius

Bromus macranthos var. setifolius, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
