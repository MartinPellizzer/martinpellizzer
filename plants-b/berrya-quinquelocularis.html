Berrya quinquelocularis

Berrya quinquelocularis, commonly known as the five-chambered berry plant, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, clustered berries that mature to a deep red color. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and it blooms in the spring.

The berries are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for their medicinal properties.
