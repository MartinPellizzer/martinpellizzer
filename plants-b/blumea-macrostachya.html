Blumea macrostachya

Blumea macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Blumea macrostachya thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
