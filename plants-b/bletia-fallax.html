Bletia fallax

Bletia fallax, commonly known as the autumn orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Bletia fallax is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
