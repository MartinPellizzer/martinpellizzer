Bambusa xueliniana

Bambusa xueliniana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo is known for its fast growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

The plant also plays a role in traditional Chinese horticulture and has been used in landscape design for its visual impact.
