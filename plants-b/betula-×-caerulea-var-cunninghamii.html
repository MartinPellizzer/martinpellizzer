Betula × caerulea var. cunninghamii

Betula × caerulea var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silver sheen beneath, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by small, triangular leaves that turn golden in autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and parks.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
