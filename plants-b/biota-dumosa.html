Biota dumosa

Biota dumosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with hairy, succulent stems and lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Biota dumosa is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to store water in its leaves and stems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
