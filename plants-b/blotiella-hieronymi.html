Blotiella hieronymi

Blotiella hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters where it relies on its specialized adaptations to obtain essential nutrients. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowering stems rise above the water surface.

Blotiella hieronymi plays an important ecological role in its habitat by controlling insect populations and contributing to the nutrient cycle.
