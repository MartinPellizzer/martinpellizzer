Begonia gemmirhiza

Begonia gemmirhiza, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped or oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Begonia gemmirhiza is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
