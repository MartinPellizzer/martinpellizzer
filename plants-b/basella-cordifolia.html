Basella cordifolia

Basella cordifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and succulent stems, which are often used in cooking. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are edible and rich in nutrients, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.

Additionally, Basella cordifolia is known for its ability to climb and spread rapidly, often used as a living fence or ground cover.
