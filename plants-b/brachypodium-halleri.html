Brachypodium halleri

Brachypodium halleri, commonly known as Haller's bristlegrass, is a small annual grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arable fields and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Brachypodium halleri is sometimes considered a weed but is also studied for its potential in ecological and agricultural research.
