Banisteria fischeriana

Banisteria fischeriana, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria fischeriana is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
