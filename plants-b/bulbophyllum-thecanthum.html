Bulbophyllum thecanthum

Bulbophyllum thecanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical conditions. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, and it prefers well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum thecanthum is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
