Baccharis odorata var. balsamifera

Baccharis odorata var. balsamifera, commonly known as the sweet baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a fragrant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
