Blumea glutinosa

Blumea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Blumea glutinosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
