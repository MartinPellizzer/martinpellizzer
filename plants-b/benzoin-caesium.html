Benzoin caesium

Benzoin caesium, commonly known as the fragrant resinous tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic sap, which is harvested to produce benzoin resin. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
