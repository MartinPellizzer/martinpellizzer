Bidens tripartita var. shimadae

Bidens tripartita var. shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
