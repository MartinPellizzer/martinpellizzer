Brachychiton chillagoensis

Brachychiton chillagoensis, commonly known as the Chillagoe flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a distinctive, thick, woody trunk with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are large, palmate, and typically have five to seven lobes.

Brachychiton chillagoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
