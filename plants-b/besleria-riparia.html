Besleria riparia

Besleria riparia, commonly known as the river besleria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Besleria riparia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
