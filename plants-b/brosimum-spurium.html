Brosimum spurium

Brosimum spurium, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is edible, though not as widely consumed as other mulberry species.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
