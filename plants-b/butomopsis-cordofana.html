Butomopsis cordofana

Butomopsis cordofana, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a water-loving plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Butomopsis cordofana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
