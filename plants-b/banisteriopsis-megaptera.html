Banisteriopsis megaptera

Banisteriopsis megaptera, commonly known as the chocolate tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that are small and round. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
