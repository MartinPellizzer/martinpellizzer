Baccharis spartea

Baccharis spartea, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture.

Baccharis spartea is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
