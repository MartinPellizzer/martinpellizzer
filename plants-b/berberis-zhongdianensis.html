Berberis zhongdianensis

Berberis zhongdianensis, commonly known as the Zhongdian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
