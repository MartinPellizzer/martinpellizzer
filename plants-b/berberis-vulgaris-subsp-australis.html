Berberis vulgaris subsp. australis

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian barberry, is a shrub native to Australia and New Zealand. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
