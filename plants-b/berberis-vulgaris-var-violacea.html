Berberis vulgaris var. violacea

Berberis vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as the purple barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its berberine-containing roots.
