Bupleurum frutescens

Bupleurum frutescens, commonly known as wild chervil or goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Bupleurum frutescens is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, growing in well-drained soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
