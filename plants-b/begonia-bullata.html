Begonia bullata

Begonia bullata, commonly known as the bullate begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often colorful leaves that are adorned with distinctive white or pale green spots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Begonia bullata is valued in horticulture for its unique leaf patterns and ease of care.
