Bitteria pediformis

Bitteria pediformis, commonly known as the walking stick plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching stem that resembles the legs of a walking stick insect, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
