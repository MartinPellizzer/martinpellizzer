Baptisia viridis

Baptisia viridis, commonly known as green false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is often found in dry, open woodlands and thrives in full sun.

Baptisia viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil health.
