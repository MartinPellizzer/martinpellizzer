Bupleurum obtusatum var. caricifolium

Bupleurum obtusatum var. caricifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their broad, rounded shape and slightly toothed margins.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
