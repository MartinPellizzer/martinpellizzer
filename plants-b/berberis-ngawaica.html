Berberis ngawaica

Berberis ngawaica, commonly known as the Ngawaica barberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
