Bupleurum lancifolium var. subacaule

Bupleurum lancifolium var. subacaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate, with a pointed tip and serrated margins, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
