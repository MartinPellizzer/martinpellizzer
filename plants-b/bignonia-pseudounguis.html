Bignonia pseudounguis

Bignonia pseudounguis, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial.
