Bacopa stemodioides

Bacopa stemodioides, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its aerial parts are often used in traditional medicine for their purported cognitive and calming effects.
