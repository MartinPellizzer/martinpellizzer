Bursera palmeri

Bursera palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bursera, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

Bursera palmeri is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
