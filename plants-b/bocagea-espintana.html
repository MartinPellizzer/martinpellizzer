Bocagea espintana

Bocagea espintana, commonly known as the Espinosa's bocageia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Bocagea espintana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
