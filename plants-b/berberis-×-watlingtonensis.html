Berberis × watlingtonensis

Berberis × watlingtonensis, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
