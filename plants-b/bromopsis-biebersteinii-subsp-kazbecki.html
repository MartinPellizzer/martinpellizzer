Bromopsis biebersteinii subsp. kazbecki

Bromopsis biebersteinii subsp.

kazbecki, commonly known as the Kazbek bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps reflect sunlight.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for studying plant resilience in mountainous regions.
