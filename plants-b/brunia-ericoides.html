Brunia ericoides

Brunia ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Brunia ericoides is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
