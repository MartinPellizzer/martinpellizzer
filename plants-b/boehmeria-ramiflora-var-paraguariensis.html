Boehmeria ramiflora var. paraguariensis

Boehmeria ramiflora var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fibrous stems are valued for their potential in textile and paper industries.

The plant contains stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
