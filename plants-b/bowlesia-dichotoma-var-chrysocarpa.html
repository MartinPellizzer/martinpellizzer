Bowlesia dichotoma var. chrysocarpa

Bowlesia dichotoma var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit bowlesia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
