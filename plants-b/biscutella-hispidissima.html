Biscutella hispidissima

Biscutella hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid biscuitflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often clustered in loose racemes. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Biscutella hispidissima is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
