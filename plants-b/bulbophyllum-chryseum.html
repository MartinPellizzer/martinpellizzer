Bulbophyllum chryseum

Bulbophyllum chryseum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in tropical environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
