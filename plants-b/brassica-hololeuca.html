Brassica hololeuca

Brassica hololeuca, commonly known as the white mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, erect stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Brassica hololeuca is an annual or biennial species that can be invasive in certain regions.
