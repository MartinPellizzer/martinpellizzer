Bromelia cachimbensis

Bromelia cachimbensis, commonly known as the Cachimbe bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of red, orange, and yellow, which help attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the rainforest.

Its unique structure supports a microhabitat for various small organisms, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
