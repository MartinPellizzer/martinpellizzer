Bauhinia corifolia

Bauhinia corifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically followed by flat, leathery pods that contain seeds.

Bauhinia corifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
