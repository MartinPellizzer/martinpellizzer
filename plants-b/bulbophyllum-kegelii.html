Bulbophyllum kegelii

Bulbophyllum kegelii, commonly known as the Kegel's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water conservation.

The flowers, which can be colorful and showy, bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects or birds.
