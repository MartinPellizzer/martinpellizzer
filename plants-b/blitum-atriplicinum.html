Blitum atriplicinum

Blitum atriplicinum, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
