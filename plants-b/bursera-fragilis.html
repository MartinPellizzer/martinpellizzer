Bursera fragilis

Bursera fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant tree or copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The tree produces a valuable resin known as copal, which has been used traditionally for incense and varnishes.
