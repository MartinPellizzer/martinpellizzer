Betula stenocarpa

Betula stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf birch, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-barked trunk and triangular, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and is often found in subalpine and alpine environments. Betula stenocarpa is adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
