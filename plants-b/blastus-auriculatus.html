Blastus auriculatus

Blastus auriculatus, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique ear-shaped lip, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Blastus auriculatus is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
