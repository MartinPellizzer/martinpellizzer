Baissea multiflora var. caudiloba

Baissea multiflora var. caudiloba, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
