Bifaria jacquemontii

Bifaria jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive bilabiate (two-lipped) structure. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Bifaria jacquemontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
