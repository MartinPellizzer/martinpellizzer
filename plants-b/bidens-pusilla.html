Bidens pusilla

Bidens pusilla, commonly known as little beggar-tick, is a small annual herb native to the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two white ray florets, which bloom from summer to fall. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.
