Bidens simplicifolia

Bidens simplicifolia, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. Its leaves are simple and usually have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow with prominent central disks.

Bidens simplicifolia is known for its ability to spread rapidly through its creeping growth habit and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
