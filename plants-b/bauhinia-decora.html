Bauhinia decora

Bauhinia decora, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often arranged in racemose inflorescences.

Bauhinia decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
