Briggsia cavaleriei

Briggsia cavaleriei, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Briggsia cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
