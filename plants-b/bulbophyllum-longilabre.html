Bulbophyllum longilabre

Bulbophyllum longilabre, commonly known as the long-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers with long, narrow petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and can bloom in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Bulbophyllum longilabre is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
