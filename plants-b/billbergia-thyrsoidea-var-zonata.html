Billbergia thyrsoidea var. zonata

Billbergia thyrsoidea var. zonata, commonly known as the Zonate Billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped leaves that display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant typically forms a rosette structure with long, narrow leaves that can reach several feet in length. It produces striking inflorescences with colorful, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
