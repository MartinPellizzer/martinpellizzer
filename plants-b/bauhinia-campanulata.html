Bauhinia campanulata

Bauhinia campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia campanulata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
