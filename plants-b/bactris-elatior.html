Bactris elatior

Bactris elatior, commonly known as the red rafaela, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible fruits that are small and round. Bactris elatior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
