Bubbia auriculata

Bubbia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with gray-green, fuzzy foliage. Its unique ear-shaped petals and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils make it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Bubbia auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
