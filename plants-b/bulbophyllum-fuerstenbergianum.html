Bulbophyllum fuerstenbergianum

Bulbophyllum fuerstenbergianum, commonly known as the Fuerstenberg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with dark markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
