Bumelia eriocarpa

Bumelia eriocarpa, commonly known as the fuzzy bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Bumelia eriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
