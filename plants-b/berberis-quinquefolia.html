Berberis quinquefolia

Berberis quinquefolia, commonly known as Ohio grapevine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five leaflets, which are alternate and serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall foliage, which turns bright red or orange.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade.
