Bignonia vargasiana

Bignonia vargasiana, commonly known as the Mexican flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a vigorous climber with long, serpentine stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly pubescent surface.

Bignonia vargasiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
