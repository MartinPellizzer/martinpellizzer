Berberis actinacantha var. typica

Berberis actinacantha var. typica, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various climates make it a popular choice for landscaping.
