Biscutella laevigata var. asperifolia

Biscutella laevigata var. asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its varietal name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
