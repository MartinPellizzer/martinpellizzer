Bambusa latideltata

Bambusa latideltata, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features sturdy, hollow stems that are typically green when young and develop a bluish-gray hue as they mature. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Bambusa latideltata is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
