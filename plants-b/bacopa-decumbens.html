Bacopa decumbens

Bacopa decumbens, commonly known as inch plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing herb that produces small, blue or violet flowers and has opposite, ovate leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers.

Bacopa decumbens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
