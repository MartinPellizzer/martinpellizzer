Begonia strigosa

Begonia strigosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is also known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and stem cuttings.
