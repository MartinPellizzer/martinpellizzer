Baccharis tola var. incarum

Baccharis tola var. incarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
