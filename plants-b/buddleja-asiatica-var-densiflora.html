Buddleja asiatica var. densiflora

Buddleja asiatica var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, fluffy flowers in shades of pale purple to pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
