Bromus segetum

Bromus segetum, commonly known as the rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces spikelets that are often found in clusters, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Bromus segetum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate both drought and frost.
