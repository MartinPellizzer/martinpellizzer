Bulbophyllum tremulum

Bulbophyllum tremulum, commonly known as the trembling orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
