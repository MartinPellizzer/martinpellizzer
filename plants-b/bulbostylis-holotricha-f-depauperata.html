Bulbostylis holotricha f. depauperata

Bulbostylis holotricha f. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-stemmed bulbostylis, is a rare grass species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in tight clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but endangered species.
