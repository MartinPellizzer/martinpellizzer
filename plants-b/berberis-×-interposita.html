Berberis × interposita

Berberis × interposita, commonly known as the golden smoketree, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Berberis thunbergii and Berberis species, showcasing a combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red foliage, which provides striking visual interest in the autumn. It produces small, red berries that are edible but often disliked by humans.

Berberis × interposita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
