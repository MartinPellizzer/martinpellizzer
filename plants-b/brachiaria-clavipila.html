Brachiaria clavipila

Brachiaria clavipila, commonly known as Brazilian grass or slender brachiaria, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Brachiaria clavipila is valued for its use in forage and soil stabilization due to its rapid growth and dense root system.
