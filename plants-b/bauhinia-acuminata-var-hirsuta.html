Bauhinia acuminata var. hirsuta

Bauhinia acuminata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are often clustered at the ends of branches, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.

The hairy bauhinia is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
