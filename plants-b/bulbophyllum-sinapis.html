Bulbophyllum sinapis

Bulbophyllum sinapis, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet floral coloration. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its tropical habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with intricate petal structures.

Bulbophyllum sinapis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique color and beauty.
