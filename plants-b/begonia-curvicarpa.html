Begonia curvicarpa

Begonia curvicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, narrow appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
