Brassica oleracea var. kashmiriana

Brassica oleracea var. kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmiri cabbage, is a cultivar native to the Kashmir region of India.

It is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its thick, compact rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value.

This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments and is well-suited for cooler growing conditions.
