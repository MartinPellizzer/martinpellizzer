Bulbostylis junciformis var. ambigua

Bulbostylis junciformis var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its morphology features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

The varietal distinction of ambigua is primarily based on subtle differences in leaf structure and floral characteristics compared to the typical form.
