Brassica oleracea subvar. angustiloba

Brassica oleracea subvar. angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leafed cabbage or Italian kale, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and sturdy. This plant is often grown for its edible leaves and stems, which have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in cool climates and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is closely related to other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.
