Begonia castilloi

Begonia castilloi, commonly known as the Castillo's begonia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is usually a deep green, and the flowers can range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety.

Begonia castilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
