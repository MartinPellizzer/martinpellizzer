Bambusa wamin

Bambusa wamin, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green culms, which stand out against its lush, dark green foliage. This bamboo thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

The plant's dense clumping growth habit also provides habitat for various wildlife species.
