Bambusa calostachya

Bambusa calostachya, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-colored culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Bambusa calostachya is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
