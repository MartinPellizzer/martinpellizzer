Brassica napiformis

Brassica napiformis, commonly known as the turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, rounded root, which is the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a rough texture and a peppery aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods containing round, hard seeds.

It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutritious root and leafy greens.
