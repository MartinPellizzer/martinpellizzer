Begonia alta

Begonia alta, commonly known as the highland begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Begonia alta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
