Bambusa thouarsii

Bambusa thouarsii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and dense, bushy growth habit. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its rhizomes are extensively branched, allowing for rapid propagation and dense clumping growth.

Bambusa thouarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
