Bidens ocymifolia

Bidens ocymifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture that distinguishes it from similar species.
