Baccharis amplexicaulis

Baccharis amplexicaulis, commonly known as greenleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically bright green and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Baccharis amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
