Bacopa beccabunga

Bacopa beccabunga, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It is known for its ability to purify water and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Bacopa beccabunga is also a popular choice for aquatic gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
