Bulbophyllum vitiense

Bulbophyllum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs, which serve as storage organs for water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
