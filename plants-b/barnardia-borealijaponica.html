Barnardia borealijaponica

Barnardia borealijaponica, commonly known as the Japanese alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan and northern China.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Barnardia borealijaponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
