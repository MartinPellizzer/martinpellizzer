Biscutella didyma f. choulettei

Biscutella didyma f. choulettei, commonly known as the Choulette's biscuit flower, is a rare and attractive member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and distinctive, waxy leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique morphology and delicate appearance make it a sought-after addition to specialized botanical collections.
