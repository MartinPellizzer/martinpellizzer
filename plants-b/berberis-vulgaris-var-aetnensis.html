Berberis vulgaris var. aetnensis

Berberis vulgaris var. aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart and acidic. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
