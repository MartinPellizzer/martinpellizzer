Boopis andicola

Boopis andicola, commonly known as the Andean boopis, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Boopis andicola is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine environments.
