Barleria micrantha

Barleria micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Barleria micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
