Bromus hordeaceus var. tunetanus

Bromus hordeaceus var. tunetanus, commonly known as the Tunisian barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
