Berberis ludlowii var. capillaris

Berberis ludlowii var. capillaris, commonly known as the Capillary Barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
