Banisteria metallicolor var. sericea

Banisteria metallicolor var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf banisteria, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded or partially sunny conditions.
