Boerhavia coccinea var. paniculata

Boerhavia coccinea var. paniculata, commonly known as redroot lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically arranged in panicles.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
