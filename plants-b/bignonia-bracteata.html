Bignonia bracteata

Bignonia bracteata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia bracteata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
