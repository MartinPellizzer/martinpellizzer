Bidens tenera var. tetracera

Bidens tenera var. tetracera, commonly known as the four-rayed tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four ray florets, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
