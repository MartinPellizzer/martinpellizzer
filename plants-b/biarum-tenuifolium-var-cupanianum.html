Biarum tenuifolium var. cupanianum

Biarum tenuifolium var. cupanianum, commonly known as the cupanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. This species thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
