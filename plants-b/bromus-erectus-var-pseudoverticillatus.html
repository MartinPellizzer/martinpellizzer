Bromus erectus var. pseudoverticillatus

Bromus erectus var. pseudoverticillatus, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
