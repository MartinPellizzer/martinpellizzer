Bulbophyllum leniae

Bulbophyllum leniae, commonly known as the Leningrad orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's unique morphology.

Bulbophyllum leniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
