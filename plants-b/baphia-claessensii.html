Baphia claessensii

Baphia claessensii, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color.

It is often found in lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
