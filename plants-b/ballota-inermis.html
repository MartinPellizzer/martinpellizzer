Ballota inermis

Ballota inermis, commonly known as the Spanish Broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Ballota inermis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
