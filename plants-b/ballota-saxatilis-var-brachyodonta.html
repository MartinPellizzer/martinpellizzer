Ballota saxatilis var. brachyodonta

Ballota saxatilis var. brachyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-fruit ballota, is a perennial herb native to rocky and coastal areas of southern Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a narrow, oblong capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
