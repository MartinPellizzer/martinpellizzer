Bursinopetalum gardnerianum

Bursinopetalum gardnerianum, commonly known as the Gardner's bursera, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and smooth, often resinous bark. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
