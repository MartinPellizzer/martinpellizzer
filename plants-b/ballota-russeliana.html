Ballota russeliana

Ballota russeliana, commonly known as Russell's ballota, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or arid environments due to its drought tolerance.

Ballota russeliana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
