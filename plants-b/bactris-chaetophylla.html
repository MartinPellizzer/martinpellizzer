Bactris chaetophylla

Bactris chaetophylla, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatch.

Bactris chaetophylla is well adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
