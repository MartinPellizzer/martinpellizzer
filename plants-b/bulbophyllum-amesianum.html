Bulbophyllum amesianum

Bulbophyllum amesianum, commonly known as the Amesian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the wet season.

The leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
