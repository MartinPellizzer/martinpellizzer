Bulbophyllum apiculatum

Bulbophyllum apiculatum, commonly known as the apiculate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in shaded forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
