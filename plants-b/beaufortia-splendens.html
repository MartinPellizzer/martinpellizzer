Beaufortia splendens

Beaufortia splendens, commonly known as the splendour bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Beaufortia splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
