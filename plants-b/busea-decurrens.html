Busea decurrens

Busea decurrens, commonly known as the running busea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

The leaves and sap of Busea decurrens have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
