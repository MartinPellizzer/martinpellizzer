Bauhinia palenquensis

Bauhinia palenquensis, commonly known as the Mexican bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization and seed dispersal.
