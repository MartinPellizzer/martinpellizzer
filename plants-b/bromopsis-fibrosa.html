Bromopsis fibrosa

Bromopsis fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a fibrous texture. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Bromopsis fibrosa is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
