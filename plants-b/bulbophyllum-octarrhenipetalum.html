Bulbophyllum octarrhenipetalum

Bulbophyllum octarrhenipetalum, commonly known as the eight-lobed phallic orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive phallic shape, which is a common feature in many Bulbophyllum species. It typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments.

The orchid's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
