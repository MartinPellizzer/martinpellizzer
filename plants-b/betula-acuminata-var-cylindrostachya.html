Betula acuminata var. cylindrostachya

Betula acuminata var. cylindrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved birch, is a variant of the birch tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical strobili and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is adapted to moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
