Brongniartia riesebergii

Brongniartia riesebergii, commonly known as the African cycad, is a species of cycad native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and long lifespan, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
