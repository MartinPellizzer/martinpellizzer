Byttneria parallelinervis

Byttneria parallelinervis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves with parallel veins. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
