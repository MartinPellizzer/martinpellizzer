Barbarea brachycarpa subsp. robusta

Barbarea brachycarpa subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the broad-leafed winter cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a distinctive yellow or orange flower cluster at the top. Its flowers are small and arranged in a compact, branched inflorescence, and it produces elongated, segmented seed pods.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
