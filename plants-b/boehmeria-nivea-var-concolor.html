Boehmeria nivea var. concolor

Boehmeria nivea var. concolor, commonly known as Chinese hemp or mulberry fiber plant, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky white fibers that are harvested from the stems, which are used in textile production. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its high fiber yield and is cultivated for both industrial and traditional uses.
