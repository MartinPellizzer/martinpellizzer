Bignonia hibiscifolia

Bignonia hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Mexican trumpetflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red throat. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces long, cylindrical seed pods. Bignonia hibiscifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
