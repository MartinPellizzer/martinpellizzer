Berberis tischleri var. abbreviata

Berberis tischleri var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is also noted for its cold hardiness and adaptability to various light conditions.
