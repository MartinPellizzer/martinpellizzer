Bocconia glauca

Bocconia glauca, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Bocconia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
