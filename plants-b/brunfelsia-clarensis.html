Brunfelsia clarensis

Brunfelsia clarensis, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in the early morning, range in color from deep purple to white, and are known for their fragrant scent.
