Blechnum moritzianum

Blechnum moritzianum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often bright green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
