Banara fagifolia

Banara fagifolia, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Banara fagifolia is valued for its hard timber and is used in traditional medicine and crafts.
