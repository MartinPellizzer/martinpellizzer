Bidens connata f. ambiversa

Bidens connata f. ambiversa, commonly known as the two-sided tickseed, is a variant of the Bidens genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with ray-like petals and prominent central disk. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
