Bulbophyllum pedilochilus

Bulbophyllum pedilochilus, commonly known as the footless orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, and its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine. It is characterized by its unique leaf structure and has a complex root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This orchid is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in certain regions.
