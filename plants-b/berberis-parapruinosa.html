Berberis parapruinosa

Berberis parapruinosa, commonly known as the rough barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Berberis parapruinosa is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
