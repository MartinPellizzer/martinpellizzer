Bystropogon origanifolius

Bystropogon origanifolius, commonly known as the lemon thyme or lemon-scented shrub, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their distinctive lemony aroma.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
