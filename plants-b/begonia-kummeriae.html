Begonia kummeriae

Begonia kummeriae, commonly known as the Kummer's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to bronze hues, often featuring intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
