Byblis lindleyana

Byblis lindleyana, commonly known as the buttercup plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant possesses sticky, hair-like structures called tentacles that trap and digest small insects. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and requires consistent moisture.

Its unique appearance and predatory behavior make it a fascinating subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
