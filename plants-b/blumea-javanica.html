Blumea javanica

Blumea javanica, commonly known as the Java Blueflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
