Buergeria stellata

Buergeria stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

Buergeria stellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
