Bulbostylis guineensis

Bulbostylis guineensis, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a small, annual grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are minute and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

Bulbostylis guineensis is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to seasonal flooding.
