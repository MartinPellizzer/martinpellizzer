Begonia gamolepis

Begonia gamolepis, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
