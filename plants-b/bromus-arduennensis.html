Bromus arduennensis

Bromus arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennes bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the Pyrenees Mountains in Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Bromus arduennensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
