Begonia microcarpa var. acuta

Begonia microcarpa var. acuta, commonly known as the acute-leaved begonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
