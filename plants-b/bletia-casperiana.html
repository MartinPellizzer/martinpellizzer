Bletia casperiana

Bletia casperiana, commonly known as the Casper's bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

Bletia casperiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
