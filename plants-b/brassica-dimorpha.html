Brassica dimorpha

Brassica dimorpha, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lobed.

Brassica dimorpha is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
