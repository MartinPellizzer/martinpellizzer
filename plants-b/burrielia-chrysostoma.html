Burrielia chrysostoma

Burrielia chrysostoma, commonly known as the golden burrielia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has a woody stem with small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
