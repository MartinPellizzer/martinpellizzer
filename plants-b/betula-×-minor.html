Betula × minor

Betula × minor, commonly known as the river birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that displays a range of colors from white to reddish-brown. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

Betula × minor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
