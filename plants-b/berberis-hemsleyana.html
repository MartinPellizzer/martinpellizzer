Berberis hemsleyana

Berberis hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and bright orange-red berries that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.

Berberis hemsleyana is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains alkaloids similar to those found in other berberis species.
