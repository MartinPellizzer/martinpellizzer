Bosistoa euodiiformis

Bosistoa euodiiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contributes to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Bosistoa euodiiformis is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
