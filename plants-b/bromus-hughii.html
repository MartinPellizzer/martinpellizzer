Bromus hughii

Bromus hughii, commonly known as Hugh's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces dense clusters of spikelets at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, often with a waxy coating.

Bromus hughii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
