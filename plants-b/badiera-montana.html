Badiera montana

Badiera montana, commonly known as the mountain badiera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Badiera montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
