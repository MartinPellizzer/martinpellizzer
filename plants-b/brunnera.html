Brunnera

Brunnera, commonly known as Siberian bugloss, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in moist, shady environments. Brunnera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
