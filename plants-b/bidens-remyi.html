Bidens remyi

Bidens remyi, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with white markings. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens remyi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
