Begonia bruneiana subsp. angustifolia

Begonia bruneiana subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
