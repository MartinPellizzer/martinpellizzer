Bossekia fragarioides

Bossekia fragarioides, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows close to the ground, with a sprawling, rhizomatous stem that allows it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
