Bulbophyllum hirsutiusculum

Bulbophyllum hirsutiusculum, commonly known as the hairy-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
