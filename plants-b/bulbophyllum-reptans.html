Bulbophyllum reptans

Bulbophyllum reptans, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats.

Bulbophyllum reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
