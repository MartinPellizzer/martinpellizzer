Biophytum calophyllum

Biophytum calophyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red-tinged leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Biophytum calophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
