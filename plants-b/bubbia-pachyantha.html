Bubbia pachyantha

Bubbia pachyantha, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Bubbia pachyantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
