Begonia × ricinifolia maculata

Begonia × ricinifolia maculata, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits striking foliage with a mix of green, white, and pink patterns.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect sunlight. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.

It is often propagated through stem cuttings to maintain its variegated leaf patterns.
