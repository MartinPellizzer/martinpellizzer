Blumea glandulosa

Blumea glandulosa, commonly known as the rough blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as forests and grasslands.

Blumea glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
