Bargemontia clivicola

Bargemontia clivicola, commonly known as the cliff-dwelling bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Bargemontia clivicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for local pollinators.
