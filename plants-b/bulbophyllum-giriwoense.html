Bulbophyllum giriwoense

Bulbophyllum giriwoense, commonly known as the Giriwoen orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in its survival in shaded environments.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
