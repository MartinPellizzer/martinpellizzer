Bertolonia maculata var. aenea

Bertolonia maculata var. aenea, commonly known as the silverleaf bertolonia, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or grayish-blue hue, often marked with dark veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
