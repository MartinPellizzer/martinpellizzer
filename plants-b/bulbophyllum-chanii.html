Bulbophyllum chanii

Bulbophyllum chanii, commonly known as the Chan's orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental needs, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
