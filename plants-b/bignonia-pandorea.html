Bignonia pandorea

Bignonia pandorea, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is part of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
