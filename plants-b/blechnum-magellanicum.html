Blechnum magellanicum

Blechnum magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellan fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, rugged appearance that aids in its identification.
