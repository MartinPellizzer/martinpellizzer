Bulbophyllum bibundiense

Bulbophyllum bibundiense, commonly known as the Lizard Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
