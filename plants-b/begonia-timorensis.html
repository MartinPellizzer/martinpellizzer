Begonia timorensis

Begonia timorensis, commonly known as the Timor begonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow as a compact shrub or trailing plant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in tropical rainforests.
