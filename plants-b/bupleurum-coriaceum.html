Bupleurum coriaceum

Bupleurum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a woody base. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in early summer.

Bupleurum coriaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
