Bocconia frutescens var. subtomentosa

Bocconia frutescens var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf bocconia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage and compact growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
