Banisteria corymbosa

Banisteria corymbosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, branching clusters, giving it a coral-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria corymbosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
