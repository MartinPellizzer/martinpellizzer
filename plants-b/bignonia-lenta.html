Bignonia lenta

Bignonia lenta, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found climbing trees or shrubs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bignonia lenta is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
