Badilloa campii

Badilloa campii, commonly known as the Camp's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Badilloa campii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
