Barleria biloba

Barleria biloba, commonly known as the red false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer.

Barleria biloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
