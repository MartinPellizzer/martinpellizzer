Bulbophyllum proteranthum

Bulbophyllum proteranthum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble spikes. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it prefers bright, indirect light.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
