Bolanthus confertifolius

Bolanthus confertifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved bolanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and arranged in a compact, bushy form, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Bolanthus confertifolius is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
