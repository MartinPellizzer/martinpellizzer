Ballota somala

Ballota somala, commonly known as the Somalian ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ballota somala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
