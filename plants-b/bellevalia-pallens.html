Bellevalia pallens

Bellevalia pallens, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system.

Bellevalia pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
