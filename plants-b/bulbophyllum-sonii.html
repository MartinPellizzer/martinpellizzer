Bulbophyllum sonii

Bulbophyllum sonii, commonly known as the Son's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, which is typical for many epiphytic orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
