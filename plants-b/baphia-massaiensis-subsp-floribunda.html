Baphia massaiensis subsp. floribunda

Baphia massaiensis subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the Massai baphia, is a shrub native to East Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
