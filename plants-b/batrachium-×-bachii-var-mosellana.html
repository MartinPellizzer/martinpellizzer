Batrachium × bachii var. mosellana

Batrachium × bachii var. mosellana, commonly known as the Mosellan marsh orchid, is a hybrid plant native to the wetlands of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or boggy habitats. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
