Brassia forgetiana

Brassia forgetiana, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that help in pollination by specific insects.

Brassia forgetiana is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
