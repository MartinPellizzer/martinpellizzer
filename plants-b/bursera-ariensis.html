Bursera ariensis

Bursera ariensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces a resin that has been historically used for incense and medicinal purposes. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Bursera ariensis is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
