Bursera pontiveteris

Bursera pontiveteris, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
