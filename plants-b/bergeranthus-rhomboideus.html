Bergeranthus rhomboideus

Bergeranthus rhomboideus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
