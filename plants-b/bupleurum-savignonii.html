Bupleurum savignonii

Bupleurum savignonii, commonly known as Savignon's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Bupleurum savignonii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate and alpine climates.
