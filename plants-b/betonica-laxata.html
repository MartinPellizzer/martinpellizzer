Betonica laxata

Betonica laxata, commonly known as loose betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
