Bauhinia bassacensis var. backeri

Bauhinia bassacensis var. backeri, commonly known as the Cambodian bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use, while its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility.
