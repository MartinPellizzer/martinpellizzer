Baccharis speciosa

Baccharis speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Baccharis speciosa is often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
