Begonia multangula

Begonia multangula, commonly known as the multangula begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow as an upright shrub or trailing vine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia multangula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
