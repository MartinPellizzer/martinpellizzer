Benstonea calcinacta

Benstonea calcinacta, commonly known as the calcined stone plant, is a unique species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. Its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils makes it well adapted to harsh environments.

The name "calcinacta" refers to its association with calcium-rich substrates, highlighting its ecological niche.
