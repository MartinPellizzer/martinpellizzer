Bambos agrestis

Bambos agrestis, commonly known as the wild bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a tall, perennial plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its hollow, segmented stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and erosion control. Bambos agrestis is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which have been traditionally used in construction and crafts.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for reforestation and sustainable land management.
