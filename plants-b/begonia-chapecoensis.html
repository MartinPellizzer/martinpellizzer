Begonia chapecoensis

Begonia chapecoensis, commonly known as the Chapecoensis begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
