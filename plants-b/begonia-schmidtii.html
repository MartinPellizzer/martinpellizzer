Begonia schmidtii

Begonia schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins, often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
