Bromus lepidus f. lasiolepis

Bromus lepidus f. lasiolepis, commonly known as the silky brome, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. The leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. The plant produces small, two-rowed spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is a valuable plant for erosion control and provides forage for livestock in certain regions.
