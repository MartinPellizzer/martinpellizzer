Besleria uleana

Besleria uleana, commonly known as the Ulean spider orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence that consists of multiple small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the wet season.

Besleria uleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
