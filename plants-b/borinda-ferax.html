Borinda ferax

Borinda ferax, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has rough, fibrous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Borinda ferax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
