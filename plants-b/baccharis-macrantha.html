Baccharis macrantha

Baccharis macrantha, commonly known as the red panic grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Baccharis macrantha is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
