Berberis hallii var. typica

Berberis hallii var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features spiny stems and small, glossy green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for both landscape and herbal purposes.
