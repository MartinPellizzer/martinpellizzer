Bidens striata

Bidens striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, narrow, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into seed clusters. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.

Bidens striata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
