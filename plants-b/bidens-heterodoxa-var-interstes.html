Bidens heterodoxa var. interstes

Bidens heterodoxa var. interstes, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
