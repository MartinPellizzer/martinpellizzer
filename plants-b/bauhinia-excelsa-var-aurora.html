Bauhinia excelsa var. aurora

Bauhinia excelsa var. aurora, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn a vibrant yellow during the dry season. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia excelsa var. aurora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
