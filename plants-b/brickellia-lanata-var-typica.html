Brickellia lanata var. typica

Brickellia lanata var. typica, commonly known as woolly brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
