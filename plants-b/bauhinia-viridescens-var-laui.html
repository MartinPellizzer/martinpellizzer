Bauhinia viridescens var. laui

Bauhinia viridescens var. laui, commonly known as the greenleaf bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often a deep green and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
