Bromelia karatas

Bromelia karatas, commonly known as the Karatas bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The leaves are typically broad and pointed, with a waxy texture that helps in water retention. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
