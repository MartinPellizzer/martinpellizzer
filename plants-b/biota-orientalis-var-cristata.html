Biota orientalis var. cristata

Biota orientalis var. cristata, commonly known as the crested or Oriental biota, is a unique variant of the Biota orientalis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branched growth form, which resembles a crest or a crown. This plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is usually dense and dark green, with a velvety texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its unusual appearance and ability to thrive in specific microclimates.
