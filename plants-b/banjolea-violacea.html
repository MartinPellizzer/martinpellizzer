Banjolea violacea

Banjolea violacea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive coloration.

Banjolea violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
