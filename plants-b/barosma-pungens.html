Barosma pungens

Barosma pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf barosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Barosma pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
