Bulbophyllum elephantinum

Bulbophyllum elephantinum, commonly known as the elephant's tusk orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs that resemble elephant tusks, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
