Baccharis cochensis

Baccharis cochensis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Baccharis cochensis is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
