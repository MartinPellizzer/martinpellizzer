Breynia officinalis var. accrescens

Breynia officinalis var. accrescens, commonly known as the creeping breynia, is a succulent shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features fleshy, branching stems that often grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a waxy, glossy surface and a distinctive bluish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, blooming during the warmer months.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.
