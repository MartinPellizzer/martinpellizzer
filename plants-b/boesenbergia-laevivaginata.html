Boesenbergia laevivaginata

Boesenbergia laevivaginata, commonly known as the narrow sheath ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and possesses long, narrow leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing similarities with other gingers.
