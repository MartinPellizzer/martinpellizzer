Betula pendula var. microlepis

Betula pendula var. microlepis, commonly known as the weeping birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, oval, and have a smooth margin, turning gold in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its unique form.
