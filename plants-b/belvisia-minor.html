Belvisia minor

Belvisia minor, commonly known as the lesser belvisia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Belvisia minor is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
