Blechnum mochaenum

Blechnum mochaenum, commonly known as the Mocha fern, is a species of fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often covered in a fine layer of brownish hair.

Blechnum mochaenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
