Begonia hirsuta

Begonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
