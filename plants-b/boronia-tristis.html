Boronia tristis

Boronia tristis, commonly known as the weeping boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Boronia tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
