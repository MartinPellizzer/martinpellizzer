Boronia prolixa

Boronia prolixa, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often emitting a citrus-like scent when crushed.

Boronia prolixa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
