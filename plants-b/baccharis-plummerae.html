Baccharis plummerae

Baccharis plummerae, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in desert environments.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
