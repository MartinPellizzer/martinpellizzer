Butea sericophylla

Butea sericophylla, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral displays that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, elongated leaflets and produces pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
