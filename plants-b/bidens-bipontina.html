Bidens bipontina

Bidens bipontina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Bidens bipontina is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes.
