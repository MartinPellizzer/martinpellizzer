Berkheya rhapontica

Berkheya rhapontica, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its scientific name honors the botanist Johann Berkhey, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
