Besha

Besha, commonly known as the "bushy plant" or *Sida acuta*, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.
