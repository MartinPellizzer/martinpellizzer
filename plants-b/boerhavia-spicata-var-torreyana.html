Boerhavia spicata var. torreyana

Boerhavia spicata var. torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
