Bignonia corymbosa

Bignonia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 15 feet tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are borne in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia corymbosa is known for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
