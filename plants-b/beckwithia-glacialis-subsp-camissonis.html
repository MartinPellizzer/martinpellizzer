Beckwithia glacialis subsp. camissonis

Beckwithia glacialis subsp.

camissonis, commonly known as the Sierra snowbell, is a rare and cold-adapted plant species native to high-elevation areas of California. It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear.

The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested slopes.
