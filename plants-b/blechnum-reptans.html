Blechnum reptans

Blechnum reptans, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread along the ground and form dense mats. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and they often have a pale green to bluish-green coloration. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a distinctive component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
