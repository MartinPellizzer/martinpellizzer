Bactris cuspidata var. mitis

Bactris cuspidata var. mitis, commonly known as the pink pineapple.

This plant is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces edible, pink-colored fruits. The leaves are long, sword-shaped, and covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, flavorful fruit.
