Bothriochloa ischaemum f. songarica

Bothriochloa ischaemum f. songarica, commonly known as the Songarica barnyard grass, is a variant of the barnyard grass species native to Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and floodplains, and is often found in disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
