Bauhinia coulteri

Bauhinia coulteri, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, fern-like segments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia coulteri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
