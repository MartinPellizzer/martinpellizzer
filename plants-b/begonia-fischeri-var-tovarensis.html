Begonia fischeri var. tovarensis

Begonia fischeri var. tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis begonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with contrasting patterns. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store moisture, reflecting its succulent nature. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued by botanists and horticulturists alike.
