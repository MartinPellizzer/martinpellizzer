Bauhinia candicans

Bauhinia candicans, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, flame-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches. Bauhinia candicans thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
