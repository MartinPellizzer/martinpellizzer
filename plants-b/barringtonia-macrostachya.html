Barringtonia macrostachya

Barringtonia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant candle bush, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble candles, with a white color and a strong, sweet scent. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces a distinctive fruit that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
