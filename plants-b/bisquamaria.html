Bisquamaria

Bisquamaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, ray florets that resemble holly leaves, giving it its common name. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments. Bisquamaria species are perennial herbs that thrive in maritime climates, often forming dense mats along rocky shores.

Their unique morphology and ecological niche make them important components of coastal ecosystems.
