Baccharis weddellii

Baccharis weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
