Begonia epibaterium var. ipomoeifolia

Begonia epibaterium var. ipomoeifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaf begonia, is a unique variant of the begonia genus characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its foliage is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its cascading nature.

The leaves are thick and waxy, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat.
