Bigelowia diffusa

Bigelowia diffusa, commonly known as the bluebell flower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
