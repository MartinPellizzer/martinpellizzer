Bignonia fagoides

Bignonia fagoides, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs.

Bignonia fagoides is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
