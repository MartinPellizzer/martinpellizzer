Barleria calophylla

Barleria calophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Barleria calophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
