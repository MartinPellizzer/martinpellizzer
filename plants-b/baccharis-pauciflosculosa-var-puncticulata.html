Baccharis pauciflosculosa var. puncticulata

Baccharis pauciflosculosa var. puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flowers that are often covered in fine, punctuated markings. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
