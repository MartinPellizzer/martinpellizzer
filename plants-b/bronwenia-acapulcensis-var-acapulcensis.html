Bronwenia acapulcensis var. acapulcensis

Bronwenia acapulcensis var. acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco firebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has thick, succulent-like leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
