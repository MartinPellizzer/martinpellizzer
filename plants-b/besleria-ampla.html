Besleria ampla

Besleria ampla, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Besleria ampla is considered a conservation concern.
