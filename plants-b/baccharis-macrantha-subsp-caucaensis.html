Baccharis macrantha subsp. caucaensis

Baccharis macrantha subsp.

caucaensis, commonly known as the Cauca baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a woody stem that supports its upright growth habit.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
