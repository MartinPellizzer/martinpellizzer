Bunchosia bonplandiana

Bunchosia bonplandiana, commonly known as the bonplandian burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its dispersal in dry landscapes.

Bunchosia bonplandiana is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils and can withstand prolonged drought conditions.
