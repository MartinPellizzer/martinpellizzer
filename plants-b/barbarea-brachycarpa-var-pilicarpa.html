Barbarea brachycarpa var. pilicarpa

Barbarea brachycarpa var. pilicarpa, commonly known as the dwarf winter cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy habitats or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces small, round seed pods that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
