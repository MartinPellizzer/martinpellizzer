Bobaea

Bobaea, commonly known as the Brazilian burr bean, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America, particularly Brazil. These plants are characterized by their hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and the pods are usually hairy and contain several seeds.

Bobaea species are often found in dry, sandy soils and are adapted to arid environments.
