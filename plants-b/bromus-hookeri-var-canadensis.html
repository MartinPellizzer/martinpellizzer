Bromus hookeri var. canadensis

Bromus hookeri var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed sites and can be an indicator of wetland or riparian environments.
