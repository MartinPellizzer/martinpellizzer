Bambusa alphonse-karrii

Bambusa alphonse-karrii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
