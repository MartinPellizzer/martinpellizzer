Bolelia insignis

Bolelia insignis, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of pale yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in star-like formations at the stem tips. Bolelia insignis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems that store water efficiently.

Its striking appearance and specialized adaptations make it a notable species in botanical studies.
