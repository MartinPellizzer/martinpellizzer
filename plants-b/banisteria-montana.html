Banisteria montana

Banisteria montana, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its striking, vivid red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
