Babiana scabrifolia var. declinata

Babiana scabrifolia var. declinata, commonly known as the Cape bell orchid, is a striking South African plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces vibrant orange to red blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with good drainage and full sun.
