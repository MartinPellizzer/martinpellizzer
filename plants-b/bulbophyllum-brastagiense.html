Bulbophyllum brastagiense

Bulbophyllum brastagiense, commonly known as the Brastagiense orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid or seasonally dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
