Boea cochinchinensis

Boea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Boea cochinchinensis is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
