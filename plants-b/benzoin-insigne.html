Benzoin insigne

Benzoin insigne, commonly known as the sacred balsam, is a small evergreen tree native to the western tropical Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clusters of dark red berries. Its resin, known as benzoin, is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in incense and traditional medicine.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting a variety of wildlife.
