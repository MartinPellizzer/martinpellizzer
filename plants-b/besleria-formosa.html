Besleria formosa

Besleria formosa, commonly known as the rainbow begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by its vibrant foliage. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
