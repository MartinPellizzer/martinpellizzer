Brassica amplexicaulis subsp. souliei

Brassica amplexicaulis subsp.

souliei, commonly known as the southern Chinese cabbage, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are tightly clustered at the base.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine, adding a mild, peppery flavor.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves and slightly different growth habits compared to other Brassica species.
