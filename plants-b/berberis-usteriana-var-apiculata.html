Berberis usteriana var. apiculata

Berberis usteriana var. apiculata, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil conditions contribute to its adaptability in different climates.
