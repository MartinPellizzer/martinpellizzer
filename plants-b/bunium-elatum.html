Bunium elatum

Bunium elatum, commonly known as wild fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. Its seeds are small, dry, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive benefits.
