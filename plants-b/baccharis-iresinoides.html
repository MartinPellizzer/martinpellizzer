Baccharis iresinoides

Baccharis iresinoides, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a resinous scent. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in traditional herbal practices.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and grasslands, and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature.
