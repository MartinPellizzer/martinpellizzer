Bursaria spinosa subsp. lasiophylla

Bursaria spinosa subsp.

lasiophylla, commonly known as the glossy leaf bursaria, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glossy, dark green leaves that are more elongated compared to other subspecies. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of local ecosystems.
