Bocagea thwaitesii

Bocagea thwaitesii, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are a food source for various wildlife, including birds. Bocagea thwaitesii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
