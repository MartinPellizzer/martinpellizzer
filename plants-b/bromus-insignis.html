Bromus insignis

Bromus insignis, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Bromus insignis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
