Banisteria bicolor

Banisteria bicolor, commonly known as the blue banana plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are often accompanied by contrasting white or yellow petals. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Banisteria bicolor is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
