Bothriocline tomentosa

Bothriocline tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Bothriocline tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
