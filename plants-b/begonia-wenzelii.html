Begonia wenzelii

Begonia wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
