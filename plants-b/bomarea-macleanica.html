Bomarea macleanica

Bomarea macleanica, commonly known as the Maclean's bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy inflorescences that bloom during the cooler months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
