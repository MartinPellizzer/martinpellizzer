Binaria rubiginosa

Binaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty borage, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.
