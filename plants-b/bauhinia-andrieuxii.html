Bauhinia andrieuxii

Bauhinia andrieuxii, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that turn reddish in color as they age. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom during the dry season. Its ornamental value is due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.

Bauhinia andrieuxii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
