Brighamia citrina var. napoliensis

Brighamia citrina var. napoliensis, commonly known as the Naples Brighamia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a flagship species for conservation efforts in Hawaii.
