Bonia saxatilis var. solida

Bonia saxatilis var. solida, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
