Brachychiton populneus

Brachychiton populneus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Brachychiton populneus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
