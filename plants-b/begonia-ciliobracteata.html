Begonia ciliobracteata

Begonia ciliobracteata, commonly known as the velvety begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
