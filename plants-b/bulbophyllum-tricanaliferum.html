Bulbophyllum tricanaliferum

Bulbophyllum tricanaliferum, commonly known as the three-channeled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces multiple flowering shoots with small, greenish-white flowers. Its unique botanical characteristics include a complex inflorescence and specialized root structures adapted for nutrient absorption.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
