Bignonia leucoxylon

Bignonia leucoxylon, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in appearance. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Bignonia leucoxylon is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
