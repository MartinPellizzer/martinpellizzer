Begonia phuthoensis

Begonia phuthoensis, commonly known as the Phutho's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking foliage and vibrant flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in its natural habitat.
