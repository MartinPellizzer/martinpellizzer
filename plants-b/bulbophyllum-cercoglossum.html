Bulbophyllum cercoglossum

Bulbophyllum cercoglossum, commonly known as the coral-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, coral-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often clustered along a branched stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bulbophyllum cercoglossum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
