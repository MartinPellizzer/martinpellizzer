Bartlettina Hemisphaerica

Bartlettina hemisphaerica is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high altitudes of the Andes. It is known for its unique hemispherical shape and striking blue-green foliage. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its scientific classification places it within the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family. Despite its beauty, it remains poorly studied and is considered a botanical curiosity.
