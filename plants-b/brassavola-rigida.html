Brassavola rigida

Brassavola rigida, commonly known as the rigid lady's slipper, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, typically growing in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive purple and white coloration. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and the plant often grows as an epiphyte or on rocky surfaces.

Brassavola rigida requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
