Blumea velutina

Blumea velutina, commonly known as the velvet blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Blumea velutina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
