Brassica chinensis f. chifu

Brassica chinensis f. chifu, commonly known as Chinese kale or Gyokuru, is a cultivated variant of the Chinese cabbage.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are tender and flavorful. The plant is typically grown in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with adequate moisture. It is a biennial plant that is usually harvested in its first year for its edible leaves.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used in Asian cuisines.
