Betula × caerulea

Betula × caerulea, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silvery hue beneath, and its delicate, drooping catkins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in temperate climates.

Betula × caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
