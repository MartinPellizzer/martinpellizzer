Billbergia splendida

Billbergia splendida, commonly known as the Splendid Billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround the central rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant forms a tight rosette that collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Billbergia splendida is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
