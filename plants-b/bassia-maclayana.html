Bassia maclayana

Bassia maclayana, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bassia maclayana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.
