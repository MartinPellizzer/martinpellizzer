Bunium nilghirense

Bunium nilghirense, commonly known as Nilgiri fennel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its mountainous habitat.
