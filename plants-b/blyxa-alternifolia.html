Blyxa alternifolia

Blyxa alternifolia, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily across the substrate.

Blyxa alternifolia is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
