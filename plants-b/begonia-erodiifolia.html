Begonia erodiifolia

Begonia erodiifolia, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features trailing stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and continuous blooming habit.
