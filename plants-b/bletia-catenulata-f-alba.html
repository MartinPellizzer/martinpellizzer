Bletia catenulata f. alba

Bletia catenulata f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered bletia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike that bears multiple blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
