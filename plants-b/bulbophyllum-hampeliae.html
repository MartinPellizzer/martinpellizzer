Bulbophyllum hampeliae

Bulbophyllum hampeliae, commonly known as the Hampeliae orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and colors that range from white to pink and yellow.
