Bia fendleri

Bia fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red to orange flowers that bloom throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Bia fendleri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ability to withstand drought conditions make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
