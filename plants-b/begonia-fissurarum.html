Begonia fissurarum

Begonia fissurarum, commonly known as the fissured begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that often display a variegated pattern of green and silver. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
