Bystropogon origanifolius var. canariae

Bystropogon origanifolius var. canariae, commonly known as the Canary Island sage, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
