Biscutella ciliata var. applanata

Biscutella ciliata var. applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive waxy coating.
