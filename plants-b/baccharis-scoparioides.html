Baccharis scoparioides

Baccharis scoparioides, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Baccharis scoparioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
