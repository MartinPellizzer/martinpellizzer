Buxus sempervirens var. microphylla

Buxus sempervirens var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, dense, dark green leaves that are typically no more than 5 millimeters in length. The plant is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to maintain a neat, rounded shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.
