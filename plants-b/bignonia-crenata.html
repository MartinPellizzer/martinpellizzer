Bignonia crenata

Bignonia crenata, commonly known as the creeping yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with wavy margins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia crenata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
