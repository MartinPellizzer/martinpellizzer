Bulbophyllum capillipes

Bulbophyllum capillipes, commonly known as the hair-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, hairy leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum capillipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
