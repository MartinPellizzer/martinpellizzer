Berberis guimpelii

Berberis guimpelii, commonly known as the Guimpel barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
