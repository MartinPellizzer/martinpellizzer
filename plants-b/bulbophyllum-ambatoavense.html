Bulbophyllum ambatoavense

Bulbophyllum ambatoavense, commonly known as the Ambatoa Valley orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
