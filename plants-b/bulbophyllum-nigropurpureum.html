Bulbophyllum nigropurpureum

Bulbophyllum nigropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to blackish flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, with a unique coloration that sets it apart from other orchid species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
