Bromopsis pseudolaevipes

Bromopsis pseudolaevipes, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Bromopsis pseudolaevipes is often found in areas with high humidity and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
