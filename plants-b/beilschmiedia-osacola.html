Beilschmiedia osacola

Beilschmiedia osacola, commonly known as the Osa mountain beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Osa Peninsula in Costa Rica.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland to montane forests.

Due to its restricted range and habitat fragmentation, it is considered vulnerable to ecological threats.
