Bartsia scabra

Bartsia scabra, commonly known as rough bartsia, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a rough or hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Bartsia scabra is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
