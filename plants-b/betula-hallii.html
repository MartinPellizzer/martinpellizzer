Betula hallii

Betula hallii, commonly known as the Mexican birch, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, triangular leaves. The tree produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. Betula hallii is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
