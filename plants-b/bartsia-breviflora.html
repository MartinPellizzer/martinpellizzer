Bartsia breviflora

Bartsia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered bartsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Bartsia breviflora is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, including a specialized calyx that aids in seed dispersal.
