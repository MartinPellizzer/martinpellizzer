Barroetea pavonii

Barroetea pavonii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, cascading clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Barroetea pavonii is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its native habitats.
