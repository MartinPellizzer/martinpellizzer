Bulbocodium robustum

Bulbocodium robustum, commonly known as the robust bulbous gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually grass-like and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Bulbocodium robustum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
