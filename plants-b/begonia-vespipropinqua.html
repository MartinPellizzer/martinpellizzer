Begonia vespipropinqua

Begonia vespipropinqua, commonly known as the yellow-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
