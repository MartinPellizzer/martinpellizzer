Bromus polyanthus f. parisiana

Bromus polyanthus f. parisiana, commonly known as the Parisian bulbous brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, spiky inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive clumps.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to alpine and rock gardens.
