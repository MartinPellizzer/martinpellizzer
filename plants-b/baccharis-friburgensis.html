Baccharis friburgensis

Baccharis friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburgensis Baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer regions.
