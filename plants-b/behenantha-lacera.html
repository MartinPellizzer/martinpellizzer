Behenantha lacera

Behenantha lacera, commonly known as the yellow candelabra, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its branching structure and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its root system is known for producing a fatty substance used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.
