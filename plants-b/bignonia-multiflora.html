Bignonia multiflora

Bignonia multiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
