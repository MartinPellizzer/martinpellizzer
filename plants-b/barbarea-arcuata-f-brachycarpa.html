Barbarea arcuata f. brachycarpa

Barbarea arcuata f. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a short, spherical silique that contains several seeds.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
