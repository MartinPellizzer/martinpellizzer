Besleria concolor

Besleria concolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
