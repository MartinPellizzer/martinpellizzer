Bignonia chrysoleuca

Bignonia chrysoleuca, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia chrysoleuca is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.
