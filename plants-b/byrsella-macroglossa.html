Byrsella macroglossa

Byrsella macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers feature large, tongue-shaped petals and are often brightly colored, attracting specific pollinators.

The botanical structure of Byrsella macroglossa includes a sympodial growth habit and a system of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
