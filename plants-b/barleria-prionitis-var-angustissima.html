Barleria prionitis var. angustissima

Barleria prionitis var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
