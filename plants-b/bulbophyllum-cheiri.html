Bulbophyllum cheiri

Bulbophyllum cheiri, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
