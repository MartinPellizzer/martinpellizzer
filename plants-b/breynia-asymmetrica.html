Breynia asymmetrica

Breynia asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric breynia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, asymmetric growth pattern, with branches that twist and curve in unusual directions. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, helping it retain moisture in dry environments. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Breynia asymmetrica is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
