Bauhinia subclavata

Bauhinia subclavata, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that are often pink or reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring and summer seasons. Bauhinia subclavata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
