Brachiaria secernenda

Brachiaria secernenda, commonly known as slender brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, low-growing growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria secernenda is often found in disturbed soils and is used in pastureland due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Its ecological significance includes soil stabilization and serving as forage for livestock.
