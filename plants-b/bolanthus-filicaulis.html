Bolanthus filicaulis

Bolanthus filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved flax or false flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Bolanthus filicaulis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
