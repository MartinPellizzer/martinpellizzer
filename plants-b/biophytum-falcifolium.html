Biophytum falcifolium

Biophytum falcifolium, commonly known as the sickle-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
