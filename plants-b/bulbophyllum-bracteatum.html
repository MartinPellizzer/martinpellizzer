Bulbophyllum bracteatum

Bulbophyllum bracteatum, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and adapted to store water, aiding survival in humid, tropical environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
