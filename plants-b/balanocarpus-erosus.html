Balanocarpus erosus

Balanocarpus erosus, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that thrives in brackish water and saline environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its roots are often submerged, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Balanocarpus erosus plays a vital role in coastal ecological balance and is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
