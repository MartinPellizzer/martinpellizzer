Brassica fruticulosa

Brassica fruticulosa, commonly known as the prickly mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, sometimes spiny leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are small and contain compounds that can be used in traditional medicine.
