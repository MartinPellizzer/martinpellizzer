Biscutella stenophylla subsp. leptophylla

Biscutella stenophylla subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
