Becium obovatum var. macrocaulon

Becium obovatum var. macrocaulon, commonly known as the large-stemmed becium, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

It features alternate, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid climates.
