Biarum macroglossum

Biarum macroglossum, commonly known as the large-tongue arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of southern Florida.

It features a showy, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a deep purple spathe and a central yellow spadix, which attracts pollinators like beetles and flies. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize wetland areas effectively. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture to withstand the humid environment.

Biarum macroglossum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
