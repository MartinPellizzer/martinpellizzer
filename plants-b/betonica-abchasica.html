Betonica abchasica

Betonica abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian betony, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Betonica abchasica is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal properties.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
