Boehmeria mauritiana var. luxurians

Boehmeria mauritiana var. luxurians, commonly known as the giant bamboo grass, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous structure, which has been historically used in textile production.
