Blumea chamissoniana

Blumea chamissoniana, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
