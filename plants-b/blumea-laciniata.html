Blumea laciniata

Blumea laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blumea laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
