Barrotia decumbens

Barrotia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping barrotia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous species with thin, sprawling stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Barrotia decumbens is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
