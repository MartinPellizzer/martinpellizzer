Begonia brevipetala var. luteyniorum

Begonia brevipetala var. luteyniorum, commonly known as the Luteyn's short-petioled begonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green ovate leaves with prominent veins and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in spring and early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded borders or as a container plant.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is a sought-after species among begonia enthusiasts.
