Bromopsis tomentella subsp. woronowii

Bromopsis tomentella subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the woolly bromeliad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps retain moisture. Bromopsis tomentella subsp.

woronowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
