Baccharis mornicola

Baccharis mornicola, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis mornicola is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
