Begonia vaccinioides

Begonia vaccinioides, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a deep red margin and its clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings due to its ornamental value.

Begonia vaccinioides is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and spread in its natural habitat.
