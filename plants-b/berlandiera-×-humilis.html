Berlandiera × humilis

Berlandiera × humilis, commonly known as the hybrid Indian blanket flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Berlandiera × humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
