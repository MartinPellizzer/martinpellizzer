Buchnera linearis var. asperata

Buchnera linearis var. asperata, commonly known as the roughleaf bucknera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, slightly hairy leaves that give it a distinctive rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Buchnera linearis var. asperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
