Bulbophyllum subium

Bulbophyllum subium, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. Its inflorescence typically consists of a raceme of small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
