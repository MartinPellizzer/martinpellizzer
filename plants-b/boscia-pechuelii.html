Boscia pechuelii

Boscia pechuelii, commonly known as the white bosco, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Boscia pechuelii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
