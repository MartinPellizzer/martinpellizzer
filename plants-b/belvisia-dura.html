Belvisia dura

Belvisia dura, commonly known as the hardy belvisia, is a rare and resilient plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle beauty to its surroundings. Belvisia dura has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic flora.
