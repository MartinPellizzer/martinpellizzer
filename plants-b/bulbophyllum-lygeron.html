Bulbophyllum lygeron

Bulbophyllum lygeron, commonly known as the Lygeron orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with red or brown markings. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Bulbophyllum lygeron thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
