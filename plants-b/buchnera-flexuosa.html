Buchnera flexuosa

Buchnera flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible waxyplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Buchnera flexuosa is often found growing in rocky soils or along dry streambeds.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
