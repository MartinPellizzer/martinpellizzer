Boberella rhombifolia

Boberella rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhomboid leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhombus-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its root system is fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.

Boberella rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
