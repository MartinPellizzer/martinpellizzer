Bulbophyllum charisae

Bulbophyllum charisae, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, with a unique, intricate pattern on the petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its humid, low-light habitat.

Bulbophyllum charisae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
