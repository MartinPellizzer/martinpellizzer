Balsamita audibertii

Balsamita audibertii, commonly known as Audibert's balsam, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, and its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Balsamita audibertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
