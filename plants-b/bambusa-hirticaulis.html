Bambusa hirticaulis

Bambusa hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Bambusa hirticaulis is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
