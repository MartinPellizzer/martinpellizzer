Brachyrhynchos

Brachyrhynchos, commonly known as the short-nosed seed beetle, is a small insect belonging to the family Bruchidae.

It is primarily associated with leguminous plants, where its larvae feed on seeds. The beetle's life cycle is closely tied to the host plants, with adults often found on the pods of plants like beans and peas. Its short snout and robust body are adaptations for accessing and feeding on seed structures.

The species plays a role in seed dispersal and decomposition in its ecosystem.
