Begonia vallicola

Begonia vallicola, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 1,500 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Begonia vallicola is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
