Barleria ventricosa

Barleria ventricosa, commonly known as the African bellviolet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from pink to purple and are arranged in spikes.

Barleria ventricosa is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
