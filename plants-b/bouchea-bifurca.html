Bouchea bifurca

Bouchea bifurca, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Bouchea bifurca is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
