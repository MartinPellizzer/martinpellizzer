Brachylepis hispidula

Brachylepis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy brachylepis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which help reduce water loss in humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Brachylepis hispidula produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
