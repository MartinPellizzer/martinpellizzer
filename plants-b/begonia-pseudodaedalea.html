Begonia pseudodaedalea

Begonia pseudodaedalea, commonly known as the cascading begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
