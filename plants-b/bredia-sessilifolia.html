Bredia sessilifolia

Bredia sessilifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bredia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in sunny conditions. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bredia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
