Begonia sympodialis

Begonia sympodialis, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
