Bashania fangiana

Bashania fangiana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps, with long, narrow leaves that are a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green inflorescences that are often overlooked but contribute to its ecological value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Bashania fangiana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticultural settings.
