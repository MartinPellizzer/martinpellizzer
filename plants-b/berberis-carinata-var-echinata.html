Berberis carinata var. echinata

Berberis carinata var. echinata, commonly known as the spiked barberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
