Baccharis mylodontis

Baccharis mylodontis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Baccharis mylodontis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
