Boehmeria macrophylla var. zeylanica

Boehmeria macrophylla var. zeylanica, commonly known as Ceylon hemp, is a flowering plant in the nettle family, Urticaceae.

It is native to southern Asia, particularly Sri Lanka, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fibers are used in traditional textiles and papermaking, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The plant has a shallow root system and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
