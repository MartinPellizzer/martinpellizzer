Blumea hirsuta

Blumea hirsuta, commonly known as hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Blumea hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
