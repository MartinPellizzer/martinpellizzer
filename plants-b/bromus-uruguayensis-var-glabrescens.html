Bromus uruguayensis var. glabrescens

Bromus uruguayensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are typically narrow and smooth, with a bluish-green color, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variant is notable for its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Bromus uruguayensis.
