Billardiera procumbens

Billardiera procumbens, commonly known as the trailing candle bush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that often trail along the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Billardiera procumbens is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and attractive plant for garden use.
