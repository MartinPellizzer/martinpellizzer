Bauhinia pachyphylla var. wenzelii

Bauhinia pachyphylla var. wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, papery leaves that are often divided into lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
