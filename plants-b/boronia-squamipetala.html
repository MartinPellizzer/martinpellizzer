Boronia squamipetala

Boronia squamipetala, commonly known as the silver-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It typically grows in heathland and woodland environments, often in well-drained soils.

Boronia squamipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
