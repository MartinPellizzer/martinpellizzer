Barbacenia aurea

Barbacenia aurea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, branched inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Barbacenia aurea is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and ability to add a tropical feel to gardens.
