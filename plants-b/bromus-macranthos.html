Bromus macranthos

Bromus macranthos, commonly known as the tall brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Bromus macranthos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
