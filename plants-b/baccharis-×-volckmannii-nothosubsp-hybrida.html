Baccharis × volckmannii nothosubsp. hybrida

Baccharis × volckmannii nothosubsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in xeriscaped gardens. Its adaptability to arid conditions and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with limited water resources.

The plant also serves as a food source for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
