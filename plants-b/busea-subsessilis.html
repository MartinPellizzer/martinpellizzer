Busea subsessilis

Busea subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved busea, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Busea subsessilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
