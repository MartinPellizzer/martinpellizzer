Blepharis ciliaris

Blepharis ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale blue. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Blepharis ciliaris is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
