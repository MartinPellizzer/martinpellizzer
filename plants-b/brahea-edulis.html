Brahea edulis

Brahea edulis, commonly known as the edible palmetto, is a species of palm native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of stiff, blue-gray leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits containing a single black seed. These fruits have been historically used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.

Brahea edulis is well adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
