Brassica deflexa var. glabrescens

Brassica deflexa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its glabrous (hairless) leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for certain insects.
