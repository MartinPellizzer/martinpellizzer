Baccharis ericoides

Baccharis ericoides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Baccharis ericoides is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
