Begonia multijugata

Begonia multijugata, commonly known as the elephant's ear begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The leaves are typically a deep green with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental species.
