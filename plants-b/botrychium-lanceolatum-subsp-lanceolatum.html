Botrychium lanceolatum subsp. lanceolatum

Botrychium lanceolatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate moonwort, is a small, terrestrial fern that belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped sporangiophores that are often covered in a dense layer of spores.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually reduced to a narrow, blade-like structure, with the sporangia borne on specialized stalks.

This species is notable for its primitive morphology and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
