Bupleurum longifolium var. vapincense

Bupleurum longifolium var. vapincense, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is valued in horticulture and herbal practices.
