Betonica alopecuros

Betonica alopecuros, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Betonica alopecuros is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
