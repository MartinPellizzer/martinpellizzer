Bignonia lilacina

Bignonia lilacina, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically lavender to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
