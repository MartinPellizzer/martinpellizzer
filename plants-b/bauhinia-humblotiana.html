Bauhinia humblotiana

Bauhinia humblotiana, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the dry season. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia humblotiana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
