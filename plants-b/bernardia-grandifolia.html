Bernardia grandifolia

Bernardia grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflorous bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pleasant fragrance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
