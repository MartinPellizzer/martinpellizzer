Bulbophyllum subclausum

Bulbophyllum subclausum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed torch orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it is known for its long-lasting blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
