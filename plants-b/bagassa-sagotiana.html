Bagassa sagotiana

Bagassa sagotiana, commonly known as the Solomon Islands palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits. Its growth habit makes it well-suited for coastal and humid environments.
