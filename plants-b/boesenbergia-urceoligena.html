Boesenbergia urceoligena

Boesenbergia urceoligena, commonly known as the "water bamboo" or "false ginger," is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Boesenbergia urceoligena thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
