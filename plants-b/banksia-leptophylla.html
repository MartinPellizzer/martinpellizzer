Banksia leptophylla

Banksia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a species native to southwestern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. Its flowers are usually orange or yellow and are arranged in dense clusters.

Banksia leptophylla plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for a variety of native wildlife.
