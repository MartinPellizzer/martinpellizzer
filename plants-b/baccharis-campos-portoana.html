Baccharis campos-portoana

Baccharis campos-portoana, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its stems are woody and branched.

Baccharis campos-portoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
