Begonia sutherlandii

Begonia sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
