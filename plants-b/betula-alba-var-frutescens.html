Betula alba var. frutescens

Betula alba var. frutescens, commonly known as the silver birch variant, is a shrub-like form of the common white birch.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and papery, peeling bark that reveals a white, smooth inner layer. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn golden in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
