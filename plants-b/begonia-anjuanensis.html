Begonia anjuanensis

Begonia anjuanensis, commonly known as the Anjuan begonia, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and deep green color. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
