Brossaea tomentosa

Brossaea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly brossaea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Brossaea tomentosa is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
