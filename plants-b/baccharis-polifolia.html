Baccharis polifolia

Baccharis polifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties. It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
