Brachyotum microdon

Brachyotum microdon, commonly known as the small-leaved brachyotum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
