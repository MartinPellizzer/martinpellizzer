Brassica campestris stirps napobrassica

Brassica campestris stirps napobrassica, commonly known as wild mustard, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are silique pods containing several small, round seeds.

This species is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
