Brosimum velutinum

Brosimum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or red in color.

Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
