Bernardia oligandra

Bernardia oligandra, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
