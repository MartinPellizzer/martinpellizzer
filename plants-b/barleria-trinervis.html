Barleria trinervis

Barleria trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Barleria trinervis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
