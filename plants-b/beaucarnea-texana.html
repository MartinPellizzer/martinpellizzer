Beaucarnea texana

Beaucarnea texana, commonly known as the Texas torch tree, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, tree-like appearance with a thick, woody trunk and a crown of stiff, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Beaucarnea texana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
