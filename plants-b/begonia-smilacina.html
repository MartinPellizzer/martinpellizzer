Begonia smilacina

Begonia smilacina, commonly known as the climbing begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems that can climb up to 3 meters in length, making it suitable for vertical gardening. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright green color. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming during the summer months.

Begonia smilacina thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
