Brunia rubra

Brunia rubra, commonly known as the red brunia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Brunia rubra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
