Brasiliorchis murilliana

Brasiliorchis murilliana, commonly known as the Murilliana orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
