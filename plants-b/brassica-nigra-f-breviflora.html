Brassica nigra f. breviflora

Brassica nigra f. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf black mustard, is a compact variant of the black mustard plant.

It is characterized by its short, broad leaves and small, dark purple flowers that bloom in early spring. This form is typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinct and easily identifiable member of the Brassicaceae family.
