Bauhinia caribaea

Bauhinia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features palmate leaves composed of three to five leaflets, which are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, clustered in racemes, and bloom during the dry season.

Bauhinia caribaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
