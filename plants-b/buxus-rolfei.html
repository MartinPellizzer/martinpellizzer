Buxus rolfei

Buxus rolfei, commonly known as the Rolf's boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a glossy appearance, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

Buxus rolfei is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
