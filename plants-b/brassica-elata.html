Brassica elata

Brassica elata, commonly known as the giant mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Brassica elata is an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes due to its hardy nature and potential as a forage crop.
