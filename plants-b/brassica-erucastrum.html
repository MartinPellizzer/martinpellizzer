Brassica erucastrum

Brassica erucastrum, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and serrated leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Brassica erucastrum is an annual plant that is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
