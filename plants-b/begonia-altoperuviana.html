Begonia altoperuviana

Begonia altoperuviana, commonly known as the Peruvian begonia, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is appreciated by both botanists and gardeners alike.
