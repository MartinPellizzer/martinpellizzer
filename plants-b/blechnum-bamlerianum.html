Blechnum bamlerianum

Blechnum bamlerianum, commonly known as the Bamler's fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.
