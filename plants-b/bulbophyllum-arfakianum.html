Bulbophyllum arfakianum

Bulbophyllum arfakianum, commonly known as the Arfak orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to New Guinea.

It is a lithophyte, typically growing on rock surfaces in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant orchid flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivation.
