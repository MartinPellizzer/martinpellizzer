Brassica oleracea var. selenisia

Brassica oleracea var. selenisia, commonly known as the Romanesco broccoli, is a unique variant of the cabbage family.

It is characterized by its striking, fractal-like inflorescence composed of tightly packed, spiral-shaped florets. The plant produces a dense cluster of small, green buds that are typically harvested before they fully open. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and distinctive appearance in culinary and ornamental applications.
