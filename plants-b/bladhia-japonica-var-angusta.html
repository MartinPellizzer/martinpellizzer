Bladhia japonica var. angusta

Bladhia japonica var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaf Japanese bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It has a creeping habit with underwater stems that can produce roots and shoots.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on aquatic plant adaptations.
