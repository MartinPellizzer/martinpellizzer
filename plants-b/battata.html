Battata

Battata, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a root vegetable belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, tuberous roots that come in a variety of colors, including orange, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces violet flowers that bloom in the summer. Battata thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich roots.

It is a staple food in many cultures and is valued for its high content of vitamins A and C.
