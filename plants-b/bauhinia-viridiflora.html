Bauhinia viridiflora

Bauhinia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets, and its striking greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Bauhinia viridiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
