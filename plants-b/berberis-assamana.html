Berberis assamana

Berberis assamana, commonly known as the Assam barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in forested areas and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, orange-red berries that are edible. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Berberis assamana is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
