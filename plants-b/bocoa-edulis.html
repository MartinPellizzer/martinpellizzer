Bocoa edulis

Bocoa edulis, commonly known as the chocolate tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant is most notable for its fruit, which contains the seeds used to produce cocoa, a key ingredient in chocolate. Its flowers are pale yellow with a red throat, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates with rich, well-drained soil.

Bocoa edulis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
