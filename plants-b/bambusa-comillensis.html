Bambusa comillensis

Bambusa comillensis, commonly known as the Colombian bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Bambusa comillensis is valued for its strong, flexible stalks, which are used in construction and crafts.
