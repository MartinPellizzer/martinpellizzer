Beyeria viscosa var. minor

Beyeria viscosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser viscous beyeria, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
