Bignonia erubescens var. subtruncata

Bignonia erubescens var. subtruncata, commonly known as the scarlet shower vine, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange to red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
