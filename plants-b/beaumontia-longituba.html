Beaumontia longituba

Beaumontia longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed beaumontia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant features a woody stem with smooth, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often during the summer months, and are known for their fragrant scent.

Beaumontia longituba is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
