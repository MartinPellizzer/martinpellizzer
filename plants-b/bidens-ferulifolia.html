Bidens ferulifolia

Bidens ferulifolia, commonly known as the ironweed or yellow beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
