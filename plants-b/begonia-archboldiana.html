Begonia archboldiana

Begonia archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a rich, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
