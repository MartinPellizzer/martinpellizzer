Bistorta leptophylla

Bistorta leptophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Bistorta leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported astringent properties.
