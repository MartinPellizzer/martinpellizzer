Bulbophyllum pallidulum

Bulbophyllum pallidulum, commonly known as the pale bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to green flowers. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted for water storage.

The orchid's flowers are often short-lived, blooming during the wet season in its natural habitat.
