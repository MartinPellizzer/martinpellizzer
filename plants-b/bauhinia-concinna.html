Bauhinia concinna

Bauhinia concinna, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Bauhinia concinna thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
