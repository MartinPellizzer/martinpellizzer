Banksia glaucifolia

Banksia glaucifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant has narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It produces woody cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.

Banksia glaucifolia is an important component of the local ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
