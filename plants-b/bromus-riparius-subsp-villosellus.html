Bromus riparius subsp. villosellus

Bromus riparius subsp.

villosellus, commonly known as the hairy rivergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, often reddish-brown stems.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pubescent surface. It thrives in moist, often saline environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
