Bambusa montana

Bambusa montana, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that often have a silvery sheen due to the fine, white hairs covering the stems. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that appear in panicle clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa montana is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
