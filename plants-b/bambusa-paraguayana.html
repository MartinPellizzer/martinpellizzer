Bambusa paraguayana

Bambusa paraguayana, commonly known as the Paraguay bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo native to South America.

It features slender, green culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa paraguayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
