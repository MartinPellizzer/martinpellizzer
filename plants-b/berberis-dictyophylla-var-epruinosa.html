Berberis dictyophylla var. epruinosa

Berberis dictyophylla var. epruinosa, commonly known as the silver-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The variety is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
