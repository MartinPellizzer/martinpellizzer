Bulbophyllum sculptum

Bulbophyllum sculptum, commonly known as the sculpted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, ridged pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
