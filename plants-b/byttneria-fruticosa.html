Byttneria fruticosa

Byttneria fruticosa, commonly known as the African rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant's fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds, often dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
