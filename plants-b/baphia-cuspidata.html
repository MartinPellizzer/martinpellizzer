Baphia cuspidata

Baphia cuspidata, commonly known as the African velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Baphia cuspidata is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
