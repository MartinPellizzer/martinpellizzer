Basella nigra

Basella nigra, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value.

Basella nigra is valued in both culinary and horticultural contexts due to its adaptability and nutritional content.
