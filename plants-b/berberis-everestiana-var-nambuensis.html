Berberis everestiana var. nambuensis

Berberis everestiana var. nambuensis, commonly known as the Nambu barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
