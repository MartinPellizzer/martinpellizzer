Bouchea hederacea

Bouchea hederacea, commonly known as the trailing bouchea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pink to red bracts. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Bouchea hederacea is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
