Betula pendula var. tauschii

Betula pendula var. tauschii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a variant of the European weeping willow.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The leaves are typically oval and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
