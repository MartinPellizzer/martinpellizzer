Berberis keikoe

Berberis keikoe, commonly known as the Kikuyu barberry, is a shrub native to eastern Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.

Berberis keikoe is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
