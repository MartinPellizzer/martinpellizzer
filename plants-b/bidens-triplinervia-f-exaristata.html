Bidens triplinervia f. exaristata

Bidens triplinervia f. exaristata, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with prominent veins. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by bracts that resemble additional flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
