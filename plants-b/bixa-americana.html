Bixa americana

Bixa americana, commonly known as the annatto plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. The plant is most notable for its seeds, which contain a red-orange pigment called bixin, used as a natural food coloring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional and modern agricultural systems.
