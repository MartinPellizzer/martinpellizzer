Berberis annaemariae

Berberis annaemariae, commonly known as the Annaemaria barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features prickly stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
