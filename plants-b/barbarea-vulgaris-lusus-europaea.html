Barbarea vulgaris lusus europaea

Barbarea vulgaris lusus europaea, commonly known as the European winter cress, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces heart-shaped seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

This species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
