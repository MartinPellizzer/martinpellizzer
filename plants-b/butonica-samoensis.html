Butonica samoensis

Butonica samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of the forest understory. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Butonica samoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of cultural significance to the local Samoan people.
