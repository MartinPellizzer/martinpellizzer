Bombycella

Bombycella, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. Bombycella species are characterized by their delicate, butterfly-like flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, often found on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of orchid ecosystems.
