Bryonia vulgaris

Bryonia vulgaris, commonly known as goat's bezoar, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces toxic, spiny fruits that contain a milky sap known for its medicinal and poisonous properties. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

Due to its toxic nature, it requires caution when handling or using in traditional remedies.
