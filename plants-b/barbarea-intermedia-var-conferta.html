Barbarea intermedia var. conferta

Barbarea intermedia var. conferta, commonly known as the dense-stemmed winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
