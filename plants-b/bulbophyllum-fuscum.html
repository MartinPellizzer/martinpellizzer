Bulbophyllum fuscum

Bulbophyllum fuscum, commonly known as the dark bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
