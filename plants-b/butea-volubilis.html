Butea volubilis

Butea volubilis, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red floral display, which appears in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into long, slender pods. Butea volubilis is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
