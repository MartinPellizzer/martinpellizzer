Bertya tasmanica subsp. tasmanica

Bertya tasmanica subsp.

tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian bertya, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience to coastal conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
