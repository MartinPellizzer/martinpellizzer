Brassavola duckeana

Brassavola duckeana, commonly known as the Ducke's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Brassavola duckeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
