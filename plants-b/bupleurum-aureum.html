Bupleurum aureum

Bupleurum aureum, commonly known as golden bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal use.

Bupleurum aureum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
