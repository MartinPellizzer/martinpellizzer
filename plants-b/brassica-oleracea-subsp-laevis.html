Brassica oleracea subsp. laevis

Brassica oleracea subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth cabbage, is a subspecies of the cultivated cabbage. It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

This plant is native to Europe and is often grown for its edible leaves and stems. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for spring and fall harvests.

The plant is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, including broccoli and cauliflower.
