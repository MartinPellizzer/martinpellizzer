Begonia echinosepala

Begonia echinosepala, commonly known as the spiky-sepaled begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
