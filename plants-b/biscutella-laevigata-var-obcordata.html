Biscutella laevigata var. obcordata

Biscutella laevigata var. obcordata, commonly known as the smooth biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silky pubescence, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
