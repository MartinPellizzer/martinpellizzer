Baumea tenax

Baumea tenax, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tinged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baumea tenax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
