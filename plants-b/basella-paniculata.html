Basella paniculata

Basella paniculata, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy, red or purple stems that are often used in cooking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and nutritious plant in various cuisines.
