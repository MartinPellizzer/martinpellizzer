Berkheya ilicifolia

Berkheya ilicifolia, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a striking South African plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
