Betula verrusosa f. serrata

Betula verrusosa f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
