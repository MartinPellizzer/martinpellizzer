Betula pubescens f. suberecta

Betula pubescens f. suberecta, commonly known as the white birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest regions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with doubly serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Betula pubescens f. suberecta plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
