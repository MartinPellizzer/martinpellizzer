Boronia scabra

Boronia scabra, commonly known as the rough boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually rough to the touch, giving it its common name, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

Boronia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
