Baccharis erioclada

Baccharis erioclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Baccharis erioclada is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
