Bulbophyllum cleistogamum

Bulbophyllum cleistogamum, commonly known as the closed-flower orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within leaf sheaths. The plant produces pseudobulbs, which serve as storage organs for water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and greenish, with a short-lived blooming period.

Bulbophyllum cleistogamum thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
