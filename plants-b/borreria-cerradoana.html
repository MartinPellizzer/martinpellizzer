Borreria cerradoana

Borreria cerradoana, commonly known as the cerrado borereria, is a shrub native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the cerrado ecosystem, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use. It plays a role in local biodiversity and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance highlights its importance in maintaining the balance of the Cerrado biome.
