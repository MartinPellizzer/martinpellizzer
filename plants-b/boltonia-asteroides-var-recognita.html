Boltonia asteroides var. recognita

Boltonia asteroides var. recognita, commonly known as the White Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cone-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
