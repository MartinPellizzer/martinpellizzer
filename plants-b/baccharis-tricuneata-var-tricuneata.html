Baccharis tricuneata var. tricuneata

Baccharis tricuneata var. tricuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
