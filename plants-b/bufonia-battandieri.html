Bufonia battandieri

Bufonia battandieri, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features tall, sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Bufonia battandieri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
