Bromus flavescens

Bromus flavescens, commonly known as yellow brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or brown. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Bromus flavescens is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
