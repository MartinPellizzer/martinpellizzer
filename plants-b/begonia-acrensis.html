Begonia acrensis

Begonia acrensis, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Begonia acrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
