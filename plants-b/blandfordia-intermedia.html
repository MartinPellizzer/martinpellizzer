Blandfordia intermedia

Blandfordia intermedia, commonly known as the Christmas Blandfordia, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen and form a dense rosette near the ground. Blandfordia intermedia thrives in well-drained soils and requires a period of cool, dry conditions to encourage flowering.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
