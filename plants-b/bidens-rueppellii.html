Bidens rueppellii

Bidens rueppellii, commonly known as the desert tickseed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Bidens rueppellii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
