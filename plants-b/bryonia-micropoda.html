Bryonia micropoda

Bryonia micropoda, commonly known as the small-fruited bryony, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by spiny, orange-red berries. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

Bryonia micropoda is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
