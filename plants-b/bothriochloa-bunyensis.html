Bothriochloa bunyensis

Bothriochloa bunyensis, commonly known as the Bunyengi grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, showing some tolerance to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Bothriochloa bunyensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
