Braxipis

Braxipis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, fleshy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. Braxipis species are typically herbaceous, with some species growing as succulents due to their ability to store water. The flowers often come in shades of purple, violet, and white, making them popular in horticulture.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
