Brachionidium brevicaudatum

Brachionidium brevicaudatum, commonly known as the short-tailed brachionid, is a type of rotifer found in freshwater environments.

It belongs to the class Rotifera and is characterized by its small, transparent body and a distinctive, short tail. The organism has a crown of cilia used for feeding and locomotion, and it is typically less than 1 millimeter in length. Brachionidium brevicaudatum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems as a primary consumer, feeding on algae and detritus.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality in ecological studies.
