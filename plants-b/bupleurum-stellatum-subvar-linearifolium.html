Bupleurum stellatum subvar. linearifolium

Bupleurum stellatum subvar. linearifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
