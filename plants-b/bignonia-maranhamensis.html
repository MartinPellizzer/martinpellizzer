Bignonia maranhamensis

Bignonia maranhamensis, commonly known as the Maranham trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces long, pendulous inflorescences. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, often found in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
