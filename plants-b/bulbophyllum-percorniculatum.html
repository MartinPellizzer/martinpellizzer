Bulbophyllum percorniculatum

Bulbophyllum percorniculatum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
