Brassavola pumilio

Brassavola pumilio, commonly known as the miniature lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, often forming dense colonies in moist, shaded forest floors. The plant produces delicate, grass-like leaves and bears intricate, fragrant inflorescences composed of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
