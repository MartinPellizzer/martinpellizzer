Braxilia media

Braxilia media, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Braxilia media thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
