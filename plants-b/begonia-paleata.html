Begonia paleata

Begonia paleata, commonly known as the pale begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique leaf patterns and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
