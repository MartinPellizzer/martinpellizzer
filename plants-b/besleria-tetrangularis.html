Besleria tetrangularis

Besleria tetrangularis, commonly known as the four-sided begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant features rhombic-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique morphology and vibrant display make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
