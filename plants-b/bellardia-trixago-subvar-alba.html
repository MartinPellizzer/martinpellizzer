Bellardia trixago subvar. alba

Bellardia trixago subvar. alba, commonly known as the white bellardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.
