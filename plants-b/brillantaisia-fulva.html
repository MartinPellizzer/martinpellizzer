Brillantaisia fulva

Brillantaisia fulva, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a dazzling display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
