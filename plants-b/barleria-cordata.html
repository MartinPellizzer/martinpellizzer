Barleria cordata

Barleria cordata, commonly known as heartleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a heart-shaped base, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the warmer months.

Barleria cordata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
