Barringtonia bicolor

Barringtonia bicolor, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often with a waxy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
