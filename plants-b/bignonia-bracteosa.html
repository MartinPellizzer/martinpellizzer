Bignonia bracteosa

Bignonia bracteosa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

Bignonia bracteosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
