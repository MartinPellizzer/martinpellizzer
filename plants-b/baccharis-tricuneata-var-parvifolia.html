Baccharis tricuneata var. parvifolia

Baccharis tricuneata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
