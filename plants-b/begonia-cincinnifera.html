Begonia cincinnifera

Begonia cincinnifera, commonly known as the slender-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It prefers shaded to partially shaded environments and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
