Blumea sinapifolia

Blumea sinapifolia, commonly known as the false chervil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea sinapifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
