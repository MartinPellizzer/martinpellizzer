Bignonia ramiflora

Bignonia ramiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with opposite, ovate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a climbing plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
