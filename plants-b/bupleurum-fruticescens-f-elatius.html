Bupleurum fruticescens f. elatius

Bupleurum fruticescens f. elatius, commonly known as the tall garden bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
