Byttneria dentata

Byttneria dentata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is often found in moist, lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance.
