Berberis khasiana

Berberis khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
