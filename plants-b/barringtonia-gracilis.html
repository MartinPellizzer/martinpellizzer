Barringtonia gracilis

Barringtonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mountain banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces a large, spherical fruit that splits open to release numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Barringtonia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
