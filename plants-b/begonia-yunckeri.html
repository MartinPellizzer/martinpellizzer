Begonia yunckeri

Begonia yunckeri, commonly known as Yuncker's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives by clinging to tree trunks or other surfaces in its natural habitat. It prefers warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
