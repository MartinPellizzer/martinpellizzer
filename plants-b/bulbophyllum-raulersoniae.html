Bulbophyllum raulersoniae

Bulbophyllum raulersoniae, commonly known as the Raulerson's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, with a distinctive lip structure. It has a pseudobulb system that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical climates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
