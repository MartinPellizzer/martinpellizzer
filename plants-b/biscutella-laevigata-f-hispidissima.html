Biscutella laevigata f. hispidissima

Biscutella laevigata f. hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in its native ecosystem.
