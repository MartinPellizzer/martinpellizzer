Bupleurum ranunculoides f. exiguum

Bupleurum ranunculoides f. exiguum, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
