Bulbophyllum imbricatum

Bulbophyllum imbricatum, commonly known as the overlapping orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
