Brassia escobariana

Brassia escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and numerous, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Brassia escobariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
