Bourgia

Bourgia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving water bodies.

The leaves are buoyant and often float on the water's surface, contributing to its ecological role in water filtration and oxygenation.
