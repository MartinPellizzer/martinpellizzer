Bulbostylis fimbristyloides

Bulbostylis fimbristyloides, commonly known as the fringed-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, linear leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale green to brown flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or used in restoration projects.
