Brachypodium × loliaceum

Brachypodium × loliaceum, commonly known as the intermediate brachypodium, is a hybrid grass species that results from the cross between Brachypodium distachyon and Brachypodium sylvaticum.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Brachypodium × loliaceum is often used in ecological and agricultural studies due to its adaptability and genetic tractability.

Its botanical features make it a valuable model organism for research on plant biology and climate resilience.
