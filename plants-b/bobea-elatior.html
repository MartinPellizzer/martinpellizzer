Bobea elatior

Bobea elatior, commonly known as the red-flowered bobea, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Bobea elatior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
