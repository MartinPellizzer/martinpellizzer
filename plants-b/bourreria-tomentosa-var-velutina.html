Bourreria tomentosa var. velutina

Bourreria tomentosa var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf bourreria, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
