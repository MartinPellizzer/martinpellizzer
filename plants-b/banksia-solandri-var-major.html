Banksia solandri var. major

Banksia solandri var. major, commonly known as the Sydney banksia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, woody structure and its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
