Baccharis grandiflora var. colombiana

Baccharis grandiflora var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
