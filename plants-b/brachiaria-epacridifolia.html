Brachiaria epacridifolia

Brachiaria epacridifolia, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria epacridifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in certain environments.
