Bambusa felix

Bambusa felix, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that exhibit a distinctive golden-yellow coloration, especially under sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear at the top of the culms. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture that adds a refined appearance to gardens.

Bambusa felix thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
