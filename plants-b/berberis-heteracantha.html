Berberis heteracantha

Berberis heteracantha, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive midrib.

Berberis heteracantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
