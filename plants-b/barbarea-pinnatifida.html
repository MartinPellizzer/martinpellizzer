Barbarea pinnatifida

Barbarea pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Barbarea pinnatifida is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that thrives in moist to moderately dry conditions.
