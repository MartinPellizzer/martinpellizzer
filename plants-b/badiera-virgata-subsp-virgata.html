Badiera virgata subsp. virgata

Badiera virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the white-flowered badiera, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features aromatic, glandular leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.
