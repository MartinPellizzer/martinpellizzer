Bromus mollis var. major

Bromus mollis var. major, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Bromus mollis var. major plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
