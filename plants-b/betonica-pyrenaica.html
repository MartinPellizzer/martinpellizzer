Betonica pyrenaica

Betonica pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Betonica pyrenaica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
