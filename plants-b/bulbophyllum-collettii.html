Bulbophyllum collettii

Bulbophyllum collettii, commonly known as the Collett's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its tropical environment.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
