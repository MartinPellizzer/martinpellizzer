Benzoin hypoleucum

Benzoin hypoleucum, commonly known as the white benzoin, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and aromatic resinous exudates. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is known for producing a gum resin used in perfumes and traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
