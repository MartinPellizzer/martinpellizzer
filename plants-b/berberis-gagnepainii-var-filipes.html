Berberis gagnepainii var. filipes

Berberis gagnepainii var. filipes, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and as a border plant in gardens with well-drained soil.
