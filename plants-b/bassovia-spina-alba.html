Bassovia spina-alba

Bassovia spina-alba, commonly known as the white-spined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and spiny bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
