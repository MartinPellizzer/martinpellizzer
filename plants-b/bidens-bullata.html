Bidens bullata

Bidens bullata, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens bullata is often found in moist to wet habitats, including swamps and marshes.
