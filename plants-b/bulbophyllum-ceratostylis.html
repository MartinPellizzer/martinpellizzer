Bulbophyllum ceratostylis

Bulbophyllum ceratostylis, commonly known as the horned-styled bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate inflorescence and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in the rainforest ecosystem.
