Baccharis papillosa

Baccharis papillosa, commonly known as the silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
