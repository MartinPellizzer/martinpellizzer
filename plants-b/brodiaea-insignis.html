Brodiaea insignis

Brodiaea insignis, commonly known as the showy brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Brodiaea insignis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
