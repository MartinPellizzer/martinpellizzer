Butyrospermum parkii var. ferrugineum

Butyrospermum parkii var. ferrugineum, commonly known as shea butter tree variant with ferrugineum characteristics, is a variant of the shea tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces a valuable nut used for extracting shea butter. The plant thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid environment. Its fruit contains a high oil content, making it economically important for local communities.

The variant is distinguished by its reddish-brown bark and unique leaf morphology compared to the standard variety.
