Berberis silva-taroucana

Berberis silva-taroucana, commonly known as the Tarouca barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
