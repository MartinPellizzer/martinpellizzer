Bromus hackelii

Bromus hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's brome, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous spikelets, each containing two to three flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus hackelii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
