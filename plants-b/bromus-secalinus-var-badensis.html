Bromus secalinus var. badensis

Bromus secalinus var. badensis, commonly known as the baden grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often have a reddish tinge. The leaves are typically narrow and flat, with a rough texture, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in supporting various insect and bird species.
