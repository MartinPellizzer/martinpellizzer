Bambusa arundo

Bambusa arundo, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a smooth, light green to white inner stem. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of sustainable materials.

Bambusa arundo is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
