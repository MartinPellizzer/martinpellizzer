Bromus squarrosus var. balsiensis

Bromus squarrosus var. balsiensis, commonly known as the Balsiopsis bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves with a bluish waxy coating. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, and its inflorescence is typically compact and cylindrical. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
