Bartschella glassii subsp. ascensionis

Bartschella glassii subsp.

ascensionis, commonly known as Ascension Island bartschella, is a flowering plant native to the remote Ascension Island in the South Atlantic Ocean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, adapted to the island's unique climate and soil conditions. It is a rare and endemic species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.

Due to its restricted range and potential threats from environmental changes, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
