Bignonia bijuga

Bignonia bijuga, commonly known as the climbing trumpetflower, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive trumpet shape, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia bijuga is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
