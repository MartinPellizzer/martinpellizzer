Barleria strigosa

Barleria strigosa, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Barleria strigosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
