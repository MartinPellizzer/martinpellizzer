Bignonia fluviatilis

Bignonia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river bignonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, featuring compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near rivers and streams.

Bignonia fluviatilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
