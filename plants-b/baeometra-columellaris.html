Baeometra columellaris

Baeometra columellaris, commonly known as the columnar baeometra, is a unique species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
