Boronia coerulescens var. spinescens

Boronia coerulescens var. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers, which are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in coastal regions, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
