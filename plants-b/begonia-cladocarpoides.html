Begonia cladocarpoides

Begonia cladocarpoides, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Begonia cladocarpoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
