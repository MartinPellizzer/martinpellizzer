Baphia vermoesenii

Baphia vermoesenii, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.
