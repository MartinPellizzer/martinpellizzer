Berberis hilliana

Berberis hilliana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional remedies.
