Bulbostylis gardneriana

Bulbostylis gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland understories or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within its native range.
