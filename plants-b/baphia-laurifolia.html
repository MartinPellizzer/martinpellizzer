Baphia laurifolia

Baphia laurifolia, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flattened, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Baphia laurifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
