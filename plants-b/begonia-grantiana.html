Begonia grantiana

Begonia grantiana, commonly known as the Grant's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
