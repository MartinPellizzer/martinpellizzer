Begonia cordifolia var. hirta

Begonia cordifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden settings.
