Betula verrucosa f. dalecarlica

Betula verrucosa f. dalecarlica, commonly known as the Swedish birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive white, peeling inner bark. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across Scandinavia and parts of Central Europe. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
