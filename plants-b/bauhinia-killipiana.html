Bauhinia killipiana

Bauhinia killipiana, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves that are palmately arranged with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are showy, typically pink or purple, and appear in clusters during the rainy season.

Bauhinia killipiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
