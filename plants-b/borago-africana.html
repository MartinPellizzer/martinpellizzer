Borago africana

Borago africana, commonly known as African borage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a mild, slightly peppery taste, while the flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
