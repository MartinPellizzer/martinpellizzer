Bupleurum coloratum

Bupleurum coloratum, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, bluish-green leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
