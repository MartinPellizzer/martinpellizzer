Banara acunae

Banara acunae, commonly known as the Andean banana tree, is a tropical plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Musaceae family, which includes other well-known banana species. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to plantains.

Due to its ecological and agricultural significance, it is often cultivated in regions with suitable climatic conditions.
