Bagassa

Bagassa, commonly known as the sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a type of palm fruit that contains a starchy seed, which is used to make sago. Its roots and stems are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Bagassa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance in various regions.
