Buceras odoratissima

Buceras odoratissima, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The tree produces distinctive, spiky spherical fruit that contains numerous small seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it has a tall, slender trunk.

Buceras odoratissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
