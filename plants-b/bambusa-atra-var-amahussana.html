Bambusa atra var. amahussana

Bambusa atra var. amahussana, commonly known as black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking dark green to blackish culms, which are often adorned with a distinctive purple sheath. This bamboo is a clump-forming species, growing in dense stands and reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers.

It is valued for ornamental purposes and is often used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
