Biasolettia elliptica

Biasolettia elliptica, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

Due to its ecological importance and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is also a subject of conservation interest.
