Bakeridesia rufinervis var. latifolia

Bakeridesia rufinervis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bakeridesia, is a variant of the species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
