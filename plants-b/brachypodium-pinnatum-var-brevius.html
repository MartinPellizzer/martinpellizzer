Brachypodium pinnatum var. brevius

Brachypodium pinnatum var. brevius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny, two-lipped flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
