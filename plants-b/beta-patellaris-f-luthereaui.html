Beta patellaris f. luthereaui

Beta patellaris f. luthereaui, commonly known as the coastal beta, is a variant of the beta plant native to specific coastal regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature essential for survival in its arid environment. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its leaves are usually a deep green color, and it produces small, white flowers during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
