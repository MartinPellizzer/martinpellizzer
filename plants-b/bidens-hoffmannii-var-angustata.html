Bidens hoffmannii var. angustata

Bidens hoffmannii var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
