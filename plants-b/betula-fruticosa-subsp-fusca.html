Betula fruticosa subsp. fusca

Betula fruticosa subsp.

fusca, commonly known as the black birch, is a shrub-like species native to North America. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental bark and historical use in traditional medicine.
