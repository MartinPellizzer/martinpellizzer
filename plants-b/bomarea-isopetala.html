Bomarea isopetala

Bomarea isopetala, commonly known as the purple-flowered bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the wet forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a cluster of tiny white flowers, which are typically hidden beneath the showy bracts. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Bomarea isopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and unique floral structure.
