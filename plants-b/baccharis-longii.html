Baccharis longii

Baccharis longii, commonly known as the longleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Baccharis longii is often found in moist or wetland habitats, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
