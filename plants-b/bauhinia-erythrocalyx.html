Bauhinia erythrocalyx

Bauhinia erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-fruited bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia erythrocalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
