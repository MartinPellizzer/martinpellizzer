Berberis cretica var. serratifolia

Berberis cretica var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and winter interest.
