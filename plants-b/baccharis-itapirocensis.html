Baccharis itapirocensis

Baccharis itapirocensis, commonly known as the Itapirocensis baccharis, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within dense forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis itapirocensis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for various insects and birds.
