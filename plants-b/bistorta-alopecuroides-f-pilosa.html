Bistorta alopecuroides f. pilosa

Bistorta alopecuroides f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bistorta, is a variant of the bistorta plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

This perennial herb belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features upright, somewhat zigzagging stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
