Berberis tibetensis

Berberis tibetensis, commonly known as Tibetan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Tibet and adjacent areas.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are usually yellow and appear in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
