Bulbophyllum savaiense subsp. subcubicum

Bulbophyllum savaiense subsp.

subcubicum, commonly known as the Subcubic Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is often cultivated for its unique floral display and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts.
