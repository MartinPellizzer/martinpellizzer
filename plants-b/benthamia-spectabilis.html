Benthamia spectabilis

Benthamia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, often in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Benthamia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
