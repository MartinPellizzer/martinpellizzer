Bauhinia chalcobapta

Bauhinia chalcobapta, commonly known as the purple bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, typical of many leguminous species.

Bauhinia chalcobapta is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
