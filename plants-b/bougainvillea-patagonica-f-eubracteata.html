Bougainvillea patagonica f. eubracteata

Bougainvillea patagonica f. eubracteata, commonly known as the Patagonian bougainvillea, is a unique variant of the bougainvillea genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that range in color from pink to red, which are often more pronounced than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
