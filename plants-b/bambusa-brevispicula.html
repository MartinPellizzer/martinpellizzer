Bambusa brevispicula

Bambusa brevispicula, commonly known as the short-awned bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Its culms (stems) are hollow and jointed, a characteristic feature of the bamboo family.

Bambusa brevispicula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
