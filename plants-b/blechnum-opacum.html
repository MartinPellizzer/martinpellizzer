Blechnum opacum

Blechnum opacum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is known for its robust and evergreen foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a fan-like shape that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
