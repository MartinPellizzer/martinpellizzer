Bauhinia variegata

Bauhinia variegata, commonly known as the Flame of the Forest, is a deciduous tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-shaped leaves that turn bright red during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Bauhinia variegata is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
