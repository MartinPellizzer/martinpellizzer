Biscutella auriculata var. bivillosa

Biscutella auriculata var. bivillosa, commonly known as the two-lobed biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
