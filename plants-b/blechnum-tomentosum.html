Blechnum tomentosum

Blechnum tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
