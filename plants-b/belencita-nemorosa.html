Belencita nemorosa

Belencita nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five lobes, and are usually blue or purple in color.

Belencita nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
