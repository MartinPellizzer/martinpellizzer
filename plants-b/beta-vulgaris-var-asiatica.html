Beta vulgaris var. asiatica

Beta vulgaris var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian beet, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its thick, edible roots and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is typically grown for its root, which can be reddish or yellowish in color depending on the variety. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional content.
