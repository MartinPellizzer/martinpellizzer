Bauhinia hirta

Bauhinia hirta, commonly known as the velvet tree or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous, woody roots and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils.

Bauhinia hirta is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
