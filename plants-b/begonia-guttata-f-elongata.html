Begonia guttata f. elongata

Begonia guttata f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated velvet begonia, is a hybrid form of the velvet begonia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This cultivar is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
