Biota orientalis var. argentea

Biota orientalis var. argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage, which gives it its common name, and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pale pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. It is often found in shaded forest understories, where it forms dense clusters along rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
