Bancroftia pendula

Bancroftia pendula, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are borne in terminal panicles and are known for their vibrant color and long-lasting bloom.
