Bursera trinitensis

Bursera trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown of glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and ritual purposes.
