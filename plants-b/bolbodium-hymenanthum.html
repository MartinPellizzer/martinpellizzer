Bolbodium hymenanthum

Bolbodium hymenanthum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, branching stem that is often covered in soft, white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of many members of the Apocynaceae family.
