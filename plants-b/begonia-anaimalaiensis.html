Begonia anaimalaiensis

Begonia anaimalaiensis, commonly known as the Anaimalai begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers are borne on slender, erect stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
