Berberis × bidentata

Berberis × bidentata, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Berberis julisciana and Berberis thunbergii.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The hybrid is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and visual appeal.
