Bomarea purpurea var. guancana

Bomarea purpurea var. guancana, commonly known as the purple bomarea, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features tuberous roots and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique coloration and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
