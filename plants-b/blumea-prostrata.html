Blumea prostrata

Blumea prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blumea prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
