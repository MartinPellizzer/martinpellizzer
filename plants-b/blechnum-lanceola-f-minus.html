Blechnum lanceola f. minus

Blechnum lanceola f. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved harts-tongue fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually a deep green, with a smooth texture and a slightly curled margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
