Bauhinia porphyrotricha

Bauhinia porphyrotricha, commonly known as the purple-veined bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves with a deep purple venation. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy inflorescences and unique leaf structure.

Bauhinia porphyrotricha is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
