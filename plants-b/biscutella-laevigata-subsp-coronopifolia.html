Biscutella laevigata subsp. coronopifolia

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

coronopifolia, commonly known as the woolly biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and basal leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. Its fibrous root system is adapted to rocky and arid soils, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
