Bambusa nana var. gracillima

Bambusa nana var. gracillima, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo or miniature bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to southern China.

It features slender, upright culms that grow to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a dense, bushy appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ease of maintenance in garden settings.
