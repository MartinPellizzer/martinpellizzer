Brassicaceae sylvestris

Brassicaceae sylvestris, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is an annual or biennial herb characterized by its slender stems and basal leaves that are typically lobed or dissected. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators and soil health.
