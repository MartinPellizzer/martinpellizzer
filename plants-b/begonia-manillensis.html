Begonia manillensis

Begonia manillensis, commonly known as the Manila begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive bilabiate shape, and bloom during the warmer months.

Begonia manillensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
