Bunchosia rhombifolia

Bunchosia rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are rhomboid in shape, hence its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Bunchosia rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
