Bulbophyllum roxburghii

Bulbophyllum roxburghii, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
