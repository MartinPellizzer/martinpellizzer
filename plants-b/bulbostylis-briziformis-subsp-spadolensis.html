Bulbostylis briziformis subsp. spadolensis

Bulbostylis briziformis subsp.

spadolensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and botanical studies.
