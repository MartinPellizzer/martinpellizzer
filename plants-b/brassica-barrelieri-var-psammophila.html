Brassica barrelieri var. psammophila

Brassica barrelieri var. psammophila, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a small annual herb that thrives in sandy and saline soils, showcasing adaptations to extreme environmental conditions. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This species is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
