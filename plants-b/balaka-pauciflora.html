Balaka pauciflora

Balaka pauciflora, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are clustered in panicles.

Balaka pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
