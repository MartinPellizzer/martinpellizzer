Bumelia obovata

Bumelia obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Bumelia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
