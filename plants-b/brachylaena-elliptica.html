Brachylaena elliptica

Brachylaena elliptica, commonly known as the flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its bark is smooth and can become fissured with age, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Brachylaena elliptica is well adapted to arid conditions and is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
