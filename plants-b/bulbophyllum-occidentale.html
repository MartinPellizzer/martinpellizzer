Bulbophyllum occidentale

Bulbophyllum occidentale, commonly known as the Western Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts but is protected in many regions.
