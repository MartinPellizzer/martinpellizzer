Begonia platanifolia var. acuminatissima

Begonia platanifolia var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the broad-leaf begonia, is a striking plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The foliage is typically a deep green, often with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside, adding visual interest. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
