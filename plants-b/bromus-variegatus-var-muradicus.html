Bromus variegatus var. muradicus

Bromus variegatus var. muradicus, commonly known as the variable brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, sometimes hairy blades. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red hues.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
