Boronia rupicola

Boronia rupicola, commonly known as the rock boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Boronia rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and blooms.
