Bulbostylis subaphylla var. longiglumis

Bulbostylis subaphylla var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and the inflorescence consists of elongated, spike-like clusters.

The variety longiglumis is distinguished by its longer glumes compared to other varieties of Bulbostylis subaphylla.
