Begonia leptantha

Begonia leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
