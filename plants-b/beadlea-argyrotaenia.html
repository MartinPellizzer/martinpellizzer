Beadlea argyrotaenia

Beadlea argyrotaenia, commonly known as the silver-veined beadlea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, linear leaves that have a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Beadlea argyrotaenia is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
