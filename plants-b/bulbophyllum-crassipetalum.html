Bulbophyllum crassipetalum

Bulbophyllum crassipetalum, commonly known as the thick-petaled orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with large, velvety petals that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
