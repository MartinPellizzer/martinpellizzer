Bromus hookeri var. marginatus

Bromus hookeri var. marginatus, commonly known as the marginated brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

It is characterized by its distinctive margins on the leaf blades, which are often darker in color.
