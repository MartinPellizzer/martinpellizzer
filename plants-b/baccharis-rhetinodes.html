Baccharis rhetinodes

Baccharis rhetinodes, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating fever and inflammation.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
