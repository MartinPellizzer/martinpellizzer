Bunium agrarium

Bunium agrarium, commonly known as field fennel, is a perennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind.

Bunium agrarium is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
