Begonia socotrana

Begonia socotrana, commonly known as Socotra begonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a distinctive red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
