Baillonella obovata

Baillonella obovata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, obovate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

The tree has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
