Bombax palmeri

Bombax palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant produces woody capsules that contain fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
