Brachystegia × welwitschii

Brachystegia × welwitschii, commonly known as the African teak or African mahogany, is a hybrid species native to central and eastern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
