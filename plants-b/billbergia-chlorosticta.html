Billbergia chlorosticta

Billbergia chlorosticta, commonly known as the blue-tongued billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central water-holding cup. The plant produces vibrant, spathaceous flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Billbergia chlorosticta is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
