Brassica arvensis

Brassica arvensis, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

Brassica arvensis is a winter annual that can become a weed in crops, particularly in cereals.
