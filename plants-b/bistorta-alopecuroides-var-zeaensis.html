Bistorta alopecuroides var. zeaensis

Bistorta alopecuroides var. zeaensis, commonly known as the eastern bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its smooth, often ribbed stems and distinctive leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
