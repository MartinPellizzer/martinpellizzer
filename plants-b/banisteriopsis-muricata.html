Banisteriopsis muricata

Banisteriopsis muricata, commonly known as the "Muricata" or "Bark Apple," is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round, orange fruits with a tangy flavor. Its bark has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits.
