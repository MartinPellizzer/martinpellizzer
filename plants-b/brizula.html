Brizula

Brizula, commonly known as the common bristlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features slender stems with dense, bristly hairs that give it its characteristic appearance. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous tiny florets, typical of grasses.

Brizula is known for its resilience, thriving in a variety of soil conditions and climates.
