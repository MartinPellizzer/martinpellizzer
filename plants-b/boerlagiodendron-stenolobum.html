Boerlagiodendron stenolobum

Boerlagiodendron stenolobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed boerlagia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
