Boehmeria sericea

Boehmeria sericea, commonly known as silky ginseng, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Boehmeria sericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
