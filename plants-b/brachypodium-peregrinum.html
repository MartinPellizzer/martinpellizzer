Brachypodium peregrinum

Brachypodium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a small annual grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are spike-like and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachypodium peregrinum is also used in ecological studies due to its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
