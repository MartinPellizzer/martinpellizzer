Brachylaena microphylla

Brachylaena microphylla, commonly known as the silver cotula, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for garden use.
