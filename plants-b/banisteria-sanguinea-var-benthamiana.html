Banisteria sanguinea var. benthamiana

Banisteria sanguinea var. benthamiana, commonly known as the red banister, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, velvety fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
