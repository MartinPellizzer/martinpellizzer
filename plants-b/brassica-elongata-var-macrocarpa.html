Brassica elongata var. macrocarpa

Brassica elongata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
