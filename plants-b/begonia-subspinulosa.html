Begonia subspinulosa

Begonia subspinulosa, commonly known as the spiny begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface and may bear small spines along the edges. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of propagation through stem cuttings.
