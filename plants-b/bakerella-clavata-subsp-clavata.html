Bakerella clavata subsp. clavata

Bakerella clavata subsp.

clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bakerella, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Bakerella clavata subsp.

clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
