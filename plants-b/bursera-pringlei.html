Bursera pringlei

Bursera pringlei, commonly known as Mexican frankincense or bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

Bursera pringlei is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
