Baldellia ranunculoides

Baldellia ranunculoides, commonly known as the bog buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and bogs in North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in marshy environments.

Baldellia ranunculoides plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
