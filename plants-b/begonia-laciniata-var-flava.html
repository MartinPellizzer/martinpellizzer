Begonia laciniata var. flava

Begonia laciniata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-leafed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its green stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
