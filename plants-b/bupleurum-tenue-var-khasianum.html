Bupleurum tenue var. khasianum

Bupleurum tenue var. khasianum, commonly known as Khasi cowplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the familyApiaceae, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. Its slender, hairy stems and small white to pale yellow flowers contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The leaves are pinnately divided, with a serrated margin, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
