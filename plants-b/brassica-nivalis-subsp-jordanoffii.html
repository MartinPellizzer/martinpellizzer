Brassica nivalis subsp. jordanoffii

Brassica nivalis subsp.

jordanoffii, commonly known as the Jordanoff's winter cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold climates with short growing seasons.

The plant has small, green, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in small, pod-like structures and are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
