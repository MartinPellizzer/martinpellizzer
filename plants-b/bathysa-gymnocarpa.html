Bathysa gymnocarpa

Bathysa gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded burr plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils along streambanks and in prairie habitats. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, racemose inflorescences.

The fruit, a smooth-seeded capsule, is adapted for wind dispersal and can persist on the ground for several years.
