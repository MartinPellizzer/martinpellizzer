Bacopa laxiflora var. scabra

Bacopa laxiflora var. scabra, commonly known as rough leaf bacopa, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in aquatic environments make it a popular choice for water gardens.
