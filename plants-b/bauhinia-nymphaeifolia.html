Bauhinia nymphaeifolia

Bauhinia nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble water lilies, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Bauhinia nymphaeifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
