Bulbophyllum elassoglossum

Bulbophyllum elassoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique structure, featuring a slender lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
