Barleria puberula

Barleria puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Barleria puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
