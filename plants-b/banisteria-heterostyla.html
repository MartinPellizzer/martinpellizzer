Banisteria heterostyla

Banisteria heterostyla, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

Banisteria heterostyla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and vigorous growth habit.
