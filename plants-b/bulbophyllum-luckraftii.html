Bulbophyllum luckraftii

Bulbophyllum luckraftii, commonly known as the Luckraft's orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest undergrowth. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip or labellum. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
