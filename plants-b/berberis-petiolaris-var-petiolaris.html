Berberis petiolaris var. petiolaris

Berberis petiolaris var. petiolaris, commonly known as the western barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are oval and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
