Blechnum occidentale var. meridionale

Blechnum occidentale var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to the southern regions of the United States.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in small, round structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and indoor cultivation.
