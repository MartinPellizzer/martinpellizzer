Brassica oleracea subvar. viridis

Brassica oleracea subvar. viridis, commonly known as curly kale, is a leafy green vegetable characterized by its dense, curled leaves that are typically dark green in color.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables such as broccoli and cauliflower. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients like vitamins A, C, and K. Its botanical structure includes a short, thick stem with tightly packed, lobed leaves that form a compact rosette.

This variety is well adapted to cooler climates and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate moisture.
