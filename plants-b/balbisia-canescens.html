Balbisia canescens

Balbisia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Balbisia canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
