Bactris herrerana

Bactris herrerana, commonly known as the Herrerana palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in parts of Colombia and Venezuela.

It is a small to medium-sized palm, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are rich in nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, Bactris herrerana is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
