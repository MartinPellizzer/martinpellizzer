Bischofia javanica var. lanceolata

Bischofia javanica var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bishop's candle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with lanceolate-shaped leaflets, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
