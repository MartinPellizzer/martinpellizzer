Berinia pannonica

Berinia pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Berinia pannonica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
