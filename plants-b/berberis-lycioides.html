Berberis lycioides

Berberis lycioides, commonly known as the desert barberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small evergreen leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for wildlife but generally unpalatable to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Berberis lycioides plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for desert animals.
