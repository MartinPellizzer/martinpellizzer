Berberis pingbienensis

Berberis pingbienensis, commonly known as the Pingbian barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
