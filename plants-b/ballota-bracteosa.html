Ballota bracteosa

Ballota bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub with small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods that mature in late summer.

Ballota bracteosa is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
