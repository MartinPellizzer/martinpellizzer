Brachypodium pinnatum var. bracteatum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
