Bryophyllum germinans

Bryophyllum germinans, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. One of its most notable features is its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, as each leaf can generate new plants.

This makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and propagation experiments.
