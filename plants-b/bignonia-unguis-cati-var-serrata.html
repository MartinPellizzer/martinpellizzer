Bignonia unguis-cati var. serrata

Bignonia unguis-cati var. serrata, commonly known as the cat's claw vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers with orange markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its woody stems are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, and it has a strong, pleasant fragrance.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
