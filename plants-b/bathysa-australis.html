Bathysa australis

Bathysa australis, commonly known as the Australian bathysa, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Bathysa australis is often found in coastal and inland regions, showing resilience to arid conditions.
