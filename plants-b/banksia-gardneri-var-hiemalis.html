Banksia gardneri var. hiemalis

Banksia gardneri var. hiemalis, commonly known as the Christmas banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring, providing nectar for a variety of pollinators. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its adaptability to coastal and inland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
