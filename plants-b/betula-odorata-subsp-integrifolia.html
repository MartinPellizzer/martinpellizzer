Betula odorata subsp. integrifolia

Betula odorata subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with smooth margins and a pointed tip. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
