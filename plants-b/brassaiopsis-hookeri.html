Brassaiopsis hookeri

Brassaiopsis hookeri, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage that features a silvery-white underside, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. Brassaiopsis hookeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored plant in horticulture, particularly in subtropical and temperate regions.
