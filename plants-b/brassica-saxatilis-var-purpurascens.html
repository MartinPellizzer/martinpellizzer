Brassica saxatilis var. purpurascens

Brassica saxatilis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and coastal areas of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
