Baccharis genistelloides var. venosa

Baccharis genistelloides var. venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a soft, hairy texture.

It produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
