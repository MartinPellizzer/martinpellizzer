Bromus ramosus f. macrostachys

Bromus ramosus f. macrostachys, commonly known as the tall brome grass, is a botanical variant of the species Bromus ramosus.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that are often more pronounced than in the typical form. This perennial grass is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its role in forage and as a component of native plant communities.
