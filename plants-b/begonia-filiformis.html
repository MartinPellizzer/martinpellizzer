Begonia filiformis

Begonia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Begonia filiformis is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor environments.
