Bulbophyllum catenulatum

Bulbophyllum catenulatum, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish or reddish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
