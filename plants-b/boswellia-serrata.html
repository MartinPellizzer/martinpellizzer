Boswellia serrata

Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian frankincense, is a small deciduous tree native to the dry regions of India, the Middle East, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-barked trunk and slender, branching structure, with leaves that are alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces a resinous sap, known as frankincense, which is harvested from incisions made in its bark. This sap is rich in boswellic acids, which have been studied for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Boswellia serrata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
