Bignonia cognata

Bignonia cognata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia cognata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
