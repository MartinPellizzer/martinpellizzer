Bruguiera parviflora

Bruguiera parviflora, commonly known as the mangrove fern, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots, which help in anchoring the plant and facilitating gas exchange. The plant thrives in intertidal zones, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal sediments. Its leaves are typically small and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bruguiera parviflora is an important component of mangrove ecosystems, providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
