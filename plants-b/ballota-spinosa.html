Ballota spinosa

Ballota spinosa, commonly known as Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to dry environments.

Ballota spinosa is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
