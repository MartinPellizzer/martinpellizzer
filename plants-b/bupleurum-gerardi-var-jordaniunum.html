Bupleurum gerardi var. jordaniunum

Bupleurum gerardi var. jordaniunum, commonly known as Jordan's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 inches tall, often with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
