Bulbine bachmanniana

Bulbine bachmanniana, commonly known as the Bachmann's bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
