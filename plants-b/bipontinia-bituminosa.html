Bipontinia bituminosa

Bipontinia bituminosa, commonly known as the bituminous bipontinia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost coal-like appearance, which is due to its high concentration of tannins and other complex organic compounds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
