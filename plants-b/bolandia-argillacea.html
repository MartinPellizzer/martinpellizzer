Bolandia argillacea

Bolandia argillacea, commonly known as the clayland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or clay soils and thrives in full sun. Bolandia argillacea has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
