Balsamina salicifolia

Balsamina salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf balsam, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
