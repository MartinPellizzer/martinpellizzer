Bromopsis flexuosa

Bromopsis flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Bromopsis flexuosa is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
