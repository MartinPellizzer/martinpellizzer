Blumea gracilis

Blumea gracilis, commonly known as slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands, and is often found in tropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Blumea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
