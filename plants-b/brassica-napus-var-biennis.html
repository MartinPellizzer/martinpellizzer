Brassica napus var. biennis

Brassica napus var. biennis, commonly known as oilseed rape, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flowers and a robust, branched stem that often grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are rich in oil, making it an important crop for agricultural and industrial purposes. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly cultivated in regions with cool, moist conditions.
