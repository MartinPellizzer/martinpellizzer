Boronia drummondii

Boronia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's boronia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Boronia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
