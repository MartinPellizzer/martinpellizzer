Bauhinia bidentata var. monticola

Bauhinia bidentata var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain bauhinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are compound and divided into small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces showy, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
