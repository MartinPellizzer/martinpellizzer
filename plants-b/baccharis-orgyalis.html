Baccharis orgyalis

Baccharis orgyalis, commonly known as golden baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis orgyalis is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and arid regions.
