Bambusa maxima

Bambusa maxima, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features stiff, golden-yellow culms that are often used in ornamental gardening and construction. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Bambusa maxima is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
