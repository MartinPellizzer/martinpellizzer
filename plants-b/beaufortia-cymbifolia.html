Beaufortia cymbifolia

Beaufortia cymbifolia, commonly known as the yellow beaufortia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathlands and woodland areas. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Beaufortia cymbifolia is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
