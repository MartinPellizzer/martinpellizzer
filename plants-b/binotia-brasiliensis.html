Binotia brasiliensis

Binotia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are violet in color, with five petals arranged in a showy cluster.

Binotia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
