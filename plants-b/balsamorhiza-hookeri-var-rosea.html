Balsamorhiza hookeri var. rosea

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate bitterroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
