Baccharis mirabilis

Baccharis mirabilis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Baccharis mirabilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
