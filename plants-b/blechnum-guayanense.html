Blechnum guayanense

Blechnum guayanense, commonly known as the Guayana shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Venezuela, Guyana, and Brazil. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane forests and cloud forests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine by local communities for their purported healing properties.
