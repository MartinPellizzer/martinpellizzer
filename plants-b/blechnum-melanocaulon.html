Blechnum melanocaulon

Blechnum melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its dark, wiry stems and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are biennial, with the sterile fronds appearing in the first year and fertile fronds in the second.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
