Baccharis cassinefolia

Baccharis cassinefolia, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the late spring and summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds.

Baccharis cassinefolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
