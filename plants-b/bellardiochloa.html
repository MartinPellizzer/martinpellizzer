Bellardiochloa

Bellardiochloa, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a genus of grasses native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Bellardiochloa species are known for their ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
