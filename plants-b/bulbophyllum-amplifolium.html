Bulbophyllum amplifolium

Bulbophyllum amplifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its fleshy, pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
