Beta vulgaris var. sicula

Beta vulgaris var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian beet, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its deep red, fleshy roots and thick, dark green leaves. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and is often grown for its nutritional value and culinary uses. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is a biennial plant that typically flowers and produces seeds in its second year.
