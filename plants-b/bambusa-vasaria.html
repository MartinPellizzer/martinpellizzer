Bambusa vasaria

Bambusa vasaria, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming a dense, columnar shape. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves that are often a deep green, providing a lush, tropical appearance. Its roots are fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Bambusa vasaria is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties.
