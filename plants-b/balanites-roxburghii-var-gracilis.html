Balanites roxburghii var. gracilis

Balanites roxburghii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender desert bean, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are typically small and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
