Baumea juncea

Baumea juncea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage that resembles a fern, with silvery-blue leaves that shimmer in the light. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.

Baumea juncea is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
