Banisterodes vitellinum

Banisterodes vitellinum, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, curved pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has a shallow root system that makes it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
