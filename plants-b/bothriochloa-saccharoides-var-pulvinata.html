Bothriochloa saccharoides var. pulvinata

Bothriochloa saccharoides var. pulvinata, commonly known as the dwarf sugar grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes in arid environments.
