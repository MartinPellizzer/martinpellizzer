Berkheya revoluta

Berkheya revoluta, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a dandelion, with tiny seeds attached to fluffy pappi.

Berkheya revoluta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
