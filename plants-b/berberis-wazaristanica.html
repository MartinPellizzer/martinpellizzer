Berberis wazaristanica

Berberis wazaristanica, commonly known as the Wazaristan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, making it well adapted to high-altitude regions.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a notable species in local flora.
