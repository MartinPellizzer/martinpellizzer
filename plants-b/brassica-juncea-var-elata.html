Brassica juncea var. elata

Brassica juncea var. elata, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a cultivar of the mustard family.

It is characterized by its thick, leafy stems and clusters of small, cabbage-like leaves. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible parts, including the stems and leaves. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and nutritious crop in many cuisines.
