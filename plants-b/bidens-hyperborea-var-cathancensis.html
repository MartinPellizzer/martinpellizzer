Bidens hyperborea var. cathancensis

Bidens hyperborea var. cathancensis, commonly known as the Alaska ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate long periods of snow cover.
