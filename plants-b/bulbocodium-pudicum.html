Bulbocodium pudicum

Bulbocodium pudicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system, which stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
