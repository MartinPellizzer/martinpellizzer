Baccharis microphylla var. pulverulenta

Baccharis microphylla var. pulverulenta, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the fall and winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat in desert landscapes.
