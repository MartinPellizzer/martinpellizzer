Bromelia mai-pouri

Bromelia mai-pouri, commonly known as the Pounamu bromeliad, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central water-holding structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with flowers that range from yellow to red, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forests or on rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
