Baccharis confertifolia var. latifolia

Baccharis confertifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common species in ecological restoration projects.
