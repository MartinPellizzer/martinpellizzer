Blechnum vestitum

Blechnum vestitum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various habitats.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a complex structure that enhances its ornamental value in horticulture.
