Betula × hybrida var. macrophylla

Betula × hybrida var. macrophylla, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid species within the Betula genus, characterized by its white, peeling bark and distinctive, pendulous catkins.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with slender, often multi-stemmed trunks. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The silver birch is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
