Bobea putaminosa

Bobea putaminosa, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod-like fruits. The plant is a small tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Bobea putaminosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
