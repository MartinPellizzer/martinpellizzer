Bromus grossus var. tomentosus

Bromus grossus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often covered in soft, hairy structures.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
