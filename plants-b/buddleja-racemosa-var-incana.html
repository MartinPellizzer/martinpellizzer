Buddleja racemosa var. incana

Buddleja racemosa var. incana, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its soft, grayish-white fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Oleaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for landscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
