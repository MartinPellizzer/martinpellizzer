Betonica officinalis var. cernagorae

Betonica officinalis var. cernagorae, commonly known as the Balkan betonica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces blue to violet flowers arranged in a loose spike, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
