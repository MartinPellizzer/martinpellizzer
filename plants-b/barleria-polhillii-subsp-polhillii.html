Barleria polhillii subsp. polhillii

Barleria polhillii subsp.

polhillii, commonly known as the Natal barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and produces spikes of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral morphology.
