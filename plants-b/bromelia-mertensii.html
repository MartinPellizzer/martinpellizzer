Bromelia mertensii

Bromelia mertensii, commonly known as the red-tipped bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant features vibrant, spathaceous bracts that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Bromelia mertensii is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
