Brachychiton velutinosus

Brachychiton velutinosus, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it a popular choice in arid and semi-arid regions.
