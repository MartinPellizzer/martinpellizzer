Briegeria cernua

Briegeria cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, typically in shades of blue or violet, and bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
