Blysmus compressus var. aristatus

Blysmus compressus var. aristatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blysmus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its slender, compressed stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
