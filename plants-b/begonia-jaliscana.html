Begonia jaliscana

Begonia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco begonia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which often features vibrant colors and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
