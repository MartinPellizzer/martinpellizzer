Bursera confusa

Bursera confusa, commonly known as Mexican birch, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, resembling birch trees. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Bursera confusa produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain three seeds.
