Bromopsis austrosibirica

Bromopsis austrosibirica, commonly known as the Siberian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Bromopsis austrosibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
