Barosma gnidioides

Barosma gnidioides, commonly known as the coastal barosma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often in regions with high salinity. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand coastal conditions and salt spray.

Barosma gnidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
