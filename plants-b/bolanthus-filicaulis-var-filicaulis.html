Bolanthus filicaulis var. filicaulis

Bolanthus filicaulis var. filicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
