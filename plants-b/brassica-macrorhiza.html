Brassica macrorhiza

Brassica macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant false turnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, lobed leaves and thick, swollen roots that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The roots are edible and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor when cooked.
