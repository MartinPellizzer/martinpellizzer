Barbarea arcuata f. pubescens

Barbarea arcuata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
