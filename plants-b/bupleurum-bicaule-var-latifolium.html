Bupleurum bicaule var. latifolium

Bupleurum bicaule var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
