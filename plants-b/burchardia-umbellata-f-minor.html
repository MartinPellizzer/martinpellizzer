Burchardia umbellata f. minor

Burchardia umbellata f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
