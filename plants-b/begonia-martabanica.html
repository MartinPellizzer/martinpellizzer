Begonia martabanica

Begonia martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Begonia martabanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic beauty.
