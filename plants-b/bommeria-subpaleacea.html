Bommeria subpaleacea

Bommeria subpaleacea, commonly known as the paleaceae grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters typical of grasses.

Bommeria subpaleacea is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
