Babiana lobata

Babiana lobata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous geophyte native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble tulips. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, and it blooms in late winter to early spring.

Babiana lobata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
