Babiana stricta var. sulphurea

Babiana stricta var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers that resemble miniature daffodils, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
