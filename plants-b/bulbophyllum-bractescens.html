Bulbophyllum bractescens

Bulbophyllum bractescens, commonly known as the bracted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of bract-like structures that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
