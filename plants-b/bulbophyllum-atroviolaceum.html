Bulbophyllum atroviolaceum

Bulbophyllum atroviolaceum, commonly known as the black violaceum orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flowers, which can be violet or dark purple, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.
