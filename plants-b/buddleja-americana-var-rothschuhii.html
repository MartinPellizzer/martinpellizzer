Buddleja americana var. rothschuhii

Buddleja americana var. rothschuhii, commonly known as the American weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, drooping clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to lavender, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slight pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful form and extended blooming period from late spring to early fall.
