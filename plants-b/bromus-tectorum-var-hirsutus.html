Bromus tectorum var. hirsutus

Bromus tectorum var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy cheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and hairy leaves, which distinguish it from other bromus species. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a significant role in ecosystem dynamics, particularly in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and high seed production, it can dominate landscapes and impact native plant communities.
