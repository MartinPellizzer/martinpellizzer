Brassica campestris var. bullatopetsai

Brassica campestris var. bullatopetsai, commonly known as the bullate petal mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, bulbous petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
