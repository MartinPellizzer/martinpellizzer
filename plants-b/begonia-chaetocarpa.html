Begonia chaetocarpa

Begonia chaetocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive hairy stems that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Begonia chaetocarpa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
