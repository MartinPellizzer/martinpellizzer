Berberis setosa

Berberis setosa, commonly known as the creeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features multiple stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Berberis setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
