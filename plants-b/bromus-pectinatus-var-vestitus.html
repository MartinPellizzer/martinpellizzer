Bromus pectinatus var. vestitus

Bromus pectinatus var. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive bluish-green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
