Blechnum andicola

Blechnum andicola, commonly known as the Andean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

The leaves are usually evergreen, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
