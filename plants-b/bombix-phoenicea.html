Bombix phoenicea

Bombix phoenicea, commonly known as the purple gromwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form clumps over time.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
