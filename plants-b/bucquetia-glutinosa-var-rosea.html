Bucquetia glutinosa var. rosea

Bucquetia glutinosa var. rosea, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, rose-pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
