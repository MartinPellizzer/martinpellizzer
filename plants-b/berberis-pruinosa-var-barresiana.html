Berberis pruinosa var. barresiana

Berberis pruinosa var. barresiana, commonly known as the Barresian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
