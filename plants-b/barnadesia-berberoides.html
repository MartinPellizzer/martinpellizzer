Barnadesia berberoides

Barnadesia berberoides, commonly known as the golden bignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
