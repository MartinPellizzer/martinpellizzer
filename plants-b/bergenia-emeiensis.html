Bergenia emeiensis

Bergenia emeiensis, commonly known as Emei Mountain bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and rosettes of broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, upright stalks. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Bergenia emeiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
