Betula verrucosa subsp. obscura

Betula verrucosa subsp.

obscura, commonly known as the black birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark that becomes more prominent with age.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with doubly serrated margins and a pubescent underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

This species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested wetlands.
