Barbacenia viscosissima

Barbacenia viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often exude a sticky resin, which is a natural defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Barbacenia viscosissima is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
