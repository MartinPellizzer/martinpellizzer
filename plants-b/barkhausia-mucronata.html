Barkhausia mucronata

Barkhausia mucronata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in a basal rosette pattern.

Barkhausia mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
