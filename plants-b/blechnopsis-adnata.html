Blechnopsis adnata

Blechnopsis adnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rock faces or in forest understories. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with closely spaced, linear pinnae that give it a distinctive, compact appearance.

Blechnopsis adnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
