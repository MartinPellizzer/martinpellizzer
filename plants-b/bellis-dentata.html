Bellis dentata

Bellis dentata, commonly known as the toothed mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, toothed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Bellis dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
