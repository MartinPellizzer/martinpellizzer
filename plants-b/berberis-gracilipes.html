Berberis gracilipes

Berberis gracilipes, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
