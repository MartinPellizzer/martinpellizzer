Bromus unioloides f. elatus

Bromus unioloides f. elatus, commonly known as the tall bentgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to purplish in color. This variant is distinguished by its taller stature compared to other forms of Bromus unioloides.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
