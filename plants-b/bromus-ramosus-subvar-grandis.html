Bromus ramosus subvar. grandis

Bromus ramosus subvar. grandis, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Bromus ramosus subvar. grandis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.
