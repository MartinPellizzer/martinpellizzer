Bulbophyllum cornutum

Bulbophyllum cornutum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pseudobulbs and its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish seed pods.

Bulbophyllum cornutum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
