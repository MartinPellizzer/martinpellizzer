Balsamita flabelliformis

Balsamita flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Balsamita flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
