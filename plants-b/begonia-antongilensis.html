Begonia antongilensis

Begonia antongilensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
