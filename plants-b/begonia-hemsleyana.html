Begonia hemsleyana

Begonia hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green and silver tones.

Begonia hemsleyana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
