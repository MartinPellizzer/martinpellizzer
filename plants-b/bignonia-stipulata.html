Bignonia stipulata

Bignonia stipulata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent stipules, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia stipulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be grown as a decorative vine or hedge.
