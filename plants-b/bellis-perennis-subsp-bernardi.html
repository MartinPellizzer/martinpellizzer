Bellis perennis subsp. bernardi

Bellis perennis subsp.

bernardi, commonly known as the English daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies bernardi is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Bellis perennis.
