Brassica rapa var. amplexicaulis

Brassica rapa var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, followed by small, rounded pods containing seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

This variety is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
