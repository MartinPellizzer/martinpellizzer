Baccharis oxyodonta var. punctulata

Baccharis oxyodonta var. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are often dotted with small, raised glands, giving it the species name *punctulata*.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
