Boronia parviflora

Boronia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

Boronia parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
