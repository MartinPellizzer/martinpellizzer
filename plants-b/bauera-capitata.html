Bauera capitata

Bauera capitata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Bauera capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
