Bromus scoparius var. lasianthus

Bromus scoparius var. lasianthus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Bromus scoparius var. lasianthus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
