Brassavola cucullata

Brassavola cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

Brassavola cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
