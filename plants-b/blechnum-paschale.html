Blechnum paschale

Blechnum paschale, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum paschale is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
