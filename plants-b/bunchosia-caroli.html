Bunchosia caroli

Bunchosia caroli, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Bunchosia caroli is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive bark and foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
