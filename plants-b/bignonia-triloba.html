Bignonia triloba

Bignonia triloba, commonly known as the crossvine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces vibrant yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like projections, which help it climb and support its growth. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Bignonia triloba is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
