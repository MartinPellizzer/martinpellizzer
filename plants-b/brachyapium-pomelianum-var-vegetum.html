Brachyapium pomelianum var. vegetum

Brachyapium pomelianum var. vegetum, commonly known as the veined mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are similar in appearance to the common mangosteen but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable ornamental and potential fruit-bearing plant in tropical horticulture.
