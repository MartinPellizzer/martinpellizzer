Barbarea vulgaris var. macrophylla

Barbarea vulgaris var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its distinctive foliage and early blooming habit make it a notable weed in agricultural settings.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of mustard greens.
