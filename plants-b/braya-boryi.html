Braya boryi

Braya boryi, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dense, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Braya boryi plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.
