Bulbine caespitosa

Bulbine caespitosa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, clump-forming growth habit with fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
