Bulbophyllum schizodon

Bulbophyllum schizodon, commonly known as the Schizodon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, and it has a distinctive pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Bulbophyllum schizodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
