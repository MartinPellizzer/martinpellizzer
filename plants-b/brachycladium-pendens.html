Brachycladium pendens

Brachycladium pendens, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescence, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

Brachycladium pendens plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and provides habitat for various small organisms.
