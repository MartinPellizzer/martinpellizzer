Begonia stipularis

Begonia stipularis, commonly known as the stipular begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often feature intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.

Begonia stipularis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
