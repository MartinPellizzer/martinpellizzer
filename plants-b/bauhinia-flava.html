Bauhinia flava

Bauhinia flava, commonly known as the yellow bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Bauhinia flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
