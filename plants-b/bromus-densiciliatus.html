Bromus densiciliatus

Bromus densiciliatus, commonly known as the dense-haired brome, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and culms that are typically erect and somewhat spreading. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, with lemmas that are usually hairy and pointed. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Bromus densiciliatus is often found in association with other grasses and is an important component of many ecological communities.
