Bauhinia collettii

Bauhinia collettii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are alternate and composed of numerous small leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its wood is hard and durable.

Bauhinia collettii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
