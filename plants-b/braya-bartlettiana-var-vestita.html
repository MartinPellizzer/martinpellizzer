Braya bartlettiana var. vestita

Braya bartlettiana var. vestita, commonly known as the white-bracted sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, succulent stems and leaves. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
