Blechnum × leopoldense

Blechnum × leopoldense, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Blechnum capillifolium and Blechnum penna-marina.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the veins. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
