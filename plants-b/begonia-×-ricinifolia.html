Begonia × ricinifolia

Begonia × ricinifolia, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
