Begonia aspleniifolia

Begonia aspleniifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Begonia aspleniifolia requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.
