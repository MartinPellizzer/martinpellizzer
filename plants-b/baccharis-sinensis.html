Baccharis sinensis

Baccharis sinensis, commonly known as Chinese baccharis, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the fall. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Baccharis sinensis is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
