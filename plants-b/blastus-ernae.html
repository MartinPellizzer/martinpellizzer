Blastus ernae

Blastus ernae, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to regions in Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
