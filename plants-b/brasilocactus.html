Brasilocactus

Brasilocactus, commonly known as the Brazilian cactus, is a genus of flowering plants native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to dry conditions with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom during the warmer months.

Brasilocactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
