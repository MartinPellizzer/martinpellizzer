Belairia parvifoliola

Belairia parvifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved belairia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, arranged in a dense, compact growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Belairia parvifoliola is well adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude habitats.
