Broussonetia brasiliensis

Broussonetia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant has a stout, often branched trunk with a rough, fissured bark. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, commonly found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Broussonetia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
