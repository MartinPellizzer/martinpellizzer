Brachypodium frenchii

Brachypodium frenchii, commonly known as French's shortgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
