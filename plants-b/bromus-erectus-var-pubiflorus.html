Bromus erectus var. pubiflorus

Bromus erectus var. pubiflorus, commonly known as the hairy floret brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces spikelets with hairy lemmas, which distinguish it from other similar species. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Bromus erectus var. pubiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
