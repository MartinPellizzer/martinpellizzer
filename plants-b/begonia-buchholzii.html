Begonia buchholzii

Begonia buchholzii, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are dark green with a distinctive red margin. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
