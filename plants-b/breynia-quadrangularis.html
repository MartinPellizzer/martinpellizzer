Breynia quadrangularis

Breynia quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle plant or red bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Breynia quadrangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
