Biscutella didyma

Biscutella didyma, commonly known as the two-color biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its scientific name reflects its unique appearance, with "didyma" referring to the two-colored flowers.

Biscutella didyma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
