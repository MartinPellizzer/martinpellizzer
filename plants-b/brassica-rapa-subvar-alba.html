Brassica rapa subvar. alba

Brassica rapa subvar. alba, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a slender, elongated silique that contains several seeds.

Field mustard is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.
