Bienertia kavirense

Bienertia kavirense, commonly known as the Kavir sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Bienertia kavirense plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
