Bupleurum trichopodum var. dichotomum

Bupleurum trichopodum var. dichotomum, commonly known as the forked valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
