Balsamina odorata

Balsamina odorata, commonly known as the fragrant balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces showy, often bi- or tri-colored blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Balsamina odorata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and unique appearance.
