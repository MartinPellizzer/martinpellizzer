Berlinia stipulacea

Berlinia stipulacea, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
