Banksia sericostachya

Banksia sericostachya, commonly known as the silky-stemmed banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous leaf sheaths and cylindrical, yellow to orange flower spikes. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Banksia sericostachya plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
