Bartonia paniculata subsp. paniculata

Bartonia paniculata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the Florida false buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
