Bulbophyllum astelidum

Bulbophyllum astelidum, commonly known as the Astelidum orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
