Brownea speciosa

Brownea speciosa, commonly known as the showy brownea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Brownea speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
