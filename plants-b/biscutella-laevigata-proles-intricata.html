Biscutella laevigata proles intricata

Biscutella laevigata proles intricata, commonly known as the intricate-fruited biscuit weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, glandular, and hairy leaves, which are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing seeds. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
