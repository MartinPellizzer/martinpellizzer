Brassica rapa var. intermedia

Brassica rapa var. intermedia, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual or biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by spherical, three-lobed pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Field mustard is notable for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated crops.
