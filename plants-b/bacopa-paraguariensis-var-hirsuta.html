Bacopa paraguariensis var. hirsuta

Bacopa paraguariensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments and has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its stems are usually square and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
