Byrsonima crassifolia subsp. insulata

Byrsonima crassifolia subsp.

insulata, commonly known as the Caribbean apple or insular byrsonima, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands. It is characterized by its large, thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
