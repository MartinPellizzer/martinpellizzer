Boronia fraseri

Boronia fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's boronia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is often found in sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Boronia fraseri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
