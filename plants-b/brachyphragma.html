Brachyphragma

Brachyphragma, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Brachyphragma species are typically herbaceous and thrive in moist, well-drained soil. They are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers often bloom in spring and summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and indoor spaces.
