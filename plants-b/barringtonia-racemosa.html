Barringtonia racemosa

Barringtonia racemosa, commonly known as the white cedar or candle bush, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that resemble candles, which bloom at night. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Barringtonia racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
