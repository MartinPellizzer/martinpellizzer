Bulbophyllum citricolor

Bulbophyllum citricolor, commonly known as the citrine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, which resembles citrus leaves, hence its common name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from yellow to orange.

Bulbophyllum citricolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
