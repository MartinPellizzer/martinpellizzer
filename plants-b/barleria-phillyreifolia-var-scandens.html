Barleria phillyreifolia var. scandens

Barleria phillyreifolia var. scandens, commonly known as the creeping barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
