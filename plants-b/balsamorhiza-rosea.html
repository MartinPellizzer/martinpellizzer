Balsamorhiza rosea

Balsamorhiza rosea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a rosette of blue-green, hairy leaves and a tall, slender stem topped with a conical inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Balsamorhiza rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
