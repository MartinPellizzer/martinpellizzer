Bursera heterophylla

Bursera heterophylla, commonly known as the Mexican bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant typically has alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that may vary in shape depending on the environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Bursera heterophylla is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, making it a resilient species in arid regions.
