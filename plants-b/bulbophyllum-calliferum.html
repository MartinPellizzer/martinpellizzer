Bulbophyllum calliferum

Bulbophyllum calliferum, commonly known as the orchid with the long-stalked flowers, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often displayed in clusters on long, slender stems. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical environments.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
