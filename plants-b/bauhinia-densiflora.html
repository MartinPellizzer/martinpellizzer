Bauhinia densiflora

Bauhinia densiflora, commonly known as the velvetwood or Brazilian wisteria, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with rounded leaflets and produces pods that contain flattened, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia densiflora also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
