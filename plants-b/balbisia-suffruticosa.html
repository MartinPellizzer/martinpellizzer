Balbisia suffruticosa

Balbisia suffruticosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsam, is a shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has woody stems that become more prominent as it matures, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Balbisia suffruticosa is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
