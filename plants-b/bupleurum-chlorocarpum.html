Bupleurum chlorocarpum

Bupleurum chlorocarpum, commonly known as the green-seeded bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white or pale blue, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule, which gives the plant its specific epithet "chlorocarpum," referring to its green fruits.
