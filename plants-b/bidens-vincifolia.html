Bidens vincifolia

Bidens vincifolia, commonly known as yellow beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bidens vincifolia is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
