Bambusa textilis

Bambusa textilis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow culms that add ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. The plant has a dense, upright growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Bambusa textilis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticulture for privacy screens and decorative purposes.
