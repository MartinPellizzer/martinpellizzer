Bauhinia finlaysoniana var. montana

Bauhinia finlaysoniana var. montana, commonly known as the Montane Bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, bipinnate leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
