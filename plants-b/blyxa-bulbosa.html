Blyxa bulbosa

Blyxa bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that emerge on slender stalks above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands.

Blyxa bulbosa is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to help control algae growth.
