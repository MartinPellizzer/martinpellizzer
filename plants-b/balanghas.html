Balanghas

Balanghas, commonly known as *Pterocarpus indicus*, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain flat, dark seeds. Balanghas is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
