Beyeria viscosa var. latifolia

Beyeria viscosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different ecological conditions.
