Begonia moluccana

Begonia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a distinctive texture.

Begonia moluccana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
