Bulbophyllum retrorsum

Bulbophyllum retrorsum, commonly known as the backward-facing orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the flowers face backward, giving it its common name. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small and colorful, with a wide range of hues including white, yellow, and pink.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
