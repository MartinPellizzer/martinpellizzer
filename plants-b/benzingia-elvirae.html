Benzingia elvirae

Benzingia elvirae, commonly known as the Elvirae's ghost flower, is a rare and endangered plant species native to a small region in Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited seed viability, efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this rare species.
