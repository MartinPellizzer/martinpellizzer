Basella rubra var. virescens

Basella rubra var. virescens, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are typically bright green, though they can vary in color depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable due to its mild flavor and nutritional value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
