Bulbine ciliata

Bulbine ciliata, commonly known as the silver bulbine, is a succulent perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with prominent purple markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
