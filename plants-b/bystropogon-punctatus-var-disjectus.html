Bystropogon punctatus var. disjectus

Bystropogon punctatus var. disjectus, commonly known as the scattered bristle grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like clusters.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
