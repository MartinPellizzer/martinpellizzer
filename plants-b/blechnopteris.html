Blechnopteris

Blechnopteris, commonly known as the water fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, finely divided fronds that often resemble the shape of a feather. They are typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream sides, where they thrive in humid conditions. Blechnopteris species are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees or rocks without being parasitic.

Their reproductive structures, including sori and spores, are essential for their propagation and ecological role in various habitats.
