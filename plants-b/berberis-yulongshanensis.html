Berberis yulongshanensis

Berberis yulongshanensis, commonly known as the Yulongshan barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
