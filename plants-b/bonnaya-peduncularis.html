Bonnaya peduncularis

Bonnaya peduncularis, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Bonnaya peduncularis is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient addition to xerophytic gardens.
