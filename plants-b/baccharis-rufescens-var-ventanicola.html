Baccharis rufescens var. ventanicola

Baccharis rufescens var. ventanicola, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
