Barnadesia ulicina

Barnadesia ulicina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
