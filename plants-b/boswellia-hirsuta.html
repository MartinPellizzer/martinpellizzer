Boswellia hirsuta

Boswellia hirsuta, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish bark and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested for its aromatic gum, used in traditional medicine and incense. Boswellia hirsuta thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
