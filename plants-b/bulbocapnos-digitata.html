Bulbocapnos digitata

Bulbocapnos digitata, commonly known as the mountain ash or white ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
