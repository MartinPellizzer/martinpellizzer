Betula × seideliana f. supernana

Betula × seideliana f. supernana, commonly known as the giant white birch, is a unique hybrid species within the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its exceptionally large, white, papery bark that peels in thin layers, creating a striking visual effect. This variety is native to the boreal forests of northern Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The tree typically grows to heights of 15–20 meters, with a broad, rounded crown.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
