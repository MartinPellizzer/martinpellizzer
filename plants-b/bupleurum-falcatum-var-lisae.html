Bupleurum falcatum var. lisae

Bupleurum falcatum var. lisae, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
