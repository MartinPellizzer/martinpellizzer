Bothriochloa saccharoides subsp. australis

Bothriochloa saccharoides subsp.

australis, commonly known as Australian sugar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
