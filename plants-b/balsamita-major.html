Balsamita major

Balsamita major, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has aromatic, woolly leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Balsamita major is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
