Bulbine latibracteata

Bulbine latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Bulbine latibracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
