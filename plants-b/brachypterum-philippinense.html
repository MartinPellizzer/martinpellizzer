Brachypterum philippinense

Brachypterum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine dwarf senna, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, while its leaves are bipinnate, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
