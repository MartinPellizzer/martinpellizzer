Basella filiformis

Basella filiformis, commonly known as slender basella, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Basellaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asia, for their mild, slightly acidic flavor.

Basella filiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated as a ornamental or edible plant.
