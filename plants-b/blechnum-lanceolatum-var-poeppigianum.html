Blechnum lanceolatum var. poeppigianum

Blechnum lanceolatum var. poeppigianum, commonly known as the Poeppig's harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, leathery, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are evergreen, with a serrated margin and a compact, rosette-like growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
