Betonica incana

Betonica incana, commonly known as hoary betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Betonica incana is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
