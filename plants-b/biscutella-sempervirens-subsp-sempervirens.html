Biscutella sempervirens subsp. sempervirens

Biscutella sempervirens subsp.

sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of linear, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes during the spring, which are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. Its root system is thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
