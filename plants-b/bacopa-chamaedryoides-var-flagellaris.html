Bacopa chamaedryoides var. flagellaris

Bacopa chamaedryoides var. flagellaris, commonly known as creeping bacopa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bacopa chamaedryoides var. flagellaris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
