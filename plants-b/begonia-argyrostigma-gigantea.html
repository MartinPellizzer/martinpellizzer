Begonia argyrostigma gigantea

Begonia argyrostigma gigantea, commonly known as the giant silver-stem begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, silver-veined stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is primarily grown in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
