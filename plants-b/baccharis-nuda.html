Baccharis nuda

Baccharis nuda, commonly known as slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Baccharis nuda is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
