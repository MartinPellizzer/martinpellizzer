Boronia umbellata

Boronia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Boronia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
