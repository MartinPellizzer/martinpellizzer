Bauhinia tamarindacea

Bauhinia tamarindacea, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The tree produces long, flat pods containing seeds, and its bark is often rough and fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
