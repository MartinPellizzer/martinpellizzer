Bobea hookeri

Bobea hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's bean, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

Bobea hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
