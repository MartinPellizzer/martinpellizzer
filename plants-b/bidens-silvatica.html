Bidens silvatica

Bidens silvatica, commonly known as woodland tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent black markings. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.
