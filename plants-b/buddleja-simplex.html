Buddleja simplex

Buddleja simplex, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja simplex is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
