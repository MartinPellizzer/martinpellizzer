Bauhinia macropoda

Bauhinia macropoda, commonly known as the giant orchid tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are trifoliate at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia macropoda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
