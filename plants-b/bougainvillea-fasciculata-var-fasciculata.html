Bougainvillea fasciculata var. fasciculata

Bougainvillea fasciculata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the common bougainvillea, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant clusters of colorful bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
