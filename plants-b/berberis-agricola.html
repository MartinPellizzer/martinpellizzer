Berberis agricola

Berberis agricola, commonly known as the agricola barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features thorny stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and winter interest, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
