Bromus lanceolatus var. dasystachys

Bromus lanceolatus var. dasystachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
