Beloperone variegata

Beloperone variegata, commonly known as the variegated beloperone, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Beloperone variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
