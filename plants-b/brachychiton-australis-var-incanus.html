Brachychiton australis var. incanus

Brachychiton australis var. incanus, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, silver-green foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its silky texture.

This species is well-suited to dry conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking visual appeal.
