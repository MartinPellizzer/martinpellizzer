Blandfordia marginata

Blandfordia marginata, commonly known as the pink honeycomb lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its unique, honeycomb-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Blandfordia marginata thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in coastal gardens.
