Brassica adpressa var. lasiocarpa

Brassica adpressa var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-haired mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The variety lasiocarpa is distinguished by its dense, hairy coating, which provides protection against environmental stressors.
