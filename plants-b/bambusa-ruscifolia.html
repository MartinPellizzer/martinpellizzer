Bambusa ruscifolia

Bambusa ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Bambusa ruscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in traditional industries.
