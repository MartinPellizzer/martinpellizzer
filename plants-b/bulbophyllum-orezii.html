Bulbophyllum orezii

Bulbophyllum orezii, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found in forest understories.
