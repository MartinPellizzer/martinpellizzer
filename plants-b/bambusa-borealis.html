Bambusa borealis

Bambusa borealis, commonly known as the northern bamboo, is a species native to the high altitudes of Asia, particularly in regions like China and Nepal.

It is a perennial grass that forms dense clumps with tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Bambusa borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its use in traditional crafts and construction.
