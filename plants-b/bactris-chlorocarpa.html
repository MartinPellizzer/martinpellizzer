Bactris chlorocarpa

Bactris chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green palm or yellow palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which give way to edible fruits resembling small, round coconuts. Bactris chlorocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in tropical regions.
