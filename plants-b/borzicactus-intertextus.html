Borzicactus intertextus

Borzicactus intertextus, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, lateral branches. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
