Bolboschoenus fernaldii

Bolboschoenus fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Bolboschoenus fernaldii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
