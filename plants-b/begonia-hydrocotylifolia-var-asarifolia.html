Begonia hydrocotylifolia var. asarifolia

Begonia hydrocotylifolia var. asarifolia, commonly known as the asarifolia begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, which is often the main attraction of the plant.
