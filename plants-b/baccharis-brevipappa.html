Baccharis brevipappa

Baccharis brevipappa, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a notable component of chaparral and desert ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
