Baillonella marginata

Baillonella marginata, commonly known as the African daisy or African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Baillonella marginata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
