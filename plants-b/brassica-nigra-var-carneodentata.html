Brassica nigra var. carneodentata

Brassica nigra var. carneodentata, commonly known as the purple sprouting broccoli, is a variety of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescences that produce small, cabbage-like flower buds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, while the stems are thick and fibrous.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for cultivation in cool seasons and for use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
