Baccaurea bivalvis

Baccaurea bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved bael, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant bears fruit that matures into a thick, fleshy, orange-red berry with a distinctive two-valved opening at the top. The fruit contains a single large, flat seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, lowland forests.
