Baptisia × stricta

Baptisia × stricta, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking blue to purple flower spikes. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Baptisia × stricta is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
