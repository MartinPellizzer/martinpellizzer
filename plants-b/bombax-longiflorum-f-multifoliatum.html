Bombax longiflorum f. multifoliatum

Bombax longiflorum f. multifoliatum, commonly known as the multileaf cotton tree, is a variant of the cotton tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage and flowers contribute to its role in both ecological and horticultural settings.
