Begonia conipila

Begonia conipila, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive silver or white vein pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia conipila is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
