Brachiaria subquadripara var. piligera

Brachiaria subquadripara var. piligera, commonly known as the hairy leaf bluestem, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically forms dense tussocks and has a fibrous root system, making it resilient in various soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This grass is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, contributing to soil stabilization and forage for livestock.
