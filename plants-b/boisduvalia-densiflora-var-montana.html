Boisduvalia densiflora var. montana

Boisduvalia densiflora var. montana, commonly known as the mountain boisduvalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble clouds, composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
