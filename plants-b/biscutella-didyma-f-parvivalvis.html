Biscutella didyma f. parvivalvis

Biscutella didyma f. parvivalvis, commonly known as the small-flowered biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of linear, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, with a preference for well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and has a low-growing habit.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
