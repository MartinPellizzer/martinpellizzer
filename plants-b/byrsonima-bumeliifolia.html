Byrsonima bumeliifolia

Byrsonima bumeliifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the red mombin, is a small to medium-sized tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
