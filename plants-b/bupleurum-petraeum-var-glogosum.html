Bupleurum petraeum var. glogosum

Bupleurum petraeum var. glogosum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
