Berberis virgata var. typica

Berberis virgata var. typica, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, bright green leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
