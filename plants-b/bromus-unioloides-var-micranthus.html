Bromus unioloides var. micranthus

Bromus unioloides var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its small, compact inflorescence and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
