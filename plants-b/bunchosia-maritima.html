Bunchosia maritima

Bunchosia maritima, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, adapting well to maritime environments. The plant features showy yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bunchosia maritima is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
