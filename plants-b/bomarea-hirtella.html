Bomarea hirtella

Bomarea hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bomarea, is a striking member of the Alstroemeriaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring elongated, tuberous roots that help it survive in its native habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bomarea hirtella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
