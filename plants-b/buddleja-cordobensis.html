Buddleja cordobensis

Buddleja cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba butterfly bush, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Buddleja cordobensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
