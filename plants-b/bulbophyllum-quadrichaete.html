Bulbophyllum quadrichaete

Bulbophyllum quadrichaete, commonly known as the four-hair lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help conserve moisture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
