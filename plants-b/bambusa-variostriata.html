Bambusa variostriata

Bambusa variostriata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that display a unique coloration of green, yellow, and white stripes along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness, Bambusa variostriata is widely cultivated in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
