Bambusa glaucescens f. shirosuji

Bambusa glaucescens f. shirosuji, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy, bluish-gray sheen that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. This ornamental bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
