Brassica trilocularis

Brassica trilocularis, commonly known as the three-loculed mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, round pods containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
