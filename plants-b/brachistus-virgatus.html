Brachistus virgatus

Brachistus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
