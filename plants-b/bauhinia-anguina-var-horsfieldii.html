Bauhinia anguina var. horsfieldii

Bauhinia anguina var. horsfieldii, commonly known as the horsfield's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a hand, with five to seven lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
