Begonia princeae

Begonia princeae, commonly known as the Prince's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Begonia princeae is a perennial species that can be propagated through stem cuttings or seeds.
