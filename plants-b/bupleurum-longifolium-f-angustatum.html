Bupleurum longifolium f. angustatum

Bupleurum longifolium f. angustatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clustered umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Bupleurum longifolium.
