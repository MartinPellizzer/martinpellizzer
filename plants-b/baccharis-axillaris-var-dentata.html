Baccharis axillaris var. dentata

Baccharis axillaris var. dentata, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a common component of local flora in its native range.
