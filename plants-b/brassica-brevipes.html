Brassica brevipes

Brassica brevipes, commonly known as the shortfooted mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils across the western United States. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The capsule fruit contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
