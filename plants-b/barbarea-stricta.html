Barbarea stricta

Barbarea stricta, commonly known as the upright winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it has a short, thick stem that supports its foliage. Barbarea stricta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in moist, temperate climates.

It is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential edible plant in certain regions.
