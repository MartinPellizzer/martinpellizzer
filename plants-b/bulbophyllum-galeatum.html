Bulbophyllum galeatum

Bulbophyllum galeatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a distinct fragrance. Its bulb-like pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum galeatum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
