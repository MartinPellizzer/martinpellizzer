Bulbophyllum melanoglossum

Bulbophyllum melanoglossum, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, narrow petals and a distinctive blackened lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled conditions.
