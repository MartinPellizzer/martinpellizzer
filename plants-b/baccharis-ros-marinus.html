Baccharis ros-marinus

Baccharis ros-marinus, commonly known as rosemary bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, herbal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
