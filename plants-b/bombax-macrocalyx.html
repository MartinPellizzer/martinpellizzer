Bombax macrocalyx

Bombax macrocalyx, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures for its fibers and medicinal properties.
