Baccharis papillosa subsp. chaparensis

Baccharis papillosa subsp.

chaparensis, commonly known as Chapare shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and northern Argentina. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is shrub-like, with silver-white, fuzzy foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
