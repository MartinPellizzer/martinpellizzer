Brassica napus subsp. rapifera

Brassica napus subsp.

rapifera, commonly known as field rape, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its robust growth, with large, alternate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant produces a thick, starchy root that is the primary edible part, used in various culinary and industrial applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high oil content in seeds.

This subspecies is also valued for its role in crop rotation and as a forage crop.
