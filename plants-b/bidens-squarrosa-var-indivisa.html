Bidens squarrosa var. indivisa

Bidens squarrosa var. indivisa, commonly known as the roughleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and a component of native plant communities.
