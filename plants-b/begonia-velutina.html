Begonia velutina

Begonia velutina, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. Begonia velutina prefers shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden beds.
