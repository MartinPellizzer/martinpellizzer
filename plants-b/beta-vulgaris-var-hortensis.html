Beta vulgaris var. hortensis

Beta vulgaris var. hortensis, commonly known as beetroot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy root that can range in color from red to yellow or white. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is cultivated for its edible root and leaves. The leaves, known as chard, are also consumed and are rich in nutrients such as vitamins and minerals.

Its growth is typically favored in cool climates and well-drained soils.
