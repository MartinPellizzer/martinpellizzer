Buchnera remotiflora

Buchnera remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered buchnera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Buchnera remotiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
