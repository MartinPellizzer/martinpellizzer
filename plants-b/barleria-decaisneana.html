Barleria decaisneana

Barleria decaisneana, commonly known as the Natal barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Barleria decaisneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
