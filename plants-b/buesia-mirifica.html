Buesia mirifica

Buesia mirifica, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive central yellow or orange spot. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the lakebed. Buesia mirifica is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor waters and thrives in full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a symbol of purity and spiritual renewal in local traditions.
