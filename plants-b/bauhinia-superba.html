Bauhinia superba

Bauhinia superba, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. Bauhinia superba thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
