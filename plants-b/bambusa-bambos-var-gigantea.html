Bambusa bambos var. gigantea

Bambusa bambos var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a striking member of the grass family (Poaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 15 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in construction and crafts.
