Brassica rapa var. frenosiana

Brassica rapa var. frenosiana, commonly known as the Mexican rapeseed, is a wild relative of cultivated brassicas.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its yellow flowers and robust, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other economically important crops like cabbage and mustard.

Its genetic diversity makes it a valuable resource for breeding programs aimed at improving resilience in Brassica species.
