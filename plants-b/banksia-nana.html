Banksia nana

Banksia nana, commonly known as the dwarf banksia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling the shape of a bottle. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia nana thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

It is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing food for various pollinators and wildlife.
