Barkhausia paludosa

Barkhausia paludosa, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Barkhausia paludosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
