Benincasa hispida var. chieh-qua

Benincasa hispida var. chieh-qua, commonly known as the Chinese wax gourd, is a variant of the hairy gourd native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy skin that becomes smooth and pale as it matures. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its versatility in culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
