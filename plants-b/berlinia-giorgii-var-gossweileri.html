Berlinia giorgii var. gossweileri

Berlinia giorgii var. gossweileri, commonly known as the African ebony, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is considered a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.
