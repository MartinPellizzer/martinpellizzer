Baccharis marginalis var. linifolia

Baccharis marginalis var. linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
