Bumelia × cassinifolia

Bumelia × cassinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Bumelia × cassinifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or scrub habitats.
