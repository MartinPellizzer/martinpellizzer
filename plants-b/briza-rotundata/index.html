Briza Rotundata

Briza rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf vernal grass, is a delicate yet resilient plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. With its slender stems and clusters of small, feathery flowers, it adds a subtle elegance to meadows and woodland edges. This species is often overlooked due to its modest appearance, yet it plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Its unique rounded leaves and early blooming period make it a valuable indicator of environmental health. Despite its quiet presence, Briza rotundata deserves recognition for its beauty and ecological significance.
