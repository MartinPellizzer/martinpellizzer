Billbergia × koechlii

Billbergia × koechlii, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia cucullata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting green bract. It produces fibrous roots and has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water.

Billbergia × koechlii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
