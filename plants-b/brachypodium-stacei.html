Brachypodium stacei

Brachypodium stacei, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Brachypodium stacei is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.

Its seeds are also utilized in some traditional herbal remedies.
