Benzoin puberulum

Benzoin puberulum, commonly known as the rough benzoin, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The tree is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, clustered leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
