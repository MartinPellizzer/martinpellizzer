Bistorta major subsp. cordifolia

Bistorta major subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lanceolate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces tall, leafy stems bearing dense clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
