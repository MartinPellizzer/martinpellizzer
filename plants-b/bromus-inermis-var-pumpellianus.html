Bromus inermis var. pumpellianus

Bromus inermis var. pumpellianus, commonly known as the pumpellian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to grayish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
