Brassica napus var. nigricans

Brassica napus var. nigricans, commonly known as black mustard, is a variety of the oilseed rape species.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are dark brown to black in color. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
