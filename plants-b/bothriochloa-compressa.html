Bothriochloa compressa

Bothriochloa compressa, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
