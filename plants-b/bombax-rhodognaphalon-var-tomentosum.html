Bombax rhodognaphalon var. tomentosum

Bombax rhodognaphalon var. tomentosum, commonly known as the silky cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a fibrous capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
