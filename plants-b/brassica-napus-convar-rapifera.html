Brassica napus convar. rapifera

Brassica napus convar. rapifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, broad lance-shaped leaves, and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are rich in oil and used in various industrial and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high oil content and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agriculture and horticulture.
