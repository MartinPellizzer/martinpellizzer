Brizula pumilio

Brizula pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf bristlegrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, wiry stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its compact size and unique foliage make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
