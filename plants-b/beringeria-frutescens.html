Beringeria frutescens

Beringeria frutescens, commonly known as the Beringian dwarf shrub, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the Arctic tundra regions of Siberia and Alaska.

It is characterized by its low-growing, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves adapted to withstand extreme cold and nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Beringeria frutescens is considered a valuable indicator species for climate change impacts in Arctic ecosystems.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for researchers studying plant adaptation in extreme environments.
