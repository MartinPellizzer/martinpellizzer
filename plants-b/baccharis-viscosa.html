Baccharis viscosa

Baccharis viscosa, commonly known as brittle bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong scent when crushed. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Baccharis viscosa is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped landscapes.
