Bulbophyllum arunachalense

Bulbophyllum arunachalense, commonly known as the Arunachal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of white and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
