Bupleurum divaricatum var. elatius

Bupleurum divaricatum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
