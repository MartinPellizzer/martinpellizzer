Biserrula pelecinus subsp. leiocarpa

Biserrula pelecinus subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the slender biserrula, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in dry regions.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both ecological restoration and livestock grazing.
