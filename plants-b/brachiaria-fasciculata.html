Brachiaria fasciculata

Brachiaria fasciculata, commonly known as slender brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Brachiaria fasciculata is valued for its use in pastureland and as a component of erosion control in tropical regions.
