Billbergia × perringiana

Billbergia × perringiana, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia temblada.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that can range from red to orange. It is native to South America and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Billbergia × perringiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
