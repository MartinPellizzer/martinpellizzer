Becium aureoviride var. lupotoense

Becium aureoviride var. lupotoense, commonly known as the Lupoto golden green hibiscus, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-green foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
