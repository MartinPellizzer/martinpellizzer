Berberis nutanticarpa

Berberis nutanticarpa, commonly known as the Florida barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant conservation.
