Benthamidia florida f. pendula

Benthamidia florida f. pendula, commonly known as the pendant flowering benthamidia, is a unique variant of the species Benthamidia florida.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with stems that droop gracefully, creating an elegant, cascading appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to horticultural collections.
