Begonia blumenaviensis subsp. brachyphylla

Begonia blumenaviensis subsp.

brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the specific habitat. This subspecies thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and texture to shaded garden spaces.
