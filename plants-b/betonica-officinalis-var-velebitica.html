Betonica officinalis var. velebitica

Betonica officinalis var. velebitica, commonly known as the Velebit betonica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
