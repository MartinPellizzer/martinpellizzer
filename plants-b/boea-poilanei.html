Boea poilanei

Boea poilanei, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
