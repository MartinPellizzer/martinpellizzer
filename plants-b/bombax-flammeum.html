Bombax flammeum

Bombax flammeum, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed pods. The tree's trunk is often smooth and may develop a flaky, peeling bark. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and shed seasonally, contributing to its deciduous nature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
