Benthamia procera

Benthamia procera, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its leaves are small, gray-green, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Benthamia procera is adapted to survive in harsh, low-water environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
