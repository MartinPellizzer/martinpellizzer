Bulbophyllum comptonii

Bulbophyllum comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum comptonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
