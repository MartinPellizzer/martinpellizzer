Brachypodium genuense

Brachypodium genuense, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its hardiness and ecological significance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
