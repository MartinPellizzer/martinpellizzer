Bidens verticillata

Bidens verticillata, commonly known as the whorled beggarlice, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features a sprawling growth habit with a taproot and slender, branching stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite or whorled, with a rough, hairy texture and toothed margins.

Bidens verticillata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
