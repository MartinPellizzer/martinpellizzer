Ballota longicalyx

Ballota longicalyx, commonly known as the long-stemmed ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

Ballota longicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
