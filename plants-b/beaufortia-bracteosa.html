Beaufortia bracteosa

Beaufortia bracteosa, commonly known as the Western Australian flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Beaufortia bracteosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and visual impact.
