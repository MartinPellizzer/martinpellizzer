Baccharis glabrata

Baccharis glabrata, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
