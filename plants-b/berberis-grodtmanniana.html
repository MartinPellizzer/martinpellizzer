Berberis grodtmanniana

Berberis grodtmanniana, commonly known as the yellow barbed wire bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and unique, thorny structure.
