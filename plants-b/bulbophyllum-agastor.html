Bulbophyllum agastor

Bulbophyllum agastor, commonly known as the Agastor orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow and orange.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
