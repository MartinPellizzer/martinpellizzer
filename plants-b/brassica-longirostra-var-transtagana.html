Brassica longirostra var. transtagana

Brassica longirostra var. transtagana, commonly known as the long-beaked mustard, is a variant of the longirostra mustard species.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its distinctive elongated siliqua fruits, which contain numerous small, round seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of soil health in certain ecosystems.
