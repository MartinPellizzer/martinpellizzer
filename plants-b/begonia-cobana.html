Begonia cobana

Begonia cobana, commonly known as the Cobana begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, vibrant red flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia cobana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
