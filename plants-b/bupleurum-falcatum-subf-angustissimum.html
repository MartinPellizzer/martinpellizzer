Bupleurum falcatum subf. angustissimum

Bupleurum falcatum subf.

angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in grasslands, woodland edges, and riverbanks.

The plant features slender, branching stems with lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in loose, terminal clusters.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
