Bauhinia viridescens var. hirsuta

Bauhinia viridescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
