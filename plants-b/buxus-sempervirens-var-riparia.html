Buxus sempervirens var. riparia

Buxus sempervirens var. riparia, commonly known as river boxwood, is a variant of the common boxwood native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This variety is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.

It is relatively slow-growing and requires minimal pruning once established.
