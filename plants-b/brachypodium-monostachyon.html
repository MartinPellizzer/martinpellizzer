Brachypodium monostachyon

Brachypodium monostachyon, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Brachypodium monostachyon is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.

Its genetic simplicity and adaptability make it a model organism in plant biology research.
