Baeckea plicata

Baeckea plicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Baeckea plicata is often found in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is a hardy species that is relatively low maintenance once established.
