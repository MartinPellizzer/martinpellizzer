Biota orientalis var. monstrosa

Biota orientalis var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant oriental biota, is a strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

Native to the temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, showcasing its adaptability to varied climates. The plant's unique varietal trait, "monstrosa," refers to its exaggerated growth patterns, including elongated stems and oversized foliage. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, range from white to pale pink, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts alike.
