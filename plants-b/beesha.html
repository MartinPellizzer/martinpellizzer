Beesha

Beesha, commonly known as the wild carrot, is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of the parsley family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its taproot is often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

Beesha is also an important plant for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
