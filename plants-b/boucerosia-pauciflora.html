Boucerosia pauciflora

Boucerosia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered buckerbrush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.

Boucerosia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
