Bonamia mattogrossensis var. obtusifolia

Bonamia mattogrossensis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wild spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a waxy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional cuisines.
