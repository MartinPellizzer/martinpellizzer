Bourreria rotata

Bourreria rotata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums.

Bourreria rotata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
