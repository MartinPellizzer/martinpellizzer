Bidens pilosa f. scandicina

Bidens pilosa f. scandicina, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Its growth habit makes it both a common weed and a plant of ecological significance.
