Bulbophyllum tricarinatum

Bulbophyllum tricarinatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while the inflorescence bears numerous small, brightly colored flowers.

Bulbophyllum tricarinatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
