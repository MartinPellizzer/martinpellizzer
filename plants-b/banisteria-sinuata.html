Banisteria sinuata

Banisteria sinuata, commonly known as the red-veined or red-vein plant, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it can fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

Banisteria sinuata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
