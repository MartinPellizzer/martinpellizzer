Bignonia caudata

Bignonia caudata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and showy yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bignonia caudata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
