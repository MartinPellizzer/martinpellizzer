Berberis turcomanica subsp. eriwanensis

Berberis turcomanica subsp.

eriwanensis, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal berries.
