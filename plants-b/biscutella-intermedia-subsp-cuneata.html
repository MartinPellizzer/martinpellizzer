Biscutella intermedia subsp. cuneata

Biscutella intermedia subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant has slender, upright stems with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf shape compared to other subspecies of Biscutella intermedia.
