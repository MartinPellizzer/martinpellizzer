Berberis gambleana

Berberis gambleana, commonly known as the Gambier barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Berberis gambleana is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various climate conditions.
