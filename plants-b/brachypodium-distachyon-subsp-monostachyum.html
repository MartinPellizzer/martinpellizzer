Brachypodium distachyon subsp. monostachyum

Brachypodium distachyon subsp.

monostachyum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a distinctive feature of this subspecies. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and disturbed sites.

Due to its genetic simplicity and adaptability, it is also used as a model organism in plant biology research.
