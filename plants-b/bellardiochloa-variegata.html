Bellardiochloa variegata

Bellardiochloa variegata, commonly known as the variegated bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades with distinctive white or silver markings along the midribs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Bellardiochloa variegata is also valued for its ability to tolerate moderate foot traffic and its low maintenance requirements.
