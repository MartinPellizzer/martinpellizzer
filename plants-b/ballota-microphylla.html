Ballota microphylla

Ballota microphylla, commonly known as the lesser ballota, is a small, perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, glandular leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Ballota microphylla is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
