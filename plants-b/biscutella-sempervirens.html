Biscutella sempervirens

Biscutella sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils.

Biscutella sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers and evergreen foliage.
