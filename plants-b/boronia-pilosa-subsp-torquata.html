Boronia pilosa subsp. torquata

Boronia pilosa subsp.

torquata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
