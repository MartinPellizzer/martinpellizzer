Brunsvigia striata

Brunsvigia striata, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright red, tubular flowers resembling firecrackers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, eye-catching appearance and is well-suited to rock gardens or well-drained soils.
