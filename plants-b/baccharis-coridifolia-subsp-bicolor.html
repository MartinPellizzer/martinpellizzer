Baccharis coridifolia subsp. bicolor

Baccharis coridifolia subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored baccharis, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The leaves are usually gray-green on the upper surface and white underneath, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
