Boronia bicolor

Boronia bicolor, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy as well.

Boronia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
