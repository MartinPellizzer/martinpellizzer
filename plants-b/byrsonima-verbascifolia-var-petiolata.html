Byrsonima verbascifolia var. petiolata

Byrsonima verbascifolia var. petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian mallow or purple mallow, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent petioles. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
