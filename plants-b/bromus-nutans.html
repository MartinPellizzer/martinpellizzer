Bromus nutans

Bromus nutans, commonly known as the prostrate brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that is often held close to the ground.

Bromus nutans is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
