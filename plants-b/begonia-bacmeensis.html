Begonia bacmeensis

Begonia bacmeensis, commonly known as the Bacmeen's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often velvety foliage and showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that can vary in shape and color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by brightly colored bracts.

Begonia bacmeensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
