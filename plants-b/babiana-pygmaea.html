Babiana pygmaea

Babiana pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky areas and is drought-tolerant once established.

Babiana pygmaea is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and compact size.
