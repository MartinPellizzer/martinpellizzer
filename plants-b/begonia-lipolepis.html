Begonia lipolepis

Begonia lipolepis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is not only visually appealing but also has a unique texture that sets it apart from other begonia species.

Begonia lipolepis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
