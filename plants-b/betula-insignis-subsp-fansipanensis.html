Betula insignis subsp. fansipanensis

Betula insignis subsp.

fansipanensis, commonly known as the Fansipan birch, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Vietnam. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in cloud forests at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive white to pale yellow coloration.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
