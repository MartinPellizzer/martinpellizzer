Begonia dealbata

Begonia dealbata, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a white or silver veining. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Begonia dealbata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
