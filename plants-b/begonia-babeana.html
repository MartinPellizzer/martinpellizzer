Begonia babeana

Begonia babeana, commonly known as the silver queen begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silvery or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia babeana is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in outdoor gardens.
