Brexia apoda

Brexia apoda, commonly known as the leafless bellflower, is a unique plant species characterized by its absence of true leaves.

Instead of leaves, it possesses modified stems that perform photosynthesis. It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its specialized habitat, Brexia apoda is considered a rare and endangered species.
