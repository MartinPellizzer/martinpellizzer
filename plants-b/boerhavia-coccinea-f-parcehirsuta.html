Boerhavia coccinea f. parcehirsuta

Boerhavia coccinea f. parcehirsuta, commonly known as the redstem goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its reddish stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with succulent leaves that store water.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
