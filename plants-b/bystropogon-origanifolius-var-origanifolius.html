Bystropogon origanifolius var. origanifolius

Bystropogon origanifolius var. origanifolius, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a serrated margin.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
