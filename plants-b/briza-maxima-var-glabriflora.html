Briza maxima var. glabriflora

Briza maxima var. glabriflora, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce delicate, feathery inflorescences with small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
