Blechnum serpentinum

Blechnum serpentinum, commonly known as the serpent fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often twisted fronds that resemble the shape of a serpent, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Blechnum serpentinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
