Banisteria ciliata

Banisteria ciliata, commonly known as the red-barked or velvetleaf banisteria, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Banisteria ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
