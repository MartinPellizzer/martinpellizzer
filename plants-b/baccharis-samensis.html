Baccharis samensis

Baccharis samensis, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are generally narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
