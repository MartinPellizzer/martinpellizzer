Byrsonima coniophylla

Byrsonima coniophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or surinam cherry, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. The fruit is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor, often used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
