Bambusa breviflora

Bambusa breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features slender, green culms that are typically no more than 2 meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Bambusa breviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
