Bryophyllum triangulare

Bryophyllum triangulare, commonly known as the triangular leaf plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically during the spring and summer months. Bryophyllum triangulare is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.

It can propagate through both vegetative means and seeds, making it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
