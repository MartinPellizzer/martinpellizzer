Bignonia lucida

Bignonia lucida, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves and is often found growing in moist, shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Bignonia lucida is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
