Bulbostylis grandibulbosa

Bulbostylis grandibulbosa, commonly known as the giant bulbostylis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous rhizomes and dense clumps of grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale green flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth form, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
