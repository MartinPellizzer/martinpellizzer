Berberis conferta var. psiloclada

Berberis conferta var. psiloclada, commonly known as the psiloclada barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
