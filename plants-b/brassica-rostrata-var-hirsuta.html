Brassica rostrata var. hirsuta

Brassica rostrata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are alternate and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed habitats.
