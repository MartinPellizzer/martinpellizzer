Bactris pectinata var. spruceana

Bactris pectinata var. spruceana, commonly known as the red-edged palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are often reddish-edged. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
