Bromus hordeaceus var. thominei

Bromus hordeaceus var. thominei, commonly known as the Thomine's brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wastelands. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
