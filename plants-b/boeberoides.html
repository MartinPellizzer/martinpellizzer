Boeberoides

Boeberoides, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of a single species, Boeberoides acutifolius, native to the southern United States. The plant is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
