Berberis libanotica

Berberis libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Berberis libanotica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
