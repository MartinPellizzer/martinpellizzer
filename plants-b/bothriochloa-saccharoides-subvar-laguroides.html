Bothriochloa saccharoides subvar. laguroides

Bothriochloa saccharoides subvar. laguroides, commonly known as the African millet, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically green or purplish in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for marginal agricultural lands.

This subvariety is often used as a forage crop and for soil conservation in arid regions.
