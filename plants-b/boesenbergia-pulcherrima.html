Boesenbergia pulcherrima

Boesenbergia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden ginger or flame ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces striking orange-yellow inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Boesenbergia pulcherrima is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in various regions.
