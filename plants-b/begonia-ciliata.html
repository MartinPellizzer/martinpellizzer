Begonia ciliata

Begonia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Begonia ciliata is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
