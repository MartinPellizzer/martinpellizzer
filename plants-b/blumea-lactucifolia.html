Blumea lactucifolia

Blumea lactucifolia, commonly known as milkweed aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
