Bertya polymorpha var. rosmarinifolia

Bertya polymorpha var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved bertya, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal practices due to its aromatic properties.
