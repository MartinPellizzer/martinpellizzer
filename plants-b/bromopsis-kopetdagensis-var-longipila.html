Bromopsis kopetdagensis var. longipila

Bromopsis kopetdagensis var. longipila, commonly known as the long-bract bromus, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
