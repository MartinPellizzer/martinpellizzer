Begonia razafinjohanyi

Begonia razafinjohanyi, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to vibrant red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
