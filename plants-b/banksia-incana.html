Banksia incana

Banksia incana, commonly known as the silver banksia, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it is well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region. Banksia incana produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
