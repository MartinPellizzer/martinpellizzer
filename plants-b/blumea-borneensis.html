Blumea borneensis

Blumea borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean blue flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Blumea borneensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
