Beyeria bickertonensis

Beyeria bickertonensis, commonly known as the Bickerton's lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features a short, stout stem with a single, large, showy spathe that encloses a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
