Bulbophyllum wendlandianum

Bulbophyllum wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical environments.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant hues make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
