Bauhinia petiolata var. caudigera

Bauhinia petiolata var. caudigera, commonly known as the hanging bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous inflorescences and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody, branching stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
