Bartlettina oresbia

Bartlettina oresbia, commonly known as the velvetleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive reddish-brown bark that becomes more prominent with age. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
