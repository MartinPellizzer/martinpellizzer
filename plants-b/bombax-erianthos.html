Bombax erianthos

Bombax erianthos, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it has a shallow root system adapted to poor soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in many regions.
