Begonia fischeri var. brasiliensis

Begonia fischeri var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of bright red, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical gardens.
