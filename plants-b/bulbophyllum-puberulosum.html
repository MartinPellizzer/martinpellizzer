Bulbophyllum puberulosum

Bulbophyllum puberulosum, commonly known as the hairy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, with its pseudobulbs covered in short, pubescent hairs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
