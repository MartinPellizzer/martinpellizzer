Barkeria scandens

Barkeria scandens, commonly known as the climbing buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that resemble those of the true buttercup, though it belongs to a different genus. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along the ground, and has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Barkeria scandens is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
