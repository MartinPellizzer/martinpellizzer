Bryophyllum poincarei

Bryophyllum poincarei, commonly known as the "Poinsettia," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant is renowned for its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Bryophyllum poincarei is often cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during the holiday season.
