Betula japonica var. szechuanica

Betula japonica var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan birch, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark that develops fissures with age. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its foliage is often a striking feature, providing ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
