Bauhinia melastomatoidea

Bauhinia melastomatoidea, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a subtle fragrance. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Bauhinia melastomatoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
