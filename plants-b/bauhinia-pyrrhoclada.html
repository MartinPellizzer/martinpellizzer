Bauhinia pyrrhoclada

Bauhinia pyrrhoclada, commonly known as the flame tree or red bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree produces flat, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
