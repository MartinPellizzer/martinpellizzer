Bulbophyllum dahlemense

Bulbophyllum dahlemense, commonly known as the Dahlem orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
