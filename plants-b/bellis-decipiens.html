Bellis decipiens

Bellis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. The plant features small, white flowers with yellow centers, which are often mistaken for true daisies. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Bellis decipiens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
