Bulbophyllum drymoda

Bulbophyllum drymoda, commonly known as the dry season orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted for moisture retention.

This orchid is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
