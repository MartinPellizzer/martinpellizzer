Brassica cephala

Brassica cephala, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stem and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Brassica cephala thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
