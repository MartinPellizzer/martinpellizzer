Barbacenia globata

Barbacenia globata, commonly known as the globular barbacenia, is a strikingly unique plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy leaves that resemble small, inflated balls, which help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Barbacenia globata is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
