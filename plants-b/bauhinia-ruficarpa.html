Bauhinia ruficarpa

Bauhinia ruficarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Bauhinia ruficarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
