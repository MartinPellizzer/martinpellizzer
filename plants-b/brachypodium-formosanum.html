Brachypodium formosanum

Brachypodium formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan false brome, is a grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, while its flowers are wind-pollinated.

Brachypodium formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
