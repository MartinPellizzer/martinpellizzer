Brunfelsia hydrangeiformis var. glabriuscula

Brunfelsia hydrangeiformis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the waxflower or Brazilian waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
