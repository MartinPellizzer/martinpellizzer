Berula burchellii

Berula burchellii, commonly known as the African water plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. The plant thrives in moist or wet environments, often found along stream banks and in marshy areas. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, which helps it survive in fluctuating moisture conditions.

Berula burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
