Bambusa regia

Bambusa regia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often display a yellowish or golden hue, especially when mature. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa regia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and structural elegance.
