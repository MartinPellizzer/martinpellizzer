Baphia spathacea subsp. polyantha

Baphia spathacea subsp.

polyantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered baphia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, violet-pink bract-like flowers that surround small, inconspicuous blooms.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
