Brickellia cylindracea var. typica

Brickellia cylindracea var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
