Brassica macrocarpa f. villosoincana

Brassica macrocarpa f. villosoincana, commonly known as the hairy or woolly cabbage, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are covered in dense, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically biennial, though it can behave as an annual in certain climates. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
