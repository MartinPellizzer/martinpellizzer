Balanops acicarpa

Balanops acicarpa, commonly known as the needle-leaf balanops, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
