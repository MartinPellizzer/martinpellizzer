Bauhinia forficata var. platypetala

Bauhinia forficata var. platypetala, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
