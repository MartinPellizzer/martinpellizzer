Berberis forsskaliana

Berberis forsskaliana, commonly known as the Arabian barberry, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are typically small, oval, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
