Biscutella didyma var. maritima

Biscutella didyma var. maritima, commonly known as sea biscuit, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in maritime environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
