Banksia anatona

Banksia anatona, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Proteaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Banksia anatona is well adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
