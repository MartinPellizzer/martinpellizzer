Baccharis papillosa subsp. longipedicellata

Baccharis papillosa subsp.

longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited baccharis, is a shrub native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silvery-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
