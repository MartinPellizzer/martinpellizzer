Berrya ameliae

Berrya ameliae, commonly known as the Amelia berry plant, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

The berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, making them a potential candidate for local foraging and culinary use.
