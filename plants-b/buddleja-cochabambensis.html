Buddleja cochabambensis

Buddleja cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba bush, is a shrub native to Bolivia.

It belongs to the Buddleja genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Buddleja cochabambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
