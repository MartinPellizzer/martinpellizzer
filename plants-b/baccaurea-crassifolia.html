Baccaurea crassifolia

Baccaurea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved baccaurea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
