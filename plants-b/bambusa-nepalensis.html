Bambusa nepalensis

Bambusa nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems that are typically green to yellowish in color. The plant grows in clusters and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its aesthetic appeal and ecological role.

Bambusa nepalensis is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.
