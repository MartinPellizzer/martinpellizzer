Bignonia quadrangularis

Bignonia quadrangularis, commonly known as the crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Bignonia quadrangularis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
