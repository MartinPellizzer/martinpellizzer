Bidens acrifolia

Bidens acrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf beggar tick, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Bidens acrifolia is known for its ability to spread through seeds and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
