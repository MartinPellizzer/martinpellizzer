Bignonia compressa

Bignonia compressa, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Bignonia compressa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
