Baccharis johnwurdackiana

Baccharis johnwurdackiana, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is aromatic and often has a grayish or silver hue, which helps reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
