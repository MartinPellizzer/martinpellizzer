Boronia juncea subsp. juncea

Boronia juncea subsp.

juncea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in coastal or heathland environments. It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, featuring dry summers and wet winters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
