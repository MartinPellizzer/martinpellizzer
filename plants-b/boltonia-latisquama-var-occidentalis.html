Boltonia latisquama var. occidentalis

Boltonia latisquama var. occidentalis, commonly known as western broadleaf boltonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like inflorescences with white to pale pink ray florets and yellow disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
