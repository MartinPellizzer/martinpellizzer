Bunias verrucosa

Bunias verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

The seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
