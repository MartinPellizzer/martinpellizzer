Bromopsis lanatipes

Bromopsis lanatipes, commonly known as the woolly bromus, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Bromopsis lanatipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
