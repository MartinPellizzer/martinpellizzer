Bursera esparzae

Bursera esparzae, commonly known as the Espinillo blanco, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces a fragrant resin that is similar to frankincense.

Bursera esparzae thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in limestone soils.
