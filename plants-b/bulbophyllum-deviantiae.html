Bulbophyllum deviantiae

Bulbophyllum deviantiae, commonly known as the Deviant Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often asymmetrical floral structures and vibrant, contrasting colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are highly specialized, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diversity, and B. deviantiae exemplifies the complex adaptations seen in orchids.
