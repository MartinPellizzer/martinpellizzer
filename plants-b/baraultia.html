Baraultia

Baraultia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
