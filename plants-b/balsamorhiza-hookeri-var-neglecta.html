Balsamorhiza hookeri var. neglecta

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. neglecta, commonly known as the desert balsam or western balsam, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.
