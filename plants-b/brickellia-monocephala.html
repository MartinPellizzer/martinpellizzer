Brickellia monocephala

Brickellia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert or arid environments. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia monocephala is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
