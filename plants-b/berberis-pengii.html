Berberis pengii

Berberis pengii, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Berberis pengii is also valued for its ability to tolerate cold climates and its seasonal display of vibrant foliage.
