Bouchea prismatica var. longirostra

Bouchea prismatica var. longirostra, commonly known as the long-beaked bouchea, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adorned with distinctive white and yellow marginal teeth. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
