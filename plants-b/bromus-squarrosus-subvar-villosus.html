Bromus squarrosus subvar. villosus

Bromus squarrosus subvar. villosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its leaves are generally narrow and have a bluish-green color, with a prominent midrib.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
