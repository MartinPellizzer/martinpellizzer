Bromus grossus var. nitidus

Bromus grossus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy annual brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a grass species characterized by its erect, slender growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
