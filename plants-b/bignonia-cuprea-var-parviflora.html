Bignonia cuprea var. parviflora

Bignonia cuprea var. parviflora, commonly known as the copperleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange to red leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a reddish tinge, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
