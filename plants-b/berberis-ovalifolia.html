Berberis ovalifolia

Berberis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Berberis ovalifolia is typically hardy in USDA zones 5 to 8 and thrives in well-drained soils.
