Brassica repanda subsp. confusa

Brassica repanda subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow.

It is often found in moist environments and is known for its potential medicinal properties.
