Bromelia agavoides

Bromelia agavoides, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with colorful, showy bracts that can range from red to orange. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy coating, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Bromelia agavoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
