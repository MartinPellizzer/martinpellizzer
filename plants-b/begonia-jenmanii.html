Begonia jenmanii

Begonia jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners who appreciate its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
