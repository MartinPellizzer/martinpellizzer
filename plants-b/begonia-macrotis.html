Begonia macrotis

Begonia macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its botanical appeal lies in its vibrant blooms and elegant foliage, which make it a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts.
