Begonia schliebenii

Begonia schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often velvety leaves that can vary in shape and color, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
