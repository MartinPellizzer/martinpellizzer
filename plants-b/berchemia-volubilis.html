Berchemia volubilis

Berchemia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a vigorous climber with long, trailing stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Berchemia volubilis is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
