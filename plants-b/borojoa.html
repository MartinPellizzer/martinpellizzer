Borojoa

Borojoa, commonly known as the "guanabana" or "soursop," is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny fruit with a green, rough exterior. The fruit contains a white, juicy pulp with numerous black seeds embedded within. Borojoa thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable for its edible fruit.
