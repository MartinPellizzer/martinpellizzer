Bryonia affinis

Bryonia affinis, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its fruits are spherical, green berries that turn orange when mature, and they contain toxic seeds.

Bryonia affinis is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
