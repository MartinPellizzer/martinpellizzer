Bulbophyllum veitchianum

Bulbophyllum veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces compact, upright pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth cycle. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, green, or yellow, with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum veitchianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral displays.
