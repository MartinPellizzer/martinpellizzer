Bauhinia hassleriana f. intermedia

Bauhinia hassleriana f. intermedia, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
