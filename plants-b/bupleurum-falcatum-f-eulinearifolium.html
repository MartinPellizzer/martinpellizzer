Bupleurum falcatum f. eulinearifolium

Bupleurum falcatum f. eulinearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Siberian burnet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are branched and bear small, clustered flowers. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
