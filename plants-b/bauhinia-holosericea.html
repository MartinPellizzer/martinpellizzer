Bauhinia holosericea

Bauhinia holosericea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral clusters that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Bauhinia holosericea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
