Brachycentrum excelsum

Brachycentrum excelsum, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the pond bottom. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring nutrient-rich soils.

Brachycentrum excelsum is often cultivated in water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
