Begonia fuchsioides var. miniata

Begonia fuchsioides var. miniata, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of cream and green, and small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often used in hanging baskets or as a decorative foliage plant due to its ornamental leaves.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
