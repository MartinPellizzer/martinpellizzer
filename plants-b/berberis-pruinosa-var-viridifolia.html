Berberis pruinosa var. viridifolia

Berberis pruinosa var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its finely textured, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rocky or dry gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
