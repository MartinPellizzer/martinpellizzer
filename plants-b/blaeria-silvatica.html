Blaeria silvatica

Blaeria silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a perennial plant native to temperate forests.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically a mix of green and silvery-white in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

Blaeria silvatica plays an important role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to the understory vegetation.
