Begonia goniotis

Begonia goniotis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia goniotis is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
