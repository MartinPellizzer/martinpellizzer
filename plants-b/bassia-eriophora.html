Bassia eriophora

Bassia eriophora, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Bassia eriophora is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
