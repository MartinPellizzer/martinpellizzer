Bambusa piscatorum

Bambusa piscatorum, commonly known as the fishing bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Bambusa piscatorum is valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
