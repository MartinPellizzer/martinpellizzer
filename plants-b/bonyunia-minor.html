Bonyunia minor

Bonyunia minor, commonly known as the lesser bonyunia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Bonyunia minor is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for arid gardens.
