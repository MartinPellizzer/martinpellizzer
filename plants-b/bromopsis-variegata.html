Bromopsis variegata

Bromopsis variegata, commonly known as the variegated bromus, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, elongated leaves that have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
