Bistorta insularis

Bistorta insularis, commonly known as the island bistorta, is a perennial herb native to certain parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible rhubarb-like stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Bistorta insularis is characterized by its tall, clustered inflorescences that produce small, greenish-white flowers.
