Buddleja × whiteana

Buddleja × whiteana, commonly known as the white buddleja, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate floral display.
