Baccharis obovata var. ecuadorensis

Baccharis obovata var. ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, ovoid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits.

This species is adapted to dry to moist habitats and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.
