Brosimum palmarum

Brosimum palmarum, commonly known as the palma de cacao or cocoa palm, is a flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits. The fruits contain a sweet, pulpy mesocarp that is often used in traditional cuisine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
