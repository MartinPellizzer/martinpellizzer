Begonia amnicola

Begonia amnicola, commonly known as the Amnicola begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers are borne on long, slender stems.

Begonia amnicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
