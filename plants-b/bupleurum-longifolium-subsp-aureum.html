Bupleurum longifolium subsp. aureum

Bupleurum longifolium subsp.

aureum, commonly known as golden thread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
