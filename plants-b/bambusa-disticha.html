Bambusa disticha

Bambusa disticha, commonly known as the bamboo stick or narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, compact form. It is a clumping bamboo that does not spread aggressively, making it suitable for ornamental and landscaping purposes.

Bambusa disticha is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in garden design and as a decorative plant.
