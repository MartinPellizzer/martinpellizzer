Bulbophyllum lemuraeoides

Bulbophyllum lemuraeoides, commonly known as the Malayan ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it forms dense clumps, which help in anchoring it to the soil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
