Betula × humilis

Betula × humilis, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Betula pendula and Betula pubescens.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in boreal and subarctic regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Betula × humilis is often found in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates.
