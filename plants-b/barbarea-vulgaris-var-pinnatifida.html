Barbarea vulgaris var. pinnatifida

Barbarea vulgaris var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed production make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
