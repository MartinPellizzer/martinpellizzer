Batidaea strigosa subsp. subcordata

Batidaea strigosa subsp.

subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf batidaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
