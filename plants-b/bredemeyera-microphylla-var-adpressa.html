Bredemeyera microphylla var. adpressa

Bredemeyera microphylla var. adpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color, providing a striking visual contrast to its grey-green bark. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forest communities.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a sought-after plant for gardeners and conservationists alike.
