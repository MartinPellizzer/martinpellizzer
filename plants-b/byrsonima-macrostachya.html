Byrsonima macrostachya

Byrsonima macrostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces bright red, cherry-like fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in jams and beverages.
