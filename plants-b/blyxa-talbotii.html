Blyxa talbotii

Blyxa talbotii, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Blyxa talbotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
