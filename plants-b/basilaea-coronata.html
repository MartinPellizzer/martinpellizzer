Basilaea coronata

Basilaea coronata, commonly known as the crowned basil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Basilaea coronata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
