Borrichia arborescens

Borrichia arborescens, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Borrichia arborescens is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
