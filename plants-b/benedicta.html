Benedicta

Benedicta, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks. The plant is noted for its unique ability to float on water surfaces, with leaves that are broad and heart-shaped. Its flowers typically open in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Benedicta has been revered in various cultures for its symbolic and medicinal properties.
