Bernardia spartioides

Bernardia spartioides, commonly known as the coastal berberis, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to salt-laden winds. Its berries, which are orange-red in color, are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
