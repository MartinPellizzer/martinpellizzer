Bromus maximus var. ambigens

Bromus maximus var. ambigens, commonly known as the broad-leaved brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and broad, flat leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Bromus maximus var. ambigens is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.
