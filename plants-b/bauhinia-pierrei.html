Bauhinia pierrei

Bauhinia pierrei, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Bauhinia pierrei is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
