Blechum sternbergii

Blechum sternbergii, commonly known as the desert blechnum, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Blechum sternbergii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
