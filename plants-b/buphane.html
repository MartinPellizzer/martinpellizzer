Buphane

Buphane, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its cruciform flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Buphane produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
