Bidaria celsicola

Bidaria celsicola, commonly known as the coastal bidaria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to saline and windy coastal environments, demonstrating resilience to harsh conditions.

Bidaria celsicola plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
