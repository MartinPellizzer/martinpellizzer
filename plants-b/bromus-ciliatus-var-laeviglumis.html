Bromus ciliatus var. laeviglumis

Bromus ciliatus var. laeviglumis, commonly known as the smooth-flowered brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a smooth, hairless surface. The inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing spikelets that are often hairy along the edges. Its flowers are typically pale green to brown, and it produces small, three- to six-angled seeds.

This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, disturbed soils, and open woodlands.
