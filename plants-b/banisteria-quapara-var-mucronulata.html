Banisteria quapara var. mucronulata

Banisteria quapara var. mucronulata, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, often fragrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
