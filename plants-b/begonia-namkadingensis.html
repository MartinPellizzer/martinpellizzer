Begonia namkadingensis

Begonia namkadingensis, commonly known as the Namkading begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among collectors and gardeners.
