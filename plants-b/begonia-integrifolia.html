Begonia integrifolia

Begonia integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia integrifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ease of care.
