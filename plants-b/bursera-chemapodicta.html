Bursera chemapodicta

Bursera chemapodicta, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant, resinous sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in limestone soils. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
