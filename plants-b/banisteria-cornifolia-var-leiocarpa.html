Banisteria cornifolia var. leiocarpa

Banisteria cornifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white banister, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds. Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and long-lasting, showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
