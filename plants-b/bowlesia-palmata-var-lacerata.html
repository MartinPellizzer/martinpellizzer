Bowlesia palmata var. lacerata

Bowlesia palmata var. lacerata, commonly known as the laceleaf bowlesia, is a rare and striking palm species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery, almost lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall. Its inflorescences produce small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects, leading to the development of fleshy, red or orange fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
