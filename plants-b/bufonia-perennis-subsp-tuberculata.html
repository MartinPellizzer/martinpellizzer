Bufonia perennis subsp. tuberculata

Bufonia perennis subsp.

tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate bufonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America. It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and strap-like leaves.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
