Bermudiana arizonica

Bermudiana arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona bermudiana, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
