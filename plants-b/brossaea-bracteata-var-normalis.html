Brossaea bracteata var. normalis

Brossaea bracteata var. normalis, commonly known as the common brossaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
