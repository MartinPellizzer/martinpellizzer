Bambusa glaucescens var. pubivagina

Bambusa glaucescens var. pubivagina, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Bambusa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue sheathes and a more compact growth habit compared to other bamboo species. This plant is native to southern China and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its foliage is dense and provides excellent coverage, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The varietal name 'pubivagina' refers to the pubescent (hairy) sheaths that distinguish it from other Bambusa species.
