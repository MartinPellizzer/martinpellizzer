Balsamina balsamina

Balsamina balsamina, commonly known as the common balsam, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Balsamina balsamina is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
