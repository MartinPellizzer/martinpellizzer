Bauhinia bidentata var. gracilipes

Bauhinia bidentata var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, oblong leaflets. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its small, woody pods contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Bauhinia bidentata var. gracilipes is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
