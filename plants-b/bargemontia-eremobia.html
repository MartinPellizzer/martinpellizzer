Bargemontia eremobia

Bargemontia eremobia, commonly known as the desert germander, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green, hairy foliage adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from underground sources.

Bargemontia eremobia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
