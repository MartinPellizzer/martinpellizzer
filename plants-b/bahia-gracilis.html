Bahia gracilis

Bahia gracilis, commonly known as slender bahia, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental use.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation in warm regions.
