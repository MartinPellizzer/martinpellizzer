Bartschella armillata

Bartschella armillata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a glossy appearance.

Bartschella armillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable microclimates.
