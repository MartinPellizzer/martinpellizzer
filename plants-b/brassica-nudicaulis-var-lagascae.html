Brassica nudicaulis var. lagascae

Brassica nudicaulis var. lagascae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada mustard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.

This variety is distinguished by its unique habitat and morphological features.
