Baeria mutica

Baeria mutica, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, spreading stems that produce small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Baeria mutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
