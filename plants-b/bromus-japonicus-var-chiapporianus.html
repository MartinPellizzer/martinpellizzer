Bromus japonicus var. chiapporianus

Bromus japonicus var. chiapporianus, commonly known as the Japanese brome, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
