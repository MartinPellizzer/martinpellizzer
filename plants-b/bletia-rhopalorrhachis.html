Bletia rhopalorrhachis

Bletia rhopalorrhachis, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Bletia rhopalorrhachis is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
