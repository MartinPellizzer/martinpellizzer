Bambusa sieberi

Bambusa sieberi, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often have a silvery sheen, giving it its common name. Bambusa sieberi typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ease of maintenance.
