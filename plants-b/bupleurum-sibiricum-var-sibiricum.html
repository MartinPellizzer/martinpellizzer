Bupleurum sibiricum var. sibiricum

Bupleurum sibiricum var. sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a robust root system and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbel-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
