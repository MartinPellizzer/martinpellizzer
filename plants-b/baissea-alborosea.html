Baissea alborosea

Baissea alborosea, commonly known as the white rose bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
