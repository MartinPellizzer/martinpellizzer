Bromus ramosus f. glabrior

Bromus ramosus f. glabrior, commonly known as the glabrous brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are typically pale green to reddish in color. This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form.

It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
