Bromus erectus var. transsilvanicus

Bromus erectus var. transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
