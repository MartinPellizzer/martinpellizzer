Bistorta vulgaris var. nitens

Bistorta vulgaris var. nitens, commonly known as the shining bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Bistorta vulgaris var. nitens is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
