Brachychiton diversifolius var. muelleri

Brachychiton diversifolius var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's trumpet tree, is a unique species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often deeply lobed, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
