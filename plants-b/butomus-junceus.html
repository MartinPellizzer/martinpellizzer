Butomus junceus

Butomus junceus, commonly known as the bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and distinctive flowering spikes.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, while its flowers form dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the plant.

Butomus junceus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
