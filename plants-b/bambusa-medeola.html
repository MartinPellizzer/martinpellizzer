Bambusa medeola

Bambusa medeola, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Bambusa medeola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
