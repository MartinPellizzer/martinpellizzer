Begonia velata

Begonia velata, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia velata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
