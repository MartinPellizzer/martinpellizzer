Bromelia michaelii

Bromelia michaelii, commonly known as the silver sword, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of long, narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Bromelia michaelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
