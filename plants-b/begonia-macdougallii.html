Begonia macdougallii

Begonia macdougallii, commonly known as the Macdougall's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that often display intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
