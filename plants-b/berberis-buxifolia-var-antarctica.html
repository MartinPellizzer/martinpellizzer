Berberis buxifolia var. antarctica

Berberis buxifolia var. antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic barberry, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with dark green leaves that have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is well adapted to harsh climates, thriving in cold and dry environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate and subtropical regions.
