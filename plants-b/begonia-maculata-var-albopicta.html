Begonia maculata var. albopicta

Begonia maculata var. albopicta, commonly known as the Variegated Begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature white and green patterns. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
