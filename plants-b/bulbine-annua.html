Bulbine annua

Bulbine annua, commonly known as the annual bulbous plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It grows from a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, annual life cycle.

Bulbine annua is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
