Borago

Borago, commonly known as blueweed or forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which includes other species with similar medicinal and ornamental uses. Borago is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
