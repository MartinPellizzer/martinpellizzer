Berberis valdiviana var. gracilifolia

Berberis valdiviana var. gracilifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
