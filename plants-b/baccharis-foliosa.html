Baccharis foliosa

Baccharis foliosa, commonly known as Brazilian tea or baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Baccharis foliosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
