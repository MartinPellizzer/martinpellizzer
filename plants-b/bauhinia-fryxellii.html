Bauhinia fryxellii

Bauhinia fryxellii, commonly known as the Fryxell's bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Belize and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, papery white or pink flowers. It is often found growing in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Bauhinia fryxellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
