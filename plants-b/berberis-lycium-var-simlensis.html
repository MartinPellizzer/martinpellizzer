Berberis lycium var. simlensis

Berberis lycium var. simlensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and horticultural displays.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
