Begonia schottiana

Begonia schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a striking red or purple underside. The plant produces showy, fuchsia or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
