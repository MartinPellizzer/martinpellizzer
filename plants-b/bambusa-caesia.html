Bambusa caesia

Bambusa caesia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This ornamental bamboo grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Bambusa caesia is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
