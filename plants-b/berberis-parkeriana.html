Berberis parkeriana

Berberis parkeriana, commonly known as the Parker's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
