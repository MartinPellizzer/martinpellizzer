Buceras tortuosa

Buceras tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisting stems that give it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Buceras tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
