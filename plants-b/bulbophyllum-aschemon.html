Bulbophyllum aschemon

Bulbophyllum aschemon, commonly known as the aschemon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a lithophyte, meaning it grows on rocks, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically featuring white and yellow hues. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
