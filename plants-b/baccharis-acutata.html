Baccharis acutata

Baccharis acutata, commonly known as narrowleaf Baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
