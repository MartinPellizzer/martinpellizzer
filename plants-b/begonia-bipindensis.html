Begonia bipindensis

Begonia bipindensis, commonly known as the African begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia bipindensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
