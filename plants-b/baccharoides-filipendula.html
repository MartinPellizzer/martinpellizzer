Baccharoides filipendula

Baccharoides filipendula, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
