Bouteloua curtipendula f. vivipara

Bouteloua curtipendula f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous dropseed, is a variant of the dropseed grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pink to purplish flowers. The plant is known for its unique viviparous trait, where the seeds germinate while still attached to the mother plant, forming small seedlings that can be detached and dispersed. This adaptation aids in its propagation and survival in various habitats.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
