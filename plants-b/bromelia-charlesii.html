Bromelia charlesii

Bromelia charlesii, commonly known as the Charlesii bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool capable of holding water, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Bromelia charlesii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
