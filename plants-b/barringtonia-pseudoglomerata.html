Barringtonia pseudoglomerata

Barringtonia pseudoglomerata, commonly known as the white ghost tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pale green leaves and distinctive, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. This plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
