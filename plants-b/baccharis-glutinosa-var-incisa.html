Baccharis glutinosa var. incisa

Baccharis glutinosa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
