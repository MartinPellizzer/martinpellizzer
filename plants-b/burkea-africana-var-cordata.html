Burkea africana var. cordata

Burkea africana var. cordata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against pests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
