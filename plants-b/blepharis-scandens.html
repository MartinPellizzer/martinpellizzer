Blepharis scandens

Blepharis scandens, commonly known as the creeping blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
