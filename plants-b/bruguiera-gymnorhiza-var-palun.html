Bruguiera gymnorhiza var. palun

Bruguiera gymnorhiza var. palun, commonly known as the mangrove fern, is a variant of the mangrove species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves and its ability to thrive in saline and tidal environments. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion. Its root system forms a complex network that helps in filtering water and supporting marine life.

It is often found in intertidal zones where it contributes to the ecological balance of mangrove ecosystems.
