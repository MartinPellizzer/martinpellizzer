Bulbophyllum furcillatum

Bulbophyllum furcillatum, commonly known as the Forked Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters and can have a variety of shapes and hues.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for under the right conditions.
