Bystropogon plumosus var. ferrensis

Bystropogon plumosus var. ferrensis, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a soft, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in narrow spikes, which are often overlooked due to their subtle nature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
