Brasilidium dasytyle

Brasilidium dasytyle, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Brasilidium dasytyle is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
