Bahiopsis microphylla

Bahiopsis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bahiopsis, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
