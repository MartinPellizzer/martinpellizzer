Balduina bicolor

Balduina bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bladderwort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines and tidal zones.

Balduina bicolor plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
