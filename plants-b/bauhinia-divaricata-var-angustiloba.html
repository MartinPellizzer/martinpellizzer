Bauhinia divaricata var. angustiloba

Bauhinia divaricata var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
