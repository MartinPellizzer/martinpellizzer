Brasiliorchis gracilis

Brasiliorchis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming briefly during the wet season, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Brasiliorchis gracilis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this unique botanical species.
