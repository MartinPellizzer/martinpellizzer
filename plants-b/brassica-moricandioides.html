Brassica moricandioides

Brassica moricandioides, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in sandy and rocky soils.
