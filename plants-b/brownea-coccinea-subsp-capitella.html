Brownea coccinea subsp. capitella

Brownea coccinea subsp.

capitella, commonly known as the red-blossom tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
