Blechnum gracile

Blechnum gracile, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping in appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories across tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clustered sporangia on the undersides of the fronds.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
