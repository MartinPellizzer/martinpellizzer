Bauhinia polycarpa

Bauhinia polycarpa, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and habitat provision.
