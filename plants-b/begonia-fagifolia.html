Begonia fagifolia

Begonia fagifolia, commonly known as the velvety begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires consistent moisture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
