Bulbophyllum mucronatum

Bulbophyllum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
