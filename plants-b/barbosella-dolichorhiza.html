Barbosella dolichorhiza

Barbosella dolichorhiza, commonly known as the long-rooted barbosella, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. Barbosella dolichorhiza thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
