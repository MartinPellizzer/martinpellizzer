Buddleja corrugata subsp. moranii

Buddleja corrugata subsp.

moranii, commonly known as the Moran's bush, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in forest margins and along stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
