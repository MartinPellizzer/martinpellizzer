Brachylobus officinalis

Brachylobus officinalis, commonly known as the lesser betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies for their purported calming and digestive properties.
