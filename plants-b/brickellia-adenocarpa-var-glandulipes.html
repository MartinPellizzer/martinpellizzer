Brickellia adenocarpa var. glandulipes

Brickellia adenocarpa var. glandulipes, commonly known as the glandular brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with glandular hairs, which give the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
