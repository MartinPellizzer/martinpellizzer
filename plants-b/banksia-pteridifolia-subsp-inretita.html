Banksia pteridifolia subsp. inretita

Banksia pteridifolia subsp.

inretita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to grey hairs.

The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences with numerous yellow flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
