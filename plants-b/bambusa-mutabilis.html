Bambusa mutabilis

Bambusa mutabilis, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a fast-growing grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray sheen.

Bambusa mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
