Bourreria cuneifolia var. glabrescens

Bourreria cuneifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf burrseria, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs, though the variety glabrescens has a smoother, hairless surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies in arid environments.
