Begonia fairchildii

Begonia fairchildii, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a striking red margin. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
