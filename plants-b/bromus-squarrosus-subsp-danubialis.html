Bromus squarrosus subsp. danubialis

Bromus squarrosus subsp.

danubialis, commonly known as the Danubian brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
