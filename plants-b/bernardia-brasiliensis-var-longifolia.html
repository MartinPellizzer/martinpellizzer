Bernardia brasiliensis var. longifolia

Bernardia brasiliensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bernardia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are typically long and narrow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its attractive foliage and floral display.
