Buxus microphylla var. intermedia

Buxus microphylla var. intermedia, commonly known as the compact boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it ideal for topiary and formal garden designs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, requiring minimal pruning to maintain its shape.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is a popular choice for hedging and ornamental landscaping.
