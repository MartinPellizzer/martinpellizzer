Baccharis alpina f. macrocephala

Baccharis alpina f. macrocephala, commonly known as the Alpine Baccharis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is native to the Andes mountains in South America. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
