Blepharis obovata

Blepharis obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed wireweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Blepharis obovata is an important species for pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
