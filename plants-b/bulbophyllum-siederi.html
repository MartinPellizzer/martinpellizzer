Bulbophyllum siederi

Bulbophyllum siederi, commonly known as the Sieder's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
