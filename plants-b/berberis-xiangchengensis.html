Berberis xiangchengensis

Berberis xiangchengensis, commonly known as the Xiangcheng barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and hedges.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a popular choice in horticulture.
