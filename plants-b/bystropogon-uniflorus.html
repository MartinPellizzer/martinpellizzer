Bystropogon uniflorus

Bystropogon uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are rich in nutrients. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which are specialized structures that attach to the host. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid environments.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in certain forest ecosystems.
