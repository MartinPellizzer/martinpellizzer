Botryopitys meridensis

Botryopitys meridensis, commonly known as the southern bottlebrush pine, is a rare and endangered conifer native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped cones and its ability to thrive in high-altitude, dry environments. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces small, resinous seeds. Due to its limited geographic range and habitat specificity, it is highly vulnerable to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this distinctive botanical species.
