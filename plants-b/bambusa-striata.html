Bambusa striata

Bambusa striata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and relatively fast growth rate. The plant is cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different climates.

Its hollow, segmented stalks are also used in construction and crafts in its native regions.
