Begonia adenodes

Begonia adenodes, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Begonia adenodes is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
