Bulbophyllum hashimotoi

Bulbophyllum hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto's Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive pattern on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
