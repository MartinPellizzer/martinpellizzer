Bambusa chungii var. petilla

Bambusa chungii var. petilla, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
