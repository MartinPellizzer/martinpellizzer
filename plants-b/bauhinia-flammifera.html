Bauhinia flammifera

Bauhinia flammifera, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

Bauhinia flammifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
