Brassia bennettiorum

Brassia bennettiorum, commonly known as Bennett's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a reddish-brown lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Brassia bennettiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
