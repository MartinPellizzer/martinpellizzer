Bromus villosus proles rigidus

Bromus villosus proles rigidus, commonly known as the hairy brome or rigid brome, is a grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open fields. The plant produces spikelets that are usually loosely arranged along the flowering stems. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered with fine, soft hairs.

Bromus villosus proles rigidus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
