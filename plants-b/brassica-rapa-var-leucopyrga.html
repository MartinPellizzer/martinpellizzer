Brassica rapa var. leucopyrga

Brassica rapa var. leucopyrga, commonly known as the white mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical siliques containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
