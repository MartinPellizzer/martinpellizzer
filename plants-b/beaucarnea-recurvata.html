Beaucarnea recurvata

Beaucarnea recurvata, commonly known as the ponytail palm, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, tree-like trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has a rosette of long, fibrous leaves that give it a distinctive ponytail appearance. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries.

Its drought tolerance and unique appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
