Brassica campestris var. dissecta

Brassica campestris var. dissecta, commonly known as the curly kale or tree kale, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and often curly. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a rosette of leaves forming a dense, bushy structure. It produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are followed by small, round pods containing seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some culinary applications.
