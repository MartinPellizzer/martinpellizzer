Bromus danthoniae var. uniaristatus

Bromus danthoniae var. uniaristatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, which is a characteristic feature of the bromus genus.

This variety is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
