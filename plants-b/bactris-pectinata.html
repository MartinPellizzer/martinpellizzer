Bactris pectinata

Bactris pectinata, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
