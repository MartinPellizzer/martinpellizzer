Begonia houttuynioides

Begonia houttuynioides, commonly known as the Houttuyn's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its attractive foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings.
