Billbergia croyana

Billbergia croyana, commonly known as the Croyana billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display vibrant colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves forms a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads.

Billbergia croyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
