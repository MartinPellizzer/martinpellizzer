Bulbophyllum medusae

Bulbophyllum medusae, commonly known as the Medusa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or green with purple or brown markings. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
