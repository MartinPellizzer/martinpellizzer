Boronia capitata subsp. gracilis

Boronia capitata subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
