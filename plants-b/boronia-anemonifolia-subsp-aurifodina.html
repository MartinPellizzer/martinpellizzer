Boronia anemonifolia subsp. aurifodina

Boronia anemonifolia subsp.

aurifodina, commonly known as golden boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
