Begonia rimarum

Begonia rimarum, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves with distinctive silver veins that contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia rimarum is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and aesthetic value.
