Barleria prionitis var. pubescens

Barleria prionitis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months.

The hairy barleria is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
