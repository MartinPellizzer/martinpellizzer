Batopedina linearifolia var. glabra

Batopedina linearifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved batopedina, is a species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, glabrous leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbellate inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
