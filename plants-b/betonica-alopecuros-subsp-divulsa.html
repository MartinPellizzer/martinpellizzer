Betonica alopecuros subsp. divulsa

Betonica alopecuros subsp.

divulsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats. Betonica alopecuros subsp.

divulsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
