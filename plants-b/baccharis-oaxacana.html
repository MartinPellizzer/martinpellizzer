Baccharis oaxacana

Baccharis oaxacana, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
