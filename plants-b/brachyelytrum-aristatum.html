Brachyelytrum aristatum

Brachyelytrum aristatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
