Byrsopteris maximowiczii

Byrsopteris maximowiczii, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving or still waters. Its fronds are divided into numerous small, finger-like segments that aid in buoyancy and light absorption.

Byrsopteris maximowiczii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
