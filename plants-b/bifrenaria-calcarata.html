Bifrenaria calcarata

Bifrenaria calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flower that resembles a small orchid with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a prominent, hairy spur that extends from the base of the flower.

Bifrenaria calcarata is a perennial herb that blooms in spring, often forming dense clusters in its natural habitat.
