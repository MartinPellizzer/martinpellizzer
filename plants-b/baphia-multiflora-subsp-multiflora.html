Baphia multiflora subsp. multiflora

Baphia multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
