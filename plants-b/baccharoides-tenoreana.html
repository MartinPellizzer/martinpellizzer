Baccharoides tenoreana

Baccharoides tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
