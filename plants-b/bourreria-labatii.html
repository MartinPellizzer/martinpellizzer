Bourreria labatii

Bourreria labatii, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions makes it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and restoration projects.

The woody stems and aromatic foliage also make it a popular plant for ornamental use in gardens.
