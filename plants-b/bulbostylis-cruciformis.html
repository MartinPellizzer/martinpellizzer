Bulbostylis cruciformis

Bulbostylis cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Bulbostylis cruciformis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
