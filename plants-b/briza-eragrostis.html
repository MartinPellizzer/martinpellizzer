Briza eragrostis

Briza eragrostis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that bear delicate, feather-like inflorescences, which are often tinged with silver or pale blue hues. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine, silky texture.

Briza eragrostis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
