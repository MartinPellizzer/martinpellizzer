Bulbine favosa

Bulbine favosa, commonly known as the yellow bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, grass-like leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically in spring or early summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
