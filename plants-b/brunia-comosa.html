Brunia comosa

Brunia comosa, commonly known as the silky brunia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Brunia comosa is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.
