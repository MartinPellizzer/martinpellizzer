Belvisia validinervis var. longissima

Belvisia validinervis var. longissima, commonly known as the long-veined belvisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves with prominent, elongated veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a subtle fragrance. Its botanical significance lies in its unique venation pattern and habitat specificity.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
