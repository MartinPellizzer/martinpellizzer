Banisteria nervosa var. lessertiana

Banisteria nervosa var. lessertiana, commonly known as the Lessor's banisteria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
