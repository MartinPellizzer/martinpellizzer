Bellota pauciflora

Bellota pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bell orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Bellota pauciflora is considered a vulnerable species.
