Bactris sphaerocarpa var. ensifolia

Bactris sphaerocarpa var. ensifolia, commonly known as the sword palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble swords, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits resembling small pineapples. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are used in traditional basket weaving and as thatching material in some regions.
