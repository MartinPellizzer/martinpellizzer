Bertiera palustris

Bertiera palustris, commonly known as the swamp bertiera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a rough texture.

Bertiera palustris is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils.
