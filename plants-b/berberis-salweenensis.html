Berberis salweenensis

Berberis salweenensis, commonly known as the Salween barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
