Bolbitis auriculata

Bolbitis auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed fronds that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces clusters of bright green, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bolbitis auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
