Begonia veitchii var. veitchii

Begonia veitchii var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
