Brickellia betonicifolia

Brickellia betonicifolia, commonly known as the slender brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brickellia betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
