Bartonia paniculata var. sabulonensis

Bartonia paniculata var. sabulonensis, commonly known as the sandplain bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and pine flatwoods.
