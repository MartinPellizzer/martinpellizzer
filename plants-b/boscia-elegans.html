Boscia elegans

Boscia elegans, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark, which peels in thin, papery layers. The plant has compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Boscia elegans is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
