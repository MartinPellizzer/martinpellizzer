Bactris kunorum

Bactris kunorum, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small, round dates. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
