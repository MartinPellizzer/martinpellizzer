Begonia adamsensis

Begonia adamsensis, commonly known as Adams' begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

Begonia adamsensis is also noted for its ability to produce numerous offspring through vegetative propagation.
