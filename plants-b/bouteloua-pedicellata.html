Bouteloua pedicellata

Bouteloua pedicellata, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or gray in color. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of native grasslands.

Blue grama is also valued for its drought tolerance and role in soil stabilization.
