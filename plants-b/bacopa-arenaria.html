Bacopa arenaria

Bacopa arenaria, commonly known as sand bacopa, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to sandy coastal areas.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces tiny, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
