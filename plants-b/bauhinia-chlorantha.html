Bauhinia chlorantha

Bauhinia chlorantha, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
