Buddleja japonica

Buddleja japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces panicles of fragrant, pale lavender to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Buddleja japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
