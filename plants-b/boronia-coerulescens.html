Boronia coerulescens

Boronia coerulescens, commonly known as the blue boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, narrow leaves and a branching structure that supports its showy inflorescences.

Boronia coerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.
