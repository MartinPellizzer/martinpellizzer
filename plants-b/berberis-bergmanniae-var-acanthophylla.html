Berberis bergmanniae var. acanthophylla

Berberis bergmanniae var. acanthophylla, commonly known as the Bergmann's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and decorative appearance in garden settings.
