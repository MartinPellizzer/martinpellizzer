Balansaea fontanesii

Balansaea fontanesii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
