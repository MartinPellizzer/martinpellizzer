Balanites roxburghii

Balanites roxburghii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, brittle shell. Its deep root system allows it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and has been used traditionally for food, medicine, and as a source of timber.
