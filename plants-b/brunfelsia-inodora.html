Brunfelsia inodora

Brunfelsia inodora, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces violet-blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, spicy scent. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Brunfelsia inodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
