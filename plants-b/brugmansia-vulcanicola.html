Brugmansia vulcanicola

Brugmansia vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic brugmansia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous white or yellow flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The plant is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its bark is thick and fissured.

Brugmansia vulcanicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
