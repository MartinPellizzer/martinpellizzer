Banksia brachyphylla

Banksia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Banksia brachyphylla plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
