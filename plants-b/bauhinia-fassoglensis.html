Bauhinia fassoglensis

Bauhinia fassoglensis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. Bauhinia fassoglensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow as a tree or shrub depending on environmental conditions.

It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
