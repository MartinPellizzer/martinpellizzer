Bellis pusilla

Bellis pusilla, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a small, low-growing flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with a mat-forming growth habit. The plant features fuzzy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with dark centers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens.

Bellis pusilla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to fill in gaps in garden beds.
