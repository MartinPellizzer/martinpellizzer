Belowia baccifera

Belowia baccifera, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
