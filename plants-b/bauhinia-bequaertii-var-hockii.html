Bauhinia bequaertii var. hockii

Bauhinia bequaertii var. hockii, commonly known as the Hock's bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
