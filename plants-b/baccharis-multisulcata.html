Baccharis multisulcata

Baccharis multisulcata, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves with prominent veins and a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

Baccharis multisulcata is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
