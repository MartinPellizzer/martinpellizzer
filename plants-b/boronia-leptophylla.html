Boronia leptophylla

Boronia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually from spring to early summer.

Boronia leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
