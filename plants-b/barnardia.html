Barnardia

Barnardia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are annual herbs native to North America. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often display shades of blue or purple.

Barnardia species are often found in dry, disturbed soils and are adapted to thrive in arid environments.
