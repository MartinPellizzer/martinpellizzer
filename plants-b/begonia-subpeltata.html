Begonia subpeltata

Begonia subpeltata, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often marked with dark green veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia subpeltata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
