Baccharis funkiae

Baccharis funkiae, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Baccharis funkiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
