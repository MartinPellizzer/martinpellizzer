Bromus sibiricus subsp. flexuosus

Bromus sibiricus subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as Siberian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the colder regions of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, sometimes arching stems.

The plant produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged, with lemmas that are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
