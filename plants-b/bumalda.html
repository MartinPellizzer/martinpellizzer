Bumalda

Bumalda, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the genus Brachychiton and is characterized by its large, showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces woody, spherical seed pods that split open when mature. Bumalda thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable feature in the botanical landscape of Australia.
