Brassica nigra var. torulosa

Brassica nigra var. torulosa, commonly known as the black mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers that form in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Native to Europe and Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
