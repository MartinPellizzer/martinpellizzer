Baccharoides prolixa

Baccharoides prolixa, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharoides prolixa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
