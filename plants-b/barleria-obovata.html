Barleria obovata

Barleria obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Barleria obovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
