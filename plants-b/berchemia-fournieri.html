Berchemia fournieri

Berchemia fournieri, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, belonging to the Fabaceae family. The plant is characterized by its pendulous yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in spring. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Berchemia fournieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
