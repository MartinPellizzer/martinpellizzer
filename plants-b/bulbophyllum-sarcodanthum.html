Bulbophyllum sarcodanthum

Bulbophyllum sarcodanthum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that produces intricate, spider-like flower spikes with showy, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in fluctuating environmental conditions.

The flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
