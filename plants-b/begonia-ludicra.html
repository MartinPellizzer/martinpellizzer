Begonia ludicra

Begonia ludicra, commonly known as the "whimsical begonia," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia ludicra thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.
