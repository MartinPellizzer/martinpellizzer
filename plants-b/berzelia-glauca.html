Berzelia glauca

Berzelia glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous berzelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with narrow, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
