Begonia calophylla

Begonia calophylla, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of pink or red, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts and gardeners.
