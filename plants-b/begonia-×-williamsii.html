Begonia × williamsii

Begonia × williamsii, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that range in color from dark green to purple, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces showy, often bi-colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.

Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate indoor conditions make it a versatile ornamental plant.
