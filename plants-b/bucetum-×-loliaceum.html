Bucetum × loliaceum

Bucetum × loliaceum, commonly known as the hybrid grassland plant, is a natural hybrid resulting from the cross between *Buchloe dactyloides* and *Lolium perenne*.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions and prevents soil erosion. It is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and forage production.
