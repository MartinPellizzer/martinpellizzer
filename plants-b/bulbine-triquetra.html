Bulbine triquetra

Bulbine triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a triangular pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its striking appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
