Barringtonia multiflora

Barringtonia multiflora, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that are often fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
