Brachiaria villosa

Brachiaria villosa, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as pastures and disturbed soils, and is well adapted to warm climates. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed.

Brachiaria villosa is often used in livestock grazing systems due to its high nutritional value and drought tolerance.
