Brickellia longifolia

Brickellia longifolia, commonly known as longleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its botanical structure includes a taproot system and fibrous roots, which help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
