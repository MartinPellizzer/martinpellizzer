Baccharis caprariifolia

Baccharis caprariifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Baccharis caprariifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
