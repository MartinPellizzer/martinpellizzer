Brassica rapa var. typica

Brassica rapa var. typica, commonly known as the field mustard, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect stems, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped, and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions.

Brassica rapa var. typica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
