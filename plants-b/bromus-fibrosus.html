Bromus fibrosus

Bromus fibrosus, commonly known as slender brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus fibrosus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in areas with moderate moisture levels.
