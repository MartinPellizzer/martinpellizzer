Baccharis racemosa var. urvillei

Baccharis racemosa var. urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its flowering stems are typically branched and bear numerous small, fluffy fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
