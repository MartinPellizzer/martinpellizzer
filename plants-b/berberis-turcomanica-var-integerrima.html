Berberis turcomanica var. integerrima

Berberis turcomanica var. integerrima, commonly known as the Turkestan barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for landscaping in dry regions.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
