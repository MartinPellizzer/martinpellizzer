Blechnum nitidum var. contracta

Blechnum nitidum var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf harts-tongue fern, is a variant of the harts-tongue fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its narrow, leathery, and often curled fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, elongated shape with a pointed tip.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
