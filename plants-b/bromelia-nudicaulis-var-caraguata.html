Bromelia nudicaulis var. caraguata

Bromelia nudicaulis var. caraguata, commonly known as the Caraguata bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette, often with a red or purple hue at the base. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height, featuring vibrant orange or yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
