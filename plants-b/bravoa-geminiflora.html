Bravoa geminiflora

Bravoa geminiflora, commonly known as the double-flowered poppy, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that often feature two layers of petals, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to gardens and landscapes.

Bravoa geminiflora is also noted for its resilience and ability to reseed itself, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
