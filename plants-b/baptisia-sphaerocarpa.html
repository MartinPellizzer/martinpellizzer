Baptisia sphaerocarpa

Baptisia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in late spring, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Baptisia sphaerocarpa thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
