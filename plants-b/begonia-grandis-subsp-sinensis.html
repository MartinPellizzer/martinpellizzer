Begonia grandis subsp. sinensis

Begonia grandis subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China. It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from summer to autumn. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and prolonged flowering period.
