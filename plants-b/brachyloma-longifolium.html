Brachyloma longifolium

Brachyloma longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved wallaby bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats.

Brachyloma longifolium is an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
