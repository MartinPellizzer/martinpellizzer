Brachyotum seorsum

Brachyotum seorsum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and pale yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with succulent stems that store water. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Brachyotum seorsum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
