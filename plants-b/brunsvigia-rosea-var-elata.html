Brunsvigia rosea var. elata

Brunsvigia rosea var. elata, commonly known as the Red Hot Poker, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, red-orange flowers resembling poker chips, which bloom in summer. The plant has fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.
