Bromus pinetorum

Bromus pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine forests. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Bromus pinetorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
