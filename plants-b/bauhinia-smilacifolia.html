Bauhinia smilacifolia

Bauhinia smilacifolia, commonly known as the climbing bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found growing as a climbing vine in moist, shaded environments.

Bauhinia smilacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
