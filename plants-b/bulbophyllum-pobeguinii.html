Bulbophyllum pobeguinii

Bulbophyllum pobeguinii, commonly known as the Pobeguin's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including yellow, white, and pink.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
