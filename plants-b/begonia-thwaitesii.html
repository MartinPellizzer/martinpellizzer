Begonia thwaitesii

Begonia thwaitesii, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker green. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
