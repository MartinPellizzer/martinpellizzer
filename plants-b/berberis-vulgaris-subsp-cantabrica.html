Berberis vulgaris subsp. cantabrica

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian barberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
