Burlingtonia farmeri

Burlingtonia farmeri, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, grape-like berries that turn a striking purple-red color in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Burlingtonia farmeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract wildlife.
