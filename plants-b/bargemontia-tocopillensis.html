Bargemontia tocopillensis

Bargemontia tocopillensis, commonly known as the Tocopilla cactus, is a unique species native to the Atacama Desert in northern Chile.

It is a spineless cactus with a globular shape and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant has adapted to the extreme arid conditions by storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
