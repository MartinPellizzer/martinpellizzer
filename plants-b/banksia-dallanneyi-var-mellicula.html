Banksia dallanneyi var. mellicula

Banksia dallanneyi var. mellicula, commonly known as the Mellicula banksia, is a shrub native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flower spikes, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant produces small, hairy seeds enclosed in woody follicles, typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
