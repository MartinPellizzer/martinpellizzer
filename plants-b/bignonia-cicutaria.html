Bignonia cicutaria

Bignonia cicutaria, commonly known as yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Bignonia cicutaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
