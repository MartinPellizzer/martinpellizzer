Brickellia cuspidata

Brickellia cuspidata, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Brickellia cuspidata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
