Brassia attenuata

Brassia attenuata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Brassia attenuata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
