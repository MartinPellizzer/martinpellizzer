Balanites triflora

Balanites triflora, commonly known as the desert date or wild date tree, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces yellow, fragrant flowers followed by woody fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures. Its leaves are small and thick, reducing water loss, and its roots can penetrate deep into the soil to access water.

Balanites triflora has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of food and fuel in local communities.
