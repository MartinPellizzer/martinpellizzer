Brachystegia stipulata var. lufirensis

Brachystegia stipulata var. lufirensis, commonly known as the Lufira brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume pods containing edible seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
