Bulbocodium chloroleucum

Bulbocodium chloroleucum, commonly known as the white bulbous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
