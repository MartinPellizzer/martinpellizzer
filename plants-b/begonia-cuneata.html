Begonia cuneata

Begonia cuneata, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, thriving in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

Begonia cuneata is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
