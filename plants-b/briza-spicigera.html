Briza spicigera

Briza spicigera, commonly known as the spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Briza spicigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
