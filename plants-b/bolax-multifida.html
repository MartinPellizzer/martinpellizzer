Bolax multifida

Bolax multifida, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Bolax multifida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
