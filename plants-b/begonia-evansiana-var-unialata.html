Begonia evansiana var. unialata

Begonia evansiana var. unialata, commonly known as the single-flowered begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
