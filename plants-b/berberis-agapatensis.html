Berberis agapatensis

Berberis agapatensis, commonly known as the Agapite barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape shrub in temperate climates.
