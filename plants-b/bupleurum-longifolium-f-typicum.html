Bupleurum longifolium f. typicum

Bupleurum longifolium f. typicum, commonly known as the long-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This subspecies is characterized by its typical leaf morphology and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.
