Berberis concinna

Berberis concinna, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis concinna is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
