Bassia wightiana

Bassia wightiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
