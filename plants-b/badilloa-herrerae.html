Badilloa herrerae

Badilloa herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's badilloa, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters, characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
