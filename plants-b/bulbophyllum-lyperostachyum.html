Bulbophyllum lyperostachyum

Bulbophyllum lyperostachyum, commonly known as the "Bull's Eye Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bulbous pseudobulbs and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its native habitat.

The flowers are borne on inflorescences and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
