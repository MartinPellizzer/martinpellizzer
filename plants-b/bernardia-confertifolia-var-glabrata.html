Bernardia confertifolia var. glabrata

Bernardia confertifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed bernardia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to form dense thickets in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
