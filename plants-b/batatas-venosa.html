Batatas venosa

Batatas venosa, commonly known as the veined batata or sweet potato with veins, is a tropical root crop native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often veined skin. The flesh of the tuber can range from yellow to orange or even white, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its edible roots but also for its ornamental leaves, which display vibrant colors.
