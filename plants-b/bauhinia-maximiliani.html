Bauhinia maximiliani

Bauhinia maximiliani, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a light gray to brown color. Bauhinia maximiliani thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
