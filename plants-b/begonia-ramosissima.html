Begonia ramosissima

Begonia ramosissima, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and homes.
