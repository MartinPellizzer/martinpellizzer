Bignonia sinclairii

Bignonia sinclairii, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with long, twisting stems that can reach lengths of up to 20 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Bignonia sinclairii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
