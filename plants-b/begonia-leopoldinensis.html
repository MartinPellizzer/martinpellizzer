Begonia leopoldinensis

Begonia leopoldinensis, commonly known as the Leopoldina begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and deeply veined. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Begonia leopoldinensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
