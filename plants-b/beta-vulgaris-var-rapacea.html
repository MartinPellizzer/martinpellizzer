Beta vulgaris var. rapacea

Beta vulgaris var. rapacea, commonly known as sugar beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its cultivation is essential in the production of sugar and other industrial by-products.
