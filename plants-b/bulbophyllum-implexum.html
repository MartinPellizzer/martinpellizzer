Bulbophyllum implexum

Bulbophyllum implexum, commonly known as the entwined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Bulbophyllum implexum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
