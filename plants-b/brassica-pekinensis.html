Brassica pekinensis

Brassica pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese kale or kai lan, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, tender leaves and thick, fibrous stalks. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary versatility. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassica pekinensis is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet.
