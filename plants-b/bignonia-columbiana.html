Bignonia columbiana

Bignonia columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian crossvine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Bignonia columbiana is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
