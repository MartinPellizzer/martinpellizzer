Buddleja sphaerocalyx

Buddleja sphaerocalyx, commonly known as the globe buddleja, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woody fruit capsules that give it its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Buddleja sphaerocalyx prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
