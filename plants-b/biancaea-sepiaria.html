Biancaea sepiaria

Biancaea sepiaria, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Biancaea sepiaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
