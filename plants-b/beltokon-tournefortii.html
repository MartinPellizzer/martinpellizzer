Beltokon tournefortii

Beltokon tournefortii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
