Brachiaria venezuelae

Brachiaria venezuelae, commonly known as Venezuela grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open areas. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Brachiaria venezuelae is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
