Bulbophyllum fruticulum

Bulbophyllum fruticulum, commonly known as the shrub orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has fleshy, succulent-like pseudobulbs that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Bulbophyllum fruticulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
