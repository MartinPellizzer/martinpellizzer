Beyeria villosa

Beyeria villosa, commonly known as the woolly beyeria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, or villose, stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that are typically clustered in a head-like structure. Beyeria villosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
