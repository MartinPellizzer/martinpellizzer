Byrsonima eriopoda

Byrsonima eriopoda, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary significance.
