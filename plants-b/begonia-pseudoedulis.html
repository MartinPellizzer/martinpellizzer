Begonia pseudoedulis

Begonia pseudoedulis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
