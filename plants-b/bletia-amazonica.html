Bletia amazonica

Bletia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white blooms, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Bletia amazonica thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in the study of tropical orchid ecology.
