Bignonia radicans var. flava

Bignonia radicans var. flava, commonly known as golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is a vigorous, fast-growing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
