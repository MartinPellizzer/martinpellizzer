Broussonetia rupicola

Broussonetia rupicola, commonly known as the rock broussonetia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found growing on rocky slopes and in disturbed soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its fruit is a dry pod that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Broussonetia rupicola is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
