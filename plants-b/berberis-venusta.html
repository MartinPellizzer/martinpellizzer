Berberis venusta

Berberis venusta, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western and central China.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often considered bitter. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis venusta is also known for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
