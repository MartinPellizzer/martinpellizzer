Billbergia euphemiae var. nudiflora

Billbergia euphemiae var. nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central reservoir to collect rainwater. The plant produces inflorescences that bear showy, red-orange flowers, which are borne directly on the leafy rosette without bracts, giving it its "nudiflora" designation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
