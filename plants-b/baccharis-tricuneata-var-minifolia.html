Baccharis tricuneata var. minifolia

Baccharis tricuneata var. minifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
