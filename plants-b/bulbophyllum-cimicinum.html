Bulbophyllum cimicinum

Bulbophyllum cimicinum, commonly known as the cimicinum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive inflorescence that features a single, showy flower. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This orchid is often found in forested areas and requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive.
