Bennettia aristifera

Bennettia aristifera, commonly known as the slender bennettia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spiny pods. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Bennettia aristifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
