Botrychium kannenbergii f. subcompositum

Botrychium kannenbergii f. subcompositum, commonly known as the composite moonwort, is a rare fern species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a delicate, fern-like frond with a central axis and small, clustered sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures are arranged in a composite-like pattern, giving it its common name.

This fern is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and distinctive botanical features.
