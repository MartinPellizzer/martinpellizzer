Borago advena

Borago advena, commonly known as Brazilian false flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Borago advena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
