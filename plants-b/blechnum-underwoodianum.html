Blechnum underwoodianum

Blechnum underwoodianum, commonly known as the Underwood's harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
