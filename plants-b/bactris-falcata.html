Bactris falcata

Bactris falcata, commonly known as the pink rafia palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in wetland and swampy habitats, where it plays an important ecological role.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
