Barbarea × subarcuata

Barbarea × subarcuata, commonly known as the hybrid winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Barbarea vulgaris and Barbarea helwanica, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Barbarea × subarcuata is typically found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
