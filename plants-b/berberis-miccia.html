Berberis miccia

Berberis miccia, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found at high altitudes.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
