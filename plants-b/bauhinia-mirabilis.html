Bauhinia mirabilis

Bauhinia mirabilis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red or orange hues during the flowering season. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
