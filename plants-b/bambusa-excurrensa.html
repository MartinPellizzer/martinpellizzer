Bambusa excurrensa

Bambusa excurrensa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, golden-green culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Bambusa excurrensa is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
