Bowiea volubilis subsp. volubilis

Bowiea volubilis subsp.

volubilis, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America. It features a swollen, colorful tuberous stem that serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients.

The plant has fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
