Begonia bakeri

Begonia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and vibrant addition to their plant collection.
