Baeckea tetragona

Baeckea tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered baeckea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a distinctive four-angled stem that gives it its common name.

Baeckea tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
