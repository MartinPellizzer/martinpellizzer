Bidens pilosa var. brachycarpa

Bidens pilosa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as horseweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Bidens pilosa var. brachycarpa is an annual herb that can spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed.
