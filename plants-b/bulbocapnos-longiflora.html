Bulbocapnos longiflora

Bulbocapnos longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, peppery scent.

Bulbocapnos longiflora thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
