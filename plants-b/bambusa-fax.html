Bambusa fax

Bambusa fax, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often a striking golden-yellow color, especially when young. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. It is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense clusters, forming a compact, upright form.

Bambusa fax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
