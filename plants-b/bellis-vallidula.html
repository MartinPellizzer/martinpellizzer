Bellis vallidula

Bellis vallidula, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bellis vallidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
