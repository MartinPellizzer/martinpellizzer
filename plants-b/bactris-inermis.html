Bactris inermis

Bactris inermis, commonly known as the pink pineapple, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and large, elongated leaves that are often tinged with pink or red at the base. The plant produces a spadix with small, clustered flowers, followed by edible berries that are yellow-orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Bactris inermis is cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in some regions.
