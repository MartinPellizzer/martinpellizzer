Begonia falcifolia

Begonia falcifolia, commonly known as the sickle begonia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that curve like sickles, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia falcifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
