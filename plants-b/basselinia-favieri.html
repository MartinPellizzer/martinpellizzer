Basselinia favieri

Basselinia favieri, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The leaves are used in traditional Māori crafts and have various medicinal properties.
