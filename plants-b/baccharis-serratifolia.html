Baccharis serratifolia

Baccharis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

Baccharis serratifolia is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
