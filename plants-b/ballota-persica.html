Ballota persica

Ballota persica, commonly known as Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Ballota persica is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
