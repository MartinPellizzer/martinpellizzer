Begonia palmata var. principalis

Begonia palmata var. principalis, commonly known as the principal begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture and prominent veins that enhance its ornamental appeal. It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Its unique leaf structure and colorful blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
