Bidens variabilis

Bidens variabilis, commonly known as the variable tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Bidens variabilis produces small yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
