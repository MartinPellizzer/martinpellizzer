Blyxa caulescens

Blyxa caulescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some emerging above the water surface. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in its environment.

Blyxa caulescens plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
