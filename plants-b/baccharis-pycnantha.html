Baccharis pycnantha

Baccharis pycnantha, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
