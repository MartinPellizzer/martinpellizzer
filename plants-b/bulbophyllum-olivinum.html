Bulbophyllum olivinum

Bulbophyllum olivinum, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid conditions.

Bulbophyllum olivinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
