Bromopsis aristata

Bromopsis aristata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Bromopsis aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
