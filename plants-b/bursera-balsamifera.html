Bursera balsamifera

Bursera balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree or copal tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The tree produces a sticky, balsam-like sap that is used in traditional medicine and for making incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
