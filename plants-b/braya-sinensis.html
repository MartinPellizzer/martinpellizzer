Braya sinensis

Braya sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of small, fleshy, rounded leaves. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes.

Braya sinensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
