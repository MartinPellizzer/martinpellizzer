Bunium bulbocastanum var. elatum

Bunium bulbocastanum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall bulbous fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous base and tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces compound umbels of small, yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a distinctive anise-like fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
