Boronia anemonifolia var. anethifolia

Boronia anemonifolia var. anethifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal or heathland environments. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with slender stems and lance-shaped foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
