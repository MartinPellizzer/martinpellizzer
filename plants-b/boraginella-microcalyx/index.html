Boraginella Microcalyx

Boraginella microcalyx is a small, delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, known for its unique blue or purple flowers and hairy leaves. Despite its modest size, this plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators. Its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study. However, it remains relatively unknown to the general public due to its limited distribution and subtle appearance.
