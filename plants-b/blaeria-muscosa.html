Blaeria muscosa

Blaeria muscosa, commonly known as the velvet moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant's leaves are typically thick, fleshy, and covered in a dense, velvety coating of fine cells, which helps it retain moisture. Blaeria muscosa lacks true roots, stems, and leaves in the traditional sense, instead having structures called thallus, which is a simple, undifferentiated body.

It plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
