Buddleja officinalis f. albiflora

Buddleja officinalis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered weeping buddleia, is a cultivar of the butterfly bush known for its soft, cascading growth habit.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.
