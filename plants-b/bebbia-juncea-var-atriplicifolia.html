Bebbia juncea var. atriplicifolia

Bebbia juncea var. atriplicifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bebbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
