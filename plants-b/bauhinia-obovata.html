Bauhinia obovata

Bauhinia obovata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
