Brassica cheiranthos var. minor

Brassica cheiranthos var. minor, commonly known as the lesser wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and bright, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
