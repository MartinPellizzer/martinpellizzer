Berberis atrocarpa

Berberis atrocarpa, commonly known as the blackleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
