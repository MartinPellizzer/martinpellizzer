Brunonia australis var. macrocephala

Brunonia australis var. macrocephala, commonly known as the giant bronzeweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that appear in late summer to autumn. The plant produces clusters of elongated, bract-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
