Bambusa ridleyi

Bambusa ridleyi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of giant bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has a rapid growth rate and is known for its strong, lightweight fibers. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often with a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa ridleyi is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of sustainable materials.
