Begonia baturongensis

Begonia baturongensis, commonly known as the Baturong Begonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage is often variegated with white, yellow, or pink markings, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
