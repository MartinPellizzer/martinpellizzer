Beilschmiedia marginata

Beilschmiedia marginata, commonly known as the marginated beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a distinct yellow or white margin. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
