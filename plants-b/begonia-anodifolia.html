Begonia anodifolia

Begonia anodifolia, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Begonia anodifolia is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
