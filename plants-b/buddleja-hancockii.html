Buddleja hancockii

Buddleja hancockii, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are typically purple or pink and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
