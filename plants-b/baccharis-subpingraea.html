Baccharis subpingraea

Baccharis subpingraea, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis subpingraea is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.
