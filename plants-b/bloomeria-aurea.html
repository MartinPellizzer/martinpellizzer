Bloomeria aurea

Bloomeria aurea, commonly known as the golden bloomers, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps.

Bloomeria aurea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
