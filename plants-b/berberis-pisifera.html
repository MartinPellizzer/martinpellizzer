Berberis pisifera

Berberis pisifera, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used as ornamental features.

Its thorny stems and adaptability to various soil types make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
