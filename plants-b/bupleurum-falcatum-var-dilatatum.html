Bupleurum falcatum var. dilatatum

Bupleurum falcatum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, somewhat hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant has a distinctive, aromatic odor.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
