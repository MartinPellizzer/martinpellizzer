Barringtonia apiculata

Barringtonia apiculata, commonly known as the candle bush or hibiscus tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble candles, with a white or cream color and a long, narrow tube. The plant produces a unique fruit that is elongated and covered in soft spines. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Barringtonia apiculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
