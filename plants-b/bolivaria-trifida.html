Bolivaria trifida

Bolivaria trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Bolivaria trifida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems, which store water.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive and interesting subject for botanical study.
