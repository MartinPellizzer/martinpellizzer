Bulbophyllum corallinum

Bulbophyllum corallinum, commonly known as the coral lip, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that produces intricate, coral-like floral structures, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated lip.

Bulbophyllum corallinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
