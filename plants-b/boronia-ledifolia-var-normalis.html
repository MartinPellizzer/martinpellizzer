Boronia ledifolia var. normalis

Boronia ledifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the common boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Boronia ledifolia var. normalis is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
