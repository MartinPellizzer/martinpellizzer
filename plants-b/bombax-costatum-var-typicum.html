Bombax costatum var. typicum

Bombax costatum var. typicum, commonly known as the silk cotton tree or ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous silky, cotton-like fibers, which are used in various industrial applications. Its trunk is often buttressed, providing stability in wet, tropical environments.

The plant is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife and its cultural significance in many indigenous communities.
