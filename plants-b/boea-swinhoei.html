Boea swinhoei

Boea swinhoei, commonly known as the silver moonvine, is a climbing perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces attractive, round, waxy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Boea swinhoei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
