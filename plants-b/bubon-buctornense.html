Bubon buctornense

Bubon buctornense, commonly known as the Crimson Veil fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a gradient of red and green hues.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are deeply lobed, and they exhibit a distinctive pattern of veins that resemble a network of capillaries. Bubon buctornense reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
