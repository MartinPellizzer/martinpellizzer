Baccharis laxiflora

Baccharis laxiflora, commonly known as slenderleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils along the coastlines of Florida and the Gulf Coast.

Baccharis laxiflora is adapted to coastal environments and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
