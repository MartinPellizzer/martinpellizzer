Bulbophyllum windsorense

Bulbophyllum windsorense, commonly known as the Windsores orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial epiphyte, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and typically thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in less-than-ideal conditions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
