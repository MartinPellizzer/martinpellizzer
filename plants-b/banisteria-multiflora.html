Banisteria multiflora

Banisteria multiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered banisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Banisteria multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
