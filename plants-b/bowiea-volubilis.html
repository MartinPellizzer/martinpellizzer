Bowiea volubilis

Bowiea volubilis, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Bowiea volubilis is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
