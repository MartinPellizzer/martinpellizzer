Brousemichea

Brousemichea, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brousemichea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
