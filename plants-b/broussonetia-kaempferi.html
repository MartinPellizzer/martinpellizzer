Broussonetia kaempferi

Broussonetia kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
