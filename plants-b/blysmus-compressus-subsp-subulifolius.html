Blysmus compressus subsp. subulifolius

Blysmus compressus subsp.

subulifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

Its adaptability to waterlogged soils makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
