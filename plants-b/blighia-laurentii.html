Blighia laurentii

Blighia laurentii, commonly known as the West African ackee, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small pineapple, contains a single seed surrounded by a sticky, white pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
