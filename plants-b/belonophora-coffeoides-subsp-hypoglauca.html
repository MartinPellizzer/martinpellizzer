Belonophora coffeoides subsp. hypoglauca

Belonophora coffeoides subsp.

hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered belonophora, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
