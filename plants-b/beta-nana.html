Beta nana

Beta nana, commonly known as the dwarf spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to the Arctic regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and have a mild, spinach-like flavor.

Beta nana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold environments.
