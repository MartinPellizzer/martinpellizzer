Berberis aridocalida

Berberis aridocalida, commonly known as the desert barberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
