Bifrenaria silvana

Bifrenaria silvana, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bifrenaria silvana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
