Begonia marmorata

Begonia marmorata, commonly known as the marble begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Begonia marmorata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
