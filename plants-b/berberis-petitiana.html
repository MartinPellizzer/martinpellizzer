Berberis petitiana

Berberis petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are dark green and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often sour. It is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also noted for its hardy nature and resistance to pests and diseases.
