Byrsonima paulista

Byrsonima paulista, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or Paulista cherry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
