Begonia pentaphylla

Begonia pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
