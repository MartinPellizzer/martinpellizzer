Bulbophyllum brachystachyum

Bulbophyllum brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients.

This species thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.
