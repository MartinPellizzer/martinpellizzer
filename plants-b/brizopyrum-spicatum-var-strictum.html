Brizopyrum spicatum var. strictum

Brizopyrum spicatum var. strictum, commonly known as rigid false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
