Bunchosia volcanica

Bunchosia volcanica, commonly known as the volcanic bunchosia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly serrated, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Bunchosia volcanica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various insect and bird species.
