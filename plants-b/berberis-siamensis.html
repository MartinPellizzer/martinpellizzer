Berberis siamensis

Berberis siamensis, commonly known as Siam barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces yellow-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
