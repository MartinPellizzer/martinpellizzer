Brownlowia sarawhensis

Brownlowia sarawhensis, commonly known as the Sarawak hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a yellow throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense tropical forests.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
