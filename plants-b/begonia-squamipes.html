Begonia squamipes

Begonia squamipes, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, scale-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia squamipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
