Birostula

Birostula, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense, misty forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer with a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Birostula thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter.
