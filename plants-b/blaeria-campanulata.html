Blaeria campanulata

Blaeria campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Blaeria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes various bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
