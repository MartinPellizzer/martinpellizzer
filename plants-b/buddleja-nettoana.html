Buddleja nettoana

Buddleja nettoana, commonly known as the nettleleaf buddleia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically purple or bluish-purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Buddleja nettoana is also valued for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
