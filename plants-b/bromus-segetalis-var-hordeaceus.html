Bromus segetalis var. hordeaceus

Bromus segetalis var. hordeaceus, commonly known as the cereal grass or barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is closely related to barley and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
