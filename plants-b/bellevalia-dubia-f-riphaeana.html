Bellevalia dubia f. riphaeana

Bellevalia dubia f. riphaeana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often found in mountainous areas of the Alps and Carpathians. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in alpine gardens and rockeries.
