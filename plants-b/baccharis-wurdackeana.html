Baccharis wurdackeana

Baccharis wurdackeana, commonly known as the Wurdack's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the late spring and summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

Baccharis wurdackeana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
