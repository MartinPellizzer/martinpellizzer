Braya versicolor

Braya versicolor, commonly known as the variable braya, is a flowering plant native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Braya versicolor is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a common sight in alpine tundra ecosystems.
