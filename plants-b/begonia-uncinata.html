Begonia uncinata

Begonia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia uncinata is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
