Bupleurum filicaule

Bupleurum filicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Bupleurum filicaule thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassy habitats.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
