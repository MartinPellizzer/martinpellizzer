Boopis gracilis var. decumbens

Boopis gracilis var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent boopis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, spreading stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
