Blechnum blechnoides var. fernandezianum

Blechnum blechnoides var. fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandez's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in moist forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
