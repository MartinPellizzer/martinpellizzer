Baccharis spartioides

Baccharis spartioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Baccharis spartioides is also known for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
