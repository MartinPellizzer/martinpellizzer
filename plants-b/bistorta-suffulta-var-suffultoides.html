Bistorta suffulta var. suffultoides

Bistorta suffulta var. suffultoides, commonly known as the purple false forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright, stout stems that are often covered in a purple bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are thick, arrow-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially when mature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
