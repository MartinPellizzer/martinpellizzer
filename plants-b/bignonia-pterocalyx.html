Bignonia pterocalyx

Bignonia pterocalyx, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bignonia pterocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
