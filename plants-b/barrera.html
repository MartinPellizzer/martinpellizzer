Barrera

Barrera, commonly known as the "barrera plant," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Barrera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
