Bignonia parkeri

Bignonia parkeri, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling small bells. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia parkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
