Baccharis grandicapitulata

Baccharis grandicapitulata, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis grandicapitulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping in regions with a subtropical climate.
