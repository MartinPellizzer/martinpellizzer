Brassica campestris f. oblonga

Brassica campestris f. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong cabbage, is a botanical variant of the turnip.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves that are typically darker green than the standard variety. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciferous traits. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and can be grown in temperate climates.

This form is valued for its robust growth and mild flavor, making it a versatile crop in gardens and farms.
