Bauhinia splendens f. meridionalis

Bauhinia splendens f. meridionalis, commonly known as the red flame bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.
