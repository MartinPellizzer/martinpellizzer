Berberis latifolia

Berberis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
