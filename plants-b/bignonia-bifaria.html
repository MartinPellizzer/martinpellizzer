Bignonia bifaria

Bignonia bifaria, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a vine or shrub, reaching up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
