Bulbophyllum adolphii

Bulbophyllum adolphii, commonly known as the Adolphii's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in less-than-ideal conditions.

The orchid's unique floral structure and adaptability make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
