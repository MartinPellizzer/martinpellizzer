Barleria hirtifructa

Barleria hirtifructa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and are also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Barleria hirtifructa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
