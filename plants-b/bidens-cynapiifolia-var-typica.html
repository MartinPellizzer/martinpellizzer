Bidens cynapiifolia var. typica

Bidens cynapiifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
