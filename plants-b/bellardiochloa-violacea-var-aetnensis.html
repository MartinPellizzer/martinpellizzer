Bellardiochloa violacea var. aetnensis

Bellardiochloa violacea var. aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna violetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the volcanic soils of Sicily.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive violet-tinged leaves. The plant typically thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is adapted to the unique microclimates of volcanic regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
