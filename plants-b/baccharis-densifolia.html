Baccharis densifolia

Baccharis densifolia, commonly known as denseleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry to moist habitats, including grasslands and woodlands. It has small, leathery leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits.

Baccharis densifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
