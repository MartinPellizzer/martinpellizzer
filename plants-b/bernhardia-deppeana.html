Bernhardia deppeana

Bernhardia deppeana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Bernhardia deppeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
