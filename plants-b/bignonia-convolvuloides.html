Bignonia convolvuloides

Bignonia convolvuloides, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red calyx. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia convolvuloides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
