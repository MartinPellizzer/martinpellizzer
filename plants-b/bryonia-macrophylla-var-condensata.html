Bryonia macrophylla var. condensata

Bryonia macrophylla var. condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf bryony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Benincaseae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and lobed, often hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
