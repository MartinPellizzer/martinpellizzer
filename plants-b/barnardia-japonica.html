Barnardia japonica

Barnardia japonica, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Barnardia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
