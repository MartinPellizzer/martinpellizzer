Bulbophyllum scutiferum

Bulbophyllum scutiferum, commonly known as the shield-lip orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Bulbophyllum scutiferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
