Bromus atticus

Bromus atticus, commonly known as the Attic brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. Bromus atticus typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
