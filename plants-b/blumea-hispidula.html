Blumea hispidula

Blumea hispidula, commonly known as the rough blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Blumea hispidula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
