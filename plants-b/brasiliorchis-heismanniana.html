Brasiliorchis heismanniana

Brasiliorchis heismanniana, commonly known as the Heismann's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green, adapted to its humid, shaded forest habitat. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in the understory of dense vegetation. Its unique floral structure suggests specialized pollination mechanisms, likely involving specific insect or avian pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native region.
