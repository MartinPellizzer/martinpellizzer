Beyeria calycina

Beyeria calycina, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a striking flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical name reflects its appearance, with "calycina" referring to its showy calyx.

Beyeria calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
