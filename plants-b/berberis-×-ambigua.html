Berberis × ambigua

Berberis × ambigua, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a cross between Berberis asiatica and Berberis julianae, resulting in a plant with distinctive yellow-orange foliage that turns red in the fall. The shrub features spiny stems and small, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
