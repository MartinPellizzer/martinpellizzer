Barbarea pyrenaica

Barbarea pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces white or pale yellow flowers from spring to early summer.

Barbarea pyrenaica is often found in open meadows and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
