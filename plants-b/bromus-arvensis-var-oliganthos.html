Bromus arvensis var. oliganthos

Bromus arvensis var. oliganthos, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with short, awned glumes, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
