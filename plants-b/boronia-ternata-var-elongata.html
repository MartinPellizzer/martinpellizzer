Boronia ternata var. elongata

Boronia ternata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often elongated, giving it its common name.

It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it a notable feature in its native habitat.
