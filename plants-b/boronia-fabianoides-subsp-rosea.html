Boronia fabianoides subsp. rosea

Boronia fabianoides subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features aromatic, pink to mauve flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
