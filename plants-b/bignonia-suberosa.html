Bignonia suberosa

Bignonia suberosa, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb by twining around supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia suberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in gardens.
