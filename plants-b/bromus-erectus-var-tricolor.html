Bromus erectus var. tricolor

Bromus erectus var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive tricolor appearance, with leaves that display a mix of green, blue, and silver hues. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish-brown, adding to its ornamental value.

Bromus erectus var. tricolor is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
