Bertiera racemosa

Bertiera racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bertiera, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
