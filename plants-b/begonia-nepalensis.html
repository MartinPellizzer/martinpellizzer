Begonia nepalensis

Begonia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
