Bromheadia humilis

Bromheadia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bromhead, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial plants. The plant typically grows up to 15 centimeters tall, featuring strap-like leaves that form a rosette shape. It produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in a spike, often blooming in the spring.

Bromheadia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
