Bidens ododrata

Bidens ododrata, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with rough textures and serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its fruit is a schizocarp composed of two winged nuts, which are dispersed by wind.

Bidens ododrata is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.
