Bursera fagaroides var. elongata

Bursera fagaroides var. elongata, commonly known as the Mexican balsam or elongated bursera, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap and its compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in its native habitats.
