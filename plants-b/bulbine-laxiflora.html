Bulbine laxiflora

Bulbine laxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
