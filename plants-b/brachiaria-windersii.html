Brachiaria windersii

Brachiaria windersii, commonly known as the slender bladegrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria windersii is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage and erosion control.
