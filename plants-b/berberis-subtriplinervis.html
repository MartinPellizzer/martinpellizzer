Berberis subtriplinervis

Berberis subtriplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
