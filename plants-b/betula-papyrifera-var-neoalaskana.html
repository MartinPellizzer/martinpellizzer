Betula papyrifera var. neoalaskana

Betula papyrifera var. neoalaskana, commonly known as the Alaska paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to northern North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish layer beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in cold climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with serrated edges.

This species plays an important ecological role in boreal and subarctic ecosystems.
