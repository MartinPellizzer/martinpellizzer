Bromheadia sylvestris

Bromheadia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest bromhead, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in vibrant colors that attract pollinators.

Bromheadia sylvestris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or in dense undergrowth.
