Brunsvigia ciliaris

Brunsvigia ciliaris, commonly known as the Cape bell or red-hot poker, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange, with a distinctive red bract at the base. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Brunsvigia ciliaris is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in summer.
