Brachiaria nigropedata

Brachiaria nigropedata, commonly known as blackstem brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria nigropedata is valued for its adaptability and is used in forage and erosion control.
