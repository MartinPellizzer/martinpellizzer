Barkhausia raphanifolia

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible berry that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Barkhausia raphanifolia is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water and nutrients.
