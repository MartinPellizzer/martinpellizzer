Berberis beesiana var. glabra

Berberis beesiana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

This variety is less prickly than other Berberis species, making it more suitable for smaller gardens and landscapes.
