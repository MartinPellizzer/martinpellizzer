Blotia leandriana

Blotia leandriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Blotia leandriana is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
