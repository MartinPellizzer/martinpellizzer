Borodiniopsis

Borodiniopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with pale, almost translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its small, fleshy leaves are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Borodiniopsis is considered a critically endangered species.
