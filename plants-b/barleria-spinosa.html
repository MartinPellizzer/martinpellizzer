Barleria spinosa

Barleria spinosa, commonly known as the spiked barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
