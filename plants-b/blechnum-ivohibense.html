Blechnum ivohibense

Blechnum ivohibense, commonly known as the Madagascar holly fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive appearance that makes it recognizable in its natural habitat.
