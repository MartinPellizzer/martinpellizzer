Bomarea petraea

Bomarea petraea, commonly known as the rock bomarea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of tubers, which store nutrients for survival. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with showy, spurred petals, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped and hairy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Bomarea petraea is considered a rare and endangered species.
