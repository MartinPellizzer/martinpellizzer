Boopis laciniata

Boopis laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf boopis, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are not particularly showy.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not widely cultivated due to its specific habitat requirements and limited availability.
