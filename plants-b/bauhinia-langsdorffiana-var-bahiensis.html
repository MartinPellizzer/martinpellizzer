Bauhinia langsdorffiana var. bahiensis

Bauhinia langsdorffiana var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, papery bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
