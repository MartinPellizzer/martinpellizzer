Ballota pilosa

Ballota pilosa, commonly known as hairy ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Ballota pilosa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
