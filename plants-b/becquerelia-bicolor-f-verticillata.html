Becquerelia bicolor f. verticillata

Becquerelia bicolor f. verticillata, commonly known as the variegated becquerelia, is a unique variant of the becquerelia genus, characterized by its striking bicolor foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are typically arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive, compact appearance. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to low-light conditions and its ornamental value due to its colorful leaf patterns.
