Bulbophyllum balgooiji

Bulbophyllum balgooiji, commonly known as the Balgooiji orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which serve as storage organs for water and nutrients. The plant typically produces multiple slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
