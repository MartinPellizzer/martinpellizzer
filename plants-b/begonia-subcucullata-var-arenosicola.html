Begonia subcucullata var. arenosicola

Begonia subcucullata var. arenosicola, commonly known as the sandhill begonia, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and uniquely shaped leaves that resemble small, cup-like structures. This variety thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
