Blepharis asteracantha

Blepharis asteracantha, commonly known as the star-stemmed blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
