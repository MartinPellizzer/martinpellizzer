Bartsia hispida

Bartsia hispida, commonly known as the hairy bartsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually white or pale pink, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators.

Bartsia hispida is known for its delicate, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in shaded, damp conditions.
