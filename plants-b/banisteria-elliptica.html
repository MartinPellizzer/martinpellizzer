Banisteria elliptica

Banisteria elliptica, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of vibrant red, spider-like flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Banisteria elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
