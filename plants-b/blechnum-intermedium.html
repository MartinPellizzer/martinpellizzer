Blechnum intermedium

Blechnum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate water fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks, where it can absorb sufficient humidity. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, silky hairs, which help in water retention.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
