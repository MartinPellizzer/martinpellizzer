Bursera galeottiana

Bursera galeottiana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, opposite leaves that turn red in the fall. It produces yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that release seeds when mature.

The tree is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
