Bonafousia disticha

Bonafousia disticha, commonly known as the two-branched balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
