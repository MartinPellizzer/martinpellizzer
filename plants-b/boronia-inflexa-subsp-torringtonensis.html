Boronia inflexa subsp. torringtonensis

Boronia inflexa subsp.

torringtonensis, commonly known as the Torrington boronia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils within coastal heathlands and woodland areas.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers during the winter months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and locally significant species.
