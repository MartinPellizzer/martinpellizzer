Bignonia triflora

Bignonia triflora, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, three-lobed leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Bignonia triflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
