Bauhinia ferruginea var. tonkinensis

Bauhinia ferruginea var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in warm regions.
