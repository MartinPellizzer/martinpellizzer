Bauhinia macranthera var. grayana

Bauhinia macranthera var. grayana, commonly known as the grayana bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
