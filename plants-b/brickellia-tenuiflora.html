Brickellia tenuiflora

Brickellia tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-flowered brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by cylindrical, hairless seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Brickellia tenuiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
