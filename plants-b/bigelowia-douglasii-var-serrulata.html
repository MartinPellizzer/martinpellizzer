Bigelowia douglasii var. serrulata

Bigelowia douglasii var. serrulata, commonly known as the Douglas's bigelowia, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
