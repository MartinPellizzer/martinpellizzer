Bambusa senanensis var. albomarginata

Bambusa senanensis var. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-edged bamboo, is a striking variety of bamboo characterized by its distinctive white margins on the leaf blades.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. Its foliage provides both ornamental value and habitat for various small animals and insects.

The white-edged leaves make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
