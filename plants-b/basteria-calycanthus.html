Basteria calycanthus

Basteria calycanthus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide dense foliage, and its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin strips. Basteria calycanthus thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
