Berberis bullata

Berberis bullata, commonly known as the clustered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Berberis bullata is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.
