Bersama tessmannii

Bersama tessmannii, commonly known as the Malayan sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and fragrant, heartwood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and woodworking. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest regions, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, while its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
