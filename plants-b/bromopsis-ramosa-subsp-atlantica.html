Bromopsis ramosa subsp. atlantica

Bromopsis ramosa subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bromopsis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in maritime climates, where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.
