Boea commersonii

Boea commersonii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its rapid growth habit makes it popular as a decorative vine in gardens and indoor spaces.

Boea commersonii is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and lacewings.
