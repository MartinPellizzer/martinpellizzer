Briza toluccensis

Briza toluccensis, commonly known as the Toluca grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

Native to Mexico, this plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It features fine, linear leaves and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with green or brown. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
