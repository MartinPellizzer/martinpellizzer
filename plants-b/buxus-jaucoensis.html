Buxus jaucoensis

Buxus jaucoensis, commonly known as the Jauco boxwood, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Buxaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are small, oval, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Buxus jaucoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
