Barbarea auriculata

Barbarea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate winter cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the plant produces yellow flowers in early spring.

Barbarea auriculata is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
