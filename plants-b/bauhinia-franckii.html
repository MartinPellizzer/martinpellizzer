Bauhinia franckii

Bauhinia franckii, commonly known as the flame tree or bracken fern tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Bauhinia franckii is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a spreading canopy.

Its seeds are encased in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.
