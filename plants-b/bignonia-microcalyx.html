Bignonia microcalyx

Bignonia microcalyx, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees, featuring a deep nectar chamber.

Bignonia microcalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
