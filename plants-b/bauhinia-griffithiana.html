Bauhinia griffithiana

Bauhinia griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian bean tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
