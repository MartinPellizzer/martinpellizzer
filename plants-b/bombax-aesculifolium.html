Bombax aesculifolium

Bombax aesculifolium, commonly known as the silk-cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces capsule fruits that contain fibrous seeds surrounded by a cotton-like covering. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used for timber and fiber extraction.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
