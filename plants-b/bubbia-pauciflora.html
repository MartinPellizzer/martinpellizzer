Bubbia pauciflora

Bubbia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered burrpear, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts. Bubbia pauciflora is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access underground water.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
