Bertiera racemosa var. dewevrei

Bertiera racemosa var. dewevrei, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow-barked tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or on rocky outcrops.

The species is valued for its ornamental features and has some traditional medicinal uses.
