Baccharis tricuneata var. ruiziana

Baccharis tricuneata var. ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's brittle bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It has a woody stem with a distinctive, brittle texture, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
