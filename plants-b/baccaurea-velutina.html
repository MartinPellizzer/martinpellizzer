Baccaurea velutina

Baccaurea velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are covered in a soft, velvety coating. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
