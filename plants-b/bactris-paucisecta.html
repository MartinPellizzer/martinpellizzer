Bactris paucisecta

Bactris paucisecta, commonly known as the small-segmented palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, lowland environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife.
