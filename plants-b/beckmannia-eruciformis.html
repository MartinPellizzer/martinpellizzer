Beckmannia eruciformis

Beckmannia eruciformis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many areas where it is introduced.
