Baldomiria chloridiformis

Baldomiria chloridiformis, commonly known as the yellow sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit with small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, adapted to attract pollinators in its harsh environment. It is well-suited to sandy soils and can thrive in conditions of limited water availability.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for erosion control in dry landscapes.
