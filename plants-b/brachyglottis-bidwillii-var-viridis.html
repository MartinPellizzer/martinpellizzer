Brachyglottis bidwillii var. viridis

Brachyglottis bidwillii var. viridis, commonly known as the green bristlebush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green, covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

The varietal name 'viridis' refers to its distinctive green coloration, which is a key characteristic of this plant.
