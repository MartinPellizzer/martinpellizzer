Bumelia pallida

Bumelia pallida, commonly known as the white bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes plants known for their milky sap. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Bumelia pallida is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
