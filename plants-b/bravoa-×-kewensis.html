Bravoa × kewensis

Bravoa × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew hybrid cornflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Bravoa iberica and Bravoa lusitana.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower colors and delicate, papery petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have contributed to its widespread use in horticulture.
