Bromus macranthes

Bromus macranthes, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelets that are often adorned with long, feathery bracts. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Bromus macranthes is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
