Bouchea dehiscens

Bouchea dehiscens, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. The plant features a woody stem with broad, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal racemes and bloom in the spring.

Bouchea dehiscens is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
