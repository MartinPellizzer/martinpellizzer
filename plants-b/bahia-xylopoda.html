Bahia xylopoda

Bahia xylopoda, commonly known as coastal bahia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found along coastal areas and in dunes. It has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in pairs.

Bahia xylopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
