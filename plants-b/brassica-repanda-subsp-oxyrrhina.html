Brassica repanda subsp. oxyrrhina

Brassica repanda subsp.

oxyrrhina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive peppery aroma.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing a high amount of glucosinolates, which are defensive compounds against herbivores.

This subspecies is valued in both traditional medicine and as a wild edible plant.
