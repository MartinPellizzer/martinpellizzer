Bryonia abyssinica

Bryonia abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian figleaf gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Benincaseae and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
