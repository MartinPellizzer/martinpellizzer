Berberis caelicolor

Berberis caelicolor, commonly known as the alpine barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Berberis caelicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
