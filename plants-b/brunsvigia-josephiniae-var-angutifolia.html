Brunsvigia josephiniae var. angutifolia

Brunsvigia josephiniae var. angutifolia, commonly known as the Cape bell or fire bell, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with striking red or orange flowers that resemble bells, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base that stores water and nutrients. This variety is characterized by its angular, leaf-like bracts that give it a unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
