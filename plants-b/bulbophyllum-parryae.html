Bulbophyllum parryae

Bulbophyllum parryae, commonly known as the Parry's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically elongated and leathery, adapted for efficient moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum parryae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral displays and unique growth habits.
