Berberis weddellii

Berberis weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bright green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
