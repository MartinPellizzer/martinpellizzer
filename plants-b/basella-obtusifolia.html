Basella obtusifolia

Basella obtusifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value.

Basella obtusifolia is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
