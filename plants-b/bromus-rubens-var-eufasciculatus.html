Bromus rubens var. eufasciculatus

Bromus rubens var. eufasciculatus, commonly known as the red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pale green to brown flowers in late summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
