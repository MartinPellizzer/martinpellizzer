Bumelia obtusifolia subsp. buxifolia

Bumelia obtusifolia subsp.

buxifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
