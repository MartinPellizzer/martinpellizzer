Bulbophyllum teimosense

Bulbophyllum teimosense, commonly known as the Timor orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the island of Timor.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Bulbophyllum teimosense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
