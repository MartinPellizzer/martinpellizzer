Begonia imperialis

Begonia imperialis, commonly known as the imperial begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
