Blakea cuneata

Blakea cuneata, commonly known as the scarlet waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and dense forests. Blakea cuneata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to local biodiversity.
