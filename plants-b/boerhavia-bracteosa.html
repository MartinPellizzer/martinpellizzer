Boerhavia bracteosa

Boerhavia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Boerhavia bracteosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, which can make it invasive in certain environments.
