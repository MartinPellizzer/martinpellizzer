Bouteloua curtipendula var. curtipendula

Bouteloua curtipendula var. curtipendula, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikelets that are arranged in panicle-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for prairie and restoration habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
