Boehmeria ternifolia

Boehmeria ternifolia, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Japanese nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibers are valued for their strength and are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.

Boehmeria ternifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
