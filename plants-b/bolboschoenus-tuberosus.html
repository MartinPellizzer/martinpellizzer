Bolboschoenus tuberosus

Bolboschoenus tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and produces tubers that serve as storage organs for nutrients. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tubers of Bolboschoenus tuberosus have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
