Barrotia macrocarpa

Barrotia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant barrotia, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences that bear showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically broad and sword-shaped, providing a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
