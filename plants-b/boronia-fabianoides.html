Boronia fabianoides

Boronia fabianoides, commonly known as the Fabian's boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Boronia fabianoides is also an important species for local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
