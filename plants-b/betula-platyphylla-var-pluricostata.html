Betula platyphylla var. pluricostata

Betula platyphylla var. pluricostata, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
