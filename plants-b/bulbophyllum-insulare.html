Bulbophyllum insulare

Bulbophyllum insulare, commonly known as the Island Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Bulbophyllum insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
