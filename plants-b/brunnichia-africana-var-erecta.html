Brunnichia africana var. erecta

Brunnichia africana var. erecta, commonly known as the African bristlebush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
