Begonia maculifolia

Begonia maculifolia, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that feature distinct reddish or purplish spots. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually small, tubular, and borne in clusters, with colors ranging from pink to white.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
