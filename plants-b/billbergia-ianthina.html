Billbergia ianthina

Billbergia ianthina, commonly known as the red-tipped billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that features red-tipped bracts. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered around the central spathes.

Billbergia ianthina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
