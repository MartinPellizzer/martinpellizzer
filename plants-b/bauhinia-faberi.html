Bauhinia faberi

Bauhinia faberi, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns, and its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Bauhinia faberi is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
