Bulbophyllum intertextum var. parvilabium

Bulbophyllum intertextum var. parvilabium, commonly known as the small-lipped bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
