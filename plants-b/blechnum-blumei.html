Blechnum blumei

Blechnum blumei, commonly known as the Blume's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in forest understories.

The plant's spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
