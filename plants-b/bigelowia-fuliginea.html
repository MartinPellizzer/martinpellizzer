Bigelowia fuliginea

Bigelowia fuliginea, commonly known as the sooty bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, and often sooty appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests and along stream margins.
