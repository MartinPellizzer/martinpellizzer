Boopis anthemoides var. andina

Boopis anthemoides var. andina, commonly known as Andean anthemis, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem structure.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
