Blitum virgatum subsp. capitatum

Blitum virgatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the clustered bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its clustered inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
