Bauhinia anamesa

Bauhinia anamesa, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and unique foliage.

Bauhinia anamesa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
