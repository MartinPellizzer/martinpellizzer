Bulbophyllum diplantherum

Bulbophyllum diplantherum, commonly known as the broad-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and showy blooms.
