Bromus madritensis var. diandrus

Bromus madritensis var. diandrus, commonly known as the Spanish brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological studies and agricultural research.
