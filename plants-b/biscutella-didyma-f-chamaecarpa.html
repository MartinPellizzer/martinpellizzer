Biscutella didyma f. chamaecarpa

Biscutella didyma f. chamaecarpa, commonly known as the creeping biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. Its flowers, which are typically pink or purple, bloom in early spring and are attractive to pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
