Begonia inanis

Begonia inanis, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green and white or silver markings.

Begonia inanis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in controlled environments.
