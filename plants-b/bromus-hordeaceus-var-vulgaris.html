Bromus hordeaceus var. vulgaris

Bromus hordeaceus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Bromus hordeaceus var. vulgaris is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
