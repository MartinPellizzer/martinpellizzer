Bactris schultesii

Bactris schultesii, commonly known as the Schultesi palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruits are typically clustered in panicles and are edible, containing a starchy pulp.

Bactris schultesii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
