Berchemia hispida

Berchemia hispida, commonly known as the hairy berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Berchemia hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
