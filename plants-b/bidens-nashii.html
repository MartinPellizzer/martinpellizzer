Bidens nashii

Bidens nashii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often have a serrated edge.

Bidens nashii produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by seeds dispersed by wind.
