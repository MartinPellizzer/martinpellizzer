Bupleurum semidiaphanum f. pindicola

Bupleurum semidiaphanum f. pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus bupleurum, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the Pindus Mountains of northern Greece.

It is characterized by its delicate, semi-transparent leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, humid microclimates of its mountainous habitat. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical specimen from threats such as habitat loss and climate change.
