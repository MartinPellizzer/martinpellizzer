Bauhinia appendiculata

Bauhinia appendiculata, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pod fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia appendiculata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
