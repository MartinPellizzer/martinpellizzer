Bomarea vargasii

Bomarea vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that feature a spatheum and a spadix, typical of the arum family. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of red, yellow, or orange, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Bomarea vargasii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
