Biarum aleppicum

Biarum aleppicum, commonly known as the Aleppo lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Biarum aleppicum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
