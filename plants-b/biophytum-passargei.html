Biophytum passargei

Biophytum passargei, commonly known as the "Ghost Plant," is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
