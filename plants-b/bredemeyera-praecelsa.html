Bredemeyera praecelsa

Bredemeyera praecelsa, commonly known as the giant star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive star-like shape and vibrant colors. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish. Bredemeyera praecelsa is also notable for its unique growth habit, often forming dense clumps with multiple stems.

Due to its habitat specificity and threats from deforestation, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
