Bromus unioloides f. pseudoparviflorus

Bromus unioloides f. pseudoparviflorus, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a variant of Bromus unioloides, characterized by its smaller, more compact inflorescence compared to the typical form. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its environment.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in various landscapes.
