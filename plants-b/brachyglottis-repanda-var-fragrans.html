Brachyglottis repanda var. fragrans

Brachyglottis repanda var. fragrans, commonly known as the sweet brachyglottis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
