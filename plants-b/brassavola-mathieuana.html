Brassavola mathieuana

Brassavola mathieuana, commonly known as the Mathieu's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing a rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Brassavola mathieuana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
