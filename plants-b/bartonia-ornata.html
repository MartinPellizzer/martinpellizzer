Bartonia ornata

Bartonia ornata, commonly known as the sweetgum or the Florida starflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Bartonia ornata is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
