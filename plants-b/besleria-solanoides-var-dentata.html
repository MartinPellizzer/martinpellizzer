Besleria solanoides var. dentata

Besleria solanoides var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed besleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
