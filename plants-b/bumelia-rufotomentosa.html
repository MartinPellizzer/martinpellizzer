Bumelia rufotomentosa

Bumelia rufotomentosa, commonly known as the roughleaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its rough, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some wildlife species. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin and are covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

Bumelia rufotomentosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.
