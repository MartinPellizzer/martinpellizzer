Blechnum squamulosum

Blechnum squamulosum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silvery fronds that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Blechnum squamulosum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
