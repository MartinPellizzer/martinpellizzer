Brickellia coulteri f. eglandulescens

Brickellia coulteri f. eglandulescens, commonly known as the glandular brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered with glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The small yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
