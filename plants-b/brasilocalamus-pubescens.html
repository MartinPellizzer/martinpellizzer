Brasilocalamus pubescens

Brasilocalamus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks covered in fine, hairy texture. Brasilocalamus pubescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
