Blepharodon triplinerve

Blepharodon triplinerve, commonly known as the triplinerved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive tripartite venation pattern in its leaves, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals arranged in a bell-like shape.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
