Bridelia microphylla

Bridelia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bridelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It features opposite, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical environments.

Bridelia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fruit.
