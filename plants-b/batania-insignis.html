Batania insignis

Batania insignis, commonly known as the purple batania, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Batania insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
