Bambusa longinodis

Bambusa longinodis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. Its culms are smooth and have a distinctive white or yellowish sheath at the nodes.

Bambusa longinodis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional construction.
