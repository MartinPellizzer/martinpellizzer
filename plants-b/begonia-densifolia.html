Begonia densifolia

Begonia densifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a soft, fuzzy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden landscapes.
