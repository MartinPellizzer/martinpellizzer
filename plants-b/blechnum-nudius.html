Blechnum nudius

Blechnum nudius, commonly known as the naked fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched fronds that are typically dark green and have a smooth, leathery texture. The plant is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including parts of South America, Africa, and Australia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blechnum nudius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
