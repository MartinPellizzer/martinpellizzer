Begonia ahooensis

Begonia ahooensis, commonly known as the Ahoo Valley begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
