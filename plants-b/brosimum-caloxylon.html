Brosimum caloxylon

Brosimum caloxylon, commonly known as the "guanacaste" or "white cedar," is a deciduous tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, and it is often found in tropical dry forests and along riverbanks.

Brosimum caloxylon is also valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
