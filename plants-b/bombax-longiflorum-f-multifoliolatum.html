Bombax longiflorum f. multifoliolatum

Bombax longiflorum f. multifoliolatum, commonly known as the multilobed silk cotton tree, is a variant of the silk cotton tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, palmate, and deeply lobed, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed pods.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is valued in traditional medicine for its various properties.
