Baptisia oxyphylla

Baptisia oxyphylla, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant produces showy, globular seedpods that split open to release its seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Baptisia oxyphylla is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.
