Bahia ehrenbergii

Bahia ehrenbergii, commonly known as desert carpetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its low-growing, dense foliage that forms a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh desert conditions.

Bahia ehrenbergii is well adapted to drought, making it a valuable plant for landscaping in xeriscaped gardens.
