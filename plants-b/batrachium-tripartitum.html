Batrachium tripartitum

Batrachium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part frog plant, is a unique species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, frog-like appearance, with a thick, fleshy stem and a broad, heart-shaped leaf. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid plants. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Central and South America.

Due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
