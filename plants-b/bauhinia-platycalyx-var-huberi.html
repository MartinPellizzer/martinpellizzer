Bauhinia platycalyx var. huberi

Bauhinia platycalyx var. huberi, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
