Bignonia copaia

Bignonia copaia, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, opposite leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
