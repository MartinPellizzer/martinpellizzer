Bactris acanthocnemis

Bactris acanthocnemis, commonly known as the red-berried palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruits are small, red berries that are often clustered together, making it visually striking.

This palm is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested or disturbed areas.
