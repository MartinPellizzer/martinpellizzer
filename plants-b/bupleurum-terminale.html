Bupleurum terminale

Bupleurum terminale, commonly known as the Indian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white to pale yellow, arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences.

The root system of Bupleurum terminale is often harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective properties.
