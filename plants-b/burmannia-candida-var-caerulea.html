Burmannia candida var. caerulea

Burmannia candida var. caerulea, commonly known as the Blue Burmannia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a star or cluster of small bells. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
