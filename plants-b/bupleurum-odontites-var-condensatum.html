Bupleurum odontites var. condensatum

Bupleurum odontites var. condensatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
