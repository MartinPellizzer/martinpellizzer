Bryonia althaeoides

Bryonia althaeoides, commonly known as the pale bryony, is a perennial climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red, spiny fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy coating, and its stems are covered in small, hooked tendrils.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and alkaloids.
