Beilia

Beilia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Beilia has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, seed-bearing capsules after flowering.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
