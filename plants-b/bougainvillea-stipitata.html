Bougainvillea stipitata

Bougainvillea stipitata, commonly known as the red-blossom bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in warm, sunny climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy display of color.

Bougainvillea stipitata is known for its ability to grow on trellises and structures, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
