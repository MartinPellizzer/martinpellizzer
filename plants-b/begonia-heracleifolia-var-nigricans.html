Begonia heracleifolia var. nigricans

Begonia heracleifolia var. nigricans, commonly known as the black-leaved begonia, is a striking plant characterized by its dark, almost black foliage.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of red or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

This variety is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique aesthetic and relatively easy care requirements.
