Bauhinia purpurea var. violacea

Bauhinia purpurea var. violacea, commonly known as the purple bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
