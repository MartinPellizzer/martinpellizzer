Bryophyllum adelae

Bryophyllum adelae, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water. The plant produces small, vibrant green leaves along its stems, which can develop into new plants when they fall to the ground. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique propagation method and low maintenance make it an attractive and easy-to-care-for plant.
