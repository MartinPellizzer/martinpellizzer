Blakwellia grandiflora

Blakwellia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered blakwellia, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to the wet tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
