Bridelia scleroneura subsp. angolensis

Bridelia scleroneura subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angola bridelia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
