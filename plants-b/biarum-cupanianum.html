Biarum cupanianum

Biarum cupanianum, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinct hooded structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, acidic soils.

Biarum cupanianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
