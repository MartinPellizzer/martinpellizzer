Bactris setipinnata

Bactris setipinnata, commonly known as the pink pineapple or pinka, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with pink or red. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary applications.

Bactris setipinnata is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
