Baccharis lundii

Baccharis lundii, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Baccharis lundii is also valued in landscaping for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
