Barleria sericea

Barleria sericea, commonly known as silverleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria sericea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
