Biserrula pelecina

Biserrula pelecina, commonly known as the serrated pelecine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing small, round seeds.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and forage in agricultural settings.
