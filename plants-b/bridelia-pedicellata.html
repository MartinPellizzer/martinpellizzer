Bridelia pedicellata

Bridelia pedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes plants known for their diverse morphological features. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted, a common trait in the family. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathium structures, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Bridelia pedicellata is also notable for its latex-producing properties, which can be used in traditional medicine.
