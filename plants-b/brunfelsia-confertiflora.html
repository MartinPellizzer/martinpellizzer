Brunfelsia confertiflora

Brunfelsia confertiflora, commonly known as the wax tree or waxflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. These flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
