Butia stolonifera

Butia stolonifera, commonly known as the areca palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its clustering growth habit and stiff, sword-shaped leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed by wildlife. Butia stolonifera is adapted to dry and tropical climates, making it a hardy and drought-resistant plant.

Its stoloniferous growth pattern allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in its natural habitat.
