Berula angustifolia var. dentata

Berula angustifolia var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed water parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a stout, ridged stem that is often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and the roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found near waterways.
