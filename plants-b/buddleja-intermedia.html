Buddleja intermedia

Buddleja intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Oleaceae family and is known for its ability to attract a wide variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Buddleja intermedia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
