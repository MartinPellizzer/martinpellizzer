Baccharis coerulescens

Baccharis coerulescens, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
