Bikukulla canadensis

Bikukulla canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, flexible branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, catkin-like flowers appear in early spring, followed by the production of small, winged seeds for wind dispersal.

The species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
