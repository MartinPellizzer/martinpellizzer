Bassia joppensis

Bassia joppensis, commonly known as the Jordanian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in drought conditions.

Bassia joppensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
