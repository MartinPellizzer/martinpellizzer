Begonia cebadillensis

Begonia cebadillensis, commonly known as the Cebadilla begonia, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and red hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
