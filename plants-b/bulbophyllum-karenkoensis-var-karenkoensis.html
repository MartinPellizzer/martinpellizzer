Bulbophyllum karenkoensis var. karenkoensis

Bulbophyllum karenkoensis var. karenkoensis, commonly known as the Karenko's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
