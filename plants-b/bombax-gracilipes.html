Bombax gracilipes

Bombax gracilipes, commonly known as the silky silk cotton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink or red. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a three-lobed capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
