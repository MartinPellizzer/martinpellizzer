Berberis vulgaris var. sanguinea

Berberis vulgaris var. sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson berberis, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are typically oval with a glossy surface and can vary in color from dark green to reddish in autumn.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
