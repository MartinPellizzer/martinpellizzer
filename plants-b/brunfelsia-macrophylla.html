Brunfelsia macrophylla

Brunfelsia macrophylla, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, violet, and pink. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
