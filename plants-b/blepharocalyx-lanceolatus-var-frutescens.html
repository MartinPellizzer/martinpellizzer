Blepharocalyx lanceolatus var. frutescens

Blepharocalyx lanceolatus var. frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
