Brassica monensis var. maroccana

Brassica monensis var. maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan mustard, is a variant of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy annual in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable crop and an ornamental plant in gardens.
