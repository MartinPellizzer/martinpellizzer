Beta vulgaris var. cicla

Beta vulgaris var. cicla, commonly known as the leaf beet or chard, is a cultivar of the beet plant characterized by its large, edible leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and has been cultivated for centuries for its nutritional value. The plant produces a thick, fibrous stem and broad, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, white, and yellow. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a valuable vegetable in various cuisines.
