Betula ajanensis

Betula ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanent birch, is a species of birch native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in high-altitude regions, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. The bark of Betula ajanensis is smooth and white, with a distinctive flaky texture. Its leaves are elliptical, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
