Bastardia angulata

Bastardia angulata, commonly known as the angular bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in coastal areas.

Bastardia angulata is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
