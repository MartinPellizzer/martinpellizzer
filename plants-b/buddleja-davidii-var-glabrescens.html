Buddleja davidii var. glabrescens

Buddleja davidii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silver-leaf buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
