Barosma odorata

Barosma odorata, commonly known as the sweet flag or sweet reed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Barosma odorata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
