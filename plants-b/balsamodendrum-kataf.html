Balsamodendrum kataf

Balsamodendrum kataf, commonly known as the Malabar balsam, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3-5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually in shades of white or yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
