Bauhinia brevipes

Bauhinia brevipes, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. Bauhinia brevipes is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warmer regions.
