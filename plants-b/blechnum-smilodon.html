Blechnum smilodon

Blechnum smilodon, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and mild temperatures. Its rhizomes are thick and robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage and contributing to the plant's ornamental value.
