Boymia ruticarpa

Boymia ruticarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf boymia, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant is characterized by its rough, woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in axillary inflorescences, and are often pink or purple in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
