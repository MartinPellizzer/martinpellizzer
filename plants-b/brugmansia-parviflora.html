Brugmansia parviflora

Brugmansia parviflora, commonly known as the little flower brugmansia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 6 meters in height under ideal conditions. It is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is known for its fragrant, showy blooms.

Brugmansia parviflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique floral structure.
