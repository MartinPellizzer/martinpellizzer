Begonia grandis var. simsii

Begonia grandis var. simsii, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range from deep green to dark red, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
