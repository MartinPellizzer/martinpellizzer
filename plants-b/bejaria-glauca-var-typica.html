Bejaria glauca var. typica

Bejaria glauca var. typica, commonly known as the blue bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
