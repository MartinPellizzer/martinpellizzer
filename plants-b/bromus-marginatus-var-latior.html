Bromus marginatus var. latior

Bromus marginatus var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
