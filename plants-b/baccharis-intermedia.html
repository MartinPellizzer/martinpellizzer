Baccharis intermedia

Baccharis intermedia, commonly known as Brazilian tea or baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and scrublands. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
