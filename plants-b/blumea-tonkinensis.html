Blumea tonkinensis

Blumea tonkinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Blumea tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some Asian cultures.
