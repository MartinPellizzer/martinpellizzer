Bartlettina luxii

Bartlettina luxii, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a faintly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Bartlettina luxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
