Boronia crenulata

Boronia crenulata, commonly known as the serrated boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are often serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces small, rounded fruits.

Boronia crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
