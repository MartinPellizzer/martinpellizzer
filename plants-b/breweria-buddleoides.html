Breweria buddleoides

Breweria buddleoides, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
