Bidens chippii

Bidens chippii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Bidens chippii is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
