Begonia bruneiana subsp. retakensis

Begonia bruneiana subsp.

retakensis, commonly known as the Bornean begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Borneo. It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a combination of green, red, and purple hues.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
