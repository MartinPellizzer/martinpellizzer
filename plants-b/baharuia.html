Baharuia

Baharuia, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Miao people, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitats.
