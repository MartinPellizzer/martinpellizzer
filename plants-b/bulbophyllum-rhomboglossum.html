Bulbophyllum rhomboglossum

Bulbophyllum rhomboglossum, commonly known as the rhomboid-tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, rhomboid-shaped lip that resembles a tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its distinctive floral morphology.
