Breonadia salicina var. galpinii

Breonadia salicina var. galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's yellowwood, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its yellowish bark and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
