Bunium bulbocastanum var. genuinum

Bunium bulbocastanum var. genuinum, commonly known as the true garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hollow, and cylindrical leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its bulbous roots are similar to garlic and are also used in cooking.

This variety is distinguished by its strong garlic-like scent and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
