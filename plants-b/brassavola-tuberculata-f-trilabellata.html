Brassavola tuberculata f. trilabellata

Brassavola tuberculata f. trilabellata, commonly known as the Trilabellate Orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with three prominent labella, giving it its specific epithet. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.
