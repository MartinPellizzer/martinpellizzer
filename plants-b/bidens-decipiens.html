Bidens decipiens

Bidens decipiens, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent white markings. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Bidens decipiens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
