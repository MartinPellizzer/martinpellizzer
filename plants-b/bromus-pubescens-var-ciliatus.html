Bromus pubescens var. ciliatus

Bromus pubescens var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with hairy leaves that have a distinctive ciliate margin. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single floret.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
