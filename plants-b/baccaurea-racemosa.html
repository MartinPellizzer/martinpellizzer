Baccaurea racemosa

Baccaurea racemosa, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
