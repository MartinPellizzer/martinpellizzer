Botrychium lunarioides

Botrychium lunarioides, commonly known as the moonwort, is a small fern with distinctive botanical features.

It is a member of the Botrychiaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphology, including a single, spore-bearing leaf called a sorus. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually divided into two parts, with the lower portion resembling a heart shape.

Botrychium lunarioides is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
