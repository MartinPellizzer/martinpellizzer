Bambusa vinhphuensis

Bambusa vinhphuensis, commonly known as the Vinh Phu bamboo, is a species native to Vietnam.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hollow stems. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil, making it useful in erosion control. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sustainable use in construction and crafts.
