Bersama maxima

Bersama maxima, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rounded, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has a showy, white to pink flower that blooms at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies.

Bersama maxima plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water life.
