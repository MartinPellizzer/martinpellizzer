Brassica somalensis

Brassica somalensis, commonly known as Somali mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. It typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Brassica somalensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and nutritional value.
