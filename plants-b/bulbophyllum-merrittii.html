Bulbophyllum merrittii

Bulbophyllum merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and delicate, translucent leaves that help it thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of white and green, which are adapted for specific pollinators. Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and faces threats from habitat loss and illegal collection.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this rare botanical gem.
