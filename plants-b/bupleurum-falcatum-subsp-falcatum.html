Bupleurum falcatum subsp. falcatum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

falcatum, commonly known as the creeping bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.
