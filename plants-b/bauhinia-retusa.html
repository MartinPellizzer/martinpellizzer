Bauhinia retusa

Bauhinia retusa, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

Bauhinia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
