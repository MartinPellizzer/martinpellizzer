Begonia flagellaris

Begonia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Begonia flagellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
