Bellevalia fallax var. maura

Bellevalia fallax var. maura, commonly known as the Maura bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
