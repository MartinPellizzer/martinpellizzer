Brickellia brachyphylla

Brickellia brachyphylla, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brickellia brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
