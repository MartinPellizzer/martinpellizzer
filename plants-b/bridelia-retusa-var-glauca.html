Bridelia retusa var. glauca

Bridelia retusa var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
