Byttneria glabra

Byttneria glabra, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
