Bertolonia hirsutissima

Bertolonia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bertolonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Bertolonia hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
