Brassica nantouensis

Brassica nantouensis, commonly known as Nantou mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern China, particularly the Nantou region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for agricultural use.
