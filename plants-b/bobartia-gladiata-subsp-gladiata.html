Bobartia gladiata subsp. gladiata

Bobartia gladiata subsp.

gladiata, commonly known as the gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to survive in various soil types. Its flowers are often cultivated for ornamental purposes, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun.

Botanically, it belongs to the Iridaceae family and is closely related to other genera such as Crocus and Gladiola.
