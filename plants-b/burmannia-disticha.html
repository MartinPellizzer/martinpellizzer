Burmannia disticha

Burmannia disticha, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring small, white, waxy flowers that resemble ghostly faces. The plant has a short, erect stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Burmannia disticha is also notable for its ability to produce a sticky, sugary substance from its leaves, which can attract various insects.
