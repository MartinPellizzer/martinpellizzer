Besseya gooddingii

Besseya gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Besseya gooddingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
