Brassica napus var. rapifera

Brassica napus var. rapifera, commonly known as field mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or serrated.

It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It has a taproot system that can become quite extensive, allowing it to survive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a significant amount of biomass, making it a common weed in many regions.
