Barbacenia ignea

Barbacenia ignea, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

Barbacenia ignea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
