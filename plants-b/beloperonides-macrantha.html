Beloperonides macrantha

Beloperonides macrantha, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. It grows up to 1.5 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Beloperonideae subfamily.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
