Beaumontia grandiflora

Beaumontia grandiflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Beaumontia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
