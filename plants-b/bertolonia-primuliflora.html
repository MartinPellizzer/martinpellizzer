Bertolonia primuliflora

Bertolonia primuliflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Bertolonia primuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
