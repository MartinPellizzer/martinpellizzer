Bistorta officinalis subsp. pacifica

Bistorta officinalis subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as Pacific bistort, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color and covered with bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.
