Baccharis oppositifolia

Baccharis oppositifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental plant in some regions.
