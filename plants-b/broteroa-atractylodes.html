Broteroa atractylodes

Broteroa atractylodes, commonly known as the Chinese yam or wild yam, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with lanceolate-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its use in treating inflammation and hormonal imbalances.
