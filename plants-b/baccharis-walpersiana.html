Baccharis walpersiana

Baccharis walpersiana, commonly known as sandbar plume, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves and fluffy, white flower clusters that appear in the fall. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along riverbanks and coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
