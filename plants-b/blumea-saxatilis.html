Blumea saxatilis

Blumea saxatilis, commonly known as rock blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Blumea saxatilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
