Bauhinia brachycarpa var. cavaleriei

Bauhinia brachycarpa var. cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
