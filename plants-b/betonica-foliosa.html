Betonica foliosa

Betonica foliosa, commonly known as broad-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Betonica foliosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
