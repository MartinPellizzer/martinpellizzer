Byttneria fluvialis

Byttneria fluvialis, commonly known as the river byttneria, is a woody shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and swamps, adapting to moist, humid environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish or yellowish in color, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
