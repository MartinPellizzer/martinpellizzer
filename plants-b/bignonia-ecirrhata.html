Bignonia ecirrhata

Bignonia ecirrhata, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Bignonia ecirrhata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
