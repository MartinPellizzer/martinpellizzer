Banksia drupacea

Banksia drupacea, commonly known as the golden banksia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Banksia drupacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
