Bumelia cruegeri

Bumelia cruegeri, commonly known as Cruger's bumelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Bumelia cruegeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
