Bombax longiflorum var. emarginatum

Bombax longiflorum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the Emarginate Silk Cotton Tree, is a variant of the silk cotton tree characterized by its distinctive leaf shape with notched margins.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is known for its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a defining feature of the Bombax genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and provide dense shade, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical gardens.
