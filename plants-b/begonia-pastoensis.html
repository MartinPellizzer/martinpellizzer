Begonia pastoensis

Begonia pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
