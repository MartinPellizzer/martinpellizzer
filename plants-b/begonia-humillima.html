Begonia humillima

Begonia humillima, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically during the rainy season. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
