Bellevalia comosa

Bellevalia comosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Bellevalia comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
