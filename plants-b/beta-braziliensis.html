Beta braziliensis

Beta braziliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves and has a taproot system that can reach considerable depths. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

Beta braziliensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
