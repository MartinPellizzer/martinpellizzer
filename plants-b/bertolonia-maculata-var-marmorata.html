Bertolonia maculata var. marmorata

Bertolonia maculata var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled bertolonia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display a marbled pattern of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
