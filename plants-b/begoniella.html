Begoniella

Begoniella, commonly known as the miniature begonia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Begoniaceae.

It consists of a single species, Begoniella minor, which is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, Begoniella is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
