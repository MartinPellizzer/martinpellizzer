Begonia piurensis

Begonia piurensis, commonly known as the Piura begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can produce flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at moderate elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
