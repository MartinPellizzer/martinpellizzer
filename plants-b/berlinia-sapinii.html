Berlinia sapinii

Berlinia sapinii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flattened, kidney-shaped seeds.

Berlinia sapinii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
