Brachyscome diversifolia var. humilis

Brachyscome diversifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bluebells, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Australia.

It features small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
