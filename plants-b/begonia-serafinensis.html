Begonia serafinensis

Begonia serafinensis, commonly known as the Serafin's begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
