Begonia montana

Begonia montana, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fleshy leaves. The plant typically blooms with showy, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It prefers cool, humid environments and well-draining soil.

Begonia montana is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
