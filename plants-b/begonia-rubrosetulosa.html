Begonia rubrosetulosa

Begonia rubrosetulosa, commonly known as the red-bristled begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, reddish hairs. The plant produces vibrant red flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
