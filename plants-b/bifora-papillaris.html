Bifora papillaris

Bifora papillaris, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Bifora papillaris is known for its toxic properties, containing solanine and other alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
