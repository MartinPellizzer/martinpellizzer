Begonia froebelii

Begonia froebelii, commonly known as the frost's begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and deep green hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
