Burchardia monantha

Burchardia monantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Burchardia monantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
