Bellis scaposa

Bellis scaposa, commonly known as the scapose daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-hairy leaves and stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces ray florets that are typically shorter than the central disk florets, giving it a unique appearance. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Bellis scaposa is a short-lived perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
