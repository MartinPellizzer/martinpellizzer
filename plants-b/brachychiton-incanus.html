Brachychiton incanus

Brachychiton incanus, commonly known as the curly willow or the velvet tree, is a distinctive plant native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height. Its most notable feature is its unique, curling leaves that resemble the shape of a willow. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.
