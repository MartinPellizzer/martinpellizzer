Bougainvillea pomacea

Bougainvillea pomacea, commonly known as the pink princess, is a striking flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that range from pink to red, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, white, four-petaled flowers inside the bracts. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to flourish.

Bougainvillea pomacea is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
