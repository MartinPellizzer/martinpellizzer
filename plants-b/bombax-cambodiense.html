Bombax cambodiense

Bombax cambodiense, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in white or pink hues. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
