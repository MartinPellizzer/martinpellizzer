Briza tandilensis

Briza tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
