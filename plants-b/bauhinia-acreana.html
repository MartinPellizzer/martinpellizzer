Bauhinia acreana

Bauhinia acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble the spread of a peacock's tail. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom throughout the year. Bauhinia acreana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability.
