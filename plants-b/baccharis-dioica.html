Baccharis dioica

Baccharis dioica, commonly known as skunkbrush, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that release a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Baccharis dioica thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
