Bidens pilosa var. subbiternata

Bidens pilosa var. subbiternata, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, hairy leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along waterways.

Its seeds are dispersed by attachment to animals or clothing, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.
