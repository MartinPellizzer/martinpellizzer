Boronia capitata

Boronia capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. Its flowers are borne on a terminal spike, and the plant is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Boronia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species for local pollinators.
