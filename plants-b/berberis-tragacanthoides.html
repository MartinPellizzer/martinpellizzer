Berberis tragacanthoides

Berberis tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
