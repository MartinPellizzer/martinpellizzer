Brassica rapa var. sativa

Brassica rapa var. sativa, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, leafy stems and compact, cabbage-like heads that form in the second year of growth. The plant is native to East Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassica rapa var. sativa is a versatile crop used in various cuisines and is also valued for its nutritional content.
