Betula occidentalis subsp. inopina

Betula occidentalis subsp.

inopina, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a subspecies native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and orange beneath.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
