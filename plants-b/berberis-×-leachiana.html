Berberis × leachiana

Berberis × leachiana, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from various Berberis species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and vibrant yellow foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
