Bambos arundo

Bambos arundo, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and marshes.

Bambos arundo is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
