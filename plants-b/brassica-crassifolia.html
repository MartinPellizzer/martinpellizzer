Brassica crassifolia

Brassica crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, broad leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
