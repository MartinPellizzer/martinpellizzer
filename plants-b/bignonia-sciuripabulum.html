Bignonia sciuripabulum

Bignonia sciuripabulum, commonly known as the squirrel bean, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia sciuripabulum is also known for its edible pods, which are used in traditional cuisine.
