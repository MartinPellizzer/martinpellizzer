Begonia fischeri var. brevipilosa

Begonia fischeri var. brevipilosa, commonly known as the short-haired begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
