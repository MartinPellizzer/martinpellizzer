Belladona

Belladona, commonly known as deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves and produces bell-shaped, violet or white flowers. Its berries, which are bright red and attractive to birds, contain high concentrations of toxic alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine.

Due to its potent toxins, it has a long history of both medicinal and lethal use.
