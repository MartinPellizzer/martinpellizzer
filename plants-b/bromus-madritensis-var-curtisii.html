Bromus madritensis var. curtisii

Bromus madritensis var. curtisii, commonly known as the Madrid grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
