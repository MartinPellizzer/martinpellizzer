Brassica campestris subvar. praecox

Brassica campestris subvar. praecox, commonly known as early cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, waxy leaves and can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in a terminal raceme, which are typical of the mustard family. Its roots are fibrous and can develop a swollen, edible root known as the head, which is harvested for culinary use.

This variety is well-suited to cooler climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.
