Bignonia serratula

Bignonia serratula, commonly known as the climbing candle bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of orange-yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth.

Bignonia serratula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
