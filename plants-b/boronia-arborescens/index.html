Boronia Arborescens

Boronia tree, scientifically known as Boronia arborescens, is a unique and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. This evergreen shrub or small tree is celebrated for its vibrant, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter and early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms. The Boronia tree is often found in rocky soils and coastal heathlands, thriving in well-drained conditions. It holds cultural significance for Indigenous communities and is a symbol of resilience and beauty in the natural landscape.
