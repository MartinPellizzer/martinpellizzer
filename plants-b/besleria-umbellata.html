Besleria umbellata

Besleria umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are ovate and often have a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, warm environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
