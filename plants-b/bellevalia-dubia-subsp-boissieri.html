Bellevalia dubia subsp. boissieri

Bellevalia dubia subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
