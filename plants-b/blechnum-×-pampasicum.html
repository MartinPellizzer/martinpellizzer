Blechnum × pampasicum

Blechnum × pampasicum, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Blechnum penna and Blechnum capillipes.

It is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The hybrid nature of Blechnum × pampasicum contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability in various ecological settings.
