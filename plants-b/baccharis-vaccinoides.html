Baccharis vaccinoides

Baccharis vaccinoides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Baccharis vaccinoides is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
