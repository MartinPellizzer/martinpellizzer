Boraginella indica

Boraginella indica, commonly known as Indian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Boraginella indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
