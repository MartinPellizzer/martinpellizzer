Beguea apetala

Beguea apetala, commonly known as the flowerless begonia, is a unique species within the Begoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of showy flowers, instead featuring specialized structures called "inflorescences" that house its reproductive organs. The plant has elongated, glabrous stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized botanical specimen.
