Bromelia acaulis

Bromelia acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Bromelia acaulis is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
