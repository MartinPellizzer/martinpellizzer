Barleria ramulosa var. dispersa

Barleria ramulosa var. dispersa, commonly known as the spreading barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
