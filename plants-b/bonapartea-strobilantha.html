Bonapartea strobilantha

Bonapartea strobilantha, commonly known as the mountain pine or the Chinese pine, is a rare and unique conifer native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Bonapartea strobilantha is considered a botanically significant species in the study of conifer evolution.
