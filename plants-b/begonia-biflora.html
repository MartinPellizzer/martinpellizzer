Begonia biflora

Begonia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a desirable ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
