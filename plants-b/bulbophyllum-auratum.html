Bulbophyllum auratum

Bulbophyllum auratum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features elongated pseudobulbs and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
