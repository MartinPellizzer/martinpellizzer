Berlandiera dealbata

Berlandiera dealbata, commonly known as the whiteleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Berlandiera dealbata is often found in open woodlands, slopes, and along stream banks.
