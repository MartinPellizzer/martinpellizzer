Berberis orthobotrys var. rubicunda

Berberis orthobotrys var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, bright red flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
