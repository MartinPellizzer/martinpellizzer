Banksia arborea

Banksia arborea, commonly known as the slender banksia, is a flowering plant native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
