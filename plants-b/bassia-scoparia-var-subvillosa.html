Bassia scoparia var. subvillosa

Bassia scoparia var. subvillosa, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
