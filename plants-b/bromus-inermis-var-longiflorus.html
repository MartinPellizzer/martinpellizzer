Bromus inermis var. longiflorus

Bromus inermis var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are elongated and contain numerous small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Bromus inermis var. longiflorus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
