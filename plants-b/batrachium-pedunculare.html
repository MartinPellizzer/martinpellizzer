Batrachium pedunculare

Batrachium pedunculare, commonly known as the spurred coralroot, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

This plant is a terrestrial orchid that lacks chlorophyll and is therefore dependent on mycorrhizal fungi for its nutrition. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spike, which are often mistaken for mushrooms due to their shape and color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as pine forests and wetlands.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
