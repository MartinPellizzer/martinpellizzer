Bactris sylvatica

Bactris sylvatica, commonly known as the mountain palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family native to South America.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, oval-shaped fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. Bactris sylvatica thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural studies.
