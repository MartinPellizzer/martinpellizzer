Betonica officinalis var. major

Betonica officinalis var. major, commonly known as greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Betonica officinalis var. major has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
