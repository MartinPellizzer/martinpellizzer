Baccharis elliptica

Baccharis elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Baccharis elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
