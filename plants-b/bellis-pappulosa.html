Bellis pappulosa

Bellis pappulosa, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features rosettes of soft, woolly, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Bellis pappulosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
