Barlerites

Barlerites, commonly known as false heather, are flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

They are native to various regions across the Americas and are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plants typically produce small, colorful blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Barlerites are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Their leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to their overall low-maintenance appeal.
