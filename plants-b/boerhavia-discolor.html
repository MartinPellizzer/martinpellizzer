Boerhavia discolor

Boerhavia discolor, commonly known as the variegated blushing broom.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental use.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit, which contribute to its aesthetic appeal.
