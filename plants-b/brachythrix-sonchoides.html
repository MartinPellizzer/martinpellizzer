Brachythrix sonchoides

Brachythrix sonchoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread through wind dispersal.

This plant is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
