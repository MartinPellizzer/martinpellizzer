Boronia xanthastrum

Boronia xanthastrum, commonly known as the golden boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring aromatic, evergreen leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Boronia xanthastrum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and distinctive fragrance.
