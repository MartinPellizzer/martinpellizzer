Belvisia callifolia

Belvisia callifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false chenopod, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Belvisia callifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
