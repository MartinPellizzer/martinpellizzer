Bidens procumbens

Bidens procumbens, commonly known as yellow beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
