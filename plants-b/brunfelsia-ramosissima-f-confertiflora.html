Brunfelsia ramosissima f. confertiflora

Brunfelsia ramosissima f. confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.
