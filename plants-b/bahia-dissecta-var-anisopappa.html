Bahia dissecta var. anisopappa

Bahia dissecta var. anisopappa, commonly known as the silverleaf bahia, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
