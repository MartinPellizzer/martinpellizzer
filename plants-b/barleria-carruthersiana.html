Barleria carruthersiana

Barleria carruthersiana, commonly known as the Natal barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
