Boehmeria argentea

Boehmeria argentea, commonly known as silverleaf nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Boehmeria argentea is also valued for its fibrous stalks, which can be processed into textile fibers.
