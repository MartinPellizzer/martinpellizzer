Boottia crassifolia

Boottia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed boottia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
