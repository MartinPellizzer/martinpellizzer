Breynia harmandii

Breynia harmandii, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Breynia harmandii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
