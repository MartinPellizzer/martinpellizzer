Buchnera multicaulis

Buchnera multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Buchnera multicaulis has small, lanceolate leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
