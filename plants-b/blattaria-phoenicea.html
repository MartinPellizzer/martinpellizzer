Blattaria phoenicea

Blattaria phoenicea, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking golden-green foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features fronds that are broad and pinnately divided, with a distinctive waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
