Bulbophyllum novae-hiberniae

Bulbophyllum novae-hiberniae, commonly known as the New Ireland orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the island of New Ireland in Papua New Guinea.

This epiphytic plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with purple markings. It grows on tree branches and thrives in humid, tropical environments with high light exposure. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a striking addition to botanical collections.
