Bletia peduncularis

Bletia peduncularis, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during the winter months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, which contribute to its common name.

Bletia peduncularis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and unique blooming season.
