Betula × murrayana

Betula × murrayana, commonly known as the silver birch hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the British Isles.

It is a natural hybrid between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula pubescens (downy birch), resulting in a plant with distinctive white bark and slender branches. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in moist or wet environments.

Betula × murrayana is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
