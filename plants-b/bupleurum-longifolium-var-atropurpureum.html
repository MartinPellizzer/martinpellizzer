Bupleurum longifolium var. atropurpureum

Bupleurum longifolium var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
