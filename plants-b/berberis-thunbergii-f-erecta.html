Berberis thunbergii f. erecta

Berberis thunbergii f. erecta, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a compact, upright shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is native to eastern Asia but has been widely introduced to other regions, including North America.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
