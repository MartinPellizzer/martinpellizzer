Banisteria elegans subsp. cordata

Banisteria elegans subsp.

cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf banisteria, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
