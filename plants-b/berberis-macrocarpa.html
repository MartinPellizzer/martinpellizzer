Berberis macrocarpa

Berberis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, lance-shaped leaves, and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

Berberis macrocarpa is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
