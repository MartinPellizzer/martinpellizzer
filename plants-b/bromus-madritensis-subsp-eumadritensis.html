Bromus madritensis subsp. eumadritensis

Bromus madritensis subsp.

eumadritensis, commonly known as the Iberian brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the apex of each branch, which is typically hairy and compact. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wastelands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
