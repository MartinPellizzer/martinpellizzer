Bryophyllum daigremontianum

Bryophyllum daigremontianum, commonly known as the "Mother of Millions," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers on spikes during the spring and summer months. One of its most notable features is its ability to propagate through leaf-cuttings, where each leaf can develop into a new plant.

This makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and rapid growth.
