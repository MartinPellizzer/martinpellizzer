Begonia macrophylla

Begonia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in shaded gardens and indoor spaces.
