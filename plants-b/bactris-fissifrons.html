Bactris fissifrons

Bactris fissifrons, commonly known as the golden star palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits resembling small pineapples. Its leaves are also valued for their durability and aesthetic appeal in ornamental gardening.

Bactris fissifrons is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
