Bellis cordifolia

Bellis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf dandelion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, surrounded by finely divided, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are composed of numerous ray florets.

Bellis cordifolia is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
