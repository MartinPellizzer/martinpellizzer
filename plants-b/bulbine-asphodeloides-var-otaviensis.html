Bulbine asphodeloides var. otaviensis

Bulbine asphodeloides var. otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
