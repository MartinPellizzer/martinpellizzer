Bartschella blossfeldiana

Bartschella blossfeldiana, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has strap-like, evergreen leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements, especially during the Easter season.
