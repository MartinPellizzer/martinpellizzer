Byrsonima cydoniifolia

Byrsonima cydoniifolia, commonly known as the "soursop" or "carambola tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to a distinctive, spiky fruit resembling a star. The fruit contains a soft, white, edible pulp with a sweet and tart flavor.

It is valued for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
