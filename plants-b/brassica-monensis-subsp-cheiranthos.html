Brassica monensis subsp. cheiranthos

Brassica monensis subsp.

cheiranthos, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in temperate regions. It produces small, elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its role in agricultural systems as a cover crop and for its potential in phytoremediation.
