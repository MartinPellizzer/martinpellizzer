Bernardina eucalyx

Bernardina eucalyx, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Bernardina eucalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
