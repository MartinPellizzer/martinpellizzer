Bredia hirsuta var. rotundifolia

Bredia hirsuta var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the hairy bredia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often rounded, giving it the specific epithet *rotundifolia*, meaning "round-leaved.".

This variety is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
