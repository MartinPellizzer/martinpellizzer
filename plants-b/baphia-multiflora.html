Baphia multiflora

Baphia multiflora, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and dense canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
