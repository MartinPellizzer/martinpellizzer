Boisduvalia macrantha

Boisduvalia macrantha, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible and have a sweet, peppery flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions of its native habitat.
