Bidens acrifolia var. acrifolia

Bidens acrifolia var. acrifolia, commonly known as the prickly beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, hairy leaves that are often serrated. The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. Its seeds are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
