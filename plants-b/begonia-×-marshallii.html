Begonia × marshallii

Begonia × marshallii, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
