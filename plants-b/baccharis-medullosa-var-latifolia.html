Baccharis medullosa var. latifolia

Baccharis medullosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, narrow to broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it useful in ecological restoration projects.
