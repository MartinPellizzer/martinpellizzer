Bystropogon

Bystropogon, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Bystropogon species are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments. They have a fibrous root system and produce flowers on short stalks emerging from the base of the leaves.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
