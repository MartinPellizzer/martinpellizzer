Bacopa peduncularis

Bacopa peduncularis, commonly known as the slender water bead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.

Bacopa peduncularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic environments.
