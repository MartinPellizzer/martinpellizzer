Brassica rapa convar. parachinensis

Brassica rapa convar. parachinensis, commonly known as field mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It produces elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.
