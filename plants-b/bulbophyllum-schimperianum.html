Bulbophyllum schimperianum

Bulbophyllum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists alike.
