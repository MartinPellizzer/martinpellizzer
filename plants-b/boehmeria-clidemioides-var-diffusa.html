Boehmeria clidemioides var. diffusa

Boehmeria clidemioides var. diffusa, commonly known as giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, valued for their starchy, edible qualities.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
