Biscutella laevigata var. ajmasiana

Biscutella laevigata var. ajmasiana, commonly known as the Ajmasian biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptic, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
