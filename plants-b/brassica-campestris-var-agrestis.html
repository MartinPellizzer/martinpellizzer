Brassica campestris var. agrestis

Brassica campestris var. agrestis, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
