Bartonia tenella var. brachiata

Bartonia tenella var. brachiata, commonly known as the Florida basketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
