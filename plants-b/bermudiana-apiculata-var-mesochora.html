Bermudiana apiculata var. mesochora

Bermudiana apiculata var. mesochora, commonly known as the Bermuda grass variant mesochora, is a grass species native to the subtropical regions of the Caribbean and parts of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, mat-like growth habit that forms a low-growing turf. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for lawns and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
