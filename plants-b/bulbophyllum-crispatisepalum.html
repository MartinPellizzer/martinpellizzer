Bulbophyllum crispatisepalum

Bulbophyllum crispatisepalum, commonly known as the crinkled sepal orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, crinkled sepals that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This epiphytic orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
