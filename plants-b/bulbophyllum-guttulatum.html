Bulbophyllum guttulatum

Bulbophyllum guttulatum, commonly known as the spotted bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with blossoms that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
