Blechnum maxonii

Blechnum maxonii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Blechnum maxonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
