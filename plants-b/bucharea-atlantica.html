Bucharea atlantica

Bucharea atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bucharia, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and is often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
