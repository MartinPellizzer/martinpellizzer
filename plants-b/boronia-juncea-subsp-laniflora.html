Boronia juncea subsp. laniflora

Boronia juncea subsp.

laniflora, commonly known as the woolly boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy tissue, giving it a woolly appearance. It typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
