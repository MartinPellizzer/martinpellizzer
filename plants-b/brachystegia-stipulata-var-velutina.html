Brachystegia stipulata var. velutina

Brachystegia stipulata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the miombo woodlands of eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
