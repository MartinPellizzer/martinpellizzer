Bargemontia foliosa

Bargemontia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bargemont, is a rare and unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Bargemontia foliosa is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
