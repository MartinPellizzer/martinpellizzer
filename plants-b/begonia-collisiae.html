Begonia collisiae

Begonia collisiae, commonly known as the Cape York begonia, is a species native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Begonia collisiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
