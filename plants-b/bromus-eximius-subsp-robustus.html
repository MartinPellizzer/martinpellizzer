Bromus eximius subsp. robustus

Bromus eximius subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The plant has erect, slender stems with dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
