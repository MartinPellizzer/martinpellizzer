Basilicum

Basilicum, commonly known as basil, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and glossy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in clusters. Basilicum thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
