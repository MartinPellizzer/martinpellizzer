Brassica napus var. trimestris

Brassica napus var. trimestris, commonly known as the winter oilseed rape, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with large, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds and is also used in agricultural practices for crop rotation and as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
