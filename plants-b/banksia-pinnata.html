Banksia pinnata

Banksia pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody cone-like inflorescences that produce abundant yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture. The plant is adapted to fire-prone environments and can resprout from its lignified stems after bushfires.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
