Bromus erectus subsp. moellendorfianus

Bromus erectus subsp.

moellendorfianus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted inflorescence.

The plant typically reaches heights of 60 to 120 cm and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing spikelets that are often arranged in panicles.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
