Brahea prominens

Brahea prominens, commonly known as the Queen's Crown palm, is a striking species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to dry climates, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a standout in both natural and cultivated settings.

Brahea prominens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
