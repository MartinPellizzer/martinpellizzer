Bejaria discolor

Bejaria discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate and covered with a fine, powdery coating.

Bejaria discolor is adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and high temperatures.
