Baillieria pedunculosa

Baillieria pedunculosa, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters at the end of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along watercourses.

Baillieria pedunculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
