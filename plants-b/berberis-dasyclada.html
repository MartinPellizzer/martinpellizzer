Berberis dasyclada

Berberis dasyclada, commonly known as the clustered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typically growing in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
