Blechum angustius

Blechum angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf blechnum, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere, particularly in South America and New Zealand. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Blechum angustius is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
