Butia lepidotispatha

Butia lepidotispatha, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are edible and often consumed by local communities.

Butia lepidotispatha is adapted to thrive in dry, tropical climates and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
