Blitum californicum

Blitum californicum, commonly known as California mouse ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or violet, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Blitum californicum is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
