Braya pinnatifida

Braya pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnate braya, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Braya pinnatifida is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in dry, sunny environments.
