Botryanthus maximus

Botryanthus maximus, commonly known as the giant snowball plant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescences that resemble giant snowballs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
