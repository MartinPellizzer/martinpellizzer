Bulbophyllum apetalum

Bulbophyllum apetalum, commonly known as the naked-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lack of petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
