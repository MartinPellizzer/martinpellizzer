Bauhinia spicata

Bauhinia spicata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of bright orange to red flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
