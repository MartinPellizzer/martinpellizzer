Ballota nigra var. grisea

Ballota nigra var. grisea, commonly known as the gray ballota, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Ballota nigra var. grisea is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
