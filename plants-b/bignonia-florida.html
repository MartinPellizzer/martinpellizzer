Bignonia florida

Bignonia florida, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Bignonia florida is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
