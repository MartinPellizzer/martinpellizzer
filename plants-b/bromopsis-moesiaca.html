Bromopsis moesiaca

Bromopsis moesiaca, commonly known as the Moesian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
