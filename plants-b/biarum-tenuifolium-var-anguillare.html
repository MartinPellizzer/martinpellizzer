Biarum tenuifolium var. anguillare

Biarum tenuifolium var. anguillare, commonly known as the slender-leaf bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
