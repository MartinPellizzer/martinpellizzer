Baccharis brachylaenoides var. polycephala

Baccharis brachylaenoides var. polycephala, commonly known as the bluebrush baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and showy, yellowish-white inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
