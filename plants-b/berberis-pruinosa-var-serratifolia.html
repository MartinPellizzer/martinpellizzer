Berberis pruinosa var. serratifolia

Berberis pruinosa var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
