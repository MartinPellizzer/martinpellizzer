Beta purpurea

Beta purpurea, commonly known as beetroot or garden beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy taproot and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tint. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its root is the primary edible part, rich in nutrients such as iron, fiber, and antioxidants.

Beta purpurea is also cultivated for its ornamental value, with varieties displaying vibrant colors ranging from purple to white.
