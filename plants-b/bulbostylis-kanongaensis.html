Bulbostylis kanongaensis

Bulbostylis kanongaensis, commonly known as the Kanonga bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This grass-like plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and seasonally flooded soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its wetland environment.
