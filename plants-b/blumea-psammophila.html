Blumea psammophila

Blumea psammophila, commonly known as sand dune blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, nutrient-poor soils. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea psammophila plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
