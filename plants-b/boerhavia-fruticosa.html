Boerhavia fruticosa

Boerhavia fruticosa, commonly known as redroot sandplum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or subshrub with branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the species. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Boerhavia fruticosa is adapted to hot, dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
