Barbarea vulgaris f. hirsuta

Barbarea vulgaris f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is an annual species that thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily in new areas.
