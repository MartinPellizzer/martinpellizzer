Barkhausia zazinthia

Barkhausia zazinthia, commonly known as the Zazinthian bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a distinctive white or pink throat. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

Barkhausia zazinthia is well-suited to rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
