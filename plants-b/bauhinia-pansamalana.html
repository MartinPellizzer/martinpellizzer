Bauhinia pansamalana

Bauhinia pansamalana, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive, papery, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
