Buddleja cordata

Buddleja cordata, commonly known as the woolly butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Buddleja cordata is also known for its adaptability to various climates and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
