Bombax kerrii

Bombax kerrii, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the dry season. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fibers, which have traditional uses in various cultures.
