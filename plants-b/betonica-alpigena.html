Betonica alpigena

Betonica alpigena, commonly known as alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Betonica alpigena thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
