Beloglottis mexicana

Beloglottis mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for several years.
