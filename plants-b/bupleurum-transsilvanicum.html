Bupleurum transsilvanicum

Bupleurum transsilvanicum, commonly known as Transylvanian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and forest edges, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
