Bupleurum brevicaule var. trinervium

Bupleurum brevicaule var. trinervium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, trifoliate leaves with three narrow, lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
