Balanites angolensis subsp. angolensis

Balanites angolensis subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the African burr tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The bark and seeds of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
