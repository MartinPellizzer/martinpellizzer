Begonia viridiflora

Begonia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to gardens and indoor spaces.

It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded borders and container gardening.
