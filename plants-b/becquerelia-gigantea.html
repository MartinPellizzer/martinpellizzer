Becquerelia gigantea

Becquerelia gigantea, commonly known as the giant becquerelia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
