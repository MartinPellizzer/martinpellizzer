Brassica chinensis f. aichi

Brassica chinensis f. aichi, commonly known as the Aichi mustard, is a cultivated variant of the Chinese mustard plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain edible seeds used in traditional Asian cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in various regional dishes.
