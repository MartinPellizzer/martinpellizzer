Brickellia coulteri var. coulteri

Brickellia coulteri var. coulteri, commonly known as Texas brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
