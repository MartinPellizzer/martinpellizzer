Blepharoglossum fulgens

Blepharoglossum fulgens, commonly known as the shining-leaved fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
