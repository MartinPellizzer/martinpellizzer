Bassia dinteri

Bassia dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low-growing habit with erect, branched stems.

Bassia dinteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
