Banksia squarrosa subsp. squarrosa

Banksia squarrosa subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or bottle-shaped flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
