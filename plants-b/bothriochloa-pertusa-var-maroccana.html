Bothriochloa pertusa var. maroccana

Bothriochloa pertusa var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact inflorescences that produce small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry grasslands. Its fibrous root system allows it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
