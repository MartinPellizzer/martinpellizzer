Bihai angustifolia

Bihai angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed yew, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Bihai angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
