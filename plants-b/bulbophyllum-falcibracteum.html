Bulbophyllum falcibracteum

Bulbophyllum falcibracteum, commonly known as the sickle-bract orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the wet season.

The species is characterized by its unique, sickle-shaped bracts that enclose the inflorescence.
