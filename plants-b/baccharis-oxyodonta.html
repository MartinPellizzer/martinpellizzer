Baccharis oxyodonta

Baccharis oxyodonta, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Baccharis oxyodonta is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
