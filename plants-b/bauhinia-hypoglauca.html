Bauhinia hypoglauca

Bauhinia hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale blue bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Bauhinia hypoglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
