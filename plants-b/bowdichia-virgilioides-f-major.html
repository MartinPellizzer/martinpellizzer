Bowdichia virgilioides f. major

Bowdichia virgilioides f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered bowdichia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species in the flora of its native region.
