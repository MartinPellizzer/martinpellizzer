Batatas triloba

Batatas triloba, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from purple to red and green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has tuberous roots that are often cultivated for their edible qualities. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Batatas triloba is widely grown as both an ornamental plant and a food source in various parts of the world.
