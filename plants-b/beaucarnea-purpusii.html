Beaucarnea purpusii

Beaucarnea purpusii, commonly known as the ponytail palm, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, tree-like stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, fibrous leaves that resemble a ponytail, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red fruits.

Due to its low maintenance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
