Boissiera bromoides

Boissiera bromoides, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Boissiera bromoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
