Bambusa cacharensis

Bambusa cacharensis, commonly known as the Assam bamboo, is a tall, evergreen bamboo species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a diameter of about 2-3 centimeters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense bamboo forests.

Bambusa cacharensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
