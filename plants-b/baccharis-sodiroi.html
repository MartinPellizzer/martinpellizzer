Baccharis sodiroi

Baccharis sodiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish bark and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis sodiroi plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
