Bolbitis × laxireticulata

Bolbitis × laxireticulata, commonly known as the hybrid spider fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Bolbitis heudelotii and Bolbitis capensis.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy fronds that resemble those of its parent species, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, thriving in shaded, humid environments. Its fronds are usually dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Bolbitis × laxireticulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
