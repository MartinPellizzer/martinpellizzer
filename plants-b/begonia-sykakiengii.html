Begonia sykakiengii

Begonia sykakiengii, commonly known as the Kankou Begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Luzon in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage with deep green to almost black hues, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
