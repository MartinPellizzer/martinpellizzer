Blechnum lellingerianum

Blechnum lellingerianum, commonly known as the Lellinger's shield fern, is a perennial fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The fronds are evergreen, providing year-round foliage in suitable climates.
