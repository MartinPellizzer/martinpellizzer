Begonia latibracteata

Begonia latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. Its growth habit is usually upright or trailing, making it suitable for both container gardening and hanging baskets.

Begonia latibracteata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
