Botrymorus paniculata

Botrymorus paniculata, commonly known as the panicle bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, with small, tubular flowers clustered in panicles. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in clusters, making it an attractive ornamental plant.
