Begonia biliranensis

Begonia biliranensis, commonly known as the Biliran begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a rich array of colors, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
