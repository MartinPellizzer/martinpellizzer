Bidens warszewicziana var. pinnata

Bidens warszewicziana var. pinnata, commonly known as the hairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a bristly pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
