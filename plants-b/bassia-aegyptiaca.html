Bassia aegyptiaca

Bassia aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it drought-tolerant. Its small, green flowers are typically clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other economically important plants.
