Bupleurum falcatum subsp. eufalcatum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

eufalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, aromatic stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
