Begonia limminghei

Begonia limminghei, commonly known as the Limminghei begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
