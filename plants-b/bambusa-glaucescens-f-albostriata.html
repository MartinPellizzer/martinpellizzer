Bambusa glaucescens f. albostriata

Bambusa glaucescens f. albostriata, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species characterized by its distinctive white or silver streaks on the culms.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

The foliage remains lush throughout the year, providing aesthetic value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
