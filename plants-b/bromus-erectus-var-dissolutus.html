Bromus erectus var. dissolutus

Bromus erectus var. dissolutus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and the flowers are usually pale green to brown in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
