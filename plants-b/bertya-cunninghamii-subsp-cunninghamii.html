Bertya cunninghamii subsp. cunninghamii

Bertya cunninghamii subsp.

cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's bertya, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to well-drained conditions.
