Berberis verruculosa f. viridescens

Berberis verruculosa f. viridescens, commonly known as the emerald barberry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright green, oval leaves that turn yellow in the autumn, providing seasonal visual interest. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its foliage and compact growth make it a popular choice for small spaces and urban landscaping.
