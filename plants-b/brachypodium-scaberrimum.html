Brachypodium scaberrimum

Brachypodium scaberrimum, commonly known as rough bristlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bristle-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Brachypodium scaberrimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
