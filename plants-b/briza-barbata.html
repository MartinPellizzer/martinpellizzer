Briza barbata

Briza barbata, commonly known as the hairy feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear soft, silky, silver-green foliage. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance, often resembling a plume or a feather. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Briza barbata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
