Bromopsis monoclada

Bromopsis monoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, often acidic soils in wetlands and marshes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its spread across wetland habitats.

Bromopsis monoclada plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
