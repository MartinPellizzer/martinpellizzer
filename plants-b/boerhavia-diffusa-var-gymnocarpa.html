Boerhavia diffusa var. gymnocarpa

Boerhavia diffusa var. gymnocarpa, commonly known as Indian spinach or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly lobed margin, and they are usually hairy on both surfaces. The flowers are small, pale pink to white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both a weed and a useful forage crop.
