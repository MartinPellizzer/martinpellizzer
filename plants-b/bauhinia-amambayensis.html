Bauhinia amambayensis

Bauhinia amambayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its trunk is often smooth and slender. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Bauhinia amambayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
