Bulbophyllum capituliflorum

Bulbophyllum capituliflorum, commonly known as the crown-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy inflorescences with numerous tiny blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and terrariums.
