Berberis walteriana

Berberis walteriana, commonly known as Walter's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis walteriana is also noted for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of berberine in its roots and stems.
