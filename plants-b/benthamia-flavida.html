Benthamia flavida

Benthamia flavida, commonly known as the yellow benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Benthamia flavida is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
