Bromus masafueranus

Bromus masafueranus, commonly known as the Masafuera brome, is a grass species native to South America, specifically found in Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

Bromus masafueranus plays a minor ecological role in its native environment, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
