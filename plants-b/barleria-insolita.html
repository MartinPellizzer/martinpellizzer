Barleria insolita

Barleria insolita, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Barleria insolita is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
