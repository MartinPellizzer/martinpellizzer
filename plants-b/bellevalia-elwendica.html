Bellevalia elwendica

Bellevalia elwendica, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
