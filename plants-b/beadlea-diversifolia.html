Beadlea diversifolia

Beadlea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable beadlea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to specific ecological niches make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
