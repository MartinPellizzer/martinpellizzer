Bourreria setosa

Bourreria setosa, commonly known as the hairy poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and produces small, greenish flowers in the center of the bracts. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for festive decorations, especially during the holiday season.
