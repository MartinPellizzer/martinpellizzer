Begonia lomensis

Begonia lomensis, commonly known as the Lombok begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the island of Lombok in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
