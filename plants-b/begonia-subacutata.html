Begonia subacutata

Begonia subacutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a trailing or shrub-like species, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
