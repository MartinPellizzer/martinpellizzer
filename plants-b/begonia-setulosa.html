Begonia setulosa

Begonia setulosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia setulosa is widely cultivated for both its foliage and floral display.
