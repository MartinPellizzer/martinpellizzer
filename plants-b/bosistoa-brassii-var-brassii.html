Bosistoa brassii var. brassii

Bosistoa brassii var. brassii, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, brush-like clusters. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal salt spray and strong winds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Bosistoa brassii var. brassii plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
