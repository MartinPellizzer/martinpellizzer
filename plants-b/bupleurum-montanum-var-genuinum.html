Bupleurum montanum var. genuinum

Bupleurum montanum var. genuinum, commonly known as the mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features a robust root system and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or loamy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
