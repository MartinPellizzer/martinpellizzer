Bromopsis cappadocica

Bromopsis cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Bromopsis cappadocica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for use in restoration projects in similar habitats.
