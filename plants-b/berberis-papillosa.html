Berberis papillosa

Berberis papillosa, commonly known as the silky barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
