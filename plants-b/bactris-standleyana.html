Bactris standleyana

Bactris standleyana, commonly known as the purple-leafed palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that often display a striking purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. Bactris standleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
