Bothriochloa leucopogon

Bothriochloa leucopogon, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and grasslands, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional landscaping and erosion control.
