Borreria carpentariae

Borreria carpentariae, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Borreria carpentariae is also known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by serving as a host plant for their larvae.
