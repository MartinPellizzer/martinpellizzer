Bidens carvifolia

Bidens carvifolia, commonly known as roughleaf beggarweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, serrated leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its fruit is a schizocarp composed of three segments, each containing a single seed.

Bidens carvifolia is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
