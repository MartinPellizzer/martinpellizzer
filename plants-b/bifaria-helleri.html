Bifaria helleri

Bifaria helleri, commonly known as the Heller's ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often ghostly appearance, with elongated, fleshy leaves that resemble ghostly fingers. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom at night, adding to its mysterious allure. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a critically endangered species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical wonder from further decline.
