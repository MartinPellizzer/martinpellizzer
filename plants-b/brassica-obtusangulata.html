Brassica obtusangulata

Brassica obtusangulata, commonly known as the obtuse-angled mustard, is an annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect stem and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain several seeds.

Brassica obtusangulata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
