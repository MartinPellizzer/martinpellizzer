Bidens sarmentosa

Bidens sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along roadsides.

Bidens sarmentosa is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons, making it a common weed in various habitats.
