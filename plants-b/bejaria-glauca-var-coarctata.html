Bejaria glauca var. coarctata

Bejaria glauca var. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
