Belairia mucronata

Belairia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Belairia mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
