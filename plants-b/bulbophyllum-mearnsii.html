Bulbophyllum mearnsii

Bulbophyllum mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small but striking, with a complex structure that includes specialized petals and sepals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
