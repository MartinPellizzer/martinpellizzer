Boronia coerulescens var. pubescens

Boronia coerulescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant, blue or violet flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in coastal and dry sclerophyllous habitats.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
