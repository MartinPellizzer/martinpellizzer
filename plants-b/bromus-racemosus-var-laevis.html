Bromus racemosus var. laevis

Bromus racemosus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically smooth and hairless, with a bluish-green coloration. This variety is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in forage agriculture.
