Bassia blackiana

Bassia blackiana, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Bassia blackiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
