Begonia triramosa

Begonia triramosa, commonly known as the three-branched begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant colors and intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
