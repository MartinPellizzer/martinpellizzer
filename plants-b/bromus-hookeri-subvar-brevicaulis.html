Bromus hookeri subvar. brevicaulis

Bromus hookeri subvar. brevicaulis, commonly known as Hooker's brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with short, rigid stems. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
