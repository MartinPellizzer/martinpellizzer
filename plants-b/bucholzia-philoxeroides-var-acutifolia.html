Bucholzia philoxeroides var. acutifolia

Bucholzia philoxeroides var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrscrub, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny burrs that contain seeds. It is adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy component of dryland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes in suitable environments.
