Bupleurum ramosissimum var. wightii

Bupleurum ramosissimum var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
