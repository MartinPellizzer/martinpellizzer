Buddleja elegans subsp. elegans

Buddleja elegans subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.
