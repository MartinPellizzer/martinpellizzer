Berberis hookeri f. viridis

Berberis hookeri f. viridis, commonly known as the Emerald Berberis, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a striking visual contrast to its yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
