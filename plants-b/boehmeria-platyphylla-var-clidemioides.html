Boehmeria platyphylla var. clidemioides

Boehmeria platyphylla var. clidemioides, commonly known as the broadleaf Boehmeria, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
