Biscutella columnae f. depressa

Biscutella columnae f. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically forms dense mats of foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
