Bromus orcuttianus var. grandis

Bromus orcuttianus var. grandis, commonly known as the grandus brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential for use in restoration projects.
