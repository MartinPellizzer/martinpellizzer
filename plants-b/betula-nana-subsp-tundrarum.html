Betula nana subsp. tundrarum

Betula nana subsp.

tundrarum, commonly known as the Arctic birch, is a small shrub native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, often prostrate branches.

The leaves are small, ovate, and deciduous, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is an important component of tundra vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various arctic organisms.
