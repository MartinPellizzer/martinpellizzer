Baliospermum malayanum

Baliospermum malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds via wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.

Baliospermum malayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
