Bulbophyllum intricatum

Bulbophyllum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, intricate floral structures with vividly colored petals and a unique, often fragrant scent. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
