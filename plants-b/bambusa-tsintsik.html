Bambusa tsintsik

Bambusa tsintsik, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It prefers well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Bambusa tsintsik is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo poles.
