Bambusa textilis f. purpurascens

Bambusa textilis f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-brown culms, which contrast sharply with its dark green leaves. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread and form thickets in suitable conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
