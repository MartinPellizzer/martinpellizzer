Bulbophyllum psittacoglossum

Bulbophyllum psittacoglossum, commonly known as the parrot's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, colorful flowers that resemble a parrot's beak. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical and feature intricate patterns of purple, yellow, and green.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
