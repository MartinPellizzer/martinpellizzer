Brahea armata

Brahea armata, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a striking evergreen tree native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-gray, fibrous leaf sheaths and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant has a distinctive, slender trunk that often branches near the base, forming a dense, rounded canopy. Its leaves are long, feathery, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brahea armata is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
