Blumea veronicifolia

Blumea veronicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea veronicifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
