Bolivicactus columnaris

Bolivicactus columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a ribbed structure with spines that provide protection from herbivores. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

Bolivicactus columnaris is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
