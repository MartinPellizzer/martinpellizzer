Bellis longifolia

Bellis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive central yellow disc, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Bellis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
