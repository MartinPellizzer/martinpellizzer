Banksia pteridifolia

Banksia pteridifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf banksia, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant golden-yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often resemble fern fronds, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
