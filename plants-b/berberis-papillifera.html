Berberis papillifera

Berberis papillifera, commonly known as the prickly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
