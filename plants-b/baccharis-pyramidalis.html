Baccharis pyramidalis

Baccharis pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and sandy soils. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall.

Baccharis pyramidalis is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
