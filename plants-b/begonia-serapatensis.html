Begonia serapatensis

Begonia serapatensis, commonly known as the Serapat begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and showy, vibrant flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not widely cultivated outside its native range.
