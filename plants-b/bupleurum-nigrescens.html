Bupleurum nigrescens

Bupleurum nigrescens, commonly known as blackroot, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its root system is notable for its dark, almost black color, which gives the plant its common name.

Bupleurum nigrescens is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
