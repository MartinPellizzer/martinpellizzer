Blysmus compressus var. dissitus

Blysmus compressus var. dissitus, commonly known as the tufted grass or mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
