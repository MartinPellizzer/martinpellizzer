Brunia macrocephala

Brunia macrocephala, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a bushy growth habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Brunia macrocephala is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
