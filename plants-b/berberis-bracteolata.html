Berberis bracteolata

Berberis bracteolata, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features stout, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Berberis bracteolata is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
