Bauhinia ungulata var. parvifolia

Bauhinia ungulata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bean, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
