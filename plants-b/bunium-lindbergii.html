Bunium lindbergii

Bunium lindbergii, commonly known as the Kashmir mountain fennel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,500 meters. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, spicy fragrance, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
