Babiana cuneata

Babiana cuneata, commonly known as the rainstorm babiana, is a South African flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a rainstorm, hence its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Babiana cuneata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
