Bulbophyllum pecten-veneris

Bulbophyllum pecten-veneris, commonly known as the Venus' comb orchid, is a fascinating orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, comb-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
