Bahia pedata

Bahia pedata, commonly known as the Mexican hat or velvet tassel plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-filled structures that resemble tassels, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial.
