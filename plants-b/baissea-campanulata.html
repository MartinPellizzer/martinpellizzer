Baissea campanulata

Baissea campanulata, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Baissea campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
