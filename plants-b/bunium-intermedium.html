Bunium intermedium

Bunium intermedium, commonly known as the European fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features a slender, branched stem and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flower heads consist of small, yellowish-green umbels, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The essential oils found in its leaves and seeds have been used for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
