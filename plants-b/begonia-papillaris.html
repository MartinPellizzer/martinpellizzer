Begonia papillaris

Begonia papillaris, commonly known as the papillate begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papillate (bumpy) leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Begonia papillaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
