Brassica campestris subsp. oleifera

Brassica campestris subsp.

oleifera, commonly known as field mustard or rapeseed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves, and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called siliques, which contain numerous seeds. It is widely cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in cooking and industrial applications.

This species is also valued for its role in crop rotation and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature.
