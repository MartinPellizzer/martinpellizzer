Brachypodium abbreviatum

Brachypodium abbreviatum, commonly known as little barley, is a small annual grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its resilience and rapid growth, it is sometimes considered a weed but is also valued for its use in ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics.
