Begonia hispidissima

Begonia hispidissima, commonly known as the roughleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
