Baccharis angusticeps

Baccharis angusticeps, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. It is drought-tolerant and has a woody stem that can grow up to several feet tall.

Baccharis angusticeps plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
