Bulbostylis parva

Bulbostylis parva, commonly known as the small bulbostylis, is a small grass-like plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Bulbostylis parva is often found in wet or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
