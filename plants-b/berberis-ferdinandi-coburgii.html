Berberis ferdinandi-coburgii

Berberis ferdinandi-coburgii, commonly known as Coburg barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for hedges and rock gardens in temperate climates.
