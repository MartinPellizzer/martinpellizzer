Bathmium seemannii

Bathmium seemannii, commonly known as the Cape mountain gum, is a unique evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal uses.

Bathmium seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
