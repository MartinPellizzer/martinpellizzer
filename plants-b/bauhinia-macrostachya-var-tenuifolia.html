Bauhinia macrostachya var. tenuifolia

Bauhinia macrostachya var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, papilionaceous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, and is often found in disturbed soils and open woodlands. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
