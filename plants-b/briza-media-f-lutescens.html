Briza media f. lutescens

Briza media f. lutescens, commonly known as the golden false beardgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that bear dense clusters of small, yellowish-green to golden flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
