Bupleurum dalhousianum

Bupleurum dalhousianum, commonly known as the Dalhousie angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Bupleurum dalhousianum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
