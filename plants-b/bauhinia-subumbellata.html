Bauhinia subumbellata

Bauhinia subumbellata, commonly known as the "pink shower tree," is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species, contributing to soil fertility. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, especially for its showy inflorescences.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
