Burlingtonia decora var. picta

Burlingtonia decora var. picta, commonly known as the painted hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns resembling paint splashes. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
