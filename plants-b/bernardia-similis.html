Bernardia similis

Bernardia similis, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its roots are also valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Bernardia similis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
