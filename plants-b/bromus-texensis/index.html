Bromus Texensis

Bromus texensis is a native grass species found primarily in the southwestern United States. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. This plant is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions and high temperatures. Its slender blades and compact growth habit make it an important component of local ecosystems. Despite its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
