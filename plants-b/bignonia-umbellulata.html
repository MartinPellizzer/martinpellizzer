Bignonia umbellulata

Bignonia umbellulata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 15 feet in length. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
