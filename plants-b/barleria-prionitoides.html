Barleria prionitoides

Barleria prionitoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria prionitoides is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
