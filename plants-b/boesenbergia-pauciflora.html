Boesenbergia pauciflora

Boesenbergia pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser ginger or narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Boesenbergia pauciflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal potential.
