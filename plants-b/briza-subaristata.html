Briza subaristata

Briza subaristata, commonly known as the slender quaking grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, zigzagging stems and feathery plumes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its slender leaves and light, airy appearance make it a distinctive feature in its native environment.

Briza subaristata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful, swaying form.
