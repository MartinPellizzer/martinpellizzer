Balanites rotundifolia var. scillin

Balanites rotundifolia var. scillin, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow, edible fruits that contain a high amount of oil. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is valued for its nutritional content.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for wildlife.
