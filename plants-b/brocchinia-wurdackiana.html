Brocchinia wurdackiana

Brocchinia wurdackiana, commonly known as the Wurdack's brocchinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Ecuador and is characterized by its striking, urn-shaped rosette of leaves that form a water-holding cavity. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, which helps in water retention in its humid, shaded habitat. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Brocchinia wurdackiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
