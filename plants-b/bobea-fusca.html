Bobea fusca

Bobea fusca, commonly known as the red-stemmed legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Bobea fusca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
