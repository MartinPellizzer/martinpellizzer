Billbergia × collevii

Billbergia × collevii, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia recurvata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in height. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which can last for several months.

Billbergia × collevii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
