Bocagea virgata

Bocagea virgata, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves and showy clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bocagea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
