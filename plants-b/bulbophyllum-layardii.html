Bulbophyllum layardii

Bulbophyllum layardii, commonly known as the Layard's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum layardii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
