Boisduvalia glabella var. campestris

Boisduvalia glabella var. campestris, commonly known as the creeping boisduvalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
