Borreria involucrata

Borreria involucrata, commonly known as the Mexican blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, featuring succulent stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Borreria involucrata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
