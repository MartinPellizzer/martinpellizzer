Burmeistera reclinata

Burmeistera reclinata, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Burmeistera reclinata is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative, easy-to-care-for plant.
