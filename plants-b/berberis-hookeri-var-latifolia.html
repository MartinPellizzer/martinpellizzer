Berberis hookeri var. latifolia

Berberis hookeri var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
