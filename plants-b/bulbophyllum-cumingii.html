Bulbophyllum cumingii

Bulbophyllum cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure, featuring showy petals and a distinctive lip. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
