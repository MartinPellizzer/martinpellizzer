Beta vulgaris convar. cicla

Beta vulgaris convar. cicla, commonly known as beetroot or garden beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy taproot and broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green in color. The plant is cultivated for its edible root, which is rich in nutrients such as iron, fiber, and antioxidants. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, and it requires a long growing season to mature.

The leaves are also edible and can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.
