Bulbophyllum rheedei

Bulbophyllum rheedei, commonly known as the Rhododendron orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens and greenhouses.
