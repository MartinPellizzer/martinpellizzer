Bidens pilosa f. triaristata

Bidens pilosa f. triaristata, commonly known as the triaristate beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant produces small, linear seeds that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
