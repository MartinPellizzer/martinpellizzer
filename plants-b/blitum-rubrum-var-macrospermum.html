Blitum rubrum var. macrospermum

Blitum rubrum var. macrospermum, commonly known as the broadleaf mountain-mint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
