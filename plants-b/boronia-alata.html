Boronia alata

Boronia alata, commonly known as the silver-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous vegetation, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boronia alata is known for its showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.
