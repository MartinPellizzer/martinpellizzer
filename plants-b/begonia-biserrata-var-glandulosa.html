Begonia biserrata var. glandulosa

Begonia biserrata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leaved begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, glandular leaves that give it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming throughout the year. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
