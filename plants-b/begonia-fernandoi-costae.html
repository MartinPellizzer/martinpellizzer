Begonia fernandoi-costae

Begonia fernandoi-costae, commonly known as the Fernando's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a rich, glossy green color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
