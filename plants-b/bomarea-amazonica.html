Bomarea amazonica

Bomarea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has tubular, spirally arranged leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along the forest floor.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
