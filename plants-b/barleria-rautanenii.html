Barleria rautanenii

Barleria rautanenii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Barleria rautanenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
