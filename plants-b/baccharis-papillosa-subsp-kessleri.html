Baccharis papillosa subsp. kessleri

Baccharis papillosa subsp.

kessleri, commonly known as Kessler's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
