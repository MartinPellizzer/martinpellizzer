Barleria involucrata var. involucrata

Barleria involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
