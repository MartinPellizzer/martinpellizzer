Baptisia bracteata var. leucophaea

Baptisia bracteata var. leucophaea, commonly known as the white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to white flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to seven oval leaflets and produces cylindrical, spikelike flower clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry to mesic soils, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also benefits surrounding vegetation.
