Bridelia pustulata

Bridelia pustulata, commonly known as the blister bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries that are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is noted for its ability to withstand drought and salt spray, making it adaptable to harsh conditions.
