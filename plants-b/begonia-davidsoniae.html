Begonia davidsoniae

Begonia davidsoniae, commonly known as the Davidson's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
