Bromus rigens subsp. macrantherus

Bromus rigens subsp.

macrantherus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are arranged in a panicle structure.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soils.
