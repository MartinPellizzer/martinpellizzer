Barkhausia versicolor

Barkhausia versicolor, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces long, slender stems with showy, tubular flowers that come in various shades of pink, red, and orange. Barkhausia versicolor thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
