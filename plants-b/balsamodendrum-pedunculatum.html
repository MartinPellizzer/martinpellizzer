Balsamodendrum pedunculatum

Balsamodendrum pedunculatum, commonly known as the red balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a waxy texture, while its stems are slender and branched.

Balsamodendrum pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
