Boscia octandra

Boscia octandra, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Boscia octandra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
