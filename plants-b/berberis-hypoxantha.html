Berberis hypoxantha

Berberis hypoxantha, commonly known as the rock barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Berberis hypoxantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
