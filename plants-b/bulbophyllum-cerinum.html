Bulbophyllum cerinum

Bulbophyllum cerinum, commonly known as the waxen orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often colorful pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum cerinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
