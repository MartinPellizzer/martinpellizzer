Bromus rechingeri

Bromus rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and dry meadows. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus rechingeri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with low nutrient availability.
