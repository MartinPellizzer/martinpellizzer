Bactris geonomoides

Bactris geonomoides, commonly known as the purple-leafed palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small coconuts. Its leaves are used in traditional thatching and basket-making, while the fruits are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Bactris geonomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
