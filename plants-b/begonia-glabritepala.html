Begonia glabritepala

Begonia glabritepala, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves that are often a deep green or reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia glabritepala is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
