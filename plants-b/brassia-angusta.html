Brassia angusta

Brassia angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, brightly colored blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which appear during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern, and it prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity.

Brassia angusta is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
