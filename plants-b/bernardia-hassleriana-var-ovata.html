Bernardia hassleriana var. ovata

Bernardia hassleriana var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf bernardia, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for gardens and conservation efforts.
