Blechnum decorum var. dilatatum

Blechnum decorum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often glossy fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with narrow, lance-shaped segments that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
