Brickellia extranea

Brickellia extranea, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes.

Brickellia extranea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
