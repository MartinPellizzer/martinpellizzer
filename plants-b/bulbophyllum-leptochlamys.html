Bulbophyllum leptochlamys

Bulbophyllum leptochlamys, commonly known as the slender-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
