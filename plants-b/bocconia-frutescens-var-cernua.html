Bocconia frutescens var. cernua

Bocconia frutescens var. cernua, commonly known as the weeping bocconia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang in drooping clusters. The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add visual interest in woodland or container gardens.
