Boopis caespitosa

Boopis caespitosa, commonly known as bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
