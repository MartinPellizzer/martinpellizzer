Blechnum caudatum

Blechnum caudatum, commonly known as the clubmoss fern, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that often have a distinctive, segmented appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its rhizomes are often thick and fleshy, aiding in water storage and nutrient absorption.

Blechnum caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
