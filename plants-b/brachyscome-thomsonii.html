Brachyscome thomsonii

Brachyscome thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's dwarf daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, surrounded by a ring of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Brachyscome thomsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
