Betula humilis f. crispa

Betula humilis f. crispa, commonly known as the curly birch, is a unique variant of the dwarf birch species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and curled leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant is native to northern Europe and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of around 1 to 2 meters.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
