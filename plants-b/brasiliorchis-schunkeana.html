Brasiliorchis schunkeana

Brasiliorchis schunkeana, commonly known as the Schunke's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, spurred flowers that typically display a combination of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, greenish-brown pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche within the forest ecosystem.

Due to habitat destruction, it is classified as critically endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
