Brasilicactus

Brasilicactus, commonly known as the Brazilian cactus, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. Many species within this genus produce vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.

They thrive in well-draining soil and full sun, making them suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
