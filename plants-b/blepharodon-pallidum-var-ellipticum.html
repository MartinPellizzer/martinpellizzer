Blepharodon pallidum var. ellipticum

Blepharodon pallidum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
