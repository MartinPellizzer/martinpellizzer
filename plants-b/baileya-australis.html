Baileya australis

Baileya australis, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and a sturdy, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Baileya australis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species well-suited for xeriscaping.
