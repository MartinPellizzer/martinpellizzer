Banksia dallanneyi subsp. pollosta

Banksia dallanneyi subsp.

pollosta, commonly known as the Geraldton banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody cones that are typically yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

This subspecies is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.
