Barleria lupulina

Barleria lupulina, commonly known as yellow ballweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Barleria lupulina is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
