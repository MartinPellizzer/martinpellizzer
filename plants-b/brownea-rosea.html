Brownea rosea

Brownea rosea, commonly known as the pink torch tree, is a striking flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that resemble torches. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
