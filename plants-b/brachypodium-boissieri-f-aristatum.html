Brachypodium boissieri f. aristatum

Brachypodium boissieri f. aristatum, commonly known as the aristate brachypodium, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant is characterized by its distinctive aristate (bristle-tipped) lemma, which gives it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
