Baccharis pluricapitulata

Baccharis pluricapitulata, commonly known as Brazilian eryngo, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
