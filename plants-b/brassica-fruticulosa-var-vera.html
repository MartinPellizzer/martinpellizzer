Brassica fruticulosa var. vera

Brassica fruticulosa var. vera, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
