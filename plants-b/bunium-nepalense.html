Bunium nepalense

Bunium nepalense, commonly known as Nepal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are hairy and have a strong, pungent odor, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Bunium nepalense is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, moist conditions.
