Brachythrix lugarensis

Brachythrix lugarensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant features tiny, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Brachythrix lugarensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
