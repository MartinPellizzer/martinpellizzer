Bistorta majanthemifolia

Bistorta majanthemifolia, commonly known as the alpine bistort, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique leaf structure and flowering habits. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous stem that often produces dense clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a waxy layer, helping it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Bistorta majanthemifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
