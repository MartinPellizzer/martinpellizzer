Begonia patula

Begonia patula, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia patula is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
