Baeckea imbricata var. typica

Baeckea imbricata var. typica, commonly known as the Coastal Baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, thriving in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
