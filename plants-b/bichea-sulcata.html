Bichea sulcata

Bichea sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments. Bichea sulcata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to climb or trail.

It prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
