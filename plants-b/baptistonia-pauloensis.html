Baptistonia pauloensis

Baptistonia pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may be floating or emergent.

Baptistonia pauloensis plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic environments.
