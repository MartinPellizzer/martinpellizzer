Bunium capillifolium

Bunium capillifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often fern-like foliage. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance, while its umbel-shaped flowers are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in rocky or arid soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
