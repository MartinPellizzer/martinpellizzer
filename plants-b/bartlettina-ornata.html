Bartlettina ornata

Bartlettina ornata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Bartlettina ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
