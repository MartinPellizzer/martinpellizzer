Bulbophyllum paucisetum

Bulbophyllum paucisetum, commonly known as the rare orchid of the Bulbophyllum genus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
