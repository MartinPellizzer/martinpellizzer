Bignonia brachypoda var. firmula

Bignonia brachypoda var. firmula, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a climbing habit, often found along fence rows and in woodland edges. It produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
