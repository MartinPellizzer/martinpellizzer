Banksia drummondii subsp. macrorufa

Banksia drummondii subsp.

macrorufa, commonly known as the Drummond's banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
