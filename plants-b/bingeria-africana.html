Bingeria africana

Bingeria africana, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that radiate from a central stem. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Bingeria africana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
