Bionia

Bionia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance and small, often blue or purple flowers. Bionia species are typically found in moist, temperate regions and thrive in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of tiny, clustered flowers.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
