Biarum rhopalospadix

Biarum rhopalospadix, commonly known as the slender spadix bellflower, is a rare and endangered species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated spadix that bears small, clustered flowers, typical of the Araceae family. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in rich woodland soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
