Bauhinia dipetala

Bauhinia dipetala, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a crown, and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia dipetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.
