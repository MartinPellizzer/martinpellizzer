Brachycladium stalactites

Brachycladium stalactites, commonly known as false grasses, are a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

They are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. These plants are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where they have adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Their reproductive structures, known as spikelets, contain both male and female flowers, allowing for self-pollination.

Brachycladium species often play a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in their native habitats.
