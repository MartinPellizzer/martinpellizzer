Banisteria muricata

Banisteria muricata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fruit that turns from green to red as it matures. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and has a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its fruit is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Banisteria muricata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
