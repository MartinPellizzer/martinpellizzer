Buddleja grisea

Buddleja grisea, commonly known as the gray buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a distinctive grayish-blue bloom. The plant produces long, panicle-like inflorescences of tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Buddleja grisea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
