Bernardia sellowii

Bernardia sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in guitar-making, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique wood and foliage.
