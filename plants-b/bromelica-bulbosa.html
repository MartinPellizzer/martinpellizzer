Bromelica bulbosa

Bromelica bulbosa, commonly known as the pineapple lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial plants. The plant features strap-like leaves that form a rosette, and it produces a striking inflorescence with showy red or orange flowers. Bromelica bulbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
