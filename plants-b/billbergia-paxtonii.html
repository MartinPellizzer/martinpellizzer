Billbergia paxtonii

Billbergia paxtonii, commonly known as the Paxton's billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, providing a habitat for small organisms. It produces a tall flowering stem with small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in succession.

Billbergia paxtonii is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
