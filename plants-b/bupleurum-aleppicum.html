Bupleurum aleppicum

Bupleurum aleppicum, commonly known as Aleppo burnet, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Bupleurum aleppicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
