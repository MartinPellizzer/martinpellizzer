Bromus japonicus var. porrectus

Bromus japonicus var. porrectus, commonly known as the Japanese brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
