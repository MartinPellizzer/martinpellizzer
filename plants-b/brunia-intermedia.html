Brunia intermedia

Brunia intermedia, commonly known as the bluebells of the Cape, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Brunia intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
