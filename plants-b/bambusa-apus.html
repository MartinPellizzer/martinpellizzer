Bambusa apus

Bambusa apus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often have a silvery or pale green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically propagated through rhizomes or cuttings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa apus is also valued for its lightweight and flexible culms, which have traditional uses in construction and crafts.
