Blumea fontinalis

Blumea fontinalis, commonly known as the water blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Blumea fontinalis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
