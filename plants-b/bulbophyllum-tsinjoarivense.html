Bulbophyllum tsinjoarivense

Bulbophyllum tsinjoarivense, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids, featuring a lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
