Bromelia caratas

Bromelia caratas, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears vibrant orange or red flowers. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Bromelia caratas is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
