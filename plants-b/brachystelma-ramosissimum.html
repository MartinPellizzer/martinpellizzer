Brachystelma ramosissimum

Brachystelma ramosissimum, commonly known as the Bushman's bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its branched stems and fleshy leaves, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
