Bulbine brunsvigiifolia

Bulbine brunsvigiifolia, commonly known as the Natal bulbous plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features a short, fleshy stem and produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is well adapted to full sun.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
