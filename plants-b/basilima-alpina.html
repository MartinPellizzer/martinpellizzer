Basilima alpina

Basilima alpina, commonly known as alpine basil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Basilima alpina is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
