Bonamia pilbarensis

Bonamia pilbarensis, commonly known as the Pilbara bonamia, is a flowering plant native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bonamia pilbarensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.
