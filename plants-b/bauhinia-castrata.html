Bauhinia castrata

Bauhinia castrata, commonly known as the flame tree or the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright orange or red flowers, which bloom in summer. Bauhinia castrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
