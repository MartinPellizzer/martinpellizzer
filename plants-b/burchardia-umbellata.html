Burchardia umbellata

Burchardia umbellata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stalks. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Burchardia umbellata is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
