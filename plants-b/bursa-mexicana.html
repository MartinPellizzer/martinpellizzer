Bursa mexicana

Bursa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican burr or Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, seed-bearing structures called burs, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. Bursa mexicana typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
