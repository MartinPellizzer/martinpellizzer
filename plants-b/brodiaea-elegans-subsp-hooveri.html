Brodiaea elegans subsp. hooveri

Brodiaea elegans subsp.

hooveri, commonly known as the Hoover's brodiaea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to California. It features grass-like, linear leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a short growing season and is often found in open, sunny areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering characteristics compared to the main species.
