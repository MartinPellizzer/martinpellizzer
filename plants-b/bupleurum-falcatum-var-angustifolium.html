Bupleurum falcatum var. angustifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It features slender, angular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in loose racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
