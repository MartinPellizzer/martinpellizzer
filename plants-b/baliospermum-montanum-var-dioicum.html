Baliospermum montanum var. dioicum

Baliospermum montanum var. dioicum, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The varietal form, dioicum, is distinguished by its dioecious nature, meaning separate male and female plants.
