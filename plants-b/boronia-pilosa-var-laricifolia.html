Boronia pilosa var. laricifolia

Boronia pilosa var. laricifolia, commonly known as the mountain boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or heathland environments.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
