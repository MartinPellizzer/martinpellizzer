Betula alba var. laciniata

Betula alba var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated birch, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The bark of this species is smooth and pale, often developing vertical fissures as the tree matures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
