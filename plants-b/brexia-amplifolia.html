Brexia amplifolia

Brexia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sunspire, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. Its unique leaf structure helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Brexia amplifolia is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
