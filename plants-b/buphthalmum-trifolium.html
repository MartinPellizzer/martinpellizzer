Buphthalmum trifolium

Buphthalmum trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Buphthalmum trifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
