Blechnum neglectum

Blechnum neglectum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a pale green to silvery hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various habitats.

The fronds are usually evergreen, providing year-round foliage in suitable climates.
