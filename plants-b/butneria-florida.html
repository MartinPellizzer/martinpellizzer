Butneria florida

Butneria florida, commonly known as the Florida buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure and bright green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. It has a shallow root system and produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Butneria florida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
