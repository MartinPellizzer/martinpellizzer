Bonaga spinosa

Bonaga spinosa, commonly known as the spiny bonaga, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, often spiny stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, triangular fruits.

Bonaga spinosa is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
