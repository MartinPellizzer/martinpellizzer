Bupleurum commutatum subsp. aequiradiatum

Bupleurum commutatum subsp.

aequiradiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small and pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
