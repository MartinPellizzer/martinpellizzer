Berberis thunbergii var. atropurpurea

Berberis thunbergii var. atropurpurea, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-red foliage, which turns bronze in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, though not typically consumed by humans.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
