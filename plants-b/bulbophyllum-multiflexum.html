Bulbophyllum multiflexum

Bulbophyllum multiflexum, commonly known as the many-branched orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and multiple branching stems that bear numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum multiflexum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
