Baeckea thymoides

Baeckea thymoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens, and it is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
