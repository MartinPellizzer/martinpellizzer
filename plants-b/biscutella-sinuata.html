Biscutella sinuata

Biscutella sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Biscutella sinuata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
