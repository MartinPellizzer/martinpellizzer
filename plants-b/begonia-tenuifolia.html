Begonia tenuifolia

Begonia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
