Batratherum molle var. tenue

Batratherum molle var. tenue, commonly known as the slender velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or agricultural fields. Its growth habit includes spreading stems that may root at the nodes, allowing it to colonize new areas rapidly.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation, particularly in leaf shape and size, which distinguishes it from other variants of the species.
