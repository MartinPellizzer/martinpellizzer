Betula alba nothovar. kusmisscheffii

Betula alba nothovar. kusmisscheffii, commonly known as the Kusmisscheff birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Betula pendula and Betula alba.

It is characterized by its white bark and slender, weeping branches, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are usually oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across Europe.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
