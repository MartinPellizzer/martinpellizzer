Begonia andersonii

Begonia andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Begonia andersonii is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
