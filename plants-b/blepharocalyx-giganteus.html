Blepharocalyx giganteus

Blepharocalyx giganteus, commonly known as the giant beautyberry, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue berries that appear in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy beneath. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive calyx that resembles eyelashes, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
