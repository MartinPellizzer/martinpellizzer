Begonia crispula

Begonia crispula, commonly known as the crinkled begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, waxy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade.

Begonia crispula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage and delicate blooms.
