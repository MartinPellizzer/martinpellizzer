Baccharis peruviana

Baccharis peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian brittle bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and produces small, dry fruits called achene.

Baccharis peruviana is known for its resilience to drought and its use in traditional medicine.
