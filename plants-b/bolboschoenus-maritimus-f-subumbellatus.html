Bolboschoenus maritimus f. subumbellatus

Bolboschoenus maritimus f. subumbellatus, commonly known as the coastal bulrush, is a variant of the marsh grass that thrives in brackish and saline wetlands.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that form compact, umbrella-like clusters, distinguishing it from the typical form. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapting to its aquatic environment. It plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wetland species.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to coastal and estuarine conditions.
