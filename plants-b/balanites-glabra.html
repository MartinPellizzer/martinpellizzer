Balanites glabra

Balanites glabra, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small, deciduous tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and palm-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, edible fruits that are highly valued for their nutritional content. Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Balanites glabra is also used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
