Blumea napifolia

Blumea napifolia, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Blumea napifolia is often found in moist, tropical environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
