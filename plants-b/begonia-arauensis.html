Begonia arauensis

Begonia arauensis, commonly known as the Ararupe begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and red hues. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
