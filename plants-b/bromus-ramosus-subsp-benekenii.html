Bromus ramosus subsp. benekenii

Bromus ramosus subsp.

benekenii, commonly known as the Beneken's brome, is a grass species native to southern South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown glumes. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, while the flowers are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.
