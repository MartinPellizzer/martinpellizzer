Begonia callosa

Begonia callosa, commonly known as the velvety begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, fleshy flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
