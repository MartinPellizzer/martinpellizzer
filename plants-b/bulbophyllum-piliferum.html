Bulbophyllum piliferum

Bulbophyllum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence and hairy, greenish-brown pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers with a prominent, hairy lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bulbophyllum piliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
