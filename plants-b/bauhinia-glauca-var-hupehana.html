Bauhinia glauca var. hupehana

Bauhinia glauca var. hupehana, commonly known as the Chinese blue bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, blue-gray leaves that are often lobed or trifoliate. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
