Banisteria metallicolor f. glandulifera

Banisteria metallicolor f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular banisteria, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its distinctive, glandular-pubescent stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by stipules that are glandular and sometimes hairy. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique growth habit.
