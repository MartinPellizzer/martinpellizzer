Berberis reticulinervis var. brevipedicellata

Berberis reticulinervis var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed barberry, is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The leaves are typically pinnately divided with a reticulate venation pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. This plant is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
