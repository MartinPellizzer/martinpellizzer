Bromus scoparius var. multiflorus

Bromus scoparius var. multiflorus, commonly known as the tufted brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a bluish-gray hue, and it is often found in low-nutrient environments.

This variety is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
