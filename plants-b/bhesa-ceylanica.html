Bhesa ceylanica

Bhesa ceylanica, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval-shaped berries that contain aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a strong cinnamon scent.

The bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
