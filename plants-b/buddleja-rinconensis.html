Buddleja rinconensis

Buddleja rinconensis, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly bush, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, dark brown seed capsules that release seeds with silky filaments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden settings.

Buddleja rinconensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
