Biscutella pilosa

Biscutella pilosa, commonly known as the hairy biscuit flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found at high altitudes.

Biscutella pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
