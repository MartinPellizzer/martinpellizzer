Barjonia harleyi

Barjonia harleyi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Barjonia harleyi is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
