Bactris vulgaris

Bactris vulgaris, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear edible fruits, which are often consumed raw or processed into beverages. Its fibrous leaves are also used in traditional crafts and thatching.

Bactris vulgaris is well adapted to warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
