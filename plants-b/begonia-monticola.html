Begonia monticola

Begonia monticola, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Begonia monticola thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
