Buddleja megalocephala

Buddleja megalocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant produces long, slender racemes of pale lavender to white flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja megalocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different soil conditions.
