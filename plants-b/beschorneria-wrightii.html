Beschorneria wrightii

Beschorneria wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's needle-leaf yucca, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flower spikes that can reach several feet in height. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
