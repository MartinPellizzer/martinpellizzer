Brachtia glumacea

Brachtia glumacea, commonly known as the slender false grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, grass-like blades with a fibrous sheath, and its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three bracts. Brachtia glumacea is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
