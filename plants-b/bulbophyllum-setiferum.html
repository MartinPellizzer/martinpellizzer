Bulbophyllum setiferum

Bulbophyllum setiferum, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil to prevent root rot.

Bulbophyllum setiferum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
