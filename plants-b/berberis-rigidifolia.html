Berberis rigidifolia

Berberis rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid leaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
