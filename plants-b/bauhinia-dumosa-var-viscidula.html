Bauhinia dumosa var. viscidula

Bauhinia dumosa var. viscidula, commonly known as the sticky bauhinia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, sticky pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its woody structure and adaptability to various soil types make it a resilient species.

However, it is important to note that it may contain toxic compounds, requiring caution during handling or consumption.
