Brassica campestris var. mana

Brassica campestris var. mana, commonly known as wild mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
