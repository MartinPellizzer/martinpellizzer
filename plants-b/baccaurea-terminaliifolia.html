Baccaurea terminaliifolia

Baccaurea terminaliifolia, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a round, segmented berry that turns from green to red when mature, containing several edible, white, fleshy segments.
