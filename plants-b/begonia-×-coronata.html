Begonia × coronata

Begonia × coronata, commonly known as the coral begonia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a striking combination of colors and textures.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia × coronata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
