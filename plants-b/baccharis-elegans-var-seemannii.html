Baccharis elegans var. seemannii

Baccharis elegans var. seemannii, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to saline and windy environments, making it a common species in coastal dunes and sandy soils. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value also make it a popular plant in coastal landscaping.
