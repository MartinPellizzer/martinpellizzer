Brodiaea laxa var. candida

Brodiaea laxa var. candida, commonly known as the white brodiaea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or open habitats. It is a perennial species that produces small, rounded bulbs for propagation.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming make it a notable addition to native plant gardens.
