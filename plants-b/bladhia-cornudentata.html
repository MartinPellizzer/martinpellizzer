Bladhia cornudentata

Bladhia cornudentata, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Bladhia cornudentata plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and habitat for aquatic life.
