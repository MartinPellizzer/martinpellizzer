Baccharis umbelliformis

Baccharis umbelliformis, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silvery hairs. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Baccharis umbelliformis is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
