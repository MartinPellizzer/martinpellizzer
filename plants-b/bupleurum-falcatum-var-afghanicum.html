Bupleurum falcatum var. afghanicum

Bupleurum falcatum var. afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan cowbane, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, often hairy surface.

This variety is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
