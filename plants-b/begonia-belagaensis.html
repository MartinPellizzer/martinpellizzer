Begonia belagaensis

Begonia belagaensis, commonly known as the Belaga begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and it often grows as an upright shrub or trailing vine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical climates.
