Blepharodon spruceanum

Blepharodon spruceanum, commonly known as the Spruce's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Blepharodon spruceanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
