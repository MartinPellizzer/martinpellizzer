Briggsia speciosa

Briggsia speciosa, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble tassels, which are composed of clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Briggsia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
