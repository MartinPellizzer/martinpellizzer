Bulbine suurbergensis

Bulbine suurbergensis, commonly known as the Suurberg bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
