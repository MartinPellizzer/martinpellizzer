Brongniartia galegoides

Brongniartia galegoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pea vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its fibrous roots help in soil stabilization, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
