Bulbophyllum conchophyllum

Bulbophyllum conchophyllum, commonly known as the conch shell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive conch-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
