Bidens cernua var. coreopsis

Bidens cernua var. coreopsis, commonly known as the hairy false coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in moist to wet habitats, including swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
