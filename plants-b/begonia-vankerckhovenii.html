Begonia vankerckhovenii

Begonia vankerckhovenii, commonly known as the Vankerkhoven's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
