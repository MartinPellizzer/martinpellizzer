Beyeria ledifolia var. genuina

Beyeria ledifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Blue-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
