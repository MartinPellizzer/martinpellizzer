Bromus tenoreanus

Bromus tenoreanus, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters. Bromus tenoreanus is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
