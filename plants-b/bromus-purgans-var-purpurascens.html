Bromus purgans var. purpurascens

Bromus purgans var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often turn purplish in color. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that are often hairy and pointed. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils, and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its rapid growth and competitive nature make it an invasive species in some regions.
