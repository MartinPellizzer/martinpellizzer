Betula × lagopina

Betula × lagopina, commonly known as the paper birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white birch (Betula pendula) and the black birch (Betula nana).

It is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia. The tree is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of yellow and silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Betula × lagopina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its unique bark and seasonal foliage changes.
