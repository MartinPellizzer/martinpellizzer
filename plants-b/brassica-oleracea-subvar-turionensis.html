Brassica oleracea subvar. turionensis

Brassica oleracea subvar. turionensis, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, serrated leaves that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Watercress thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils near streams, rivers, and other water sources.

It is valued for its peppery flavor and nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins A, C, and K.
