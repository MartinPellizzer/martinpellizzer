Babiana quadripartita

Babiana quadripartita, commonly known as the Rainforest Bell, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is adapted to the moist, shaded environments of the Eastern Cape and southern KwaZulu-Natal. Its bulbs are an important part of its growth cycle, storing nutrients for flowering.

Babiana quadripartita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
