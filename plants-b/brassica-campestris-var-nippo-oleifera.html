Brassica campestris var. nippo-oleifera

Brassica campestris var. nippo-oleifera, commonly known as Japanese mustard.

It is a biennial plant native to East Asia, characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are edible and often used in salads or as a garnish.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
