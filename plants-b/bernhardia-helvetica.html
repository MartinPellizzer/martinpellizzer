Bernhardia helvetica

Bernhardia helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
