Bursera graveolens var. pilosa

Bursera graveolens var. pilosa, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces a fragrant resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and incense. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in pharmacological research.

The species is also notable for its ability to adapt to dry conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in arid regions.
