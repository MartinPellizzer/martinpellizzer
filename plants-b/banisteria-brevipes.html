Banisteria brevipes

Banisteria brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its woody vines and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
