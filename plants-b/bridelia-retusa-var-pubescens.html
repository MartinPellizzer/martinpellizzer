Bridelia retusa var. pubescens

Bridelia retusa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
