Baccharis odorata

Baccharis odorata, commonly known as sweet baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring aromatic leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climate conditions.
