Bromus racemosus var. monostachyus

Bromus racemosus var. monostachyus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
