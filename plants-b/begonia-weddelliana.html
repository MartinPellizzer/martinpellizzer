Begonia weddelliana

Begonia weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
