Biscutella gibraltarica

Biscutella gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar's biscuit plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the rocky habitats of the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and sunny conditions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers.

Biscutella gibraltarica is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
