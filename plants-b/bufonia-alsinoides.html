Bufonia alsinoides

Bufonia alsinoides, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces bulbous underground storage structures called corms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
