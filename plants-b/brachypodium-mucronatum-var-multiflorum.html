Brachypodium mucronatum var. multiflorum

Brachypodium mucronatum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface that aids in reducing water loss.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
