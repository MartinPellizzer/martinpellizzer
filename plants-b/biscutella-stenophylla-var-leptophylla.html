Biscutella stenophylla var. leptophylla

Biscutella stenophylla var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are yellowish-white and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
