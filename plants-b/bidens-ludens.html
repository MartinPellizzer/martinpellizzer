Bidens ludens

Bidens ludens, commonly known as the common beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Bidens ludens is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of habitats.

It is also known for its ability to spread through seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
