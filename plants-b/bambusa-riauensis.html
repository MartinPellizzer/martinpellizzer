Bambusa riauensis

Bambusa riauensis, commonly known as the Riau bamboo, is a species of grass found primarily in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Bambusa riauensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional construction and handicrafts.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a distinctive member of the bamboo genus.
