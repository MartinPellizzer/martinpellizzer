Baccharis poeppigiana subsp. austropedicellata

Baccharis poeppigiana subsp.

austropedicellata, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
