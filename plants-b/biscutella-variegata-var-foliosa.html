Biscutella variegata var. foliosa

Biscutella variegata var. foliosa, commonly known as the variegated biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, pale green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, which enhances its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant collections.
