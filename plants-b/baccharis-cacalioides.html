Baccharis cacalioides

Baccharis cacalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the fall.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
