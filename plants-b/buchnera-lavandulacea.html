Buchnera lavandulacea

Buchnera lavandulacea, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Buchnera lavandulacea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
