Biscutella didyma subsp. ciliata

Biscutella didyma subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the hairy biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
