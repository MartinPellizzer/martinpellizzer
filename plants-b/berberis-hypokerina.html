Berberis hypokerina

Berberis hypokerina, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, spiny stems and alternate, narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly found in woodland edges and open forests.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
