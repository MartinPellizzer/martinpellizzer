Barbilus

Barbilus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Barbilus species typically grow as compact, low-lying plants with a rosette formation of leaves. They prefer well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and ease of care.
