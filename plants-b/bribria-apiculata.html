Bribria apiculata

Bribria apiculata, commonly known as the woolly burrgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Bribria apiculata is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
