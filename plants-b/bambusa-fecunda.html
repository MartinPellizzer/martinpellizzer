Bambusa fecunda

Bambusa fecunda, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-green foliage. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature. It grows as a clump-forming plant, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Bambusa fecunda is also valued for its strong and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
