Begonia sordidissima

Begonia sordidissima, commonly known as the dirty begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that help it survive in its natural habitat. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
