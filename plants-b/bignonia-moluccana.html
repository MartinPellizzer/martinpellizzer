Bignonia moluccana

Bignonia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan crossvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb using tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia moluccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
