Boisduvalia tasmanica

Boisduvalia tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian boin.

It is a flowering plant native to Tasmania, characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Boisduvalia tasmanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
