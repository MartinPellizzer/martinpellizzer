Begonia rubriflora

Begonia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and foliage, which add color and texture to botanical collections.
