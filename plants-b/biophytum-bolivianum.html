Biophytum bolivianum

Biophytum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian flame plant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Biophytum bolivianum is also notable for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and stabilize in its natural habitat.
