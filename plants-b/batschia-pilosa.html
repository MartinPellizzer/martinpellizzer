Batschia pilosa

Batschia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bat's-hair, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant prefers arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Batschia pilosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
