Bidaria indica

Bidaria indica, commonly known as Indian grass or Indian wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of many grass species. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and wetlands.

Bidaria indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
