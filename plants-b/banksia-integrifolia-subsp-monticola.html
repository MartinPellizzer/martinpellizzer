Banksia integrifolia subsp. monticola

Banksia integrifolia subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow to orange flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, often thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
