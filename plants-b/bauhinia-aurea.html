Bauhinia aurea

Bauhinia aurea, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the dry season. Bauhinia aurea is typically found in dry, open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
