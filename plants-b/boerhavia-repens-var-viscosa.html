Boerhavia repens var. viscosa

Boerhavia repens var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky bluestem, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered with fine, sticky hairs, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is drought-tolerant and has adaptations that allow it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
