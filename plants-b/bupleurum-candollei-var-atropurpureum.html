Bupleurum candollei var. atropurpureum

Bupleurum candollei var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, clustered umbels of flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display.
