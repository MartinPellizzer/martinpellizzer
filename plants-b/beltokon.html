Beltokon

Beltokon, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
