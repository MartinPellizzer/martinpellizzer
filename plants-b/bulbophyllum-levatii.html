Bulbophyllum levatii

Bulbophyllum levatii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

The flowers are often pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
