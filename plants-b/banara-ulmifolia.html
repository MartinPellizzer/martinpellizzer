Banara ulmifolia

Banara ulmifolia, commonly known as the Indian ash or sacred ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for their therapeutic benefits.
