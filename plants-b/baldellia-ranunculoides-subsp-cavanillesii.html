Baldellia ranunculoides subsp. cavanillesii

Baldellia ranunculoides subsp.

cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' water buttercup, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

The plant features delicate, floating leaves with a waxy coating and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system helps stabilize sediment and improve water quality.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
