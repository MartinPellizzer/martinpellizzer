Barleria lugardii

Barleria lugardii, commonly known as the red-flowered barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
