Begonia rudatisii

Begonia rudatisii, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and requires careful cultivation.
