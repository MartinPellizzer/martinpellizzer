Bignonia macrostachya

Bignonia macrostachya, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly lobed or serrated margin.

Bignonia macrostachya thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
