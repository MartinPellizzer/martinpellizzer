Bulbophyllum odontopetalum

Bulbophyllum odontopetalum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive tooth-like appearance on the petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
