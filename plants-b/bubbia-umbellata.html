Bubbia umbellata

Bubbia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella burr, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Bubbia umbellata is often found in sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
