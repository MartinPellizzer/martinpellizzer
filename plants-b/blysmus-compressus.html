Blysmus compressus

Blysmus compressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blysmus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, compressed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Blysmus compressus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
