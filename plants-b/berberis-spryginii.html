Berberis spryginii

Berberis spryginii, commonly known as the Khabarovsk barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of Russia and China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Berberis spryginii is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
