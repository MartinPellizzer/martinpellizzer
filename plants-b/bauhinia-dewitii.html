Bauhinia dewitii

Bauhinia dewitii, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Bauhinia dewitii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climatic conditions.
