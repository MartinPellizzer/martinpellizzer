Brachycarpaea linifolia

Brachycarpaea linifolia, commonly known as the silky brachycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Brachycarpaea linifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
