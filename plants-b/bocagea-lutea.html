Bocagea lutea

Bocagea lutea, commonly known as the yellow bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bocagea lutea thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
