Bellis hyrcanica

Bellis hyrcanica, commonly known as the Hyrcanian daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white with yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellis hyrcanica is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
