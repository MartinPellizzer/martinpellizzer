Baroniella acuminata

Baroniella acuminata, commonly known as the spiny cactus or baroniella, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to access underground water sources.

Baroniella acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
