Besleria montana

Besleria montana, commonly known as the mountain besleria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in various colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually ovate and glossy, and it produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Besleria montana is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
