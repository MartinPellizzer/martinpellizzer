Banksia pulchella

Banksia pulchella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow to orange flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
