Betula browicziana

Betula browicziana, commonly known as the Polish birch, is a species of birch native to eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The tree has smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, a characteristic feature of many birch species. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula browicziana is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
