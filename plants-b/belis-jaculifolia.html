Belis jaculifolia

Belis jaculifolia, commonly known as the shooting star plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and somewhat woody.

Belis jaculifolia is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
