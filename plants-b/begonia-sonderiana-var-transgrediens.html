Begonia sonderiana var. transgrediens

Begonia sonderiana var. transgrediens, commonly known as the climbing begonia, is a tropical plant characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. Its flowers are typically red or pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in gardens.
