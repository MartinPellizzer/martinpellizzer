Blechnum erectum

Blechnum erectum, commonly known as the upright fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its upright, leathery fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

The fronds are usually evergreen, providing year-round foliage in its natural habitat.
