Bomarea cruenta

Bomarea cruenta, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange tubers, which are often mistaken for roots, and its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the early spring. Its unique appearance and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotany.

However, due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in many parts of its native range.
