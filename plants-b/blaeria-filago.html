Blaeria filago

Blaeria filago, commonly known as the common blaeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming a daisy-like cluster, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Blaeria filago is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
