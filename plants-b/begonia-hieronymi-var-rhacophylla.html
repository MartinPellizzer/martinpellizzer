Begonia hieronymi var. rhacophylla

Begonia hieronymi var. rhacophylla, commonly known as the Rhacophylla Begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
