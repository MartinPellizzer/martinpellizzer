Bromus erectus f. laxus

Bromus erectus f. laxus, commonly known as the loose spikegrass, is a botanical variant of the annual bromus species.

It is characterized by its loosely arranged inflorescence, which distinguishes it from the more compact form of the species. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural fields. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This form is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
