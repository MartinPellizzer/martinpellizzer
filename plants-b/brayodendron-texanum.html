Brayodendron texanum

Brayodendron texanum, commonly known as Texas brayodendron, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Brayodendron texanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
