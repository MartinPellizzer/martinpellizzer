Bocconia cordata var. japonica

Bocconia cordata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese bocconia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
