Baphia cymosa

Baphia cymosa, commonly known as the clustered baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Baphia cymosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
