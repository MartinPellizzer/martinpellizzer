Byrsonima sericea

Byrsonima sericea, commonly known as silky balsa, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
