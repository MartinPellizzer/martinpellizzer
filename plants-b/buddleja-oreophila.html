Buddleja oreophila

Buddleja oreophila, commonly known as the mountain buddleja, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

Buddleja oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
