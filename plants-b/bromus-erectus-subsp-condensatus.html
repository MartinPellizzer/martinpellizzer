Bromus erectus subsp. condensatus

Bromus erectus subsp.

condensatus, commonly known as the dense bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
