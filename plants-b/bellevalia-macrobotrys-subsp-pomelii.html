Bellevalia macrobotrys subsp. pomelii

Bellevalia macrobotrys subsp.

pomelii, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
