Brassica rapa subf. depressa

Brassica rapa subf.

depressa, commonly known as the Chinese kale or broccoli rape, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a biennial or annual herb characterized by its rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its roots are often thick and fleshy, adapted for storage.

This species is cultivated for its edible leaves and is valued in various Asian cuisines.
