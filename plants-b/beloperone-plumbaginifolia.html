Beloperone plumbaginifolia

Beloperone plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plumbaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and somewhat woody.

Beloperone plumbaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
