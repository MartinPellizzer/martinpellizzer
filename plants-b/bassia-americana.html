Bassia americana

Bassia americana, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a shrub native to the United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a common food source for birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its drought tolerance and adaptability make it a hardy plant in various ecological settings.
