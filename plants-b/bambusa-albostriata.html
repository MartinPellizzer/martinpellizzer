Bambusa albostriata

Bambusa albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a species of clumping bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white stripes on the culms, which contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant has a dense, upright growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa albostriata is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
