Bignonia leucoxyla

Bignonia leucoxyla, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bignonia leucoxyla is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
