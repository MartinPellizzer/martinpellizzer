Begonia cyanescens

Begonia cyanescens, commonly known as the blue begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and velvety, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives it a unique texture.

Begonia cyanescens thrives in shaded, moist environments and is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value.
