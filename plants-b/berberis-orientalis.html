Berberis orientalis

Berberis orientalis, commonly known as the Persian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.

Berberis orientalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
