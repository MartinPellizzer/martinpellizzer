Biarum tenuifolium var. gramineum

Biarum tenuifolium var. gramineum, commonly known as the slender-leaf lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, grass-like foliage that is narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
