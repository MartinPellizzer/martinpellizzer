Bromus marginatus var. breviaristatus

Bromus marginatus var. breviaristatus, commonly known as the narrow-awned brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow leaves that have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with short, bristly awns, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
