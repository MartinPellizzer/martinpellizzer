Berberis leptodonta

Berberis leptodonta, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
