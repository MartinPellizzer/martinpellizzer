Bactris cuesa

Bactris cuesa, commonly known as the Cuesta palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The Cuesta palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional agriculture.
