Bupleurum falcatum var. oblongifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in open woodland or grassy areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, with a distinctive wavy margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
