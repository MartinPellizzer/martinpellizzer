Berberis incrassata var. fugongensis

Berberis incrassata var. fugongensis, commonly known as the Fugong barberry, is a shrub native to the Yunnan province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, decorative plant.
