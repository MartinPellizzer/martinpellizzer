Bonamia spectabilis

Bonamia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bonamia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy inflorescence consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
