Byttneria integrifolia

Byttneria integrifolia, commonly known as African star gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that ripens to a bright orange or red color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
