Banksia sphaerocarpa var. caesia

Banksia sphaerocarpa var. caesia, commonly known as the Cape Banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
