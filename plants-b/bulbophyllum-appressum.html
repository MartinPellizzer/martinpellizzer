Bulbophyllum appressum

Bulbophyllum appressum, commonly known as the compact orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a wide range of colors, typically in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
