Berberis chrysacantha

Berberis chrysacantha, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
