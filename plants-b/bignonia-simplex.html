Bignonia simplex

Bignonia simplex, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia simplex is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
