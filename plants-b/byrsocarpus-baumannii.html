Byrsocarpus baumannii

Byrsocarpus baumannii, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals that eat the fruit.
