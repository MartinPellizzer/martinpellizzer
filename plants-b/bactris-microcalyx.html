Bactris microcalyx

Bactris microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small-fruited palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, robust trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by small, round fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
