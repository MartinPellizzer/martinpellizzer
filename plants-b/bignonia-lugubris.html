Bignonia lugubris

Bignonia lugubris, commonly known as the mourning bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth edges and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia lugubris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant.
