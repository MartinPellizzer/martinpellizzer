Bulbophyllum subcrenulatum

Bulbophyllum subcrenulatum, commonly known as the crested violet-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure with a single, fleshy leaf and a cluster of slender, pale green to reddish stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
