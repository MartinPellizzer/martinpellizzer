Bambusa burmanica

Bambusa burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa burmanica is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
