Butea suberecta

Butea suberecta, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright orange to red floral display, which appears in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture, while its root system is deep and fibrous.

Butea suberecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
