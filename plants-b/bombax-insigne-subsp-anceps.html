Bombax insigne subsp. anceps

Bombax insigne subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the red cotton tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in red or pink hues.

The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seed pods that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are broad and palmately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
