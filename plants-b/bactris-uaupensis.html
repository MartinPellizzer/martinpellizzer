Bactris uaupensis

Bactris uaupensis, commonly known as the Pão-de-Açúcar palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. Its fruit is an important food source for various animals and is also consumed by humans.

Bactris uaupensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional qualities.
