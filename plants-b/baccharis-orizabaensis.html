Baccharis orizabaensis

Baccharis orizabaensis, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Baccharis orizabaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
