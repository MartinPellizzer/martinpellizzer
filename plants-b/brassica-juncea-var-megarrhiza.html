Brassica juncea var. megarrhiza

Brassica juncea var. megarrhiza, commonly known as the giant mustard, is a variant of the mustard plant characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and elongated, yellow flowers.

It is a biennial plant native to the Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are often used in traditional Indian cuisine, while the seeds are harvested for oil production. It exhibits a robust growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
