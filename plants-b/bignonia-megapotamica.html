Bignonia megapotamica

Bignonia megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
