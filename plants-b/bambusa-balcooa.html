Bambusa balcooa

Bambusa balcooa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often display a striking golden or yellowish hue, especially in its younger stages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight.

Bambusa balcooa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
