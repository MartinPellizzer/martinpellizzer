Bromus pseudosecalinus

Bromus pseudosecalinus, commonly known as the false rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Bromus pseudosecalinus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
