Boerhavia linearifolia subsp. glandulosa

Boerhavia linearifolia subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular boerhavia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, ascending stems that often branch near the base.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with glandular dots that give the plant its subspecies name. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
