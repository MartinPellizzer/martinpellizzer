Berberis hibberdiana

Berberis hibberdiana, commonly known as the Hibberd's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to grow in rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry, arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
