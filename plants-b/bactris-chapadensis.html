Bactris chapadensis

Bactris chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are clustered in panicles. Bactris chapadensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
