Baccharis riograndensis

Baccharis riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features gray-green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
