Byrsonima rodriguesii

Byrsonima rodriguesii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pata de vaca*, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
