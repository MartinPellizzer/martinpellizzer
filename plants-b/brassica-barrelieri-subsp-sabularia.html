Brassica barrelieri subsp. sabularia

Brassica barrelieri subsp.

sabularia, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine, while its seeds are rich in glucosinolates.

The species is valued for its resilience to drought and its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
