Bremeria hymenopogonoides

Bremeria hymenopogonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pteridium, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to various regions in South America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in a bipinnate structure.

Bremeria hymenopogonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
