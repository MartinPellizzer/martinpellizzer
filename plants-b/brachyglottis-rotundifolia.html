Brachyglottis rotundifolia

Brachyglottis rotundifolia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with small, rounded leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Brachyglottis rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
