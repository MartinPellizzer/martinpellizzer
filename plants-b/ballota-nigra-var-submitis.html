Ballota nigra var. submitis

Ballota nigra var. submitis, commonly known as the black nightshade or Spanish thorn.

This plant is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
