Bothriochloa assimilis

Bothriochloa assimilis, commonly known as fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Bothriochloa assimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
