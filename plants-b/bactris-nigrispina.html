Bactris nigrispina

Bactris nigrispina, commonly known as the black-spined palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. This palm is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The fruit is a small, edible drupe that is often consumed by local populations.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
