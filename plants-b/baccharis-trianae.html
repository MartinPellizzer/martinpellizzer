Baccharis trianae

Baccharis trianae, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal areas or disturbed soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental plant in landscaping.
