Barleria trispinosa subsp. trispinosa

Barleria trispinosa subsp.

trispinosa, commonly known as the three-needle barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive three-spined leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
