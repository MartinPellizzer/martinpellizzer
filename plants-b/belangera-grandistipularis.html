Belangera grandistipularis

Belangera grandistipularis, commonly known as the large-stippled bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Belangera grandistipularis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
