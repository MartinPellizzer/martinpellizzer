Buchnera tinctoria

Buchnera tinctoria, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Buchnera tinctoria is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
