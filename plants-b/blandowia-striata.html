Blandowia striata

Blandowia striata, commonly known as the striped bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water, relying on its specialized adaptations to obtain necessary nutrients. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may float on the water's surface.

Blandowia striata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by controlling populations of tiny aquatic prey.
