Bromus squarrosus var. contortus

Bromus squarrosus var. contortus, commonly known as the twisted festuca, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often twisted stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
