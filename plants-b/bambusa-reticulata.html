Bambusa reticulata

Bambusa reticulata, commonly known as the netted bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, light green culms with a distinctive reticulate pattern on its surface. The plant is a tall, fast-growing bamboo that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa reticulata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in construction and as a source of bamboo shoots.
