Buphthalmum flosculosum

Buphthalmum flosculosum, commonly known as the yellow ox-eye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of pale yellow to white disk florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is known for its showy, daisy-like appearance. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Buphthalmum flosculosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
