Blechnum heringeri

Blechnum heringeri, commonly known as the Heringer's holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its fronds are pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of small, rounded lobes along the leaflets.

Blechnum heringeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
