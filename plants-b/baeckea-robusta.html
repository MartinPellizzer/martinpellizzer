Baeckea robusta

Baeckea robusta, commonly known as the robust baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species, suitable for cultivation in a range of soil types.

Baeckea robusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
