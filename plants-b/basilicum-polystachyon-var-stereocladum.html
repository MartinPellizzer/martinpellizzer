Basilicum polystachyon var. stereocladum

Basilicum polystachyon var. stereocladum, commonly known as mountain basil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are opposite and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and estragole, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and potential medicinal uses.
