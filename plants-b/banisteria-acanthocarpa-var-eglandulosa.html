Banisteria acanthocarpa var. eglandulosa

Banisteria acanthocarpa var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular banisteria, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, globose, red or orange berries. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for horticultural and ecological studies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
