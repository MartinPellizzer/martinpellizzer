Begonia undulata

Begonia undulata, commonly known as the undulating begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-edged leaves that often display vibrant shades of green and red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
