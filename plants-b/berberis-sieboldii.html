Berberis sieboldii

Berberis sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
