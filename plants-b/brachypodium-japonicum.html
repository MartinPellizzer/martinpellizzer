Brachypodium japonicum

Brachypodium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.
