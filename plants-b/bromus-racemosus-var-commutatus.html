Bromus racemosus var. commutatus

Bromus racemosus var. commutatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.
