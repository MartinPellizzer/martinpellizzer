Brassica rapa subvar. flavescens

Brassica rapa subvar. flavescens, commonly known as yellow mustard, is an annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are pollinated by insects. Its seeds, enclosed in siliques, are used in the production of mustard oil and condiments.

This species is also valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
