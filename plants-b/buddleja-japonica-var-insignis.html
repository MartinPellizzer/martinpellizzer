Buddleja japonica var. insignis

Buddleja japonica var. insignis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, arching stems that create a weeping appearance, often used in ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flowers.

Buddleja japonica var. insignis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
