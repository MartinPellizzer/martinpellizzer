Brachyloma preissii

Brachyloma preissii, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Brachyloma preissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
