Bauhinia pottsii

Bauhinia pottsii, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often palmate leaves that are covered in a velvety, soft texture. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia pottsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
