Brassica tournefortii f. dentata

Brassica tournefortii f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed field mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, toothed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ecological importance includes supporting pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
