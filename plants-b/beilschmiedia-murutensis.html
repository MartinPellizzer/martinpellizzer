Beilschmiedia murutensis

Beilschmiedia murutensis, commonly known as the Malayan beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
