Bulnesia retama var. weberbaueri

Bulnesia retama var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's bursera, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
