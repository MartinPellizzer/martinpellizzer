Batatas cissoides

Batatas cissoides, commonly known as the Cissoid sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its tuberous roots, which are the edible part, are rich in starch and have a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
