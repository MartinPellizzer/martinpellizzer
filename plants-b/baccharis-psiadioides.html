Baccharis psiadioides

Baccharis psiadioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis psiadioides is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is used in some regions for ornamental purposes.
