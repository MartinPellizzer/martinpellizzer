Begonia wangii

Begonia wangii, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, heart-shaped leaves with intricate silver veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia wangii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
