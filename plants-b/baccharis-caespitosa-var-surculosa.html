Baccharis caespitosa var. surculosa

Baccharis caespitosa var. surculosa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage that reflects light effectively. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the fall and attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form low, mounded shapes, often used in landscaping for erosion control.
