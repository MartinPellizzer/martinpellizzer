Brassica nudicaulis var. africana

Brassica nudicaulis var. africana, commonly known as the African mustard, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, sharing similar ecological and botanical traits.
