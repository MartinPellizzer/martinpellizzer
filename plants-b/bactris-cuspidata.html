Bactris cuspidata

Bactris cuspidata, commonly known as the spined palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in spines. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples.

Bactris cuspidata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
