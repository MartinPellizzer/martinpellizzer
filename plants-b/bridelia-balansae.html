Bridelia balansae

Bridelia balansae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks, and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
