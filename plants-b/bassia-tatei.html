Bassia tatei

Bassia tatei, commonly known as the Tibetan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to the arid and cold conditions of its habitat.

Bassia tatei is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
