Biasolettia cretica

Biasolettia cretica, commonly known as the African cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones rather than flowers. The plant has a thick, unbranched trunk with a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and long lifespan, it is considered a living fossil and is of significant ecological and evolutionary interest.
