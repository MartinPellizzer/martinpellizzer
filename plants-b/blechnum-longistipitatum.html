Blechnum longistipitatum

Blechnum longistipitatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that can form dense clumps. Its fronds are pinnate, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often slightly curled at the edges.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
