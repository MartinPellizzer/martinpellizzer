Baccharis variabiliflora

Baccharis variabiliflora, commonly known as variable Baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy, yellowish-white flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and open fields, and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
