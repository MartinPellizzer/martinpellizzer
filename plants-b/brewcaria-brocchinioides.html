Brewcaria brocchinioides

Brewcaria brocchinioides, commonly known as the creeping brocchini, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its trailing stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Brewcaria brocchinioides is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
