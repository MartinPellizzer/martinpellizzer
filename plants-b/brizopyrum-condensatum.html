Brizopyrum condensatum

Brizopyrum condensatum, commonly known as the dense feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Brizopyrum condensatum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
