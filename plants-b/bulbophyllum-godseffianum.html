Bulbophyllum godseffianum

Bulbophyllum godseffianum, commonly known as the Godseff's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.
