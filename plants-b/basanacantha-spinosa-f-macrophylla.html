Basanacantha spinosa f. macrophylla

Basanacantha spinosa f. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf spinous thorn.

This plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elongated and have a distinctive spinous texture. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains several seeds.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
