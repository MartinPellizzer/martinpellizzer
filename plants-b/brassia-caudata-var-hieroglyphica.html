Brassia caudata var. hieroglyphica

Brassia caudata var. hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Hieroglyphic Brassia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

This variant is distinguished by its unique, intricate patterns on its petals that resemble ancient hieroglyphics, giving it its common name. The plant features pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting markings. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
