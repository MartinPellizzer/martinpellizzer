Behen vulgaris f. grandifolius

Behen vulgaris f. grandifolius, commonly known as Indian mustard or wild mustard, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, followed by elongated, cylindrical siliques containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
