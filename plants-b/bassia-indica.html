Bassia indica

Bassia indica, commonly known as Indian spinach or false spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by cylindrical, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and fields.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated as a leafy vegetable in certain regions.
