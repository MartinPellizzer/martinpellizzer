Brassica campestris subvar. depressa

Brassica campestris subvar. depressa, commonly known as the curly kale, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are curled and wrinkled. The plant produces yellow flowers in the form of four-petaled blossoms, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassica genus, including broccoli and cauliflower.
