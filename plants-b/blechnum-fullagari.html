Blechnum fullagari

Blechnum fullagari, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and it produces spores in small, clustered clusters on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
