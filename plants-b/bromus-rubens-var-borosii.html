Bromus rubens var. borosii

Bromus rubens var. borosii, commonly known as the red brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive red-tipped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Bromus rubens var. borosii is often found in association with other grasses and forage plants in arid environments.
