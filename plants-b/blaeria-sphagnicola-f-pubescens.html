Blaeria sphagnicola f. pubescens

Blaeria sphagnicola f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bog bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used for capturing prey.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic wetlands and bogs, where it plays a role in the aquatic food web. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often floating on the water surface. Its pubescent (hairy) foliage is a distinguishing feature, aiding in moisture retention and possibly deterring herbivores.

This fern-like plant is an important indicator species for healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
