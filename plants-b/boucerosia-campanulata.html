Boucerosia campanulata

Boucerosia campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Boucerosia campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
