Barbosella schista

Barbosella schista, commonly known as the rock barbosella, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with narrow, leathery leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Barbosella schista is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
