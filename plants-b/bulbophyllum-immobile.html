Bulbophyllum immobile

Bulbophyllum immobile, commonly known as the immobile orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces small, intricate flowers with a striking coloration that varies from white to pink and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
