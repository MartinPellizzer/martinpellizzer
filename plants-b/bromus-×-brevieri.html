Bromus × brevieri

Bromus × brevieri, commonly known as the Brevier's brome, is a hybrid grass species that occurs naturally in regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear spikelets at the base. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in open fields, and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters.

Bromus × brevieri plays a minor role in local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
