Bursera staphyleoides

Bursera staphyleoides, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing three seeds.

Bursera staphyleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its aromatic resins.
