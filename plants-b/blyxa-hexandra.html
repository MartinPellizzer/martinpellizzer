Blyxa hexandra

Blyxa hexandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing both male and female reproductive structures.

Blyxa hexandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
