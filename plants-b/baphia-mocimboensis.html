Baphia mocimboensis

Baphia mocimboensis, commonly known as the Mocimbo baphia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
