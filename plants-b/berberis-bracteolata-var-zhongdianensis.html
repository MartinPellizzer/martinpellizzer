Berberis bracteolata var. zhongdianensis

Berberis bracteolata var. zhongdianensis, commonly known as the Zhongdian barberry, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of China.

It features dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
