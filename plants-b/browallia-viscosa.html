Browallia viscosa

Browallia viscosa, commonly known as sticky browallia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, often with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
