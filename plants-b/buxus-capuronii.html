Buxus capuronii

Buxus capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's boxwood, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Buxus capuronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for topiary and hedging.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
