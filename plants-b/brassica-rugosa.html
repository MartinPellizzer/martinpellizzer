Brassica rugosa

Brassica rugosa, commonly known as the rough mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are dry pods that contain several seeds, typical of the mustard family.

Brassica rugosa is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
