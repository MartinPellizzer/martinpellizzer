Bulbophyllum andreeae

Bulbophyllum andreeae, commonly known as the Andree's orchid, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native region.
