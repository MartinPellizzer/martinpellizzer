Blepharodon bicolor

Blepharodon bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
