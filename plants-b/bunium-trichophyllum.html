Bunium trichophyllum

Bunium trichophyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
