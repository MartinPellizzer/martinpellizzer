Brassica integrifolia var. carinata

Brassica integrifolia var. carinata, commonly known as Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, slender pods containing several seeds. It is native to Ethiopia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
