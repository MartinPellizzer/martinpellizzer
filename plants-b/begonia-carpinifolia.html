Begonia carpinifolia

Begonia carpinifolia, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
