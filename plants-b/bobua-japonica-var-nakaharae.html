Bobua japonica var. nakaharae

Bobua japonica var. nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara's Japanese bobua, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a slight variegation. It produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
