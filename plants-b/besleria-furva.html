Besleria furva

Besleria furva, commonly known as the red-flowered spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow floral bracts that resemble spider-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.

The botanical features of Besleria furva make it a unique and visually captivating member of the orchid family.
