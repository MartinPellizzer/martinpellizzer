Batschia fimbriata

Batschia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue petals with distinctive fringed edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Batschia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
