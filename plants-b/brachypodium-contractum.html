Brachypodium contractum

Brachypodium contractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics make it a useful model organism in plant genetics and ecology.

It is also sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
