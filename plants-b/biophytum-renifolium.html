Biophytum renifolium

Biophytum renifolium, commonly known as the woolly leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
