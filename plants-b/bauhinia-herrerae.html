Bauhinia herrerae

Bauhinia herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Bauhinia herrerae thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
