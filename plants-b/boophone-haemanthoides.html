Boophone haemanthoides

Boophone haemanthoides, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.

Boophone haemanthoides is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
