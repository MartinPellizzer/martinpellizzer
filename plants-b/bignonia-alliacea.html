Bignonia alliacea

Bignonia alliacea, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along its long, trailing stems. The plant produces small, green, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Bignonia alliacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
