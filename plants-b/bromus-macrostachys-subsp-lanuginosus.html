Bromus macrostachys subsp. lanuginosus

Bromus macrostachys subsp.

lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, coarse stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it identifiable in ecological studies and invasive plant monitoring.
