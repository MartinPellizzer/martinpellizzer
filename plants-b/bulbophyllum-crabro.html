Bulbophyllum crabro

Bulbophyllum crabro, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, wiry stems and large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum crabro is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
