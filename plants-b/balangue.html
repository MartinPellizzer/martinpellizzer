Balangue

Balangue, commonly known as *Barringtonia asiatica*, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, lantern-like flowers that hang from the branches. The fruit, which resembles a sponge, contains a fleshy outer layer and a hard, fibrous interior with seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in urban and coastal areas.
