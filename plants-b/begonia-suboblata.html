Begonia suboblata

Begonia suboblata, commonly known as the suboblata begonia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
