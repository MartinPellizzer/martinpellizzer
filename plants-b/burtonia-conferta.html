Burtonia conferta

Burtonia conferta, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Burtonia conferta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
