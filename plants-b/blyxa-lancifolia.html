Blyxa lancifolia

Blyxa lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in spikes and are pollinated by wind.

Blyxa lancifolia is often used in water garden landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize aquatic soils.
