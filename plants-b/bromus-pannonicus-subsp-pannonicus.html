Bromus pannonicus subsp. pannonicus

Bromus pannonicus subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian brome, is a grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and agricultural areas. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated landscapes.
