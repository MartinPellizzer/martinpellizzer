Bassia blackei

Bassia blackei, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils.

Bassia blackei plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in desert ecosystems.
