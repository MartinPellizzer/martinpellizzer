Besleria trichostegia

Besleria trichostegia, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, with small, inconspicuous true flowers nestled within. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is primarily grown in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
