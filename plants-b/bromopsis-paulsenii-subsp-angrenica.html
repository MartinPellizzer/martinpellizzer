Bromopsis paulsenii subsp. angrenica

Bromopsis paulsenii subsp.

angrenica, commonly known as the Angrenica bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Bromopsis paulsenii subsp.

angrenica is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
