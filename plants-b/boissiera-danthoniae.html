Boissiera danthoniae

Boissiera danthoniae, commonly known as the Danthonia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Boissiera danthoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
