Brassica pubescens

Brassica pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and compact, cabbage-like inflorescence. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils.

Brassica pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
