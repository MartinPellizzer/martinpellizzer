Begonia novoguineensis

Begonia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically has heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and anchor itself in its natural habitat.
