Bubon albanicum

Bubon albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.
