Berberis poiretii

Berberis poiretii, commonly known as the Corsican barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal flowering.
