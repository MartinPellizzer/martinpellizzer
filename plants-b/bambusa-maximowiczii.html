Bambusa maximowiczii

Bambusa maximowiczii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bambusa maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts.
