Bradleia lucida

Bradleia lucida, commonly known as the shining bradleia, is a rare and striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that reflect light, giving it its common name. Its flowers are tubular and typically appear in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Bradleia lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its rarity and beauty.
