Batrachium fluitans

Batrachium fluitans, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in temperate regions.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving water, where it can anchor itself with its root-like rhizomes. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for buoyancy and photosynthesis.

Batrachium fluitans plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling microorganism populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
