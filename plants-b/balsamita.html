Balsamita

Balsamita, commonly known as wild basil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Balsamita thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
