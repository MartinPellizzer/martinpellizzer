Bauhinia moultonii

Bauhinia moultonii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, rounded leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia moultonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
