Bauhinia andrade-limae

Bauhinia andrade-limae, commonly known as the Andrade-Limae burrbean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
