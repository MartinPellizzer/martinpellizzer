Beyeria opaca

Beyeria opaca, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers. The plant's berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. Beyeria opaca is characterized by its ability to photosynthesize partially, relying on its host for water and nutrients.

Its ecological role includes providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
