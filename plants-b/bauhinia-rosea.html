Bauhinia rosea

Bauhinia rosea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind or pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia rosea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
