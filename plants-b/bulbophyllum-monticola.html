Bulbophyllum monticola

Bulbophyllum monticola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers with a wide range of colors. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.
