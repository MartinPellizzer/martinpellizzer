Bromus decolarans

Bromus decolarans, commonly known as the decolored brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that often lean or lie flat, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of bracts, with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Bromus decolarans is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
