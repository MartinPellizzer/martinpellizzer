Bocagea asymmetrica

Bocagea asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrically shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is often described as having a unique, irregular leaf shape that sets it apart from other similar species.

Bocagea asymmetrica thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
