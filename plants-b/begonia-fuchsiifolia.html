Begonia fuchsiifolia

Begonia fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the fuchsia-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that have a distinctive fuchsia or purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Begonia fuchsiifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and ornamental flowers.
