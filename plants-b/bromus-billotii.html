Bromus billotii

Bromus billotii, commonly known as the Patagonian brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in temperate to cold climates and is often found in grasslands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct blue-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of dense, compact clusters.

Bromus billotii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native range.
