Betula alleghaniensis var. fallax

Betula alleghaniensis var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and its slender, spreading crown. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
