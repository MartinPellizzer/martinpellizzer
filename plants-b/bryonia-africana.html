Bryonia africana

Bryonia africana, commonly known as the African bryony, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its prickly stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to orange, spiky fruits. Its latex contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
