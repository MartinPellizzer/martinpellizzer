Brickellia enigmatica

Brickellia enigmatica, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
