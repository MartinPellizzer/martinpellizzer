Blakea trinervia var. normanii

Blakea trinervia var. normanii, commonly known as the Norman's Blakea, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
