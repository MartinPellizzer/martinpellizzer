Buchnera aquatica

Buchnera aquatica, commonly known as the waterbuchnera, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters or moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often submerged or floating, contributing to its aquatic habitat.

Buchnera aquatica plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic insects and other small organisms.
