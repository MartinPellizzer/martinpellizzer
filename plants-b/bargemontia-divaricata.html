Bargemontia divaricata

Bargemontia divaricata, commonly known as the variable thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Bargemontia divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
