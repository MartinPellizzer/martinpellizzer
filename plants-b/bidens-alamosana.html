Bidens alamosana

Bidens alamosana, commonly known as the Alamosa tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Bidens alamosana is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
