Bacopa laxiflora

Bacopa laxiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.
