Brizopyrum scirpoideum

Brizopyrum scirpoideum, commonly known as the broadleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Brizopyrum scirpoideum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
