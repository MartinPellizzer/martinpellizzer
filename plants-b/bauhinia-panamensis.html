Bauhinia panamensis

Bauhinia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama burr or Panama bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces flat, woody pods that contain seeds with a hard, bumpy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
