Bursera malacophylla

Bursera malacophylla, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
