Bulbostylis capillaris var. abortiva

Bulbostylis capillaris var. abortiva, commonly known as the abortive hairroot sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

The inflorescence consists of tiny, clustered flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.
