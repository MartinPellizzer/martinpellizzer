Bonnaya oppositifolia

Bonnaya oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf bonnaya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
