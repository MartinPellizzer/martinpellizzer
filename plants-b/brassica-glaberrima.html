Brassica glaberrima

Brassica glaberrima, commonly known as the African mustard, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known crops like cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
