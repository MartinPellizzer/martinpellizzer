Bambusa vulgaris var. atter

Bambusa vulgaris var. atter, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a rapid growth rate, making it popular for ornamental purposes. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control.
