Bauhinia mollicella

Bauhinia mollicella, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, velvety leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
