Bulbophyllum iboense

Bulbophyllum iboense, commonly known as the Ibo Orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and striking floral structure. The plant typically produces intricate, colorful flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic.

Bulbophyllum iboense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
