Bonamia media var. emarginata

Bonamia media var. emarginata, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
