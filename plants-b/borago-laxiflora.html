Borago laxiflora

Borago laxiflora, commonly known as the slender bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

Borago laxiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
