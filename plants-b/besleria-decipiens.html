Besleria decipiens

Besleria decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive besleria, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates and greenhouses.
