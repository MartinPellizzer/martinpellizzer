Buxus gonoclada subsp. toldoensis

Buxus gonoclada subsp.

toldoensis, commonly known as the Sicilian boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slow growth rate, making it ideal for topiary and ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are arranged in dense clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and urban settings.
