Berrya simonis

Berrya simonis, commonly known as the Indian wolfberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Berrya simonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
