Bulbocapnos bracteata

Bulbocapnos bracteata, commonly known as the bracted bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is notable for its unique bracts that enclose the flower buds, giving it its common name.
