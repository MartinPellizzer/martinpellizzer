Bahia desertorum

Bahia desertorum, commonly known as the desert bahia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in desert environments.
