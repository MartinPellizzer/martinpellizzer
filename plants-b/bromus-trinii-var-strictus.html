Bromus trinii var. strictus

Bromus trinii var. strictus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often less than 30 cm in height, with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in certain regions.
