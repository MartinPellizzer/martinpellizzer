Bauhinia ramosissima var. uniflora

Bauhinia ramosissima var. uniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr acacia, is a small shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 7 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that are woody and often covered in a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
