Bignonia fulva

Bignonia fulva, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and can climb by twining around supports.

Bignonia fulva is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in full sun.
