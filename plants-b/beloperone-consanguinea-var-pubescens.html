Beloperone consanguinea var. pubescens

Beloperone consanguinea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy beloperone, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
