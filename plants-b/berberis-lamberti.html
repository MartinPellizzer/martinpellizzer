Berberis lamberti

Berberis lamberti, commonly known as the lambert's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries, which attract wildlife.
