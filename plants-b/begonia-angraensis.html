Begonia angraensis

Begonia angraensis, commonly known as the Angraima begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia angraensis is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which can be used for propagation.
