Brachiaria atrisola

Brachiaria atrisola, commonly known as black brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, coarse leaves and tufted, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is often used in forage production and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.
