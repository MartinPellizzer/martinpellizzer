Brassica trimestris

Brassica trimestris, commonly known as the three-week mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Brassica trimestris is noted for its rapid life cycle, completing its growth within three weeks under favorable conditions.
