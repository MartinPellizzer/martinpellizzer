Boronia pulchella var. alba

Boronia pulchella var. alba, commonly known as the white boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
