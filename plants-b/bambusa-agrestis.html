Bambusa agrestis

Bambusa agrestis, commonly known as the bamboo grass or wild bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types and climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Bambusa agrestis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
