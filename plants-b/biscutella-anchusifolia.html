Biscutella anchusifolia

Biscutella anchusifolia, commonly known as the anchus flower or mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant is characterized by its upright stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Biscutella anchusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
