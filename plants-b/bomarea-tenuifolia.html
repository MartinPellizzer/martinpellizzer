Bomarea tenuifolia

Bomarea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its slender, often vine-like stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
