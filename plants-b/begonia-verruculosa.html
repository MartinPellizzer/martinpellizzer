Begonia verruculosa

Begonia verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia verruculosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
