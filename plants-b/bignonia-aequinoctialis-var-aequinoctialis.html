Bignonia aequinoctialis var. aequinoctialis

Bignonia aequinoctialis var. aequinoctialis, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and can grow up to 20 feet in length. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
