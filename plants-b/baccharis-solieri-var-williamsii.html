Baccharis solieri var. williamsii

Baccharis solieri var. williamsii, commonly known as Williams' baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
