Bourreria retusa

Bourreria retusa, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, similar to other members of the Asteraceae family.
