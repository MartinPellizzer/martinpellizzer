Boerhavia anisophylla f. polytricha

Boerhavia anisophylla f. polytricha, commonly known as the hairy leaf boerhavia, is a variant of the Boerhavia genus native to certain regions of Africa.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves that are hairy and have a unique shape, often with a lobed or serrated edge. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions such as reduced leaf surface area. The flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
