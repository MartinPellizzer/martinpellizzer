Belamcanda chinensis f. flava

Belamcanda chinensis f. flava, commonly known as the yellow jack-in-the-pulpit, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix of bright yellow flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with green and white patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and botanical collections.
