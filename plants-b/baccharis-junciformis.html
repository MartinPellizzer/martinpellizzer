Baccharis junciformis

Baccharis junciformis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its upright, branching structure. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Baccharis junciformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
