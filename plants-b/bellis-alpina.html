Bellis alpina

Bellis alpina, commonly known as the alpine daisy, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Bellis alpina is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
