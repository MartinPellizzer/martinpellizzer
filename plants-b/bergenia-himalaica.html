Bergenia himalaica

Bergenia himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and rosettes of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Bergenia himalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
