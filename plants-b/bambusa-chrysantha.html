Bambusa chrysantha

Bambusa chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as China, India, and Indonesia. This bamboo is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and functional plantings.
