Benzoin geniculatum

Benzoin geniculatum, commonly known as the fragrant gum tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested to make benzoin, a substance used in perfumes and traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes a distinctive zigzag or winding trunk, which gives it its scientific name.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
