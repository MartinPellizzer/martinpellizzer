Brassica exaltata

Brassica exaltata, commonly known as the tall mustard, is an annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its erect stem, which can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, and its small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a distinctive mustard aroma.

Brassica exaltata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
