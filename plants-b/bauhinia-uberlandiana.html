Bauhinia uberlandiana

Bauhinia uberlandiana, commonly known as the "Brazilian flame tree," is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, bright orange to red flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their vibrant color and seasonal display.

The tree also has a shallow root system, making it suitable for areas with well-drained soil.
