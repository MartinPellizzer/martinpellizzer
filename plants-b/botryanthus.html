Botryanthus

Botryanthus, commonly known as the "crown of thorns," is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Botryanthus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
