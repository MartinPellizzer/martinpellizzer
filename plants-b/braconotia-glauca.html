Braconotia glauca

Braconotia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture, while its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Braconotia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
