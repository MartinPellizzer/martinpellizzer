Bulbophyllum gorumense

Bulbophyllum gorumense, commonly known as the Gorumense orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

Bulbophyllum gorumense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
