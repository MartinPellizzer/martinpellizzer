Bahelia longifolia

Bahelia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bahelia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a sweet scent, particularly when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance.

Bahelia longifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
