Baccaurea bracteata var. crassifolia

Baccaurea bracteata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved starfruit, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. Its fruit, similar to the common starfruit, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for ornamental and possibly agricultural purposes in suitable climates.
