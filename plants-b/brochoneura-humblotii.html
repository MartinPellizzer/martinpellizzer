Brochoneura humblotii

Brochoneura humblotii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
