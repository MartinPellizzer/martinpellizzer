Bulbophyllum bakossorum

Bulbophyllum bakossorum, commonly known as the Bakossi orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Africa.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring showy, often fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, where it derives moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Bulbophyllum bakossorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
