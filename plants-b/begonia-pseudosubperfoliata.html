Begonia pseudosubperfoliata

Begonia pseudosubperfoliata, commonly known as the subperfoliate begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, where the leaves have a distinct subperfoliate base that partially encircles the stem. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor plant enthusiasts.
