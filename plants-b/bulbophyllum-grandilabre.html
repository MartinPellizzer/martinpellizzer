Bulbophyllum grandilabre

Bulbophyllum grandilabre, commonly known as the large-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex inflorescence that supports multiple blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
