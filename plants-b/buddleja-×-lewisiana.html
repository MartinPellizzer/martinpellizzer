Buddleja × lewisiana

Buddleja × lewisiana, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, spike-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Buddleja × lewisiana is an important plant for pollinators like butterflies and bees due to its nectar-rich flowers.
