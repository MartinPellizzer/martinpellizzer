Bursaria spinosa

Bursaria spinosa, commonly known as the prickly bursaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, spiny fruits that resemble burrs, which help in seed dispersal. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought.

Bursaria spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
