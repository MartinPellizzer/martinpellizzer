Barleriola inermis

Barleriola inermis, commonly known as the slender sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows low to the ground, often in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, spiny burrs that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Barleriola inermis is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
