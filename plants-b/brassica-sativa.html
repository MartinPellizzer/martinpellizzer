Brassica sativa

Brassica sativa, commonly known as mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, pinnately lobed leaves, and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain round, edible seeds used in various culinary applications. Brassica sativa thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its oilseed or as a green vegetable.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agriculture and nutrition.
