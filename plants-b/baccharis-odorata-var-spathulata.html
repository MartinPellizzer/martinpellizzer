Baccharis odorata var. spathulata

Baccharis odorata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow to broad leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
