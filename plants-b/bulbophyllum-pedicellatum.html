Bulbophyllum pedicellatum

Bulbophyllum pedicellatum, commonly known as the pedicellate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure with fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in shaded, moist habitats. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a contrasting purple or brown lip, and they bloom in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
