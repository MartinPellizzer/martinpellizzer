Boronia lepidota

Boronia lepidota, commonly known as the rough-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually leathery and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name.

Boronia lepidota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
