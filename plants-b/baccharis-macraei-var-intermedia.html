Baccharis macraei var. intermedia

Baccharis macraei var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
