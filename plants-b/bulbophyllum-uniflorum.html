Bulbophyllum uniflorum

Bulbophyllum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, solitary flower that often resembles a miniature orchid. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flower's coloration can vary, often displaying shades of white, pink, or purple, making it a visually striking plant.
