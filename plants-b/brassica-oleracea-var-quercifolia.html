Brassica oleracea var. quercifolia

Brassica oleracea var. quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf cabbage, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a compact, bushy growth habit. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and dark green, often with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its unique appearance.

It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, and it is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
