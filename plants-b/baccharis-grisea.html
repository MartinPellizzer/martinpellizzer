Baccharis grisea

Baccharis grisea, commonly known as gray baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their distinctive color.

Baccharis grisea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
