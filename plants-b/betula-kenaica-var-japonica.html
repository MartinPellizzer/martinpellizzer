Betula kenaica var. japonica

Betula kenaica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and orange beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
