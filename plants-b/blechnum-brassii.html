Blechnum brassii

Blechnum brassii, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow fronds that contrast with its dark green base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the fronds are sometimes used as a decorative element.

The botanical structure of Blechnum brassii includes a creeping rhizome with erect, pinnately compound leaves.
