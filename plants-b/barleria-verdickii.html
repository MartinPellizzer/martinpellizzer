Barleria verdickii

Barleria verdickii, commonly known as the African bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are clustered in spikes, displaying shades of blue and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
