Brassia neglecta

Brassia neglecta, commonly known as the neglected orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellow-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Brassia neglecta has a rhizomatous root system and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and shaded gardens.
