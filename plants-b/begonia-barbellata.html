Begonia barbellata

Begonia barbellata, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, vibrant orange-red flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
