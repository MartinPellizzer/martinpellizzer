Begonia × erythrophylla

Begonia × erythrophylla, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous true flowers. The plant typically has thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated or patterned, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season makes it a favored choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
