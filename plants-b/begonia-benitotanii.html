Begonia benitotanii

Begonia benitotanii, commonly known as the Benito's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

It prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
