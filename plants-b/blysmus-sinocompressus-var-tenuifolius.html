Blysmus sinocompressus var. tenuifolius

Blysmus sinocompressus var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
