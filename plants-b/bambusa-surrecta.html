Bambusa surrecta

Bambusa surrecta, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green to golden-yellow culms that often have a striking variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa surrecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
