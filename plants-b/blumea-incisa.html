Blumea incisa

Blumea incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters, producing a delicate, mist-like appearance.

Blumea incisa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
