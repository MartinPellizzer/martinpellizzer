Bellevalia trifoliata var. typica

Bellevalia trifoliata var. typica, commonly known as the three-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.
