Bauhinia eilertsii

Bauhinia eilertsii, commonly known as the flame tree or the orchid tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble orchids, typically in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia eilertsii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
