Berberis beauverdiana

Berberis beauverdiana, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitats.
