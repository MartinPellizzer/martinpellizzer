Brasilium hirsutum

Brasilium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy Brazil plant, is a flowering species native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
