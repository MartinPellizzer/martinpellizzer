Bulbine striata

Bulbine striata, commonly known as the striped bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive yellow or green stripes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to arid landscapes.
