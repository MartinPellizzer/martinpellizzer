Brownanthus simplex

Brownanthus simplex, commonly known as the simple brownanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand coastal salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is often found in dune environments.

Brownanthus simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes.
