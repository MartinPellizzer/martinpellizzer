Blumea heyneana

Blumea heyneana, commonly known as white blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
