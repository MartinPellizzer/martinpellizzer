Baccharis congesta

Baccharis congesta, commonly known as the clustered baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature.

Baccharis congesta is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.
