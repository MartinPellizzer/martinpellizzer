Beta vulgaris var. coniciformis

Beta vulgaris var. coniciformis, commonly known as the莙菜 or chard, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stalks and colorful, edible leaves that come in shades of white, red, and yellow. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its nutritional value and culinary uses. Its leaves are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like magnesium and potassium.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
