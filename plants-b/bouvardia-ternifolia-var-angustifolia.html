Bouvardia ternifolia var. angustifolia

Bouvardia ternifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bouvardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.

Bouvardia ternifolia var. angustifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
