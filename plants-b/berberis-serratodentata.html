Berberis serratodentata

Berberis serratodentata, commonly known as the serrated toothed barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often armed with spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
