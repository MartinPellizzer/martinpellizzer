Bauhinia megacarpa

Bauhinia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant capuchin bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces large, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Bauhinia megacarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
