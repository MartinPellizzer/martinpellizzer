Biscutella didyma subsp. columnae

Biscutella didyma subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the columnar biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions. Biscutella didyma subsp.

columnae is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of rocky and grassy slopes.
