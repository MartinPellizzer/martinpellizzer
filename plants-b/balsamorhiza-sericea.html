Balsamorhiza sericea

Balsamorhiza sericea, commonly known as the silky balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Balsamorhiza sericea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
