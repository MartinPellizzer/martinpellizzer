Beilschmiedia percoriacea

Beilschmiedia percoriacea, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible, dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
