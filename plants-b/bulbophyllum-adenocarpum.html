Bulbophyllum adenocarpum

Bulbophyllum adenocarpum, commonly known as the glandular fruit orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among experienced orchid growers.
