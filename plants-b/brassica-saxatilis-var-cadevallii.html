Brassica saxatilis var. cadevallii

Brassica saxatilis var. cadevallii, commonly known as the rock cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
