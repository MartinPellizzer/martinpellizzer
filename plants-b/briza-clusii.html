Briza clusii

Briza clusii, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching inflorescences that resemble plumes, composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with slender leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Briza clusii is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
