Bahiella infundibuliflora

Bahiella infundibuliflora, commonly known as the hooded rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a hood-like structure, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
