Berberis pluvisylvatica

Berberis pluvisylvatica, commonly known as the rainforest barberry, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually striking plant.
