Bothriocline trifoliata

Bothriocline trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
