Brassica chinensis f. honsaitai

Brassica chinensis f. honsaitai, commonly known as the Japanese mustard spinach, is a cultivated form of the Chinese mustard plant.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. This variety is often grown for its edible leaves and stems, which are used in Asian cuisine. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is a biennial plant that can be harvested during its first growing season.
