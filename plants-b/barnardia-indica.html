Barnardia indica

Barnardia indica, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to violet in color, blooming from summer to autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Barnardia indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
