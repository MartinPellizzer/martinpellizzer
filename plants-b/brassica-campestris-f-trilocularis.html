Brassica campestris f. trilocularis

Brassica campestris f. trilocularis, commonly known as the Chinese kale or kale, is a cultivar of the mustard family.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are often curled and have a slightly bitter taste. This plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and is rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features include a short, sturdy stem and a rosette formation of leaves.
