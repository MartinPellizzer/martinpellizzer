Borzicactus zanderi

Borzicactus zanderi, commonly known as the Zander's cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Its roots are shallow and adapted to quickly absorb moisture after infrequent rains.

Borzicactus zanderi is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
