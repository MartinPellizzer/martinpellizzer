Begonia complicata

Begonia complicata, commonly known as the curly begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Begonia complicata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
