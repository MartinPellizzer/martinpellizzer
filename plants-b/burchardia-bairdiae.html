Burchardia bairdiae

Burchardia bairdiae, commonly known as the red-flowered burchardia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Burchardia bairdiae is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
