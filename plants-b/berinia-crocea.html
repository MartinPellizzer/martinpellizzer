Berinia crocea

Berinia crocea, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces showy yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Berinia crocea is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
