Berresfordia

Berresfordia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It features showy, violet to purple ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. Berresfordia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowering period spans from late spring to early fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
