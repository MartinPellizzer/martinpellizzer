Betula × raymundii

Betula × raymundii, commonly known as the Raymund's birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a salmon-colored inner layer. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forested areas.

Betula × raymundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
