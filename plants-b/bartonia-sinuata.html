Bartonia sinuata

Bartonia sinuata, commonly known as the wavyleaf bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, narrow leaves with a distinctive wavy edge, which gives it its common name. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
