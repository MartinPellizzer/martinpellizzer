Baccharis × chillanensis

Baccharis × chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean shrub or Chilean baccharis, is a hybrid plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody growth habit and small, often aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flower heads that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in coastal and temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
