Brachiaria malacodes

Brachiaria malacodes, commonly known as Brazilian grass or signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachiaria malacodes is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
