Babiana undulatovenosa

Babiana undulatovenosa, commonly known as the waved-veined babiana, is a bulbous geophyte native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that die back after flowering. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
