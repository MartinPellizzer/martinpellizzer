Betonica hirta

Betonica hirta, commonly known as hedge woundwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of blue or purple flowers in early summer.

Betonica hirta is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
