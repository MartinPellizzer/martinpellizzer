Blechnum danaeaceum

Blechnum danaeaceum, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a glossy, dark green appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
