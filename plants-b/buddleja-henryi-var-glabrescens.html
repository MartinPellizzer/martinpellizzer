Buddleja henryi var. glabrescens

Buddleja henryi var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bush willow, is a shrub native to China.

It features slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable addition to landscapes.
