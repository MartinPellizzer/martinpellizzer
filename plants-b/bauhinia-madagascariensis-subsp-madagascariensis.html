Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp. madagascariensis

Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences composed of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
