Byttneria ancistrodonta

Byttneria ancistrodonta, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to regions across Africa and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
