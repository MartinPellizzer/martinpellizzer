Bambusa pubescens

Bambusa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, green culms that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly serrated edge.

Bambusa pubescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
