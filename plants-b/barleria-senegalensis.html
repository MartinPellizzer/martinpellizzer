Barleria senegalensis

Barleria senegalensis, commonly known as African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Barleria senegalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
