Bulbophyllum xanthoacron

Bulbophyllum xanthoacron, commonly known as the yellow-capped orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with red markings, which are arranged in a loose inflorescence. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
