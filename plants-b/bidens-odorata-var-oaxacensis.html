Bidens odorata var. oaxacensis

Bidens odorata var. oaxacensis, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have serrated edges, while the flowers consist of yellow disc florets surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
