Baccharis lejia

Baccharis lejia, commonly known as the yellow baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

Baccharis lejia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
