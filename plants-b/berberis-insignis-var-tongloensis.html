Berberis insignis var. tongloensis

Berberis insignis var. tongloensis, commonly known as the Tonglung barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
