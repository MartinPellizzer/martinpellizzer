Bourreria cuneifolia var. gracilis

Bourreria cuneifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as slenderleaf burr bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species often used in xeriscaping.
