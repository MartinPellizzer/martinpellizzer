Bupleurum ranunculoides f. arcticum

Bupleurum ranunculoides f. arcticum, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its flowers are yellow with five petals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
