Biscutella didyma subsp. applanata

Biscutella didyma subsp.

applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many economically important plants such as mustard and cabbage.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, glandular hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.
