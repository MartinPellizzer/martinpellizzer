Burchardia grandiflora

Burchardia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Burchardia grandiflora is valued in gardens for its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in partial shade.
