Bulbophyllum korthalsii

Bulbophyllum korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals' orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty.
