Byrsonima cornifolia

Byrsonima cornifolia, commonly known as "caraibe" or "brazilian peach," is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits resembling small peaches.
