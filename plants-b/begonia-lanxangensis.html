Begonia lanxangensis

Begonia lanxangensis, commonly known as the Laos begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a deep green color and prominent veins, while the flowers can be red, pink, or white, often with a contrasting base. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticulture and botanical research.
