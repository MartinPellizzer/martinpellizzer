Bombax costatum var. houardii

Bombax costatum var. houardii, commonly known as the Houard's ceiba, is a variant of the ceiba tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a massive, buttressed trunk and can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 30 meters. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it produces woody pods that contain fibrous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
