Brassica persica

Brassica persica, commonly known as the Persian kale, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia and is adapted to temperate climates.

Brassica persica is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
