Bromus ramosus var. algeriensis

Bromus ramosus var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian brome, is a perennial grass native to North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a slender, upright growth habit with hairy leaves and produces spikelets that are clustered along the stems. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and summer months, and it is often found in open habitats such as steppes and hillsides.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
