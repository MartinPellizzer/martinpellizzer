Brachypodium distachyon var. pentastachyon

Brachypodium distachyon var. pentastachyon, commonly known as the pentastachyon variant, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive inflorescence structure, featuring multiple spikelets arranged in a dense, branched pattern. This variety is often studied for its genetic simplicity and adaptability, making it a model organism in plant biology research. The plant exhibits a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which enhances its drought tolerance and makes it suitable for arid environments.

Its rapid growth cycle and ease of cultivation further contribute to its importance in agricultural and ecological studies.
