Bulbophyllum ornithoglossum

Bulbophyllum ornithoglossum, commonly known as the bird's tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved flower resembling a bird's tongue. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.
