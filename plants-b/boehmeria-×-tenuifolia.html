Boehmeria × tenuifolia

Boehmeria × tenuifolia, commonly known as Chinese hemp or false flax, is a flowering plant in the nettle family (Urticaceae).

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its slender, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant is known for its strong, durable fibers, which have been historically used in textiles and papermaking. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.
