Bauhinia kockiana var. angustifolia

Bauhinia kockiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, stipitate pods that split open to reveal a cluster of seeds. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces showy, violet to pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive foliage and floral display.
