Betula vladimirii

Betula vladimirii, commonly known as Vladimir's birch, is a rare and ancient species of birch native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and white, peeling bark that resembles that of the paper birch. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Betula vladimirii is considered a vulnerable species.
