Brachistus neesianus

Brachistus neesianus, commonly known as the New Zealand bristlebur, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
