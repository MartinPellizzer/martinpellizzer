Berberis lecomtei

Berberis lecomtei, commonly known as Lecomte's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It features spiny, thorned stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Berberis lecomtei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
