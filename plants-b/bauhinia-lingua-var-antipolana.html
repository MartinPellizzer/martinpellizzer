Bauhinia lingua var. antipolana

Bauhinia lingua var. antipolana, commonly known as the Antipolana bauhinia, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

However, it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive optimally.
