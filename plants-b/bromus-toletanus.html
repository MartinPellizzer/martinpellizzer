Bromus toletanus

Bromus toletanus, commonly known as the Toletan grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Bromus toletanus is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
