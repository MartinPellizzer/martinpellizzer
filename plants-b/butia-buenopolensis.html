Butia buenopolensis

Butia buenopolensis, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and parts of Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, spherical fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Butia buenopolensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
