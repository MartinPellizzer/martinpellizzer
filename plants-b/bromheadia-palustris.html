Bromheadia palustris

Bromheadia palustris, commonly known as the marsh bromhead, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and swamps. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear small, clustered white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

Bromheadia palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and aquatic organisms.
