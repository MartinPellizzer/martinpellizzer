Barbarea sicula

Barbarea sicula, commonly known as Sicilian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The plant is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
