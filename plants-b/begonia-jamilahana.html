Begonia jamilahana

Begonia jamilahana, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
