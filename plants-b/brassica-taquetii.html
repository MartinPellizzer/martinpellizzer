Brassica taquetii

Brassica taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Brassica taquetii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological and agricultural contexts.
