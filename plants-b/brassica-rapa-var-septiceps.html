Brassica rapa var. septiceps

Brassica rapa var. septiceps, commonly known as the giant mustard or tree mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.
