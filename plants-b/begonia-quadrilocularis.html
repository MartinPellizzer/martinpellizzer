Begonia quadrilocularis

Begonia quadrilocularis, commonly known as the four-chambered begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
