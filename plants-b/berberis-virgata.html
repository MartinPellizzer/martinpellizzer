Berberis virgata

Berberis virgata, commonly known as the California barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features dark green, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and bright red berries that persist through winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Berberis virgata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
