Blepharoglossum maotiense

Blepharoglossum maotiense, commonly known as the Maotian fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blepharoglossum maotiense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
