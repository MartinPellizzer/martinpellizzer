Buddleja buxifolia

Buddleja buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of purple or pink. The plant has narrow, glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja buxifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
