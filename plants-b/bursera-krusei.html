Bursera krusei

Bursera krusei, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces fragrant resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

Bursera krusei typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
