Buddleja sterniana

Buddleja sterniana, commonly known as the dwarf butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Buddleja sterniana is known for its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering season, typically from late spring to early autumn.
