Butyrospermum parkii var. mangifolium

Butyrospermum parkii var. mangifolium, commonly known as shea butter tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa, particularly the savannas of West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, oily pulp. The plant's fruit contains a high amount of fatty acids, which are extracted to make shea butter. This butter is widely used in cosmetics and skincare products for its moisturizing properties.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
