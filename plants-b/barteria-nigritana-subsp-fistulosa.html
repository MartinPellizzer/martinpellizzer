Barteria nigritana subsp. fistulosa

Barteria nigritana subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the African violets or barteria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender stems.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
