Bernardia axillaris var. houlletiana

Bernardia axillaris var. houlletiana, commonly known as the Houllet's Bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
