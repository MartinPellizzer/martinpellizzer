Bomarea acutifolia var. punctata

Bomarea acutifolia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and can climb or trail. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady, moist environments.

Its unique floral structure and variegated foliage make it a notable addition to botanical gardens and shade gardens.
