Blumea okinawensis

Blumea okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa blumea, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Blumea okinawensis is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine and its ornamental appeal.
