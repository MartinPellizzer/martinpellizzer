Bauhinia longiflora

Bauhinia longiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Bauhinia longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
