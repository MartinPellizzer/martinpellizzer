Billbergia brachysiphon

Billbergia brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer of trichomes. The plant produces strikingly colorful inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, featuring vibrant red, orange, or yellow bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
