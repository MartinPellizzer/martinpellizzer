Buchnera multicaulis var. grandifolia

Buchnera multicaulis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered buchnera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
