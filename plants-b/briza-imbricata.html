Briza imbricata

Briza imbricata, commonly known as common quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, dunes, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Briza imbricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
