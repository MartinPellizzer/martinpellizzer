Bassovia strigosa

Bassovia strigosa, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Bassovia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
