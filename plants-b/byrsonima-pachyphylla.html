Byrsonima pachyphylla

Byrsonima pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved soursop, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its fruit contains a white, fleshy pulp that is sometimes used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
