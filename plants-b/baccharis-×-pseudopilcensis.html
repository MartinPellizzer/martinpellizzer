Baccharis × pseudopilcensis

Baccharis × pseudopilcensis, commonly known as the hybrid baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
