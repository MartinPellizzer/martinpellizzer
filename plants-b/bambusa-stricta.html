Bambusa stricta

Bambusa stricta, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers and is known for its rapid growth rate, making it popular for ornamental and landscaping purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Bambusa stricta is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
