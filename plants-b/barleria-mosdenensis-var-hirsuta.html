Barleria mosdenensis var. hirsuta

Barleria mosdenensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy textures, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
