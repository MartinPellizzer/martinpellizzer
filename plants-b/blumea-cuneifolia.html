Blumea cuneifolia

Blumea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate blue or purple coloration that attracts pollinators.
