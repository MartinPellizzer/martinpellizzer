Berberis colombiana

Berberis colombiana, commonly known as Colombian barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, adding aesthetic value to gardens and natural landscapes.
