Bactris pectinata subsp. hylophila

Bactris pectinata subsp.

hylophila, commonly known as the red-barked palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk with a distinctive reddish-brown bark.

The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescences are typically clustered at the base of the trunk, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

This species is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
