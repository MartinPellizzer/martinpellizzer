Baccharis jocheniana

Baccharis jocheniana, commonly known as the Jochen's Baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis jocheniana is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
