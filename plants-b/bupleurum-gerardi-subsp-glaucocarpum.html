Bupleurum gerardi subsp. glaucocarpum

Bupleurum gerardi subsp.

glaucocarpum, commonly known as the glaucocarpum bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its fruits are schizocarps, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
