Bambusa bitung

Bambusa bitung, commonly known as the Bitung bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a smooth, slightly woody texture. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa bitung is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and is also valued for its strong, durable bamboo poles.
