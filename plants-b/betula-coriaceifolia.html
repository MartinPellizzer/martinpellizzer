Betula coriaceifolia

Betula coriaceifolia, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The tree has slender, pendulous branches and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Betula coriaceifolia plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
