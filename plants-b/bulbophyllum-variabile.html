Bulbophyllum variabile

Bulbophyllum variabile, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its bulbs, which are modified stems, store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers of Bulbophyllum variabile are usually small and clustered, with a wide range of colors, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
