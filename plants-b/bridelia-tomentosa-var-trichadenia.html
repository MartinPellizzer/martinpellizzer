Bridelia tomentosa var. trichadenia

Bridelia tomentosa var. trichadenia, commonly known as the hairy bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.
