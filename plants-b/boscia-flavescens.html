Boscia flavescens

Boscia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Boscia flavescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
