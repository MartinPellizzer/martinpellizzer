Blechnum plumieri var. nigrocarinatum

Blechnum plumieri var. nigrocarinatum, commonly known as the black-ribbed harts-tongue fern, is a variant of the harts-tongue fern characterized by its darkly veined fronds.

It is a terrestrial fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds with prominent, contrasting midribs. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
