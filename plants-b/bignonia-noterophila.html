Bignonia noterophila

Bignonia noterophila, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-red tubular flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or in woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
