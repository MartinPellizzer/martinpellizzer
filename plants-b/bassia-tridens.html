Bassia tridens

Bassia tridens, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Bassia tridens is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability.
