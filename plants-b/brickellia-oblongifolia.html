Brickellia oblongifolia

Brickellia oblongifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brickellia oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
