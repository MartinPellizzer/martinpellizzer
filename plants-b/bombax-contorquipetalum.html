Bombax contorquipetalum

Bombax contorquipetalum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, lantern-like flowers that are typically pink or red. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its bark is thick and corky, providing a unique texture and making it useful in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
