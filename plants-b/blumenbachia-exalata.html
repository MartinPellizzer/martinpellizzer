Blumenbachia exalata

Blumenbachia exalata, commonly known as the highland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Blumenbachia exalata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
