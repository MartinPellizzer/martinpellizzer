Betula × intermedia nothovar. intermedia

Betula × intermedia nothovar. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a deciduous tree that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Betula pendula and Betula pubescens.

It typically has smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, and its leaves are triangular with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, and its flowers are wind-pollinated.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of North America.
