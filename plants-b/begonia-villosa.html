Begonia villosa

Begonia villosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Begonia villosa is valued for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
