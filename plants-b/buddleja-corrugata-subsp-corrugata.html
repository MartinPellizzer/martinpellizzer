Buddleja corrugata subsp. corrugata

Buddleja corrugata subsp.

corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated bush willow, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive corrugated bark and clusters of small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
