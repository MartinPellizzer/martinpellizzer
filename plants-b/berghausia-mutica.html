Berghausia mutica

Berghausia mutica, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Berghausia mutica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
