Bulbophyllum bidenticulatum

Bulbophyllum bidenticulatum, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
