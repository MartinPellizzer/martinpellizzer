Brassica elongata subsp. elongata

Brassica elongata subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits typical of the Brassicaceae family.

This species is valued for its potential in agriculture and as a wild plant for ecological studies.
