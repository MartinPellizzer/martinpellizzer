Buddleja sundaica

Buddleja sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda bush willow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Buddlejaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Buddleja sundaica is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
