Bignonia boliviana

Bignonia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian crossvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces long, climbing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia boliviana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
