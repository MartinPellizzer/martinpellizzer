Balsamiflua euphratica f. pruinosa

Balsamiflua euphratica f. pruinosa, commonly known as the Prunose Euphrasia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its name refers to the balsamic scent it emits and the prunose (grainy) texture of its foliage.

This species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
