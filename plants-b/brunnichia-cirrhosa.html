Brunnichia cirrhosa

Brunnichia cirrhosa, commonly known as the silky beach grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that help reduce water loss in salty, windy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late summer, which are adapted for wind pollination. Brunnichia cirrhosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy shorelines and preventing erosion.

Its ability to thrive in harsh coastal conditions makes it a key species in maritime ecosystems.
