Burmannia foliosa

Burmannia foliosa, commonly known as the "shrimp plant," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescences that resemble tiny shrimp, hence its common name. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas of the forest undergrowth.

Burmannia foliosa is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful flowers.
