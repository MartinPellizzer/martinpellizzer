Bulbophyllum cruttwellii

Bulbophyllum cruttwellii, commonly known as the Cruttwell's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces numerous small, clustered flowers during the wet season.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
