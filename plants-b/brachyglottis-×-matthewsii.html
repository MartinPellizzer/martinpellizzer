Brachyglottis × matthewsii

Brachyglottis × matthewsii, commonly known as the Stewart Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in association with other endemic flora.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
