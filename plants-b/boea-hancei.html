Boea hancei

Boea hancei, commonly known as the silver ponytail plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking silver-gray leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to trail or climb, making it popular in hanging baskets and as a ground cover. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Boea hancei is relatively low maintenance, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
