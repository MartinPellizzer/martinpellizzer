Bulbophyllum otochilum

Bulbophyllum otochilum, commonly known as the Otochilum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to yellowish hairs. The plant produces intricate, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or green with red or orange markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
