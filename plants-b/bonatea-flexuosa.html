Bonatea flexuosa

Bonatea flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bonatea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight curl at the edges.

Bonatea flexuosa is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
