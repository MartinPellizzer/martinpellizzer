Bowlesia tripartita var. reichei

Bowlesia tripartita var. reichei, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a strong, spicy fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
