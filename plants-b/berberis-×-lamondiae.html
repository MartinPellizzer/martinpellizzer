Berberis × lamondiae

Berberis × lamondiae, commonly known as the Lamond barberry, is a hybrid shrub resulting from the cross between Berberis darwinii and Berberis thunbergii.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and vibrant yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
