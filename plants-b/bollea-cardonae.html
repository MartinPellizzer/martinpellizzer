Bollea cardonae

Bollea cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a unique succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features a short, thick stem with spines and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, adding a striking visual element to its native habitat.

Bollea cardonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its ecosystem.
