Baccharis meyeniana

Baccharis meyeniana, commonly known as Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Baccharis meyeniana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
