Berberis vulgaris var. amurensis

Berberis vulgaris var. amurensis, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
