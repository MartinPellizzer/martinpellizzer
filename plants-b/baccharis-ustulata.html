Baccharis ustulata

Baccharis ustulata, commonly known as Texas baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas such as roadsides and prairies. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Baccharis ustulata is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
