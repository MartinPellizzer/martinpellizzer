Bauhinia tessmannii

Bauhinia tessmannii, commonly known as the blue bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its bark is smooth and often greyish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Bauhinia tessmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
