Borzicactus crassiserpens

Borzicactus crassiserpens, commonly known as the thick-tongued cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing, succulent plant adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its thick, fleshy stems store water, making it well-suited for desert conditions.
