Bignonia exoleta

Bignonia exoleta, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia exoleta is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
