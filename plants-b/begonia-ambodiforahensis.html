Begonia ambodiforahensis

Begonia ambodiforahensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often with upright stems and broad, heart-shaped foliage. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, making it a visually appealing species.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
