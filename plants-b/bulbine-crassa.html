Bulbine crassa

Bulbine crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf bulbous plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its root system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Bulbine crassa is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
