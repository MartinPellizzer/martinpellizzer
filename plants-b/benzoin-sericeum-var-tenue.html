Benzoin sericeum var. tenue

Benzoin sericeum var. tenue, commonly known as the silky benzoin, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or reddish in color. Its bark exudes a resinous sap that has been used in traditional medicine and incense.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments.
