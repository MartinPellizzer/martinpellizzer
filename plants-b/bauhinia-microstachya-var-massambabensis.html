Bauhinia microstachya var. massambabensis

Bauhinia microstachya var. massambabensis, commonly known as the Massambaba bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring or early summer. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
