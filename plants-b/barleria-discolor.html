Barleria discolor

Barleria discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria discolor is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
