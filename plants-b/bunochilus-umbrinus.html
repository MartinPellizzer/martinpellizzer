Bunochilus umbrinus

Bunochilus umbrinus, commonly known as the darkleaf shieldbug, is a species of shieldbug native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black body with subtle metallic sheen, which helps it blend into its natural environment. The plant it inhabits, typically a variety of shrubs and grasses, provides both shelter and a source of nutrients for the insect. Its life cycle involves several nymph stages before reaching adulthood, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to its cryptic coloration, it is often overlooked by both predators and humans.
