Briza maxima f. unicolor

Briza maxima f. unicolor, commonly known as the silver grass, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its tall, slender culms and fluffy, silver-white inflorescences.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely textured, bluish-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and naturalized areas.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
