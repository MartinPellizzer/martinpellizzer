Banisteria sellowiana f. grandifolia

Banisteria sellowiana f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with prominent veins. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
