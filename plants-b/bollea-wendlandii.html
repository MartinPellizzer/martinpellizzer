Bollea wendlandii

Bollea wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's bollea, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads, which are surrounded by velvety, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense, bushy crown. Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that provide year-round structure.

Bollea wendlandii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
