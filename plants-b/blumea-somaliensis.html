Blumea somaliensis

Blumea somaliensis, commonly known as Somali blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and narrow, while its flowers are small and clustered, giving it a delicate appearance.

Blumea somaliensis is also noted for its aromatic properties, which may have traditional uses in local communities.
