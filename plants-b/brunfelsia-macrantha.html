Brunfelsia macrantha

Brunfelsia macrantha, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming at night. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful if ingested. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

Brunfelsia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking floral display.
