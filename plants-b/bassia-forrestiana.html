Bassia forrestiana

Bassia forrestiana, commonly known as the Tibetan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Bassia forrestiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
