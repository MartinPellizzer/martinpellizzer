Beta vulgaris var. pusilla

Beta vulgaris var. pusilla, commonly known as the leaf beet, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, edible roots that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its size and appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
