Bulbophyllum derchianum

Bulbophyllum derchianum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid conditions.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
