Barjonia warmingii

Barjonia warmingii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Barjonia warmingii is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
