Brachychiton australis subsp. incanus

Brachychiton australis subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the grey brachychiton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces distinctive lantern-shaped, yellow-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
