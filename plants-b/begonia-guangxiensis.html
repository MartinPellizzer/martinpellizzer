Begonia guangxiensis

Begonia guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to China, specifically Guangxi Province.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
