Bunium alpinum subsp. petraeum

Bunium alpinum subsp.

petraeum, commonly known as alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its seeds are small and contain essential oils, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
