Bupleurum falcatum var. sibthorpianum

Bupleurum falcatum var. sibthorpianum, commonly known as the Sibthorpian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically elongated and slightly hairy, with a distinctive, often wavy margin. This variety is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
