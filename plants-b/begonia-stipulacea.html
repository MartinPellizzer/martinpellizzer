Begonia stipulacea

Begonia stipulacea, commonly known as the leafy begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is notable for its striking coloration, ranging from deep green to variegated yellow and white.

Begonia stipulacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
