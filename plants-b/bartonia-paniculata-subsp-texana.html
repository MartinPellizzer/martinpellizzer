Bartonia paniculata subsp. texana

Bartonia paniculata subsp.

texana, commonly known as Texas bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces distinctive, yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
