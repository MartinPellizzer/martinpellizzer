Bromus volgensis

Bromus volgensis, commonly known as the wandering bromegrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Bromus volgensis is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, where it can form dense colonies.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
