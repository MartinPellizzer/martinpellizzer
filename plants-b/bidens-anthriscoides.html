Bidens anthriscoides

Bidens anthriscoides, commonly known as lesser beggarlice, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs and alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets and five toothed petals, typically blooming from June to September. Its seeds are small, three-angled, and equipped with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Bidens anthriscoides is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, and it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
