Boea hygrometrica

Boea hygrometrica, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silvery-green leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Boea hygrometrica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
