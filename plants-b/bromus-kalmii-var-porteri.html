Bromus kalmii var. porteri

Bromus kalmii var. porteri, commonly known as Kalm's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of the grass.

Bromus kalmii var. porteri is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
