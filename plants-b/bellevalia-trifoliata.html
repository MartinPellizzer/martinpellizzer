Bellevalia trifoliata

Bellevalia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
