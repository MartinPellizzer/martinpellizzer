Begonia smithiana

Begonia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a variety of colors, including deep green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens.
