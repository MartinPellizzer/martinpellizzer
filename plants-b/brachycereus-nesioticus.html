Brachycereus nesioticus

Brachycereus nesioticus, commonly known as the narrow-capped cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its growth form is highly modified to survive in harsh, dry environments, with reduced leaves and thick, fleshy stems.

Brachycereus nesioticus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
