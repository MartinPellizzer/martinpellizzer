Begonia perijaensis

Begonia perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan begonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Peru and northern Bolivia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in the cooler months. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
