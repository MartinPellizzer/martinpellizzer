Boehmeria sieboldiana var. sikokiana

Boehmeria sieboldiana var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese nettle or Japanese flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, hollow stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its strong, durable fibers, which have been historically used in textile production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and industrial contexts.
