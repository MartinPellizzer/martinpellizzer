Berberis nitida

Berberis nitida, commonly known as dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Berberis nitida is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
