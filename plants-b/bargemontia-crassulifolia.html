Bargemontia crassulifolia

Bargemontia crassulifolia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, Bargemontia crassulifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
