Breweria semidigyna

Breweria semidigyna, commonly known as the narrowleaf brewer's grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Breweria semidigyna is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
