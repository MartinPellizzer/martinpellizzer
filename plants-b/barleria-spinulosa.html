Barleria spinulosa

Barleria spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and branched. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, stiff hairs.

Barleria spinulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive flowers and drought tolerance.
