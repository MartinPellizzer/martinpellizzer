Baccharis madagascariensis

Baccharis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar baccharis, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
