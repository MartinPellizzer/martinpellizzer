Begonia fenicis

Begonia fenicis, commonly known as the fire begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Begonia fenicis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value.
