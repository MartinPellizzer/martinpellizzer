Boykinia nuttallii

Boykinia nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's boykinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf morphology and flowering habits.
