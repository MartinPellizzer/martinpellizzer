Bipinnula ctenopetala

Bipinnula ctenopetala, commonly known as the featherleaf ctenopetala, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and hardiness in temperate climates.
