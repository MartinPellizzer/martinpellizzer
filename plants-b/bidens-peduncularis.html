Bidens peduncularis

Bidens peduncularis, commonly known as the tickseed or common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are yellow with dark centers, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
