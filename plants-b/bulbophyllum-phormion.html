Bulbophyllum phormion

Bulbophyllum phormion, commonly known as the Phormion orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapting to its epiphytic or lithophytic growth habits.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
