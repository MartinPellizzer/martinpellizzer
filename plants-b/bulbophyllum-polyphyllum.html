Bulbophyllum polyphyllum

Bulbophyllum polyphyllum, commonly known as the orchid with many leaves, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
