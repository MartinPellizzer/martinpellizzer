Bomarea foertheriana

Bomarea foertheriana, commonly known as the Peruvian bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bomarea foertheriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
