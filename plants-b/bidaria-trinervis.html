Bidaria trinervis

Bidaria trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined bidaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. Bidaria trinervis is typically found in grasslands and open woodland areas, often growing in dry or semi-arid conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
