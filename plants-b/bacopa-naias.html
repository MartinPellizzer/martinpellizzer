Bacopa naias

Bacopa naias, commonly known as water basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and creeping growth habit, often found in wetland habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapting to aquatic environments. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in shallow water.

Bacopa naias is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
