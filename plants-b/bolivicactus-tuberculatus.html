Bolivicactus tuberculatus

Bolivicactus tuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate bolivian cactus, is a species native to the Andes mountains of Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its adaptations to high-altitude environments include thick, waxy stems that store water and reduce transpiration.

Bolivicactus tuberculatus is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in arid conditions.
