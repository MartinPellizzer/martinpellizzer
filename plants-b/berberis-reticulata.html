Berberis reticulata

Berberis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, and its small, bright yellow flowers appear in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used as a decorative plant in gardens.

Berberis reticulata is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
