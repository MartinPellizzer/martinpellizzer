Baliospermum calycinum var. corymbiferum

Baliospermum calycinum var. corymbiferum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a vigorous trailing plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, tuberous roots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its rapid growth, it is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a ground cover in gardens.
