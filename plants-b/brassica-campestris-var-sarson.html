Brassica campestris var. sarson

Brassica campestris var. sarson, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are rich in essential oils and have been used historically in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sunlight.

Brassica campestris var. sarson is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
