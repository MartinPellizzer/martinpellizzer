Bunochilus macrosepalus

Bunochilus macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers with prominent, elongated sepals. It typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid tropical environments.

The orchid's botanical features include a slender stem and broad, fleshy leaves that aid in water retention.
