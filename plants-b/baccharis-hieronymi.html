Baccharis hieronymi

Baccharis hieronymi, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are usually aromatic and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis hieronymi is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
