Brassica campestris subsp. chinensis

Brassica campestris subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or serrated.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by siliqua fruits containing several seeds. It is a biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is widely grown for its edible leaves and seeds.

This subspecies is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
