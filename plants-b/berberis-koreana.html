Berberis koreana

Berberis koreana, commonly known as Korean barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of East Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and small, oval leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Berberis koreana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
