Bupleurum tenuissimum f. subracemosum

Bupleurum tenuissimum f. subracemosum, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a rare and lesser-known subspecies of the Bupleurum genus.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to specific regions of Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its niche habitat, with a compact growth form and specialized root systems.
