Begonia sutherlandii var. subcuneata

Begonia sutherlandii var. subcuneata, commonly known as the Sutherland's begonia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a short flowering period, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid conditions and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
