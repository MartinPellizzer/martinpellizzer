Baeria coronaria f. mutica

Baeria coronaria f. mutica, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variant of Baeria coronaria is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
