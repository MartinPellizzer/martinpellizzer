Bulbophyllum teretilabre

Bulbophyllum teretilabre, commonly known as the slender-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive elongated labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
