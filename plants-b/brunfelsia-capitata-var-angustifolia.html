Brunfelsia capitata var. angustifolia

Brunfelsia capitata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of stamens. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
