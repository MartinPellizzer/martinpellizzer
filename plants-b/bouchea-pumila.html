Bouchea pumila

Bouchea pumila, commonly known as the silky bouchea, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features soft, silvery foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Bouchea pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
