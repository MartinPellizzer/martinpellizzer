Buchnera elongata f. genuina

Buchnera elongata f. genuina, commonly known as the slender leafbuchnera, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
