Begonia jubar

Begonia jubar, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, often appearing in the spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and modest blooms, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
