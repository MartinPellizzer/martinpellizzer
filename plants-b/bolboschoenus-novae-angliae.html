Bolboschoenus novae-angliae

Bolboschoenus novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, white flowers in the summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments. It thrives in brackish or freshwater marshes and is often found in tidal areas.

Bolboschoenus novae-angliae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
