Bunias spinosa

Bunias spinosa, commonly known as the prickly mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Bunias spinosa is known for its strong odor and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
