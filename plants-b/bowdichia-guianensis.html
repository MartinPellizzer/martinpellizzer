Bowdichia guianensis

Bowdichia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan burrbean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is known for its distinctive, clustered pods that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Bowdichia guianensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
