Biscutella didyma var. pseudoalgeriensis

Biscutella didyma var. pseudoalgeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in loose racemes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
