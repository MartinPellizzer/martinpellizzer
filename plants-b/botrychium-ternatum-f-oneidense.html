Botrychium ternatum f. oneidense

Botrychium ternatum f. oneidense, commonly known as the Oneida brittle bladder fern, is a fern species characterized by its unique morphology and ecological niche.

It features a small, delicate frond with a distinctive, brittle, and segmented structure, typical of the Botrychium genus. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are clustered in a dense, club-like formation at the base of the frond.

This fern is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements.
