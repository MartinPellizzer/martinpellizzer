Bartsia garidelii

Bartsia garidelii, commonly known as the golden bartsia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bartsia garidelii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
