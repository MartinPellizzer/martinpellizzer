Brenesia

Brenesia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible stems. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots help stabilize the soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Brenesia is also valued for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
