Boopis anthemoides var. subscandens

Boopis anthemoides var. subscandens, commonly known as the cliff rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
