Bromus condensatus var. pomoensis

Bromus condensatus var. pomoensis, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it has small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.
