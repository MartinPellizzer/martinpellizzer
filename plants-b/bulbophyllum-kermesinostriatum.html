Bulbophyllum kermesinostriatum

Bulbophyllum kermesinostriatum, commonly known as the Kermesino-striated orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that display a striking combination of colors, typically with a yellow or orange base and intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks or branches.

This orchid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
