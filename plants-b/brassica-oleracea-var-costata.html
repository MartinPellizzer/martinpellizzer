Brassica oleracea var. costata

Brassica oleracea var. costata, commonly known as the kohlrabi, is a leafy vegetable with a swollen stem that is typically eaten as a vegetable.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant has thick, ribbed, and often purple or green colored stems that are tender and crunchy when harvested young. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly hairy, while the flowering stems produce small, yellow flowers.

Kohlrabi is rich in vitamins C and B6, and it is valued for its mild, sweet flavor and nutritional benefits.
