Bucco spicata

Bucco spicata, commonly known as the spiked buccus, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiky leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and requires high humidity to flourish. Its botanical name reflects its unique morphology, with "spicata" referring to the spiky appearance of its foliage.

Due to habitat loss, Bucco spicata is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
