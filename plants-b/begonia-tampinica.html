Begonia tampinica

Begonia tampinica, commonly known as the Tampinica begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Begonia tampinica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
