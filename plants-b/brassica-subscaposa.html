Brassica subscaposa

Brassica subscaposa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and small, alternate leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy. Its flowers are bright yellow, arranged in racemes, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
