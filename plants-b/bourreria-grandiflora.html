Bourreria grandiflora

Bourreria grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are bright purple berries that mature in the fall and are edible for humans and wildlife.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly aromatic scent.
