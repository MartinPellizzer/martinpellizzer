Bactris monticola

Bactris monticola, commonly known as the mountain palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife. Bactris monticola thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various species in its native range.
