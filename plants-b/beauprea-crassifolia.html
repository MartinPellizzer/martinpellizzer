Beauprea crassifolia

Beauprea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf beauprea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
