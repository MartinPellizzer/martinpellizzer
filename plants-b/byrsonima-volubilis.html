Byrsonima volubilis

Byrsonima volubilis, commonly known as the Brazilian mulberry or wild mulberry, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Byrsonima volubilis thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.
