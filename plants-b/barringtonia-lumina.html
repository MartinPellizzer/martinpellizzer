Barringtonia lumina

Barringtonia lumina, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has a distinctive, spiny, tree-like structure with smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, providing a lush green appearance.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature.
