Bolboschoenus yagara

Bolboschoenus yagara, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
