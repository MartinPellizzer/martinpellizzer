Begonia sinuata var. pantiensis

Begonia sinuata var. pantiensis, commonly known as the Pantiense begonia, is a tropical plant native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that display a vibrant mix of green and red hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
