Bignonia inaequilatera

Bignonia inaequilatera, commonly known as the cross vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
