Betula pendula subsp. fontqueri

Betula pendula subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the weeping birch, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by small, triangular leaves that turn golden in autumn. Its bark is smooth and white when young, developing into a flaky, darker texture as the tree matures.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
