Banisteria aculeata

Banisteria aculeata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Banisteria aculeata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical environments.
