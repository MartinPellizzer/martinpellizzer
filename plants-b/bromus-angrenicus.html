Bromus angrenicus

Bromus angrenicus, commonly known as the Angren grass, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and spikelets that are typically two-ranked. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Bromus angrenicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
