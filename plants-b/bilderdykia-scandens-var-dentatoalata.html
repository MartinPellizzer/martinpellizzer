Bilderdykia scandens var. dentatoalata

Bilderdykia scandens var. dentatoalata, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
