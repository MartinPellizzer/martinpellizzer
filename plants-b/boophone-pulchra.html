Boophone pulchra

Boophone pulchra, commonly known as the "Rainbow Boophone," is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers that range from violet to yellow. Boophone pulchra is a bulbous perennial, known for its ability to store water in its underground corm.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
