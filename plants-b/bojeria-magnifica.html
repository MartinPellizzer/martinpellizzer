Bojeria magnifica

Bojeria magnifica, commonly known as the magnolia tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree can grow up to 60 feet tall and has a pyramidal shape when young, becoming more rounded with age.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
