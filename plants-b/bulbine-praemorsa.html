Bulbine praemorsa

Bulbine praemorsa, commonly known as the yellow bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun with minimal water.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
