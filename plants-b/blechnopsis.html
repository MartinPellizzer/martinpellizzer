Blechnopsis

Blechnopsis, commonly known as the sword fern, is a genus of evergreen ferns native to the Southern Hemisphere.

These plants are characterized by their tall, arching fronds that can reach several feet in length. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Blechnopsis species have rhizomatous growth and produce spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of their leaves.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to different climatic conditions across their native ranges.
