Brassica

Brassica, commonly known as mustard, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Brassica species produce small, cross-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, and they are followed by pods containing seeds. Many Brassica plants are cultivated for their edible leaves, flowers, or seeds, such as broccoli, cauliflower, and cabbage.

The genus is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates and soils, making it a versatile crop worldwide.
