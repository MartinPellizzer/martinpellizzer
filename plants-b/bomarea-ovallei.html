Bomarea ovallei

Bomarea ovallei, commonly known as the ovalleaf bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth, waxy texture, while its stems are often tuberous.

Bomarea ovallei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
