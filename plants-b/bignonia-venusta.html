Bignonia venusta

Bignonia venusta, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and hairy underneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Bignonia venusta is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
