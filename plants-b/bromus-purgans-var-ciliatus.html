Bromus purgans var. ciliatus

Bromus purgans var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with hairy foliage, and its leaves are often pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous spikelets.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
