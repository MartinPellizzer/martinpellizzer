Boehmeria japonica var. tricuspis

Boehmeria japonica var. tricuspis, commonly known as Japanese nettle or Japanese flax, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with finely toothed, triangular leaves that have a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
