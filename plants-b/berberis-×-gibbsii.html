Berberis × gibbsii

Berberis × gibbsii, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Berberidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and bright, variegated foliage that often features yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders and rock gardens.
