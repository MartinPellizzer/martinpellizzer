Butyrospermum parkii var. parvifolium

Butyrospermum parkii var. parvifolium, commonly known as small-leaf shea, is a variant of the shea tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smaller, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is valued for its nut, which yields a rich, creamy butter used in cosmetics and food. It thrives in dry, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
