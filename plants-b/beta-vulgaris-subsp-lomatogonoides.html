Beta vulgaris subsp. lomatogonoides

Beta vulgaris subsp.

lomatogonoides, commonly known as the leaf beet or beetroot leaf, is a lesser-known subspecies of the common beet. It is characterized by its fibrous, leafy stems and a distinct, slightly bitter taste. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamin C. Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
