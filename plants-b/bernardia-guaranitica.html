Bernardia guaranitica

Bernardia guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani berry, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used traditionally in South American medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
