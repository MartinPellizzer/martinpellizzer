Briza reniformis

Briza reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear delicate, feathery inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds a subtle aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
