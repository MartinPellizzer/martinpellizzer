Bulbophyllum vietnamense

Bulbophyllum vietnamense, commonly known as the Vietnam bulbophyllum, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
