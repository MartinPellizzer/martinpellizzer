Brachiaria platyrhachis

Brachiaria platyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-ridge brachiaria, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage production due to its high nutritional value.

Brachiaria platyrhachis is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
