Bertiera racemosa var. racemosa

Bertiera racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the African false daisy, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
