Barbarea vulgaris var. sibirica

Barbarea vulgaris var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in colder regions.
