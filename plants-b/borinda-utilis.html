Borinda utilis

Borinda utilis, commonly known as the red-stemmed borinda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
