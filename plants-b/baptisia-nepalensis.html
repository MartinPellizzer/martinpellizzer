Baptisia nepalensis

Baptisia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature.

Baptisia nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
