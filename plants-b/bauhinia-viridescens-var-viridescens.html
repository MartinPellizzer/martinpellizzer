Bauhinia viridescens var. viridescens

Bauhinia viridescens var. viridescens, commonly known as the green bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It features palmate leaves composed of three to five lance-shaped leaflets, which are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. Its fruits are pods that split open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
