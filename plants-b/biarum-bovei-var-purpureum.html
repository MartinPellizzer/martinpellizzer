Biarum bovei var. purpureum

Biarum bovei var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique color and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
