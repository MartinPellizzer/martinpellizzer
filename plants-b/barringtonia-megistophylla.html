Barringtonia megistophylla

Barringtonia megistophylla, commonly known as the giant fan-leafed screwtree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are typically pollinated by bats and moths. Its fruit is a large, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional medicinal uses.
