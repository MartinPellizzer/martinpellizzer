Brassica repanda subsp. galissieri

Brassica repanda subsp.

galissieri, commonly known as the Galissier's mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly the Pyrenees and the Alps.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lobed and have a pungent mustard aroma, characteristic of the genus Brassica.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
