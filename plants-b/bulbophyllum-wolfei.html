Bulbophyllum wolfei

Bulbophyllum wolfei, commonly known as the Wolfe's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, tropical environments.

The flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
