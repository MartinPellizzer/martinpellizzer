Binaria uruguayensis

Binaria uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Binaria uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
