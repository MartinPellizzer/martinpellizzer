Berberis heterophylla

Berberis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and alternate, compound leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
