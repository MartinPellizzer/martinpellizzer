Braunsia edentula

Braunsia edentula, commonly known as the toothless braunsia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their purported tonic properties. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Braunsia edentula is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
