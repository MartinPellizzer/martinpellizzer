Berberis morana

Berberis morana, commonly known as the Morana barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance.

The berries that follow the flowers are dark red to black and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
