Berberis chilensis var. diffusa

Berberis chilensis var. diffusa, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or ornamental plant.
