Biscutella algeriensis

Biscutella algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a distinctive hairy stem and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Biscutella algeriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
