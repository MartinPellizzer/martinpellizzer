Begonia henrilaportei

Begonia henrilaportei, commonly known as the Henri Laporte begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a subtle sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among collectors and horticulturists.
