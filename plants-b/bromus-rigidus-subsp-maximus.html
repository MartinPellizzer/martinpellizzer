Bromus rigidus subsp. maximus

Bromus rigidus subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect culms with rough, stiff leaves that are often covered in short hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is often tinged with purple.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
