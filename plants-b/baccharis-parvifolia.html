Baccharis parvifolia

Baccharis parvifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

Baccharis parvifolia is drought-tolerant and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
