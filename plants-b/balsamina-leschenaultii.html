Balsamina leschenaultii

Balsamina leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are zygomorphic, with a spurred petal that serves as a nectar guide for pollinators.
