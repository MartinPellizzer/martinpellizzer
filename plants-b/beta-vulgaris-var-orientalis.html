Beta vulgaris var. orientalis

Beta vulgaris var. orientalis, commonly known as the beetroot or garden beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically red in color, though they can also be yellow or white. The plant produces small, green, triangular leaves that are edible and often consumed as a vegetable. The roots are rich in nutrients such as iron, fiber, and various vitamins, making it a valuable crop.

It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
