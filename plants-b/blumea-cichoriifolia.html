Blumea cichoriifolia

Blumea cichoriifolia, commonly known as the dandelion leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Blumea cichoriifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
