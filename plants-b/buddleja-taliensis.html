Buddleja taliensis

Buddleja taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis Buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja taliensis is relatively hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
