Begonia intermedia

Begonia intermedia, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Begonia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
