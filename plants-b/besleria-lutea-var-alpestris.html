Besleria lutea var. alpestris

Besleria lutea var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine yellow gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces showy, tubular blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
