Bakeridesia rufivela

Bakeridesia rufivela, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
