Bouteloua pilosa

Bouteloua pilosa, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, tufted seed heads. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
