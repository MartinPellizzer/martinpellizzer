Bupleurum imaicola

Bupleurum imaicola, commonly known as the Japanese angelica root, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its root system is notable for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Bupleurum imaicola thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
