Barleria boivinii

Barleria boivinii, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and grows in a spreading, low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Barleria boivinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
