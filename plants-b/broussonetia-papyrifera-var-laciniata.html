Broussonetia papyrifera var. laciniata

Broussonetia papyrifera var. laciniata, commonly known as the Japanese paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fuzzy, spherical fruits. Its bark is fibrous and has been historically used for making paper and textiles.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
