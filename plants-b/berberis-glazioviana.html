Berberis glazioviana

Berberis glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
