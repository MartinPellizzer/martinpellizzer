Bolivicactus saint-pieanus

Bolivicactus saint-pieanus, commonly known as the Saint-Pierre's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
