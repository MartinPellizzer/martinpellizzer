Bulbophyllum nemorale

Bulbophyllum nemorale, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
