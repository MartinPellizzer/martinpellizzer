Bromus intermedius var. polystachus

Bromus intermedius var. polystachus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
