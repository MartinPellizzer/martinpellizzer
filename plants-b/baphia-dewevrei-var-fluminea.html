Baphia dewevrei var. fluminea

Baphia dewevrei var. fluminea, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a species native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its natural habitat. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The bark is smooth and dark brown, often exuding a faintly aromatic resin.
