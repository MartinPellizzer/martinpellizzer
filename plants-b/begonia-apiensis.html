Begonia apiensis

Begonia apiensis, commonly known as the Api Mountains begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Api Mountains in Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, silver, and reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
