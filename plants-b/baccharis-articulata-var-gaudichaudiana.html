Baccharis articulata var. gaudichaudiana

Baccharis articulata var. gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a woody stem with distinctive branching patterns and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
