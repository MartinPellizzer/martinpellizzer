Becium burchellianum

Becium burchellianum, commonly known as the Burchell's becium, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its small, elliptical leaves are usually arranged opposite each other on the stems.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
