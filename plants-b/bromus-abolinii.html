Bromus abolinii

Bromus abolinii, commonly known as the Aboline brome, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Bromus abolinii plays a minor ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing forage for local wildlife.
