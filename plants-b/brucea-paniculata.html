Brucea paniculata

Brucea paniculata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are often borne on long, branching stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Brucea paniculata is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
