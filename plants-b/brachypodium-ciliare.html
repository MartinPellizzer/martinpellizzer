Brachypodium ciliare

Brachypodium ciliare, commonly known as mouse barley, is a small annual grass native to Europe and Asia.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Brachypodium ciliare is characterized by its short stature and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
