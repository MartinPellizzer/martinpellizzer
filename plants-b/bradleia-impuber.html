Bradleia impuber

Bradleia impuber, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Bradleia impuber thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of swamps.
