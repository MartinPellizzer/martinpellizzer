Blechnum anthracinum

Blechnum anthracinum, commonly known as the red-stemmed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

The fronds are pinnate, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often covered in small, scale-like structures.
