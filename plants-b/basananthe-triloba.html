Basananthe triloba

Basananthe triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features a short rhizome with aerial roots and produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Its leaves are typically broad, glossy, and have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
