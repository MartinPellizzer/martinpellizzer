Baccharis bifrons

Baccharis bifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of waterways, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baccharis bifrons is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local ecosystems.
