Bomarea subglobosa

Bomarea subglobosa, commonly known as the globular bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bulbous, often colorful underground tubers, which are a key feature of its botanical structure. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
