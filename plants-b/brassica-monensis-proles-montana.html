Brassica monensis proles montana

Brassica monensis proles montana, commonly known as the mountain cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as broccoli and mustard. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
