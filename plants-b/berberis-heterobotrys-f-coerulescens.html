Berberis heterobotrys f. coerulescens

Berberis heterobotrys f. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered barberry, is a cultivar of the Berberis genus native to China.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that appear in late spring, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and small, leathery leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
