Brachystachyum densiflorum

Brachystachyum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brachystachyum densiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ability to stabilize soil and its aesthetic value in garden settings.
