Bupleurum aristatum var. contractum

Bupleurum aristatum var. contractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
