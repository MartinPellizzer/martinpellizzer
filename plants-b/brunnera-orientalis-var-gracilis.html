Brunnera orientalis var. gracilis

Brunnera orientalis var. gracilis, commonly known as Siberian bugloss, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, silvery-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces delicate blue to purple flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
