Bombax mossambicense

Bombax mossambicense, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures for its fibers and bark.
