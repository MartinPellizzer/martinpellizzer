Betula glabra

Betula glabra, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Betula glabra plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
