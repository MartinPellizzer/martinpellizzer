Baeckea carnosa

Baeckea carnosa, commonly known as the pink mistflower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, pink to pale pink flowers that appear in late summer to autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Baeckea carnosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
