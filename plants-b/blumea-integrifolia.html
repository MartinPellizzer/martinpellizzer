Blumea integrifolia

Blumea integrifolia, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Blumea integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
