Blumea longifolia

Blumea longifolia, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is often found in moist, tropical environments, growing along stream banks and in forest understories.

Blumea longifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
