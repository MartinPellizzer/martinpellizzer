Brassica humilis var. repanda

Brassica humilis var. repanda, commonly known as the dwarf cabbage, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, cabbage-like flavor, making it suitable for culinary use.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
