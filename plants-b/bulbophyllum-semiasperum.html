Bulbophyllum semiasperum

Bulbophyllum semiasperum, commonly known as the rough-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

The flowers are small but numerous, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
