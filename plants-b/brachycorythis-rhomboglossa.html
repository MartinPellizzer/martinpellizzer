Brachycorythis rhomboglossa

Brachycorythis rhomboglossa, commonly known as the diamond-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive leaves that are rhombic in shape, hence the specific epithet *rhomboglossa*. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
