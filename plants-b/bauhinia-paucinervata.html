Bauhinia paucinervata

Bauhinia paucinervata, commonly known as the flame tree or the poinciana, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Bauhinia paucinervata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Its seeds are encased in a leathery pod and can remain viable for several years.
