Bulbophyllum longiscapum

Bulbophyllum longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and water storage.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
