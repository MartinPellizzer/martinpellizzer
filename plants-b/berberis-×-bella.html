Berberis × bella

Berberis × bella, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features prickly, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis × bella is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
