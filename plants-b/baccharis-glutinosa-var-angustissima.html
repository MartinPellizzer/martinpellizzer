Baccharis glutinosa var. angustissima

Baccharis glutinosa var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf gumplant, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in arid regions.

Its aromatic foliage and flowering habits make it an important component of local ecosystems.
