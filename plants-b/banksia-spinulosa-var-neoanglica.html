Banksia spinulosa var. neoanglica

Banksia spinulosa var. neoanglica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to coastal and sandy environments.
