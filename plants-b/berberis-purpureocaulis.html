Berberis purpureocaulis

Berberis purpureocaulis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.
