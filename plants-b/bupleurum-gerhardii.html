Bupleurum gerhardii

Bupleurum gerhardii, commonly known as the mountain angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Bupleurum gerhardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
