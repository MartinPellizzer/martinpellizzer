Bauhinia corniculata

Bauhinia corniculata, commonly known as the Crown flower or the Pea flower, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Bauhinia corniculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
