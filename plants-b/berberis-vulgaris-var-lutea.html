Berberis vulgaris var. lutea

Berberis vulgaris var. lutea, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange foliage, which provides striking visual interest in gardens. The plant features spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, though often tart, and attract birds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
