Brassica cheiranthos var. brachypodos

Brassica cheiranthos var. brachypodos, commonly known as the short-stemmed wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
