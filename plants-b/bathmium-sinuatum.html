Bathmium sinuatum

Bathmium sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are covered in specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. These bladders are filled with water and can rapidly inflate to capture tiny aquatic animals. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in ponds and ditches.

Its unique adaptations for nutrient acquisition make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
