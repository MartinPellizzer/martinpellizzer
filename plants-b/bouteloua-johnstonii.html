Bouteloua johnstonii

Bouteloua johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, blue-green leaves and produces fluffy, spike-like seed heads that are characteristic of the grass family. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to arid environments, making it a valuable component of native plant communities.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
