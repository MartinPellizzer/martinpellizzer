Bothriochloa bladhii

Bothriochloa bladhii, commonly known as bladh's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
