Begonia × bertinii

Begonia × bertinii, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a decorative plant prized for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pavonina, combining the best traits of both parents. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of tubular or bell-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, Begonia × bertinii is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
