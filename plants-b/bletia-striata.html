Bletia striata

Bletia striata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce multiple leaves and flower spikes. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a metallic sheen. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive green stripe along the petals.

Bletia striata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
