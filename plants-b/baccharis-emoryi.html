Baccharis emoryi

Baccharis emoryi, commonly known as Emory's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Baccharis emoryi plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
