Baccharis gnidiifolia

Baccharis gnidiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
