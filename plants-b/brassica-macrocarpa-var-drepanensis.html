Brassica macrocarpa var. drepanensis

Brassica macrocarpa var. drepanensis, commonly known as the giant cabbage or giant mustard, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible leaves in certain regions.
