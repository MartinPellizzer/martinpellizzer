Brassica rapa var. parachinensis

Brassica rapa var. parachinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or field mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its mustard oils and glucosinolates.

This species is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
