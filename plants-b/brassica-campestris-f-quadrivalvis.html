Brassica campestris f. quadrivalvis

Brassica campestris f. quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved cabbage, is a botanical variant of the common cabbage.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-valved structure in its inflorescence, which sets it apart from typical Brassica species. This cultivar is primarily cultivated for its edible leaves and stalks, which are used in various culinary applications. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both agricultural and horticultural study.
