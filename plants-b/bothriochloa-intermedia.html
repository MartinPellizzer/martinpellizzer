Bothriochloa intermedia

Bothriochloa intermedia, commonly known as red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish tawny coloration, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in semi-arid regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that is often tinged with red or brown hues.

Bothriochloa intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
