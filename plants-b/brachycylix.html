Brachycylix

Brachycylix, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, which are typical of gymnosperms. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.

Brachycylix is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
