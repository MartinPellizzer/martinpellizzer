Bignonia pentaphylla

Bignonia pentaphylla, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Bignonia pentaphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
