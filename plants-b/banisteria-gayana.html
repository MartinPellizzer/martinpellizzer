Banisteria gayana

Banisteria gayana, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Banisteria gayana is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
