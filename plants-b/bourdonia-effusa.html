Bourdonia effusa

Bourdonia effusa, commonly known as the red-flowered false lily, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, showy red flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
