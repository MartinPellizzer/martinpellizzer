Begonia bambusetorum

Begonia bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo begonia, is a unique plant characterized by its bamboo-like stems and striking foliage.

It features elongated, upright stems that resemble bamboo, with smooth, often reddish or greenish bark. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy texture and a vibrant green color. This species is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
