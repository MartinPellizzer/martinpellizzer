Bruguiera eriopetala var. exsetata

Bruguiera eriopetala var. exsetata, commonly known as the mangrove fern, is a variant of the bruguiera species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, often found in mangrove swamps and estuaries. The plant has pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that aid in gas exchange in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a leathery texture, adapted to withstand saltwater spray.

This variant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
