Barlia prisca

Barlia prisca, commonly known as the ancient bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Barlia prisca is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its historical significance is reflected in its name, which references its long-standing presence in botanical records.
