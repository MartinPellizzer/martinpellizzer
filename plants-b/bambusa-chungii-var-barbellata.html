Bambusa chungii var. barbellata

Bambusa chungii var. barbellata, commonly known as the barbellate bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved and barbed leaf sheaths that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo with slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage is lush and dense, making it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
