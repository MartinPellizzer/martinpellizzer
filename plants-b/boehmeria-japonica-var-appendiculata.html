Boehmeria japonica var. appendiculata

Boehmeria japonica var. appendiculata, commonly known as Japanese arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features fibrous, rhizomatous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seeds. It is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a source of natural fiber.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and utilitarian purposes in its native habitat.
