Brassaiopsis palmata

Brassaiopsis palmata, commonly known as the giant lemur palm, is a strikingly tall palm species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant has a slender trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits. Brassaiopsis palmata thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
