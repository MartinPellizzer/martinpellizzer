Bubon galbanum var. dentatum

Bubon galbanum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed galbanum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green resinous sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name "dentatum.". It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
