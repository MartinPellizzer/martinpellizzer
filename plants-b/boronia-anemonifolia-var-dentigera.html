Boronia anemonifolia var. dentigera

Boronia anemonifolia var. dentigera, commonly known as the serrated boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, often with serrated edges, giving the plant its specific epithet. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or understoreys.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
