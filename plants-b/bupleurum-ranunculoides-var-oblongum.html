Bupleurum ranunculoides var. oblongum

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
