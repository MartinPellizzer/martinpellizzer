Brassica napus subsp. oleifera

Brassica napus subsp.

oleifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and large, triangular leaves. It is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

The plant produces a significant amount of oil, which is extracted from the seeds and used in cooking, biodiesel, and industrial applications. It has a robust root system that helps in nutrient uptake and soil stabilization.

The plant's rapid growth and high yield make it an important crop in agricultural systems worldwide.
