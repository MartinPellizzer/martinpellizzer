Bignonia cupulata

Bignonia cupulata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are woody and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 15 meters in height.

Bignonia cupulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
