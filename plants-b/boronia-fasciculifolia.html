Boronia fasciculifolia

Boronia fasciculifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boronia fasciculifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
