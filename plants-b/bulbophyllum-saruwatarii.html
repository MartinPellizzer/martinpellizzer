Bulbophyllum saruwatarii

Bulbophyllum saruwatarii, commonly known as the Saruwatari's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive shape that aids in pollination.

The bulbophyllum genus is known for its diverse morphology, and B. saruwatarii is no exception, showcasing adaptations suited to its specific ecological niche.
