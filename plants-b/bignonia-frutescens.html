Bignonia frutescens

Bignonia frutescens, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces long, pendulous clusters of tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist, wooded areas.

Bignonia frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
