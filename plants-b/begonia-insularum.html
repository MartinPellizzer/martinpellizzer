Begonia insularum

Begonia insularum, commonly known as the island begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Begonia insularum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
