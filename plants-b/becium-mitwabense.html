Becium mitwabense

Becium mitwabense, commonly known as the Mitwabense bean, is a rare leguminous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a slightly hairy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Becium mitwabense is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
