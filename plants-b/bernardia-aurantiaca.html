Bernardia aurantiaca

Bernardia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

Bernardia aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
