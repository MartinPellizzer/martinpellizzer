Begonia × buimontana

Begonia × buimontana, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a cross between two species of begonias, typically Begonia semperflorens and Begonia masoniana.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often displaying a striking contrast to the flowers. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia × buimontana is popular in gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
