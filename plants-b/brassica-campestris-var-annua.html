Brassica campestris var. annua

Brassica campestris var. annua, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth cycle, typically completing its life cycle within a single growing season. The plant features alternate, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are small and contained in siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits.

Wild mustard is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.
