Bilderdykia scandens var. cristata

Bilderdykia scandens var. cristata, commonly known as the crested wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive crest or ridge running down the center, giving it its unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
