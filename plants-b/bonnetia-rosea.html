Bonnetia rosea

Bonnetia rosea, commonly known as the rose bush or rose tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bonnetia rosea is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
