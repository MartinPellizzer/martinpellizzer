Begonia monantha

Begonia monantha, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent white veins and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with a fibrous root system, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

The flowers are usually borne on short pedicels and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
