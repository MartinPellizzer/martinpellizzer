Berlinia occidentalis

Berlinia occidentalis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It is often found in moist forests and is valued for its durable wood.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
