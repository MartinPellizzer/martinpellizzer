Bromus bolivianus

Bromus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian brome, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus bolivianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be a component of local ecosystems in its native range.
