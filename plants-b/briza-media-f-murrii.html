Briza media f. murrii

Briza media f. murrii, commonly known as the narrowleaf goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble a beard. The plant produces small, greenish to pale blue flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Briza media f. murrii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
