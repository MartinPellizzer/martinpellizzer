Bulbophyllum acehense

Bulbophyllum acehense, commonly known as the Aceh's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Bulbophyllum acehense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
