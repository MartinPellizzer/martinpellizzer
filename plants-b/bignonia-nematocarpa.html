Bignonia nematocarpa

Bignonia nematocarpa, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, orange-red tubular flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Bignonia nematocarpa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
