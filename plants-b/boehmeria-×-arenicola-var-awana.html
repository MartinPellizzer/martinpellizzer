Boehmeria × arenicola var. awana

Boehmeria × arenicola var. awana, commonly known as the awana flax, is a hybrid plant in the nettle family (Urticaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It is valued for its strong, lightweight fibers, which are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.

Its growth habit is typically as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.
