Boronia pilonema

Boronia pilonema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, with a distinctive, leathery texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
