Boopis australis var. leptophylla

Boopis australis var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf boopis, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
