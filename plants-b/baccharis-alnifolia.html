Baccharis alnifolia

Baccharis alnifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis alnifolia is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
