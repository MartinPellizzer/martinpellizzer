Betula nana subsp. relicta

Betula nana subsp.

relicta, commonly known as the dwarf birch or relic dwarf birch, is a small shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, typically reaching heights of less than 50 cm, with hairy, often reddish stems.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and deciduous, turning yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting cold tolerance and a preference for well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.
