Berberis vulgaris var. purpurifolia

Berberis vulgaris var. purpurifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, spiny stems and striking purple-tinged leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and hardy nature.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions and is sometimes considered a weed.
