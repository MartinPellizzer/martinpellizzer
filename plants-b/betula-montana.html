Betula montana

Betula montana, commonly known as the mountain birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its white to yellowish bark, which peels in thin, papery strips. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal and alpine regions.

Betula montana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
