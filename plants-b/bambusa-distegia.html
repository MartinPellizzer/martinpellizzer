Bambusa distegia

Bambusa distegia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This bamboo species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.

Its culms are also used in construction and crafts in its native regions.
