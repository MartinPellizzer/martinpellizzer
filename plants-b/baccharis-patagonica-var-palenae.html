Baccharis patagonica var. palenae

Baccharis patagonica var. palenae, commonly known as Patagonian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, thriving in arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
