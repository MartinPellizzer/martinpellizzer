Begonia vanoverberghii

Begonia vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to purplish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments with high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
