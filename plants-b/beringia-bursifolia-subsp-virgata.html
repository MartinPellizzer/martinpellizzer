Beringia bursifolia subsp. virgata

Beringia bursifolia subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Alaska bursifolia, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct ecological and morphological features compared to other members of the genus.
