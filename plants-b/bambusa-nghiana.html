Bambusa nghiana

Bambusa nghiana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its lightweight yet strong stalks, which are used in construction and crafts.
