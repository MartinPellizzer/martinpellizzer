Baccharis arborea

Baccharis arborea, commonly known as the Brazilian broom or green baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis arborea is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
