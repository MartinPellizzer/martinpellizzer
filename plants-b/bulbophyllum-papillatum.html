Bulbophyllum papillatum

Bulbophyllum papillatum, commonly known as the papillate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in less-than-ideal conditions.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
