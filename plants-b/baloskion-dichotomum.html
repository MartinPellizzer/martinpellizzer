Baloskion dichotomum

Baloskion dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Baloskion dichotomum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and along stream banks.
