Bufonia ephedrina var. minor

Bufonia ephedrina var. minor, commonly known as Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and elongated, woody stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have medicinal properties. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to extreme temperatures.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional Chinese medicine.
