Boea speluncarum

Boea speluncarum, commonly known as the cave ivy, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or trees in mountainous regions. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces delicate white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
