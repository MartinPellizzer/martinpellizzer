Banisteria paniculata

Banisteria paniculata, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and showy inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in warm climates.
