Bignonia jasminoides rosea

Bignonia jasminoides rosea, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces long, slender seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bignonia jasminoides rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
