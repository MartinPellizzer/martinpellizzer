Bulbophyllum trisetosum

Bulbophyllum trisetosum, commonly known as the three-bristled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a lithophytic epiphyte, typically growing on rocks or trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and fragrant, usually appearing in clusters, and come in shades of white, yellow, and green.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
