Bupleurum commelynoideum var. elatissimum

Bupleurum commelynoideum var. elatissimum, commonly known as the tall yellow bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it one of the tallest species in the Bupleurum genus. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbel-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
