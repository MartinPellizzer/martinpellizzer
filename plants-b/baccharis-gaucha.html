Baccharis gaucha

Baccharis gaucha, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Baccharis gaucha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
