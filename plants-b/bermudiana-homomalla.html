Bermudiana homomalla

Bermudiana homomalla, commonly known as the Bermuda grass variant, is a perennial grass species native to the subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, sod-forming growth habit, making it highly adaptable to various soil types. The plant typically thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, which contributes to its widespread use in lawns and pastures. Its reproductive strategy involves both vegetative propagation through rhizomes and seed production.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
