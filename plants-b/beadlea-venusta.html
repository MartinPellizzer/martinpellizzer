Beadlea venusta

Beadlea venusta, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Beadlea venusta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various wetland environments.
