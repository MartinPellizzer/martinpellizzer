Bursera schaffneri

Bursera schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

Bursera schaffneri produces small, fragrant flowers that are often followed by orange or red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.
