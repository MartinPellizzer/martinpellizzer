Begonia cladotricha

Begonia cladotricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
