Bonnetia steyermarkii

Bonnetia steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered with fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warm season, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
