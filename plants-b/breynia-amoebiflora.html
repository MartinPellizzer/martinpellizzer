Breynia amoebiflora

Breynia amoebiflora, commonly known as the blob plant, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its unique appearance, with its swollen, almost gelatinous leaves, has made it a popular specimen in botanical gardens.

Breynia amoebiflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its low maintenance requirements.
