Bourreria albopunctata

Bourreria albopunctata, commonly known as the white-spotted broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinctive white or pale green stippling. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
