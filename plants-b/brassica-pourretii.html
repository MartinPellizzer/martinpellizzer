Brassica pourretii

Brassica pourretii, commonly known as the Algerian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lobed and slightly hairy, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
