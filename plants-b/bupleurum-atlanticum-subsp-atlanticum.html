Bupleurum atlanticum subsp. atlanticum

Bupleurum atlanticum subsp.

atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic goat's-beard, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the Atlantic coast, thriving in saline and well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and adaptation to coastal habitats, making it a notable component of maritime flora.
