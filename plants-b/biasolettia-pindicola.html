Biasolettia pindicola

Biasolettia pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus biasolettia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Pindos Mountains in northern Greece.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is adapted to the harsh, mountainous climate of its native habitat. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are essential to protect this unique botanical species.
