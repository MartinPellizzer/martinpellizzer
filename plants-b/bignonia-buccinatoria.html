Bignonia buccinatoria

Bignonia buccinatoria, commonly known as the scarlet shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with pinnately compound leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of orange to red. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, viscous substance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Bignonia buccinatoria is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
