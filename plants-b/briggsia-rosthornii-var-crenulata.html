Briggsia rosthornii var. crenulata

Briggsia rosthornii var. crenulata, commonly known as the crenulate bristle fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, bristle-like hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical or subtropical forests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, this variety is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
