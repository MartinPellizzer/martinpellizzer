Bulbophyllum acutispicatum

Bulbophyllum acutispicatum, commonly known as the sharp-spined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal habitats. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and bears inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
