Boronia revoluta

Boronia revoluta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

Boronia revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
