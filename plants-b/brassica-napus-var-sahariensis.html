Brassica napus var. sahariensis

Brassica napus var. sahariensis, commonly known as the Saharan mustard, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, spherical pods containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and can tolerate high temperatures and limited water availability.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for drought resistance and genetic diversity.
