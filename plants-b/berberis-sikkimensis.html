Berberis sikkimensis

Berberis sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The Sikkim barberry is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and landscaping.
