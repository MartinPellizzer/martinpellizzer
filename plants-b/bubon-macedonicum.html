Bubon macedonicum

Bubon macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
