Bulbophyllum venulosum

Bulbophyllum venulosum, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, veined leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum venulosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
