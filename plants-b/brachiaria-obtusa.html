Brachiaria obtusa

Brachiaria obtusa, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or disturbed soils. It is a common weed in pastures and agricultural fields.

Brachiaria obtusa is known for its ability to thrive in high-light conditions and is sometimes used for forage in livestock farming.
