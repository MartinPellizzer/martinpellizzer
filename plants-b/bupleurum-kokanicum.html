Bupleurum kokanicum

Bupleurum kokanicum, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small white to pale yellow flowers in late spring.

Bupleurum kokanicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
