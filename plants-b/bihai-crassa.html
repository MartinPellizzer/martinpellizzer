Bihai crassa

Bihai crassa, commonly known as the giant panda plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
