Biscutella montana var. subdecurrens

Biscutella montana var. subdecurrens, commonly known as the mountain biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
