Bulbophyllum allenkerrii

Bulbophyllum allenkerrii, commonly known as the Allenkerri's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow, and are often fragrant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
