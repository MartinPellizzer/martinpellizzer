Brassica austriaca

Brassica austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Central Europe and typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features rosettes of dark green, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It produces yellow flowers in the second year, followed by small, round pods containing seeds.

Brassica austriaca is valued for its nutritional content and historical agricultural significance.
