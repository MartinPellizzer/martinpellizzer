Brodiaea terrestris subsp. terrestris

Brodiaea terrestris subsp.

terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial brodiaea, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

The subspecies is distinguished by its low-growing habit and the presence of a well-developed bulb.
