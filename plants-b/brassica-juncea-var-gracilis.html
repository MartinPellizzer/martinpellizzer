Brassica juncea var. gracilis

Brassica juncea var. gracilis, commonly known as Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a cultivar of the mustard plant family.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and dark green, edible leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable in Asian cuisines.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
