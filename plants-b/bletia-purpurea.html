Bletia purpurea

Bletia purpurea, commonly known as the purple bletia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and are relatively short-lived.

Bletia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
