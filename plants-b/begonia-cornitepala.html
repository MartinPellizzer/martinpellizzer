Begonia cornitepala

Begonia cornitepala, commonly known as the horned begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, horn-like bracts that surround its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Begonia cornitepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
