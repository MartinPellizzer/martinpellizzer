Bellevalia micrantha

Bellevalia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Bellevalia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
