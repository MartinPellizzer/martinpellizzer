Buxus sempervirens f. rosmarinifolium

Buxus sempervirens f. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its distinctive foliage, which features narrow, leathery leaves with a silvery-green hue and a strong rosemary scent. The leaves are typically arranged in whorls and are slightly serrated along the edges. This cultivar is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in formal gardens and topiary designs.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
