Botryopleuron villosulum

Botryopleuron villosulum, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, where it relies on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves that help retain water in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with a distinctive, pleated lip.

Botryopleuron villosulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
