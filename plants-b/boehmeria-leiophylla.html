Boehmeria leiophylla

Boehmeria leiophylla, commonly known as silky phlox, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Boehmeria leiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
