Broussonetia × kazinoki var. ruyangensis

Broussonetia × kazinoki var. ruyangensis, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it adaptable to different environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
