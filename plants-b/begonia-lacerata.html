Begonia lacerata

Begonia lacerata, commonly known as the laceleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
