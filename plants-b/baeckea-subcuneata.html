Baeckea subcuneata

Baeckea subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baeckea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Baeckea subcuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
