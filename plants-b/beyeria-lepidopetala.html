Beyeria lepidopetala

Beyeria lepidopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf beyeria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Beyeria lepidopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
