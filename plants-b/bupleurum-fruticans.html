Bupleurum fruticans

Bupleurum fruticans, commonly known as wild chervil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Bupleurum fruticans is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
