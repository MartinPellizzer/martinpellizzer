Bryanthus taxifolius

Bryanthus taxifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved moss cushion, is a low-growing plant belonging to the family Bryaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Bryanthus taxifolius is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.

Its botanical structure is well-adapted to its specific ecological niche, making it an important component of its habitat.
