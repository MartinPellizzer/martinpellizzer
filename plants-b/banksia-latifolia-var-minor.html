Banksia latifolia var. minor

Banksia latifolia var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive yellow to orange flower spikes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
