Brassaia albida

Brassaia albida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white petals and a distinctive, fragrant floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their elegant appearance and subtle scent.

Brassaia albida requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
