Bellevalia comosa subsp. clusiana

Bellevalia comosa subsp.

clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
