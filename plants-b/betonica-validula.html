Betonica validula

Betonica validula, commonly known as spring betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
