Begonia setulosopeltata

Begonia setulosopeltata, commonly known as the hairy-leaved begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and vibrant, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a textured surface, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
