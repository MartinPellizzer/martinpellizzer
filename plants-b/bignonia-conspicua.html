Bignonia conspicua

Bignonia conspicua, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces long, pendulous racemes of flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Bignonia conspicua is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
