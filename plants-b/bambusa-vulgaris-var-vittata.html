Bambusa vulgaris var. vittata

Bambusa vulgaris var. vittata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow and green striped leaves. This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
