Baptisia tinctoria var. crebra

Baptisia tinctoria var. crebra, commonly known as wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and can tolerate a variety of soil conditions, though it prefers well-drained soils.

Baptisia tinctoria var. crebra is also valued for its historical use in dyeing fabrics and as a source of medicinal compounds.
