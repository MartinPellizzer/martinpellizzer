Bredemeyera mollis

Bredemeyera mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Bredemeyera mollis is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.
