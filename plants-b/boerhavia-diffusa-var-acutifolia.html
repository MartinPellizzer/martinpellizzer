Boerhavia diffusa var. acutifolia

Boerhavia diffusa var. acutifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf horsegram, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves with acute tips, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and dry fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
