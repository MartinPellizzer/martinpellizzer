Boronia exilis

Boronia exilis, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a slight citrus scent.

Boronia exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
