Besleria diabolica

Besleria diabolica, commonly known as the devil's flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators like flies.

Due to its dramatic appearance and rare occurrence, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors.
