Briggsia agnesiae

Briggsia agnesiae, commonly known as the Agnesiae's ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to float above the stem, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically between November and February. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its unique aesthetic.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Briggsia agnesiae is considered a critically endangered species.
