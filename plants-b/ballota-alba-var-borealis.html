Ballota alba var. borealis

Ballota alba var. borealis, commonly known as the northern ballota, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, giving it a distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Ballota alba var. borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
