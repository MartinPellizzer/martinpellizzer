Bulbophyllum dischidiifolium

Bulbophyllum dischidiifolium, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a spider, with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and green, and are highly prized in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
