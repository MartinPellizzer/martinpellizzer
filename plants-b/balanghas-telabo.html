Balanghas telabo

Balanghas telabo, commonly known as the Philippine dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Balanghas telabo plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various insects.
