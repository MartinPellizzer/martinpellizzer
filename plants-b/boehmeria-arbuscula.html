Boehmeria arbuscula

Boehmeria arbuscula, commonly known as the small tree nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Boehmeria arbuscula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
