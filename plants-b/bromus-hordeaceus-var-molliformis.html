Bromus hordeaceus var. molliformis

Bromus hordeaceus var. molliformis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, soft, and often hairy stems.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces spikelets that are loosely arranged, with lemmas that are often hairy and slightly compressed. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
