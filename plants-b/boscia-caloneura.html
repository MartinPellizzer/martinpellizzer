Boscia caloneura

Boscia caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow venation pattern. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Boscia caloneura is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate.
