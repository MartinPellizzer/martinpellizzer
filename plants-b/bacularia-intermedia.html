Bacularia intermedia

Bacularia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bacularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bacularia intermedia is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates a degree of drought tolerance.
