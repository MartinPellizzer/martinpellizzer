Bryophyllum × rechingeri

Bryophyllum × rechingeri, commonly known as the Mother of Millions, is a hybrid species within the Crassulaceae family.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces numerous small plantlets along the edges of its leaves, which can detach and root to form new plants, contributing to its rapid propagation. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
