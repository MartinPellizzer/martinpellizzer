Bromus elongatus var. pubescens

Bromus elongatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent brome, is a grass species characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. The plant has a slender, erect growth habit with spikelets that are usually arranged in panicles. Its foliage is generally narrow and grass-like, with the lower leaves often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
