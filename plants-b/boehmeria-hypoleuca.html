Boehmeria hypoleuca

Boehmeria hypoleuca, commonly known as Chinese hemp or white flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky leaves and pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is known for its fibrous stems, which are used in traditional textile production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its fibrous properties make it an important resource in both ecological and economic contexts.
