Brassica cheiranthos var. sabulicola

Brassica cheiranthos var. sabulicola, commonly known as the sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dunes.
