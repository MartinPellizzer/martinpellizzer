Brachychiton × excellens

Brachychiton × excellens, commonly known as the Sydney morning glory, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to eastern Australia, this plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or water.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
