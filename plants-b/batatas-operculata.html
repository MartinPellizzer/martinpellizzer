Batatas operculata

Batatas operculata, commonly known as the hooded sweet potato.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its tuberous roots that are often swollen and covered in a thin, papery layer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are valued for their starchy, sweet flavor and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible gardening.
