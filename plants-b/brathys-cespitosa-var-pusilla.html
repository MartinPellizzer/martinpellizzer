Brathys cespitosa var. pusilla

Brathys cespitosa var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf brathys, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features dense, compact foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
