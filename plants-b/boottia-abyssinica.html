Boottia abyssinica

Boottia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Boottia abyssinica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
