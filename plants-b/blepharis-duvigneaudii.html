Blepharis duvigneaudii

Blepharis duvigneaudii, commonly known as the Duvigneaud's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color and are adapted for pollination by insects. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a perennial plant that displays a unique morphology suited to its harsh, dry environment.
