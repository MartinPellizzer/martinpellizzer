Bogoria laxus

Bogoria laxus, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland and bog environments in eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture, making it a typical inhabitant of marshes and bogs. Its reproductive strategy involves insect pollination, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.

Bogoria laxus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in moist garden settings.
