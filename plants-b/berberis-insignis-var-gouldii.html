Berberis insignis var. gouldii

Berberis insignis var. gouldii, commonly known as Gould's barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
