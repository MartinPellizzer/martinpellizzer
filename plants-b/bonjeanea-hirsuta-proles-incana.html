Bonjeanea hirsuta proles incana

Bonjeanea hirsuta proles incana, commonly known as the hairy bonjeanea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
