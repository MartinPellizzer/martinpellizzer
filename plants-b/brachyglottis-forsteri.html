Brachyglottis forsteri

Brachyglottis forsteri, commonly known as the Stewart Island bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features hairy, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and on the island of Stewart. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Brachyglottis forsteri is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its habitat.
