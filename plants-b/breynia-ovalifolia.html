Breynia ovalifolia

Breynia ovalifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaf breynia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.

Breynia ovalifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
