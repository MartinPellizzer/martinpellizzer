Bursaria longisepala var. pilosa

Bursaria longisepala var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bursaria, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a rough texture. The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

This plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal dunes.
