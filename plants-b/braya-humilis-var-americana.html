Braya humilis var. americana

Braya humilis var. americana, commonly known as American rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically features small, bright yellow flowers and basal rosettes of fleshy, toothed leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
