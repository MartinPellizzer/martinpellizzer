Bomarea subspicata

Bomarea subspicata, commonly known as the red-flowered bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and eye-catching floral display.

Bomarea subspicata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
