Bystropogon canariensis var. smithianus

Bystropogon canariensis var. smithianus, commonly known as the Canary Islands spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in its arid native environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens or containers due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic landscapes and coastal areas.
