Brachionidium valerioi

Brachionidium valerioi, commonly known as the Valerio's water flea, is a species of copepod found in freshwater environments.

It belongs to the order Cyclopoida and is characterized by its small, transparent body and elongated shape. The organism has a distinct head with a prominent rostrum and a segmented body covered in fine setae. It plays a role in aquatic food webs as both a predator of microorganisms and a prey item for larger aquatic animals.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often used in studies related to water quality and ecosystem health.
