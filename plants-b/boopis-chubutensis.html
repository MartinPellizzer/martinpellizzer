Boopis chubutensis

Boopis chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grass species. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of sunlight and temperature fluctuations.

Boopis chubutensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant plant species.
