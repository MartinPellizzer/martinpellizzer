Blaberopus villosus

Blaberopus villosus, commonly known as the hairy stick insect, is a species of stick insect native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender body covered in fine, hairy setae, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant it inhabits, typically tropical rainforests, provides the moist, shaded environment it requires for survival. Its diet consists mainly of leaves from various plants, including ferns and broadleaf trees.

The species is notable for its ability to camouflage effectively within its environment, aiding in predator avoidance.
