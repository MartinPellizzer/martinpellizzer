Baldingera arundinacea var. rotgesii

Baldingera arundinacea var. rotgesii, commonly known as the Rotgesii reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
