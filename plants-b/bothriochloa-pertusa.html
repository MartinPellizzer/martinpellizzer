Bothriochloa pertusa

Bothriochloa pertusa, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in grassland and savanna ecosystems. Bothriochloa pertusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in such environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used for forage in livestock grazing.
