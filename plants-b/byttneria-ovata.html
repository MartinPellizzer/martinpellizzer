Byttneria ovata

Byttneria ovata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
