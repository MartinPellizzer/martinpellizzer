Belvisia mucronata var. dura

Belvisia mucronata var. dura, commonly known as the hardy belvisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
