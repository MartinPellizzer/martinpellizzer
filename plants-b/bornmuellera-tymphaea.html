Bornmuellera tymphaea

Bornmuellera tymphaea, commonly known as the sandstone lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
