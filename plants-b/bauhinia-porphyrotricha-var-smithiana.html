Bauhinia porphyrotricha var. smithiana

Bauhinia porphyrotricha var. smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to provide shade and support for other plants.
