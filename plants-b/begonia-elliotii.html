Begonia elliotii

Begonia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
