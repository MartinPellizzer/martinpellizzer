Bulbophyllum birmense

Bulbophyllum birmense, commonly known as the Birmenshaw's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence features a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a distinct lip.

Bulbophyllum birmense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
