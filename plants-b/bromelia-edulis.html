Bromelia edulis

Bromelia edulis, commonly known as the edible bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers, often in red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and fibrous, making them suitable for use in traditional cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
