Brassica campestris f. briggsii

Brassica campestris f. briggsii, commonly known as the field mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.
