Bowringia callicarpa

Bowringia callicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited bowringia, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

Bowringia callicarpa is often found in humid, high-altitude environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
