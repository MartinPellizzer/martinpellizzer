Bursera serrata var. serratissima

Bursera serrata var. serratissima, commonly known as the Mexican lac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, glossy dark green foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and its historical use in the production of natural resins and lac.
