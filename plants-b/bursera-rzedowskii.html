Bursera rzedowskii

Bursera rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Mexican states of Oaxaca and Chiapas.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in limestone soils and mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The plant is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
