Baptistonia sarcodes

Baptistonia sarcodes, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring. The plant has a distinctive root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

Baptistonia sarcodes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
