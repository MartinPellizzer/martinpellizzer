Benthamia erinacea

Benthamia erinacea, commonly known as the woolly benthamia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
