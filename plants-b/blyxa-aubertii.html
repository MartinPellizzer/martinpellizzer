Blyxa aubertii

Blyxa aubertii, commonly known as the water thyme, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or floating.

Blyxa aubertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize aquatic environments.
