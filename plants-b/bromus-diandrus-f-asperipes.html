Bromus diandrus f. asperipes

Bromus diandrus f. asperipes, commonly known as the rough brome, is a grass species characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features dense, tufted clumps with long, narrow leaves that have a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, elongated clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
