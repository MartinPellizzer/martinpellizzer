Begonia opuliflora

Begonia opuliflora, commonly known as the showy begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia opuliflora is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
