Beloere crispa

Beloere crispa, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a crinkled appearance that gives it its scientific name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Beloere crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
