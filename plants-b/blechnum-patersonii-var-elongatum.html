Blechnum patersonii var. elongatum

Blechnum patersonii var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has a delicate, lace-like appearance with finely divided, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage in suitable conditions.
