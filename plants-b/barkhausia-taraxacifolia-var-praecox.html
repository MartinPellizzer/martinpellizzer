Barkhausia taraxacifolia var. praecox

Barkhausia taraxacifolia var. praecox, commonly known as the early yellow bell, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are erect and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
