Besleria cyrtanthemum

Besleria cyrtanthemum, commonly known as the waxflower or waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
