Bencomia exstipulata

Bencomia exstipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bencomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Bencomia exstipulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
