Bromus lanceolatus var. divaricatus

Bromus lanceolatus var. divaricatus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate climates.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
