Begonia palmaris

Begonia palmaris, commonly known as the hand begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hand-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia palmaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
