Bupleurum petraeum f. globosum

Bupleurum petraeum f. globosum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and bears small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with finely divided lobes, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
