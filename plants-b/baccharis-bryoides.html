Baccharis bryoides

Baccharis bryoides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
