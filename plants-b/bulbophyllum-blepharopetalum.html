Bulbophyllum blepharopetalum

Bulbophyllum blepharopetalum, commonly known as the veined lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, veined petals and a distinctive, often colorful lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small but numerous, arranged in clusters that bloom during the rainy season.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diverse morphology, and this species is no exception, showcasing unique adaptations for its ecological niche.
