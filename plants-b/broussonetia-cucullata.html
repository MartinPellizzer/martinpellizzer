Broussonetia cucullata

Broussonetia cucullata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seeds are encased in tough pods, which can remain on the plant for an extended period, aiding in seed dispersal.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its vigorous growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
