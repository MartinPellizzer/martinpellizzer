Bahia ourolepis

Bahia ourolepis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bahia ourolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
