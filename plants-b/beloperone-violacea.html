Beloperone violacea

Beloperone violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Beloperone violacea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
