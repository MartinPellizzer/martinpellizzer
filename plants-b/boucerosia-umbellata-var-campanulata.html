Boucerosia umbellata var. campanulata

Boucerosia umbellata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped boucerosia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
