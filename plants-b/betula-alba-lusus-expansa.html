Betula alba lusus expansa

Betula alba lusus expansa, commonly known as the white birch variant with expanded leaves, is a botanical variant of the white birch species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often broader leaves compared to the standard white birch. The plant belongs to the Betulaceae family and is known for its delicate, papery bark that peels in thin layers. It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
