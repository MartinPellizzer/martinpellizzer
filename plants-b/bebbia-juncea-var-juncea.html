Bebbia juncea var. juncea

Bebbia juncea var. juncea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
