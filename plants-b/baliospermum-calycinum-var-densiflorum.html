Baliospermum calycinum var. densiflorum

Baliospermum calycinum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
