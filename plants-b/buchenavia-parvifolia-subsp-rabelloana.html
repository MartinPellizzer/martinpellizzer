Buchenavia parvifolia subsp. rabelloana

Buchenavia parvifolia subsp.

rabelloana, commonly known as the Rabelloan bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
