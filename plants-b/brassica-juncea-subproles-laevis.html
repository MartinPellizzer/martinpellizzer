Brassica juncea subproles laevis

Brassica juncea subproles laevis, commonly known as Chinese mustard or yellow sarson, is an annual plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several small, round seeds. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants such as cabbage and mustard.

This species is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
