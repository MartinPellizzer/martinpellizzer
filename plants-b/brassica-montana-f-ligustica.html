Brassica montana f. ligustica

Brassica montana f. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligusticum form of the mountain cabbage, is a botanical variant of the species Brassica montana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. It is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
