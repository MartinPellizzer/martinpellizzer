Begonia longipedunculata

Begonia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncled begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces long, slender peduncles that bear multiple blooms, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Begonia longipedunculata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
