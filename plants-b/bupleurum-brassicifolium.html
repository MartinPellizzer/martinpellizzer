Bupleurum brassicifolium

Bupleurum brassicifolium, commonly known as yellow bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bupleurum brassicifolium is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
