Betonica jacquinii subsp. albanica

Betonica jacquinii subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Betonica jacquinii subsp.

albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
