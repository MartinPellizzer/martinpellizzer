Bauhinia albiflora

Bauhinia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features distinctive bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Bauhinia albiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
