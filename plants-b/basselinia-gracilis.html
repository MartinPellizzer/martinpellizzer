Basselinia gracilis

Basselinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender moss-fern, is a delicate aquatic fern found in freshwater environments.

It has slender, creeping stems that form dense mats on the water surface or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating that helps it float and retain moisture. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Basselinia gracilis plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
