Banksia repens

Banksia repens, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that are often bright yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is well adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Banksia repens plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
