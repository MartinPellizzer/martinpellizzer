Berberis lagenaria

Berberis lagenaria, commonly known as the giant barbed wire bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive orange-red berries. These berries are toxic to humans and animals, making the plant a potential hazard.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
