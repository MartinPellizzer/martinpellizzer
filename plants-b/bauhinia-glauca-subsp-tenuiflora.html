Bauhinia glauca subsp. tenuiflora

Bauhinia glauca subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
