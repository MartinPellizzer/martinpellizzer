Baccharoides guineensis

Baccharoides guineensis, commonly known as the Guinean baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters during the dry season.

Baccharoides guineensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.
