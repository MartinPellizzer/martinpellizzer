Ballota hispanica subsp. macedonica

Ballota hispanica subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
