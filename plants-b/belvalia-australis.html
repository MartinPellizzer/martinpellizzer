Belvalia australis

Belvalia australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Belvalia australis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
