Baeckea teretifolia

Baeckea teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, opposite leaves and a woody structure, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

It is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.
