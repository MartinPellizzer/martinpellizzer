Brassica caespitosa

Brassica caespitosa, commonly known as wall rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky or coastal environments. The plant is often used in culinary applications due to its peppery flavor.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a common weed in disturbed soils.
