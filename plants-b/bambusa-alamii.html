Bambusa alamii

Bambusa alamii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect culms that are often covered in a golden or yellowish hue, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. It is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Bambusa alamii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
