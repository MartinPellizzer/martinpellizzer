Brandesia echinocephala

Brandesia echinocephala, commonly known as the spiny-headed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiny inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a variety of colors, and it produces small, round fruits.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
