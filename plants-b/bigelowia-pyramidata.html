Bigelowia pyramidata

Bigelowia pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Bigelowia pyramidata is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
