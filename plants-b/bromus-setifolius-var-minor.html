Bromus setifolius var. minor

Bromus setifolius var. minor, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
