Brachypodium pinnatum f. cespitosum

Brachypodium pinnatum f. cespitosum, commonly known as the tufted false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
