Barkhausia leontodon

Barkhausia leontodon, commonly known as the elephant's foot plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often branched stems that resemble the shape of an elephant's foot. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its ability to store water in its stem makes it highly drought-tolerant.

The leaves are typically small and scale-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
