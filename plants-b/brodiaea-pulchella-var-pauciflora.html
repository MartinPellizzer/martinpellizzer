Brodiaea pulchella var. pauciflora

Brodiaea pulchella var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This variety is characterized by its reduced number of flowers compared to other varieties within the species.
