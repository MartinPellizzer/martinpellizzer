Bursera heliae

Bursera heliae, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant produces a fragrant sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine and as a natural incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

Bursera heliae typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
