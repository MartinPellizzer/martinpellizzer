Begonia puspitae

Begonia puspitae, commonly known as the Puspita begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and colors. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or pink in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and botanists.
