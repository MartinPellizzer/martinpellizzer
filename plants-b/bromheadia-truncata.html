Bromheadia truncata

Bromheadia truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bromheadia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

Bromheadia truncata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
