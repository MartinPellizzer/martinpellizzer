Bowlesia elata

Bowlesia elata, commonly known as the tall bowlesia, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Bowlesia elata is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
