Byttneria scabra var. typica

Byttneria scabra var. typica, commonly known as the rough bark balsam, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and rough, fissured bark that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
