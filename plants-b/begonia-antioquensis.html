Begonia antioquensis

Begonia antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually a deep green with contrasting veins, and it produces flowers in shades of red, pink, or white.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
