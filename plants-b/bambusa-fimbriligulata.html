Bambusa fimbriligulata

Bambusa fimbriligulata, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, graceful stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a silvery sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa fimbriligulata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
