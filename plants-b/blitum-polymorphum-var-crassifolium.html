Blitum polymorphum var. crassifolium

Blitum polymorphum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf blitum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptations.
