Berberis apiculata

Berberis apiculata, commonly known as the prickly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in sharp, spine-like thorns. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and winter interest.

Berberis apiculata is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
