Bauhinia persichii

Bauhinia persichii, commonly known as the Persian bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring or early summer. Bauhinia persichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for tropical and subtropical climates.
