Buddleja griffithii

Buddleja griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmiri bush or the purple buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a dark green color. The plant produces striking clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. Buddleja griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also a popular choice for attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
