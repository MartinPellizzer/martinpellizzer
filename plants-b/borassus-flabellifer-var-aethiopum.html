Borassus flabellifer var. aethiopum

Borassus flabellifer var. aethiopum, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a variant of the fan palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a striking visual presence. The plant produces tall, slender stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, often with a single trunk. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in various cultural and practical applications in its native habitats.
