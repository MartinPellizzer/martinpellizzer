Banisteria mucronata

Banisteria mucronata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Banisteria mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
