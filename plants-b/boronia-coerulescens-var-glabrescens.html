Boronia coerulescens var. glabrescens

Boronia coerulescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form and small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, hairless surface, which is a distinguishing feature of the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
