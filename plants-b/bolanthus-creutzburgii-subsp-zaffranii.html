Bolanthus creutzburgii subsp. zaffranii

Bolanthus creutzburgii subsp.

zaffranii, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
