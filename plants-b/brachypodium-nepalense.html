Brachypodium nepalense

Brachypodium nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal grass, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing tiny, two-lipped flowers.

Brachypodium nepalense is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
