Boopis anthemoides var. rigidula

Boopis anthemoides var. rigidula, commonly known as rigid leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
