Barkleyanthus salicifolius

Barkleyanthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved carnation, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
