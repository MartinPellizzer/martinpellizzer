Biscutella orientalis

Biscutella orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Biscutella orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
