Begonia parvistipulata

Begonia parvistipulata, commonly known as the small-leaf begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.
