Bromus squarrosus subsp. consimilis

Bromus squarrosus subsp.

consimilis, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, often tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its drought tolerance and deep root system.
