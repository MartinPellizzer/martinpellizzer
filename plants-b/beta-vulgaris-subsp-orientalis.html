Beta vulgaris subsp. orientalis

Beta vulgaris subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as sugar beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is native to Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its industrial and agricultural importance.
