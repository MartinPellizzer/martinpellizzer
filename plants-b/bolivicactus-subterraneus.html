Bolivicactus subterraneus

Bolivicactus subterraneus, commonly known as the underground cactus, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces a cluster of fleshy, cylindrical stems that emerge above ground during specific seasons. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

This plant is adapted to survive in arid and cold environments, making it a fascinating example of desert flora.
