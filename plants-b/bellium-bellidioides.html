Bellium bellidioides

Bellium bellidioides, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is well adapted to arid conditions and has a low water requirement.

Bellium bellidioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
