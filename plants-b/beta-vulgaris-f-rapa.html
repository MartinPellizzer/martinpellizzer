Beta vulgaris f. rapa

Beta vulgaris f. rapa, commonly known as the beetroot or garden beet, is a cultivated variant of the broader Beta vulgaris species.

It is characterized by its swollen, taproot-like structure that is typically reddish-purple in color. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes, while the root is a popular vegetable in many cuisines.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of iron, fiber, and antioxidants.
