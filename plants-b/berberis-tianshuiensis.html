Berberis tianshuiensis

Berberis tianshuiensis, commonly known as the Tianshui barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
