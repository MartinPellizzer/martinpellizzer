Betula papyrifera var. kenaica

Betula papyrifera var. kenaica, commonly known as the Kenaica paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a lighter inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
