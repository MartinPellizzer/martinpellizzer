Bromus erectus var. pomoensis

Bromus erectus var. pomoensis, commonly known as the Sierra brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
