Besleria emendata

Besleria emendata, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Begoniaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting markings.

Besleria emendata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
