Bursera sarukhanii

Bursera sarukhanii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, leathery leaves that are arranged opposite each other on the stems. It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing small, hard seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
