Basanacantha

Basanacantha, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like seed pods that open explosively to disperse seeds. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Basanacantha has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
