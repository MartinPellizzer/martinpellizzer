Bulbophyllum calceilabium

Bulbophyllum calceilabium, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of white, pink, and yellow. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
