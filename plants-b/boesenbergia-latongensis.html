Boesenbergia latongensis

Boesenbergia latongensis, commonly known as the Malabar ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in clusters, adding ornamental value.
