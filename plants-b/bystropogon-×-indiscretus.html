Bystropogon × indiscretus

Bystropogon × indiscretus, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Bystropogon hirsutus and Bystropogon paniculatus. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
