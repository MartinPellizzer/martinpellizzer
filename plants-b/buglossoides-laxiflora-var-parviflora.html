Buglossoides laxiflora var. parviflora

Buglossoides laxiflora var. parviflora, commonly known as narrowleaf bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
