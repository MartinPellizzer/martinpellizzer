Begonia raynaliorum

Begonia raynaliorum, commonly known as the Raynal's begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from red to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
