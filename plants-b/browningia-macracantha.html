Browningia macracantha

Browningia macracantha, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
