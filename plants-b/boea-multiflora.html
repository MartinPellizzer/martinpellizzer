Boea multiflora

Boea multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Boea multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
