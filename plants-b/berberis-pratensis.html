Berberis pratensis

Berberis pratensis, commonly known as the creeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic to ovate, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface.

Berberis pratensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
