Borzicactus madisoniorum

Borzicactus madisoniorum, commonly known as the Madison's borzicactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its columnar stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
