Bamlera insignis

Bamlera insignis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds. Bamlera insignis is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
