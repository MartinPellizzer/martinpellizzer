Bauhinia decandra

Bauhinia decandra, commonly known as the flame tree or red bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble hands. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Bauhinia decandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
