Betula papyrifera var. occidentalis

Betula papyrifera var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
