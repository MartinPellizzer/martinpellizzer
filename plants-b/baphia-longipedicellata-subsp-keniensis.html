Baphia longipedicellata subsp. keniensis

Baphia longipedicellata subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan baphia, is a member of the Leguminosae family. It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is native to East Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and medicinal research.
