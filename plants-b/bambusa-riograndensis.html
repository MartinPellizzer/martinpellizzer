Bambusa riograndensis

Bambusa riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bambusa riograndensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
