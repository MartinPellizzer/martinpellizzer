Brittonrosea coptonogona

Brittonrosea coptonogona, commonly known as the woolly brittonrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy pink to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a soft, fuzzy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
