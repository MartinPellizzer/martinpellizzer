Bignonia vitalba var. aequinoctialis

Bignonia vitalba var. aequinoctialis, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
