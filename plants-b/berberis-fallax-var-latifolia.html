Berberis fallax var. latifolia

Berberis fallax var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
