Bupleurum aricense

Bupleurum aricense, commonly known as Japanese angelica root, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its slender, branched stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
