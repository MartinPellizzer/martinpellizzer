Biancaea sappan

Biancaea sappan, commonly known as Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye. The plant has opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
