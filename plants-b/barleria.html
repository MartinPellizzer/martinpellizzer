Barleria

Barleria, commonly known as red balm or false balm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It comprises around 100 species, predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants are typically herbaceous, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. Barleria species often thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them popular in ornamental horticulture.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
