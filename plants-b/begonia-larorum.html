Begonia larorum

Begonia larorum, commonly known as the larorum begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.

Begonia larorum is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it adaptable to a variety of indoor settings.
