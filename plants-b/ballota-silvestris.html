Ballota silvestris

Ballota silvestris, commonly known as wild ballota, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, also known as the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Ballota silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
