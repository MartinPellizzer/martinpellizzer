Bromus mollis var. elatior

Bromus mollis var. elatior, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into pale to dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Bromus mollis var. elatior plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
