Bauhinia excelsa

Bauhinia excelsa, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Bauhinia excelsa is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
