Banksia recurvistylis

Banksia recurvistylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is an Australian shrub or small tree belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the south-western region of Western Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and its distinctive cylindrical or cone-shaped flower spikes. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
