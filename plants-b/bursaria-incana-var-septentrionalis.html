Bursaria incana var. septentrionalis

Bursaria incana var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern woolly bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
