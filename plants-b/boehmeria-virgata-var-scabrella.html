Boehmeria virgata var. scabrella

Boehmeria virgata var. scabrella, commonly known as roughleaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive silver-green coloration. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its stems are often covered in fine stinging hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
