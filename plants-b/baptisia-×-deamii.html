Baptisia × deamii

Baptisia × deamii, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, spiky seedpods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant spring blooms and long-lasting foliage.
