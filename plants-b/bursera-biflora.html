Bursera biflora

Bursera biflora, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in pairs, giving it the species name *biflora*.

The tree is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
