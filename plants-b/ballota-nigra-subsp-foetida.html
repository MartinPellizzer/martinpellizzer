Ballota nigra subsp. foetida

Ballota nigra subsp.

foetida, commonly known as the fetid hawthorn, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, black, egg-shaped fruits that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its strong, unpleasant odor, it is sometimes referred to as the stinkbush.
