Bulbophyllum corneri

Bulbophyllum corneri, commonly known as the Corner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that often feature a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bulbophyllum corneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral displays.
