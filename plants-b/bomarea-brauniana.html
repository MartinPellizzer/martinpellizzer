Bomarea brauniana

Bomarea brauniana, commonly known as the pink bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Bomarea brauniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
