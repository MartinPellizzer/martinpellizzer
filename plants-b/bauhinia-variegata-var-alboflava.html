Bauhinia variegata var. alboflava

Bauhinia variegata var. alboflava, commonly known as the white-flowered flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia variegata var. alboflava is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
