Betula oycowiensis var. majuscula

Betula oycowiensis var. majuscula, commonly known as the giant birch, is a variant of the birch tree characterized by its large, showy white flowers.

It is native to specific regions in East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant has slender, silver-green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
