Brownea excelsa

Brownea excelsa, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
