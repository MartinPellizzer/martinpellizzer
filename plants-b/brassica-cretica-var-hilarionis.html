Brassica cretica var. hilarionis

Brassica cretica var. hilarionis, commonly known as the Hilarionis mustard, is a rare and ancient variety of mustard plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and upright growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, round pods containing dark, peppery seeds that are used in traditional herbal and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
