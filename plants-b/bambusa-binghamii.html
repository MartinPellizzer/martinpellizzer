Bambusa binghamii

Bambusa binghamii, commonly known as the Bingham bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green culms and dense, feathery foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. This bamboo is clump-forming, with a relatively slow growth rate compared to other bamboo species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and good air circulation.

Bambusa binghamii is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
