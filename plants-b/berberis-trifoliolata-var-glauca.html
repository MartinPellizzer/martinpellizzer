Berberis trifoliolata var. glauca

Berberis trifoliolata var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically a vibrant green with a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
