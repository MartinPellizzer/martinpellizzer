Betula pubescens var. cryptocarpa

Betula pubescens var. cryptocarpa, commonly known as the downy birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forests. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
