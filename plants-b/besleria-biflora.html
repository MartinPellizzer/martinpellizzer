Besleria biflora

Besleria biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often with a short, stocky stem and broad, ovate leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
