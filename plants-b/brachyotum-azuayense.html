Brachyotum azuayense

Brachyotum azuayense, commonly known as the Azuay nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes potatoes, tomatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
