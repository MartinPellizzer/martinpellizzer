Baccharis aliena

Baccharis aliena, commonly known as the smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

Baccharis aliena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
