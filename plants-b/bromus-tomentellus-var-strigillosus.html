Bromus tomentellus var. strigillosus

Bromus tomentellus var. strigillosus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often covered in short, bristly hairs.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
