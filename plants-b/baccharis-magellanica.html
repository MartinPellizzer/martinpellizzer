Baccharis magellanica

Baccharis magellanica, commonly known as Magellanic baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and shrublands, and is well adapted to coastal and dry environments.

Baccharis magellanica is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
