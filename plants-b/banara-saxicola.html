Banara saxicola

Banara saxicola, commonly known as the rock banara, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Banara saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
