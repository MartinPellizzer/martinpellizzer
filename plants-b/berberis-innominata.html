Berberis innominata

Berberis innominata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. Its bright yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
