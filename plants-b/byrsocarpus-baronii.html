Byrsocarpus baronii

Byrsocarpus baronii, commonly known as the desert raisin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small shrub that produces distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling raisins, which are edible and used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
