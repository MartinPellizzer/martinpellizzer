Begonia khasiana

Begonia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often bi-colored foliage that displays shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
