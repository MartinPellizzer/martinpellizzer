Byblis liniflora subsp. occidentalis

Byblis liniflora subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Sun Orchid, is a carnivorous plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like structures that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and specialized growth requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
