Blephilia hirsuta var. glabra

Blephilia hirsuta var. glabra, commonly known as smooth blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and open areas.
