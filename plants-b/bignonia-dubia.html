Bignonia dubia

Bignonia dubia, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Bignonia dubia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
