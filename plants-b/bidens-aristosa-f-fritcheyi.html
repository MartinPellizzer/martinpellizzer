Bidens aristosa f. fritcheyi

Bidens aristosa f. fritcheyi, commonly known as the prairie tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing yellow petals and prominent white to yellow tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
