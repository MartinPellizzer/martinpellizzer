Bunias aspera

Bunias aspera, commonly known as the rough mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant features erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in small, rough protrusions. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces round, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
