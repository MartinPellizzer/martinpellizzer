Betonica alopecuros var. jacquinii

Betonica alopecuros var. jacquinii, commonly known as Jacquin's betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Betonica alopecuros var. jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
