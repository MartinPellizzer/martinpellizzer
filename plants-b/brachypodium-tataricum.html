Brachypodium tataricum

Brachypodium tataricum, commonly known as false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads, which are typically green to reddish in color. Brachypodium tataricum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

It is valued in ecological studies for its adaptability and use in research on grassland ecosystems.
