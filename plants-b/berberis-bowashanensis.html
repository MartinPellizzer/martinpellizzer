Berberis bowashanensis

Berberis bowashanensis, commonly known as the Shikoku barberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Berberis bowashanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
