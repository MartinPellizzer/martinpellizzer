Bambusa crinita

Bambusa crinita, commonly known as the fountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching, feather-like leaves that give it a flowing, ornamental appearance. The plant produces long, slender culms that are often used in landscaping for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa crinita is valued for its ability to grow quickly and form dense, compact clumps.
