Bidens riedelii var. hirsutus

Bidens riedelii var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
