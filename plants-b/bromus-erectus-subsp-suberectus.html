Bromus erectus subsp. suberectus

Bromus erectus subsp.

suberectus, commonly known as the upright brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
