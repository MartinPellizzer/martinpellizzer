Baptistonia cruciata

Baptistonia cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its common name.

Baptistonia cruciata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
