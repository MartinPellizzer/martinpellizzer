Baccharis auriculata

Baccharis auriculata, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver underside.

Baccharis auriculata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
