Bulbophyllum blepharoglossum

Bulbophyllum blepharoglossum, commonly known as the "Blepharoglossum Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
