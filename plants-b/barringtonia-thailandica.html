Barringtonia thailandica

Barringtonia thailandica, commonly known as the Thai lantern tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The tree produces distinctive lantern-shaped fruits that hang from the branches, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, fibrous pulp. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides in its native range.
