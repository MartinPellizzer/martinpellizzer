Berinia andryaloides

Berinia andryaloides, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Berinia andryaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
