Bambusa lingnanioides

Bambusa lingnanioides, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are arranged in panicles, typical of many bamboo species. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Bambusa lingnanioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
