Beloperone violacea var. pulchella

Beloperone violacea var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
