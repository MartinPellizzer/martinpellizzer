Babiana tubiflora subsp. saxicola

Babiana tubiflora subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock babiana, is a perennial herb native to the Cape Province of South Africa. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean-type climates with wet winters and dry summers.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bulbs are an important food source for certain insect species.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in garden settings.
