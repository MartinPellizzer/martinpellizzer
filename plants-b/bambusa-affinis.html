Bambusa affinis

Bambusa affinis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms. The plant forms dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, while its culms are strong and durable.

Bambusa affinis is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
