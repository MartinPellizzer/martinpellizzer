Buphthalmum maritimum

Buphthalmum maritimum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-blue leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

Buphthalmum maritimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal ecosystems.
