Bassovia tomentosa var. lanceolata

Bassovia tomentosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often covered in a thick layer of white tomentum, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
