Barleria louiseana

Barleria louiseana, commonly known as the Louise's barleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Barleria louiseana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
