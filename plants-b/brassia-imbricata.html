Brassia imbricata

Brassia imbricata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Brassia imbricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
