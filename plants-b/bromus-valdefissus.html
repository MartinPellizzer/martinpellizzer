Bromus valdefissus

Bromus valdefissus, commonly known as the Valdefissa brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a characteristic feature of the Poaceae family.

Bromus valdefissus is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a range of soil types.
