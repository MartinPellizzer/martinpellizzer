Baccharis veneta

Baccharis veneta, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis veneta is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
