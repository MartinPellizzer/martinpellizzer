Bruckenthalia spiculifolia

Bruckenthalia spiculifolia, commonly known as the spiky bruckenthalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
