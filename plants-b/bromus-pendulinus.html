Bromus pendulinus

Bromus pendulinus, commonly known as the pendling brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, grasslands, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Bromus pendulinus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in regions with moderate to high rainfall.
