Baccharis fuchsiifolia

Baccharis fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the Fuchsia-leaf Baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that often have a bluish or silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
