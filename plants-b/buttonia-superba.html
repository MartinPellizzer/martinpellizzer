Buttonia superba

Buttonia superba, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a spectrum of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, Buttonia superba is classified as a member of the Nelumbonaceae family, which is closely related to the water lilies.
