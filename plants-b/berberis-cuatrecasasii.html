Berberis cuatrecasasii

Berberis cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
