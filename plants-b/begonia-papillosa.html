Begonia papillosa

Begonia papillosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia papillosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.
