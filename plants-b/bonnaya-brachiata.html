Bonnaya brachiata

Bonnaya brachiata, commonly known as the climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Bonnaya brachiata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
