Bolboschoenus maritimus f. sarmaticus

Bolboschoenus maritimus f. sarmaticus, commonly known as the Sarmatian marsh grass, is a variant of the common marsh grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to grow in wetland environments.

This form is typically found in coastal and inland marshes, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
