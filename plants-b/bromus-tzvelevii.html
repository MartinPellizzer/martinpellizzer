Bromus tzvelevii

Bromus tzvelevii, commonly known as Tzvelev's brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Bromus tzvelevii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
