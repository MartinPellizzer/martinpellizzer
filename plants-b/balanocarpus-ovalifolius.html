Balanocarpus ovalifolius

Balanocarpus ovalifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
