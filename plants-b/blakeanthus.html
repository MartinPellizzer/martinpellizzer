Blakeanthus

Blakeanthus, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a unique flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of small lanterns. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds. Blakeanthus has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for coastal ecosystems and its potential medicinal properties.
