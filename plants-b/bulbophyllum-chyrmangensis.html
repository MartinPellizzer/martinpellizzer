Bulbophyllum chyrmangensis

Bulbophyllum chyrmangensis, commonly known as the Chyrmangensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its often humid and shaded environment.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
