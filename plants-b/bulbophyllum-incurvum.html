Bulbophyllum incurvum

Bulbophyllum incurvum, commonly known as the curling lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and cylindrical, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with curled petals. The plant prefers indirect light and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

It is valued for its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal.
