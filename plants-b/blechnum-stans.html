Blechnum stans

Blechnum stans, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to soil or rock surfaces.

Blechnum stans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
