Bambusa tacuara

Bambusa tacuara, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and dense, feathery foliage. This bamboo is known for its strong and durable structure, making it suitable for construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
