Bupleurum ranunculoides f. majus

Bupleurum ranunculoides f. majus, commonly known as the greater yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
