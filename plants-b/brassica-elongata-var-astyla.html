Brassica elongata var. astyla

Brassica elongata var. astyla, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and oval.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
