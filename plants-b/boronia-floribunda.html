Boronia floribunda

Boronia floribunda, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Boronia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and floral display.
