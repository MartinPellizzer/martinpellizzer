Blepharis burundiensis

Blepharis burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi grass, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of Burundi.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Blepharis burundiensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
