Brassica rapa var. chinensis

Brassica rapa var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese mustard or napa cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a compact head, often with a pale green or white color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its roots are fibrous, and it prefers cool climates with well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, used in various Asian cuisines.
