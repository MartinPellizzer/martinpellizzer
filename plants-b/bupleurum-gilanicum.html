Bupleurum gilanicum

Bupleurum gilanicum, commonly known as Gilan bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Bupleurum gilanicum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
