Boerhavia diffusa var. paludosa

Boerhavia diffusa var. paludosa, commonly known as marsh spurges, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often root at the nodes, and it produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
