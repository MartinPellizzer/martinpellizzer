Brachypodium pinnatum var. megastachyum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
