Berberis conferta var. karsteniana

Berberis conferta var. karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's barberry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
