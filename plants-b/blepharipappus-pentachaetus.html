Blepharipappus pentachaetus

Blepharipappus pentachaetus, commonly known as the five-hooked bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by inflated, bladder-like seed pods that give it its common name.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
