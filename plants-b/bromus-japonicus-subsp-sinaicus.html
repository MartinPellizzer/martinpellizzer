Bromus japonicus subsp. sinaicus

Bromus japonicus subsp.

sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai brome, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
