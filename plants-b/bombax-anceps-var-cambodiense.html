Bombax anceps var. cambodiense

Bombax anceps var. cambodiense, commonly known as the Cambodian silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in the early spring. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and as a shade tree.
