Baccharis grisebachii

Baccharis grisebachii, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baccharis grisebachii is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
