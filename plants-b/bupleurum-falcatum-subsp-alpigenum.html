Bupleurum falcatum subsp. alpigenum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

alpigenum, commonly known as the Alpine siebold's angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
