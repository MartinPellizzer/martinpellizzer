Bastardiastrum gracile

Bastardiastrum gracile, commonly known as slender sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its growth habit allows it to cover the ground, preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.

Bastardiastrum gracile is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
