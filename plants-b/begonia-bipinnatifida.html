Begonia bipinnatifida

Begonia bipinnatifida, commonly known as the laceleaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
