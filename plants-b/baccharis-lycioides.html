Baccharis lycioides

Baccharis lycioides, commonly known as sand baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
