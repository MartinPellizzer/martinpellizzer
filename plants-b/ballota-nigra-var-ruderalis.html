Ballota nigra var. ruderalis

Ballota nigra var. ruderalis, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in urban areas, making it a common weed. It has a taproot system and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
