Bulbophyllum buntingii

Bulbophyllum buntingii, commonly known as the Bunting's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often brightly colored. It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
