Baccharis heterothalmoides

Baccharis heterothalmoides, commonly known as the yellow baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
