Blechnum arborescens var. subspinulososerrulata

Blechnum arborescens var. subspinulososerrulata, commonly known as the spinulose serrulate holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins that are both spinulose and serrulate, giving it a unique texture. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps in identifying the plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
