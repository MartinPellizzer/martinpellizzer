Bupleurum falcatum f. typicum

Bupleurum falcatum f. typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
