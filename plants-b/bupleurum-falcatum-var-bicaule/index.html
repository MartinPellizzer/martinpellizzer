Bupleurum Falcatum Var. Bicaule

Bupleurum falcatum var. bicaule is a lesser-known member of the Apiaceae family known for its unique, bi-pinnate leaves and delicate, yellowish flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, particularly in traditional herbal practices. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and biodiversity. This article explores its characteristics, habitat, and historical significance.
