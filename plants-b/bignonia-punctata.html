Bignonia punctata

Bignonia punctata, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bignonia punctata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
