Boronia ternata var. austrofoliosa

Boronia ternata var. austrofoliosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
