Brickellia multiflora

Brickellia multiflora, commonly known as the golden brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Brickellia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
