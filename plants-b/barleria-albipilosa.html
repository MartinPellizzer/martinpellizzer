Barleria albipilosa

Barleria albipilosa, commonly known as the white-haired barleria, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Barleria albipilosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
