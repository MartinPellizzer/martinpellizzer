Brittonamra caribaea var. amorphifolia

Brittonamra caribaea var. amorphifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its economic value, it is often harvested for timber, leading to conservation concerns.
