Besleria mirifica

Besleria mirifica, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, often found in shady, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are predominantly yellow with red and orange streaks, making it a popular ornamental plant.
