Bixa orellana var. leiocarpa

Bixa orellana var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the annatto tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red capsules. The plant is notable for its seed-containing pods, which are used to produce annatto, a natural dye and food coloring agent. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
