Baccharis wendlandii

Baccharis wendlandii, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry to moist habitats, including woodlands and open areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Baccharis wendlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
