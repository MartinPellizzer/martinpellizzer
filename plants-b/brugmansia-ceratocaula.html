Brugmansia ceratocaula

Brugmansia ceratocaula, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Brugmansia ceratocaula is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes, though it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
