Bulbophyllum melanopogon

Bulbophyllum melanopogon, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with a single, narrow leaf and a long, slender inflorescence. The flowers are typically small, with dark purple petals and a distinctive blackish lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
