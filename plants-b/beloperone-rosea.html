Beloperone rosea

Beloperone rosea, commonly known as the rose bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle rose hue. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Beloperone rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
