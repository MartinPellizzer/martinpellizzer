Buddleja cana

Buddleja cana, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical, and often hairy stems, which support clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy spikes of purple or pink blossoms that attract butterflies and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Buddleja cana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
