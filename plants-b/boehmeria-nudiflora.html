Boehmeria nudiflora

Boehmeria nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower nettle, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive white, fuzzy underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels.

Boehmeria nudiflora is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional textiles and papermaking.
