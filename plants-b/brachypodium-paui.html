Brachypodium paui

Brachypodium paui, commonly known as the dwarf brome, is a grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its short stature and drought tolerance make it a useful species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.

Brachypodium paui plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
