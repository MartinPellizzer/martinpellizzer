Bromus Variegatus Subsp. Villosulus

Variable brome, scientifically known as Bromus variegatus subsp. villosulus, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its variable leaf coloration and hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Due to its adaptability, it is considered both a pioneer species and a potential weed. Its ecological role and agricultural impact make it an important subject for further study.
