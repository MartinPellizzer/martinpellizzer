Bulbophyllum guttatum

Bulbophyllum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually green or yellow with red or brown markings, and they bloom in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
