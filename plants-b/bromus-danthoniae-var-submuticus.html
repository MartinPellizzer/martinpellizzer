Bromus danthoniae var. submuticus

Bromus danthoniae var. submuticus, commonly known as the velvet fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and landscaping purposes.
