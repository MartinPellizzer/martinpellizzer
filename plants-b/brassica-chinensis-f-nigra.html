Brassica chinensis f. nigra

Brassica chinensis f. nigra, commonly known as black mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant.

It is characterized by its dark green, curly leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a biennial, typically growing to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. Its leaves are often used in Asian cuisines for their peppery flavor.

The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
