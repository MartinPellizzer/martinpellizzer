Butyrospermum paradoxum subsp. niloticum

Butyrospermum paradoxum subsp.

niloticum, commonly known as shea butter tree, is a tropical plant native to the savannas of Africa. It is characterized by its large, thick leaves and produces a unique fruit with a hard, spiny exterior.

The fruit contains a fleshy, yellowish pulp that encases a single seed. The seed is rich in oils and is the source of shea butter, widely used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological and economic importance in the regions where it grows.
