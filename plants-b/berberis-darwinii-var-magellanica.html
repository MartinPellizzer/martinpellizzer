Berberis darwinii var. magellanica

Berberis darwinii var. magellanica, commonly known as the Darwin's barberry, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It features spiny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
