Biscutella lyrata var. apula

Biscutella lyrata var. apula, commonly known as the Apulian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from the typical form of Biscutella lyrata.
