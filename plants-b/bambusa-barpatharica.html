Bambusa barpatharica

Bambusa barpatharica, commonly known as the Barpeta bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its aesthetic appeal and ecological role.

Bambusa barpatharica is valued for its rapid growth and sustainable properties, making it an important resource in local ecosystems and human communities.
