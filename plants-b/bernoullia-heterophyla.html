Bernoullia heterophyla

Bernoullia heterophyla, commonly known as the variable mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southern Australia.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for native birds. Its life cycle involves both asexual and sexual reproduction, with seeds dispersed by birds.
