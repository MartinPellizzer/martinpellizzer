Botrys pratericola

Botrys pratericola, commonly known as the Pratericola cluster vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences, which consist of small, white, clustered flowers that resemble a bunch of grapes. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that mature to a yellowish color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botrys pratericola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
