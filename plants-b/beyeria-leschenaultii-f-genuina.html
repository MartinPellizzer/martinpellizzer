Beyeria leschenaultii f. genuina

Beyeria leschenaultii f. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beyeria, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
