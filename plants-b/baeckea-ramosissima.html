Baeckea ramosissima

Baeckea ramosissima, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Baeckea ramosissima is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
