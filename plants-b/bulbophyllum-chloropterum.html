Bulbophyllum chloropterum

Bulbophyllum chloropterum, commonly known as the green-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with distinctive, elongated petals and sepals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
