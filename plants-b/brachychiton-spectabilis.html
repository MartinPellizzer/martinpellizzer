Brachychiton spectabilis

Brachychiton spectabilis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Brachychiton spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
