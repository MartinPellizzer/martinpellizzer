Bactris tomentosa subsp. capillacea

Bactris tomentosa subsp.

capillacea, commonly known as the silky palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetlands or near water bodies. Its fruit is a small, spiny drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
