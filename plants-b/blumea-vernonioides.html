Blumea vernonioides

Blumea vernonioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a misty appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
