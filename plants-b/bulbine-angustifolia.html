Bulbine angustifolia

Bulbine angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
