Bergenia stracheyi var. alba

Bergenia stracheyi var. alba, commonly known as the white bergenia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
