Baccharis subscandens

Baccharis subscandens, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Baccharis subscandens is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
