Baccharis moritziana var. subcrenatoserrata

Baccharis moritziana var. subcrenatoserrata, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in coastal areas.
