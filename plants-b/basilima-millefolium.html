Basilima millefolium

Basilima millefolium, commonly known as thousand-leaf basil, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features delicate, feather-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
