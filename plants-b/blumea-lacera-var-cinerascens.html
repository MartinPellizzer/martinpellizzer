Blumea lacera var. cinerascens

Blumea lacera var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
