Bambusa palmata

Bambusa palmata, commonly known as the river bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, making it a common sight in wetland and riparian habitats.

Bambusa palmata is also valued for its lightweight and durable material, which is used in construction and decorative purposes.
