Bulbophyllum hirudiniferum

Bulbophyllum hirudiniferum, commonly known as the blood-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and can exhibit a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
