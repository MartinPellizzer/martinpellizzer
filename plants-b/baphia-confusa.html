Baphia confusa

Baphia confusa, commonly known as the African baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Baphia confusa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
