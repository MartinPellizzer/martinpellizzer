Brassica oleracea var. percrispa

Brassica oleracea var. percrispa, commonly known as the curly cabbage, is a cultivated variety of the species Brassica oleracea.

It is characterized by its tightly curled, dense leaves that form a compact head, often with a pale green or white coloration. The plant is a biennial, typically grown as an annual in temperate climates, and is valued for its nutritional content and use in culinary applications. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a rosette growth habit, which helps it thrive in cooler conditions.

The leaves are rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a popular choice in gardens and agriculture.
