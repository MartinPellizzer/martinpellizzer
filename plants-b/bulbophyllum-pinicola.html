Bulbophyllum pinicola

Bulbophyllum pinicola, commonly known as the pineapple orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, with a distinctive pineapple-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Bulbophyllum pinicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
