Bonnaya grandiflora

Bonnaya grandiflora, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming at night and closing during the day. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Bonnaya grandiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
