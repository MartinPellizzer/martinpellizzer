Borassus flabelliformis

Borassus flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan palm, is a tall, slender palm native to southern Asia.

It features a slender trunk covered with persistent leaf bases, and its leaves are large, fan-shaped, and often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of three-winged fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found near water sources.

Its ecological and cultural significance makes it an important species in many regions.
