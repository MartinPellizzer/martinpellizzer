Brachystelmaria longifolia

Brachystelmaria longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved starflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces seed capsules after flowering.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
