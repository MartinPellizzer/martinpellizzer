Bastardia viscosa var. luteovirens

Bastardia viscosa var. luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
