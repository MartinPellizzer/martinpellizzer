Begonia × antonietae

Begonia × antonietae, commonly known as the Antoinette begonia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pavonina.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely appreciated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a potted specimen.
