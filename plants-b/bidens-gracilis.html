Bidens gracilis

Bidens gracilis, commonly known as slender beggarticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.

Bidens gracilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
