Brachyloma erianthum

Brachyloma erianthum, commonly known as the woolly brachyloma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils within open forests or heathlands. The plant features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Brachyloma erianthum is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with limited rainfall.
