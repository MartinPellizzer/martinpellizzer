Bothriochloa insculpta var. vegetior

Bothriochloa insculpta var. vegetior, commonly known as the green panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential use in erosion control.
