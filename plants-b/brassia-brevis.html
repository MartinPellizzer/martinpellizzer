Brassia brevis

Brassia brevis, commonly known as the short orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. Its flowers are borne on short, erect spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

Brassia brevis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
