Bakeriella disaco

Bakeriella disaco, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater during drought periods.

Bakeriella disaco is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
