Bromus squarrosus var. minor

Bromus squarrosus var. minor, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray appearance due to a dense covering of hairs.

It is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
