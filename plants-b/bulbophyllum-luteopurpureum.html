Bulbophyllum luteopurpureum

Bulbophyllum luteopurpureum, commonly known as the Purple-Orange Pterostigma, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry environments. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, typically in shades of orange, red, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

This species is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
