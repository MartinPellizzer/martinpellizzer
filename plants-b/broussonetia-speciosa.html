Broussonetia speciosa

Broussonetia speciosa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Broussonetia speciosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
