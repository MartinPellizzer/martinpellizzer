Boehmeria didymogyne

Boehmeria didymogyne, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or broadleaf nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its fibrous stalks, which are used in traditional papermaking. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
