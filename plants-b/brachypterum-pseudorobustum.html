Brachypterum pseudorobustum

Brachypterum pseudorobustum, commonly known as the narrow-winged acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are pollinated by birds or insects.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
