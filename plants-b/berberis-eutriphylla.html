Berberis eutriphylla

Berberis eutriphylla, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which turns red in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
