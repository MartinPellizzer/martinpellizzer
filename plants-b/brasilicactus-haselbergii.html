Brasilicactus haselbergii

Brasilicactus haselbergii, commonly known as the Haselberg's cactus, is a unique species native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms dense clusters of short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
