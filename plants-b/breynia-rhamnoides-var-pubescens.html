Breynia rhamnoides var. pubescens

Breynia rhamnoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broom.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a landscape plant.
