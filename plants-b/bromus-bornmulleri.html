Bromus bornmulleri

Bromus bornmulleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous bracteate florets. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Bromus bornmulleri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
