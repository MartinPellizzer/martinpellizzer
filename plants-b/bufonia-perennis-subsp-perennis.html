Bufonia perennis subsp. perennis

Bufonia perennis subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the everlasting bluebell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
