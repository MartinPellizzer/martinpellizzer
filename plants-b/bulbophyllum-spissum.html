Bulbophyllum spissum

Bulbophyllum spissum, commonly known as the dense-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Bulbophyllum spissum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
