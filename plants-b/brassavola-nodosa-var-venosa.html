Brassavola nodosa var. venosa

Brassavola nodosa var. venosa, commonly known as the Venose Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant, orchid flowers that display a unique venous pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
