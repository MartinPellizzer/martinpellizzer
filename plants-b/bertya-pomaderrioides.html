Bertya pomaderrioides

Bertya pomaderrioides, commonly known as the coastal bertya, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

Bertya pomaderrioides is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.
