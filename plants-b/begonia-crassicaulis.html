Begonia crassicaulis

Begonia crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
