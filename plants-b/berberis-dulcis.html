Berberis dulcis

Berberis dulcis, commonly known as the sweet barberry, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small yellow flowers, and red-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The plant also serves as a host for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
