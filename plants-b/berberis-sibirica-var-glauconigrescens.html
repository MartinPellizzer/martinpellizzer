Berberis sibirica var. glauconigrescens

Berberis sibirica var. glauconigrescens, commonly known as the Siberian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in colder climates.

The variety glauconigrescens is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which adds a unique visual appeal to the landscape.
