Bromus odoratus

Bromus odoratus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in meadows and open woods. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight aromatic scent when crushed.

Bromus odoratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
