Baccharis pseudotridentata

Baccharis pseudotridentata, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across arid environments.

Baccharis pseudotridentata is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid regions.
