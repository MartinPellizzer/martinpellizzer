Brassaiopsis karmalaica

Brassaiopsis karmalaica, commonly known as the Karmala bracts.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern India, particularly in the state of Karnataka. The plant is characterized by its unique, palm-like leaves and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
