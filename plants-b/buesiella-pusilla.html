Buesiella pusilla

Buesiella pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf buesiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and usually display shades of white or pale pink.
