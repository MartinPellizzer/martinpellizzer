Begonia sucrensis

Begonia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. Begonia sucrensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
