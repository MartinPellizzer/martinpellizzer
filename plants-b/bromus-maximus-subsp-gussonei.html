Bromus maximus subsp. gussonei

Bromus maximus subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the Italian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces loose, feathery inflorescences that are often pinkish to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
