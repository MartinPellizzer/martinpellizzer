Ballota simonii

Ballota simonii, commonly known as the Mediterranean ballota, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Ballota simonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
