Bidens sambucifolia

Bidens sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucus-leaved ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, often hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, typical of the genus Bidens.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
