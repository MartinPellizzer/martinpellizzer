Baccharis ibaguensis

Baccharis ibaguensis, commonly known as the Ibaguense baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
