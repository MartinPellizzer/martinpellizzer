Bolax echinus

Bolax echinus, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with distinctive, spiny bracts. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bolax echinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
