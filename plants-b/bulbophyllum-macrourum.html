Bulbophyllum macrourum

Bulbophyllum macrourum, commonly known as the long-tongued bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and long, slender inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and horticultural appeal, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
