Braya humilis var. interior

Braya humilis var. interior, commonly known as the Interior bladderleaf, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically features rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters near the base of the plant.

This variety is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in areas with limited water availability.
