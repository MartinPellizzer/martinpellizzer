Brasolia roezlii

Brasolia roezlii, commonly known as the Zonal Balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants known for their cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Brasolia roezlii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
