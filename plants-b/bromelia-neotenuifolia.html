Bromelia neotenuifolia

Bromelia neotenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that form a rosette shape, often accumulating water in their central cup. Its flowering stems produce vibrant orange or red flowers, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Bromelia neotenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
