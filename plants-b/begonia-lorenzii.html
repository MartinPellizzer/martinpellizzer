Begonia lorenzii

Begonia lorenzii, commonly known as the Lorentz begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
