Bejaranoa balansae

Bejaranoa balansae, commonly known as the Balansae palm, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, Bejaranoa balansae is classified as critically endangered.

Its botanical study is crucial for understanding the biodiversity of the region and for conservation efforts.
