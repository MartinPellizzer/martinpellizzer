Bromopsis moellendorfiana

Bromopsis moellendorfiana, commonly known as the Moellendorff's bulbous grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces compact, rounded clumps with dense foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Bromopsis moellendorfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and foliage texture.
