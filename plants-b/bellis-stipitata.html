Bellis stipitata

Bellis stipitata, commonly known as the upright daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Bellis stipitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
