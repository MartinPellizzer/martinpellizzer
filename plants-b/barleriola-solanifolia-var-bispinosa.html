Barleriola solanifolia var. bispinosa

Barleriola solanifolia var. bispinosa, commonly known as the two-spined barleriola, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, egg-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are often sparsely covered with small, stiff spines, giving it its varietal name.

It is commonly found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.
