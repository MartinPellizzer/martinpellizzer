Barroetea glutinosa

Barroetea glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which is used by indigenous peoples for various purposes. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or red hues, and it produces small, round fruits.

Barroetea glutinosa is valued not only for its unique botanical features but also for its traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
