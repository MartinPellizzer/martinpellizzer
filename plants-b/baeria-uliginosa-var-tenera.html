Baeria uliginosa var. tenera

Baeria uliginosa var. tenera, commonly known as the bog moss, is a variant of the bog moss species native to wetland environments.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in bogs and fens. The plant forms dense mats of prostrate stems covered with leaf-like structures called phyllodes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or brownish and are borne in clusters.

The plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
