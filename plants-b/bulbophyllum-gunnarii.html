Bulbophyllum gunnarii

Bulbophyllum gunnarii, commonly known as the Gunner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often colorful flower spikes that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking addition to botanical collections.
