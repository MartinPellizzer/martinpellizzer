Begonia warburgii

Begonia warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green veins and a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink, often with contrasting yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia warburgii is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
