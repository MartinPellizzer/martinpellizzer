Barleria pseudoprionitis

Barleria pseudoprionitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
