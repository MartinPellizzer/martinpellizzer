Biarum alexandrinum

Biarum alexandrinum, commonly known as the Alexandrian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, strap-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Biarum alexandrinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
