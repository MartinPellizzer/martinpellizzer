Buddleja elegans

Buddleja elegans, commonly known as the silver-leaf butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Buddleja elegans is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
