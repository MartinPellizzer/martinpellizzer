Baccharis poeppigiana

Baccharis poeppigiana, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Baccharis poeppigiana is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture.
