Bromus andinus var. scabrivalva

Bromus andinus var. scabrivalva, commonly known as the Andean roughleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for livestock and habitat for various invertebrates.
