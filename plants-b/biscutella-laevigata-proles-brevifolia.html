Biscutella laevigata proles brevifolia

Biscutella laevigata proles brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
