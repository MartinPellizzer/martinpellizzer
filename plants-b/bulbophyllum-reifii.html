Bulbophyllum reifii

Bulbophyllum reifii, commonly known as the Reif's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features pseudo-bulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum reifii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical environment.
