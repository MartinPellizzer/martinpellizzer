Buceras chebula

Buceras chebula, commonly known as the Indian date tree or chebuli, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the fruit containing compounds like chebulinic acid and tannins. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.

The tree's adaptability and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
