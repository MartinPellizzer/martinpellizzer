Bignonia fluminensis

Bignonia fluminensis, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in humid environments.

Bignonia fluminensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
