Brenandendron titanophyllum

Brenandendron titanophyllum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in segments rather than from a single stem, allowing it to spread across the forest floor. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to capture sunlight in the dense undergrowth.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is a rare and endangered species, threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
