Berberis tortuosa

Berberis tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its twisted, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
