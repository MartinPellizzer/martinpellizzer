Begonia vincentina

Begonia vincentina, commonly known as Vincent's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia vincentina is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
