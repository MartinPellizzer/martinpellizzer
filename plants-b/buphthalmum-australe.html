Buphthalmum australe

Buphthalmum australe, commonly known as the Australian sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a hairy texture.

Buphthalmum australe is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and rapid growth in disturbed soils.
