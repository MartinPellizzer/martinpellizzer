Boea acutifolia

Boea acutifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Boea acutifolia is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or other structures.
