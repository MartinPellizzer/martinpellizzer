Bunchosia nitida var. domingensis

Bunchosia nitida var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican whitebark, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
