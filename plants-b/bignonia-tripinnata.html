Bignonia tripinnata

Bignonia tripinnata, commonly known as the crossvine, is a vigorous, climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Bignonia tripinnata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
