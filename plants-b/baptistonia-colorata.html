Baptistonia colorata

Baptistonia colorata, commonly known as the showy blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, blue-violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baptistonia colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant gardens.
