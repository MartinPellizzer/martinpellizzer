Betula platyphylloides var. microphylla

Betula platyphylloides var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch characterized by its smaller, more densely packed leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is known for its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of yellow and orange.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
