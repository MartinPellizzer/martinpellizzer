Breonadia salicina

Breonadia salicina, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable addition to xeriscapes.
