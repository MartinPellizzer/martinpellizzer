Biscutella montana var. densiflora

Biscutella montana var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually erect with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in alpine meadows and dry slopes.
