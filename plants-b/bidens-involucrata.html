Bidens involucrata

Bidens involucrata, commonly known as the slender tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Bidens involucrata produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
