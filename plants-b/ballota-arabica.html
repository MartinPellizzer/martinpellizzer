Ballota arabica

Ballota arabica, commonly known as the Arabian ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Ballota arabica is often found in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
