Bursera amplifolia

Bursera amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and simple, elliptic or ovate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Bursera amplifolia is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought and heat.
