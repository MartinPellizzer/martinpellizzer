Bartsia integrifolia

Bartsia integrifolia, commonly known as the common bartsia, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that often grow from the base and bear opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Bartsia integrifolia is known for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local insect populations.
