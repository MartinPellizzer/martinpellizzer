Bernardina clethreides

Bernardina clethreides, commonly known as the golden clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn red in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered panicles during late summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage.
