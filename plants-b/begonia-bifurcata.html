Begonia bifurcata

Begonia bifurcata, commonly known as the forked begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia bifurcata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
