Brassica napus f. micrantha

Brassica napus f. micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a botanical variant of the oilseed rape species.

It is characterized by its compact, leafy rosette and small, clustered flowers, which distinguish it from other Brassica species. The plant is native to East Asia and is cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
