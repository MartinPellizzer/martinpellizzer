Blumea metziana

Blumea metziana, commonly known as the goldenbutton dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by a crown of bracts, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Blumea metziana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
