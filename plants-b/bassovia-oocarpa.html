Bassovia oocarpa

Bassovia oocarpa, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
