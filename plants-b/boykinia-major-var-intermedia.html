Boykinia major var. intermedia

Boykinia major var. intermedia, commonly known as the Great Basin stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, showy yellow or greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
