Berberis vulgaris var. innominata

Berberis vulgaris var. innominata, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
