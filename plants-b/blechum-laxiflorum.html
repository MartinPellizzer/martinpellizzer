Blechum laxiflorum

Blechum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered blechum, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and are arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the stems.

Blechum laxiflorum is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
