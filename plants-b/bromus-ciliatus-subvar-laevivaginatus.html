Bromus ciliatus subvar. laevivaginatus

Bromus ciliatus subvar. laevivaginatus, commonly known as the smooth brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth surface. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged in a panicle, and the flowers are wind-pollinated. This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
