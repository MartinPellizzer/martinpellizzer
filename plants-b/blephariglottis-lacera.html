Blephariglottis lacera

Blephariglottis lacera, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish powdery coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
