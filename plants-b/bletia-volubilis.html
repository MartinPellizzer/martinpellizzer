Bletia volubilis

Bletia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its climbing habit, with stems that twine around supporting structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bletia volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
