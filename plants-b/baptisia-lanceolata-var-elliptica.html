Baptisia lanceolata var. elliptica

Baptisia lanceolata var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with lanceolate to elliptic-shaped leaflets, which are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Baptisia lanceolata var. elliptica is valued for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant in ecological landscapes.
