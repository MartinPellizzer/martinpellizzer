Bourreria virgata var. vestita

Bourreria virgata var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
