Bambusa farinacea

Bambusa farinacea, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.

Bambusa farinacea is also valued for its use in basket-making and as a sustainable resource in some regions.
