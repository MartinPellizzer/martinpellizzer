Bridelia rhamnoides

Bridelia rhamnoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
