Begonia gigabracteata

Begonia gigabracteata, commonly known as the giant bract begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that often resemble flowers, which serve to attract pollinators. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color.

Begonia gigabracteata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and unique foliage.
