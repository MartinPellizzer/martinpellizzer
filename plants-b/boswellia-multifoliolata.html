Boswellia multifoliolata

Boswellia multifoliolata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to the dry regions of East Africa, including Somalia and Kenya. The plant is characterized by its dense, aromatic resin that is harvested for use in incense and traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The tree thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its deep root system.
