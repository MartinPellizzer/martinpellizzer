Banisteria glauca

Banisteria glauca, commonly known as the blue banister, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its waxy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spindle-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, tropical-looking plant.
