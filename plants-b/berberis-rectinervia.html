Berberis rectinervia

Berberis rectinervia, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Berberis genus. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, contributing to its scientific name.

Berberis rectinervia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
