Bauhinia bredemeyeri

Bauhinia bredemeyeri, commonly known as the flame tree or the crown of thorns, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and can be up to 30 cm long, with small, ovate leaflets. Bauhinia bredemeyeri thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
