Berberis orthobotrys subsp. orthobotrys

Berberis orthobotrys subsp.

orthobotrys, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
