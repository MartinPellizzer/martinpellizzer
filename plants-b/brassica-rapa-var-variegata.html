Brassica rapa var. variegata

Brassica rapa var. variegata, commonly known as the rainbow chard or variegated turnip, is a cultivated variety of the mustard family.

It is characterized by its colorful, leafy stems and leaves that display a range of variegated patterns, including red, yellow, and white. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary uses.
