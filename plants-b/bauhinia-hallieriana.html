Bauhinia hallieriana

Bauhinia hallieriana, commonly known as the velvetwood or Indian wisteria, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish-brown before falling in autumn. The plant produces showy, pendant-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils.

Bauhinia hallieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
