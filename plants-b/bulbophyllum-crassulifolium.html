Bulbophyllum crassulifolium

Bulbophyllum crassulifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
