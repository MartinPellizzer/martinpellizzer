Berberis corallina

Berberis corallina, commonly known as coral berberis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its bright red or orange berries and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by its distinctive, coral-colored fruits. Berberis corallina thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
