Bernardia polymorpha f. salicina

Bernardia polymorpha f. salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf bernardia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, usually in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in moist, acidic conditions.
