Brachyactis alpina

Brachyactis alpina, commonly known as the Alpine brachyactis, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments.

Brachyactis alpina is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny locations.
