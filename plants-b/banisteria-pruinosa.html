Banisteria pruinosa

Banisteria pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, purplish-blue foliage and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Banisteria pruinosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
