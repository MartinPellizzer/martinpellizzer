Berkheya oppositifolia

Berkheya oppositifolia, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Berkheya oppositifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
