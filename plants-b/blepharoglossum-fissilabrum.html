Blepharoglossum fissilabrum

Blepharoglossum fissilabrum, commonly known as the fissured leaf fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
