Bucholzia diffusa

Bucholzia diffusa, commonly known as the African balsa or white mulga, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Bucholzia diffusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
