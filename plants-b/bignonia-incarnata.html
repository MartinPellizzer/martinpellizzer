Bignonia incarnata

Bignonia incarnata, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along fences, walls, and trees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms.
