Berberis dielsiana

Berberis dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall foliage, which turns a striking red or orange.
