Broussonetia × kazinoki var. humilis

Broussonetia × kazinoki var. humilis, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, fuzzy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
