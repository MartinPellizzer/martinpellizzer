Bauhinia suffruticosa

Bauhinia suffruticosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmately compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. Bauhinia suffruticosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitats.
