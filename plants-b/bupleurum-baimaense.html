Bupleurum baimaense

Bupleurum baimaense, commonly known as the Baima skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Bupleurum baimaense is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
