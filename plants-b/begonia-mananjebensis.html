Begonia mananjebensis

Begonia mananjebensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a deep green color and a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
