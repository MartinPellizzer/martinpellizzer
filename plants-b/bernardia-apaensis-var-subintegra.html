Bernardia apaensis var. subintegra

Bernardia apaensis var. subintegra, commonly known as the slender bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its botanical features include woody stems and a shrub-like growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
