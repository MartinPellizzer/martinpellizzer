Brassica purpuraria

Brassica purpuraria, commonly known as the purple sprouting broccoli, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, purple-colored flower heads that resemble broccoli, which develop in the spring following a period of cold weather. The plant produces small, white flowers that eventually give way to round, green pods containing seeds. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible florets and leaves.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to both gardens and culinary traditions.
