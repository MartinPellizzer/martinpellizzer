Brassica napus var. dichotoma

Brassica napus var. dichotoma, commonly known as wild radish, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, and it often grows in disturbed soils along roadsides or in fields.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
