Bravaisia integerrima var. pilosa

Bravaisia integerrima var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy brachylecithum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
