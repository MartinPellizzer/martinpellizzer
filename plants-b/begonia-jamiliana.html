Begonia jamiliana

Begonia jamiliana, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to gardens and greenhouses.

Begonia jamiliana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its role in local ecosystems.
