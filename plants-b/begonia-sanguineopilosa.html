Begonia sanguineopilosa

Begonia sanguineopilosa, commonly known as the blood-red begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, reddish hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
