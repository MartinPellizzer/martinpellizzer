Brachypodium paui f. fontqueri

Brachypodium paui f. fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri's brachypodium, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a model organism in botanical research.
