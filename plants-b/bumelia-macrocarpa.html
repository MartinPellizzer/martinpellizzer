Bumelia macrocarpa

Bumelia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, inflated, greenish-yellow fruits that turn reddish when mature.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
