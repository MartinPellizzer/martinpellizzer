Bidens pilosa f. ternata

Bidens pilosa f. ternata, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which often have a sticky texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
