Bulbophyllum concatenatum

Bulbophyllum concatenatum, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often pendulous inflorescence and its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Bulbophyllum concatenatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
