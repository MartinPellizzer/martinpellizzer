Berberis chillanensis

Berberis chillanensis, commonly known as the Chillan barberry, is a shrub native to Chile.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
