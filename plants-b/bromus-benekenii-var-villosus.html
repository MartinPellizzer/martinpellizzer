Bromus benekenii var. villosus

Bromus benekenii var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
