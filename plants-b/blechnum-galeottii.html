Blechnum galeottii

Blechnum galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist, well-drained soil.

The fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and can be propagated through spores or division.
