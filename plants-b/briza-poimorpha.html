Briza poimorpha

Briza poimorpha, commonly known as the slender quaking grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate, drooping inflorescence that resembles a quaking or trembling motion in the wind. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct blue-green coloration.

Briza poimorpha produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
