Bauhinia ramosissima

Bauhinia ramosissima, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright orange to red bract clusters that resemble flames, which appear during the flowering season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. Bauhinia ramosissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
