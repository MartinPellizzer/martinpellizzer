Bambusa atra

Bambusa atra, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its dark purple to black stems, which are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a glossy green color, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa atra is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
