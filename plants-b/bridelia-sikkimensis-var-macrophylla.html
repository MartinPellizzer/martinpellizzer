Bridelia sikkimensis var. macrophylla

Bridelia sikkimensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bridelia, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
