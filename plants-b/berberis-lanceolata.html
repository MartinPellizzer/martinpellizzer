Berberis lanceolata

Berberis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold climates, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
