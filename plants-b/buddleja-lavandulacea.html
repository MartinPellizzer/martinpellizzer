Buddleja lavandulacea

Buddleja lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender bush or summer lilac, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Buddleja lavandulacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
