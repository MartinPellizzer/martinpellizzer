Barfussia robusta

Barfussia robusta, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Barfussia robusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
