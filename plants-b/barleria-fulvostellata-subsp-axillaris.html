Barleria fulvostellata subsp. axillaris

Barleria fulvostellata subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the yellow barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to local wildlife.
