Begonia subtruncata

Begonia subtruncata, commonly known as the truncated begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and often have a slight reddish tinge along the veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal inflorescences, and come in shades of pink or red.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
