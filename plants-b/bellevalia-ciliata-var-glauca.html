Bellevalia ciliata var. glauca

Bellevalia ciliata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are linear and often have a blue-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
