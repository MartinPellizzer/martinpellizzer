Bonamia media var. villosa

Bonamia media var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bonamia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
