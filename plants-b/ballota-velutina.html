Ballota velutina

Ballota velutina, commonly known as velvet ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds.

Ballota velutina is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
