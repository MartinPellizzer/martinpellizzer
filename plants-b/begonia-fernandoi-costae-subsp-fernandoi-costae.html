Begonia fernandoi-costae subsp. fernandoi-costae

Begonia fernandoi-costae subsp.

fernandoi-costae, commonly known as the Fernando's begonia, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists.
