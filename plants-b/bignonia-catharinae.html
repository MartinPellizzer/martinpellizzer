Bignonia catharinae

Bignonia catharinae, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, showy blooms.

Bignonia catharinae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
