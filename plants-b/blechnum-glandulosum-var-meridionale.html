Blechnum glandulosum var. meridionale

Blechnum glandulosum var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern hairy shield fern, is a fern native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its common name.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
