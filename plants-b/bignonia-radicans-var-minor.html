Bignonia radicans var. minor

Bignonia radicans var. minor, commonly known as the lesser trumpet vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth, it is often used as a decorative vine in gardens and landscapes.
