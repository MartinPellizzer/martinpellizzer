Babiana atrodeltoidea

Babiana atrodeltoidea, commonly known as the black-veined babiana, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Babiana atrodeltoidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
