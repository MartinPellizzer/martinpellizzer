Blumea membranacea

Blumea membranacea, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in flat, disc-like clusters.

Blumea membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
