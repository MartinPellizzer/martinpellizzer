Bromopsis gordjaginii

Bromopsis gordjaginii, commonly known as the Armenian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. Bromopsis gordjaginii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it is primarily appreciated for its ecological role in its native environment.
