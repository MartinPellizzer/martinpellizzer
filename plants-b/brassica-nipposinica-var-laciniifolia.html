Brassica nipposinica var. laciniifolia

Brassica nipposinica var. laciniifolia, commonly known as the Japanese kale or ornamental kale, is a cultivar of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed, and often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant typically grows as an upright, herbaceous perennial with a rosette-like structure. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, similar to other members of the brassica family.
