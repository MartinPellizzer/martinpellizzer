Bambusa humilis

Bambusa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense stands with short, stout culms. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Bambusa humilis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
