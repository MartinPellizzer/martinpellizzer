Bulbophyllum muscicola

Bulbophyllum muscicola, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
