Barleria bechuanensis

Barleria bechuanensis, commonly known as the Bechuanaland barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Barleria bechuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
