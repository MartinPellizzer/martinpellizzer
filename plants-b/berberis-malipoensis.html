Berberis malipoensis

Berberis malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo barberry, is a shrub native to China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
