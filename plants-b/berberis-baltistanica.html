Berberis baltistanica

Berberis baltistanica, commonly known as the Baltistan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
