Boesenbergia hirta

Boesenbergia hirta, commonly known as the hairy ginger or Malabar ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces erect, leafy stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Boesenbergia hirta is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
