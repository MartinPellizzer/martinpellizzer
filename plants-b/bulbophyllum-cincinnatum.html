Bulbophyllum cincinnatum

Bulbophyllum cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it has a delicate, epiphytic growth habit.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
