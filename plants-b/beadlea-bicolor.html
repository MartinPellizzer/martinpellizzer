Beadlea bicolor

Beadlea bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with the upper part of the flower typically being white or pale yellow and the lower part a deep red or orange. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear during the warmer months. Beadlea bicolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
