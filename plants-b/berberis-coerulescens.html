Berberis coerulescens

Berberis coerulescens, commonly known as the blueberry barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the autumn, and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red-orange berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans due to their tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and borders.
