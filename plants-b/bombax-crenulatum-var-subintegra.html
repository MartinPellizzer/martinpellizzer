Bombax crenulatum var. subintegra

Bombax crenulatum var. subintegra, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
