Buddleja teucrioides

Buddleja teucrioides, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy spikes of purple or pink blossoms in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with ecological value.

Buddleja teucrioides is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
