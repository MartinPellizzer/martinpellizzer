Byttneria catalpifolia subsp. africana

Byttneria catalpifolia subsp.

africana, commonly known as African catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental features and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
