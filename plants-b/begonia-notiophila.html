Begonia notiophila

Begonia notiophila, commonly known as the New Guinea begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Begonia notiophila thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value.
