Bulbophyllum cavibulbum

Bulbophyllum cavibulbum, commonly known as the Cavibulb Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in seasonal dry periods.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
