Balanophora oosterzeeana

Balanophora oosterzeeana, commonly known as the Oosterzeeana ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the orchid family.

It is a myco-heterotrophic plant, meaning it lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on fungal associations for nutrients. The plant produces small, white flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators. It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it grows in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
