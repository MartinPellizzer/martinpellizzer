Barleria mucronata

Barleria mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
