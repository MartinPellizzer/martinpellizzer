Byrsonima dealbata var. montana

Byrsonima dealbata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain chuchuhuasi, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. The plant is often found in moist, temperate climates at higher altitudes. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
