Berberis sellowiana var. typica

Berberis sellowiana var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.
