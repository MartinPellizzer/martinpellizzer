Bidens grandiflora var. serrulata

Bidens grandiflora var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered with stiff, bristly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings, typically blooming in late summer to early fall.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
