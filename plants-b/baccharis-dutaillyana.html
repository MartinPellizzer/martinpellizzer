Baccharis dutaillyana

Baccharis dutaillyana, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its structure includes woody stems and dense foliage, making it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
