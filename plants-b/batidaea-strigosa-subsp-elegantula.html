Batidaea strigosa subsp. elegantula

Batidaea strigosa subsp.

elegantula, commonly known as the elegant lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, fragrant, white to pale pink floral spikes.

The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster, typical of the orchid family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender leaves and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form of Batidaea strigosa.
