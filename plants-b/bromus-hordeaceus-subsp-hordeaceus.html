Bromus hordeaceus subsp. hordeaceus

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

hordeaceus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it flowers in late spring to early summer.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
