Banalia occidentalis

Banalia occidentalis, commonly known as the western banalia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Banalia occidentalis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations.
