Brachystegia tamarindoides

Brachystegia tamarindoides, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and long, pendulous pods. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in dry savannas and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.
