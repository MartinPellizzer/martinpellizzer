Bumelia cubensis var. oblongata

Bumelia cubensis var. oblongata, commonly known as the Cuban bumelia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry to moist tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
