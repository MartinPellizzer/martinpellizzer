Baileya thurberi

Baileya thurberi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its fruit is a small, wind-dispersed achene, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Baileya thurberi is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
