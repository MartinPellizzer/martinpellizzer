Baccharis bracteata

Baccharis bracteata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Baccharis bracteata is also valued for its drought tolerance and use in xeriscaping.
