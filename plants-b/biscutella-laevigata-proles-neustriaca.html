Biscutella laevigata proles neustriaca

Biscutella laevigata proles neustriaca, commonly known as the Neustrian biscuitweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

This species is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
