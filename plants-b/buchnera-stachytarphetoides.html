Buchnera stachytarphetoides

Buchnera stachytarphetoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
