Boscia foetida subsp. filipes

Boscia foetida subsp.

filipes, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
