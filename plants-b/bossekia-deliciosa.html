Bossekia deliciosa

Bossekia deliciosa, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from purple to red and green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by heart-shaped, edible tubers. It thrives in warm climates with well-draining soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Bossekia deliciosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and is also valued for its edible roots in some cultures.
