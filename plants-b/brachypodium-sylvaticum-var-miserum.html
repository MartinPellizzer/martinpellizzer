Brachypodium sylvaticum var. miserum

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. miserum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
