Begonia dubia

Begonia dubia, commonly known as the ghost begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia dubia requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
