Bignonia rubescens

Bignonia rubescens, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
