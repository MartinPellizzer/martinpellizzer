Bulbophyllum rubrolingue

Bulbophyllum rubrolingue, commonly known as the red-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking red-brown markings on its leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
