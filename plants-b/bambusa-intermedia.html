Bambusa intermedia

Bambusa intermedia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking golden-green culms that add a vibrant aesthetic to gardens and landscapes. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it ideal for smaller spaces or as a decorative element. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa intermedia is relatively low maintenance, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
