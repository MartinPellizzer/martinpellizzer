Bystropogon glabrescens

Bystropogon glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-barked tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
