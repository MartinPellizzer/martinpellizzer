Berberis pachyacantha

Berberis pachyacantha, commonly known as the thick-stemmed barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of berberine. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Berberis pachyacantha is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
