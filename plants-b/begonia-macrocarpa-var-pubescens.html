Begonia macrocarpa var. pubescens

Begonia macrocarpa var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvety begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
