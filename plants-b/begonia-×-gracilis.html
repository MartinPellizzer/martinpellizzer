Begonia × gracilis

Begonia × gracilis, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia × gracilis is popular in hanging baskets and as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
