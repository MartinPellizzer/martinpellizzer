Blumea bovei

Blumea bovei, commonly known as the Bornean blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Blumea bovei is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and role in local ecosystems.
