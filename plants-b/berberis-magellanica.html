Berberis magellanica

Berberis magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
