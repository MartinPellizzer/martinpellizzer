Bidens hillebrandiana

Bidens hillebrandiana, commonly known as the yellow beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers feature yellow petals and prominent white ray florets.

Bidens hillebrandiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a decorative plant in gardens.
