Brachiaria stipitata

Brachiaria stipitata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Brachiaria stipitata is known for its ability to form dense stands, which can help prevent soil erosion.

Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, appearing in late summer to early fall.
