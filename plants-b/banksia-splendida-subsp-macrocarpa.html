Banksia splendida subsp. macrocarpa

Banksia splendida subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the Red Banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its striking red flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces long, cylindrical flower heads that are composed of numerous small, fluffy flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture.

This subspecies is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
