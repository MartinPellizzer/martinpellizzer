Butia capitata var. erythrospatha

Butia capitata var. erythrospatha, commonly known as the red-spotted palm, is a striking variant of the bottle palm native to Brazil.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive red or pink spines that adorn the leaf stems, giving it a unique visual appearance. The leaves are large, feather-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is relatively slow-growing and drought-tolerant, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
