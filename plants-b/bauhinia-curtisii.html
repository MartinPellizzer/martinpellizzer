Bauhinia curtisii

Bauhinia curtisii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
