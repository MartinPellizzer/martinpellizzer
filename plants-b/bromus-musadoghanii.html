Bromus musadoghanii

Bromus musadoghanii, commonly known as the Musadoghan grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
