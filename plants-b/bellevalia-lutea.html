Bellevalia lutea

Bellevalia lutea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bellevalia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
