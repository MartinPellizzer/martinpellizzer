Bigelowia glauca

Bigelowia glauca, commonly known as the alpine snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are small, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps to conserve moisture.

Bigelowia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
