Bauhinia candida

Bauhinia candida, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that resemble flames. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Bauhinia candida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
