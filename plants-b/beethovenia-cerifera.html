Beethovenia cerifera

Beethovenia cerifera, commonly known as the candlewax plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its waxy, candle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
