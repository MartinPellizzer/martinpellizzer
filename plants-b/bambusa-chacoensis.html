Bambusa chacoensis

Bambusa chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bambusa chacoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some local construction and crafts.
