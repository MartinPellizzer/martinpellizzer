Bacopa marginata

Bacopa marginata, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies or in shaded areas. Its aerial parts are often used in traditional medicine for their cognitive-enhancing properties.

Bacopa marginata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify water.
