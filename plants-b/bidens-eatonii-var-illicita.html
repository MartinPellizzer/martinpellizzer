Bidens eatonii var. illicita

Bidens eatonii var. illicita, commonly known as the illicit bidens, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with slightly serrated edges. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, producing both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
