Begonia parcifolia

Begonia parcifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically features clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and modest flowering make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
