Brittonrosea lancifera

Brittonrosea lancifera, commonly known as the lanceleaf Brittonrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and other flowering shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
