Bactris turbinata

Bactris turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by elongated, edible fruits.

Bactris turbinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
