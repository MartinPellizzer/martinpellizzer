Banksia bipinnatifida subsp. multifida

Banksia bipinnatifida subsp.

multifida, commonly known as the broad-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant features long, narrow, and often recurved leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for a variety of native pollinators.
