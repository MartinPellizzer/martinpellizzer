Biscutella valentina var. guestphalica

Biscutella valentina var. guestphalica, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or pink rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb with slender, ascending stems and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
