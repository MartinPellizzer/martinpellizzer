Brassica elongata f. genuina

Brassica elongata f. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and cabbage. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
