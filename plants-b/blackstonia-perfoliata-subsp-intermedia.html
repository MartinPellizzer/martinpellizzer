Blackstonia perfoliata subsp. intermedia

Blackstonia perfoliata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate blackstonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often perforated or have a fused base, forming a tube around the stem.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
