Baccharis kellermanii

Baccharis kellermanii, commonly known as Kellerman's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, yellowish inflorescences.

Baccharis kellermanii is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
