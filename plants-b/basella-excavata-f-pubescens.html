Basella excavata f. pubescens

Basella excavata f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white, cruciform flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. Its fruits are rounded pods that contain several small, round seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive weed in some regions.
