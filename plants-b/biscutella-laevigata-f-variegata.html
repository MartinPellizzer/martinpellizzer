Biscutella laevigata f. variegata

Biscutella laevigata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
