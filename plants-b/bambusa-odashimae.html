Bambusa odashimae

Bambusa odashimae, commonly known as the Oda Shimae bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa odashimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable utility in various applications.
