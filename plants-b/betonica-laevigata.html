Betonica laevigata

Betonica laevigata, commonly known as lesser betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Betonica laevigata is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
