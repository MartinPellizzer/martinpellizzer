Biscutella coronopifolia subsp. mediterranea

Biscutella coronopifolia subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The subspecies mediterranea is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other subspecies.
