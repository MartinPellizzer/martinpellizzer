Bulbophyllum cuniculiforme

Bulbophyllum cuniculiforme, commonly known as the "rabbit-shaped orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bulbous pseudobulbs that resemble a rabbit's head, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
