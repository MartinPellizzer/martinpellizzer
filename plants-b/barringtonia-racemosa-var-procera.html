Barringtonia racemosa var. procera

Barringtonia racemosa var. procera, commonly known as the giant white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree has a distinctive, buttressed trunk and produces large, fibrous fruits that split open to release seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
