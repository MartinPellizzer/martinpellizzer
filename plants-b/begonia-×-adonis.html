Begonia × adonis

Begonia × adonis, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pavonina, combining the best traits of both parents. The plant typically produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is often heart-shaped and can be variegated, adding to its visual appeal.

Begonia × adonis thrives in well-draining soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
