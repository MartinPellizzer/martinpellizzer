Bletia perrinii

Bletia perrinii, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces tall, slender spikes bearing small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Bletia perrinii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

Its unique botanical features make it a notable species in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
