Brittonastrum neomexicanum

Brittonastrum neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico brittlestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing some protection against desiccation.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.
