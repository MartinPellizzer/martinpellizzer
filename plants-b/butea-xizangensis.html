Butea xizangensis

Butea xizangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of China, Nepal, and India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant display to its natural habitat. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Butea xizangensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
