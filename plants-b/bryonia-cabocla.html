Bryonia cabocla

Bryonia cabocla, commonly known as the Brazilian fig-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests.

Bryonia cabocla is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
