Barnadesia caryophylla

Barnadesia caryophylla, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
