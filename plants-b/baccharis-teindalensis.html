Baccharis teindalensis

Baccharis teindalensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is known for its ability to thrive in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowering heads are small and yellow, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
