Brittonamra caribaea var. albida

Brittonamra caribaea var. albida, commonly known as the white mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. It is valued for its durable, lightweight wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
