Berlandiera monocephala

Berlandiera monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed blazingstar, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, hairy seeds in a composite flower head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Berlandiera monocephala is adapted to arid environments and is considered a valuable plant for pollinators and native landscaping.
