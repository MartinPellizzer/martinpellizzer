Beilschmiedia brassii

Beilschmiedia brassii, commonly known as the yellow beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
