Berberis

Berberis, commonly known as barberry, is a genus of about 500 species of flowering plants in the family Berberidaceae.

These shrubs are characterized by their thorny stems, small leaves, and brightly colored berries that range from yellow to red. Many species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Asia, Europe, and North America. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, and some species are used in traditional medicine.

The berries contain berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
