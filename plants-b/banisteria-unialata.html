Banisteria unialata

Banisteria unialata, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
