Bromus erectus var. hercegovinus

Bromus erectus var. hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright blades with a bluish-green color. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to reddish-brown flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and resilience.
