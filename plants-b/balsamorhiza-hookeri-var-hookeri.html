Balsamorhiza hookeri var. hookeri

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a thick, hairy coating. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
