Biscutella macrocarpa

Biscutella macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a thick, fleshy taproot that stores nutrients. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Biscutella macrocarpa is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
