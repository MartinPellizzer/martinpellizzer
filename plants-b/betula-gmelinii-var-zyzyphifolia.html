Betula gmelinii var. zyzyphifolia

Betula gmelinii var. zyzyphifolia, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a variant of the birch tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, triangular leaves that are often grayish-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typical of the Betula genus. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
