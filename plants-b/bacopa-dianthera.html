Bacopa dianthera

Bacopa dianthera, commonly known as the small-leaf water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Bacopa dianthera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
