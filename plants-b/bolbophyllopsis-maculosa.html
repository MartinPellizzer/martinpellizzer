Bolbophyllopsis maculosa

Bolbophyllopsis maculosa, commonly known as the spotted leaf fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, pinnately divided fronds with a distinctive pattern of spots on the leaves. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a layer of moss-like tissue.

Bolbophyllopsis maculosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
