Braya humilis var. arctica

Braya humilis var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, spoon-shaped leaves and clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its habitat and is an important part of the tundra ecosystem.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
