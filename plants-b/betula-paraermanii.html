Betula paraermanii

Betula paraermanii, commonly known as the Korean birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

Betula paraermanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
