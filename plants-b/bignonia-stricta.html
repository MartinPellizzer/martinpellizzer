Bignonia stricta

Bignonia stricta, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a climbing vine or shrub. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Bignonia stricta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
