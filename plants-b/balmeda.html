Balmeda

Balmeda, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Balmeda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in garden settings.
