Bromus erectus subsp. fibrosus

Bromus erectus subsp.

fibrosus, commonly known as the fibrous brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wastelands.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and fibrous, leafy stems. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

The species is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
