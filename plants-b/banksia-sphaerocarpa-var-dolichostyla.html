Banksia sphaerocarpa var. dolichostyla

Banksia sphaerocarpa var. dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled banksia, is a variant of the banksia genus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and fire. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native fauna.
