Bromus viciosoi

Bromus viciosoi, commonly known as the devil's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Bromus viciosoi is considered a weed in some agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
