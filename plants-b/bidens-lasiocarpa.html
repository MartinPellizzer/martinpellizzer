Bidens lasiocarpa

Bidens lasiocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Bidens lasiocarpa is often found in arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
