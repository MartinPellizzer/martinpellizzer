Boehmeria alienata

Boehmeria alienata, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its fibrous stems are sometimes used in traditional crafts and textiles.
