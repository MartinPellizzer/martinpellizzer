Brassavola cordata

Brassavola cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Brassavola cordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
