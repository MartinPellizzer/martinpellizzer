Bromopsis riparia subsp. fibrosa

Bromopsis riparia subsp.

fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous river rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with long, cylindrical leaves that are often fibrous in texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its fibrous structure provides habitat and stabilizing benefits to the surrounding environment.
