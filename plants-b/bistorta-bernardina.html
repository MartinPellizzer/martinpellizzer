Bistorta bernardina

Bistorta bernardina, commonly known as the alpine bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Bistorta bernardina thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
