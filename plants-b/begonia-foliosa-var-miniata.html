Begonia foliosa var. miniata

Begonia foliosa var. miniata, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, mottled foliage.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and exhibit a unique combination of green, white, and pink hues. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
