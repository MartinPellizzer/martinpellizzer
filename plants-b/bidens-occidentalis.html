Bidens occidentalis

Bidens occidentalis, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy, and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and woodland edges.

Bidens occidentalis is known for its ability to spread rapidly through its seeds, which are dispersed by attaching to animals or clothing.
