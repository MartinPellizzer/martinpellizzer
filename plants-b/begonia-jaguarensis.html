Begonia jaguarensis

Begonia jaguarensis, commonly known as the jaguarensis begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can produce small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
