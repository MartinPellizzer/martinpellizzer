Barleria tomentosa var. glandulosa

Barleria tomentosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the woolly barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid regions.
