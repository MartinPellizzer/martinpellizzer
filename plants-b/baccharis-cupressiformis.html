Baccharis cupressiformis

Baccharis cupressiformis, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and a compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Baccharis cupressiformis is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
