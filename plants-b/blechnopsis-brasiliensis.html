Blechnopsis brasiliensis

Blechnopsis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is a perennial aquatic fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, and is native to South America. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic sediments.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in wetland ecosystems.
