Batrachium rionii

Batrachium rionii, commonly known as the Rion wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical characteristics include tendrils for climbing and a distinctive, warty texture on its fruit surface.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
