Beddomea sarmentosa

Beddomea sarmentosa, commonly known as the slender beddomea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it has thin, vine-like stems that often trail along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a somewhat waxy texture.

Beddomea sarmentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
