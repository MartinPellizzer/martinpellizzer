Bulbophyllum tokioi

Bulbophyllum tokioi, commonly known as the Tokyo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and delicate blooms.
