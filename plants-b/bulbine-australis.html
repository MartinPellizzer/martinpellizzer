Bulbine australis

Bulbine australis, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
