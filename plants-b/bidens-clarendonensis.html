Bidens clarendonensis

Bidens clarendonensis, commonly known as the Clarendon ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Bidens clarendonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
