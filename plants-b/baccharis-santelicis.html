Baccharis santelicis

Baccharis santelicis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a grayish or silver underside, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Baccharis santelicis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
