Brickellia amplexicaulis var. typica

Brickellia amplexicaulis var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
