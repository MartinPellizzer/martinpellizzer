Berberis moranensis

Berberis moranensis, commonly known as the Moran's barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
