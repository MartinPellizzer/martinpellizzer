Bartonia Virginica Var. Paniculata

Bartonia virginica var. paniculata is a unique flowering plant native to the southeastern united states. it belongs to the family malvaceae and is known for its distinctive white flowers that bloom in late spring. the plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. its showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects. despite its beauty, it remains relatively underappreciated in the horticultural world.
