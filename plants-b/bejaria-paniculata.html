Bejaria paniculata

Bejaria paniculata, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Bejaria paniculata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
