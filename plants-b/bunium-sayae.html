Bunium sayae

Bunium sayae, commonly known as the Armenian fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Bunium sayae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
