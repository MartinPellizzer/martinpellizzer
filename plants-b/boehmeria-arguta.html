Boehmeria arguta

Boehmeria arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and textile production.
