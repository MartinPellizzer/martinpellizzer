Barkhausia foetida var. stricta

Barkhausia foetida var. stricta, commonly known as the African sarsaparilla, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark, which contains compounds used in traditional medicine. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots and bark are harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
