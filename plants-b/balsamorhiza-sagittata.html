Balsamorhiza sagittata

Balsamorhiza sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
