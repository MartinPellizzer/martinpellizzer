Buddleja polycephala var. peruviana

Buddleja polycephala var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian butterfly bush, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
