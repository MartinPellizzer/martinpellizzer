Berberis umbellata

Berberis umbellata, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, bright red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climates.
