Bulbine disimilis

Bulbine disimilis, commonly known as the red-flowered bulbine, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
