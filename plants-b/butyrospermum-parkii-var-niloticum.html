Butyrospermum parkii var. niloticum

Butyrospermum parkii var. niloticum, commonly known as shea butter tree, is a species native to the savannas of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan and South Sudan.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that produces small, yellowish flowers and large, palmate leaves. The plant is valued for its seeds, which yield a rich source of natural oils used in cosmetics and traditional medicine. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in both ecological systems and economic livelihoods in local communities.
