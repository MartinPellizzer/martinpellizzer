Betula cylindrostachya var. pilosa

Betula cylindrostachya var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy birch, is a variant of the cylindrostachya birch species.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a smooth upper surface. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forest regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
