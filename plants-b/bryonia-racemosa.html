Bryonia racemosa

Bryonia racemosa, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into green, spiny fruits. Its roots contain toxic compounds, including bicyclic peptides, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
