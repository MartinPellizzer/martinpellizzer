Barbarea duralii

Barbarea duralii, commonly known as the Duralii rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are basal and pinnately divided, while the stems are usually unbranched.

Barbarea duralii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
