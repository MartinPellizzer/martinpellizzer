Benkara esculenta

Benkara esculenta, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in carbohydrates and are often used as a staple food in many African countries. The tree is also valued for its timber, which is durable and suitable for construction.

Its growth is typically rapid, making it an important species for reforestation and agricultural purposes.
