Bulbostylis coarctata

Bulbostylis coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbostylis, is a species of grass found primarily in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds or streams.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
