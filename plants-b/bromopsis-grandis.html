Bromopsis grandis

Bromopsis grandis, commonly known as the giant bromus, is a tall grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets at the top of its stems. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
