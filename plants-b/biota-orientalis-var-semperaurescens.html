Biota orientalis var. semperaurescens

Biota orientalis var. semperaurescens, commonly known as the Everlasting Biota, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The species is notable for its ability to retain its foliage and flowers for an extended period, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.
