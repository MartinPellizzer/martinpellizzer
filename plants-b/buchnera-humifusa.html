Buchnera humifusa

Buchnera humifusa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Buchnera humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
