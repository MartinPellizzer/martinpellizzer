Begonia ledermannii

Begonia ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's begonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its ornamental value.
