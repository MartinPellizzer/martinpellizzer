Berberis discolorifolia

Berberis discolorifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which vary in color and shape depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and ecological benefits.
