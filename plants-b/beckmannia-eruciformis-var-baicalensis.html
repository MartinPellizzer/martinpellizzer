Beckmannia eruciformis var. baicalensis

Beckmannia eruciformis var. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical Beckmannia, is a variant of the Beckmannia eruciformis species native to specific regions of China.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy leaves that resemble a caterpillar, giving it its species name *eruciformis*. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
