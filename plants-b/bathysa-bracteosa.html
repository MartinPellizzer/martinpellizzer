Bathysa bracteosa

Bathysa bracteosa, commonly known as the Texas lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, erect stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a hairy texture.

Bathysa bracteosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
