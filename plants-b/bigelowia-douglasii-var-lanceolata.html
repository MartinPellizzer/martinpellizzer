Bigelowia douglasii var. lanceolata

Bigelowia douglasii var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Douglas's bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its lance-shaped leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.
