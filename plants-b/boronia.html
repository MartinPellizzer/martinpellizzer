Boronia

Boronia, commonly known as the honeycomb bush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes around 70 species, many of which are shrubs or small trees. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Boronia species typically thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in coastal regions or rocky habitats.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
