Begonia octopetala subsp. ovatiformis

Begonia octopetala subsp.

ovatiformis, commonly known as the oval-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and veined prominently.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
