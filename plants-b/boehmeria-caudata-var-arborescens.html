Boehmeria caudata var. arborescens

Boehmeria caudata var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like Boehmeria, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with a fibrous, woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This species is valued for its strong fibers, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
