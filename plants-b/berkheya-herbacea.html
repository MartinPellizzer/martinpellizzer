Berkheya herbacea

Berkheya herbacea, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and are usually heart-shaped.

Berkheya herbacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
