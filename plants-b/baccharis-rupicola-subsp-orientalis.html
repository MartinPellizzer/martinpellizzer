Baccharis rupicola subsp. orientalis

Baccharis rupicola subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern rough baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the fall.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
