Baccharis espadae

Baccharis espadae, commonly known as Espada's baccharis, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
