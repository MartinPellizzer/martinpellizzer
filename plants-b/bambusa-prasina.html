Bambusa prasina

Bambusa prasina, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, green stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has narrow, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Bambusa prasina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for erosion control and as a sustainable resource.
