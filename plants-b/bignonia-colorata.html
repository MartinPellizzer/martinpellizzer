Bignonia colorata

Bignonia colorata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 30 feet in length.

Bignonia colorata is relatively drought-tolerant once established and prefers well-drained soil.
