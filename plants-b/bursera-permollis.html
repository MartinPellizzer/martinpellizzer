Bursera permollis

Bursera permollis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces fragrant resin, which has been used for centuries in incense and as a remedy for various ailments.

Bursera permollis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and shrublands.
