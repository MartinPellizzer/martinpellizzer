Brosimum costaricanum

Brosimum costaricanum, commonly known as the Guatemalan mulberry, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

The tree is also used in traditional medicine and for its fibrous bark.
