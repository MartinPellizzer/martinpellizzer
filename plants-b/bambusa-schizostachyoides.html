Bambusa schizostachyoides

Bambusa schizostachyoides, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are often folded along the midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bambusa schizostachyoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
