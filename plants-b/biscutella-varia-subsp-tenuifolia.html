Biscutella varia subsp. tenuifolia

Biscutella varia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring.

The plant has a distinctive root system that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
