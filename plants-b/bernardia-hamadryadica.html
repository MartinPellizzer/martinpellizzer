Bernardia hamadryadica

Bernardia hamadryadica, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
