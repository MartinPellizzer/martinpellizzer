Blepharipappus nudatus

Blepharipappus nudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are usually simple and sessile, with a hairy surface.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.
