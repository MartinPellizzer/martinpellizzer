Bellevalia coelestis

Bellevalia coelestis, commonly known as the sky-blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals that resemble small bells, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the spring.

Bellevalia coelestis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
