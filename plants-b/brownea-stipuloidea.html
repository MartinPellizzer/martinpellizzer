Brownea stipuloidea

Brownea stipuloidea, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous pink to purple flowers clustered together. The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain seeds covered in a fibrous layer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Brownea stipuloidea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
