Bromus lanceolatus f. coloratus

Bromus lanceolatus f. coloratus, commonly known as the colored speargrass, is a variant of the speargrass species native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive floral coloration, with inflorescences displaying a range of hues from pink to red. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking flower color and compact growth habit.
