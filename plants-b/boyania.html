Boyania

Boyania, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Boyania is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been introduced to other regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often invasive species in certain ecosystems.
