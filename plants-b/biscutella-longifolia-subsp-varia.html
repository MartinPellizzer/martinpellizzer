Biscutella longifolia subsp. varia

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

varia, commonly known as the variable biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are adapted for pollination by insects. Biscutella longifolia subsp.

varia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
