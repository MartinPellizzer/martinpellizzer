Brassica monensis f. recurvata

Brassica monensis f. recurvata, commonly known as the curly kale or ornamental kale, is a cultivar of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and deeply curled, lobed leaves that range in color from dark green to purple. The plant is typically grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is also valued for its edible leaves, which can be used in cooking similar to other Brassica species.
