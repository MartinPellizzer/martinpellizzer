Bollaea canariensis

Bollaea canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus or the red cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Bollaea canariensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
