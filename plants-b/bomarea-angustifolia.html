Bomarea angustifolia

Bomarea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, often showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features tuberous roots and produces clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
