Buddleja delavayi var. tomentosa

Buddleja delavayi var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly butterfly bush, is a shrub native to western China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to lilac flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract a wide range of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
