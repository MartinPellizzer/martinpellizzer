Baccharis subdentata var. incogitata

Baccharis subdentata var. incogitata, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and traditional herbal remedies.
