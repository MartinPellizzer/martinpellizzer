Brassaiopsis phanerophlebia

Brassaiopsis phanerophlebia, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Brassaiopsis phanerophlebia is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
