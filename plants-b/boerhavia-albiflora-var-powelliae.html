Boerhavia albiflora var. powelliae

Boerhavia albiflora var. powelliae, commonly known as Powell's whiteflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
