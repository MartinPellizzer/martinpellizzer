Bambusa amplexicaulis

Bambusa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple coloration on its culms, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant forms dense clumps and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its culms are strong and hollow, making it suitable for construction and handicrafts.

Bambusa amplexicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
