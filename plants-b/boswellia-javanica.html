Boswellia javanica

Boswellia javanica, commonly known as Indian frankincense, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant produces aromatic resin, known as frankincense, which is harvested from its wounded bark. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slight pubescence.
