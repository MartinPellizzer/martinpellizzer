Bambusa auriculata

Bambusa auriculata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or auriculate bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-green foliage and tall, slender culms. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base, contributing to its unique appearance.

Bambusa auriculata is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of fiber in some regions.
