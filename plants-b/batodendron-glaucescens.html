Batodendron glaucescens

Batodendron glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green batodendron, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which is coated in a waxy cuticle that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the late spring and early summer months. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
