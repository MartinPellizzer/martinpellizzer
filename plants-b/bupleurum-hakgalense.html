Bupleurum hakgalense

Bupleurum hakgalense, commonly known as Korean angelica or Korean peony root, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features a slender, branched stem with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that have a distinctive fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The roots of Bupleurum hakgalense are often used in traditional Korean medicine for their purported health benefits.
