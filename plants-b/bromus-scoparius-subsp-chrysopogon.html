Bromus scoparius subsp. chrysopogon

Bromus scoparius subsp.

chrysopogon, commonly known as golden brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct golden hue on the underside. Its inflorescence is a panicle composed of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
