Begonia hainanensis

Begonia hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often marked with intricate patterns and color variations, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining soil.
