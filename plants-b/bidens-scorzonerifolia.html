Bidens scorzonerifolia

Bidens scorzonerifolia, commonly known as the scarlet beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers with dark markings. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.
