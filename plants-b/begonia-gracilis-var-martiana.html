Begonia gracilis var. martiana

Begonia gracilis var. martiana, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
