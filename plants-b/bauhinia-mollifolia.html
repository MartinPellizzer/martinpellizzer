Bauhinia mollifolia

Bauhinia mollifolia, commonly known as the velvetwood or silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of showy, pink or white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Bauhinia mollifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
