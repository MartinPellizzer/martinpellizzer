Brachistus macrophyllus

Brachistus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed brachistus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
