Bulbostylis schimperiana

Bulbostylis schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This plant is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves and a basal rosette of leaves that form a dense cluster. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of many Cyperaceae species. The plant is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
