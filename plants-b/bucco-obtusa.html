Bucco obtusa

Bucco obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

The botanical features of Bucco obtusa make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
