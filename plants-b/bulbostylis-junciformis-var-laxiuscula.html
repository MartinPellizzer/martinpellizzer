Bulbostylis junciformis var. laxiuscula

Bulbostylis junciformis var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. Its bulbous base and rhizomatous growth habit allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it plays a significant role in wetland ecosystems.
