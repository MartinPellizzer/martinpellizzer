Byrsonima verbascifolia

Byrsonima verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
