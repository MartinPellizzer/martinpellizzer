Brittonamra caribaea var. jacquiniana

Brittonamra caribaea var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The tree is valued for its dense, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
