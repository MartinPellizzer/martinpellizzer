Bidens tripartita var. heterodoxa

Bidens tripartita var. heterodoxa, commonly known as the three-parted tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetland environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated and hairy.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
