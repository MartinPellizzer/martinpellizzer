Boopis agglomerata var. pinnatifida

Boopis agglomerata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boopis, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
