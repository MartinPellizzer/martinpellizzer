Batatas edulis var. platanifolia

Batatas edulis var. platanifolia, commonly known as the sweet potato or ube in some regions, is a tropical root vegetable belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, tuberous roots that come in a variety of colors, including purple, yellow, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale green or white. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and various vitamins, making it a nutritious food source.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
