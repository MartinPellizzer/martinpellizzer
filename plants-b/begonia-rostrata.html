Begonia rostrata

Begonia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that resemble a beak, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, adding vibrant color to its foliage.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
