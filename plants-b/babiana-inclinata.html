Babiana inclinata

Babiana inclinata, commonly known as the leaning babiana, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a single flower, which is often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Babiana inclinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
