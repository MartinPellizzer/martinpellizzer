Botryanthus odorus var. acutilobus

Botryanthus odorus var. acutilobus, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its subtle, pleasant fragrance.
