Begonia subviridis

Begonia subviridis, commonly known as the silverleaf begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver or white, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardens and shaded outdoor spaces.
