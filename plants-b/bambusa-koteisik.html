Bambusa koteisik

Bambusa koteisik, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Its culms are hollow and jointed, a defining feature of the grass family to which it belongs.

Bambusa koteisik is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
