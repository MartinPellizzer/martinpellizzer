Balsamina communis

Balsamina communis, commonly known as the common balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Balsamina communis is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
