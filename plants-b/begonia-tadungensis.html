Begonia tadungensis

Begonia tadungensis, commonly known as the Tadung begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant patterns of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
