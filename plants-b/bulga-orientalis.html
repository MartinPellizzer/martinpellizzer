Bulga orientalis

Bulga orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Bulga, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including moist woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where it thrives in partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
