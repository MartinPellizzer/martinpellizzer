Bulbophyllum dentiferum

Bulbophyllum dentiferum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its unique, tooth-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces striking, colorful flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, intricate pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
