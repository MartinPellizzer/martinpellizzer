Brassica chinensis f. mana

Brassica chinensis f. mana, commonly known as the Chinese kale or neep, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and edible stems, which are often used in Asian cuisine. The plant is typically grown as an annual and thrives in cool climates, making it a popular crop in temperate regions. Its botanical features include a robust root system and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing round, seed-like structures.

The plant's leaves are rich in nutrients, contributing to its value as both a food and medicinal plant.
