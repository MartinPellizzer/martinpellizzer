Begonia macdanielsii

Begonia macdanielsii, commonly known as the Macdaniels' begonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
