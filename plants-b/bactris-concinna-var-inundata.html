Bactris concinna var. inundata

Bactris concinna var. inundata, commonly known as the water bamboo palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant is adapted to wetland environments and can thrive in swampy or flooded areas. Its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.

The fruit of this plant is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines by indigenous communities.
