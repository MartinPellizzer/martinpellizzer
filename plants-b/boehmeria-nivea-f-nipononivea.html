Boehmeria nivea f. nipononivea

Boehmeria nivea f. nipononivea, commonly known as the Japanese cotton plant, is a variant of Boehmeria nivea, a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky fibers that are harvested from the inner bark of its stems. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its fiber quality and has been cultivated for traditional textile production in Japan.
