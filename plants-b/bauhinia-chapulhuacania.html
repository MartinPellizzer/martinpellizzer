Bauhinia chapulhuacania

Bauhinia chapulhuacania, commonly known as the Mexican orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in racemes.

Bauhinia chapulhuacania is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
