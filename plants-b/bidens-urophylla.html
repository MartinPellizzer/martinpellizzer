Bidens urophylla

Bidens urophylla, commonly known as the scarlet false beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and wetlands. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with prominent black markings. Its fruit is a schizocarp composed of two small, hairy seeds.

Bidens urophylla is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
