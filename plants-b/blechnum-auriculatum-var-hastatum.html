Blechnum auriculatum var. hastatum

Blechnum auriculatum var. hastatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Dryopteridaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
