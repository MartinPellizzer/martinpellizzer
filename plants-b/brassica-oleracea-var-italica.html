Brassica oleracea var. italica

Brassica oleracea var. italica, commonly known as broccoli, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its dense, green flower heads.

It is an annual plant that thrives in cool climates and is typically grown in temperate regions. The plant has thick, fibrous stems and broad, dark green leaves that surround the central inflorescence.

Its edible parts include the flower buds, stalks, and leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins C and K. Broccoli is widely cultivated for its health benefits and is a staple in many cuisines around the world.
