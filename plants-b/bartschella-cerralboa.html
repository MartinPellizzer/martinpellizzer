Bartschella cerralboa

Bartschella cerralboa, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant that typically forms dense mats of fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
