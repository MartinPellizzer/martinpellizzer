Boopis anthemoides var. subintegrifolia

Boopis anthemoides var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert bell, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive subintegrifoliate margin that gives it its botanical name. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
