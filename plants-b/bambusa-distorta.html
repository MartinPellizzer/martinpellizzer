Bambusa distorta

Bambusa distorta, commonly known as the twisted bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its unique, twisted growth pattern, which gives it a distinctive appearance. Its culms are hollow and segmented, with a smooth, fibrous surface.

Bambusa distorta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
