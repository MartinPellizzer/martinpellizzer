Bulbophyllum elbertii

Bulbophyllum elbertii, commonly known as the Elbert's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
