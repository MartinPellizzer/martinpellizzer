Borya porulosa

Borya porulosa, commonly known as the porulate borya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. Borya porulosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
