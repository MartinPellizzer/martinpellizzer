Bergera integerrima

Bergera integerrima, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with purple markings. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Bergera integerrima is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
