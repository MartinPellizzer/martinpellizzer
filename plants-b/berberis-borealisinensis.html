Berberis borealisinensis

Berberis borealisinensis, commonly known as the Alaska barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
