Bupleurum sibiricum var. jeholense

Bupleurum sibiricum var. jeholense, commonly known as the Jehol bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are usually white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
