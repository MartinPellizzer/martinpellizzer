Bromus scoparius var. villiglumis

Bromus scoparius var. villiglumis, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
