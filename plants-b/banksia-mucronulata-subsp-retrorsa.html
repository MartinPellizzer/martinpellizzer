Banksia mucronulata subsp. retrorsa

Banksia mucronulata subsp.

retrorsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
