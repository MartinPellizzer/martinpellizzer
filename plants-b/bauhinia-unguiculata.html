Bauhinia unguiculata

Bauhinia unguiculata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

Bauhinia unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
