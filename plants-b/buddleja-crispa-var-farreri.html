Buddleja crispa var. farreri

Buddleja crispa var. farreri, commonly known as the Farri's butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features delicate, fragrant, panicle-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce long, drooping flower clusters that add visual interest throughout the growing season.
