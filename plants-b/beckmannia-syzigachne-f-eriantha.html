Beckmannia syzigachne f. eriantha

Beckmannia syzigachne f. eriantha, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a variant of the species Beckmannia syzigachne, characterized by its fine, soft foliage.

It is a perennial grass native to Australia, typically found in moist, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This grass is often used in landscaping for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
