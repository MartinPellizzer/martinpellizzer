Berberis thomsoniana

Berberis thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage and hardy nature.
