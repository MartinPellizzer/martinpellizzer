Baeria chrysostoma f. clementina

Baeria chrysostoma f. clementina, commonly known as the golden-flowered baeria, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
