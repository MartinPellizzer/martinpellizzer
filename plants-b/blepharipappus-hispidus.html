Blepharipappus hispidus

Blepharipappus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers are adaptations to its arid environment.

It is often found in disturbed areas and has a long blooming period from summer to fall.
