Bambusa heterostachya

Bambusa heterostachya, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It features sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight.

Bambusa heterostachya is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in construction and crafts.
