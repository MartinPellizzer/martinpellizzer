Billardiera paniculata

Billardiera paniculata, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to eastern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and woodlands, and often grows on the branches of host trees.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host if not managed carefully.
