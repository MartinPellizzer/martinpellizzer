Brachypodium pinnatum var. glaucovirens

Brachypodium pinnatum var. glaucovirens, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-blue foliage due to the presence of fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
