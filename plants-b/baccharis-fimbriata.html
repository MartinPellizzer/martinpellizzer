Baccharis fimbriata

Baccharis fimbriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation.

Baccharis fimbriata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
