Brassica chinensis var. narinosa

Brassica chinensis var. narinosa, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or narinosa mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Native to East Asia, it is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
