Bona narbonensis var. serratifolia

Bona narbonensis var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated narbonensis, is a variant of the Bona narbonensis species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast in its natural habitat.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white hues, and contribute to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
