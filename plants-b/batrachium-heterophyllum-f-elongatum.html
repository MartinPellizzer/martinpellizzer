Batrachium heterophyllum f. elongatum

Batrachium heterophyllum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated frog's-bit, is a variant of the frog's-bit plant native to tropical regions.

It is a small aquatic plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and dense clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are typically longer and more elongated compared to the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued in aquariums for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
