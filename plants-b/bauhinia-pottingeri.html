Bauhinia pottingeri

Bauhinia pottingeri, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system. Bauhinia pottingeri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscapes in warm climates.
