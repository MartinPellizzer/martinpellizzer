Buddleja verbascifolia

Buddleja verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly candle bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja verbascifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
