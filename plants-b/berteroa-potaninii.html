Berteroa potaninii

Berteroa potaninii, commonly known as the African burweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its annual growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Berteroa potaninii is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a potential invasive species in some regions.
