Bulbophyllum granulosum

Bulbophyllum granulosum, commonly known as the granular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, green, or yellow.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
