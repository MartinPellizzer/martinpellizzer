Berchemia trichantha

Berchemia trichantha, commonly known as the hairy berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Berchemia trichantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
