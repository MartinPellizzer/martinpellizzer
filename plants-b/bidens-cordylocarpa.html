Bidens cordylocarpa

Bidens cordylocarpa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Bidens cordylocarpa is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
