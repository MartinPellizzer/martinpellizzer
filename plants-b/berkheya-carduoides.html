Berkheya carduoides

Berkheya carduoides, commonly known as the African burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
