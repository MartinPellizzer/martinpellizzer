Brassica campestris var. oleifera

Brassica campestris var. oleifera, commonly known as field mustard or rapeseed, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds, which are a source of oil used in cooking and industry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high oil content.

This variety is also valued for its role in crop rotation and as a green manure.
