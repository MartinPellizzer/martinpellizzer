Bowdichia pubescens

Bowdichia pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
