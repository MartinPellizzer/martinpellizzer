Biscutella laevigata subsp. fontqueri

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri's biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, dry habitats such as slopes and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
