Bastardia parvifolia

Bastardia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal and inland areas with full sun exposure. It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Bastardia parvifolia is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
