Biarum russellianum

Biarum russellianum, commonly known as the Russell's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it has a fibrous root system.

Biarum russellianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
