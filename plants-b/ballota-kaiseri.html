Ballota kaiseri

Ballota kaiseri, commonly known as Turkish ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Ballota kaiseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
