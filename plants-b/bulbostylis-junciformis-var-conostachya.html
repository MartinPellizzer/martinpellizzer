Bulbostylis junciformis var. conostachya

Bulbostylis junciformis var. conostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and establish in wetland habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its conical inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "conostachya.".
