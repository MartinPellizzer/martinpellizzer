Batschia albiflora

Batschia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, beard-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Batschia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
