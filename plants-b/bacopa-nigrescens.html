Bacopa nigrescens

Bacopa nigrescens, commonly known as black bacopa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its small, dark blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are creeping and can root at the nodes.

Bacopa nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive and calming effects.
