Botrychium ternatum f. dissectum

Botrychium ternatum f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected moonwort, is a fern species characterized by its unique frond structure.

The plant features a basal, often heart-shaped sorus that is covered with a protective indusium. Its fronds are typically divided into lanceolate segments, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

This fern is notable for its intricate morphology and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
