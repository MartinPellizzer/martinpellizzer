Bactris wendlandiana

Bactris wendlandiana, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish flowers, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small dates. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
