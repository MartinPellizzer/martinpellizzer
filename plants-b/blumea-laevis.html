Blumea laevis

Blumea laevis, commonly known as the smooth blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent.

Blumea laevis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
