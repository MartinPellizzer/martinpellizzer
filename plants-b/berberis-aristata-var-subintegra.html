Berberis aristata var. subintegra

Berberis aristata var. subintegra, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It features thorny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses, particularly in Ayurvedic practices.
