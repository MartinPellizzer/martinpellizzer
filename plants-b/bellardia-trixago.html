Bellardia trixago

Bellardia trixago, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
