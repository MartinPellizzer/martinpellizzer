Brachiaria balansae

Brachiaria balansae, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or balansae grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria balansae is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
