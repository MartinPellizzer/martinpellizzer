Busbeckea thozetiana

Busbeckea thozetiana, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as sandy soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
