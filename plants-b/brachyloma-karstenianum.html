Brachyloma karstenianum

Brachyloma karstenianum, commonly known as the Karsten's sun-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Brachyloma karstenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
