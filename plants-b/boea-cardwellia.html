Boea cardwellia

Boea cardwellia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Boea cardwellia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the winter months, giving it its common name.
