Banisteria emarginata

Banisteria emarginata, commonly known as the winged bean or angel's trumpets, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds and a starchy, tuberous root known as the "winged bean.". It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Banisteria emarginata is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in various indigenous cultures.
