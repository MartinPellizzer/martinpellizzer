Bidens setigera subsp. bipinnatopartita

Bidens setigera subsp.

bipinnatopartita, commonly known as the bipinnate beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are deeply divided into small, thread-like segments.

The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent white markings, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.
