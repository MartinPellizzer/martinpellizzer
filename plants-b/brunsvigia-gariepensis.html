Brunsvigia gariepensis

Brunsvigia gariepensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
