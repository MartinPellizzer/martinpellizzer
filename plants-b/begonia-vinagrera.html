Begonia vinagrera

Begonia vinagrera, commonly known as the vinegar begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
