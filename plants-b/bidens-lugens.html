Bidens lugens

Bidens lugens, commonly known as the yellow beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and it has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
