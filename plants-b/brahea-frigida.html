Brahea frigida

Brahea frigida, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a species of palm native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a short, stout trunk with a fibrous, spongy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Brahea frigida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
