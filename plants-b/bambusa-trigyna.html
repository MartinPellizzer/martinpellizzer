Bambusa trigyna

Bambusa trigyna, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 meters and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bambusa trigyna is also valued for its strong, flexible stalks, which are used in construction and crafts.
