Ballota nigra var. vulgaris

Ballota nigra var. vulgaris, commonly known as black hawthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ballota nigra var. vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
