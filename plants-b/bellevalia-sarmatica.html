Bellevalia sarmatica

Bellevalia sarmatica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine downy layer.

Bellevalia sarmatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
