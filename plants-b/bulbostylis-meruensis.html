Bulbostylis meruensis

Bulbostylis meruensis, commonly known as the Meru sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Bulbostylis meruensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
