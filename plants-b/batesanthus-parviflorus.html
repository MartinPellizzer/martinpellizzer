Batesanthus parviflorus

Batesanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Batesanthus parviflorus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
