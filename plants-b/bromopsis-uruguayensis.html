Bromopsis uruguayensis

Bromopsis uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Bromopsis uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
