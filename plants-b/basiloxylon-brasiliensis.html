Basiloxylon brasiliensis

Basiloxylon brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and precious resource.
