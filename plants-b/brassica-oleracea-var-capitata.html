Brassica oleracea var. capitata

Brassica oleracea var. capitata, commonly known as head lettuce, is a cultivar of the mustard family characterized by its compact, leafy head.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible leaves, which are typically crisp and tender. The plant is often grown in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is a biennial plant, though it is usually harvested as an annual.

The leaves can vary in color, ranging from green to red or purple, depending on the variety.
