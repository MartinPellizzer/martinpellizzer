Biscutella didyma f. ciliata

Biscutella didyma f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with hairy margins that give it its specific epithet "ciliata.". It produces yellow flowers in racemate inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
