Baccharis mapirensis

Baccharis mapirensis, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry to semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Baccharis mapirensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
