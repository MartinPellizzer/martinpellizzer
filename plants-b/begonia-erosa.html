Begonia erosa

Begonia erosa, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features trailing stems that produce small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and prolonged flowering period.
