Betula lyalliana

Betula lyalliana, commonly known as Lyall's birch, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are alternate, triangular, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

This species is often found in forest margins and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
