Blumea appendiculata

Blumea appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and produce small, oval-shaped fruits that are dispersed by wind.
