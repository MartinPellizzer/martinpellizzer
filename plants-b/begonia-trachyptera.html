Begonia trachyptera

Begonia trachyptera, commonly known as the roughleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its attractive foliage and blooms.
