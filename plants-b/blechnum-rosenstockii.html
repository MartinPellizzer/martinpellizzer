Blechnum rosenstockii

Blechnum rosenstockii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing structures called sorus on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in various parts of the world.
