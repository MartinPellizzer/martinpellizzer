Brachypodium pinnatum subsp. juzepczukii

Brachypodium pinnatum subsp.

juzepczukii, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
