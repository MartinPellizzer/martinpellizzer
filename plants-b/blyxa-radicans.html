Blyxa radicans

Blyxa radicans, commonly known as the creeping waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some emerging above the water surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikes, and it reproduces through both seeds and rhizomes.

Blyxa radicans plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
