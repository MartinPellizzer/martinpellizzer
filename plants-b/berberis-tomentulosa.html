Berberis tomentulosa

Berberis tomentulosa, commonly known as the woolly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and winter interest make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
