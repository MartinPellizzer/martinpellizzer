Bryonia melanocarpa

Bryonia melanocarpa, commonly known as the black bryony, is a flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a trailing or climbing stem with spiny tendrils, and its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy texture. It produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and cucurbitacin.
