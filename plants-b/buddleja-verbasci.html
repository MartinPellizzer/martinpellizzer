Buddleja verbasci

Buddleja verbasci, commonly known as the common butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for attracting a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Buddleja verbasci is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
