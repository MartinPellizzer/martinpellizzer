Boberella pubescens

Boberella pubescens, commonly known as the hairy boberella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Boberella pubescens is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
