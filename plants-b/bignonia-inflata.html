Bignonia inflata

Bignonia inflata, commonly known as the crown vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, climbing stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bignonia inflata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is compound, with opposite leaves that have a glossy appearance.
