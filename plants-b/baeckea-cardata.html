Baeckea cardata

Baeckea cardata, commonly known as the coastal baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white or cream-colored flowers. Baeckea cardata is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
