Bupleurum apiculatum f. albicans

Bupleurum apiculatum f. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered bupleurum, is a botanical variant of the common bupleurum species.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers, which contrast with the typical green or yellow hues of the species. This plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to varied environmental conditions.
