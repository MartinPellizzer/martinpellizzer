Baccharis bahiensis

Baccharis bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and woodland edges.

Baccharis bahiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
