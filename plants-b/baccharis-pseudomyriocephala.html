Baccharis pseudomyriocephala

Baccharis pseudomyriocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Baccharis pseudomyriocephala is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
