Ballota inaequidens

Ballota inaequidens, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. Ballota inaequidens grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
