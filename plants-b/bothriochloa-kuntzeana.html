Bothriochloa kuntzeana

Bothriochloa kuntzeana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects or as a forage plant for livestock.
