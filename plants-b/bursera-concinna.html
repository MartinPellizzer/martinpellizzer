Bursera concinna

Bursera concinna, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The tree has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
