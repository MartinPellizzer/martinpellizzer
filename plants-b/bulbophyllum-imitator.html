Bulbophyllum imitator

Bulbophyllum imitator, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble other plants or animals. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its roots are typically epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
