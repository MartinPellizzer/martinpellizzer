Brassica oleracea var. jodocephala

Brassica oleracea var. jodocephala, commonly known as the Japanese kale, is a cultivar of the cabbage species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant is a biennial, meaning it completes its life cycle over two growing seasons, though it is typically grown as an annual. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable vegetable for both culinary and nutritional purposes.
