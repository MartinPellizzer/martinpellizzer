Berberis brevifolia

Berberis brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and winter interest.
