Blitum sanguisorba

Blitum sanguisorba, commonly known as bloody betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
