Bupleurum ramosissimum var. mucronatum

Bupleurum ramosissimum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
