Brodiaea capitata var. alba

Brodiaea capitata var. alba, commonly known as the white-headed brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, branched stem topped with a dense cluster of white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
