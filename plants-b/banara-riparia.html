Banara riparia

Banara riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, featuring five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies, making it a common sight along rivers and streams. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite tall.

Banara riparia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
