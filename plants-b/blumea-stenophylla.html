Blumea stenophylla

Blumea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Blumea stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
