Bulbophyllum oreodoxa

Bulbophyllum oreodoxa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and dark green, adapted to conserve moisture.

Bulbophyllum oreodoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
