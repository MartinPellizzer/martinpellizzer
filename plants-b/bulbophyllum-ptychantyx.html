Bulbophyllum ptychantyx

Bulbophyllum ptychantyx, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that resemble lips, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their vibrant color and unique shape.
