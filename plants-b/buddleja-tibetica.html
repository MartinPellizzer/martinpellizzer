Buddleja tibetica

Buddleja tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan butterfly bush, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
