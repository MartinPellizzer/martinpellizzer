Bellium pumilum

Bellium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. Bellium pumilum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these specialized ecosystems.
