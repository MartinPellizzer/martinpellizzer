Brassica chinensis f. nozawensis

Brassica chinensis f. nozawensis, commonly known as Chinese mustard or napa cabbage, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, pale green leaves that form a dense, cabbage-like head. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in Asian cuisine.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and high nutritional content, including vitamins A and C.
