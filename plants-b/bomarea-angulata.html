Bomarea angulata

Bomarea angulata, commonly known as the zigzag bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Bomarea angulata is often grown in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
