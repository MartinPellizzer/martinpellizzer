Bupleurum longeradiatum f. australe

Bupleurum longeradiatum f. australe, commonly known as the Australian longroot bugle, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
