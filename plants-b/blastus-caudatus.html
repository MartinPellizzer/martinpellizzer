Blastus caudatus

Blastus caudatus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, dark purple pods. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The fruit, which matures into a pod, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
