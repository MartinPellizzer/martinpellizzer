Baccharis subpenninervis

Baccharis subpenninervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined baccharis, is a shrub native to the south-central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Baccharis subpenninervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
