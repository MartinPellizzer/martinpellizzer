Begonia asperifolia

Begonia asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in tiny, rough textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia asperifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
