Bartholina pectinata

Bartholina pectinata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is exuded when the stems are broken. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

Bartholina pectinata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
