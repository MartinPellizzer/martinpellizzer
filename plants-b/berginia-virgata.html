Berginia virgata

Berginia virgata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are usually woolly or hairy, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. Berginia virgata is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
