Bidens mannii

Bidens mannii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Bidens mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
