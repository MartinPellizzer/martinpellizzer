Bromelia lindmanii

Bromelia lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its epiphytic nature allows it to grow on trees or rocks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air.

Bromelia lindmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
