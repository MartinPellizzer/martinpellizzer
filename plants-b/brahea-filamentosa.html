Brahea filamentosa

Brahea filamentosa, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a striking evergreen plant native to the coastal regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-gray, feather-like leaves that give it a unique and dramatic appearance. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that grows slowly and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a specimen plant in arid and semi-arid landscapes.
