Bulbophyllum francoisii

Bulbophyllum francoisii, commonly known as the Francois's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or green with intricate patterns. It is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and its linear, grass-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
