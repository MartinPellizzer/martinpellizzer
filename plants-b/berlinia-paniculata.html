Berlinia paniculata

Berlinia paniculata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces flat, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
