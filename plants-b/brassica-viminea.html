Brassica viminea

Brassica viminea, commonly known as slender cabbage, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica viminea is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
