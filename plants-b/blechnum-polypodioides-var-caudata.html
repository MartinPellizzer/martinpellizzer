Blechnum polypodioides var. caudata

Blechnum polypodioides var. caudata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that often have a hairy or pubescent texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a unique appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
