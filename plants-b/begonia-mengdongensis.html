Begonia mengdongensis

Begonia mengdongensis, commonly known as the Mengdong begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, and it produces showy, bisexual flowers in shades of red, pink, or white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
