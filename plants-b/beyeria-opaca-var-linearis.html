Beyeria opaca var. linearis

Beyeria opaca var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert lily, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
