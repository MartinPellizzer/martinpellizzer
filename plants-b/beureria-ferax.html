Beureria ferax

Beureria ferax, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruit and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Beureria ferax is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
