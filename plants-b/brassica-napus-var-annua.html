Brassica napus var. annua

Brassica napus var. annua, commonly known as oilseed rape, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flowers and thick, rough stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are rich in oil, making it an important crop for agricultural and industrial uses. Its rapid growth cycle and high yield make it suitable for cultivation in various climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain pests and beneficial insects.
