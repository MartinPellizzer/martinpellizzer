Berberis feddei

Berberis feddei, commonly known as the fedde barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but have a sharp taste. Berberis feddei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Berberidaceae family.
