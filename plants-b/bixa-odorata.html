Bixa odorata

Bixa odorata, commonly known as the "Brazil nut tree," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing up to 20 elongated seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts.

The plant is also valued for its oil-rich seeds and has cultural and economic significance in the Amazon region.
