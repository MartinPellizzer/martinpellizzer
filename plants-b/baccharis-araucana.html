Baccharis araucana

Baccharis araucana, commonly known as the Andean shrub or araucaria baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis araucana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
