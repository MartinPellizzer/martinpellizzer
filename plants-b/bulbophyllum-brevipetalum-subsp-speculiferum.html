Bulbophyllum brevipetalum subsp. speculiferum

Bulbophyllum brevipetalum subsp.

speculiferum, commonly known as the speckled bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, hairy pseudobulbs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
