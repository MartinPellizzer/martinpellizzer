Brachypodium manschuricum

Brachypodium manschuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian false brome, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
