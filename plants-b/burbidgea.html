Burbidgea

Burbidgea, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Burbidgea species are typically shrubs or small trees with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. They produce capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in desert ecosystems.
