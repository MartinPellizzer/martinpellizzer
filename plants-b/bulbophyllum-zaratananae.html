Bulbophyllum zaratananae

Bulbophyllum zaratananae, commonly known as the Zaratanana orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
