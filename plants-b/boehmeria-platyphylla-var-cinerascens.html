Boehmeria platyphylla var. cinerascens

Boehmeria platyphylla var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silky flax or silver flax, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional crafts and textiles due to their soft and durable qualities.
