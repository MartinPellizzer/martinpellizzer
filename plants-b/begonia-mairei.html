Begonia mairei

Begonia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base.

Begonia mairei thrives in humid, shady environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
