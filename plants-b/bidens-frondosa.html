Bidens frondosa

Bidens frondosa, commonly known as common tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often hairy, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets.

Bidens frondosa is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
