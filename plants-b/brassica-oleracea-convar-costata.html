Brassica oleracea convar. costata

Brassica oleracea convar. costata, commonly known as kohlrabi, is a leafy vegetable characterized by its swollen, bulbous stem.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is closely related to other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. Its edible portion consists of the swollen stem, which is typically pale green or purple in color. The leaves are usually small and tender, often consumed as well.

Kohlrabi thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for its unique texture and mild, sweet flavor.
