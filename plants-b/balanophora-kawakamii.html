Balanophora kawakamii

Balanophora kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami's ghost flower, is a rare parasitic plant belonging to the family Balanophoraceae.

It is found in the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly in the Philippines. This plant lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, making it a holoparasite. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered an endangered species.
