Bunium elegans var. noeanum

Bunium elegans var. noeanum, commonly known as the Kashmiri fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their pungent aroma.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.
