Berberis × diantha

Berberis × diantha, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species, Berberis thunbergii and Berberis asiatica.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Berberis genus. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.

Berberis × diantha is adaptable to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
