Bauhinia beguinotii var. gorgonae

Bauhinia beguinotii var. gorgonae, commonly known as the Gorgon's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces showy, raceme-like inflorescences of pink or purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are flat, pod-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
