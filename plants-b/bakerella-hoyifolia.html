Bakerella hoyifolia

Bakerella hoyifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Bakerella hoyifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
