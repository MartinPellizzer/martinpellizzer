Bactris gracilior

Bactris gracilior, commonly known as the slender palm or slender bactris, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling small pineapples. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
