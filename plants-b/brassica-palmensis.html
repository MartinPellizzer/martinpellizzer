Brassica palmensis

Brassica palmensis, commonly known as the palm tree mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its palm-like leaves, which are large and deeply lobed, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Brassica palmensis produces small, elongated pods containing seeds that are used in some traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and as a forage crop.
