Baccharis organensis

Baccharis organensis, commonly known as the organensis baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

Baccharis organensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
