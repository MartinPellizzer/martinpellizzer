Berberis libertatis

Berberis libertatis, commonly known as the Libertas barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
