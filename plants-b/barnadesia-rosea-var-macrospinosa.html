Barnadesia rosea var. macrospinosa

Barnadesia rosea var. macrospinosa, commonly known as the large-spined rose bush, is a unique variant of the Barnadesia rosea species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, spiny bracts that give it an unusual appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its structure. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.
