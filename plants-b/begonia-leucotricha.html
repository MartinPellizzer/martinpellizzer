Begonia leucotricha

Begonia leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and has a velvety texture.

Begonia leucotricha prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
