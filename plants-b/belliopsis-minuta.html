Belliopsis minuta

Belliopsis minuta, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Belliopsis minuta is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.
