Banksia sphaerocarpa var. sphaerocarpa

Banksia sphaerocarpa var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical, red or orange flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has long, narrow, needle-like leaves that are usually grey-green in color. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The species is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
