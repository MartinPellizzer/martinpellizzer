Begonia glabra var. coralipetiolis

Begonia glabra var. coralipetiolis, commonly known as the coral-petioled begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and vibrant floral display.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, coral-pink bracts that encase its small, tubular flowers, creating an eye-catching appearance. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and the stems are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
