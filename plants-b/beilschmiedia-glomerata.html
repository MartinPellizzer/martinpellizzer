Beilschmiedia glomerata

Beilschmiedia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
