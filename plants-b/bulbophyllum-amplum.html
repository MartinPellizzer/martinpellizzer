Bulbophyllum amplum

Bulbophyllum amplum, commonly known as the large-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces a single, fleshy stem that bears numerous leaves. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum amplum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
