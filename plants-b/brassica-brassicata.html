Brassica brassicata

Brassica brassicata, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and flowering stems that bear racemes of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces siliques, which are long, slender pods containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Brassica brassicata is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
