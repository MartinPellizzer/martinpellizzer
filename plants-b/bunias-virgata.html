Bunias virgata

Bunias virgata, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, producing a distinctive musty odor.

The plant is known for its edible leaves, which are used in some traditional cuisines.
