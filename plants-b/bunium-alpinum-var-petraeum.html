Bunium alpinum var. petraeum

Bunium alpinum var. petraeum, commonly known as the alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent aroma, similar to that of fennel.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
