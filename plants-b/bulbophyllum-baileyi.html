Bulbophyllum baileyi

Bulbophyllum baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and sometimes red. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its often-dry environment.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
