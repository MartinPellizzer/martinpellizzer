Bulbine transvaalensis

Bulbine transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bulbous, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rhizomatous stems and rosette-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
