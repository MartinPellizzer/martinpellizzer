Berberis yushuensis

Berberis yushuensis, commonly known as the Yushu barberry, is a shrub native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
