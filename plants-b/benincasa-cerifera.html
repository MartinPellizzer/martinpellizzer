Benincasa cerifera

Benincasa cerifera, commonly known as the wax gourd or buttercup squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces large, elongated fruits with a waxy, pale green skin. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The fruit can be harvested when mature, and its texture becomes tender and slightly sweet when cooked.
