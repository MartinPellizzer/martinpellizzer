Bactris simplicifrons var. acanthocnemis

Bactris simplicifrons var. acanthocnemis, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often display a reddish hue, especially when young. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
