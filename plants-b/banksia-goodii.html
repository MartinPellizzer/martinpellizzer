Banksia goodii

Banksia goodii, commonly known as the Grey Banksia, is a species native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically grey or white in color. The plant produces small, woody follicles that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia goodii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing nectar for various pollinators.
