Barnadesia glochidiata

Barnadesia glochidiata, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. Its most distinctive feature is the presence of glochids, which are small, barbed spines that can cause irritation to the skin.

This cactus is adapted to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions with minimal water availability.
