Bourgaea

Bourgaea, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small trumpets, blooming in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. Bourgaea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
