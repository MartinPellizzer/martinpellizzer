Bidens ferulifolia var. foeniculifolia

Bidens ferulifolia var. foeniculifolia, commonly known as the feathery beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a serrated edge. This variant is distinguished by its finely divided, feathery foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
