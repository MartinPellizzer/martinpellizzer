Boopis pterocalyx

Boopis pterocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a woody shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with a velvety texture, often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Boopis pterocalyx is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
