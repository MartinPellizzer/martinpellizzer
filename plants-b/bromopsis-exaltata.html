Bromopsis exaltata

Bromopsis exaltata, commonly known as the tall bromus, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Bromopsis exaltata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
