Berberis verschaffeltii

Berberis verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Van der Vegt barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that turn red in the fall, and it produces orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

Berberis verschaffeltii is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh climates with low moisture.
