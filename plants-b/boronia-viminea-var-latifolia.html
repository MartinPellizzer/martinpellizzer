Boronia viminea var. latifolia

Boronia viminea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with wiry stems and small, rounded leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
