Bistorta officinalis subsp. officinalis

Bistorta officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as false bartsia, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its rhomboidal leaf shapes and distinctive, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are thick and succulent, often covered in a waxy cuticle, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

This subspecies is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of herbal remedies.
