Bidens andicola f. cosmantha

Bidens andicola f. cosmantha, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, and its yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
