Barkhausia senecioides

Barkhausia senecioides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pale green, egg-shaped fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

The bark of this plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
