Berberis benoistiana

Berberis benoistiana, commonly known as the Benoist barberry, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Berberis benoistiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
