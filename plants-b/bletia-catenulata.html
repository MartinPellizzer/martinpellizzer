Bletia catenulata

Bletia catenulata, commonly known as the catenulate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. Bletia catenulata typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
