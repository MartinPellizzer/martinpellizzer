Bidens demissa

Bidens demissa, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Bidens demissa is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
