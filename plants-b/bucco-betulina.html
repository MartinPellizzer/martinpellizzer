Bucco betulina

Bucco betulina, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, drooping catkins. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in forest edges or open woodlands.

The silver birch is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
