Boechera holboellii var. pinetorum

Boechera holboellii var. pinetorum, commonly known as the Sierra Mountains rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and is of interest in botanical research.
