Bargemontia sedifolia

Bargemontia sedifolia, commonly known as the leafy broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
