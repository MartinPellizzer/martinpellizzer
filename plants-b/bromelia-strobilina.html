Bromelia strobilina

Bromelia strobilina, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bromelia strobilina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and unique foliage structure.
