Bromus racemosus var. elongatus

Bromus racemosus var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often loosely arranged.

This variety is characterized by its elongated lemma and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
