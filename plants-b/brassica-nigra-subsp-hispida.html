Brassica nigra subsp. hispida

Brassica nigra subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid black mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae). It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that form dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and western Asia. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) surfaces, which give it its common name.
