Berberis borealis

Berberis borealis, commonly known as the northern barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along streambanks. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often a dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that attract birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
