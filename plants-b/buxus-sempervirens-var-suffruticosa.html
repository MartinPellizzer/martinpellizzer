Buxus sempervirens var. suffruticosa

Buxus sempervirens var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the common boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It features dense, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in a compact, rounded form, making it ideal for hedges and topiaries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
