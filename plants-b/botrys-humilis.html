Botrys humilis

Botrys humilis, commonly known as the cluster bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are usually oblanceolate or elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Botrys humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
