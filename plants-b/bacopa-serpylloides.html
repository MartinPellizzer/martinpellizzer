Bacopa serpylloides

Bacopa serpylloides, commonly known as aquatic bacopa, is a semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or ovate in shape. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, often blue or purple in color.

Bacopa serpylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
