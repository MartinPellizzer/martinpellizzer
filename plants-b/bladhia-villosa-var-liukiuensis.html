Bladhia villosa var. liukiuensis

Bladhia villosa var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a velvety appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
