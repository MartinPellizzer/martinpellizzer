Bonnaya minima

Bonnaya minima, commonly known as the dwarf poinsettia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It features small, waxy, dark green leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers surrounded by bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during the holiday season.
