Bactris plumeriana

Bactris plumeriana, commonly known as the red rata or red palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are rich in nutrients. Bactris plumeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
