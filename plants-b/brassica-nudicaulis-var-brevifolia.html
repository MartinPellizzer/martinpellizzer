Brassica nudicaulis var. brevifolia

Brassica nudicaulis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact leaves compared to other varieties of Brassica nudicaulis.
