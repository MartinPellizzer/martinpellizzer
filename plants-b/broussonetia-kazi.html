Broussonetia kazi

Broussonetia kazi, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
