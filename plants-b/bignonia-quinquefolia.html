Bignonia quinquefolia

Bignonia quinquefolia, commonly known as crossvine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five distinct leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Bignonia quinquefolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
