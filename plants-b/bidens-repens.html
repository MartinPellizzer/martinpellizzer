Bidens repens

Bidens repens, commonly known as common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers with five petals. Its fruits are schizocarpic, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wetlands.
