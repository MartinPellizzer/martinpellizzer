Berberis victoriana

Berberis victoriana, commonly known as the Victoria barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
