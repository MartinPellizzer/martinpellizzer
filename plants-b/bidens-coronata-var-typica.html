Bidens coronata var. typica

Bidens coronata var. typica, commonly known as the crownleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early fall, and it is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
