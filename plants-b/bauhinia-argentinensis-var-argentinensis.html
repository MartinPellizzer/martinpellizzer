Bauhinia argentinensis var. argentinensis

Bauhinia argentinensis var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers.
