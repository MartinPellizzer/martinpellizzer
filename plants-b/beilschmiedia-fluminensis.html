Beilschmiedia fluminensis

Beilschmiedia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in conservation areas.
