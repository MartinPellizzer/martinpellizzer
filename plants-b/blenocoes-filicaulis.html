Blenocoes filicaulis

Blenocoes filicaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, often arranged in clusters.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays a minor role in local ecosystems, primarily as a predator of small aquatic invertebrates.
