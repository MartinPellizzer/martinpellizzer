Berberis integerrima var. turcomannica

Berberis integerrima var. turcomannica, commonly known as the Turkestan barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or as a landscape plant in colder climates.
