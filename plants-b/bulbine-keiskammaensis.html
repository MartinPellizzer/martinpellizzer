Bulbine keiskammaensis

Bulbine keiskammaensis, commonly known as the Keiskamma bulbous plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
