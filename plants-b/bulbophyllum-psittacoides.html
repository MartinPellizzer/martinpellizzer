Bulbophyllum psittacoides

Bulbophyllum psittacoides, commonly known as the parrot orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble parrots, hence its common name. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
