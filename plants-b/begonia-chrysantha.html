Begonia chrysantha

Begonia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their long-lasting display, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
