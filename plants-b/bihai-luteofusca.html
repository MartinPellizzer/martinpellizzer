Bihai luteofusca

Bihai luteofusca, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its yellowish wood and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is often cultivated for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some areas.
