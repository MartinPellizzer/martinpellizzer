Brachiaria brachylopha

Brachiaria brachylopha, commonly known as slender brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Brachiaria brachylopha is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
