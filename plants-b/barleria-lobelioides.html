Barleria lobelioides

Barleria lobelioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Barleria lobelioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
