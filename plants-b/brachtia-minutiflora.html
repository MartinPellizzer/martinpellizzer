Brachtia minutiflora

Brachtia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf brachtia, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
