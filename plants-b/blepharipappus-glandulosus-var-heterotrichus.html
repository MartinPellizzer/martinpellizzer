Blepharipappus glandulosus var. heterotrichus

Blepharipappus glandulosus var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the glandular leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in water retention.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white or pale pink ray flowers.
