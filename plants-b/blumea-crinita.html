Blumea crinita

Blumea crinita, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough surface. Blumea crinita produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
