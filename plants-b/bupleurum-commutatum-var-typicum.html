Bupleurum commutatum var. typicum

Bupleurum commutatum var. typicum, commonly known as the common threadleaf bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems with small, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pale yellow and clustered in umbels, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
