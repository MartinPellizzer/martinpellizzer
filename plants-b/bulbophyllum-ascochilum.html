Bulbophyllum ascochilum

Bulbophyllum ascochilum, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar leaf shape, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a striking coloration that ranges from white to pink.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its resilience in various growing conditions.
