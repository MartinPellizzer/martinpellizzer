Bonamia krapovickasii

Bonamia krapovickasii, commonly known as the yellow bonamia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
