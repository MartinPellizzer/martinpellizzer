Brassia hinksoniana

Brassia hinksoniana, commonly known as the Hinkson's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flower spikes that bear multiple fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Brassia hinksoniana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
