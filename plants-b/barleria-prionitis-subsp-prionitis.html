Barleria prionitis subsp. prionitis

Barleria prionitis subsp.

prionitis, commonly known as African violets, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
