Baccharis alpina

Baccharis alpina, commonly known as mountain baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in high-altitude environments, including rocky slopes and grasslands. Its structure is typically low-growing with multiple stems, and it has a dense, compact form.

Baccharis alpina is adapted to cold climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
