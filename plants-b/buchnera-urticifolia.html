Buchnera urticifolia

Buchnera urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, and it is typically found in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. Buchnera urticifolia is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, and it plays a role in supporting various pollinators.

Its leaves are edible when cooked, though they require careful preparation to neutralize the irritating compounds.
