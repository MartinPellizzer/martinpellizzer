Bomarea caldasii

Bomarea caldasii, commonly known as the Caldasii bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, Bomarea caldasii is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
