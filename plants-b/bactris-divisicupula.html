Bactris divisicupula

Bactris divisicupula, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits. Bactris divisicupula thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
