Baccharis Rufidula

Baccharis rufidula is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is commonly found in coastal and desert regions. This plant is known for its small, grayish-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds. Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects and landscaping.
