Biscutella laxiflora

Biscutella laxiflora, commonly known as the slender biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Biscutella laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
