Berberis mitis

Berberis mitis, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, spiny foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
