Baphia crassifolia var. dusenii

Baphia crassifolia var. dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's broadleaf baphia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
