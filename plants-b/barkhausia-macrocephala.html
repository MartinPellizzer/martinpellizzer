Barkhausia macrocephala

Barkhausia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and thick, trunk-like stem. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones on separate plants, which are essential for its reproduction. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.

The plant's distinctive appearance and ecological significance contribute to its importance in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
