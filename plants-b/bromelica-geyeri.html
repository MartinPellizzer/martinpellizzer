Bromelica geyeri

Bromelica geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri pineapple, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. It produces a tall flower spike with colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Bromelica geyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
