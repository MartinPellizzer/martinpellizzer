Breynia rhynchocarpa

Breynia rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned breynia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Breynia rhynchocarpa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
