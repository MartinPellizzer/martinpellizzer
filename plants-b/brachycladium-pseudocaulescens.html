Brachycladium pseudocaulescens

Brachycladium pseudocaulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for soil stabilization.
