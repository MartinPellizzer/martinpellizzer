Bauhinia forficata

Bauhinia forficata, commonly known as the "blushing bauhinia," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that turn red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.

Bauhinia forficata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.
