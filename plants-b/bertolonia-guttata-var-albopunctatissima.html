Bertolonia guttata var. albopunctatissima

Bertolonia guttata var. albopunctatissima, commonly known as the white-spotted bertolonia, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, which give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to add visual interest to shaded garden spaces.
