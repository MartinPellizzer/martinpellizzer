Bassia blancoi

Bassia blancoi, commonly known as the Mexican rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky soils and full sun, showcasing compact foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a woody base and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to harsh, semi-desert environments.
