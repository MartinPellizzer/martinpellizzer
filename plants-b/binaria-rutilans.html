Binaria rutilans

Binaria rutilans, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Binaria rutilans thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
