Boronia parvifolia

Boronia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in heathlands and open forests. It is part of the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Boronia parvifolia is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
