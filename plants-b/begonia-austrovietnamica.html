Begonia austrovietnamica

Begonia austrovietnamica, commonly known as the Vietnamese begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with specific ecological and horticultural significance.
