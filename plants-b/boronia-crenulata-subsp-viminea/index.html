Boronia Crenulata Subsp. Viminea

Viminea boronia, scientifically known as Boronia crenulata subsp. viminea, is a rare and unique plant species native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. This subshrub is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces significant conservation challenges. Its distinctive appearance and ecological importance make it a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
