Bocquetia sublanata

Bocquetia sublanata, commonly known as the velvetleaf bocquetia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers in shades of pink and white, often clustered in racemes. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
