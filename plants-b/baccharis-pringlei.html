Baccharis pringlei

Baccharis pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
