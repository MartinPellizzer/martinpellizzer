Bauhinia williamsii

Bauhinia williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, leathery seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
