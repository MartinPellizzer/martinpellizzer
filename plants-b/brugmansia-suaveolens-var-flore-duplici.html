Brugmansia suaveolens var. flore-duplici

Brugmansia suaveolens var. flore-duplici, commonly known as the double-flowered angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, with the unique feature of having two rows of petals. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and glossy, while its bark is smooth and light-colored.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes, though it requires warm climates and well-drained soil to thrive.
