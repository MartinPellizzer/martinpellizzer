Brachypodium salzmannii

Brachypodium salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's brome, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Brachypodium salzmannii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
