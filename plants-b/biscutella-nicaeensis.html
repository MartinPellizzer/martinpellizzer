Biscutella nicaeensis

Biscutella nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
