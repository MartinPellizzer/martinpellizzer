Bridelia morotaea

Bridelia morotaea, commonly known as the white bridelia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
