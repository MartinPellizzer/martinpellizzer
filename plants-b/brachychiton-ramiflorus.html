Brachychiton ramiflorus

Brachychiton ramiflorus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces woody, spiny fruits that contain several seeds.

Brachychiton ramiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
