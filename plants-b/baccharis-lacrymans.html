Baccharis lacrymans

Baccharis lacrymans, commonly known as the weeping baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its attractive form and adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
