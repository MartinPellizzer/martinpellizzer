Behen vulgaris f. fallacinus

Behen vulgaris f. fallacinus, commonly known as the fallacinus variant of castor oil plant, is a botanical variant of the castor plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage and seed morphology. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds. It shares many features with the standard castor plant but exhibits unique traits in its growth habits.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for specialized agricultural uses.
