Baloghia parviflora

Baloghia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered baloghia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Baloghia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
