Brachychiton viscidulus

Brachychiton viscidulus, commonly known as the sticky trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a rough, viscid (sticky) bark that gives it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Brachychiton viscidulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
