Bomarea ovallei f. albiplumbea

Bomarea ovallei f. albiplumbea, commonly known as the white-veined bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a broad, white or cream-colored spathe. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often adorned with pale green veins, giving it its common name. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
