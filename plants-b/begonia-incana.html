Begonia incana

Begonia incana, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique texture and vibrant blooms make it a standout addition to both outdoor and indoor gardens.
