Bunchosia articulata

Bunchosia articulata, commonly known as the brittle bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its slender, often woody stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough texture.

Bunchosia articulata is known for its ability to thrive in saline and sandy soils, making it a common plant in coastal ecosystems.
