Blepharipappus hieracioides f. anomala

Blepharipappus hieracioides f. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous leaf hawkweed, is a variant of the common hawkweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and a unique inflorescence structure.

The f. anomala form is distinguished by its morphological variations, particularly in leaf shape and size compared to the typical form.
