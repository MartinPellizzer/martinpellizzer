Bothriocline argentea

Bothriocline argentea, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Bothriocline argentea is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
