Brotera trinervata

Brotera trinervata, commonly known as the African broom.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

Brotera trinervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
