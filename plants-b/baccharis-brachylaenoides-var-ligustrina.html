Baccharis brachylaenoides var. ligustrina

Baccharis brachylaenoides var. ligustrina, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
