Bromus erectus var. depauperatus

Bromus erectus var. depauperatus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, bristly florets.

This variety is characterized by its reduced floral structures and is often found in association with other grasses in mixed vegetation.
