Bupleurum armenum

Bupleurum armenum, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Bupleurum armenum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
