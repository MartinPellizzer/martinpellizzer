Bignonia vespertilia

Bignonia vespertilia, commonly known as evening glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that open in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
