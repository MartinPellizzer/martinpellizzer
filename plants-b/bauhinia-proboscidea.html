Bauhinia proboscidea

Bauhinia proboscidea, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and sometimes fissured. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Bauhinia proboscidea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
