Bactris tenuis var. inermis

Bactris tenuis var. inermis, commonly known as the slender pindo palm, is a compact palm species native to South America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, elongated fruits. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
