Bupleurum canaliculatum

Bupleurum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a serrated margin, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Bupleurum canaliculatum is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
