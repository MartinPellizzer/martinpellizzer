Brassaiopsis quercifolia

Brassaiopsis quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved brassaiopsis, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are a source of food for birds.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
