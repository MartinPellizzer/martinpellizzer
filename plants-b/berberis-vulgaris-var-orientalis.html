Berberis vulgaris var. orientalis

Berberis vulgaris var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It features spiny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions due to its vigorous growth and ability to spread through seeds.
