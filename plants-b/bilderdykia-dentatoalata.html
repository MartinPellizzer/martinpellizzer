Bilderdykia dentatoalata

Bilderdykia dentatoalata, commonly known as the serrated leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
