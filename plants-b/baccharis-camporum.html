Baccharis camporum

Baccharis camporum, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. It is often found in sandy or clay soils along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
