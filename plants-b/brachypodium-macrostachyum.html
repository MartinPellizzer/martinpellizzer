Brachypodium macrostachyum

Brachypodium macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, two-lobed flowers in late summer, which are arranged in clusters along the stem.

Brachypodium macrostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
