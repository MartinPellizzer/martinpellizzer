Bauhinia cuprea

Bauhinia cuprea, commonly known as the copperleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, which consists of two opposite leaflets that resemble a heart shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Bauhinia cuprea is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
