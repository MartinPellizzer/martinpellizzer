Buceras mutica

Buceras mutica, commonly known as the African violets, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Buceras mutica has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for indoor cultivation and garden beds.
