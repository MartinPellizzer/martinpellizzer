Baccharis roraimae

Baccharis roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima baccharis, is a shrub native to the Amazon region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Baccharis roraimae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
