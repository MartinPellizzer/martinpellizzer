Begonia × vesuvius

Begonia × vesuvius, commonly known as the Vesuvius begonia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia xerographica.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a striking contrast against its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
