Bambos hatsik

Bambos hatsik, commonly known as the Malagasy bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems. The plant grows in dense clusters, often forming thickets in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Bambos hatsik is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
