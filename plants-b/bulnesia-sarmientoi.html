Bulnesia sarmientoi

Bulnesia sarmientoi, commonly known as the Argentine cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to the arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. The tree has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fragrance, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
