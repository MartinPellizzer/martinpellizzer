Begonia gampura

Begonia gampura, commonly known as the Gampura begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with hairy stems and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
