Boehmeria tosaensis

Boehmeria tosaensis, commonly known as Tosa flax, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the nettle family, Urticaceae, and is characterized by its slender, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fibers are highly valued for their strength and are used in traditional crafts and textiles.

Boehmeria tosaensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
