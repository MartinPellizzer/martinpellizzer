Burdachia prismatocarpa f. intermedia

Burdachia prismatocarpa f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate prismatocarpa burdachia, is a variant of the Burdachia genus native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, prism-like fruit structure, which is a defining botanical feature of the species. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and potential ecological significance.
