Brachystelma nigidianum

Brachystelma nigidianum, commonly known as the purple-flowered stone orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces compact, hairy stems with clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Brachystelma nigidianum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and drought tolerance.
