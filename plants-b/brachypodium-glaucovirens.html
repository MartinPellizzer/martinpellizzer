Brachypodium glaucovirens

Brachypodium glaucovirens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers produce lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed.

Brachypodium glaucovirens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
