Bobartia gracilis

Bobartia gracilis, commonly known as slender bobartia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Bobartia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
