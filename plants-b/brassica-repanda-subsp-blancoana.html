Brassica repanda subsp. blancoana

Brassica repanda subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as the Blancoana mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and broccoli.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
