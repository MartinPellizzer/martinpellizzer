Bulbophyllum rarum

Bulbophyllum rarum, commonly known as the rare orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
