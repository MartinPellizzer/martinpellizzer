Brassica grandiflora

Brassica grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Brassica grandiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its showy flowers and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
