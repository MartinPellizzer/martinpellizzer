Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. typica

Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. typica, commonly known as the Harrison's bifrenaria, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
