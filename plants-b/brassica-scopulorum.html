Brassica scopulorum

Brassica scopulorum, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually narrow and serrated, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
