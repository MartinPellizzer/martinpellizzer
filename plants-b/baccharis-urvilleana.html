Baccharis urvilleana

Baccharis urvilleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its woody stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Baccharis urvilleana is commonly found in dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
