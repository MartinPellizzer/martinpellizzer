Bikinia

Bikinia, commonly known as the "bikinia plant," is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored leaves that feature a mix of green and purple hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, the bikinia plant is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
