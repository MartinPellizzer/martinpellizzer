Bothriocline argentea var. ungurensis

Bothriocline argentea var. ungurensis, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in silvery-white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery sheen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
