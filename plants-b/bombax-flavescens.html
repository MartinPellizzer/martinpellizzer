Bombax flavescens

Bombax flavescens, commonly known as the yellow cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white cotton-like seeds. The tree has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oblong, with a smooth margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
