Borreria verticillata var. sulcata

Borreria verticillata var. sulcata, commonly known as the Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Borreria verticillata var. sulcata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
