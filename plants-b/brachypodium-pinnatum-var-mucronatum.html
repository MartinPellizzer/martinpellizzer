Brachypodium pinnatum var. mucronatum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate little grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose panicle, with each spikelet containing a single floret. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
