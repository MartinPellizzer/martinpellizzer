Bupleurum ranunculoides var. laricense

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. laricense, commonly known as the Larice Mountain Bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
