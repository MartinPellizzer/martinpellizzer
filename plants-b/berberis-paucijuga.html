Berberis paucijuga

Berberis paucijuga, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
