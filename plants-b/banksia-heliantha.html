Banksia heliantha

Banksia heliantha, commonly known as the yellow banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Banksia heliantha plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly nectar-feeding birds and insects.
