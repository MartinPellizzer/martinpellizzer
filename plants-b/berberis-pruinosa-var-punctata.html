Berberis pruinosa var. punctata

Berberis pruinosa var. punctata, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features striking, waxy, dark green leaves with white or yellow spots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to ornamental landscapes.
