Blyxa graminea

Blyxa graminea, commonly known as narrowleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Blyxa graminea plays an important role in filtering water and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
