Bromelia hookeri

Bromelia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a waxy layer, which helps in reducing water loss.

Bromelia hookeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
