Bulbophyllum monosepalum

Bulbophyllum monosepalum, commonly known as the single-sepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a single sepal that is prominently displayed. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
