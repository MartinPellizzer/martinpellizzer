Berberis variiflora

Berberis variiflora, commonly known as the variable barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves that vary in shape, often with a combination of spiny and smooth margins. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Berberis variiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid conditions.
