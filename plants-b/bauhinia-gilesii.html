Bauhinia gilesii

Bauhinia gilesii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Bauhinia gilesii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
