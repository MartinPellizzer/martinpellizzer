Baccharis tola subsp. fimbriata

Baccharis tola subsp.

fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a suitable climate.
