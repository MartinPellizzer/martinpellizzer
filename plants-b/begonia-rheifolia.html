Begonia rheifolia

Begonia rheifolia, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in a variety of garden settings.
