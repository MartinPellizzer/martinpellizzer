Baccharis arunca

Baccharis arunca, commonly known as white baccharis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis arunca is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its drought tolerance and visual interest.
