Boerhavia coulteri

Boerhavia coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's purslane, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features prostrate stems covered in small, sticky hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically oval or rounded in shape. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Coulter's purslane is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its ability to store water in its leaves and stems.
