Berberis × wisleyensis

Berberis × wisleyensis, commonly known as the Wisley barberry, is a hybrid shrub resulting from the cross between Berberis julianae and Berberis thunbergii.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

The Wisley barberry is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resistance to many common pests and diseases.
