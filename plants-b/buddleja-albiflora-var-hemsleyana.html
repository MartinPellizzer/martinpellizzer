Buddleja albiflora var. hemsleyana

Buddleja albiflora var. hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's white butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
