Begonia acetosella var. acetosella

Begonia acetosella var. acetosella, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
