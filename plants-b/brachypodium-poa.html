Brachypodium poa

Brachypodium poa, commonly known as false brome, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachypodium poa thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important plant in ecological studies and agricultural research.
