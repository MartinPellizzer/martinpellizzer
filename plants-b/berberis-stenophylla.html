Berberis stenophylla

Berberis stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Berberis stenophylla is also notable for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
