Betula alba subsp. pubescens

Betula alba subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the silver birch variant with pubescent features, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and small, oval leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
