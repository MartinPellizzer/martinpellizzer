Byrsonima sericea var. angustifolia

Byrsonima sericea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nickerbean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
