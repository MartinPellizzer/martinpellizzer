Brassica rapa var. campestris

Brassica rapa var. campestris, commonly known as turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, swollen root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are rough to the touch. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits.

This versatile crop is cultivated for its edible root and leaves, and it is also used as a fodder plant in agriculture.
