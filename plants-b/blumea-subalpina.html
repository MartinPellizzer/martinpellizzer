Blumea subalpina

Blumea subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine Blumea, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Blumea subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
