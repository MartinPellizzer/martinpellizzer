Bactris microspatha

Bactris microspatha, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional thatching and as roofing material.

Bactris microspatha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
