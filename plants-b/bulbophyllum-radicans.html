Bulbophyllum radicans

Bulbophyllum radicans, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are modified to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies among different cultivars.
