Bromus dolichostachys

Bromus dolichostachys, commonly known as the long-stalked brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small and wind-pollinated.

Bromus dolichostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
