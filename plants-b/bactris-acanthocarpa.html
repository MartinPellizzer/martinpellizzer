Bactris acanthocarpa

Bactris acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
