Bothriochloa laguroides subsp. torreyana

Bothriochloa laguroides subsp.

torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's bermuda grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and has a clumping growth habit.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and flowering morphology.
