Balania henryi

Balania henryi, commonly known as Henry's balanites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Balanitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with compound leaves. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Balania henryi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
