Bulbophyllum acutilingue

Bulbophyllum acutilingue, commonly known as the sharp-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
