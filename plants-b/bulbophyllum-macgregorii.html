Bulbophyllum macgregorii

Bulbophyllum macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the production of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
