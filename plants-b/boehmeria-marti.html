Boehmeria marti

Boehmeria marti, commonly known as the Japanese nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many species of nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and stinging hairs on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Boehmeria marti is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
