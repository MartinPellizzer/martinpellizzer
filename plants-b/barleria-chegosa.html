Barleria chegosa

Barleria chegosa, commonly known as the red barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Barleria chegosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
