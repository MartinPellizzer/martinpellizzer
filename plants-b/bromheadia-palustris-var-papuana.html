Bromheadia palustris var. papuana

Bromheadia palustris var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan swamp bromheadia, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the wetlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water in its native humid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which bloom during the wet season.
