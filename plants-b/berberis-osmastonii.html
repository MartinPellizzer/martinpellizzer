Berberis osmastonii

Berberis osmastonii, commonly known as the Osmaston barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Berberis osmastonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability.
