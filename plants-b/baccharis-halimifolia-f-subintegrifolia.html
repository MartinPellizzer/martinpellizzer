Baccharis halimifolia f. subintegrifolia

Baccharis halimifolia f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
