Bulbophyllum crassiusculum

Bulbophyllum crassiusculum, commonly known as the thick-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy pseudobulbs and broad, leathery leaves that help store water. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
