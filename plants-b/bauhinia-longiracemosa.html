Bauhinia longiracemosa

Bauhinia longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia longiracemosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
