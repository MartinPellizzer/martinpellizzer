Bulbophyllum chaunobulbon

Bulbophyllum chaunobulbon, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing multiple flowers. The flowers are typically small, with a distinctive purple or pink coloration and intricate veining. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the diverse flora of its native habitats.
