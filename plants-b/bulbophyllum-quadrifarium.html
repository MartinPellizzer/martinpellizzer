Bulbophyllum quadrifarium

Bulbophyllum quadrifarium, commonly known as the four-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native tropical environment.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
