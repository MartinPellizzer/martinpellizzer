Begonia algaia

Begonia algaia, commonly known as the Algae Begonia, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.

However, it requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive, making it a sought-after but challenging plant for growers.
