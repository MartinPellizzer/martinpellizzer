Bombax samauma

Bombax samauma, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a large deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red bracts that surround its small, capsule-like flowers. The tree produces fluffy, white seeds encased in a cotton-like fiber, which is used in various traditional and commercial applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
