Blechnum pygmaeum

Blechnum pygmaeum, commonly known as the fairy fern, is a small, compact fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, intricate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
