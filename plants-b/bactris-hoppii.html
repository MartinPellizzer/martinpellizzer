Bactris hoppii

Bactris hoppii, commonly known as the red rafaela, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Bactris hoppii is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
