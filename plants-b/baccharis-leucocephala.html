Baccharis leucocephala

Baccharis leucocephala, commonly known as whitehead bush or whitebrush, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of aromatic oils.

Its adaptability makes it a common invasive species in some areas.
