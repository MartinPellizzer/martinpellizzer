Boopis crassifolia var. spinuligera

Boopis crassifolia var. spinuligera, commonly known as the spiny leaf boopis, is a distinctive plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in small spines, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
