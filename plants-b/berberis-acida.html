Berberis acida

Berberis acida, commonly known as sour barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. Berberis acida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
