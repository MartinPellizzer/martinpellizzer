Besleria duarteana

Besleria duarteana, commonly known as the Duarte's flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting dark markings. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
