Betula occidentalis subsp. fecunda

Betula occidentalis subsp.

fecunda, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to western North America. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
