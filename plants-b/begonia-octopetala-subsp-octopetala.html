Begonia octopetala subsp. octopetala

Begonia octopetala subsp.

octopetala, commonly known as the eight-petaled begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature eight petals, giving it its common name.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation and botanical gardens.
