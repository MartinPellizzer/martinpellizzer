Begonia mendumae

Begonia mendumae, commonly known as the Mendum's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Begonia mendumae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to botanical collections.
