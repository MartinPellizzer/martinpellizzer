Benzoin salicifolium

Benzoin salicifolium, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or salicifolium tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested and used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
