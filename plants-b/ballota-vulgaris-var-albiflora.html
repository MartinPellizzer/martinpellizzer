Ballota vulgaris var. albiflora

Ballota vulgaris var. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate flowers make it an attractive addition to botanical gardens.
