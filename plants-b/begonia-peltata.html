Begonia peltata

Begonia peltata, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers indirect sunlight.

Begonia peltata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
