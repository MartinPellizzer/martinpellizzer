Bulbophyllum amblyacron

Bulbophyllum amblyacron, commonly known as the narrow-headed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow floral bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
