Bignonia australis

Bignonia australis, commonly known as the Australian trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Australia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting tendrils that help it climb supports. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
