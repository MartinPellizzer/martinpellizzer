Berberis dolichostemon

Berberis dolichostemon, commonly known as the long-stemmed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
