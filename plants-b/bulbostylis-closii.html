Bulbostylis closii

Bulbostylis closii, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
