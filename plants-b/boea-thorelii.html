Boea thorelii

Boea thorelii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Boea thorelii thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
