Begonia aggeloptera

Begonia aggeloptera, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia aggeloptera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
