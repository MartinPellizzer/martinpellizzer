Banksia hewardiana

Banksia hewardiana, commonly known as the Heward's banksia, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests. The plant is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are usually yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators and wildlife.
