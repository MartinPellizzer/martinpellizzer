Burmannia bifurca

Burmannia bifurca, commonly known as the red lady's slipper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its striking, fleshy, reddish-pink petals that resemble a slipper. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within its leaf-like bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Burmannia bifurca is noted for its unique morphology and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
