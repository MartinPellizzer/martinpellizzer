Buckleya joan

Buckleya joan, commonly known as the Japanese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double-flowered blooms that appear in early spring. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers, which are often followed by bright red berries. Buckleya joan has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside.

It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
