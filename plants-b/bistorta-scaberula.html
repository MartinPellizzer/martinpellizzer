Bistorta scaberula

Bistorta scaberula, commonly known as the rough bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, rhizomatous stems that are covered in rough, hairy texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in late summer.

Bistorta scaberula is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
