Begonia dentatiloba

Begonia dentatiloba, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Begonia dentatiloba prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
