Bromus sericeus subsp. fallax

Bromus sericeus subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the silky brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous slender, awned flowers. Bromus sericeus subsp.

fallax is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration projects.
