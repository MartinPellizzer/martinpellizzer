Begonia villosula

Begonia villosula, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with branching stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Begonia villosula prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
