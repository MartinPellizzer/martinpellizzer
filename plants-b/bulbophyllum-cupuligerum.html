Bulbophyllum cupuligerum

Bulbophyllum cupuligerum, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped pseudobulbs and is typically found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, and it thrives in well-drained, loose soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking appearance and resilience.
