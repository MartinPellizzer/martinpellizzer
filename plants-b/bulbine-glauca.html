Bulbine glauca

Bulbine glauca, commonly known as the blue bulbous plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
