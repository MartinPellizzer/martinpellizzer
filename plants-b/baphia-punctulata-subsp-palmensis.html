Baphia punctulata subsp. palmensis

Baphia punctulata subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as the West African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flattened seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
