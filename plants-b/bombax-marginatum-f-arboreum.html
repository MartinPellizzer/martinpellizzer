Bombax marginatum f. arboreum

Bombax marginatum f. arboreum, commonly known as the red silk cotton tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and practical uses.
