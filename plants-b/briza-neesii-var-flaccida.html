Briza neesii var. flaccida

Briza neesii var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, arching stems that bear feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
