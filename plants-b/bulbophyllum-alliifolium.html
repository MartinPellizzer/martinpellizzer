Bulbophyllum alliifolium

Bulbophyllum alliifolium, commonly known as the garlic leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, garlic-scented leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
