Bystropogon setosus var. citronella

Bystropogon setosus var. citronella, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant contains essential oils, primarily citral, which contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The leaves are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
