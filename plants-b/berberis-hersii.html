Berberis hersii

Berberis hersii, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Berberis hersii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
