Bursera bahiensis

Bursera bahiensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.
