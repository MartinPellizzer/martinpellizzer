Bredemeyera floribunda f. subvestita

Bredemeyera floribunda f. subvestita, commonly known as the silky thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
