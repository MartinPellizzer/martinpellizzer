Brownanthus solutifolius

Brownanthus solutifolius, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, drooping panicles of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and wildlife habitat.
