Bejaria parvifolia

Bejaria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bejaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface with serrated edges. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season.

The plant is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
