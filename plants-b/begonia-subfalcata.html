Begonia subfalcata

Begonia subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Begonia subfalcata is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
