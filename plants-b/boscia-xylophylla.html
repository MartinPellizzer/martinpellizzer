Boscia xylophylla

Boscia xylophylla, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, woody stems and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Boscia xylophylla is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
