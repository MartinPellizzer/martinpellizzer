Brizopyrum pilosum

Brizopyrum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy woodoak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Brizopyrum pilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
