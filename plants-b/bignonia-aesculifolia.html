Bignonia aesculifolia

Bignonia aesculifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and its ability to grow as a shrub or small tree.
