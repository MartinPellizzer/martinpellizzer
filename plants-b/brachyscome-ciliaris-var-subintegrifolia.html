Brachyscome ciliaris var. subintegrifolia

Brachyscome ciliaris var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf star bush, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or white, with a central yellow disc. The leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and its resilience to coastal conditions.
