Blumea balsamifera var. microcephala

Blumea balsamifera var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
