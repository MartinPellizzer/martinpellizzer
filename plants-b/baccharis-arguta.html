Baccharis arguta

Baccharis arguta, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Baccharis arguta is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
