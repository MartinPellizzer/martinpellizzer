Breynia thoii

Breynia thoii, commonly known as the silverleaf breynia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Breynia thoii is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting succulent properties that help it retain moisture.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
