Bromus occidentalis

Bromus occidentalis, commonly known as western bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing two to three small, awned flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, open areas.

Bromus occidentalis is often found in disturbed sites and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
