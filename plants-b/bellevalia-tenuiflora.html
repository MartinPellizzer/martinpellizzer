Bellevalia tenuiflora

Bellevalia tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue or violet flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Bellevalia tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming, bell-shaped blooms.
