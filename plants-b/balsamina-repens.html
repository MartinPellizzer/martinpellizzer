Balsamina repens

Balsamina repens, commonly known as the crawling balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Balsamina repens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
