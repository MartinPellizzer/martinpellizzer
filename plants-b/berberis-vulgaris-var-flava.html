Berberis vulgaris var. flava

Berberis vulgaris var. flava, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are toxic to humans and livestock, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
