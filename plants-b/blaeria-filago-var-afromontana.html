Blaeria filago var. afromontana

Blaeria filago var. afromontana, commonly known as the African mountain blaeria, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
