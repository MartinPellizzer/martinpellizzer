Begonia cyathophora

Begonia cyathophora, commonly known as the cup-shaped begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like inflorescence that holds both showy bracts and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy, ovate leaves that have a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.
