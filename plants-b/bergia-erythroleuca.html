Bergia erythroleuca

Bergia erythroleuca, commonly known as the red-leaved Bergia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Bergia erythroleuca thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

It is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
