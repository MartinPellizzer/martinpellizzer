Barkhausia albida var. major

Barkhausia albida var. major, commonly known as the white bark lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow to white bark that becomes more prominent with age. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance in botanical gardens.
