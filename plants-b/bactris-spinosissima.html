Bactris spinosissima

Bactris spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Bactris spinosissima is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

Its unique spiny features and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
