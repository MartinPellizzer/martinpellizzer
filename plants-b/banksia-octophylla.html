Banksia octophylla

Banksia octophylla, commonly known as the eight-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are a key feature of the genus Banksia. The plant produces woody follicles that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Banksia octophylla is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for honeyeaters and other pollinators.
