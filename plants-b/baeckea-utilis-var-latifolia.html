Baeckea utilis var. latifolia

Baeckea utilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved baeckea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and long-lasting floral display.
