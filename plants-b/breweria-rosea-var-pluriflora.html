Breweria rosea var. pluriflora

Breweria rosea var. pluriflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered breweria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
