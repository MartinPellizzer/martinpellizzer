Biscutella microcarpa f. scabrida

Biscutella microcarpa f. scabrida, commonly known as the scabrous biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, scabrous texture, which is a distinguishing feature of this forma. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
