Begonia elmeri

Begonia elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green veins and vibrant red undersides. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia elmeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique leaf patterns and aesthetic appeal.
