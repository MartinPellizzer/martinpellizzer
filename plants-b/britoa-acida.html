Britoa acida

Britoa acida, commonly known as sour britoa, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and waxy, helping to store water and reduce transpiration.

Britoa acida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
