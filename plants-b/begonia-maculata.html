Begonia maculata

Begonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that feature distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia maculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
