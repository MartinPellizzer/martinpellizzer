Berberis garhwalensis

Berberis garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in the spring. Its berries, which are edible, are red and turn bright red in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
