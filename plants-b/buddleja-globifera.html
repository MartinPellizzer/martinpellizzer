Buddleja globifera

Buddleja globifera, commonly known as the globeflower or globeflower bush, is a shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower clusters, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, globe-like inflorescences, giving it its common name. Buddleja globifera is a deciduous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
