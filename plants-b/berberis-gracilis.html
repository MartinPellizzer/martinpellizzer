Berberis gracilis

Berberis gracilis, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching branches covered with small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in colder climates, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
