Brachiaria glabrinodis

Brachiaria glabrinodis, commonly known as the smooth-node grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as pastures and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria glabrinodis is valued for its forage quality and its ability to stabilize soil in agricultural settings.
