Bacopa semiserrata

Bacopa semiserrata, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water edges.

Bacopa semiserrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
