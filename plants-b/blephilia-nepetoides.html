Blephilia nepetoides

Blephilia nepetoides, commonly known as woolly blue curls, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.
