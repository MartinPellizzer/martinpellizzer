Bupleurum falcatum var. brassicifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. brassicifolium, commonly known as the brassica-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical characteristics.
