Bryantea dealbata

Bryantea dealbata, commonly known as the silverleaf bryantea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a distinctive silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Bryantea dealbata is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
