Boronia hippopalus

Boronia hippopalus, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Boronia hippopalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
