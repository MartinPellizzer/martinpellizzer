Bothriospermum zeylanicum

Bothriospermum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or Malabar sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pale yellow wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and has cultural and economic significance in traditional medicine and perfumery.
