Boldu nitidum

Boldu nitidum, commonly known as Brazilian peppermint or boldo, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, mint-like scent. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.

Boldu nitidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
