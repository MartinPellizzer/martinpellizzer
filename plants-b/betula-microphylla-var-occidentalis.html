Betula microphylla var. occidentalis

Betula microphylla var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it its common name. The tree has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, winged seeds in catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
