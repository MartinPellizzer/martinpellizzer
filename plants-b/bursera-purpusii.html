Bursera purpusii

Bursera purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, fragrant yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
