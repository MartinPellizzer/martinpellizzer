Bignonia angrensis

Bignonia angrensis, commonly known as the Angren bignonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous liana that can climb to considerable heights, often found in shrublands and woodlands. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, with lance-shaped leaflets.

Bignonia angrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
