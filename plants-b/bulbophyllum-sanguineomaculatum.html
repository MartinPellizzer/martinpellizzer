Bulbophyllum sanguineomaculatum

Bulbophyllum sanguineomaculatum, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short rhizome with numerous pseudobulbs, each bearing a single leaf and a flower spike. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pink or red with contrasting white or yellow markings.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and attractive blooms.
