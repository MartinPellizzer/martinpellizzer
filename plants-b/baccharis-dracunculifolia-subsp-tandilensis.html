Baccharis dracunculifolia subsp. tandilensis

Baccharis dracunculifolia subsp.

tandilensis, commonly known as Tandil shrub, is a shrub native to Argentina. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Its resilience makes it a useful plant for restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
