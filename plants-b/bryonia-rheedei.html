Bryonia rheedei

Bryonia rheedei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a member of the genus Bryonia in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, particularly found in the Cape Province. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a rough, spiny husk. These fruits are typically harvested when fully mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant's botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and a distinct, bitter-tasting fruit.
