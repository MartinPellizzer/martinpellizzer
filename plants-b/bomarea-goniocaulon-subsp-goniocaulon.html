Bomarea goniocaulon subsp. goniocaulon

Bomarea goniocaulon subsp.

goniocaulon, commonly known as the pink bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
