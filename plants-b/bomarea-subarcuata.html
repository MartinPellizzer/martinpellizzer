Bomarea subarcuata

Bomarea subarcuata, commonly known as the zigzag bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with a climbing habit, featuring tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Bomarea subarcuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
