Brandisia

Brandisia, commonly known as the "flowering fig tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Sapotaceae.

It consists of about 150 species, predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. They produce non-edible fruits that are often enclosed in a fleshy, fig-like structure.

Brandisia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
