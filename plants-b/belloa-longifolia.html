Belloa longifolia

Belloa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stem. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to alpine and coastal gardens.
