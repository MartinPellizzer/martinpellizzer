Barleria ameliae

Barleria ameliae, commonly known as the Natal barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Barleria ameliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
