Begonia pearcei

Begonia pearcei, commonly known as the pearce begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia pearcei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
