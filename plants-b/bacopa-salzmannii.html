Bacopa salzmannii

Bacopa salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's water bead, is a small, aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are often clustered together, forming a dense, bead-like structure on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens for ornamental purposes.
