Buddleja polystachya var. parvifolia

Buddleja polystachya var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
