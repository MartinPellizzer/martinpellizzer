Bactris tefensis

Bactris tefensis, commonly known as the Tefensis palm, is a species of palm native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are similar in appearance to chikos. Bactris tefensis is adapted to thrive in warm, wet climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
