Berberis hayatana

Berberis hayatana, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Berberis hayatana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
