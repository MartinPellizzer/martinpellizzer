Begonia pastoensis var. hirsutior

Begonia pastoensis var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that provide a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
