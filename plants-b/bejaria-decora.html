Bejaria decora

Bejaria decora, commonly known as the ornamental bejaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bejaria decora is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
