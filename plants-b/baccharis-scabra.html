Baccharis scabra

Baccharis scabra, commonly known as rough baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Baccharis scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
