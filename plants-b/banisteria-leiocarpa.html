Banisteria leiocarpa

Banisteria leiocarpa, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a climbing habit, often forming dense foliage and producing a fragrant, showy inflorescence. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Banisteria leiocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
