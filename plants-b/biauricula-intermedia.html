Biauricula intermedia

Biauricula intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from blue to violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Biauricula intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
