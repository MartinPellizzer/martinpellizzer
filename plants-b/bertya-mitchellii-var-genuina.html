Bertya mitchellii var. genuina

Bertya mitchellii var. genuina, commonly known as the Mitchell's bertya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal and inland areas with low rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
