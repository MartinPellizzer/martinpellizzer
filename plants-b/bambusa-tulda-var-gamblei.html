Bambusa tulda var. gamblei

Bambusa tulda var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gamble's bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright culms that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The leaves are long and lance-shaped, with a smooth, dark green surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by rod-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
