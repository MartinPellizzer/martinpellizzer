Bambusa virgata

Bambusa virgata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its stiff, golden-yellow stems and its ability to form dense clumps. Bambusa virgata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural strength.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
