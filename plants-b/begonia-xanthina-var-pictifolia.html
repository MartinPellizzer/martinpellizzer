Begonia xanthina var. pictifolia

Begonia xanthina var. pictifolia, commonly known as the Painted Begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, mottled leaves with a mix of green, yellow, and red hues, creating a visually appealing foliage pattern. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer.

This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
