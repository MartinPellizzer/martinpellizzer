Blaeria articulata

Blaeria articulata, commonly known as the jointed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic insects. The plant's stems are segmented and often float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the sediment. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, including the production of seeds and vegetative propagation.

Its botanical features are adapted for nutrient acquisition in nutrient-poor aquatic environments.
