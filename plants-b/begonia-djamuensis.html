Begonia djamuensis

Begonia djamuensis, commonly known as the Djamuensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often vibrant foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
