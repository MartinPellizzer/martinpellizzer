Bidens andicola var. mandonii

Bidens andicola var. mandonii, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
