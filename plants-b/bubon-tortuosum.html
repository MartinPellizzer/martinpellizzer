Bubon tortuosum

Bubon tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bubon tortuosum is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
