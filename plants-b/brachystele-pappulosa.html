Brachystele pappulosa

Brachystele pappulosa, commonly known as the hairy stoloniferous fern, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, creeping stems that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces small, delicate fronds with a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in humid environments. Its reproductive structures are borne on specialized spore-bearing leaves, typical of the fern family.

Brachystele pappulosa thrives in shaded, moist habitats and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
