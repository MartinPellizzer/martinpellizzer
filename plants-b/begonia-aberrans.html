Begonia aberrans

Begonia aberrans, commonly known as the variable begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that can vary greatly in shape and texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia aberrans is popular among collectors for its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor cultivation.
