Bauhinia pottsii var. velutina

Bauhinia pottsii var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, papery flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, and the plant is well adapted to arid environments.

Bauhinia pottsii var. velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
