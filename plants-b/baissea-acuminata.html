Baissea acuminata

Baissea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and pointed at the tips. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Baissea acuminata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
