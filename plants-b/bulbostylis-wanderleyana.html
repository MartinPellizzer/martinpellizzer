Bulbostylis wanderleyana

Bulbostylis wanderleyana, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, tufted growth habit.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming colonies in suitable environments.

While not widely cultivated, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
