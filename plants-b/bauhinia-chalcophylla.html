Bauhinia chalcophylla

Bauhinia chalcophylla, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often hairy and have a fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia chalcophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
