Boltonia caroliniana

Boltonia caroliniana, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, adorned with large, showy white to pale pink ray flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its conical, yellow-centered flower head, which resembles a dandelion. Boltonia caroliniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along streambanks, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
