Bromopsis malyschevii

Bromopsis malyschevii, commonly known as the Malyschev's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan range. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in rocky or alpine environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bromopsis malyschevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
