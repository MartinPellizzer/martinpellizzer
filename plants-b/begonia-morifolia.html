Begonia morifolia

Begonia morifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in inflorescences at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Begonia morifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
