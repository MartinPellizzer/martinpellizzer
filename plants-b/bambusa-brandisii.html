Bambusa brandisii

Bambusa brandisii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Bambusa brandisii is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens.
