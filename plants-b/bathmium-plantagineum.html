Bathmium plantagineum

Bathmium plantagineum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are typically borne on upright stems, adding a decorative touch to indoor spaces.
