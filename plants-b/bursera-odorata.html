Bursera odorata

Bursera odorata, commonly known as the fragrant bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its aromatic resin, which is often used in traditional medicine and incense.

Bursera odorata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.
