Berberis capillaris

Berberis capillaris, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and red, orange, or yellow berries that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Berberis capillaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
