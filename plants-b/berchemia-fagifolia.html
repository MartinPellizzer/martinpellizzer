Berchemia fagifolia

Berchemia fagifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, white spherical flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Berchemia fagifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
