Bromheadia longifolia

Bromheadia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bromheadia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family, which includes plants like taro and philodendrons, and is characterized by its showy, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it attaches itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often reaching several feet in length, and are a prominent feature of its appearance.

Bromheadia longifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
