Berkheya erysithales

Berkheya erysithales, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces bright red bracts that surround the yellow flowers, creating a visually dramatic display. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Berkheya erysithales is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
