Bactris simplicifrons var. subpinnata

Bactris simplicifrons var. subpinnata, commonly known as the pink-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves with a distinctive pinkish or reddish vein pattern. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of the coconut, with a fibrous, starchy interior. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
