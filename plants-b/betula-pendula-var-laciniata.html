Betula pendula var. laciniata

Betula pendula var. laciniata, commonly known as the weeping willow with lobed leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are finely toothed and lobed, giving the plant a delicate, feathery look. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide shade in wetland environments.
