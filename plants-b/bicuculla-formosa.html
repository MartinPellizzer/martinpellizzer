Bicuculla formosa

Bicuculla formosa, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment, and it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

The flowering period of Bicuculla formosa usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a notable species in its ecological habitat.
