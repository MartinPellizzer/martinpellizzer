Berberis gyalaica var. maximiflora

Berberis gyalaica var. maximiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to horticultural collections.
