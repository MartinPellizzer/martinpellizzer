Brassica sabularia subsp. oxyrrhina

Brassica sabularia subsp.

oxyrrhina, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, and it produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
