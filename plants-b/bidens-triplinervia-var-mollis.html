Bidens triplinervia var. mollis

Bidens triplinervia var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
