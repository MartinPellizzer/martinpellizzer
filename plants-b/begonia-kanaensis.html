Begonia kanaensis

Begonia kanaensis, commonly known as the Kanaensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
