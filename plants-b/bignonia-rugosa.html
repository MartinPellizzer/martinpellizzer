Bignonia rugosa

Bignonia rugosa, commonly known as the rough bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or trellises.

Bignonia rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms.
