Brahea dulcis f. humilis

Brahea dulcis f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bottle tree, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, thick trunk that stores water, typical of the Cactaceae family, and is adorned with clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to desert landscapes.
