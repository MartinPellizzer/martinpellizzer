Boea caerulescens

Boea caerulescens, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, round, green berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.
