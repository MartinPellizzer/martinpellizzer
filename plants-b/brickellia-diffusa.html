Brickellia diffusa

Brickellia diffusa, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It features showy, bright orange to red composite flowers that bloom from summer to fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Brickellia diffusa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
