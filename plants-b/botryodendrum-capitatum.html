Botryodendrum capitatum

Botryodendrum capitatum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Botryodendrum capitatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
