Betula × hybrida var. rotundifolia

Betula × hybrida var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid species within the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and oval-shaped leaves that are slightly rounded at the base. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
