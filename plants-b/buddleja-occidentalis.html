Buddleja occidentalis

Buddleja occidentalis, commonly known as the butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces fragrant, nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Buddleja occidentalis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
