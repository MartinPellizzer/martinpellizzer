Banisteria hyposerica

Banisteria hyposerica, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
