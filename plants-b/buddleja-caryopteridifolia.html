Buddleja caryopteridifolia

Buddleja caryopteridifolia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, nutlet-like fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja caryopteridifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
