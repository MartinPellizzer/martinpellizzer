Billbergia × cappei

Billbergia × cappei, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia recurvata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America. The plant features striking, rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. It produces colorful inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and insects.

Billbergia × cappei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
