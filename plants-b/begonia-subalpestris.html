Begonia subalpestris

Begonia subalpestris, commonly known as the alpine begonia, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who grow alpine plants.
