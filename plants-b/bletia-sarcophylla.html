Bletia sarcophylla

Bletia sarcophylla, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers with a contrasting white or green base. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Bletia sarcophylla is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
