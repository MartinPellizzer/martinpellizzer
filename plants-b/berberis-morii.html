Berberis morii

Berberis morii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and alternate, compound leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal.
