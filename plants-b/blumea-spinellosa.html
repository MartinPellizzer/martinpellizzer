Blumea spinellosa

Blumea spinellosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blumea spinellosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
