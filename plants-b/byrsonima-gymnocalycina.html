Byrsonima gymnocalycina

Byrsonima gymnocalycina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble cherries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tart flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
