Begonia anceps

Begonia anceps, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Begonia anceps is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact size.
