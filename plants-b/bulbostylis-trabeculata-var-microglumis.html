Bulbostylis trabeculata var. microglumis

Bulbostylis trabeculata var. microglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive structure that aids in water retention.

This variety is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
