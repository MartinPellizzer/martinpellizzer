Bulbophyllum pidacanthum

Bulbophyllum pidacanthum, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with yellow and green hues, featuring a unique spider-like appearance. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum pidacanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
