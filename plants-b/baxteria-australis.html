Baxteria australis

Baxteria australis, commonly known as the Australian baxteria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Baxteria australis is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
