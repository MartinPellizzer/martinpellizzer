Blepharipappus scaber subsp. laevis

Blepharipappus scaber subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf blepharipappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that are smooth and somewhat succulent.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and heat.
