Biancaea scandens

Biancaea scandens, commonly known as the climbing false indigo, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using tendrils and can grow quite long, sometimes reaching several meters in height. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
