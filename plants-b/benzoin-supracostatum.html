Benzoin supracostatum

Benzoin supracostatum, commonly known as the fragrant resin tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a valuable aromatic resin, which is harvested from its trunk and used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its growth habit is typically as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The leaves are alternate and emit a strong, sweet scent when crushed, contributing to its commercial importance.
