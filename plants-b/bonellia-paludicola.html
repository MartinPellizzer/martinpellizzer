Bonellia paludicola

Bonellia paludicola, commonly known as the marsh bonellia, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Bonellieae and is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along waterways. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they are adapted for pollination by insects.

Bonellia paludicola plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
