Begonia praetermissa

Begonia praetermissa, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
