Betonica glabrata

Betonica glabrata, commonly known as the smooth betony, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Betonica glabrata is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming and antispasmodic properties.
