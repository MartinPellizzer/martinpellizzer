Betula glandulosa var. rotundifolia

Betula glandulosa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf birch, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often pubescent underneath. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and female flowers are typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
