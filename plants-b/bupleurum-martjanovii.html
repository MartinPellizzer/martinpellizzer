Bupleurum martjanovii

Bupleurum martjanovii, commonly known as the Caucasian bilewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in umbel-like clusters. It is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Bupleurum martjanovii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
