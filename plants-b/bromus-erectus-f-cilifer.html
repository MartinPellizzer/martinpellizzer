Bromus erectus f. cilifer

Bromus erectus f. cilifer, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a variant of the common brome grass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive hairy leaves. This plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial grass that produces spikelets with two to three flowers.

The inflorescence is usually slender and cylindrical, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain landscapes.
