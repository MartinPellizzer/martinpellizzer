Bunium macuca subsp. nivale

Bunium macuca subsp.

nivale, commonly known as the snow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold and sunny conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant is known for its aromatic, pungent scent.

It is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
