Brassia maculata

Brassia maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature contrasting spots or stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture.

Brassia maculata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
