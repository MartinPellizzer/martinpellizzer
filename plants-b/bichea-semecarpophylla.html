Bichea semecarpophylla

Bichea semecarpophylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive, serrated edge, and its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.
