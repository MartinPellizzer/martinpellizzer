Biscutella saxatilis var. alpina

Biscutella saxatilis var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes at high elevations.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and fragile species.
