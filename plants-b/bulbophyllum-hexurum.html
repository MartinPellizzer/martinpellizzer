Bulbophyllum hexurum

Bulbophyllum hexurum, commonly known as the six-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum hexurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
