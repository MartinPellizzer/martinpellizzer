Blechnum corbassonii

Blechnum corbassonii, commonly known as the Corbasson's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system and produces lanceolate, serrated fronds that are usually dark green in color. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum corbassonii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
