Berberis vulgaris var. serotina

Berberis vulgaris var. serotina, commonly known as the late-leaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that mature in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The variety is distinguished by its delayed leaf fall compared to other barberry species.
