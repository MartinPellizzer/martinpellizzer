Brachyotum microphyllum

Brachyotum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved brachyotum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
