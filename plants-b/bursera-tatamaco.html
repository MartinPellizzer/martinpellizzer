Bursera tatamaco

Bursera tatamaco, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. Its fragrant sap, similar to frankincense, has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

Bursera tatamaco thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along coastal areas.
