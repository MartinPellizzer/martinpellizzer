Bernardina elegans

Bernardina elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Bernardina elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
