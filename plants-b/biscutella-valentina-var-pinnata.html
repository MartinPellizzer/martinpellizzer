Biscutella valentina var. pinnata

Biscutella valentina var. pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its distinctive pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.
