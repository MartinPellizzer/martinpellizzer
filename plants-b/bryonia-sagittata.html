Bryonia sagittata

Bryonia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf bryony, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils.
