Brachanthemum krascheninnikovii

Brachanthemum krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's brachanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is typically low-growing, with silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in its survival in hot, dry environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

Brachanthemum krascheninnikovii is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.
