Badilloa guantivana var. guicanensis

Badilloa guantivana var. guicanensis, commonly known as the Guianese badilloa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Venezuela and Guyana.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
