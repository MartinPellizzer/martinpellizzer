Bulbophyllum oreodorum

Bulbophyllum oreodorum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Bulbophyllum oreodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
