Bernardia laurentii

Bernardia laurentii, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
