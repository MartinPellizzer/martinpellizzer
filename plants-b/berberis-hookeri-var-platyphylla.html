Berberis hookeri var. platyphylla

Berberis hookeri var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

The variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other Berberis species.
