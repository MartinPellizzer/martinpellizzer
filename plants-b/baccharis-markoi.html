Baccharis markoi

Baccharis markoi, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Baccharis markoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
