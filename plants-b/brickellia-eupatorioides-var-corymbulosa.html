Brickellia eupatorioides var. corymbulosa

Brickellia eupatorioides var. corymbulosa, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a woody base, characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
