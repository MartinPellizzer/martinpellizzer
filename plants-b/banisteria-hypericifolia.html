Banisteria hypericifolia

Banisteria hypericifolia, commonly known as the red-veined or red-vein balsam, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
