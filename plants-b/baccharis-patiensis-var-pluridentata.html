Baccharis patiensis var. pluridentata

Baccharis patiensis var. pluridentata, commonly known as the Brazilian bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive serrated margin, while its inflorescences are clustered in terminal heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
