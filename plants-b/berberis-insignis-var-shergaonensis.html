Berberis insignis var. shergaonensis

Berberis insignis var. shergaonensis, commonly known as the Shergaon barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh alpine environments.
