Basella crassifolia

Basella crassifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown as an ornamental or vegetable crop. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including iron and calcium, making it a valuable addition to diets in many regions.

Basella crassifolia is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and versatile plant.
