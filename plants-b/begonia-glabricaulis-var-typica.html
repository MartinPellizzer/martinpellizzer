Begonia glabricaulis var. typica

Begonia glabricaulis var. typica, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
