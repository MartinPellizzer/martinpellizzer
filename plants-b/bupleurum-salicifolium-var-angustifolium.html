Bupleurum salicifolium var. angustifolium

Bupleurum salicifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boldo, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
