Blattaria virgata

Blattaria virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's Blattaria, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its alpine habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices or on slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
