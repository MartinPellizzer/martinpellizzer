Bothriochloa perforata

Bothriochloa perforata, commonly known as the punctured grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive perforated base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem in its native habitat.
