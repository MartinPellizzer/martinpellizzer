Bromus secalinus var. rhenanthus

Bromus secalinus var. rhenanthus, commonly known as the rye brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

This variety is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
