Bupleurum angulosum var. pyrenaeum

Bupleurum angulosum var. pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching towards the upper part. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
