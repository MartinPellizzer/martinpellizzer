Bergeranthus cookii

Bergeranthus cookii, commonly known as Cook's bergeranthus, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive central column. The plant features velvety, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture. Bergeranthus cookii is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a compact and bushy growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
