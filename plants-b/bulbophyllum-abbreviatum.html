Bulbophyllum abbreviatum

Bulbophyllum abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to its natural habitat in shaded, humid environments.

Bulbophyllum abbreviatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
