Bromus velutinus var. triaristatus

Bromus velutinus var. triaristatus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically three-angled, giving it its specific epithet "triaristatus.". It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Bromus velutinus var. triaristatus is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
