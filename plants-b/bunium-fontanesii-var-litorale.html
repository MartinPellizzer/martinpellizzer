Bunium fontanesii var. litorale

Bunium fontanesii var. litorale, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils along the shoreline, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
