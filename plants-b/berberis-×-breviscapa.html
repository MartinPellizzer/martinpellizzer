Berberis × breviscapa

Berberis × breviscapa, commonly known as the yellow barberry, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Berberis asiatica and Berberis darwinii.

It is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its vibrant yellow flowers and attractive foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features thorny stems, which are a common trait in the Berberis genus. Its leaves are small, oval-shaped, and have a glossy texture, while its berries are orange-red and edible.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
