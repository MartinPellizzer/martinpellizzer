Bomarea multiflora

Bomarea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences. The plant features tuberous roots and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and aromatic.

Bomarea multiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
