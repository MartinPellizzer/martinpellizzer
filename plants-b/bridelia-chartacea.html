Bridelia chartacea

Bridelia chartacea, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous inner layer, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Bridelia chartacea is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
