Byrsonima peruviana var. eglandulosa

Byrsonima peruviana var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the Peruvian Guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and ovate, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
