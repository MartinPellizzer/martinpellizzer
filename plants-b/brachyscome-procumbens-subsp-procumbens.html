Brachyscome procumbens subsp. procumbens

Brachyscome procumbens subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion or small-flowered bluebell, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Australia. It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom from spring to autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
