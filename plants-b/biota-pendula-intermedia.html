Biota pendula intermedia

Biota pendula intermedia, commonly known as the hanging mistflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that resemble delicate chains of flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy white to pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
