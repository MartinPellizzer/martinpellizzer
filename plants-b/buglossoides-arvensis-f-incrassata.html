Buglossoides arvensis f. incrassata

Buglossoides arvensis f. incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved field scabious, is a variant of the field scabious plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, thick, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
