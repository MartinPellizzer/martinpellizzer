Batatas papiru

Batatas papiru, commonly known as sweet potato, is a root vegetable belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, tuberous roots that come in a variety of colors, including orange, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces flowering stems that resemble vines.

Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and essential vitamins like vitamin A. Batatas papiru thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, making it a staple in many tropical and subtropical regions.
