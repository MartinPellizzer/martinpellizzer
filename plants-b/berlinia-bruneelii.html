Berlinia bruneelii

Berlinia bruneelii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
