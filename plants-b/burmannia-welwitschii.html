Burmannia welwitschii

Burmannia welwitschii, commonly known as the red bladderwort, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its bright red, inflated leaves that resemble bladders.

Native to regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. The plant is part of the Balsaminaceae family, which includes other gesneriad-like species. Its unique morphology and vivid coloration make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it is not typically used for medicinal or agricultural purposes.
