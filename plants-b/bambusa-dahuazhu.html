Bambusa dahuazhu

Bambusa dahuazhu, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to southern China.

It features sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a smooth, greenish-brown exterior. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese architecture.
