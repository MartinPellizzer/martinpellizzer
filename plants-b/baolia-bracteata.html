Baolia bracteata

Baolia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate baolia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracts that encase the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.

Baolia bracteata produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color, attracting various pollinators.
