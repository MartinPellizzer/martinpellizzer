Bromopsis triniana

Bromopsis triniana, commonly known as the Trinian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Bromopsis triniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
