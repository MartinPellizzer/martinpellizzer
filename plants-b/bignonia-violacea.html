Bignonia violacea

Bignonia violacea, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with red veins, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Bignonia violacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
