Belvala dodecapetala

Belvala dodecapetala, commonly known as the twelve-petaled beauty, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multi-colored flowers that typically bloom in clusters of twelve petals, giving it its scientific name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, Belvala dodecapetala is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
