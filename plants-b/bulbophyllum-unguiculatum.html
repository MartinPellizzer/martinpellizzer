Bulbophyllum unguiculatum

Bulbophyllum unguiculatum, commonly known as the clawed bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring deep purple and yellow hues. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
