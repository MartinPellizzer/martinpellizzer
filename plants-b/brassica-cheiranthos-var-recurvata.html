Brassica cheiranthos var. recurvata

Brassica cheiranthos var. recurvata, commonly known as the curly leaf mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive curled or crinkled appearance, which gives the plant its common name. Small yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
