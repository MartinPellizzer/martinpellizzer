Brachystelmaria occidentalis

Brachystelmaria occidentalis, commonly known as the western dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
