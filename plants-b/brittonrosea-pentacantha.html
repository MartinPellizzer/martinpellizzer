Brittonrosea pentacantha

Brittonrosea pentacantha, commonly known as the five-armed Britton rose.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
