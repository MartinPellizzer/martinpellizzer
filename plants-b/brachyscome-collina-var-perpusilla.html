Brachyscome collina var. perpusilla

Brachyscome collina var. perpusilla, commonly known as the "Blue Mistflower," is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species with silvery-blue foliage and small, daisy-like white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety perpusilla is distinguished by its compact growth habit and more refined floral display compared to the typical form of B. collina.
