Bauhinia coclensis

Bauhinia coclensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble the feathers of a peacock. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight purple tinge. Bauhinia coclensis is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
