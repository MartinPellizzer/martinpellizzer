Begonia insularis

Begonia insularis, commonly known as the island begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia insularis is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
