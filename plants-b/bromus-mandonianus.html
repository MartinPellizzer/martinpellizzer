Bromus mandonianus

Bromus mandonianus, commonly known as the Mandon's brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Bromus mandonianus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
