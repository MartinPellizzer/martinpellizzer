Blepharandra fimbriata

Blepharandra fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed margin. The plant produces inflorescences that resemble a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are usually small and green, with a spathe that can vary in color.

Blepharandra fimbriata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
