Bienertia przewalskii

Bienertia przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-resistant.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats, contributing to the stabilization of dunes and preventing soil erosion.
