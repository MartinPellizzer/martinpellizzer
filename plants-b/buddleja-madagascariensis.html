Buddleja madagascariensis

Buddleja madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Madagascar.

It features slender, woody stems and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buddleja madagascariensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
