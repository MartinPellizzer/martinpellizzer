Bowdichia martiusii

Bowdichia martiusii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bowdichia martiusii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
