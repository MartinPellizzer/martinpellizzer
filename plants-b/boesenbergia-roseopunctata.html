Boesenbergia roseopunctata

Boesenbergia roseopunctata, commonly known as the red-rooted ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant bears small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique appearance.
