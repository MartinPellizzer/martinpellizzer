Boswellia occidentalis

Boswellia occidentalis, commonly known as western frankincense, is a small evergreen tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is tapped from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.
