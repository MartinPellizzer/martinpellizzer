Boehmeria miqueliana var. platanifolia

Boehmeria miqueliana var. platanifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fibers of its stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
