Bassia gardneri

Bassia gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's basin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Bassia gardneri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
