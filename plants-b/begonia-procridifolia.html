Begonia procridifolia

Begonia procridifolia, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
