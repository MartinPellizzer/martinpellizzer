Berlinia chevalieri

Berlinia chevalieri, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened, dark brown seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in local ecosystems.
