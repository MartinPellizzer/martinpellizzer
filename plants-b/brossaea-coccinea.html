Brossaea coccinea

Brossaea coccinea, commonly known as the red brossaea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range.

Brossaea coccinea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
