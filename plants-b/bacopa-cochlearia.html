Bacopa cochlearia

Bacopa cochlearia, commonly known as the lesser water hyssop, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Bacopa cochlearia is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cognitive and calming properties.
