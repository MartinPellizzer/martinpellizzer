Bidens involucrata var. retrorsa

Bidens involucrata var. retrorsa, commonly known as the blackleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
