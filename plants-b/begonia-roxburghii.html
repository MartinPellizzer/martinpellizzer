Begonia roxburghii

Begonia roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan begonia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
