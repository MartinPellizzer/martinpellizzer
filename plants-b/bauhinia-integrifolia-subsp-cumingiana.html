Bauhinia integrifolia subsp. cumingiana

Bauhinia integrifolia subsp.

cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
