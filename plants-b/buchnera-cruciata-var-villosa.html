Buchnera cruciata var. villosa

Buchnera cruciata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant has hairy foliage, giving it its varietal name "villosa," and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and foothills.

Buchnera cruciata var. villosa is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
