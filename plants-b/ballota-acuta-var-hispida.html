Ballota acuta var. hispida

Ballota acuta var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features dense, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which attract various pollinators.
