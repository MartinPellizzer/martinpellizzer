Bernardia fruticulosa

Bernardia fruticulosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are usually red or orange, which bloom in the spring. These flowers are clustered in racemes and are known for their vivid color and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant leaves and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
