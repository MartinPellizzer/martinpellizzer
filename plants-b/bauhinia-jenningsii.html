Bauhinia jenningsii

Bauhinia jenningsii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
