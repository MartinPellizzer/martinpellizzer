Beta vulgaris subsp. esculenta

Beta vulgaris subsp.

esculenta, commonly known as sugarbeet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose.

The plant has a rosette of large, lobed leaves that are typically green with a hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The primary economic value of sugarbeet lies in its ability to produce sugar through industrial processing.
