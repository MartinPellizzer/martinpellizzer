Bupleurum falcatum f. kotschyanum

Bupleurum falcatum f. kotschyanum, commonly known as the Kotschyan's goat's beard, is a botanical variant of the goat's beard plant.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features long, slender leaves that are often described as resembling a goat's beard, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
