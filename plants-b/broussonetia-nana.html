Broussonetia nana

Broussonetia nana, commonly known as dwarf Chinese wisteria, is a small shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Broussonetia nana is also noted for its adaptability to different climates and its role in traditional medicine.
