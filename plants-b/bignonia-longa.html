Bignonia longa

Bignonia longa, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in the spring and early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Bignonia longa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a landscape vine.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
