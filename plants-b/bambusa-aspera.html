Bambusa aspera

Bambusa aspera, commonly known as the rough bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, clump-forming grass that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has stiff, ridged culms that are often used in construction and crafts. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture that gives it its common name.

Bambusa aspera is valued for its durability and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
