Bromus pseudoramosus

Bromus pseudoramosus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually terminal and elongated.

Bromus pseudoramosus is characterized by its small, spikelet-like flowers and is often found in association with other grassland species.
