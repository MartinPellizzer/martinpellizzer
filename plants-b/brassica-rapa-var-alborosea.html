Brassica rapa var. alborosea

Brassica rapa var. alborosea, commonly known as the white mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight white or yellowish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are encased in a triangular, hairy capsule, and the plant is known for its rapid growth cycle.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
