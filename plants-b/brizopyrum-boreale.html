Brizopyrum boreale

Brizopyrum boreale, commonly known as the northern feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Brizopyrum boreale is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
