Begonia angulata

Begonia angulata, commonly known as the angular begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, angular leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in horticulture.
