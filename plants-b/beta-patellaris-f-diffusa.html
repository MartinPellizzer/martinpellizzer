Beta patellaris f. diffusa

Beta patellaris f. diffusa, commonly known as the hairy beets or wild beet, is a variant of the beetroot plant characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the roots are less commonly consumed.

This plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in traditional herbal practices.
