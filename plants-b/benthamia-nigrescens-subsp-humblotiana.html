Benthamia nigrescens subsp. humblotiana

Benthamia nigrescens subsp.

humblotiana, commonly known as the darkening benthamia, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
