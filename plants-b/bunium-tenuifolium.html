Bunium tenuifolium

Bunium tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their carminative properties.

The entire plant has a distinctive anise-like fragrance due to the presence of volatile oils.
