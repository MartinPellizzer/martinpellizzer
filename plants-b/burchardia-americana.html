Burchardia americana

Burchardia americana, commonly known as the American burrweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that produce small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Burchardia americana is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is known for its medicinal properties.
