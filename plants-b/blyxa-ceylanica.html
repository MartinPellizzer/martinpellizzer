Blyxa ceylanica

Blyxa ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help anchor it in the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Blyxa ceylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
