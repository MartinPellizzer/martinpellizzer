Berzelia callunoides

Berzelia callunoides, commonly known as the bog heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wet, acidic environments of northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heathers and related plants. The plant has small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Berzelia callunoides thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in sphagnum moss-dominated bogs and heathlands.
