Baphia gabonensis

Baphia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon baphia, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical forests and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.
