Boehmeria macrophylla var. canescens

Boehmeria macrophylla var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy giant mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fuzzy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its fibrous bark and leaves have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
