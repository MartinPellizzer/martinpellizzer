Begonia subhumilis

Begonia subhumilis, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
