Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. mucronatum

Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate little barley, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a useful species for ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics.
