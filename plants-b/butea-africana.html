Butea africana

Butea africana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a thick, buttressed trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
