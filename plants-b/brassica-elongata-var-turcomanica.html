Brassica elongata var. turcomanica

Brassica elongata var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
