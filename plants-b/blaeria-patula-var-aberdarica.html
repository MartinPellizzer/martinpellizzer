Blaeria patula var. aberdarica

Blaeria patula var. aberdarica, commonly known as the Aberdaric Blaeria, is a rare variant of the Blaeria patula species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
