Buckleya angulosa

Buckleya angulosa, commonly known as the angular buckleya, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, red berries. Buckleya angulosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
