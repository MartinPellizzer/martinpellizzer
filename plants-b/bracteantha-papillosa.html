Bracteantha papillosa

Bracteantha papillosa, commonly known as the silky bracteantha, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically a vivid shade of red or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bracteantha papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
