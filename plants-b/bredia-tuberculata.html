Bredia tuberculata

Bredia tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tuberculate (knobbed) stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Bredia tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
