Benzoin micrantodaphne

Benzoin micrantodaphne, commonly known as the benzoin tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant produces aromatic resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in incense and perfumes. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a waxy texture.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
