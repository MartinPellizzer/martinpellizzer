Boehmeria virgata var. velutina

Boehmeria virgata var. velutina, commonly known as silky flax or velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems and leaves make it a valuable resource for traditional textile and paper-making practices.
