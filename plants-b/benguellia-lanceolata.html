Benguellia lanceolata

Benguellia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Benguellia lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
