Bromus racemosus var. submuticus

Bromus racemosus var. submuticus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually hairy, especially along the edges, giving it its common name.

This variety is often found in temperate climates and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
