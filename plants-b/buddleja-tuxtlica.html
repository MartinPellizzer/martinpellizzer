Buddleja tuxtlica

Buddleja tuxtlica, commonly known as Mexican butterfly bush, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Buddleja tuxtlica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
