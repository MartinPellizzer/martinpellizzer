Briza violascens

Briza violascens, commonly known as the violet false beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a violet hue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, delicate spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Briza violascens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
