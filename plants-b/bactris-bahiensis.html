Bactris bahiensis

Bactris bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
