Bursera macgregorii

Bursera macgregorii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree produces small, white to yellowish flowers and has compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and sap contain volatile oils, which have been historically used for incense and medicinal purposes.

The plant is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
