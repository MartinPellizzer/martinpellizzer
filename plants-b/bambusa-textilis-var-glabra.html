Bambusa textilis var. glabra

Bambusa textilis var. glabra, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, smooth culms that are often covered in a silvery-white sheen, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
