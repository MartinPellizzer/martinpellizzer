Bocagea latifolia

Bocagea latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, white to pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Bocagea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
