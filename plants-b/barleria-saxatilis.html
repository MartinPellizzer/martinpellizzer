Barleria saxatilis

Barleria saxatilis, commonly known as the rock barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
