Banksia plumosa subsp. denticulata

Banksia plumosa subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the serrated banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, yellow to orange cylindrical inflorescences that appear in late summer.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight serration along the edges, giving the subspecies its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
