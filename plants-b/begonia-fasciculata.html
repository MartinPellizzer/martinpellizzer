Begonia fasciculata

Begonia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Begonia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor and outdoor settings.
