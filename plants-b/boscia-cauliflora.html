Boscia cauliflora

Boscia cauliflora, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, and it is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Boscia cauliflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
