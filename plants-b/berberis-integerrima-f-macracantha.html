Berberis integerrima f. macracantha

Berberis integerrima f. macracantha, commonly known as the giant barberry, is a vigorous shrub characterized by its large, spiny leaves and bright yellow flowers.

It is native to the Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Its dense growth habit makes it effective for erosion control and as a hedge.

The variety 'macracantha' is distinguished by its larger leaves and more robust structure compared to the typical form.
