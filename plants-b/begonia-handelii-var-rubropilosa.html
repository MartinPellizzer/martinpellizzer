Begonia handelii var. rubropilosa

Begonia handelii var. rubropilosa, commonly known as the red-haired begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, hairy stems that give it its common name, along with its showy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
