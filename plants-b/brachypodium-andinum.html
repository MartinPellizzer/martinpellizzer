Brachypodium andinum

Brachypodium andinum, commonly known as the Andean false brome, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. This plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Brachypodium andinum is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies related to plant adaptation and ecological resilience.
