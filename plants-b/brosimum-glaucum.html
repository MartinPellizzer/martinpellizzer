Brosimum glaucum

Brosimum glaucum, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. The tree produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that give way to edible, black berries. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Brosimum glaucum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types and climates.
