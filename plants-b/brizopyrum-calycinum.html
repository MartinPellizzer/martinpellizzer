Brizopyrum calycinum

Brizopyrum calycinum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet with small, blue-green flowers.

Brizopyrum calycinum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in maintaining ecological balance in native habitats.
