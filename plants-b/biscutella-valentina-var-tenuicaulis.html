Biscutella valentina var. tenuicaulis

Biscutella valentina var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed valentine biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
