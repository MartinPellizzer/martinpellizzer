Barbarea praecox var. longisiliqua

Barbarea praecox var. longisiliqua, commonly known as the early winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and leafy stems that often grow in moist, calcareous soils. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing small, rounded pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
