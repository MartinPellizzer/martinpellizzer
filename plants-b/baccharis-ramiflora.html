Baccharis ramiflora

Baccharis ramiflora, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Baccharis ramiflora is also valued for its use in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
