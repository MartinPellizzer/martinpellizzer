Bupleurum aureum subsp. porfirii

Bupleurum aureum subsp.

porfirii, commonly known as golden bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
