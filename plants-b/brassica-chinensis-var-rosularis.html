Brassica chinensis var. rosularis

Brassica chinensis var. rosularis, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or Chinese broccoli, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and edible stems and flowers. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is cultivated for its nutritious leaves and flowers. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate regions.

Its botanical features include small white or purple flowers arranged in racemes.
