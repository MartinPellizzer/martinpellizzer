Bromus mollis var. leptostachyus

Bromus mollis var. leptostachyus, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
