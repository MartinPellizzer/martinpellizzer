Brodiaea ixioides var. lugens

Brodiaea ixioides var. lugens, commonly known as the Sierra Valley brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and meadows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
