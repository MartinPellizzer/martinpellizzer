Bauhinia parviflora

Bauhinia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia parviflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
