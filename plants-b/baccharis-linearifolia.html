Baccharis linearifolia

Baccharis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its linear, often gray-green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis linearifolia is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
