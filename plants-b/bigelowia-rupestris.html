Bigelowia rupestris

Bigelowia rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Bigelowia rupestris is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
