Begonia glaberrima

Begonia glaberrima, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
