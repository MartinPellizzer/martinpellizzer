Bidens microcephala

Bidens microcephala, commonly known as littleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
