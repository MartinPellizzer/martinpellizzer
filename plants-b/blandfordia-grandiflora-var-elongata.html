Blandfordia grandiflora var. elongata

Blandfordia grandiflora var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed Christmas lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Hyacinthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that becomes more prominent as the bloom matures. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic winter blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal regions.
