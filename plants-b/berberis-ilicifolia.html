Berberis ilicifolia

Berberis ilicifolia, commonly known as the evergreen barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
