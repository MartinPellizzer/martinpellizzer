Bromus catharticus var. elatus

Bromus catharticus var. elatus, commonly known as the tall smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields.

Its fibrous root system makes it a valuable forage plant for livestock.
