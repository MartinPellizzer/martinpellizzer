Beckmannia syzigachne subsp. baicalensis

Beckmannia syzigachne subsp.

baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical Beckmannia, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
