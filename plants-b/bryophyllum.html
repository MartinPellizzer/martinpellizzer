Bryophyllum

Bryophyllum, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a type of succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Bryophyllum is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
