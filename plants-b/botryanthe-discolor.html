Botryanthe discolor

Botryanthe discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Botryanthe discolor is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
