Blechnopsis latifolia

Blechnopsis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in lines along the veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Blechnopsis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
