Bombycilaena erecta

Bombycilaena erecta, commonly known as the upright silk grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, producing a delicate, feathery appearance.

Bombycilaena erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful form and resilience.
