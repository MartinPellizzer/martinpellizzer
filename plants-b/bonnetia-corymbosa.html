Bonnetia corymbosa

Bonnetia corymbosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
