Begonia clivalis

Begonia clivalis, commonly known as the cliff begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or cliffside habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance and resilience.
