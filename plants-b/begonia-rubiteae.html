Begonia rubiteae

Begonia rubiteae, commonly known as the Red-veined Begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a deep red or maroon coloration with prominent green veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in garden settings.
