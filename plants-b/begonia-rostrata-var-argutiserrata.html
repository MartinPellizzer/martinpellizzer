Begonia rostrata var. argutiserrata

Begonia rostrata var. argutiserrata, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a favorite among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
