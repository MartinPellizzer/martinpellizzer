Begonia imperialis var. smaragdina

Begonia imperialis var. smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant, waxy leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy sheen, often marked by intricate patterns or veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners seeking unique foliage plants.
