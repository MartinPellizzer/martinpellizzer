Bignonia martiusiana

Bignonia martiusiana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a vigorous climber.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
