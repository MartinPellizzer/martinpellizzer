Baccharis breviseta var. colombianum

Baccharis breviseta var. colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky slopes. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
