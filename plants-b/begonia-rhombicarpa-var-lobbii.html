Begonia rhombicarpa var. lobbii

Begonia rhombicarpa var. lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually dark green with a glossy texture, and the flowers are usually pink or red.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
