Berberis kansuensis var. procera

Berberis kansuensis var. procera, commonly known as the tall Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the tallest varieties in the Berberis genus. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its bright yellow flowers appear in clusters during late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
