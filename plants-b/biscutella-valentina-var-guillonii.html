Biscutella valentina var. guillonii

Biscutella valentina var. guillonii, commonly known as the Guillon's biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

The flowers, which are pale yellow to cream, bloom in early spring and are followed by elongated, slender seed pods.
