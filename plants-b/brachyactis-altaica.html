Brachyactis altaica

Brachyactis altaica, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Brachyactis altaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
