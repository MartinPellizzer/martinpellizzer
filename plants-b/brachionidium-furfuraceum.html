Brachionidium furfuraceum

Brachionidium furfuraceum, commonly known as the woolly brachionidium, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Brachionidium furfuraceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and resilience.
