Berberis aurahuacensis

Berberis aurahuacensis, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
