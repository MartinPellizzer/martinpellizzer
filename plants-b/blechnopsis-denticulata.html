Blechnopsis denticulata

Blechnopsis denticulata, commonly known as the toothed holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnopsis denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
