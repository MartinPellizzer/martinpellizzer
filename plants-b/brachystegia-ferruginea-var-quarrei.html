Brachystegia ferruginea var. quarrei

Brachystegia ferruginea var. quarrei, commonly known as the red-stemmed brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the miombo woodlands of eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and dense, dark green foliage that turns reddish in the dry season. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in well-drained soils. It produces small yellow flowers followed by winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
