Beta noeana

Beta noeana, commonly known as the white goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, heart-shaped fruits. Beta noeana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is a perennial species that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
