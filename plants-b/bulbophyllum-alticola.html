Bulbophyllum alticola

Bulbophyllum alticola, commonly known as the highland orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its often harsh, high-altitude environment. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant hues to the forest understory.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
