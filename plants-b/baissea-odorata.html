Baissea odorata

Baissea odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while the roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its pleasant aroma.
