Bupleurum australe var. patens

Bupleurum australe var. patens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local insect populations.
