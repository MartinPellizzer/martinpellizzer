Begonia faureana var. metallica

Begonia faureana var. metallica, commonly known as the silver-leaved begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green foliage that reflects light, giving it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to rhombic in shape and often have a velvety texture. This variety produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the warmer months.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
