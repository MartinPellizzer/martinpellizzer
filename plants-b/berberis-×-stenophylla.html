Berberis × stenophylla

Berberis × stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Berberis darwinii and Berberis empetrifolia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny branches, which are typical features of the Berberis genus. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often considered bitter.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
