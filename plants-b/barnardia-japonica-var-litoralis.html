Barnardia japonica var. litoralis

Barnardia japonica var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal barnardia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often found in maritime environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
