Brachypodium miserum

Brachypodium miserum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a small annual grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
