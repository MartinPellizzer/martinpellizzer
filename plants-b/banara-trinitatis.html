Banara trinitatis

Banara trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a conservation concern in its native habitat.
