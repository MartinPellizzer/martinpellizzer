Bactris hirta var. spruceana

Bactris hirta var. spruceana, commonly known as the spruce's palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and of interest in horticultural cultivation.
