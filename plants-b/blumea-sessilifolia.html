Blumea sessilifolia

Blumea sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually sessile, meaning they lack stalks, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Blumea sessilifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
