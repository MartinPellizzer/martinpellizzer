Banisteriopsis discolor

Banisteriopsis discolor, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and green variegated leaves, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition from white to pink as they age. Its fruits are edible berries that turn from green to black when mature.

Banisteriopsis discolor is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
