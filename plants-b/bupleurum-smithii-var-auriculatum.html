Bupleurum smithii var. auriculatum

Bupleurum smithii var. auriculatum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
