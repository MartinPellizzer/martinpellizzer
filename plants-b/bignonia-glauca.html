Bignonia glauca

Bignonia glauca, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bignonia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
