Bupleurum nodiflorum

Bupleurum nodiflorum, commonly known as the nodding bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its roots are often fibrous and branched. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Bupleurum nodiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
