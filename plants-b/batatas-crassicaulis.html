Batatas crassicaulis

Batatas crassicaulis, commonly known as the giant sweet potato or elephant tuber, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its large, edible, starchy tubers that can weigh several kilograms. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been traditionally used as a food source in various regions.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
