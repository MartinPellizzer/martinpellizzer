Banksia conferta subsp. penicillata

Banksia conferta subsp.

penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
