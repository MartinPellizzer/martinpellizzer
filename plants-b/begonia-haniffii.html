Begonia haniffii

Begonia haniffii, commonly known as the Haniff's begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
