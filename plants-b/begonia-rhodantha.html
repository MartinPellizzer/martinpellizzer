Begonia rhodantha

Begonia rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with contrasting green or yellow bracts. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from both its striking flowers and foliage, making it a sought-after addition to gardens and collections.
