Brassica oleracea var. pentovillea

Brassica oleracea var. pentovillea, commonly known as the Purple Sprouting Broccoli, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its dense, violet-colored flower heads.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thick, fleshy stalks and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces its edible flower buds in early spring, which are rich in vitamins and minerals. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is notable for its unique coloration and nutritional value, making it a popular choice in gourmet and health-conscious diets.
