Brassica villosa

Brassica villosa, commonly known as woolly cabbage, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by round, green pods containing several seeds. Brassica villosa is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
