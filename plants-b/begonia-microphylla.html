Begonia microphylla

Begonia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or trailing plant, making it suitable for container gardening. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns.

Begonia microphylla thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
