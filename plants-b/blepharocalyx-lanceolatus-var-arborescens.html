Blepharocalyx lanceolatus var. arborescens

Blepharocalyx lanceolatus var. arborescens, commonly known as the silverleaf mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a silvery wax, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
