Byrsonima crassifolia var. cinerea

Byrsonima crassifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as the cinerea woodstar, is a shrub native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically grayish-white in color, providing a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
