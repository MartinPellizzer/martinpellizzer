Bignonia regalis

Bignonia regalis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
