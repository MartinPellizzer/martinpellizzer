Brachionidium galeatum

Brachionidium galeatum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
