Brachycladium villosum

Brachycladium villosum, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hairy structures, which give it its common name. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, typical of many grass species.

Brachycladium villosum is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
