Bolbodium procumbens

Bolbodium procumbens, commonly known as the creeping bulbous plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bolbodium procumbens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
