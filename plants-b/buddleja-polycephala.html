Buddleja polycephala

Buddleja polycephala, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract a variety of pollinators. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly serrated margin. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing long, racemose inflorescences.

Buddleja polycephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
