Berberis validisepala

Berberis validisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. Its thorny stems and compact form make it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Berberis validisepala is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
