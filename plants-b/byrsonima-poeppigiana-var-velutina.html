Byrsonima poeppigiana var. velutina

Byrsonima poeppigiana var. velutina, commonly known as velvet soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are alternate, elliptic in shape, and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, spiny fruits.

Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
