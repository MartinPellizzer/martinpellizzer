Begonia peponifolia

Begonia peponifolia, commonly known as the melon begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Begonia peponifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique appearance.
