Bignonia xanthinum

Bignonia xanthinum, commonly known as the yellow trumpetvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological value in local habitats.
