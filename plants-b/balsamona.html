Balsamona

Balsamona, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, adding a touch of color during the colder months.

Balsamona thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
