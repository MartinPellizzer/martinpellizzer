Balsamita major subsp. balsamitoides

Balsamita major subsp.

balsamitoides, commonly known as Mexican balsamweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
