Bassovia tomentosa

Bassovia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly bassovia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
