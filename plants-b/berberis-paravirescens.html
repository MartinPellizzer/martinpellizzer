Berberis paravirescens

Berberis paravirescens, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
