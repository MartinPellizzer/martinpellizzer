Bupleurum himalayense

Bupleurum himalayense, commonly known as Himalayan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing bioactive compounds with potential health benefits.

Bupleurum himalayense thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
