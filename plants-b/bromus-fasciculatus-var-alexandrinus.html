Bromus fasciculatus var. alexandrinus

Bromus fasciculatus var. alexandrinus, commonly known as Alexandrian brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are usually yellow-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
