Brickellia pinifolia

Brickellia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and scrubland habitats. Its small, yellowish to reddish flowers bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Brickellia pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its hardy nature.
