Bambusa tsangii

Bambusa tsangii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Bambusa tsangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its elegant appearance.
