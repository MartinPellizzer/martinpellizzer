Bactris xanthocarpa

Bactris xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its clustered growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
