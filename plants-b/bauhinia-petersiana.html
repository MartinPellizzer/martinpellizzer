Bauhinia petersiana

Bauhinia petersiana, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation projects.
