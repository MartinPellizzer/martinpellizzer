Bupleurum euphorbioides var. alpestre

Bupleurum euphorbioides var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive serrated edge. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in challenging climates.
