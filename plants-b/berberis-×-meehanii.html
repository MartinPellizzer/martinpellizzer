Berberis × meehanii

Berberis × meehanii, commonly known as the Meehan barberry, is a hybrid shrub resulting from the cross between Berberis darwinii and Berberis julianae.

It is native to the Andes region of South America, specifically in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant features dense, thorny stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year. Its yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and vibrant foliage.
