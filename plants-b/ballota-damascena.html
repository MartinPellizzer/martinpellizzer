Ballota damascena

Ballota damascena, commonly known as Turkish clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Ballota damascena is also valued as a forage plant for livestock.
