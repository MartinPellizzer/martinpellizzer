Baccharis odorata var. latifolia

Baccharis odorata var. latifolia, commonly known as sweet baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
