Braya lycopodioides var. contracta

Braya lycopodioides var. contracta, commonly known as the compact braya, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are tightly clustered, giving it a dense, cushion-like appearance. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees.

This species is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils and crevices.
