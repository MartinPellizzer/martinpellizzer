Baccharis palustris

Baccharis palustris, commonly known as marsh baccharis, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Baccharis palustris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
