Bubroma crinitum

Bubroma crinitum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
