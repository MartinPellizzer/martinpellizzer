Bulbophyllum ornithorhynchum

Bulbophyllum ornithorhynchum, commonly known as the duckbill orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive duck-billed appearance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
