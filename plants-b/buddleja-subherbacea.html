Buddleja subherbacea

Buddleja subherbacea, commonly known as the herbaceous buddleja, is a flowering plant native to central and southern China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in summer.

Buddleja subherbacea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
