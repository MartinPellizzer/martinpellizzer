Berberis montevidensis

Berberis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
