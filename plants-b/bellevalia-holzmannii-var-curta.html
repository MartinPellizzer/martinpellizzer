Bellevalia holzmannii var. curta

Bellevalia holzmannii var. curta, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
