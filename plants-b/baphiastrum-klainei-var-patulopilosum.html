Baphiastrum klainei var. patulopilosum

Baphiastrum klainei var. patulopilosum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
