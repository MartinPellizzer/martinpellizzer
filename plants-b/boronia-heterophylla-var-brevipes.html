Boronia heterophylla var. brevipes

Boronia heterophylla var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, pink to mauve flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
