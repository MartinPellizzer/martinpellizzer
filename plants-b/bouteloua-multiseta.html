Bouteloua multiseta

Bouteloua multiseta, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or dandelion fluff. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

Bouteloua multiseta is an important component of native grasslands and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
