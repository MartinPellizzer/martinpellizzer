Backhousia floribunda

Backhousia floribunda, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

Backhousia floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
