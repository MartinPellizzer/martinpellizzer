Berchemia corollata

Berchemia corollata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
