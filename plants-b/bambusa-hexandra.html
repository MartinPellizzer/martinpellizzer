Bambusa hexandra

Bambusa hexandra, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its hollow, jointed stems are used in construction and crafts, while its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable resource for sustainable materials.

Bambusa hexandra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
