Berlinia magnistipulata

Berlinia magnistipulata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and large, compound leaves with stout, sessile stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and tool-making.
