Bambusa deformis

Bambusa deformis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, sturdy culms that rarely exceed one meter in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a clump-forming bamboo that does not spread aggressively, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

Bambusa deformis is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban conditions.
