Begonia panchtharensis

Begonia panchtharensis, commonly known as the Panchtharensis begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens or indoor spaces.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often grown in controlled environments.
