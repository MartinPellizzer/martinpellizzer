Bulbophyllum densibulbum

Bulbophyllum densibulbum, commonly known as the dense-bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its tropical and subtropical habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
