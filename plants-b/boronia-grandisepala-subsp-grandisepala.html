Boronia grandisepala subsp. grandisepala

Boronia grandisepala subsp.

grandisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color.

The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments. Boronia grandisepala subsp.

grandisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
