Bumelia lanuginosa subsp. typica

Bumelia lanuginosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the wooly bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
