Biota

Biota, commonly known as the collective term for all living organisms in a particular area, encompasses a diverse array of plant and animal life.

In botanical contexts, biota refers to the variety of plant species that inhabit a specific ecosystem. These plants play crucial roles in maintaining ecological balance through processes like photosynthesis and nutrient cycling. The study of biota helps scientists understand biodiversity and environmental health.

Botanical research on biota often focuses on species distribution, adaptation, and conservation efforts.
