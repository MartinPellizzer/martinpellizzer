Bulbophyllum pokapindjangense

Bulbophyllum pokapindjangense, commonly known as the Papuan ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with intricate patterns resembling ghostly figures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
