Betula × pallescens

Betula × pallescens, commonly known as the silver birch.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white birch (Betula pendula) and the silver birch (Betula pendula var. argyrophylla). This tree is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silver hue beneath. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish catkins and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
