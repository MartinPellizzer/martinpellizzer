Baeckea pentandra

Baeckea pentandra, commonly known as the coastal baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a woody stem with a distinctive flaky bark.

Baeckea pentandra plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native insects and birds.
