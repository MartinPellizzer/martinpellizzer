Bauhinia alba

Bauhinia alba, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Bauhinia alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
