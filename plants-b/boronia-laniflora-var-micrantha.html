Boronia laniflora var. micrantha

Boronia laniflora var. micrantha, commonly known as the fine-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
