Boronia pauciflora

Boronia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, and the species is noted for its fragrant scent.

Boronia pauciflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to local pollinators.
