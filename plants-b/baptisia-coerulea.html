Baptisia coerulea

Baptisia coerulea, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Baptisia coerulea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
