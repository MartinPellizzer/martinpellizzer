Bauhinia bonii

Bauhinia bonii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in bright orange-red during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bauhinia bonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
