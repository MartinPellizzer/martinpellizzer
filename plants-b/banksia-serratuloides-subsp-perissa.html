Banksia serratuloides subsp. perissa

Banksia serratuloides subsp.

perissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
