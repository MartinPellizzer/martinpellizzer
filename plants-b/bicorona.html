Bicorona

Bicorona, commonly known as the double crown, is a rare and striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct corona layers that give it its name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Bicorona is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
