Boronia cremnophila

Boronia cremnophila, commonly known as the coastal boronia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

Boronia cremnophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
