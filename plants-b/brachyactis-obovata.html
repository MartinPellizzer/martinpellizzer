Brachyactis obovata

Brachyactis obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate brachyactis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
