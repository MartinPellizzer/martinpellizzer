Barbarea vulgaris subsp. sylvestris

Barbarea vulgaris subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as wild winter cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer plant in ecological succession.
