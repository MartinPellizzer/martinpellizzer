Baissea baillonii

Baissea baillonii, commonly known as the Bailloni's flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Baissea baillonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
