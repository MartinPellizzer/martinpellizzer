Blechnum lehmannii

Blechnum lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically lance-shaped. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the fronds arising from a central stem. Its leaves are usually dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
