Begonia fuchsioides f. miniata

Begonia fuchsioides f. miniata, commonly known as the miniature begonia, is a compact, trailing plant characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant red flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features striking, fuchsia-pink bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is popular in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its trailing growth habit and colorful blooms.
