Buddleja floribunda

Buddleja floribunda, commonly known as the summer lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, round, black fruits that are edible for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
