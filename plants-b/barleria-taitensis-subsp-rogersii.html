Barleria taitensis subsp. rogersii

Barleria taitensis subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the Taiti barleria, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Tahiti. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
