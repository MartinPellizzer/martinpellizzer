Bidens coccinea

Bidens coccinea, commonly known as redstem ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Bidens coccinea produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
