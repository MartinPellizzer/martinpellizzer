Bupleurum laxum

Bupleurum laxum, commonly known as the slender bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Bupleurum laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
