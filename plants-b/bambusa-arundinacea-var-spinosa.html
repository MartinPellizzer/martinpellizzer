Bambusa arundinacea var. spinosa

Bambusa arundinacea var. spinosa, commonly known as the thorned bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thick-walled culms that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and durability, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical purposes.
