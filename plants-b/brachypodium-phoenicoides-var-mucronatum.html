Brachypodium phoenicoides var. mucronatum

Brachypodium phoenicoides var. mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
