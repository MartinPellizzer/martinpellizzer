Benstonea poronaliva

Benstonea poronaliva, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Benstonea poronaliva is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
