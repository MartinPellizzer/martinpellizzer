Bothriochloa parviflora f. villosula

Bothriochloa parviflora f. villosula, commonly known as the hairy small-flowered grass, is a variant of the species Bothriochloa parviflora.

It is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and slender, erect stems. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

This grass is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.
