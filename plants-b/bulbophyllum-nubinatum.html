Bulbophyllum nubinatum

Bulbophyllum nubinatum, commonly known as the Nubian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure, featuring brightly colored petals and a distinct lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a striking combination of hues that attract pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and threatened species in its native region.
