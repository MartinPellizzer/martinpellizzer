Brassica besseriana

Brassica besseriana, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect stem and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are short pods containing several seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Brassica besseriana is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in local cuisines.
