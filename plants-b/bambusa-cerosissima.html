Bambusa cerosissima

Bambusa cerosissima, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a V-shaped pattern along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa cerosissima is also valued for its strong and lightweight bamboo, which can be used in construction and crafts.
