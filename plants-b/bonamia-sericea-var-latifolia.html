Bonamia sericea var. latifolia

Bonamia sericea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bonamia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
