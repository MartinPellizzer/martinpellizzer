Brassaia blancoi

Brassaia blancoi, commonly known as the white-stemmed bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Brassaia blancoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
