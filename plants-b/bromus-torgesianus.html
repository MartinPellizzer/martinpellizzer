Bromus torgesianus

Bromus torgesianus, commonly known as the Torgese grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Torgese in the Mediterranean Sea.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus torgesianus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
