Blachia poilanei

Blachia poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Blachia poilanei is considered endangered, highlighting the need for protective measures.
