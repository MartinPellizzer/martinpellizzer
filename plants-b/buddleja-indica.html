Buddleja indica

Buddleja indica, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain seeds dispersed by wind. Buddleja indica is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
