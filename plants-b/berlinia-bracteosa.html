Berlinia bracteosa

Berlinia bracteosa, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, wooded savannas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
