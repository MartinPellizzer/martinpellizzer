Bambusa glaucescens f. albovariegata

Bambusa glaucescens f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species characterized by its striking white and green variegation on its leaves.

This plant is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with slender, smooth culms that have a pale blue-gray sheen. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes and indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for decorative purposes in both tropical and temperate climates.
