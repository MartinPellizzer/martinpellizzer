Bastardia berlandieri

Bastardia berlandieri, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, green, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Bastardia berlandieri is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
