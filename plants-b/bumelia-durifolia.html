Bumelia durifolia

Bumelia durifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, elliptical leaves with rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Bumelia durifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
