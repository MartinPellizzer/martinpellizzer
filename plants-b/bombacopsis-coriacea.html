Bombacopsis coriacea

Bombacopsis coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in various regions for its beauty and ecological benefits.
