Bromelia laevis

Bromelia laevis, commonly known as the smooth bromeliad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The leaves are typically broad, fleshy, and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
