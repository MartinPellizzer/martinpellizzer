Belangera glabra var. intermedia

Belangera glabra var. intermedia, commonly known as the smooth bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack species native to coastal areas of the North Atlantic.

It is a marine plant belonging to the family Fucaceae, characterized by its ribbon-like, flattened thallus that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a structure composed of a central axis with branching holdfasts for anchoring. It thrives in intertidal zones and is often found attached to rocks or other substrates in shallow waters.

Its smooth, leathery surface and ability to tolerate fluctuating salinity and wave action make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in coastal ecosystems.
