Bergenia thysanodes

Bergenia thysanodes, commonly known as the woolly bergenia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Bergenia thysanodes is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in cold climates.
