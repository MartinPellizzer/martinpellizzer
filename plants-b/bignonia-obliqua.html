Bignonia obliqua

Bignonia obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Bignonia obliqua is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
