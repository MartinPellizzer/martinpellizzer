Bonnaya cyanea

Bonnaya cyanea, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Bonnaya cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
