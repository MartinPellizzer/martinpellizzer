Begonia dewildei

Begonia dewildei, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
