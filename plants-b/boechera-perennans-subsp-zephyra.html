Boechera perennans subsp. zephyra

Boechera perennans subsp.

zephyra, commonly known as the western rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
