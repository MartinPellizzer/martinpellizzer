Bromus erectus var. scaber

Bromus erectus var. scaber, commonly known as rough brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
