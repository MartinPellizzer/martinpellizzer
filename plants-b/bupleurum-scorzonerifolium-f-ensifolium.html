Bupleurum scorzonerifolium f. ensifolium

Bupleurum scorzonerifolium f. ensifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
