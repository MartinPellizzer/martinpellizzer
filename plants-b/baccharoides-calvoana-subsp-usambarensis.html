Baccharoides calvoana subsp. usambarensis

Baccharoides calvoana subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis baccharoid, is a shrub native to the Eastern African highlands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract various pollinators.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
