Bouteloua hirticulmis

Bouteloua hirticulmis, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Hairy bluestem is an important component of native grasslands and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
