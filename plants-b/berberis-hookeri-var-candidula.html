Berberis hookeri var. candidula

Berberis hookeri var. candidula, commonly known as the white-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
