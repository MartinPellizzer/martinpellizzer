Bromus commutatus subsp. neglectus

Bromus commutatus subsp.

neglectus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to different environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
