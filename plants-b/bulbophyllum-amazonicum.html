Bulbophyllum amazonicum

Bulbophyllum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with periods of drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
