Betula papyrifera subsp. neoalaskana

Betula papyrifera subsp.

neoalaskana, commonly known as the white birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips.

The tree has slender, often pendulous branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
