Bupleurum khasianum

Bupleurum khasianum, commonly known as Khasi angelica, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbel clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
