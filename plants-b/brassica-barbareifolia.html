Brassica barbareifolia

Brassica barbareifolia, commonly known as the alpine cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and mustard-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, high-altitude climates. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Brassica barbareifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
