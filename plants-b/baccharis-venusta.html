Baccharis venusta

Baccharis venusta, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and erosion control in desert landscapes.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their purported healing properties.
