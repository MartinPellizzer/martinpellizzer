Brassica baetica

Brassica baetica, commonly known as African mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Brassica baetica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
