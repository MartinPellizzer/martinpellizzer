Blechnum faberi

Blechnum faberi, commonly known as the Chinese shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds, which are typically dark green and leathery. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its fronds.

Blechnum faberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
