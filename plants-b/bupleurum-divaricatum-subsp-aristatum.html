Bupleurum divaricatum subsp. aristatum

Bupleurum divaricatum subsp.

aristatum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
