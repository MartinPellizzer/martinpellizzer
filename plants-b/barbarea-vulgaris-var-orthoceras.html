Barbarea vulgaris var. orthoceras

Barbarea vulgaris var. orthoceras, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in racemes. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in cool climates and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
