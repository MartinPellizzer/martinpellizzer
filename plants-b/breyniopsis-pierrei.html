Breyniopsis pierrei

Breyniopsis pierrei, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Breyniopsis pierrei is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
