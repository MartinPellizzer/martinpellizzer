Begonia brasiliensis

Begonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that can be green, red, or purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming during the rainy season. It prefers shady, humid environments and thrives in well-draining, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
