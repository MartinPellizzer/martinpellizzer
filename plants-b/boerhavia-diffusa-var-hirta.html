Boerhavia diffusa var. hirta

Boerhavia diffusa var. hirta, commonly known as Indian spinach or Purslane, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and stems that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
