Borinda pleniculmis

Borinda pleniculmis, commonly known as the lace-leaf borinda, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely lobed leaves that give it a lace-like appearance, making it a visually appealing species for botanical gardens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow in color.

Borinda pleniculmis thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
