Brachiaria dura

Brachiaria dura, commonly known as blackgrass or durra, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust growth and dense foliage, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces spikelets that are often arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Brachiaria dura is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control.
