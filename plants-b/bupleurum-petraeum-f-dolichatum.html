Bupleurum petraeum f. dolichatum

Bupleurum petraeum f. dolichatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed rock angelica, is a variant of the rock angelica species native to rocky and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in umbel-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high sunlight exposure.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
