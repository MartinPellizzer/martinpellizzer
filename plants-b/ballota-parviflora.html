Ballota parviflora

Ballota parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Ballota parviflora is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
