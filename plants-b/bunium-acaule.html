Bunium acaule

Bunium acaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and finely divided, feathery leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Bunium acaule is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
