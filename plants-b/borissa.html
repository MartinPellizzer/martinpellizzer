Borissa

Borissa, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It has long, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Borissa is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.

This plant is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in other moist habitats.
