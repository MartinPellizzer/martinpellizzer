Baccharis czermakii var. genuina

Baccharis czermakii var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow seed heads.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
