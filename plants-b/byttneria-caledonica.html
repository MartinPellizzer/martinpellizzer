Byttneria caledonica

Byttneria caledonica, commonly known as the Cape York gum, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense rainforest vegetation.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
