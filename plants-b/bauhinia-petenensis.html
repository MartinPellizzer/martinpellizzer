Bauhinia petenensis

Bauhinia petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to produce flowers throughout the year.

Bauhinia petenensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
