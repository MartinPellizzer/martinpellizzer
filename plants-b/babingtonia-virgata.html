Babingtonia virgata

Babingtonia virgata, commonly known as the silver she-oak, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Babingtonia virgata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
