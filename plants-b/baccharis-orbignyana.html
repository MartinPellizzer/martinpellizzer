Baccharis orbignyana

Baccharis orbignyana, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal uses.

Baccharis orbignyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
