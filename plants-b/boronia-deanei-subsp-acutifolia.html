Boronia deanei subsp. acutifolia

Boronia deanei subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
