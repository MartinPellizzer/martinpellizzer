Bulbophyllum rigidipes

Bulbophyllum rigidipes, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The inflorescence consists of multiple small, showy flowers that bloom in a raceme, attracting various pollinators.
