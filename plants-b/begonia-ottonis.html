Begonia ottonis

Begonia ottonis, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a bold splash of color to gardens. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia ottonis is popular for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
