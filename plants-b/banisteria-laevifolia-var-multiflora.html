Banisteria laevifolia var. multiflora

Banisteria laevifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
