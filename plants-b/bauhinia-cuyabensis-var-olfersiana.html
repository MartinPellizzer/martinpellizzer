Bauhinia cuyabensis var. olfersiana

Bauhinia cuyabensis var. olfersiana, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features distinctive bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
