Bridelia tomentosa var. nayarii

Bridelia tomentosa var. nayarii, commonly known as the silky bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
