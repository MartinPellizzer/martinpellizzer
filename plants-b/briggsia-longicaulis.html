Briggsia longicaulis

Briggsia longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed bristle plant, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spiky leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that emerges from the center of the leaf rosette.

Briggsia longicaulis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
