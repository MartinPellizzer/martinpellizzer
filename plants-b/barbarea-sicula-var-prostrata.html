Barbarea sicula var. prostrata

Barbarea sicula var. prostrata, commonly known as Sicilian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
