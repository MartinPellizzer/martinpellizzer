Bursera pubescens

Bursera pubescens, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
