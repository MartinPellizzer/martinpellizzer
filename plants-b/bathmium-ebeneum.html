Bathmium ebeneum

Bathmium ebeneum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, velvety leaves that reflect sunlight, helping it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
