Bursa heegeri

Bursa heegeri, commonly known as the Heeger's burr, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky seed heads that resemble burrs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive seed pods. Bursa heegeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
