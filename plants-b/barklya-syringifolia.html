Barklya syringifolia

Barklya syringifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Barklya syringifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
