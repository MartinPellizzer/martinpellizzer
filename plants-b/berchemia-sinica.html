Berchemia sinica

Berchemia sinica, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

Berchemia sinica prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.
