Bromelia clandestina

Bromelia clandestina, commonly known as the hidden bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, often holding water. Its vibrant, long-lasting flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
