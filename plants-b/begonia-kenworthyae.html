Begonia kenworthyae

Begonia kenworthyae, commonly known as the Kenworthy's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and shades of green, red, and silver. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
