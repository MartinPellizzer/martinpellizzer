Banisteriopsis martiniana var. martiniana

Banisteriopsis martiniana var. martiniana, commonly known as the Amazonian tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also notable for its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native ecosystem.
