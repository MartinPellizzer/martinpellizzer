Betula alba var. soongorica

Betula alba var. soongorica, commonly known as the Soongor white birch, is a variant of the white birch native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of silver and yellow. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in autumn. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
