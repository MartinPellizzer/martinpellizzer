Blumea tetraptera

Blumea tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive four-winged structure when mature.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
