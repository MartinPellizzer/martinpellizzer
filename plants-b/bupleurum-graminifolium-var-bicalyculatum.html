Bupleurum graminifolium var. bicalyculatum

Bupleurum graminifolium var. bicalyculatum, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
