Bulbophyllum falcipetalum

Bulbophyllum falcipetalum, commonly known as the sickle-petaled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and adapted for storing water, which helps it survive in humid tropical environments.

The flowers are striking, with petals that often curve backward, giving the plant its common name.
