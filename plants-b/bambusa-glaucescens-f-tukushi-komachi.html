Bambusa glaucescens f. tukushi-komachi

Bambusa glaucescens f. tukushi-komachi, commonly known as the Tukushi-Komachi bamboo, is a decorative variety native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue sheathes and pale green culms, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with a slender, upright growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This bamboo is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is often used in bonsai and landscape design.
