Berginia

Berginia, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Berberidaceae.

These perennial herbs are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, particularly in mountainous areas. They typically have basal rosettes of leaves and produce small, yellow or white flowers in early spring. Berginia species are often found in rocky or alpine environments, where they thrive in well-drained soils.

Their foliage and flowers contribute to the ecological diversity of their habitats.
