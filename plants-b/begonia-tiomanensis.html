Begonia tiomanensis

Begonia tiomanensis, commonly known as the Tioman begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Malaysian island of Tioman.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is relatively rare and is considered a valuable ornamental plant in horticulture.
