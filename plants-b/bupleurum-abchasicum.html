Bupleurum abchasicum

Bupleurum abchasicum, commonly known as the Abchazian boldo, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic compounds. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Bupleurum abchasicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
