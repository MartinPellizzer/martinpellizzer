Bunium mauritanicum var. glaucum

Bunium mauritanicum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
