Bulbophyllum hexarhopalon

Bulbophyllum hexarhopalon, commonly known as the six-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.
