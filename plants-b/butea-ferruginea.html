Butea ferruginea

Butea ferruginea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and flowers of Butea ferruginea have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
