Begonia pteridoides

Begonia pteridoides, commonly known as the fern-like begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a unique and delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.
