Begonia hygrophila

Begonia hygrophila, commonly known as the water begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to almost black hues. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in terrariums.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
