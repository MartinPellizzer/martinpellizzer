Basilicum polystachyon

Basilicum polystachyon, commonly known as sweet basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Basilicum polystachyon is widely cultivated for its fragrance and medicinal properties.
