Bunium ferulifolium

Bunium ferulifolium, commonly known as wild fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and a tall, branched flowering stem that produces small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and digestive properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill.
