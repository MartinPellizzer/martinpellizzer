Begonia vinagrera var. pomecensis

Begonia vinagrera var. pomecensis, commonly known as the Pomecensis Begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a rich green color with distinctive reddish or purple veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
