Bromus glabratus

Bromus glabratus, commonly known as smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are typically narrow, flat, and have a smooth, hairless surface, giving the plant its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three bracts.

This plant is often found in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and is known for its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
