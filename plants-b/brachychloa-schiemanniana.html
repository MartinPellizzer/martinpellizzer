Brachychloa schiemanniana

Brachychloa schiemanniana, commonly known as the Schiemann's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
