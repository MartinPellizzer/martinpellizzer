Biarum bovei subsp. haenseleri

Biarum bovei subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the Haenseler's bellflower, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
