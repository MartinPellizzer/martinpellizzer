Brachistus affinis

Brachistus affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf brachistus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments with minimal water. Brachistus affinis is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to the harsh conditions of its native range.
