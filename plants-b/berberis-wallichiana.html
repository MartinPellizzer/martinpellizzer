Berberis wallichiana

Berberis wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red or orange berries that are edible but often have a sharp taste. It is valued for its ornamental value and is commonly used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Berberis wallichiana is also noted for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
