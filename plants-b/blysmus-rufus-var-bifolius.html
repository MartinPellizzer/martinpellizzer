Blysmus rufus var. bifolius

Blysmus rufus var. bifolius, commonly known as the red blysmus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear reddish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
