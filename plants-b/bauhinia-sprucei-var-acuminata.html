Bauhinia sprucei var. acuminata

Bauhinia sprucei var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
