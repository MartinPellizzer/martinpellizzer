Baccharis antioquensis

Baccharis antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andes region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in heads.

Baccharis antioquensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
