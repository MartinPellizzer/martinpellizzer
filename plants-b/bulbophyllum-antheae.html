Bulbophyllum antheae

Bulbophyllum antheae, commonly known as the Anthea's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, lip-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diverse floral structures, and B. antheae is no exception, showcasing intricate morphological adaptations for reproduction.
