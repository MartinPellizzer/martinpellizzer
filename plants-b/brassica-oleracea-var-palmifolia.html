Brassica oleracea var. palmifolia

Brassica oleracea var. palmifolia, commonly known as the tree kale, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

It is a perennial plant that can grow up to several meters in height, forming a tree-like structure with a woody stem. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and often have a slightly bitter taste, making them suitable for cooking. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown in regions with temperate conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and nutritional content.
