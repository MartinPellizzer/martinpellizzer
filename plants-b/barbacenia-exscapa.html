Barbacenia exscapa

Barbacenia exscapa, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Barbacenia exscapa is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
