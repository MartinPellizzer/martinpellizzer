Baccharis aracatubensis

Baccharis aracatubensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite, lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or white, and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
