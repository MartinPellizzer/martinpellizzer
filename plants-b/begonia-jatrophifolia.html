Begonia jatrophifolia

Begonia jatrophifolia, commonly known as the leafy begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
