Begonia varistyla

Begonia varistyla, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
