Bromus squarrosus var. coloratus

Bromus squarrosus var. coloratus, commonly known as the colorful bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with awned lemmas, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in arid and semi-arid regions.
