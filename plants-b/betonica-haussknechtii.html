Betonica haussknechtii

Betonica haussknechtii, commonly known as the alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
