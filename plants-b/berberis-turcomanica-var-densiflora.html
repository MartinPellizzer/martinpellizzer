Berberis turcomanica var. densiflora

Berberis turcomanica var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold temperatures, contributing to its adaptability in various climates.
