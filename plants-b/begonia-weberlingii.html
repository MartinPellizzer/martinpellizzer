Begonia weberlingii

Begonia weberlingii, commonly known as the Weberlingii begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
