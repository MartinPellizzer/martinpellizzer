Blechnum regnellianum

Blechnum regnellianum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive zigzag pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rock crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
