Bidens squarrosa var. tereticaulis

Bidens squarrosa var. tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small yellow flowers with prominent white veins, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

Its seeds are dispersed by the clinging hairs on the fruit, which attach to animals or clothing.
