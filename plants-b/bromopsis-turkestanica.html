Bromopsis turkestanica

Bromopsis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions.

Bromopsis turkestanica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
