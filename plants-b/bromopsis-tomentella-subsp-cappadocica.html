Bromopsis tomentella subsp. cappadocica

Bromopsis tomentella subsp.

cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Turkey. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage that gives it a fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery texture.
