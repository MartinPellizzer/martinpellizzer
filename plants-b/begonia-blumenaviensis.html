Begonia blumenaviensis

Begonia blumenaviensis, commonly known as the Blumenaviensis begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
