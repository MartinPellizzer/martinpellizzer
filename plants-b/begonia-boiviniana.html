Begonia boiviniana

Begonia boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
