Baccharis serrifolia

Baccharis serrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Baccharis serrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
