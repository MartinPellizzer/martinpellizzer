Bactris microspadix

Bactris microspadix, commonly known as the red raphia, is a palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.
