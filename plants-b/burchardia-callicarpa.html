Burchardia callicarpa

Burchardia callicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Burchardia callicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
