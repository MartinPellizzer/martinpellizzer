Bromopsis plurinodis

Bromopsis plurinodis, commonly known as the nodular bromus, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear nodules along their length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromopsis plurinodis is often found in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks.
