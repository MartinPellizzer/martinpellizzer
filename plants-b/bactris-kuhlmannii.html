Bactris kuhlmannii

Bactris kuhlmannii, commonly known as the pink raphia, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, elongated fruits. Its trunk is covered in a fibrous, spongy material that is similar to that of the raphia palm.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fibrous material in traditional crafts.
