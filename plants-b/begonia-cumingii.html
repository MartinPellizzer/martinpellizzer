Begonia cumingii

Begonia cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Begonia cumingii is valued for its foliage as much as its flowers, making it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
