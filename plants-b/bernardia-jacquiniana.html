Bernardia jacquiniana

Bernardia jacquiniana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.
