Barleria laeta

Barleria laeta, commonly known as the velvetleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
