Bupleurum rotundifolium f. oblongum

Bupleurum rotundifolium f. oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in hue, arranged in loose clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
