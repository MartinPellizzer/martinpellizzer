Bidens segetum var. silvatica

Bidens segetum var. silvatica, commonly known as woodland ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers with purple markings. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
