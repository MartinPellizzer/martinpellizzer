Bromus anatolicus

Bromus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian brome, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Bromus anatolicus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
