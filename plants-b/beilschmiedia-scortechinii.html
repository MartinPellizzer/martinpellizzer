Beilschmiedia scortechinii

Beilschmiedia scortechinii, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense canopy of elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
