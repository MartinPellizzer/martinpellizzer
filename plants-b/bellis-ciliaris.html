Bellis ciliaris

Bellis ciliaris, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Bellis ciliaris is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
