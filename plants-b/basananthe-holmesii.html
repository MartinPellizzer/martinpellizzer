Basananthe holmesii

Basananthe holmesii, commonly known as the Holmes' banana orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, banana-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Basananthe holmesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
