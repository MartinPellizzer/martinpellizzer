Bulbophyllum verrucibracteum

Bulbophyllum verrucibracteum, commonly known as the疣状苞兰, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy bracts that surround the flower spikes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
