Bromus unioloides subf. achalensis

Bromus unioloides subf.

achalensis, commonly known as the Achal mountain brome, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable component of alpine ecosystems.
