Bromelia bracteata var. aquilega

Bromelia bracteata var. aquilega, commonly known as the Aquilega bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. Its foliage is often broad and waxy, with a glossy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its vibrant display and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
