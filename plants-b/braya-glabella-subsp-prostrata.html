Braya glabella subsp. prostrata

Braya glabella subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate bladderleaf, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its prostrate habit, with stems and leaves lying close to the ground.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a common species in desert and steppe habitats.
