Bromus chrysopogon

Bromus chrysopogon, commonly known as golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellowish-green in color.

Bromus chrysopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
