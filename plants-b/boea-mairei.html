Boea mairei

Boea mairei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail.

Boea mairei is also known for its strong scent, which can attract certain insects.
