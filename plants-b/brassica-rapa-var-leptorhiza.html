Brassica rapa var. leptorhiza

Brassica rapa var. leptorhiza, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with smooth, hairless stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its flowers are followed by siliques, which are elongated pods containing several seeds.

This species is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
