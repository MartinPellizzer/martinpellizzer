Bulbophyllum oncidiochilum

Bulbophyllum oncidiochilum, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its bulbs are a key feature, allowing it to store water and nutrients for extended periods.

The flowers are usually borne on long, branched inflorescences, making it a visually appealing species for orchid enthusiasts.
