Balanites orbicularis

Balanites orbicularis, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The tree produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-resistant.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
