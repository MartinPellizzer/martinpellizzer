Baccharis hookeriana

Baccharis hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.
