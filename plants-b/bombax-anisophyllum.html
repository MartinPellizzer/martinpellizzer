Bombax anisophyllum

Bombax anisophyllum, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
