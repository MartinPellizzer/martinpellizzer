Baccharis fluminensis

Baccharis fluminensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist or wet areas, such as riverbanks and floodplains. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
