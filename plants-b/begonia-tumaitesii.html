Begonia tumaitesii

Begonia tumaitesii, commonly known as the Tumai's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins, often displaying a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
