Bomarea crinita

Bomarea crinita, commonly known as the woolly bomarea, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces long, hairy stems and possesses heart-shaped, glossy leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
