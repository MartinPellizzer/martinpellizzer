Biasolettia tuberosa

Biasolettia tuberosa, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, waxy leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular houseplant.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks above the foliage.
