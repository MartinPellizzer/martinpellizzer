Bulbophyllum barbilabium

Bulbophyllum barbilabium, commonly known as the bearded orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
