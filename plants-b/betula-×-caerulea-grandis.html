Betula × caerulea-grandis

Betula × caerulea-grandis, commonly known as the silver birch hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a hybrid between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula jacquemontii (blue birch), resulting in a plant with distinctive silvery-white bark. The leaves are triangular with serrated edges, and they turn golden in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental species in temperate climates.

The tree is valued for its fast growth and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
