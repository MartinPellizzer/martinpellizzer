Bowdichia taubertiana

Bowdichia taubertiana, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody capsule. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils.

Bowdichia taubertiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
