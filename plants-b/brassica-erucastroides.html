Brassica erucastroides

Brassica erucastroides, commonly known as the wild mustard or wild radish, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

Brassica erucastroides is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source.
