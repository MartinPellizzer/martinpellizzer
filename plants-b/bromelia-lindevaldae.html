Bromelia lindevaldae

Bromelia lindevaldae, commonly known as the Lindesay's bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often displaying vibrant green or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a tall spike bearing small, colorful flowers, typically pink or red, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds.

Bromelia lindevaldae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
