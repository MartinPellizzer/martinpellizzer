Blastus multiflorus

Blastus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are often submerged or floating. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The botanical features of Blastus multiflorus are adapted to its nutrient-poor habitat, making it an efficient aquatic predator.
