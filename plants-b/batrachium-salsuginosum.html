Batrachium salsuginosum

Batrachium salsuginosum, commonly known as the salt marsh bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in brackish or saline wetlands.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with thin, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in coastal areas where it can tolerate fluctuating salinity levels.

Batrachium salsuginosum plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms.
