Bakeridesia jaliscana

Bakeridesia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and green.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
