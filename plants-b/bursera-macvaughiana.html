Bursera macvaughiana

Bursera macvaughiana, commonly known as the MacVaugh bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. It produces yellowish-white flowers followed by small, spiny fruits containing seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in scrublands and chaparral ecosystems.
