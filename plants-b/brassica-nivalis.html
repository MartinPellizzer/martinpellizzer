Brassica nivalis

Brassica nivalis, commonly known as the snow mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in cold, alpine environments. Brassica nivalis typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude ecosystems.
