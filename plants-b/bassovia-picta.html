Bassovia picta

Bassovia picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bassovia picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in cooler climates.
