Begonia lebrunii

Begonia lebrunii, commonly known as the Lebrun's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
