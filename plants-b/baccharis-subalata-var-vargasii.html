Baccharis subalata var. vargasii

Baccharis subalata var. vargasii, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its green stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for garden use in dry climates.
