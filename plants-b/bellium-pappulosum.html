Bellium pappulosum

Bellium pappulosum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.
