Boronia lanuginosa

Boronia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Boronia lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
