Brachiaria polyphylla

Brachiaria polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
