Biscutella laevigata var. gredensis

Biscutella laevigata var. gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
