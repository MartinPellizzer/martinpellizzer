Berberis wallichiana var. pallida

Berberis wallichiana var. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant seasonal color changes and unique floral display.
