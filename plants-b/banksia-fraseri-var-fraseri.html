Banksia fraseri var. fraseri

Banksia fraseri var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically orange to yellow in color. The plant produces woody cone-like structures that contain the seeds, a feature common in many banksia species. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland and open forests.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife.
