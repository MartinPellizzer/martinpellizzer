Bulbophyllum chrysochilum

Bulbophyllum chrysochilum, commonly known as the golden-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and its striking golden-yellow lips. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
