Balantium

Balantium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer under sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Balantium thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its unique adaptations.
