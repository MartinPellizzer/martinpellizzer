Bulbophyllum crenilabium

Bulbophyllum crenilabium, commonly known as the crenulated phallic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often pendulous inflorescence and striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its epiphytic or terrestrial growth habits.

This orchid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
