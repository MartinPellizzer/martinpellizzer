Brachistus oblongifolius

Brachistus oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single head. Brachistus oblongifolius is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
