Begonia rhodochaeta

Begonia rhodochaeta, commonly known as the red-stemmed begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
