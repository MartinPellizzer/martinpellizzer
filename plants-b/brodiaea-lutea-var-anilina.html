Brodiaea lutea var. anilina

Brodiaea lutea var. anilina, commonly known as the pink brodiaea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to California.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from pink to lavender in color, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
