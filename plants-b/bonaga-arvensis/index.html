Bonaga Arvensis

Bonaga arvensis is a lesser-known flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the legume or pea family. This species is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Despite its modest appearance, it plays a role in soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation. Its ecological significance and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
