Bulbophyllum lanuginosum

Bulbophyllum lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and delicate blooms.
