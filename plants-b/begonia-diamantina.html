Begonia diamantina

Begonia diamantina, commonly known as the diamond begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving them a shimmering appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
