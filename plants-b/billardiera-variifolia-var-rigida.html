Billardiera variifolia var. rigida

Billardiera variifolia var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and typically grows on the branches of eucalyptus and other host trees. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its flowers are fragrant and white to pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries.

The rigid mistletoe is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
