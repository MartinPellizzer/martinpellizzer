Begonia decaryana

Begonia decaryana, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features palmate leaves with a textured surface, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
