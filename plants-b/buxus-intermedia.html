Buxus intermedia

Buxus intermedia, commonly known as the common boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is typically glossy and oval in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Buxus intermedia is valued for its compact growth habit and is often used in topiary and hedging.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
