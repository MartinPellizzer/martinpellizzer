Berberis nanifolia

Berberis nanifolia, commonly known as dwarf barberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and seasonal color changes.
