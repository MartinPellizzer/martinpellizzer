Brassica oleracea subvar. rubra

Brassica oleracea subvar. rubra, commonly known as red cabbage, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage plant characterized by its dense, compact head of dark red to purple-colored leaves.

This biennial plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is closely related to other Brassica species such as broccoli and cauliflower. The leaves are rich in anthocyanins, which give the plant its vibrant color and contribute to its nutritional value. It is typically grown in temperate climates and requires a cool growing season to develop properly.

The plant is valued for its use in culinary applications and as a source of vitamins and antioxidants.
