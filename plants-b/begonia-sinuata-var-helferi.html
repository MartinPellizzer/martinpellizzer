Begonia sinuata var. helferi

Begonia sinuata var. helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that often display a vibrant green color with subtle variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is a popular choice among collectors and botanical enthusiasts.
