Bupleurum longicaule var. clarkeanum

Bupleurum longicaule var. clarkeanum, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
