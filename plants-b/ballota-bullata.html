Ballota bullata

Ballota bullata, commonly known as the hairy ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, hairy stipules, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Ballota bullata is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
