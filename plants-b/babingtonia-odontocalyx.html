Babingtonia odontocalyx

Babingtonia odontocalyx, commonly known as the honeywood, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and anthers that release pollen in a unique, explosive manner.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
