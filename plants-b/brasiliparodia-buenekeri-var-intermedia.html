Brasiliparodia buenekeri var. intermedia

Brasiliparodia buenekeri var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its distinctive morphology makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservatories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated for conservation purposes.
