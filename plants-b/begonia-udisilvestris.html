Begonia udisilvestris

Begonia udisilvestris, commonly known as the forest begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety foliage with deep green to reddish hues, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated as a rare ornamental plant.
