Bupleurum ranunculoides var. oblongifolium

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a hairy surface that gives them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
