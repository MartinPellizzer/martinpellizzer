Breynia heteroblasta

Breynia heteroblasta, commonly known as the variable breynia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often contrasting leaf patterns, which can vary in shape and color along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and disturbed soils, often growing in tropical and subtropical regions.

Breynia heteroblasta is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a common species in coastal environments.
