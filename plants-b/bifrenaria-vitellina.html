Bifrenaria vitellina

Bifrenaria vitellina, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bifrenaria vitellina is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
