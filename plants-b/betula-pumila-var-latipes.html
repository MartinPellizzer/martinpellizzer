Betula pumila var. latipes

Betula pumila var. latipes, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that release pollen in the spring. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and other moist habitats.

Betula pumila var. latipes is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
