Bactris pectinata subsp. turbinata

Bactris pectinata subsp.

turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Cabbage Palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by edible, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
