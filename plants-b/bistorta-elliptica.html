Bistorta elliptica

Bistorta elliptica, commonly known as narrowleaf bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Bistorta elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
