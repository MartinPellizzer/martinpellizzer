Begonia pulvinata

Begonia pulvinata, commonly known as the swollen begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, and often colorful stems that resemble inflated balloons. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia pulvinata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
