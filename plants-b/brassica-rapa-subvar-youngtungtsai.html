Brassica rapa subvar. youngtungtsai

Brassica rapa subvar. youngtungtsai, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or napa cabbage, is a leafy green vegetable with a distinctively thick, pale-green stem.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its tightly wrapped, fleshy leaves that form a compact head. The plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

Its botanical features include cruciform flowers and a short, sturdy stem that supports its dense foliage.
