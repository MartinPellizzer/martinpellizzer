Bumelia sericea

Bumelia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Bumelia sericea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
