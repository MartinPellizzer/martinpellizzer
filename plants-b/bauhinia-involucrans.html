Bauhinia involucrans

Bauhinia involucrans, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia involucrans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
