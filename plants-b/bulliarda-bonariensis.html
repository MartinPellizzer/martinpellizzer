Bulliarda bonariensis

Bulliarda bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires bulliarda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is known for its showy, yellow to orange-colored flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Bulliarda bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
