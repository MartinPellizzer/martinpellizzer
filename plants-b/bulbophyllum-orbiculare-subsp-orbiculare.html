Bulbophyllum orbiculare subsp. orbiculare

Bulbophyllum orbiculare subsp.

orbiculare, commonly known as the orbiculate bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which are often yellow or greenish-yellow, bloom during the rainy season and attract various pollinators.
