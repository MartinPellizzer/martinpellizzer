Banksia serratuloides

Banksia serratuloides, commonly known as the serrated banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Banksia serratuloides plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
