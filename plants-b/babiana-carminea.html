Babiana carminea

Babiana carminea, commonly known as the red babiana, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
