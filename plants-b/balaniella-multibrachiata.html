Balaniella multibrachiata

Balaniella multibrachiata, commonly known as the branched coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Balaniella multibrachiata is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
