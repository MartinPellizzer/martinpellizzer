Berberis standleyi

Berberis standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in spring followed by red, berry-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
