Butea peltata

Butea peltata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Butea peltata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
