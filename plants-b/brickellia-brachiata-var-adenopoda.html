Brickellia brachiata var. adenopoda

Brickellia brachiata var. adenopoda, commonly known as the woolly brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often glandular and produce a sticky resin, which contributes to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
