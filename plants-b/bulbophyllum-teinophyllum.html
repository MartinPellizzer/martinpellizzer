Bulbophyllum teinophyllum

Bulbophyllum teinophyllum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, velvety-textured leaves and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, colorful blooms that can range from white to pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
