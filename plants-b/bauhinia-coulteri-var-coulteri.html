Bauhinia coulteri var. coulteri

Bauhinia coulteri var. coulteri, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique floral display and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation.
