Begonia rongjiangensis

Begonia rongjiangensis, commonly known as the Rongjiang begonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its limited natural range and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern.
