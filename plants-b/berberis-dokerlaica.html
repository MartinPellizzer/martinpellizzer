Berberis dokerlaica

Berberis dokerlaica, commonly known as the Dokerlaic barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Berberis genus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
