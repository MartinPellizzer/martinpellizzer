Bupleurum ranunculoides var. giganteum

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. giganteum, commonly known as giant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the common buttercup, with 5 to 7 petals. The plant has a stout, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are palmately divided into 5 to 7 lobes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
