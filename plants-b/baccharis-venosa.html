Baccharis venosa

Baccharis venosa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Baccharis venosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
