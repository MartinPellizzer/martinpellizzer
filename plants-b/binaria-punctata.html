Binaria punctata

Binaria punctata, commonly known as the speckled fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are adorned with small, dark spots along the veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures located on the underside of the fronds.

Binaria punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
