Betula nana subsp. rotundifolia

Betula nana subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf birch, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter.

The plant produces small, yellowish catkins and is adapted to harsh, cold environments. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. Betula nana subsp.

rotundifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
