Blepharis subglabra

Blepharis subglabra, commonly known as the smooth blepharis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color, often arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a common sight in desert regions.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
