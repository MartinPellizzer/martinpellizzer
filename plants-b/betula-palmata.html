Betula palmata

Betula palmata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The tree has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall before dropping. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Betula palmata plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
