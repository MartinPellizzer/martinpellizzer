Berrya sansibarensis

Berrya sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar berry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.
