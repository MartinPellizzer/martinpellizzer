Biscutella lucida

Biscutella lucida, commonly known as the glossy biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a glossy surface. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Biscutella lucida is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
