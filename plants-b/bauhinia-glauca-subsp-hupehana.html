Bauhinia glauca subsp. hupehana

Bauhinia glauca subsp.

hupehana, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves with stipules that resemble small leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
