Brahea filifera

Brahea filifera, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a species of palm native to the Mexican states of Jalisco and Nayarit.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray leaves and a slender, unbranched trunk. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Brahea filifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience to drought.
