Baccharis adnata

Baccharis adnata, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or for erosion control.
