Baccharis aracatubaensis

Baccharis aracatubaensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry forests and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, woody fruits that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
