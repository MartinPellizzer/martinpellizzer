Bignonia rosea

Bignonia rosea, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with a feathery plume, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia rosea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
