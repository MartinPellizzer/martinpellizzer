Berberis ruscifolia var. subintegrifolia

Berberis ruscifolia var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with smooth, entire margins, distinguishing it from other barberry varieties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
