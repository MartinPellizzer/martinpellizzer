Bolanthus hirsutus

Bolanthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bolanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bolanthus hirsutus is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
