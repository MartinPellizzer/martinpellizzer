Bulbostylis flexuosa

Bulbostylis flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
