Bargemontia peruviana

Bargemontia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Bargemontia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
