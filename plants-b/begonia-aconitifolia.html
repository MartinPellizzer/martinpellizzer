Begonia aconitifolia

Begonia aconitifolia, commonly known as the alpine begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety purple leaves with a waxy texture, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, often with a slight bluish tinge. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded rockeries. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.

Begonia aconitifolia is also noted for its ability to tolerate frost, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
