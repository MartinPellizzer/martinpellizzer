Bromopsis porteri

Bromopsis porteri, commonly known as the Porteri's bromus, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Bromopsis porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
