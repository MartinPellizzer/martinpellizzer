Brassica turgida

Brassica turgida, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, spherical seed pods. Brassica turgida is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
