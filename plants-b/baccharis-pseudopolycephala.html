Baccharis pseudopolycephala

Baccharis pseudopolycephala, commonly known as the hairy baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
