Buddleja acutifolia f. albiflora

Buddleja acutifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered weeping buddleia, is a cultivar of the butterfly bush native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading, pale yellow to white flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period attract a variety of pollinators.
