Brassica forsskalii

Brassica forsskalii, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Brassica forsskalii plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
