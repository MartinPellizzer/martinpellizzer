Bromheadia scirpoidea

Bromheadia scirpoidea, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale pink, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Bromheadia scirpoidea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
