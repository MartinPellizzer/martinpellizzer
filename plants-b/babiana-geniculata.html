Babiana geniculata

Babiana geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzag-shaped flower stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Babiana geniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
