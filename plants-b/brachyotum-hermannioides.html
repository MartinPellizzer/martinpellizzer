Brachyotum hermannioides

Brachyotum hermannioides, commonly known as Hermann's dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
