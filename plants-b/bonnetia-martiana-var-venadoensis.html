Bonnetia martiana var. venadoensis

Bonnetia martiana var. venadoensis, commonly known as the Venadoes Islands bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the islands of the Greater Antilles.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and semi-shaded environments, often found in limestone soils. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to withstand salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal landscapes.
