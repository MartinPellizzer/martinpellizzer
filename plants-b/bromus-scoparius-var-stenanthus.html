Bromus scoparius var. stenanthus

Bromus scoparius var. stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
