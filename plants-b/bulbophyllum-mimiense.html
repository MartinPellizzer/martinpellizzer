Bulbophyllum mimiense

Bulbophyllum mimiense, commonly known as the Mimiense orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
