Berberis japonica var. bealei

Berberis japonica var. bealei, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are glossy and dark green during the growing season. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
