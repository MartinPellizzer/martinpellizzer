Bursera nashii

Bursera nashii, commonly known as Nash's bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Bursera nashii is part of the Burseraceae family, known for its aromatic resins and medicinal properties in some species.
