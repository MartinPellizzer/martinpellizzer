Bulbophyllum inacootesiae

Bulbophyllum inacootesiae, commonly known as the Cusco orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a greenish lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in conservation efforts.
