Brassica taiwanensis

Brassica taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan mustard, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
