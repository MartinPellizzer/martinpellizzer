Berberis africana

Berberis africana, commonly known as African barberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Berberis africana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
