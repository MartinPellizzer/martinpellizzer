Brownlowia arachnoidea

Brownlowia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, spider-like flowers that are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Brownlowia arachnoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
