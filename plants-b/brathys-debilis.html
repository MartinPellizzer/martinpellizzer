Brathys debilis

Brathys debilis, commonly known as the slender brathys, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features delicate, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in botanical studies.
