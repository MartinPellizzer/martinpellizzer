Begonia roseopunctata

Begonia roseopunctata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with red or purple spots. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
