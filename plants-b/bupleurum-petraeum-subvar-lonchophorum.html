Bupleurum petraeum subvar. lonchophorum

Bupleurum petraeum subvar. lonchophorum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, with white to pale blue petals that bloom in early summer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
