Bromus gussonei var. rigidus

Bromus gussonei var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
