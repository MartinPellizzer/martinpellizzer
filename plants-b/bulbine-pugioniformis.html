Bulbine pugioniformis

Bulbine pugioniformis, commonly known as the sword-shaped bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, sword-like leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in racemes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both garden and container cultivation.
