Boronia amabilis

Boronia amabilis, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, leathery leaves and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in coastal regions.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
