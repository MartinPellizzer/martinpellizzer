Begonia nicaraguensis

Begonia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant colors and intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
