Baccharis diptera

Baccharis diptera, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
