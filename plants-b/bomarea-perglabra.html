Bomarea perglabra

Bomarea perglabra, commonly known as the silky bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy in appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, tubular shape that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
