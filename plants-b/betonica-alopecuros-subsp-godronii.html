Betonica alopecuros subsp. godronii

Betonica alopecuros subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
