Brunsvigia massaiana

Brunsvigia massaiana, commonly known as the "Rainbow Plant" or "Fireplant," is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green with a waxy coating, and it produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its underground bulb, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. It blooms after rainfall, making it a symbol of resilience and renewal in its native habitat.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
