Bactris tenerrima

Bactris tenerrima, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris tenerrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
