Bulbophyllum singulare

Bulbophyllum singulare, commonly known as the single-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces a single, showy flower that is often brightly colored, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in fluctuating environmental conditions.

Bulbophyllum singulare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
