Barringtonia anacardiifolia

Barringtonia anacardiifolia, commonly known as the white mangosteen or mountain mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a segmented, juicy interior. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a distinctive, umbrella-like crown.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
