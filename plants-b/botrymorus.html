Botrymorus

Botrymorus, commonly known as the cluster fungus, is a genus of fungi characterized by its unique growth pattern.

These fungi typically form dense, clustered structures that resemble small, rounded formations. They are often found in humid environments, such as forests or near water sources. Botrymorus species are known for their intricate mycelial networks and spore-producing structures.

Their ecological role includes decomposition and nutrient cycling in their habitats.
