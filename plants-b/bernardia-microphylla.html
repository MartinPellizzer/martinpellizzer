Bernardia microphylla

Bernardia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bernardia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
