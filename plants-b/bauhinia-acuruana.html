Bauhinia acuruana

Bauhinia acuruana, commonly known as the acuruana bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its striking, papery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
