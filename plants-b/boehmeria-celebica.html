Boehmeria celebica

Boehmeria celebica, commonly known as the Celebes nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Boehmeria celebica is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
