Brassica cheiranthos proles monticola

Brassica cheiranthos proles monticola, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia.
