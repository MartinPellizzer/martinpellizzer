Bignonia myriantha

Bignonia myriantha, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia myriantha is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
