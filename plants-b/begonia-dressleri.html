Begonia dressleri

Begonia dressleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
