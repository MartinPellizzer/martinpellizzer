Bulbophyllum bariense

Bulbophyllum bariense, commonly known as the Bariense Pteridium, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
