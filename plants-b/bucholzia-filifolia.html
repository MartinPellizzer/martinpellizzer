Bucholzia filifolia

Bucholzia filifolia, commonly known as the silky filifera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used for commercial purposes.
