Bidens anthriscoides var. decomposita

Bidens anthriscoides var. decomposita, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by distinctive bristly seeds that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.
