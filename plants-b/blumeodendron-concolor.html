Blumeodendron concolor

Blumeodendron concolor, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
