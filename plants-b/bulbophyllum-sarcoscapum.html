Bulbophyllum sarcoscapum

Bulbophyllum sarcoscapum, commonly known as the naked sheath orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy pseudobulbs and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
