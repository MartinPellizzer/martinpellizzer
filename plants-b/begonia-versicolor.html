Begonia versicolor

Begonia versicolor, commonly known as the variable begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly variable leaf colors, which can range from deep green to purple, red, and even variegated patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
