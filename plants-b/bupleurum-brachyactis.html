Bupleurum brachyactis

Bupleurum brachyactis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bupleurum brachyactis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
