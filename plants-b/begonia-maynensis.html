Begonia maynensis

Begonia maynensis, commonly known as the Maynas begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
