Baccharis sagittalis var. montevidensis

Baccharis sagittalis var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidensis baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting well to saline environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
