Bauhinia bidentata var. fraseri

Bauhinia bidentata var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves with small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
