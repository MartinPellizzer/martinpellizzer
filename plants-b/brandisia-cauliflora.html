Brandisia cauliflora

Brandisia cauliflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the trunk and branches. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, making it attractive to birds and other wildlife.

Brandisia cauliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
