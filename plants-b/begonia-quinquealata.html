Begonia quinquealata

Begonia quinquealata, commonly known as the five-lobed begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five lobes and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas. Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its delicate, long-lasting blooms.

Begonia quinquealata is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
