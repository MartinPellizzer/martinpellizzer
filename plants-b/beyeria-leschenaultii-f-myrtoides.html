Beyeria leschenaultii f. myrtoides

Beyeria leschenaultii f. myrtoides, commonly known as the Myrtaceous Beyeria, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
