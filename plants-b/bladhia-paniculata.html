Bladhia paniculata

Bladhia paniculata, commonly known as the white panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
