Berlinia delevoyi

Berlinia delevoyi, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing flat, hard seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
