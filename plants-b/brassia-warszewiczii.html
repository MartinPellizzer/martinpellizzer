Brassia warszewiczii

Brassia warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features elongated, pendulous inflorescences with showy, butterfly-shaped blooms. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Brassia warszewiczii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
