Byrsella

Byrsella, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or elliptic, with a rough texture. They produce small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the dandelion-like appearance.
