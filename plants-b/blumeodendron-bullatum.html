Blumeodendron bullatum

Blumeodendron bullatum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers with red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Blumeodendron bullatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
