Bambusa eutuldoides var. basistriata

Bambusa eutuldoides var. basistriata, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast sharply with its bright green, narrow leaves. This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. The plant is cold-hardy and can be grown in USDA zones 6 to 9, adding vertical interest to any setting.

Its unique appearance makes it a favorite among bamboo enthusiasts and landscape designers.
