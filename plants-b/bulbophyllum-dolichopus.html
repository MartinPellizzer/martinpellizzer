Bulbophyllum dolichopus

Bulbophyllum dolichopus, commonly known as the long-armed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, elongated greenish-yellow flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum dolichopus is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and adaptability to cultivation.
