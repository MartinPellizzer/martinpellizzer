Berberis zabeliana

Berberis zabeliana, commonly known as the Zabel's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest throughout the year.
