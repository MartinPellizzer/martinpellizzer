Baumea complanata

Baumea complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaved baumea, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Baumea complanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
