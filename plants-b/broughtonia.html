Broughtonia

Broughtonia, commonly known as the "Cape Peninsula flower," is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, showy inflorescences that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. Broughtonia species are typically shrubs or small trees, with simple, alternate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. They are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The genus is part of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
