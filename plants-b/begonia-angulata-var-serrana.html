Begonia angulata var. serrana

Begonia angulata var. serrana, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and as a decorative filler in gardens.
