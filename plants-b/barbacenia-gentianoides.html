Barbacenia gentianoides

Barbacenia gentianoides, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Barbacenia gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
