Blumea oxyodonta

Blumea oxyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces elliptical, woody fruits.

Blumea oxyodonta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
