Betula megrelica

Betula megrelica, commonly known as the Georgian birch, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Betula megrelica is adapted to mountainous environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
