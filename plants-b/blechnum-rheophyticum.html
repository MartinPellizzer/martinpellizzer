Blechnum rheophyticum

Blechnum rheophyticum, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. The plant has long, lance-shaped fronds that are typically green and glossy. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum rheophyticum plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
