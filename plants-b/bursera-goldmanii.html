Bursera goldmanii

Bursera goldmanii, commonly known as the Mexican birch, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
