Bocagea weddelliana

Bocagea weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's bocagea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bocagea weddelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
