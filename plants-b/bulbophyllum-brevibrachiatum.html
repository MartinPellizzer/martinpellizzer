Bulbophyllum brevibrachiatum

Bulbophyllum brevibrachiatum, commonly known as the short-bracted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two ranks, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
