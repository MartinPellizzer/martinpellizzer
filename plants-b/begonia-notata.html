Begonia notata

Begonia notata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red and green markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Begonia notata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
