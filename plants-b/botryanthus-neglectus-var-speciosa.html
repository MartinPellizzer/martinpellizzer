Botryanthus neglectus var. speciosa

Botryanthus neglectus var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like pods that turn red or purple as they mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botryanthus neglectus var. speciosa is a perennial herb that is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
