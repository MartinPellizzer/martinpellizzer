Boerhavia paniculata f. esetosa

Boerhavia paniculata f. esetosa, commonly known as the silky boerhavia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered in fine pubescence, giving them a soft, silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically range in color from pink to red.

This plant is often cultivated as a ornamental ground cover due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
