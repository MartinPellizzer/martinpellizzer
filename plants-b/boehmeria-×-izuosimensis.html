Boehmeria × izuosimensis

Boehmeria × izuosimensis, commonly known as the Japanese nettle or Izuo nettle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its fibrous stems are also valued for their potential in textile production.
