Berberis kohistanensis

Berberis kohistanensis, commonly known as the Kohistan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region, specifically Pakistan and northern India.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Berberis kohistanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
