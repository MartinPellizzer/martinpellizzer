Balaka macrocarpa

Balaka macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can span several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its robust trunk and slow growth rate make it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Balaka macrocarpa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
