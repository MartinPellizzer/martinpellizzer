Buddleja usambarensis

Buddleja usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambaras bush or Usambara butterfly bush, is a shrub native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Buddleja usambarensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
