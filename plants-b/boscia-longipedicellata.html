Boscia longipedicellata

Boscia longipedicellata, commonly known as the knobthorn tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, knobby branches and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Boscia longipedicellata is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
