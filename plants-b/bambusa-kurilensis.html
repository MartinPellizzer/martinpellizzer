Bambusa kurilensis

Bambusa kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa kurilensis is valued for its durability and versatility in traditional and modern applications.
