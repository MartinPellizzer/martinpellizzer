Bromus japonicus var. multiflorus

Bromus japonicus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Japanese bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
