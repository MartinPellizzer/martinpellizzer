Baptisia × microphylla

Baptisia × microphylla, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, seed-filled pods that mature to a reddish-brown color. It has a deep root system that makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Baptisia × microphylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
