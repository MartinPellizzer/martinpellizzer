Biscutella strictifolia

Biscutella strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. Biscutella strictifolia is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
