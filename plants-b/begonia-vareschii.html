Begonia vareschii

Begonia vareschii, commonly known as the violet-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that often display a striking violet or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
