Bambusa excelsa

Bambusa excelsa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa excelsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in construction and as a source of fiber.
