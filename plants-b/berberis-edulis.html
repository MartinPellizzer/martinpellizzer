Berberis edulis

Berberis edulis, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible for humans and animals. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

The plant's hardy nature allows it to thrive in harsh alpine environments.
