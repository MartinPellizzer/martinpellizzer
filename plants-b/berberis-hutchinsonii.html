Berberis hutchinsonii

Berberis hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Berberis hutchinsonii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
