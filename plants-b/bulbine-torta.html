Bulbine torta

Bulbine torta, commonly known as the tortured bulbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
