Berberis concolor

Berberis concolor, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. Berberis concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden environments.
