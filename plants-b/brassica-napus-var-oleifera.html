Brassica napus var. oleifera

Brassica napus var. oleifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce canola oil. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rapeseed is also used as a cover crop and for animal feed due to its rapid growth and nutrient density.
