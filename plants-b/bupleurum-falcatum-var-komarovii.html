Bupleurum falcatum var. komarovii

Bupleurum falcatum var. komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, slightly hairy stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose, branched clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
