Burroughsia appendiculata

Burroughsia appendiculata, commonly known as the red-veined mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, red-veined leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
