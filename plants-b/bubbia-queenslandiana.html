Bubbia queenslandiana

Bubbia queenslandiana, commonly known as the Queensland burrpear, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Bubbia queenslandiana thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.
