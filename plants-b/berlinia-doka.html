Berlinia doka

Berlinia doka, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

The tree is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
