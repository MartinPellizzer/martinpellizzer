Bromopsis condensata

Bromopsis condensata, commonly known as the dense hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet with short, awned lemmas.

Bromopsis condensata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
