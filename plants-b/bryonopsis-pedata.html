Bryonopsis pedata

Bryonopsis pedata, commonly known as the common bryonopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.
