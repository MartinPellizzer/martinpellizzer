Bombacopsis nervosa

Bombacopsis nervosa, commonly known as the silky walnut tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
