Begonia rubropilosa

Begonia rubropilosa, commonly known as the red-haired begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a reddish or bronze hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia rubropilosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
