Bassia malaccensis

Bassia malaccensis, commonly known as Malacca goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round, greenish-brown seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
