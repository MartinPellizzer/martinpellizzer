Buxella madagascarica

Buxella madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar boxwood, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Buxaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Buxella madagascarica is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
