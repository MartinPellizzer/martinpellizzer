Bactris kuhlmannii var. aculeata

Bactris kuhlmannii var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit. The plant features a short trunk with a dense crown of long, narrow leaves that are often spiny at the edges. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, lowland environments.

The spiny leaves serve as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores.
