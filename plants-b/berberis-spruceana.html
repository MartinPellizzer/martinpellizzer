Berberis spruceana

Berberis spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and winter structure.

Berberis spruceana is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
