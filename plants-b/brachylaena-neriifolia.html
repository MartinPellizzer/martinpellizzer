Brachylaena neriifolia

Brachylaena neriifolia, commonly known as the red-hot-rod, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and wildlife gardens.
