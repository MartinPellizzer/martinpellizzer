Bellevalia cyrenaica

Bellevalia cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
