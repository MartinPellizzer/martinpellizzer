Bulbophyllum septatum

Bulbophyllum septatum, commonly known as the seven-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive septate (seven-lobed) lip. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
