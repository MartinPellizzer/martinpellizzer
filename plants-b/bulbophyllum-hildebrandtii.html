Bulbophyllum hildebrandtii

Bulbophyllum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or green with purple or red markings. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Bulbophyllum hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
