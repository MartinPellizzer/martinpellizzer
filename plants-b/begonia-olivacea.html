Begonia olivacea

Begonia olivacea, commonly known as the olive begonia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
