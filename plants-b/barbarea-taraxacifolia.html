Barbarea taraxacifolia

Barbarea taraxacifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many mustard species, and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, kidney-shaped seed pods that split open to release the seeds, a feature typical of the mustard family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it is primarily grown for its ecological and botanical interest.
