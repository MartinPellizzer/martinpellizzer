Brassica propera

Brassica propera, commonly known as the African mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes.

The seeds are small and contained within siliques, which are elongated pods typical of the Brassicaceae family.
