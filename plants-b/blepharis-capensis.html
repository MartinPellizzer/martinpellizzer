Blepharis capensis

Blepharis capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Blepharis capensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
