Brachypodium distachyon subvar. platystachyum

Brachypodium distachyon subvar. platystachyum, commonly known as the flat-stalked brachypodium, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its role in ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
