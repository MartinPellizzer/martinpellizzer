Bomarea calyculata

Bomarea calyculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Bomarea calyculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
