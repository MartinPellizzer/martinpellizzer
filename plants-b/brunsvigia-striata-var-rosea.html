Brunsvigia striata var. rosea

Brunsvigia striata var. rosea, commonly known as the red-hot poker or torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in summer, producing vivid red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and succulent, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
