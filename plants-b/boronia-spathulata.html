Boronia spathulata

Boronia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a slightly rough texture.

Boronia spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in native gardens.
