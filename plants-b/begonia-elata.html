Begonia elata

Begonia elata, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often display vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Begonia elata is widely cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
