Biscutella didyma var. scabrida

Biscutella didyma var. scabrida, commonly known as the rough biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
