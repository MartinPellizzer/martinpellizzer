Brassica fruticulosa subsp. numidica

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

numidica, commonly known as the Numidian mustard, is a shrub-like plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
