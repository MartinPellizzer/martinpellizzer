Barbarea vulgaris var. sylvestris

Barbarea vulgaris var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, dry pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it plays a role in ecological succession and supports pollinators.
