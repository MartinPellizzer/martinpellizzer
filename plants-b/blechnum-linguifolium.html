Blechnum linguifolium

Blechnum linguifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Blechnum linguifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
