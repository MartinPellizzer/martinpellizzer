Bobartia tubata

Bobartia tubata, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bobartia tubata is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
