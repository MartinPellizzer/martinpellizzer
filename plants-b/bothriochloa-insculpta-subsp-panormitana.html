Bothriochloa insculpta subsp. panormitana

Bothriochloa insculpta subsp.

panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitana grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
