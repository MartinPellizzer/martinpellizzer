Borreria podocephala var. pumila

Borreria podocephala var. pumila, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
