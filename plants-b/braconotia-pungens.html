Braconotia pungens

Braconotia pungens, commonly known as the spiny leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, prickly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles small, woody capsules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
