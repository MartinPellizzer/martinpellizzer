Balanites wilsoniana var. wilsoniana

Balanites wilsoniana var. wilsoniana, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, rugged trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single large seed. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The tree is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in reforestation efforts in dryland areas.
