Byttneria dahomensis

Byttneria dahomensis, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is often found in moist forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
