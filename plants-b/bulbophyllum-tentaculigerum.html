Bulbophyllum tentaculigerum

Bulbophyllum tentaculigerum, commonly known as the tentacle-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, brightly colored flowers with intricate, curling petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique morphology and ornamental value.
