Betula chinensis var. fargesii

Betula chinensis var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges birch, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow bark that peels in thin, papery strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cooler climates.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique bark and seasonal foliage.
