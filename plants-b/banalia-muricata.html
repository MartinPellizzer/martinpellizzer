Banalia muricata

Banalia muricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved banania, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, which give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Banalia muricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
