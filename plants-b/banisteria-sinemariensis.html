Banisteria sinemariensis

Banisteria sinemariensis, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Banisteria sinemariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
