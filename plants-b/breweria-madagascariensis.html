Breweria madagascariensis

Breweria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar brewer's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
