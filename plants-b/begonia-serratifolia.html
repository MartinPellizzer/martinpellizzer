Begonia serratifolia

Begonia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, serrated leaves that range in color from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
