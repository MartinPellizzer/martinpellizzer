Brevilongium trachycaulon

Brevilongium trachycaulon, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features elongated, cylindrical stems covered in a rough, textured bark, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

Brevilongium trachycaulon thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
