Brachionostylum pullei

Brachionostylum pullei, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along its stem. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or yellow in color. Its root system includes haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients.

Brachionostylum pullei plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various bird species.
