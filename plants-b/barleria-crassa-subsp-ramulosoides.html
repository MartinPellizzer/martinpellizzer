Barleria crassa subsp. ramulosoides

Barleria crassa subsp.

ramulosoides, commonly known as the clustered barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
