Benzoin malaccense

Benzoin malaccense, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or Malacca benjamin, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is exuded from its bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The resin, known as benjamin, is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in incense and perfumes.
