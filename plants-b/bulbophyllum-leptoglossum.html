Bulbophyllum leptoglossum

Bulbophyllum leptoglossum, commonly known as the slender-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
