Bolandra oregana

Bolandra oregana, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, blue-black berries. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along streams.

Bolandra oregana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
