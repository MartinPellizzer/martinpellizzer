Boronia vilhelmii

Boronia vilhelmii, commonly known as the Grey Boronia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Boronia vilhelmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
