Bromus fasciculatus var. multiflorus

Bromus fasciculatus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the clustered brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features tufted growth with dense clusters of spikelets along its stem. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, pale green to brownish flowers in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
