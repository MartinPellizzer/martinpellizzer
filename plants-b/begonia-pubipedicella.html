Begonia pubipedicella

Begonia pubipedicella, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
