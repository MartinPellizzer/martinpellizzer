Bauhinia jucunda

Bauhinia jucunda, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia jucunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
