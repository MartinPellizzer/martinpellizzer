Begonia tribracteata

Begonia tribracteata, commonly known as the tribracteate begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on erect stems. The leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and unique foliage, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
