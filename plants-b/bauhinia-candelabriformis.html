Bauhinia candelabriformis

Bauhinia candelabriformis, commonly known as the candlestick bauhinia, is a striking leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched, candle-like inflorescences that bear clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven oval leaflets, giving it a unique, feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Bauhinia candelabriformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
