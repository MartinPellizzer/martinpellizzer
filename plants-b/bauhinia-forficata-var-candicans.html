Bauhinia forficata var. candicans

Bauhinia forficata var. candicans, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, which appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its fruits are flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
