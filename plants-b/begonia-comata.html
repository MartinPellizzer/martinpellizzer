Begonia comata

Begonia comata, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value makes it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
