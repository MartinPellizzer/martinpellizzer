Begonia dampae

Begonia dampae, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia dampae is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
