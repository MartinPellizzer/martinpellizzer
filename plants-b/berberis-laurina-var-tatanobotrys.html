Berberis laurina var. tatanobotrys

Berberis laurina var. tatanobotrys, commonly known as the Tatanobotrys barberry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage, floral display, and seasonal fruiting.
