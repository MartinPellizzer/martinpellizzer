Brachypodium wattii

Brachypodium wattii, commonly known as the wattii false brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Brachypodium wattii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stabilization and serving as a food source for local wildlife.
