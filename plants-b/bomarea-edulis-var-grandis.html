Bomarea edulis var. grandis

Bomarea edulis var. grandis, commonly known as the giant edible bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, tubular leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
