Beta vulgaris var. globosalba

Beta vulgaris var. globosalba, commonly known as the globe artichoke, is a cultivated variant of the common beet.

It is characterized by its large, globe-shaped leaves that form a dense, compact rosette. The plant produces an edible, fleshy stem that is typically harvested before flowering. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely grown for its tender, flavorful stalks used in culinary applications.
