Biscutella didyma var. leiocarpa

Biscutella didyma var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other forms of Biscutella didyma.
