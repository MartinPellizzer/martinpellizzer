Brachiaria keniensis

Brachiaria keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria keniensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly found in savanna and grassland ecosystems.

It plays a significant role in both agricultural and ecological systems in its native range.
