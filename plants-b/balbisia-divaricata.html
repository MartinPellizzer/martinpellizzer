Balbisia divaricata

Balbisia divaricata, commonly known as the pink bignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are often pollinated by bees and other insects.
