Berberis delavayi

Berberis delavayi, commonly known as Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often sour. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its berberine content.

This species is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
