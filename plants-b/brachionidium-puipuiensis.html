Brachionidium puipuiensis

Brachionidium puipuiensis, commonly known as the Puipui sponge spore plant, is a rare and specialized species native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its unique, sponge-like spores that are produced in specialized structures called sporangia. The botanical features of Brachionidium puipuiensis include its delicate, translucent leaves and a root system adapted for nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils. It is primarily found in shaded, moist forest understories where it forms symbiotic relationships with certain fungi.
