Brassica oleracea var. semiclausa

Brassica oleracea var. semiclausa, commonly known as the Romanesco broccoli, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped inflorescence.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is a variant of the common cabbage species. The plant produces dense, cone-like clusters of small, tightly packed flower buds that are typically green or purple in color. It is valued for its unique appearance and nutritional content, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary and decorative purposes due to its striking visual appeal.
