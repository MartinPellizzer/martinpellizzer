Babingtonia papillosa

Babingtonia papillosa, commonly known as the silky babingtonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Babingtonia papillosa is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
