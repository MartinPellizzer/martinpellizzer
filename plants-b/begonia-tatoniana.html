Begonia tatoniana

Begonia tatoniana, commonly known as the Taton's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a tendency to trail or cascade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
