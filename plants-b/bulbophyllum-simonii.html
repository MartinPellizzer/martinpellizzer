Bulbophyllum simonii

Bulbophyllum simonii, commonly known as the Simon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small, colorful, and fragrant, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Bulbophyllum simonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
