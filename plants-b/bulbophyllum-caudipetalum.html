Bulbophyllum caudipetalum

Bulbophyllum caudipetalum, commonly known as the long-petaled bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescence and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in reducing water loss.

This orchid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
