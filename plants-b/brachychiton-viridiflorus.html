Brachychiton viridiflorus

Brachychiton viridiflorus, commonly known as the emerald flame tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a deciduous tree, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and often peels to reveal a lighter, mottled underlayer.

Brachychiton viridiflorus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.
