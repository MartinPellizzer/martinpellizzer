Begonia majungaensis

Begonia majungaensis, commonly known as the Majunga begonia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Begonia majungaensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it suitable for indoor or shaded garden settings.
