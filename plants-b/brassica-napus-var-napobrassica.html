Brassica napus var. napobrassica

Brassica napus var. napobrassica, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, leafy stalks and clusters of small, green, cabbage-like florets. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used in Asian cuisine.
