Braya tibetica var. breviscapa

Braya tibetica var. breviscapa, commonly known as the Tibetan rock-braya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescence compared to other varieties within the species.
