Bulbostylis leucostachya var. aturensis

Bulbostylis leucostachya var. aturensis, commonly known as the African umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles an umbrella. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
