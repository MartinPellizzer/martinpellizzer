Brassica glauca

Brassica glauca, commonly known as the pale mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it its scientific name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
