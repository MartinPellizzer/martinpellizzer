Brassica fruticulosa var. mauritanica

Brassica fruticulosa var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a shrub-like plant with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
