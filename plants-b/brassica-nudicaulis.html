Brassica nudicaulis

Brassica nudicaulis, commonly known as the mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its small, basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Brassica nudicaulis is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
