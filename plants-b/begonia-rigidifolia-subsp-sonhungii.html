Begonia rigidifolia subsp. sonhungii

Begonia rigidifolia subsp.

sonhungii, commonly known as the Sonhung begonia, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red or pink, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is notable for its rigid texture and vibrant green color, which contrasts beautifully with the flowering parts.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
