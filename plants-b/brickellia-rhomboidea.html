Brickellia rhomboidea

Brickellia rhomboidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and branched.

Brickellia rhomboidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
