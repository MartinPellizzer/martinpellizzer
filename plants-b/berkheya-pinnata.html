Berkheya pinnata

Berkheya pinnata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, resembling those of the oxalis genus. The plant has finely dissected, pinnately compound leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Berkheya pinnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
