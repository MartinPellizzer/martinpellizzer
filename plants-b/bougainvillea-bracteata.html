Bougainvillea bracteata

Bougainvillea bracteata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The actual flowers are small and white, clustered within the bracts. This plant is a member of the Nyctaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
