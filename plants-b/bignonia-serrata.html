Bignonia serrata

Bignonia serrata, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its native habitat.

Bignonia serrata is also known for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
