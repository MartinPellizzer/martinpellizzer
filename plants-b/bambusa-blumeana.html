Bambusa blumeana

Bambusa blumeana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight yellowish tinge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bambusa blumeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
