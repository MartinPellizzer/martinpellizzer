Bermudiana azurea

Bermudiana azurea, commonly known as the Bermuda bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Bermuda Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Bermudiana azurea is an important component of Bermuda's native flora and is often found in maritime shrublands.
