Begonia faureana var. argentea

Begonia faureana var. argentea, commonly known as the silver-leaved begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
