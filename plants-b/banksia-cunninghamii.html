Banksia cunninghamii

Banksia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Sydney banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are released after a fire. Banksia cunninghamii thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
