Bomarea chontalensis

Bomarea chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal mountain arum, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, often colorful spathe that can range from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
