Brassica petrosa

Brassica petrosa, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
