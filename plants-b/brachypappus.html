Brachypappus

Brachypappus, commonly known as the "short-pappus" plants, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, daisy-like flowers and distinctive, short, hair-like structures called pappus on their seeds. They are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, North America, and Australia. Brachypappus species often have succulent leaves and are adapted to survive in dry environments.

Their botanical features make them interesting subjects for study in plant ecology and evolutionary biology.
