Bauhinia choriophylla

Bauhinia choriophylla, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves that are often red or purple in color, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Bauhinia choriophylla is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to horticultural settings.
