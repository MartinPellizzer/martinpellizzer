Buxus sinica var. intermedia

Buxus sinica var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 8 millimeters long. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for topiary and hedging.

Its compact growth habit and durability make it a versatile plant for ornamental landscaping.
