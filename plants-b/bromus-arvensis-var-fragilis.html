Bromus arvensis var. fragilis

Bromus arvensis var. fragilis, commonly known as the slender annual brome, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a short-lived annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields. The plant has slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas quickly.

This species is often considered a weed in cultivated landscapes due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
