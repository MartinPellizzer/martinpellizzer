Beta stricta

Beta stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beet, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Beta stricta is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and requires minimal maintenance.
