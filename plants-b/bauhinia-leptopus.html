Bauhinia leptopus

Bauhinia leptopus, commonly known as the small-leaved bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its small, woody pods contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Bauhinia leptopus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
