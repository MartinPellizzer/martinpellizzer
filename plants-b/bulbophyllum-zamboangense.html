Bulbophyllum zamboangense

Bulbophyllum zamboangense, commonly known as the Zamboanga Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
