Berberis montana var. chillanensis

Berberis montana var. chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
