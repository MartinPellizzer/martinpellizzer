Bassia everistiana

Bassia everistiana, commonly known as Everist's bryony, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in rocky, arid environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.
