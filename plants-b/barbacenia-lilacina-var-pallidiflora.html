Barbacenia lilacina var. pallidiflora

Barbacenia lilacina var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered barbacenia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and typically have a pale lilac or pinkish hue, giving it its common name. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for shaded garden environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
