Bauhinia caudigera

Bauhinia caudigera, commonly known as the hanging bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia caudigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
