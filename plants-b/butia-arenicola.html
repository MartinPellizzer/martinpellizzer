Butia arenicola

Butia arenicola, commonly known as the arenicola palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits.

Butia arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
