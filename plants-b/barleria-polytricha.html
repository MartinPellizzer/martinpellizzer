Barleria polytricha

Barleria polytricha, commonly known as the fringed barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves, which are adapted to arid conditions. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Barleria polytricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
