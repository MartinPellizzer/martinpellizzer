Bonatea bilabrella

Bonatea bilabrella, commonly known as the twin-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has two distinct lip lobes, which give it its scientific name, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Bonatea bilabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
