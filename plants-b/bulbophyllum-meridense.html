Bulbophyllum meridense

Bulbophyllum meridense, commonly known as the southern orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
