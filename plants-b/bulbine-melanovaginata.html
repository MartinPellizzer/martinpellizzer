Bulbine melanovaginata

Bulbine melanovaginata, commonly known as the black-veined bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tubular leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
