Betonica brachydonta

Betonica brachydonta, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Betonica brachydonta is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
