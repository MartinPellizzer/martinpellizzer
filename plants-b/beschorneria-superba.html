Beschorneria superba

Beschorneria superba, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a striking evergreen perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking red or orange flower spikes in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a favorite among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
