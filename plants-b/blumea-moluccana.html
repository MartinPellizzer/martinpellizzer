Blumea moluccana

Blumea moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Blumea moluccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
