Begonia chepoensis

Begonia chepoensis, commonly known as the Chepo begonia, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, which helps to retain moisture.

Begonia chepoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
