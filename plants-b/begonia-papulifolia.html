Begonia papulifolia

Begonia papulifolia, commonly known as the papular begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papillate (bumpy) leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia papulifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
