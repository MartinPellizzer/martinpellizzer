Baccharoides bracteosa

Baccharoides bracteosa, commonly known as the silky bract baccharoides, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Baccharoides bracteosa is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
