Briquetia

Briquetia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Briquetia species are typically shrubs or small trees, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The genus includes several species, such as Briquetia multiflora, which is popular for its compact growth and vibrant blooms.
