Banisteria picta

Banisteria picta, commonly known as the painted banisteria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate patterns on its leaves, which resemble painted designs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Banisteria picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
