Byttneria fernandesii

Byttneria fernandesii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
