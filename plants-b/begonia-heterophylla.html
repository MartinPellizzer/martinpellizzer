Begonia heterophylla

Begonia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly varied leaf shapes, which can range from heart-shaped to elongated and serrated. The plant typically produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Begonia heterophylla is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
