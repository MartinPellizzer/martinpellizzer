Bambusa glaucescens f. gimmei

Bambusa glaucescens f. gimmei, commonly known as the Blue Bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species characterized by its unique bluish-gray foliage.

It is a clumping bamboo, forming dense, upright canes that grow to a height of up to 6 meters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic value and ability to add a tropical feel to any setting.
