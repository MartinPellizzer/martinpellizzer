Boehmeria acuminata

Boehmeria acuminata, commonly known as Chinese grass or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and wetlands.

Boehmeria acuminata is valued for its fibrous stalks, which have been used in traditional crafts and papermaking.
