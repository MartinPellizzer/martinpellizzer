Burmannia javanica

Burmannia javanica, commonly known as the crimson bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, fleshy flowers that resemble bladders, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has a short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a few weeks. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption from waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

Despite its short life span, it is an important species in the study of plant adaptations to wetland environments.
