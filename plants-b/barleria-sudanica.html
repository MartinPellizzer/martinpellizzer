Barleria sudanica

Barleria sudanica, commonly known as Sudan grass or Sudanese grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Barleria sudanica is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
