Betula liaotungensis

Betula liaotungensis, commonly known as the Liaotung birch, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern China.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that becomes fissured with age, and it produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall before shedding. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
