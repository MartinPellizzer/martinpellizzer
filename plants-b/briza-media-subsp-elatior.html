Briza media subsp. elatior

Briza media subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the tall quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that sway in the wind.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
