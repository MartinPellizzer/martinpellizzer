Bulbophyllum lombokense

Bulbophyllum lombokense, commonly known as the Lombok orchid, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Lombok.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

The flowers often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues, making it a popular subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
