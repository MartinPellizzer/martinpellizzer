Bistorta honanensis

Bistorta honanensis, commonly known as the Chinese bistort, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and sometimes edible rhubarb-like stems. The plant features upright, somewhat hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. Bistorta honanensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within its native range, though it is less commonly cultivated outside of its natural habitat.
