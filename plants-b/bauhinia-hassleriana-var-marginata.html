Bauhinia hassleriana var. marginata

Bauhinia hassleriana var. marginata, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often adorned with a pink or white margin. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a tendency to peel in thin, papery layers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
