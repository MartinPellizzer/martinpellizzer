Buddleja acutifolia

Buddleja acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja acutifolia is relatively low maintenance and suitable for garden use in temperate climates.
