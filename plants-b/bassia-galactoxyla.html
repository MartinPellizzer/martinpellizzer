Bassia galactoxyla

Bassia galactoxyla, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southwestern North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bassia galactoxyla is adapted to thrive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
