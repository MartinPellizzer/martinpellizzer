Bassia lanata

Bassia lanata, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a useful plant in certain cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
