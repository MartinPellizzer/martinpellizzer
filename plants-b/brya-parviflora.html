Brya parviflora

Brya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered moss, is a member of the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often curved and possess a smooth, waxy surface to reduce water loss. Brya parviflora produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered near the base of the plant, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This moss is well adapted to its environment, showcasing the resilience and specialized features of bryophytes.
