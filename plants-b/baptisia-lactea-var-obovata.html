Baptisia lactea var. obovata

Baptisia lactea var. obovata, commonly known as the white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to white flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy and erect. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
