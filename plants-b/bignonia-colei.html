Bignonia colei

Bignonia colei, commonly known as the yellow trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 15 meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
