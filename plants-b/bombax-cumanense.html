Bombax cumanense

Bombax cumanense, commonly known as the Cuban silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
