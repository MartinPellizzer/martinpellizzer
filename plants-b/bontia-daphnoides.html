Bontia daphnoides

Bontia daphnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf daphniphyllum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bontia daphnoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
