Blutaparon portulacoides var. commersonii

Blutaparon portulacoides var. commersonii, commonly known as Commerson's portulacoid, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions through its thick, waxy cuticle. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
