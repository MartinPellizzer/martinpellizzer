Biscutella Laevigata Subsp. Gredensis

Biscutella laevigata subsp. gredensis is a rare and specialized plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. This subspecies is distinguished by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy leaves. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered endangered and faces threats from habitat loss. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
