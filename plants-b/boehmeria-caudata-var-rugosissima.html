Boehmeria caudata var. rugosissima

Boehmeria caudata var. rugosissima, commonly known as roughleaf nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is noted for its rugged appearance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
