Beckwithia

Beckwithia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a unique species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 feet. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Beckwithia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
