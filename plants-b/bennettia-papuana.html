Bennettia papuana

Bennettia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bennettia, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Bennettiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are opposite or alternate in structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Bennettia papuana is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments and is often found in primary forest understoreys.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical research.
