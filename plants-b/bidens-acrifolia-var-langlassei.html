Bidens acrifolia var. langlassei

Bidens acrifolia var. langlassei, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

It is an annual herb that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
