Begonia naumoniensis

Begonia naumoniensis, commonly known as the Naumon Island begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Naumoni in the South Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
