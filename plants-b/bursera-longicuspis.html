Bursera longicuspis

Bursera longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined bursera, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

Bursera longicuspis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
