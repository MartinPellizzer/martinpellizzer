Buddleja martii

Buddleja martii, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Buddleja martii is known for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
