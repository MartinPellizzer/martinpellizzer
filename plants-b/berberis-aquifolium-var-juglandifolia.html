Berberis aquifolium var. juglandifolia

Berberis aquifolium var. juglandifolia, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dark green, holly-like leaves with a glossy surface and yellow to orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has thorned stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with the berries containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is particularly hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
