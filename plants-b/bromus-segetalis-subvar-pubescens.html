Bromus segetalis subvar. pubescens

Bromus segetalis subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences covered in fine, hairy bracts. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often found in agricultural settings. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, facilitating its spread in various environments.
