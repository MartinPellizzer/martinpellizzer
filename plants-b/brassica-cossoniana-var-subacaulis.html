Brassica cossoniana var. subacaulis

Brassica cossoniana var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, with a distinctive mustard scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
