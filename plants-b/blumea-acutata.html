Blumea acutata

Blumea acutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist or wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Blumea acutata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
