Boronia suberosa

Boronia suberosa, commonly known as the rough boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in coastal regions of southern Australia. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Boronia suberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
