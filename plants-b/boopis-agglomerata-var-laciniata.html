Boopis agglomerata var. laciniata

Boopis agglomerata var. laciniata, commonly known as the dissected boopis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
