Begonia symgeraniifolia

Begonia symgeraniifolia, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.
