Ballota nigra subsp. anatolica

Ballota nigra subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian blackball, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. It is a member of the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
