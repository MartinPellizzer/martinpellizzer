Bistorta abbreviata

Bistorta abbreviata, commonly known as narrowleaf bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as sorrel and buckwheat. The plant is characterized by its upright, somewhat zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a distinctive white powdery coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose, terminal spikes.

Bistorta abbreviata is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
