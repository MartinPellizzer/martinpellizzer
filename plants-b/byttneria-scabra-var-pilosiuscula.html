Byttneria scabra var. pilosiuscula

Byttneria scabra var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the rough bark bignay, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
