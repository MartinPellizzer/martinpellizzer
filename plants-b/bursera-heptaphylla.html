Bursera heptaphylla

Bursera heptaphylla, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It produces a fragrant resin that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The tree is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
