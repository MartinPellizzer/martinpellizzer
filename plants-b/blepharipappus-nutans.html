Blepharipappus nutans

Blepharipappus nutans, commonly known as the nodding wingedweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its most distinctive feature is the nodding, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The seeds are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal.
