Berberis brevisepala

Berberis brevisepala, commonly known as the short-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
