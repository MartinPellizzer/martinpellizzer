Begonia pedata

Begonia pedata, commonly known as the spurred begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals, which give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a unique structure that includes elongated, spurs at the base of the petals.

Begonia pedata prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and indoor cultivation.
