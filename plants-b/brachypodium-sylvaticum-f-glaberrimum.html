Brachypodium sylvaticum f. glaberrimum

Brachypodium sylvaticum f. glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous wood grass, is a variant of the common wood grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in various soil conditions make it a notable species in ecological studies.
