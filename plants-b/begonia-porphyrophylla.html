Begonia porphyrophylla

Begonia porphyrophylla, commonly known as the purple-leaved begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety purple foliage that contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it an ideal choice for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
