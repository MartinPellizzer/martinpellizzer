Bambusa pseudoarundinacea

Bambusa pseudoarundinacea, commonly known as golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically measuring up to 30 centimeters in length.

Bambusa pseudoarundinacea is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create dense screens or hedges.
