Bombacopsis mucronulata

Bombacopsis mucronulata, commonly known as the African mahogany.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
