Baissea brachyantha

Baissea brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baissea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Baissea brachyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
