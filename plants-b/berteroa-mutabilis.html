Berteroa mutabilis

Berteroa mutabilis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow to orange-red composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open areas.

Berteroa mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
