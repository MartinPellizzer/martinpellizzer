Begonia truncicola

Begonia truncicola, commonly known as the African begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often deeply lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in mild climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.
