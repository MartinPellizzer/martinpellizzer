Bellevalia speciosa

Bellevalia speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are basal and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Bellevalia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
