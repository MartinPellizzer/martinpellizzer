Bassia reuteriana

Bassia reuteriana, commonly known as Russian spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable due to its mild, spinach-like flavor.

Bassia reuteriana is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
