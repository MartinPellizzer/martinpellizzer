Bougainvillea glabra var. typica

Bougainvillea glabra var. typica, commonly known as the common bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant clusters of colorful bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. It has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or vine, depending on pruning.

Its foliage consists of small, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green color.
