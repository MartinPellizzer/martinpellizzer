Bromus asper var. angustifolius

Bromus asper var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. It is generally herbaceous, with a short stature and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability.
