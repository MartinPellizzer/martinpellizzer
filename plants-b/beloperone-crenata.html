Beloperone crenata

Beloperone crenata, commonly known as the mountain holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Beloperone crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
