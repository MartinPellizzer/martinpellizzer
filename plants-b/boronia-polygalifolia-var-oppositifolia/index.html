Boronia Polygalifolia Var. Oppositifolia

Boronia with opposite leaves, scientifically known as Boronia polygalifolia var. oppositifolia, is a unique and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. This variant of Boronia polygalifolia is distinguished by its opposite leaf arrangement, a rare trait within the genus Boronia. Its fragrant, tubular flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and white, adding a delicate beauty to its natural habitat. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
