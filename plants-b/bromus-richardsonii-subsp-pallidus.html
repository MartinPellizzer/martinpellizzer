Bromus richardsonii subsp. pallidus

Bromus richardsonii subsp.

pallidus, commonly known as the pallid brome, is a grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
