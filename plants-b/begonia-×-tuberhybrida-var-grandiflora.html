Begonia × tuberhybrida var. grandiflora

Begonia × tuberhybrida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the tuberous begonia, is a hybrid species prized for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in various conditions. The plant typically produces clusters of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves and large, colorful blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value has led to the development of numerous cultivars with varying flower sizes and colors.
