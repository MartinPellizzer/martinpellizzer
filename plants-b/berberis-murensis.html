Berberis murensis

Berberis murensis, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Berberis murensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and partial shade.
