Balsamorhiza macrolepis var. platylepis

Balsamorhiza macrolepis var. platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a composite head. Its root system is thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in arid environments.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
