Bothriospermum majusculum

Bothriospermum majusculum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a notable species in botanical studies.
