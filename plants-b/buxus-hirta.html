Buxus hirta

Buxus hirta, commonly known as the hairy boxwood, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Buxus hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for hedges and topiaries.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
