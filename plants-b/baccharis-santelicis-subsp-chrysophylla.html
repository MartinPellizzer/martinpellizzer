Baccharis santelicis subsp. chrysophylla

Baccharis santelicis subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as goldenleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is distinguished by its bright yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its structure and appearance make it an important component of local ecosystems.
