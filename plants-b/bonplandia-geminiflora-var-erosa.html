Bonplandia geminiflora var. erosa

Bonplandia geminiflora var. erosa, commonly known as the erose bonplandia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often adorned with wavy or erose margins, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a dense, bushy form that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is noted for its adaptability to humid, well-drained soils and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
