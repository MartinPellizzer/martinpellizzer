Barringtonia sarcostachys subsp. dolichophylla

Barringtonia sarcostachys subsp.

dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences that hang from the branches.

The plant has large, palmate leaves that are typically green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
