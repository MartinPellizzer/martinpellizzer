Baccharis curvifolia

Baccharis curvifolia, commonly known as curlleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert and chaparral environments. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Baccharis curvifolia is drought-tolerant and is used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
