Buddleja paniculata

Buddleja paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in garden settings.
