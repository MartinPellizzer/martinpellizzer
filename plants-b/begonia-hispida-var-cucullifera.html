Begonia hispida var. cucullifera

Begonia hispida var. cucullifera, commonly known as the hooded begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique leaf morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
