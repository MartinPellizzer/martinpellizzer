Bidens pentamera

Bidens pentamera, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Bidens pentamera is known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
