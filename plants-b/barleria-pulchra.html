Barleria pulchra

Barleria pulchra, commonly known as the red-hot poker or firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Barleria pulchra is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
