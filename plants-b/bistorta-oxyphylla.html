Bistorta oxyphylla

Bistorta oxyphylla, commonly known as the meadow bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, rhizomatous stems that are often covered in a reddish or purple hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in upright spikes, producing pinkish to reddish blooms.

Bistorta oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
