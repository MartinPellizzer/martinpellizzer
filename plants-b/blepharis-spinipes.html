Blepharis spinipes

Blepharis spinipes, commonly known as the spiny blepharis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and distinctive appearance.
