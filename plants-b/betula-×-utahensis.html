Betula × utahensis

Betula × utahensis, commonly known as the Utah paper birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a papery, silver inner layer. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-green catkins and has serrated, oval-shaped leaves.

Betula × utahensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
