Bouteloua litigiosa

Bouteloua litigiosa, commonly known as the prairie dropseed, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
