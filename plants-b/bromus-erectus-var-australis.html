Bromus erectus var. australis

Bromus erectus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and inland regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is known for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils.
