Brassica villosa subsp. bivoniana

Brassica villosa subsp.

bivoniana, commonly known as the hairy cabbage or woolly mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
