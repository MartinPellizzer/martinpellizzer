Biropteris

Biropteris, commonly known as the giant fern, is a large, tropical fern species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive fronds, which can reach lengths of up to 6 meters, making it one of the tallest ferns in the world. The plant has a complex rhizome system that allows it to spread and establish itself in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, feather-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Biropteris thrives in humid climates and is often found in rainforests where it plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for various organisms.
