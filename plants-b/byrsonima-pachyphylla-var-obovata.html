Byrsonima pachyphylla var. obovata

Byrsonima pachyphylla var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pata de vaca*, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are obovate in shape, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
