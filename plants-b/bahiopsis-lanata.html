Bahiopsis lanata

Bahiopsis lanata, commonly known as the woolly bahiopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
