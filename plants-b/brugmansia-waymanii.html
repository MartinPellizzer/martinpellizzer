Brugmansia waymanii

Brugmansia waymanii, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, funnel-shaped flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The flowers are typically white, yellow, or orange, and they emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Brugmansia waymanii is valued for its ornamental beauty but is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
