Bulbophyllum watsonianum

Bulbophyllum watsonianum, commonly known as the Watson's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded conditions.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.
