Bredemeyera aphylla

Bredemeyera aphylla, commonly known as the leafless cycad, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, instead having a stout, unbranched trunk. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, typical of cycads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
