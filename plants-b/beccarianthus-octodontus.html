Beccarianthus octodontus

Beccarianthus octodontus, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structures that resemble horns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
