Banisteria lanuginosa

Banisteria lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky banisteria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Banisteria lanuginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
