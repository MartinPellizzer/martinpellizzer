Boronia ramosa

Boronia ramosa, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with slender, often hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their subtle, citrus-like scent.

Boronia ramosa is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
