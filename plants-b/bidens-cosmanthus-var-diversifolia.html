Bidens cosmanthus var. diversifolia

Bidens cosmanthus var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable false beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that vary in shape and size, contributing to its varietal name "diversifolia.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
