Brassica rapa var. ruvo

Brassica rapa var. ruvo, commonly known as field mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, thin pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. Native to Europe and Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environments.
