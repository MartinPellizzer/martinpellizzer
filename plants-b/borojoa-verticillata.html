Borojoa verticillata

Borojoa verticillata, commonly known as the wild cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls of three to five. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to a unique fruit with a hard, spiky exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp inside.
