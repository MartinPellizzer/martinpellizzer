Ballota byblensis

Ballota byblensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean ballota, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ballota byblensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
