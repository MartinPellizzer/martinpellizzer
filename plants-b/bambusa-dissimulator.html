Bambusa dissimulator

Bambusa dissimulator, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black, culms that are often covered in a fine, silky sheen. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
