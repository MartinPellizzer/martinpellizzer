Beckmannia syzigachne var. hirsutiflora

Beckmannia syzigachne var. hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered beakvetch, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy inflorescences that bear small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the seed pods are elongated and often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
