Bromus scoparius var. humilis

Bromus scoparius var. humilis, commonly known as the slender brome, is a low-growing grass species native to western North America.

It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
