Babingtonia pentandra

Babingtonia pentandra, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Babingtonia pentandra is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
