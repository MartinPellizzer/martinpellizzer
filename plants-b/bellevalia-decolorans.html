Bellevalia decolorans

Bellevalia decolorans, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are pale blue or white, arranged in a loose raceme, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its ornamental value in garden settings.
