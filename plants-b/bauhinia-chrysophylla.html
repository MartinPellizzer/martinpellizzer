Bauhinia chrysophylla

Bauhinia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn a vibrant yellow during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Bauhinia chrysophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
