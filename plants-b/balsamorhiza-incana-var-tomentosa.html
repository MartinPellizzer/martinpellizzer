Balsamorhiza incana var. tomentosa

Balsamorhiza incana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly false sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and large, showy yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its robust root system.

The varietal form *tomentosa* distinguishes it from other varieties through its dense, woolly pubescence.
