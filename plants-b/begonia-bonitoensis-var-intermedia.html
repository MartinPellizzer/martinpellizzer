Begonia bonitoensis var. intermedia

Begonia bonitoensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Begonia, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that range from dark green to bronze in color. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
