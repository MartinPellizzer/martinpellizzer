Begonia rubrovenia

Begonia rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that display deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Begonia rubrovenia is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
