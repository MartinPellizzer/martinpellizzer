Berberis suberecta

Berberis suberecta, commonly known as the upright barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used ornamentally. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
