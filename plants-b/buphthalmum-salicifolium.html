Buphthalmum salicifolium

Buphthalmum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Buphthalmum salicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
