Berberis triacanthophora

Berberis triacanthophora, commonly known as the thorned barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are densely covered with sharp, thorn-like spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
