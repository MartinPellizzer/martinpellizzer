Butyrospermum parkii var. floccosum

Butyrospermum parkii var. floccosum, commonly known as shea butter tree variant floccosum, is a variant of the shea tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces a high-quality shea butter used in cosmetics and food. The tree has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it an important component of local ecosystems. Its fruit contains a high amount of fat, which is extracted through cold pressing.

The variant is valued for its unique fatty acid composition, contributing to its versatility in various applications.
