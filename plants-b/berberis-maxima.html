Berberis maxima

Berberis maxima, commonly known as the giant barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Berberis maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
