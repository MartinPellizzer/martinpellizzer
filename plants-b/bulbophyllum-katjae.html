Bulbophyllum katjae

Bulbophyllum katjae, commonly known as the Katja's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
