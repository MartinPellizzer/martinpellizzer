Baillonia spicata

Baillonia spicata, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Baillonia spicata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
