Bambos hoteitsiku

Bambos hoteitsiku, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Bambos hoteitsiku is also valued for its lightweight and durable bamboo shoots, which are a delicacy in Asian cuisine.
