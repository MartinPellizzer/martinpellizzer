Bursera coyucensis

Bursera coyucensis, commonly known as the coyote bush or Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules.

Bursera coyucensis is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
