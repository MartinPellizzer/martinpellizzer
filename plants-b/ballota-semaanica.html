Ballota semaanica

Ballota semaanica, commonly known as the Levantine ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Ballota semaanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
