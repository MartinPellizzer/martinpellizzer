Belladonna blanda

Belladonna blanda, commonly known as the small belladonna or lesser deadly nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features smooth, dark green leaves and produces bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. Its berries are bright red and contain toxic alkaloids, making it dangerous if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
