Bromus magnificus

Bromus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent brome, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Bromus magnificus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
