Barkhausia caroliniana

Barkhausia caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina balm of Gilead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval in shape with a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and natural habitats.

The bark of the plant is thick and fissured, contributing to its common name and providing structural support to the plant.
