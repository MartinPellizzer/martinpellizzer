Butia quaraimana

Butia quaraimana, commonly known as the purple pampa palm, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in branched clusters, followed by small, edible fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
