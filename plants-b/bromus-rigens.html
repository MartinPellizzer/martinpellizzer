Bromus rigens

Bromus rigens, commonly known as rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, and it is often found in temperate climates.

Bromus rigens is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
