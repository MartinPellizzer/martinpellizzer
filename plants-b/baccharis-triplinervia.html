Baccharis triplinervia

Baccharis triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
