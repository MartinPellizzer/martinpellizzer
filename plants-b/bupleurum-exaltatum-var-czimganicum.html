Bupleurum exaltatum var. czimganicum

Bupleurum exaltatum var. czimganicum, commonly known as the Czimganica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective properties.
