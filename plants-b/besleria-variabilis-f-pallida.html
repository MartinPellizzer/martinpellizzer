Besleria variabilis f. pallida

Besleria variabilis f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered besleria, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its strap-shaped, often variegated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The pale coloration of its flowers is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
