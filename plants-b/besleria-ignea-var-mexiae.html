Besleria ignea var. mexiae

Besleria ignea var. mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
