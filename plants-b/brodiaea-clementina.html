Brodiaea clementina

Brodiaea clementina, commonly known as the Santa Barbara ghostflower, is a species of flowering plant in the amaryllis family.

It is native to California and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a cluster of small, white or pink flowers on a tall, slender stem, often blooming in late spring. It has linear, grass-like leaves and is typically found in open, sandy soils.

Brodiaea clementina is a short-lived perennial that is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
