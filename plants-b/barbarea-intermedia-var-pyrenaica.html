Barbarea intermedia var. pyrenaica

Barbarea intermedia var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It is native to the Pyrenees region and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

This species is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold climates.
