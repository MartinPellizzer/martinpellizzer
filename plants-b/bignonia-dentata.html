Bignonia dentata

Bignonia dentata, commonly known as the toothed bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Bignonia dentata is often used as a decorative vine due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
