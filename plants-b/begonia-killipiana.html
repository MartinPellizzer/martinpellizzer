Begonia killipiana

Begonia killipiana, commonly known as the Killip's begonia, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to its surroundings.
