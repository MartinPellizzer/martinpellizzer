Baccharis libertadensis

Baccharis libertadensis, commonly known as the Libertad shrub, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, woody structure and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
