Brassaiopsis papayoides

Brassaiopsis papayoides, commonly known as the papaya tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant can grow up to 10 meters tall and has a smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit that is rich in nutrients.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
