Beta vulgaris var. depressirubra

Beta vulgaris var. depressirubra, commonly known as the red beet or Swiss chard, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its deep red, slightly wrinkled leaves and thick, edible stalks. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Europe. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its nutrient-rich leaves and roots.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
