Beesia cordata

Beesia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf beesia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. Beesia cordata thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
