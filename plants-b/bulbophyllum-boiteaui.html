Bulbophyllum boiteaui

Bulbophyllum boiteaui, commonly known as the Boiteau's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
