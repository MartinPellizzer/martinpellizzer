Borbonia cordata var. commutata

Borbonia cordata var. commutata, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it suitable for indoor or outdoor gardens.
