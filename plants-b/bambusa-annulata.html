Bambusa annulata

Bambusa annulata, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking black or dark green culms, which are often marked with white or yellow stripes. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are highly durable. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa annulata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional construction and crafts.
