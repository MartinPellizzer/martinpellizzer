Blumea serrata

Blumea serrata, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

Blumea serrata is often found in moist, open habitats such as forests and grasslands.
