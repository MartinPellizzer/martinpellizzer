Brickellia scoparia var. typica

Brickellia scoparia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, narrow leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
