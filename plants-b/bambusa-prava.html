Bambusa prava

Bambusa prava, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green culms, which give it a striking appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern. It is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Bambusa prava is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
