Butomus lanceolatus

Butomus lanceolatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a wetland species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often bright green and can be submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a spadix surrounded by bracts, and they bloom in late summer.

Butomus lanceolatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
