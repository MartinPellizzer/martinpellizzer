Barkhausia nana var. lyratifolia

Barkhausia nana var. lyratifolia, commonly known as the dwarf barkhausia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly hairy texture, and its leaves are often described as having a lyre-shaped appearance, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
