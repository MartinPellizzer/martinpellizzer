Blumea paniculata var. jacquemontii

Blumea paniculata var. jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in paniculate inflorescences, making it a visually striking plant in its native habitat.
