Bupleurum contractum

Bupleurum contractum, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its small, blue or purple flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Bupleurum contractum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
