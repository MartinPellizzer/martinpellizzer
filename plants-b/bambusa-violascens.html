Bambusa violascens

Bambusa violascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its striking violet to purple-colored culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. It grows as a clump-forming perennial, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Bambusa violascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
