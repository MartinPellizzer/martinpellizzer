Bryomorpha

Bryomorpha, commonly known as the moss family, is a group of non-vascular plants that thrive in moist, shaded environments.

These plants lack true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called rhizoids for anchoring and absorbing nutrients. Bryomorpha species reproduce through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia. They play a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in ecosystems.

Their simple structure and ability to colonize various substrates make them important indicators of environmental conditions.
