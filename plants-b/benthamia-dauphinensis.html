Benthamia dauphinensis

Benthamia dauphinensis, commonly known as the Dauphine benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
