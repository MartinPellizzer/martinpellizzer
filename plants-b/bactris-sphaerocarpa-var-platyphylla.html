Bactris sphaerocarpa var. platyphylla

Bactris sphaerocarpa var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-fruit palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its short, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small and round, with a fibrous outer covering. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable agriculture, it has gained interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
