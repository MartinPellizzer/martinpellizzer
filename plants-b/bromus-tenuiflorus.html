Bromus tenuiflorus

Bromus tenuiflorus, commonly known as slender-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which produce pale to brownish flowers.

Bromus tenuiflorus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
