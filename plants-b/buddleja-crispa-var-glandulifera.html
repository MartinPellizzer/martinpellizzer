Buddleja crispa var. glandulifera

Buddleja crispa var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular buddleja, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
