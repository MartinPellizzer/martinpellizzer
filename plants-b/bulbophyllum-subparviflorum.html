Bulbophyllum subparviflorum

Bulbophyllum subparviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
