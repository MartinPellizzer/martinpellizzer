Begonia carletonii

Begonia carletonii, commonly known as the Carleton's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, adding a unique texture to its appearance.

Begonia carletonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown as a decorative plant in both gardens and homes.
