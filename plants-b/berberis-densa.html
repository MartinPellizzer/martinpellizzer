Berberis densa

Berberis densa, commonly known as the dense barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Berberis densa is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
