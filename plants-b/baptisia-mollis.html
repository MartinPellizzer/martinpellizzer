Baptisia mollis

Baptisia mollis, commonly known as the eastern false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. Baptisia mollis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
