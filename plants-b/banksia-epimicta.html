Banksia epimicta

Banksia epimicta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Banksia epimicta is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native wildlife species.
