Bougainvillea praecox var. rhombifolia

Bougainvillea praecox var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate bougainvillea, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant clusters of colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant features narrow, rhombus-shaped leaves that give it its variety name. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
