Betula nana subsp. exilis

Betula nana subsp.

exilis, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its low growth habit, typically reaching heights of less than 50 centimeters.

The plant has smooth, grayish bark and oval to elliptic leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters long. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Betula nana subsp.

exilis plays an important ecological role in tundra and boreal ecosystems.
