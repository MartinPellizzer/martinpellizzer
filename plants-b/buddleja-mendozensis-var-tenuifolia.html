Buddleja mendozensis var. tenuifolia

Buddleja mendozensis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buddleja, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
