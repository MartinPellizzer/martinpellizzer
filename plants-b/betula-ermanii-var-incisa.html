Betula ermanii var. incisa

Betula ermanii var. incisa, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, white bark that peels in papery strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by small, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
