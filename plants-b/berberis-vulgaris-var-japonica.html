Berberis vulgaris var. japonica

Berberis vulgaris var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is characterized by its thorny stems and adaptability to various soil types.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
