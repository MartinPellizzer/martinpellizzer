Bromus hookerianus var. minor

Bromus hookerianus var. minor, commonly known as Hooker's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
