Benzoin trilobum var. pilosum

Benzoin trilobum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy benzoin, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is often harvested for commercial use. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, hairy glands.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance.
