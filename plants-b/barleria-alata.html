Barleria alata

Barleria alata, commonly known as the spreading barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria alata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
