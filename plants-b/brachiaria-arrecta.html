Brachiaria arrecta

Brachiaria arrecta, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Brachiaria arrecta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth and adaptability.
