Bleasdalea bleasdalei

Bleasdalea bleasdalei, commonly known as the New Zealand rock fern, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rugged alpine regions of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and dark green, leathery fronds that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing on rocky outcrops or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sporangia, are clustered at the tips of its fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Bleasdalea bleasdalei is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
