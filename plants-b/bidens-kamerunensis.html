Bidens kamerunensis

Bidens kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps and riverbanks, and it can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bidens kamerunensis is also known for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
