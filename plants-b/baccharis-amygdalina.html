Baccharis amygdalina

Baccharis amygdalina, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small white flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Baccharis amygdalina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
