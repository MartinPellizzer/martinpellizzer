Briza dura

Briza dura, commonly known as rigid false oat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Briza dura is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
