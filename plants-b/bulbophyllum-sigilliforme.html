Bulbophyllum sigilliforme

Bulbophyllum sigilliforme, commonly known as the seal-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique, seal-like shape, which gives it its common name. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat.

The orchid's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
