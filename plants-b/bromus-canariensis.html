Bromus canariensis

Bromus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a species of grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Bromus canariensis is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
