Bromopsis pumpelliana var. barguzinensis

Bromopsis pumpelliana var. barguzinensis, commonly known as the Barguzin bromus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

This species is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
