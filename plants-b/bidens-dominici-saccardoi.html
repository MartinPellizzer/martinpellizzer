Bidens dominici-saccardoi

Bidens dominici-saccardoi, commonly known as the Dominican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Bidens dominici-saccardoi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
