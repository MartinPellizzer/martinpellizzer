Baccharis erosoricola

Baccharis erosoricola, commonly known as the Brazilian bristleweed, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is adapted to humid tropical climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity make it a notable species in its native habitat.
