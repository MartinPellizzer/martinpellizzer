Blatti griffithii

Blatti griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's blister beetle, is a species of beetle belonging to the family Meloidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical body and the presence of a soft, segmented abdomen. The beetle is typically black or dark brown in color, with a distinctive hardened prothorax. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

While not a plant, it is sometimes mistaken for a plant due to its appearance and habitat.
