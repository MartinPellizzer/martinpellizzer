Begonia bracteata

Begonia bracteata, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.

Begonia bracteata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
