Brachycarpaea juncea

Brachycarpaea juncea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plant adaptations.
