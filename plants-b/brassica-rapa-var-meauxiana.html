Brassica rapa var. meauxiana

Brassica rapa var. meauxiana, commonly known as the Meaux cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and compact rosette growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its roots are often harvested for their edible, crisp texture and mild flavor.

It is native to Europe and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
