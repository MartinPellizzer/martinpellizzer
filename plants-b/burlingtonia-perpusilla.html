Burlingtonia perpusilla

Burlingtonia perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, making it suitable for container gardening or as a ground cover. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Burlingtonia perpusilla thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
