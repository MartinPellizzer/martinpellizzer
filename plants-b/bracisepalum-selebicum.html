Bracisepalum selebicum

Bracisepalum selebicum, commonly known as the Sunda Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in tropical climates. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in various colors, including white, yellow, and orange, which attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Bracisepalum selebicum thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
