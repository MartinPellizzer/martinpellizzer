Batatas viscida

Batatas viscida, commonly known as the sticky batata or viscous sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its tuberous roots are typically yellow or orange in color and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is cultivated in some regions for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
