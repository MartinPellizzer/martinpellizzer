Bromelia inermis

Bromelia inermis, commonly known as the silver vase bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces an inflorescence that consists of a tall, spathaceous flower surrounded by a cluster of small, white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bromelia inermis is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
