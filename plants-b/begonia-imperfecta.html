Begonia imperfecta

Begonia imperfecta, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and white, adding ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
