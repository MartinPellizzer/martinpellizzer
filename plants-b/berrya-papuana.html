Berrya papuana

Berrya papuana, commonly known as the Papuan berry or mountain berry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations. Its berries are red when mature and are often consumed raw or used in local culinary traditions.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
