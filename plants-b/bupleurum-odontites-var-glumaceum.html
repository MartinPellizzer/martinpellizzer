Bupleurum odontites var. glumaceum

Bupleurum odontites var. glumaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
