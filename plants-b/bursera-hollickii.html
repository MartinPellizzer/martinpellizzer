Bursera hollickii

Bursera hollickii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant features smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
