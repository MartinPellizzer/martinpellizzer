Banisteria pubiflora

Banisteria pubiflora, commonly known as the silky banisteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
