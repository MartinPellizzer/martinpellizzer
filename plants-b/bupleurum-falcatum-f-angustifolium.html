Bupleurum falcatum f. angustifolium

Bupleurum falcatum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, angular stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
