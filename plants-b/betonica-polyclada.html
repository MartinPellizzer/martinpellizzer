Betonica polyclada

Betonica polyclada, commonly known as greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Betonica polyclada is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
