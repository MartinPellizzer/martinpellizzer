Berberis jamesiana var. saepium

Berberis jamesiana var. saepium, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant seasonal color changes and hardy nature.
