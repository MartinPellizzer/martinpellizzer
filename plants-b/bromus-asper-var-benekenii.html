Bromus asper var. benekenii

Bromus asper var. benekenii, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to the high-elevation regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
