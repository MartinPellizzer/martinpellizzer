Bellis americana

Bellis americana, commonly known as the American daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Bellis americana is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
