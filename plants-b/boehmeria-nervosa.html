Boehmeria nervosa

Boehmeria nervosa, commonly known as China grass, is a flowering plant in the nettle family, Urticaceae.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its fibrous, soft-stemmed structure and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is widely cultivated for its strong, durable fibers used in textiles and papermaking. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its scientific name.

Boehmeria nervosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
