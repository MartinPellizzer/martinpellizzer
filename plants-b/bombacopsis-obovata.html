Bombacopsis obovata

Bombacopsis obovata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn bright yellow before falling. The tree produces showy, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
