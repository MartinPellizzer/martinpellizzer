Bupleurum semicompositum var. pseudodontites

Bupleurum semicompositum var. pseudodontites, commonly known as the false toothed bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, exhibiting a bushy growth habit with numerous branched stems. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
