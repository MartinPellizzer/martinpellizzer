Bulbophyllum jacquetii

Bulbophyllum jacquetii, commonly known as the Jacquet's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its bulbs, which are essential for its survival, store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.
