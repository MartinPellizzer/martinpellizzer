Bouvardia hirtella var. quaternifolia

Bouvardia hirtella var. quaternifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf bouvardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
