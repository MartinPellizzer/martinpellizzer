Begonia corzoensis

Begonia corzoensis, commonly known as the Corzo's begonia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a visually striking addition to gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
