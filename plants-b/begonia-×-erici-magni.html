Begonia × erici-magni

Begonia × erici-magni, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia ericifolia and Begonia magnifica.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening. It thrives in partial shade to bright indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
