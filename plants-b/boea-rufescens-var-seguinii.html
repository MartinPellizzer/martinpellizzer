Boea rufescens var. seguinii

Boea rufescens var. seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover in gardens.
