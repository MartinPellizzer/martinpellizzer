Berberis rechingeri

Berberis rechingeri, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The species is also cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
