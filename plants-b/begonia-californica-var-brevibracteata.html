Begonia californica var. brevibracteata

Begonia californica var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the California begonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, bract-like structures that give it its specific variety name.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
