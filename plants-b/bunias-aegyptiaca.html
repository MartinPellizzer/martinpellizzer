Bunias aegyptiaca

Bunias aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its stout, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Bunias aegyptiaca is also valued for its peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in some traditional cuisines.
