Banisteria sellowiana

Banisteria sellowiana, commonly known as the red moringa or Brazilian trumpet tree, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its stems are woody and can grow quite long. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
