Blumea lanceolata

Blumea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shady areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Blumea lanceolata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
