Basanacantha spinifex

Basanacantha spinifex, commonly known as the spiny thornplant, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often adorned with spines for protection against herbivores.

Basanacantha spinifex is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
