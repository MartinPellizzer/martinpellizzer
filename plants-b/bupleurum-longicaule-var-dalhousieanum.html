Bupleurum longicaule var. dalhousieanum

Bupleurum longicaule var. dalhousieanum, commonly known as the Dalhousie's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
