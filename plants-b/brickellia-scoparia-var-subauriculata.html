Brickellia scoparia var. subauriculata

Brickellia scoparia var. subauriculata, commonly known as the silverleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
