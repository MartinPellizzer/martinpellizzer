Begonia laciniata subsp. difformis

Begonia laciniata subsp.

difformis, commonly known as the jagged-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
