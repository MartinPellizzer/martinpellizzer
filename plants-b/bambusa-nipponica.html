Bambusa nipponica

Bambusa nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and provide a lush appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa nipponica is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in temperate climates.
