Berberis vulgaris var. alba

Berberis vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as white barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its foliage consists of dark green, holly-like leaves with spiny margins.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
