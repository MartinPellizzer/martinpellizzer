Barleria longiflora

Barleria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Barleria longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
