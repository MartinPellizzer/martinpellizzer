Bambusa diffusa

Bambusa diffusa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a robust and hollow stem structure typical of bamboo species. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Bambusa diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
