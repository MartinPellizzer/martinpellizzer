Bahia neomexicana

Bahia neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
