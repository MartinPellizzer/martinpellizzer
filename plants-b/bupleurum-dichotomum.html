Bupleurum dichotomum

Bupleurum dichotomum, commonly known as the forked bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a reddish-brown coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a somewhat aromatic scent.

Bupleurum dichotomum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
