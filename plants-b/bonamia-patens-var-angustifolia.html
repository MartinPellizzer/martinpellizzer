Bonamia patens var. angustifolia

Bonamia patens var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bonamia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
