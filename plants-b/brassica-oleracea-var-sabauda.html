Brassica oleracea var. sabauda

Brassica oleracea var. sabauda, commonly known as the Brussels sprout, is a cool-season crop belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, cabbage-like buds that form along the stem. These buds are typically harvested when they reach a diameter of about 1 to 2 centimeters. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires a long growing season with consistent moisture.

Its botanical structure includes a thick, fibrous stem and leaves that are alternate and oblong in shape.
