Berberis guilache

Berberis guilache, commonly known as the Guianan barberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round structure in its natural habitat.

Berberis guilache is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
