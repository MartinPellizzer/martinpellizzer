Betonica nivea var. abchasica

Betonica nivea var. abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Betonica nivea var. abchasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
