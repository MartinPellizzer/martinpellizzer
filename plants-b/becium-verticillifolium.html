Becium verticillifolium

Becium verticillifolium, commonly known as the spiralleaf becium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
