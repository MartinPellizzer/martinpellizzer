Berberis × provincialis

Berberis × provincialis, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The hybrid is a cross between Berberis julianae and Berberis corylifolia, showcasing a mix of traits from both parent species.
