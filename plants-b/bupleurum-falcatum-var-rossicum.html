Bupleurum falcatum var. rossicum

Bupleurum falcatum var. rossicum, commonly known as Ross's goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, fern-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose, branched clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
