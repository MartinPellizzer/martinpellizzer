Bulbophyllum variifolium

Bulbophyllum variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
