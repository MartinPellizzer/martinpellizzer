Bossiaea ensata

Bossiaea ensata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Bossiaea ensata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
