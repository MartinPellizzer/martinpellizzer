Baccharis alamosana

Baccharis alamosana, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its green stems. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Baccharis alamosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
