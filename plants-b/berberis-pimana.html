Berberis pimana

Berberis pimana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Berberis pimana thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
