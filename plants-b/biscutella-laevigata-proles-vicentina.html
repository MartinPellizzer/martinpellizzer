Biscutella laevigata proles vicentina

Biscutella laevigata proles vicentina, commonly known as the Vicentine biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soils.
