Begonia andina

Begonia andina, commonly known as the Andean begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant prefers cool, humid conditions and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shade gardens or terrariums. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia andina is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
