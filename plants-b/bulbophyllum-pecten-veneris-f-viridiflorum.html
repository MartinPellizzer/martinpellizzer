Bulbophyllum pecten-veneris f. viridiflorum

Bulbophyllum pecten-veneris f. viridiflorum, commonly known as the Venus' comb orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, comb-like inflorescence that resembles the shell of the Venus shell, giving it its common name. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the wet season.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
