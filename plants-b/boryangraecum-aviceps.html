Boryangraecum aviceps

Boryangraecum aviceps, commonly known as the Cape rose or the coastal rose, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are typically small and borne in dense inflorescences, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Boryangraecum aviceps is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
