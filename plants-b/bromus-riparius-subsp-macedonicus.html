Bromus riparius subsp. macedonicus

Bromus riparius subsp.

macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian riparian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is adapted to riparian habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
