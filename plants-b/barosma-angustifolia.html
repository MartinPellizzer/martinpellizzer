Barosma angustifolia

Barosma angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved barosma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Barosma angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
