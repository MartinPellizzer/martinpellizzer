Barleria integrisepala

Barleria integrisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Barleria integrisepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
