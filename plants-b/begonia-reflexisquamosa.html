Begonia reflexisquamosa

Begonia reflexisquamosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
