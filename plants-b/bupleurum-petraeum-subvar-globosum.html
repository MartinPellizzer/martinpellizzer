Bupleurum petraeum subvar. globosum

Bupleurum petraeum subvar. globosum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
