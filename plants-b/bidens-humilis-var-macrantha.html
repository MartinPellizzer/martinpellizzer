Bidens humilis var. macrantha

Bidens humilis var. macrantha, commonly known as the largeflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its flowers are notable for their size and showy appearance, which attract various pollinators.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
