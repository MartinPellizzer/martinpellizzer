Bulbophyllum karreri

Bulbophyllum karreri, commonly known as the Karrer's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.
