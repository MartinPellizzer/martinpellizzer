Boea lancifolia

Boea lancifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that have a silvery sheen. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Boea lancifolia is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its preference for moist, well-drained conditions.
