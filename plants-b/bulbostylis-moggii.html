Bulbostylis moggii

Bulbostylis moggii, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that are arranged in dense spikelets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
