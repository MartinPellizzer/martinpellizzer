Bulbophyllum peniculus

Bulbophyllum peniculus, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feather-like inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers that come in various colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts.
