Blakea caudata

Blakea caudata, commonly known as the red-veined or red-leaf Blakea, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety, red-veined leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blakea caudata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
