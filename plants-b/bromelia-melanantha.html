Bromelia melanantha

Bromelia melanantha, commonly known as the black-leaved bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater, a feature common among bromeliads. The leaves are typically dark green to black in color, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom at the center of the leaf rosette.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
