Bupleurum falcatum subsp. flexuosum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
