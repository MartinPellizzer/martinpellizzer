Bulbophyllum apiferum

Bulbophyllum apiferum, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a bee, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Bulbophyllum apiferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
