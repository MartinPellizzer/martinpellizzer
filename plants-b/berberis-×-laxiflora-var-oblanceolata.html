Berberis × laxiflora var. oblanceolata

Berberis × laxiflora var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features elliptical to oblanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is native to regions of East Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in horticulture.

This variety is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
