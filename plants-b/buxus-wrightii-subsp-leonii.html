Buxus wrightii subsp. leonii

Buxus wrightii subsp.

leonii, commonly known as the Mexican boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to Mexico. It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate pruning make it suitable for hedges and topiaries.
