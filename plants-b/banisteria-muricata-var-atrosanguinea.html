Banisteria muricata var. atrosanguinea

Banisteria muricata var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

However, it is important to note that it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
