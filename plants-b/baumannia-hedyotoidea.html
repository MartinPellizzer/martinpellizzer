Baumannia hedyotoidea

Baumannia hedyotoidea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Baumannia hedyotoidea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
