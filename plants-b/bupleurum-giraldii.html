Bupleurum giraldii

Bupleurum giraldii, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Bupleurum giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
