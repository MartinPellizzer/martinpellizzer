Bromus carinatus var. arizonicus

Bromus carinatus var. arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona brome, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed areas, and rocky soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually erect and borne on a short stalk.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
