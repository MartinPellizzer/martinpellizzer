Bulbophyllum trifolium

Bulbophyllum trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical environments. The plant has three lance-shaped leaves that are clustered at the base, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, appearing in clusters of white or pale yellow.

Bulbophyllum trifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
