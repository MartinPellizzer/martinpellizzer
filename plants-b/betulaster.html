Betulaster

Betulaster, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub belonging to the family Rutaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Betulaster is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
