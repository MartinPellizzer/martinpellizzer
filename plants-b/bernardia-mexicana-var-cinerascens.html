Bernardia mexicana var. cinerascens

Bernardia mexicana var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray-flowered bernardia, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
