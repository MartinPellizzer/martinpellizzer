Bursera anderssonii

Bursera anderssonii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing resinous sap. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown of leathery, elliptical leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing one or two seeds.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used for various purposes by indigenous communities.
