Babiana mucronata var. minor

Babiana mucronata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf African bell orchid, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial herb features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
