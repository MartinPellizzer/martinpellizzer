Bosqueia orientalis

Bosqueia orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Bosque, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscaped areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blooms and evergreen foliage.
