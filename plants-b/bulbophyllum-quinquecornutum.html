Bulbophyllum quinquecornutum

Bulbophyllum quinquecornutum, commonly known as the five-angled bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
