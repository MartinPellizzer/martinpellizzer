Bromus erectus subsp. transsilvanicus

Bromus erectus subsp.

transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian brome, is a grass species native to Europe, particularly found in the Carpathian region. It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
