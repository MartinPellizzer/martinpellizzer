Bignonia modesta

Bignonia modesta, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth.

Bignonia modesta is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
