Baccharis grandiflora subsp. crassivestita

Baccharis grandiflora subsp.

crassivestita, commonly known as the broad-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
