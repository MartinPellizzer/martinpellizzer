Biscutella hirsuta

Biscutella hirsuta, commonly known as hairy biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, grayish-green leaves that are typically clustered at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Biscutella hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
