Baccharis quitensis

Baccharis quitensis, commonly known as the Andean baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or scrublands. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters.

Baccharis quitensis is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
