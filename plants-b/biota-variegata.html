Biota variegata

Biota variegata, commonly known as the variegated biota, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Biota variegata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and ease of care.
