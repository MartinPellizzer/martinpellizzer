Baccharis gracilis

Baccharis gracilis, commonly known as slender baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which appear in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas, dry forests, and disturbed soils.

Baccharis gracilis is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
