Bulbostylis coleotricha var. hirsuta

Bulbostylis coleotricha var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bulbostylis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This grass-like plant is often found in wetlands and along waterways, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
