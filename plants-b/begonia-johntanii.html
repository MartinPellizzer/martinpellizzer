Begonia johntanii

Begonia johntanii, commonly known as the John T. Howell begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to maroon hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative houseplant.
