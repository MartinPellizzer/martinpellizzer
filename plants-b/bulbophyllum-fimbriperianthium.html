Bulbophyllum fimbriperianthium

Bulbophyllum fimbriperianthium, commonly known as the fringed-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with delicate, fringed petals that give it its common name.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
