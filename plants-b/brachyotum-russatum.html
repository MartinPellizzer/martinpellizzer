Brachyotum russatum

Brachyotum russatum, commonly known as the Russian brachyotum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in the summer, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Brachyotum russatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
