Baumea mariscoides

Baumea mariscoides, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil in its wetland habitat.

Baumea mariscoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
