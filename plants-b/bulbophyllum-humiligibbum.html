Bulbophyllum humiligibbum

Bulbophyllum humiligibbum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
