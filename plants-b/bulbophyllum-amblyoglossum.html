Bulbophyllum amblyoglossum

Bulbophyllum amblyoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped bryophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it absorb moisture from the air. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
