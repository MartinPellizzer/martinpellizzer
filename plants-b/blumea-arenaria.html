Blumea arenaria

Blumea arenaria, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Blumea arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
