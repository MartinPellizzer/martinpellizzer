Berberis vulgaris var. crataegina

Berberis vulgaris var. crataegina, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features thorned stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense growth habit and vibrant autumn foliage.
