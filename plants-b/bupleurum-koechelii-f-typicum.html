Bupleurum koechelii f. typicum

Bupleurum koechelii f. typicum, commonly known as the Koechel's bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.
