Boronia capitata subsp. clavata

Boronia capitata subsp.

clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense, rounded heads.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This species is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
