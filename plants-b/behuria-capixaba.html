Behuria capixaba

Behuria capixaba, commonly known as the Capixaba tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
