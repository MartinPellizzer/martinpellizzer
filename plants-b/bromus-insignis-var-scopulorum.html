Bromus insignis var. scopulorum

Bromus insignis var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often grow in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish spikelets, which contain the seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in open, sunny areas such as slopes, plains, and disturbed sites.
