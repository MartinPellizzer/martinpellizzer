Balsamina comorensis

Balsamina comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, velvety texture, while the flowers are often borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Balsamina comorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant blooms.
