Barbacenia salmonea

Barbacenia salmonea, commonly known as the salmon flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, salmon-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Barbacenia salmonea is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique color and ability to add a tropical touch to landscapes.
