Bulbophyllum falcatum var. bufo

Bulbophyllum falcatum var. bufo, commonly known as the frog's tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, lance-shaped flowers that bloom sequentially.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist conditions.
