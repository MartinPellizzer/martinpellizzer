Becium obovatum

Becium obovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf becium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, obovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Becium obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
