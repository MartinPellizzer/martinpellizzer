Bergera macrophylla

Bergera macrophylla, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a woody, shrub-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Bergera macrophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
