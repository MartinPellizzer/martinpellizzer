Begonia pseudodryadis var. bitepala

Begonia pseudodryadis var. bitepala, commonly known as the twin-leaf begonia, is a unique hybrid plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
