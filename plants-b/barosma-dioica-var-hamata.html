Barosma dioica var. hamata

Barosma dioica var. hamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barosma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
