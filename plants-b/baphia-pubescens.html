Baphia pubescens

Baphia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences, and are typically yellow or greenish in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
