Brunoniella acaulis

Brunoniella acaulis, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops and slopes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern to conserve moisture.

Brunoniella acaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
