Baccharis lundii var. punctigera

Baccharis lundii var. punctigera, commonly known as the punctate baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or yellowish dot along the midrib, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing copious amounts of wind-dispersed seeds.
