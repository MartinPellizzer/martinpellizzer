Bromus macounii

Bromus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, riparian areas, and open woodlands. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, bearing spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to reddish-brown flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairless, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus macounii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
