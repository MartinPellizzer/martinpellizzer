Betula × ungavensis

Betula × ungavensis, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a cross between the paper birch (Betula papyrifera) and the white birch (Betula pendula).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate climates, characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, and its leaves are oval with serrated edges. Betula × ungavensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking bark and graceful form.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for parks and gardens.
