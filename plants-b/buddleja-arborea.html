Buddleja arborea

Buddleja arborea, commonly known as the tree buddleia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja arborea is well adapted to coastal and sunny environments, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in gardens.
