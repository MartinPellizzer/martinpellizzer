Barbarea vulgaris subsp. pinnata

Barbarea vulgaris subsp.

pinnata, commonly known as the common winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and moist environments.

The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive yellow or greenish color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
