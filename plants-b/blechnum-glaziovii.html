Blechnum glaziovii

Blechnum glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's holly fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive appearance that includes a central rachis and tightly packed, lanceolate pinnae.

Blechnum glaziovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
