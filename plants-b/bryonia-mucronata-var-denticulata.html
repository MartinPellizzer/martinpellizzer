Bryonia mucronata var. denticulata

Bryonia mucronata var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate bryony, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into spiny, orange-red fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided by livestock and humans.
