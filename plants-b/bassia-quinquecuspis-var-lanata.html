Bassia quinquecuspis var. lanata

Bassia quinquecuspis var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly jointedweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments.
