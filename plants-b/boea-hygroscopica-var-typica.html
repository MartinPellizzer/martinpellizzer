Boea hygroscopica var. typica

Boea hygroscopica var. typica, commonly known as the silver thread vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardens and trellises.
