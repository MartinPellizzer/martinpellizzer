Baccharis helichrysoides

Baccharis helichrysoides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
