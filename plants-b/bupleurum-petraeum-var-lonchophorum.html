Bupleurum petraeum var. lonchophorum

Bupleurum petraeum var. lonchophorum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
