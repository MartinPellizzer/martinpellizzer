Butonica alata

Butonica alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in scrubland or woodland habitats. Butonica alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
