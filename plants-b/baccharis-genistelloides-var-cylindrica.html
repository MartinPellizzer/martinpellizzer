Baccharis genistelloides var. cylindrica

Baccharis genistelloides var. cylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
