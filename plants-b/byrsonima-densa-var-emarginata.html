Byrsonima densa var. emarginata

Byrsonima densa var. emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
