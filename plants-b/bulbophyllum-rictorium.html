Bulbophyllum rictorium

Bulbophyllum rictorium, commonly known as the Rictor's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
