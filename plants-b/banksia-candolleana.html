Banksia candolleana

Banksia candolleana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Banksia, is a species native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow-orange flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The flower spikes attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
