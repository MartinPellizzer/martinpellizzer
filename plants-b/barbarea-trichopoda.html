Barbarea trichopoda

Barbarea trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a cylindrical silique containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
