Bupleurum linczevskii

Bupleurum linczevskii, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing at high altitudes.

Bupleurum linczevskii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
