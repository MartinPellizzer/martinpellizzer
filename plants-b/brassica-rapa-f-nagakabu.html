Brassica rapa f. nagakabu

Brassica rapa f. nagakabu, commonly known as the Japanese radish or takana, is a cultivated variant of the wild radish.

It is characterized by its swollen, cylindrical root that is typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a vegetable for its crisp, mildly spicy root.

The leaves are also edible and can be used in various culinary preparations.
