Begonia vagans

Begonia vagans, commonly known as the trailing begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Begonia vagans thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardens.

Its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season adds to its value as a decorative plant.
