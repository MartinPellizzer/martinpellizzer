Blepharodon asparagoides

Blepharodon asparagoides, commonly known as the African lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in subtropical and temperate regions.
