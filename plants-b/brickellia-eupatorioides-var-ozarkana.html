Brickellia eupatorioides var. ozarkana

Brickellia eupatorioides var. ozarkana, commonly known as Ozark brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as prairies and limestone glades. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
