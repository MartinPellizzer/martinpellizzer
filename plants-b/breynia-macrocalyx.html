Breynia macrocalyx

Breynia macrocalyx, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, making it a visually appealing species. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Breynia macrocalyx is also noted for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions, making it suitable for seaside gardens.
