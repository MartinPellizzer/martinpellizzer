Brassica-napus sylvestris

Brassica-napus sylvestris, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy leaves and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated pods typical of the mustard family.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.
