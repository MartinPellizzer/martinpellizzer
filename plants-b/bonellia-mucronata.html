Bonellia mucronata

Bonellia mucronata, commonly known as the slender bonellia, is a marine polychaete worm found in tropical and subtropical waters.

It belongs to the family Bonellidae and is characterized by its elongated, segmented body and lack of a distinct head. The plant-like appearance of its anterior end, with a root-like structure, is a notable botanical feature. It does not perform photosynthesis and relies on its symbiotic relationship with bacteria for nutrition.

Bonellia mucronata plays a role in marine ecosystems as a detritivore and contributes to nutrient cycling in coastal environments.
