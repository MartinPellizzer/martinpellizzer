Byrsonima microphylla

Byrsonima microphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian plum or kiri fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
