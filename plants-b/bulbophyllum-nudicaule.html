Bulbophyllum nudicaule

Bulbophyllum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in conserving moisture.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
