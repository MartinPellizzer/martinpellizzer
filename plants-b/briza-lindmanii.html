Briza lindmanii

Briza lindmanii, commonly known as Lindman's quaking grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its delicate, nodding inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Briza lindmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
