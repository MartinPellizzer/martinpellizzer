Bulbophyllum dagamense

Bulbophyllum dagamense, commonly known as the Dagamense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
