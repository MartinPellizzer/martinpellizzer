Batrachium hederaceum subsp. coenosum

Batrachium hederaceum subsp.

coenosum, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively along the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
