Brassica timoriana

Brassica timoriana, commonly known as Timor mustard, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Brassica timoriana is valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
