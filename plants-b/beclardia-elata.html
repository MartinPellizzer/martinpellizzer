Beclardia elata

Beclardia elata, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Beclardia elata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
