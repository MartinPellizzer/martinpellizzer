Byrsonima poeppigiana

Byrsonima poeppigiana, commonly known as the "Brazilian cherry" or "pata de vaca," is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional properties.
