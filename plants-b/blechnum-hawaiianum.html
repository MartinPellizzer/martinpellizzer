Blechnum hawaiianum

Blechnum hawaiianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian shield fern, is a fern species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates.

Blechnum hawaiianum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
