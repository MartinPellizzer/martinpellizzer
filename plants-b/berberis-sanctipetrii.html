Berberis sanctipetrii

Berberis sanctipetrii, commonly known as the Saint Peter's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
