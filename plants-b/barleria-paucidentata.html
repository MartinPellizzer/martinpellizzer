Barleria paucidentata

Barleria paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
