Beccariella lucens

Beccariella lucens, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in fascicles. The plant's leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. Beccariella lucens is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
