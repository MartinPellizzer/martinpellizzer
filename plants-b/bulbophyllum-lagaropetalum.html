Bulbophyllum lagaropetalum

Bulbophyllum lagaropetalum, commonly known as the orchid with the butterfly-like petals, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, often with a strong, sweet fragrance.

The leaves are usually fleshy and green, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.
