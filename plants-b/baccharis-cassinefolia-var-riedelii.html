Baccharis cassinefolia var. riedelii

Baccharis cassinefolia var. riedelii, commonly known as Riedel's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the genus Baccharis.

This species is often found in dry, disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
