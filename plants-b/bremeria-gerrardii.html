Bremeria gerrardii

Bremeria gerrardii, commonly known as the Texas aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bremeria gerrardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
