Buddleja lythroides

Buddleja lythroides, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buddleja lythroides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
