Bursera kerberi

Bursera kerberi, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
