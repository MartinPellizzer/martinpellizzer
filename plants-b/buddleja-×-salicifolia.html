Buddleja × salicifolia

Buddleja × salicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its ability to attract a wide variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja × salicifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of care.
