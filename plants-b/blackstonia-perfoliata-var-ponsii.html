Blackstonia perfoliata var. ponsii

Blackstonia perfoliata var. ponsii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the botanist John Blackston.
