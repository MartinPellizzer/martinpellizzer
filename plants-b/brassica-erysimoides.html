Brassica erysimoides

Brassica erysimoides, commonly known as the yellow mustardweed, is an annual or biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Brassica erysimoides is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
