Brachypodium sanctum

Brachypodium sanctum, commonly known as sacred brome, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Brachypodium sanctum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
