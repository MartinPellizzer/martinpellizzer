Bulbocodium eichleri

Bulbocodium eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri's bulbous cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, spherical fruits known as siliques, which contain several seeds.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
