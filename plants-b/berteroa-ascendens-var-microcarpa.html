Berteroa ascendens var. microcarpa

Berteroa ascendens var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and small, blue to purple flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
