Bactris integrifolia

Bactris integrifolia, commonly known as the pink pataua or pink pataua palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for landscaping and agricultural purposes.
