Bromus sitchensis var. polyanthus

Bromus sitchensis var. polyanthus, commonly known as the Sitka brome, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with pink or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
