Begonia stilandra

Begonia stilandra, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with proper care.

Begonia stilandra is also noted for its ability to tolerate dry conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
