Becium citriodorum

Becium citriodorum, commonly known as the lemon-scented becium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry and coastal conditions.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
