Buena acutifolia

Buena acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
