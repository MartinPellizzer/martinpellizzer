Begonia taniana

Begonia taniana, commonly known as the Tanian begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
