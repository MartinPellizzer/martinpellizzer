Begonia pseudomuricata

Begonia pseudomuricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy, and textured leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
