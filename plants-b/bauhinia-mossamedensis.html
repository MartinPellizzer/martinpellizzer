Bauhinia mossamedensis

Bauhinia mossamedensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

Bauhinia mossamedensis also has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility.
