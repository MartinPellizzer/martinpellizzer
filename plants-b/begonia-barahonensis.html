Begonia barahonensis

Begonia barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahon begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
