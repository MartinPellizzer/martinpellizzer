Bulbophyllum discolor

Bulbophyllum discolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a striking contrast in coloration.

The plant's ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
