Betonica drymophila

Betonica drymophila, commonly known as the alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Betonica drymophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
