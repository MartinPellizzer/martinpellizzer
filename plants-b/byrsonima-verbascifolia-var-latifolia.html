Byrsonima verbascifolia var. latifolia

Byrsonima verbascifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf snowbell, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and velvety. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
