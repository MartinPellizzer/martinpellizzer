Biarum auraniticum

Biarum auraniticum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking red or orange throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Biarum auraniticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
