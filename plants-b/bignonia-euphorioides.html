Bignonia euphorioides

Bignonia euphorioides, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside and produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers resembling those of the genus *Euphorbia*. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, light green bark and can grow up to 10 feet tall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
