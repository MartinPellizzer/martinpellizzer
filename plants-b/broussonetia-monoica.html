Broussonetia monoica

Broussonetia monoica, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Broussonetia monoica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
