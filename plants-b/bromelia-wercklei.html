Bromelia wercklei

Bromelia wercklei, commonly known as the red-tipped bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the growth of its striking inflorescence. Its flowers, often bright red or orange, bloom in the center of the rosette and are surrounded by showy bracts.

Bromelia wercklei is popular as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
