Barbacenia ionantha

Barbacenia ionantha, commonly known as the velvet tunicate or the glassy leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to purple, with intricate vein patterns. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Barbacenia ionantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.
