Baccharis lanuginosa

Baccharis lanuginosa, commonly known as silky baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Baccharis lanuginosa plays a role in providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
