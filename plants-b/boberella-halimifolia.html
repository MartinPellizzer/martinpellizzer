Boberella halimifolia

Boberella halimifolia, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
