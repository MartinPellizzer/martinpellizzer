Brassica spinescens

Brassica spinescens, commonly known as the prickly mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Brassica spinescens is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
