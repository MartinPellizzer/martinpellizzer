Bifrenaria caparaoensis

Bifrenaria caparaoensis, commonly known as the Caparaó bifrenaria, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Brazilian state of Espírito Santo.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescences. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces spadices that are typically green with a yellow or white spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered and is of significant interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
