Brachystegia appendiculata

Brachystegia appendiculata, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for reforestation and soil conservation efforts.
