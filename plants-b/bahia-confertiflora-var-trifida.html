Bahia confertiflora var. trifida

Bahia confertiflora var. trifida, commonly known as the three-leaf bahia, is a tropical grass species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and can produce small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for warm, humid climates and full sun exposure.
