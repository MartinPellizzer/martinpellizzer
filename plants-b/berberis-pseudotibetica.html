Berberis pseudotibetica

Berberis pseudotibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of China, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Berberis pseudotibetica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
