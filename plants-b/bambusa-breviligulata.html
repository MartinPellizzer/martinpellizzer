Bambusa breviligulata

Bambusa breviligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.

Bambusa breviligulata is also notable for its ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for erosion control in horticulture.
