Bulbophyllum chalcochloron

Bulbophyllum chalcochloron, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic or lithophytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting to its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
