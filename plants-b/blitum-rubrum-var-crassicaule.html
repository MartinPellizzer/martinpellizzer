Blitum rubrum var. crassicaule

Blitum rubrum var. crassicaule, commonly known as broad-leaved blitum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and flowering habits.
