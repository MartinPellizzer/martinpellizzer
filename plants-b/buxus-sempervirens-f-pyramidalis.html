Buxus sempervirens f. pyramidalis

Buxus sempervirens f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the upright boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, narrow, pyramid-shaped growth habit.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant is native to western Europe and is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant for hedges and topiary. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape.

Due to its hardy nature, it is suitable for a variety of garden settings, including rock gardens and borders.
