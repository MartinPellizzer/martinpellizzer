Barleriola saturejoides subsp. acunae

Barleriola saturejoides subsp.

acunae, commonly known as the Acuna's narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, pink or white flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow leaves and specific geographic distribution within the Andes.
