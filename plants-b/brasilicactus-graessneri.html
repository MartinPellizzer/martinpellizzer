Brasilicactus graessneri

Brasilicactus graessneri, commonly known as the Graessner's cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and sparse, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
