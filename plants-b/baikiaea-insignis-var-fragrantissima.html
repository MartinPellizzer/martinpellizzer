Baikiaea insignis var. fragrantissima

Baikiaea insignis var. fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant African mahogany, is a species of tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.
