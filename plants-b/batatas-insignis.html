Batatas insignis

Batatas insignis, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are typically purple in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and unique root coloration.
