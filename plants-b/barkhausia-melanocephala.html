Barkhausia melanocephala

Barkhausia melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This perennial herb features a compact, upright growth habit with a short, fibrous rhizome and a cluster of dark green, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, dark purple to black flowers with a distinctive yellow lip, which are arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation programs.
