Berinia jubata

Berinia jubata, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Berinia jubata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
