Benzoin grandifolium

Benzoin grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf benzoin, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic properties. This resin, known as benjamin, is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
