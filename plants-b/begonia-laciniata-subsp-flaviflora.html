Begonia laciniata subsp. flaviflora

Begonia laciniata subsp.

flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with red or orange bracts.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped or lobed, with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in both indoor and outdoor settings.
