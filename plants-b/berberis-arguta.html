Berberis arguta

Berberis arguta, commonly known as the prickly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis arguta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
