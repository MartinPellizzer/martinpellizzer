Buergersiochloa macrophylla

Buergersiochloa macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed grass or *B. macrophylla*, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, erect culms with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and have a bluish-green hue, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
