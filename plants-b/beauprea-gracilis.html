Beauprea gracilis

Beauprea gracilis, commonly known as the slender beauprea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

Beauprea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
