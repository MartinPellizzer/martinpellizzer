Buddleja dysophylla

Buddleja dysophylla, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. Buddleja dysophylla is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a striking visual element to gardens during the warmer months.
