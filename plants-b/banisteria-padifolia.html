Banisteria padifolia

Banisteria padifolia, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and violet flowers, which are often visited by hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green with a velvety texture. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Banisteria padifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
