Boraginella khasiana

Boraginella khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

It plays a significant role in the local flora and is often found in understory vegetation.
