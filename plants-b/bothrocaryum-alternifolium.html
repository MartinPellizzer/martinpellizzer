Bothrocaryum alternifolium

Bothrocaryum alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaved sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often covered in fine hairs.

Bothrocaryum alternifolium is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and succulence to store water.
