Bignonia filicifolia

Bignonia filicifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 20 feet long, often climbing or trailing along structures. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its rapid growth and showy blooms.
