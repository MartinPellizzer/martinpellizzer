Byrsonima laevis

Byrsonima laevis, commonly known as the white cedar or Brazilian white cedar, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and is valued for its durable wood.

The species also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
