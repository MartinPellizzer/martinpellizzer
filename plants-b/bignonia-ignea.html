Bignonia ignea

Bignonia ignea, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it both visually striking and relatively easy to cultivate.
