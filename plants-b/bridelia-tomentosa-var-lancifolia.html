Bridelia tomentosa var. lancifolia

Bridelia tomentosa var. lancifolia, commonly known as the woolly bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
