Bulbophyllum simondii

Bulbophyllum simondii, commonly known as the Simond's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its rhizome system allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
