Betula alba f. dalecarlica

Betula alba f. dalecarlica, commonly known as the white birch of Dalecarlia, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its unique bark patterns and growth habits.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe, particularly in Sweden and Norway. The plant exhibits a slender, upright growth form with smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. Its leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
