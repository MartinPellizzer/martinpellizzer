Bromus optimae

Bromus optimae, commonly known as the optimal brome, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus optimae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
