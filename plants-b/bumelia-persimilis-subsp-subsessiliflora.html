Bumelia persimilis subsp. subsessiliflora

Bumelia persimilis subsp.

subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, typical of the genus Bumelia. Its fruits are follicles that mature into small, leathery pods containing seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf stalks and is often found in moist, wooded habitats.
