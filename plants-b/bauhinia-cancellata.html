Bauhinia cancellata

Bauhinia cancellata, commonly known as the lacewood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble lace, with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

Bauhinia cancellata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
