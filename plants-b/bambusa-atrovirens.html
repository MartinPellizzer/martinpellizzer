Bambusa atrovirens

Bambusa atrovirens, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark green to blackish stems that darken with age. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, often glossy surface.

Bambusa atrovirens is valued for ornamental purposes and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
