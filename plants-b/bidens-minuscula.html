Bidens minuscula

Bidens minuscula, commonly known as the small beggar's tick, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers with ray florets that bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant has hairy, slender stems and linear leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Bidens minuscula is known for its ability to spread via seeds that attach to animal fur or clothing, aiding in its dispersal.
