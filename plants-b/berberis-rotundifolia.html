Berberis rotundifolia

Berberis rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
