Berberis acanthifolia

Berberis acanthifolia, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and distinctive thorns.
