Braya thomsonii var. pilosa

Braya thomsonii var. pilosa, commonly known as Thomson's braya, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important component of its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
