Barnadesia spinosa

Barnadesia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny barnadesia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Barnadesia spinosa is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can thrive with minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
