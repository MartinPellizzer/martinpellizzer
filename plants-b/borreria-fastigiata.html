Borreria fastigiata

Borreria fastigiata, commonly known as the upright borreria, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the dry season. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a branching structure that supports its ornamental value in gardens.

Borreria fastigiata is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
