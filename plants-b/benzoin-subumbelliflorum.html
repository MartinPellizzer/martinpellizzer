Benzoin subumbelliflorum

Benzoin subumbelliflorum, commonly known as the fragrant benzoin tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that emit a strong, balsamic scent. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its species name. Its resin, known as benzoin, has been used for centuries in incense and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
