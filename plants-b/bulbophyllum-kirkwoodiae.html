Bulbophyllum kirkwoodiae

Bulbophyllum kirkwoodiae, commonly known as the Kirkwood's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often arranged in a raceme. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
