Brachyglottis monroi var. elongata

Brachyglottis monroi var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed brachyglottis, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
