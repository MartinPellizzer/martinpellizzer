Boronia crenulata var. gracilis

Boronia crenulata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and heathland environments. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a compact and upright growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
