Bambusa orientalis

Bambusa orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense stands, making it suitable for ornamental and landscaping purposes.

The plant is also valued for its strong and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
