Begonia valdensium

Begonia valdensium, commonly known as the Valdensian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Begonia valdensium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
