Blumea anagallidifolia

Blumea anagallidifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
