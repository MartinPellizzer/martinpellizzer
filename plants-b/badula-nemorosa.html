Badula nemorosa

Badula nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Badula nemorosa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
