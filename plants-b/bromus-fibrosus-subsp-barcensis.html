Bromus fibrosus subsp. barcensis

Bromus fibrosus subsp.

barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, fibrous stalks that often have a hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
