Boraginella physaloides

Boraginella physaloides, commonly known as the bladder mustard, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by its often hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and dry conditions.

Boraginella physaloides is also notable for its seed pods, which resemble bladders and can burst to disperse seeds.
