Betula pendula subsp. mandshurica

Betula pendula subsp.

mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian weeping birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, and its bark is smooth and often pale in color.

The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
