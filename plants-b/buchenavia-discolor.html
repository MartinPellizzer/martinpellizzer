Buchenavia discolor

Buchenavia discolor, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits containing a single seed.

Buchenavia discolor is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
