Bactris concinna var. sigmoidea

Bactris concinna var. sigmoidea, commonly known as the giant panda palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. Its trunk is typically segmented and covered with a rough, fibrous bark.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its impressive size and distinctive appearance.
