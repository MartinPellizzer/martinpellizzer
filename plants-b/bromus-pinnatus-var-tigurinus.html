Bromus pinnatus var. tigurinus

Bromus pinnatus var. tigurinus, commonly known as the tigurinus brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
