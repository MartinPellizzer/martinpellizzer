Begonia brevicaulis

Begonia brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
