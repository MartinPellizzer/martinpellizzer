Brachionidium piuntzae

Brachionidium piuntzae, commonly known as the Piuntza brachionidium, is a species of aquatic rotifer found in freshwater environments.

It belongs to the family Brachionidae and is characterized by its small, transparent body with a distinctive head and a crown of cilia used for feeding. The organism is typically less than 1 millimeter in length and plays a role in the microfood web by grazing on algae and bacteria. It is often found in temporary pools and is an indicator species for certain aquatic habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to researchers studying biodiversity and ecosystem health.
