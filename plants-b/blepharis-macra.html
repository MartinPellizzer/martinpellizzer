Blepharis macra

Blepharis macra, commonly known as the common blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it an important component of local flora.
