Bernardia dichotoma var. macrocarpa

Bernardia dichotoma var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited Bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
