Bulbocodium dichotomum

Bulbocodium dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous bulbous plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native flora.
