Brachyloma daphnoides subsp. daphnoides

Brachyloma daphnoides subsp.

daphnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features small, aromatic leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive, daphne-like appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
