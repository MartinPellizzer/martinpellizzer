Begonia poeppigiana

Begonia poeppigiana, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
