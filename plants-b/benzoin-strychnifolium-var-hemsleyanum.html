Benzoin strychnifolium var. hemsleyanum

Benzoin strychnifolium var. hemsleyanum, commonly known as Hemsley's benzoin, is a member of the Styracaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by aromatic, resinous gum which is harvested for its balsamic scent.

This variety is valued for its unique chemical composition and traditional uses in perfumery and medicine.
