Bombacopsis pachiroides

Bombacopsis pachiroides, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
