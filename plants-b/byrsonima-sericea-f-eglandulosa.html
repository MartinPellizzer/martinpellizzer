Byrsonima sericea f. eglandulosa

Byrsonima sericea f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular silky mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
