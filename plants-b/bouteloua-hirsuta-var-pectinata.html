Bouteloua hirsuta var. pectinata

Bouteloua hirsuta var. pectinata, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces fluffy, pinkish to reddish seed heads. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience.
