Basilicum myriostachyum

Basilicum myriostachyum, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct aromatic scent. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale purple, attracting pollinators like bees.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
