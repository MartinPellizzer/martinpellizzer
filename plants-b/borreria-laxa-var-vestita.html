Borreria laxa var. vestita

Borreria laxa var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet borreria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its unique texture and color make it a visually appealing addition to native plant gardens.
