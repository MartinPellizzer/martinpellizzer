Brassica napus var. brevirostris

Brassica napus var. brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked rape, is a variant of the oilseed rape species.

It is characterized by its compact, short beak-like structure on the siliqua, distinguishing it from other Brassica species. This variety is primarily cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agricultural systems, particularly for biodiesel production.
