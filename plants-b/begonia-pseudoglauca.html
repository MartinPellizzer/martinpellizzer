Begonia pseudoglauca

Begonia pseudoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish or silver sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
