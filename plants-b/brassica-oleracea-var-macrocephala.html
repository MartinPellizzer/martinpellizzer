Brassica oleracea var. macrocephala

Brassica oleracea var. macrocephala, commonly known as the cauliflower, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence of white or green flower buds that are harvested before they open. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. Its botanical structure includes thick, waxy leaves that form a protective layer around the central head.

The edible part is the tightly packed flower buds, which develop from the apical meristem.
