Bomarea chimboracensis

Bomarea chimboracensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bomarea, is a striking member of the Alismataceae family native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Bomarea chimboracensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
