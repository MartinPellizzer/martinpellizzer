Banisteriopsis ferruginea

Banisteriopsis ferruginea, commonly known as the red-bark bignon, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a reddish or rusty-colored bark, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
