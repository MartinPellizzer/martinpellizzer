Breynia reticulata

Breynia reticulata, commonly known as the laceleaf breynia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Breynia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
