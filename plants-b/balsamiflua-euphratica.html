Balsamiflua euphratica

Balsamiflua euphratica, commonly known as the Euphrates balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance that gives it its balsam-like scent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
