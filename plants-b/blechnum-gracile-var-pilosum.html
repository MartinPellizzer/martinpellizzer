Blechnum gracile var. pilosum

Blechnum gracile var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Blechnum gracile species.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
