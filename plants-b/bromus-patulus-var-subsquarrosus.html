Bromus patulus var. subsquarrosus

Bromus patulus var. subsquarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color, with a prominent midrib. The inflorescence consists of several spikelets, each containing two to three flowering glumes.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
