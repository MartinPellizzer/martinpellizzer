Beta vulgaris var. grisea

Beta vulgaris var. grisea, commonly known as the leaf beet or chard, is a variant of the beet plant characterized by its thick, fibrous leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green with a white or grayish underside, giving it its scientific name "grisea," which means gray in Latin. It is cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and farms.

Its botanical structure includes a short, thick root and a robust stem with broad, lobed leaves.
