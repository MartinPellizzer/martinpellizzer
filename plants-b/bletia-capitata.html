Bletia capitata

Bletia capitata, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Bletia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
