Bothriochloa parviflora

Bothriochloa parviflora, commonly known as slender grass or small-flowered bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in stabilizing landscapes.
