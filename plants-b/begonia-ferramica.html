Begonia ferramica

Begonia ferramica, commonly known as the Ferramica begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purplish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia ferramica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
