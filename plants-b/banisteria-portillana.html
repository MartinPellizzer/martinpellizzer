Banisteria portillana

Banisteria portillana, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated fruit that splits open when mature.

Banisteria portillana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
