Bulbophyllum karenkoensis

Bulbophyllum karenkoensis, commonly known as the Karenko's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, tropical conditions.

The flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors.
