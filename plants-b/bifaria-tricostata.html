Bifaria tricostata

Bifaria tricostata, commonly known as the three-costed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three prominent costae, giving it its scientific name.

Bifaria tricostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
