Betula × intermedia var. alpestris

Betula × intermedia var. alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine birch, is a hybrid species within the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, triangular leaves that are typically white or silver underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. It produces small, catkin-like flowers and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in cooler regions.
