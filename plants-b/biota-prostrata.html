Biota prostrata

Biota prostrata, commonly known as creeping biota, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Biota prostrata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
