Blysmus distichus

Blysmus distichus, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Blysmus distichus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for grazing animals and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
