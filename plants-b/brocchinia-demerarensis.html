Brocchinia demerarensis

Brocchinia demerarensis, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its unique rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, which is used to trap and digest insects. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, relying on the nutrients obtained from decomposed prey. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that prevents water loss and aids in the adhesion of prey.

Brocchinia demerarensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
