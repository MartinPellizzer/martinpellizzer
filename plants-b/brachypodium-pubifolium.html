Brachypodium pubifolium

Brachypodium pubifolium, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, sturdy stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachypodium pubifolium is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species in its native range.
