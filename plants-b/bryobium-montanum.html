Bryobium montanum

Bryobium montanum, commonly known as the mountain moss, is a member of the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its small, compact growth form.

It typically grows on rocky substrates in mountainous regions, thriving in harsh, dry environments. The plant has a dense, cushion-like structure with closely packed leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a thick layer of papillae. Bryobium montanum is well adapted to extreme conditions, including high altitude and low nutrient availability.

Its reproductive structures, such as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores under favorable conditions.
