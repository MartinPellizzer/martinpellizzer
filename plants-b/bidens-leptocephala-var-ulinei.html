Bidens leptocephala var. ulinei

Bidens leptocephala var. ulinei, commonly known as the narrow-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as roadside verges and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with distinctive ray florets, which are often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
