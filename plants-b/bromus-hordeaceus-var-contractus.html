Bromus hordeaceus var. contractus

Bromus hordeaceus var. contractus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in ecological succession.
