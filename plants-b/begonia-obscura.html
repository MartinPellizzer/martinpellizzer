Begonia obscura

Begonia obscura, commonly known as the obscure begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Begonia obscura is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
