Brachycorythis conica

Brachycorythis conica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its short, stout trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
