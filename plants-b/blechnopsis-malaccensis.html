Blechnopsis malaccensis

Blechnopsis malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca holly fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, lanceolate fronds that are glossy and dark green. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

The botanical characteristics of Blechnopsis malaccensis make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
