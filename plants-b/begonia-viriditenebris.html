Begonia viriditenebris

Begonia viriditenebris, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
