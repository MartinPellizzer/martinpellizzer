Baptisia auriculata

Baptisia auriculata, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Baptisia auriculata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
