Begonia chlorosticta

Begonia chlorosticta, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often marked with dark green veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in shaded garden areas.
