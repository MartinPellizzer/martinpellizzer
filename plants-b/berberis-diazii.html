Berberis diazii

Berberis diazii, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. Berberis diazii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
