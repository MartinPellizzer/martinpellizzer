Berberis buchananii

Berberis buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and attractive appearance, it is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
