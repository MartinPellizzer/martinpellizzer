Brickellia umbellata

Brickellia umbellata, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Brickellia umbellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
