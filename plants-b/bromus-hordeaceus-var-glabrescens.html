Bromus hordeaceus var. glabrescens

Bromus hordeaceus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often smooth and glabrous. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
