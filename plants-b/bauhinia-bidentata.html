Bauhinia bidentata

Bauhinia bidentata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental species. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

Bauhinia bidentata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
