Bambusa arundinacea var. gigantea

Bambusa arundinacea var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters or more. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically with a bluish-green hue.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has applications in construction and as a source of renewable material.
