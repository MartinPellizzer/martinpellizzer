Buergeria salicifolia

Buergeria salicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often bright green and slightly hairy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Buergeria salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
