Boelia

Boelia, commonly known as the black root or Indian snakeroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily native to Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their aromatic, often hairy leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. Boelia species are typically herbaceous, with some having taproots that are used in traditional medicine.
