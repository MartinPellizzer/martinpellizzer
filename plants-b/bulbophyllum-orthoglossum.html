Bulbophyllum orthoglossum

Bulbophyllum orthoglossum, commonly known as the "Lipstick Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, lip-like petals that are often brightly colored, especially in the species with orange or red hues. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the rainy season.

Bulbophyllum orthoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
