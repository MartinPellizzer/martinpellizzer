Binghamia decumbens

Binghamia decumbens, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with distinctive, waxy, white or pale blue flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps to reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant is notable for its ability to produce flowers even when it is not in full bloom, making it a rare and intriguing species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant adaptation and conservation.
