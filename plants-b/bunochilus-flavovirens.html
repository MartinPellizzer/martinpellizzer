Bunochilus flavovirens

Bunochilus flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive yellow-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that produce wind-pollinated flowers.
