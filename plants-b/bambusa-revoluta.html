Bambusa revoluta

Bambusa revoluta, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and clumping habit, making it suitable for ornamental and landscaping purposes. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color, adding visual interest to gardens. Its culms, or stems, are hollow and segmented, typical of the grass family.

Bambusa revoluta is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
