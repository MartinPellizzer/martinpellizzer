Blechnum leyboldtianum

Blechnum leyboldtianum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its sporangia are borne on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
