Balsamodendrum agallocha

Balsamodendrum agallocha, commonly known as the silver balsam, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a gelatinous coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
