Bambusa stricta var. argentea

Bambusa stricta var. argentea, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching culms and striking silvery-white to pale yellow foliage, which adds a unique visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its rapid growth and clumping habit make it an excellent choice for privacy screens or as a focal point in landscaped areas.
