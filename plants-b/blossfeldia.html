Blossfeldia

Blossfeldia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, hence its common name. Its leaves are modified into flattened, fleshy stems that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments.

Blossfeldia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
