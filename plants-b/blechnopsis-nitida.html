Blechnopsis nitida

Blechnopsis nitida, commonly known as the slender holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnopsis nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
