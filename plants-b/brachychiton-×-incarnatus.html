Brachychiton × incarnatus

Brachychiton × incarnatus, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a hybrid plant that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is a deciduous tree native to Australia, characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and the bark is smooth and often fissured. It produces large, woody fruits that split open to reveal seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
