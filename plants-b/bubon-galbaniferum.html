Bubon galbaniferum

Bubon galbaniferum, commonly known as the golden bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, enabling the plant to obtain nutrients through carnivory. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves.

Its reproductive structures are often submerged, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling algae and insect populations.
