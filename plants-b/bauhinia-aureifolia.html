Bauhinia aureifolia

Bauhinia aureifolia, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven pointed lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and showy flowers, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Bauhinia aureifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
