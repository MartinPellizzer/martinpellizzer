Baccharis Aliena

Baccharis aliena is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Known for its small, white or yellowish flowers, it plays a role in supporting local pollinators. The plant is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements. Despite its hardy nature, it can become invasive in certain areas if not managed properly.
