Bupleurum falcatum subsp. exaltatum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

exaltatum, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching towards the top.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

This subspecies is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
