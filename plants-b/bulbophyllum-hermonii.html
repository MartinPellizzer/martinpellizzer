Bulbophyllum hermonii

Bulbophyllum hermonii, commonly known as the Hermann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, intricate flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bulbophyllum hermonii is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
