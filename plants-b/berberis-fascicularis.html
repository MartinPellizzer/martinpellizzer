Berberis fascicularis

Berberis fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Berberis fascicularis is also known for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
