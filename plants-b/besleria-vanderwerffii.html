Besleria vanderwerffii

Besleria vanderwerffii, commonly known as the Vanderwerff's flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the humid regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in warm, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
