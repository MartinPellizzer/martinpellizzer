Bentinckia condapanna

Bentinckia condapanna, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Bentinckia condapanna plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is a symbol of Australian biodiversity.
