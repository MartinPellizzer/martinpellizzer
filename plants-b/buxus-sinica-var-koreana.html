Buxus sinica var. koreana

Buxus sinica var. koreana, commonly known as Korean boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary. The plant typically features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
