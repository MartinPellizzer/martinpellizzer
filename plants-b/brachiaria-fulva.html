Brachiaria fulva

Brachiaria fulva, commonly known as yellow brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Brachiaria fulva is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used for forage in some regions.
