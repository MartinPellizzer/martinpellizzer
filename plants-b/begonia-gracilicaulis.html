Begonia gracilicaulis

Begonia gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thin, upright stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
