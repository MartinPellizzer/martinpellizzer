Bauhinia macrostachya var. parvifolia

Bauhinia macrostachya var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
