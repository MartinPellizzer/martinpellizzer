Berberis × stenophylla var. latifolia

Berberis × stenophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features compound leaves that are broader and more elongated compared to typical barberry species, with a leathery texture and a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is native to the Himalayas and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
