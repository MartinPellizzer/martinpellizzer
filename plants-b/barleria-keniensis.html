Barleria keniensis

Barleria keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Barleria keniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
