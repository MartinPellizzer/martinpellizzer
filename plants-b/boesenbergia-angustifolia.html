Boesenbergia angustifolia

Boesenbergia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Boesenbergia angustifolia is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its medicinal properties.
