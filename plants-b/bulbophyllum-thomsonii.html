Bulbophyllum thomsonii

Bulbophyllum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive lip structure. It is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and its strap-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
