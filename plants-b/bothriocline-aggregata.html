Bothriocline aggregata

Bothriocline aggregata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
