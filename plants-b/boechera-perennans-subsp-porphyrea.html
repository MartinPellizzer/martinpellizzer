Boechera perennans subsp. porphyrea

Boechera perennans subsp.

porphyrea, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
