Buccaferrea rufescens

Buccaferrea rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
