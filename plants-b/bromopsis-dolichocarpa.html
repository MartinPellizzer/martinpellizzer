Bromopsis dolichocarpa

Bromopsis dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed feathergrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Bromopsis dolichocarpa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
