Byrsonima crassifolia f. ferruginea

Byrsonima crassifolia f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf nickerbean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, reddish-brown foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal applications.
