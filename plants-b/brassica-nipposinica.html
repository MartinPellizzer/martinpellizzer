Brassica nipposinica

Brassica nipposinica, commonly known as the Japanese mustard leaf, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, dark green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, peppery flavor.

Brassica nipposinica is valued in both traditional medicine and culinary applications across its native regions.
