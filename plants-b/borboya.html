Borboya

Borboya, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are also used in herbal remedies for their medicinal properties.

Borboya thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
