Berkheya mckenii

Berkheya mckenii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are tightly clustered at the tips of its branched stems. Native to the Cape Province, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Berkheya mckenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
