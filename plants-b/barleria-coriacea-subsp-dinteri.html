Barleria coriacea subsp. dinteri

Barleria coriacea subsp.

dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's barleria, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
