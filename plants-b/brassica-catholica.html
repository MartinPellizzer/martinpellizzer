Brassica catholica

Brassica catholica, commonly known as the Roman Mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has serrated, edible leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Brassica catholica is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
