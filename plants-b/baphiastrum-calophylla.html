Baphiastrum calophylla

Baphiastrum calophylla, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

Baphiastrum calophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
