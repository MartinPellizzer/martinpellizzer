Baccharis trifurcata

Baccharis trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branch baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the aster family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and shrublands, and is often found in coastal areas.

Baccharis trifurcata is known for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
