Balsamina minor

Balsamina minor, commonly known as the dwarf balsam, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Balsamina minor is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
