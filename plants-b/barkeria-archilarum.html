Barkeria archilarum

Barkeria archilarum, commonly known as the mountain barkeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Barkeria archilarum is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
