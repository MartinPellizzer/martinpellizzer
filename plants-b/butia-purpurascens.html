Butia purpurascens

Butia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple palm or the red palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Butia purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
