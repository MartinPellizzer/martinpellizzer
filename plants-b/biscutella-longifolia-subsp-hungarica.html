Biscutella longifolia subsp. hungarica

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical form of Biscutella longifolia.
