Botrychium multifidum var. oneidense

Botrychium multifidum var. oneidense, commonly known as the Oneida moonwort, is a small fern belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound fronds that are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in a unique, knob-like structure called a sorus, which is positioned near the base of the fronds.

This species is relatively rare and is found primarily in the northeastern United States, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
