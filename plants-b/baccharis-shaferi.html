Baccharis shaferi

Baccharis shaferi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Baccharis shaferi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and preventing soil erosion.
