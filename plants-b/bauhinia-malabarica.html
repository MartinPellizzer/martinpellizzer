Bauhinia malabarica

Bauhinia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia malabarica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local medicine.
