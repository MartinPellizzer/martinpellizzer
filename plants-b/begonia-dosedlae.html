Begonia dosedlae

Begonia dosedlae, commonly known as the Dosedlae begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia dosedlae is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
