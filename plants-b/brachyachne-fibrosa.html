Brachyachne fibrosa

Brachyachne fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous brachyachne, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, fibrous stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Brachyachne fibrosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
