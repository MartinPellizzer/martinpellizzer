Bromus squarrosus var. scoparius

Bromus squarrosus var. scoparius, commonly known as the scaly brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with dense, scaly leaves that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

This species is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
