Barleria spinifolia

Barleria spinifolia, commonly known as the spiked barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
