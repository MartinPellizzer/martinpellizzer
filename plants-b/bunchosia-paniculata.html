Bunchosia paniculata

Bunchosia paniculata, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Bunchosia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
