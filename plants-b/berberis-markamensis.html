Berberis markamensis

Berberis markamensis, commonly known as the Markham barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
