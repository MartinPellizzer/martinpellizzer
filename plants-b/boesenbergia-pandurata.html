Boesenbergia pandurata

Boesenbergia pandurata, commonly known as the yellow ginger or wild ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features strap-shaped, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Boesenbergia pandurata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in herbal practices.
