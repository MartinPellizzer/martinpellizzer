Begonia montaniformis

Begonia montaniformis, commonly known as the mountain-form begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Begonia montaniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
