Boronia polygalifolia var. trifoliolata

Boronia polygalifolia var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
