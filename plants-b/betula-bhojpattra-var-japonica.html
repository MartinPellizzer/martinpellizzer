Betula bhojpattra var. japonica

Betula bhojpattra var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of light gray beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, turning yellow in the autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
