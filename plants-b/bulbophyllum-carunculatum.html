Bulbophyllum carunculatum

Bulbophyllum carunculatum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This orchid thrives in humid conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
