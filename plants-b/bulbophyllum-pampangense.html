Bulbophyllum pampangense

Bulbophyllum pampangense, commonly known as the Pampangan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical environments.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.
