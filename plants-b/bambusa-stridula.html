Bambusa stridula

Bambusa stridula, commonly known as the bamboo whistling reed, is a species of bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that produce a distinctive whistling sound when wind passes through them. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Bambusa stridula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
