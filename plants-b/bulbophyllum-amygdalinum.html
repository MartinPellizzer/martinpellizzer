Bulbophyllum amygdalinum

Bulbophyllum amygdalinum, commonly known as the almond orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less favorable conditions. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
