Banksia fililoba

Banksia fililoba, commonly known as the slender banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fibrous, woody follicles contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Banksia fililoba is adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
