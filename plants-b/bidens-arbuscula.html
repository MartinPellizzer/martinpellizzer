Bidens arbuscula

Bidens arbuscula, commonly known as the redroot ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Bidens arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
