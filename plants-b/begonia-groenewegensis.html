Begonia groenewegensis

Begonia groenewegensis, commonly known as the Groeneweg's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
