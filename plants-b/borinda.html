Borinda

Borinda, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Rutaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that often resemble butterflies, hence its common name. Borinda species typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, fleshy fruits.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
