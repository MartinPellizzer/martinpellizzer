Bursera jonesii

Bursera jonesii, commonly known as the desert ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Bursera jonesii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
