Betula shikokiana

Betula shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku birch, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and triangular leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins that appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Betula shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
