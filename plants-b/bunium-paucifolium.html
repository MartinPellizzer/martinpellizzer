Bunium paucifolium

Bunium paucifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stem and small, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds are small, dark, and have a strong, aromatic scent, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
