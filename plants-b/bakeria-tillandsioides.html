Bakeria tillandsioides

Bakeria tillandsioides, commonly known as the silver leaf bromeliad, is a unique epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant's foliage is covered in dense, silvery-white trichomes, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil or bark substrates.

Bakeria tillandsioides is prized for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
