Begonia purdieana

Begonia purdieana, commonly known as the Purdie's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often velvety foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with some varieties displaying intricate patterns or variegation.

Begonia purdieana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
