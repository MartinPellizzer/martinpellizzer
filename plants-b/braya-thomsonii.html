Braya thomsonii

Braya thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bladder grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
