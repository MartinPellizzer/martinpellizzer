Brassica napus stirps esculenta

Brassica napus stirps esculenta, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are the primary agricultural product. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been widely cultivated in temperate regions worldwide.

Brassica napus stirps esculenta is valued for its oilseed, which is used in cooking, biodiesel production, and as a source of plant-based protein.
