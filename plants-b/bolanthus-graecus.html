Bolanthus graecus

Bolanthus graecus, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Bolanthus graecus has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
