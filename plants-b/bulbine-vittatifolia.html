Bulbine vittatifolia

Bulbine vittatifolia, commonly known as the striped bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often striped with contrasting colors, aiding in water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
