Benthamia herminioides subsp. arcuata

Benthamia herminioides subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the slender benthamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, linear leaves.

The species produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
