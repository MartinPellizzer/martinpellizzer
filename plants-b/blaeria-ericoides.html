Blaeria ericoides

Blaeria ericoides, commonly known as the heath blaeria, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Blaeria ericoides is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
