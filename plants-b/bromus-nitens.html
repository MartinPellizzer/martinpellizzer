Bromus nitens

Bromus nitens, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bromus nitens is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
