Bulbophyllum renipetalum

Bulbophyllum renipetalum, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fleshy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of red and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
