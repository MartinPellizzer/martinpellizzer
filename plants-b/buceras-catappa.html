Buceras catappa

Buceras catappa, commonly known as the Indian almond or tamarind tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, bipinnate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its fruit, which has various culinary and medicinal uses.
