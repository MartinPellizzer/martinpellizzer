Bastardia

Bastardia, commonly known as sea spurrey, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in coastal and saline environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. Bastardia is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
