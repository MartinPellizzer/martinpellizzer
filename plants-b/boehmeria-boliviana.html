Boehmeria boliviana

Boehmeria boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Boehmeria boliviana is also noted for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
