Brachionidium calypso

Brachionidium calypso, commonly known as the Calypso brachionidium, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, branching structure.

It typically grows in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in still or slow-moving waters. The plant features small, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It is known for its ability to form dense mats, which can provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

However, its growth can sometimes lead to water quality issues if it becomes too abundant.
