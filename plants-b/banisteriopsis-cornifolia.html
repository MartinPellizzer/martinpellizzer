Banisteriopsis cornifolia

Banisteriopsis cornifolia, commonly known as the "Coral Tree" or "Redwood Tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while the bark is thick and fissured. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
