Bougainvillea spectabilis

Bougainvillea spectabilis, commonly known as the princess flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, red, and white, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, tubular true flowers that are typically white or yellow, located inside the bracts. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to flourish.

Bougainvillea spectabilis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
