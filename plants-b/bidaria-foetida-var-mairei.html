Bidaria foetida var. mairei

Bidaria foetida var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's stinkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often pungent leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
