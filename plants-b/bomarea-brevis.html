Bomarea brevis

Bomarea brevis, commonly known as the dwarf bomarea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant features striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Bomarea brevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant foliage.
