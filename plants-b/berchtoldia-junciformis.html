Berchtoldia junciformis

Berchtoldia junciformis, commonly known as the watergrass or swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in poorly drained soils, thriving in consistently moist conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Berchtoldia junciformis plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
