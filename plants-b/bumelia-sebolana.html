Bumelia sebolana

Bumelia sebolana, commonly known as the Mexican gum tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, particularly in dry and semi-arid regions.
