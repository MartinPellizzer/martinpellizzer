Bifora vaginata

Bifora vaginata, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bifora vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
