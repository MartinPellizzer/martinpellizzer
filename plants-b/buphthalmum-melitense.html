Buphthalmum melitense

Buphthalmum melitense, commonly known as the Maltese sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Malta.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features rough, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Buphthalmum melitense is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
