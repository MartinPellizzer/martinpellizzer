Bidens cochlearis

Bidens cochlearis, commonly known as the coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds.
