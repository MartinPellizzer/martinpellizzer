Bacopa hassleriana

Bacopa hassleriana, commonly known as the silver leaf bacopa, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Bacopa hassleriana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
