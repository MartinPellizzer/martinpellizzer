Bletia antillana

Bletia antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Bletia antillana thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
