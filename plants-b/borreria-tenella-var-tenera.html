Borreria tenella var. tenera

Borreria tenella var. tenera, commonly known as Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Borreria tenella var. tenera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
