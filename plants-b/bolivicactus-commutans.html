Bolivicactus commutans

Bolivicactus commutans, commonly known as the Bolivian cactus, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by spherical, green fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Bolivicactus commutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
