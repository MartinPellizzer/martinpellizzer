Betonica frutescens

Betonica frutescens, commonly known as spring betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Betonica frutescens is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
