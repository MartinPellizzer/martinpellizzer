Bulbophyllum hamatipes

Bulbophyllum hamatipes, commonly known as the hairy-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Bulbophyllum hamatipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
