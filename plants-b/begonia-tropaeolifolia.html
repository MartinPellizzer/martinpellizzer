Begonia tropaeolifolia

Begonia tropaeolifolia, commonly known as the pepper begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that resemble tiny peppers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, easy-to-care-for plants.
