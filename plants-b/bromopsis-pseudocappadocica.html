Bromopsis pseudocappadocica

Bromopsis pseudocappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in early summer. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Bromopsis pseudocappadocica is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in dry, rocky habitats and supports local pollinator populations.
