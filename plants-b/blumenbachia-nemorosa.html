Blumenbachia nemorosa

Blumenbachia nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, thriving in rich, loamy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Campanulaceae family.
