Berberis vulgaris var. sulcata

Berberis vulgaris var. sulcata, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and red berries, which appear in late summer. The plant has thick, waxy leaves with prominent veins and spiny thorns along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
