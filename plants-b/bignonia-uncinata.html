Bignonia uncinata

Bignonia uncinata, commonly known as the creeping yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Bignonia uncinata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
