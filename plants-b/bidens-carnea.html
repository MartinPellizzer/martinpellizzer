Bidens carnea

Bidens carnea, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens carnea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
