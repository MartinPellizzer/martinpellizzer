Bupleurum falcatum proles tenuifolius

Bupleurum falcatum proles tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow upright, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and narrow, with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
