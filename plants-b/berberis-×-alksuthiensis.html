Berberis × alksuthiensis

Berberis × alksuthiensis, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii.

It features thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is native to hybrid zones in Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

Berberis × alksuthiensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
