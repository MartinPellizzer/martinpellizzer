Banksia dryandroides

Banksia dryandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody cones that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture.

Banksia dryandroides is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.
