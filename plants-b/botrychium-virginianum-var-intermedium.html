Botrychium virginianum var. intermedium

Botrychium virginianum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate moonwort, is a fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-bearing leaves that are typically divided into two unequal parts, with the upper portion often shaped like a crescent. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually short and compact, with a pale green to brownish coloration.
