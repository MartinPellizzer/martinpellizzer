Bromopsis nottowayana

Bromopsis nottowayana, commonly known as the Florida panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Bromopsis nottowayana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
