Briza canadensis

Briza canadensis, commonly known as Canada quillwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically borne in dense clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Canada quillwort thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
