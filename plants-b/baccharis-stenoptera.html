Baccharis stenoptera

Baccharis stenoptera, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Baccharis stenoptera is known for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
