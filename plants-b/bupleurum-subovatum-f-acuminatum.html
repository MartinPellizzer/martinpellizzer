Bupleurum subovatum f. acuminatum

Bupleurum subovatum f. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower genus native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, acuminate leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.

The subspecies is noted for its subtle differences in leaf shape and flower structure compared to the typical form of Bupleurum subovatum.
