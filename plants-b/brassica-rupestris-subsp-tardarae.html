Brassica rupestris subsp. tardarae

Brassica rupestris subsp.

tardarae, commonly known as the late-flowering wallflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

This subspecies is distinguished by its later blooming period compared to other wallflower species, typically flowering from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its yellow or orange flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
