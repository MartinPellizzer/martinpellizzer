Berberis atrocarpa var. suijiangensis

Berberis atrocarpa var. suijiangensis, commonly known as the Suijiang barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
