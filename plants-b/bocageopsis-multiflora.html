Bocageopsis multiflora

Bocageopsis multiflora, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Bocageopsis multiflora thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
