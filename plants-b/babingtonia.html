Babingtonia

Babingtonia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which resemble the iconic emblem of the country. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, featuring compound leaves and pod-like fruits. Babingtonia species are often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
