Bulbophyllum inflatum

Bulbophyllum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
