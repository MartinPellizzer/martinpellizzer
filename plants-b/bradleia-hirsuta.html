Bradleia hirsuta

Bradleia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bradleia, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Bradleia hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
