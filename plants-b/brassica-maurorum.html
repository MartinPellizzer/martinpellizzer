Brassica maurorum

Brassica maurorum, commonly known as the Mauritius cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant features basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, with a distinctive mustard-like odor. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
