Bidens denudata

Bidens denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

Bidens denudata is an annual herb that can spread aggressively through its seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
