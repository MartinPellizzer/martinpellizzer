Betonica officinalis subsp. officinalis

Betonica officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Betonica officinalis has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
