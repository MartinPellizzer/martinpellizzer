Bidens attenuata

Bidens attenuata, commonly known as the slender beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and bristly stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Bidens attenuata is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
