Banisteriopsis maguirei

Banisteriopsis maguirei, commonly known as the Amazonian tamarind tree, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the humid, nutrient-poor soils of the Amazon.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic applications.
