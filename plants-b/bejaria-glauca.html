Bejaria glauca

Bejaria glauca, commonly known as the bluebeard plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bejaria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
