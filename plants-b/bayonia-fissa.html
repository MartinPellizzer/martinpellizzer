Bayonia fissa

Bayonia fissa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bayonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, ascending stems with linear, narrow leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bayonia fissa is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
