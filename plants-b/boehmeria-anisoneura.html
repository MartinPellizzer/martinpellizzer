Boehmeria anisoneura

Boehmeria anisoneura, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or broadleaf nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to East Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its smooth, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Boehmeria anisoneura is valued for its fibrous stalks, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
