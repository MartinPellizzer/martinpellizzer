Barbosella crassifolia var. minor

Barbosella crassifolia var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barbosella, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
