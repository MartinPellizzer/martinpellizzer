Bauhinia adansoniana

Bauhinia adansoniana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne on long, branched peduncles. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
