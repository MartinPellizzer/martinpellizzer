Biscutella montana subvar. laevis

Biscutella montana subvar. laevis, commonly known as the smooth biscutella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its subspecies designation, 'laevis,' refers to its smooth, hairless leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties within the species.
