Betula humilis f. reticulata

Betula humilis f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate birch, is a shrub-like form of the dwarf birch.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a distinctive reticulate pattern of veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are typical of the Betula genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in northern and mountainous regions.

Its unique leaf structure makes it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological landscapes.
