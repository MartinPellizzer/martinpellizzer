Brassica oleracea subvar. procerior

Brassica oleracea subvar. procerior, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by round, green, edible pods. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
