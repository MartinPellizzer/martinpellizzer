Bulbophyllum lingii

Bulbophyllum lingii, commonly known as the Ling's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diverse morphology, and B. lingii is no exception, showcasing the adaptive versatility of orchids in tropical environments.
