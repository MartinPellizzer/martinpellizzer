Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp. compactus

Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp.

compactus, commonly known as sea club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
