Begonia malabarica var. dipetala

Begonia malabarica var. dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature two prominent petals, giving it its varietal name. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
