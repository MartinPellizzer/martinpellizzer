Braya parvia

Braya parvia, commonly known as the small-bracted bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are oblanceolate or elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Braya parvia is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
