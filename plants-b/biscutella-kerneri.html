Biscutella kerneri

Biscutella kerneri, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Biscutella kerneri is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
