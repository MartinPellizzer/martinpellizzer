Betula × alpestris

Betula × alpestris, commonly known as the paper birch, is a hybrid species within the genus Betula.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark that resembles paper, which is a key botanical feature. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cold climates, such as boreal forests and alpine regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with serrated margins.

Betula × alpestris produces small, yellowish-green catkins and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
