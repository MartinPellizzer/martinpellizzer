Boronia inflexa subsp. montiazurea

Boronia inflexa subsp.

montiazurea, commonly known as the Montezuma boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, shrub-like habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
