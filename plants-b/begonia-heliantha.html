Begonia heliantha

Begonia heliantha, commonly known as the sun begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
