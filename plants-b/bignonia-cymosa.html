Bignonia cymosa

Bignonia cymosa, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers and woody stems. Bignonia cymosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb structures make it both a decorative and functional plant in landscaping.
