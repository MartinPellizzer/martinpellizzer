Bomarea vitellina

Bomarea vitellina, commonly known as the golden bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that often display vibrant yellow and white colors. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes, with reflexed tepals that give it a unique appearance. Its leaves are usually alternate and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bomarea vitellina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
