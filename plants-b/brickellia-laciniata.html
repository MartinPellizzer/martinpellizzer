Brickellia laciniata

Brickellia laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply cut, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy yellow flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, blooming in the spring.

Brickellia laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
