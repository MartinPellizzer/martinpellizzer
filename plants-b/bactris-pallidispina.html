Bactris pallidispina

Bactris pallidispina, commonly known as the white-spined palm, is a species of palm native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Bactris pallidispina is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable environments.
