Bulbophyllum goniopterum

Bulbophyllum goniopterum, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, organic soil. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive, often iridescent appearance. Its pseudobulbs are thick and store water, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
