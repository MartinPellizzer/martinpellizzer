Bellis perennis proles pumila

Bellis perennis proles pumila, commonly known as the common daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow central disk. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along roadsides.

Bellis perennis proles pumila is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
