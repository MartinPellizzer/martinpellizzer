Bulbophyllum secundum

Bulbophyllum secundum, commonly known as the clustered flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stem. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diverse floral forms and is part of the Orchidaceae family.
