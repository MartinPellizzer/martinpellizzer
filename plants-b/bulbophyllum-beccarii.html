Bulbophyllum beccarii

Bulbophyllum beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to yellow and orange. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with periods of drought.

The orchid's unique morphology and adaptability make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
