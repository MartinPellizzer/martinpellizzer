Brochoneura pterocarpa

Brochoneura pterocarpa, commonly known as the African star fruit or *Cucurbita pterocarpa*, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that give way to orange, star-shaped fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet-tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
