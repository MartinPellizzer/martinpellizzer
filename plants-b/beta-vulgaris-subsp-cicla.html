Beta vulgaris subsp. cicla

Beta vulgaris subsp.

cicla, commonly known as莙菜 or leaf beet, is a cultivated variant of the sugar beet plant. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often eaten raw or cooked.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically grown for its nutritional leaves rather than its roots.

The leaves are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, making it a valuable leafy green vegetable.
