Bobartia longicyma subsp. longicyma

Bobartia longicyma subsp.

longicyma, commonly known as the long-stemmed bobartia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters.

The subspecies is characterized by its elongated cymes and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
