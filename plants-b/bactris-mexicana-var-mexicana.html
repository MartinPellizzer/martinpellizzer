Bactris mexicana var. mexicana

Bactris mexicana var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small, yellow berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forest and savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
