Bothriochloa campii

Bothriochloa campii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of surviving in poor soils.

Bothriochloa campii is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
