Biophytum talbotii

Biophytum talbotii, commonly known as the African violet or talbot's biophytum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Biophytum talbotii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering.
