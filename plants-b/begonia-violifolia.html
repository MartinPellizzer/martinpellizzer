Begonia violifolia

Begonia violifolia, commonly known as the violet-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety foliage that ranges from dark green to purplish hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
