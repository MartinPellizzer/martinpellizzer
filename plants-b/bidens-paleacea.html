Bidens paleacea

Bidens paleacea, commonly known as the paleface ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bidens paleacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
