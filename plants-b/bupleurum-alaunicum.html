Bupleurum alaunicum

Bupleurum alaunicum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, gray-green leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems. Bupleurum alaunicum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
