Blumea baccharoides

Blumea baccharoides, commonly known as the golden torch or false sandarac, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often accompanied by glandular hairs. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
