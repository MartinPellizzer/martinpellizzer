Botrydium

Botrydium, commonly known as a type of green alga, is a genus of unicellular aquatic organisms belonging to the order Botrydiaceae.

These organisms are typically found in freshwater environments and are characterized by their filamentous growth patterns. Botrydium species are known for their ability to form dense colonies, which can sometimes lead to water blooms. They play a role in nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems and are often used as indicators of water quality.

Their simple structure and rapid reproduction make them important subjects in ecological and biological studies.
