Biscutella picridifolia

Biscutella picridifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Biscutella picridifolia is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
