Bulbophyllum resupinatum

Bulbophyllum resupinatum, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and thick, adapted to store water in its native environment.

The orchid's flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
