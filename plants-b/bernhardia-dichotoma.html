Bernhardia dichotoma

Bernhardia dichotoma, commonly known as the twin-branch berberis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with two opposite leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
