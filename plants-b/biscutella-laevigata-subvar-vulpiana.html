Biscutella laevigata subvar. vulpiana

Biscutella laevigata subvar. vulpiana, commonly known as the foxglove biscutella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
