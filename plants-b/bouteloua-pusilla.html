Bouteloua pusilla

Bouteloua pusilla, commonly known as little bluestem, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with blue or purple, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable component of native grasslands.

Bouteloua pusilla is also known for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
