Blechnum kaulfussianum

Blechnum kaulfussianum, commonly known as the Kaulfuss' harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like fronds that resemble a harts-tongue shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, and thrives in humid climates. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive central vein.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
