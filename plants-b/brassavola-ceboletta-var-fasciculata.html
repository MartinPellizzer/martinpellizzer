Brassavola ceboletta var. fasciculata

Brassavola ceboletta var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered night orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette formation, and the stems are often branched, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
