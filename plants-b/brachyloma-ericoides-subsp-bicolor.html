Brachyloma ericoides subsp. bicolor

Brachyloma ericoides subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-coloured heath, is a small shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white. It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned appearance, with contrasting leaf colors.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and inland regions, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
