Brownea coccinea subsp. angustiflora

Brownea coccinea subsp.

angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scarlet brownea, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow center.

The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
