Bulbophyllum leonii

Bulbophyllum leonii, commonly known as the Leon's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

Bulbophyllum leonii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
