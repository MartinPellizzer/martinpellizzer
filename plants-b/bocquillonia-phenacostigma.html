Bocquillonia phenacostigma

Bocquillonia phenacostigma, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular shape with a dense covering of yellowish-green spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its botanical structure includes thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
