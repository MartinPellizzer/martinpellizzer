Brassia mendozae

Brassia mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its native habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Brassia mendozae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
