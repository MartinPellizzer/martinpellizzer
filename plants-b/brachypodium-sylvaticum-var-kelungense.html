Brachypodium sylvaticum var. kelungense

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. kelungense, commonly known as the Kelung grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is of interest in ecological and genetic studies due to its adaptability and potential for use in bioenergy research.
