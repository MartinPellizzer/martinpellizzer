Bignonia quadripinnata

Bignonia quadripinnata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, orange-red tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological importance.
