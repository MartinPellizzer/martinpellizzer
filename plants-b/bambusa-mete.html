Bambusa mete

Bambusa mete, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. Its culms are often used in construction and as a decorative element due to their strength and aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa mete thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a tropical or subtropical climate for optimal growth.
