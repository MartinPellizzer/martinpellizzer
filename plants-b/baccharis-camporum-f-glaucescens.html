Baccharis camporum f. glaucescens

Baccharis camporum f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
