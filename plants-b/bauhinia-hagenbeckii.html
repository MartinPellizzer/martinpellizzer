Bauhinia hagenbeckii

Bauhinia hagenbeckii, commonly known as the Hagenbeck's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral displays.
