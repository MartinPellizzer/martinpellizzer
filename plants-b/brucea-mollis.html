Brucea mollis

Brucea mollis, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Brucea mollis is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
