Baphia capparidifolia subsp. multiflora

Baphia capparidifolia subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the family Capparaceae.
