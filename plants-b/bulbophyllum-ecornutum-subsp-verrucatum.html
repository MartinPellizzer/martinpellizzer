Bulbophyllum ecornutum subsp. verrucatum

Bulbophyllum ecornutum subsp.

verrucatum, commonly known as the疣状玉女兰, is a rare orchid species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often hairy or warty pseudobulbs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
