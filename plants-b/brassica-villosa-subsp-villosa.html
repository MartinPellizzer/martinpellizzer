Brassica villosa subsp. villosa

Brassica villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Brassica villosa subsp.

villosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
