Bauhinia detergens

Bauhinia detergens, commonly known as the soap tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a soapy sap, which has been traditionally used for cleansing purposes. Its growth habit includes a woody shrub or small tree form, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Bauhinia detergens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
