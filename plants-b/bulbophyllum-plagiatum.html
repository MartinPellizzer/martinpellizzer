Bulbophyllum plagiatum

Bulbophyllum plagiatum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure and striking coloration, which often mimic other plants or animals. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and display a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red.

Bulbophyllum plagiatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique appearance.
