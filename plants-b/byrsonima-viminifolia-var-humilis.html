Byrsonima viminifolia var. humilis

Byrsonima viminifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf wild cashew, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a white, fleshy aril. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
