Bauhinia surinamensis

Bauhinia surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces flattened, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Bauhinia surinamensis is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural settings.
