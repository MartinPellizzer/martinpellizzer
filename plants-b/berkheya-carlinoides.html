Berkheya carlinoides

Berkheya carlinoides, commonly known as the golden burkheya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its unique, spherical seed heads are covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Berkheya carlinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
