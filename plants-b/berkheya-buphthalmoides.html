Berkheya buphthalmoides

Berkheya buphthalmoides, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds resembling dandelion fluff. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
