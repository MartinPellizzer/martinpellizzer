Bomarea parvifolia

Bomarea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.
