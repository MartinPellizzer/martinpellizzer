Boehmeria caudata var. tomentosa

Boehmeria caudata var. tomentosa, commonly known as silky flax, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
