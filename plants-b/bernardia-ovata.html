Bernardia ovata

Bernardia ovata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble bells. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Bernardia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
