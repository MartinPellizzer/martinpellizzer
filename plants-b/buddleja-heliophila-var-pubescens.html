Buddleja heliophila var. pubescens

Buddleja heliophila var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its profuse display of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are ovate and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
