Bulbophyllum subcubicum var. coccineum

Bulbophyllum subcubicum var. coccineum, commonly known as the Red-flowered Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking red or pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically dark green and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant floral display.
