Bidens connata var. anomala

Bidens connata var. anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller, more compact foliage compared to other Bidens species.
