Begonia philodendroides

Begonia philodendroides, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow and red, which are followed by decorative, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its trailing growth habit makes it ideal for hanging baskets or as a ground cover in humid environments.
