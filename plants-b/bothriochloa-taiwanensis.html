Bothriochloa taiwanensis

Bothriochloa taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
