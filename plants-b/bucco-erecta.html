Bucco erecta

Bucco erecta, commonly known as the upright bucco, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
