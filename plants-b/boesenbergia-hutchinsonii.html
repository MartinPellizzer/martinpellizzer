Boesenbergia hutchinsonii

Boesenbergia hutchinsonii, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.
