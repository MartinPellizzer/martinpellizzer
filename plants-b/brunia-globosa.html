Brunia globosa

Brunia globosa, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Brunia globosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
