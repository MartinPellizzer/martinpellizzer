Bridelia cathartica f. melanthesoides

Bridelia cathartica f. melanthesoides, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
