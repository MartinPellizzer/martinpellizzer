Benkara galia

Benkara galia, commonly known as the Galia melon, is a hybrid watermelon originating from Israel.

It is characterized by its vibrant green rind with a subtle yellowish tint and a sweet, crisp, pale yellow flesh. The plant is a vigorous climber with large, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by bees. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in fresh consumption and is prized for its unique flavor and high water content.
