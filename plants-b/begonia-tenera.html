Begonia tenera

Begonia tenera, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Begonia tenera is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
