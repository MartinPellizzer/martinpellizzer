Borzicactus laniceps

Borzicactus laniceps, commonly known as the hairy-bush cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green hairs, providing insulation and reducing water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its roots are shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Borzicactus laniceps is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually distinctive member of its ecosystem.
