Boopis multicaulis var. patagonica

Boopis multicaulis var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian boopis, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
