Bromus erectus subsp. australis

Bromus erectus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow, awned lemmas. Its leaves are typically narrow and flat, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Bromus erectus subsp.

australis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to temperate climates.
