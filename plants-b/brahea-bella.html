Brahea bella

Brahea bella, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a compact, bushy form with a short trunk and dense clusters of blue-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, with thick, waxy foliage that reduces water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by black, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

Brahea bella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique look and low maintenance requirements.
