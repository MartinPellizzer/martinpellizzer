Brassicella erucastrum var. arvensis

Brassicella erucastrum var. arvensis, commonly known as the field crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically oval with a slightly serrated edge. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and agricultural fields, and is often found in temperate regions. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential role in plant ecology studies.
