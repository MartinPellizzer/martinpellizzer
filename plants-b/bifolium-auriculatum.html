Bifolium auriculatum

Bifolium auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate bifolium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Bifolium auriculatum is considered endangered in its native range.
