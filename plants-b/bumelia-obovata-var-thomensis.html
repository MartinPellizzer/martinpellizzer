Bumelia obovata var. thomensis

Bumelia obovata var. thomensis, commonly known as the Thomas's bumelia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are broadly ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
