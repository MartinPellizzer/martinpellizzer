Bignonia pandorana

Bignonia pandorana, commonly known as the Pandora's vine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves and woody stems that can grow quite long. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bignonia pandorana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
