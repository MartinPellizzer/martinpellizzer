Bromus temulentus

Bromus temulentus, commonly known as the醉草 or醉麦, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a noxious weed and can be harmful to livestock.
