Blechnum penna-marina subsp. bolivianum

Blechnum penna-marina subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to South America. It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost fan-like appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
