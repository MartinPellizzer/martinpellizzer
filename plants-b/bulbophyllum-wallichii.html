Bulbophyllum wallichii

Bulbophyllum wallichii, commonly known as the Walsingham's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
