Begonia laciniata subsp. flava

Begonia laciniata subsp.

flava, commonly known as the yellow-leaf begonia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, bright yellow leaves that are often variegated with white or green markings.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red or orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vibrant leaf coloration compared to other members of the Begonia laciniata species.
