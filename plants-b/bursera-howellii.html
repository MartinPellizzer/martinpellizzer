Bursera howellii

Bursera howellii, commonly known as the Mexican birch, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, resembling the bark of a birch tree. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

Bursera howellii produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.
