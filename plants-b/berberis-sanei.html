Berberis sanei

Berberis sanei, commonly known as the Sane's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Berberis sanei is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
