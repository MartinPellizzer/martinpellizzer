Blechnum squamipes

Blechnum squamipes, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-white fronds that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a unique, recurved shape that gives it its common name.

Blechnum squamipes plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
