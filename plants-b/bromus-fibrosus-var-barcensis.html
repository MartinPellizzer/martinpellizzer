Bromus fibrosus var. barcensis

Bromus fibrosus var. barcensis, commonly known as the Barcanese fibrous brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, fibrous blades that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
