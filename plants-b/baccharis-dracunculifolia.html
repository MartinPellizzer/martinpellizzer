Baccharis dracunculifolia

Baccharis dracunculifolia, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and riparian areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters.

Baccharis dracunculifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
