Bromus willdenowii

Bromus willdenowii, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

Bromus willdenowii is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
