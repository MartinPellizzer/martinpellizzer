Brachystephanus coeruleus

Brachystephanus coeruleus, commonly known as the blue stephanus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
