Brassica oxyrrhina proles nostalgica

Brassica oxyrrhina proles nostalgica, commonly known as the nostalgic mustard, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, purple-tinged leaves that resemble those of its wild relatives. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
