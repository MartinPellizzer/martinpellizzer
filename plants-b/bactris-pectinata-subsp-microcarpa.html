Bactris pectinata subsp. microcarpa

Bactris pectinata subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited pita palm, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for sustainable agriculture.
