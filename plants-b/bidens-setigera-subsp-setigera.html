Bidens setigera subsp. setigera

Bidens setigera subsp.

setigera, commonly known as the slender beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
