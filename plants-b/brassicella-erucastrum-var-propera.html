Brassicella erucastrum var. propera

Brassicella erucastrum var. propera, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
