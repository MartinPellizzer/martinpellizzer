Borzicactella serpens

Borzicactella serpens, commonly known as the creeping rose cactus, is a low-growing, stem succulent native to South America.

It features a distinctive appearance with its segmented, ribbed stems that often curl into a spiral shape. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny form. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
