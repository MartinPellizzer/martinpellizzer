Baccharis xiphophylla

Baccharis xiphophylla, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Baccharis xiphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
