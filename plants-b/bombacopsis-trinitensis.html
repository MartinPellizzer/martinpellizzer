Bombacopsis trinitensis

Bombacopsis trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, including Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
