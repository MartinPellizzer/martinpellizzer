Brassica campestris f. spontanea

Brassica campestris f. spontanea, commonly known as wild mustard, is a wild variety of the cabbage family.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It is known for its strong mustard odor and is often considered a weed.

This species is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to many other regions.
