Biscutella lucida var. coriacea

Biscutella lucida var. coriacea, commonly known as the glossy biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy, waxy surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
