Bambusa merrillii

Bambusa merrillii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow culms that stand upright and form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing an attractive visual contrast to its yellow stems. It is a fast-growing plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Bambusa merrillii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a tropical ambiance in gardens.
