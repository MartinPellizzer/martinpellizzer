Bartonia micrantha

Bartonia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bartonia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
