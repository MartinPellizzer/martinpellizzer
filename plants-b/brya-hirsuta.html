Brya hirsuta

Brya hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bryum, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Bryaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy leaf surfaces, which give it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Brya hirsuta plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
