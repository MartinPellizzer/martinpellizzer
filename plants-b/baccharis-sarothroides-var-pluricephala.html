Baccharis sarothroides var. pluricephala

Baccharis sarothroides var. pluricephala, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
