Blechnum parrisiae

Blechnum parrisiae, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching appearance.

Blechnum parrisiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
