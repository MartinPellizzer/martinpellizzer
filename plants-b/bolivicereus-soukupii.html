Bolivicereus soukupii

Bolivicereus soukupii, commonly known as the Santa Cruz cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its spines are typically white to yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Bolivicereus soukupii is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems to survive prolonged droughts.
