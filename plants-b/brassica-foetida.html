Brassica foetida

Brassica foetida, commonly known as stinking mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent, mustard-like odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Brassica foetida thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or arid environments.
