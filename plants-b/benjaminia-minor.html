Benjaminia minor

Benjaminia minor, commonly known as the narrow-leafed benjaminia, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. Benjaminia minor is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and sparse vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
