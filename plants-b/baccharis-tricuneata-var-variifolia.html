Baccharis tricuneata var. variifolia

Baccharis tricuneata var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable thimbleweed, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
