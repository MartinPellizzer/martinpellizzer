Baccharis papillosa subsp. integrifolia

Baccharis papillosa subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a hardy and adaptable species.
