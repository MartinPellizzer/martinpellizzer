Brassica napus var. italica

Brassica napus var. italica, commonly known as Italian rapeseed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are rich in oil. It is often cultivated for its oilseed, which is used in cooking and industrial applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in agricultural systems.
