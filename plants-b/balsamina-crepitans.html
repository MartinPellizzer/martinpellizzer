Balsamina crepitans

Balsamina crepitans, commonly known as the crackling balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Balsamina crepitans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
