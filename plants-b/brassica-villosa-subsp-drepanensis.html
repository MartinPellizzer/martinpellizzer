Brassica villosa subsp. drepanensis

Brassica villosa subsp.

drepanensis, commonly known as the woolly cabbage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
