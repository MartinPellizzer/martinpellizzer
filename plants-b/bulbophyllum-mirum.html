Bulbophyllum mirum

Bulbophyllum mirum, commonly known as the miraculous orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
