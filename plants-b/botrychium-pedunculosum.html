Botrychium pedunculosum

Botrychium pedunculosum, commonly known as the slender moonwort, is a small, fern-like plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique structure, featuring a short, erect peduncle bearing a single, broad, ovate frond. The frond is divided into two lobes, with a distinct midrib and a network of veins that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.

This plant is often found in the northern hemisphere and is known for its delicate, ephemeral growth cycle.
