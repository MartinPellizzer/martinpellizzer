Brocchinia delicatula

Brocchinia delicatula, commonly known as the glassy bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, translucent, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The leaves form a rosette that collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful management to prevent root rot.
