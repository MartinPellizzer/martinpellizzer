Baccharis trilobata

Baccharis trilobata, commonly known as Three-lobed Baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid regions. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, including treating respiratory ailments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and erosion control.
