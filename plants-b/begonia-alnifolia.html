Begonia alnifolia

Begonia alnifolia, commonly known as the alpine begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
