Boehmeria rubricaulis

Boehmeria rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like China, India, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typical of the Urticaceae family.

Boehmeria rubricaulis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
