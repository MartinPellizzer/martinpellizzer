Blaeria tenuipilosa

Blaeria tenuipilosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blazer, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and bloom in shades of pink to purple.

Blaeria tenuipilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
