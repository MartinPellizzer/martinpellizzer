Baccharis marginalis var. araucana

Baccharis marginalis var. araucana, commonly known as the Araucanian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, coastal areas and is adapted to withstand strong winds and saline conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
