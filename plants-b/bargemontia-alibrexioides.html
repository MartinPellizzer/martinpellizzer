Bargemontia alibrexioides

Bargemontia alibrexioides, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bargemontia alibrexioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
