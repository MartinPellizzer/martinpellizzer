Baillonia amabilis var. longifolia

Baillonia amabilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered baillonia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody stems are often thorny, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
