Bomarea longipes

Bomarea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bomarea, is a striking member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, tubular stems and clusters of showy, spatheate flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Bomarea longipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
