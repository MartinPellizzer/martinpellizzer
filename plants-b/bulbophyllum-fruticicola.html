Bulbophyllum fruticicola

Bulbophyllum fruticicola, commonly known as the shrub orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
