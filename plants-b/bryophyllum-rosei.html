Bryophyllum rosei

Bryophyllum rosei, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, rosette-shaped leaves along its stems, which can develop into new plants, making it highly propagative. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Bryophyllum rosei is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
