Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. korotkiji

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

korotkiji, commonly known as the Korotkij's bromus, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
