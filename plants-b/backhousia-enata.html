Backhousia enata

Backhousia enata, commonly known as the lemon-scented backhousia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Backhousia enata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
