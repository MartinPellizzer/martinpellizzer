Bidens evapelliana

Bidens evapelliana, commonly known as the common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers with ray florets and a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a weed and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
