Bursera leptophloeos

Bursera leptophloeos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf frankincense tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces a fragrant resin that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It typically grows in dry, tropical, and subtropical regions, often in limestone soils.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and potential economic value through resin harvesting.
