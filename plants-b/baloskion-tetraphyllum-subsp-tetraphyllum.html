Baloskion tetraphyllum subsp. tetraphyllum

Baloskion tetraphyllum subsp.

tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leafed dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of oblanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Asteraceae family.
