Begonia charadrophila

Begonia charadrophila, commonly known as the dark-red begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, dark red to almost black flowers, which are typically borne on upright spikes. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and can vary in color from deep green to almost black, adding to its dramatic appearance. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among begonia enthusiasts.
