Bulbophyllum loxophyllum

Bulbophyllum loxophyllum, commonly known as the twisted-leaf orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, twisted leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
