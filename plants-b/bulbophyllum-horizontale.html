Bulbophyllum horizontale

Bulbophyllum horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, horizontally oriented pseudobulbs and its ability to produce multiple flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Bulbophyllum horizontale is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
