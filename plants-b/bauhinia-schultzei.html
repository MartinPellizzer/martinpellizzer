Bauhinia schultzei

Bauhinia schultzei, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, papery flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its structure is typically tree-like with a straight trunk and spreading branches. Bauhinia schultzei thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
