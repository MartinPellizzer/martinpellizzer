Bulbophyllum pyrrosifolium

Bulbophyllum pyrrosifolium, commonly known as the flame-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This orchid is prized in horticulture for its vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
