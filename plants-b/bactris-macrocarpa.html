Bactris macrocarpa

Bactris macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant palm or macaranga palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, often grayish trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. Bactris macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
