Berberis basumchuensis

Berberis basumchuensis, commonly known as the Altai barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are yellow, and it produces red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
