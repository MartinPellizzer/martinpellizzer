Baccharis leptocephala

Baccharis leptocephala, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that are often fragrant.

Baccharis leptocephala is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
