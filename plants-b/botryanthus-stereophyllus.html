Botryanthus stereophyllus

Botryanthus stereophyllus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant is typically evergreen and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers indirect sunlight and is often cultivated as a popular houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
