Boechera fendleri var. spatifolia

Boechera fendleri var. spatifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
