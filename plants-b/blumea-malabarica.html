Blumea malabarica

Blumea malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar Blueflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are blue or violet, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.
