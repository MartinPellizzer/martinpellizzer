Biglandularia conspicua

Biglandularia conspicua, commonly known as the showy biglandularia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Biglandularia conspicua thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
