Bignonia rufinervis

Bignonia rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined crossvine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces vibrant orange to red tubular flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia rufinervis is also valued in ecological gardens for attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
