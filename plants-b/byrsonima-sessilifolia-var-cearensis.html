Byrsonima sessilifolia var. cearensis

Byrsonima sessilifolia var. cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Sapodilla, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet, and sometimes fibrous fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
