Bulbophyllum strigosum

Bulbophyllum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
