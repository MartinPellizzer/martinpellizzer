Bunium aromaticum

Bunium aromaticum, commonly known as wild cumin, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are small and ribbed, are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Bunium aromaticum is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
