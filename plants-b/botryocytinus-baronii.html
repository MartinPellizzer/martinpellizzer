Botryocytinus baronii

Botryocytinus baronii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a short, thick stem with spiny areoles, and its leaves are modified into spines for protection. Botryocytinus baronii is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its stem and reducing transpiration through its waxy surface.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
