Bhesa indica

Bhesa indica, commonly known as Indian bhesa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, where it thrives in humid climates. The plant features woody stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are ovate and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

Bhesa indica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
