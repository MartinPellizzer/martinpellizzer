Borzicactus paucicostatus

Borzicactus paucicostatus, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its spiny appearance provides protection from herbivores, while its shallow root system helps it absorb moisture efficiently.

Borzicactus paucicostatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
