Banksia robur var. minor

Banksia robur var. minor, commonly known as the small banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has rough, fibrous bark that peels away in strips, revealing a lighter-colored inner bark. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is part of the Proteaceae family and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.
