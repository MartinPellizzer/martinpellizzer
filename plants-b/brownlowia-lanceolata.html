Brownlowia lanceolata

Brownlowia lanceolata, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Brownlowia lanceolata is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
