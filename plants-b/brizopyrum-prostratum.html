Brizopyrum prostratum

Brizopyrum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Brizopyrum prostratum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
