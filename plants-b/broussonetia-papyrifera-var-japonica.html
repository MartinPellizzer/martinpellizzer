Broussonetia papyrifera var. japonica

Broussonetia papyrifera var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous bark, which has been historically used for papermaking. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
