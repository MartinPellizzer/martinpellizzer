Baccharis scandens

Baccharis scandens, commonly known as Spanish needle, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in slender, bristly structures that can be dispersed by wind.

Baccharis scandens is known for its adaptability and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
