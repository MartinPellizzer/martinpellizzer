Bridelia stipularis var. ciliata

Bridelia stipularis var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, ciliate margins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
