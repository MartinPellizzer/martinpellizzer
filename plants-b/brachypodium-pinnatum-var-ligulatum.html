Brachypodium pinnatum var. ligulatum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false brome, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
