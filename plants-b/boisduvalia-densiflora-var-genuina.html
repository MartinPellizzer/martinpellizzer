Boisduvalia densiflora var. genuina

Boisduvalia densiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the dense-flowered boisduvalia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
