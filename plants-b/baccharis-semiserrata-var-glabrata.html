Baccharis semiserrata var. glabrata

Baccharis semiserrata var. glabrata, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
