Balsamorhiza hookeri var. lanata

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
