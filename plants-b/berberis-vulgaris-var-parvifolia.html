Berberis vulgaris var. parvifolia

Berberis vulgaris var. parvifolia, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it a dense, carpet-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also used in traditional medicine for its berberine content, which has antimicrobial properties.
