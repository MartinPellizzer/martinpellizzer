Berberis × provincialis var. serrata

Berberis × provincialis var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its spiny stems and dense foliage.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often growing in rocky or woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
