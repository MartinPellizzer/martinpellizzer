Bonatea bracteata

Bonatea bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South and Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Bonatea bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
