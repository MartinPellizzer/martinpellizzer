Begonia robusta

Begonia robusta, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a unique, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Begonia robusta is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
