Bernoullia reptans

Bernoullia reptans, commonly known as the creeping berberis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Bernoullia reptans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
