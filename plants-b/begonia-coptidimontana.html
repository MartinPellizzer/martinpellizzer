Begonia coptidimontana

Begonia coptidimontana, commonly known as the Andean begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with smooth, glossy foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
