Bambusa albomarginata

Bambusa albomarginata, commonly known as the white-edged bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms with distinctive white margins along the edges of the leaves. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and sustainable construction materials.

The leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
