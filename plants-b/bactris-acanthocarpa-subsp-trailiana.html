Bactris acanthocarpa subsp. trailiana

Bactris acanthocarpa subsp.

trailiana, commonly known as the Trinidadian palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family. It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Trinidad and Tobago, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

This palm is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The fruit is typically a small, round drupe, which is an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in tropical gardens.
