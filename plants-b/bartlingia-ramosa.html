Bartlingia ramosa

Bartlingia ramosa, commonly known as the branched cypress, is a unique coniferous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and scale-like leaves that give it a dense, shrub-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of conifers. Bartlingia ramosa thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
