Berberis subsessiliflora

Berberis subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered near the base of the plant. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
