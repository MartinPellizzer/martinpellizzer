Baumea laxa

Baumea laxa, commonly known as the slender baumea, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Baumea laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
