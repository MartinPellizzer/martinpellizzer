Brassica oleracea var. sabellica

Brassica oleracea var. sabellica, commonly known as the curly kale, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage species.

It is characterized by its dense, curly leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant is a biennial, though it is often grown as an annual in temperate climates. It thrives in cool weather and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

The leaves are edible and are commonly used in cooking, particularly in soups and stews.
