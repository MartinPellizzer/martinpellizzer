Brassica rapa var. laxa

Brassica rapa var. laxa, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a mustard-like odor when crushed.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.
