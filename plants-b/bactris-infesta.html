Bactris infesta

Bactris infesta, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits that are often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are also used for thatching and weaving, making it an important resource in traditional practices.

Bactris infesta thrives in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to coastal and riverine environments.
