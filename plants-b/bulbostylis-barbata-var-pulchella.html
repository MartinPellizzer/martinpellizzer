Bulbostylis barbata var. pulchella

Bulbostylis barbata var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
