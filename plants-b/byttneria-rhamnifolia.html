Byttneria rhamnifolia

Byttneria rhamnifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
