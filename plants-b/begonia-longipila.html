Begonia longipila

Begonia longipila, commonly known as the long-stalked begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and blooms.
