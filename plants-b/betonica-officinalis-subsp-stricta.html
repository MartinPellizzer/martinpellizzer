Betonica officinalis subsp. stricta

Betonica officinalis subsp.

stricta, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces spikes of blue or violet flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Betonica officinalis subsp.

stricta has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
