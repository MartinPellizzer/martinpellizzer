Bambusa garuchokua

Bambusa garuchokua, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, fast-growing grass that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features slender, hollow stalks with a distinctive golden-yellow coloration, especially in the younger shoots. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa garuchokua is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its use in basketry and construction.
