Begonia seemanniana

Begonia seemanniana, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula begonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a perennial species that produces basal leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
