Bactris pectinata var. subintegrifolia

Bactris pectinata var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the red rafaela, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often reddish in color when young. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
