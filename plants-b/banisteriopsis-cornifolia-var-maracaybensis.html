Banisteriopsis cornifolia var. maracaybensis

Banisteriopsis cornifolia var. maracaybensis, commonly known as the Maracaybense bignay, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
