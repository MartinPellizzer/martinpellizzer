Beaucarnea palmeri

Beaucarnea palmeri, commonly known as the "palm tree of the desert," is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a thick, tree-like stem with a distinctive crown of soft, fuzzy leaves that resemble a brush. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its swollen, fleshy trunk. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Beaucarnea palmeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
