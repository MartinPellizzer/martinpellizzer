Bulbophyllum carunculilabrum

Bulbophyllum carunculilabrum, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a bright red lip, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
