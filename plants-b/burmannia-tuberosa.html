Burmannia tuberosa

Burmannia tuberosa, commonly known as the red bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and distinctive red, inflated leaves that resemble bladders. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is often found near water sources.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
