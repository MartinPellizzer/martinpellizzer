Bauhinia gracillima

Bauhinia gracillima, commonly known as the slender bauhinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often divided into small, triangular lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Bauhinia gracillima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
