Bulbophyllum weddellii

Bulbophyllum weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical environments.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
