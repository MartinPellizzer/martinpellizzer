Bidens tripartita

Bidens tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals and distinctive bristly seeds that adhere to clothing and animal fur. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
