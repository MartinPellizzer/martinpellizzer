Bactyrilobium javanica

Bactyrilobium javanica, commonly known as the Javanese orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with brightly colored petals and a unique labellum that aids in pollination.

Bactyrilobium javanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
