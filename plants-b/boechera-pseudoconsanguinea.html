Boechera pseudoconsanguinea

Boechera pseudoconsanguinea, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.
