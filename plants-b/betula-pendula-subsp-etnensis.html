Betula pendula subsp. etnensis

Betula pendula subsp.

etnensis, commonly known as the Etna silver birch, is a subspecies native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

The bark is smooth and pale, often with a faintly flaky texture. Its leaves are elliptical, with a doubly serrated margin and a pointed tip.

This subspecies is adapted to the unique microclimatic conditions of its high-altitude habitat.
