Banksia rufa subsp. chelomacarpa

Banksia rufa subsp.

chelomacarpa, commonly known as the red banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically reddish in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
