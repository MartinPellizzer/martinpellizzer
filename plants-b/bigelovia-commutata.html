Bigelovia commutata

Bigelovia commutata, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
