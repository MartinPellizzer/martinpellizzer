Barbarea elata

Barbarea elata, commonly known as the tall winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually lobed and hairy.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
