Bignonia rivularis

Bignonia rivularis, commonly known as the river bignonia, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near streams and riverbanks.

Bignonia rivularis is a fast-growing plant that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
