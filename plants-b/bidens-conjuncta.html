Bidens conjuncta

Bidens conjuncta, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow with dark centers and are arranged in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Bidens conjuncta is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.
