Bahiopsis triangularis

Bahiopsis triangularis, commonly known as the triangular bahiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
