Beckmannia

Beckmannia, commonly known as the watergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and water margins. Beckmannia species often have small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
