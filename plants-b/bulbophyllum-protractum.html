Bulbophyllum protractum

Bulbophyllum protractum, commonly known as the long-stalked torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant inflorescence that bears numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
