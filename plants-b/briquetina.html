Briquetina

Briquetina, commonly known as the "false mallow," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Briquetina has a branching stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
