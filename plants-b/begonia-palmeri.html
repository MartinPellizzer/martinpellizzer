Begonia palmeri

Begonia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's begonia, is a species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often a vibrant green with reddish veins, adding to its ornamental value.

Begonia palmeri is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, semi-shaded conditions.
