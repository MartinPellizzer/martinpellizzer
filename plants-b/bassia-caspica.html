Bassia caspica

Bassia caspica, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Bassia caspica thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
