Bulbophyllum concavibasalis

Bulbophyllum concavibasalis, commonly known as the concave-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
