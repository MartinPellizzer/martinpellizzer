Byttneria sparrei

Byttneria sparrei, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds.

Its bark and wood are valued for their durability and have been used traditionally in various applications, including construction and furniture-making.
