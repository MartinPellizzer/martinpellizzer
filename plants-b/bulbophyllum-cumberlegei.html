Bulbophyllum cumberlegei

Bulbophyllum cumberlegei, commonly known as the Cumberlege's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red.

The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in tropical environments.
