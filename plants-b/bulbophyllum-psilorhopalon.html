Bulbophyllum psilorhopalon

Bulbophyllum psilorhopalon, commonly known as the pink-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a unique, spurred shape. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in less-than-ideal conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
