Bulbophyllum membranaceum

Bulbophyllum membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
