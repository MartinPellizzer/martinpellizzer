Boronia hapalophylla

Boronia hapalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is usually found in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

Boronia hapalophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
