Blepharis furcata

Blepharis furcata, commonly known as the forked blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
