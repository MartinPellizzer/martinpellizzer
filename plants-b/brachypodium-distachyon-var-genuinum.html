Brachypodium distachyon var. genuinum

Brachypodium distachyon var. genuinum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its genetic simplicity and adaptability, it is widely used in plant biology research.
