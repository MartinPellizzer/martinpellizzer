Banisteria longifolia

Banisteria longifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along its twining stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria longifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
