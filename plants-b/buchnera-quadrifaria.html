Buchnera quadrifaria

Buchnera quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, often with a fourth small leaflet, making it a symbol of good luck. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

Buchnera quadrifaria is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
