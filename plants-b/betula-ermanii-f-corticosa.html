Betula ermanii f. corticosa

Betula ermanii f. corticosa, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch.

It is a variant of the paper birch, characterized by its unique bark that resembles tree bark rather than the typical papery texture of the species. This plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a serrated margin.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscaped areas.
