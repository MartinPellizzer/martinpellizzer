Baccharis glaucescens

Baccharis glaucescens, commonly known as silver baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Silver baccharis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
