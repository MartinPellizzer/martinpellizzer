Butia missionera

Butia missionera, commonly known as the Paraguay date palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates, which are rich in nutrients. Butia missionera is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy and drought-resistant species.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance have led to its cultivation in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
