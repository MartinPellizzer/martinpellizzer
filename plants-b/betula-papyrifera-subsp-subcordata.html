Betula papyrifera subsp. subcordata

Betula papyrifera subsp.

subcordata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, papery strips.

The plant has slender, pendulous branches and heart-shaped leaves that are doubly serrated. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
