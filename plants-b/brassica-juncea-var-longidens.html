Brassica juncea var. longidens

Brassica juncea var. longidens, commonly known as the Chinese kale or gai lan, is a leafy green vegetable characterized by its thick, elongated, dark green leaves with a slightly bitter taste.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage.

The plant is native to East Asia and is cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. Its botanical features include a short, stocky stem and a rosette of leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance.
