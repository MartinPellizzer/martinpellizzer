Bellardia trixago subvar. bicolor

Bellardia trixago subvar. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellardia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
