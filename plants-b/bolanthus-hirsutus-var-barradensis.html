Bolanthus hirsutus var. barradensis

Bolanthus hirsutus var. barradensis, commonly known as the hairy bolanthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important component of its native ecosystem.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
