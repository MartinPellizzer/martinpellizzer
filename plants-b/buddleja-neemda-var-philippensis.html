Buddleja neemda var. philippensis

Buddleja neemda var. philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine weeping bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
