Betula lenta var. papyrifera

Betula lenta var. papyrifera, commonly known as the paper birch, is a variant of the sweet birch that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
