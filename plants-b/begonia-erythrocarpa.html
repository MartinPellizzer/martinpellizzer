Begonia erythrocarpa

Begonia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia erythrocarpa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
