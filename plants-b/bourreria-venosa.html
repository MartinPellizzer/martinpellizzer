Bourreria venosa

Bourreria venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
