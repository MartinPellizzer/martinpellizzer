Begonia semperflorens var. hookeri

Begonia semperflorens var. hookeri, commonly known as the trailing begonia or wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. This variety is popular in horticulture for its ability to thrive in containers and hanging baskets, making it a favored choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, with regular watering to maintain its lush appearance.
