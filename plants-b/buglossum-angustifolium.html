Buglossum angustifolium

Buglossum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers. Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
