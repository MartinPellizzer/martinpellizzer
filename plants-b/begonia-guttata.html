Begonia guttata

Begonia guttata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with red or purple spots. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and modest blooms, which make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
