Bauhinia macrosiphon

Bauhinia macrosiphon, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble the shape of a crown, and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It produces woody pods that contain flat, paper-like seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bauhinia macrosiphon is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
