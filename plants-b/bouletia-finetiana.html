Bouletia finetiana

Bouletia finetiana, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a thick, flaky bark and produces flat, compound leaves. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods that split open to release the seeds.

Bouletia finetiana is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.
