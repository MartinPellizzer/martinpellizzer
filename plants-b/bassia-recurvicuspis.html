Bassia recurvicuspis

Bassia recurvicuspis, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
