Basella oleracea var. ramosa

Basella oleracea var. ramosa, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable or for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or vertical gardening.
