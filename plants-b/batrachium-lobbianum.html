Batrachium lobbianum

Batrachium lobbianum, commonly known as the climbing batrachium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
