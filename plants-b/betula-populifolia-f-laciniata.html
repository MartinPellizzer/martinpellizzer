Betula populifolia f. laciniata

Betula populifolia f. laciniata, commonly known as the shredded birch, is a unique variant of the paper birch.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens for its visual interest.
