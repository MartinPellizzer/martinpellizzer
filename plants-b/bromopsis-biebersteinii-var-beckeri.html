Bromopsis biebersteinii var. beckeri

Bromopsis biebersteinii var. beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's bromegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
