Bulbostylis hispidula subsp. pyriformis

Bulbostylis hispidula subsp.

pyriformis, commonly known as the hairy bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
