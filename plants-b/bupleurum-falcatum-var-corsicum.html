Bupleurum falcatum var. corsicum

Bupleurum falcatum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lanceolate leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
