Bougainvillea spinosa f. parviflora

Bougainvillea spinosa f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf bougainvillea, is a compact, flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, colorful bracts that surround tiny, true flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink, red, or white bracts that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is well-suited for container gardening or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking display of colorful foliage and minimal maintenance requirements.
