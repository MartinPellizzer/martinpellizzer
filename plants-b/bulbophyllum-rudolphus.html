Bulbophyllum rudolphus

Bulbophyllum rudolphus, commonly known as the Rudolph's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
