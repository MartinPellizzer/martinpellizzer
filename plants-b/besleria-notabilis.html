Besleria notabilis

Besleria notabilis, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, adding a dramatic visual appeal to gardens and greenhouses.
