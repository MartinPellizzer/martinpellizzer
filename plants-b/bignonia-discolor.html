Bignonia discolor

Bignonia discolor, commonly known as the yellow bignonia, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia discolor is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
