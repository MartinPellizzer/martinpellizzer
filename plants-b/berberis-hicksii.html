Berberis hicksii

Berberis hicksii, commonly known as the Hick's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

The plant's hardy nature and attractive foliage contribute to its value in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
