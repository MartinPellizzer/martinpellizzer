Blechum blechioides

Blechum blechioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blechum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green.

Blechum blechioides plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
