Brassavola gibbsiana

Brassavola gibbsiana, commonly known as the Gibbs' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light conditions.

Brassavola gibbsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
