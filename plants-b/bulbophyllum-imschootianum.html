Bulbophyllum imschootianum

Bulbophyllum imschootianum, commonly known as the Imschoot's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often found clinging to tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
