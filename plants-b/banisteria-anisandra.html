Banisteria anisandra

Banisteria anisandra, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Banisteria anisandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
