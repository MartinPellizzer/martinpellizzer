Brugmansia aurantiaca

Brugmansia aurantiaca, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow to orange. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Brugmansia aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
