Banksia integrifolia var. compar

Banksia integrifolia var. compar, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and its ornamental value in gardens.
