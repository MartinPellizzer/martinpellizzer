Bignonia laurifolia

Bignonia laurifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Bignonia laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
