Blechnum serrulatum var. distans

Blechnum serrulatum var. distans, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a fern with a metallic sheen, hence its common name. The plant has a rosette-like growth habit, with fronds that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of this plant include its intricate, segmented fronds and its ability to form dense clumps over time.
