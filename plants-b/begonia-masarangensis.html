Begonia masarangensis

Begonia masarangensis, commonly known as the Masarangensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow as a trailing or shrub-like species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value.

Begonia masarangensis is also noted for its ability to produce small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.
