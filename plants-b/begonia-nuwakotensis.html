Begonia nuwakotensis

Begonia nuwakotensis, commonly known as the Nuwakot begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Nepal.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with intricate patterns, and the flowers are usually red or pink, adding visual appeal to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
