Berberis metapolyantha

Berberis metapolyantha, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It features thorned stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and berries containing bioactive compounds.
