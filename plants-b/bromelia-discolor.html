Bromelia discolor

Bromelia discolor, commonly known as the silver vase bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to hold water. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or white center. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bromelia discolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant flowers.
