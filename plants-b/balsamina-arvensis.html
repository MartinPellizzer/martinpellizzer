Balsamina arvensis

Balsamina arvensis, commonly known as the common balsam, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Balsamina arvensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
