Borreria geeingwa var. dilatata

Borreria geeingwa var. dilatata, commonly known as the yellow borage, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is also noted for its aromatic properties, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and horticulture.
