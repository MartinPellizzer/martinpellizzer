Bellium bellioides

Bellium bellioides, commonly known as the bell-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bellium bellioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
