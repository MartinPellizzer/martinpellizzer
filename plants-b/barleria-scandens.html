Barleria scandens

Barleria scandens, commonly known as creeping barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a prostrate or trailing herb that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Barleria scandens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
