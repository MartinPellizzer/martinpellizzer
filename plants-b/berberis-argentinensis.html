Berberis argentinensis

Berberis argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
