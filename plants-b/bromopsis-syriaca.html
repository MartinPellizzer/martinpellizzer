Bromopsis syriaca

Bromopsis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It features slender, tufted clumps with fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bromopsis syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
