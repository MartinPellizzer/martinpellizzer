Busea asperifolia

Busea asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved busea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Busea asperifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
