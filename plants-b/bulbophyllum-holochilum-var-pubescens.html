Bulbophyllum holochilum var. pubescens

Bulbophyllum holochilum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, hairy pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
