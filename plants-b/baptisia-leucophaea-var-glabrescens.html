Baptisia leucophaea var. glabrescens

Baptisia leucophaea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking blue to lavender flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and distinctive pod fruits.
