Bambusa beecheyana var. pubescens

Bambusa beecheyana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its soft, pubescent foliage.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features tall, slender culms that are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This bamboo is relatively slow-growing and is appreciated for its unique texture and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
