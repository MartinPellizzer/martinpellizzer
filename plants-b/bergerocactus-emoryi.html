Bergerocactus emoryi

Bergerocactus emoryi, commonly known as the Emory cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny plant that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in arid environments. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem covered in clusters of spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring. Bergerocactus emoryi is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
