Barleria lancifolia

Barleria lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Barleria lancifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
