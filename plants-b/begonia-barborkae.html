Begonia barborkae

Begonia barborkae, commonly known as the "Bornean begonia," is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming during the rainy season. Begonia barborkae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
