Bulbophyllum uviflorum

Bulbophyllum uviflorum, commonly known as the grapefruit orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, forming a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The orchid's unique floral structure and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
