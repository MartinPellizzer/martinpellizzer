Batrachium aquatile var. caespitosum

Batrachium aquatile var. caespitosum, commonly known as the aquatic marsh bladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its fleshy, inflated stems that store water.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant's leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its seeds are dispersed by water.

This species is notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
