Bulbophyllum migueldavidii

Bulbophyllum migueldavidii, commonly known as the David's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically features elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in seasonal environments. Its flowers are usually borne on terminal inflorescences and display a range of colors, from white to pink and purple.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral morphology.
