Biota orientalis var. cupressoides

Biota orientalis var. cupressoides, commonly known as the Chinese juniper or Chinese cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by blue-gray, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative shrub.

Its ornamental value lies in its winter interest and ability to maintain its form for many years.
