Blepharis subvolubilis var. longifolia

Blepharis subvolubilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats. The plant features slender, trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale blue to violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
