Brachyandra pusilla

Brachyandra pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf feather grass, is a small, mat-forming perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It features fine, wiry leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for moss or lichen due to their soft, grayish appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

Brachyandra pusilla is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of native plant communities in arid regions.
