Bidens dahlioides

Bidens dahlioides, commonly known as the tropical ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. The plant produces tiny, spiky seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
