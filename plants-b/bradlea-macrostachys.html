Bradlea macrostachys

Bradlea macrostachys, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and provide a habitat for small organisms.

Bradlea macrostachys is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
