Brachylaena ramiflora var. comorensis

Brachylaena ramiflora var. comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
