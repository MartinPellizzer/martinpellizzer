Baccharis patiensis

Baccharis patiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark and produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Baccharis patiensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
