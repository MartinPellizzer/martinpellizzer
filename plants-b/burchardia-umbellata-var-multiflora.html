Burchardia umbellata var. multiflora

Burchardia umbellata var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are arranged in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.
