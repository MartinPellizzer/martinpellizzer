Brenesia lappiformis

Brenesia lappiformis, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and leathery leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
