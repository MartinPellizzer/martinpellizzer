Brunfelsia ramosissima f. parcifolia

Brunfelsia ramosissima f. parcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved passionflower, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.
