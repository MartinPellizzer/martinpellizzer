Balisaea genistoides

Balisaea genistoides, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
