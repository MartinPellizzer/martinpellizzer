Biscutella laevigata var. hispidissima

Biscutella laevigata var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species found in alpine and woodland habitats.
