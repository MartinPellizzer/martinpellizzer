Baccharoides guineensis var. guineensis

Baccharoides guineensis var. guineensis, commonly known as Guinea bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and local horticulture.
