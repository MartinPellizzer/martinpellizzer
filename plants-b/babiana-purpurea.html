Babiana purpurea

Babiana purpurea, commonly known as the purple bulbous lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to deep purple. The plant produces underground bulbs that store nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Babiana purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also a popular choice for rock gardens.
