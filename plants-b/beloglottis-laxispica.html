Beloglottis laxispica

Beloglottis laxispica, commonly known as the slender-leafed tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Beloglottis laxispica is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
