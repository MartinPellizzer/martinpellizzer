Bletia anceps

Bletia anceps, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, fragrant blooms.

Bletia anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
