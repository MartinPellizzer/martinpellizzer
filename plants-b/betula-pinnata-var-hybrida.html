Betula pinnata var. hybrida

Betula pinnata var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, white-barked trunk and slender, pendulous branches. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
