Billardiera variifolia

Billardiera variifolia, commonly known as the variable flame vine, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with a red or purple throat. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Billardiera variifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and insects.
