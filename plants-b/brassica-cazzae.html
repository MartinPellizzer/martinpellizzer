Brassica cazzae

Brassica cazzae, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its edible seeds and leaves.

Brassica cazzae is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
