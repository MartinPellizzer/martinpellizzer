Bennettia filiformis

Bennettia filiformis, commonly known as the slender bennettia, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the forests of southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Bennettia family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are thin and filamentous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Bennettia filiformis is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments and its historical significance in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
