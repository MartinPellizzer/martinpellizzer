Baccharis heterophylla

Baccharis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, ranging from linear to ovate, which change with age and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.

Baccharis heterophylla is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
