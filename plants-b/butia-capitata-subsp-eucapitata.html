Butia capitata subsp. eucapitata

Butia capitata subsp.

eucapitata, commonly known as the royal palm, is a subtropical plant native to Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that give it a regal appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into orange-colored fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a favored species in both natural and cultivated settings.
