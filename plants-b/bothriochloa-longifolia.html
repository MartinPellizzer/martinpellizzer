Bothriochloa longifolia

Bothriochloa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Bothriochloa longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth habit.
