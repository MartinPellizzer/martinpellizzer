Bulbine thomasiae

Bulbine thomasiae, commonly known as Thomas's bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with minimal care.
