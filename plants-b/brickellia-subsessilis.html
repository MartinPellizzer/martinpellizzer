Brickellia subsessilis

Brickellia subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, glandular hairs on the foliage, and small, capsule-like fruits.

Brickellia subsessilis is often found in regions with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
