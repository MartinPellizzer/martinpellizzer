Briza maxima var. fusca

Briza maxima var. fusca, commonly known as the black oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

It is often used in ornamental gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
