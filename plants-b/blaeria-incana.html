Blaeria incana

Blaeria incana, commonly known as the silverleaf blaeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
