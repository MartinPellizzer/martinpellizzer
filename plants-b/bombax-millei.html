Bombax millei

Bombax millei, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red bracts that resemble lanterns, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree has a spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters in height.
