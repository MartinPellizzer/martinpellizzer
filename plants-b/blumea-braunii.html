Blumea braunii

Blumea braunii, commonly known as the golden button bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Blumea braunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
