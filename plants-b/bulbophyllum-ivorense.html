Bulbophyllum ivorense

Bulbophyllum ivorense, commonly known as the Ivory Coast orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Bulbophyllum ivorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
