Bignonia pseudoquercus

Bignonia pseudoquercus, commonly known as the Florida crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which is characterized by showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of orange to yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a hardy species in humid climates.
