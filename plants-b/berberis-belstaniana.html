Berberis belstaniana

Berberis belstaniana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
