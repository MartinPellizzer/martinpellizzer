Bauhinia amplifolia

Bauhinia amplifolia, commonly known as the giant bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia amplifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
