Bidens fastigiata

Bidens fastigiata, commonly known as the upright tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, upright leaves that are often hairy. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens fastigiata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
