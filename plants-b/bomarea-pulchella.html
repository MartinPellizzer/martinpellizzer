Bomarea pulchella

Bomarea pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful bomarea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Alstroemeriaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Bomarea pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance.
