Bunias erucago var. brachyptera

Bunias erucago var. brachyptera, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically lobed or deeply cut, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. It thrives in cool climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
