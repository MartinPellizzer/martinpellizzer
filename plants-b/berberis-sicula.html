Berberis sicula

Berberis sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, rocky environments.
