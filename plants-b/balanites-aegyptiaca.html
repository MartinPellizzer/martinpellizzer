Balanites aegyptiaca

Balanites aegyptiaca, commonly known as the desert date, is a drought-tolerant shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It has thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss, and its fruit is a spiny capsule containing edible seeds. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its roots can extend deep into the ground to access water, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Balanites aegyptiaca is also valued for its timber and traditional uses in various cultures.
