Bromelia nidus-puellae

Bromelia nidus-puellae, commonly known as the "Puellae's Nest Bromeliad," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bromelia nidus-puellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
