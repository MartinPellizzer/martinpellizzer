Brunfelsia amazonica

Brunfelsia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are usually a vibrant purple or red, and they bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The fruit is a fleshy capsule that splits open to reveal seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
