Besleria comosa

Besleria comosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
