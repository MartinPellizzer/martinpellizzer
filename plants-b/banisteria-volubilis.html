Banisteria volubilis

Banisteria volubilis, commonly known as the climbing balsam or red balsam, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Banisteria volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant floral display.
