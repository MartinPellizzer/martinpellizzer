Bulbophyllum pubiflorum

Bulbophyllum pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its robust rhizome and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous small, hairy-textured flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
