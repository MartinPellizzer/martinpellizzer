Barleria grandis

Barleria grandis, commonly known as the red false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria grandis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
