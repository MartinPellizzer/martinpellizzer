Berenice arguta

Berenice arguta, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries are typically red or purple when mature and contain toxic compounds.

Berenice arguta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
