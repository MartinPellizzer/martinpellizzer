Barleria albomarginata

Barleria albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or red, arranged in dense spikes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
