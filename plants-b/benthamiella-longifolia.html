Benthamiella longifolia

Benthamiella longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved benthamiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Benthamiella longifolia is adapted to dry environments and is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
