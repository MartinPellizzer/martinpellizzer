Bulbostylis pachypoda

Bulbostylis pachypoda, commonly known as the broad-leafed bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
