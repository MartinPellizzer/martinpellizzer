Bryonopsis laciniosa f. erythrocarpa

Bryonopsis laciniosa f. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited rock cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy fruits that mature in late summer.

This variety is often found in alpine or arctic environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
