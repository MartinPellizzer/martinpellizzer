Brachiaria rovumensis

Brachiaria rovumensis, commonly known as the Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Brachiaria rovumensis is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
