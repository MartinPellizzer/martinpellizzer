Banksia australis var. depressa

Banksia australis var. depressa, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically orange to yellow in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces woody follicles that contain the seeds.

This species is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
