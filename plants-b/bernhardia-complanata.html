Bernhardia complanata

Bernhardia complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf berberis, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are broadly ovate and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its roots contain alkaloids, making it part of the Berberidaceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.
