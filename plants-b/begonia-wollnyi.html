Begonia wollnyi

Begonia wollnyi, commonly known as the Wollny's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia wollnyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
