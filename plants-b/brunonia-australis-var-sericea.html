Brunonia australis var. sericea

Brunonia australis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky brunonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky-hairy leaves and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands and scrub habitats. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, oval fruits containing seeds.

The silky brunonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
