Bulbocastanum mauritanicum

Bulbocastanum mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritanian bulbocastanum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bulb-like structure, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a touch of color to its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to survive in drought conditions.
