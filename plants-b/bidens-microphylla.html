Bidens microphylla

Bidens microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Bidens microphylla is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
