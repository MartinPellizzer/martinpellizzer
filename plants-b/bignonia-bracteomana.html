Bignonia bracteomana

Bignonia bracteomana, commonly known as the Brazilian crossvine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and is often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia bracteomana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
