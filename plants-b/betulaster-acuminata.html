Betulaster acuminata

Betulaster acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with pointed tips. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Betulaster acuminata is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
