Bognera recondita

Bognera recondita, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
