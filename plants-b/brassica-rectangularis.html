Brassica rectangularis

Brassica rectangularis, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and China, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Brassica rectangularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
