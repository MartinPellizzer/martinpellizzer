Bromus erectus f. hirtiflorus

Bromus erectus f. hirtiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-flowered erect brome, is a variant of the erect brome grass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and open grasslands. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are generally hairy along the edges.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are covered in fine, pubescent hairs.
