Bulbophyllum anjae

Bulbophyllum anjae, commonly known as the Anja's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
