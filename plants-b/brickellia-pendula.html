Brickellia pendula

Brickellia pendula, commonly known as the weeping brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woods or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Brickellia pendula is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 feet, making it a notable plant in native plant gardens.
