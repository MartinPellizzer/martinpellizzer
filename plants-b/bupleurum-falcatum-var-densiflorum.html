Bupleurum falcatum var. densiflorum

Bupleurum falcatum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
