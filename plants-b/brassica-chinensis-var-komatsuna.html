Brassica chinensis var. komatsuna

Brassica chinensis var. komatsuna, commonly known as Japanese mustard spinach, is a leafy vegetable native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are tender and slightly peppery in flavor. The plant grows upright, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with a rosette of leaves at the base. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.

This variety is particularly rich in vitamins A and C, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet.
