Bifrenaria melanopoda

Bifrenaria melanopoda, commonly known as the black-leaved fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as streams and ponds, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. The plant has finely divided, dark green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Bifrenaria melanopoda plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
