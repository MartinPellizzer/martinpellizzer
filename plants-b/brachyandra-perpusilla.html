Brachyandra perpusilla

Brachyandra perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf feather grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Brachyandra perpusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
