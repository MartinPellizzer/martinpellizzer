Blumenbachia insignis

Blumenbachia insignis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant produces showy yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Blumenbachia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
