Bellevalia maritima subsp. weissii

Bellevalia maritima subsp.

weissii, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas, adapting to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
