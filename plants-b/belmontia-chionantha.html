Belmontia chionantha

Belmontia chionantha, commonly known as the snow-flower belmontia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a white, spatulate bract. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, with the bract serving as a modified leaf to attract pollinators. Belmontia chionantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking, waxy white bracts that resemble snow.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
