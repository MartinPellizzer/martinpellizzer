Bargemontia leptophylla

Bargemontia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed margemont, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Bargemontia leptophylla is considered vulnerable and is often protected in conservation reserves.
