Bulbophyllum octorhopalon

Bulbophyllum octorhopalon, commonly known as the eight-armed lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, branched inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Bulbophyllum octorhopalon is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
