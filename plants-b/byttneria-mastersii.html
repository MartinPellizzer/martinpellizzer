Byttneria mastersii

Byttneria mastersii, commonly known as the African rubber tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine and industry.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
