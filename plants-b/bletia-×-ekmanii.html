Bletia × ekmanii

Bletia × ekmanii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Bletia obtusa and Bletia purpurea.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are showy, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Bletia × ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
