Braya aenea subsp. pseudoaenea

Braya aenea subsp.

pseudoaenea, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid regions of North America. It features small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance.

The flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of several small, greenish-yellow petals surrounded by modified leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
