Brassica deflexa subsp. leptocarpa

Brassica deflexa subsp.

leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinct pubescence.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in phytoremediation due to its tolerance of heavy metals.
