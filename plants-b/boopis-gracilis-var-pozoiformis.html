Boopis gracilis var. pozoiformis

Boopis gracilis var. pozoiformis, commonly known as the slender boopis, is a rare and delicate plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
