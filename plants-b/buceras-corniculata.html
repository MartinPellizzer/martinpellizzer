Buceras corniculata

Buceras corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Buceras corniculata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
