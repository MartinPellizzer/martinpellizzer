Bulbophyllum brookeanum

Bulbophyllum brookeanum, commonly known as the Brooke's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
