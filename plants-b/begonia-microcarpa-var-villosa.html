Begonia microcarpa var. villosa

Begonia microcarpa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture and a serrated margin.

This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and indoor cultivation.
