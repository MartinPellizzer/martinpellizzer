Blotiella lanceifolia

Blotiella lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf blotiella, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
