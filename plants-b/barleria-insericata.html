Barleria insericata

Barleria insericata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves and spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Barleria insericata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
