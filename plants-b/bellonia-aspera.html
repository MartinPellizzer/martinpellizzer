Bellonia aspera

Bellonia aspera, commonly known as the rough bellonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is typically found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Bellonia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
