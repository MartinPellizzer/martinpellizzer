Bursera altijuga

Bursera altijuga, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant typically grows in dry, tropical, and subtropical regions, often in limestone soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds.
