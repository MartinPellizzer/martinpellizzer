Baccharis dentosa

Baccharis dentosa, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Baccharis dentosa is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
