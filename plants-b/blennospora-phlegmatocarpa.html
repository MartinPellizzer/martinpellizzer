Blennospora phlegmatocarpa

Blennospora phlegmatocarpa, commonly known as the sticky fruit bladdergrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like fruits that contain the seeds. The plant typically grows in wet, disturbed soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to form dense colonies.

The botanical features of Blennospora phlegmatocarpa make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
