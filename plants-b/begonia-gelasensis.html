Begonia gelasensis

Begonia gelasensis, commonly known as the Gelaseni begonia, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and red hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable ornamental plant.
