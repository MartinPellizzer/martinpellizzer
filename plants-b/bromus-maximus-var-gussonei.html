Bromus maximus var. gussonei

Bromus maximus var. gussonei, commonly known as the Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This variety is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil stabilization.
