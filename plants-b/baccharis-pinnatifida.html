Baccharis pinnatifida

Baccharis pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often narrow and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and grasslands, and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.

Baccharis pinnatifida is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
