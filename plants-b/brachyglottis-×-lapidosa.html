Brachyglottis × lapidosa

Brachyglottis × lapidosa, commonly known as the stone flax or silver flax, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub with silvery-gray, hairy leaves that are often mistaken for stone or rock due to their texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often found on cliffs and slopes.

Brachyglottis × lapidosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
