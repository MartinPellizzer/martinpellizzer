Blumeodendron subrotundifolium

Blumeodendron subrotundifolium, commonly known as the rounded-leaf blumeodendron, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
