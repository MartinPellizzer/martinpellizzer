Bromus nottowayanus

Bromus nottowayanus, commonly known as the Nottoway grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, tufted culms with spikelets that are often pinkish or purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bromus nottowayanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
