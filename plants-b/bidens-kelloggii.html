Bidens kelloggii

Bidens kelloggii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Bidens kelloggii is often found in disturbed areas and can spread through seeds dispersed by wind and water.
