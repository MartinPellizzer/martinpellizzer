Bulbophyllum levyae

Bulbophyllum levyae, commonly known as the Levy's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
