Bidens acutiloba

Bidens acutiloba, commonly known as the sharpleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has serrated, narrow leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread and ecological impact.
