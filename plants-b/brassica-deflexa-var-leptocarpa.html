Brassica deflexa var. leptocarpa

Brassica deflexa var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender stemmed wild cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and pollinators.
