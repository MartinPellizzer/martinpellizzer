Bothriocline emilioides

Bothriocline emilioides, commonly known as the Emilioides dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
