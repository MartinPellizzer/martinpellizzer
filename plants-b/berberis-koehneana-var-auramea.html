Berberis koehneana var. auramea

Berberis koehneana var. auramea, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.
