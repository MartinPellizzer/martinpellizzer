Banksia bipinnatifida

Banksia bipinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a striking Australian plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the south-western region of Western Australia. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a tight, cone-like structure, which attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is well adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
