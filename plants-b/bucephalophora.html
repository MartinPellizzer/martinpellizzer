Bucephalophora

Bucephalophora, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. They are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. Bucephalophora species are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, with Bucephalophora hookeri being one of the most commonly cultivated varieties.
