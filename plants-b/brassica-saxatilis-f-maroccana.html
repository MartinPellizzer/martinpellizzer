Brassica saxatilis f. maroccana

Brassica saxatilis f. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan rock mustard, is a variant of the rock mustard species native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to rocky and arid environments. It typically grows in coastal and mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil.
