Bupleurum aegaeum

Bupleurum aegaeum, commonly known as the Greek valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Bupleurum aegaeum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
