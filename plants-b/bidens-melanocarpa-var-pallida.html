Bidens melanocarpa var. pallida

Bidens melanocarpa var. pallida, commonly known as pale false beggar ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems and produces small, yellowish-white flowers with distinctive ray florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

This species is noted for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
