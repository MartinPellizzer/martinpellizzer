Bidens pilosa var. bimucronata

Bidens pilosa var. bimucronata, commonly known as the rough ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in groups of three, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is often found in open areas such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed habitats.
