Bromus mollis var. thyrsiformis

Bromus mollis var. thyrsiformis, commonly known as the tufted brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in arid environments.
