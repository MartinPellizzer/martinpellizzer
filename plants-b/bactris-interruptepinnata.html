Bactris interruptepinnata

Bactris interruptepinnata, commonly known as the interrupted pinnate palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves with a distinctive interrupted pattern along the leaflets. Its fruits are small, clustered drupes that contain edible seeds, making it valuable in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
