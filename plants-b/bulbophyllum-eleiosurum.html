Bulbophyllum eleiosurum

Bulbophyllum eleiosurum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive purple hue. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's unique floral structure.

The orchid's botanical features make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
