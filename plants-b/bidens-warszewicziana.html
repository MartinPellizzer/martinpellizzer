Bidens warszewicziana

Bidens warszewicziana, commonly known as the warzewiczian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Bidens warszewicziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
