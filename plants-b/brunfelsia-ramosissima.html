Brunfelsia ramosissima

Brunfelsia ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white, purple, and pink, often at night. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with multiple branches and thick, waxy leaves. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Brunfelsia ramosissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and nocturnal blooming pattern.
