Bignonia tabebuya

Bignonia tabebuya, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
