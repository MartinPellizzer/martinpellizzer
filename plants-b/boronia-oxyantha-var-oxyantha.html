Boronia oxyantha var. oxyantha

Boronia oxyantha var. oxyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
