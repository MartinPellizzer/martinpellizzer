Bulbophyllum effusum

Bulbophyllum effusum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate, lip-like structures.

Bulbophyllum effusum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
