Bupleurum praealtum

Bupleurum praealtum, commonly known as the tall bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bupleurum praealtum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical properties remain under study.
