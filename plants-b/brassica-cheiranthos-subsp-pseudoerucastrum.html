Brassica cheiranthos subsp. pseudoerucastrum

Brassica cheiranthos subsp.

pseudoerucastrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and has hairy, narrow leaves. It blooms in spring and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
