Blepharoglossum crenulatum

Blepharoglossum crenulatum, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive crest or ridge along the central vein. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Blepharoglossum crenulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in tropical climates.
