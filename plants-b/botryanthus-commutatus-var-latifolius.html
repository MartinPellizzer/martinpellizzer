Botryanthus commutatus var. latifolius

Botryanthus commutatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf botryanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that resemble small, rounded clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
