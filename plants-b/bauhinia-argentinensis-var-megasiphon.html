Bauhinia argentinensis var. megasiphon

Bauhinia argentinensis var. megasiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
