Boehmeria littoralis

Boehmeria littoralis, commonly known as coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found along shorelines and tidal flats. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its fibers are valued for their strength and have been used traditionally in textile production.

Boehmeria littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
