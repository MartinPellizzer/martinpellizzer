Bampsia

Bampsia, commonly known as the golden balm, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Bampsia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and vibrant blooms contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
