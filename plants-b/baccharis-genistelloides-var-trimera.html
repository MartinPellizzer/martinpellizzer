Baccharis genistelloides var. trimera

Baccharis genistelloides var. trimera, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it helps stabilize the coastline. Its aromatic foliage and adaptability to salt spray make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
