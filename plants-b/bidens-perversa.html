Bidens perversa

Bidens perversa, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bidens perversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
