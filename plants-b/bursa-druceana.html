Bursa druceana

Bursa druceana, commonly known as the African burr or Cape burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its unique, spiny fruits that resemble small burrs, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. Bursa druceana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
