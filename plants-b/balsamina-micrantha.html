Balsamina micrantha

Balsamina micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive bilabiate corolla. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Balsamina micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
