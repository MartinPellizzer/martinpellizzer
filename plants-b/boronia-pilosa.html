Boronia pilosa

Boronia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boronia pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract native pollinators.
