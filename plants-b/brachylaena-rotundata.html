Brachylaena rotundata

Brachylaena rotundata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small, deciduous shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
