Biscutella candollei

Biscutella candollei, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Biscutella candollei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
