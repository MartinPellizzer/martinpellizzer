Boehmeria utilis

Boehmeria utilis, commonly known as Chinese hemp or ramie, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, fibrous stems that are used for textile production. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Boehmeria utilis is valued for its strong, durable fibers and has been cultivated for thousands of years.
