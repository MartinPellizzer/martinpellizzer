Begonia brevibracteata

Begonia brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
