Bromelia ananas var. pyramidalis

Bromelia ananas var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces a tall, branching inflorescence with colorful flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically sword-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
