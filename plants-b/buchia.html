Buchia

Buchia, commonly known as the "water hyacinth," is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has vibrant violet-blue flowers and broad, waxy leaves that allow it to float on the surface of water. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats covering water bodies. Buchia is often used in ornamental ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
