Beta longespicata

Beta longespicata, commonly known as the long-spurred hogweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can often be found near waterways.

Due to its tall stature and showy inflorescences, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
