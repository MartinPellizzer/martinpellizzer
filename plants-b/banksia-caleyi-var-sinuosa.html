Banksia caleyi var. sinuosa

Banksia caleyi var. sinuosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight curvature, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is part of the Proteaceae family and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
