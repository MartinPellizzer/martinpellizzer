Bignonia cujabana

Bignonia cujabana, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, climbing stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
