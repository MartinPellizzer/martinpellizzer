Brasolia uribei

Brasolia uribei, commonly known as the Uribei's cactus, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems adapted for arid environments. The plant typically features clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Brasolia uribei is valued for its ecological role and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
