Bidens tenera var. paucidentata

Bidens tenera var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
