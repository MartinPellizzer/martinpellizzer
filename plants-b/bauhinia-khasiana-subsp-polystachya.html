Bauhinia khasiana subsp. polystachya

Bauhinia khasiana subsp.

polystachya, commonly known as the Himalayan flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking, papery, pink to red flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months.

The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
