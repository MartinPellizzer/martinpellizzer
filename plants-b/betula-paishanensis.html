Betula paishanensis

Betula paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan birch, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and smooth, white bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
