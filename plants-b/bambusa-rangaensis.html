Bambusa rangaensis

Bambusa rangaensis, commonly known as the Rangaensis bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to 15 meters in height, with narrow, green leaves. The plant has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its culms are hollow and segmented, typical of the grass family.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and handicrafts.
