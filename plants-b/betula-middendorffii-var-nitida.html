Betula middendorffii var. nitida

Betula middendorffii var. nitida, commonly known as the Siberian weeping birch, is a variant of the Siberian birch characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The tree has smooth, white bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
