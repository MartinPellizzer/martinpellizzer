Bactris bifida var. puruensis

Bactris bifida var. puruensis, commonly known as the Puruense palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates, with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential agricultural uses.
