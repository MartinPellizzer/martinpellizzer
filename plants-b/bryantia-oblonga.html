Bryantia oblonga

Bryantia oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf bryantia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Bryantia oblonga produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
