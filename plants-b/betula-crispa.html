Betula crispa

Betula crispa, commonly known as the wavy birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it its common name. The tree has smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Betula crispa is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
