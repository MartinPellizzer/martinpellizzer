Baileya pleniradiata var. multiradiata

Baileya pleniradiata var. multiradiata, commonly known as the multiradiate bush daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
