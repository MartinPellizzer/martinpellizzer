Bellevalia alexandrina

Bellevalia alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Bellevalia alexandrina is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
