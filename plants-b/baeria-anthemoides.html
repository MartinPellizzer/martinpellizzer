Baeria anthemoides

Baeria anthemoides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Baeria anthemoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
