Brassica camarae

Brassica camarae, commonly known as the wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces siliques that contain numerous small, round seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Brassica camarae is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
