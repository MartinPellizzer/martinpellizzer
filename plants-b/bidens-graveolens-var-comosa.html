Bidens graveolens var. comosa

Bidens graveolens var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features small yellow flowers with prominent bracts, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It produces seeds that are often attached to hair or fur, aiding in their dispersal.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
