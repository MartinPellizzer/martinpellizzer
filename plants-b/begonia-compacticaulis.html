Begonia compacticaulis

Begonia compacticaulis, commonly known as the compact cushion begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit, with fleshy, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, visually appealing plant.
