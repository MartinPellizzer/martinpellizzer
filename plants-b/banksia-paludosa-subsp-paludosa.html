Banksia paludosa subsp. paludosa

Banksia paludosa subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the swamp banksia, is a shrub native to the wetter regions of southwestern Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant thrives in swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, often found in heathlands and wetlands. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color and attract a variety of nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
