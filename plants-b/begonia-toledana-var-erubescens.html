Begonia toledana var. erubescens

Begonia toledana var. erubescens, commonly known as the Crimson Begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
