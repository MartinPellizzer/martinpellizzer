Brittonamra caribaea var. glandulifera

Brittonamra caribaea var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Caribbean gumbo limbo, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing three to four seeds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
