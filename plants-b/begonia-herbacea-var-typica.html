Begonia herbacea var. typica

Begonia herbacea var. typica, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright stems that produce heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is popular for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in both garden beds and containers.
