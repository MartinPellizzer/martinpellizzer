Bombax paraguayense

Bombax paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay rosewood or Paraguay cotton tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged around a central axis.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
