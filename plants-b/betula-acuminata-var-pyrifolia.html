Betula acuminata var. pyrifolia

Betula acuminata var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by triangular, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic bark and seasonal foliage changes.
