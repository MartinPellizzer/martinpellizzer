Bulbophyllum weberbauerianum

Bulbophyllum weberbauerianum, commonly known as the Weberbauer's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique morphology.
