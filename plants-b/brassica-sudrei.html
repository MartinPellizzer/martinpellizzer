Brassica sudrei

Brassica sudrei, commonly known as the southern mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Brassica sudrei thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

It is an annual plant that plays a role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
