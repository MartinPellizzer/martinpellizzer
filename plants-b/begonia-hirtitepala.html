Begonia hirtitepala

Begonia hirtitepala, commonly known as the hairy-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
