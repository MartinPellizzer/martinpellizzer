Baccharis buchtienii

Baccharis buchtienii, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the dry season. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Baccharis buchtienii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
