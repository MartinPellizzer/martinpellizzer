Brassica rapa subvar. nigricans

Brassica rapa subvar. nigricans, commonly known as black mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing small, black, triangular seeds. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural fields.
