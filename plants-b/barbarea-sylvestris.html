Barbarea sylvestris

Barbarea sylvestris, commonly known as the wild winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by seed pods that split open to release the seeds.

Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
