Brachystephanus coeruleus subsp. coeruleus

Brachystephanus coeruleus subsp.

coeruleus, commonly known as the blue-flowered stephanus, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically features finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
