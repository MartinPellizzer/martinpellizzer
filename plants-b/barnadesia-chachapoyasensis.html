Barnadesia chachapoyasensis

Barnadesia chachapoyasensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya orchid, is a rare orchid species native to the cloud forests of northern Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
