Baccharis subpingraea f. pseudulicina

Baccharis subpingraea f. pseudulicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
