Bignonia costata

Bignonia costata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with long, trailing stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet. The plant produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Bignonia costata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
