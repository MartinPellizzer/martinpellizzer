Bulbophyllum pleurothallidanthum

Bulbophyllum pleurothallidanthum, commonly known as the pleurothallidane orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often pendulous flower spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
