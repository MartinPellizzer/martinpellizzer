Bridelia roxburghiana

Bridelia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian brideweed, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed sites and along roadsides due to its adaptability.

Bridelia roxburghiana is also noted for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine.
