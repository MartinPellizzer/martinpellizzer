Barkhausia radicata

Barkhausia radicata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in loose panicles, followed by edible, sweet-tasting berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
