Bromus ramosus var. irmischii

Bromus ramosus var. irmischii, commonly known as the Irnisch bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brownish spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and usually covered with short, stiff hairs.

This variety is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
