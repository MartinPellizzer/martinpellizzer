Bulbophyllum vittatum

Bulbophyllum vittatum, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Bulbophyllum vittatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
