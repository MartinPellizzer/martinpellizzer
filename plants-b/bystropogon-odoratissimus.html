Bystropogon odoratissimus

Bystropogon odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant is notable for its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant scent, especially when crushed. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Bystropogon odoratissimus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
