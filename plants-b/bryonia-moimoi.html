Bryonia moimoi

Bryonia moimoi, commonly known as the creeping bryony, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a round, rough, and spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Bryonia moimoi is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
