Bidens basalis

Bidens basalis, commonly known as yellow fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, yellow composite flowers with ray florets. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its rapid growth and prolific seed production.

Bidens basalis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
