Bulbophyllum undecifilum

Bulbophyllum undecifilum, commonly known as the twelve-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a distinct lip and column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and leathery, adapting to the humid and shaded conditions of its environment.

The orchid's blooming period occurs during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to its ecological niche.
