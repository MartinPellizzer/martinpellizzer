Buxus koreana f. elongata

Buxus koreana f. elongata, commonly known as the Korean boxwood variegated form, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, rounded foliage. The leaves are typically dark green with a subtle yellow or white variegation, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ability to maintain a neat, tidy shape with minimal pruning.
