Bartlettina tamaulipana

Bartlettina tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan barberry, is a shrub native to the Tamaulipan moist forests of northeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and consist of several elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with ample sunlight.

Bartlettina tamaulipana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
