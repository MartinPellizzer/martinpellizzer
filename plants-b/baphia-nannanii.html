Baphia nannanii

Baphia nannanii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
