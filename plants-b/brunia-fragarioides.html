Brunia fragarioides

Brunia fragarioides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright orange, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and trailing or prostrate.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a tangy, sweet flavor.
