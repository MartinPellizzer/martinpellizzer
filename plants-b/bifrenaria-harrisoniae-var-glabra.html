Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. glabra

Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. glabra, commonly known as the naked leaf fern, is a unique variant of the Bifrenaria harrisoniae species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that lack the typical hairy texture found in other varieties. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically long and arching, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in the right conditions.
