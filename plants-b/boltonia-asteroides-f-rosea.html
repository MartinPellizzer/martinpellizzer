Boltonia asteroides f. rosea

Boltonia asteroides f. rosea, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flower heads that resemble small cones, surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly toothed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical characteristics of this cultivar make it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
