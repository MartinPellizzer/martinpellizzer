Bromus cyrii

Bromus cyrii, commonly known as the Cyrii brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. Bromus cyrii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
