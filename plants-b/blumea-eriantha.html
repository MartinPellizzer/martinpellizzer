Blumea eriantha

Blumea eriantha, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Blumea eriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
