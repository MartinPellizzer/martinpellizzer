Begonia rhombicarpa

Begonia rhombicarpa, commonly known as the rhombicarpa begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its growth habit is usually compact, making it suitable for container gardening or as a houseplant.

The botanical features of Begonia rhombicarpa make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
