Bothriocline ituriensis

Bothriocline ituriensis, commonly known as the Ituri daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
