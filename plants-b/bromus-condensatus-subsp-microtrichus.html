Bromus condensatus subsp. microtrichus

Bromus condensatus subsp.

microtrichus, commonly known as the dwarf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, tufted foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spikelike cluster of spikelets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its small, hairy lemma and palea, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
