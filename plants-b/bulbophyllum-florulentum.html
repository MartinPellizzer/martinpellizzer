Bulbophyllum florulentum

Bulbophyllum florulentum, commonly known as the showy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

The flowers bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
