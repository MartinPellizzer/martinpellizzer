Begonia mattos-silvae

Begonia mattos-silvae, commonly known as the Mattos-Silvae begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of red and orange, attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
