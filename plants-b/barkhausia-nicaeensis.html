Barkhausia nicaeensis

Barkhausia nicaeensis, commonly known as the Cyprus pine, is a coniferous tree native to the island of Cyprus.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Barkhausia nicaeensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate with its drought-resistant properties.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
