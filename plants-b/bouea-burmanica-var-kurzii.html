Bouea burmanica var. kurzii

Bouea burmanica var. kurzii, commonly known as the Burmese starfruit or golden starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruits, which are often covered in a waxy, star-shaped pattern. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and emits a mild, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
