Burmabambus elegans

Burmabambus elegans, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green color, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale, often variegated stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
