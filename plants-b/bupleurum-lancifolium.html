Bupleurum lancifolium

Bupleurum lancifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white or pale blue, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Bupleurum lancifolium is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
