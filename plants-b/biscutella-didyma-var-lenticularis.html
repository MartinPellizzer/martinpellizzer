Biscutella didyma var. lenticularis

Biscutella didyma var. lenticularis, commonly known as the lenticular biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, erect stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with two prominent lobes. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
