Baccharis sordescens

Baccharis sordescens, commonly known as black baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis sordescens is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
