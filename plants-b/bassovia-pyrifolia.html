Bassovia pyrifolia

Bassovia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons, ranging from red to orange and yellow. The plant has alternate, palmately compound leaves that are typically lobed and serrated. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bassovia pyrifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
