Balsamea opobalsamum

Balsamea opobalsamum, commonly known as the purple pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped structures. These pitchers secrete a sticky nectar to lure insects, which then drown in the liquid. The plant's botanical features include a dense network of veins and specialized glands that aid in digestion.

It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.
