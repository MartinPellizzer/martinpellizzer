Bauhinia grahamii

Bauhinia grahamii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves that are often bi-lobed and have a papery texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a unique, spurred shape.

Bauhinia grahamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
