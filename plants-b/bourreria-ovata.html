Bourreria ovata

Bourreria ovata, commonly known as the smoothleaf burr, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in xerophytic plant research.
