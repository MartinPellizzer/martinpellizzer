Barleria gibsonii

Barleria gibsonii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Barleria gibsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
