Blumea hookeri

Blumea hookeri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. Blumea hookeri is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
