Bermudiana californica

Bermudiana californica, commonly known as the California bermudiana, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of California.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale lavender, blooming during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
