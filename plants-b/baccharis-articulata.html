Baccharis articulata

Baccharis articulata, commonly known as brittle bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, often branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are typically yellow to brown in color.

Baccharis articulata is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
