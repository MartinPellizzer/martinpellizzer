Berberis × carminea

Berberis × carminea, commonly known as the Red Barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which turns orange in the fall, and its yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Berberis × carminea is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
