Bystropogon suaveolens

Bystropogon suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikelets. Its aromatic foliage emits a mild, sweet scent, which is most noticeable when crushed.

Bystropogon suaveolens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
