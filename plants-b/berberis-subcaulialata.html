Berberis subcaulialata

Berberis subcaulialata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
