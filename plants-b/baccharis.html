Baccharis

Baccharis, commonly known as fragrant sumac, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of over 300 species, primarily found in the Americas, with a significant presence in South America. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their aromatic leaves and clusters of small, yellowish or white flowers. Many species of Baccharis are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

They also play an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
