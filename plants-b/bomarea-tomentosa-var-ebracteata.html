Bomarea tomentosa var. ebracteata

Bomarea tomentosa var. ebracteata, commonly known as the woolly bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
