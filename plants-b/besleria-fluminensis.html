Besleria fluminensis

Besleria fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazonian flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces long, pendant-like flower spikes.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
