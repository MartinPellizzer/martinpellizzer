Betula pubescens f. mellifera

Betula pubescens f. mellifera, commonly known as the silver birch, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its silvery-white bark and delicate, trifoliate leaves.

It is native to northern Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a spreading crown and pendulous branches. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
