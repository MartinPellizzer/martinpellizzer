Byrsonima verbascifolia subsp. discolor

Byrsonima verbascifolia subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the whiteleaf mallow, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Malvaceae family.
