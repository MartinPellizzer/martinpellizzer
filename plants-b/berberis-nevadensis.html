Berberis nevadensis

Berberis nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is found in mountainous regions, particularly in Nevada and adjacent states. The plant features small, bright green leaves with a waxy coating and produces yellow flowers in the spring. Its berries are red and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

The Nevada barberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
