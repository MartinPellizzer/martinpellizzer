Berberis hypoleuca

Berberis hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage consists of spiny, elliptical leaves that are typically a dark green.

Berberis hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
