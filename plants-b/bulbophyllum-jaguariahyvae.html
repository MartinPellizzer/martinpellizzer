Bulbophyllum jaguariahyvae

Bulbophyllum jaguariahyvae, commonly known as the Jaguar River orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in the humid, shaded environments of the understory.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
