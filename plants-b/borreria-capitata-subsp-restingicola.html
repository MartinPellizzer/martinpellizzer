Borreria capitata subsp. restingicola

Borreria capitata subsp.

restingicola, commonly known as the Mexican redroot, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
