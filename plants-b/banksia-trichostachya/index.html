Banksia Trichostachya

Coast banksia, scientifically known as Banksia trichostachya, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. This plant is renowned for its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, adding vibrant visual interest to its natural habitat. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, where it plays a vital role in local ecosystems. The Coast banksia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens across Australia. Its resilience and adaptability make it a symbol of the unique biodiversity found in coastal environments.
