Baccharis auriculigera

Baccharis auriculigera, commonly known as the golden baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
