Briggsia aurantiaca

Briggsia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Briggsia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
