Boscia engleri

Boscia engleri, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Boscia engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
