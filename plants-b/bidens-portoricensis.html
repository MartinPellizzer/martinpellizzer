Bidens portoricensis

Bidens portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid tropical environments.

Bidens portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various small organisms.
