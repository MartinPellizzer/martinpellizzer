Bulbophyllum wagneri

Bulbophyllum wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates. The plant produces multiple flowering stems with clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to cultivation.
