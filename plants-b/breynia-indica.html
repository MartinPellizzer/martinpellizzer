Breynia indica

Breynia indica, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Breynia indica is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
