Blumea bullata

Blumea bullata, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often mistaken for individual blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Blumea bullata is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
