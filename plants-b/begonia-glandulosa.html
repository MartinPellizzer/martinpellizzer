Begonia glandulosa

Begonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia glandulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
