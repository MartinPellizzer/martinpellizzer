Buddleja parviflora

Buddleja parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, pale lavender to white flowers. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its preference for full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Buddleja parviflora typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
