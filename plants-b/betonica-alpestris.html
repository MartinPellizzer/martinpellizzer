Betonica alpestris

Betonica alpestris, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Betonica alpestris is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
