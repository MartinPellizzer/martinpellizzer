Blepharacanthus

Blepharacanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, broad leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The genus includes several species, such as Blepharacanthus grandiflorus, which is popular for its ornamental value. They thrive in humid, shaded environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Blepharacanthus plants are often cultivated as houseplants due to their ability to bloom year-round under proper care.
