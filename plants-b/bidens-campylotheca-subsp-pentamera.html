Bidens campylotheca subsp. pentamera

Bidens campylotheca subsp.

pentamera, commonly known as the five-lobed ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its five-lobed leaves, which set it apart from other Bidens species.
