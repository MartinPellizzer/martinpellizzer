Brassica oleracea stirps capitata

Brassica oleracea stirps capitata, commonly known as cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact head of dark green, fleshy leaves that form a tight, rounded structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which develop into siliques containing seeds. It is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and flowers, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its genetic diversity has led to the development of numerous cultivars with varying colors and textures.
