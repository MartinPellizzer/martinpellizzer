Bartsia spissifolia

Bartsia spissifolia, commonly known as the woolly bartsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Bartsia spissifolia is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
