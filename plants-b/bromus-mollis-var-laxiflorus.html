Bromus mollis var. laxiflorus

Bromus mollis var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of loose, branching spikelets, giving it its common name.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
