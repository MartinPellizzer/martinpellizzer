Banisteria hexandra

Banisteria hexandra, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Banisteria hexandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
