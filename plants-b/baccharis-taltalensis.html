Baccharis taltalensis

Baccharis taltalensis, commonly known as Taltal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime environments. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
