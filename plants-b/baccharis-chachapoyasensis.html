Baccharis chachapoyasensis

Baccharis chachapoyasensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya baccharis, is a shrub native to the Peruvian Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
