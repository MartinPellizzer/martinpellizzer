Babiana tubulosa var. tubata

Babiana tubulosa var. tubata, commonly known as the red-hot poker lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to the genus Babiana, which includes several species with similar floral characteristics.
