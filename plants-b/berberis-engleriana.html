Berberis engleriana

Berberis engleriana, commonly known as the Engler barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its hardiness and decorative value.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
