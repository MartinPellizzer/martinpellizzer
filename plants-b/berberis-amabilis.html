Berberis amabilis

Berberis amabilis, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Berberis amabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
