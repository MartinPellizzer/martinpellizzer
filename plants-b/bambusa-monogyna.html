Bambusa monogyna

Bambusa monogyna, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant yellow-green coloration. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its visual charm.

Bambusa monogyna is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in construction and crafts.
