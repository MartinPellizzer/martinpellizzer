Blechnum patersonii

Blechnum patersonii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are protected by a specialized structure called a sorus.

Blechnum patersonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
