Bothriochloa picta

Bothriochloa picta, commonly known as the painted grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Bothriochloa picta is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
