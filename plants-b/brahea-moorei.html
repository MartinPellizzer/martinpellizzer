Brahea moorei

Brahea moorei, commonly known as the Moore's blue agave, is a species of agave native to the coastal regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
