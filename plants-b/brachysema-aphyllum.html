Brachysema aphyllum

Brachysema aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless brachysema, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its stems and floral bracts. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and display a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies on adaptive plant structures.
