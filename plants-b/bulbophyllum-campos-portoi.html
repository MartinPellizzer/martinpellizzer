Bulbophyllum campos-portoi

Bulbophyllum campos-portoi, commonly known as the Campos-Portoi orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
