Brizopyrum spicatum

Brizopyrum spicatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, usually in shades of green to reddish-brown. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Brizopyrum spicatum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
