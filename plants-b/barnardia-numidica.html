Barnardia numidica

Barnardia numidica, commonly known as the Numidian bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
