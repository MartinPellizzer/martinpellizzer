Bolbodium striatellum

Bolbodium striatellum, commonly known as the streaked bulbous grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and cylindrical, segmented stems that often bear longitudinal stripes. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Bolbodium striatellum thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
