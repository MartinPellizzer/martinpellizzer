Begonia coronensis

Begonia coronensis, commonly known as the crown begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and red hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in the late afternoon. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia coronensis is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
