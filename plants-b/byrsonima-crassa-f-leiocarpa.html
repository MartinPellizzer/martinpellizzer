Byrsonima crassa f. leiocarpa

Byrsonima crassa f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved soursop, is a botanical variant of the soursop tree.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, spiky rind. The fruit's interior contains a white, fleshy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit and medicinal properties.
