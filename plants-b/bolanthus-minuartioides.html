Bolanthus minuartioides

Bolanthus minuartioides, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky regions of North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on rocky slopes.

Bolanthus minuartioides is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
