Baccharis zumbadorensis

Baccharis zumbadorensis, commonly known as Zumbadorensis baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, white to yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
