Bambusa sinica

Bambusa sinica, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
