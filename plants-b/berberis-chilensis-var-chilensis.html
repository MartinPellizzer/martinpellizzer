Berberis chilensis var. chilensis

Berberis chilensis var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features prickly, thorned stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
