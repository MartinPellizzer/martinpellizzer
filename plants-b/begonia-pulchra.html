Begonia pulchra

Begonia pulchra, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver or white veins. The plant produces showy, fleshy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor landscapes.
