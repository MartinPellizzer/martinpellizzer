Begonia velloziana

Begonia velloziana, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
