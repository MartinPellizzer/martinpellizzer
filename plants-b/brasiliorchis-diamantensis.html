Brasiliorchis diamantensis

Brasiliorchis diamantensis, commonly known as the Diamond Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, diamond-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with white petals and a deep purple lip, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
