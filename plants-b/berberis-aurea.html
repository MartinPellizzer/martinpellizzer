Berberis aurea

Berberis aurea, commonly known as golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow foliage, which turns orange in the fall, and its clusters of small, orange-red berries. The plant has spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Berberis aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds with its fruit.
