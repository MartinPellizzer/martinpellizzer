Bulbostylis hispidula subsp. macroglumis

Bulbostylis hispidula subsp.

macroglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulbostylis, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.
