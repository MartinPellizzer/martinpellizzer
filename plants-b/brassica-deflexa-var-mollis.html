Brassica deflexa var. mollis

Brassica deflexa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft curled mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fleshy leaves that are often curled and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
