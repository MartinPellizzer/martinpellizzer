Blennodia lasiocarpa

Blennodia lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed pod that contains several seeds.

Blennodia lasiocarpa is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
