Begonia goldingiana

Begonia goldingiana, commonly known as the Golding's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern and bright red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
