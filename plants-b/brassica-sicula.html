Brassica sicula

Brassica sicula, commonly known as Sicilian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has edible leaves and seeds that are used in local cuisines. Its botanical characteristics include a short, sturdy stem and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

Brassica sicula is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
