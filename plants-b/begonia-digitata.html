Begonia digitata

Begonia digitata, commonly known as the finger begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately divided leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia digitata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
