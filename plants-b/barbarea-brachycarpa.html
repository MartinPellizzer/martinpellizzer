Barbarea brachycarpa

Barbarea brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by round, smooth pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive mustard-like odor when crushed.

Barbarea brachycarpa is a perennial herb that is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
