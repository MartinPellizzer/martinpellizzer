Bactris hondurensis

Bactris hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras palm, is a species of palm native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are similar in appearance to dates. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bactris hondurensis is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential agricultural uses.
