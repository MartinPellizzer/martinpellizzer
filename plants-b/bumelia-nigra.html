Bumelia nigra

Bumelia nigra, commonly known as black bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Bumelia in the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes plants known for their milky sap. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, typically appearing in late spring.

The bark is dark and fissured, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.
