Bupleurum junceum var. brachyphyllum

Bupleurum junceum var. brachyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface, and produces small, blue or purple flowers in racemes. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
