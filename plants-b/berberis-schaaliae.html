Berberis schaaliae

Berberis schaaliae, commonly known as the Schaal's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Berberis schaaliae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
