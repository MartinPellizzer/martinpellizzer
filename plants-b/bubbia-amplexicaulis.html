Bubbia amplexicaulis

Bubbia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping burrplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Bubbia amplexicaulis is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
