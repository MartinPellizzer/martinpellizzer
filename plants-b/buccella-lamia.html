Buccella lamia

Buccella lamia, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air, and it often grows in shaded, forested areas.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant blooms make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
