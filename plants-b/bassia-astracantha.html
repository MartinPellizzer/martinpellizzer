Bassia astracantha

Bassia astracantha, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
