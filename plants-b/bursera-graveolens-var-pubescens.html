Bursera graveolens var. pubescens

Bursera graveolens var. pubescens, commonly known as the Mexican balsam or copal tree, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, fragrant capsules containing aromatic resin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry to semi-humid forests.

The resin, known as copal, has been used traditionally for incense, varnishes, and medicinal purposes.
