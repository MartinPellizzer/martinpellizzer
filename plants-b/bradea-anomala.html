Bradea anomala

Bradea anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bradea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale green. Bradea anomala is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
