Berberis diaphana var. uniflora

Berberis diaphana var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
