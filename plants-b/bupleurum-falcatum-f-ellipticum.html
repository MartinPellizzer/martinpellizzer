Bupleurum falcatum f. ellipticum

Bupleurum falcatum f. ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often branching stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are typically elliptic or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded areas and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
