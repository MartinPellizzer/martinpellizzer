Borago verrucosa

Borago verrucosa, commonly known as the rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. Its seeds are small and contain oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

Borago verrucosa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
