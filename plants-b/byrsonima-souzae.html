Byrsonima souzae

Byrsonima souzae, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or surinam cherry, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces small, bright red berries resembling cherries. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
