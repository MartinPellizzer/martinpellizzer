Ballochia rotundifolia

Ballochia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ballochia, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Ballochia rotundifolia thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
