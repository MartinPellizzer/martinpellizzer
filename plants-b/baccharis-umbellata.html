Baccharis umbellata

Baccharis umbellata, commonly known as white sagebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Baccharis umbellata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
