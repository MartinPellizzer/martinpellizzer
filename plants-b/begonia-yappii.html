Begonia yappii

Begonia yappii, commonly known as the Yapp's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage displays a range of colors, from deep greens to reddish hues, and it blooms with bright red or orange flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
