Bulbine fragilis

Bulbine fragilis, commonly known as the brittle bulbous plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often grown in containers to protect it from harsh weather conditions.
