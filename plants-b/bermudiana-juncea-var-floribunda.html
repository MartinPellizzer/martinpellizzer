Bermudiana juncea var. floribunda

Bermudiana juncea var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering flax, is a variety of the flax plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its fibrous stalks can also be used for textile production, similar to other flax species.
