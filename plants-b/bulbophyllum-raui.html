Bulbophyllum raui

Bulbophyllum raui, commonly known as the Rau's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often pale yellow to greenish-white in color. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum raui is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structures and adaptability to various growing conditions.
