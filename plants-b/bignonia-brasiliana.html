Bignonia brasiliana

Bignonia brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian crossvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, orange-yellow flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous blooms that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

Bignonia brasiliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
