Bowringia liberica

Bowringia liberica, commonly known as the West African coffee plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark, round berries containing seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Bowringia liberica is valued for its caffeine content and is cultivated in regions with warm, humid climates.
