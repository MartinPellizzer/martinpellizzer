Begonia symbracteosa

Begonia symbracteosa, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a deep red margin that contrasts beautifully with its glossy green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts and garden designers.
