Balsamorhiza hookeri var. platylepis

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its basal rosette of leaves helps conserve moisture, while its flowering stems rise above the foliage.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
