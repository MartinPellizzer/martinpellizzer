Barleria prionitis

Barleria prionitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Barleria prionitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
