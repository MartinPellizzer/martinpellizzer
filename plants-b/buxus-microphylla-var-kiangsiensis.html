Buxus microphylla var. kiangsiensis

Buxus microphylla var. kiangsiensis, commonly known as the Kiangsi boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, dense, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a topiary plant.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
