Briggsia amabilis var. toliensis

Briggsia amabilis var. toliensis, commonly known as the Toliense Bristlebush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
