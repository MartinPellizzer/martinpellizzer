Bignonia triphylla

Bignonia triphylla, commonly known as the triphylla bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a yellowish-green hue. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Bignonia triphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
