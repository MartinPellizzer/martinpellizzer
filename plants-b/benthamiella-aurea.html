Benthamiella aurea

Benthamiella aurea, commonly known as the golden benthamiella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a compact growth habit.

Benthamiella aurea is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and rocky areas.
