Brunia squalida

Brunia squalida, commonly known as the sticky bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. Brunia squalida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
