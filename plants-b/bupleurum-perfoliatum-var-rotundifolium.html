Bupleurum perfoliatum var. rotundifolium

Bupleurum perfoliatum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed cicely, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, with rounded or heart-shaped leaflets that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

It is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
