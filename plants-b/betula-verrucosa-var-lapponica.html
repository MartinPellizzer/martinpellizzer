Betula verrucosa var. lapponica

Betula verrucosa var. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to northern regions.

It is characterized by its small, white, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal and tundra ecosystems. Its leaves are triangular with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

The Lapland birch plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
