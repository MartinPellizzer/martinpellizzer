Blackia

Blackia, commonly known as the "blackberry tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the Rubiaceae family.

It consists of several species, most notably Blackia elliptica, which is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, clustered flowers that produce edible berries. Blackia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and fruit, which can be used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
