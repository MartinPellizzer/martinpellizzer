Baphia incerta

Baphia incerta, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a source of protein. Baphia incerta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on the environmental conditions.
