Bracteocarpus compactus

Bracteocarpus compactus, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk covered with a fibrous, spongy layer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinct, palm-like appearance.

Bracteocarpus compactus is notable for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
