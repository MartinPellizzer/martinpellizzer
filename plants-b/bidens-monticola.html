Bidens monticola

Bidens monticola, commonly known as mountain ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
