Balsamina coccinea

Balsamina coccinea, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a bold visual impact to gardens. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to the colorful flowers.

Balsamina coccinea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
