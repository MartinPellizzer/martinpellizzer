Begonia bombycina

Begonia bombycina, commonly known as the silk begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, velvety flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Begonia bombycina is also valued for its ability to produce numerous offspring through runners, enhancing its propagation and garden presence.
