Beilia spicata

Beilia spicata, commonly known as the spike vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces slender, bean-like pods.

Beilia spicata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
