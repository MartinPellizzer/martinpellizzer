Bactris juruensis var. lissospatha

Bactris juruensis var. lissospatha, commonly known as the pink-leafed palm, is a striking tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, pinkish leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces long, slender fruits that are typically harvested for their edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
