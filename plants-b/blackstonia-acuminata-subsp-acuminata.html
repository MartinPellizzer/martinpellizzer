Blackstonia acuminata subsp. acuminata

Blackstonia acuminata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackstonia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf shape and habitat preferences, making it a notable component of its native ecosystem.
