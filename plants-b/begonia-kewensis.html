Begonia kewensis

Begonia kewensis, commonly known as the Kew begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with smooth, glossy foliage that can vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with intricate patterns on the petals, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Begonia kewensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.
