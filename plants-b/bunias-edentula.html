Bunias edentula

Bunias edentula, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has smooth, glaucous leaves that are lobed or toothless, giving it its scientific name "edentula," meaning "without teeth.". Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
