Betula × harcynica

Betula × harcynica, commonly known as the Harcynica birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula jacquemontii (Jacquemont's birch).

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, weeping habit, making it a striking ornamental tree. The plant is native to the Carpathian Mountains and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Betula × harcynica is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique form and seasonal interest.
