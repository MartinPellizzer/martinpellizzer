Barba-jovis montana

Barba-jovis montana, commonly known as the mountain beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.

Barba-jovis montana is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
