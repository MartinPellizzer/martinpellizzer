Beta vulgaris var. scotica

Beta vulgaris var. scotica, commonly known as the Scottish beet, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its deep purple to red leaves and edible roots. This variety is typically smaller than the common table beet and has a slightly sweeter flavor. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown for both its leaves and roots.

The plant is a biennial, though it is usually harvested as an annual crop.
