Bombax marginatum var. obcordatum

Bombax marginatum var. obcordatum, commonly known as the scarlet woolly ball, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with a velvety, woolly texture. The plant produces a distinctive fruit that is spherical and covered in a thick, fibrous layer. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
