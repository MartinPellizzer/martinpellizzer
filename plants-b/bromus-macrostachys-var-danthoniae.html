Bromus macrostachys var. danthoniae

Bromus macrostachys var. danthoniae, commonly known as Danthonia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed florets. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal dunes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
