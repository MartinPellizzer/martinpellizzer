Brittonastrum barberi

Brittonastrum barberi, commonly known as the Arizona desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Brittonastrum barberi plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a characteristic species of its habitat.
