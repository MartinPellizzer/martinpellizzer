Bulbostylis junciformis var. congesta

Bulbostylis junciformis var. congesta, commonly known as the dense bulblet sedge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
