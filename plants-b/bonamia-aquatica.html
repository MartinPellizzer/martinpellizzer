Bonamia aquatica

Bonamia aquatica, commonly known as water bonamia, is a semi-aquatic plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It has elongated, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis while providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from both water and soil, allowing it to thrive in fluctuating water levels.

Bonamia aquatica plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and improving water quality through natural filtration.
