Bupleurum baldense var. oeneum

Bupleurum baldense var. oeneum, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white to pale green.

The roots are fibrous and have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
