Begonia strachwitzii

Begonia strachwitzii, commonly known as the Strachwitz begonia, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
