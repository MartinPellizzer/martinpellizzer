Berberis pingbaensis

Berberis pingbaensis, commonly known as the Pingba barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
