Brachypodium asperum

Brachypodium asperum, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Brachypodium asperum is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
