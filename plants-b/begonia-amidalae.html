Begonia amidalae

Begonia amidalae, commonly known as the Amidala begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
