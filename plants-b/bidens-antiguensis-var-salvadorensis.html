Bidens antiguensis var. salvadorensis

Bidens antiguensis var. salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
