Bidens coreopsidis var. incisa

Bidens coreopsidis var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and woodland edges.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
