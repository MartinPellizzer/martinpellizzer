Bupleurum breviradiatum var. longiradiatum

Bupleurum breviradiatum var. longiradiatum, commonly known as the longrayed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of long, fibrous roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
