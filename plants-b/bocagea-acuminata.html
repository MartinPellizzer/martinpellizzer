Bocagea acuminata

Bocagea acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Bocagea acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
