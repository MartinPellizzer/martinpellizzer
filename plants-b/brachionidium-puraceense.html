Brachionidium puraceense

Brachionidium puraceense, commonly known as the Andean dwarf orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and shaded environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
