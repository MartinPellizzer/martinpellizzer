Berberis chingii subsp. chingii

Berberis chingii subsp.

chingii, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It features thorny stems and small, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
