Begonia lucychongiana

Begonia lucychongiana, commonly known as the Chong's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals, which serve to attract pollinators. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
