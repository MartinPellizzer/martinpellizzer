Bauhinia mexicana

Bauhinia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican burr acacia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces distinctive papery, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Bauhinia mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
