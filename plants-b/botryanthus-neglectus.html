Botryanthus neglectus

Botryanthus neglectus, commonly known as the neglected beauty, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of grapes, hence its scientific name. Botryanthus neglectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
