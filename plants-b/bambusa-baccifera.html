Bambusa baccifera

Bambusa baccifera, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Bambusa baccifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
