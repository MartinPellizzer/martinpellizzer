Bromus lividus

Bromus lividus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous narrow, awned lemmas.

Bromus lividus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
