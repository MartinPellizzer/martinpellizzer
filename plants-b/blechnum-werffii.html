Blechnum werffii

Blechnum werffii, commonly known as the Werff's holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum werffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
