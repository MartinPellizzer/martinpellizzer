Barleria jucunda var. pilosula

Barleria jucunda var. pilosula, commonly known as silky barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and also densely hairy, contributing to its distinctive look. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering.
