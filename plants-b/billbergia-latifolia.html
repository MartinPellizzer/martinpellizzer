Billbergia latifolia

Billbergia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia latifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
