Bactris capillacea

Bactris capillacea, commonly known as the silky palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris capillacea thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
