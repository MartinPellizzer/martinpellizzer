Bidens peucedanifolia var. cochabambensis

Bidens peucedanifolia var. cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, disc-like flowers.

This variant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
