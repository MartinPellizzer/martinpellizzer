Baccharis trimeroides

Baccharis trimeroides, commonly known as bluebrush baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Baccharis trimeroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
