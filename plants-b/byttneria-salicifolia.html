Byttneria salicifolia

Byttneria salicifolia, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
