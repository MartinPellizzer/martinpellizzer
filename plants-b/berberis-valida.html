Berberis valida

Berberis valida, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant for gardens.

Berberis valida is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
