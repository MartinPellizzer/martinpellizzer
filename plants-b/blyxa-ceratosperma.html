Blyxa ceratosperma

Blyxa ceratosperma, commonly known as the spiked watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in muddy or sandy substrates.

Blyxa ceratosperma plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
