Betonica officinalis var. angustifolia

Betonica officinalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color, and it produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge, often with a fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
