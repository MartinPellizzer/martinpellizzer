Berberis montana var. gracilis

Berberis montana var. gracilis, commonly known as the Alpine barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spiny leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
