Bupleurum divaricatum f. humile

Bupleurum divaricatum f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese angelica, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with narrow, lance-shaped lobes.

This subspecies is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
