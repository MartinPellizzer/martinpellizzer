Bowiea gariepensis

Bowiea gariepensis, commonly known as the Orange River bulb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically broad and succulent, adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Bowiea gariepensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
