Betonica officinalis var. polyclada

Betonica officinalis var. polyclada, commonly known as greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a sturdy, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
