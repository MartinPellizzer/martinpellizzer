Banksia sessilis var. cordata

Banksia sessilis var. cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear orange to yellow flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is known for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
