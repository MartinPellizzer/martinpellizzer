Begonia punctata

Begonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
