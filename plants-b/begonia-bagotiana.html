Begonia bagotiana

Begonia bagotiana, commonly known as the Bagot's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
