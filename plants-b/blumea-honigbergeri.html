Blumea honigbergeri

Blumea honigbergeri, commonly known as the Honigberger's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
