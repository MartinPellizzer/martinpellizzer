Bauhinia longifolia var. acuminata

Bauhinia longifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
