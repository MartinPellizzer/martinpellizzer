Banara excisa

It is native to coastal regions of the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America. This plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. It has opposite, elliptical leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
