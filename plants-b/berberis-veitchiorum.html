Berberis veitchiorum

Berberis veitchiorum, commonly known as the Veitch's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features upright, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant for gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
