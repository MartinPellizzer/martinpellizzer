Boehmeria holosericea var. strigosa

Boehmeria holosericea var. strigosa, commonly known as hairy nettle or Chinese nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems have been traditionally used in papermaking and textile production.
