Bambusa munroi

Bambusa munroi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast beautifully with its dark green leaves. This bamboo thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its rhizome system allows for rapid propagation and dense clumping growth, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

The plant's botanical features include narrow, pointed leaves and a robust, hollow stem structure typical of the grass family.
