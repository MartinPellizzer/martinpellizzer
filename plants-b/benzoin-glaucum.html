Benzoin glaucum

Benzoin glaucum, commonly known as the white benzoin, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its resinous sap, which can be harvested and used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
