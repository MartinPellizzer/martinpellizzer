Bignonia flavida

Bignonia flavida, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Bignonia flavida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
