Blaeria caduca

Blaeria caduca, commonly known as the fragrant leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often emitting a sweet, spicy fragrance. Its leaves are known for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Blaeria caduca thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
