Barnardia pulchella

Barnardia pulchella, commonly known as the showy barnardia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Barnardia pulchella is often found in coastal areas and is known for its attractive, showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
