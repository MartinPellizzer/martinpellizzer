Bunium lendneri

Bunium lendneri, commonly known as the Kashmiri fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
