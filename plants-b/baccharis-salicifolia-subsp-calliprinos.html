Baccharis salicifolia subsp. calliprinos

Baccharis salicifolia subsp.

calliprinos, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its dark green stems.

The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.

Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
