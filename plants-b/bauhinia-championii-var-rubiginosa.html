Bauhinia championii var. rubiginosa

Bauhinia championii var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, bi-lobed leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or greyish hue, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
