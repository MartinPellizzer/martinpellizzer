Begonia albomaculata

Begonia albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale green spots. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia albomaculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
