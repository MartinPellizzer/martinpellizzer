Brassica brachyloma

Brassica brachyloma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, alternate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brassica brachyloma is also known for its potential use in agricultural systems due to its ability to suppress weeds and improve soil health.
