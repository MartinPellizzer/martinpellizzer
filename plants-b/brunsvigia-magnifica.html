Brunsvigia magnifica

Brunsvigia magnifica, commonly known as the giant gladiolus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of long, narrow leaves at the base, which are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Brunsvigia magnifica is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic floral display and resilience in garden settings.
