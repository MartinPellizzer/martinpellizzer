Bubonium odorum

Bubonium odorum, commonly known as the fragrant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

Bubonium odorum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
