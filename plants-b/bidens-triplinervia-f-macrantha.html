Bidens triplinervia f. macrantha

Bidens triplinervia f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent central disks and ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its flowering period occurs during the warm seasons, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
