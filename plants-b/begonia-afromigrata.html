Begonia afromigrata

Begonia afromigrata, commonly known as the African begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often velvety foliage and showy bract-like flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces clusters of small, colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Begonia afromigrata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
