Bactris microcarpa

Bactris microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small-fruited palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its fruit is small, round, and covered in a thick, fibrous husk, containing a single seed.

Bactris microcarpa is well adapted to tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within rainforests.
