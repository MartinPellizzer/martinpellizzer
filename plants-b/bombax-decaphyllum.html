Bombax decaphyllum

Bombax decaphyllum, commonly known as the red silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures for its fibers and medicinal properties.
