Bartlingia obovata

Bartlingia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, providing a striking visual contrast to its floral display. Bartlingia obovata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
