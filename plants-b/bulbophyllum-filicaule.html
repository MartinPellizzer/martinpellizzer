Bulbophyllum filicaule

Bulbophyllum filicaule, commonly known as the leafy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the diverse flora of tropical regions.
