Bubbia sylvestris

Bubbia sylvestris, commonly known as the mountain borage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. Its botanical classification places it in the Boraginaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often hairy foliage.

Bubbia sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
