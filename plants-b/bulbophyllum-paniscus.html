Bulbophyllum paniscus

Bulbophyllum paniscus, commonly known as the Panisca orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant's pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrate.

Bulbophyllum paniscus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
