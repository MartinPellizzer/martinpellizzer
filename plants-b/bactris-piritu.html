Bactris piritu

Bactris piritu, commonly known as the Piritu palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are typically consumed raw or cooked. Bactris piritu is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
