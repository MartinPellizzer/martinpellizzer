Brickellia glomerata

Brickellia glomerata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Brickellia glomerata is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
