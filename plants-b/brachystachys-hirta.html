Brachystachys hirta

Brachystachys hirta, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly found in regions like South Africa and Namibia.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has slender, erect stems with hairy leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of small, spike-like branches, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Brachystachys hirta is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
