Bombax pyramidale

Bombax pyramidale, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
