Bromus arvensis var. nutans

Bromus arvensis var. nutans, commonly known as the bentgrass or nodding brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that nod or bend downward, a characteristic feature of the species. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it establish in various environments.

Bromus arvensis var. nutans is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
