Bromus erectus var. angustifolius

Bromus erectus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, open cluster.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
