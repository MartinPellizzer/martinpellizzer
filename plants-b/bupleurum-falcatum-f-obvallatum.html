Bupleurum falcatum f. obvallatum

Bupleurum falcatum f. obvallatum, commonly known as the wavyleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its unique foliage.
