Bryonia gracilis

Bryonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender bryony, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. It is native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

Bryonia gracilis is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
