Bellucia villosa

Bellucia villosa, commonly known as the hairy bellucia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
