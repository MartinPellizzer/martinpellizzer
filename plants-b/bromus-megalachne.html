Bromus megalachne

Bromus megalachne, commonly known as sandberg bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Bromus megalachne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
