Bignonia aequinoctialis f. spectabilis

Bignonia aequinoctialis f. spectabilis, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to ecological landscapes.
