Bloomeria humilis

Bloomeria humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bloomery, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
