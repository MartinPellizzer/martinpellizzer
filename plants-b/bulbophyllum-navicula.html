Bulbophyllum navicula

Bulbophyllum navicula, commonly known as the canoe orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, boat-shaped pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
