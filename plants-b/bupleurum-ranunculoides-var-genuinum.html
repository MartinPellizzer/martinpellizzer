Bupleurum ranunculoides var. genuinum

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
