Brassia antherotes

Brassia antherotes, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native habitat with seasonal rainfall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brassia antherotes is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a home environment.
