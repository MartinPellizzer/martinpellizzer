Begonia andamensis

Begonia andamensis, commonly known as the Andaman begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, often brightly colored foliage. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with specific ecological and horticultural significance.
