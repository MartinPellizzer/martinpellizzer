Bunium strictum

Bunium strictum, commonly known as narrowleaf fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely divided. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is known for its strong, anise-like aroma and has been used historically for its purported health benefits.
