Blechnum deflexum

Blechnum deflexum, commonly known as the drooping harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, elongated fronds that are often curved or drooping, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help it spread and establish new colonies.

Its foliage is typically dark green and can be used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
