Begonia falciloba

Begonia falciloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
