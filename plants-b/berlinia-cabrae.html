Berlinia cabrae

Berlinia cabrae, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Berlinia cabrae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
