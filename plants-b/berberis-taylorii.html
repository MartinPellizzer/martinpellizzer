Berberis taylorii

Berberis taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
