Begonia inversa f. nana

Begonia inversa f. nana, commonly known as the miniature begonia or dwarf begonia, is a compact, low-growing plant characterized by its small, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a striking metallic sheen. Its flowers, which typically appear in shades of red, orange, or yellow, are borne in dense inflorescences at the stem tips.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
