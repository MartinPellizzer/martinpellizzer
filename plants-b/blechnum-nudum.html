Blechnum nudum

Blechnum nudum, commonly known as the naked fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically unbranched and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near water sources. Its leaves are usually light green and lack the spore-producing structures found in many other ferns.

Blechnum nudum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
