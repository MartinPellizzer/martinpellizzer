Brassica amplexicaulis var. occidentalis

Brassica amplexicaulis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western curled mustard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional plant use.
