Buprestis fruticosa

Buprestis fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Buprestis fruticosa plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
