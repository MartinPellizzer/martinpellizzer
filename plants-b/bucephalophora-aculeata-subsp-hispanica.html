Bucephalophora aculeata subsp. hispanica

Bucephalophora aculeata subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thorned bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively uncommon in the wild.
