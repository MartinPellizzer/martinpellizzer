Berberis tsailunii

Berberis tsailunii, commonly known as the Tsai-lun barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
