Bomarea lanata

Bomarea lanata, commonly known as the woolly bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Bomarea lanata is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
