Badamia

Badamia, commonly known as the Indian almond or betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. The seeds, often referred to as betel nuts, are used in traditional practices and have economic significance.

Botanically, Badamia belongs to the family Arecaceae and is valued for its ornamental and commercial uses.
