Brickellia vollmeri

Brickellia vollmeri, commonly known as the Mexican brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brickellia vollmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
