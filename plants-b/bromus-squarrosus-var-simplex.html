Bromus squarrosus var. simplex

Bromus squarrosus var. simplex, commonly known as the simple cock's-tail, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
