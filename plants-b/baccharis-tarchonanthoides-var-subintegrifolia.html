Baccharis tarchonanthoides var. subintegrifolia

Baccharis tarchonanthoides var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and its inflorescence consists of small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
