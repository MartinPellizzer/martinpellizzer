Benstonea pumila

Benstonea pumila, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, trunk-like stem and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
