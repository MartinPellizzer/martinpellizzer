Bidens speciosa var. silvatica

Bidens speciosa var. silvatica, commonly known as the woodland ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet woodlands and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
