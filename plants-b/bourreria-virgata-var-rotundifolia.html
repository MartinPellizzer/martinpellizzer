Bourreria virgata var. rotundifolia

Bourreria virgata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed burr bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive burr-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
