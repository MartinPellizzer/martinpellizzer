Byrsocarpus coccineus

Byrsocarpus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright red, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
