Blephilia brevifolia

Blephilia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Blephilia brevifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
