Byrsonima diphylla

Byrsonima diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved mombin, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy, orange or red fruits that are edible. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
