Bothriochloa glabra subsp. haenkei

Bothriochloa glabra subsp.

haenkei, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually terminal and slightly compressed.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
