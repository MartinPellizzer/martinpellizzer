Bossiaea rosmarinifolia

Bossiaea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary bush or coastal bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a small shrub with aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Bossiaea rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
