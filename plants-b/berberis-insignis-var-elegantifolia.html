Berberis insignis var. elegantifolia

Berberis insignis var. elegantifolia, commonly known as the elegant leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used ornamentally. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
