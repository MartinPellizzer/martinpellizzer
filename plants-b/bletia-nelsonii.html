Bletia nelsonii

Bletia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in a spike. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bletia nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
