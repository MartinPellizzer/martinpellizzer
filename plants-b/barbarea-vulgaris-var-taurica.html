Barbarea vulgaris var. taurica

Barbarea vulgaris var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric glory of the snow.

This plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and curly, lobed leaves. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often in open areas with full sun exposure.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms in early spring and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
