Becium serpyllifolium

Becium serpyllifolium, commonly known as woolly becium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
