Blepharis pruinosa

Blepharis pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in arid environments.
