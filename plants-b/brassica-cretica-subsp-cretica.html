Brassica cretica subsp. cretica

Brassica cretica subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, pinnately lobed leaves. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
