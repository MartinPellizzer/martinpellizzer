Bartlettina montigena

Bartlettina montigena, commonly known as the mountain barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
