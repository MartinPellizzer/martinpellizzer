Bonellia longifolia

Bonellia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bonellia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bonellia longifolia is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
