Babiana longibracteata

Babiana longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces long, narrow bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Babiana longibracteata is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
