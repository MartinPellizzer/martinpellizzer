Belliopsis crassifolia

Belliopsis crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
