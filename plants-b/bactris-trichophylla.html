Bactris trichophylla

Bactris trichophylla, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small, round coconuts. The leaves are often used in traditional crafts, while the fruit is consumed fresh or processed into various food products.

This palm thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
