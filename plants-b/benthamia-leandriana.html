Benthamia leandriana

Benthamia leandriana, commonly known as the Leandriana benthamia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals, typically blooming in spring. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
