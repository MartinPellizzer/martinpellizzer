Batrachium aquatile var. flaccidum

Batrachium aquatile var. flaccidum, commonly known as the soft water frog plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can receive ample sunlight for photosynthesis. The plant's stems are flexible and often trail along the water's surface, supporting the buoyant foliage. Its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, appear in clusters and are pollinated by wind or insects.

This variant is known for its adaptability to varying water conditions, making it a resilient addition to aquatic ecosystems.
