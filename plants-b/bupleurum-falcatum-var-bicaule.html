Bupleurum falcatum var. bicaule

Bupleurum falcatum var. bicaule, commonly known as the two-stemmed goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often branching stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and ecological significance.
