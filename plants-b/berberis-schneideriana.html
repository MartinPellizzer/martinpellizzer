Berberis schneideriana

Berberis schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
