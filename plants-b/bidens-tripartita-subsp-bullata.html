Bidens tripartita subsp. bullata

Bidens tripartita subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the bullate beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces seeds with barbed hairs, which attach to animals and clothing, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
