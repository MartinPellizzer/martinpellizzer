Boronia pinnata var. alba

Boronia pinnata var. alba, commonly known as the white boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
