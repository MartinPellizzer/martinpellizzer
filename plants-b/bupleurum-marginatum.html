Bupleurum marginatum

Bupleurum marginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

Bupleurum marginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
