Bocconia integrifolia subf. glaucescens

Bocconia integrifolia subf.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous bocconia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub characterized by its smooth, often glaucous (bluish-gray) stems and broad, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Barberinae subfamily.
