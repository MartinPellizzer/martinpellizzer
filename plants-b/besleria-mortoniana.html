Besleria mortoniana

Besleria mortoniana, commonly known as the Morton's flame plant, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among tropical plant enthusiasts and collectors.
