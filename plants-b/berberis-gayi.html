Berberis gayi

Berberis gayi, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and temperate regions.

Berberis gayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
