Barkhausia baetica

Barkhausia baetica, commonly known as the Canary Island heath, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Barkhausia baetica is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
