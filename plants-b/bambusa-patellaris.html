Bambusa patellaris

Bambusa patellaris, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Bambusa patellaris is also valued for its use in construction and handicrafts due to its strong and durable bamboo stalks.
