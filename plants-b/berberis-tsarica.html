Berberis tsarica

Berberis tsarica, commonly known as the Tsar's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage, which turns a striking red or orange.
