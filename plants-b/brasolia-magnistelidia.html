Brasolia magnistelidia

Brasolia magnistelidia, commonly known as the silverleaf starbloom, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, silver-green leaves that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale blue, which bloom in late spring. Brasolia magnistelidia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
