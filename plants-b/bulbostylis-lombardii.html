Bulbostylis lombardii

Bulbostylis lombardii, commonly known as the Lombardii bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to brown flowers. Its bulbs, which are the primary means of vegetative reproduction, are adapted for survival in waterlogged soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
