Brassica obtusangula subsp. integrifolia

Brassica obtusangula subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features large, broad, dark green leaves that are typically lobed or entire, depending on the subspecies.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are borne on sturdy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
