Begonia tenuipila

Begonia tenuipila, commonly known as the slender-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
