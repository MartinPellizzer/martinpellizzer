Boehmeria cylindrica var. drummondiana

Boehmeria cylindrica var. drummondiana, commonly known as Drummond's cottonwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes nettles and related species. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the genus Boehmeria.

This variety is noted for its fibrous stalks, which have historically been used in the production of textiles and paper.
