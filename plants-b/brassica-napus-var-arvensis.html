Brassica napus var. arvensis

Brassica napus var. arvensis, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and a flowering stem that bears racemes of white or yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, three-lobed pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
