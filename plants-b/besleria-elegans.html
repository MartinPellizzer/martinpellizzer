Besleria elegans

Besleria elegans, commonly known as the elegant besleria, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it attaches itself to other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and it produces clusters of fragrant, colorful blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
