Butia witeckii

Butia witeckii, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are a striking golden-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
