Brassica alba

Brassica alba, commonly known as white mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellow, four-petaled flowers. Its seeds are oval and can be either yellow or black, depending on the variety.

White mustard is known for its rapid growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
