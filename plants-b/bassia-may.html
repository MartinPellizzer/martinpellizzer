Bassia may

Bassia may, commonly known as May's bassia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

May's bassia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
