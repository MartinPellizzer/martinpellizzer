Boea harroviana

Boea harroviana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, which give it its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Boea harroviana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
