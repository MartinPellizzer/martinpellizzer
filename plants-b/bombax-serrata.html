Bombax serrata

Bombax serrata, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain cotton-like fibers, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
