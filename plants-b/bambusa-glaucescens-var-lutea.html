Bambusa glaucescens var. lutea

Bambusa glaucescens var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species characterized by its distinct yellowish-green culms.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China and Vietnam. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters with slender, hollow stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa glaucescens var. lutea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in tropical and subtropical climates.
