Brassica cheiranthos var. cheiranthiflora

Brassica cheiranthos var. cheiranthiflora, commonly known as the ornamental mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
