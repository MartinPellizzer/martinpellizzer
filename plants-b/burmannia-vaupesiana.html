Burmannia vaupesiana

Burmannia vaupesiana, commonly known as the red-flowered burmannia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the Burmanniaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble those of orchids. The plant typically grows as a low herb, with fleshy, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near the forest floor.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
