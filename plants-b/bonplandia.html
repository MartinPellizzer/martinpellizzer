Bonplandia

Bonplandia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like beetles. Bonplandia thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
