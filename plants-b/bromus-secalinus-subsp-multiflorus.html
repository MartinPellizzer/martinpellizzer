Bromus secalinus subsp. multiflorus

Bromus secalinus subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the six-row barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in disturbed soils.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in groups of three, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
