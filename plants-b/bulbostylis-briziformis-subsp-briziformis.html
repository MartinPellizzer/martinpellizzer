Bulbostylis briziformis subsp. briziformis

Bulbostylis briziformis subsp.

briziformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbostylis, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowering stems are usually erect and bear tiny, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
