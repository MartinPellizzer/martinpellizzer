Byrsella bonplandii

Byrsella bonplandii, commonly known as the bonplandii orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive fragrance.

Byrsella bonplandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
