Bactris mitis subsp. uaupensis

Bactris mitis subsp.

uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its smooth, grayish trunk and large, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small, round dates. Its leaves are used in traditional basket weaving, and the fruit is consumed by both humans and wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
