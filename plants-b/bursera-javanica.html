Bursera javanica

Bursera javanica, commonly known as the Java frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical fruits containing one or two seeds.

The plant is valued for its fragrant gum resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense-making.
