Boronia repanda var. alba

Boronia repanda var. alba, commonly known as the white boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in spring and are clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual beauty.
