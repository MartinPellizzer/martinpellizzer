Bulbophyllum farinulentum subsp. farinulentum

Bulbophyllum farinulentum subsp.

farinulentum, commonly known as the Farinulentum Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, where it grows in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, often fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
