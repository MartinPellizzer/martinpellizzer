Beloperone aurantiaca

Beloperone aurantiaca, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Beloperone aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
