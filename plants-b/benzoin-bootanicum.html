Benzoin bootanicum

Benzoin bootanicum, commonly known as the benzoin tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in perfumery and traditional medicine. The plant has a dense canopy with large, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its resin, known as benzoin, has a sweet, balsamic scent and is used in incense and as a fixative in perfumes.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
