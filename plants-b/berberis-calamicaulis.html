Berberis calamicaulis

Berberis calamicaulis, commonly known as the Sierra madre barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

Berberis calamicaulis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
