Boottia crispa

Boottia crispa, commonly known as the curly willow or curly grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Boottia crispa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
