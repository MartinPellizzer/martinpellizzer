Berberis repens var. rotundifolia

Berberis repens var. rotundifolia, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in various climates.
