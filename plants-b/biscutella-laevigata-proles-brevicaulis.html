Biscutella laevigata proles brevicaulis

Biscutella laevigata proles brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
