Bonaga

Bonaga, commonly known as *Celtis tetrandra*, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ulmaceae, which includes elms and related species. The tree is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
