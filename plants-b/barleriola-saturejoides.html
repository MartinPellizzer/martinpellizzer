Barleriola saturejoides

Barleriola saturejoides, commonly known as the mountain thyme or narrowleaf satureia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically pink or purple, adding a colorful touch to rocky habitats.

Barleriola saturejoides is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in grasslands and scrublands.
