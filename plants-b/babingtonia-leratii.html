Babingtonia leratii

Babingtonia leratii, commonly known as the red-flowered babingtonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Babingtonia leratii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
