Brickellia amblyolepis

Brickellia amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Brickellia amblyolepis is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
