Blumea squarrosa

Blumea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Blumea squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
