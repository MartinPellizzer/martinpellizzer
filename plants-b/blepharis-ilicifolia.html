Blepharis ilicifolia

Blepharis ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and produces small, delicate blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Blepharis ilicifolia is often found in open woodlands and along rocky outcrops.
