Bumelia lucida

Bumelia lucida, commonly known as white prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Bumelia lucida is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
