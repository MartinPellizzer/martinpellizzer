Brocchinia reducta

Brocchinia reducta, commonly known as the water-storing bromeliad, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature that helps it survive in arid environments. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water retention and provide protection against intense sunlight. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne on a central stem.

Brocchinia reducta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
