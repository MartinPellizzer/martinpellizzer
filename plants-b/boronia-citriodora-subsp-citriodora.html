Boronia citriodora subsp. citriodora

Boronia citriodora subsp.

citriodora, commonly known as lemon boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms.
