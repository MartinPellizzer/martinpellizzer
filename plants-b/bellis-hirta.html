Bellis hirta

Bellis hirta, commonly known as the Shiny White Daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a yellow center. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.

Bellis hirta is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
