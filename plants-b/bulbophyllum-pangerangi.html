Bulbophyllum pangerangi

Bulbophyllum pangerangi, commonly known as the Pangeran orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a complex morphology, featuring a pseudobulb that serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
