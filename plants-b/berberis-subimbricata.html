Berberis subimbricata

Berberis subimbricata, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
