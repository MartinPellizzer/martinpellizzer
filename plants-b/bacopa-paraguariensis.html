Bacopa paraguariensis

Bacopa paraguariensis, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its square stems, small blue or violet flowers, and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shaded areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported cognitive-enhancing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic habitats.
