Bromelia moensii

Bromelia moensii, commonly known as the Guiana bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bromelia moensii is also known for its ability to support diverse microfauna in its water reservoir.
