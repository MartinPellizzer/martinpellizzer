Bomarea anceps

Bomarea anceps, commonly known as the purple bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces tubular, showy bracts that surround the actual flowers. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bomarea anceps is often cultivated for its vibrant color and unique floral structure in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
