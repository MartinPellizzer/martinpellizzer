Baccharis hutchisonii

Baccharis hutchisonii, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Baccharis hutchisonii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
