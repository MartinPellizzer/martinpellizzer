Begonia pendulina

Begonia pendulina, commonly known as the weeping begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its cascading foliage and vibrant flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to regions in Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that hang in a pendulous manner, creating a lush, waterfall-like appearance. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Begonia pendulina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
