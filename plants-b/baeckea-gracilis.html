Baeckea gracilis

Baeckea gracilis, commonly known as the slender baeckea, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often described as delicate and ornamental, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Baeckea gracilis is well adapted to well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
