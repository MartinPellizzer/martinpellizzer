Banksia microstachya

Banksia microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked banksia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Banksia microstachya is an important species for local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
