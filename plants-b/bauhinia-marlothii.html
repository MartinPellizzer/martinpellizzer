Bauhinia marlothii

Bauhinia marlothii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

Bauhinia marlothii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
