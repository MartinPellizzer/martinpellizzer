Blumea

Blumea, commonly known as blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Blumea species are characterized by their small, daisy-like flowers and opposite, often aromatic leaves. Many species have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The genus includes over 100 species, some of which are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
