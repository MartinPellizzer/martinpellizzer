Biscutella prealpina

Biscutella prealpina, commonly known as the Alpine biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Biscutella prealpina is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
