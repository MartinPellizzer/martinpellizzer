Byttneria hirsuta

Byttneria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy byttneria, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its role in local ecosystems.
