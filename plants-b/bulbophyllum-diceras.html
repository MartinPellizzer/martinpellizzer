Bulbophyllum diceras

Bulbophyllum diceras, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
