Bellucia quinquenervia

Bellucia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined bellucia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
