Brauneria pallida

Brauneria pallida, commonly known as the pale brauneria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Brauneria pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
