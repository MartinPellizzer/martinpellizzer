Buddleja oblongifolia

Buddleja oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Loganiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Buddleja oblongifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
