Bulbophyllum petrophilum

Bulbophyllum petrophilum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in mountainous regions. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate patterns, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural arid environment.

Bulbophyllum petrophilum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
