Brassica rapa var. erythroplax

Brassica rapa var. erythroplax, commonly known as the red-rooted mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red or purple roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal.
