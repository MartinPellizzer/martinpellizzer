Boehmeria penduliflora

Boehmeria penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and pendant inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in pendulous spikes, giving it its common name. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Boehmeria penduliflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
