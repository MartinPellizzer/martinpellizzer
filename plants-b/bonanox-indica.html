Bonanox indica

Bonanox indica, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
