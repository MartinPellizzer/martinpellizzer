Buddleja otophylla

Buddleja otophylla, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Buddleja otophylla is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
