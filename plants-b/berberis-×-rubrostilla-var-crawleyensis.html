Berberis × rubrostilla var. crawleyensis

Berberis × rubrostilla var. crawleyensis, commonly known as the Crawley barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for landscaping purposes.
