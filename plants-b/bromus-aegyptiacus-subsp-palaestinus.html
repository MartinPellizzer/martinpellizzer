Bromus aegyptiacus subsp. palaestinus

Bromus aegyptiacus subsp.

palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel and Palestine. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.
