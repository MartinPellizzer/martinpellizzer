Begonia praerupta

Begonia praerupta, commonly known as the red begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Begonia praerupta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
