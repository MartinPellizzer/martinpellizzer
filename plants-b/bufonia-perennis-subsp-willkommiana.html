Bufonia perennis subsp. willkommiana

Bufonia perennis subsp.

willkommiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bufonia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
