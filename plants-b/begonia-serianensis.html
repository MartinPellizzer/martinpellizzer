Begonia serianensis

Begonia serianensis, commonly known as the Serianese begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers, which can be red, pink, or white, are borne on long, slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
