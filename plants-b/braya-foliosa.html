Braya foliosa

Braya foliosa, commonly known as the leafy braya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and basal leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Braya foliosa is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in its native range.
