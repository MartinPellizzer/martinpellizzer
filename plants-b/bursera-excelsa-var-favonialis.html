Bursera excelsa var. favonialis

Bursera excelsa var. favonialis, commonly known as the yellow bursera, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, resinous fruits. It thrives in dry to semi-arid climates and is often found in limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
