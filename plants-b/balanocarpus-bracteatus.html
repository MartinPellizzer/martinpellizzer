Balanocarpus bracteatus

Balanocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and its unique bract-like structures that surround its small flowers. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
