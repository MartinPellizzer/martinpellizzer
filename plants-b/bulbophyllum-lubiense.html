Bulbophyllum lubiense

Bulbophyllum lubiense, commonly known as the Lubiense orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.
