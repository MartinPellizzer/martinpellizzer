Boraginella ehrenbergii

Boraginella ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a low-growing, annual plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by hairy stems and often blue or violet flowers. The plant has small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a compact growth form to conserve water and withstand extreme temperatures.
