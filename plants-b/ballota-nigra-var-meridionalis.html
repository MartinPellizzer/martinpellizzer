Ballota nigra var. meridionalis

Ballota nigra var. meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Blackball, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark purple to black, velvety flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Its historical use in folk medicine is attributed to its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
