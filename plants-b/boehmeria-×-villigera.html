Boehmeria × villigera

Boehmeria × villigera, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Chinese mulberry, is a hybrid plant in the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is native to southern China and Southeast Asia. Boehmeria × villigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its fibrous stems are also used in the production of paper and textiles in some regions.
