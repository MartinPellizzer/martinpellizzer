Boronia tetrandra var. terminiflora

Boronia tetrandra var. terminiflora, commonly known as the coastal boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its terminal inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "terminiflora.".
