Bauhinia bracteata var. marcanii

Bauhinia bracteata var. marcanii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often bright green and deeply divided. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
