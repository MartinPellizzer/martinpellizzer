Bertiera pedicellata

Bertiera pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with rough, bark-like stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
