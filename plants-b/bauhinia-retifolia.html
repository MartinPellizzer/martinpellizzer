Bauhinia retifolia

Bauhinia retifolia, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Bauhinia retifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
