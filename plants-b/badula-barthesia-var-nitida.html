Badula barthesia var. nitida

Badula barthesia var. nitida, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery or grayish underside due to the presence of fine, hairy structures. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant appearance.
