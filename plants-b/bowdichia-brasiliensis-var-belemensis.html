Bowdichia brasiliensis var. belemensis

Bowdichia brasiliensis var. belemensis, commonly known as the Belem burr or Belem thornapple, is a shrub or small tree native to the Brazilian Amazon.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods that split open when mature. Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
