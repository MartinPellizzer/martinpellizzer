Bignonia aequinoctialis var. hirtella

Bignonia aequinoctialis var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy poinciana, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, raceme-like inflorescences of orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and unique foliage, often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
