Brachiaria argentea

Brachiaria argentea, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray sheathes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as pastures and disturbed soils, and is often found in humid climates. Brachiaria argentea is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is sometimes used for forage in livestock farming due to its nutritional value and hardiness.
