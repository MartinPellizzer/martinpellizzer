Bersama angolensis

Bersama angolensis, commonly known as the Angola sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces yellowish flowers and palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its attractive foliage.
