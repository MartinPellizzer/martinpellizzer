Bursera penicillata

Bursera penicillata, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Bursera penicillata is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
