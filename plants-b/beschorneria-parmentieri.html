Beschorneria parmentieri

Beschorneria parmentieri, commonly known as the Mexican bracted agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, white to pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
