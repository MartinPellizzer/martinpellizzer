Bidens rubifolia var. coreopsidis

Bidens rubifolia var. coreopsidis, commonly known as the scarlet false beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive reddish hue on the underside.

This variant is often found in moist, open habitats like wetlands and along stream banks.
