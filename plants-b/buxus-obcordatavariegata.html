Buxus obcordatavariegata

Buxus obcordatavariegata, commonly known as the variegated boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves with white or yellow variegation. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 feet and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for borders, hedges, and topiaries.

Buxus obcordatavariegata is also known for its resilience to pruning, which allows for intricate shaping and maintenance.
