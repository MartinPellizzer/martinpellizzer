Bactris flavispina

Bactris flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris flavispina is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
