Begonia umbraculifolia var. umbraculifolia

Begonia umbraculifolia var. umbraculifolia, commonly known as the shaded begonia, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden beds.
