Besleria crassifolia

Besleria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved gesneriad, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it adaptable to dry conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
