Buddleja canescens

Buddleja canescens, commonly known as the woolly false buddleia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for Mediterranean climates. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Buddleja canescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
