Bupleurum subuniflorum

Bupleurum subuniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a slender, branched stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers, which are blue to purple in color, appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.
