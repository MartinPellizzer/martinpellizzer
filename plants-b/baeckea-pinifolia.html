Baeckea pinifolia

Baeckea pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved baeckea, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Baeckea pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
