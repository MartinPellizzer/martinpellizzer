Baptisia simplicifolia

Baptisia simplicifolia, commonly known as false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is known for its deep taproot. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Baptisia simplicifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
