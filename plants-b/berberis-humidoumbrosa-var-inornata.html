Berberis humidoumbrosa var. inornata

Berberis humidoumbrosa var. inornata, commonly known as the unadorned barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
