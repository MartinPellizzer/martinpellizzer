Batatas edulis

Batatas edulis, commonly known as sweet potato, is a root vegetable belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America but is now cultivated worldwide. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces tuberous roots that vary in color, including orange, white, and purple. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and essential vitamins like vitamin A.

The plant thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, making it a versatile crop for many regions.
