Baumea gunnii

Baumea gunnii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, silver-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. Baumea gunnii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
