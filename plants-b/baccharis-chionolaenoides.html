Baccharis chionolaenoides

Baccharis chionolaenoides, commonly known as white feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy white inflorescences that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and adaptability to different soil types.
