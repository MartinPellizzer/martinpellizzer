Blakea darcyana

Blakea darcyana, commonly known as the Darcy's baneberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.
