Bidens ramosissima

Bidens ramosissima, commonly known as the prickly beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its bristly, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers with distinctive white markings, typically blooming in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens ramosissima is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
