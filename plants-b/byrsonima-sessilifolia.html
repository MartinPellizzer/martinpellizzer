Byrsonima sessilifolia

Byrsonima sessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or murumuru butter tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by reddish, fleshy fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

Its latex contains a high amount of oleic acid, making it valuable for producing natural cosmetics and pharmaceuticals.
