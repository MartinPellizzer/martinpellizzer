Bombax spruceanum

Bombax spruceanum, commonly known as the white silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a unique botanical feature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
