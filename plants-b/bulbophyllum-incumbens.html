Bulbophyllum incumbens

Bulbophyllum incumbens, commonly known as the upright bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
