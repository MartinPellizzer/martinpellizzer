Bignonia samydoides

Bignonia samydoides, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a hairy surface.

Bignonia samydoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
