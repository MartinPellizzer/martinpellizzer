Bambusa manipureana

Bambusa manipureana, commonly known as the Manipur bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.

Bambusa manipureana is also valued for its rapid growth and sustainable use in various traditional applications.
