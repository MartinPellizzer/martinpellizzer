Brassica oleracea var. maritima

Brassica oleracea var. maritima, commonly known as sea kale, is a hardy, salt-tolerant plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. The plant produces edible leaves that are often harvested before flowering, resembling those of other Brassica species. It is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and exhibits a rosette growth habit.

Sea kale has been used in traditional cuisine and has potential for cultivation in coastal agricultural systems.
