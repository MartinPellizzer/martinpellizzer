Bulbophyllum kestron

Bulbophyllum kestron, commonly known as the Kestron orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, adapting to low-light conditions.

The flowers are usually borne on erect stems and display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.
