Begonia superciliaris

Begonia superciliaris, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that often have a silvery or metallic sheen, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor gardens.
