Baccharis sagittalis var. poeppigii

Baccharis sagittalis var. poeppigii, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
