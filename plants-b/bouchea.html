Bouchea

Bouchea, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. Bouchea species are valued in gardens for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
