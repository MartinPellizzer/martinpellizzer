Bogoria raciborskii

Bogoria raciborskii, commonly known as the freshwater cyanobacterium, is a type of blue-green algae found in nutrient-rich, shallow waters.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and ability to thrive in high-temperature environments. This organism plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to primary production. It can form dense blooms under favorable conditions, which may impact water quality.

Due to its rapid growth and potential to produce toxins, it is closely monitored in environmental studies.
