Begonia sootepensis

Begonia sootepensis, commonly known as the Sootep begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often speckled with dark spots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Begonia sootepensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
