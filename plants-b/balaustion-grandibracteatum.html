Balaustion grandibracteatum

Balaustion grandibracteatum, commonly known as the giant bract balaustion, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that can reach up to 30 cm in length, which serve as modified leaves to attract pollinators. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shady environments.

Its unique morphology and vibrant display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
