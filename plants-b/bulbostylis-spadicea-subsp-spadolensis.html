Bulbostylis spadicea subsp. spadolensis

Bulbostylis spadicea subsp.

spadolensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbostylis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is typically found in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
