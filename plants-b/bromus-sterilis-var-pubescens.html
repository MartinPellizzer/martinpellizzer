Bromus sterilis var. pubescens

Bromus sterilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
