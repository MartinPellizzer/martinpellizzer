Berberis brachystachys

Berberis brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often sour. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Berberis brachystachys is also known for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
