Brassica oleracea var. botrytis

Brassica oleracea var. botrytis, commonly known as cauliflower, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence composed of tightly packed flower buds, which are typically white, green, or purple in color. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and broad, dark green leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cauliflower is a popular vegetable cultivated worldwide for its edible flower heads.
