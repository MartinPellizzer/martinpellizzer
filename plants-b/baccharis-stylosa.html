Baccharis stylosa

Baccharis stylosa, commonly known as slender baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, and is often found along the edges of water bodies. It plays a role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
