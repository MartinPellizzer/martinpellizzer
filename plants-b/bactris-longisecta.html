Bactris longisecta

Bactris longisecta, commonly known as the long-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by edible, spherical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Bactris longisecta is well adapted to wet tropical climates and is often found in swampy or flooded areas.
