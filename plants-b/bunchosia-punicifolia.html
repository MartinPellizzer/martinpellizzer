Bunchosia punicifolia

Bunchosia punicifolia, commonly known as the red mangrove or red mangrove tree, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize shorelines and filter water. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bunchosia punicifolia plays a vital role in mangrove ecosystems, providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
