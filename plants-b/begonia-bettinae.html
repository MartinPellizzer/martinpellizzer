Begonia bettinae

Begonia bettinae, commonly known as the Betts begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Begonia bettinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
