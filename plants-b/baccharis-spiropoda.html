Baccharis spiropoda

Baccharis spiropoda, commonly known as Mexican ragweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

Baccharis spiropoda is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
