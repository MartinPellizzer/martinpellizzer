Balsamina mysorensis

Balsamina mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with fleshy, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Balsamina mysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
