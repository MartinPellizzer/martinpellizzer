Bidens paradoxa

Bidens paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox bidens, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two prominent lobes at the tip, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Bidens paradoxa is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
