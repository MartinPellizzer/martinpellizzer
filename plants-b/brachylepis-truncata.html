Brachylepis truncata

Brachylepis truncata, commonly known as the African rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with four petals. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
