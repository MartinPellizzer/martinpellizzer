Begonia triloba

Begonia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia triloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
