Bidens rosea

Bidens rosea, commonly known as the roseflower tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom from summer to fall, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens rosea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
