Brachiaria scalaris

Brachiaria scalaris, commonly known as fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often rolled in the bud, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Brachiaria scalaris is also known for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
