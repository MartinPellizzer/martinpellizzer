Bromus rotundatus

Bromus rotundatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or roundleaf brome, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Bromus rotundatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
