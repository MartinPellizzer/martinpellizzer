Bupleurum falcatum var. longiradiatum

Bupleurum falcatum var. longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-rayed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and usually have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
