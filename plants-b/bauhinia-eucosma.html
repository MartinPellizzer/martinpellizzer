Bauhinia eucosma

Bauhinia eucosma, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.
