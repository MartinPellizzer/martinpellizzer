Beloperone kukulcan

Beloperone kukulcan, commonly known as the Yucatán moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in tropical deciduous forests and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, while its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds.

Beloperone kukulcan is not only valued for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in local medicine.
