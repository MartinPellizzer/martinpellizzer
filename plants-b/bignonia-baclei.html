Bignonia baclei

Bignonia baclei, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous membrane. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia baclei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
