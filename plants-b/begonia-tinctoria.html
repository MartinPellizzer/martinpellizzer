Begonia tinctoria

Begonia tinctoria, commonly known as the dye begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purple and red. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce vibrant, colorful foliage throughout the growing season.
