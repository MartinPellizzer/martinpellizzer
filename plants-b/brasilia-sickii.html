Brasilia sickii

Brasilia sickii, commonly known as the Brazilian sickle vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, sickle-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a vigorous climbing habit.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
