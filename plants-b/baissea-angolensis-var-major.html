Baissea angolensis var. major

Baissea angolensis var. major, commonly known as the Angola batis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid ecosystems.
