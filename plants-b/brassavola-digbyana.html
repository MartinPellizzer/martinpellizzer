Brassavola digbyana

Brassavola digbyana, commonly known as the Digby's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing several showy flowers, which bloom during the rainy season.

Brassavola digbyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
