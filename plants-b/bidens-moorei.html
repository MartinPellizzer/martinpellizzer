Bidens moorei

Bidens moorei, commonly known as the yellow beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, alternate leaves with toothed margins. Its bright yellow flowers are typically arranged in pairs, with two ray florets and numerous disk florets.

Bidens moorei is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
