Bambusa aristata

Bambusa aristata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-green foliage and tall, slender culms. The plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive yellowish hue, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Bambusa aristata is also valued for its use in construction and as a sustainable resource in various parts of its native range.
