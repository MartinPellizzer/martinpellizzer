Balsamita major var. tanacetoides

Balsamita major var. tanacetoides, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and bright yellow ray flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, resinous scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
