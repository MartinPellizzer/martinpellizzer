Bunium macuca subsp. balearicum

Bunium macuca subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive anise-like fragrance.

The small white flowers appear in umbrella-like clusters, followed by small, dry fruits that contain the seeds.
