Bupleurum canescens var. ramosissimum

Bupleurum canescens var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
