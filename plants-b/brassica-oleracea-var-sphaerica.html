Brassica oleracea var. sphaerica

Brassica oleracea var. sphaerica, commonly known as the cauliflower, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded inflorescence composed of tightly packed flower buds, which are typically white, green, or purple in color. The plant produces a thick, fibrous stem and broad, dark green leaves that are edible. It thrives in cool climates and requires a long growing season to develop its distinctive head.

Cauliflower is cultivated for its edible florets, which are harvested before the flowers open.
