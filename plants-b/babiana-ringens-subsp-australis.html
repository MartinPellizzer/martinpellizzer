Babiana ringens subsp. australis

Babiana ringens subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Cape bell orchid, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during this time.

The subspecies australis is distinguished by its slightly different flower morphology compared to other subspecies of Babiana ringens.
