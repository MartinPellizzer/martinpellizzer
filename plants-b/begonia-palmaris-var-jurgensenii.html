Begonia palmaris var. jurgensenii

Begonia palmaris var. jurgensenii, commonly known as the Jurgensen's rock begonia, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized by collectors for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
