Begonia yapenensis

Begonia yapenensis, commonly known as the Yapen begonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Yapen in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
