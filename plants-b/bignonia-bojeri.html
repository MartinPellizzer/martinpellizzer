Bignonia bojeri

Bignonia bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's crossvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a vigorous climber with pinnately compound leaves and long, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bignonia bojeri thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
