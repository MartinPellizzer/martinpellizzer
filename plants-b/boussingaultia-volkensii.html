Boussingaultia volkensii

Boussingaultia volkensii, commonly known as the East African orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Boussingaultia volkensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
