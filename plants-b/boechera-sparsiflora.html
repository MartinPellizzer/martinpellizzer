Boechera sparsiflora

Boechera sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
