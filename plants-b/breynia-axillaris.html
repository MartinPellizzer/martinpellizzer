Breynia axillaris

Breynia axillaris, commonly known as the red-breynia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Breynia axillaris is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
