Berlinia immaculata

Berlinia immaculata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, woody pods containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
