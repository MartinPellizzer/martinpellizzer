Bischofia leptopoda

Bischofia leptopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bishop's candle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicles and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
