Bathmium plantagineum var. serratum

Bathmium plantagineum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated bathmium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of succulent plants.
