Bassia coriacea

Bassia coriacea, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a hardy annual plant native to Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, round, greenish-brown fruits. It thrives in poor, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
