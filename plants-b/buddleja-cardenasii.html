Buddleja cardenasii

Buddleja cardenasii, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
