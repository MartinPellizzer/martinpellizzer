Ballota nigra

Ballota nigra, commonly known as black ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has trifoliate leaves. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Ballota nigra is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
