Balsamina rosmarinifolia

Balsamina rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary leaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Balsamina rosmarinifolia is relatively easy to grow and is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and fragrant foliage.
