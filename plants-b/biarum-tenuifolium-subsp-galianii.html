Biarum tenuifolium subsp. galianii

Biarum tenuifolium subsp.

galianii, commonly known as the Galiani bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plant species.
