Bonannia officinalis

Bonannia officinalis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its oval, glossy green leaves and small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the winter. The plant is highly valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to its high vitamin C content. Its fruits are often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and are also utilized in culinary applications.

Bonannia officinalis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
