Berberis buchananii var. tawangensis

Berberis buchananii var. tawangensis, commonly known as Tawang barberry, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
