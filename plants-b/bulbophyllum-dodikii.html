Bulbophyllum dodikii

Bulbophyllum dodikii, commonly known as the Dodik's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often with a distinctive coloration pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
