Berberis jingguensis

Berberis jingguensis, commonly known as the Jinggu barberry, is a shrub native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of berberine. It thrives in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
