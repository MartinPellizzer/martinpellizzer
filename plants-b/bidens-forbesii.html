Bidens forbesii

Bidens forbesii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens forbesii is often found in moist habitats like wet meadows and along stream banks.
