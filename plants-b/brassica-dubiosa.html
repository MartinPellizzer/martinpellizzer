Brassica dubiosa

Brassica dubiosa, commonly known as the ghost mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
