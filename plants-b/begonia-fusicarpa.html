Begonia fusicarpa

Begonia fusicarpa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a trailing or upright form depending on the cultivar. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
