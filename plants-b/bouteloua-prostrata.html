Bouteloua prostrata

Bouteloua prostrata, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed areas. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
