Begonia sosefiana

Begonia sosefiana, commonly known as the Sosef's begonia, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often dark green with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
