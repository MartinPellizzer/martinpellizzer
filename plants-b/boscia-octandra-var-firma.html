Boscia octandra var. firma

Boscia octandra var. firma, commonly known as the red bosco or red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, woody fruits that contain a single seed. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
