Blitum maius

Blitum maius, commonly known as the greater bittercress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, producing tiny, elongated pods that contain the seeds.
