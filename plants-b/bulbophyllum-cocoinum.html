Bulbophyllum cocoinum

Bulbophyllum cocoinum, commonly known as the coconut orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces intricate, showy inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its bulbs are an essential part of its structure, storing nutrients and water for survival.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
