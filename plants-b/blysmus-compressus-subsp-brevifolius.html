Blysmus compressus subsp. brevifolius

Blysmus compressus subsp.

brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blysmus, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing dune ecosystems.
