Banisteriopsis brevipedicellata

Banisteriopsis brevipedicellata, commonly known as the "Brazillian cherry" or "mamoeira," is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's ecological role is significant in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
