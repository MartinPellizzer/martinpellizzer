Benthamia nivea subsp. parviflora

Benthamia nivea subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the white benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and the species is characterized by its compact growth habit.
