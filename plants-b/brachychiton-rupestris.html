Brachychiton rupestris

Brachychiton rupestris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Brachychiton rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
