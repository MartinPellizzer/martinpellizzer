Brachypodium pinnatum var. setifolium

Brachypodium pinnatum var. setifolium, commonly known as silky brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
