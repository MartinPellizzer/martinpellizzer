Berendtia rugosa

Berendtia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Berendtia rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
