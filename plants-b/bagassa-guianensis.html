Bagassa guianensis

Bagassa guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese soursop, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that contains a juicy, seed-filled pulp. Its botanical features include rough, fissured bark and a tendency to form dense thickets.
