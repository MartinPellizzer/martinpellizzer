Boerhavia marlothii

Boerhavia marlothii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or yellow bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its branched stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit, which resembles a small berry, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Boerhavia marlothii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
