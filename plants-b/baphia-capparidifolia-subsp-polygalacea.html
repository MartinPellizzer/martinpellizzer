Baphia capparidifolia subsp. polygalacea

Baphia capparidifolia subsp.

polygalacea, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or savanna habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its botanical features are less well-documented compared to other species in the Baphia genus.
