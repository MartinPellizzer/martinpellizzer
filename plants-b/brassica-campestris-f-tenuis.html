Brassica campestris f. tenuis

Brassica campestris f. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a botanical variant of the cabbage family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates. It is often used in agricultural settings as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and ability to suppress weeds.

The plant is also valued for its potential in biofuel production and as a source of natural compounds.
