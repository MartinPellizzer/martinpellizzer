Britoa lundiana

Britoa lundiana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Britoa lundiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
