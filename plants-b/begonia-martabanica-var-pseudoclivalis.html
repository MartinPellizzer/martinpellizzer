Begonia martabanica var. pseudoclivalis

Begonia martabanica var. pseudoclivalis, commonly known as the Martaban begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that often display deep green with reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts and horticulturists.
