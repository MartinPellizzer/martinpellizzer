Boronia denticulata subsp. denticulata

Boronia denticulata subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The flowers bloom throughout the spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
