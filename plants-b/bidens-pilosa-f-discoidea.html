Bidens pilosa f. discoidea

Bidens pilosa f. discoidea, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bracts that resemble small leaves. Its stems are typically hairy and can become woody with age, while the leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
