Bulbophyllum ulcerosum

Bulbophyllum ulcerosum, commonly known as the ulcerated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often wrinkled pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical rainforests.
