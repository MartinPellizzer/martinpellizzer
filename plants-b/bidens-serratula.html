Bidens serratula

Bidens serratula, commonly known as the serrated tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets and disk flowers, blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Bidens serratula is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
