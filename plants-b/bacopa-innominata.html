Bacopa innominata

Bacopa innominata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water bead, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and wetlands.

Bacopa innominata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in water gardens.
