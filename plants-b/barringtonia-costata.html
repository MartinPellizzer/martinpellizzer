Barringtonia costata

Barringtonia costata, commonly known as the Whitebeard's tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree has a distinctive, rough, greyish bark and produces a spherical fruit that splits open when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
