Boechera crandallii subsp. crandallii

Boechera crandallii subsp.

crandallii, commonly known as the Crandall's rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

This subspecies is native to western North America and is adapted to harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
