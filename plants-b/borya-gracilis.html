Borya gracilis

Borya gracilis, commonly known as the slender borya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Borya gracilis is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in full sun.
