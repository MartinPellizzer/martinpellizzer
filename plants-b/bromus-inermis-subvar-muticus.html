Bromus inermis subvar. muticus

Bromus inermis subvar. muticus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually less than 5 cm in length.

This subspecies is characterized by its low-growing habit and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
