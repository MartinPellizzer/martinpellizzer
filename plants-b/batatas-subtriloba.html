Batatas subtriloba

Batatas subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces tuberous roots that are typically orange or purple in color, rich in nutrients like beta-carotene and fiber. Its growth habit is vine-like, with sprawling stems that can reach several meters in length.

The tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and are valued for their sweet flavor and nutritional benefits.
