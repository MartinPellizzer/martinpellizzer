Brachypodium distachyon var. paradoxum

Brachypodium distachyon var. paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox variety of brachypodium, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is often used in ecological and genetic studies due to its short life cycle and genetic simplicity. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal environments.

The plant's flowering stems produce small, two-flowered spikes, which are typical of the Brachypodium genus.
