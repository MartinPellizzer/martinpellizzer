Bertolonia violacea

Bertolonia violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
