Brassica subhastata

Brassica subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Brassica subhastata is an annual plant that is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which can be used as a spice or in traditional medicine.
