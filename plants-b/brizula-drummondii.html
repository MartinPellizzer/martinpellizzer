Brizula drummondii

Brizula drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Brizula drummondii is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can thrive in conditions with limited rainfall.
