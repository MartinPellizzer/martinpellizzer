Bignonia catharinensis

Bignonia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cross vine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Bignonia catharinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping or as a climbing plant.
