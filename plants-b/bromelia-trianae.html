Bromelia trianae

Bromelia trianae, commonly known as the Trianas bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can last for several months. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface, which helps in water retention.

Bromelia trianae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
