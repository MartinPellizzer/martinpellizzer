Baphia preussii

Baphia preussii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces pods containing flat, winged seeds, which aid in its dispersal. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
