Bursera schlechtendalii

Bursera schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Mexican birch or bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds.

Bursera schlechtendalii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
