Besleria salicifolia

Besleria salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, willow-like leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
