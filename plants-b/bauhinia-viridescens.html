Bauhinia viridescens

Bauhinia viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves that turn a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Bauhinia viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
