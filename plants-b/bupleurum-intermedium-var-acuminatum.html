Bupleurum intermedium var. acuminatum

Bupleurum intermedium var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated threadleaf bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pale yellow and arranged in umbel-like clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
