Bellevalia persica

Bellevalia persica, commonly known as Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.
