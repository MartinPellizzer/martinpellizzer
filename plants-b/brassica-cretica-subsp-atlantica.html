Brassica cretica subsp. atlantica

Brassica cretica subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

Native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in maritime environments and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.
