Bignonia sagrana

Bignonia sagrana, commonly known as the golden glory vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, trumpet-shaped form. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and long, twisting stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
