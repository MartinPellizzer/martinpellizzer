Begonia ovatifolia var. cretacea

Begonia ovatifolia var. cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous begonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded indoor environments.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists.
