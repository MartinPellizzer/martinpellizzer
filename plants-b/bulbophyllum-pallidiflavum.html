Bulbophyllum pallidiflavum

Bulbophyllum pallidiflavum, commonly known as the pale yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal environments. The plant produces showy, yellow-green flowers with intricate patterns, often blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss in the wild.
