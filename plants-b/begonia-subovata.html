Begonia subovata

Begonia subovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved begonia, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
