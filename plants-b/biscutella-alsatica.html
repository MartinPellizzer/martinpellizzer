Biscutella alsatica

Biscutella alsatica, commonly known as the alpine biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Biscutella alsatica is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it thrives in full sun.
