Bletia platyphylla

Bletia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf bletia, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the wet season. Bletia platyphylla thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
