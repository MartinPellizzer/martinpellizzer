Berthelotia lanceolata var. indica

Berthelotia lanceolata var. indica, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
