Bacopa simulans

Bacopa simulans, commonly known as the small water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically rounded or ovate, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
