Bromus erectus var. pubiculmis

Bromus erectus var. pubiculmis, commonly known as the pubescent browntop, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, pubescent leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Bromus erectus var. pubiculmis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
