Brassica chenopodiifolia

Brassica chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and has a low, spreading growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
