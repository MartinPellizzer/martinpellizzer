Bursera sarcopoda

Bursera sarcopoda, commonly known as the "bark tree" or "Mexican frankincense tree," is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers and spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of dry forest and scrub ecosystems.
