Beilschmiedia purpurascens

Beilschmiedia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by dark purple or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
