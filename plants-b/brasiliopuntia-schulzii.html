Brasiliopuntia schulzii

Brasiliopuntia schulzii, commonly known as the Schultze's barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its spines are modified leaves, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

Brasiliopuntia schulzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
