Blakwellia deplanchei

Blakwellia deplanchei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient weed in some regions.
