Bauhinia emarginella

Bauhinia emarginella, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its fruit is a flattened pod containing several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Bauhinia emarginella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
