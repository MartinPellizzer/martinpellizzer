Berkheyopsis bechuanensis

Berkheyopsis bechuanensis, commonly known as the Bechuanian geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Berkheyopsis bechuanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
