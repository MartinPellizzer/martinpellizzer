Blumea crispata var. appendiculata

Blumea crispata var. appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
