Bellardia latifolia subsp. flaviflora

Bellardia latifolia subsp.

flaviflora, commonly known as the goldenflower bellardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to arid conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
