Bryonopsis pancheri

Bryonopsis pancheri, commonly known as the Kashmir stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
