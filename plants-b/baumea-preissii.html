Baumea preissii

Baumea preissii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, white or pale blue plumes during the flowering season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Baumea preissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its elegant, feathery foliage.
