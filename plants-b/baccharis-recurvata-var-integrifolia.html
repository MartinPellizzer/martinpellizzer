Baccharis recurvata var. integrifolia

Baccharis recurvata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, often in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
