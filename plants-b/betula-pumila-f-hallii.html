Betula pumila f. hallii

Betula pumila f. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's paper birch, is a rare and distinctive variant of the paper birch species.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and finely textured, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in cool, shaded environments such as northern forests. Its small, rounded leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and turn yellow in the fall.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
