Baccharis resinosa

Baccharis resinosa, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces resinous sap, which gives it a distinctive scent and has been used traditionally for various purposes. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis resinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
