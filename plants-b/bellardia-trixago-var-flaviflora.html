Bellardia trixago var. flaviflora

Bellardia trixago var. flaviflora, commonly known as the golden bellardia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the stems often forming dense clumps.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
