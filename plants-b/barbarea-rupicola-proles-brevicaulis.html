Barbarea rupicola proles brevicaulis

Barbarea rupicola proles brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and lobed, often with a slightly succulent texture.

Native to rocky and alpine environments, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
