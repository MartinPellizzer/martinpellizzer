Bulbostylis multispiculata

Bulbostylis multispiculata, commonly known as the many-spined bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
