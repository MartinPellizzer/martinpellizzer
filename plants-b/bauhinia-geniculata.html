Bauhinia geniculata

Bauhinia geniculata, commonly known as the climbing bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Bauhinia geniculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
