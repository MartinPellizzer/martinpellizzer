Brachiaria distachyoides

Brachiaria distachyoides, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically clustered in pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Brachiaria distachyoides is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
