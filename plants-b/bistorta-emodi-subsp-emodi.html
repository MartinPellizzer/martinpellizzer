Bistorta emodi subsp. emodi

Bistorta emodi subsp.

emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stem and dark purple to black rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy, pink to red bracteate flowers in late summer, which are clustered at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Bistorta emodi subsp.

emodi thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
