Butinia libanotica

Butinia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Butinia libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
