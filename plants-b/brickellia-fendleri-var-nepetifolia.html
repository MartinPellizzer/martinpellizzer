Brickellia fendleri var. nepetifolia

Brickellia fendleri var. nepetifolia, commonly known as the Mexican brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, with a unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
