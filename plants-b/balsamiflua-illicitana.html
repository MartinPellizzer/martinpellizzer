Balsamiflua illicitana

Balsamiflua illicitana, commonly known as the secret balsam, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a sticky, balsamic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its unique chemical composition includes compounds with potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, it remains a subject of botanical interest and conservation efforts.
