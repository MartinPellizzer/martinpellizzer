Botryanthus lelievrei var. strangwaysii

Botryanthus lelievrei var. strangwaysii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular houseplant due to its compact size and long blooming period.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among both amateur and professional gardeners.
