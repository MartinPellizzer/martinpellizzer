Burlingtonia leeana

Burlingtonia leeana, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in spring, contributing to its ecological role in arid ecosystems.

Burlingtonia leeana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
