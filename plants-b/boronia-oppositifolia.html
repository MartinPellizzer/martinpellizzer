Boronia oppositifolia

Boronia oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in heathlands and dry forests.

Boronia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
