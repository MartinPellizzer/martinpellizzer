Bernardia peduncularis var. hirsutissima

Bernardia peduncularis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bernardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. This species is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
