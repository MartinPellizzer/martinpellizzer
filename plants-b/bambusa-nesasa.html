Bambusa nesasa

Bambusa nesasa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Vietnam. This bamboo is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. It grows in dense clumps and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its strong and lightweight culms, which can be used in construction and crafts.
