Baccharis schiedeana

Baccharis schiedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common component of coastal and inland vegetation.
