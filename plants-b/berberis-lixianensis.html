Berberis lixianensis

Berberis lixianensis, commonly known as the Lixian barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
