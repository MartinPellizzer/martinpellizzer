Berberis baluchistanica

Berberis baluchistanica, commonly known as the Baluchistan barberry, is a shrub native to the rugged regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp, tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
