Bryonia hederifolia

Bryonia hederifolia, commonly known as the common bryony, is a perennial climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces toxic, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and western Asia.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
