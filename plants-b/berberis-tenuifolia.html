Berberis tenuifolia

Berberis tenuifolia, commonly known as dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, turning reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Berberis tenuifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
