Bryophyllum beauverdii

Bryophyllum beauverdii, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the warmer months. Its unique method of vegetative propagation involves tiny plantlets forming along the edges of its leaves, which can easily take root and grow into new plants.

This adaptability makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
