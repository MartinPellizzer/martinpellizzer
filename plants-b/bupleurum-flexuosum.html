Bupleurum flexuosum

Bupleurum flexuosum, commonly known as flexible bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Bupleurum flexuosum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
