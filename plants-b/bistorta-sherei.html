Bistorta sherei

Bistorta sherei, commonly known as Siberian bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible roots. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typically blooming in shades of pink or purple.

Bistorta sherei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
