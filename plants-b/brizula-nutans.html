Brizula nutans

Brizula nutans, commonly known as the drooping brizula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Brizula nutans is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.
