Baccharis magellanica var. subviscosa

Baccharis magellanica var. subviscosa, commonly known as the sticky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often glandular foliage that gives it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough, sometimes hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
