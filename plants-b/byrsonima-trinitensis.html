Byrsonima trinitensis

Byrsonima trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad cherry or red mulberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are a significant food source for local wildlife.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in the region.
