Biscutella macroclada

Biscutella macroclada, commonly known as the large-fruited biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Biscutella macroclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
