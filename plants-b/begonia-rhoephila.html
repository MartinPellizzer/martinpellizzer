Begonia rhoephila

Begonia rhoephila, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Begonia rhoephila is widely cultivated by gardeners and botanists alike.
