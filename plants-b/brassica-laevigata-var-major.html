Brassica laevigata var. major

Brassica laevigata var. major, commonly known as the giant mustard, is a robust annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.
