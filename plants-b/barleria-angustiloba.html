Barleria angustiloba

Barleria angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with two lobes, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
