Begonia valvata

Begonia valvata, commonly known as the valley begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
