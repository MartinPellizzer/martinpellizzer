Beta trigyna

Beta trigyna, commonly known as garden beets, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, edible taproot and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with smooth, ribbed stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Beta trigyna is cultivated for its nutritious root and leaves, which are rich in vitamins and minerals.
