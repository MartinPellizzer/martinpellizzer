Berberis petiolaris var. extensa

Berberis petiolaris var. extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
