Bactris acanthocarpa var. trailiana

Bactris acanthocarpa var. trailiana, commonly known as the Trinidadian palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a small to medium-sized palm, typically growing to a height of 5–8 meters, with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits that are approximately 3–5 centimeters in diameter.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material, while the palm is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
