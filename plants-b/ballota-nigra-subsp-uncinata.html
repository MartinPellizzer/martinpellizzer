Ballota nigra subsp. uncinata

Ballota nigra subsp.

uncinata, commonly known as the hooked ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hooked stipules on the leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
