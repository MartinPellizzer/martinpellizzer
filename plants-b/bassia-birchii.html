Bassia birchii

Bassia birchii, commonly known as silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Bassia birchii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
