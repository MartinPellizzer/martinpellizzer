Brachypodium gandogeri

Brachypodium gandogeri, commonly known as the dwarf brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

Brachypodium gandogeri is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
