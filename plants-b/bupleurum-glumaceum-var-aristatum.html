Bupleurum glumaceum var. aristatum

Bupleurum glumaceum var. aristatum, commonly known as the aristate bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
