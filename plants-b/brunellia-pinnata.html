Brunellia pinnata

Brunellia pinnata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
