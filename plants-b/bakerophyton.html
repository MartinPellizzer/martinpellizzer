Bakerophyton

Bakerophyton, commonly known as the "baker's plant," is a type of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that can spread across the water's surface. Bakerophyton is known for its ability to absorb pollutants, making it useful in water purification.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizome system that helps it anchor and spread in wetland habitats.
