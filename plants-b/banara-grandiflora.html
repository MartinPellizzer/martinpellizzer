Banara grandiflora

Banara grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered banara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Banara grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.
