Bauhinia armata

Bauhinia armata, commonly known as the crown flower or flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like clusters of pink or red flowers that appear in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Bauhinia armata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
