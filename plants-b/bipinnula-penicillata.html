Bipinnula penicillata

Bipinnula penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately compound leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields. Its rhizomes are often submerged, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This fern plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
