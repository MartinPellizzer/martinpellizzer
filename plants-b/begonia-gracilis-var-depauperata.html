Begonia gracilis var. depauperata

Begonia gracilis var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
