Baikiaea insignis

Baikiaea insignis, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, durable timber and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The tree has compound leaves that are typically alternate and can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruits are pod-like and contain several seeds.

Baikiaea insignis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
