Boehmeria platanifolia var. tricuspis

Boehmeria platanifolia var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed banana plant, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinct three lobes, which give it its common name. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green, sometimes with a slight variegation, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
