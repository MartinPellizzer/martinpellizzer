Berberis australis var. hackeliana

Berberis australis var. hackeliana, commonly known as the Hackel's barberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but generally not palatable to humans. It is often found in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler climates.
