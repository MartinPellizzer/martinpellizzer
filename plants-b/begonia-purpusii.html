Begonia purpusii

Begonia purpusii, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which features a waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
