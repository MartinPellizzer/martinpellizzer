Begonia georgei

Begonia georgei, commonly known as the George's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a velvety underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia georgei is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
