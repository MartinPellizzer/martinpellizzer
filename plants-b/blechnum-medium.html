Blechnum medium

Blechnum medium, commonly known as the intermediate water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or near water sources. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum medium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
