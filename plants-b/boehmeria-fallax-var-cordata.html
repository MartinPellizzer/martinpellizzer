Boehmeria fallax var. cordata

Boehmeria fallax var. cordata, commonly known as the silky fiber plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family. It is valued for its fibrous stalks, which are used in traditional crafts and textiles.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is also cultivated as a decorative plant in certain regions.
