Brachylaena discolor subsp. transvaalensis

Brachylaena discolor subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal brachylaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and grayish, fissured bark.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It produces small, spherical fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
