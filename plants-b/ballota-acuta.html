Ballota acuta

Ballota acuta, commonly known as the sharpweed or narrowleaf ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with narrow, pointed leaflets.

Ballota acuta is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
