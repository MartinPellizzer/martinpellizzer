Berberis favosa

Berberis favosa, commonly known as the yellow barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Berberis favosa is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
