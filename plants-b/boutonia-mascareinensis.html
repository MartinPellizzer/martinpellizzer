Boutonia mascareinensis

Boutonia mascareinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a single, fan-shaped leaf that can reach up to 2 meters in length. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense cluster, and are highly fragrant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
