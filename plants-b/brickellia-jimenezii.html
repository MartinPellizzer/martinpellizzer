Brickellia jimenezii

Brickellia jimenezii, commonly known as Mexican brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a low, sprawling growth habit, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Brickellia jimenezii is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
