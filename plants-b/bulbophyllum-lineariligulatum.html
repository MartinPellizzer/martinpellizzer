Bulbophyllum lineariligulatum

Bulbophyllum lineariligulatum, commonly known as the slender-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive, narrow lip that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Bulbophyllum lineariligulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
