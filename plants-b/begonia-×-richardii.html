Begonia × richardii

Begonia × richardii, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pavonina.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, orange, and yellow. The plant typically has heart-shaped or oval leaves with a waxy texture and a deep green color. It thrives in well-draining soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for container gardening and indoor spaces.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia × richardii is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
