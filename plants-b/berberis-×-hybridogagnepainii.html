Berberis × hybridogagnepainii

Berberis × hybridogagnepainii, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. The plant is a cross between Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii, resulting in a variety with enhanced ornamental traits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
