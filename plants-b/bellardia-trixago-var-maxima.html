Bellardia trixago var. maxima

Bellardia trixago var. maxima, commonly known as the giant bellardia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
