Begonia holttumii

Begonia holttumii, commonly known as the Holttum's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
