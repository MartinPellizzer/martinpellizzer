Begonia stictopoda

Begonia stictopoda, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive pattern of dark green and silver markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, fertile soil.

Begonia stictopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
