Begonia tetragona

Begonia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often adorned with silver or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for container gardening and shaded garden beds.
