Begonia barsalouxiae

Begonia barsalouxiae, commonly known as the Barceloneta begonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a striking red and green coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
