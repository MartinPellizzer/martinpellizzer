Bromopsis tomentella subsp. shelkovnikovii

Bromopsis tomentella subsp.

shelkovnikovii, commonly known as the woolly bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that bears small, pale flowers.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
