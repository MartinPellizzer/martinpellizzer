Bulbophyllum deuterolimbatum

Bulbophyllum deuterolimbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces numerous showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

This epiphytic orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.
