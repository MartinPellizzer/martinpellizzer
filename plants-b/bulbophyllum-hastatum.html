Bulbophyllum hastatum

Bulbophyllum hastatum, commonly known as the lance-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
