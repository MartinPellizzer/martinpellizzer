Brachypodium fontanesianum

Brachypodium fontanesianum, commonly known as the African grass or Fontanesian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
