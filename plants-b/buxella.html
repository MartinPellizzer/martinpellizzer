Buxella

Buxella, commonly known as the boxwood plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Buxaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it popular in horticulture for topiary and ornamental purposes. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in a compact, rounded shape. Buxella species are native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

They thrive in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring minimal pruning to maintain their shape.
