Brachypodium phoenicoides subvar. gracile

Brachypodium phoenicoides subvar. gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its botanical features include slender stems, fine hairs on the leaves, and small, pale green flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
