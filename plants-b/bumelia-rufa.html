Bumelia rufa

Bumelia rufa, commonly known as red buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, reddish drupe that ripens in the summer, attracting various wildlife species.

Bumelia rufa has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
