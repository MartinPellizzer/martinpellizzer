Berberis diffusa

Berberis diffusa, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a shrub native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground or climb. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically oval with a leathery texture and a distinctive yellow or orange midrib.

Berberis diffusa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
