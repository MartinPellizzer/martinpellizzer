Brassica rapa subsp. napus

Brassica rapa subsp.

napus, commonly known as oilseed rape, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and thick, lobed leaves. It is an annual or biennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are harvested for oil extraction. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees. Brassica rapa subsp.

napus is also known for its rapid growth cycle and ability to accumulate glucosinolates, which contribute to its characteristic flavor and potential health benefits.
