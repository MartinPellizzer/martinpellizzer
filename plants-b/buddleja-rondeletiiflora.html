Buddleja rondeletiiflora

Buddleja rondeletiiflora, commonly known as the golden buddleia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Buddleja rondeletiiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
