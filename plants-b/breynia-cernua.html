Breynia cernua

Breynia cernua, commonly known as the hanging breynia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fleshy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain seeds. Breynia cernua thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
