Berchemia calophylla

Berchemia calophylla, commonly known as the golden bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Berchemia calophylla is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
