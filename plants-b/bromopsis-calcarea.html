Bromopsis calcarea

Bromopsis calcarea, commonly known as the limestone moss, is a species of moss that thrives in calcareous environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, leafy structure and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its habitat and contributes to nutrient cycling.

Bromopsis calcarea is a low-growing, non-vascular plant that is well adapted to harsh, alkaline conditions.
