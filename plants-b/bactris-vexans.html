Bactris vexans

Bactris vexans, commonly known as the red rata or red palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
