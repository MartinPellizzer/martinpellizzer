Blechum brownei f. coeruleum

Blechum brownei f. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue-leaved blechum, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage, which is due to a dense covering of fine hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
