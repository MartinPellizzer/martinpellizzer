Baptisia × bicolor

Baptisia × bicolor, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive, cylindrical seedpods that split open to release its seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Baptisia × bicolor is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and attractive addition to gardens.
