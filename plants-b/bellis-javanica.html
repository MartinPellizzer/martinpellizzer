Bellis javanica

Bellis javanica, commonly known as the Javan daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flower heads surrounded by narrow, greenish leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, producing small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators.

Bellis javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
