Bashania spanostachya

Bashania spanostachya, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a towering perennial grass native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its massive, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its size and structural strength, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
