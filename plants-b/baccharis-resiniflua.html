Baccharis resiniflua

Baccharis resiniflua, commonly known as the resinous baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that exude a sticky resin when damaged. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often have a yellow or greenish hue.

Baccharis resiniflua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
