Bellis paschalis

Bellis paschalis, commonly known as the paschal bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like white or pink flowers. The plant is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. It blooms in early spring, typically between March and May.

Bellis paschalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
