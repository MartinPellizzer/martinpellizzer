Banksia Drummondii Subsp. Macrorufa

Banksia drummondii subsp. macrorufa is a distinctive plant native to the southwestern region of Western Australia. It is known for its striking red and yellow flower spikes that appear in late summer and early autumn. This subspecies thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts. Despite its beauty, it is also a rare and vulnerable species requiring careful protection.
