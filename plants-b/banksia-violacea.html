Banksia violacea

Banksia violacea, commonly known as the violet banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Banksia violacea is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It plays an important role in attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.
