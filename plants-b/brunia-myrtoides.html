Brunia myrtoides

Brunia myrtoides, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
