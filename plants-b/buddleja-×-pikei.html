Buddleja × pikei

Buddleja × pikei, commonly known as the summer lilac, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Buddleja genus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, spike-like flowers. The plant produces showy, pale lavender to purple blossoms that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Buddleja × pikei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
