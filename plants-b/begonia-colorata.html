Begonia colorata

Begonia colorata, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Begonia colorata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
