Balsamita balsamita

Balsamita balsamita, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has aromatic, fuzzy leaves that emit a distinctive mint-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Balsamita balsamita is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
