Bauhinia bowkeri

Bauhinia bowkeri, commonly known as the velvetwood or red bauhinia, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often velvety leaves that are bipinnate, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia bowkeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
