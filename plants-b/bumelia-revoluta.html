Bumelia revoluta

Bumelia revoluta, commonly known as the smooth bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Bumelia revoluta is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
