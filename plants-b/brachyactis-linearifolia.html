Brachyactis linearifolia

Brachyactis linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leafed brachyactis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
