Bystropogon mollis

Bystropogon mollis, commonly known as the soft-hair balm, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
