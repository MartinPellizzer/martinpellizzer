Bupleurum antiochium

Bupleurum antiochium, commonly known as the Antioch bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iran.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbel-like flower clusters and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are white to pale blue, appearing in late summer, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Bupleurum antiochium is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.
