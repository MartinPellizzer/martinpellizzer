Bauhinia comosa

Bauhinia comosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular ornamental species. Bauhinia comosa produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
