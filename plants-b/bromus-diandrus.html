Bromus diandrus

Bromus diandrus, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides. Bromus diandrus is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution and ecological adaptability.
