Bauhinia morondavensis

Bauhinia morondavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, ornamental leaves that resemble orchid petals. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Bauhinia morondavensis is adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
