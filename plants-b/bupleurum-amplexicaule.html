Bupleurum amplexicaule

Bupleurum amplexicaule, commonly known as narrowleaf silvery root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often clasp the stem, giving it its specific epithet "amplexicaule.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported tonic and antispasmodic properties.

Bupleurum amplexicaule thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
