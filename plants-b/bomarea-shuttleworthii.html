Bomarea shuttleworthii

Bomarea shuttleworthii, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often described as resembling lilies. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Bomarea shuttleworthii is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively rare in the wild.
