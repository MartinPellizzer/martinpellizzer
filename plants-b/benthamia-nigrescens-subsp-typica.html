Benthamia nigrescens subsp. typica

Benthamia nigrescens subsp.

typica, commonly known as the black-flowered benthamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
