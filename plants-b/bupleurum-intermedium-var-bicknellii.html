Bupleurum intermedium var. bicknellii

Bupleurum intermedium var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's bellflower, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, shaded habitats at high elevations. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a species of conservation concern due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
