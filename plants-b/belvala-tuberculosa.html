Belvala tuberculosa

Belvala tuberculosa, commonly known as the warty bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty tubercles on its stem and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Belvala tuberculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
