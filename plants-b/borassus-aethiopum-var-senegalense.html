Borassus aethiopum var. senegalense

Borassus aethiopum var. senegalense, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the savannas of West Africa.

It features a straight trunk with a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, three-angled fruits containing edible seeds. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses in local communities.
