Begonia ferruginea var. dilatata

Begonia ferruginea var. dilatata, commonly known as the rusty begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive rust-colored underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation or shaded garden settings.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of tropical plants.
