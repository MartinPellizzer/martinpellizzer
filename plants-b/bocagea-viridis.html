Bocagea viridis

Bocagea viridis, commonly known as the green bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green color. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Bocagea viridis thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in native plant gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
