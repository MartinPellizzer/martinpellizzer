Bauhinia flexuosa

Bauhinia flexuosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, and its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
