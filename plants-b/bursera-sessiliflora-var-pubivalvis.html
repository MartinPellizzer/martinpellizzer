Bursera sessiliflora var. pubivalvis

Bursera sessiliflora var. pubivalvis, commonly known as the Mexican lac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in the production of natural resins.
