Banksia incana var. incana

Banksia incana var. incana, commonly known as the silver banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green, silver-white, and sometimes blue-green foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in well-drained soils in heathlands and open forests.

The species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is a key component of the local flora.
