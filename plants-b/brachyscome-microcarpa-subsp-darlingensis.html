Brachyscome microcarpa subsp. darlingensis

Brachyscome microcarpa subsp.

darlingensis, commonly known as the Darlingensis Daisy, is a small flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
