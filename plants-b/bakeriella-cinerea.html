Bakeriella cinerea

Bakeriella cinerea, commonly known as the pale baker's weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, rocky areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Bakeriella cinerea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
