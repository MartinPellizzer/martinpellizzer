Bambusa pseudopallida

Bambusa pseudopallida, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking silvery-gray culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in tropical and subtropical landscapes. Its hollow, jointed stems are used in traditional crafts and construction, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping.

Bambusa pseudopallida thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.
