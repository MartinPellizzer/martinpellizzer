Brassica quercifolia

Brassica quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf mustard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It belongs to the mustard family (Brassicaceae), which is known for its cruciform flowers and often pungent scent.

Brassica quercifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
