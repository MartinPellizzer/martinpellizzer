Brassica villosa var. tineoi

Brassica villosa var. tineoi, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

The seeds of this species are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
