Bothriochloa caucasica

Bothriochloa caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often slightly drooping.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
