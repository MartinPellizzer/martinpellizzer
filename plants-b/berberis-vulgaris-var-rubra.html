Berberis vulgaris var. rubra

Berberis vulgaris var. rubra, commonly known as red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
