Berchemia pauciflora

Berchemia pauciflora, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Berchemia pauciflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
