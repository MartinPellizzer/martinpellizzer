Bobea glabra

Bobea glabra, commonly known as the silky bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique morphology and adaptability to various ecological conditions.
