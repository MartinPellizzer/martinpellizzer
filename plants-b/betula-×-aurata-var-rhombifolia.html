Betula × aurata var. rhombifolia

Betula × aurata var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the weeping birch, is a hybrid species of birch tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental appeal. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its unique appearance in gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscape design due to its aesthetic value and adaptability.
