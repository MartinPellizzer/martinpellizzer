Bomarea purpurea var. amoena

Bomarea purpurea var. amoena, commonly known as the purple bomarea, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a fibrous root system adapted to its mountainous habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
