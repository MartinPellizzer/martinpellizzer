Buddleja utilis

Buddleja utilis, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing showy panicles of purple or pink blooms.

Buddleja utilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
