Berberis aquifolium var. repens

Berberis aquifolium var. repens, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features glossy, dark green, holly-like leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, carpet-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in shady, moist environments.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in acidic soils and its tolerance to partial shade.
