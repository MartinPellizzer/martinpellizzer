Barringtonia magnifica

Barringtonia magnifica, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, colorful rind and a segmented, juicy interior. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, and it has a distinctive, symmetrical branching pattern.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
