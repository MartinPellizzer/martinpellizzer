Berberis concinna var. brevior

Berberis concinna var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.

The variety brevior is distinguished by its shorter stems compared to the typical form of Berberis concinna.
