Brassica repanda var. tripontina

Brassica repanda var. tripontina, commonly known as the Tripontine wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from yellow to orange, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
