Barbarea vulgaris subsp. vulgaris

Barbarea vulgaris subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common winter cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates.

The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
