Beccariella crebrifolia

Beccariella crebrifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved beccariella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. Beccariella crebrifolia is often found in shaded, humid environments and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in tropical horticulture and ecological studies.
