Baeckea umbellifera

Baeckea umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella bush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which give it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Baeckea umbellifera is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
