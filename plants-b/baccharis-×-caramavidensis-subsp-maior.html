Baccharis × caramavidensis subsp. maior

Baccharis × caramavidensis subsp.

maior, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
