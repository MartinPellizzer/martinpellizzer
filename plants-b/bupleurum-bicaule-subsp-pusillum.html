Bupleurum bicaule subsp. pusillum

Bupleurum bicaule subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
