Bromelia araujoi

Bromelia araujoi, commonly known as the golden bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful foliage. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom infrequently and are followed by distinctive, showy bracts.

Bromelia araujoi is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical climates.
