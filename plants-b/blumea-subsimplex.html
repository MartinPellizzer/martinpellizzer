Blumea subsimplex

Blumea subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea subsimplex is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
