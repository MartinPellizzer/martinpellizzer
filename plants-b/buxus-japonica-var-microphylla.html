Buxus japonica var. microphylla

Buxus japonica var. microphylla, commonly known as Japanese boxwood dwarf form, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, compact growth with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green. The plant is renowned for its fine texture and is often used in formal garden designs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a topiaried specimen.

Due to its slow growth rate, it requires minimal pruning and is highly resistant to pests and diseases.
