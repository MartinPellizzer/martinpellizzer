Bowdichia virgilioides var. ferruginea

Bowdichia virgilioides var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bowdichia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a reddish or rusty-colored underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and savannas.
