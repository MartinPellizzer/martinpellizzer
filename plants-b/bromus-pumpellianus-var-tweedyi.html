Bromus pumpellianus var. tweedyi

Bromus pumpellianus var. tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. This variety is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and spikelet arrangement typical of the Bromus genus. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and small, pale green to brownish spikelets.
