Ballosporum segetum

Ballosporum segetum, commonly known as the wheat rust fungus, is a significant plant pathogen that affects cereal crops, particularly wheat.

It belongs to the order Pucciniales and is characterized by its complex life cycle involving multiple spore stages. The fungus produces reddish-orange pustules on the leaves, which contain urediospores that are responsible for its spread. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and can cause substantial yield losses if left uncontrolled.

Management often involves resistant crop varieties and integrated pest management strategies.
