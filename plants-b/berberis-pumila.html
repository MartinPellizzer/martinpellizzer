Berberis pumila

Berberis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
