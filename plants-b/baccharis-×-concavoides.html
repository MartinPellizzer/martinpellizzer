Baccharis × concavoides

Baccharis × concavoides, commonly known as the hybrid baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Baccharis halimifolia and Baccharis candida, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
