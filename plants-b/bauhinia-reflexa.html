Bauhinia reflexa

Bauhinia reflexa, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Bauhinia reflexa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
