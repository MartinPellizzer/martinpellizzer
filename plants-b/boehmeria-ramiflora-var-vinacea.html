Boehmeria ramiflora var. vinacea

Boehmeria ramiflora var. vinacea, commonly known as the red-flowered nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that surround the small, greenish flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is also noted for its fibrous stems, which can be processed into textiles.
