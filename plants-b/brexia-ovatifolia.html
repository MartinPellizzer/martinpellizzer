Brexia ovatifolia

Brexia ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed brexia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Brexia ovatifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
