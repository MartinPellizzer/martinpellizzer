Batrachium peltatum subsp. truncatum

Batrachium peltatum subsp.

truncatum, commonly known as the truncated pellucid fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its unique, peltate fronds that are typically truncate at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually light green and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its humid habitat.
