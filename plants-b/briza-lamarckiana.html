Briza lamarckiana

Briza lamarckiana, commonly known as the quaking grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Briza lamarckiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
