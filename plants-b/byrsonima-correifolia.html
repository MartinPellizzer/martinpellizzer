Byrsonima correifolia

Byrsonima correifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or acerola, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnate, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.
