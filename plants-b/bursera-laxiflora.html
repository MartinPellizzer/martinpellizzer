Bursera laxiflora

Bursera laxiflora, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is also valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
