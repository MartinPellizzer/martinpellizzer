Banisteria parviflora var. boliviensis

Banisteria parviflora var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian spiderflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade-loving landscapes.
