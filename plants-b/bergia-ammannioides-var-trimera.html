Bergia ammannioides var. trimera

Bergia ammannioides var. trimera, commonly known as the coastal bergia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky coastal environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions.

Bergia ammannioides var. trimera is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
