Boechera californica

Boechera californica, commonly known as the California rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with basal leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants.
