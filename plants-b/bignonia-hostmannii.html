Bignonia hostmannii

Bignonia hostmannii, commonly known as the desert crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Bignonia hostmannii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
