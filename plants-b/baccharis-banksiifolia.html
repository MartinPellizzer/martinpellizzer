Baccharis banksiifolia

Baccharis banksiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
