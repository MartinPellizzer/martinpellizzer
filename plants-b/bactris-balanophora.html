Bactris balanophora

Bactris balanophora, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-trunked stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that are clustered in panicles, which are often consumed by various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Bactris balanophora make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native range.
