Betonica macrantha

Betonica macrantha, commonly known as the greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and it produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often hairy on both surfaces. Betonica macrantha is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
