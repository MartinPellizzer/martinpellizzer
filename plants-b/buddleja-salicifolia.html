Buddleja salicifolia

Buddleja salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and have a soft, silvery underside. The plant produces panicles of fragrant, pale purple to lilac flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Buddleja salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
