Bulbostylis megastachys

Bulbostylis megastachys, commonly known as the giant bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common component of aquatic vegetation.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels, which contributes to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
