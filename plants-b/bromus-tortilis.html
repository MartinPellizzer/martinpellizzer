Bromus tortilis

Bromus tortilis, commonly known as the curlleaf brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive curled leaves that form a dense, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Bromus tortilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
