Bidens kasaiensis

Bidens kasaiensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as marshes and riverbanks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Bidens kasaiensis is valued for its traditional use in treating ailments like fever and inflammation.
