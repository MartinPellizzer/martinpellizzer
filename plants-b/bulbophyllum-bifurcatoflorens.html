Bulbophyllum bifurcatoflorens

Bulbophyllum bifurcatoflorens, commonly known as the bifurcated flowering orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a pseudobulb and fleshy roots that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
