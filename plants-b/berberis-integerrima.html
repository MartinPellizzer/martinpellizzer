Berberis integerrima

Berberis integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
