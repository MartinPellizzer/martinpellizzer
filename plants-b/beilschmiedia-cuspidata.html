Beilschmiedia cuspidata

Beilschmiedia cuspidata, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
