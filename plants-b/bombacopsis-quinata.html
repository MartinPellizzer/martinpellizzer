Bombacopsis quinata

Bombacopsis quinata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It produces woody pods containing seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
