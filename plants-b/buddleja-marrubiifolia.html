Buddleja marrubiifolia

Buddleja marrubiifolia, commonly known as the woolly buddleja, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Buddleja marrubiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens in suitable climates.
