Bulbophyllum olorinum

Bulbophyllum olorinum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
