Bromus diandrus subsp. maximus

Bromus diandrus subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread and form dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

The leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a hairy surface, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
