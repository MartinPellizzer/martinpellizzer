Bergia ammannioides var. pentandra

Bergia ammannioides var. pentandra, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often prostrate or upright depending on the growing conditions.

Bergia ammannioides var. pentandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
