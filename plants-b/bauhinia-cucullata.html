Bauhinia cucullata

Bauhinia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers typically range in color from white to pink.

Bauhinia cucullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
