Baccharis toxicarius

Baccharis toxicarius, commonly known as poison baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant contains toxic compounds, particularly in its leaves and stems, which can cause skin irritation or more severe reactions upon contact. It often grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
