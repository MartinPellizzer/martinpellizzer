Bignonia ferruginea

Bignonia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bignonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Bignonia ferruginea is typically grown as a vine or shrub and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
