Berberis chekiangensis

Berberis chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Berberis chekiangensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
