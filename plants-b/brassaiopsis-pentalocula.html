Brassaiopsis pentalocula

Brassaiopsis pentalocula, commonly known as the five-lobed bracken, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Brassaiopsis pentalocula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
