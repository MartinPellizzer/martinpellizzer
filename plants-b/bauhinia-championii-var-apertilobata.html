Bauhinia championii var. apertilobata

Bauhinia championii var. apertilobata, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are accompanied by small, leathery pods that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to well-drained soils in warm climates.
