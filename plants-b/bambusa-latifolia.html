Bambusa latifolia

Bambusa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a fast-growing evergreen bamboo species native to southern China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a diameter of about 3 to 5 centimeters. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clusters of woody, cylindrical fruits.

Bambusa latifolia is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
