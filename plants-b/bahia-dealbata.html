Bahia dealbata

Bahia dealbata, commonly known as the white bahia or white clover, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a creeping growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
