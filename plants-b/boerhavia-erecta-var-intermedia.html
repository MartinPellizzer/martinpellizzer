Boerhavia erecta var. intermedia

Boerhavia erecta var. intermedia, commonly known as redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
