Blumea cunninghamii

Blumea cunninghamii, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The showy, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
