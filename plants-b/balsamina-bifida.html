Balsamina bifida

Balsamina bifida, commonly known as the split balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their delicate, fragrant appearance.

Balsamina bifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
