Bromus trinii var. manicatus

Bromus trinii var. manicatus, commonly known as the Manicatus brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
