Berberis diaphana var. tachiensis

Berberis diaphana var. tachiensis, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in ornamental gardening.

Its presence in the wild can sometimes lead to ecological concerns due to its invasive tendencies in certain regions.
