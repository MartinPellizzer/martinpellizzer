Bulbophyllum xantanthum

Bulbophyllum xantanthum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
