Basella lucida

Basella lucida, commonly known as the "Indian spinach" or "Malabar spinach," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds.

Basella lucida is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage.
