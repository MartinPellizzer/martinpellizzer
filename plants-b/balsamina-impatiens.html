Balsamina impatiens

Balsamina impatiens, commonly known as the common garden impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Balsamina impatiens is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
