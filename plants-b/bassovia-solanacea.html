Bassovia solanacea

Bassovia solanacea, commonly known as the nightshade bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue, typically at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

The fruit is a dry, capsule-like structure that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
