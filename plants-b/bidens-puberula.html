Bidens puberula

Bidens puberula, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens puberula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
