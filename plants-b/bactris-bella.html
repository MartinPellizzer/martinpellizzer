Bactris bella

Bactris bella, commonly known as the pink palm, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a clumping palm, reaching heights of up to 6 meters with a slender trunk. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are often adorned with a reddish tinge.

Bactris bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance.
