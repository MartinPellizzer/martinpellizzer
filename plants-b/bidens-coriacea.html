Bidens coriacea

Bidens coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Bidens coriacea is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a plant of ecological interest.
