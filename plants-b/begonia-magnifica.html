Begonia magnifica

Begonia magnifica, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
