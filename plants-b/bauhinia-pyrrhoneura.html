Bauhinia pyrrhoneura

Bauhinia pyrrhoneura, commonly known as the scarlet bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are flat, pod-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Bauhinia pyrrhoneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
