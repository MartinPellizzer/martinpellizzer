Bromus kashmirensis

Bromus kashmirensis, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Kashmir Valley.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with small, awned florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
