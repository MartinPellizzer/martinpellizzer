Bauhinia kurzii

Bauhinia kurzii, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers range in color from white to pink and red. Bauhinia kurzii produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
