Bactris coloniata

Bactris coloniata, commonly known as the red-brown palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers, which are followed by edible, round fruits. Bactris coloniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
