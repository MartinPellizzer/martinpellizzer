Balanophora truncata

Balanophora truncata, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its roots. The plant has a short, underground stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
