Betula platyphylla var. kamtschatica

Betula platyphylla var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka birch, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, and its leaves are oval with serrated edges. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions and boreal forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
