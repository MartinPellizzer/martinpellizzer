Bunchosia hedraiophylla

Bunchosia hedraiophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or rock fig, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
