Bernardia pulchella f. breviserrata

Bernardia pulchella f. breviserrata, commonly known as the narrow-serrate bernardia, is a variant of the bernardia pulchella species native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically narrow with finely serrated edges, giving it its specific epithet. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences, and bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils in mild climates.
