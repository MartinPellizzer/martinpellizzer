Bauhinia megasiphon

Bauhinia megasiphon, commonly known as the giant siphon bauhinia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
