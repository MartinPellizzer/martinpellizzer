Bactris platyspina

Bactris platyspina, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a species of palm native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically consumed raw or processed into beverages. Its fibrous leaves are also used for making ropes and mats, highlighting its economic and cultural significance.

Bactris platyspina thrives in humid, lowland environments and is often found in forested areas near rivers.
