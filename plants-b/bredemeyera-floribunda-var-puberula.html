Bredemeyera floribunda var. puberula

Bredemeyera floribunda var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
