Baccharis viscosissima

Baccharis viscosissima, commonly known as brittle bluemink, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, blue-gray leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, grayish-white seeds. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Baccharis viscosissima is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife.
