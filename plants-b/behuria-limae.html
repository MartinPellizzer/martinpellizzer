Behuria limae

Behuria limae, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Behuria limae make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a cut flower.
