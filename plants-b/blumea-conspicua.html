Blumea conspicua

Blumea conspicua, commonly known as the golden flame or Chinese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture. Blumea conspicua typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
