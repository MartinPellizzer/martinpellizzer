Baccharis myrsinites

Baccharis myrsinites, commonly known as waxmyrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Baccharis myrsinites is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
