Boehmeria nivea var. nipononivea

Boehmeria nivea var. nipononivea, commonly known as Japanese nettle or Japanese flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky fibers in the stem, which are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface and can vary in size.

It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences.
