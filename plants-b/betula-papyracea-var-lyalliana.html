Betula papyracea var. lyalliana

Betula papyracea var. lyalliana, commonly known as Lyall's birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and green beneath. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
