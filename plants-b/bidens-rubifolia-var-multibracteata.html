Bidens rubifolia var. multibracteata

Bidens rubifolia var. multibracteata, commonly known as the red-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts that are often reddish or purplish. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and can be covered in fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.
