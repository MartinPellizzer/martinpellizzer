Boehmeria virgata var. rotundifolia

Boehmeria virgata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant features heart-shaped or round leaves with a rough texture and a prominent central vein. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional crafts and textiles due to their strong, durable fibers.
