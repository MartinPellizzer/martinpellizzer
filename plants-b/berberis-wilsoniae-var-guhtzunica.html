Berberis wilsoniae var. guhtzunica

Berberis wilsoniae var. guhtzunica, commonly known as Wilson's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, adding aesthetic value to landscapes.
