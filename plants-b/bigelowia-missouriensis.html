Bigelowia missouriensis

Bigelowia missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in prairie habitats and is known for its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its growth habit is usually erect and bushy.

Missouri bigelowia is often found in open, disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic species of tallgrass prairies.
