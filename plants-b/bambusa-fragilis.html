Bambusa fragilis

Bambusa fragilis, commonly known as the delicate bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Bambusa fragilis is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
