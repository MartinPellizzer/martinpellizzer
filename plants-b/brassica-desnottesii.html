Brassica desnottesii

Brassica desnottesii, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compact, hairy foliage adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, round pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Brassica desnottesii is often found in disturbed soils and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or rocky habitats.
