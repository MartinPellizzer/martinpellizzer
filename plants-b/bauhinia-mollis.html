Bauhinia mollis

Bauhinia mollis, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Bauhinia mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
