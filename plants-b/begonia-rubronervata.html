Begonia rubronervata

Begonia rubronervata, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins that create a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia rubronervata is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
