Bouchea prismatica var. brevirostra

Bouchea prismatica var. brevirostra, commonly known as the narrow-beaked bouchea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
