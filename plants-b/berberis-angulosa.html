Berberis angulosa

Berberis angulosa, commonly known as the angular barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
