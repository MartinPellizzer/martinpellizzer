Brassica rupestris

Brassica rupestris, commonly known as rock mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand harsh conditions.

Brassica rupestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
