Bridelia grandis subsp. puberula

Bridelia grandis subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the hairy bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
