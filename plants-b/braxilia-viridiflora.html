Braxilia viridiflora

Braxilia viridiflora, commonly known as the emerald-flowered brax, is a rare flowering plant native to the dense rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow petals and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that contain seeds with a unique spiral pattern. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Braxilia viridiflora is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
