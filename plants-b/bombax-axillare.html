Bombax axillare

Bombax axillare, commonly known as the silky ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a fragrant scent. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is often covered in a thick, silky layer of fibers. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged radiating from a central point.

The silky ceiba plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
