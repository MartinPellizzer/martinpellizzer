Bulbophyllum sanguineum

Bulbophyllum sanguineum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Bulbophyllum sanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
