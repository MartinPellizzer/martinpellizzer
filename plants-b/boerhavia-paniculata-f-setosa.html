Boerhavia paniculata f. setosa

Boerhavia paniculata f. setosa, commonly known as the hairy bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Boerhavia paniculata f. setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
