Buergersiochloa bambusoides

Buergersiochloa bambusoides, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in dense tufts. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming compact clusters that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its culms are often hollow and jointed, resembling bamboo, which is a notable botanical feature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
