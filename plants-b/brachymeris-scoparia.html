Brachymeris scoparia

Brachymeris scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-winged grasshopper, is a species of insect rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects.

However, it is often found in grassy habitats where it feeds on various plant materials. Its life cycle involves egg, nymph, and adult stages, with the nymphs resembling the adults but lacking wings. The plant species in its environment provide both food and shelter for the insect.

Due to its role in the ecosystem, it contributes to nutrient cycling and serves as prey for other animals.
