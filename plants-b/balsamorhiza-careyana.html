Balsamorhiza careyana

Balsamorhiza careyana, commonly known as Carey's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Balsamorhiza careyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
