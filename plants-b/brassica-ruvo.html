Brassica ruvo

Brassica ruvo, commonly known as the wild radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or annual herb that typically grows in temperate regions. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruits are long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica ruvo is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
