Biscutella stenophylla var. laxa

Biscutella stenophylla var. laxa, commonly known as the lax leaf biscutella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
