Bignonia hebantha

Bignonia hebantha, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia hebantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
