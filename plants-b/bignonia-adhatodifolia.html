Bignonia adhatodifolia

Bignonia adhatodifolia, commonly known as the Indian bignonia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features elongated, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
