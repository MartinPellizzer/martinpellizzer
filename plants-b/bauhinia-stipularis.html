Bauhinia stipularis

Bauhinia stipularis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia stipularis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
