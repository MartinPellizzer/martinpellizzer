Bambusa tagoara

Bambusa tagoara, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types contribute to its widespread cultivation.

Bambusa tagoara is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
