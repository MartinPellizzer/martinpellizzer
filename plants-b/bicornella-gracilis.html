Bicornella gracilis

Bicornella gracilis, commonly known as the slender bicornell, is a unique species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and ecological role within its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
