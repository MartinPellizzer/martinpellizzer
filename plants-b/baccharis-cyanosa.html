Baccharis cyanosa

Baccharis cyanosa, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a silvery underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Baccharis cyanosa is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
