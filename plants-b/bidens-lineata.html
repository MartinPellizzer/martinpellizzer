Bidens lineata

Bidens lineata, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with black markings and hairy stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Bidens lineata is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
