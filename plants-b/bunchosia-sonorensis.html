Bunchosia sonorensis

Bunchosia sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran bunchosia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as desert regions and rocky soils. The plant features small, green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bunchosia sonorensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
