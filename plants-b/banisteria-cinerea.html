Banisteria cinerea

Banisteria cinerea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Banisteria cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
