Brassica rapa var. dissecta

Brassica rapa var. dissecta, commonly known as the curly kale or Russian kale, is a cultivated variety of the mustard family.

It is characterized by its dense, curly leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its edible leaves. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
