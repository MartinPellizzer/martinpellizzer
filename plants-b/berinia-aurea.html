Berinia aurea

Berinia aurea, commonly known as golden berinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical name reflects its golden hue, with "aurea" meaning golden in Latin.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure.
