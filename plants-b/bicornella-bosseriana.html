Bicornella bosseriana

Bicornella bosseriana, commonly known as the African horned vulture plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, fleshy leaves that resemble horns, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Bicornella bosseriana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and unique appearance.
