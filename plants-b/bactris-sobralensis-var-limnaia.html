Bactris sobralensis var. limnaia

Bactris sobralensis var. limnaia, commonly known as the marsh palm, is a species native to the wetlands and swampy areas of northeastern Brazil.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a short trunk and a dense crown of narrow, arching leaves. The plant thrives in humid, waterlogged environments and is adapted to tolerate seasonal flooding. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The fruit, which is small and red, is edible and contains a single seed.
