Bursera palaciosii

Bursera palaciosii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the dry forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage, small elliptical leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Bursera palaciosii is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in limestone soils.
