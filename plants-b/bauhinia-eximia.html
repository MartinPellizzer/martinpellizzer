Bauhinia eximia

Bauhinia eximia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. Bauhinia eximia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and public spaces.

It is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in warm climates.
