Benzoin gemmiflorum

Benzoin gemmiflorum, commonly known as the sacred balsam tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its resinous exudates, which are harvested for their balsamic scent and medicinal properties. Its growth habit includes a compact form with multiple stems, often forming a bushy appearance.

The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a waxy texture.
