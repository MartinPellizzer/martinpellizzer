Berberis chaquirensis

Berberis chaquirensis, commonly known as the Chaquirensis barberry, is a species of shrub native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
