Bupleurum falcatum subsp. persicum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

persicum, commonly known as Persian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
