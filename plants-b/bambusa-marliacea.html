Bambusa marliacea

Bambusa marliacea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden green culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. The plant forms dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa marliacea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
