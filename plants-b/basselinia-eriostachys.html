Basselinia eriostachys

Basselinia eriostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass tree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces panicles of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and climates.
