Bomarea licaulis

Bomarea licaulis, commonly known as the purple-veined bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its tuberous roots are an important feature, storing nutrients and enabling it to survive in its natural habitat.

Bomarea licaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
