Balaka insularis

Balaka insularis, commonly known as the island balaka, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

Due to habitat loss, Balaka insularis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
