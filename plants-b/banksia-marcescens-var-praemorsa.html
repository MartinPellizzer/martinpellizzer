Banksia marcescens var. praemorsa

Banksia marcescens var. praemorsa, commonly known as the Blackbutt Banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
