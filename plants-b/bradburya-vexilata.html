Bradburya vexilata

Bradburya vexilata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, bell-shaped structure. Bradburya vexilata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
