Brassavola rhopalorrhachis

Brassavola rhopalorrhachis, commonly known as the long-stalked lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender inflorescence that bears numerous small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose spiral. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base.

Brassavola rhopalorrhachis is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
