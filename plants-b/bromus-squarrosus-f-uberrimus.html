Bromus squarrosus f. uberrimus

Bromus squarrosus f. uberrimus, commonly known as the tufted brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray or white-tomentose (hairy) foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown glumes.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
