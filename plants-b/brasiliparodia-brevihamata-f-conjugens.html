Brasiliparodia brevihamata f. conjugens

Brasiliparodia brevihamata f. conjugens, commonly known as the Brazilian parodia, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure, typical of many cacti. The plant features are small, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It is native to the semi-arid regions of Brazil, where it has adapted to thrive in hot and dry conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
