Bartonia decapetala

Bartonia decapetala, commonly known as the clustered bartronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Bartonia decapetala is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
