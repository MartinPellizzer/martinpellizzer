Begonia mamedeana

Begonia mamedeana, commonly known as the Mamede's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found growing in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
