Behenantha saponariifolia

Behenantha saponariifolia, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that contain a milky latex sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a distinctive fruit structure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
