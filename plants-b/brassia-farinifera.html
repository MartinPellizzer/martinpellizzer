Brassia farinifera

Brassia farinifera, commonly known as the powdered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety, and powdery appearance on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Brassia farinifera is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
