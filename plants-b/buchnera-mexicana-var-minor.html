Buchnera mexicana var. minor

Buchnera mexicana var. minor, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Buchnera mexicana var. minor is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
