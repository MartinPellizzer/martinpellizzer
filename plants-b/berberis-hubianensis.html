Berberis hubianensis

Berberis hubianensis, commonly known as the Hubia barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.
