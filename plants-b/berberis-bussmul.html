Berberis bussmul

Berberis bussmul, commonly known as the Bussmul barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a hedging plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice in horticulture.
