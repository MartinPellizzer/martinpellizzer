Berberis angulosa var. fasciculata

Berberis angulosa var. fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, angular stems and small, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
