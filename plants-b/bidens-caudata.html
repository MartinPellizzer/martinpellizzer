Bidens caudata

Bidens caudata, commonly known as horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Bidens caudata is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
