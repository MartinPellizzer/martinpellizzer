Barbaceniopsis vargasiana

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery hair that helps protect it from excessive sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
