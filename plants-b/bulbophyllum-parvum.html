Bulbophyllum parvum

Bulbophyllum parvum, commonly known as the small bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, and it has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Bulbophyllum parvum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
