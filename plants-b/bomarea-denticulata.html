Bomarea denticulata

Bomarea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and possesses lance-shaped, often hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
