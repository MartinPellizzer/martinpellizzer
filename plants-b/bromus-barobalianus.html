Bromus barobalianus

Bromus barobalianus, commonly known as the barobalian brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant has slender, fibrous stems and produces spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Bromus barobalianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant plant species.
