Bambusa glomerata

Bambusa glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, slender culms that are typically green and smooth, with a hollow structure typical of bamboo. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa glomerata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
