Begonia racemosa

Begonia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

This species is valued for its attractive foliage and continuous blooming throughout the growing season.
