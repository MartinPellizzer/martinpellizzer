Begonia duruisseaui

Begonia duruisseaui, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
