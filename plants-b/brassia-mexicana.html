Brassia mexicana

Brassia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Brassia mexicana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
