Banisteria peruviana

Banisteria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian trumpet flower, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling a trumpet. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Banisteria peruviana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
