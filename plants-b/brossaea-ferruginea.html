Brossaea ferruginea

Brossaea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty brossaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name.

Brossaea ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
