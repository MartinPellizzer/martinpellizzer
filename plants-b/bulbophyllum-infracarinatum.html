Bulbophyllum infracarinatum

Bulbophyllum infracarinatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a faintly colored lip.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
