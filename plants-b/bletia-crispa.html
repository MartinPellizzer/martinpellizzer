Bletia crispa

Bletia crispa, commonly known as the crisped bletia, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow lips, which bloom during the rainy season. Bletia crispa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to tropical gardens.
