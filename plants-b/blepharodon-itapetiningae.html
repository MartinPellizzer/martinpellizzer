Blepharodon itapetiningae

Blepharodon itapetiningae, commonly known as the Itapetininga orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem and a single, showy violet-blue flower. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate petal structure and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered and is protected by conservation efforts.
