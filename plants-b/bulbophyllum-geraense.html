Bulbophyllum geraense

Bulbophyllum geraense, commonly known as the Geraense orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

The botanical study of Bulbophyllum geraense provides insights into the adaptations of orchids in diverse tropical ecosystems.
