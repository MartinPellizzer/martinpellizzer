Brassica oleracea subvar. sabellica

Brassica oleracea subvar. sabellica, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a hardy perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by round, green pods containing seeds. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting local biodiversity.
