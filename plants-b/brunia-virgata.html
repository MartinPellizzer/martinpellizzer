Brunia virgata

Brunia virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf brunia, is a South African plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Brunia virgata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
