Batocydia capreolata

Batocydia capreolata, commonly known as the mountain beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with a dark green to black upper surface and a lighter, often whitish lower surface. The thallus is loosely branched and can form dense mats on rocks or tree trunks. It contains various secondary metabolites that contribute to its chemical complexity.

This lichen is often used in ecological studies to assess air quality and habitat health.
