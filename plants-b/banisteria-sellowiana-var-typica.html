Banisteria sellowiana var. typica

Banisteria sellowiana var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that appear in dense clusters, often resembling a shower of blossoms. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, reddish berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
