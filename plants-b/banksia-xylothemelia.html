Banksia xylothemelia

Banksia xylothemelia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces woody follicles that contain the seeds.

Banksia xylothemelia is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
