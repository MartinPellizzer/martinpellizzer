Begonia organensis

Begonia organensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, often velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique leaf patterns and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
