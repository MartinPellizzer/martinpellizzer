Baccharis tola subsp. santelicis

Baccharis tola subsp.

santelicis, commonly known as the Santa Catalina baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
