Brachiaria urocoides

Brachiaria urocoides, commonly known as blackgrass or Brazilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Brachiaria urocoides is widely used in forage and soil conservation due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
