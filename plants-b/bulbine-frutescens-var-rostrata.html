Bulbine frutescens var. rostrata

Bulbine frutescens var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing form with fleshy, ribbed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically from autumn to spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
