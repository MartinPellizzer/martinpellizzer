Begonia acutifolia

Begonia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia acutifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
