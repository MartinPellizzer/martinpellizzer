Bryophyllum macrochlamys

Bryophyllum macrochlamys, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Bryophyllum macrochlamys is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
