Brachiaria caboverdiana

Brachiaria caboverdiana, commonly known as Cape Verde grass, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, slender blades. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Brachiaria caboverdiana is often used for erosion control due to its dense root system and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
