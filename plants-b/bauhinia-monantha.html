Bauhinia monantha

Bauhinia monantha, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that turn red or purple in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. Bauhinia monantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
