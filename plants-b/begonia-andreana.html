Begonia andreana

Begonia andreana, commonly known as the Andes begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins, often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers cool, humid conditions and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts and horticulturists.
