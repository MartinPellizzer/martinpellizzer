Berberis tarokoensis

Berberis tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko barberry, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
