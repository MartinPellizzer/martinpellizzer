Bauhinia brachycarpa

Bauhinia brachycarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing small, round seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in nitrogen fixation.
