Bidens bigelovii var. typica

Bidens bigelovii var. typica, commonly known as the yellow beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling daisies. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Bidens bigelovii var. typica is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
