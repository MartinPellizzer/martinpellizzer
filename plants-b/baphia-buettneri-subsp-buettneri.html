Baphia buettneri subsp. buettneri

Baphia buettneri subsp.

buettneri, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemate inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
