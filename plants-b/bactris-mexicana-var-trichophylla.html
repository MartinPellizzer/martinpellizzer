Bactris mexicana var. trichophylla

Bactris mexicana var. trichophylla, commonly known as the Mexican palm or trichophylla palm, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates, with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential agricultural uses.
