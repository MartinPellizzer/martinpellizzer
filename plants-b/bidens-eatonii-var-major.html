Bidens eatonii var. major

Bidens eatonii var. major, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which are clustered in composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
