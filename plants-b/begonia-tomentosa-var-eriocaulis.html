Begonia tomentosa var. eriocaulis

Begonia tomentosa var. eriocaulis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving the plant a lush, textured appearance. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental leaves and unique growth habit.
