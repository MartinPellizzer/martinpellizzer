Buxus olivacea

Buxus olivacea, commonly known as the olive boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a glossy appearance, and its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark purple berries. Buxus olivacea is valued for its compact growth habit and is often used in formal gardens and topiary.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
