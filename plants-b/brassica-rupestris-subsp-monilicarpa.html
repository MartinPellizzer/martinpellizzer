Brassica rupestris subsp. monilicarpa

Brassica rupestris subsp.

monilicarpa, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes and cliffs.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a short, thick silique that contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
