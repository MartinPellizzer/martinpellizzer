Buchnera splendens

Buchnera splendens, commonly known as the splendour bugwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Buchnera splendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
