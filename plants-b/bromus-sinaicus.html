Bromus sinaicus

Bromus sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai brome, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Bromus sinaicus plays a minor role in local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
