Bursera suntui

Bursera suntui, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Maya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The tree has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, white flowers followed by orange-yellow berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has cultural significance in indigenous communities.
