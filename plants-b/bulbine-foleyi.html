Bulbine foleyi

Bulbine foleyi, commonly known as the Cape bulbous, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
