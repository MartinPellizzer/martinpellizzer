Beta trojana

Beta trojana, commonly known as the Trojan beetroot, is a lesser-known variant of the Beta vulgaris species.

It is characterized by its deep purple roots and leafy greens, which are rich in nutrients such as betalains and antioxidants. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is typically cultivated for both its edible roots and leaves, which can be used in various culinary applications.

Due to its rarity and specific growing conditions, Beta trojana is not as widely cultivated as other beetroot varieties.
