Bidaria elegans

Bidaria elegans, commonly known as the elegant bidaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or pale in color. Bidaria elegans is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
