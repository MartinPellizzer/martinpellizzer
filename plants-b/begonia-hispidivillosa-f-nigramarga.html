Begonia hispidivillosa f. nigramarga

Begonia hispidivillosa f. nigramarga, commonly known as the black-haired begonia, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its distinctive dark, almost black, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green to almost black coloration. Its flowers are typically small, with five petals and a contrasting color scheme, often in shades of red or orange.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
