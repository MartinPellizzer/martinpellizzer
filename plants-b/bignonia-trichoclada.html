Bignonia trichoclada

Bignonia trichoclada, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy, and it produces long, pendulous fruit capsules that split open to release seeds. Bignonia trichoclada is typically found in woodland edges and moist habitats, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, colorful blooms.
