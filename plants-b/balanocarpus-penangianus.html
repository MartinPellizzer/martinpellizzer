Balanocarpus penangianus

Balanocarpus penangianus, commonly known as the Penang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Malay Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its hardiness and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
