Bursa-pastoris

Bursa-pastoris, commonly known as shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant has distinctive triangular seed pods that split open when mature, releasing small, rounded seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its leaves are lobed and slightly hairy, and it produces small white or purple flowers.
