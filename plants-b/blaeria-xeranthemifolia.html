Blaeria xeranthemifolia

Blaeria xeranthemifolia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
