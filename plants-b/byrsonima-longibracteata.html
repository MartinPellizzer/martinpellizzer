Byrsonima longibracteata

Byrsonima longibracteata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing edible seeds, commonly referred to as Brazil nuts. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic significance due to the high commercial demand for its seeds.
