Bidens oligocarpa var. viereckii

Bidens oligocarpa var. viereckii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
