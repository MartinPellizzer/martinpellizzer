Blumia

Blumia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Blumia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
