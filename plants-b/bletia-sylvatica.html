Bletia sylvatica

Bletia sylvatica, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Bletia sylvatica is a perennial plant that is often found in association with other orchids in its native habitat.
