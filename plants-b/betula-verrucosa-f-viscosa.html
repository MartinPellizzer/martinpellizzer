Betula verrucosa f. viscosa

Betula verrucosa f. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its thick, sticky resin that coats the bark and young shoots. The leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
