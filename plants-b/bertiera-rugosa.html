Bertiera rugosa

Bertiera rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf bertiera, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation.

Bertiera rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
