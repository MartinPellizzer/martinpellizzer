Biscutella austriaca subsp. hungarica

Biscutella austriaca subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
