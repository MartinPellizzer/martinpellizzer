Bunium mauritanicum var. crassifolium

Bunium mauritanicum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are broad and robust. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and aromatic, similar to those of other fennel species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
