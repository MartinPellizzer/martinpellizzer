Brugmansia dolichocarpa

Brugmansia dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed angel's trumpet, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are large, broad, and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Brugmansia dolichocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value but is also known for its toxic properties.
