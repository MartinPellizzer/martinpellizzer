Bauhinia bonatiana

Bauhinia bonatiana, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia bonatiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
