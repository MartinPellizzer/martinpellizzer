Buchnera rupestris

Buchnera rupestris, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange. Buchnera rupestris is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
