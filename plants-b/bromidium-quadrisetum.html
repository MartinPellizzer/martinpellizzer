Bromidium quadrisetum

Bromidium quadrisetum, commonly known as the four-rowed bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are covered in fine trichomes, which help in capturing moisture from the air.

Bromidium quadrisetum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
