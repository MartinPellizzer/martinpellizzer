Baccharis brachystachys

Baccharis brachystachys, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a wildlife plant.
