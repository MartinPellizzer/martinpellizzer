Barbarea lepuznica

Barbarea lepuznica, commonly known as the mountain fleaweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, alpine, or arid environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Barbarea lepuznica is adapted to harsh conditions, with succulent leaves that help store water.
