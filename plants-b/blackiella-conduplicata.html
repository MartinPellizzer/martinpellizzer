Blackiella conduplicata

Blackiella conduplicata, commonly known as the double-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually clustered and have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

Blackiella conduplicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
