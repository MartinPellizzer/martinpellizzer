Buddleja malmei

Buddleja malmei, commonly known as the Malme's bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.

Buddleja malmei is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
