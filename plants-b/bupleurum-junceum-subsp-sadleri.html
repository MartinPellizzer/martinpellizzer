Bupleurum junceum subsp. sadleri

Bupleurum junceum subsp.

sadleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and wildflower displays.
