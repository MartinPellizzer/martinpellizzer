Brachypodium cespitosum var. phoenicoides

Brachypodium cespitosum var. phoenicoides, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish or purplish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
