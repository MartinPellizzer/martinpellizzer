Begonia melanosticta

Begonia melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, stems and leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often marked with contrasting patterns, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among gardeners for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
