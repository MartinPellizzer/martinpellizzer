Brachionidium uxorium

Brachionidium uxorium, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Brachionidium uxorium is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields due to its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and potential as an invasive species in some areas.
