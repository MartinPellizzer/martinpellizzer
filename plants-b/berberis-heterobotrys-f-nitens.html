Berberis heterobotrys f. nitens

Berberis heterobotrys f. nitens, commonly known as the white-flowered barberry, is a variant of the barberry genus native to China.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its berries are small and red when mature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
