Bauhinia tomentosa var. candida

Bauhinia tomentosa var. candida, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
