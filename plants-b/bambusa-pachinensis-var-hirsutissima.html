Bambusa pachinensis var. hirsutissima

Bambusa pachinensis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features a dense covering of fine, silky hairs on its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and as a source of durable bamboo material.
