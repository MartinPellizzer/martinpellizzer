Buglossum officinale

Buglossum officinale, commonly known as common bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and bluish-gray leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Buglossum officinale is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
