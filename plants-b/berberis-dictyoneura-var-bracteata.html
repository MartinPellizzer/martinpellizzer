Berberis dictyoneura var. bracteata

Berberis dictyoneura var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically glossy green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy plant in temperate climates.
