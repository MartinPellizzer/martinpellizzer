Brachiaria stefaninii

Brachiaria stefaninii, commonly known as the Stefanini's blady grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria stefaninii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
