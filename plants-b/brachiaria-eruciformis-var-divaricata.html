Brachiaria eruciformis var. divaricata

Brachiaria eruciformis var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is adapted to warm climates and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and pastures.
