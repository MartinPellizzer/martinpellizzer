Bignonia leucantha

Bignonia leucantha, commonly known as the white cross vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble crosses. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bignonia leucantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
