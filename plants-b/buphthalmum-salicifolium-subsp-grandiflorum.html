Buphthalmum salicifolium subsp. grandiflorum

Buphthalmum salicifolium subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered ox-eye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in spring and summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
