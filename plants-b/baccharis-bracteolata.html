Baccharis bracteolata

Baccharis bracteolata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and shrublands, and is often found in regions with warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and use in traditional medicine.
