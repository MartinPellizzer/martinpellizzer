Brassica oleracea subvar. conica

Brassica oleracea subvar. conica, commonly known as the cabbages, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage species.

It is characterized by its tightly packed, fleshy leaves that form a compact head. The plant is a biennial, typically grown as an annual in temperate climates. It thrives in cool weather and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are usually green, though some varieties may have purple or red hues.
