Begonia bachulkarii

Begonia bachulkarii, commonly known as the Khasi begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
