Begonia lithophila

Begonia lithophila, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its ability to grow on rocks, making it a lithophytic species. The plant features fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically vibrant, with colors ranging from pink to red, and are borne on short, erect stems.

Begonia lithophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to container gardening.
