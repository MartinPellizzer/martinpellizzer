Balsamorhiza lanata

Balsamorhiza lanata, commonly known as the woolly balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Balsamorhiza lanata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
