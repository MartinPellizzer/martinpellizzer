Begonia nuda

Begonia nuda, commonly known as the naked begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.

However, it requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
