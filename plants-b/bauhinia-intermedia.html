Bauhinia intermedia

Bauhinia intermedia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Bauhinia intermedia is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
