Bromopsis shelkovnikovii

Bromopsis shelkovnikovii, commonly known as the Shelkovnikov's feathergrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Bromopsis shelkovnikovii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying high-altitude flora.
