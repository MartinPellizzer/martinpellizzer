Bumelia egensis

Bumelia egensis, commonly known as the Mexican gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Bumelia egensis is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
