Begonia multinervia

Begonia multinervia, commonly known as the veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia multinervia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
