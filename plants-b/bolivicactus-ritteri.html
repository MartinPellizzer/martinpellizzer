Bolivicactus ritteri

Bolivicactus ritteri, commonly known as the Ritter's bolivian cactus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Bolivicactus ritteri is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for succulent collections and xeriscaping.
