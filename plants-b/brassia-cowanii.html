Brassia cowanii

Brassia cowanii, commonly known as the Cowan's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brassia cowanii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
