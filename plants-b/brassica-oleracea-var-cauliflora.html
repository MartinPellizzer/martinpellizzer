Brassica oleracea var. cauliflora

Brassica oleracea var. cauliflora, commonly known as cauliflower, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence.

The plant produces a swollen, white or green curd made up of tightly clustered flower buds, which are harvested before they fully open. It is typically grown in temperate climates and requires cool temperatures for optimal growth. The leaves are dark green and form a loose head around the central inflorescence.

This vegetable is rich in nutrients and is widely cultivated for both culinary and nutritional purposes.
