Bactris lakoi

Bactris lakoi, commonly known as the Lakoi palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits resembling small coconuts. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for sustainable use in local communities.
