Bulbophyllum ebracteolatum

Bulbophyllum ebracteolatum, commonly known as the naked leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to its tropical habitat.

Bulbophyllum ebracteolatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
