Bartsia simulans

Bartsia simulans, commonly known as the false pimpernel, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers in clusters. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Bartsia simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
