Banisteriopsis martiniana

Banisteriopsis martiniana, commonly known as the red mangrove or *Rhizophora mangle*, is a tropical tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive aerial roots, which help it anchor in waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in brackish or saltwater environments, often forming dense mangrove forests.

Its ecological importance includes coastal protection and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial life.
