Banisteria multifoliolata

Banisteria multifoliolata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it a lush, feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
