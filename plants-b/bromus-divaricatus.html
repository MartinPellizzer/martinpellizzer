Bromus divaricatus

Bromus divaricatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or variable brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with two to three bracts at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Bromus divaricatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
