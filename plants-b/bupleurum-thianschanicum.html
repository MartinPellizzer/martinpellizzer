Bupleurum thianschanicum

Bupleurum thianschanicum, commonly known as the Tian Shan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hairy stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

The roots of Bupleurum thianschanicum are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional herbal medicine.
