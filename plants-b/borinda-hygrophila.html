Borinda hygrophila

Borinda hygrophila, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Borinda hygrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
