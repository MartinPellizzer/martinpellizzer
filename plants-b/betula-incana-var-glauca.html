Betula incana var. glauca

Betula incana var. glauca, commonly known as the white birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall before dropping. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in moist, cool climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
