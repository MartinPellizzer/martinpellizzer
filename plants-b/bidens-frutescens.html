Bidens frutescens

Bidens frutescens, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small yellow flowers with five petals. The plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
