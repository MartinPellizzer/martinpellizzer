Bunium majus

Bunium majus, commonly known as the greater fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, hollow stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, and it produces umbellate inflorescences with small, white to greenish flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are small, dry, and contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Bunium majus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
