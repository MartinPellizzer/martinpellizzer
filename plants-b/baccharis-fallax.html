Baccharis fallax

Baccharis fallax, commonly known as sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
