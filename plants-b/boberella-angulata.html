Boberella angulata

Boberella angulata, commonly known as the angular boberella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-white blooms that appear in late spring.

Boberella angulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
