Begonia digitata var. rufescens

Begonia digitata var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
