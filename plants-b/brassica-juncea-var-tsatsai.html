Brassica juncea var. tsatsai

Brassica juncea var. tsatsai, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant native to China.

It is characterized by its rapid growth cycle and is often used as a leafy green vegetable. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinct, pungent flavor due to the presence of glucosinolates. Its leaves are typically harvested before the plant matures, making it a popular crop in Asian cuisines.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.
