Brachypodium distachyon

Brachypodium distachyon, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a small perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is widely used as a model organism in plant biology due to its compact genome, short life cycle, and genetic similarity to cereals like wheat and rice. The plant has a robust root system and produces dense, tufted foliage with spikelets containing two bracts. Its flowering is influenced by photoperiod, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Brachypodium distachyon is also valued for its potential in biofuel production and ecological restoration.
