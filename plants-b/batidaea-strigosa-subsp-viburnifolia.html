Batidaea strigosa subsp. viburnifolia

Batidaea strigosa subsp.

viburnifolia, commonly known as the viburnifoliate batidaea, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks.

The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are typically white or pale green, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and subtle floral display.
