Bromus tomentellus var. tauricola

Bromus tomentellus var. tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric brome, is a perennial grass native to the steppes of the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a tufted growth habit with slender, leafy stems that often lie flat along the ground. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is loosely arranged.

The leaves are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving the plant its scientific name "tomentellus.".
