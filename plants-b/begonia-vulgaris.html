Begonia vulgaris

Begonia vulgaris, commonly known as the common begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens for its ornamental value.

Begonia vulgaris is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
