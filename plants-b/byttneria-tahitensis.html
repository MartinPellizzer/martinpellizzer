Byttneria tahitensis

Byttneria tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian silk-cotton tree, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the South Pacific and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, woolly flowers. The tree produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds surrounded by a cotton-like fiber. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
