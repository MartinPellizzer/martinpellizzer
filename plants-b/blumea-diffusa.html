Blumea diffusa

Blumea diffusa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and are borne in clusters, creating a mist-like appearance.

Blumea diffusa is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
