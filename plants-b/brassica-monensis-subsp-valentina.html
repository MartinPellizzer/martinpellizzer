Brassica monensis subsp. valentina

Brassica monensis subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both ornamental and edible purposes.
