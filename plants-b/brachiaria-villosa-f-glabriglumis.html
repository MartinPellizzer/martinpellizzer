Brachiaria villosa f. glabriglumis

Brachiaria villosa f. glabriglumis, commonly known as the glabrous lemmas grass, is a variant of the species Brachiaria villosa, characterized by its reduced pubescence on the lemma.

This grass is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
