Berberis brachybotria var. brevispina

Berberis brachybotria var. brevispina, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
