Badusa corymbifera subsp. corymbifera

Badusa corymbifera subsp.

corymbifera, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has a rosette-forming base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a corymb.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
