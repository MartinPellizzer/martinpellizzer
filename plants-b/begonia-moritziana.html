Begonia moritziana

Begonia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often velvety to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
