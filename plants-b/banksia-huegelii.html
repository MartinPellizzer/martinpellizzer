Banksia huegelii

Banksia huegelii, commonly known as the Hugel's banksia, is a species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Banksia huegelii is well adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
