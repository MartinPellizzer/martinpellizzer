Brachypodium sylvaticum var. wattii

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. wattii, commonly known as the mountain bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and as a model for grass research.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
