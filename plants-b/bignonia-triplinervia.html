Bignonia triplinervia

Bignonia triplinervia, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for covering trellises or fences in gardens.
