Bistorta tubistipulis

Bistorta tubistipulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and sometimes edible rhizomes. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, erect inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Bistorta tubistipulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
