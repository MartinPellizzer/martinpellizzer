Bulbophyllum involutum

Bulbophyllum involutum, commonly known as the spiral lip orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss.
