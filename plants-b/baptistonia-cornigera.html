Baptistonia cornigera

Baptistonia cornigera, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer, and it thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Baptistonia cornigera plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
