Bulbophyllum glaucum

Bulbophyllum glaucum, commonly known as the blue bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and clusters of small, pale blue or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it store water.

Bulbophyllum glaucum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
