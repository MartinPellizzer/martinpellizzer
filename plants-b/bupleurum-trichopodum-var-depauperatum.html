Bupleurum trichopodum var. depauperatum

Bupleurum trichopodum var. depauperatum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or semi-upright, with small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
