Brassica campestris var. setulosa

Brassica campestris var. setulosa, commonly known as the curly kale or tree kale, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its dense, leafy structure.

The plant features large, wavy, and often curly leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly fuzzy texture. It is a biennial plant that thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves. The leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A, C, and K, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.

Its botanical structure supports its use in both culinary and ornamental applications.
