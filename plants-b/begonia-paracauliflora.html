Begonia paracauliflora

Begonia paracauliflora, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, adapting to poor soil conditions with its specialized root system. Its unique growth habit, with flowers emerging from the base of the stem, makes it a striking addition to rock gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in semi-shaded environments.
