Bignonia unguis-cati var. guatemalensis

Bignonia unguis-cati var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemala trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Central America.

It features showy, orange-yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces long, serpentine seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
