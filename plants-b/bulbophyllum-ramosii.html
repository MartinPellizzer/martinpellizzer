Bulbophyllum ramosii

Bulbophyllum ramosii, commonly known as the branched orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with multiple flowering stems that produce clusters of fragrant, greenish-white flowers. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting the plant's unique morphology.

Bulbophyllum ramosii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
