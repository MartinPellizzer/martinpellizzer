Briggsia amabilis

Briggsia amabilis, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences that resemble tassels, composed of small, clustered flowers. The plant has hairy, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. Briggsia amabilis produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to container gardening.
