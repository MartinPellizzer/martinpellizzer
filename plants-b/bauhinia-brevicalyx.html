Bauhinia brevicalyx

Bauhinia brevicalyx, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular ornamental species. Its pods are typically flat and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bauhinia brevicalyx plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
