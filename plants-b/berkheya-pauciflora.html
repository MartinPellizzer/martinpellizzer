Berkheya pauciflora

Berkheya pauciflora, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Berkheya pauciflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
