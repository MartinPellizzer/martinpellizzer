Blancoa porphyrocarpa

Blancoa porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited blancoa, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is distinctive, being fleshy and red, which attracts various bird species.

The botanical features of Blancoa porphyrocarpa make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
