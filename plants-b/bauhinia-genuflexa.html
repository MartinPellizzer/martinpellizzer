Bauhinia genuflexa

Bauhinia genuflexa, commonly known as the hanging bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous racemes of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia genuflexa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
