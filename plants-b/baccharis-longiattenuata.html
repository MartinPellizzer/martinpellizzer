Baccharis longiattenuata

Baccharis longiattenuata, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract various pollinators. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in ecological restoration projects.
