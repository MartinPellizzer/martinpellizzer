Bletia mandonii

Bletia mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Bletia mandonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
