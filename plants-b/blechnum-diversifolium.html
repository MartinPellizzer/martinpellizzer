Blechnum diversifolium

Blechnum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that vary in shape and size, often displaying a range of morphological features. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a feathery appearance, and it produces spores on the underside of its fronds.

Blechnum diversifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
