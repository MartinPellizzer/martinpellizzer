Brassica integrifolia

Brassica integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are siliques, which contain numerous small, round seeds.

Brassica integrifolia is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
