Bambusa macrotis

Bambusa macrotis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, providing shade and habitat for various wildlife.

Bambusa macrotis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for sustainable bamboo products.
