Bignonia triantha

Bignonia triantha, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia triantha thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
