Bulbophyllum culex

Bulbophyllum culex, commonly known as the Culex orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in shaded environments.

Bulbophyllum culex is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
