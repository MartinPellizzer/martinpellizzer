Bignonia rollisonii

Bignonia rollisonii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant produces long, pendulous blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bignonia rollisonii is a vigorous climber that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
