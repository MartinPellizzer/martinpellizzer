Banksia traversii

Banksia traversii, commonly known as the West Australian banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and it is adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat. Banksia traversii is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its resilience to fire and drought.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
