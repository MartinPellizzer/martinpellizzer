Beloperone formosa

Beloperone formosa, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky hillsides. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Beloperone formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
