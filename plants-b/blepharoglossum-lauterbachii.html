Blepharoglossum lauterbachii

Blepharoglossum lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
