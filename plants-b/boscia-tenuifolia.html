Boscia tenuifolia

Boscia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.

The leaves and roots of Boscia tenuifolia have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
