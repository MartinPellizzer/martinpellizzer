Binghamia acanthura

Binghamia acanthura, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a unique Australian plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The tree has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the cooler months.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
