Baccharis boliviensis var. latifolia

Baccharis boliviensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that gives the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
