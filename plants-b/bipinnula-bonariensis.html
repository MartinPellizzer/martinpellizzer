Bipinnula bonariensis

Bipinnula bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its fronds are often described as having a graceful, feathery appearance.

Bipinnula bonariensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
