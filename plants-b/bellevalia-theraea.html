Bellevalia theraea

Bellevalia theraea, commonly known as the Aegean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Bellevalia theraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
