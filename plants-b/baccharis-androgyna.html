Baccharis androgyna

Baccharis androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis androgyna is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
