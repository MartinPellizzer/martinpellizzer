Berberis kansuensis

Berberis kansuensis, commonly known as Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
