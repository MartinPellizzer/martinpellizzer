Bergia oryzetorum

Bergia oryzetorum, commonly known as the ricegrass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Bergia oryzetorum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its life cycle is closely tied to seasonal flooding, making it an indicator species for wetland health.
