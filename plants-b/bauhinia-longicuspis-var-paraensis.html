Bauhinia longicuspis var. paraensis

Bauhinia longicuspis var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Bauhinia, is a species of legume native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, raceme-like inflorescences that produce small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
