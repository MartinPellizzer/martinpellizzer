Bulbophyllum hirsutissimum

Bulbophyllum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, with long, wiry stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its bulbs are an essential part of its life cycle, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

This orchid is popular among horticultural enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
