Bulbillaria gageoides

Bulbillaria gageoides, commonly known as the Cape town daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, oval-shaped bulbils on its stems, which are used for vegetative propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens.
