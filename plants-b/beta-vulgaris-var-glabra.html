Beta vulgaris var. glabra

Beta vulgaris var. glabra, commonly known as the beetroot or garden beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy taproot, which is typically red in color but can also be yellow or white. The plant produces small, green, triangular leaves that are edible and often consumed raw or cooked. The root is rich in nutrients such as iron, fiber, and vitamins, making it a valuable crop.

It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, requiring moderate water and full sunlight for optimal growth.
