Berrya pacifica

Berrya pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific berry or Pacific sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
