Bromus pseudobrachystachys

Bromus pseudobrachystachys, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often lie flat on the ground, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage.

Bromus pseudobrachystachys is characterized by its small, awned seeds and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
