Bactris bifida

Bactris bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bifurcated leaves that split into two narrow lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized palm, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and pinnate, with a fibrous texture, and it produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits.

Bactris bifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
