Banksia subpinnatifida var. imberbis

Banksia subpinnatifida var. imberbis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
