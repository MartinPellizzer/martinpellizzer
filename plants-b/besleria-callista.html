Besleria callista

Besleria callista, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow flowers with red-brown markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Besleria callista is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and unique floral display.
