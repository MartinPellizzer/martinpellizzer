Baccharis boliviensis var. boliviensis

Baccharis boliviensis var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flower clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
