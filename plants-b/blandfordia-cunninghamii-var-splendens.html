Blandfordia cunninghamii var. splendens

Blandfordia cunninghamii var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to red, with a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant produces a single upright stem that bears several flower buds, which open sequentially over a period of weeks. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, sheltered environment to flourish.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
