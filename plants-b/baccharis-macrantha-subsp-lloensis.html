Baccharis macrantha subsp. lloensis

Baccharis macrantha subsp.

lloensis, commonly known as the Lloendo thornbush, is a shrub native to southern South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered fruit capsules.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
