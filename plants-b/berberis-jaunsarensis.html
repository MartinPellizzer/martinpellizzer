Berberis jaunsarensis

Berberis jaunsarensis, commonly known as the Jaunsari barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It features spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, astringent taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Berberis jaunsarensis thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or mountainous terrain.
