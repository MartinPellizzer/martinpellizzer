Brachystelma plocamoides

Brachystelma plocamoides, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and has a compact, cushion-like growth form. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.
