Banisteria oxyclada var. rhombifolia

Banisteria oxyclada var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate banisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
