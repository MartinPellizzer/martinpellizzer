Bryobium senile

Bryobium senile, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a unique liverwort that exhibits a distinctive appearance.

It forms dense, hanging clusters of leaf-like structures that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on decaying wood or in humid forest floors. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, develop from the gametophyte stage, which is the dominant phase in its life cycle.

Bryobium senile plays an important ecological role by contributing to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
