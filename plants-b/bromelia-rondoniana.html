Bromelia rondoniana

Bromelia rondoniana, commonly known as the Rondonian bromeliad, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant's foliage is often vibrant, displaying shades of green, red, and purple, depending on the species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the rainforest.

Bromelia rondoniana plays a vital ecological role by providing habitat and water sources for various small organisms.
