Begonia taraw

Begonia taraw, commonly known as the Taraw begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by its colorful bracts.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
