Bituminaria atropurpurea

Bituminaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, dark purple to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white woolly hair.

Bituminaria atropurpurea is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
