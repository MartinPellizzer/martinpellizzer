Batatas pentaphylla

Batatas pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots that can vary in color from white to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The tubers are edible and often used in local cuisines for their sweet, nutty flavor.
