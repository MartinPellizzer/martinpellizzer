Bonaveria securidaca

Bonaveria securidaca, commonly known as the white sand lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy white flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green throat, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Bonaveria securidaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
