Baccharis aegyptiaca

Baccharis aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian baccharis, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, distinctive scent. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions.

Baccharis aegyptiaca is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
