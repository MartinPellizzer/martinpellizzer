Bulbophyllum rojasii

Bulbophyllum rojasii, commonly known as the Red-veined Lipstick Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bulbophyllum rojasii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
