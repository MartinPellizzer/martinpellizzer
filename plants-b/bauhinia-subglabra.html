Bauhinia subglabra

Bauhinia subglabra, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy inflorescences.

Bauhinia subglabra is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
