Baccharis incarum f. spathulata

Baccharis incarum f. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
