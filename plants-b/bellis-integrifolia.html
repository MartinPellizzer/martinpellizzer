Bellis integrifolia

Bellis integrifolia, commonly known as the lawn daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to many garden landscapes.
