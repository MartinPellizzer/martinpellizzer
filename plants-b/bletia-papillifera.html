Bletia papillifera

Bletia papillifera, commonly known as the papillate bletia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or papillae, giving it its common name. Bletia papillifera produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, typically blooming during the dry season.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
