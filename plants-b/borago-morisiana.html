Borago morisiana

Borago morisiana, commonly known as the Madagascar bean or Morisian bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
