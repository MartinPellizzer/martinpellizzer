Brathys juniperina

Brathys juniperina, commonly known as the silver juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a fine layer of silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Brathys juniperina is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
