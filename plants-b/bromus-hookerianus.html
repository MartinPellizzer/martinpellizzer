Bromus hookerianus

Bromus hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Bromus hookerianus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
