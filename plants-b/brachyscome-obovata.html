Brachyscome obovata

Brachyscome obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are narrow and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color and form even when dried.
