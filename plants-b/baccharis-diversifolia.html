Baccharis diversifolia

Baccharis diversifolia, commonly known as the variable baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by insects and birds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and shrublands, and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

Baccharis diversifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
