Baccharis tridentata f. integrifolia

Baccharis tridentata f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in arid and semi-arid regions.
