Bulbophyllum atroviride

Bulbophyllum atroviride, commonly known as the black-green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a greenish lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
