Barkhausia auriculifolia

Barkhausia auriculifolia, commonly known as the ear-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, ear-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Barkhausia auriculifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
