Boronia juncea subsp. micrantha

Boronia juncea subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
