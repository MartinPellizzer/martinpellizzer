Bignonia grandiflora

Bignonia grandiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 feet long, making it a vigorous climber. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
