Barkhausia spathulata

Barkhausia spathulata, commonly known as the Cape rose or Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spathaceous bract that resembles a leaf, giving it its scientific name.

Barkhausia spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
