Bernardia capitellata

Bernardia capitellata, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique features, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a specimen plant in warm climates.
