Bulbophyllum emunitum

Bulbophyllum emunitum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small but numerous, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

The botanical features of Bulbophyllum emunitum make it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
