Boehmeria strigosifolia var. mollis

Boehmeria strigosifolia var. mollis, commonly known as silky bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its fibrous stalks are also valued for their strength and have been used in making paper and textiles.
