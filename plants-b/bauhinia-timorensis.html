Bauhinia timorensis

Bauhinia timorensis, commonly known as the Timor bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery leaves that are often divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. It is often found growing in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
