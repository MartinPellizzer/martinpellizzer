Bupleurum rotundifolium f. typicum

Bupleurum rotundifolium f. typicum, commonly known as the round-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
