Balsamorhiza hookeri var. idahoensis

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that gives it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
