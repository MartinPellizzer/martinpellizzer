Begonia spatulata

Begonia spatulata, commonly known as the spoonleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia spatulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
