Begonia alepensis

Begonia alepensis, commonly known as the Aleppo begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Begonia alepensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
