Banksia cylindrostachya

Banksia cylindrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a striking plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical flower spikes that produce abundant yellow to orange flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Banksia cylindrostachya plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly honeyeaters and other nectar-feeding birds.
