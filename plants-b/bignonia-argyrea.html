Bignonia argyrea

Bignonia argyrea, commonly known as silverleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia argyrea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant in certain ecological contexts.
