Brunfelsia americana

Brunfelsia americana, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as scopolamine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Brunfelsia americana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
