Becium grandiflorum var. frutescens

Becium grandiflorum var. frutescens, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
