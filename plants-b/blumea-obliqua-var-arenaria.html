Blumea obliqua var. arenaria

Blumea obliqua var. arenaria, commonly known as sand blumea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, adapting to well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
