Bupleurum odontites var. opacum

Bupleurum odontites var. opacum, commonly known as the opacum bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
