Begonia aguiabrancensis

Begonia aguiabrancensis, commonly known as the Aguiabrancense begonia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and exhibit a range of colors, from deep green to variegated patterns. The flowers, which bloom in spring, come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive, spurred shape.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
