Bambusa argentea var. vittata

Bambusa argentea var. vittata, commonly known as the silver bamboo or variegated bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its striking white and green variegated foliage.

It is a clump-forming plant that produces tall, slender culms with a smooth, often silver-tinted surface. Native to Southeast Asia, this species is cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its elegant appearance in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its use in construction and crafts due to its strong and flexible stalks.
