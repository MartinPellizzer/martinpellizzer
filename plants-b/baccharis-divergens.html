Baccharis divergens

Baccharis divergens, commonly known as swamp baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Baccharis divergens plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
