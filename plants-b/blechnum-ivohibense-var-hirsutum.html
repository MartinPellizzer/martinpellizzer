Blechnum ivohibense var. hirsutum

Blechnum ivohibense var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Blechnum, is a fern species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy or pubescent texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are leathery and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The varietal name "hirsutum" refers to the hairy appearance of its foliage, which is a notable botanical feature.
