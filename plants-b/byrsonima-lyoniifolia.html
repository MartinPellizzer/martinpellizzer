Byrsonima lyoniifolia

Byrsonima lyoniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
