Barkeria chinensis subsp. naevosa

Barkeria chinensis subsp.

naevosa, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The subspecies naevosa is distinguished by its distinctive leaf patterns and slightly different flower morphology compared to other subspecies of Barkeria chinensis.
