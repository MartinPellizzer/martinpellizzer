Brachyscias verecundus

Brachyscias verecundus, commonly known as the velvet leaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that gives it a velvety texture, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid climates.

Brachyscias verecundus is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
