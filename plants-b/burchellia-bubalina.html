Burchellia bubalina

Burchellia bubalina, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate in shape. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

The plant contains milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Burchellia.
