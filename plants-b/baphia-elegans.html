Baphia elegans

Baphia elegans, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, smooth bark and pinnately compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing bitter, fibrous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
