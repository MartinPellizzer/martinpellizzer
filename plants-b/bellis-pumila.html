Bellis pumila

Bellis pumila, commonly known as the common daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming from spring to autumn. The plant has a rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along paths.

Bellis pumila is known for its ability to spread via stolons, forming dense mats that can suppress weeds.
