Bauhinia kockiana var. bakoensis

Bauhinia kockiana var. bakoensis, commonly known as the Bako bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruits are flat, pod-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
