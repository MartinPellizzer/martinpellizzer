Brassica Sativa Subsp. Napus

Brassica sativa subsp. napus is a widely cultivated crop known for its versatility and nutritional value. This subspecies is commonly referred to as canola and is primarily grown for its oil-rich seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant's ability to produce high yields makes it a key player in global agriculture. Its importance extends beyond food production, as it also plays a role in sustainable farming practices.
