Bellevalia wilhelmsii

Bellevalia wilhelmsii, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of around 15-30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
