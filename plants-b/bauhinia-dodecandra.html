Bauhinia dodecandra

Bauhinia dodecandra, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods containing flat, seed-containing structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Bauhinia dodecandra make it a popular choice for landscaping and floral displays.
