Bocconia integrifolia

Bocconia integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf bocconia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Bocconia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
