Bupleurum falcatum f. giganteum

Bupleurum falcatum f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant cowparsley, is a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a feathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
