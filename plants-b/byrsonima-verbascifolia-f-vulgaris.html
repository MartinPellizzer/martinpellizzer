Byrsonima verbascifolia f. vulgaris

Byrsonima verbascifolia f. vulgaris, commonly known as the yellow wolfberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and fleshy, edible fruits that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal uses.
