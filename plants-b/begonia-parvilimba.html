Begonia parvilimba

Begonia parvilimba, commonly known as the small-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
