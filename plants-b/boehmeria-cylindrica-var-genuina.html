Boehmeria cylindrica var. genuina

Boehmeria cylindrica var. genuina, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or woolly flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and its fibrous stalks are valued for their textile properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its potential in sustainable fiber production and ecological restoration projects.
