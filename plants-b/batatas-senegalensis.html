Batatas senegalensis

Batatas senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trailing vines. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. Its flowers are typically white or purple and are borne in clusters.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible roots and ornamental value.
