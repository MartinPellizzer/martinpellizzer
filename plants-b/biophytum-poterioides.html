Biophytum poterioides

Biophytum poterioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Biophytum poterioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
