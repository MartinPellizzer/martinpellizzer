Betula sajanensis

Betula sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan birch, is a species of birch native to eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula sajanensis plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
