Buddleja virgata

Buddleja virgata, commonly known as the silver-leaf buddleja, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought and pollution, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping. Buddleja virgata attracts a wide range of pollinators, including butterflies and bees, due to its nectar-rich flowers.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
