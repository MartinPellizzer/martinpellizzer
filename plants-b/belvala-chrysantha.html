Belvala chrysantha

Belvala chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Belvala chrysantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
