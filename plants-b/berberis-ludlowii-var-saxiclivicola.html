Berberis ludlowii var. saxiclivicola

Berberis ludlowii var. saxiclivicola, commonly known as the rock spurge barberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
