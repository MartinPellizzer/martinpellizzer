Boehmeria rubescens

Boehmeria rubescens, commonly known as redleaf nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in color as they mature. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Boehmeria rubescens is often cultivated for its fibrous stems, which are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.
