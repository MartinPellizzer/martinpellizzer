Brassica saxatilis var. silenifolia

Brassica saxatilis var. silenifolia, commonly known as the rock cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly serrated edge, adapted to thrive in rocky and coastal environments. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand salt spray and poor soil conditions.

It is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
