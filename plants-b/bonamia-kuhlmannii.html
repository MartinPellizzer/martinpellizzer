Bonamia kuhlmannii

Bonamia kuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
