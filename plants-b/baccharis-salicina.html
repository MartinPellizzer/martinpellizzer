Baccharis salicina

Baccharis salicina, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coast. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture.

Baccharis salicina is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
