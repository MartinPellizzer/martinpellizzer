Begonia humilis var. porteriana

Begonia humilis var. porteriana, commonly known as the Porter's begonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking to add color to low-light environments.
