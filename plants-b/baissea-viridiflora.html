Baissea viridiflora

Baissea viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered baissea, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying a combination of green and white hues.

Baissea viridiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
