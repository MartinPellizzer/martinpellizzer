Barbarea vulgaris var. plantaginea

Barbarea vulgaris var. plantaginea, commonly known as the common rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
