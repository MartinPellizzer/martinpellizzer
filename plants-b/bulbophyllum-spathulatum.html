Bulbophyllum spathulatum

Bulbophyllum spathulatum, commonly known as the spatulate bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, spatulate-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle scent. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
