Brachystele chlorops

Brachystele chlorops, commonly known as the green-stemmed staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically broad and strap-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves, producing spores for propagation.

Brachystele chlorops is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to add a lush, green aesthetic to indoor or outdoor gardens.
