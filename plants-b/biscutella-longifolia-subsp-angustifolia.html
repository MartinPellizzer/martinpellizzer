Biscutella longifolia subsp. angustifolia

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is notable for its thick, fleshy roots, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
