Bouchea linifolia

Bouchea linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bouchea linifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
