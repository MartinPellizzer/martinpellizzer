Bignonia hirsuta

Bignonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy plectranthus, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia hirsuta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
