Bloomeria crocea

Bloomeria crocea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Bloomeria crocea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in woodland edges, wetlands, and along stream banks.
