Bocagea elliptica

Bocagea elliptica, commonly known as the waxleaf bocagea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Bocagea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
