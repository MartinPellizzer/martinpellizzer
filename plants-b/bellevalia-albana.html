Bellevalia albana

Bellevalia albana, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, often in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats.

Bellevalia albana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
