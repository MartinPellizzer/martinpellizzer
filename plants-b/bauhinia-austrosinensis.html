Bauhinia austrosinensis

Bauhinia austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.

The seeds are encased in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.
