Brachiaria tsiafajavonensis

Brachiaria tsiafajavonensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
