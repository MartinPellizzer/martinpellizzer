Bulbine brevifolia

Bulbine brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbine, is a perennial succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and ideal for arid environments.
