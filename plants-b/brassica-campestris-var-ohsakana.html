Brassica campestris var. ohsakana

Brassica campestris var. ohsakana, commonly known as the Japanese mustard cabbage, is a cultivar of the mustard plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, pale green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, which have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown in temperate regions.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
