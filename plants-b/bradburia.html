Bradburia

Bradburia, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has thick, waxy leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, reflective coating. The plant produces striking purple or blue flowers that bloom above the water surface. Bradburia is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on water surfaces.

Its botanical features make it both a popular ornamental plant and a significant invasive species in many regions.
