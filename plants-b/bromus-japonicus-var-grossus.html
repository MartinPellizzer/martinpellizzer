Bromus japonicus var. grossus

Bromus japonicus var. grossus, commonly known as Japanese grass or broadleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and coarse, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several small, oval-shaped florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.
