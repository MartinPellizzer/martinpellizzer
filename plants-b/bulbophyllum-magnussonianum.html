Bulbophyllum magnussonianum

Bulbophyllum magnussonianum, commonly known as the Magnusson's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in low-light conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
