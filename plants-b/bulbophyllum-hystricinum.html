Bulbophyllum hystricinum

Bulbophyllum hystricinum, commonly known as the spiny orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky pseudobulbs and intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and resilience.
