Broussonetia × kazinoki f. koreana

Broussonetia × kazinoki f. koreana, commonly known as the Korean mulberry, is a hybrid species within the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of Korea and Japan.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural purposes.
