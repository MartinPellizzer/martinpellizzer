Berberis × gladwynensis

Berberis × gladwynensis, commonly known as the Gladwyn barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
