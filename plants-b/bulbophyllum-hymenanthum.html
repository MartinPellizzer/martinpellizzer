Bulbophyllum hymenanthum

Bulbophyllum hymenanthum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
