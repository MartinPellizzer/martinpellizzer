Begonia hispida

Begonia hispida, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a unique tactile texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Begonia hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
