Brassica arborea

Brassica arborea, commonly known as the tree mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Brassica arborea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
