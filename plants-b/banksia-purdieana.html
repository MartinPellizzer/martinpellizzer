Banksia purdieana

Banksia purdieana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

Banksia purdieana is an important component of local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
