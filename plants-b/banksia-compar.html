Banksia compar

Banksia compar, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Banksia compar is typically found in heathlands and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
