Baikiaea lescrauwaetii

Baikiaea lescrauwaetii, commonly known as the Lesrauwaet's cola, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The tree is typically found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while its wood is valued for its durability and workability.
