Buddleja stenoptera

Buddleja stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja stenoptera is relatively low maintenance and can be pruned to maintain its shape and encourage new growth.
