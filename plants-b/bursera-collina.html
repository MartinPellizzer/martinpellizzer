Bursera collina

Bursera collina, commonly known as Mexican balsam, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Bursera collina is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
