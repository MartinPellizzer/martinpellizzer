Bulbophyllum taeniophyllum

Bulbophyllum taeniophyllum, commonly known as the stripe-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, linear leaves that often display distinct markings. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
