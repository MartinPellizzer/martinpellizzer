Banisteria billbergiana

Banisteria billbergiana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Banisteria billbergiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
