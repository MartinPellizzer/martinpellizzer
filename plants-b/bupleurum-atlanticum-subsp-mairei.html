Bupleurum atlanticum subsp. mairei

Bupleurum atlanticum subsp.

mairei, commonly known as Atlantic sea holly, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the western Mediterranean. It features distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for coastal ecosystem studies and ecological restoration projects.
