Barleria venosa

Barleria venosa, commonly known as the red-veined barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Barleria venosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
