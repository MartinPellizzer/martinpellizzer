Begonia leptophylla

Begonia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes.
