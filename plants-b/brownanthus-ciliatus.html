Brownanthus ciliatus

Brownanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
