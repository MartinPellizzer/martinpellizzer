Bunias syriaca

Bunias syriaca, commonly known as Syrian mustard, is a hardy annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. Its leaves are large, lobed, and often have a peppery taste, making it a useful plant in some traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.
