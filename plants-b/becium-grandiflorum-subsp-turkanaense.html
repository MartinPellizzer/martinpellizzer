Becium grandiflorum subsp. turkanaense

Becium grandiflorum subsp.

turkanaense, commonly known as Turkana becium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of East Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange capitula, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
