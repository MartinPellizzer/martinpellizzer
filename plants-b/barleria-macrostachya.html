Barleria macrostachya

Barleria macrostachya, commonly known as the red-flowered barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal spikes, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Barleria macrostachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
