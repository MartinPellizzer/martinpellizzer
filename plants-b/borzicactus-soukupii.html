Borzicactus soukupii

Borzicactus soukupii, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red, apple-like fruits. Its adaptations include a thick cuticle to reduce water loss and spines that provide protection from herbivores.

Borzicactus soukupii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
