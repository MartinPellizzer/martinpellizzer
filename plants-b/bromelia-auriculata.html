Bromelia auriculata

Bromelia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that form a central cup. The leaves are often colored in shades of green, red, or purple, depending on the variety, and they are covered in a fine layer of trichomes. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can range in color from yellow to orange to red.

It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
