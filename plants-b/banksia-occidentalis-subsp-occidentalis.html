Banksia occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Banksia occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow to orange flowering spikes that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces woody cones that contain follicles, each holding a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
