Brassicella monensis

Brassicella monensis, commonly known as the Monensis Mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its potential in both ornamental gardening and as a source of natural compounds.
