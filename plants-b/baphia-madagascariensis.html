Baphia madagascariensis

Baphia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
