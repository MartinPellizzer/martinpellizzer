Berzelia intermedia var. alopecuroides

Berzelia intermedia var. alopecuroides, commonly known as the sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spiny burs that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a common weed in many regions.
