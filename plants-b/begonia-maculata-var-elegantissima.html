Begonia maculata var. elegantissima

Begonia maculata var. elegantissima, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
