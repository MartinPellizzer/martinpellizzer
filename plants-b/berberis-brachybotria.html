Berberis brachybotria

Berberis brachybotria, commonly known as the short-stemmed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorned stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
