Bulbostylis junciformis

Bulbostylis junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This perennial herb is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
