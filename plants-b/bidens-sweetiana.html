Bidens sweetiana

Bidens sweetiana, commonly known as the sweetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes.

Bidens sweetiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
