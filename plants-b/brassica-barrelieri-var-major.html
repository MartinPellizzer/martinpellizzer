Brassica barrelieri var. major

Brassica barrelieri var. major, commonly known as the giant African violet, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features large, fleshy, oval leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces round, greenish-brown siliques that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
