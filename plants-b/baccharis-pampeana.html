Baccharis pampeana

Baccharis pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas devil's ivy, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina, Uruguay, and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and shrublands, thriving in dry to moderately moist conditions.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
