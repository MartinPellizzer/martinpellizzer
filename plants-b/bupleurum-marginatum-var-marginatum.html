Bupleurum marginatum var. marginatum

Bupleurum marginatum var. marginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive white or pale green margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
