Baccharis thuyoides

Baccharis thuyoides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its strong, pleasant scent and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.

Baccharis thuyoides is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
