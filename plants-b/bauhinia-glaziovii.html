Bauhinia glaziovii

Bauhinia glaziovii, commonly known as the glaziovii bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
