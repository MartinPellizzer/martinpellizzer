Barbacenia tubiflora

Barbacenia tubiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
