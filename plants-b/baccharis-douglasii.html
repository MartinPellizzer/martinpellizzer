Baccharis douglasii

Baccharis douglasii, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It has a woody stem and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Baccharis douglasii is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
