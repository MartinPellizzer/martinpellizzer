Billardiera cymosa

Billardiera cymosa, commonly known as the wattlevine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its twining stems and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen.

Billardiera cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
