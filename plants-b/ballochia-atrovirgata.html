Ballochia atrovirgata

Ballochia atrovirgata, commonly known as the black-veined bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with dark veins that contrast sharply against the lighter petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ballochia atrovirgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
