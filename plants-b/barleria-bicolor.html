Barleria bicolor

Barleria bicolor, commonly known as the red-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature bright red petals with a yellow center, resembling daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often hairy, while its stems are usually woody at the base.

Barleria bicolor is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.
