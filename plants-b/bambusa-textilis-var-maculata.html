Bambusa textilis var. maculata

Bambusa textilis var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that exhibits distinctive variegated foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue with white or silver markings, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The culms are sturdy and can be harvested for use in crafts or as a sustainable building material.
