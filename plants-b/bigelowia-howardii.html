Bigelowia howardii

Bigelowia howardii, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled spheres that are both ornamental and attractively structured. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Bigelowia howardii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
