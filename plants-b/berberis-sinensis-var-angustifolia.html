Berberis sinensis var. angustifolia

Berberis sinensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. The plant is native to China and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and winter interest.

Its berries are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
