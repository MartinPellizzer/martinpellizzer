Banisteria subcordata

Banisteria subcordata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
