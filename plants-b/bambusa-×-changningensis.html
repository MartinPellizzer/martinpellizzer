Bambusa × changningensis

Bambusa × changningensis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also valued for its strong and lightweight bamboo shoots, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
