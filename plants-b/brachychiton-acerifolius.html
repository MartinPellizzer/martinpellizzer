Brachychiton acerifolius

Brachychiton acerifolius, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The tree is also notable for its ability to shed its leaves during dry periods, adapting to its arid environment.
