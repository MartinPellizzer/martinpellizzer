Bulbostylis itremoensis

Bulbostylis itremoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
