Buddleja ususch

Buddleja ususch, commonly known as the Usutu buddleja, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Buddleja ususch is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
