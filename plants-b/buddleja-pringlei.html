Buddleja pringlei

Buddleja pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Buddleja pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
