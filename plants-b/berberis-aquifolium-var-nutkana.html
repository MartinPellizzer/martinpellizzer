Berberis aquifolium var. nutkana

Berberis aquifolium var. nutkana, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are glossy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for some birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
