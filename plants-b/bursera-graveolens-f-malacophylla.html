Bursera graveolens f. malacophylla

Bursera graveolens f. malacophylla, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a variant of the Bursera genus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grey bark and dense, evergreen foliage. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its unique botanical features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
