Beadlea argyrifolia

Beadlea argyrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf beadlea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Beadlea argyrifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
