Bromus squarrosus var. exilis

Bromus squarrosus var. exilis, commonly known as the slender sandberg bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a hardy, adaptable species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
