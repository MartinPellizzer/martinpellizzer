Bauhinia suaveolens

Bauhinia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
