Bulbophyllum polycyclum

Bulbophyllum polycyclum, commonly known as the many-cycled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and can be found in clusters, often displaying a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments with indirect light.
