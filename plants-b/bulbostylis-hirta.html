Bulbostylis hirta

Bulbostylis hirta, commonly known as the hairy bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
