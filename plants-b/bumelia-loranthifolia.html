Bumelia loranthifolia

Bumelia loranthifolia, commonly known as the red buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites, thriving in partial to full sun.
