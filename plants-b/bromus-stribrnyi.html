Bromus stribrnyi

Bromus stribrnyi, commonly known as the striped brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit with dense foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are characterized by their distinctive striped appearance, which gives it its common name. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Bromus stribrnyi plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
