Bolelia tricolor

Bolelia tricolor, commonly known as the three-color fern, is a unique species characterized by its strikingly variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically divided into three distinct color zones—green, yellow, and white—creating a visually appealing pattern. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forests. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is known for its delicate, feathery fronds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to maintain its vibrant coloration.
