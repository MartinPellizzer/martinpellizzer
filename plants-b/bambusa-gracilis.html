Bambusa gracilis

Bambusa gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It is a clumping bamboo, meaning it forms dense groups rather than spreading aggressively.

Bambusa gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
