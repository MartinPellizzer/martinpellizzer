Brunsvigia coranica

Brunsvigia coranica, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that produce showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Brunsvigia coranica is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other popular bulbous plants.
