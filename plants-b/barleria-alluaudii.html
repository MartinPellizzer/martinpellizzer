Barleria alluaudii

Barleria alluaudii, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Barleria alluaudii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
