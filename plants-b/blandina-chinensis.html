Blandina chinensis

Blandina chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Blandina chinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
