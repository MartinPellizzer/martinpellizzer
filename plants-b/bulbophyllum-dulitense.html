Bulbophyllum dulitense

Bulbophyllum dulitense, commonly known as the Dullit Island orchid, is a species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
