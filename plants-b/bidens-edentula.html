Bidens edentula

Bidens edentula, commonly known as beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Bidens edentula is an annual herb that plays a role in pollination and serves as a food source for various insects.
