Bambusa viridivittata

Bambusa viridivittata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, narrow leaves with a distinctive green stripe running along the midrib, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by clusters of edible, round seeds. Its hollow, segmented stems are strong and flexible, making it suitable for construction and crafts.

Bambusa viridivittata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
