Begonia letestui

Begonia letestui, commonly known as the Letestu's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its unique foliage and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among botanists and collectors.
