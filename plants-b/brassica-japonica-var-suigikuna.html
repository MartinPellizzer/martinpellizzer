Brassica japonica var. suigikuna

Brassica japonica var. suigikuna, commonly known as Japanese mustard spinach, is a leafy vegetable native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly bitter leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional Asian cuisines.
