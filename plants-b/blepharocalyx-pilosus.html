Blepharocalyx pilosus

Blepharocalyx pilosus, commonly known as the hairy-eyed maple, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded canopy. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that appear in clusters, often with a pink or red hue. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in lowland forests.
