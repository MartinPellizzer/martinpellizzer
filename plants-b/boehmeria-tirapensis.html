Boehmeria tirapensis

Boehmeria tirapensis, commonly known as the Tirap hemp or Philippine nettle, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species of nettles. The plant is characterized by its slender, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Boehmeria tirapensis is valued for its strong fibers, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
