Ballota royleoides

Ballota royleoides, commonly known as the Persian ballota, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Ballota royleoides is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive floral display.
