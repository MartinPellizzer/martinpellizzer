Borinda glabrifolia

Borinda glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf borinda, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, narrow leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
