Begonia discolor

Begonia discolor, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia discolor is often used in decorative plant arrangements and as a houseplant.
