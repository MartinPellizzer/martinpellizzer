Brassica lanceolata

Brassica lanceolata, commonly known as wall rocket, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant is known for its peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in culinary applications.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
