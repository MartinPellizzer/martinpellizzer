Barleria aromatica

Barleria aromatica, commonly known as sweet barleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and attractive appearance, it is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
