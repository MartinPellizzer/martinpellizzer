Baccharis philippensis

Baccharis philippensis, commonly known as Philippine baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in moist to wet conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and yellowish, often arranged in clusters.

Baccharis philippensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
