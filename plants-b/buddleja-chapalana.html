Buddleja chapalana

Buddleja chapalana, commonly known as the Chapala bush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Buddleja chapalana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
