Brassica elongata var. armoracioides

Brassica elongata var. armoracioides, commonly known as the giant mustard, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lobed leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing round, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional Mediterranean cuisines.
