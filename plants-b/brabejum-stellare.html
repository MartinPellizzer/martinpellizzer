Brabejum stellare

Brabejum stellare, commonly known as the star brabejum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or woodland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brabejum stellare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
