Begonia jamesoniana

Begonia jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns of green, red, and silver. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically appearing in the spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
