Bryonia convolvuloides

Bryonia convolvuloides, commonly known as the creeping bryony, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems, which often root at the nodes. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and it is typically found in disturbed soils in temperate regions.

Bryonia convolvuloides is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and cucurbitacin.
