Brachypodium distachyon f. intermedium

Brachypodium distachyon f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate brachypodium, is a variant of the model grass species Brachypodium distachyon.

It is characterized by its intermediate morphological traits between the typical annual and perennial forms of the species. This form exhibits a more robust growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in research for its genetic simplicity and potential applications in bioenergy and grassland ecology.
