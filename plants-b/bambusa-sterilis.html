Bambusa sterilis

Bambusa sterilis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows to a height of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Bambusa sterilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
