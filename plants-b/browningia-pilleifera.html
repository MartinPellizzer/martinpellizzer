Browningia pilleifera

Browningia pilleifera, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens.
