Brassica angustifolia

Brassica angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cabbage, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that form clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
