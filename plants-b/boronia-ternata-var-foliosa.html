Boronia ternata var. foliosa

Boronia ternata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has glossy, oval-shaped leaves. It is often found in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
