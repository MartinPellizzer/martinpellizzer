Balanophora latipes

Balanophora latipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a rare and fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its roots. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, humid environments within dense forests. The plant is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often difficult to locate in the wild.
