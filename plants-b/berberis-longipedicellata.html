Berberis longipedicellata

Berberis longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant seasonal color changes and architectural form.
