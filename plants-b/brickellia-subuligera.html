Brickellia subuligera

Brickellia subuligera, commonly known as the slender brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Brickellia subuligera is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
