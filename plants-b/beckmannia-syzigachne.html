Beckmannia syzigachne

Beckmannia syzigachne, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which produce tiny seeds.

Beckmannia syzigachne plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
