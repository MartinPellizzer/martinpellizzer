Bernardia celastrinea var. intermedia

Bernardia celastrinea var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian bittersweet, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary significance.
