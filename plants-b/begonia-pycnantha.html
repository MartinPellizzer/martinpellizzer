Begonia pycnantha

Begonia pycnantha, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia pycnantha is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
