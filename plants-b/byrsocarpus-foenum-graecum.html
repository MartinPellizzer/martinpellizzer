Byrsocarpus foenum-graecum

Byrsocarpus foenum-graecum, commonly known as the common senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds, a typical trait of the legume family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
