Blumea pachycephala

Blumea pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded heads of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Blumea pachycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
