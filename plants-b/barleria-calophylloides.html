Barleria calophylloides

Barleria calophylloides, commonly known as the golden barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Barleria calophylloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
