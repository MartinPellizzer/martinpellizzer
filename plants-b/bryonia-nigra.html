Bryonia nigra

Bryonia nigra, commonly known as black bryony, is a climbing plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its tendrils and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, orange-red berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

Bryonia nigra is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and cucurbitacin.
