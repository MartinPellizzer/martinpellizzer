Bignonia reveilii

Bignonia reveilii, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia reveilii is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and ability to climb or trail along structures.
