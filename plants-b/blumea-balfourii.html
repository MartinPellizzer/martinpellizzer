Blumea balfourii

Blumea balfourii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Blumea balfourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
