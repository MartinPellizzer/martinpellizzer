Borzicactus sepium subsp. morleyanus

Borzicactus sepium subsp.

morleyanus, commonly known as the Morley's bursera, is a species of flowering plant in the family Burseraceae. It is native to parts of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala.

The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in limestone soils.

The subspecies is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
