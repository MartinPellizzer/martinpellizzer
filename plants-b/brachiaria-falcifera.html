Brachiaria falcifera

Brachiaria falcifera, commonly known as the swordgrass or speargrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria falcifera is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.

It is often found in pastures, disturbed areas, and along roadsides due to its hardy and aggressive growth habit.
