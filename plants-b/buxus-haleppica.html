Buxus haleppica

Buxus haleppica, commonly known as the Turkish boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in compact clusters. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for topiary and hedging.

Buxus haleppica prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
