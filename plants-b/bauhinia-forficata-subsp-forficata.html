Bauhinia forficata subsp. forficata

Bauhinia forficata subsp.

forficata, commonly known as the "Brazilian peacock flower," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil. It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
