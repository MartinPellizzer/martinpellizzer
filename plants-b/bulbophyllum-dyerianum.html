Bulbophyllum dyerianum

Bulbophyllum dyerianum, commonly known as the Dyer's Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive pattern on the petals. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, storing water and nutrients to help it survive in its natural habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
