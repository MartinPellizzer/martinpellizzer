Buxus benguellensis

Buxus benguellensis, commonly known as the East African boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal forests of East Africa.

It has dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
