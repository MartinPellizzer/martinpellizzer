Begonia fischeri var. crenulatoglabra

Begonia fischeri var. crenulatoglabra, commonly known as the crenulate begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive wavy edge. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
