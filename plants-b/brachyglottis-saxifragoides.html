Brachyglottis saxifragoides

Brachyglottis saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the harsh, rocky environments it inhabits.

Brachyglottis saxifragoides is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
