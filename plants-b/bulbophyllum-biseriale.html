Bulbophyllum biseriale

Bulbophyllum biseriale, commonly known as the two-rowed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive greenish-brown lip. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
