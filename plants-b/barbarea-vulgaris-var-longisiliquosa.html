Barbarea vulgaris var. longisiliquosa

Barbarea vulgaris var. longisiliquosa, commonly known as the long-seed winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its upright stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
