Begonia parvifolia

Begonia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Begonia parvifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
