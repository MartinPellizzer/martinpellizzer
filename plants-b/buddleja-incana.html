Buddleja incana

Buddleja incana, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Buddleja incana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
