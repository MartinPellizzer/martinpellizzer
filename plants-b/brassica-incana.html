Brassica incana

Brassica incana, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual or biennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. Brassica incana is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
