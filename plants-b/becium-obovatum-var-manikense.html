Becium obovatum var. manikense

Becium obovatum var. manikense, commonly known as the Manikense becium, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
