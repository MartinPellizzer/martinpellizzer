Bramia semiserrata

Bramia semiserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Bramia semiserrata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
