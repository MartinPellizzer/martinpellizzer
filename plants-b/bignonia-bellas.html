Bignonia bellas

Bignonia bellas, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces long, slender leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Bignonia bellas is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or fences.
