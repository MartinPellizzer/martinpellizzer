Bauhinia blancoi

Bauhinia blancoi, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant white to pink flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
