Berberis × spaethii

Berberis × spaethii, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Berberis thunbergii and Berberis species.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, turning red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Berberis × spaethii is often used as a decorative hedge or in rock gardens due to its hardiness and visual interest throughout the growing season.
