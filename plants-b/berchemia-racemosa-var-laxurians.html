Berchemia racemosa var. laxurians

Berchemia racemosa var. laxurians, commonly known as the slender berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
