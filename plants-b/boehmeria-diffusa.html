Boehmeria diffusa

Boehmeria diffusa, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Boehmeria diffusa is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in the production of textiles and paper.
