Brassica cernua var. chirimenna

Brassica cernua var. chirimenna, commonly known as the Chirimena mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
