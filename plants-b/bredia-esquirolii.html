Bredia esquirolii

Bredia esquirolii, commonly known as the dwarf red bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red floral spikes that appear in spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Bredia esquirolii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
