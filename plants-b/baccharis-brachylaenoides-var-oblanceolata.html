Baccharis brachylaenoides var. oblanceolata

Baccharis brachylaenoides var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
