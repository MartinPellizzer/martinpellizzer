Behuria glutinosa

Behuria glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that secrete a sticky, resinous substance. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Behuria glutinosa make it both ecologically interesting and potentially valuable in pharmacological research.
