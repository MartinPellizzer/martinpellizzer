Bignonia cordata

Bignonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bignonia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
