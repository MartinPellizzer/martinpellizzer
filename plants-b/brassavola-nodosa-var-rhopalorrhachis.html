Brassavola nodosa var. rhopalorrhachis

Brassavola nodosa var. rhopalorrhachis, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats and has fibrous roots adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
