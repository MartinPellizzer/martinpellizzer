Bunium denudatum var. pyrenaeum

Bunium denudatum var. pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a distinct umbel-like inflorescence.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
