Brickellia grayana

Brickellia grayana, commonly known as gray's brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
