Bauhinia saigonensis var. poilanei

Bauhinia saigonensis var. poilanei, commonly known as the Saigon bauhinia, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
