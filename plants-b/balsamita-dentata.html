Balsamita dentata

Balsamita dentata, commonly known as the toothed balsam, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Balsamita dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some indigenous cultures for medicinal purposes.
