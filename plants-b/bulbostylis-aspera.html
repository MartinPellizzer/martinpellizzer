Bulbostylis aspera

Bulbostylis aspera, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Bulbostylis aspera is often found in swampy areas and is an important component of aquatic plant communities.
