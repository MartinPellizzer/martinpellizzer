Begonia convallariodora

Begonia convallariodora, commonly known as the Christmas Begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom during the winter months, giving it its common name.
