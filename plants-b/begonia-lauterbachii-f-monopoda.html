Begonia lauterbachii f. monopoda

Begonia lauterbachii f. monopoda, commonly known as the single-stem begonia, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its solitary stem structure.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant green coloration. Its flowers, though less showy than its foliage, are typically small and pink or red in hue.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
