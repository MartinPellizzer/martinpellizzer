Boronia zeteticorum

Boronia zeteticorum, commonly known as the Zetetic Boronia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its flowers are often visited by native bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and is part of the Rutaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.
