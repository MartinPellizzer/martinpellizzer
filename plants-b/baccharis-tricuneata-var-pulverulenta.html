Baccharis tricuneata var. pulverulenta

Baccharis tricuneata var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, powdery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
