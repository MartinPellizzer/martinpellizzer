Bifrenaria

Bifrenaria, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in saline or coastal environments, where they thrive in harsh, mineral-rich soils. Bifrenaria species are characterized by their succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers that often appear in shades of red or pink. The genus includes several species, such as Bifrenaria hirta, which is native to South America.

These plants play an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
