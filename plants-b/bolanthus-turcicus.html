Bolanthus turcicus

Bolanthus turcicus, commonly known as Turkish thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and hillsides. Bolanthus turcicus has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above them.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
