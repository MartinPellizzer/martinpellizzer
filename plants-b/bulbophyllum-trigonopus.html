Bulbophyllum trigonopus

Bulbophyllum trigonopus, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, triangular-shaped pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
