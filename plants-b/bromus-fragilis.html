Bromus fragilis

Bromus fragilis, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, meadows, and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, two-rowed flowers.

Bromus fragilis is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural settings.
