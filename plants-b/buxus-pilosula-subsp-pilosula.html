Buxus pilosula subsp. pilosula

Buxus pilosula subsp.

pilosula, commonly known as the hairy boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for hedges and topiary. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is relatively drought tolerant once established.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
