Berberis chochoco

Berberis chochoco, commonly known as the Chocó barberry, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It features narrow, spiny leaves and produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the high rainfall and misty conditions of the Chocó region. Its berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in tropical climates.
