Boebera incana

Boebera incana, commonly known as the woolly boebera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Boebera incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
