Behuria souza-limae var. pallescens

Behuria souza-limae var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-bean tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in tropical ecosystems. Its pale green to yellowish leaves and small, clustered flowers make it visually distinct within its habitat.

This variant is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
