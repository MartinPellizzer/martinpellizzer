Berberis aitchisonii

Berberis aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's barberry, is a shrub native to the western Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, orange, or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries.
