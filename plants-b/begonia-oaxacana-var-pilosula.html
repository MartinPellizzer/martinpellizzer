Begonia oaxacana var. pilosula

Begonia oaxacana var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
