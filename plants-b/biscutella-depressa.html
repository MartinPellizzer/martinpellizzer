Biscutella depressa

Biscutella depressa, commonly known as the creeping biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Biscutella depressa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
