Berberis humbertiana

Berberis humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for birds and some mammals.
