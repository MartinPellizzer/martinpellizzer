Bromelia exudans

Bromelia exudans, commonly known as the red bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding vibrant color to its foliage. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention.

Bromelia exudans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
