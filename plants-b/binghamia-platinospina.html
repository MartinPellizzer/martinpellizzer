Binghamia platinospina

Binghamia platinospina, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.

Binghamia platinospina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australian biodiversity.
