Byrsonima cydoniifolia var. cearensis

Byrsonima cydoniifolia var. cearensis, commonly known as the cearense wax apple, is a shrub or small tree native to the northeastern regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local culinary practices.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in tropical climates.
