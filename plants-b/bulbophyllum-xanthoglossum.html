Bulbophyllum xanthoglossum

Bulbophyllum xanthoglossum, commonly known as the golden-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow lips and greenish-white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, often arranged in a dense inflorescence.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
