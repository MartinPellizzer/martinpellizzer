Barringtonia alba

Barringtonia alba, commonly known as the white cedar or candle bush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, pendulous white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a crown-like structure, which contains a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found near mangrove forests and coastal areas.

Barringtonia alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
