Butomus umbellatus f. terrestris

Butomus umbellatus f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense, compact tufts near the ground.

This subspecies is adapted to saturated soils and plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
