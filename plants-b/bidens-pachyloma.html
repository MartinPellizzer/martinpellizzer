Bidens pachyloma

Bidens pachyloma, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is an annual herb that grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs.

Bidens pachyloma is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
