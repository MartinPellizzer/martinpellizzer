Brachycorythis baumii

Brachycorythis baumii, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern. The plant features a short, unbranched trunk and a dense crown of long, feather-like leaves. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, including drought resistance and efficient nutrient uptake.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study.
