Bulbophyllum geminiflorum

Bulbophyllum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces multiple flower spikes, each bearing several small, fragrant blossoms. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
