Buddleja adenantha

Buddleja adenantha, commonly known as the African butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features showy, clustered spikes of fragrant, pale purple to pink flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja adenantha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
