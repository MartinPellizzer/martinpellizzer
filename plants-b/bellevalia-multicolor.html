Bellevalia multicolor

Bellevalia multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Bellevalia multicolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
