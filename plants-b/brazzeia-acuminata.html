Brazzeia acuminata

Brazzeia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
