Boottia aschersoniana

Boottia aschersoniana, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a low, spreading growth habit, featuring fleshy, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features reflect its adaptation to extreme conditions, with thick cuticles and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
