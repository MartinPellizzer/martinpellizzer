Bursera subsessiliformis

Bursera subsessiliformis, commonly known as the Mexican bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs, and it produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits.

It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils, making it a common species in arid and semi-arid regions.
