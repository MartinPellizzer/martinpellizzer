Bulbostylis nudiuscula

Bulbostylis nudiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that contribute to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
