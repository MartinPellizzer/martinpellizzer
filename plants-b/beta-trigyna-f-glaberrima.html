Beta trigyna f. glaberrima

Beta trigyna f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf beta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its glabrous, or smooth, foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
