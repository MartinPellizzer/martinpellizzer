Boea reticulata

Boea reticulata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Boea reticulata is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for neglect.
