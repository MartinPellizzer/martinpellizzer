Benzoin blumeanum

Benzoin blumeanum, commonly known as the resinous tree or benzoin tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in various traditional and commercial applications. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
