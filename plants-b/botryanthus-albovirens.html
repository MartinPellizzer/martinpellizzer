Botryanthus albovirens

Botryanthus albovirens, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale green to white flowers with intricate veining. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Botryanthus albovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
