Barkeria obovata

Barkeria obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed Barkeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Barkeria obovata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
