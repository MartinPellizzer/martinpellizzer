Biscutella montana var. longifolia

Biscutella montana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are yellowish-green and are clustered in racemes, producing small, elongated pods that contain the seeds.

It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
