Bletia repanda

Bletia repanda, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the forest understory. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking pink to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Bletia repanda is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical classification places it within the Orchidaceae family, highlighting its evolutionary relationship to other orchids.
