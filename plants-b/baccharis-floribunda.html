Baccharis floribunda

Baccharis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry regions.

Baccharis floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
