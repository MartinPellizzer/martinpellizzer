Bursera copallifera

Bursera copallifera, commonly known as Mexican frankincense or copal tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by resinous fruits containing winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
