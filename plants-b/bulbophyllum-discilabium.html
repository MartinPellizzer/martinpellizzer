Bulbophyllum discilabium

Bulbophyllum discilabium, commonly known as the disc-like lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, disc-shaped lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

It requires specific conditions such as high humidity and indirect light to thrive.
