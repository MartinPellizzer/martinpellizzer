Begonia robusta var. hirsutior

Begonia robusta var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a tropical plant characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

It originates from the humid forests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture, and the plant produces striking flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
