Berberis macrosepala

Berberis macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
