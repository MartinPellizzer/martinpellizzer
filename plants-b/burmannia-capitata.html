Burmannia capitata

Burmannia capitata, commonly known as the golden burmannia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, umbrella-like clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Burmannia capitata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
