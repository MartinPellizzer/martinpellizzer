Banksia penicillata

Banksia penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

Banksia penicillata is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
