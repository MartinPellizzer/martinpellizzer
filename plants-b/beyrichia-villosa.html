Beyrichia villosa

Beyrichia villosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Beyrichia villosa is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
