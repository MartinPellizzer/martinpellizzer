Bulbophyllum fissibrachium

Bulbophyllum fissibrachium, commonly known as the fissured branch orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
