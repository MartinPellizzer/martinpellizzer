Berberis gagnepainii var. omeiensis

Berberis gagnepainii var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand mild frosts, enhancing its adaptability in various climates.
