Bolax floccipes

Bolax floccipes, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bolax floccipes has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
