Begonia xanthina var. lazuli

Begonia xanthina var. lazuli, commonly known as the lazuli begonia, is a hybrid plant originating from the cross between Begonia xanthina and Begonia semperflorens.

It is characterized by its striking, dark blue to purple foliage, which is one of its most distinctive botanical features. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom throughout the growing season.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
