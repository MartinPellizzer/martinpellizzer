Brachycladium cardiocheilum

Brachycladium cardiocheilum, commonly known as the heart-lipped grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
