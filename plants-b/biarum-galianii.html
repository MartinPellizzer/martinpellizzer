Biarum galianii

Biarum galianii, commonly known as the Galiani bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and hairy.

Biarum galianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
