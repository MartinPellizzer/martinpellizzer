Blitum virgatum subsp. montanum

Blitum virgatum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain bladderpod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, often appearing in early spring.

The species is characterized by its distinctive bladder-like seed pods that split open when mature.
