Brodiaea capitata var. insularis

Brodiaea capitata var. insularis, commonly known as the Island Brodiaea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or rocky habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its compact size and ornamental value in native gardens.
