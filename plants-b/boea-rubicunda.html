Boea rubicunda

Boea rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered ivy, is a climbing perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Boea rubicunda is also valued for its ability to climb and cover walls or trellises effectively.
