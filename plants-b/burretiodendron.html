Burretiodendron

Burretiodendron, commonly known as the cycad-like plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Burretiodendraceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. Burretiodendron species are often found in lowland rainforests, growing in shaded, humid environments.

Due to their rarity and specific habitat requirements, these plants are of significant interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
