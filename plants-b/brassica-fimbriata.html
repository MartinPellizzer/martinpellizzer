Brassica fimbriata

Brassica fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive fringed petals and is part of the mustard family, Brassicaceae. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.

Brassica fimbriata is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
