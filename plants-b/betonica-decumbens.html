Betonica decumbens

Betonica decumbens, commonly known as creeping betony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of succulent, hairy leaves that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Betonica decumbens is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
