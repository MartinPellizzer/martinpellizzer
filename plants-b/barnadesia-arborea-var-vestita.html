Barnadesia arborea var. vestita

Barnadesia arborea var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark that gives it its common name, and its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are often white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
