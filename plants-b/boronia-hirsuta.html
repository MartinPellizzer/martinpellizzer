Boronia hirsuta

Boronia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, oval fruits that are edible.
