Bihai aurantiaca

Bihai aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden dragon tree, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Bihai aurantiaca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and unique foliage.
