Begonia josephi var. macrocarpa

Begonia josephi var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among horticulturists for its dramatic foliage and unique floral display.
