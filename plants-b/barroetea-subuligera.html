Barroetea subuligera

Barroetea subuligera, commonly known as the slender leaf barroetea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often found in rocky or gravelly terrains.
