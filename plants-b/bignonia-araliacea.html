Bignonia araliacea

Bignonia araliacea, commonly known as the crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often arranged in a distinctive, fern-like pattern. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

Bignonia araliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
