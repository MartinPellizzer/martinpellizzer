Bunium tenerum

Bunium tenerum, commonly known as the slender fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are small and have a mild, anise-like scent.

Bunium tenerum is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
