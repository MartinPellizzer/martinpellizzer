Benthamina alyxifolia

Benthamina alyxifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf benthamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Benthamina alyxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
