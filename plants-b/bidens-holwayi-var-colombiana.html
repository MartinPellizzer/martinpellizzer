Bidens holwayi var. colombiana

Bidens holwayi var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
