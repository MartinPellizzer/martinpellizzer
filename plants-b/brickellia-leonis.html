Brickellia leonis

Brickellia leonis, commonly known as the golden brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
