Brickellia ambigens

Brickellia ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Brickellia ambigens is often found in disturbed soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
