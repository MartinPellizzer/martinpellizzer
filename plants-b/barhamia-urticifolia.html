Barhamia urticifolia

Barhamia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species with stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, irritating trichomes. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically flowering from late spring to early autumn.

Barhamia urticifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
