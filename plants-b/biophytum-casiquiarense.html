Biophytum casiquiarense

Biophytum casiquiarense, commonly known as the "Cape Flats dandelion," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
