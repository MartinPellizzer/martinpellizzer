Berberis amurensis f. latifolia

Berberis amurensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the Amur barberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

Its berries also contain berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
