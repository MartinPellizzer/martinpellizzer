Blumea membranacea var. gardneri

Blumea membranacea var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
