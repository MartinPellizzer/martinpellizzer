Bouteloua barbata var. major

Bouteloua barbata var. major, commonly known as black grama, is a warm-season grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, wiry foliage and distinctive, bristly seed heads. This grass thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant landscapes.
