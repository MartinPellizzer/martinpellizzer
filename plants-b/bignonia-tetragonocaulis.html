Bignonia tetragonocaulis

Bignonia tetragonocaulis, commonly known as the four-angle trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have four angular sides, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth, it is often used as a decorative vine in gardens and landscapes.
