Begonia lepidota

Begonia lepidota, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to purple hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia lepidota is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and compact growth habit.
