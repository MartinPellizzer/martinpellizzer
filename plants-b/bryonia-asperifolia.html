Bryonia asperifolia

Bryonia asperifolia, commonly known as the rough bryony, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, warty texture, and its flowers are yellow with five lobes, typical of the family. The fruit is a spiny, orange-yellow berry that matures in late summer.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
