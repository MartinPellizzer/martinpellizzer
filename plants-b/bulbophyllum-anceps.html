Bulbophyllum anceps

Bulbophyllum anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizome and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Bulbophyllum anceps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
