Barleria vix-dentata

Barleria vix-dentata, commonly known as the toothed barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Barleria vix-dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
