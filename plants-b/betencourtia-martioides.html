Betencourtia martioides

Betencourtia martioides, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is not as widely cultivated for this purpose as other date palms. Betencourtia martioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience in coastal environments.

It plays a role in the local ecosystems of the Canary Islands and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological contributions.
