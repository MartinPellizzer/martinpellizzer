Bambusa simonii var. variegata

Bambusa simonii var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features elegant, slender culms with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white on its leaves, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 6 meters, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round color, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This bamboo is prized for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
