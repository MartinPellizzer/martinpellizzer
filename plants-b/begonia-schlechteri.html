Begonia schlechteri

Begonia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they often have a glossy texture. The flowers are usually red or pink, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base, which is a key feature for attracting pollinators.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
