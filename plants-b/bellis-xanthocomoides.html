Bellis xanthocomoides

Bellis xanthocomoides, commonly known as the yellow-centred daisy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive yellow center surrounded by white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Europe. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small flowers in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
