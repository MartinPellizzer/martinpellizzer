Bourreria microphylla

Bourreria microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
