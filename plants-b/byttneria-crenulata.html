Byttneria crenulata

Byttneria crenulata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which resembles a star, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a pear. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
