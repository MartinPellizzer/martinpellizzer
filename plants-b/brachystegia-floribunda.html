Brachystegia floribunda

Brachystegia floribunda, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It is often found in miombo woodlands and can grow up to 15 meters tall.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.
