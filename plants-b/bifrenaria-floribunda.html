Bifrenaria floribunda

Bifrenaria floribunda, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in marshy environments.

Bifrenaria floribunda is known for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
