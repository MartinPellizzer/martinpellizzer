Bursera graveolens var. villosula

Bursera graveolens var. villosula, commonly known as the woolly bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by spherical, resinous fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Its aromatic resins have been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
