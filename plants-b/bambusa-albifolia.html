Bambusa albifolia

Bambusa albifolia, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its striking white-tinged leaves and slender, often hollow stems. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with a fast growth rate under suitable conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, sweet scent when crushed.

Bambusa albifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
