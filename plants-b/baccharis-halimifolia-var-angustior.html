Baccharis halimifolia var. angustior

Baccharis halimifolia var. angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf sandbar willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
