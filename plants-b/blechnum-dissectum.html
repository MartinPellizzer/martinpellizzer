Blechnum dissectum

Blechnum dissectum, commonly known as the fragrant fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually green and have a faintly aromatic scent when crushed.

Blechnum dissectum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
