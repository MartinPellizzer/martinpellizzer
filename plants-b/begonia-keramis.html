Begonia keramis

Begonia keramis, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
