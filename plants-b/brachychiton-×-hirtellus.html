Brachychiton × hirtellus

Brachychiton × hirtellus, commonly known as the horticultural hybrid of the sycamore fig and the hirsute sycamore fig, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Brachychiton australis* and *Brachychiton hirtellus*.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is native to Australia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
