Begonia fischeri var. moritziana

Begonia fischeri var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
