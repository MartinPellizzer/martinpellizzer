Bromus erectus var. purpurascens

Bromus erectus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a reddish or purplish tint, especially along the nodes. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
