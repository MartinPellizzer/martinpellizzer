Begonia poculifera

Begonia poculifera, commonly known as the cup-leaved begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia poculifera is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
