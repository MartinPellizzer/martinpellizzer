Brickellia villarrealii

Brickellia villarrealii, commonly known as the desert brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Brickellia villarrealii is well-suited to xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
