Byrsopteris rigidissima

Byrsopteris rigidissima, commonly known as the rigid fern, is a prehistoric plant that lived during the late Paleozoic era.

It is characterized by its robust, strap-like fronds that were likely adapted for survival in swampy, humid environments. The plant's structure suggests it was a vascular fern, capable of transporting water and nutrients efficiently. Its fossils are often found in coal-bearing rock layers, indicating its prevalence in ancient forests.

The rigid fern provides valuable insights into the evolution of early plant life and the development of modern ecosystems.
