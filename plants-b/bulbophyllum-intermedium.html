Bulbophyllum intermedium

Bulbophyllum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum intermedium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique growth form.
