Betula × vologdensis

Betula × vologdensis, commonly known as the Vologda birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white birch (Betula pendula) and the silver birch (Betula pendula var. jacquemontii).

It is characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Betula × vologdensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
