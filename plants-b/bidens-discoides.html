Bidens discoides

Bidens discoides, commonly known as common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pink ray florets.

Bidens discoides is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
