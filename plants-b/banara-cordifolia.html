Banara cordifolia

Banara cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Banara cordifolia is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
