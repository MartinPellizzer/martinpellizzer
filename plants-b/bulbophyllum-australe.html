Bulbophyllum australe

Bulbophyllum australe, commonly known as the Australian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually greenish-yellow or white, with intricate patterns. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients, which is an adaptation to its often humid and nutrient-poor habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
