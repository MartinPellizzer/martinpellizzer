Bignonia meyeniana

Bignonia meyeniana, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
