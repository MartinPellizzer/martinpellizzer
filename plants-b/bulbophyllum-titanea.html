Bulbophyllum titanea

Bulbophyllum titanea, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a golden-orange lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Bulbophyllum titanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
