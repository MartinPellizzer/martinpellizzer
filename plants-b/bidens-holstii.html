Bidens holstii

Bidens holstii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Bidens holstii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
