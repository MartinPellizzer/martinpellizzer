Berberis pachyacantha subsp. zabeliana

Berberis pachyacantha subsp.

zabeliana, commonly known as the Zabel's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance.
