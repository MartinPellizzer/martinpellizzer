Berberis circumserrata var. occidentalior

Berberis circumserrata var. occidentalior, commonly known as the western barbed wire bush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
