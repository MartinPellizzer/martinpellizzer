Berberis thunbergii var. erecta

Berberis thunbergii var. erecta, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features upright, spiny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in various climates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.
