Bidens cuneata

Bidens cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in heads and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Bidens cuneata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
