Berberis anhweiensis

Berberis anhweiensis, commonly known as the Anhwei barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
