Bismalva fastigiata

Bismalva fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces showy pink or purple flowers in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a stout, branching stem and capsule fruits that contain small, round seeds.

Bismalva fastigiata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
