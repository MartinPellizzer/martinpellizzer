Bulbine meiringii

Bulbine meiringii, commonly known as the Meiring's bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rhizomatous stems and rosette-forming leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Bulbine meiringii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
