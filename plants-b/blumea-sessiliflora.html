Blumea sessiliflora

Blumea sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
