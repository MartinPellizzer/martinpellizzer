Begonia mariaensis

Begonia mariaensis, commonly known as the Mariaensis begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves and showy bracts. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its foliage is usually velvety and comes in shades of green, red, and purple, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Begonia mariaensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
