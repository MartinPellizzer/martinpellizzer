Bertolonia punctata

Bertolonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted bertolonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green markings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Bertolonia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
