Baccharis graveolens

Baccharis graveolens, commonly known as sweet baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis graveolens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
