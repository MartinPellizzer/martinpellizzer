Bulbocodium campanulatum

Bulbocodium campanulatum, commonly known as the bluebell of the sea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bulbocodium campanulatum is often found in dune formations and is an important component of coastal plant communities.
