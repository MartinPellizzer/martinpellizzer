Bernardia albida

Bernardia albida, commonly known as the white-flowered bernardia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Bernardia albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
