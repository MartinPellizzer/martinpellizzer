Barleria pauciflora

Barleria pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

Barleria pauciflora is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
