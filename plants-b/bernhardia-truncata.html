Bernhardia truncata

Bernhardia truncata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
