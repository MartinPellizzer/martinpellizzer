Beta brasiliensis

Beta brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beet, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences that resemble those of other beta species.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
