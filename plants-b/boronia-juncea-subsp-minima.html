Boronia juncea subsp. minima

Boronia juncea subsp.

minima, commonly known as the small-flowered boronia, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
