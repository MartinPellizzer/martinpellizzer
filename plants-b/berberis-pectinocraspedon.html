Berberis pectinocraspedon

Berberis pectinocraspedon, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds in high quantities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy fruit, though care must be taken due to its potential toxicity.
