Brachiaria villosa var. glaberrima

Brachiaria villosa var. glaberrima, commonly known as hairy brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both bisexual and staminate flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachiaria villosa var. glaberrima is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
