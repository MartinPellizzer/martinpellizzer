Bauhinia coulteri f. albiflora

Bauhinia coulteri f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered burr tree, is a variant of the burr tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its unique, pod-like fruit that resembles a burr. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
