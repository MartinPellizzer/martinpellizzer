Biscutella auriculata var. candollei

Biscutella auriculata var. candollei, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
