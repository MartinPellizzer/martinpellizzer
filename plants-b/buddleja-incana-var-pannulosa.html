Buddleja incana var. pannulosa

Buddleja incana var. pannulosa, commonly known as the woolly bluemouth, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
