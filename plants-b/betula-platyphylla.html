Betula platyphylla

Betula platyphylla, commonly known as the Manchurian birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and has oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Betula platyphylla is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
