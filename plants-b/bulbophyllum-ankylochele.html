Bulbophyllum ankylochele

Bulbophyllum ankylochele, commonly known as the thick-veined orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pseudo-bulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum ankylochele is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
