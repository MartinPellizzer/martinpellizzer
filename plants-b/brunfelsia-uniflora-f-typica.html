Brunfelsia uniflora f. typica

Brunfelsia uniflora f. typica, commonly known as the single-flower Brunfelsia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.

The f. typica form is distinguished by its more uniform and compact growth compared to other forms of Brunfelsia uniflora.
