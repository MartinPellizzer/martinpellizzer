Biscutella montenegrina

Biscutella montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro biscuit plant, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Biscutella montenegrina is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
