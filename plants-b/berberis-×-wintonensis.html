Berberis × wintonensis

Berberis × wintonensis, commonly known as the Winton barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries, which attract birds and add visual interest to gardens.
