Buchnera asperata

Buchnera asperata, commonly known as the roughleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Buchnera asperata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes but is valued in ecological gardens for supporting pollinators.
