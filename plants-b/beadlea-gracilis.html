Beadlea gracilis

Beadlea gracilis, commonly known as the slender beadleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish or white in color, and appear in clusters.

Beadlea gracilis is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
