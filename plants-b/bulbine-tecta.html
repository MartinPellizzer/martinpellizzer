Bulbine tecta

Bulbine tecta, commonly known as the rock restio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Bulbine tecta is drought-tolerant and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
