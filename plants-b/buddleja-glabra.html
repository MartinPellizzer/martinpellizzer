Buddleja glabra

Buddleja glabra, commonly known as the silver bush or woolly buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, velvety bark and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping. Its flowers attract a wide range of pollinators, including butterflies and bees, contributing to ecological diversity.

Buddleja glabra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
