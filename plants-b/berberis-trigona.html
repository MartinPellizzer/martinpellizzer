Berberis trigona

Berberis trigona, commonly known as the thorned barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Berberis trigona is characterized by its sharp thorns and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.
