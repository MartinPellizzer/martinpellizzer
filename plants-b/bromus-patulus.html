Bromus patulus

Bromus patulus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike, which is usually loose and branched.

Bromus patulus is known for its adaptability and is often found in environments with moderate moisture and well-drained soils.
