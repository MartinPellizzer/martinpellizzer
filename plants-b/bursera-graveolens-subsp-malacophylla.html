Bursera graveolens subsp. malacophylla

Bursera graveolens subsp.

malacophylla, commonly known as the Mexican balsam or copal tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and leathery, elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, resinous fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
