Bauhinia inermis

Bauhinia inermis, commonly known as the velvetwood or Brazilian wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in spring. Bauhinia inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
