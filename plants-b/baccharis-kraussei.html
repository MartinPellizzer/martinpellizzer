Baccharis kraussei

Baccharis kraussei, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers appear in clusters.

Baccharis kraussei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
