Banara quinquenervis

Banara quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined banara, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing one or two seeds. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
