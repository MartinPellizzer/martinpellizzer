Breynia bacciformis

Breynia bacciformis, commonly known as the red-stemmed breynia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some animals. Breynia bacciformis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
