Boucerosia umbellata

Boucerosia umbellata, commonly known as the white umbellate corymbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, umbellate inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Boucerosia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers.
