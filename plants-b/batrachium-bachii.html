Batrachium bachii

Batrachium bachii, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent leaves that resemble the shape of a frog, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a delicate, moth-like appearance, which bloom in the summer months. Batrachium bachii is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its habitat specificity and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
