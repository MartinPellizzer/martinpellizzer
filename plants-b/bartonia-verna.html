Bartonia verna

Bartonia verna, commonly known as the spring bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Bartonia verna is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
