Berberis vulgaris var. aspera

Berberis vulgaris var. aspera, commonly known as the rough barberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
