Baccharis nipensis

Baccharis nipensis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Baccharis nipensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
