Blepharis hirtinervia

Blepharis hirtinervia, commonly known as the hairy-veined blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with hairy veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
