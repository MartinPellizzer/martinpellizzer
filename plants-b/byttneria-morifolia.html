Byttneria morifolia

Byttneria morifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
