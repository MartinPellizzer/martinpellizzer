Baccharis padifolia

Baccharis padifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis padifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
