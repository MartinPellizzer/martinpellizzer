Bignonia candicans

Bignonia candicans, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a climbing vine or shrub.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
