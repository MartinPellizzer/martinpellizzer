Byrsonima carraoana

Byrsonima carraoana, commonly known as the surinam cherry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The botanical features of Byrsonima carraoana make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native regions.
