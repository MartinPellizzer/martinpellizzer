Basella hookeriana

Basella hookeriana, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often used in cooking for their mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
