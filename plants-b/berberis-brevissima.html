Berberis brevissima

Berberis brevissima, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Berberis brevissima is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
