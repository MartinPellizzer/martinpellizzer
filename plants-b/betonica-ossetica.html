Betonica ossetica

Betonica ossetica, commonly known as Ossetian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the summer months. Betonica ossetica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
