Beschorneria tonelii

Beschorneria tonelii, commonly known as the Mexican bristle fern, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or pale green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
