Bromus heterophyllus

Bromus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable brome, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has distinctive leaf morphology, with variable leaf shapes and sizes depending on environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
