Berberis pangharengensis

Berberis pangharengensis, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Berberis pangharengensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine forests and rocky slopes.
