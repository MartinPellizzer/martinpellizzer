Bonafousia albiflora

Bonafousia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bonafousia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
