Blumea lacera var. subdivaricata

Blumea lacera var. subdivaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces oval, woody fruits.
