Betencourtia scarlatina

Betencourtia scarlatina, commonly known as the scarlet betencourtia, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptation to the island's arid climate.

Betencourtia scarlatina is also noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
