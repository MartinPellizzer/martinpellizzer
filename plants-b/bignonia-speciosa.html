Bignonia speciosa

Bignonia speciosa, commonly known as crossvine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with thorns and can grow up to 30 feet long, making it an effective climber. Its flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

Bignonia speciosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
