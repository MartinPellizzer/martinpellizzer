Blitum rubrum var. crassifolium

Blitum rubrum var. crassifolium, commonly known as broadleaf red paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped, basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
