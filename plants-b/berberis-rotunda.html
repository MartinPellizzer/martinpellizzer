Berberis rotunda

Berberis rotunda, commonly known as the globe barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
