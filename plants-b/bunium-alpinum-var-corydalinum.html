Bunium alpinum var. corydalinum

Bunium alpinum var. corydalinum, commonly known as alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, distinctive odor when crushed.

The flowers, which are white to greenish in color, attract pollinators and contribute to the plant's ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
