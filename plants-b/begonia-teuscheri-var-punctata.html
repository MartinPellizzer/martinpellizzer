Begonia teuscheri var. punctata

Begonia teuscheri var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with distinctive red and green spots. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer.

This plant thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
