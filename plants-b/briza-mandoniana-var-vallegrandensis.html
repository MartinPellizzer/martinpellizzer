Briza mandoniana var. vallegrandensis

Briza mandoniana var. vallegrandensis, commonly known as the valley grandens sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, triangular spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for wetland restoration and water garden environments.
