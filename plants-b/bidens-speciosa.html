Bidens speciosa

Bidens speciosa, commonly known as the showy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Bidens speciosa is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.
