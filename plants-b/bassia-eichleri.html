Bassia eichleri

Bassia eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are greenish-white and appear in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.
