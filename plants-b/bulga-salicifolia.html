Bulga salicifolia

Bulga salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulga, is a perennial herb native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters, typically appearing in spring.

Bulga salicifolia is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to a range of environmental conditions.
