Bulbophyllum phitamii

Bulbophyllum phitamii, commonly known as the Phitamii orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
