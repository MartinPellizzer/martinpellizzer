Boronia stricta

Boronia stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Boronia stricta is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
