Bromus rubens var. glabellus

Bromus rubens var. glabellus, commonly known as the smooth brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous (smooth) leaf blades. The plant produces spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color, with two to three florets per spikelet. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its use in forage and soil stabilization due to its robust growth and adaptability.
