Baccharis scolopendra

Baccharis scolopendra, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Baccharis scolopendra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
