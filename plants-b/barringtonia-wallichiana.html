Barringtonia wallichiana

Barringtonia wallichiana, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal white, fleshy arils surrounding the seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
