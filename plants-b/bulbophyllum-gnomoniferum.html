Bulbophyllum gnomoniferum

Bulbophyllum gnomoniferum, commonly known as the golden gnomon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is often found in forest understories.
