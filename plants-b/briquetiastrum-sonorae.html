Briquetiastrum sonorae

Briquetiastrum sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran bristlebush, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.

Briquetiastrum sonorae is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy areas.
