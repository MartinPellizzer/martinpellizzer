Biscutella raphanifolia var. algeriensis

Biscutella raphanifolia var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
