Balantium magnificum

Balantium magnificum, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking herbaceous species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant shade of deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a luminous contrast to its foliage. Its roots are known to contain alkaloids with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest to botanists and pharmacologists.

Due to habitat loss, it is now considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
