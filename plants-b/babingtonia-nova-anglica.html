Babingtonia nova-anglica

Babingtonia nova-anglica, commonly known as the New England babingtonia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-green appearance.

Babingtonia nova-anglica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
