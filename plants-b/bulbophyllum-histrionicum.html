Bulbophyllum histrionicum

Bulbophyllum histrionicum, commonly known as the Showy Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that often display a range of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its flowers are typically borne on long, branching stems and are known for their intricate, often fragrant structure.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained potting mix.
