Bupleurum ranunculoides var. telonense

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. telonense, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
