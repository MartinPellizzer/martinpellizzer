Bursera rupicola

Bursera rupicola, commonly known as the rock bursera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-white or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Bursera rupicola thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its aromatic resins have been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
