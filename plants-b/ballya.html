Ballya

Ballya, commonly known as the "buttercup plant," is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ballya is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to both humans and animals if ingested.
