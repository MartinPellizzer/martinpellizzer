Berberis sabulicola

Berberis sabulicola, commonly known as the sandbar barberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features woody stems with thorned branches and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Berberis sabulicola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
