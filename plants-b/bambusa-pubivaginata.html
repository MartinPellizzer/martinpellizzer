Bambusa pubivaginata

Bambusa pubivaginata, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, smooth, and often reddish-brown culms. The plant features dense, overlapping leaf sheaths that give it a velvety appearance, hence its common name. Bambusa pubivaginata is a clumping bamboo that grows in clusters, forming a compact and sturdy structure.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
