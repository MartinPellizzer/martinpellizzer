Berberis empetrifolia var. typica

Berberis empetrifolia var. typica, commonly known as the red-twigged barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, thorny stems that are typically reddish in color, providing striking visual interest throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
