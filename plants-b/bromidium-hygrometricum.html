Bromidium hygrometricum

Bromidium hygrometricum, commonly known as the humidity bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. The plant produces striking, bell-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bromidium hygrometricum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique ecological features.
