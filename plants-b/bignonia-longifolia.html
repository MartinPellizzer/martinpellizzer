Bignonia longifolia

Bignonia longifolia, commonly known as the yellow cross vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
