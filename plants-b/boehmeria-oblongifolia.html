Boehmeria oblongifolia

Boehmeria oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hemp or Chinese nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Boehmeria oblongifolia is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used historically for making textiles and paper.
