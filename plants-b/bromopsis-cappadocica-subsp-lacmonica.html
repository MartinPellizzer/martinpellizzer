Bromopsis cappadocica subsp. lacmonica

Bromopsis cappadocica subsp.

lacmonica, commonly known as the Cappadocian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It is known for its ornamental value, with its fine, silver-gray leaves and light green flowering spikes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
