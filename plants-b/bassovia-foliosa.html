Bassovia foliosa

Bassovia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bassovia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
