Boscia fadeniorum

Boscia fadeniorum, commonly known as the red boschia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Boscia fadeniorum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals and birds.
