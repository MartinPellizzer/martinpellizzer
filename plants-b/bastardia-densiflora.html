Bastardia densiflora

Bastardia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Bastardia densiflora is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
