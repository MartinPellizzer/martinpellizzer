Bulbophyllum dyphoniae

Bulbophyllum dyphoniae, commonly known as the Dyphonia orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant hues make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
