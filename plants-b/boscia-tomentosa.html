Boscia tomentosa

Boscia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly boschia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Boscia tomentosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
