Bruschia macrocarpa

Bruschia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bruschia, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a splash of color to its arid habitat. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Bruschia macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky or sandy soils.
