Blumea chinensis var. riparia

Blumea chinensis var. riparia, commonly known as the river blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas near rivers and streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
