Bucquetia vernicosa

Bucquetia vernicosa, commonly known as the waxplant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
