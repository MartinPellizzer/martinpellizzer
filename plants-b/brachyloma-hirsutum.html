Brachyloma hirsutum

Brachyloma hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Brachyloma hirsutum is often found in open woodlands and on rocky outcrops, where it forms part of the local flora.
