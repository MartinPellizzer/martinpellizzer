Bignonia arvensis

Bignonia arvensis, commonly known as yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in open woods and along roadsides. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia arvensis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
