Boronia nana

Boronia nana, commonly known as the dwarf boronia, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray.

Boronia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
