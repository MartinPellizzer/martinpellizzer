Brassica melanosinapis

Brassica melanosinapis, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing round, black seeds that are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
