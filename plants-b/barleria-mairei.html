Barleria mairei

Barleria mairei, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Barleria mairei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
