Berberis deinacantha

Berberis deinacantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries.
