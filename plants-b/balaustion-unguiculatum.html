Balaustion unguiculatum

Balaustion unguiculatum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Balaustion unguiculatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
