Beta vulgaris var. morina

Beta vulgaris var. morina, commonly known as the莙菜 or leaf beet, is a cultivated variant of the beet plant.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and often curly leaves that are typically harvested before the root develops. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its nutrient-rich leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are commonly used in salads, soups, and as a cooked vegetable, offering a slightly earthy flavor.
