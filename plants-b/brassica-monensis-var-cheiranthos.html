Brassica monensis var. cheiranthos

Brassica monensis var. cheiranthos, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or Indian mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are siliques, containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its edible seeds and leaves.

This variety is native to Asia and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
