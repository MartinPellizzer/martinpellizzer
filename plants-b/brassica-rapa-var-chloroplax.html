Brassica rapa var. chloroplax

Brassica rapa var. chloroplax, commonly known as the curly leaf mustard, is a variant of the common mustard species.

It is characterized by its dense, curled leaves that form a compact rosette, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small yellow flowers in the spring. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular crop in some regions.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.
