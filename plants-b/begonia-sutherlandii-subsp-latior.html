Begonia sutherlandii subsp. latior

Begonia sutherlandii subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
