Blechnum asperum

Blechnum asperum, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, serrated fronds that give it a rugged appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive texture and coloration that aids in identification.

Blechnum asperum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
