Boehmeria sieboldiana f. hirtella

Boehmeria sieboldiana f. hirtella, commonly known as the velvetleaf nettle, is a variant of the Boehmeria sieboldiana species.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant is native to regions in East Asia, including Japan and China. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber due to their strong, fibrous structure.
