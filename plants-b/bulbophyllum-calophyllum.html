Bulbophyllum calophyllum

Bulbophyllum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and features fleshy, strap-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
