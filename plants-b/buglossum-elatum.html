Buglossum elatum

Buglossum elatum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Buglossum elatum is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
