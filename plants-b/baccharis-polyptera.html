Baccharis polyptera

Baccharis polyptera, commonly known as the silver leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis polyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
