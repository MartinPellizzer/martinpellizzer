Boronia purdieana subsp. purdieana

Boronia purdieana subsp.

purdieana, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a species of conservation interest in some regions.
