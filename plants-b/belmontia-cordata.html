Belmontia cordata

Belmontia cordata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its striking pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Belmontia cordata thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
