Biscutella patulipes

Biscutella patulipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Biscutella patulipes is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
