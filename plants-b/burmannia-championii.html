Burmannia championii

Burmannia championii, commonly known as the red lady's slipper, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble a slipper, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is often found in the understory of dense vegetation. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Burmannia championii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
