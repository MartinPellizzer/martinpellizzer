Bambusa vulgaris var. striata

Bambusa vulgaris var. striata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms with distinct black stripes running along the length of the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental bamboo is popular in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
