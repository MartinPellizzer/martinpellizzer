Bauhinia siqueirae

Bauhinia siqueirae, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, though it requires careful management due to its aggressive growth habit.
