Begonia sandalifolia

Begonia sandalifolia, commonly known as the sandalwood begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often veined with a lighter color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
