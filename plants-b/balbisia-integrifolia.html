Balbisia integrifolia

Balbisia integrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its violet-blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, with serrated edges.

Balbisia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
