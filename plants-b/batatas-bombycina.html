Batatas bombycina

Batatas bombycina, commonly known as the silky batata, is a tropical tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical tubers with a smooth, silky texture. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them a valued crop in certain regions.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
