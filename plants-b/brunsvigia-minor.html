Brunsvigia minor

Brunsvigia minor, commonly known as the rainstorm lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces showy, red or orange flowers that resemble fireworks, blooming after rainfall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. It is also known for its ability to survive drought periods by going dormant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
