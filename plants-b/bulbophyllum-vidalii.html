Bulbophyllum vidalii

Bulbophyllum vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

Bulbophyllum vidalii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration.
