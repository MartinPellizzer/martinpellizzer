Brassica rapa subsp. narinosa

Brassica rapa subsp.

narinosa, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or napa cabbage, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its thick, leafy stems and large, fleshy leaves that form a compact head.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. Its leaves are typically dark green and can be used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.
