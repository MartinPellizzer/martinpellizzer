Briza virens

Briza virens, commonly known as green oat or slender oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, greenish-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green coloration.

Briza virens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
