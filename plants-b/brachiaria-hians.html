Brachiaria hians

Brachiaria hians, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas. Brachiaria hians is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
