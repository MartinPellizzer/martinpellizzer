Brassica sinapistrum var. schkuhriana

Brassica sinapistrum var. schkuhriana, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, slightly hairy leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by round, pale yellow or green siliques containing numerous seeds. The plant is native to the Himalayan regions of India and is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of mustard oil. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in temperate zones.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and traditional agricultural practices.
