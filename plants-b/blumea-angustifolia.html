Blumea angustifolia

Blumea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds.
