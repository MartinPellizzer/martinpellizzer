Brassica patens

Brassica patens, commonly known as the Brazilian mustang grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces spherical seed pods that split open when mature.

Brassica patens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
