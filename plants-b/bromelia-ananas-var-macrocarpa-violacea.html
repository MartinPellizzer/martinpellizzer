Bromelia ananas var. macrocarpa-violacea

Bromelia ananas var. macrocarpa-violacea, commonly known as the purple pineapple bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces an inflorescence with vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy, glossy coating, which helps in water retention.

This species is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and long-lasting blooms.
