Buxus sempervirens var. fruticosa

Buxus sempervirens var. fruticosa, commonly known as the dense boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is highly prized for its ability to be shaped into intricate forms. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its slow growth and durability, it is often used in topiary and ornamental landscaping.
