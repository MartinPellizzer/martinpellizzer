Buxus variegata

Buxus variegata, commonly known as the variegated boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow variegation. The leaves are small, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary.

This plant is also valued for its ability to withstand pruning, allowing for intricate shaping and maintenance in garden landscapes.
