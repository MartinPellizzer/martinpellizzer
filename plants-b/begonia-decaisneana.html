Begonia decaisneana

Begonia decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne's begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
