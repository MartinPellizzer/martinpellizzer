Biscutella laevigata subsp. raffaelliana

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

raffaelliana, commonly known as the Raffaello's biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open, sunny habitats.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
