Blyxa senegalensis

Blyxa senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne on spike-like inflorescences.

Blyxa senegalensis is valued for its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
