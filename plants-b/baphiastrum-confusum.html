Baphiastrum confusum

Baphiastrum confusum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Baphiastrum confusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
