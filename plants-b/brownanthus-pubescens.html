Brownanthus pubescens

Brownanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy brownanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Brownanthus pubescens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
