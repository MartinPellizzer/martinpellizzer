Bouteloua piurensis

Bouteloua piurensis, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with delicate, blue-gray florets. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.

Bouteloua piurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water conditions, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
