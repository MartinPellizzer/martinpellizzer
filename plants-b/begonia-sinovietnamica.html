Begonia sinovietnamica

Begonia sinovietnamica, commonly known as the Vietnamese begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and southern China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically features heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
