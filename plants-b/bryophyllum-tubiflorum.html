Bryophyllum tubiflorum

Bryophyllum tubiflorum, commonly known as the tuberous bryophyllum, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and thick, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the growing season. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bryophyllum tubiflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
