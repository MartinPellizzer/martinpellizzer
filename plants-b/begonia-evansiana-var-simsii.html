Begonia evansiana var. simsii

Begonia evansiana var. simsii, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
