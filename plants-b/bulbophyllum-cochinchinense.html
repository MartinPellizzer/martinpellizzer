Bulbophyllum cochinchinense

Bulbophyllum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Orchid of the South, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth on trees or rocks.

The flowers typically bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
