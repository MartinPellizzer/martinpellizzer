Begonia magnicarpa

Begonia magnicarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
