Bupleurum papillosum

Bupleurum papillosum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Bupleurum papillosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
