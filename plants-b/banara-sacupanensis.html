Banara sacupanensis

Banara sacupanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Banara sacupanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
