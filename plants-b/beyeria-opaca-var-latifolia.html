Beyeria opaca var. latifolia

Beyeria opaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved beyeria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
