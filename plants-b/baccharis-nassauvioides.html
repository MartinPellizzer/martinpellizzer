Baccharis nassauvioides

Baccharis nassauvioides, commonly known as the Nassauvian baccharis, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis nassauvioides plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
