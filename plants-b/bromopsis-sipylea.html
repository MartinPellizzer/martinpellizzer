Bromopsis sipylea

Bromopsis sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescence that appears in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Bromopsis sipylea is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and botanical collections.
