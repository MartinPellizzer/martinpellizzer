Bromus uruguayensis var. genuinus

Bromus uruguayensis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a dense panicle, with small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
