Begonia imitans

Begonia imitans, commonly known as the mimic begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that resemble the leaves of other begonia species, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.

Begonia imitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper watering and light conditions.
