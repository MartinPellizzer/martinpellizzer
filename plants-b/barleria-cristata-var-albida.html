Barleria cristata var. albida

Barleria cristata var. albida, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fluffy, white flower heads that resemble a crown, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
