Begonia parvula

Begonia parvula, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for container gardening or as a ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Begonia parvula is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
