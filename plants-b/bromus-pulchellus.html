Bromus pulchellus

Bromus pulchellus, commonly known as the beautiful brome, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into seed heads.

Bromus pulchellus is often used in landscaping for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
