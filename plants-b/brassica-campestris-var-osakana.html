Brassica campestris var. osakana

Brassica campestris var. osakana, commonly known as Osaka mustard, is a variety of the mustard family native to Japan.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are small, dry pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in Japan for its unique appearance.
