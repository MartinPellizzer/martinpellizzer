Berberis hemsleyi

Berberis hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Berberis hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
