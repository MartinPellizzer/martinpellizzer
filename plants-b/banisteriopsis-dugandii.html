Banisteriopsis dugandii

Banisteriopsis dugandii, commonly known as the "Bourbon tree" or "Cocculus tree," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, inflated, spiny fruits.

The plant is of interest in botany due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
