Bumelia lanuginosa var. texana

Bumelia lanuginosa var. texana, commonly known as the Texas woollybumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
