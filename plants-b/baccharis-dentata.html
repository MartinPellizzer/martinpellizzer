Baccharis dentata

Baccharis dentata, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, toothed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
