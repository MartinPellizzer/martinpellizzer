Blepharocalyx tweediei f. lanceolatus

Blepharocalyx tweediei f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf wax tree, is a unique variant of the wax tree species native to the Philippines.

This plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are glossy and often covered in a waxy coating, providing a distinct appearance. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its visual appeal.

The plant is well-suited for tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens and as a houseplant.
