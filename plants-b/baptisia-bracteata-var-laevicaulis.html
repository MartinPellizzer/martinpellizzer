Baptisia bracteata var. laevicaulis

Baptisia bracteata var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system consists of extensive, fibrous roots. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
