Bauhinia sanitwongsei

Bauhinia sanitwongsei, commonly known as the Thai bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is named in honor of Dr. Sanitwongsei, a Thai botanist, highlighting its cultural and scientific significance.
