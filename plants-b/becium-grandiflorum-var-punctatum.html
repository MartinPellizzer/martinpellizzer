Becium grandiflorum var. punctatum

Becium grandiflorum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted becium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often marked with dark green spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
