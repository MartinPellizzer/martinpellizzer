Berlinia giorgii var. gilletii

Berlinia giorgii var. gilletii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
