Bumelia riograndis

Bumelia riograndis, commonly known as the Rio Grande bumelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
