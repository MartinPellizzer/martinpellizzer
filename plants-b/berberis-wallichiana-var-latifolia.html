Berberis wallichiana var. latifolia

Berberis wallichiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and winter interest.

This variety is more robust than the typical Berberis wallichiana and can thrive in a range of soil conditions.
