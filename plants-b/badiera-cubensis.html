Badiera cubensis

Badiera cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Badiera cubensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and woodlands.
