Brassica prolongi

Brassica prolongi, commonly known as the long-stemmed mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
