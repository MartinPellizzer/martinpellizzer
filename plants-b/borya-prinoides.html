Borya prinoides

Borya prinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are bell-shaped, usually in shades of blue or purple. Borya prinoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small invertebrates.
