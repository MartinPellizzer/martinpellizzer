Buxus moana

Buxus moana, commonly known as the Hawaiian boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, forming a rounded shape. Its leaves are oval to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a pointed tip.

Buxus moana thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
