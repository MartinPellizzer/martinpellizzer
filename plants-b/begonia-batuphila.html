Begonia batuphila

Begonia batuphila, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia batuphila is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
