Brassica rifana

Brassica rifana, commonly known as wild mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Brassica rifana is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
