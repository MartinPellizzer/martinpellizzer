Berberis densiflora var. bungeana

Berberis densiflora var. bungeana, commonly known as Bunge's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
