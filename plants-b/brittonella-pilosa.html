Brittonella pilosa

Brittonella pilosa, commonly known as the hairy brittonella, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Brittonella pilosa is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
