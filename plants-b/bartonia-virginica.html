Bartonia virginica

Bartonia virginica, commonly known as the Carolina pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Bartonia virginica is often found in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along streambanks.
