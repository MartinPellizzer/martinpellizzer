Blumea sagittata

Blumea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, open habitats.

Blumea sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
