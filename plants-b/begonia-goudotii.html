Begonia goudotii

Begonia goudotii, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
