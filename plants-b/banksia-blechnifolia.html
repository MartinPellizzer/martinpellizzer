Banksia blechnifolia

Banksia blechnifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Banksia blechnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
