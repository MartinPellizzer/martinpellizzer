Briza media f. intermedia

Briza media f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate grass or velvet grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of delicate, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Briza media f. intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and streambanks.
