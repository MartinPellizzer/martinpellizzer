Batschia disticha

Batschia disticha, commonly known as the two-branched bat's tail, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-branched inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as moths and bees. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.

Batschia disticha is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
