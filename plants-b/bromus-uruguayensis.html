Bromus uruguayensis

Bromus uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Bromus uruguayensis is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widely distributed species.
