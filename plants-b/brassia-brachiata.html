Brassia brachiata

Brassia brachiata, commonly known as the orchid with the raceme of flowers, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy inflorescence. The plant produces pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Brassia brachiata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
