Beythea

Beythea, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a sacred plant in various cultures, particularly in Hinduism and Buddhism.

It is a water-lily species native to the wetlands of Asia, including regions in India, China, and Southeast Asia. Beythea has large, showy flowers that float on the water surface, with vibrant colors ranging from white to deep pink. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

The plant is symbolically significant, representing purity and spiritual awakening in many traditions.
