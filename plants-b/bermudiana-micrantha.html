Bermudiana micrantha

Bermudiana micrantha, commonly known as the Bermuda buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bermudiana micrantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
