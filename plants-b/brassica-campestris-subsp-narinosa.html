Brassica campestris subsp. narinosa

Brassica campestris subsp.

narinosa, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It has hairy stems and oblong, serrated leaves.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
