Bromus unioloides f. oliveus

Bromus unioloides f. oliveus, commonly known as the olive-green brome, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically olive-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with a reddish hue. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
