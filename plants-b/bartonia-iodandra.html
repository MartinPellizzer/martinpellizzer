Bartonia iodandra

Bartonia iodandra, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals and a central cone-like structure composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to its appearance.

Bartonia iodandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
