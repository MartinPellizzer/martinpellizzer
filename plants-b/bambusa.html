Bambusa

Bambusa, commonly known as bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant is native to Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Bambusa species are known for their rapid growth and ability to regenerate from rhizomes, making them highly sustainable.

The leaves are typically long and narrow, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers.
