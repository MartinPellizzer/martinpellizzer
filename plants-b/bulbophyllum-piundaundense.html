Bulbophyllum piundaundense

Bulbophyllum piundaundense, commonly known as the Piundaundense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
