Bartsia filiformis

Bartsia filiformis, commonly known as the slender bartsia, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces capsule fruits that release minute seeds.

Bartsia filiformis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its aesthetic appeal in natural habitats.
