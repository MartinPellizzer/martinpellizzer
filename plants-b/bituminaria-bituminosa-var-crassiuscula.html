Bituminaria bituminosa var. crassiuscula

Bituminaria bituminosa var. crassiuscula, commonly known as the thick-leaved bituminous broom, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand dry conditions, and the plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
