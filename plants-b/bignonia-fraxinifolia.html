Bignonia fraxinifolia

Bignonia fraxinifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange to yellow in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can become quite long, allowing it to climb trees or trellises.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
