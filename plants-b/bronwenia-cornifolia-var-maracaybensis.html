Bronwenia cornifolia var. maracaybensis

Bronwenia cornifolia var. maracaybensis, commonly known as the Maracaybense firebush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display, which attracts pollinators such as birds and insects.
