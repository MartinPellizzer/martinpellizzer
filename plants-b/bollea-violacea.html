Bollea violacea

Bollea violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of violet-blue flowers resembling small bells. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bollea violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
