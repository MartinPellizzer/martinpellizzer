Borojoa patinoi

Borojoa patinoi, commonly known as the wild mango or pitahaya, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles a mango in appearance but has a fibrous, inedible pulp. Borojoa patinoi is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
