Biscutella laevigata var. densiflora

Biscutella laevigata var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many economically important plants such as cabbage and mustard.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
