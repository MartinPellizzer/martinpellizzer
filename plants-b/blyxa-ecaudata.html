Blyxa ecaudata

Blyxa ecaudata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. Blyxa ecaudata thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and reservoirs.

It plays an important role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and juvenile fish.
