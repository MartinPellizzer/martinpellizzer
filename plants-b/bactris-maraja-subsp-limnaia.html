Bactris maraja subsp. limnaia

Bactris maraja subsp.

limnaia, commonly known as the Marajo palm or Marajoa palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
