Bambusa glaucescens var. floribunda

Bambusa glaucescens var. floribunda, commonly known as the silver bamboo or blue bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers, which are borne on long, drooping inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, flowing form.
