Bulbophyllum hamatifolium

Bulbophyllum hamatifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy foliage and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
