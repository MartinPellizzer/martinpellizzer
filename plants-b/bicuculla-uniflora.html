Bicuculla uniflora

Bicuculla uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, solitary flower that typically blooms in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, thick rhizome. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
