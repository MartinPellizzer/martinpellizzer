Beccariophoenix

Beccariophoenix, commonly known as the Socotra coryphaceae, is a unique palm native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown of feathery leaves and its thick, ridged trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Beccariophoenix is adapted to the arid climate of Socotra, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a striking and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
