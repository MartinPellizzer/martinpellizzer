Berberis hallii var. wagneriana

Berberis hallii var. wagneriana, commonly known as Wagner's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
