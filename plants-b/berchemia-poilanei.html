Berchemia poilanei

Berchemia poilanei, commonly known as the Orange Tree or Orange Bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, edible pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Berchemia poilanei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
