Bonnaya veronicifolia var. grandiflora

Bonnaya veronicifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and showy blooms, it is a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
