Baccharoides phyllolaenum

Baccharoides phyllolaenum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
