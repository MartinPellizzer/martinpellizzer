Brassica vogelii

Brassica vogelii, commonly known as Vogel's mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica vogelii is often used in traditional medicine and is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic diversity.
