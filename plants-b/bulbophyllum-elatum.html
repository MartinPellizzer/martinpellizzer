Bulbophyllum elatum

Bulbophyllum elatum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that produces striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its bulbs are an essential part of its growth cycle, storing nutrients for periods of dormancy.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
