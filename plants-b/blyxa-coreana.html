Blyxa coreana

Blyxa coreana, commonly known as the Korean water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually bright green and often float on the water surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or insects.

Blyxa coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
