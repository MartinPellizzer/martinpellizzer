Berberis trifolia

Berberis trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in threes, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. Berberis trifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
