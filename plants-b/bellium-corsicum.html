Bellium corsicum

Bellium corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Bellium corsicum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.
