Begonia imperialis var. brunnea

Begonia imperialis var. brunnea, commonly known as the imperial begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to dark brown, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific climate requirements.
