Bulbophyllum conchidioides

Bulbophyllum conchidioides, commonly known as the shell-like orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive shell-like shape, often in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
