Baccharis prunifolia var. subprunifolia

Baccharis prunifolia var. subprunifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.

This species is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.
