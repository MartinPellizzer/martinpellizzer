Blandfordia cunninghamii

Blandfordia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, bell-shaped flower that is typically pink or red, appearing during the late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem. Blandfordia cunninghamii is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soils to thrive.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the Christmas season in its native habitat.
