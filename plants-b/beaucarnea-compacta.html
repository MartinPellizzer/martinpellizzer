Beaucarnea compacta

Beaucarnea compacta, commonly known as the elephant tree, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted, and gnarled trunk that resembles an elephant's leg, hence its common name. The plant has sparse, grass-like foliage that grows at the top of the trunk. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

Beaucarnea compacta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
