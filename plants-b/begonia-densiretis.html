Begonia densiretis

Begonia densiretis, commonly known as the dense-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
