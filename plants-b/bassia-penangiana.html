Bassia penangiana

Bassia penangiana, commonly known as the Penang sandburs, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and spiny fruits. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to thrive in arid or sandy environments.

Bassia penangiana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
