Berberis pinnata subsp. pinnata

Berberis pinnata subsp.

pinnata, commonly known as the Allegheny shrub. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is native to the eastern United States.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
