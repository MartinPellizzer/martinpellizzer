Bupleurum ciliatum

Bupleurum ciliatum, commonly known as the curly-leaved bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Bupleurum ciliatum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
