Bystropogon bipinnatus

Bystropogon bipinnatus, commonly known as the slender featherleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage with finely divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Bystropogon bipinnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
