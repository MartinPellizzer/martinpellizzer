Begonia brevilobata

Begonia brevilobata, commonly known as the small-lobed begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is characterized by a velvety texture and vibrant green color.

Begonia brevilobata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
