Bursera instabilis

Bursera instabilis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It produces yellowish-white flowers followed by small, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
