Bromus chiapporianus

Bromus chiapporianus, commonly known as the Chiappori grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Bromus chiapporianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
