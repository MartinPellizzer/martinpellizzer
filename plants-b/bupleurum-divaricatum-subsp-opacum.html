Bupleurum divaricatum subsp. opacum

Bupleurum divaricatum subsp.

opacum, commonly known as the opaque bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
