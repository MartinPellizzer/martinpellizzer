Bellevalia macrobotrys

Bellevalia macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, violet-blue petals that are often adorned with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows.

The leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
