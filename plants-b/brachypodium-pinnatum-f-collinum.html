Brachypodium pinnatum f. collinum

Brachypodium pinnatum f. collinum, commonly known as the mountain bristlegrass, is a variant of the slender wheatgrass species.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage is covered with fine, bristle-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is notable for its adaptation to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
