Bebbia filifolia

Bebbia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf bebbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to the forest floor.

Bebbia filifolia plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
