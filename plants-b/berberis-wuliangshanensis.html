Berberis wuliangshanensis

Berberis wuliangshanensis, commonly known as the Wuliangshan barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in the Wuliangshan region of Sichuan province.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a temperate climate.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
