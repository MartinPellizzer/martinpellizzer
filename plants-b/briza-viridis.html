Briza viridis

Briza viridis, commonly known as green false oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or bluish-green in color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Briza viridis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
