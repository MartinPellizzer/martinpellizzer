Balsamina oppositifolia

Balsamina oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Balsamina oppositifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
