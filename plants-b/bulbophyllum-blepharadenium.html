Bulbophyllum blepharadenium

Bulbophyllum blepharadenium, commonly known as the "Horned Violet" or "Bull's Horn Orchid," is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, horn-like pseudobulbs and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss.
