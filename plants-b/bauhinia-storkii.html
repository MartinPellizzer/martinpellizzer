Bauhinia storkii

Bauhinia storkii, commonly known as the stork's bill, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia storkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
