Barbarea intermedia var. fallax

Barbarea intermedia var. fallax, commonly known as fallax winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are fleshy and lobed, with a slightly peppery taste, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
