Bellevalia dichroa

Bellevalia dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that typically display two distinct colors, often blue and white, on the same bloom. The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Bellevalia dichroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
