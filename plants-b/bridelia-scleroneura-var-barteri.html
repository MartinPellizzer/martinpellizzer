Bridelia scleroneura var. barteri

Bridelia scleroneura var. barteri, commonly known as the Barter's bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
