Bulbophyllum lepidum

Bulbophyllum lepidum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The plant is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
