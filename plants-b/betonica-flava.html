Betonica flava

Betonica flava, commonly known as yellow betony, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers bloom in whorls along the stem.

Betonica flava is valued for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
