Barbarea vulgaris

Barbarea vulgaris, commonly known as common rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of kidney-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Barbarea vulgaris is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
