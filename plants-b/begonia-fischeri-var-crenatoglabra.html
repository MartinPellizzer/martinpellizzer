Begonia fischeri var. crenatoglabra

Begonia fischeri var. crenatoglabra, commonly known as the crenate-leaved begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, and slightly hairy leaves that have a distinctive wavy edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
