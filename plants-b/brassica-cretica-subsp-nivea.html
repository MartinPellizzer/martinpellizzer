Brassica cretica subsp. nivea

Brassica cretica subsp.

nivea, commonly known as the white cabbage or white mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It is a biennial or perennial herb that can grow up to 30 cm in height.

The subspecies nivea is distinguished by its white or pale green coloration compared to other subspecies of Brassica cretica.
