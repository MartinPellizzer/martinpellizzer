Breynia tonkinensis

Breynia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin breynia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Breynia tonkinensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
