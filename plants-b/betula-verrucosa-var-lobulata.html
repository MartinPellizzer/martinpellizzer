Betula verrucosa var. lobulata

Betula verrucosa var. lobulata, commonly known as the warty birch, is a variant of the paper birch characterized by its distinctive, warty bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America, particularly in boreal and subarctic climates. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
