Bulbophyllum valeryi

Bulbophyllum valeryi, commonly known as the Valery's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

This orchid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its striking floral displays.
