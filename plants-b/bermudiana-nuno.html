Bermudiana nuno

Bermudiana nuno, commonly known as the Bermuda grass variant, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers. It is widely used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
