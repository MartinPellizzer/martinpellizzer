Bauhinia brachycarpa var. densiflora

Bauhinia brachycarpa var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, papery flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Its fruit is a flat, elliptical pod that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
