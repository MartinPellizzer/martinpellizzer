Bauhinia lecomtei

Bauhinia lecomtei, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin layers.

Bauhinia lecomtei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
