Batatas purga

Batatas purga, commonly known as the "purging sweet potato," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange or red tuberous roots that are rich in nutrients. Its name derives from its traditional use in herbal medicine to induce bowel movements.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
