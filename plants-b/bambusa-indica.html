Bambusa indica

Bambusa indica, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clusters of pale blue or white seeds. Bambusa indica is highly valued for its rapid growth and strong, flexible stalks, making it useful in construction and crafts.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
