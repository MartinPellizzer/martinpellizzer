Baccharis rosmarinifolia

Baccharis rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for erosion control.
