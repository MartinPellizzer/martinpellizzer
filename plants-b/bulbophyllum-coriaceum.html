Bulbophyllum coriaceum

Bulbophyllum coriaceum, commonly known as the hard-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to store water, making it resilient in dry environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
