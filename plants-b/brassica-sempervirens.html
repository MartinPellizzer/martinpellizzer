Brassica sempervirens

Brassica sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen mustard, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

Brassica sempervirens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
