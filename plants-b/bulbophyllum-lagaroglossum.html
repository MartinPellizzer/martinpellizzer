Bulbophyllum lagaroglossum

Bulbophyllum lagaroglossum, commonly known as the "Coral Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, coral-like inflorescence and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture. It has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

This orchid requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
