Begonia heracleifolia f. punctata

Begonia heracleifolia f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a hybrid variety of the begonia genus.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature distinctive spots and markings. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, heart-shaped foliage. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, appearing in shades of red, pink, or white.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
