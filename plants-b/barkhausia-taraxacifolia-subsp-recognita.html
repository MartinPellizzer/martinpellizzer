Barkhausia taraxacifolia subsp. recognita

Barkhausia taraxacifolia subsp.

recognita, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that resemble those of a dandelion.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
