Balantium antarcticum

Balantium antarcticum, commonly known as the Antarctic lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae.

It thrives in extreme environments, particularly in the cold and dry regions of Antarctica. The plant's structure consists of a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to rocky surfaces. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its harsh habitat.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is one of the few organisms capable of surviving in such extreme conditions.
