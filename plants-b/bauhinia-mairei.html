Bauhinia mairei

Bauhinia mairei, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Bauhinia mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
