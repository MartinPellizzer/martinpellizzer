Barringtonia calophylla

Barringtonia calophylla, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pale green leaves and distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale yellow. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant has both ornamental and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
