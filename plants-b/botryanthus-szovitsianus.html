Botryanthus szovitsianus

Botryanthus szovitsianus, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Hungary and surrounding regions.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Botryanthus szovitsianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
