Banisteria cornifolia

Banisteria cornifolia, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Banisteria cornifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
