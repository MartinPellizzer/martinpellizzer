Betula baicalensis f. obscura

Betula baicalensis f. obscura, commonly known as the dark birch, is a rare variant of the Baikal birch native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which distinguishes it from the more common light-colored varieties of birch. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers and oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
