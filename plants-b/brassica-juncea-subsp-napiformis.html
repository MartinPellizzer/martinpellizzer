Brassica juncea subsp. napiformis

Brassica juncea subsp.

napiformis, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, edible leaves and a swollen, root-like stem known as the head.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables such as broccoli and cauliflower. Its botanical features include small white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and it produces round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
