Begonia elisabethae

Begonia elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the island of Java.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
