Beyeria leschenaultii f. salsoloides

Beyeria leschenaultii f. salsoloides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
