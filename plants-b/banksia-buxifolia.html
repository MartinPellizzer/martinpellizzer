Banksia buxifolia

Banksia buxifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often green with a waxy coating. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, appearing in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Banksia buxifolia is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathland and open forests.
