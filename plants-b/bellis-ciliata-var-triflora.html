Bellis ciliata var. triflora

Bellis ciliata var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered mouse-ear.

This plant is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, rounded leaves covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The species is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
