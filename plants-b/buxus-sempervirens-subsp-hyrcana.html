Buxus sempervirens subsp. hyrcana

Buxus sempervirens subsp.

hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian boxwood, is a subspecies native to the temperate regions of the Caucasus and southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are typically narrow and glossy, with a dark green coloration that remains throughout the year. This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.

It is often used in formal gardens and as a hedging plant due to its slow growth and ease of shaping.
