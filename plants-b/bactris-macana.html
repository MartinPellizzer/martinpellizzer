Bactris macana

Bactris macana, commonly known as the red raphia, is a tropical palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruits are three-angled, fibrous berries that mature to a reddish color, containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its fibers and as a food source.
