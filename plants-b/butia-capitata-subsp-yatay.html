Butia capitata subsp. yatay

Butia capitata subsp.

yatay, commonly known as the yatay palm, is a small, cold-hardy palm native to southern Brazil. It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and a slender trunk that often remains unbranched.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into small, edible fruits resembling dates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drier climates.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
