Baccharis microptera

Baccharis microptera, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall.

Baccharis microptera is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping.
