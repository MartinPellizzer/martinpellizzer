Bupleurum thracicum

Bupleurum thracicum, commonly known as the Thrace bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bupleurum thracicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
