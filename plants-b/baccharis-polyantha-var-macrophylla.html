Baccharis polyantha var. macrophylla

Baccharis polyantha var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
