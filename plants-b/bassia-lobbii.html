Bassia lobbii

Bassia lobbii, commonly known as the Namib Desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to the sandy landscape. It is well adapted to extreme drought, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Bassia lobbii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
