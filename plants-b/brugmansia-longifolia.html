Brugmansia longifolia

Brugmansia longifolia, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous flowers that can grow up to 30 cm in length, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes, and it contains toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Brugmansia longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it requires careful handling due to its poisonous properties.
