Bulbine abyssinica

Bulbine abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian bulbine, is a succulent perennial plant native to the arid regions of eastern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular garden plant in arid and semi-arid climates.
