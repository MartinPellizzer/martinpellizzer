Bejaria phillyreifolia

Bejaria phillyreifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bejaria, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bejaria phillyreifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
