Beta vulgaris convar. cruenta

Beta vulgaris convar. cruenta, commonly known as the red beet or chard, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous stems that are typically green or red in color. The leaves are large, edible, and often cultivated for their nutritional value. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is widely grown for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
