Bommeria pedata

Bommeria pedata, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Bommeria pedata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
