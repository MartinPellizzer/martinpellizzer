Berberis incrassata var. bucahwangensis

Berberis incrassata var. bucahwangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh climates of its native habitat.
