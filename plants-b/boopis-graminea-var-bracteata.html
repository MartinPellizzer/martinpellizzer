Boopis graminea var. bracteata

Boopis graminea var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate graminoid, is a variant of the grass species Boopis graminea.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
