Berberis actinacantha var. grevilleana

Berberis actinacantha var. grevilleana, commonly known as the Greville's barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
