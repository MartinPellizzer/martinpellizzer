Brachypodium ramosum var. roemeri

Brachypodium ramosum var. roemeri, commonly known as the roemer's false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, greenish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
