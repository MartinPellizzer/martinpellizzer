Begonia satelloides

Begonia satelloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia satelloides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
