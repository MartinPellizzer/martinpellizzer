Brachystelma letestui

Brachystelma letestui, commonly known as the Letestu's stonecrop, is a member of the Crassulaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a small, succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and its ecological role in its native habitat.
