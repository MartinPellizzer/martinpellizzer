Brassica rapa var. biennis

Brassica rapa var. biennis, commonly known as the oilseed rape or field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flowers and can grow up to 2 meters tall. The plant has a taproot system and produces a thick, fleshy stem that supports its broad, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they contain both male and female reproductive structures.

The plant is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce oil and as a source of animal feed.
