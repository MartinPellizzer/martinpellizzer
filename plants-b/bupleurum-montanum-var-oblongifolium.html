Bupleurum montanum var. oblongifolium

Bupleurum montanum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
