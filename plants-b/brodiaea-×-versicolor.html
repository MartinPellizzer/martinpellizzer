Brodiaea × versicolor

Brodiaea × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid blue-eyed grass, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, star-shaped petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of color variations, including white, pink, and purple.
