Blechnum sprucei

Blechnum sprucei, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a perennial fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that allow it to spread along the forest floor. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Blechnum sprucei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.
