Bunium ferulaceum

Bunium ferulaceum, commonly known as wild fennel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
