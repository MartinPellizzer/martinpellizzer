Brachionidium neblinense

Brachionidium neblinense, commonly known as the misty mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in humid, tropical environments.

It possesses small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, white flowers that are often overlooked due to their size. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its berries, contributing to its distribution in forested areas.

Due to its limited geographic range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
