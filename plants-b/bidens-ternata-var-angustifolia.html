Bidens ternata var. angustifolia

Bidens ternata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with black centers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It is an annual herb that produces seeds in clusters, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in wetland ecosystems across its native range.
