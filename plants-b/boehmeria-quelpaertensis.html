Boehmeria quelpaertensis

Boehmeria quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly Jeju Island.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle species known for their stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fibers of its stems are used in traditional crafts and have been studied for their potential in eco-friendly textile production.
