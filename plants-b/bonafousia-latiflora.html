Bonafousia latiflora

Bonafousia latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf bonafousia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, round fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
