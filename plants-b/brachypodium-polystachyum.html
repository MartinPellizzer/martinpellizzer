Brachypodium polystachyum

Brachypodium polystachyum, commonly known as the clustered false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Brachypodium polystachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
