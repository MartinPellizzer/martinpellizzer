Bacopa reptans

Bacopa reptans, commonly known as inch plant, is a trailing herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or slightly heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its trailing habit.

Bacopa reptans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.
