Bermudiana striata

Bermudiana striata, commonly known as the striped sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Bermuda.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found growing along the shorelines.

Bermudiana striata is a hardy species that is well adapted to coastal environments with salt spray and strong winds.
