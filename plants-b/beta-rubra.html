Beta rubra

Beta rubra, commonly known as red beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically red in color, though they can vary in hue depending on the variety. The plant produces small, green, triangular leaves that are edible and rich in nutrients. Beta rubra thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

Its cultivation dates back thousands of years, with historical use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
