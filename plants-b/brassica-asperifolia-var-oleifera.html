Brassica asperifolia var. oleifera

Brassica asperifolia var. oleifera, commonly known as the African mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

This variety is noted for its potential as a wild edible and its use in traditional medicine.
