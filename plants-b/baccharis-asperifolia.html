Baccharis asperifolia

Baccharis asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, rough-textured leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its drought tolerance makes it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
