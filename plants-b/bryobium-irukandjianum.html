Bryobium irukandjianum

Bryobium irukandjianum, commonly known as the Irukandji bryobium, is a unique member of the bryophyte group, specifically a liverwort.

It is characterized by its small, flat, and often greenish or reddish thallus, which is the main body of the plant. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can absorb moisture effectively. Its structure lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on rhizoids for anchorage and nutrient absorption.

Bryobium irukandjianum plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
