Brodiaea lactea var. major

Brodiaea lactea var. major, commonly known as the giant milk lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
