Browningia candelaris subsp. candelaris

Browningia candelaris subsp.

candelaris, commonly known as the candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and long-lasting, fragrant blooms.
