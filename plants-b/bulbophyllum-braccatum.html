Bulbophyllum braccatum

Bulbophyllum braccatum, commonly known as the bearded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy pseudobulbs and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Bulbophyllum braccatum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivation.
