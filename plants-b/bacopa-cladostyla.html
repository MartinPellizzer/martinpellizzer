Bacopa cladostyla

Bacopa cladostyla, commonly known as water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in shallow water. Its stems are square and can grow up to several feet in length, supporting the plant's herbaceous structure.

Bacopa cladostyla is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or traditional medicine.
