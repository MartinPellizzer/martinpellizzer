Begonia asympeltata

Begonia asympeltata, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins that contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
