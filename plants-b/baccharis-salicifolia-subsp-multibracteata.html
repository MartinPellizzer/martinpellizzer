Baccharis salicifolia subsp. multibracteata

Baccharis salicifolia subsp.

multibracteata, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its leaves are often aromatic and may have a slight fuzzy texture.
