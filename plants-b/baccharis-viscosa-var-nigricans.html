Baccharis viscosa var. nigricans

Baccharis viscosa var. nigricans, commonly known as black baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and small, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish flowers that appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along creek banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
