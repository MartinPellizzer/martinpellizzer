Blackallia connata

Blackallia connata, commonly known as the Western Australian ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it survive in sandy, well-drained soils. Its hardy nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal gardens.

Blackallia connata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
