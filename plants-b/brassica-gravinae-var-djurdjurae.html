Brassica gravinae var. djurdjurae

Brassica gravinae var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura mustard, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of the Djurdjura Mountains in Algeria.

This plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a hardy adaptation to harsh climatic conditions. The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
