Bergenia purpurascens var. macrantha

Bergenia purpurascens var. macrantha, commonly known as the purple-stemmed bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy rhizomes and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers in early spring, which are borne on sturdy, upright stems. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing ornamental value in gardens.

Bergenia purpurascens var. macrantha is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
