Bulbophyllum latibrachiatum

Bulbophyllum latibrachiatum, commonly known as the broad-armed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

The species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
