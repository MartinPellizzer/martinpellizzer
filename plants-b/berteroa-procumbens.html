Berteroa procumbens

Berteroa procumbens, commonly known as the creeping berteroa, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
