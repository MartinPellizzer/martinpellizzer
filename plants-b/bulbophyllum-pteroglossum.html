Bulbophyllum pteroglossum

Bulbophyllum pteroglossum, commonly known as the feather-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped flowers with a distinctive feather-like lip. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, making it an epiphytic species. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum pteroglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
