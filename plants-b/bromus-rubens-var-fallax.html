Bromus rubens var. fallax

Bromus rubens var. fallax, commonly known as fallax brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, bluish-green foliage.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and soil stabilization properties.
