Blainvillea rhomboidea var. lanceolata

Blainvillea rhomboidea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate blainvillea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub-like form, with woody stems and dense foliage. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticultural collections.
