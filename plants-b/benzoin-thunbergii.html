Benzoin thunbergii

Benzoin thunbergii, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or resin tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The resin produced by Benzoin thunbergii has been historically valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
