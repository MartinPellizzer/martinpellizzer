Baccharis gilliesii

Baccharis gilliesii, commonly known as gilgillies' baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Baccharis gilliesii is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
