Bidens seemannii

Bidens seemannii, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers that consist of ray florets and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Bidens seemannii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
