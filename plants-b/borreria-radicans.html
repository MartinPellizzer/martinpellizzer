Borreria radicans

Borreria radicans, commonly known as creeping borreria, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Borreria radicans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
