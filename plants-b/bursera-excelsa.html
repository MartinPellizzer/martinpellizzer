Bursera excelsa

Bursera excelsa, commonly known as the sacred tree or copal tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and aromatic resin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often shed during the dry season.

The tree is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally for incense and medicinal purposes.
