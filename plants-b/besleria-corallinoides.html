Besleria corallinoides

Besleria corallinoides, commonly known as the coral-like begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
