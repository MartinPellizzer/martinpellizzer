Brassavola odoratissima

Brassavola odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, vanilla-like aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its unique fragrance, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
