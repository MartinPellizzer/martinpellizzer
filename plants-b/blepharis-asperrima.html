Blepharis asperrima

Blepharis asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale purple, bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
