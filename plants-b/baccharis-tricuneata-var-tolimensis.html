Baccharis tricuneata var. tolimensis

Baccharis tricuneata var. tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolimes baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
