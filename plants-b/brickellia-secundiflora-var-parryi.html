Brickellia secundiflora var. parryi

Brickellia secundiflora var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically opposite and elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
