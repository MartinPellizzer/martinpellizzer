Begonia daveauana

Begonia daveauana, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
