Bupleurum ramosissimum f. virgatum

Bupleurum ramosissimum f. virgatum, commonly known as the slender bupleurum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
