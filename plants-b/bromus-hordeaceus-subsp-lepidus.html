Bromus hordeaceus subsp. lepidus

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

lepidus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
