Bupleurum graminifolium var. dolichotum

Bupleurum graminifolium var. dolichotum, commonly known as the long-leaf bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
