Berberis silvicola

Berberis silvicola, commonly known as the mountain barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Berberis silvicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
