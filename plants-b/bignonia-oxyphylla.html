Bignonia oxyphylla

Bignonia oxyphylla, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia oxyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscape design as a climbing plant or espalier.
