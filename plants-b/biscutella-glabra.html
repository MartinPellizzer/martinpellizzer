Biscutella glabra

Biscutella glabra, commonly known as the smooth biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, also known as the mustard family, and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its capsule fruits contain numerous small, elliptical seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Biscutella glabra is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
