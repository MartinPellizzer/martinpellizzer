Bredia hirsuta var. scandens

Bredia hirsuta var. scandens, commonly known as the hairy bredia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.
