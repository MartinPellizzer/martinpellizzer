Berberis humidoumbrosa

Berberis humidoumbrosa, commonly known as the wet forest barberry, is a shrub native to the humid temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
