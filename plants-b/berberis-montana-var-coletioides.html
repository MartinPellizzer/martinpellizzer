Berberis montana var. coletioides

Berberis montana var. coletioides, commonly known as the Alpine barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate cold climates.
