Bulbophyllum cylindrocarpum

Bulbophyllum cylindrocarpum, commonly known as the cylindrical fruit orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, cylindrical-shaped seed pods. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
