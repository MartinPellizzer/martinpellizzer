Berlinia micrantha

Berlinia micrantha, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense canopy. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several hard, angular seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
