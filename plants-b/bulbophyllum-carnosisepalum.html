Bulbophyllum carnosisepalum

Bulbophyllum carnosisepalum, commonly known as the hairy-sepal orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
