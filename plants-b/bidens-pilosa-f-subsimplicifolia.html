Bidens pilosa f. subsimplicifolia

Bidens pilosa f. subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the hairy beggarlice, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

It features small, yellow flowers with two ray florets and a crown of disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a rough texture due to the presence of glandular hairs. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
