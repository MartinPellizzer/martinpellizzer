Blumea oblongifolia

Blumea oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Blumea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
