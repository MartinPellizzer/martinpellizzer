Bergia occultipetala

Bergia occultipetala, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Bergia occultipetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and arid conditions.
