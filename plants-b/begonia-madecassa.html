Begonia madecassa

Begonia madecassa, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Madagascar.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
