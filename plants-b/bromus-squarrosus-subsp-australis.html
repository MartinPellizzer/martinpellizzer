Bromus squarrosus subsp. australis

Bromus squarrosus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, sometimes hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish spikelets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. Bromus squarrosus subsp.

australis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
