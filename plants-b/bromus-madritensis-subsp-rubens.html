Bromus madritensis subsp. rubens

Bromus madritensis subsp.

rubens, commonly known as the red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and often have a reddish tinge, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
