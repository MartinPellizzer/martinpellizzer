Brassicella wrightii

Brassicella wrightii, commonly known as Wright's mustards, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard species, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its fruits are small, pod-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Brassicella wrightii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
