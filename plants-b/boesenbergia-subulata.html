Boesenbergia subulata

Boesenbergia subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by red berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Boesenbergia subulata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in herbal practices.
