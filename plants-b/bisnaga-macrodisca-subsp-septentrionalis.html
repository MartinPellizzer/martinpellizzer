Bisnaga macrodisca subsp. septentrionalis

Bisnaga macrodisca subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern bisnaga, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
