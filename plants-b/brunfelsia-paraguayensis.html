Brunfelsia paraguayensis

Brunfelsia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lanceolate, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
