Buchenavia parvifolia

Buchenavia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry or semi-arid habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
