Begonia dominicalis

Begonia dominicalis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia dominicalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
