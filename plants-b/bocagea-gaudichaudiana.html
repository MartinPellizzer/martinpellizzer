Bocagea gaudichaudiana

Bocagea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the coastal bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Bocagea gaudichaudiana is well adapted to salt spray and can withstand periods of drought.
