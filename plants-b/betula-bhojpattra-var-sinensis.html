Betula bhojpattra var. sinensis

Betula bhojpattra var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and small, triangular leaves. The tree produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
