Bombax nicoyense

Bombax nicoyense, commonly known as the Nicoya ceiba, is a large tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed pods. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and is also used in local crafts and as a source of fiber.
