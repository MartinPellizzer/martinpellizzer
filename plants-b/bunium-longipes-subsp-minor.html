Bunium longipes subsp. minor

Bunium longipes subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that emit a mild anise-like scent. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations.
