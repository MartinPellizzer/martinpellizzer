Bufonia tenuifolia subsp. parviflora

Bufonia tenuifolia subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bufonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing small, pale yellow blooms.

The subspecies is characterized by its reduced flower size compared to the typical form of Bufonia tenuifolia.
