Byrsonima latifolia

Byrsonima latifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects. The tree's most notable feature is its heavy, woody pods that contain the edible Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it plays a vital role in the biodiversity and sustainability of the Amazon ecosystem.
