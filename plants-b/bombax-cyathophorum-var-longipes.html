Bombax cyathophorum var. longipes

Bombax cyathophorum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a swollen base, often referred to as a "ceiba tree" or "kapok tree.". Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it produces fibrous seeds enclosed in a woody capsule.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
