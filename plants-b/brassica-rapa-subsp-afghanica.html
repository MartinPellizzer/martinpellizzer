Brassica rapa subsp. afghanica

Brassica rapa subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan mustard, is a wild plant native to Central Asia. It is an annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has small, yellow flowers and produces long, slender pods containing seeds. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to harsh conditions, contributing to its survival in diverse ecosystems.
