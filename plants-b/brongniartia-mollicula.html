Brongniartia mollicula

Brongniartia mollicula, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Brongniartia mollicula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
