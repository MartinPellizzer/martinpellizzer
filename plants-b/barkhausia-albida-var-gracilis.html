Barkhausia albida var. gracilis

Barkhausia albida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender white barkhouseia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
