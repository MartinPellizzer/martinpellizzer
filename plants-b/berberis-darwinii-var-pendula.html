Berberis darwinii var. pendula

Berberis darwinii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading branches that bear small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.
