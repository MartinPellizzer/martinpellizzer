Berkheya spinosa

Berkheya spinosa, commonly known as the spiked berkheya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Berkheya spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
