Bombyx scindicus

Bombyx scindicus, commonly known as the Indian silk moth, is a species of moth native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Bombycidae and is known for its strikingly colorful wings, which display intricate patterns of orange, black, and white. The larvae of this species feed on a variety of plants, including mulberry and other shrubs, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems. The plant's foliage serves as a vital food source for the caterpillars, which are important in the life cycle of the moth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a host plant for this economically important insect.
