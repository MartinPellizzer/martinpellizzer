Bulbophyllum jayjayveeanum

Bulbophyllum jayjayveeanum, commonly known as the Jayjayveean Orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It possesses a unique bulb-like structure at its base, which stores water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded forest habitat. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of pink and white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, this species is considered endangered and is protected in many countries.
