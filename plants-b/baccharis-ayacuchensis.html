Baccharis ayacuchensis

Baccharis ayacuchensis, commonly known as Ayacucho baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis ayacuchensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
