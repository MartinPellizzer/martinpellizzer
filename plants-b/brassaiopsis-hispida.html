Brassaiopsis hispida

Brassaiopsis hispida, commonly known as the rough-leaved brassaiopsis, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is drought-tolerant and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique foliage.
