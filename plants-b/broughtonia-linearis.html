Broughtonia linearis

Broughtonia linearis, commonly known as the slender bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Broughtonia linearis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
