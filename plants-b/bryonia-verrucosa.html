Bryonia verrucosa

Bryonia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough bryony, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, warty stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange, fleshy fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
