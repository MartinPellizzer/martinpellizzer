Bauhinia farec

Bauhinia farec, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, velvety leaves that are often bi-lobed. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Bauhinia farec is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
