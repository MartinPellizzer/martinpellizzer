Bambusa thouarsii var. atter

Bambusa thouarsii var. atter, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a striking variety of bamboo characterized by its dark, almost black stems.

It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense, upright canes, making it ideal for ornamental purposes. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy green appearance, adding to its visual appeal.

This bamboo is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in construction and crafts.
