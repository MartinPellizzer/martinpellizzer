Boea wallichii

Boea wallichii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Boea wallichii is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens.
