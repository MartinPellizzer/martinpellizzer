Begonia masoniana var. maculata

Begonia masoniana var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red and green mottling, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
