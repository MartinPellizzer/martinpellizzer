Berberis hookeri

Berberis hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Berberis hookeri is adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
