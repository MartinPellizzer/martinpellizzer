Blechnum moorei

Blechnum moorei, commonly known as the Moore's holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping rhizomes that help it spread across rocky or forested areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive shape that resembles a holly leaf, giving it its common name.

Blechnum moorei is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
