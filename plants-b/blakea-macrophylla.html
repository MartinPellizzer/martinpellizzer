Blakea macrophylla

Blakea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas.

Its unique adaptations allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
