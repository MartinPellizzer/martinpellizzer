Ballantinia antipoda

Ballantinia antipoda, commonly known as the New Zealand star bush.

It is a flowering plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive star-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Ballantinia antipoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
