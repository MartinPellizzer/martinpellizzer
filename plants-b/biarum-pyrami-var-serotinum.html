Biarum pyrami var. serotinum

Biarum pyrami var. serotinum, commonly known as the late-flowering bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom later in the season compared to other biarum species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
