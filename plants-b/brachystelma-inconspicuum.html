Brachystelma inconspicuum

Brachystelma inconspicuum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
