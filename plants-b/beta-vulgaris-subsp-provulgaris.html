Beta vulgaris subsp. provulgaris

Beta vulgaris subsp.

provulgaris, commonly known as the sugar beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store a high concentration of sucrose.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is cultivated primarily for its sugar content. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a white, powdery coating called epicuticular wax.

The species is widely grown in temperate climates for industrial and agricultural purposes.
