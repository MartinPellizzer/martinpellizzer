Bulbophyllum jolandae

Bulbophyllum jolandae, commonly known as the Joland's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the wet season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
