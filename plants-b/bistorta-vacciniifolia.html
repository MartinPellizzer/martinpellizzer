Bistorta vacciniifolia

Bistorta vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.
