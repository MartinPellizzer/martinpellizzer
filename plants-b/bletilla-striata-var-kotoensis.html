Bletilla striata var. kotoensis

Bletilla striata var. kotoensis, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and showy, pinkish-white to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
