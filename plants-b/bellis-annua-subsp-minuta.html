Bellis annua subsp. minuta

Bellis annua subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the lesser mouse ear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their mild medicinal properties.
