Begonia plumieri var. barahonensis

Begonia plumieri var. barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahonensis Begonia, is a tropical plant native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among collectors for its unique foliage and ornamental value.
