Banksia laevigata subsp. fuscolutea

Banksia laevigata subsp.

fuscolutea, commonly known as the golden banksia, is a subshrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical yellow flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

This subspecies is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
