Berkheya cynaroides var. kraussii

Berkheya cynaroides var. kraussii, commonly known as the Krauss's yellow oxalis, is a striking South African plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the Cape Province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features palmate leaves with serrated edges and produces daisy-like blooms in shades of yellow to orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to full sun and dry conditions.

This variant is noted for its resilience and is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
