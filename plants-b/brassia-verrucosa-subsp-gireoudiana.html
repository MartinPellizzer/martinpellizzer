Brassia verrucosa subsp. gireoudiana

Brassia verrucosa subsp.

gireoudiana, commonly known as the Gireoud's spider orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, wiry stems and clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces small, fragrant orchid flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
