Brassica napobrassica convar. sibirica

Brassica napobrassica convar. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, swollen root that is typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive mustard-like odor. It thrives in cooler climates and is often cultivated for its edible root.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient crop.
