Bridelia ovata var. curtisii

Bridelia ovata var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's Bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
