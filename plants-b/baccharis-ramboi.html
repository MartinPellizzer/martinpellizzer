Baccharis ramboi

Baccharis ramboi, commonly known as Rambo's baccharis, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Baccharis ramboi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
