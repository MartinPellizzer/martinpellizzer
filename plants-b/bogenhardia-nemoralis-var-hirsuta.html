Bogenhardia nemoralis var. hirsuta

Bogenhardia nemoralis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bogenhardia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
