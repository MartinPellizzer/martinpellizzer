Bulbophyllum uhl-gabrielianum

Bulbophyllum uhl-gabrielianum, commonly known as the Uhl-Gabrielian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's unique structure.

The flowers are borne on lateral branches and are known for their intricate, showy appearance.
