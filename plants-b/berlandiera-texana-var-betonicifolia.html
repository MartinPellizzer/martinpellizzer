Berlandiera texana var. betonicifolia

Berlandiera texana var. betonicifolia, commonly known as the Texas bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. It has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
