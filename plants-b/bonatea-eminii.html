Bonatea eminii

Bonatea eminii, commonly known as the Emu Plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, slender stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by spherical, red berries that contain several seeds. Bonatea eminii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
