Bignonia aurea

Bignonia aurea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
