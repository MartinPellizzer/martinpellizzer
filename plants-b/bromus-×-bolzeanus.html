Bromus × bolzeanus

Bromus × bolzeanus, commonly known as the Bolzean brome, is a hybrid grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and along roadsides.

Bromus × bolzeanus is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
