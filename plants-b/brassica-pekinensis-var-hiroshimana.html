Brassica pekinensis var. hiroshimana

Brassica pekinensis var. hiroshimana, commonly known as the Hiroshima mustard, is a variety of mustard plant characterized by its distinctive purple and green foliage.

It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible leaves and seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional cuisines.
