Brachyclados caespitosus

Brachyclados caespitosus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
