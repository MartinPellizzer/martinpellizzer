Blastocaulon speleicola

Blastocaulon speleicola, commonly known as the cave crust fungus, is a unique and rare species of lichen that thrives in subterranean environments.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form, which allows it to adhere to rock surfaces in dark, humid caves. This fungus is primarily found in limestone caves and is adapted to low-light conditions, relying on sporadic light penetration for photosynthesis. Its symbiotic relationship with algae or cyanobacteria is essential for its survival, as it cannot perform photosynthesis alone.

Due to its specialized habitat, Blastocaulon speleicola is considered an indicator species for cave ecosystems and is often used in studies of biodiversity in underground environments.
