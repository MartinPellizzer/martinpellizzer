Biscutella saxatilis var. obcordata

Biscutella saxatilis var. obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, basal leaves that are narrowly elliptic and often have a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
