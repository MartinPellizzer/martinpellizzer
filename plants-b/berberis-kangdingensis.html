Berberis kangdingensis

Berberis kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan in China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
