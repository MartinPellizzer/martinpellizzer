Bupleurum ranunculoides subsp. telonense

Bupleurum ranunculoides subsp.

telonense, commonly known as the Carpathian angel's trumpet, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
