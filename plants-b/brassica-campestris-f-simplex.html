Brassica campestris f. simplex

Brassica campestris f. simplex, commonly known as Chinese kale or neep, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often used in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and long, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It is typically cultivated for its edible leaves and is adapted to temperate climates.

This variety is known for its robust growth and nutritional value, making it a popular choice in vegetable gardening.
