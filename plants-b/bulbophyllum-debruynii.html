Bulbophyllum debruynii

Bulbophyllum debruynii, commonly known as the De Bruyn's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, making it a striking addition to botanical collections.
