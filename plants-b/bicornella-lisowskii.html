Bicornella lisowskii

Bicornella lisowskii, commonly known as the Lisowsky's bicorn, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for arid ecosystems and its potential for medicinal uses.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
