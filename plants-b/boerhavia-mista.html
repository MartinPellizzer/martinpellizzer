Boerhavia mista

Boerhavia mista, commonly known as redroot lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Boerhavia mista is often used in erosion control due to its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil.
