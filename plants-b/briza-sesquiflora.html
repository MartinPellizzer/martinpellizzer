Briza sesquiflora

Briza sesquiflora, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Briza sesquiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
