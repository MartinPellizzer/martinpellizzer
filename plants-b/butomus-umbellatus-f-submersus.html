Butomus umbellatus f. submersus

Butomus umbellatus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged bulrush, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its submerged growth habit.

It features long, slender stems that are typically fully submerged in water, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to habitat structure for aquatic life.

This subspecies is often found in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.
