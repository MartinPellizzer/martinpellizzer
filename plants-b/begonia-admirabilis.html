Begonia admirabilis

Begonia admirabilis, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental landscapes.
