Bucco ovata

Bucco ovata, commonly known as the oval-mouthed bucco, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are ovate in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface that reflects sunlight efficiently. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution, Bucco ovata is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its ecological adaptations.
