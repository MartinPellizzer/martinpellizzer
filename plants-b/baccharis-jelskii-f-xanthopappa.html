Baccharis jelskii f. xanthopappa

Baccharis jelskii f. xanthopappa, commonly known as the yellow baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This species is often found in dry grasslands and coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
