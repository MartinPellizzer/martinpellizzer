Brugmansia pittieri

Brugmansia pittieri, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers.
