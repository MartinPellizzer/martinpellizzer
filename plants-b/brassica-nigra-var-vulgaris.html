Brassica nigra var. vulgaris

Brassica nigra var. vulgaris, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in finely divided, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, triangular seed pods. Black mustard is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
