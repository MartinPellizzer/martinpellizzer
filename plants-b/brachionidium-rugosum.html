Brachionidium rugosum

Brachionidium rugosum, commonly known as the rough brachionid, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Brachionidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, branched, and often hairy stems that grow close to the water surface. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Brachionidium rugosum plays a role in providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and can be an indicator of water quality in its ecosystem.
