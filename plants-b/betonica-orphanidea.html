Betonica orphanidea

Betonica orphanidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Betonica orphanidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
