Banksia gnidia

Banksia gnidia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a species native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathland and open forests. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating.

The plant is adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
