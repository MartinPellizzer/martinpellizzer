Berberis gagnepainii var. praestans

Berberis gagnepainii var. praestans, commonly known as the white-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in spring, followed by red, orange, or yellow berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in the right environment.
