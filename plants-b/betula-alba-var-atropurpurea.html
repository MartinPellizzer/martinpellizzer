Betula alba var. atropurpurea

Betula alba var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple birch, is a variety of the white birch characterized by its distinctive purple-gray bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 meters. The leaves are oval with a doubly serrated margin and turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees, and it produces small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking bark and seasonal foliage.
