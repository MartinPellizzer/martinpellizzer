Bignonia cuneata

Bignonia cuneata, commonly known as the crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 feet long, often twining around supports.

Bignonia cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
