Bulbophyllum tripetalum

Bulbophyllum tripetalum, commonly known as the three-petaled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Bulbophyllum tripetalum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
