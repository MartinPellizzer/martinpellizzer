Beta vulgaris var. xanthopleura

Beta vulgaris var. xanthopleura, commonly known as the golden beet, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange-colored leaves and deep red, elongated roots. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is grown primarily for its edible roots and leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are often used in salads, while the roots are cooked or roasted for their sweet, earthy flavor.
