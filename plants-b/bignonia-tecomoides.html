Bignonia tecomoides

Bignonia tecomoides, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Bignonia tecomoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
