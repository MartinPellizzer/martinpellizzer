Brassica oleracea var. rubra

Brassica oleracea var. rubra, commonly known as red cabbage, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its tightly packed, dark purple to red colored leaves that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and is typically harvested before the leaves become too bitter.

Its distinctive coloration is due to high concentrations of anthocyanin pigments.
