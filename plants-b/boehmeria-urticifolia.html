Boehmeria urticifolia

Boehmeria urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, green stems and serrated leaves that often have a stinging hair covering. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. Boehmeria urticifolia is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional industries for making textiles and paper.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in various applications.
