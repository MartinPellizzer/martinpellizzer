Bulbophyllum erythrostictum

Bulbophyllum erythrostictum, commonly known as the Red-streaked Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure and vibrant, often red-tinged petals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid and tropical environments.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
