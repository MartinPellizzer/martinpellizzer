Butea spirei

Butea spirei, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of Butea spirei have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
