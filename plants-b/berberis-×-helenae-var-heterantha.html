Berberis × helenae var. heterantha

Berberis × helenae var. heterantha, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of berberine. Its hardy nature makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy texture and turn reddish in autumn.
