Bauhinia kappleri

Bauhinia kappleri, commonly known as the kappler's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Bauhinia kappleri thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
