Betula viminea var. serrulata

Betula viminea var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated weeping birch, is a variant of the weeping birch characterized by its drooping branches and distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, with a pale green upper surface and a silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure, making it a versatile choice for landscape design.
