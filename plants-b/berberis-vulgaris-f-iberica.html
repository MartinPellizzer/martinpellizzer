Berberis vulgaris f. iberica

Berberis vulgaris f. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian barberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
