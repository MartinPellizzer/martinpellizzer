Boscia angustifolia var. corymbosa

Boscia angustifolia var. corymbosa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, red or orange fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses and resilience to drought conditions.
