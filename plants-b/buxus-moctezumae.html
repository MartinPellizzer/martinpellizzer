Buxus moctezumae

Buxus moctezumae, commonly known as the Mexican boxwood, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Buxaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, oval leaves are usually a dark green and remain throughout the year.

Buxus moctezumae is often used in horticulture for its compact growth and ability to be shaped into ornamental forms.
