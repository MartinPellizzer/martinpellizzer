Begonia tovarensis

Begonia tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a rich green or bronze hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
