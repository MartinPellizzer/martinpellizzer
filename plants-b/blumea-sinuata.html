Blumea sinuata

Blumea sinuata, commonly known as the broadleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a rough texture. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Blumea sinuata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
