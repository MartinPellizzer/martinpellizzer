Buddleja crispa

Buddleja crispa, commonly known as the silver-leaf bush or white buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom of fuzzy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale to white flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Buddleja crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
