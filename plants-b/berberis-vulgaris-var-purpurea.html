Berberis vulgaris var. purpurea

Berberis vulgaris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, alternate leaves that are typically oval and have a serrated edge, and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes.

It is valued in gardens for its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
