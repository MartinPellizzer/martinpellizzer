Brassica barrelieri var. oxyrrhina

Brassica barrelieri var. oxyrrhina, commonly known as the African mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
