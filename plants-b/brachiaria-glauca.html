Brachiaria glauca

Brachiaria glauca, commonly known as bluegrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks. Brachiaria glauca has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
