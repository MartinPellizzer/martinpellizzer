Buddleja euryphylla

Buddleja euryphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are opposite and often slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja euryphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
