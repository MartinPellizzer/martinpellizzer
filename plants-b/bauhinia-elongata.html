Bauhinia elongata

Bauhinia elongata, commonly known as the longpod bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Bauhinia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.

It also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine systems.
