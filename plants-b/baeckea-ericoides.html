Baeckea ericoides

Baeckea ericoides, commonly known as the coastal baeckea, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring. It is a member of the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Baeckea ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
