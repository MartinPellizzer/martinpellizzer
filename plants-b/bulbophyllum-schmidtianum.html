Bulbophyllum schmidtianum

Bulbophyllum schmidtianum, commonly known as the Schmidt's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
