Bulbophyllum polyflorum

Bulbophyllum polyflorum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth and fleshy, succulent-like pseudobulbs. The plant produces multiple racemes of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
