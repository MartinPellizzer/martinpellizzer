Bignonia gigantea

Bignonia gigantea, commonly known as the giant crossvine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, slender stems that can grow up to 30 feet in length, often twining around supports. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern, which bloom in spring. Its heart-shaped leaves are opposite and have a velvety texture.

Bignonia gigantea is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant in warm climates.
