Butonica caffra

Butonica caffra, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Butonica caffra is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant being used in traditional herbal remedies.
