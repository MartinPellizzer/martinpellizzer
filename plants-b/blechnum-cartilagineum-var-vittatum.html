Blechnum cartilagineum var. vittatum

Blechnum cartilagineum var. vittatum, commonly known as the variegated shield fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, leathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive variegation pattern of green and white or yellow markings. Native to New Zealand, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clumps over time.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
