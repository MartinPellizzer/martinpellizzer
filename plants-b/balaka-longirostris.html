Balaka longirostris

Balaka longirostris, commonly known as the long-nosed orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender petals and a distinctive elongated spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small but exhibit intricate patterns and vibrant colors, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Balaka longirostris is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
