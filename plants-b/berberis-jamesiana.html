Berberis jamesiana

Berberis jamesiana, commonly known as the redleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge. Its flowers are yellow and appear in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Berberis jamesiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
