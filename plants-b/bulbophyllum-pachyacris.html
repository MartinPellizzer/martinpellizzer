Bulbophyllum pachyacris

Bulbophyllum pachyacris, commonly known as the thick-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with prominent veining. The plant typically features thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water, aiding in survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
