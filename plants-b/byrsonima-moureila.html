Byrsonima moureila

Byrsonima moureila, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or araçá-moureila, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
