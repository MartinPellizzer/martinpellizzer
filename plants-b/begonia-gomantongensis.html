Begonia gomantongensis

Begonia gomantongensis, commonly known as the Gomantong begonia, is a rare tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
