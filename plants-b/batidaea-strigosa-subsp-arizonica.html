Batidaea strigosa subsp. arizonica

Batidaea strigosa subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona bristly bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds, which are released when the pods dry and split open. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation to harsh climates.
