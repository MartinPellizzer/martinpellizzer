Bulbophyllum gerlandianum

Bulbophyllum gerlandianum, commonly known as the Gerland's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in moisture retention.

The orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
