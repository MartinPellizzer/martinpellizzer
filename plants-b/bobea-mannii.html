Bobea mannii

Bobea mannii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Bobea mannii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
