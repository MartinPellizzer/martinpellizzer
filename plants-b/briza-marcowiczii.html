Briza marcowiczii

Briza marcowiczii, commonly known as the Polish feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Briza marcowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland or marshy environments.
