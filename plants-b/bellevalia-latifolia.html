Bellevalia latifolia

Bellevalia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
