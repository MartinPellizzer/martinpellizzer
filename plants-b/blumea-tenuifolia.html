Blumea tenuifolia

Blumea tenuifolia, commonly known as slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Blumea tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
