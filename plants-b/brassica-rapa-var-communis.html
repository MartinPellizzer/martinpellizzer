Brassica rapa var. communis

Brassica rapa var. communis, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) characterized by its thick, leafy stem and tightly wrapped leaves.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with a rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and occasionally its flowers, which are used in various Asian cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Brassica rapa var. communis is also known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a popular crop in regions with distinct seasonal changes.
