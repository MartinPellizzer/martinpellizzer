Blakwellia cerasifolia

Blakwellia cerasifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
