Banisteria purpurea

Banisteria purpurea, commonly known as the purple banisteria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and greenhouses.

Banisteria purpurea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
