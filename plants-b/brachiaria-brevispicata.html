Brachiaria brevispicata

Brachiaria brevispicata, commonly known as Brazilian grass or shortawn blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it a common component of grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has short, slender leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Brachiaria brevispicata is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
