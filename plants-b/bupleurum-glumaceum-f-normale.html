Bupleurum glumaceum f. normale

Bupleurum glumaceum f. normale, commonly known as the common bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
