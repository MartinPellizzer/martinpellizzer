Bauhinia japonica var. subrhombicarpa

Bauhinia japonica var. subrhombicarpa, commonly known as the Japanese bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with oval to elliptic leaflets that are usually glabrous. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
