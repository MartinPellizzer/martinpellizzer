Baptisia australis var. aberrans

Baptisia australis var. aberrans, commonly known as the scarlet false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its root system consists of a taproot with lateral branches, which helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
