Brachyelytrum erectum subsp. japonicum

Brachyelytrum erectum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
