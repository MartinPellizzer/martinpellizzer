Birchea

Birchea, commonly known as the birch tree, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs in the family Betulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their papery, peeling bark and alternate, simple leaves that are often serrated. Birchea species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They produce small, catkin-like flowers and winged fruits called samaras.

The genus includes several species, such as the paper birch and the yellow birch, which are valued for their ecological and ornamental significance.
