Barleriola solanifolia

Barleriola solanifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Barleriola solanifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinator populations.
