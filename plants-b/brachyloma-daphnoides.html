Brachyloma daphnoides

Brachyloma daphnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
