Brassica rapa var. sarcorhiza

Brassica rapa var. sarcorhiza, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters and develops round, smooth pods containing edible seeds. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, seeds, and oil, and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various climates.

Brassica rapa var. sarcorhiza is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in agricultural systems.
