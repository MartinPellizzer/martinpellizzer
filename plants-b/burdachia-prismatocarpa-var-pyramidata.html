Burdachia prismatocarpa var. pyramidata

Burdachia prismatocarpa var. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pyramid-shaped flower clusters that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
