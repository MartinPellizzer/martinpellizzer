Begonia fissisepala

Begonia fissisepala, commonly known as the fissured sepals begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its sepals are notably split or fissured, giving it its scientific name.

Begonia fissisepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
