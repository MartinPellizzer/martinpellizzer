Barbarea patens

Barbarea patens, commonly known as the creeping winter cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is characterized by its low-growing habit with fleshy, lobed leaves. Its small yellow flowers appear in spring and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Barbarea patens is often considered a weed but can also serve as a food source for certain insects and animals.
