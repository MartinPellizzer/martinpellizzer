Baccharis avicenniifolia

Baccharis avicenniifolia, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy, saline soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
