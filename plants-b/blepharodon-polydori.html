Blepharodon polydori

Blepharodon polydori, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
