Broussonetia × kazinoki

Broussonetia × kazinoki, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its adaptability to various soil types.
