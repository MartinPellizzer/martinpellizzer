Baccaurea bracteata

Baccaurea bracteata, commonly known as the red rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit that turns red when mature. The plant thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, fibrous pulp and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
