Bromus erectus subsp. barcensis

Bromus erectus subsp.

barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows.

The plant has erect, sometimes spreading stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.
