Bromus pumpellianus

Bromus pumpellianus, commonly known as the pumpellian brome, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Bromus pumpellianus is often found in alpine meadows and is a component of the vegetation in arid and semi-arid regions.
