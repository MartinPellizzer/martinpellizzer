Bidens eatonii var. fallax

Bidens eatonii var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
