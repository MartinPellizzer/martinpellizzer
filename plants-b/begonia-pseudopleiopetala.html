Begonia pseudopleiopetala

Begonia pseudopleiopetala, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to gardens and indoor spaces.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
