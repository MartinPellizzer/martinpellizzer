Blepharocalyx cuspidatus

Blepharocalyx cuspidatus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
