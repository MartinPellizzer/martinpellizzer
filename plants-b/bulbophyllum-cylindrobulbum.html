Bulbophyllum cylindrobulbum

Bulbophyllum cylindrobulbum, commonly known as the cylindrical bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare orchids.
