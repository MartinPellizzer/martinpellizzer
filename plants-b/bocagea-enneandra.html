Bocagea enneandra

Bocagea enneandra, commonly known as the Florida bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Bocagea enneandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
