Brassica barrelieri

Brassica barrelieri, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows as a low, compact shrub with grayish-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in hot, dry environments and is adapted to conserve water through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Brassica barrelieri is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
