Bolboschoenus capensis

Bolboschoenus capensis, commonly known as the Cape bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation for various wildlife.

Bolboschoenus capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
