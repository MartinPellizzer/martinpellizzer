Bloomeria montana

Bloomeria montana, commonly known as the mountain bloom, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

Bloomeria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
