Bursera martiana

Bursera martiana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.

The tree is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine for its resin and leaves.
