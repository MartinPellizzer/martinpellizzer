Begonia argentea

Begonia argentea, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia argentea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and ease of care.
