Byrsella atahualpa

Byrsella atahualpa, commonly known as the Atahualpa cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically ribbed and covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. It is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
