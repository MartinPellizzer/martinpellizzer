Bignonia angustifolia

Bignonia angustifolia, commonly known as crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, narrow, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing trees or shrubs.

Bignonia angustifolia is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in warm climates.
