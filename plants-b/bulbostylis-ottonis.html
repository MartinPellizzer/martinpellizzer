Bulbostylis ottonis

Bulbostylis ottonis, commonly known as the golden bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a specialty plant.
