Butea pottingeri

Butea pottingeri, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Butea pottingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
