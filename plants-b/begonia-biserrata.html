Begonia biserrata

Begonia biserrata, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Begonia biserrata is known for its ability to produce numerous small, tuberous roots that help it survive in varying environmental conditions.
