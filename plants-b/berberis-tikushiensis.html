Berberis tikushiensis

Berberis tikushiensis, commonly known as the Georgian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
