Butia capitata var. purpurascens

Butia capitata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple palm, is a striking variation of the bottle palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-pink coloration on the trunk and leaves, which becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces long, feather-like leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique coloration, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscapes and botanical gardens.
