Billbergia paniculata

Billbergia paniculata, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, with vibrant pink bracts surrounding small, white flowers. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can store water, a feature adapted to its natural habitat in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
