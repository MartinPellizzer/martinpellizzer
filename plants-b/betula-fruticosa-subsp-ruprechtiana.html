Betula fruticosa subsp. ruprechtiana

Betula fruticosa subsp.

ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Ruprecht's birch, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, triangular leaves that are typically hairy on both surfaces.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its bark is thin and peeling, often revealing a pale, papery texture. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
