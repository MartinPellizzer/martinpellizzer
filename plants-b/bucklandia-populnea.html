Bucklandia populnea

Bucklandia populnea, commonly known as the red swamp lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows from a bulbous rootstock and has strap-like, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Bucklandia populnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
