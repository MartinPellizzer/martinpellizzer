Blumea lanuginosa

Blumea lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Blumea lanuginosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as forest edges and disturbed areas.
