Baptisia australis

Baptisia australis, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its root system is fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Baptisia australis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
