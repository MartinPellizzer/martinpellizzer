Banisteriopsis vernoniifolia

Banisteriopsis vernoniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or "cereja do Brasil," is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
