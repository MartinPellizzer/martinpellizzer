Bulbophyllum singaporeanum

Bulbophyllum singaporeanum, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It requires well-draining substrates and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
