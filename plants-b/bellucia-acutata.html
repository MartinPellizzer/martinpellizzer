Bellucia acutata

Bellucia acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bellucia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by colorful bracts.

Bellucia acutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
