Berchemia brachycarpa

Berchemia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, narrow, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Berchemia brachycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
