Bidens phelloptera

Bidens phelloptera, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has feather-like leaves that are often hairy, and it produces seeds with pappus structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and wetlands.

Bidens phelloptera is also known for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
