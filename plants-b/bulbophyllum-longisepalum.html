Bulbophyllum longisepalum

Bulbophyllum longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs and its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Bulbophyllum longisepalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
