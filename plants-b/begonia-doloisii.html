Begonia doloisii

Begonia doloisii, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
