Bignonia longisiliqua

Bignonia longisiliqua, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb trees and structures.

Bignonia longisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
