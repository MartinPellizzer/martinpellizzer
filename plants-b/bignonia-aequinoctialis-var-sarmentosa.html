Bignonia aequinoctialis var. sarmentosa

Bignonia aequinoctialis var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping bignonia, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its vigorous growth habit, it is often used as a decorative vine in gardens and landscapes.
