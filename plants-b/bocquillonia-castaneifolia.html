Bocquillonia castaneifolia

Bocquillonia castaneifolia, commonly known as the chestnut-leaved bocquillonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which resemble those of the chestnut tree, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
