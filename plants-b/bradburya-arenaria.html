Bradburya arenaria

Bradburya arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Bradburya arenaria thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of desert ecosystems.
