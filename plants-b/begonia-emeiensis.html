Begonia emeiensis

Begonia emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei begonia, is a species native to China, specifically the Sichuan province.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

Begonia emeiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
