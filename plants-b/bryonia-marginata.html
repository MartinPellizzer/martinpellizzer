Bryonia marginata

Bryonia marginata, commonly known as the marginated bryony, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, prickly stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to orange, spiny fruits. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Bryonia marginata contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
