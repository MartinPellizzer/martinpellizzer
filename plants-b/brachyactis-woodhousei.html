Brachyactis woodhousei

Brachyactis woodhousei, commonly known as the shortleaf flax or narrowleaf flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in loose spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
