Begonia × ameliae

Begonia × ameliae, commonly known as the Amelia begonia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia × tuberhybrida.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with contrasting patterns. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia × ameliae is widely cultivated in both indoor and outdoor settings.
