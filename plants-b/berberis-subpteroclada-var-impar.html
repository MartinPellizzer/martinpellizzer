Berberis subpteroclada var. impar

Berberis subpteroclada var. impar, commonly known as the irregular-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to specific regions of China.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive irregular shape, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and specialized collections.
