Bacopa salzmannii var. coerulea

Bacopa salzmannii var. coerulea, commonly known as blue bacopa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically opposite and oval in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive benefits.
