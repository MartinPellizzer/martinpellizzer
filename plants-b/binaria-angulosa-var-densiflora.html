Binaria angulosa var. densiflora

Binaria angulosa var. densiflora, commonly known as the angular leaf fern, is a variant of the angular fern characterized by its dense, clustered fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
