Bomarea glomerata var. longifolia

Bomarea glomerata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
