Bifaria disticha

Bifaria disticha, commonly known as the twin-leaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or reddish hues.

Bifaria disticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
