Berberis wangii

Berberis wangii, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often sour. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.

Berberis wangii is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional Chinese medicine.
