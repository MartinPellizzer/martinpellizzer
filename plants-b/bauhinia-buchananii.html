Bauhinia buchananii

Bauhinia buchananii, commonly known as the African maple or the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves that turn a striking blue-green color during the dry season. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or violet, appearing in clusters during the warmer months. Bauhinia buchananii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
