Bactris piscatorum

Bactris piscatorum, commonly known as the fishing palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems and traditional agriculture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
