Blakea micrantha

Blakea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered blakea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
