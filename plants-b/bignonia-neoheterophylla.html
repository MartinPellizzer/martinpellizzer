Bignonia neoheterophylla

Bignonia neoheterophylla, commonly known as the Neoheterophylla crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow to orange tubular flowers. It typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
