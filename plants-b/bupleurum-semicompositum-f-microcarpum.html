Bupleurum semicompositum f. microcarpum

Bupleurum semicompositum f. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited bishop's weed, is a botanical variant of the Bupleurum genus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Its fruits are small and capsule-like, containing several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological traits and potential medicinal properties.
