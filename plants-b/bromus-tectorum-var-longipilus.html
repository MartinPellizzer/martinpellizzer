Bromus tectorum var. longipilus

Bromus tectorum var. longipilus, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive plant in some regions.
