Baccharis umbelliformis var. vulgaris

Baccharis umbelliformis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and dry woodlands.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in many regions of its native range.
