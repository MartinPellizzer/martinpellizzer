Bidens colpophila

Bidens colpophila, commonly known as the slender tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
