Bernardia hirsutissima var. genuina

Bernardia hirsutissima var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly Bernardia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies.
