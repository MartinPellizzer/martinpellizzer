Brassica deflexa

Brassica deflexa, commonly known as the curled mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and its distinctive, curled leaves that give it its common name. Brassica deflexa typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is an annual plant that produces small, round pods containing several seeds.
