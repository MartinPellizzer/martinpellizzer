Bergia pentheriana

Bergia pentheriana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bergia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Bergia pentheriana is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and delicate blooms, which make it a popular choice among gardeners.
