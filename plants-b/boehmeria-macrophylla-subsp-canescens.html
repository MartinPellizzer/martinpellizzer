Boehmeria macrophylla subsp. canescens

Boehmeria macrophylla subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the woolly giant bamboo, is a member of the Cannabaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant grows as a tall, herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of several meters. Its stems are fibrous and can be harvested for use in papermaking and textile production.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
