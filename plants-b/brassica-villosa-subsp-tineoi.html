Brassica villosa subsp. tineoi

Brassica villosa subsp.

tineoi, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glabrous, or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
