Boesenbergia regalis

Boesenbergia regalis, commonly known as the red ginger or *Boesenbergia regalis*, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green or reddish-purple in color. The plant produces striking, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomes are valued in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Boesenbergia regalis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage and flowers.
