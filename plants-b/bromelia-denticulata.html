Bromelia denticulata

Bromelia denticulata, commonly known as the serrated bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
