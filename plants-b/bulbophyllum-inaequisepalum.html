Bulbophyllum inaequisepalum

Bulbophyllum inaequisepalum, commonly known as the unequal-sepal orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring sepals of varying sizes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
