Brasolia boliviensis

Brasolia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
