Baileya pleniradiata var. thurberi

Baileya pleniradiata var. thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber's plumeless dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
