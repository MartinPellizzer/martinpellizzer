Barbarea rupestris

Barbarea rupestris, commonly known as the rock chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in crevices and on rocky slopes. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Barbarea rupestris is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
