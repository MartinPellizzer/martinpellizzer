Brachypodium rigidum

Brachypodium rigidum, commonly known as rigid brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but is also valued for its use in ecological restoration projects.
