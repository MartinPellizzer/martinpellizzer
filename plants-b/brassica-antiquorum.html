Brassica antiquorum

Brassica antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has dark green, lobed leaves. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Brassica antiquorum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
