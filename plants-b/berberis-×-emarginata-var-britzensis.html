Berberis × emarginata var. britzensis

Berberis × emarginata var. britzensis, commonly known as the Britzensis barberry, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Berberis emarginata and Berberis × emarginata.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that typically have three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
