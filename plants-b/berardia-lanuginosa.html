Berardia lanuginosa

Berardia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly berardia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm months of the year. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
