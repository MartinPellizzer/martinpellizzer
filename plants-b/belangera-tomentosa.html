Belangera tomentosa

Belangera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to varying climatic conditions.

Belangera tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
