Buddleja cordata subsp. tomentella

Buddleja cordata subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its greyish, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Oleaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its subspecies status highlights genetic variation within the broader Buddleja cordata species.
