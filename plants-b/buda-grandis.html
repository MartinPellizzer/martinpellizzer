Buda grandis

Buda grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum budi*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Buda grandis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
