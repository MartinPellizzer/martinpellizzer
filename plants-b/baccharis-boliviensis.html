Baccharis boliviensis

Baccharis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis boliviensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
