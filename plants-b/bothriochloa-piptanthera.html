Bothriochloa piptanthera

Bothriochloa piptanthera, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
