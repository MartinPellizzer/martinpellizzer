Bambusa rutila

Bambusa rutila, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its hollow, segmented stalks are strong and flexible, making it suitable for various ornamental and practical uses.

Bambusa rutila is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
