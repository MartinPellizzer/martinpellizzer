Blechnum japonicum

Blechnum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Blechnum japonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
