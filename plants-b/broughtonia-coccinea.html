Broughtonia coccinea

Broughtonia coccinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red stems and colorful, long-lasting bracts. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy of humid forests. Its leaves form a rosette that collects rainwater, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Broughtonia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
