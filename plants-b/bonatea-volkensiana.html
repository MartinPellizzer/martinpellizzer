Bonatea volkensiana

Bonatea volkensiana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid conditions and can withstand periods of drought.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
