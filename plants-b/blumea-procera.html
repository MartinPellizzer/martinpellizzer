Blumea procera

Blumea procera, commonly known as the tall blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blumea procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
