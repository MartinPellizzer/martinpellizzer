Begonia fusialata var. parviflora

Begonia fusialata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
