Boehmeria blinii var. podocarpa

Boehmeria blinii var. podocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Chinese mulberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which has historical uses in papermaking and textiles.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
