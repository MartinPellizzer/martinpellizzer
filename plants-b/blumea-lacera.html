Blumea lacera

Blumea lacera, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Blumea lacera is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
