Baroniella longicornis

Baroniella longicornis, commonly known as the longhorned baroniella, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in its native habitat.
