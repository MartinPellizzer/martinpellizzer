Bromus canus

Bromus canus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are covered in fine hairs.

Bromus canus is often found in temperate climates and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
