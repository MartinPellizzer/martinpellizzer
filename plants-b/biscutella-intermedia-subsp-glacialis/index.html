Biscutella Intermedia Subsp. Glacialis

Biscutella intermedia subsp. glacialis is a rare and cold-tolerant plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and Carpathians. It thrives in rocky and arid environments where few other species can survive. This subspecies is known for its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its unique adaptations make it an important subject for ecological and botanical research. Despite its resilience, it faces threats from climate change and habitat loss.
