Baccharoides calvoana subsp. leucocalyx

Baccharoides calvoana subsp.

leucocalyx, commonly known as white-flowered baccharoides, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are usually pubescent.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its inflorescences are typically terminal and consist of numerous small, showy blooms.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as cloud forests and mountain valleys.
