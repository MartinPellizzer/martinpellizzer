Bulbophyllum upupops

Bulbophyllum upupops, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to the plant's dark foliage.
