Breynia micrasterias

Breynia micrasterias, commonly known as the coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

Breynia micrasterias is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
