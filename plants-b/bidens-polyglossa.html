Bidens polyglossa

Bidens polyglossa, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features small yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of two, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils.

Bidens polyglossa is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
