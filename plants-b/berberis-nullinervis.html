Berberis nullinervis

Berberis nullinervis, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
