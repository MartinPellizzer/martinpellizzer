Botrys ambrosioides

Botrys ambrosioides, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Botrys ambrosioides is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
