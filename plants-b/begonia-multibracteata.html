Begonia multibracteata

Begonia multibracteata, commonly known as the multibracteata begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
