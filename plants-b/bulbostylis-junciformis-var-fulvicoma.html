Bulbostylis junciformis var. fulvicoma

Bulbostylis junciformis var. fulvicoma, commonly known as the golden-thread sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
