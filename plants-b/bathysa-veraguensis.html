Bathysa veraguensis

Bathysa veraguensis, commonly known as the Veraguas bathysa, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Bathysa veraguensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
