Bulbostylis eustachii

Bulbostylis eustachii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
