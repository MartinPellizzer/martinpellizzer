Brassica papillaris

Brassica papillaris, commonly known as the warty mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and rough, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are dry, pod-like structures typical of the mustard family.

Brassica papillaris is primarily cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.
