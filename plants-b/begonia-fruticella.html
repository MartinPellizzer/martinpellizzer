Begonia fruticella

Begonia fruticella, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found growing in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually dark green, glossy, and have a velvety texture.

Begonia fruticella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
