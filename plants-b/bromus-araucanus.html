Bromus araucanus

Bromus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucanian brome, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it flowers during the spring and early summer.

Bromus araucanus is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
