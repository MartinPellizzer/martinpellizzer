Brassica oleracea var. frutescens

Brassica oleracea var. frutescens, commonly known as mustard greens, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and rough, serrated leaves that are often harvested for culinary use. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves and seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
