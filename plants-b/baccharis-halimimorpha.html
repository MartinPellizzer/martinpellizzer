Baccharis halimimorpha

Baccharis halimimorpha, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Baccharis halimimorpha is also known for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in xeriscaped gardens.
