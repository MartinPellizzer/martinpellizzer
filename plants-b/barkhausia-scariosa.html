Barkhausia scariosa

Barkhausia scariosa, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, scaly bark, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Barkhausia scariosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
