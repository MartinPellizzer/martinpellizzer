Begonia adenopoda

Begonia adenopoda, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscape design.
