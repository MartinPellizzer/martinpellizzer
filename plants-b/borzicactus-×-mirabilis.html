Borzicactus × mirabilis

Borzicactus × mirabilis, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a fascinating plant that combines traits from its parent species, Borzicactus and Echinocactus.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often adorned with spines and sometimes flowers. The plant is native to arid regions of the Americas, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

However, it requires careful handling due to its sharp spines and needs a specific climate to flourish.
