Bignonia giesbreghtii

Bignonia giesbreghtii, commonly known as the Giesbrecht's crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia giesbreghtii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
