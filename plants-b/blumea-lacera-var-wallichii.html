Blumea lacera var. wallichii

Blumea lacera var. wallichii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as forests and grasslands.
