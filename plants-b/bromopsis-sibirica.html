Bromopsis sibirica

Bromopsis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that resembles a feather. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. Bromopsis sibirica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
