Bupleurum semicompositum f. normale

Bupleurum semicompositum f. normale, commonly known as the common silvery bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, pinnately compound leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
