Brassica campestris var. afghanica

Brassica campestris var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan mustard, is a variety of the mustard plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with serrated leaves and a strong, pungent odor. Its seeds are small and black, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments.
