Bromus hordeaceus f. leptostachys

Bromus hordeaceus f. leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stalked brome, is a botanical variant of the species Bromus hordeaceus.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, often tufted stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. This form is typically found in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
