Bactris glandulosa

Bactris glandulosa, commonly known as the red raphia, is a tropical palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are small and round. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used for thatching and the fruit is consumed both fresh and dried.
