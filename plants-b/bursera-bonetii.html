Bursera bonetii

Bursera bonetii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, clustered leaves that are often shed during dry seasons. It produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and woodland areas.
