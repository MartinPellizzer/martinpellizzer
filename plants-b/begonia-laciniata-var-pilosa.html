Begonia laciniata var. pilosa

Begonia laciniata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage.

The leaves are typically large, deeply lobed, and have a textured surface that gives the plant a unique appearance. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, adding visual interest. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
