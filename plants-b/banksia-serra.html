Banksia serra

Banksia serra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a species native to southwestern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and its unique cylindrical flower spikes. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and are arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences.

Banksia serra plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
