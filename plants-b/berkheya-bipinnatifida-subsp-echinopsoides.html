Berkheya bipinnatifida subsp. echinopsoides

Berkheya bipinnatifida subsp.

echinopsoides, commonly known as the African cowpea or the horned burkea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny, globular seed pods that resemble small horns.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its unique, woody capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
