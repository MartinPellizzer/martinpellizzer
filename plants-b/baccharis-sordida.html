Baccharis sordida

Baccharis sordida, commonly known as black baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, often fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in arid regions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
