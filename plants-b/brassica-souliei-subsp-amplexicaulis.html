Brassica souliei subsp. amplexicaulis

Brassica souliei subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to other mustard species.

It is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes in certain regions.
