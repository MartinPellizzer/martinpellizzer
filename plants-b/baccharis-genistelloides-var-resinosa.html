Baccharis genistelloides var. resinosa

Baccharis genistelloides var. resinosa, commonly known as the resinous baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves that emit a distinct scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters during the late spring and summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
