Bulbophyllum boulbetii

Bulbophyllum boulbetii, commonly known as the Boulbet's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitat. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
