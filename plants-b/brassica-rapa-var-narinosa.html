Brassica rapa var. narinosa

Brassica rapa var. narinosa, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or nose-root, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, followed by spherical, hairy seed pods. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

The variety is notable for its aromatic properties and use in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
