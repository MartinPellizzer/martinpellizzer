Brassica cossoniana var. rifana

Brassica cossoniana var. rifana, commonly known as the Rif mustard, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of mustard oil.
