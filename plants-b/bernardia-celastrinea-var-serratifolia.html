Bernardia celastrinea var. serratifolia

Bernardia celastrinea var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf bernardia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
