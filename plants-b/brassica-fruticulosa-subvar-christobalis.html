Brassica fruticulosa subvar. christobalis

Brassica fruticulosa subvar. christobalis, commonly known as the Christobal's mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lobed and have a peppery taste, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
