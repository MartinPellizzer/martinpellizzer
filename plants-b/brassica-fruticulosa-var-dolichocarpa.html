Brassica fruticulosa var. dolichocarpa

Brassica fruticulosa var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-seeded mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with small, dark green leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
