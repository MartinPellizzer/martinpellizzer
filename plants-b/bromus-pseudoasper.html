Bromus pseudoasper

Bromus pseudoasper, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Bromus pseudoasper is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
