Bauhinia lingua

Bauhinia lingua, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. Bauhinia lingua has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
