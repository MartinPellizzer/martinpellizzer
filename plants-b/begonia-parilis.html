Begonia parilis

Begonia parilis, commonly known as the Parilis begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or in shaded, moist environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring contrasting patterns of green and white or yellow.

Begonia parilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
