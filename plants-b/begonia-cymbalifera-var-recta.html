Begonia cymbalifera var. recta

Begonia cymbalifera var. recta, commonly known as the straight-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and striking red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
