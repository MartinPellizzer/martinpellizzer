Baldingeria pygmaea

Baldingeria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf false indigo, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
