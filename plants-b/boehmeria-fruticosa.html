Boehmeria fruticosa

Boehmeria fruticosa, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Chinese mulberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fibrous stems are used in traditional crafts and for making paper.

Boehmeria fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
