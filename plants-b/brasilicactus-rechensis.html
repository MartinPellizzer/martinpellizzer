Brasilicactus rechensis

Brasilicactus rechensis, commonly known as the Rech's brasilicactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
