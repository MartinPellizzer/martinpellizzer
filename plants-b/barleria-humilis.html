Barleria humilis

Barleria humilis, commonly known as the dwarf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
