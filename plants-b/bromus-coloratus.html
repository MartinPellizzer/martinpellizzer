Bromus coloratus

Bromus coloratus, commonly known as the purple grass or bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purple in color. Bromus coloratus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
