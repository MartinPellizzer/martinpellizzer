Bulbophyllum selangorense

Bulbophyllum selangorense, commonly known as the Selangor orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Bulbophyllum selangorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
