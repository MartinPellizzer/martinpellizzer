Bambusa argentea

Bambusa argentea, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking silver-white sheathes on its stems. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its hollow, jointed stalks are used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility.

Bambusa argentea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
