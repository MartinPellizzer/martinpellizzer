Bauhinia cardinalis

Bauhinia cardinalis, commonly known as the crimson bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like bipinnate leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bauhinia cardinalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
