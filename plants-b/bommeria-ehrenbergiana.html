Bommeria ehrenbergiana

Bommeria ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in dry climates.

Bommeria ehrenbergiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
