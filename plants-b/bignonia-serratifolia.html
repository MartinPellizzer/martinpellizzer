Bignonia serratifolia

Bignonia serratifolia, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bignonia serratifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, flowering vine.
