Brillantaisia pubescens

Brillantaisia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy brillantaisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
