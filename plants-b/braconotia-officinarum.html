Braconotia officinarum

Braconotia officinarum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface. This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
