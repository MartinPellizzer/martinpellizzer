Berberis chilensis var. brachybotria

Berberis chilensis var. brachybotria, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
