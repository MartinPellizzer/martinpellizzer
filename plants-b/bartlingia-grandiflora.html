Bartlingia grandiflora

Bartlingia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered barlingia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bartlingia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
