Burnatastrum spicatum

Burnatastrum spicatum, commonly known as the spike blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with narrow, gray-green leaves and showy yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Burnatastrum spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
