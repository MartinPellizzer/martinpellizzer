Baeria consanguinea

Baeria consanguinea, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Baeria consanguinea is known for its stinging hairs, similar to those found in true nettles.
