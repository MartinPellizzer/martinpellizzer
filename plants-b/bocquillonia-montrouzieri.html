Bocquillonia montrouzieri

Bocquillonia montrouzieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and distinctive plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant collectors.
