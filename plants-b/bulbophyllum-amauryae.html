Bulbophyllum amauryae

Bulbophyllum amauryae, commonly known as the Amaury's orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its pseudobulbs are thick and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum amauryae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
