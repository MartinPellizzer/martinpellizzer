Bulbostylis juncoides var. ampliceps

Bulbostylis juncoides var. ampliceps, commonly known as the broad-headed bulblet sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets that are typically found in wetland or marshy habitats. The plant produces bulb-like structures at the base of its stems, which are adaptations for vegetative propagation and survival in fluctuating water levels. It is native to regions of Africa and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
