Bromus racemosus var. subsecalinus

Bromus racemosus var. subsecalinus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear spikelets arranged in dense, raceme-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
