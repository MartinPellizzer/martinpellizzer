Begonia siregarii

Begonia siregarii, commonly known as the Siregari begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
