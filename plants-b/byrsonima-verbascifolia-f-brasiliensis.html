Byrsonima verbascifolia f. brasiliensis

Byrsonima verbascifolia f. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian wild mallow, is a shrub native to the Brazilian Cerrado and Atlantic Forest ecosystems.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil, contributing to ecological balance.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
