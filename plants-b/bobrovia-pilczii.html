Bobrovia pilczii

Bobrovia pilczii, commonly known as the Pilczynski's broom, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Bobrovia pilczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
