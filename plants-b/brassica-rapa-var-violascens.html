Brassica rapa var. violascens

Brassica rapa var. violascens, commonly known as purple sprouting broccoli, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, violet-colored flower clusters that emerge in the spring, typically after the plant has overwintered. The plant produces thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are edible, often used in cooking for their mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in both gardens and culinary applications.
