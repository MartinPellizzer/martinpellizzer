Begonia berhamanii

Begonia berhamanii, commonly known as the Khasi begonia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
