Brassavola flagellaris

Brassavola flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers have a distinctive, elongated shape, which gives the species its common name.

Brassavola flagellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
