Brassica tenuifolia

Brassica tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, round pods containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a leafy green vegetable.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
