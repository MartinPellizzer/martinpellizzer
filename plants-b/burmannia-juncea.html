Burmannia juncea

Burmannia juncea, commonly known as the yellow burmannia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has a short, hairy stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Burmannia juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
