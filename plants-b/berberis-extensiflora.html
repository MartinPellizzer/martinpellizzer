Berberis extensiflora

Berberis extensiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Berberis extensiflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
