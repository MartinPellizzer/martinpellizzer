Blepharipappus douglasii var. oligochaeta

Blepharipappus douglasii var. oligochaeta, commonly known as the Douglas's ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

The species is notable for its ephemeral blooming period, typically occurring in late spring to early summer.
