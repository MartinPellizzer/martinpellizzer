Betonica virgultorum

Betonica virgultorum, commonly known as the lesser betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, producing spikes of blue to purple flowers.

Betonica virgultorum is known for its medicinal properties, historically used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
