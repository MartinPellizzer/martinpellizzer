Bakerella clavata var. peralata

Bakerella clavata var. peralata, commonly known as the silverleaf bakerella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
