Baccharis limnophila

Baccharis limnophila, commonly known as marsh baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, woody fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

Baccharis limnophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
