Bothriospermum hispidissimum

Bothriospermum hispidissimum, commonly known as the rough-seeded sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
