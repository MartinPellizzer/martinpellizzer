Baccharis guianensis

Baccharis guianensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, such as riverbanks and floodplains. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
