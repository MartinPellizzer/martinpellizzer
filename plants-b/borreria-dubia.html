Borreria dubia

Borreria dubia, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting well to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, helping to reduce water loss.

Borreria dubia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
