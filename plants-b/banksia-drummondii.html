Banksia drummondii

Banksia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody cones that contain seeds, a feature common among many banksia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Banksia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
