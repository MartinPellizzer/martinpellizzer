Bromus wolgensis var. turgidus

Bromus wolgensis var. turgidus, commonly known as the turgid bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often exhibit a bluish-green color. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that are usually hairy and pointed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
