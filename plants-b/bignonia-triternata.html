Bignonia triternata

Bignonia triternata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia triternata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.
