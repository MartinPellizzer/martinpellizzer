Begonia turrialbae

Begonia turrialbae, commonly known as the Turrialba begonia, is a species native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
