Bulbophyllum flabellum-veneris

Bulbophyllum flabellum-veneris, commonly known as the Venus's Fan Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fan-shaped inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular specimen among horticultural enthusiasts.
