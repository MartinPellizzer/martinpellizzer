Bulbophyllum clandestinum

Bulbophyllum clandestinum, commonly known as the hidden bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically linear and grass-like, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
