Berberis qamdoensis

Berberis qamdoensis, commonly known as the Qamdo barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, holly-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Berberis qamdoensis is often found in rocky soils and is a common plant in the alpine regions of western China.
