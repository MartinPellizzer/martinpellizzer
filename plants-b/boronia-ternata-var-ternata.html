Boronia ternata var. ternata

Boronia ternata var. ternata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a slight reddish hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
