Bromus scaberrimus

Bromus scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and spikelets that are often bristly or scabrous. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. It is an annual or perennial grass that can thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant's distinctive appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in its native range.
