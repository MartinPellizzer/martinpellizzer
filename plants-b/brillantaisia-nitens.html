Brillantaisia nitens

Brillantaisia nitens, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It prefers warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its vibrant floral display.
