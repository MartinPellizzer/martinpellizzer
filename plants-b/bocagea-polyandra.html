Bocagea polyandra

Bocagea polyandra, commonly known as the Florida beauty or redflower bocageia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

Bocagea polyandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
