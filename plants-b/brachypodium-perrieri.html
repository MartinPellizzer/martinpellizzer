Brachypodium perrieri

Brachypodium perrieri, commonly known as the dwarf brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a small, annual plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or arid environments.

Brachypodium perrieri is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic simplicity and potential use in ecological and agricultural research.
