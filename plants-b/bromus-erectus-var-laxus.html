Bromus erectus var. laxus

Bromus erectus var. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
