Baphia latiloi

Baphia latiloi, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Baphia latiloi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
