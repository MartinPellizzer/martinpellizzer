Barringtonia calyptrocalyx var. calyptrocalyx

Barringtonia calyptrocalyx var. calyptrocalyx, commonly known as the white ghost tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that emit a strong fragrance, especially at night. The tree has smooth, greyish bark and long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
