Brassica repanda subsp. humilis

Brassica repanda subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
