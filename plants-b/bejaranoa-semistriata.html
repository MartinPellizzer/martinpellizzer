Bejaranoa semistriata

Bejaranoa semistriata, commonly known as the Madagascan orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and often fragrant blooms. The plant typically features slender stems and leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Bejaranoa semistriata is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Madagascar, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.
