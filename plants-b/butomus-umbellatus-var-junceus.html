Butomus umbellatus var. junceus

Butomus umbellatus var. junceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spikelike flower clusters.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and the edges of lakes. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically greenish-yellow and produce small, wind-pollinated seeds.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
