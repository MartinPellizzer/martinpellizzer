Brachyotum andreanum

Brachyotum andreanum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Brachyotum andreanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
