Bassia tianschanica

Bassia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan rockweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
