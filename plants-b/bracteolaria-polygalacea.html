Bracteolaria polygalacea

Bracteolaria polygalacea, commonly known as the golden bellvile, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred corolla, which is a distinctive feature of the genus.

Bracteolaria polygalacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
