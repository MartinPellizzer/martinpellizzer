Bidens foeniculifolia

Bidens foeniculifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that give the plant a distinctive appearance.

Bidens foeniculifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, open habitats.
