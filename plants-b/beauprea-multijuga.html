Beauprea multijuga

Beauprea multijuga, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its growth in moist, well-drained soils.

The fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique feature that aids in seed dispersal by attracting nocturnal pollinators.
