Begonia locii

Begonia locii, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Begonia locii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in containers or rock gardens.
