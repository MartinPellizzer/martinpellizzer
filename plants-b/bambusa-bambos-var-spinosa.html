Bambusa bambos var. spinosa

Bambusa bambos var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure that makes it highly durable and suitable for various practical applications. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This bamboo variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
