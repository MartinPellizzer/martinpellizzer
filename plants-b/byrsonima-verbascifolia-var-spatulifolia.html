Byrsonima verbascifolia var. spatulifolia

Byrsonima verbascifolia var. spatulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wolfberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
