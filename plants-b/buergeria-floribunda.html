Buergeria floribunda

Buergeria floribunda, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Buergeria floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
