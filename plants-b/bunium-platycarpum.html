Bunium platycarpum

Bunium platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruit fennel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels and has a distinct, licorice-like scent. Its seeds are small, dark, and clustered in spherical heads, which are used in traditional medicine.

Bunium platycarpum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments and respiratory conditions.
