Begonia sulcata

Begonia sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Begonia sulcata thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
