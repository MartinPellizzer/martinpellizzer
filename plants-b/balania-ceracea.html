Balania ceracea

Balania ceracea, commonly known as the silky sponge plant, is a unique species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, silky coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments where it plays a role in stabilizing the sand. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Balania ceracea is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations to coastal ecosystems.
