Begonia grata

Begonia grata, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from silver to deep green. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
