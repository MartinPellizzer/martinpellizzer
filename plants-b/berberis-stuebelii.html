Berberis stuebelii

Berberis stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive form and seasonal color changes.
