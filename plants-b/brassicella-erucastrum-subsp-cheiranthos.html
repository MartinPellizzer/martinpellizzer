Brassicella erucastrum subsp. cheiranthos

Brassicella erucastrum subsp.

cheiranthos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant features pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators in its native habitat.
