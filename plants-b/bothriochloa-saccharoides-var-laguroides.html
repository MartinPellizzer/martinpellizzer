Bothriochloa saccharoides var. laguroides

Bothriochloa saccharoides var. laguroides, commonly known as Laguroides grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
