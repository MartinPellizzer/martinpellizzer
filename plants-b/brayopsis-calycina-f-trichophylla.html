Brayopsis calycina f. trichophylla

Brayopsis calycina f. trichophylla, commonly known as the trichophylla form of the velvetleaf, is a variant of the species Brayopsis calycina.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and have a soft texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
