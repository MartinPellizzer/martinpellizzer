Brassaiopsis petelotii

Brassaiopsis petelotii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Brassaiopsis petelotii is considered a conservation concern.
