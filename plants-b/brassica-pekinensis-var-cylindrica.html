Brassica pekinensis var. cylindrica

Brassica pekinensis var. cylindrica, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, stalked leaves and flower clusters that resemble small cabbage heads. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible stems and leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and versatility in culinary applications.
