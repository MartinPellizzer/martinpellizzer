Brosimum lecointei

Brosimum lecointei, commonly known as the Guianese ceiba, is a large tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers and fruits that are important for local wildlife and traditional uses.
