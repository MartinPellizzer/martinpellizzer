Barleria pannosa

Barleria pannosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Barleria pannosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
