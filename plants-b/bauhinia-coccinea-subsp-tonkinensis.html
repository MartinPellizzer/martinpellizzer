Bauhinia coccinea subsp. tonkinensis

Bauhinia coccinea subsp.

tonkinensis, commonly known as the red bauhinia or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, papery flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
