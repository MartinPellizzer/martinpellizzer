Bidens tereticaulis var. sordida

Bidens tereticaulis var. sordida, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its flowering heads consist of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable presence in its native range.
