Brillantaisia subcordata

Brillantaisia subcordata, commonly known as the broad-leaved brillantaisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Brillantaisia subcordata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
