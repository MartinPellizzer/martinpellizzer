Berberis ambrozyana

Berberis ambrozyana, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Berberis ambrozyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
