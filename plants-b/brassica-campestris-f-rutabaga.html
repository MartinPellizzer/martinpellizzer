Brassica campestris f. rutabaga

Brassica campestris f. rutabaga, commonly known as rutabaga, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, yellowish root that is typically harvested in the fall. The plant has alternate, rough, and hairy leaves that are lobed or heart-shaped. It produces small, yellow, cross-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.

The rutabaga is a cool-season crop that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible root.
