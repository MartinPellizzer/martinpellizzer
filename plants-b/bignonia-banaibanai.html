Bignonia banaibanai

Bignonia banaibanai, commonly known as the Banaibanai trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia banaibanai is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
