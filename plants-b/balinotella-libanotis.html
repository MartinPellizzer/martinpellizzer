Balinotella libanotis

Balinotella libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive blooms.
