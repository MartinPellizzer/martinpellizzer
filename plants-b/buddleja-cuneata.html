Buddleja cuneata

Buddleja cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces panicles of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in a variety of garden conditions.
