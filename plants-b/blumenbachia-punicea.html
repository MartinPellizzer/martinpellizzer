Blumenbachia punicea

Blumenbachia punicea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to crimson petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it is noted for its sympodial growth habit and the presence of specialized structures called resupinate petals.
