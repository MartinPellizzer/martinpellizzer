Bulbophyllum longipiliferum

Bulbophyllum longipiliferum, commonly known as the long-hair lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring long, hairy filaments on the lip, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a complex arrangement that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
