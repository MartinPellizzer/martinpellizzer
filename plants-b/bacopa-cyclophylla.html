Bacopa cyclophylla

Bacopa cyclophylla, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the morning hours. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
