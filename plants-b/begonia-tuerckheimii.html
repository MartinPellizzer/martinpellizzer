Begonia tuerckheimii

Begonia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
