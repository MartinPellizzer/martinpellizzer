Buphthalmum aureum

Buphthalmum aureum, commonly known as the golden-eyed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble dandelions, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Buphthalmum aureum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sun-like blooms.
