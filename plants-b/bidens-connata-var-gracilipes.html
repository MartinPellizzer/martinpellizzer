Bidens connata var. gracilipes

Bidens connata var. gracilipes, commonly known as slender false beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its fruits are small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils.
