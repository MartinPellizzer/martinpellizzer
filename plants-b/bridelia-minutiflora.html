Bridelia minutiflora

Bridelia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Bridelia minutiflora is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
