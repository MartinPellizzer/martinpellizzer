Brosimum longistipulatum

Brosimum longistipulatum, commonly known as the long-stipule brosimum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in traditional medicine.
