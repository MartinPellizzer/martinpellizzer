Bulbine rigidula

Bulbine rigidula, commonly known as the rigid bulbous, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
