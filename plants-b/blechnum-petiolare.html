Blechnum petiolare

Blechnum petiolare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, serrated margin.
