Bactris trailiana

Bactris trailiana, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a straight, unbranched stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts by local communities. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains edible seeds.

Bactris trailiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
