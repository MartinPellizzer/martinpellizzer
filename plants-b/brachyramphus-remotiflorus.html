Brachyramphus remotiflorus

Brachyramphus remotiflorus, commonly known as the distant-flowered brachyramphus, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. Its flowers are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals that are slightly elongated.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
