Bulbophyllum papillosum

Bulbophyllum papillosum, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery inflorescence and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The leaves are typically elongated and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
