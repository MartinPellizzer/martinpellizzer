Bletia catenulata var. caerulea

Bletia catenulata var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
