Bulbinella peronata

Bulbinella peronata, commonly known as the pink rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas, and is adapted to thrive in harsh, arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
