Batemannia burtii

Batemannia burtii, commonly known as the African violets.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Africa, characterized by its rosette of velvety, waxy leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular houseplant.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
