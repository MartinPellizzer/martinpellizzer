Baccharis mucuchiesensis

Baccharis mucuchiesensis, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract various pollinators.

Baccharis mucuchiesensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
