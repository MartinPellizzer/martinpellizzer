Batis

Batis, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and slender, edible stems. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies or in wetlands. Batis is valued for its nutritional content, containing vitamins A and C, as well as minerals like iron and calcium.

It is widely cultivated in many Asian countries for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
