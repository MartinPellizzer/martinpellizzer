Bourreria succulenta var. canescens

Bourreria succulenta var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf burrgrass, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spiny, burr-like fruits. It is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
