Bulbophyllum cruciatum

Bulbophyllum cruciatum, commonly known as the Crossed Lip Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring lip segments that are crossed or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The genus Bulbophyllum is one of the largest in the orchid family, with many species exhibiting unique adaptations for survival in their natural habitats.
