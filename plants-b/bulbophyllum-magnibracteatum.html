Bulbophyllum magnibracteatum

Bulbophyllum magnibracteatum, commonly known as the large-bracted bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
