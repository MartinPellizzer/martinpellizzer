Bupleurum villosum

Bupleurum villosum, commonly known as hairy bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Bupleurum villosum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
