Bisquamaria macrophylla

Bisquamaria macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed bisquamaria, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
