Bauhinia harmsiana

Bauhinia harmsiana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink or red. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bauhinia harmsiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
