Bidens alba var. radiata

Bidens alba var. radiata, commonly known as the white beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with white ray florets, which resemble a daisy. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

This annual herb is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
