Balsamina mastersiana

Balsamina mastersiana, commonly known as the Malabar balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Balsamina mastersiana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
