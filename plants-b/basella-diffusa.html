Basella diffusa

Basella diffusa, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are thick and fleshy, with a slightly tangy flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for both ornamental and edible purposes.
