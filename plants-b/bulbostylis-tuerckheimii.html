Bulbostylis tuerckheimii

Bulbostylis tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clustered, bulb-like rhizomes that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
