Boea magellanica

Boea magellanica, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in the Andes region. The plant features slender, trailing stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside. Its flowers are pale blue or violet, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Boea magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
