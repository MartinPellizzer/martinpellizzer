Berberis wuyiensis

Berberis wuyiensis, commonly known as the Wuyi barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
