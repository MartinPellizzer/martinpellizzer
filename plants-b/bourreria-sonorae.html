Bourreria sonorae

Bourreria sonorae, commonly known as Sonoran burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, spiny fruits known as burrs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
