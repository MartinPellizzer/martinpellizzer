Bidens formosa

Bidens formosa, commonly known as the painted nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with distinctive white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, open areas like wetlands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Bidens formosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
