Begonia tagbanua

Begonia tagbanua, commonly known as the Tagbanua begonia, is a striking flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming during the warm months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
