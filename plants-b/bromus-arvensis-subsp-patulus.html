Bromus arvensis subsp. patulus

Bromus arvensis subsp.

patulus, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions.
