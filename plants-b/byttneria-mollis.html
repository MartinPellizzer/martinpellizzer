Byttneria mollis

Byttneria mollis, commonly known as silky mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.
