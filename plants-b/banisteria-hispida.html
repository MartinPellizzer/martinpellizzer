Banisteria hispida

Banisteria hispida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a unique, aromatic scent.

Banisteria hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
