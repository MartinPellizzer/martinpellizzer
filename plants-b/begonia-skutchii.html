Begonia skutchii

Begonia skutchii, commonly known as the Skutch's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Begonia skutchii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
