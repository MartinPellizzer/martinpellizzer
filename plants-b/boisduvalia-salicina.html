Boisduvalia salicina

Boisduvalia salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf boisduvalia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Boisduvalia salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
