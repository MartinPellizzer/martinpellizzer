Butinia nodosa

Butinia nodosa, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color and are clustered in loose racemes.

Butinia nodosa thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.
