Begonia gironellae

Begonia gironellae, commonly known as the Gironella begonia, is a species native to Ecuador.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Begoniaceae family, characterized by its distinctive foliage and vibrant blooms. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red or pink. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
