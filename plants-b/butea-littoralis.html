Butea littoralis

Butea littoralis, commonly known as the coastal flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Butea littoralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
