Brocchinia plumieri

Brocchinia plumieri, commonly known as the water tank bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, boat-shaped rosette of waxy, overlapping leaves that form a central water-holding reservoir. The leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface, and they are adapted to collect and store rainwater. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree branches or rocks.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
