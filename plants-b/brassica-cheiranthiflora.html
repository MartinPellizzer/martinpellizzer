Brassica cheiranthiflora

Brassica cheiranthiflora, commonly known as the rock cabbage, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
