Begonia goegoensis

Begonia goegoensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green color with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
