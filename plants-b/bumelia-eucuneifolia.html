Bumelia eucuneifolia

Bumelia eucuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Bumelia eucuneifolia is an important species for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
