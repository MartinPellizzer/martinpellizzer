Biscutella apula var. microcarpa

Biscutella apula var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited biscutella, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate and are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The fruits are tiny capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
