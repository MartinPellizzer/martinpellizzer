Bambusa textilis var. fusca

Bambusa textilis var. fusca, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast sharply with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves. This variety grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a dramatic addition to gardens and landscapes. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its rapid growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and structural purposes in horticulture.
