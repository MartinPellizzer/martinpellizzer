Basella volubilis

Basella volubilis, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
