Bignonia incarnata var. caribaea

Bignonia incarnata var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking orange to red tubular flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
