Bomarea jacquesiana

Bomarea jacquesiana, commonly known as the Jacques' bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Bomarea jacquesiana is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
