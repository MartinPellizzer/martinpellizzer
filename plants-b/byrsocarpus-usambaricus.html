Byrsocarpus usambaricus

Byrsocarpus usambaricus, commonly known as the Usambara mountain cucumber, is a climbing vine native to the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, gourds, and squash. The plant produces small, green, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and woody.
