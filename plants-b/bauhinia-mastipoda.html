Bauhinia mastipoda

Bauhinia mastipoda, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a small, deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia mastipoda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
