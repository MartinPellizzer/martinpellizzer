Bupleurum salicifolium subsp. aciphyllum

Bupleurum salicifolium subsp.

aciphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, usually appearing in shades of yellow or white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of B. salicifolium.
