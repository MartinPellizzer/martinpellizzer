Brassia spathacea

Brassia spathacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brassia spathacea thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.
