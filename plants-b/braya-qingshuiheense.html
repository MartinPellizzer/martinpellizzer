Braya qingshuiheense

Braya qingshuiheense, commonly known as the Qingshuihe brakecress, is a rare and endemic species native to the Qinghai Lake region in China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and stipitate glands. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
