Berkheya bipinnatifida

Berkheya bipinnatifida, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnatifid leaves, which are deeply divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Berkheya bipinnatifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
