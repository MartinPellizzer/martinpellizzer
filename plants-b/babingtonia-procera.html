Babingtonia procera

Babingtonia procera, commonly known as the mountain gum or white gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Babingtonia procera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
