Blechnum violaceum

Blechnum violaceum, commonly known as the violet fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark purple to violet-colored fronds, which are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is known for its delicate, finely divided leaves that often have a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are clustered in sori, a key feature of fern reproduction.
