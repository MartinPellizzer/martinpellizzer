Bothriocline grandicapitulata

Bothriocline grandicapitulata, commonly known as the large-headed bushveld daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow with a central disc. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local biodiversity and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
