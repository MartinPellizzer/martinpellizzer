Bulbine pendula

Bulbine pendula, commonly known as the hanging bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its stems are often pendulous, giving it a distinctive cascading appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
