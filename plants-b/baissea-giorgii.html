Baissea giorgii

Baissea giorgii, commonly known as the Ethiopian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Baissea giorgii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in regions with a warm climate.
