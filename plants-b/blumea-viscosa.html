Blumea viscosa

Blumea viscosa, commonly known as sticky blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture, which is a key botanical feature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Blumea viscosa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
