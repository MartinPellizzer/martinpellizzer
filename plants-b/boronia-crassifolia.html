Boronia crassifolia

Boronia crassifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It grows in a variety of habitats, including heathlands and rocky soils, and is often found in coastal areas.

Boronia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate fragrance and visual interest.
