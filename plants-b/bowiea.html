Bowiea

Bowiea, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often tuberous stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are usually large, dark green, and somewhat leathery, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bowiea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unusual form and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
