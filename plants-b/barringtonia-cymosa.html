Barringtonia cymosa

Barringtonia cymosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, often blooming during the holiday season. The plant produces a unique fruit that resembles a lantern, which contains a single, large seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.
