Bromus sitchensis var. sitchensis

Bromus sitchensis var. sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka brome, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, cool environments such as forests and wet meadows. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of flowering stems.

The seeds are small, three-angled, and often have a distinctive purplish hue.
