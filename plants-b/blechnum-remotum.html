Blechnum remotum

Blechnum remotum, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, upright growth habit.

Blechnum remotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
