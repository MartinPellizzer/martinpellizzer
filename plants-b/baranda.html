Baranda

Baranda, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical perennial plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, velvety flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. Baranda is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, white, and blue.
