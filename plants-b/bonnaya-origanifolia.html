Bonnaya origanifolia

Bonnaya origanifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
