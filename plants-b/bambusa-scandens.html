Bambusa scandens

Bambusa scandens, commonly known as the climbing bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, often climbing trees or structures for support. The leaves are long, narrow, and glossy green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This bamboo is valued for its versatility in landscaping and as a sustainable material for construction and crafts.
