Bambusa hirsuta

Bambusa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional construction and crafts.
