Bryophyllum gastonis-bonnieri

Bryophyllum gastonis-bonnieri, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant produces numerous small plantlets along the edges of its leaves, which can take root and grow into new plants. These plantlets are a key feature in its propagation and contribute to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xerophytic gardens.
