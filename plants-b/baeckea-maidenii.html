Baeckea maidenii

Baeckea maidenii, commonly known as the golden baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.
