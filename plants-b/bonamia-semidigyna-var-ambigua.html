Bonamia semidigyna var. ambigua

Bonamia semidigyna var. ambigua, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and tobacco. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five lobes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests or along stream banks.
