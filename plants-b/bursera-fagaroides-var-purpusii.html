Bursera fagaroides var. purpusii

Bursera fagaroides var. purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn red in autumn. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
