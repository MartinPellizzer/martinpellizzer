Bromopsis condensata subsp. australis

Bromopsis condensata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Southern Alpaca grass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
