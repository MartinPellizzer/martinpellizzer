Begonia bufoderma

Begonia bufoderma, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
