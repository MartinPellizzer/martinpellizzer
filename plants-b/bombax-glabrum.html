Bombax glabrum

Bombax glabrum, commonly known as the cotton tree or ceiba, is a large, tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The plant has cultural and ecological significance in its native regions.
