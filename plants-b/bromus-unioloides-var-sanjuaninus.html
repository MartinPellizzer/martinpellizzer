Bromus unioloides var. sanjuaninus

Bromus unioloides var. sanjuaninus, commonly known as the San Juan needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
