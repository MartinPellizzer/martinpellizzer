Bidens vulgata f. puberula

Bidens vulgata f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy beggarweed, is a variant of the common beggarweed species.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two white ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its hairy foliage and stems are distinguishing features of this form.
