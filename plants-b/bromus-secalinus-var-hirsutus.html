Bromus secalinus var. hirsutus

Bromus secalinus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.

The species is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often used for erosion control due to its dense growth.
