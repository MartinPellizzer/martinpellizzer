Bursera ovalifolia

Bursera ovalifolia, commonly known as the Mexican lac tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The tree is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or greenish hues, and it produces small, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some regions.
