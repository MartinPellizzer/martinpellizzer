Bobua glauca

Bobua glauca, commonly known as the blue bobua, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Bobua glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
