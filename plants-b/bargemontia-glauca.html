Bargemontia glauca

Bargemontia glauca, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a unique species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense underwater mats. The plant's leaves are typically a vibrant blue-green color, which helps it absorb light efficiently in low-light conditions. Bargemontia glauca reproduces through spores, a common method among ferns, and plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, well-oxygenated water in its habitat.
