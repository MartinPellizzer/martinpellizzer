Buddleja australis

Buddleja australis, commonly known as the Australian butterfly bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Buddleja australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
