Brassica oleracea var. veris

Brassica oleracea var. veris, commonly known as turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant also produces small, green, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a raceme, typical of the mustard family.

This variety is cultivated for both its root and leafy greens, making it a versatile vegetable in various cuisines.
