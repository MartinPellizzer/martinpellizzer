Berberidopsis corallina

Berberidopsis corallina, commonly known as the coral lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of waxy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in the spring, which are borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
