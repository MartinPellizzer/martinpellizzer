Billbergia musaica

Billbergia musaica, commonly known as the Musaic Billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small reservoir for its epiphytic growth habit. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
