Bignonia longiflora

Bignonia longiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking, pendulous yellow flowers with a distinctive bell-shaped appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bignonia longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
