Bauhinia longistyla

Bauhinia longistyla, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple color in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that range from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it often has a sprawling growth habit.

Bauhinia longistyla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
