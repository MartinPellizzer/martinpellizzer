Begonia micranthera subsp. rhacophylla

Begonia micranthera subsp.

rhacophylla, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a unique species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic plants.
