Behenantha glaucescens

Behenantha glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
