Brunsvigia × bidwillii

Brunsvigia × bidwillii, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a hybrid plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble fireworks. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
