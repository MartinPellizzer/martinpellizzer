Boswellia popoviana

Boswellia popoviana, commonly known as the Afghan frankincense tree, is a small, deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, known as frankincense, which is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 3,000 meters.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
