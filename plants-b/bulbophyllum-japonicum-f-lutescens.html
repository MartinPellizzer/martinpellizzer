Bulbophyllum japonicum f. lutescens

Bulbophyllum japonicum f. lutescens, commonly known as the Yellow-flowered Bulbophyllum, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its pseudobulbs are stout and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
