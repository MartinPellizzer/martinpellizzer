Blepharodon glaucescens

Blepharodon glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often display a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blepharodon glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
