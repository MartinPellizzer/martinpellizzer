Betonica danica

Betonica danica, commonly known as the greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Betonica danica is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
