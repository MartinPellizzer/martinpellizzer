Begonia wilsonii

Begonia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
