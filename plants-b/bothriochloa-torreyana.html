Bothriochloa torreyana

Bothriochloa torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is often found in disturbed sites such as roadsides and dry open areas.
