Bulbophyllum leptobulbon

Bulbophyllum leptobulbon, commonly known as the slender bulb orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its bulbs are small and serve as storage organs for nutrients and water.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive shape that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
