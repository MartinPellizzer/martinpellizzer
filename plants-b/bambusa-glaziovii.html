Bambusa glaziovii

Bambusa glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has hollow, jointed stems with a smooth, green surface. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a fan-like pattern at the nodes.

Bambusa glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
