Barteria fistulosa

Barteria fistulosa, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Barteria fistulosa is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
