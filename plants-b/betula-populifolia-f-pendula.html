Betula populifolia f. pendula

Betula populifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping paper birch, is a unique variant of the paper birch tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This ornamental tree features smooth, white bark that peels to reveal a pale yellowish inner layer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is a member of the Betulaceae family.
