Brocchinia secunda

Brocchinia secunda, commonly known as the glassy pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent pitchers that trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized structures that secrete digestive enzymes and nectar to lure prey. Brocchinia secunda thrives in nutrient-poor, high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem by controlling insect populations.

Its striking appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of both botanical interest and conservation concern.
