Bambusa rumphiana

Bambusa rumphiana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This species is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and garden design. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Bambusa rumphiana is also known for its strong, lightweight culms, which have been used in traditional crafts and construction.
