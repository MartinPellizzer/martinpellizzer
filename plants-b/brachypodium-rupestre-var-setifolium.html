Brachypodium rupestre var. setifolium

Brachypodium rupestre var. setifolium, commonly known as the rock bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and rocky environments in Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, bristle-like hairs. Its inflorescence consists of racemes of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
