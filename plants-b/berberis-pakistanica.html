Berberis pakistanica

Berberis pakistanica, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Berberis pakistanica is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in some regions of South Asia.
