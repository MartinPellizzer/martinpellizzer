Begonia tayloriana

Begonia tayloriana, commonly known as the Taylor's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage with a distinctive green and red coloration, and its showy, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant typically grows as an upright herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its unique leaf patterns and long-lasting blooms.
