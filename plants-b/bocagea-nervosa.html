Bocagea nervosa

Bocagea nervosa, commonly known as the Florida beauty or narrow-leaf bocageia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

Bocagea nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
