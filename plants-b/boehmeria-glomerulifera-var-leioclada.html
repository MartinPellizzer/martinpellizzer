Boehmeria glomerulifera var. leioclada

Boehmeria glomerulifera var. leioclada, commonly known as the silky fiber plant, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky fibers that are harvested from the inner bark of its stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are large, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the inflorescences are clustered and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

This plant is valued for its fibrous material, which has been traditionally used in the production of textiles and paper.
