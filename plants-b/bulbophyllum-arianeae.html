Bulbophyllum arianeae

Bulbophyllum arianeae, commonly known as the Ariane's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, with a unique lip structure. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
