Bauhinia bombaciflora

Bauhinia bombaciflora, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and often peels off in thin, papery layers.

Bauhinia bombaciflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its vibrant floral display.
