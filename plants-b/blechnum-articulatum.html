Blechnum articulatum

Blechnum articulatum, commonly known as the articulated harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that are divided into segments, giving it a unique, segmented appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer that helps anchor it to the substrate.

Blechnum articulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
