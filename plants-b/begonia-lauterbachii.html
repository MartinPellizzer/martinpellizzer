Begonia lauterbachii

Begonia lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. The flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in clusters and are borne on long, slender stalks.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and hanging baskets.
