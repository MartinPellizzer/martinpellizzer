Breynia reclinata

Breynia reclinata, commonly known as the hanging breynia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Breynia reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
