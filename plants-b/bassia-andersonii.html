Bassia andersonii

Bassia andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and high sunlight exposure.
