Botrychium × pulchrum

Botrychium × pulchrum, commonly known as the showy moonwort, is a hybrid fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, erect, branching fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually pale green to reddish in color, and the fronds are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Botrychium × pulchrum is a rare and endangered species, with limited geographic distribution and specific ecological requirements.
