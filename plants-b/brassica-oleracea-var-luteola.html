Brassica oleracea var. luteola

Brassica oleracea var. luteola, commonly known as the turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen root that is typically white or yellow in color, and its leaves that are usually dark green and slightly lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is cultivated for its edible root and leaves, and it thrives in cool climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassica genus, including cabbage and broccoli.
