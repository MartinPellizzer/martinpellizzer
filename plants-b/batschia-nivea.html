Batschia nivea

Batschia nivea, commonly known as the snowy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant has a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Batschia nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
