Begonia sizemoreae

Begonia sizemoreae, commonly known as the Sizemore's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
