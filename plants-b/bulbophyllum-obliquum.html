Bulbophyllum obliquum

Bulbophyllum obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped inflorescence and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
