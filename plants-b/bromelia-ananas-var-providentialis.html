Bromelia ananas var. providentialis

Bromelia ananas var. providentialis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white or pink flowers, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored plant for both collectors and home growers.
