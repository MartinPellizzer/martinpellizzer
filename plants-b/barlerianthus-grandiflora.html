Barlerianthus grandiflora

Barlerianthus grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora barleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
