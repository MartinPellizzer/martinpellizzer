Brizula muelleri

Brizula muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Brizula muelleri is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
