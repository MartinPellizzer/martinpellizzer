Boehmeria nivea var. candicans

Boehmeria nivea var. candicans, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky white down. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence. It is cultivated for its fibrous stems, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
