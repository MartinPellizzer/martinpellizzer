Bulbophyllum sphaeracron

Bulbophyllum sphaeracron, commonly known as the globe-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded pseudobulbs and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
