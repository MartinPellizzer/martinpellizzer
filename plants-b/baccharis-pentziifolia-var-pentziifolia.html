Baccharis pentziifolia var. pentziifolia

Baccharis pentziifolia var. pentziifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a finely serrated margin, while its stems are usually covered in a soft, hairy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
