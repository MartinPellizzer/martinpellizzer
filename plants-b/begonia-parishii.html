Begonia parishii

Begonia parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay begonia, is a species native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Begonia parishii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
