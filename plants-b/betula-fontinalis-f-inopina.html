Betula fontinalis f. inopina

Betula fontinalis f. inopina, commonly known as the white birch variant, is a rare form of the paper birch.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark and slender, graceful branches. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in northern temperate regions. It plays an important role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
