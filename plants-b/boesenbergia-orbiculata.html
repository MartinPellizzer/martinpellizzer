Boesenbergia orbiculata

Boesenbergia orbiculata, commonly known as the golden ginger or round-leaf ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Boesenbergia orbiculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
