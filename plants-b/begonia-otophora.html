Begonia otophora

Begonia otophora, commonly known as the ear-shaped begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia otophora is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
