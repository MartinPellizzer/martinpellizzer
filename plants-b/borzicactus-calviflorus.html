Borzicactus calviflorus

Borzicactus calviflorus, commonly known as the hairless flower cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to tubercles, a common adaptation in desert plants to minimize water loss.

Borzicactus calviflorus is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water to survive.
