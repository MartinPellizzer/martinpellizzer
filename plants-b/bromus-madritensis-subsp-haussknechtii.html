Bromus madritensis subsp. haussknechtii

Bromus madritensis subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the Spanish veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
