Bystropogon origanifolius var. odoratissimus

Bystropogon origanifolius var. odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and often have a slight aromatic scent, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
