Bolbitis appendiculata var. vivipara

Bolbitis appendiculata var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Bolbitis.

It is characterized by its ability to produce offspring through viviparity, where spores are released from specialized structures called sori on the undersides of its fronds. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its fronds are typically lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is notable for its unique reproductive strategy and is often found in temperate regions with stable aquatic habitats.
