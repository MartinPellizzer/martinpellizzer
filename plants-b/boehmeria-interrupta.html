Boehmeria interrupta

Boehmeria interrupta, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Japanese nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its fibrous stalks, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles. Boehmeria interrupta is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
