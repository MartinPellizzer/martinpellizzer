Bromus squarrosus var. normalis

Bromus squarrosus var. normalis, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces, and its leaves are narrow and often bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in areas with low precipitation and well-drained soils.
