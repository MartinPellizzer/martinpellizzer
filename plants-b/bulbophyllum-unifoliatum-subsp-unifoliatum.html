Bulbophyllum unifoliatum subsp. unifoliatum

Bulbophyllum unifoliatum subsp.

unifoliatum, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its single, broad, lance-shaped leaf.

The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
