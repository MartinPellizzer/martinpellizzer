Balanaulax rumphii

Balanaulax rumphii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in dense mats, helping to anchor it to the forest floor or tree trunks.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Balanaulax rumphii is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
