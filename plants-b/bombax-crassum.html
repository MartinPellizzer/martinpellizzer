Bombax crassum

Bombax crassum, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, white seed pods that contain fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. Its bark is thick and corky, providing resistance to pests and diseases.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
