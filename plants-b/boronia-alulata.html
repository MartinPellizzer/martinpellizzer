Boronia alulata

Boronia alulata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Boronia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found growing in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as citrus and rue.

Boronia alulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
