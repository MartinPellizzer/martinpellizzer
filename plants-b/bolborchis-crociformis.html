Bolborchis crociformis

Bolborchis crociformis, commonly known as the crooked-leaf fern, is a unique fern species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a complex structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Bolborchis crociformis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
