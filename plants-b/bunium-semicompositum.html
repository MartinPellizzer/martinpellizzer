Bunium semicompositum

Bunium semicompositum, commonly known as the lesser fennel, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem topped with a compound umbel of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are small, winged, and arranged in a single row along the central axis of the umbel.

Bunium semicompositum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
