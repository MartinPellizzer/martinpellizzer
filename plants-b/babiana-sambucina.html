Babiana sambucina

Babiana sambucina, commonly known as the purple rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, showy inflorescences that resemble lilies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique flowering habit.
