Blechum trinitense

Blechum trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidadian blechum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, specifically Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
