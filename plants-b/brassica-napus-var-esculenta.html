Brassica napus var. esculenta

Brassica napus var. esculenta, commonly known as rapeseed, is a cultivated variety of the oilseed rape plant.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and thick, stem-like leaves. This plant is primarily grown for its seeds, which are used to produce canola oil and animal feed. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its role in crop rotation due to its ability to suppress weeds and improve soil fertility.
