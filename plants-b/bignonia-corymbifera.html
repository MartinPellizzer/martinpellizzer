Bignonia corymbifera

Bignonia corymbifera, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct glandular appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges.

Bignonia corymbifera is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 15 feet tall, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
