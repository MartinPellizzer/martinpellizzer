Bartsia imbricata

Bartsia imbricata, commonly known as the imbricated bartsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Bartsia imbricata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
