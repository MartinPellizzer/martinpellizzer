Berchemia barbigera

Berchemia barbigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, rounded crown and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Berchemia barbigera is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
