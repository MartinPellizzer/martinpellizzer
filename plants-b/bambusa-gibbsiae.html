Bambusa gibbsiae

Bambusa gibbsiae, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray culms that are covered in a fine, silky sheen. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa gibbsiae is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
