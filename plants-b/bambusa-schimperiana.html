Bambusa schimperiana

Bambusa schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green culms that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, grass-like pattern along the culms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa schimperiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense thickets in suitable environments.
