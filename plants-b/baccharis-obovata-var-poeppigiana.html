Baccharis obovata var. poeppigiana

Baccharis obovata var. poeppigiana, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a rough texture.
