Baccharis megapotamica

Baccharis megapotamica, commonly known as Brazilian erica, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
