Byrsopteris amabilis

Byrsopteris amabilis, commonly known as the beautiful water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.
