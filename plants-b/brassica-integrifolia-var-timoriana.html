Brassica integrifolia var. timoriana

Brassica integrifolia var. timoriana, commonly known as the Timor mustard, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and alternate leaves that are entire or slightly lobed. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its peppery taste and is sometimes used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
