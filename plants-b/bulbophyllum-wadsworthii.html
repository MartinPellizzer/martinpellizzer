Bulbophyllum wadsworthii

Bulbophyllum wadsworthii, commonly known as the Wadsworth's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
