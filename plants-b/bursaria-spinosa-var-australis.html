Bursaria spinosa var. australis

Bursaria spinosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
