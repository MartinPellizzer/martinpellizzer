Bromus erectus var. velutinus

Bromus erectus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
