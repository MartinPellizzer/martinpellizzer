Bigelowia graveolens var. hololeuca

Bigelowia graveolens var. hololeuca, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
