Bulbophyllum croceodon

Bulbophyllum croceodon, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate veining. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for their ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
