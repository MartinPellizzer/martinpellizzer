Brassica tournefortii var. dasycarpa

Brassica tournefortii var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

The species is noted for its resilience and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
