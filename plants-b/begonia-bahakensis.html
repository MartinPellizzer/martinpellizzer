Begonia bahakensis

Begonia bahakensis, commonly known as the Java begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia bahakensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
