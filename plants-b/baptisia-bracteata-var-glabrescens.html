Baptisia bracteata var. glabrescens

Baptisia bracteata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
