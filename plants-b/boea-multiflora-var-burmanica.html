Boea multiflora var. burmanica

Boea multiflora var. burmanica, commonly known as Burman's bluebell, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
