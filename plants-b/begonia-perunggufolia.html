Begonia perunggufolia

Begonia perunggufolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a deep green color with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
