Boophone intermedia

Boophone intermedia, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Boophone intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
