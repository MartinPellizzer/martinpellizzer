Brisegnoa

Brisegnoa, commonly known as the "carmen plant," is a unique species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of pink or white.

Brisegnoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
