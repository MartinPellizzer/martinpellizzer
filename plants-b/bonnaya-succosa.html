Bonnaya succosa

Bonnaya succosa, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it store water in its native habitat. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming during the rainy season. Its growth pattern is typically shrub-like, with branches that can become woody over time.

Bonnaya succosa is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
