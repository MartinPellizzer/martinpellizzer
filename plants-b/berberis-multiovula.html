Berberis multiovula

Berberis multiovula, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used ornamentally. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
