Brassica cretica var. attica

Brassica cretica var. attica, commonly known as Greek mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, elongated pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
