Bursa bursa-pastoris var. simplex

Bursa bursa-pastoris var. simplex, commonly known as the common velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
