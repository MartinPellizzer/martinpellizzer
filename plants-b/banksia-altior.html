Banksia altior

Banksia altior, commonly known as the taller banksia, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or spiky flower spikes. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Banksia altior thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
