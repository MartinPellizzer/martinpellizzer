Bauhinia kunthiana

Bauhinia kunthiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, feather-like segments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia kunthiana is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
