Bulbophyllum igneum

Bulbophyllum igneum, commonly known as the fire orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Bulbophyllum igneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
