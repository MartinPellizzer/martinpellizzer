Bassia californica

Bassia californica, commonly known as popcorn flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive seed heads that resemble popped corn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
