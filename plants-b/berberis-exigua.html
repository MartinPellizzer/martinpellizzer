Berberis exigua

Berberis exigua, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used as ornamental elements. It is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Berberis exigua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
