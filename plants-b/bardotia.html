Bardotia

Bardotia, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to yellow, often resembling a rainbow. Bardotia has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
