Balsamorhiza hookeri

Balsamorhiza hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive, aromatic foliage. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a strong, balsamic scent. It produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Balsamorhiza hookeri is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
