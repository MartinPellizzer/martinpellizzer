Bassia hyssopifolia

Bassia hyssopifolia, commonly known as Indian spinach or slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Bassia hyssopifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments.
