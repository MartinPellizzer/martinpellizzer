Bracteanthus atlanticus

Bracteanthus atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic bracteanthus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces bracts that are often mistaken for flowers.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
