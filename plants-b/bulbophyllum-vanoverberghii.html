Bulbophyllum vanoverberghii

Bulbophyllum vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in seasonal habitats. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of green, yellow, and red.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
