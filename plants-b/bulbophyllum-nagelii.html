Bulbophyllum nagelii

Bulbophyllum nagelii, commonly known as the Nagel's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry periods. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
