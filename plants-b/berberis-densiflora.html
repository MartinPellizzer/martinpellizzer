Berberis densiflora

Berberis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
