Baccharis ledifolia

Baccharis ledifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its green stems and branches. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Baccharis ledifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
