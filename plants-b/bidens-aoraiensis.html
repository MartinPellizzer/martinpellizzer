Bidens aoraiensis

Bidens aoraiensis, commonly known as the golden beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flowers, which consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Bidens aoraiensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields, where it thrives in full sun.
