Bulbophyllum singuliflorum

Bulbophyllum singuliflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, solitary flower that often stands out from the foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The plant's bulbous pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
