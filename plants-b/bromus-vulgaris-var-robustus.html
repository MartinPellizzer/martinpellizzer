Bromus vulgaris var. robustus

Bromus vulgaris var. robustus, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, disturbed soils, and agricultural fields, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has dense, fibrous roots and forms tufts with long, rigid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which contain small, pale-green flowers.

This variety is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in both ecological and agricultural settings.
