Berberis turcomanica var. buhseana

Berberis turcomanica var. buhseana, commonly known as the Turkestan barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically alternate. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
