Briza ambigua

Briza ambigua, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, dunes, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight zigzag pattern along the edges, contributing to its common name.

Briza ambigua is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
