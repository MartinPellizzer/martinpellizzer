Borago orientalis

Borago orientalis, commonly known as blueweed or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, making it popular in herbal remedies.

Borago orientalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
