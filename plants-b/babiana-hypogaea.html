Babiana hypogaea

Babiana hypogaea, commonly known as the rain lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a short stem and narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to seasonal rainfall and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Babiana hypogaea is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
