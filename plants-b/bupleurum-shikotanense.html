Bupleurum shikotanense

Bupleurum shikotanense, commonly known as Shikotan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and southern parts of Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Bupleurum shikotanense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
