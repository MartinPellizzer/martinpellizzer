Bulbophyllum gjellerupii

Bulbophyllum gjellerupii, commonly known as the Gjellerup's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces intricate, often colorful inflorescences with elongated, lance-shaped petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
