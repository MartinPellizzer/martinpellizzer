Bomarea spectabilis var. parvifolia

Bomarea spectabilis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a tuberous-rooted herb that produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright blue or violet flowers. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. Its flowers are typically borne on erect, branched stems and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
