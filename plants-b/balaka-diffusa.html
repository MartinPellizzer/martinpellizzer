Balaka diffusa

Balaka diffusa, commonly known as the wandering wisteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Balaka diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.
