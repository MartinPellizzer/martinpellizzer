Bigelovia cassinoides

Bigelovia cassinoides, commonly known as the Cassin's bigelovia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Bigelovia cassinoides is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
