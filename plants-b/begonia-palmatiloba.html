Begonia palmatiloba

Begonia palmatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
