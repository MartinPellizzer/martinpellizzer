Begonia fagopyroides var. fendleriana

Begonia fagopyroides var. fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored leaves that typically feature a deep green background with red or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
