Begonia geoffrayi

Begonia geoffrayi, commonly known as the Geoffray's begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
