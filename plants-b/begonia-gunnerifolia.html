Begonia gunnerifolia

Begonia gunnerifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia gunnerifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
