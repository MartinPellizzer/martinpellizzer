Betula × intermedia nothosubsp. seideliana

Betula × intermedia nothosubsp.

seideliana, commonly known as the silver birch variant, is a hybrid species within the Betulaceae family. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, often pendulous branches.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, typically turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
