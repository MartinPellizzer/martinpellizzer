Buxus calcarea

Buxus calcarea, commonly known as the limestone boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of small, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value lies in its neat, sculptural form and ability to be shaped into topiaries.

Buxus calcarea is often used in horticulture for its durability and adaptability to various garden conditions.
