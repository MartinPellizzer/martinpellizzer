Bauhinia cavaleriei

Bauhinia cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
