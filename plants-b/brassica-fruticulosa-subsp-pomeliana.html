Brassica fruticulosa subsp. pomeliana

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

pomeliana, commonly known as the Mediterranean mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and steppe regions.

The plant features a low-growing, branched structure with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lobed, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of mustard oil.
