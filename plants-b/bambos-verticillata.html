Bambos verticillata

Bambos verticillata, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Bambos verticillata is valued for its rapid growth and is used in construction, fencing, and as a source of thatching.
