Bassia tricornis

Bassia tricornis, commonly known as the three-angled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually triangular in shape, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
