Bactris megalocarpa

Bactris megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant palmetto, is a large, evergreen palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-orange inflorescences, and its fruit is a large, fibrous, spiky capsule containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The plant has ecological and economic significance, particularly in traditional practices and local agriculture.
