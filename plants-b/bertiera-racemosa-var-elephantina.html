Bertiera racemosa var. elephantina

Bertiera racemosa var. elephantina, commonly known as the elephant tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
