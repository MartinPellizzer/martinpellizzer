Baumannia geminiflora

Baumannia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant produces two flowers per stem, giving it its species name "geminiflora," which means "twice-flowered.". It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Baumannia geminiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
