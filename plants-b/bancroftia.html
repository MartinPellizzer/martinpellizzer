Bancroftia

Bancroftia, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their attractive, velvety leaves and showy, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors. They are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments. Bancroftia species are known for their ability to thrive in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

The genus includes several species, with *Bancroftia erosa* being one of the most commonly cultivated varieties.
