Bothriochloa bladhii var. punctata

Bothriochloa bladhii var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted bladh grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
