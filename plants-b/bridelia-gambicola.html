Bridelia gambicola

Bridelia gambicola, commonly known as the African bridelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Bridelia gambicola is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
