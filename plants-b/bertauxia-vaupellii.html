Bertauxia vaupellii

Bertauxia vaupellii, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a unique foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its pale green to silver-gray thallus, which is covered in a fine, powdery layer of soredia. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with specific cyanobacteria, which contribute to its ability to photosynthesize. It is often found on bark, stone, or soil in temperate regions.

Due to its delicate nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is considered an indicator species for air quality.
