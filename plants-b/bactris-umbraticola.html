Bactris umbraticola

Bactris umbraticola, commonly known as the shadow palm, is a species of palm native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. Bactris umbraticola thrives in shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in tropical rainforests.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various forest species.
