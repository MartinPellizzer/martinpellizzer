Bromus squarrosus f. nanus

Bromus squarrosus f. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf speargrass, is a compact, mat-forming grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features short, stiff leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its small stature and dense growth habit make it well-suited for stabilizing soils in arid environments.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
