Baccharis phyliciformis

Baccharis phyliciformis, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic properties.

Baccharis phyliciformis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
