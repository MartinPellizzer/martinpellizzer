Borya sphaerocephala

Borya sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed borya, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and often pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Borya sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
