Billia hippocastanum

Billia hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, with distinctive ridges that become more pronounced with age.

The horse chestnut is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens across Europe and North America.
