Baccharis ulicina var. multifida

Baccharis ulicina var. multifida, commonly known as the Florida goldenweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swamps.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
