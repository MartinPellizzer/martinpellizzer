Blastus longiflorus var. apricus

Blastus longiflorus var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot-flowered blastus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of small, apricot-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
