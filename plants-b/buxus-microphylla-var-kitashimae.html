Buxus microphylla var. kitashimae

Buxus microphylla var. kitashimae, commonly known as the Japanese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant is prized for its ability to be shaped into intricate forms, making it popular in topiary and ornamental gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cool, moist environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often used in landscape design for hedging and as a decorative element.
