Bulnesia schichendanzii

Bulnesia schichendanzii, commonly known as the desert ironwood, is a slow-growing, deciduous tree native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, fissured bark that provides protection against extreme temperatures. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds by wind. It is highly drought-tolerant and plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in reforestation projects in degraded landscapes.
