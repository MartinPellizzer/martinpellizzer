Bredemeyera laurifolia

Bredemeyera laurifolia, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and emit a sweet scent, especially when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Bredemeyera laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
