Bassia londesia

Bassia londesia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is well adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Bassia londesia is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
