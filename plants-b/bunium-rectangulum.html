Bunium rectangulum

Bunium rectangulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of other members of its family. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Bunium rectangulum is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
