Bromus bonassorum

Bromus bonassorum, commonly known as the bonassor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Bromus bonassorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
