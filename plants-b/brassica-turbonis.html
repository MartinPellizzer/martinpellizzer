Brassica turbonis

Brassica turbonis, commonly known as the turban squash, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped fruit that can range in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically self-pollinated. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a rough texture.

Brassica turbonis is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
