Bidens pilosa f. calcicola

Bidens pilosa f. calcicola, commonly known as the limestone milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, which is reflected in its specific epithet "calcicola.". Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
