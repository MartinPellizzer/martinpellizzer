Baumea teretifolia

Baumea teretifolia, commonly known as the slender baumea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Baumea teretifolia is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
