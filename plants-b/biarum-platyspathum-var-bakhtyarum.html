Biarum platyspathum var. bakhtyarum

Biarum platyspathum var. bakhtyarum, commonly known as the Bakhtyari bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
