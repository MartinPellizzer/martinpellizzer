Bromopsis ciliata

Bromopsis ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Bromopsis ciliata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
