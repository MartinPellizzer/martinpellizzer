Basigyne

Basigyne, commonly known as the female plant of certain ferns and mosses, is characterized by its specialized reproductive structures.

It typically produces archegonia, which are the female reproductive organs where fertilization occurs. Unlike the male gametophyte, the basigyne is responsible for generating the next generation of plants through spore production. Its morphology often differs from the male gametophyte, exhibiting distinct growth patterns and structures.

This dimorphic life cycle is a key feature in the reproductive biology of certain bryophytes.
