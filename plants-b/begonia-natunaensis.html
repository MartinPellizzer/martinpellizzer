Begonia natunaensis

Begonia natunaensis, commonly known as the Natuna Begonia, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and distinctive foliage. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with conservation concerns.
