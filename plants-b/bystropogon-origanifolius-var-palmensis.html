Bystropogon origanifolius var. palmensis

Bystropogon origanifolius var. palmensis, commonly known as the palm-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
