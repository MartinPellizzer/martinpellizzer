Betula × saxophila

Betula × saxophila, commonly known as the Siberian white birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Betula pendula and Betula platyphylla.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellow or cream-colored layer beneath. The plant typically grows in cold climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula × saxophila is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
