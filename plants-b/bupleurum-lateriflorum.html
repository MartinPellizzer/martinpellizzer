Bupleurum lateriflorum

Bupleurum lateriflorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Bupleurum lateriflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
