Baccharis solomonii

Baccharis solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits.

Baccharis solomonii is also known for its aromatic leaves, which release a strong scent when crushed.
