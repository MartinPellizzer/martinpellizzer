Benthamia disciflora

Benthamia disciflora, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its succulent leaves store moisture, and it blooms during the cooler months to maximize pollination opportunities.

Benthamia disciflora is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in full sun.
