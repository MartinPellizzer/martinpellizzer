Begonia potamophila

Begonia potamophila, commonly known as the river begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
