Berlinia viridicans

Berlinia viridicans, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a large, evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, dark seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
