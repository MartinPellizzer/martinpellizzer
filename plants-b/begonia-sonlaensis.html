Begonia sonlaensis

Begonia sonlaensis, commonly known as the Son La begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
