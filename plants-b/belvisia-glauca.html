Belvisia glauca

Belvisia glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive glaucous appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Belvisia glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
