Bolivicactus ocampoi

Bolivicactus ocampoi, commonly known as the Ocampo's bolivian cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spiny areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months.

Bolivicactus ocampoi is valued for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
