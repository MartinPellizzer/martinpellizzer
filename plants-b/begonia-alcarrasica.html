Begonia alcarrasica

Begonia alcarrasica, commonly known as the Sierra de Alcarras begonia, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in woodland areas. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
