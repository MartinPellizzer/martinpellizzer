Bidens pilosa f. dissecta

Bidens pilosa f. dissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The leaves are deeply lobed or dissected, giving the plant its common name, and are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive seed capsules.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and open woodlands.
