Brassica parra

Brassica parra, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by round, green pods containing seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Brassica parra is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
