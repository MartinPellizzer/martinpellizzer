Bupleurum badium

Bupleurum badium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Bupleurum badium is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
