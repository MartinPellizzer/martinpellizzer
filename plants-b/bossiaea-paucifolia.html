Bossiaea paucifolia

Bossiaea paucifolia, commonly known as the slender bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

Bossiaea paucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
