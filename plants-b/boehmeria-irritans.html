Boehmeria irritans

Boehmeria irritans, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Boehmeria irritans contains irritant chemicals in its leaves, which cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
