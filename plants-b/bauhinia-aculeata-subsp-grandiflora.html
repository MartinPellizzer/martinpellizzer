Bauhinia aculeata subsp. grandiflora

Bauhinia aculeata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered burr legume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and its thorn-like structures are a notable feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes where it adds visual interest with its vibrant blooms.
