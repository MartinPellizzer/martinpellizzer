Bignonia albida

Bignonia albida, commonly known as the white trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring and early summer. Bignonia albida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
