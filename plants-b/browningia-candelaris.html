Browningia candelaris

Browningia candelaris, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble candlesticks, hence its common name. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

The flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance.
