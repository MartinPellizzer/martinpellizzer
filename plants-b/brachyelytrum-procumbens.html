Brachyelytrum procumbens

Brachyelytrum procumbens, commonly known as the creeping rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheathing base.

Brachyelytrum procumbens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
