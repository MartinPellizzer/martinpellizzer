Berkheya carlinopsis var. sylvicola

Berkheya carlinopsis var. sylvicola, commonly known as the forest berkheya, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
