Bletia ecuadorensis

Bletia ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bletia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structure. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with three petals and three sepals.

Bletia ecuadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
