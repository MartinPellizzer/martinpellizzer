Bidens tripartita var. quinqueloba

Bidens tripartita var. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through its hairy, clinging seeds, making it both a common weed and a valuable source of nectar for pollinators.
