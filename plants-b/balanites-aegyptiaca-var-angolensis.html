Balanites aegyptiaca var. angolensis

Balanites aegyptiaca var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible, date-like seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used as a food source by local communities.
