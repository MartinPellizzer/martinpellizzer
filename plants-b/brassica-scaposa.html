Brassica scaposa

Brassica scaposa, commonly known as the scapose mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching structure and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Brassica scaposa is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cold climates.
