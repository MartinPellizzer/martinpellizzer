Bonatea benghalensis

Bonatea benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense colonies along the edges of water bodies. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
