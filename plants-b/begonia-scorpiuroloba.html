Begonia scorpiuroloba

Begonia scorpiuroloba, commonly known as the Scorpius begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
