Begonia xenos

Begonia xenos, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in spring. Its growth habit is usually compact, with a short, upright stem.

Begonia xenos thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
