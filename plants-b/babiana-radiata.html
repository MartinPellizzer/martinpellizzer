Babiana radiata

Babiana radiata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Babiana radiata is also known for its resilience, able to withstand drought and frost once established.
