Bidens sharpii var. sharpii

Bidens sharpii var. sharpii, commonly known as the sharpleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
