Begonia fuscisetosa

Begonia fuscisetosa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its ornamental value.

Begonia fuscisetosa prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
