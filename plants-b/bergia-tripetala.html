Bergia tripetala

Bergia tripetala, commonly known as the three-lobed bergia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of plants in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Bergia tripetala thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found growing in dunes or along the edges of coastal forests.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, showcasing its resilience to harsh coastal climates.
