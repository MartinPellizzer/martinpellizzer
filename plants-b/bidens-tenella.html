Bidens tenella

Bidens tenella, commonly known as common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has hairy stems and serrated leaves, often growing in moist or disturbed soils. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
