Bulbophyllum cootesii

Bulbophyllum cootesii, commonly known as the Cootes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.
