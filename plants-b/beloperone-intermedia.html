Beloperone intermedia

Beloperone intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian false hellebore, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem that bears opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in umbels. Beloperone intermedia is known for its toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in shaded, moist environments within its native habitat.
