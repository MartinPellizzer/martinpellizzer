Bonatea panduana

Bonatea panduana, commonly known as the slender bonatea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Bonatea panduana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
