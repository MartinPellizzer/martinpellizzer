Begonia maurandiae

Begonia maurandiae, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are often marked with intricate patterns of red and purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
