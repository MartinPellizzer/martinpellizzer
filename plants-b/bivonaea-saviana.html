Bivonaea saviana

Bivonaea saviana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Bivonaea saviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
