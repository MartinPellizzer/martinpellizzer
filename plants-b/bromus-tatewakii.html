Bromus tatewakii

Bromus tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese brome, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest clearings.

Bromus tatewakii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
