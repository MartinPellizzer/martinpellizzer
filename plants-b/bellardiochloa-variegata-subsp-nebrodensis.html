Bellardiochloa variegata subsp. nebrodensis

Bellardiochloa variegata subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive variegated coloration.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
