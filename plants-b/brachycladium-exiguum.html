Brachycladium exiguum

Brachycladium exiguum, commonly known as the slender bristlegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to sandy soils in coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species in challenging conditions. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize sandy soils, contributing to dune stabilization.

Brachycladium exiguum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
