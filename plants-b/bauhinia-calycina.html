Bauhinia calycina

Bauhinia calycina, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
