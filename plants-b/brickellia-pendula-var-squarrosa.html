Brickellia pendula var. squarrosa

Brickellia pendula var. squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
