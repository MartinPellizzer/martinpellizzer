Bignonia apurensis

Bignonia apurensis, commonly known as the Apure trumpetflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bignonia apurensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
