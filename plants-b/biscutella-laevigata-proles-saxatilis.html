Biscutella laevigata proles saxatilis

Biscutella laevigata proles saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
