Biscutella didyma var. raphanifolia

Biscutella didyma var. raphanifolia, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces striking clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long flowering period.
