Brassica kaber

Brassica kaber, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Brassica kaber is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
