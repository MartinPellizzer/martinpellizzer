Bignonia elegans

Bignonia elegans, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia elegans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
