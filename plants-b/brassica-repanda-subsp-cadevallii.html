Brassica repanda subsp. cadevallii

Brassica repanda subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevall's mustard, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

Native to specific regions in southern Europe, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a plant of conservation interest.
