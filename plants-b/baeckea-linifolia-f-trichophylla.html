Baeckea linifolia f. trichophylla

Baeckea linifolia f. trichophylla, commonly known as the silky baeckea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
