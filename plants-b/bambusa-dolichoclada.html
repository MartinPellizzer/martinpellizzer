Bambusa dolichoclada

Bambusa dolichoclada, commonly known as the long-leaf bamboo, is a tall, slender bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and strong, straight culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

Bambusa dolichoclada is valued for its use in construction, handicrafts, and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
