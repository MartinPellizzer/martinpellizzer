Bulbophyllum gibbsiae

Bulbophyllum gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
