Bupleurum odontites subsp. opacum

Bupleurum odontites subsp.

opacum, commonly known as the opacum bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions.
