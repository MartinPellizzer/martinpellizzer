Baccharis chiloensis

Baccharis chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Baccharis chiloensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Chile and Argentina.
