Boswellia holstii

Boswellia holstii, commonly known as frankincense tree, is a species of the genus Boswellia native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves. It produces a resinous sap, which is harvested for use in incense, perfumes, and traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Boswellia holstii make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
