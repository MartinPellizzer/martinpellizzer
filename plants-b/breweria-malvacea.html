Breweria malvacea

Breweria malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Breweria malvacea is often found in disturbed soils and can become a weed in agricultural settings.
