Bigelovia mitracarpoides

Bigelovia mitracarpoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, desert conditions.
