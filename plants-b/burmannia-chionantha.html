Burmannia chionantha

Burmannia chionantha, commonly known as the snow-flower burmannia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens.
