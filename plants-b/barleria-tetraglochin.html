Barleria tetraglochin

Barleria tetraglochin, commonly known as the four-cornered barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and usually appear in shades of pink or purple.

Barleria tetraglochin is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
