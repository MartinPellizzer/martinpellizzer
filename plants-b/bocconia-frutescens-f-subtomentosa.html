Bocconia frutescens f. subtomentosa

Bocconia frutescens f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvet bocconia, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, tactile appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a pale, hairy underside. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique texture and foliage rather than its flowers.
