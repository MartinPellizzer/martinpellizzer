Brassica amplexicaulis

Brassica amplexicaulis, commonly known as the heartleaf mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Brassica amplexicaulis is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
