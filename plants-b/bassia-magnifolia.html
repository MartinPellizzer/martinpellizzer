Bassia magnifolia

Bassia magnifolia, commonly known as the showy bassia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

Bassia magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in semi-arid environments.
