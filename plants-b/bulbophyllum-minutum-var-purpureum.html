Bulbophyllum minutum var. purpureum

Bulbophyllum minutum var. purpureum, commonly known as the Purple Miniature Lady's Slipper, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This variant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that display a range of purple hues, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and colorful blooms.
