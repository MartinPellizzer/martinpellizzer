Brachiaria pungipes

Brachiaria pungipes, commonly known as the spiked brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Brachiaria pungipes is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
