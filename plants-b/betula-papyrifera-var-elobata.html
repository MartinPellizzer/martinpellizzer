Betula papyrifera var. elobata

Betula papyrifera var. elobata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch species native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a papery texture, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, triangular, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
