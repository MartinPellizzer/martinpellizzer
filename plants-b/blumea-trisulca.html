Blumea trisulca

Blumea trisulca, commonly known as the three-lobed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in composite heads, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

Blumea trisulca is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
