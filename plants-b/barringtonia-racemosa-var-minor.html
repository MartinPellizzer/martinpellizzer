Barringtonia racemosa var. minor

Barringtonia racemosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser candlewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.
