Bromus rubens subsp. kunkelii

Bromus rubens subsp.

kunkelii, commonly known as the Kunkel's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
