Benthamidia japonica var. angustata

Benthamidia japonica var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf benthamidia, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
