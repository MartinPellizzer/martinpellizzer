Bonellia macrocarpa

Bonellia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are often submerged, and it thrives in nutrient-poor waters. Its leaves are slender and adapted for efficient water movement.

Bonellia macrocarpa plays a unique ecological role by supplementing its nutrient intake through prey capture.
