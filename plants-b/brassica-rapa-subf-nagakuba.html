Brassica rapa subf. nagakuba

Brassica rapa subf.

nagakuba, commonly known as the Japanese kale or napa cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often curled and coated with a waxy layer.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are typical of the mustard family. Its roots are fibrous and can become quite substantial, especially in mature plants.

This variety is cultivated for its edible leaves and is adapted to temperate climates.
