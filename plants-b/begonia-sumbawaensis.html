Begonia sumbawaensis

Begonia sumbawaensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
