Browneopsis disepala

Browneopsis disepala, commonly known as the two-winged palm, is a unique monocot native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-winged structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant features a single, unbranched stem with a crown of feather-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in pairs, which is a notable botanical trait.

This rare species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited geographic distribution.
