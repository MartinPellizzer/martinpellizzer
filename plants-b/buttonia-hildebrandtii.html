Buttonia hildebrandtii

Buttonia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Buttonia hildebrandtii is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
