Brassica glareosa

Brassica glareosa, commonly known as the wild mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Brassica glareosa is also known for its role in ecological systems as a food source for insects and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
