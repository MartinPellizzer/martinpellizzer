Badilloa guantivana var. guantivana

Badilloa guantivana var. guantivana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
