Brassica rapa subsp. japonica

Brassica rapa subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Chinese kale or neep, is a leafy green vegetable native to East Asia. It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves and thick, edible stems.

The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary versatility.

This subspecies is valued for its high content of vitamins A, C, and K, as well as its use in traditional Asian cuisines.
