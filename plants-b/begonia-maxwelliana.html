Begonia maxwelliana

Begonia maxwelliana, commonly known as Maxwell's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly velvety in texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia maxwelliana is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
