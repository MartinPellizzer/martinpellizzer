Bletia catenulata f. caerulea

Bletia catenulata f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered bletia, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its distinctive blue-violet flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of several showy flowers arranged in a spike, each with three petals and a unique lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
