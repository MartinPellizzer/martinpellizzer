Bemsetia paniculata

Bemsetia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in panicles, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, often colorful bracts that surround the true flowers, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Bemsetia paniculata is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display in gardens and as a houseplant.
