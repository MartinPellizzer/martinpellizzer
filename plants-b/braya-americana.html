Braya americana

Braya americana, commonly known as American sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
