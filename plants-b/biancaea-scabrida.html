Biancaea scabrida

Biancaea scabrida, commonly known as the rough biancaea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Biancaea scabrida is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
