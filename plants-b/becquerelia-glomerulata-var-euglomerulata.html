Becquerelia glomerulata var. euglomerulata

Becquerelia glomerulata var. euglomerulata, commonly known as the clustered becquerelia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
