Boretta cantabrica var. bicolor

Boretta cantabrica var. bicolor, commonly known as the Cantabrian Boretta, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, which typically display a combination of green and silver or white hues. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or cliffside habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of pale blue or violet, adding to its ornamental appeal.
