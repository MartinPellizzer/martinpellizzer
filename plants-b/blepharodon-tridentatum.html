Blepharodon tridentatum

Blepharodon tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are usually submerged, while its stems may be floating or rooted in muddy substrates. It thrives in nutrient-poor waters, using its prey to supplement its nutrient intake.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
