Begonia prismatocarpa subsp. delobata

Begonia prismatocarpa subsp.

delobata, commonly known as the pink-stemmed begonia, is a unique species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, prism-like stems that are often tinged with pink or red hues.

The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and can range in color from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
