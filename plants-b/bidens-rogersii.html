Bidens rogersii

Bidens rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and wetlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces seeds in clusters called achenes.

Bidens rogersii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
