Borassus flabellifer var. madagascariensis

Borassus flabellifer var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that form a fan shape at the top. The plant produces large, creamy-white flowers that eventually develop into small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local communities.
