Batschia laurifolia

Batschia laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Batschia laurifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
