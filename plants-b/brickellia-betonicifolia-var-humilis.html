Brickellia betonicifolia var. humilis

Brickellia betonicifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the low brickellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
