Baptisia × fragilis

Baptisia × fragilis, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking clusters of blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Baptisia × fragilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
