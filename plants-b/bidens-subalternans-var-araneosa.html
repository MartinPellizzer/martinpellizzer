Bidens subalternans var. araneosa

Bidens subalternans var. araneosa, commonly known as the spider-veined beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive, spider-like venation pattern.

It thrives in wetlands and disturbed soils, often spreading through its prickly seeds that adhere to clothing and animals.
