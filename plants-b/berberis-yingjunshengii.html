Berberis yingjunshengii

Berberis yingjunshengii, commonly known as the Yungui barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
