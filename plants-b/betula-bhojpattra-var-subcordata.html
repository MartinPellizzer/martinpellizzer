Betula bhojpattra var. subcordata

Betula bhojpattra var. subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate birch, is a variant of the bhojpattra birch native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, white bark and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
