Blechnum boreale

Blechnum boreale, commonly known as the northern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it can maintain the high humidity required for its survival. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Blechnum boreale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
