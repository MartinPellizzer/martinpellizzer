Brassica sinapioides

Brassica sinapioides, commonly known as the yellow sarson, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, with a slightly serrated edge and a pungent odor when crushed.

Brassica sinapioides is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
