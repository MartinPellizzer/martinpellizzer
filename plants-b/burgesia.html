Burgesia

Burgesia, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a unique genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant blooms that can resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plant typically features slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Burgesia species are primarily found in Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
