Brassica loncholoma

Brassica loncholoma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environments.
