Bomarea salsilloides var. sepium

Bomarea salsilloides var. sepium, commonly known as the purple-veined bomarea, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, often with intricate veining. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy texture, and its stems are often adorned with bracts that resemble small flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical plants.
