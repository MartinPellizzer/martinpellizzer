Baccharis adscendens

Baccharis adscendens, commonly known as the green baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and coastal areas, and is well adapted to sandy soils.

Baccharis adscendens is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
