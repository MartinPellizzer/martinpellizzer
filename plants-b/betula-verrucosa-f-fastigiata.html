Betula verrucosa f. fastigiata

Betula verrucosa f. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved birch, is a cultivar of the paper birch characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cooler climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal. The bark of this variety is similar to that of the parent species, featuring a papery, peeling texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its unique form and seasonal color changes.
