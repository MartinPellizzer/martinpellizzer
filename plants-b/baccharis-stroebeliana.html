Baccharis stroebeliana

Baccharis stroebeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Baccharis stroebeliana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
