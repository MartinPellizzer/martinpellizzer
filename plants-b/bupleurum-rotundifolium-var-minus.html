Bupleurum rotundifolium var. minus

Bupleurum rotundifolium var. minus, commonly known as the lesser round-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, rounded or ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.
