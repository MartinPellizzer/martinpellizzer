Bergera compressa

Bergera compressa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Bergera compressa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its resilience and beauty.
