Bidens phalangiphylla

Bidens phalangiphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, alternate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into spiny, three-lobed seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bidens phalangiphylla is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
