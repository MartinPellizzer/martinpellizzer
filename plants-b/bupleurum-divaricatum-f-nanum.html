Bupleurum divaricatum f. nanum

Bupleurum divaricatum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf divaricatum bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
