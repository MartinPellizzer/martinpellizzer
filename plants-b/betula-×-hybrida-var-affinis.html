Betula × hybrida var. affinis

Betula × hybrida var. affinis, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silver sheen beneath, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring. The tree has lance-shaped leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Betula × hybrida var. affinis is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
