Berberis densiflora var. macracantha

Berberis densiflora var. macracantha, commonly known as the dense-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
