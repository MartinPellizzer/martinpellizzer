Boesenbergia cordata

Boesenbergia cordata, commonly known as the red ginger or heartleaf ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herb with lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that have a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces fragrant, orange-red flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental gardening. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Boesenbergia cordata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal benefits.
