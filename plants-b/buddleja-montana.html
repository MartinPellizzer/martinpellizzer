Buddleja montana

Buddleja montana, commonly known as the Tibetan butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath, and it produces long, panicles of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
