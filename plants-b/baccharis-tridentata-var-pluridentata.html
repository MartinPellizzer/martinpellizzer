Baccharis tridentata var. pluridentata

Baccharis tridentata var. pluridentata, commonly known as the desert mistletoe, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in scrublands and along rocky slopes.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
