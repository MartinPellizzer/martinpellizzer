Bisnaga hamatacantha subsp. sinuata

Bisnaga hamatacantha subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuata bisnaga, is a member of the Acanthaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically clustered in whorls, displaying a range of colors from pink to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
