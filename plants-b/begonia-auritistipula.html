Begonia auritistipula

Begonia auritistipula, commonly known as the ear-tipped begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive ear-like projections at the base. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green and silver tones.

Begonia auritistipula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
