Bourdonia

Bourdonia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bignoniaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Bourdonia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant floral displays.
