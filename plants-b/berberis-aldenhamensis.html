Berberis aldenhamensis

Berberis aldenhamensis, commonly known as Aldenham barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the UK.

It features thorny stems and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
