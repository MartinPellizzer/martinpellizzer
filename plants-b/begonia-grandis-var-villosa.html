Begonia grandis var. villosa

Begonia grandis var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, long-stemmed flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
