Berberis hartwegii

Berberis hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features prickly stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Berberis hartwegii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
