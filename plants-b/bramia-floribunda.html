Bramia floribunda

Bramia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering bramia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae, which includes many tropical plants known for their vibrant foliage and floral displays. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure, typical of bromeliads. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Bramia floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
