Brachypodium pinnatum var. abbreviatum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short stature, typically growing to a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
