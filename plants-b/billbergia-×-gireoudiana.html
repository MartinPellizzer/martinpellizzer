Billbergia × gireoudiana

Billbergia × gireoudiana, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia guzmanae.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia × gireoudiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
