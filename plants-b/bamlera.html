Bamlera

Bamlera, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America. The plants typically have basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Bamlera species are often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
