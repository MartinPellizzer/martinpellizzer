Betula × zabelii

Betula × zabelii, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a deciduous tree that results from the cross between the paper birch (Betula papyrifera) and the white birch (Betula pendula).

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and papery, white bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Betula × zabelii is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America, where it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability to a range of soil types and climates makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
