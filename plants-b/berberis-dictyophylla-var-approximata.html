Berberis dictyophylla var. approximata

Berberis dictyophylla var. approximata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically yellow, appearing in clusters. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
