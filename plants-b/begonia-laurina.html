Begonia laurina

Begonia laurina, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
