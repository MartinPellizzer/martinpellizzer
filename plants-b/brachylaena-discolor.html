Brachylaena discolor

Brachylaena discolor, commonly known as the red-hot-plate or fireplant, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, creating a striking visual effect. The plant has greyish-green, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Brachylaena discolor is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and coastal conditions, enhancing its adaptability in various environments.
