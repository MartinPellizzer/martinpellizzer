Bidens ostruthioides var. typica

Bidens ostruthioides var. typica, commonly known as the common tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and prominent white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
