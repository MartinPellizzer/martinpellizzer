Botherbe campestris

Botherbe campestris, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
