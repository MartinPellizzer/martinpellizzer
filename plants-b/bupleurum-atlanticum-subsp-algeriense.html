Bupleurum atlanticum subsp. algeriense

Bupleurum atlanticum subsp.

algeriense, commonly known as Algerian sea holly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, arid conditions.

The plant features compound leaves with a silvery-blue appearance, and its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, often with a pale blue or white color. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and coastal landscaping.
