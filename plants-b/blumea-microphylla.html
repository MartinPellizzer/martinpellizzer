Blumea microphylla

Blumea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blumea microphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
