Begonia × pseudophyllomaniaca

Begonia × pseudophyllomaniaca, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pendula.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant typically features heart-shaped or rounded leaves with a glossy texture, and it thrives in well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant, often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its ability to spread readily.

This hybrid is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
