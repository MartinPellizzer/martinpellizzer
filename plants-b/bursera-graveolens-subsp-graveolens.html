Bursera graveolens subsp. graveolens

Bursera graveolens subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as copal tree or sacred tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America. It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap.

The tree has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually leathery and dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in religious and medicinal practices.
