Breweria ferruginea

Breweria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty brewer's weed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Breweria ferruginea is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
