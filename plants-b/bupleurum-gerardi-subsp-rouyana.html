Bupleurum gerardi subsp. rouyana

Bupleurum gerardi subsp.

rouyana, commonly known as the Rouyana bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features slender, branched stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
