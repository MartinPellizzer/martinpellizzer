Bambusa albolineata

Bambusa albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined bamboo, is a striking species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often exhibit a pale green or almost white coloration along the midribs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

Bambusa albolineata is also valued in traditional medicine and as a sustainable building material in some regions.
