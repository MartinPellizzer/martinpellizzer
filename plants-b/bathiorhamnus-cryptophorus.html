Bathiorhamnus cryptophorus

Bathiorhamnus cryptophorus, commonly known as the cryptophore hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rhamnaceae family.
