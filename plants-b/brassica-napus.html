Brassica-napus

Brassica napus, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual or biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy, while the upper leaves are linear and alternate. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are the primary product of the plant.

Brassica napus is native to Europe and Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its oilseed and agricultural uses.
