Botryadenia

Botryadenia, commonly known as the "Coral Tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, South America, and the Caribbean. Botryadenia species typically have compound leaves and produce small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant floral displays.
