Bombax longiflorum

Bombax longiflorum, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically ovate or lanceolate in shape.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
