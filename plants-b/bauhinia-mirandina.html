Bauhinia mirandina

Bauhinia mirandina, commonly known as the mirror bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its small, flat pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Bauhinia mirandina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
