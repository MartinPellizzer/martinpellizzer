Boronia barkeriana subsp. angustifolia

Boronia barkeriana subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to the wetter regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and environmental changes.
