Boerhavia arborea

Boerhavia arborea, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its branching structure, with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a low water requirement.

Boerhavia arborea is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
