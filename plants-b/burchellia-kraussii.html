Burchellia kraussii

Burchellia kraussii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Burchellia kraussii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
