Buddleja variabilis var. magnifica

Buddleja variabilis var. magnifica, commonly known as the Giant Butterfly Bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a wide range of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
