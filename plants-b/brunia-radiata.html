Brunia radiata

Brunia radiata, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of silvery-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long blooming period.
