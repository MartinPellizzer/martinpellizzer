Barleria setosa

Barleria setosa, commonly known as the slender barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, usually in shades of pink or red. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
