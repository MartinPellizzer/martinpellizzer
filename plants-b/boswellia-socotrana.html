Boswellia socotrana

Boswellia socotrana, commonly known as frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Socotra Archipelago.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny leaves and pale yellow or white resinous sap, which is harvested for its aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family and is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat. Its resin, known as olibanum, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and religious ceremonies.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various local species.
