Benthamia longicalceata

Benthamia longicalceata, commonly known as the long calyx benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant's most distinctive feature is its elongated, fleshy calyx, which persists even after the flowers have withered. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Benthamia longicalceata is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
