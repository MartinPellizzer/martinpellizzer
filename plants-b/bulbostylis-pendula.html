Bulbostylis pendula

Bulbostylis pendula, commonly known as the hanging bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to submerged soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Bulbostylis pendula is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant, drooping appearance.
