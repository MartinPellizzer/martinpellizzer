Berberis edgeworthiana

Berberis edgeworthiana, commonly known as the Edgeworth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its berries are toxic to humans and livestock, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
