Bryophyllum cochleatum

Bryophyllum cochleatum, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces rosettes of foliage with showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate light, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings is a notable botanical feature.
