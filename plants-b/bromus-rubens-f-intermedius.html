Bromus rubens f. intermedius

Bromus rubens f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate red brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flowers in the summer.

This subspecies is often found in the western United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
