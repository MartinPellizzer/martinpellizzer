Begonia pseudodaxinensis

Begonia pseudodaxinensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical regions of China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a unique pattern of veins, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
