Bulbophyllum ochthochilum

Bulbophyllum ochthochilum, commonly known as the Orchid with a crown, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical environments. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, with a distinctive crown-like structure at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
