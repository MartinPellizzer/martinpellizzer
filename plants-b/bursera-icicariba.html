Bursera icicariba

Bursera icicariba, commonly known as the "Balsam of the Caribbean," is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing three seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
