Begonia enoplocampa

Begonia enoplocampa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
