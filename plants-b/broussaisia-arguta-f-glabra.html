Broussaisia arguta f. glabra

Broussaisia arguta f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth broussaisia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
