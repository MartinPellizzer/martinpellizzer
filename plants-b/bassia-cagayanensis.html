Bassia cagayanensis

Bassia cagayanensis, commonly known as the Cagayan goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential horticultural value.
