Bambusa emeiensis f. flavidovirens

Bambusa emeiensis f. flavidovirens, commonly known as the golden-green bamboo, is a variant of the Emei bamboo species characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage.

This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique coloration. It typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo with slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates.
