Beaucarnea erumpens

Beaucarnea erumpens, commonly known as the ponytail palm, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, tree-like stem with a swollen base that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its most distinctive characteristic is the tuft of long, curly leaves at the top, which resemble a ponytail. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant.

It requires minimal water and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight.
