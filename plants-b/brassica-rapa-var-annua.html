Brassica rapa var. annua

Brassica rapa var. annua, commonly known as Chinese mustard or bird's mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, which can grow up to 1 meter in height, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in cooking or as a spice. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth cycle and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution.
