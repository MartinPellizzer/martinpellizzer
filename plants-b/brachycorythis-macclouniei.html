Brachycorythis macclouniei

Brachycorythis macclouniei, commonly known as the MacCloun's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. Native to Madagascar, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
