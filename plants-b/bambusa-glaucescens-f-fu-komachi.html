Bambusa glaucescens f. fu-komachi

Bambusa glaucescens f. fu-komachi, commonly known as the Fu-Komachi bamboo, is a decorative grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that exhibit a striking silver-blue sheen, especially when young. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by feathery, light green to yellowish seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and landscapes.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for bamboo enthusiasts and landscape designers.
