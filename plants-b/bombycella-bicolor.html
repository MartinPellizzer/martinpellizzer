Bombycella bicolor

Bombycella bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, contrasting colors on its petals and sepals, which range from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
