Barringtonia novae-hiberniae subsp. novae-hiberniae

Barringtonia novae-hiberniae subsp.

novae-hiberniae, commonly known as the New Ireland banyan, is a tropical tree native to the island of New Ireland in the Bismarck Archipelago. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous inflorescences that produce fragrant, white flowers.

The tree produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
