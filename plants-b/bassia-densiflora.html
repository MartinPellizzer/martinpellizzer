Bassia densiflora

Bassia densiflora, commonly known as denseflower goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically greenish-white in color.

Bassia densiflora is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep taproots and the ability to store water in its tissues.
