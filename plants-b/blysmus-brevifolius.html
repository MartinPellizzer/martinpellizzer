Blysmus brevifolius

Blysmus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blysmus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months.

Blysmus brevifolius is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
