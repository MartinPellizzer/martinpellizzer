Babiana villosula

Babiana villosula, commonly known as the woolly babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly leaves and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
