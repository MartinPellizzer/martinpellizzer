Byttneria acuminata

Byttneria acuminata, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that ripens in late summer, and it is known for its medicinal and economic uses in traditional African medicine.
