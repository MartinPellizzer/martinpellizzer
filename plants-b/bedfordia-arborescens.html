Bedfordia arborescens

Bedfordia arborescens, commonly known as the tree bedfordia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bedfordia arborescens is often found in moist, sandy soils in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
