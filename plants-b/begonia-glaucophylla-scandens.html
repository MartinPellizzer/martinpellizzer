Begonia glaucophylla scandens

Begonia glaucophylla scandens, commonly known as the silver-leaf climbing begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its velvety, silver-green foliage.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy texture that reflects light, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in greenhouses.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
