Brassica fruticulosa subsp. mauritanica

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian mustard, is a perennial herb native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of Mediterranean and Saharan flora.
