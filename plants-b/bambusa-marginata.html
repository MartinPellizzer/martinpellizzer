Bambusa marginata

Bambusa marginata, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass that typically grows to a height of 2-4 meters. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with silver or white along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value.

Bambusa marginata is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
