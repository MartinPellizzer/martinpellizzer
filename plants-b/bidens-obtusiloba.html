Bidens obtusiloba

Bidens obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with prominent white markings.

Bidens obtusiloba is often found in wetlands and is an important plant for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
