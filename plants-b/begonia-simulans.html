Begonia simulans

Begonia simulans, commonly known as the variable begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.

Begonia simulans is also noted for its ability to produce numerous offspring through vegetative propagation.
