Brunfelsia hopeana var. australis

Brunfelsia hopeana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
