Brachiaria paucispicata

Brachiaria paucispicata, commonly known as slender brachiaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Brachiaria paucispicata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common species in grasslands and pasture areas.
