Betonica vulgaris

Betonica vulgaris, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Betonica vulgaris has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing and healing properties.
