Bambusa basihirsutoides

Bambusa basihirsutoides, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by light green, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fast growth rate, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
