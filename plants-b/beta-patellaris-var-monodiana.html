Beta patellaris var. monodiana

Beta patellaris var. monodiana, commonly known as the Patellaris beet, is a rare variant of the beta genus native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, leaf-like structures that resemble leaves rather than the typical fleshy roots of other beta species. The plant exhibits a unique coloration, with vibrant purple and red hues in its foliage, which is attributed to high concentrations of anthocyanins. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
