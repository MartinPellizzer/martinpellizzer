Berlinia preussii

Berlinia preussii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
