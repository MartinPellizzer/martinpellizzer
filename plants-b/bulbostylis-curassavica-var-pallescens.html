Bulbostylis curassavica var. pallescens

Bulbostylis curassavica var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-stemmed bulrush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
