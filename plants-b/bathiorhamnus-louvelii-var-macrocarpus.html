Bathiorhamnus louvelii var. macrocarpus

Bathiorhamnus louvelii var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited bathiorhamnus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that mature to a red color, containing several seeds.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
