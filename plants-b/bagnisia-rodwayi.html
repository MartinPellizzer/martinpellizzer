Bagnisia rodwayi

Bagnisia rodwayi, commonly known as the velvetleaf bagnisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Bagnisia rodwayi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological studies.
