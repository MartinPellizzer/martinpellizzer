Bistella digyna

Bistella digyna, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by insects. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds.

Bistella digyna is notable for its ability to derive some of its nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
