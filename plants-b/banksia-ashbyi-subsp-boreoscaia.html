Banksia ashbyi subsp. boreoscaia

Banksia ashbyi subsp.

boreoscaia, commonly known as the southern banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
