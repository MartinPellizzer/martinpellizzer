Buesiella ornata

Buesiella ornata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Buesiella ornata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
