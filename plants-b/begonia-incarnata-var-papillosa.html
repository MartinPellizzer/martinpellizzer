Begonia incarnata var. papillosa

Begonia incarnata var. papillosa, commonly known as the papillate begonia, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive papillate (bumpy) texture on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Its unique floral structure and foliage make it a popular choice for both horticultural and botanical study.
