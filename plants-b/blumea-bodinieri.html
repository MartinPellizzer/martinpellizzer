Blumea bodinieri

Blumea bodinieri, commonly known as the Vietnamese blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in a mist-like appearance, giving it its common name. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Blumea bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
