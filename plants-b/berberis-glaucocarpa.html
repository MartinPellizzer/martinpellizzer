Berberis glaucocarpa

Berberis glaucocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves with a waxy coating, and small, blue-black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is part of the Berberidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so care should be taken when handling or planting this species.
