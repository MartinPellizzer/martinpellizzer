Buchnera major

Buchnera major, commonly known as the giant buchnera, is a unique plant species characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, fleshy leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in low-water environments. The plant's leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. Buchnera major produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
