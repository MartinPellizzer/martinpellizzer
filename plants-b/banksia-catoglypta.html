Banksia catoglypta

Banksia catoglypta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers provide nectar for a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
