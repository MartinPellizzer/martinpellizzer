Bumelia lycioides var. reclinata

Bumelia lycioides var. reclinata, commonly known as the slender bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often ascending stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
