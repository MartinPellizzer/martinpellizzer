Blepharis abyssinica

Blepharis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian wireweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
