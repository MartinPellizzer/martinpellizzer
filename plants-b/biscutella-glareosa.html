Biscutella glareosa

Biscutella glareosa, commonly known as the glaucous biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are typically glaucous, covered in a waxy coating that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Biscutella glareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
