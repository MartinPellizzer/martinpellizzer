Boerhavia repens var. glabra

Boerhavia repens var. glabra, commonly known as smooth bluet, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
