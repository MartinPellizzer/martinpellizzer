Bromus inermis f. muticus

Bromus inermis f. muticus, commonly known as the silent brome, is a variant of the slender brome grass.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its fine, soft leaves and tufted inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its botanical features include slender stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in various environments.
