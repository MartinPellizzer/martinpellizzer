Berberis tianchiensis

Berberis tianchiensis, commonly known as the Tianchi barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
