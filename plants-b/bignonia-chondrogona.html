Bignonia chondrogona

Bignonia chondrogona, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow to orange tubular flowers, which bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and twining. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia chondrogona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
