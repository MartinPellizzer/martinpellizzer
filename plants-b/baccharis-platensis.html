Baccharis platensis

Baccharis platensis, commonly known as the river holly, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Baccharis platensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
