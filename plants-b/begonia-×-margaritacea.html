Begonia × margaritacea

Begonia × margaritacea, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a hybrid species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage, which is often the main attraction of the plant.
