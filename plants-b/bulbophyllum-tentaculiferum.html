Bulbophyllum tentaculiferum

Bulbophyllum tentaculiferum, commonly known as the tentacle-armed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with intricate patterns, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This epiphytic plant requires well-draining substrate and consistent moisture to flourish.
