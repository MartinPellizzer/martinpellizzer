Bidens decussata

Bidens decussata, commonly known as common beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. The plant produces small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by attachment to animals or clothing. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many regions.
