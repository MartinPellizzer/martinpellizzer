Baptisia × bushii

Baptisia × bushii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant purple to blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its root system consists of deep taproots that help it thrive in well-drained soils. Baptisia × bushii is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
