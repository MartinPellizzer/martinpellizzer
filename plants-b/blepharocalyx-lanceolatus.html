Blepharocalyx lanceolatus

Blepharocalyx lanceolatus, commonly known as the white-flowered trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
