Bidens praecox

Bidens praecox, commonly known as early tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features hairy, slender stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets and a distinctive central disk, blooming from early summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Bidens praecox is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
