Belutta sessilis

Belutta sessilis, commonly known as the sessile bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. Its stem is erect and can grow up to 30 cm in height, supporting the clusters of flowers.

Belutta sessilis prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
