Besleria violacea

Besleria violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered gesneriad, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant has fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowering stems are usually short and branching, producing clusters of showy, tubular flowers.

Besleria violacea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care in suitable conditions.
