Bacopa hamiltoniana var. hamiltoniana

Bacopa hamiltoniana var. hamiltoniana, commonly known as the "water bead" or "Java moss," is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, creeping plant that forms dense mats on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

This plant is valued in aquariums and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
