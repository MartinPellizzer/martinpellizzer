Baloghia drimiflora

Baloghia drimiflora, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Baloghia drimiflora is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and ecological benefits.
