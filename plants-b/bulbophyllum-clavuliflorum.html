Bulbophyllum clavuliflorum

Bulbophyllum clavuliflorum, commonly known as the club-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow with a faintly colored lip. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the forest floor.

Bulbophyllum clavuliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
