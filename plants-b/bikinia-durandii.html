Bikinia durandii

Bikinia durandii, commonly known as the Durand's cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It is native to southern Africa, particularly in regions of South Africa and Namibia.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
