Bubbia comptonii

Bubbia comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, burr-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and succulent, helping it retain moisture.

Bubbia comptonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
