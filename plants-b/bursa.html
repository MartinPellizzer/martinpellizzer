Bursa

Bursa, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Bursa has small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its leaves are usually alternate and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.
