Boea hygroscopica

Boea hygroscopica, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Its leaves are glossy and have a slight silvery sheen, giving it its common name.
