Bulbophyllum nigrescens

Bulbophyllum nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and usually greenish or brownish, with a unique structure that helps in pollination by specific insect species. The plant's name refers to the darkening of its flowers as they age, a characteristic feature of the species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
