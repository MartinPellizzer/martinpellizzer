Begonia calciphila

Begonia calciphila, commonly known as the limestone begonia, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to grow in calcium-rich environments.

It thrives in rocky soils and limestone substrates, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive shape.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
