Blyttia fruticulosa

Blyttia fruticulosa, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra or mountainous habitats.

Blyttia fruticulosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its ecosystem.
