Bromus gilliesii

Bromus gilliesii, commonly known as Gillies' brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Bromus gilliesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
