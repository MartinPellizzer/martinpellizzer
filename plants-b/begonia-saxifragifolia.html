Begonia saxifragifolia

Begonia saxifragifolia, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
