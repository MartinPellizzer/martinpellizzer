Bauhinia xanthoneura

Bauhinia xanthoneura, commonly known as the yellow flame bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the spring and early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and divided into numerous small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
