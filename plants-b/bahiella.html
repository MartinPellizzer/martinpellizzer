Bahiella

Bahiella, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Bahiella is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
