Bouchea pterygocarpa

Bouchea pterygocarpa, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the leaves and a distinctive fruit capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Bouchea pterygocarpa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
