Begonia solitudinis

Begonia solitudinis, commonly known as the solitary begonia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
