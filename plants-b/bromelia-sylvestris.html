Bromelia sylvestris

Bromelia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The leaves are typically broad, waxy, and brightly colored, often in shades of green, red, or orange. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to epiphytic growth on trees or rocks.

Its vibrant foliage and unique structure make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
