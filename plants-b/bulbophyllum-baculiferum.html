Bulbophyllum baculiferum

Bulbophyllum baculiferum, commonly known as the club-bearing orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, club-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and horticultural enthusiasts.
