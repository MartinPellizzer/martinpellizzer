Brodiaea californica subsp. californica

Brodiaea californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California brodiaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California. It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is characterized by its bulbous underground storage structures, which help it survive in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
