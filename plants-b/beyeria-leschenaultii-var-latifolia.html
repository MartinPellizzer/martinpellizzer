Beyeria leschenaultii var. latifolia

Beyeria leschenaultii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved beyeria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
