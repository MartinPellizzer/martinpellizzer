Bromus villosus subsp. gussonei

Bromus villosus subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
