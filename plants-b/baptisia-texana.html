Baptisia texana

Baptisia texana, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive, bean-like seed pods that split open when mature.

Baptisia texana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native landscaping.
