Bactris anisitsii

Bactris anisitsii, commonly known as the red palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Its inflorescences produce small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible fruits resembling small, elongated berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
