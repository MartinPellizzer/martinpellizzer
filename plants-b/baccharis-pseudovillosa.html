Baccharis pseudovillosa

Baccharis pseudovillosa, commonly known as the silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and coastal areas.

Baccharis pseudovillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
