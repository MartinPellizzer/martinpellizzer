Bulbophyllum cavipes

Bulbophyllum cavipes, commonly known as the hollow-footed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hollowed base and elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers with a wide range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
