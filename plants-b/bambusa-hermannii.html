Bambusa hermannii

Bambusa hermannii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and its ability to form dense stands, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a distinctive golden hue along the leaf margins. Its culms are hollow and segmented, with a smooth, pale yellowish-brown surface.

Bambusa hermannii is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
