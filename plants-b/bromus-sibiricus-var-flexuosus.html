Bromus sibiricus var. flexuosus

Bromus sibiricus var. flexuosus, commonly known as the Siberian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often curve downward. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
