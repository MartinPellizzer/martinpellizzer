Betonica angustifolia

Betonica angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.
