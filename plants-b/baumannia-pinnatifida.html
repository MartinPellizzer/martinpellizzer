Baumannia pinnatifida

Baumannia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed baumannia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies in its native habitat.
