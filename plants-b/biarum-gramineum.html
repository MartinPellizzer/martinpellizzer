Biarum gramineum

Biarum gramineum, commonly known as the grassy lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland gardens.

Biarum gramineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
