Brickellia chlorolepis

Brickellia chlorolepis, commonly known as the yellow brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are arranged in dense clusters.

Brickellia chlorolepis is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
