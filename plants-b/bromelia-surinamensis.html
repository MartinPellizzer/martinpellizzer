Bromelia surinamensis

Bromelia surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can last for several months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
