Bauhinia fleuryi

Bauhinia fleuryi, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, flame-like leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, which bloom during the wet season. Bauhinia fleuryi is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a rounded crown and smooth, greyish bark.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
