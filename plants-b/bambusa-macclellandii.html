Bambusa macclellandii

Bambusa macclellandii, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant typically grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa macclellandii is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.
