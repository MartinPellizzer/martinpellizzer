Brasiliocroton

Brasiliocroton, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically features thick, waxy leaves that can vary in shape and color, often with a striking green or variegated pattern. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a central cup-like structure called a cyathium.

Brasiliocroton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
