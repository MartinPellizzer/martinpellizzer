Boeica glandulosa

Boeica glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular boeica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are covered in tiny glands, which contribute to its distinctive scent and appearance.

Boeica glandulosa is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in herbal medicine.
