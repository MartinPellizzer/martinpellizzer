Beta cicla var. argentea

Beta cicla var. argentea, commonly known as silver beet, is a cultivar of the beetroot plant characterized by its striking silver-green foliage.

The leaves are broad and have a slightly puckered texture, with a pale, almost metallic sheen that gives it its common name. The plant produces edible leaves and roots, with the latter being particularly prized for its sweet, earthy flavor. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and nutritional value.
