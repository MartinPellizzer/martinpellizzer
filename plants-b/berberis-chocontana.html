Berberis chocontana

Berberis chocontana, commonly known as the Choconta barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features spiny, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Berberis chocontana is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
