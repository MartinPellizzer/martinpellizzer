Bikukulla chrysantha

Bikukulla chrysantha, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
