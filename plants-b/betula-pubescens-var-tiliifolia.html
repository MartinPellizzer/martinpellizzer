Betula pubescens var. tiliifolia

Betula pubescens var. tiliifolia, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
