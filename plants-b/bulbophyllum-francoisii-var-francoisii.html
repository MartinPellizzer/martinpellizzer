Bulbophyllum francoisii var. francoisii

Bulbophyllum francoisii var. francoisii, commonly known as the Francois's Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and often hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
