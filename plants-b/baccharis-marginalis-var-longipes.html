Baccharis marginalis var. longipes

Baccharis marginalis var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in xeriscaping and erosion control efforts.
