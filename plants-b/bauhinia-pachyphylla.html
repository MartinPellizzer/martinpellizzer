Bauhinia pachyphylla

Bauhinia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces striking clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Bauhinia pachyphylla is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
