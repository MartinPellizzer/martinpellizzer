Bromus hordeaceus var. devesanus

Bromus hordeaceus var. devesanus, commonly known as the Devesan brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has narrow, linear leaves and small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of grassland vegetation.
