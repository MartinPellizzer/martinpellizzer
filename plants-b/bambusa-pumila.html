Bambusa pumila

Bambusa pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often clumping stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, making it suitable for small garden spaces. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
