Bromopsis pamirica

Bromopsis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Bromopsis pamirica is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its contribution to soil stabilization in mountainous regions.
