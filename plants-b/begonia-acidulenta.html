Begonia acidulenta

Begonia acidulenta, commonly known as the sour begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Begonia acidulenta is also known for its ability to produce runners, which can be used for propagation.
