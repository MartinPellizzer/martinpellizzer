Bistella dichotoma

Bistella dichotoma, commonly known as the branching mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to parts of Africa.

It has a distinctive appearance with its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal branches. The plant possesses small, ovate leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It relies on host trees for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis.

Bistella dichotoma is often found in forested areas and can be a significant component of certain ecosystems.
