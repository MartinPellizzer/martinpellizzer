Berteroa incana

Berteroa incana, commonly known as hoary alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Berteroa incana is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.
