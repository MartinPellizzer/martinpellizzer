Beta vulgaris var. aurantia

Beta vulgaris var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden beet, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flesh and thick, smooth skin. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is typically grown for its edible roots and leaves. Its roots are often used in cooking, while the leaves can be consumed like spinach.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
