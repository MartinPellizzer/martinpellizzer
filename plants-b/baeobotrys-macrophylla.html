Baeobotrys macrophylla

Baeobotrys macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, making it a prominent feature in freshwater habitats. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still water, often found in marshes and ponds. Its leaves are typically a deep green, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Baeobotrys macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
