Begonia fischeri var. ermanii

Begonia fischeri var. ermanii, commonly known as the Erman's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
