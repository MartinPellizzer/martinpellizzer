Baccharis amambayensis

Baccharis amambayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay feverfew, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in local remedies.
