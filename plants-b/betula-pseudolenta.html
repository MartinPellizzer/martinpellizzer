Betula pseudolenta

Betula pseudolenta, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of light brown beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has slender, pendulous branches and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Betula pseudolenta prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
