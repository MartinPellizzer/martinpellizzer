Bromus korotkiji

Bromus korotkiji, commonly known as the Korotkij's brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Bromus korotkiji is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
