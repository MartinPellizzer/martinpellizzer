Baccharis reticulata

Baccharis reticulata, commonly known as the laceleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Baccharis reticulata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
