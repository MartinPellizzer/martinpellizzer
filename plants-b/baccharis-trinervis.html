Baccharis trinervis

Baccharis trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
