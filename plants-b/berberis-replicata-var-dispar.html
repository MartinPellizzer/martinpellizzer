Berberis replicata var. dispar

Berberis replicata var. dispar, commonly known as the variable barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various garden environments.
