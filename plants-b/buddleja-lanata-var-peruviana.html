Buddleja lanata var. peruviana

Buddleja lanata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian woolly buddleia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white downy foliage and clusters of fragrant, lavender-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its nectar-rich flowers attract butterflies and other pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
