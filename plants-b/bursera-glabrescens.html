Bursera glabrescens

Bursera glabrescens, commonly known as the white bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by rounded, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical dry forests and scrublands.

Bursera glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
