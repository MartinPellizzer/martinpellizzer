Bunchosia martiana

Bunchosia martiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Bunchosia martiana is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for crafting and construction.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
