Boehmeria repanda

Boehmeria repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and India. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fibers of its stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional textile production.
