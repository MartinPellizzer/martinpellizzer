Bauhinia oaxacana

Bauhinia oaxacana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orchid-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia oaxacana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
