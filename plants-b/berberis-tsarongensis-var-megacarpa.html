Berberis tsarongensis var. megacarpa

Berberis tsarongensis var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant-fruited barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its robust, thorny stems and dense foliage of dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.
