Berberis magnifica

Berberis magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that provide ornamental value in gardens.

Berberis magnifica is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
