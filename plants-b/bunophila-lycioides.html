Bunophila lycioides

Bunophila lycioides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers that turn into fluffy seed heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy leaves and stems, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Bunophila lycioides is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
