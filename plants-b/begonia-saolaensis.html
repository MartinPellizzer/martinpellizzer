Begonia saolaensis

Begonia saolaensis, commonly known as the Saola Mountains begonia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Vietnam and southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in dense forests at high altitudes. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, very little is known about its ecological role and reproductive habits.
