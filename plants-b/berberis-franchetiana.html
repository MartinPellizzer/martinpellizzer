Berberis franchetiana

Berberis franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine is also noted.
