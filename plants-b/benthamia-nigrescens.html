Benthamia nigrescens

Benthamia nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening benthamia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spiny fruits.

Benthamia nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
