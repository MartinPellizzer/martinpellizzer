Bursera aromatica

Bursera aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and fragrant, aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries.

Bursera aromatica is often found in dry, tropical, and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
