Belvisia caerulea

Belvisia caerulea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Belvisia caerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
