Bromelia fastuosa var. bergmannii

Bromelia fastuosa var. bergmannii, commonly known as the Bergmann's bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often covered in a dense, woolly layer of trichomes, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
