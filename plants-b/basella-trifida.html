Basella trifida

Basella trifida, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and edible stems and leaves, which are used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Basella trifida is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal.
