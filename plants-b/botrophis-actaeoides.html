Botrophis actaeoides

Botrophis actaeoides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on the bark of trees, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a single, elongated flower spike.

Due to its habitat specificity and declining populations, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
