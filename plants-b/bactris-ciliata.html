Bactris ciliata

Bactris ciliata, commonly known as the pink rafia palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Bactris ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

Its leaves are also used in traditional crafts, particularly for making thatch and baskets.
