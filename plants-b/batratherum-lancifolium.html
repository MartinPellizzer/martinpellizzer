Batratherum lancifolium

Batratherum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved battherum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a distinctive pointed tip, contributing to its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in the spring.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
