Bupleurum baumgartenianum

Bupleurum baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters.
