Baccharoides adoensis var. mossambiquensis

Baccharoides adoensis var. mossambiquensis, commonly known as the Mozambique buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland savanna regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
