Bignonia

Bignonia, commonly known as crossvine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Bignoniaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The leaves are usually opposite, simple, and often ovate or lanceolate in shape. Bignonia species are native to the Americas, particularly the southeastern United States and parts of South America.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
