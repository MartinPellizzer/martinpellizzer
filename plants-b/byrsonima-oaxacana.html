Byrsonima oaxacana

Byrsonima oaxacana, commonly known as the Mexican crabapple, is a small tree or shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, crabapple-like fruits. The fruits are typically orange or red when ripe and are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry to moist forests.
