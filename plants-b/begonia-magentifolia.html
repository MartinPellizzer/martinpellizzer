Begonia magentifolia

Begonia magentifolia, commonly known as the magenta-leaved begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety magenta leaves that contrast beautifully with its delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is often variegated, with a unique combination of green and red tones, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Begonia magentifolia requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
