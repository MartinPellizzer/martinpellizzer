Brachiaria reticulata

Brachiaria reticulata, commonly known as horse grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to form extensive mats. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, giving it its scientific name. It is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a common species in pastures and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria reticulata is also valued for its forage quality and erosion control capabilities.
