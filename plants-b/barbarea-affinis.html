Barbarea affinis

Barbarea affinis, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically in spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Barbarea affinis is often considered a weed but has ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers.
