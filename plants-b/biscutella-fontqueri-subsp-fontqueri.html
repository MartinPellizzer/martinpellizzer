Biscutella fontqueri subsp. fontqueri

Biscutella fontqueri subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada biscutella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
