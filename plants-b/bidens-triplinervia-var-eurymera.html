Bidens triplinervia var. eurymera

Bidens triplinervia var. eurymera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, open habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
