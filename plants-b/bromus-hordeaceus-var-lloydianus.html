Bromus hordeaceus var. lloydianus

Bromus hordeaceus var. lloydianus, commonly known as the Lloyd's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
