Bromelia longifolia

Bromelia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The leaves are typically long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. It produces vibrant flowers, often in red or orange hues, which bloom from the center of the leaf rosette.

This plant is well adapted to growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
