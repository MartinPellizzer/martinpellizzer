Bulbophyllum vulcanorum

Bulbophyllum vulcanorum, commonly known as the Vulcan's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often vibrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in the harsh, nutrient-poor environments of its habitat.

The orchid's adaptability to volcanic soils and its striking floral display make it a notable species in botanical studies.
