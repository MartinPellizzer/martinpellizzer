Bromus erectus var. roxolanicus

Bromus erectus var. roxolanicus, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted clumps of blue-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically tinged with purple or reddish hues, which are distinctive features. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance.
