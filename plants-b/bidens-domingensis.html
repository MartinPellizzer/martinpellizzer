Bidens domingensis

Bidens domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wetlands, and it features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
