Blechnum squamatum

Blechnum squamatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in silvery scales, giving it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces spores on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blechnum squamatum is a hardy plant that is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
