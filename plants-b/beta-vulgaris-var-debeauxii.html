Beta vulgaris var. debeauxii

Beta vulgaris var. debeauxii, commonly known as the red beet or chard, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that can range in color from deep red to purple. The leaves are typically large, edible, and have a slightly bitter taste. This variety is often grown for both its roots and leaves, which are used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
