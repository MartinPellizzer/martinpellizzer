Breweria campanulata

Breweria campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower breweria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody.

Breweria campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking floral display.
