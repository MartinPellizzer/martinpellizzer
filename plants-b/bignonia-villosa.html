Bignonia villosa

Bignonia villosa, commonly known as the hairy bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
