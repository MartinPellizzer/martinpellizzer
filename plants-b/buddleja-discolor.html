Buddleja discolor

Buddleja discolor, commonly known as the silver-leaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance for full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and attraction to butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja discolor is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different climates.
