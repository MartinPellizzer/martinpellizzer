Bupleurum oblongifolium

Bupleurum oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Bupleurum oblongifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
