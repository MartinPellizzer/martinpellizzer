Ballota hirsuta var. hispida

Ballota hirsuta var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Ballota hirsuta var. hispida is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
