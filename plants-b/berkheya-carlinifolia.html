Berkheya carlinifolia

Berkheya carlinifolia, commonly known as the golden burkea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
