Boerhavia triquetra

Boerhavia triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple, typically blooming in the morning. Boerhavia triquetra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hanging baskets.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it both ornamental and adaptable to various horticultural settings.
