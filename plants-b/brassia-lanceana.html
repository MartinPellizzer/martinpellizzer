Brassia lanceana

Brassia lanceana, commonly known as the lance orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped petals and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Brassia lanceana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
