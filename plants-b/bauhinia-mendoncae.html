Bauhinia mendoncae

Bauhinia mendoncae, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia mendoncae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
