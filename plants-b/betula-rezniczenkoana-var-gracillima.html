Betula rezniczenkoana var. gracillima

Betula rezniczenkoana var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender birch, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and finely toothed, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive biology. Its bark is smooth and pale, often developing fissures with age.

This species is adapted to cold, moist environments and plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
