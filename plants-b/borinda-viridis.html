Borinda viridis

Borinda viridis, commonly known as the green borinda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Borinda viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
