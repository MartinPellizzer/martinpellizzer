Batatas auriculata

Batatas auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces edible roots that are rich in carbohydrates and have a sweet, moist texture. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
