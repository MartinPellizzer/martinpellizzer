Bubonium graveolens subsp. odorum

Bubonium graveolens subsp.

odorum, commonly known as the sweet-smelling bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple.

The flowers are characterized by their strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
