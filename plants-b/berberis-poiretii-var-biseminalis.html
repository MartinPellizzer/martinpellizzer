Berberis poiretii var. biseminalis

Berberis poiretii var. biseminalis, commonly known as the double-seeded barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green foliage, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

The varietal name "biseminalis" refers to the unique feature of having two seeds per fruit.
