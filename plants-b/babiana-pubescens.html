Babiana pubescens

Babiana pubescens, commonly known as the woolly babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and striking, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, often purple or pink blooms that appear in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Babiana pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
