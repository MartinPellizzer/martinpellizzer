Baccharis trinervis var. trinervis

Baccharis trinervis var. trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
