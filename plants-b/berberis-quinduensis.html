Berberis quinduensis

Berberis quinduensis, commonly known as the Quindu barberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Berberidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia and Ecuador, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features dense, thorny shrubs with small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
