Blaeria albida

Blaeria albida, commonly known as the white blazer, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Blaeria albida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
