Bidens tripartita var. radiata

Bidens tripartita var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive radiate pattern, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
