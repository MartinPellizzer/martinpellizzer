Barringtonia coccinea

Barringtonia coccinea, commonly known as the flamboyant tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer or early autumn. The tree has a distinctive, buttressed trunk and broad, palmate leaves that are glossy and leathery. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
