Banksia paludosa

Banksia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp banksia, is a shrub native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or heathland environments.

Banksia paludosa plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
