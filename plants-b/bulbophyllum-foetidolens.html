Bulbophyllum foetidolens

Bulbophyllum foetidolens, commonly known as the stinking orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant or foul-smelling flowers, which can vary in color from green to yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in survival in its tropical environment.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and interesting growth habits.
