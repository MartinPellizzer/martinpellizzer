Bupleurum anatolicum

Bupleurum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian cicely, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small white to yellowish flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The root and leaves of Bupleurum anatolicum are valued for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
