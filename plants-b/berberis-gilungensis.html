Berberis gilungensis

Berberis gilungensis, commonly known as the Gilungensis barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
