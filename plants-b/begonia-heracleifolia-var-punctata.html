Begonia heracleifolia var. punctata

Begonia heracleifolia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale yellow spots. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
