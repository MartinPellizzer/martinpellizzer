Brassica saxatilis subsp. blancoana

Brassica saxatilis subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as the Canary Island cabbage, is a hardy, herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
