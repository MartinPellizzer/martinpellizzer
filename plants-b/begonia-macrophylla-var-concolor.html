Begonia macrophylla var. concolor

Begonia macrophylla var. concolor, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a rich, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its lush foliage and vibrant floral display, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
