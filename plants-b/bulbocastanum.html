Bulbocastanum

Bulbocastanum, commonly known as the bulbous chestnut, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique bulb-like structure that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiky husk. Its leaves are typically broad and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss.
