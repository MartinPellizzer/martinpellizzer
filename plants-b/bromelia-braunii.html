Bromelia braunii

Bromelia braunii, commonly known as the Braun's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Bromelia braunii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
