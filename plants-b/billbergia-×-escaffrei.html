Billbergia × escaffrei

Billbergia × escaffrei, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nana and Billbergia nutans.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically red or orange and are borne on tall, branching inflorescences.

Billbergia × escaffrei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
