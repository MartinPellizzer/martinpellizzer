Berberis asiatica

Berberis asiatica, commonly known as the Asiatic barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Berberis asiatica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a hedge plant.
