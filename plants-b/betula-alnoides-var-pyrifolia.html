Betula alnoides var. pyrifolia

Betula alnoides var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a striking appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with doubly serrated margins, and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, pendulous catkins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
