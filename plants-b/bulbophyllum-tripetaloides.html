Bulbophyllum tripetaloides

Bulbophyllum tripetaloides, commonly known as the three-petaled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with three prominent petals and a lip that is often brightly colored. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and researchers.
