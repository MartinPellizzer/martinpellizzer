Berberis trigona var. longifolia

Berberis trigona var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
