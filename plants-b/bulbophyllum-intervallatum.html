Bulbophyllum intervallatum

Bulbophyllum intervallatum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
