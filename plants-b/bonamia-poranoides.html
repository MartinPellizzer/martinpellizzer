Bonamia poranoides

Bonamia poranoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bonamia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Bonamia poranoides is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.
