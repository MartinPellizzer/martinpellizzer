Bidens oligocarpa

Bidens oligocarpa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are composed of two distinct types of petals, with the outer ones being larger and more showy.

Bidens oligocarpa is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
