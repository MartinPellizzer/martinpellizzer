Brassica oleracea f. violascens

Brassica oleracea f. violascens, commonly known as violet cauliflower, is a botanical variant of the broccoli species characterized by its deep purple or violet flower heads.

The coloration arises from high concentrations of anthocyanins, which are pigments responsible for the vivid hues in many plants. This variety is typically cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible florets, which are similar in texture to regular cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other cruciferous vegetables.
