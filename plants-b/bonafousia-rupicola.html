Bonafousia rupicola

Bonafousia rupicola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its arid habitat.

Bonafousia rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
