Boea paniculata

Boea paniculata, commonly known as the silver thread vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Boea paniculata is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
