Bauhinia obtusata

Bauhinia obtusata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with soft, velvety surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

Bauhinia obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
