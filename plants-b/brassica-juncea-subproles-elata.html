Brassica juncea subproles elata

Brassica juncea subproles elata, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or winged mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in agricultural systems.
