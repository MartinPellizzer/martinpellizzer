Barbosella ricii

Barbosella ricii, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as moths and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Barbosella ricii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
