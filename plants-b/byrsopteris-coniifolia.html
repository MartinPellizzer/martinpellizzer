Byrsopteris coniifolia

Byrsopteris coniifolia, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble feathers, with a distinctive pinnae arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, which are covered by a protective indusium.

Byrsopteris coniifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
