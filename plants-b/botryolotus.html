Botryolotus

Botryolotus, commonly known as the "bottle tree," is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their thick, swollen trunks that store water, allowing them to survive in arid environments. The leaves are typically small and arranged in a rosette pattern, reducing water loss through transpiration. Botryolotus species produce showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and drought tolerance.
