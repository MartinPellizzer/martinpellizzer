Berberis wallichiana var. xanthoxylon

Berberis wallichiana var. xanthoxylon, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
