Buxus sempervirens f. glauca

Buxus sempervirens f. glauca, commonly known as the silver boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

Its foliage is a distinguishing feature, with leaves that are small, thick, and have a distinctive bluish-gray hue due to a waxy coating. This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its slow growth and ability to maintain a compact form with minimal pruning. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for formal gardens and topiary.

The plant is also resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity in landscape design.
