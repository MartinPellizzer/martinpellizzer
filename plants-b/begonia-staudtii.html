Begonia staudtii

Begonia staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia staudtii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
