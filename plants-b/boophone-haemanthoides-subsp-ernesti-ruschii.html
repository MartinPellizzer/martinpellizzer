Boophone haemanthoides subsp. ernesti-ruschii

Boophone haemanthoides subsp.

ernesti-ruschii, commonly known as the Orange River boophone, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa, particularly found in the Orange Free State and Lesotho.

The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
