Bupleurum rotundifolium var. petiolatum

Bupleurum rotundifolium var. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are rounded with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
