Bryophyllum fedtschenkoi

Bryophyllum fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the "Mother of Thousands," is a succulent plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in the spring. One of its most distinctive features is the production of tiny plantlets along the edges of its leaves, which can take root and grow into new plants.

This unique propagation method makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and propagation enthusiasts.
