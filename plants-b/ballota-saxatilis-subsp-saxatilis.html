Ballota saxatilis subsp. saxatilis

Ballota saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock ballota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters. It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Ballota saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
