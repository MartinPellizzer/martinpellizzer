Blepharodon salicinum var. tetraglandulosum

Blepharodon salicinum var. tetraglandulosum, commonly known as the four-gland willow-leaf plant, is a unique variant of the Blepharodon salicinum species.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-glandular structures on its leaves, which are a key botanical feature. The plant typically has elongated, willow-like leaves with a smooth surface and a serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, making it a notable subject in botanical studies.
