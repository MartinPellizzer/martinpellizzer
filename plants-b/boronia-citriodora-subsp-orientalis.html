Boronia citriodora subsp. orientalis

Boronia citriodora subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the lemon boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in coastal and heathland environments. Its foliage is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive citrus fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
