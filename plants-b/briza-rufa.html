Briza rufa

Briza rufa, commonly known as red feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a soft, flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Briza rufa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
