Berrebera caffra

Berrebera caffra, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. Berrebera caffra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
