Bromus erectus subvar. angustifolius

Bromus erectus subvar. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single floret.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
