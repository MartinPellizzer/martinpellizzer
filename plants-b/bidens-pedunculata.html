Bidens pedunculata

Bidens pedunculata, commonly known as the common beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features opposite leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate with serrated edges. Its small yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, bloom from summer to fall.

Bidens pedunculata is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a plant of ecological interest.
