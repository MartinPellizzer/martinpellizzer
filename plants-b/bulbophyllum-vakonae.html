Bulbophyllum vakonae

Bulbophyllum vakonae, commonly known as the Vakona orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, adapted to store water and nutrients in its native habitat.

Bulbophyllum vakonae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and rarity.
