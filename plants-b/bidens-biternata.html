Bidens biternata

Bidens biternata, commonly known as the three-parted beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals and three leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.

Bidens biternata is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
