Bupleurum falcatum var. imaicola

Bupleurum falcatum var. imaicola, commonly known as the Japanese cowplant, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, with pale yellow to greenish-white petals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
