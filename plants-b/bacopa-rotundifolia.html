Bacopa rotundifolia

Bacopa rotundifolia, commonly known as dwarf Indian pennywort, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often used in ornamental water gardens.

Bacopa rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to help purify water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
