Baccharis pilularis subsp. pilularis

Baccharis pilularis subsp.

pilularis, commonly known as coast prickly-pear, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong scent when crushed. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to coastal and inland desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
