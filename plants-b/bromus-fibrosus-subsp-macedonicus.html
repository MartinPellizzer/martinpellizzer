Bromus fibrosus subsp. macedonicus

Bromus fibrosus subsp.

macedonicus, commonly known as Macedonian fibrous brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
