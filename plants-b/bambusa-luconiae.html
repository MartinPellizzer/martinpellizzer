Bambusa luconiae

Bambusa luconiae, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Bambusa luconiae is also valued for its use in construction and as a sustainable resource.
