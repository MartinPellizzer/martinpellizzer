Buprestis latifolia

Buprestis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf jewel beetle plant, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Buprestidae, which includes jewel beetles, and is characterized by its striking, metallic-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked despite its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
