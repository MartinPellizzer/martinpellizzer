Begonia yentuensis

Begonia yentuensis, commonly known as the Yentu begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display vibrant variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
