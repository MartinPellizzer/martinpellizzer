Brassica insularis var. aquellae

Brassica insularis var. aquellae, commonly known as the Mediterranean cabbage, is a hardy biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features robust, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces compact, cabbage-like heads that are edible and often used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and utilitarian in horticultural applications.
