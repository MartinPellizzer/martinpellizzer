Barringtonia novae-hiberniae subsp. kassamii

Barringtonia novae-hiberniae subsp.

kassamii, commonly known as the Kassam's candlewood, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves.

The plant produces distinctive, lantern-like inflorescences that are pale yellow to green in color, containing numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release fibrous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking appearance.
