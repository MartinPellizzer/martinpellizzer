Bulbophyllum linearipetalum

Bulbophyllum linearipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, linear petals and a striking, often fragrant flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in shaded, high-humidity conditions.

The orchid's flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other orchid species.
