Brachysiphon ericifolius

Brachysiphon ericifolius, commonly known as the heathleaf pygmy orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, leathery leaves that resemble heath plants. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spike, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, coastal conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
