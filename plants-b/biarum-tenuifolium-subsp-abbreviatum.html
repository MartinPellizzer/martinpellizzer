Biarum tenuifolium subsp. abbreviatum

Biarum tenuifolium subsp.

abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features slender, hairy stems that produce bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its delicate, ephemeral bloom period in spring.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
