Bomarea conferta

Bomarea conferta, commonly known as the velvetleaf bomarea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant features tuberous roots and possesses a distinctive, often hairy stem that supports its showy blooms. Its leaves are typically large, lance-shaped, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Bomarea conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
