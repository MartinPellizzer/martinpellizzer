Berberis gyaitangensis

Berberis gyaitangensis, commonly known as the Gyaitang barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The Gyaitang barberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
