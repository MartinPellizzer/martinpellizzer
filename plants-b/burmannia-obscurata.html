Burmannia obscurata

Burmannia obscurata, commonly known as the dark burmannia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in humid conditions.

Burmannia obscurata is known for its unique ability to absorb nutrients through its leaves, making it a facultative epiphyte.
