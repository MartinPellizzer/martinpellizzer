Bauhinia peninsularis

Bauhinia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Bauhinia peninsularis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
