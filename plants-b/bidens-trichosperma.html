Bidens trichosperma

Bidens trichosperma, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in groups of two, giving it its scientific name. Its seeds are covered in fine hairs, which aid in wind dispersal.

Bidens trichosperma is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and forest edges.
