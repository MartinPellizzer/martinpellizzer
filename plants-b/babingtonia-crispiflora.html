Babingtonia crispiflora

Babingtonia crispiflora, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Babingtonia crispiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australian flora.
