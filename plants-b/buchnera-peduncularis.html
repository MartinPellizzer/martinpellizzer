Buchnera peduncularis

Buchnera peduncularis, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Buchnera peduncularis is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
