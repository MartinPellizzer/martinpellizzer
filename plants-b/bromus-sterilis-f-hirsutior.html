Bromus sterilis f. hirsutior

Bromus sterilis f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a variant of the false brome grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, coarse leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain grassland ecosystems.
