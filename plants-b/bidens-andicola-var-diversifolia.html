Bidens andicola var. diversifolia

Bidens andicola var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disk.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its propagation in its native habitat.
