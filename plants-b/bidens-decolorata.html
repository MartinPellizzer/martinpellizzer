Bidens decolorata

Bidens decolorata, commonly known as the yellow beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals and bracts that often appear white or pale. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually heart-shaped.

Bidens decolorata is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.
