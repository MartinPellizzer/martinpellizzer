Bulbophyllum curranii

Bulbophyllum curranii, commonly known as the Curran's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

The leaves are broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in cultivation.
