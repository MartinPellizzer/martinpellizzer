Beadlea miradorensis

Beadlea miradorensis, commonly known as the Mirador orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a unique, intricate floral structure that resembles a miniature orchid. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its flowers bloom during the late spring and early summer, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and moths.
