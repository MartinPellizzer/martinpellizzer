Bentleya

Bentleya, commonly known as the "star-of-Bethlehem," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are herbaceous perennials native to southern Africa. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and produce showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Bentleya species are adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefer full sun to partial shade.
