Blepharis madagascariensis

Blepharis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or dwarf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
