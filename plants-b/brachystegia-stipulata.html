Brachystegia stipulata

Brachystegia stipulata, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with stipules. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gallery forests and riverine habitats.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional medicinal uses.
