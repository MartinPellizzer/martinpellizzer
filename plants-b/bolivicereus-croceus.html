Bolivicereus croceus

Bolivicereus croceus, commonly known as the yellow organ pipe cactus, is a species native to Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive yellowish-green color and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are often followed by red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Bolivicereus croceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
