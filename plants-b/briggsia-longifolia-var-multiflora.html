Briggsia longifolia var. multiflora

Briggsia longifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The species is known for its ability to form dense clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
