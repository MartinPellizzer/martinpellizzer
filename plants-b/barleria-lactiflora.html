Barleria lactiflora

Barleria lactiflora, commonly known as the milky barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive milky sap that exudes when the stems are broken.

Barleria lactiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
