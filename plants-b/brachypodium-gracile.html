Brachypodium gracile

Brachypodium gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a small annual grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachypodium gracile is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
