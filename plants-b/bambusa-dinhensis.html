Bambusa dinhensis

Bambusa dinhensis, commonly known as the Dinh's bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a tall, clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant features slender, green culms with a smooth, often slightly hairy surface. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Bambusa dinhensis is valued for its strong, durable stalks, making it suitable for construction and crafts.
