Bambusa scriptoria

Bambusa scriptoria, commonly known as the bamboo grass or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Bambusa scriptoria is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
