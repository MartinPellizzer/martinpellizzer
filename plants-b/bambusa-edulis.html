Bambusa edulis

Bambusa edulis, commonly known as the edible bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has hollow, jointed stems that are typically green when young and turn yellow or brown as they mature. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Bambusa edulis is widely cultivated for its edible shoots, which are harvested during the spring season.
