Baptisia gibbesii

Baptisia gibbesii, commonly known as the eastern false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Baptisia gibbesii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
