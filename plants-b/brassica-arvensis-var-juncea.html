Brassica arvensis var. juncea

Brassica arvensis var. juncea, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and siliqua fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and vary in shape from lanceolate to ovate.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
