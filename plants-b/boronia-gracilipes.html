Boronia gracilipes

Boronia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant grows as an erect shrub, often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Boronia gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
