Bulbophyllum vermiculare

Bulbophyllum vermiculare, commonly known as the Vermiculate Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, often brightly colored pseudobulbs. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a distinctive, waxy coating.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
