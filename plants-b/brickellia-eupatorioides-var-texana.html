Brickellia eupatorioides var. texana

Brickellia eupatorioides var. texana, commonly known as Texas brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
