Bidens elliptica subvar. minima

Bidens elliptica subvar. minima, commonly known as the narrowleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange composite flowers. The leaves are elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip and a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

The plant is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
