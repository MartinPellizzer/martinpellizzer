Boronia tetrandra var. floribunda

Boronia tetrandra var. floribunda, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with small, glossy leaves and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It has a compact growth habit and is well-suited for cultivation in gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
