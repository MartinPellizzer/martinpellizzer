Bryophyllum tsaratananense

Bryophyllum tsaratananense, commonly known as the Madagascar leaf plant, is a succulent species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Bryophyllum tsaratananense is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it easy to cultivate.
