Brachypodium distachyon var. brevisetum

Brachypodium distachyon var. brevisetum, commonly known as the short-setose brachypodium, is a grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more dense inflorescence compared to the typical variety. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, two-branched spikes that bear florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

This variety is also valued in botanical research for its genetic similarity to wheat and its use as a model organism in plant biology.
