Besseya rubra

Besseya rubra, commonly known as the redstem false foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Besseya rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
