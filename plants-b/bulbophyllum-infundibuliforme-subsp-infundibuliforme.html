Bulbophyllum infundibuliforme subsp. infundibuliforme

Bulbophyllum infundibuliforme subsp.

infundibuliforme, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped inflorescence and is typically found in humid, shaded environments.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its tropical habitat.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
