Baeckea pygmaea

Baeckea pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf baeckea, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring dense, rounded foliage composed of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Baeckea pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact size and attractive foliage.
