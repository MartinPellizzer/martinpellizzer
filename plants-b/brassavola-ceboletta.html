Brassavola ceboletta

Brassavola ceboletta, commonly known as the Ceboletta orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spicy scent. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Brassavola ceboletta is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
