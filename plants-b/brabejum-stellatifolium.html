Brabejum stellatifolium

Brabejum stellatifolium, commonly known as the star-leaved brabejum, is a shrub native to the fynbos biome of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are arranged in a whorl around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
