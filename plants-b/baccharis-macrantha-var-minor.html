Baccharis macrantha var. minor

Baccharis macrantha var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and coastal areas.
