Barjonia deltoidea

Barjonia deltoidea, commonly known as the delta balm, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Barjonia deltoidea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
