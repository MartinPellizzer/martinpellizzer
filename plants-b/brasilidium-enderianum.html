Brasilidium enderianum

Brasilidium enderianum, commonly known as the Enderian bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
