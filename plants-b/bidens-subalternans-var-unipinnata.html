Bidens subalternans var. unipinnata

Bidens subalternans var. unipinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Asteraceae family. The leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often hairy, with a serrated margin. It produces numerous seeds in a cluster, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and wetlands.
