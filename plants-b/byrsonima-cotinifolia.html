Byrsonima cotinifolia

Byrsonima cotinifolia, commonly known as the Surinam cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing seeds. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
