Boehmeria virgata var. longissima

Boehmeria virgata var. longissima, commonly known as giant bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can reach several feet in length. This plant belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is known for its fibrous, woody stalks that are used in traditional crafts and papermaking. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
