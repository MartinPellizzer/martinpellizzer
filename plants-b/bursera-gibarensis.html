Bursera gibarensis

Bursera gibarensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds.

The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
