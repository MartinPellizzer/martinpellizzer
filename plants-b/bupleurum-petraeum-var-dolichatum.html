Bupleurum petraeum var. dolichatum

Bupleurum petraeum var. dolichatum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
