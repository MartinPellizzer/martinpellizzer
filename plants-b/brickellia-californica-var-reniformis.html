Brickellia californica var. reniformis

Brickellia californica var. reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are broad at the base. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
