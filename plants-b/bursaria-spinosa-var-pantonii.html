Bursaria spinosa var. pantonii

Bursaria spinosa var. pantonii, commonly known as the coastal burr bush, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in sandy soils near the coast. It is adapted to saline and well-drained environments, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.

Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
