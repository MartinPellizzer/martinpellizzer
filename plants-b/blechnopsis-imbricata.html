Blechnopsis imbricata

Blechnopsis imbricata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a silvery-white coating of trichomes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Blechnopsis imbricata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
