Biscutella maxima

Biscutella maxima, commonly known as the large biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, also known as the mustard family, and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical silique containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Biscutella maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
