Botryanthus commutatus var. albovirens

Botryanthus commutatus var. albovirens, commonly known as the white-green botryanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble small, rounded clusters. The leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, which bloom during the warmer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
