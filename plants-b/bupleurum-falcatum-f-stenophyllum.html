Bupleurum falcatum f. stenophyllum

Bupleurum falcatum f. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barbed wire plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
