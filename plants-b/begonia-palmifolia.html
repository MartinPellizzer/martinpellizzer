Begonia palmifolia

Begonia palmifolia, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, giving it a unique and dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
