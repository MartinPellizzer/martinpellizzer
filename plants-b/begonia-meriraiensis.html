Begonia meriraiensis

Begonia meriraiensis, commonly known as the Merirai begonia, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with a deep green base and red or purple veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
