Brassica barrelieri subsp. tournefortii

Brassica barrelieri subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Canary Island mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.

The subspecies tournefortii is distinguished by its specific morphology and ecological preferences compared to other subspecies of Brassica barrelieri.
