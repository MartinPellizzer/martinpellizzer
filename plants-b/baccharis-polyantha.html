Baccharis polyantha

Baccharis polyantha, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Baccharis polyantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought.
