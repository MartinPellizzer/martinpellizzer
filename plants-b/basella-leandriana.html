Basella leandriana

Basella leandriana, commonly known as the Malabar spinach, is a tropical perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a mild, spinach-like flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable or ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical regions.
