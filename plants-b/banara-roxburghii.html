Banara roxburghii

Banara roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, red drupes. It is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in traditional carpentry and musical instruments.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
