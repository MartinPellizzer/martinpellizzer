Bulbophyllum inunctum

Bulbophyllum inunctum, commonly known as the Spotted Pterostylis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest undergrowth. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, distinctive white or greenish-white flower with a yellowish lip. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and the plant is known for its unique, intricate floral structure.

Bulbophyllum inunctum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance.
