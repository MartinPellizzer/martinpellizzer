Boehmeria angustifolia

Boehmeria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hemp or Chinese hemp, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its fibrous stems, which are used in traditional papermaking and textiles.

Boehmeria angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
