Bumelia tenax f. anomala

Bumelia tenax f. anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bumelia, is a rare variant of the broadleaf bumelia.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, producing a distinctive fragrance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
