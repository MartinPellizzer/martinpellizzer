Bongardia chrysogonum

Bongardia chrysogonum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Bongardia chrysogonum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
