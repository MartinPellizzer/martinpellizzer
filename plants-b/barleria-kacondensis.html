Barleria kacondensis

Barleria kacondensis, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Barleria kacondensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
