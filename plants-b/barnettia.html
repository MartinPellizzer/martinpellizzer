Barnettia

Barnettia, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plants typically have basal leaves and produce clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. They are often found in dry, sandy soils and are adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Barnettia species are known for their delicate appearance and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value.
