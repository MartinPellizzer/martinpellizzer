Brachypodium pinnatum var. genuense

Brachypodium pinnatum var. genuense, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

The species is also of interest in botanical research for its potential in phytoremediation and as a model organism for studying grass physiology.
