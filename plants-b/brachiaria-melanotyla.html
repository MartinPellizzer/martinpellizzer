Brachiaria melanotyla

Brachiaria melanotyla, commonly known as blackstem brachiaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Brachiaria melanotyla is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is commonly found in grasslands, savannas, and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and serving as forage for livestock.
