Bidens grandiflora var. longiloba

Bidens grandiflora var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in moist to wet habitats, including swamps and marshes, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
