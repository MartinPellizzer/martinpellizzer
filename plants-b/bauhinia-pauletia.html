Bauhinia pauletia

Bauhinia pauletia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. Bauhinia pauletia is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
