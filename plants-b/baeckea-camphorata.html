Baeckea camphorata

Baeckea camphorata, commonly known as the camphoraceous baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which emit a camphor-like scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
