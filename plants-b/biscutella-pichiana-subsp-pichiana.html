Biscutella pichiana subsp. pichiana

Biscutella pichiana subsp.

pichiana, commonly known as the Pichian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are blue to purple, bell-shaped, and appear in clusters during the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
