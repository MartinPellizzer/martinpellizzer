Bignonia ciliata

Bignonia ciliata, commonly known as the scarlet bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and long, pendulous clusters of bright orange-yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or trellises.

Bignonia ciliata is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
