Begonia mollis

Begonia mollis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
