Biscutella intermedia var. subscaposa

Biscutella intermedia var. subscaposa, commonly known as the narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid regions.
