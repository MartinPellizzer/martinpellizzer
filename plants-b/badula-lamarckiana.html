Badula lamarckiana

Badula lamarckiana, commonly known as the Lamarck's badula, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
