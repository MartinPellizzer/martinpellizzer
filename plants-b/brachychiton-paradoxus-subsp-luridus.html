Brachychiton paradoxus subsp. luridus

Brachychiton paradoxus subsp.

luridus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
