Brassica oleracea subvar. latiloba

Brassica oleracea subvar. latiloba, commonly known as the tree cabbage, is a perennial plant characterized by its woody stem and large, broad leaves.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows up to 2-3 meters tall, with a dense canopy of fleshy, lobed leaves that are often used in cooking.

Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, and it produces round, green fruits that can be harvested when mature.
