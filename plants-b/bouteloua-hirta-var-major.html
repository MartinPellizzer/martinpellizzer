Bouteloua hirta var. major

Bouteloua hirta var. major, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are arranged in panicles and produce small, wind-pollinated seeds.

Hairy grama is an important forage plant for livestock and supports a variety of wildlife.
