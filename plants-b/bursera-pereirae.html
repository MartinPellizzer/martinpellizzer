Bursera pereirae

Bursera pereirae, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in dry, tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
