Bystropogon canariensis var. meridiani

Bystropogon canariensis var. meridiani, commonly known as the Canary Island heather, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
