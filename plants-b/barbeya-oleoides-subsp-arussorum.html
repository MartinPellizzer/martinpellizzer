Barbeya oleoides subsp. arussorum

Barbeya oleoides subsp.

arussorum, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Barbeya.
