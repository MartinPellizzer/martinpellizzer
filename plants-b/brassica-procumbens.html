Brassica procumbens

Brassica procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate mustard, is a low-growing plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically features small, green, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Brassica procumbens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in moist environments.
