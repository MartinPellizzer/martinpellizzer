Barbacenia glauca

Barbacenia glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Barbacenia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Barbacenia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
