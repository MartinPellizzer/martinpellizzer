Binaria sprucei var. acuminata

Binaria sprucei var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender pine, is a variant of the binaria sprucei species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved. The plant produces small, clustered cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
