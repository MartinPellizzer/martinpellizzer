Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. glabrescens

Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth, hence its subspecies name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
