Bellidiastrum

Bellidiastrum, commonly known as the "beardtongue," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. Bellidiastrum species typically grow in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The flowers are usually pink, purple, or blue, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Many species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are used in native plant gardens.
