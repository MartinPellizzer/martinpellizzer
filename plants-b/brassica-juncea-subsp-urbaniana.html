Brassica juncea subsp. urbaniana

Brassica juncea subsp.

urbaniana, commonly known as the Chinese mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the mustard family, Brassicaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, slender pods containing edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine, and for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
