Brassica oleracea convar. fruticosa

Brassica oleracea convar. fruticosa, commonly known as cauliflower, is a biennial plant characterized by its compact, leafy head.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is cultivated for its dense, white or colored curd, which is the edible part. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that form a tight cluster around the central flower buds. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and economically important crop in global agriculture.
