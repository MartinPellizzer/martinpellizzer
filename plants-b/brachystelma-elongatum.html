Brachystelma elongatum

Brachystelma elongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stone orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, thick stem with narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters along the stem.

Brachystelma elongatum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
