Breea arvensis

Breea arvensis, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Breea arvensis has lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
