Begonia warpurii

Begonia warpurii, commonly known as the Warpur begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, cream, and red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
