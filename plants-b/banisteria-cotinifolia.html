Banisteria cotinifolia

Banisteria cotinifolia, commonly known as the red cedar or redgum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and smooth, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Banisteria cotinifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
