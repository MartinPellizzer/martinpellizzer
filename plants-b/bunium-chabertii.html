Bunium chabertii

Bunium chabertii, commonly known as the alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive anise-like scent, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

Bunium chabertii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
