Bambusa glaucophylla

Bambusa glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives the plant its common name. This bamboo is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued for its potential in sustainable landscaping and erosion control.
