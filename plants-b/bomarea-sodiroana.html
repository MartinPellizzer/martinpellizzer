Bomarea sodiroana

Bomarea sodiroana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bomarea, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and Colombia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular inflorescence that resembles a spathaceous structure, often with vibrant purple or red hues. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Bomarea sodiroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
