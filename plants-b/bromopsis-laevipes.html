Bromopsis laevipes

Bromopsis laevipes, commonly known as the smoothleaf bromus, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, flat leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Bromopsis laevipes is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
