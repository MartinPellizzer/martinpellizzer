Bulbophyllum newportii

Bulbophyllum newportii, commonly known as the Newport's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive lip structure. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
