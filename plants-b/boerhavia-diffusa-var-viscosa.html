Boerhavia diffusa var. viscosa

Boerhavia diffusa var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky nightshade, is a sprawling herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
