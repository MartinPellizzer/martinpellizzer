Bromus crassipes

Bromus crassipes, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence is a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus crassipes is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
