Bletia acaulis

Bletia acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem bletia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its short, often underground stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip. Bletia acaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
