Brassica oleracea var. chamjodusa

Brassica oleracea var. chamjodusa, commonly known as kohlrabi, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, edible stem that develops above the leaves, which are typically curly and dark green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that eventually give way to round, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Kohlrabi thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical structure supports both culinary use and agricultural significance in various regions.
