Bulbophyllum insulsum

Bulbophyllum insulsum, commonly known as the island orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
