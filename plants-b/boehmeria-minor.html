Boehmeria minor

Boehmeria minor, commonly known as woolly nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fibrous stems of Boehmeria minor are also valued for their potential in textile production.
