Bulliarda magellanica

Bulliarda magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic bulliarda, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Bulliarda magellanica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
