Butea monosperma

Butea monosperma, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a thick, flaky bark and produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Butea monosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
