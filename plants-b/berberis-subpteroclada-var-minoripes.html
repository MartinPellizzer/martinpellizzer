Berberis subpteroclada var. minoripes

Berberis subpteroclada var. minoripes, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features small, thick, spiny leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
