Banksia borealis subsp. elatior

Banksia borealis subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its long, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, which appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for a variety of native pollinators.
