Boesenbergia bractescens

Boesenbergia bractescens, commonly known as the purple ginger or lesser galangal, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues along the veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on upright stems. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Boesenbergia bractescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal applications.
