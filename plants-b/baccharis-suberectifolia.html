Baccharis suberectifolia

Baccharis suberectifolia, commonly known as the erect-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often covered with fine hairs, while its inflorescences consist of small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Baccharis suberectifolia is known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common component of the flora in its native range.
