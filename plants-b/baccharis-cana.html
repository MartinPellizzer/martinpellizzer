Baccharis cana

Baccharis cana, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, woody fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis cana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
