Butia

Butia, commonly known as the palm tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the Arecaceae family.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. They are characterized by their tall, slender trunks and large, fan-shaped leaves. Butia palms are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Their flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.
