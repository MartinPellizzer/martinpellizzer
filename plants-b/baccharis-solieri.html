Baccharis solieri

Baccharis solieri, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, sunny areas such as riverbanks and coastal regions. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in the region.
