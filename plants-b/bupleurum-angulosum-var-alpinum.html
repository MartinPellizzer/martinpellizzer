Bupleurum angulosum var. alpinum

Bupleurum angulosum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
