Brunsvigia rosea var. major

Brunsvigia rosea var. major, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red, tubular flowers, which are arranged in dense, torch-like inflorescences. The plant has fibrous roots and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
