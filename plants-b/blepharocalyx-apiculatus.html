Blepharocalyx apiculatus

Blepharocalyx apiculatus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in pendulous racemes, blooming during the wet season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape with smooth margins. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
