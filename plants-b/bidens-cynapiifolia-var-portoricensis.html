Bidens cynapiifolia var. portoricensis

Bidens cynapiifolia var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forests and coastal areas, and it has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by distinctive burr-like seeds that aid in its dispersal.
