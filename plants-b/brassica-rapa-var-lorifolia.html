Brassica rapa var. lorifolia

Brassica rapa var. lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing rounded seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
