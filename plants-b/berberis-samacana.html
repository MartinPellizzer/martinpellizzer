Berberis samacana

Berberis samacana, commonly known as the redleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that turn a striking red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely cultivated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
