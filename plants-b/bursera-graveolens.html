Bursera graveolens

Bursera graveolens, commonly known as incense tree or copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is used to produce incense and varnishes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
