Brachylaena trinervia

Brachylaena trinervia, commonly known as the red-hot-plate or fireplant, is a small, deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red bracts that give it its common name, which are arranged in dense, circular clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Brachylaena trinervia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant foliage and drought tolerance.
