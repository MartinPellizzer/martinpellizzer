Blumea bicolor

Blumea bicolor, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flower heads that resemble mist, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. Blumea bicolor typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.
