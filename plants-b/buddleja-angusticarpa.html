Buddleja angusticarpa

Buddleja angusticarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf buddleja, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Oleaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Buddleja angusticarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy flowers and hardy nature.
