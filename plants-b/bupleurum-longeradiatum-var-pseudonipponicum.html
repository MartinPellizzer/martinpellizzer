Bupleurum longeradiatum var. pseudonipponicum

Bupleurum longeradiatum var. pseudonipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese angelica root, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its root system consists of long, tapering roots that are typically harvested for medicinal use.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical characteristics.
