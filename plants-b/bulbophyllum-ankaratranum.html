Bulbophyllum ankaratranum

Bulbophyllum ankaratranum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful inflorescences. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its flowers are typically small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and vivid hues that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a conservation concern.
