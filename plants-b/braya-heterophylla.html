Braya heterophylla

Braya heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leafed braya, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and variable leaf shapes, which can range from linear to spoon-shaped. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Braya heterophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
