Banksia dallanneyi subsp. agricola

Banksia dallanneyi subsp.

agricola, commonly known as the agricultural banksia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that typically bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and agricultural areas.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to disturbed environments and its role in local ecosystems.
