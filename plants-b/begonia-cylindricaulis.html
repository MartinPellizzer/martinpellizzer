Begonia cylindricaulis

Begonia cylindricaulis, commonly known as the cylindrical-stemmed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia cylindricaulis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
