Bulbine audreyae

Bulbine audreyae, commonly known as Audrey's bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
