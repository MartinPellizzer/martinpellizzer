Bidens selenactis

Bidens selenactis, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-white undersides on its leaves, giving it its common name, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Bidens selenactis is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

It is a perennial herb that is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
