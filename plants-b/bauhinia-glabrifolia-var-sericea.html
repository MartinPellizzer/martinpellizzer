Bauhinia glabrifolia var. sericea

Bauhinia glabrifolia var. sericea, commonly known as the silky bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces showy, racemose inflorescences with pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are elongated pods that split open to release seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Bauhinia glabrifolia var. sericea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
