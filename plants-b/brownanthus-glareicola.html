Brownanthus glareicola

Brownanthus glareicola, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Brownanthus glareicola is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
