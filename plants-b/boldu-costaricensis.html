Boldu costaricensis

Boldu costaricensis, commonly known as *Boldu* or *Boldu de Costa Rica*, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
