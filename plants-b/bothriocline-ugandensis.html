Bothriocline ugandensis

Bothriocline ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda thistle, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that attract pollinators. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems and has potential for medicinal or horticultural use.
