Brabejum stellulifolium

Brabejum stellulifolium, commonly known as the star-leaved brabejum, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a distinctive star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brabejum stellulifolium is also valued for its drought tolerance and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
