Berberis knightii

Berberis knightii, commonly known as the Knight's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically bright green, with small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Berberis knightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
