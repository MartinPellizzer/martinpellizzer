Bulbophyllum cercanthum

Bulbophyllum cercanthum, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of flowers along the inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, intricate orchid blooms that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments.

The botanical features of Bulbophyllum cercanthum make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
