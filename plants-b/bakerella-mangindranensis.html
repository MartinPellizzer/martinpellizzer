Bakerella mangindranensis

Bakerella mangindranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Bakerella mangindranensis is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect pollinators.
