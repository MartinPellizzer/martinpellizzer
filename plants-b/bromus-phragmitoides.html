Bromus phragmitoides

Bromus phragmitoides, commonly known as the tall brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Bromus phragmitoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
