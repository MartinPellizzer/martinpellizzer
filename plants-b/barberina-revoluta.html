Barberina revoluta

Barberina revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Barberina revoluta is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
