Bleekrodea insignis

Bleekrodea insignis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
