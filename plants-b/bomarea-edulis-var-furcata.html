Bomarea edulis var. furcata

Bomarea edulis var. furcata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is cultivated in gardens and for food in various parts of the world.
