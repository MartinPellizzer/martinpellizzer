Baptisia exaltata

Baptisia exaltata, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its distinctive blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Baptisia exaltata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
