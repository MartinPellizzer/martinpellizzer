Bursa hiatula

Bursa hiatula, commonly known as the bladder violet, is a small flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple, bladder-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Bursa hiatula is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters along the ground.
