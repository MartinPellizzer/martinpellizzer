Betula pubescens f. turgida

Betula pubescens f. turgida, commonly known as the silver birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silvery hue beneath. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
