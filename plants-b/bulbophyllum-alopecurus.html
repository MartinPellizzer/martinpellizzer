Bulbophyllum alopecurus

Bulbophyllum alopecurus, commonly known as the hairy bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, hairy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
