Bulbophyllum trilobum

Bulbophyllum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media. It is known for its striking, intricate floral patterns and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
