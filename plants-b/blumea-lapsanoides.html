Blumea lapsanoides

Blumea lapsanoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and lowland areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Blumea lapsanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
