Blechnum trifoliatum

Blechnum trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, leathery fronds that are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
