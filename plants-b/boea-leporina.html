Boea leporina

Boea leporina, commonly known as the rabbit's ear plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, lobed leaves that resemble a rabbit's ears, typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Boea leporina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
