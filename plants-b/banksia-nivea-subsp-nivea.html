Banksia nivea subsp. nivea

Banksia nivea subsp.

nivea, commonly known as the white banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its white, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are released after the follicles open.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
