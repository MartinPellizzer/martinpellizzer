Boea flocculosa

Boea flocculosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Boea flocculosa is also valued for its ability to climb and its attractive foliage.
