Bisnaga haematacantha subsp. sinuata

Bisnaga haematacantha subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the red-flowered bisnaga, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The subspecies sinuata is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins and slightly curved petals.
