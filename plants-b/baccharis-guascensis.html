Baccharis guascensis

Baccharis guascensis, commonly known as the Andean baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Baccharis guascensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
