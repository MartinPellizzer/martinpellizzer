Belonophora hypoglauca

Belonophora hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually a pale green to blue-green color, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Belonophora hypoglauca is an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for native wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees.
