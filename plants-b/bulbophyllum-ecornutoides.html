Bulbophyllum ecornutoides

Bulbophyllum ecornutoides, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or green, with intricate patterns.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
