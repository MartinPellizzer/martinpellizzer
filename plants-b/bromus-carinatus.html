Bromus carinatus

Bromus carinatus, commonly known as the hooked brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and agricultural areas, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Bromus carinatus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be considered a weed in some agricultural contexts.
