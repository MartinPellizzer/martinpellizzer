Brachypodium kawakamii

Brachypodium kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a slight sheen, and it typically thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Brachypodium kawakamii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
