Bromus transsilvanicus var. densiflorus

Bromus transsilvanicus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to brownish florets. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
