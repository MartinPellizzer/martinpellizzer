Berberis huiliensis

Berberis huiliensis, commonly known as the Huili barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
