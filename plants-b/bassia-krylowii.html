Bassia krylowii

Bassia krylowii, commonly known as sandmat or sandburs, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in deserts and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bristly seed pods. Its adaptive traits, such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces, enable it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Bassia krylowii is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy landscapes.
