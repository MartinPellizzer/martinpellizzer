Bulbocodium autumnale

Bulbocodium autumnale, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a short, hairy stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its scientific name reflects its seasonal blooming pattern and its association with bulbous structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
