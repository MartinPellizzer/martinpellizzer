Basiloxylon rex

Basiloxylon rex, commonly known as the royal wood basil, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It possesses a woody stem with a fibrous texture, which contributes to its structural strength. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that aids in water retention. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of purple or white.

This plant is native to arid regions and has adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
