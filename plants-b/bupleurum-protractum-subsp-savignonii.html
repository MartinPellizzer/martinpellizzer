Bupleurum protractum subsp. savignonii

Bupleurum protractum subsp.

savignonii, commonly known as the Savignon's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, branched stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have a distinctive silvery-blue hue. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
