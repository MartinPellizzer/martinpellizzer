Bromus squarrosus var. velutinus

Bromus squarrosus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and dry grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
