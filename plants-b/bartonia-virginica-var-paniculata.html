Bartonia virginica var. paniculata

Bartonia virginica var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
