Buddleja abbreviata

Buddleja abbreviata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to lilac. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja abbreviata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
