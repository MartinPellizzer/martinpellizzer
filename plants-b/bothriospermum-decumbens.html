Bothriospermum decumbens

Bothriospermum decumbens, commonly known as the creeping sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually hairy and have a glandular surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often with five petals that are pink to purple in color.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
