Biota aurea

Biota aurea, commonly known as the golden biota, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Biota aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
