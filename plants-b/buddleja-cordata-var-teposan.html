Buddleja cordata var. teposan

Buddleja cordata var. teposan, commonly known as the teposan buddleia, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that are typically pale purple to blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into fall.
