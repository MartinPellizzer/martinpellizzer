Begonia cirrosa

Begonia cirrosa, commonly known as the curled-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically appearing in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia cirrosa is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of cultivation.
