Boronia teretifolia

Boronia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Boronia teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and blooms.
