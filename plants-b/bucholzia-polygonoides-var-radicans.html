Bucholzia polygonoides var. radicans

Bucholzia polygonoides var. radicans, commonly known as the creeping bucholzia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
