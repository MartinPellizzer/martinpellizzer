Bactris subglobosa

Bactris subglobosa, commonly known as the red-skeleton palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, edible fruits resembling small pineapples. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
