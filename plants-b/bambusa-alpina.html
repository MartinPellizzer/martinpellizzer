Bambusa alpina

Bambusa alpina, commonly known as the alpine bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact and ornamental shape. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a lush appearance. It is cold-hardy and can thrive in temperate climates, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Bambusa alpina is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
