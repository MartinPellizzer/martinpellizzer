Bulbophyllum melanorrhachis

Bulbophyllum melanorrhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, wiry stems and clustered, small orchid flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
