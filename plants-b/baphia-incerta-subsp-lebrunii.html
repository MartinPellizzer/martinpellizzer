Baphia incerta subsp. lebrunii

Baphia incerta subsp.

lebrunii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

The subspecies lebrunii is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to the main species.
