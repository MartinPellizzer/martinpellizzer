Brachychiton paradoxus subsp. bidwillii

Brachychiton paradoxus subsp.

bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. It produces woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in suitable climates.
