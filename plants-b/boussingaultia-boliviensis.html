Boussingaultia boliviensis

Boussingaultia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian ironwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the dry valleys of Bolivia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually small and yellow or greenish in color. Boussingaultia boliviensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
