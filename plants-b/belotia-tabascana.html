Belotia tabascana

Belotia tabascana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds.

Belotia tabascana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
