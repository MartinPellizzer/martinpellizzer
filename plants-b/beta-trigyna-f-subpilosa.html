Beta trigyna f. subpilosa

Beta trigyna f. subpilosa, commonly known as the hairy beet, is a variant of the Beta trigyna species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and stout, reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, similar to other Beta species.

It is valued both for its nutritional content and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
