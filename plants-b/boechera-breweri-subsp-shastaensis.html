Boechera breweri subsp. shastaensis

Boechera breweri subsp.

shastaensis, commonly known as Shasta rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
