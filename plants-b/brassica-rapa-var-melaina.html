Brassica rapa var. melaina

Brassica rapa var. melaina, commonly known as black mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, round seeds that are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful crop and a potential weed.
