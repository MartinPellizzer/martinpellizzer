Bombax guineense

Bombax guineense, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
