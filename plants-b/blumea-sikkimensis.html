Blumea sikkimensis

Blumea sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in a compact cluster.

Blumea sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
