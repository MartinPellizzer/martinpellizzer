Bergeranthus granulatus

Bergeranthus granulatus, commonly known as the granulate mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, granular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Bergeranthus granulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
