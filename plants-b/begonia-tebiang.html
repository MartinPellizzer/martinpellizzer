Begonia tebiang

Begonia tebiang, commonly known as the Tebiang begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
