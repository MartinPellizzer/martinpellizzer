Begonia pseudoleprosa

Begonia pseudoleprosa, commonly known as the pink begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Begonia pseudoleprosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
