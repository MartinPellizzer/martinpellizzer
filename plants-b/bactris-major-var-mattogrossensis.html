Bactris major var. mattogrossensis

Bactris major var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clustered inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers, followed by edible, round fruits that are about 3-5 cm in diameter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some local uses in traditional medicine.
