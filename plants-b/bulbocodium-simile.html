Bulbocodium simile

Bulbocodium simile, commonly known as the rock garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground storage structures. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
