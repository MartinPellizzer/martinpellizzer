Baconia

Baconia, commonly known as the "bacon plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Baconia species are relatively easy to cultivate, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
