Bupleurum foliosum

Bupleurum foliosum, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bupleurum foliosum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
