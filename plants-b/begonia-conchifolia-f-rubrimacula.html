Begonia conchifolia f. rubrimacula

Begonia conchifolia f. rubrimacula, commonly known as the red-spotted begonia, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking foliage and vibrant flowers.

This plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive red or maroon mottling on the upper surface. The flowers are typically small, tubular, and come in shades of red or orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This begonia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
