Bactris corossilla

Bactris corossilla, commonly known as the red raphia, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Its fruits are small, three-angled berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Bactris corossilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.
