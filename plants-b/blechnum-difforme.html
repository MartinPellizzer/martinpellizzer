Blechnum difforme

Blechnum difforme, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to various regions in the Americas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
