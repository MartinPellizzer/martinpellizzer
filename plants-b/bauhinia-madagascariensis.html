Bauhinia madagascariensis

Bauhinia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
