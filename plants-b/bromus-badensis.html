Bromus badensis

Bromus badensis, commonly known as the Baden grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Bromus badensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
