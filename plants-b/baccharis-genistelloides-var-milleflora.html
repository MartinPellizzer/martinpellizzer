Baccharis genistelloides var. milleflora

Baccharis genistelloides var. milleflora, commonly known as the Mexican heath or milleflora baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for native gardens and landscaping.
