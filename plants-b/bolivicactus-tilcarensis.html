Bolivicactus tilcarensis

Bolivicactus tilcarensis, commonly known as the Tilara cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically clustered at the top of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens for its ornamental value.
