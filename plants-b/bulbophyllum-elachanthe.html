Bulbophyllum elachanthe

Bulbophyllum elachanthe, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. It features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in racemes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts and botanical studies.
