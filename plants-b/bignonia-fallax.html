Bignonia fallax

Bignonia fallax, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Bignonia fallax is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
