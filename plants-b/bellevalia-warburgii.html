Bellevalia warburgii

Bellevalia warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or purple, and bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
