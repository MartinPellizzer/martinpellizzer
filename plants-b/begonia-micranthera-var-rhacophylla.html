Begonia micranthera var. rhacophylla

Begonia micranthera var. rhacophylla, commonly known as the rhacophylla begonia, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the ground or hang from supports. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

This variety is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
