Berzelia wendlandiana

Berzelia wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's berzelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of horticultural circles.
