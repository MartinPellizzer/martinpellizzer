Barosma graveolens

Barosma graveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
