Bonamia multicaulis

Bonamia multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered bonamia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bonamia multicaulis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
