Bauhinia integrifolia var. nymphaeifolia

Bauhinia integrifolia var. nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water bean tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures containing flat, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
