Beta hastata

Beta hastata, commonly known as wild beets, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Beta hastata is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
