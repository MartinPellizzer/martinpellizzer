Begonia vittariifolia

Begonia vittariifolia, commonly known as the silver velvet begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant typically grows as a trailing or upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
