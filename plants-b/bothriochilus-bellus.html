Bothriochilus bellus

Bothriochilus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

The orchid's intricate floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, showcasing its evolutionary specialization.
