Bumelia monticola

Bumelia monticola, commonly known as the mountain bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes plants known for their unique floral structures. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Bumelia monticola is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
