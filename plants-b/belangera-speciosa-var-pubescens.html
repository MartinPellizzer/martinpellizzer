Belangera speciosa var. pubescens

Belangera speciosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ornamental bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
