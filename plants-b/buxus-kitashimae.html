Buxus kitashimae

Buxus kitashimae, commonly known as the Japanese boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Buxaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red berries. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its ability to form neat, rounded shapes.

Buxus kitashimae prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden borders and topiary.
