Barbarea minor subsp. brachycarpa

Barbarea minor subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. Its capsule fruits are short and broadly elliptical, containing several seeds.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
