Bupleurum falcatum f. bracteatum

Bupleurum falcatum f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant produces distinctive bracts that resemble small leaves beneath the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
