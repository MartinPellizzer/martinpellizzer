Broussonetia × kazinoki var. australis

Broussonetia × kazinoki var. australis, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a hybrid species native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, and fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
