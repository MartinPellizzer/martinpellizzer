Babiana buchubergensis

Babiana buchubergensis, commonly known as the Buchberg mountain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces dense clusters of small, grass-like leaves and blooms in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
