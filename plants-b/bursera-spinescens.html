Bursera spinescens

Bursera spinescens, commonly known as the spiny bursera, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Bursera spinescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
