Berinia grandiflora

Berinia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered berinia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Berinia grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
