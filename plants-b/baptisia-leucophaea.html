Baptisia leucophaea

Baptisia leucophaea, commonly known as white false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to lavender flower spikes. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also benefits surrounding plants.
