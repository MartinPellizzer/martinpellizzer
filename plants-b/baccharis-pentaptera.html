Baccharis pentaptera

Baccharis pentaptera, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in open woodlands and dry areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it its scientific name.

Baccharis pentaptera is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
