Brachypodium setifolium

Brachypodium setifolium, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Brachypodium setifolium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
