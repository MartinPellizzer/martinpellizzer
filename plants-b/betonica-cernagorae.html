Betonica cernagorae

Betonica cernagorae, commonly known as the Balkan betony, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Betonica cernagorae is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
