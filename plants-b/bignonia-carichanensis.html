Bignonia carichanensis

Bignonia carichanensis, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a climbing plant that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has pinnate leaves and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, tropical environments. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia carichanensis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
