Berberis aetnensis var. calabrica

Berberis aetnensis var. calabrica, commonly known as the Sicilian barberry, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
