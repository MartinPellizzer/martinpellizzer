Brickellia oblongifolia var. abbreviata

Brickellia oblongifolia var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaped gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable plant in arid landscapes.
