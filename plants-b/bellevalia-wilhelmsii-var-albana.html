Bellevalia wilhelmsii var. albana

Bellevalia wilhelmsii var. albana, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
