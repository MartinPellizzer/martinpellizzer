Bromus hordeaceus subsp. longipedicellatus

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-awned brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, slender awns on its florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some horticultural settings.
