Beilschmiedia rigida

Beilschmiedia rigida, commonly known as the rigid beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
