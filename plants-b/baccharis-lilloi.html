Baccharis lilloi

Baccharis lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Baccharis lilloi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
