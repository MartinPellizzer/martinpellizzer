Barringtonia marcanii

Barringtonia marcanii, commonly known as the Malabar starfruit tree, is a tropical species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
