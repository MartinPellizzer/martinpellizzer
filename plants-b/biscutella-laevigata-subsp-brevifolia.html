Biscutella laevigata subsp. brevifolia

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
