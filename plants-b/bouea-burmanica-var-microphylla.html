Bouea burmanica var. microphylla

Bouea burmanica var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf bouea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a desirable addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
