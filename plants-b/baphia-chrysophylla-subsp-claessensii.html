Baphia chrysophylla subsp. claessensii

Baphia chrysophylla subsp.

claessensii, commonly known as the golden-leaf baphia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the growing season.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
