Bambusa andropogonifolia

Bambusa andropogonifolia, commonly known as the Andropogonifolia bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This bamboo is valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
