Buchnera crassifolia

Buchnera crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed Buchnera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
