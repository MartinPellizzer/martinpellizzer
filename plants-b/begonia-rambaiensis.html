Begonia rambaiensis

Begonia rambaiensis, commonly known as the Rambai begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a prominent midrib. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
