Blastus cochinchinensis

Blastus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically fragrant and come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive lip petal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
