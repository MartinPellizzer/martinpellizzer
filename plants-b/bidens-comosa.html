Bidens comosa

Bidens comosa, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. The plant has small, yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are usually hairy and heart-shaped. It produces seeds that are attached to silky, hair-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal.

Bidens comosa is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
