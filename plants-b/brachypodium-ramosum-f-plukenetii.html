Brachypodium ramosum f. plukenetii

Brachypodium ramosum f. plukenetii, commonly known as the plukenetii form of the slender wheatgrass, is a variant of the species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial grass characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its use in ecological restoration projects.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important species for research in plant genetics and adaptation.
