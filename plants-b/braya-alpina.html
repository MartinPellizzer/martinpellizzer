Braya alpina

Braya alpina, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Braya alpina is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.
