Berberis mikuna

Berberis mikuna, commonly known as the Mikuna barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and hedges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
