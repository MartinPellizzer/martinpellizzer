Berthelotia lanceolata var. senegalensis

Berthelotia lanceolata var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese false heather, is a shrub native to the savannas of West Africa.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are borne in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure reflects its resilience to arid conditions, making it a characteristic species of its native habitat.
