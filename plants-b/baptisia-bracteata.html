Baptisia bracteata

Baptisia bracteata, commonly known as the eastern blue-eyed Mary, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a feathery appearance, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Baptisia bracteata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
