Bystropogon pavonianus

Bystropogon pavonianus, commonly known as the Western Australian daisy, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Bystropogon pavonianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
