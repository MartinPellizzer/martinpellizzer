Bambusa procera

Bambusa procera, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and glossy. Bambusa procera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of bamboo products.
