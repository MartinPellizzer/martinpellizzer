Brachypodium allionii

Brachypodium allionii, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a small perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying grassland ecosystems.
