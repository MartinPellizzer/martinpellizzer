Betula bhojpattra var. glandulifera

Betula bhojpattra var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular birch, is a variant of the bhojpattra birch native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, often pendulous branches. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Betula genus.
