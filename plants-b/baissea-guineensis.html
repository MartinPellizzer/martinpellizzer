Baissea guineensis

Baissea guineensis, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, clustered fruits that resemble breadfruit. The fruit is edible when ripe, with a starchy, sweet pulp that can be cooked and consumed.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
