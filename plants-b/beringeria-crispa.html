Beringeria crispa

Beringeria crispa, commonly known as the Beringian crinkled fern, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Arctic regions of North America and Siberia.

It is characterized by its delicate, crinkled fronds that resemble the leaves of a fern, though it is not closely related to true ferns. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.

Beringeria crispa plays a minor but ecologically significant role in its habitat, supporting local insect and microbial life.
