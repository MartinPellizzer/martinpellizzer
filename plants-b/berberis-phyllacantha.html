Berberis phyllacantha

Berberis phyllacantha, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, orange-yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
