Brachistus pringlei

Brachistus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's bristlebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces green to reddish bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. Brachistus pringlei thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and dry slopes.

It is a perennial shrub that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
