Besleria obtusa

Besleria obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Besleria obtusa requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
