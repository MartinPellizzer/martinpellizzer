Bigelowia menziesii

Bigelowia menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple center.

Bigelowia menziesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
