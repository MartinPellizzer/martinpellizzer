Bidens canescens

Bidens canescens, commonly known as the white beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Bidens canescens is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
