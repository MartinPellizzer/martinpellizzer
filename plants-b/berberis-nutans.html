Berberis nutans

Berberis nutans, commonly known as the drooping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged alternately along the stems, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Berberis nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It also contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
