Brya microphylla

Brya microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on stalks and contain spores for dispersal.

Brya microphylla plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
