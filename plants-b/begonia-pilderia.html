Begonia pilderia

Begonia pilderia, commonly known as the Pilderia begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with leaves that are often heart-shaped and adorned with intricate patterns. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a unique, spurred structure that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value.
