Bistorta subscaposa

Bistorta subscaposa, commonly known as the alpine bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

Bistorta subscaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
