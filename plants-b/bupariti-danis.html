Bupariti danis

Bupariti danis, commonly known as the Indonesian orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
