Bulbophyllum spathulifolium

Bulbophyllum spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its fleshy, pseudobulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
