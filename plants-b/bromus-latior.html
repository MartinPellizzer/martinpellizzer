Bromus latior

Bromus latior, commonly known as the broadleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three bracts.

Bromus latior is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural environments.
