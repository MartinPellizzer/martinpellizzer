Bellis armena

Bellis armena, commonly known as Armenian daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellis armena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
