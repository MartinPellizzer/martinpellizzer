Bellardia trixago subvar. versicolor

Bellardia trixago subvar. versicolor, commonly known as the variable bellardia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
