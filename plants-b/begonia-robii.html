Begonia robii

Begonia robii, commonly known as the Robii begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green tones. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia robii is often cultivated as a houseplant or in terrariums.
