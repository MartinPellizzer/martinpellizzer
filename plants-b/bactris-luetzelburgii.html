Bactris luetzelburgii

Bactris luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Peru.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, elongated fruits resembling small cucumbers. The fruit's outer covering is fibrous, while the inner pulp is juicy and often consumed by local communities.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its ecological and potential economic importance.
