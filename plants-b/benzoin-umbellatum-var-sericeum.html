Benzoin umbellatum var. sericeum

Benzoin umbellatum var. sericeum, commonly known as silver benzoin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is often harvested for use in perfumes and traditional medicine. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the genus.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical lowland forests.
