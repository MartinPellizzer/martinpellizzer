Bifrenaria clavigera

Bifrenaria clavigera, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, star-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant features intricate, bipinnate leaves with a bright yellow or golden coloration along the leaflets, which stand out against the green foliage. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
