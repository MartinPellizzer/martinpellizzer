Brittonastrum triphyllum

Brittonastrum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its seed pods are covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur.

The species is considered a perennial herb and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
