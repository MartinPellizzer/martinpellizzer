Baccharis tolimensis

Baccharis tolimensis, commonly known as Tolima baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish or silver underside.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance in its native environment.
