Berberis barbeyana

Berberis barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
