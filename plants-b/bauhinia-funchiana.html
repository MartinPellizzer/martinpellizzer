Bauhinia funchiana

Bauhinia funchiana, commonly known as the yellow bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it often grows in dry, sunny environments.

Bauhinia funchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
