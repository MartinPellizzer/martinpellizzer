Byrsonima chrysophylla f. kunthiana

Byrsonima chrysophylla f. kunthiana, commonly known as the yellow leaf mallow, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
