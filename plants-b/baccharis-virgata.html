Baccharis virgata

Baccharis virgata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis virgata is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
