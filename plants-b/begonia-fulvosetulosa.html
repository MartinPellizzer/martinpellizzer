Begonia fulvosetulosa

Begonia fulvosetulosa, commonly known as the golden-haired begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and reddish hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy structures that give it its common name.

Begonia fulvosetulosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance.
