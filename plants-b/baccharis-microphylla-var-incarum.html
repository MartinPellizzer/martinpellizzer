Baccharis microphylla var. incarum

Baccharis microphylla var. incarum, commonly known as the small-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
