Brassavola subulifolia

Brassavola subulifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
