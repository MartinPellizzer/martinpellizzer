Brunfelsia fawcettii

Brunfelsia fawcettii, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant produces large, velvety berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are glossy and evergreen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
