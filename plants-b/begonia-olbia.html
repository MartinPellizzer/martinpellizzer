Begonia olbia

Begonia olbia, commonly known as the Olbia begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of small, vibrant flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the flowers bloom in spring and summer.

Begonia olbia is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
