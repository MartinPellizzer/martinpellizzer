Blyxa japonica var. japonica

Blyxa japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese water sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, marshes, and along stream edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
