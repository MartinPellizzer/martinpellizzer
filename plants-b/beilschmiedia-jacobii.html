Beilschmiedia jacobii

Beilschmiedia jacobii, commonly known as the Jacob's beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a valuable component of understory vegetation in tropical ecosystems.
