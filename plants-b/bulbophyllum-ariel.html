Bulbophyllum ariel

Bulbophyllum ariel, commonly known as the Ariel's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers that are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Bulbophyllum ariel is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
