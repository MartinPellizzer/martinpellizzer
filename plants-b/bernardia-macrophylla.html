Bernardia macrophylla

Bernardia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
