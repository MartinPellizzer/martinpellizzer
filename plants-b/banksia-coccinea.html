Banksia coccinea

Banksia coccinea, commonly known as the red banksia, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant's leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Banksia coccinea plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for a variety of native wildlife.
