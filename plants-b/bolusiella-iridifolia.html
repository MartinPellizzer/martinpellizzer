Bolusiella iridifolia

Bolusiella iridifolia, commonly known as the bluebells of the Kalahari, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with a striking iridescence that gives the plant its common name. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
