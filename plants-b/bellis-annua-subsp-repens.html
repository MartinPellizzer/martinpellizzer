Bellis annua subsp. repens

Bellis annua subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.
