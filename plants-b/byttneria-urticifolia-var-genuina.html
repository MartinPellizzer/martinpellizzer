Byttneria urticifolia var. genuina

Byttneria urticifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the African horsewhin, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are three-winged samaras, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
