Begonia mexicana

Begonia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican begonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its foliage is usually green with reddish or bronze hues, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Begonia mexicana thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
