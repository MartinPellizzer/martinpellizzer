Bejaria sandiensis

Bejaria sandiensis, commonly known as the sand forest shrub, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Bejaria sandiensis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its ecological significance makes it a valuable species in conservation efforts within its native habitat.
