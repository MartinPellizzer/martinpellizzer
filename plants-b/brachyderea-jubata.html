Brachyderea jubata

Brachyderea jubata, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns resembling spider webs. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Brachyderea jubata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
