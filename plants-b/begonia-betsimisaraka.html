Begonia betsimisaraka

Begonia betsimisaraka, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern region of Madagascar.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
