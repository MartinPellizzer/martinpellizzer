Boeica ferruginea

Boeica ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty boeica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Boeica ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
