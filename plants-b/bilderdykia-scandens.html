Bilderdykia scandens

Bilderdykia scandens, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features a trailing or climbing growth habit with thin, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
