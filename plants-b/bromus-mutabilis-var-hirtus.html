Bromus mutabilis var. hirtus

Bromus mutabilis var. hirtus, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that often appear in pairs.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
