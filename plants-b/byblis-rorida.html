Byblis rorida

Byblis rorida, commonly known as the rainbow plant or the weeping plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairless stems and striking, colorful flowers that range from red to orange. The plant's most distinctive feature is its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky fluid to trap insects. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and requires high humidity and bright, indirect light.

Byblis rorida is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
