Bambusa longispatha

Bambusa longispatha, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to grow to heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has sturdy, hollow stems that are often used in construction and as a source of fiber. Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

Bambusa longispatha is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
