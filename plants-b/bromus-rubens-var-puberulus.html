Bromus rubens var. puberulus

Bromus rubens var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often bear spikelets at the nodes. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its potential in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
