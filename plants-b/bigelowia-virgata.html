Bigelowia virgata

Bigelowia virgata, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
