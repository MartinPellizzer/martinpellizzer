Bupleurum aristatum subsp. opacum

Bupleurum aristatum subsp.

opacum, commonly known as the opacum bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features wiry stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a distinctive white powdery coating. Its flowers are small and blue to violet in color, arranged in clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
