Biscutella varia subsp. guestphalica

Biscutella varia subsp.

guestphalica, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and usually pink or purple, arranged in terminal cymes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
