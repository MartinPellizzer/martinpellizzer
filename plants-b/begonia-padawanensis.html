Begonia padawanensis

Begonia padawanensis, commonly known as the Padawan begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves that range in color from deep green to bronze, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
