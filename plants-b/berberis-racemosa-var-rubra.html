Berberis racemosa var. rubra

Berberis racemosa var. rubra, commonly known as red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter, providing seasonal interest. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so care should be taken when cultivating this plant.
