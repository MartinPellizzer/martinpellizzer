Beccarina

Beccarina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Beccarina produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
