Blechum haenkei

Blechum haenkei, commonly known as the red-flowered blechnum, is a fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechum haenkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
