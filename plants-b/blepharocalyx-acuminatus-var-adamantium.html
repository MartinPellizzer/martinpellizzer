Blepharocalyx acuminatus var. adamantium

Blepharocalyx acuminatus var. adamantium, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in panicle clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
