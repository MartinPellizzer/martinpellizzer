Banisteria tiliifolia

Banisteria tiliifolia, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
