Bilderdykia convolvulus var. pumilio

Bilderdykia convolvulus var. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
