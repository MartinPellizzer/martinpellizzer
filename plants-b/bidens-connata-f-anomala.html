Bidens connata f. anomala

Bidens connata f. anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, with the ray florets typically being yellow.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields, where it thrives in full sun.
