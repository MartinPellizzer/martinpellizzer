Bambusa austrosinensis

Bambusa austrosinensis, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden cane, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along waterways.

Bambusa austrosinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
