Baldellia ranunculoides var. ranunculoides

Baldellia ranunculoides var. ranunculoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-cress, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are bright yellow and arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
