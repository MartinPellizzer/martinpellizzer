Bulbine narcissifolia

Bulbine narcissifolia, commonly known as the yellow bulbine, is a striking South African succulent characterized by its fleshy, strap-like leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is native to regions with arid or semi-arid climates. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are borne on tall, leafless stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bulbine narcissifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in gardens.
