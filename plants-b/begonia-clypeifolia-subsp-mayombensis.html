Begonia clypeifolia subsp. mayombensis

Begonia clypeifolia subsp.

mayombensis, commonly known as the Mayombensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to vibrant green.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
