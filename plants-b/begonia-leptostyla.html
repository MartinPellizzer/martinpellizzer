Begonia leptostyla

Begonia leptostyla, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners seeking ornamental value.
