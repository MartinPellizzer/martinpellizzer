Bulbine hallii

Bulbine hallii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that are often blue-green in color, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners in arid climates.
