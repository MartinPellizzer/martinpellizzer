Bulbophyllum mastersianum

Bulbophyllum mastersianum, commonly known as the mastersian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the wet season. The plant's leaves are strap-like and evergreen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
