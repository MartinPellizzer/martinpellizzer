Bougainvillea campanulata

Bougainvillea campanulata, commonly known as the white paperflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white bract flowers that surround tiny, inconspicuous true flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber with woody stems and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to bloom profusely.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscaping.
