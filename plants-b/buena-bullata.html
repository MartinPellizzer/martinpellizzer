Buena bullata

Buena bullata, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a cylindrical stem with ribbed segments and spines that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Buena bullata is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
