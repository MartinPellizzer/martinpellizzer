Bassia bawun

Bassia bawun, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Bassia bawun is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found growing on sandy or rocky soils.
