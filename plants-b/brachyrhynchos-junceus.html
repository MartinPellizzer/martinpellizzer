Brachyrhynchos junceus

Brachyrhynchos junceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in dense stands, contributing to the structure of wetland ecosystems.

Brachyrhynchos junceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
