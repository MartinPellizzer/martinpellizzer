Beta rosea

Beta rosea, commonly known as the red-rooted angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

However, due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
