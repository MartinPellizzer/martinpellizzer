Bumelia lanuginosa subsp. rigida

Bumelia lanuginosa subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the stiff-hairy bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
