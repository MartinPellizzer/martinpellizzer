Baccharis haitiensis

Baccharis haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian baccharis, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti and the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical environments.
