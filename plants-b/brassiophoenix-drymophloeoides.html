Brassiophoenix drymophloeoides

Brassiophoenix drymophloeoides, commonly known as the African silver fan palm, is a rare and striking palm species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feathery, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
