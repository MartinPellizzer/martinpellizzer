Biscutella eriocarpa var. eriocarpa

Biscutella eriocarpa var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the downy biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a fuzzy appearance.

The species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.
