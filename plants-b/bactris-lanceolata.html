Bactris lanceolata

Bactris lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. It produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits.

Bactris lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
