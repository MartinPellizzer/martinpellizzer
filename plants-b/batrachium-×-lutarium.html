Batrachium × lutarium

Batrachium × lutarium, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
