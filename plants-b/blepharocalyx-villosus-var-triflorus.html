Blepharocalyx villosus var. triflorus

Blepharocalyx villosus var. triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its pale, waxy leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The flowers are typically clustered in groups of three, giving the plant its specific epithet "triflorus.". It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
