Baccharis leucolepis

Baccharis leucolepis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and have a leathery texture, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis leucolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping in regions with mild climates.
