Bellevalia ciliata subsp. glauca

Bellevalia ciliata subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the glaucous bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves that are often tinged with a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
