Bredia longiradiosa var. pulchella

Bredia longiradiosa var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink-flowered bredia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish powder, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its floral display attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
