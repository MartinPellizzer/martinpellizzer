Brassica agrestis

Brassica agrestis, commonly known as field mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

Brassica agrestis is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
