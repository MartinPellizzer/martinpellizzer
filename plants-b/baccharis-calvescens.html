Baccharis calvescens

Baccharis calvescens, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Baccharis calvescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
