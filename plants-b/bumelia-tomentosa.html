Bumelia tomentosa

Bumelia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Bumelia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
