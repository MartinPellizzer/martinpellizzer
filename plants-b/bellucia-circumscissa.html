Bellucia circumscissa

Bellucia circumscissa, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub-like species that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Bellucia circumscissa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, moist climates.
