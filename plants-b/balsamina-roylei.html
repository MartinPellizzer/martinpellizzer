Balsamina roylei

Balsamina roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Balsamina roylei prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.
