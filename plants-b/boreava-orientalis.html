Boreava orientalis

Boreava orientalis, commonly known as the oriental boreavine, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Boreava orientalis thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
