Bellidium pappulosum

Bellidium pappulosum, commonly known as the sticky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Bellidium pappulosum is often found in coastal areas and is known for its resilience to drought and salt exposure.
