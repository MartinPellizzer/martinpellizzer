Berberis × irwinii

Berberis × irwinii, commonly known as the Oregon grape.

It is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thick, spiny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, blue-black berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
