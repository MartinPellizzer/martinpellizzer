Baccharis chilco

Baccharis chilco, commonly known as Chilean baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis chilco is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
