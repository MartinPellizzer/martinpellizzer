Bignonia rupestris

Bignonia rupestris, commonly known as the rock bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative vine.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
