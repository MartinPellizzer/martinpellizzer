Brickellia hebecarpa

Brickellia hebecarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and its yellow flowers bloom in the spring.

Brickellia hebecarpa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
