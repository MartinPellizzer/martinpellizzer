Bactris dahlgreniana

Bactris dahlgreniana, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a solitary tree with a short trunk and a crown of elongated, pinnately compound leaves. Its fruits are small, round, and yellow when mature, containing edible seeds.

Bactris dahlgreniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf color and compact size.
