Boronia polygalifolia

Boronia polygalifolia, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Boronia polygalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
