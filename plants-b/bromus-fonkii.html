Bromus fonkii

Bromus fonkii, commonly known as the Fonk's brome, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Bromus fonkii is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.
