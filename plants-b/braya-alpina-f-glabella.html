Braya alpina f. glabella

Braya alpina f. glabella, commonly known as the glabrous alpine braya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas across Europe, Asia, and North America.
