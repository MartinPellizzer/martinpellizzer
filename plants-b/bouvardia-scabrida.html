Bouvardia scabrida

Bouvardia scabrida, commonly known as the rough bouvardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has linear, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for a compact, ornamental plant.

Bouvardia scabrida is also known for its ability to thrive in containers, making it versatile for both indoor and outdoor settings.
