Buchanania zeylanica

Buchanania zeylanica, commonly known as the Sri Lankan satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
