Bignonia grandifolia

Bignonia grandifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
