Bidens rufovenosa

Bidens rufovenosa, commonly known as red-stemmed tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers with dark brown centers, surrounded by pale green ray florets, and has hairy, reddish stems that give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Bidens rufovenosa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
