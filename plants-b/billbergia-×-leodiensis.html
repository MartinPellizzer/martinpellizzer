Billbergia × leodiensis

Billbergia × leodiensis, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nasta and Billbergia recurvata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous covering known as a trichome layer. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Billbergia × leodiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
