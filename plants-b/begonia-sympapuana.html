Begonia sympapuana

Begonia sympapuana, commonly known as the Papuan begonia, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, often with contrasting markings. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable ornamental plant.
