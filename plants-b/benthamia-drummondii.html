Benthamia drummondii

Benthamia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Benthamia drummondii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
