Blaeria spicata var. nimbana

Blaeria spicata var. nimbana, commonly known as the cloud spire cactus, is a unique succulent native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a tall, spindly stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a cloud-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-draining soils.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
