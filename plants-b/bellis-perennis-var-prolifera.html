Bellis perennis var. prolifera

Bellis perennis var. prolifera, commonly known as the spreading daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of hairy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers. The plant is known for its ability to spread through stolons, forming dense mats that are effective for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
