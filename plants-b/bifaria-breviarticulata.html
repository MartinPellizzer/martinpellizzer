Bifaria breviarticulata

Bifaria breviarticulata, commonly known as the short-jointed burrweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Bifaria breviarticulata is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
