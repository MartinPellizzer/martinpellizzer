Bunium pyrenaeum

Bunium pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant's aromatic properties are attributed to its high content of volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
