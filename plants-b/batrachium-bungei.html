Batrachium bungei

Batrachium bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wet, muddy habitats and is often found in slow-moving water or damp soil. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for both photosynthesis and trapping prey.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and specialized adaptations for nutrient acquisition in nutrient-poor environments.
