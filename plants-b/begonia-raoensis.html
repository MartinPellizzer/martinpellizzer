Begonia raoensis

Begonia raoensis, commonly known as Rao's begonia, is a rare and striking species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces showy, fleshy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
