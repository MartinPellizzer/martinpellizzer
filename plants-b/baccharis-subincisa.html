Baccharis subincisa

Baccharis subincisa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies.
