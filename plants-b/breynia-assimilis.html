Breynia assimilis

Breynia assimilis, commonly known as the fireplant or flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has thick, succulent-like leaves that are usually opposite and can vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate. Breynia assimilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
