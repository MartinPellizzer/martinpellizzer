Betula × piperi

Betula × piperi, commonly known as the paper birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white birch (Betula papyrifera) and the black birch (Betula nigra).

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and silver. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, with a slender trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

Betula × piperi prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
