Bidens connata var. fultior

Bidens connata var. fultior, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in full sun and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize various habitats.
