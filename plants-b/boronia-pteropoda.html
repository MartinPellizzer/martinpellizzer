Boronia pteropoda

Boronia pteropoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. It produces small, clustered flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Boronia pteropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive fragrance and visual interest.
