Bromus vestitus var. purpurascens

Bromus vestitus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple velvet brome, is a variant of the velvet brome grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the western United States, particularly in California. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged foliage. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a soft, velvety texture that gives it its common name.

This grass is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage and erosion control.
