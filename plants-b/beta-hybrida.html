Beta hybrida

Beta hybrida, commonly known as the garden beet, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically red in color. The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are edible and often consumed as a leafy green. Beta hybrida is grown for both its roots and leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and fiber.

Its cultivation requires well-drained soil and a cool climate for optimal growth.
