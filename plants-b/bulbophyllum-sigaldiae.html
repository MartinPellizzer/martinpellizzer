Bulbophyllum sigaldiae

Bulbophyllum sigaldiae, commonly known as the Sigaldia orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, often colorful inflorescences and intricate floral structure typical of the Bulbophyllum genus. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex arrangement of sepals and petals.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
