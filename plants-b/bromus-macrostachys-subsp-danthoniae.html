Bromus macrostachys subsp. danthoniae

Bromus macrostachys subsp.

danthoniae, commonly known as Danthonia brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
