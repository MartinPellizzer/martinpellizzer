Bakeridesia esculenta

Bakeridesia esculenta, commonly known as the edible orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers and fleshy, tuberous roots that store nutrients. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms during the wet season.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
