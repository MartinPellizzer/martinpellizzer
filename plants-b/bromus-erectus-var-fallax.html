Bromus erectus var. fallax

Bromus erectus var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
