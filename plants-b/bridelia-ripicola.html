Bridelia ripicola

Bridelia ripicola, commonly known as the rock apple or rock pear, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Bridelia ripicola thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
