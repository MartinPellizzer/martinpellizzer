Bauhinia benthamiana

Bauhinia benthamiana, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Bauhinia benthamiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
