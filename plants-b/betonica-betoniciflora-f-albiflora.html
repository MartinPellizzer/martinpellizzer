Betonica betoniciflora f. albiflora

Betonica betoniciflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, giving it its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
