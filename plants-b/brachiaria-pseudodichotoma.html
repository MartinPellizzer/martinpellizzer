Brachiaria pseudodichotoma

Brachiaria pseudodichotoma, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or false dichotomous grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its rapid growth and extensive root system, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage crop.
