Bellis croatica

Bellis croatica, commonly known as the Croatian daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow-centered white flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing small, daisy-like inflorescences. Its leaves are toothed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Bellis croatica is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments.
