Bletia violacea

Bletia violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Bletia violacea is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
