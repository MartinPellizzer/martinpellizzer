Bougainvillea frondosa

Bougainvillea frondosa, commonly known as the Brazilian paper flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The true flowers are small and white, hidden within the bracts. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This plant is popular for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens.
