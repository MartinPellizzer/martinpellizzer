Biscutella valentina var. intricata

Biscutella valentina var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features dense, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, aiding in water retention in arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with four petals and numerous stamens, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
