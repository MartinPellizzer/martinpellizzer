Brachiaria leucacrantha

Brachiaria leucacrantha, commonly known as white-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, whitish to pale green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spicate, with spikelets arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Brachiaria leucacrantha is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
