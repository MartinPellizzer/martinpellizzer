Begonia kennedyi

Begonia kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, purple, and green. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
