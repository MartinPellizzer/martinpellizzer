Brodiaea hendersonii var. galeae

Brodiaea hendersonii var. galeae, commonly known as the Henderson's brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
