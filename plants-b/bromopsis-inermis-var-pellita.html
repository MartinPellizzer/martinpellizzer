Bromopsis inermis var. pellita

Bromopsis inermis var. pellita, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
