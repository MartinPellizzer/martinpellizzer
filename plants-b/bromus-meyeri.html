Bromus meyeri

Bromus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several small, awned florets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Bromus meyeri is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.
