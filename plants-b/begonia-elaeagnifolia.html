Begonia elaeagnifolia

Begonia elaeagnifolia, commonly known as silverleaf begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
