Bladhia lentiginosa var. taquetii

Bladhia lentiginosa var. taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
