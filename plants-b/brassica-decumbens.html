Brassica decumbens

Brassica decumbens, commonly known as the creeping mustard, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its seeds are small and round, typically maturing in late summer or early autumn.

Brassica decumbens is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
