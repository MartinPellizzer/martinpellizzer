Buchanania racemiflora

Buchanania racemiflora, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its bark is thick and smooth, with a greyish-brown color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and shade-providing capabilities.
