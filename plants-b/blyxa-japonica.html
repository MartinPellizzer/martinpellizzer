Blyxa japonica

Blyxa japonica, commonly known as Japanese watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged, with some floating shoots that can produce small, white flowers. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Blyxa japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
