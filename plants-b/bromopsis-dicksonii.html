Bromopsis dicksonii

Bromopsis dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson's feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Bromopsis dicksonii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
