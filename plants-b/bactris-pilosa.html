Bactris pilosa

Bactris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, elongated fruits. Bactris pilosa is often found in wetland and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, tropical conditions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species within the Arecaceae family.
