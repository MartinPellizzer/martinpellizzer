Berberis lycium var. subfasciculatum

Berberis lycium var. subfasciculatum, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that ripen in late summer. Its berries are edible but should be consumed in moderation due to their high acidity.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
