Beyeria cyanescens

Beyeria cyanescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and grass-like foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Beyeria cyanescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
