Blechnum dendrophilum

Blechnum dendrophilum, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a rhizome that supports the upright stem. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Blechnum dendrophilum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
