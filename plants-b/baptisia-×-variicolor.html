Baptisia × variicolor

Baptisia × variicolor, commonly known as the false indigo or blue false indigo, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower spikes that appear in early summer. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden conditions. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Baptisia × variicolor is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
