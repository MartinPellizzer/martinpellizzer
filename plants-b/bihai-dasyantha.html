Bihai dasyantha

Bihai dasyantha, commonly known as the Hawaiian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a daisy-like appearance, and bloom throughout the year.

Bihai dasyantha is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to tropical climates.
