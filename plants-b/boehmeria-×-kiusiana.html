Boehmeria × kiusiana

Boehmeria × kiusiana, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Japanese nettle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Boehmeria × kiusiana is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
