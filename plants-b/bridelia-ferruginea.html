Bridelia ferruginea

Bridelia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a rusty or reddish-brown pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
