Boronia pilosa subsp. tasmanensis

Boronia pilosa subsp.

tasmanensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian boronia, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is known for its aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
