Baccharis caldasiana

Baccharis caldasiana, commonly known as the Caldasian Baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis caldasiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
