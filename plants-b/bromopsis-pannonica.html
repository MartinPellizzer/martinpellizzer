Bromopsis pannonica

Bromopsis pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian grass, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bromopsis pannonica plays a role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
