Bromus pannonicus subsp. monocladus

Bromus pannonicus subsp.

monocladus, commonly known as the single-awned brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with distinctive single awns on the glumes. It typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
