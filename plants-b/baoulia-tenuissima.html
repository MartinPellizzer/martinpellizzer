Baoulia tenuissima

Baoulia tenuissima, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, pentagonal flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Baoulia tenuissima is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
