Blepharocalyx tweediei var. longipes

Blepharocalyx tweediei var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, dark purple to black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly glossy surface.
