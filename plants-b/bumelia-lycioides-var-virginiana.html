Bumelia lycioides var. virginiana

Bumelia lycioides var. virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia bumelia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
