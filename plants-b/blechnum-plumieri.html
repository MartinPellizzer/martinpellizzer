Blechnum plumieri

Blechnum plumieri, commonly known as the broad fern or plumier's fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that are often divided into segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

The fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.
