Bonamia sphaerocephala

Bonamia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and marshes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Bonamia sphaerocephala plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
