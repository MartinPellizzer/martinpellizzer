Brosimum terrabanum

Brosimum terrabanum, commonly known as the Cuban strangler fig, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its rapid growth and ability to climb other trees make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments if left unchecked.
