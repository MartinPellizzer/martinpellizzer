Brassica nigra var. japonica

Brassica nigra var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese black mustard, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. The plant produces small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
