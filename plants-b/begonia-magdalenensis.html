Begonia magdalenensis

Begonia magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalena begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often grown in controlled environments.
