Buchnera ramosa var. pauciflora

Buchnera ramosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender-branching buchnera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with multiple branching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of green or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The species is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.
