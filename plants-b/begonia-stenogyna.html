Begonia stenogyna

Begonia stenogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.

Begonia stenogyna prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
