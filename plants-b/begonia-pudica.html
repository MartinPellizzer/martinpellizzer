Begonia pudica

Begonia pudica, commonly known as the shy begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that often open in response to light. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Begonia pudica thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight.
