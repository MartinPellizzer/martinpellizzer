Bignonia meridionalis

Bignonia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern cross vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, orange-yellow floral bracts. The plant produces long, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Bignonia meridionalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun.
