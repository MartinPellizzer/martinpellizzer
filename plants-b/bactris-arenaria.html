Bactris arenaria

Bactris arenaria, commonly known as the Areca palm or desert palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible, round fruits. Bactris arenaria thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its utility in traditional crafts and local economies.
