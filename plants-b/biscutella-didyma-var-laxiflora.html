Biscutella didyma var. laxiflora

Biscutella didyma var. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflorous biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems and its potential use in horticulture.
