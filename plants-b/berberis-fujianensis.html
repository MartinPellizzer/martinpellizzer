Berberis fujianensis

Berberis fujianensis, commonly known as the Fujian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
