Baloghia inophylla

Baloghia inophylla, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that are arranged in drooping clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Baloghia inophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
