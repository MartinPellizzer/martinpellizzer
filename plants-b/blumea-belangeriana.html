Blumea belangeriana

Blumea belangeriana, commonly known as the blue ginger or blue flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
