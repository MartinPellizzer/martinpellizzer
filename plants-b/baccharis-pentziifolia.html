Baccharis pentziifolia

Baccharis pentziifolia, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months.

Baccharis pentziifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and disturbed soils.
