Bambusa funghomii

Bambusa funghomii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo is known for its rapid growth rate and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
