Bursa laevigata

Bursa laevigata, commonly known as the smooth burr vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, smooth, spiny fruits that resemble small burrs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Bursa laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
