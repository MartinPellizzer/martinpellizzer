Boronia ramosa subsp. anethifolia

Boronia ramosa subsp.

anethifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that emit a distinctive citrus-like scent.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Boronia ramosa subsp.

anethifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
