Bulbophyllum quadrangulare

Bulbophyllum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-sided orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, quadrangular pseudobulbs and linear, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
