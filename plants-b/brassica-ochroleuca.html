Brassica ochroleuca

Brassica ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous species. It typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate climates.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.
