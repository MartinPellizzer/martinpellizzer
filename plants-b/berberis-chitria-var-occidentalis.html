Berberis chitria var. occidentalis

Berberis chitria var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
