Berberis reicheana

Berberis reicheana, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
