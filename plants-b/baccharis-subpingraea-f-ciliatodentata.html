Baccharis subpingraea f. ciliatodentata

Baccharis subpingraea f. ciliatodentata, commonly known as the ciliate-toothed baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with ciliate (fringed) margins, giving it its specific epithet. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
