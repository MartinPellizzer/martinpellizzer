Beta alba

Beta alba, commonly known as the white beta, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly succulent texture.

Beta alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
