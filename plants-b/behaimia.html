Behaimia

Behaimia, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, arid environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Behaimia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their nocturnal blooming habit.
