Begonia subrectangula

Begonia subrectangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
