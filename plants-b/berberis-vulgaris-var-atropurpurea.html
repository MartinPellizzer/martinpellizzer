Berberis vulgaris var. atropurpurea

Berberis vulgaris var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and dark green, oval leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments.
