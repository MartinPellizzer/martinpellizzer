Bignonia potosina

Bignonia potosina, commonly known as the Mexican trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering climbers.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
