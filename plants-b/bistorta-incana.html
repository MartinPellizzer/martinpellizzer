Bistorta incana

Bistorta incana, commonly known as white bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheats and sorrels. The plant features upright, hollow stems covered in a whitish, woolly coating. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

Bistorta incana produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.
