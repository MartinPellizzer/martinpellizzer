Blakwellia acuminata

Blakwellia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blakwellia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Blakwellia acuminata is often found in open habitats such as spinifex plains and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
