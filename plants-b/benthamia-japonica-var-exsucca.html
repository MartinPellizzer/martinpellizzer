Benthamia japonica var. exsucca

Benthamia japonica var. exsucca, commonly known as the Japanese benthamia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
