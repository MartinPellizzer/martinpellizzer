Bucida buceras

Bucida buceras, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or false hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
