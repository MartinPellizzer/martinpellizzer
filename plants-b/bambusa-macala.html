Bambusa macala

Bambusa macala, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa macala is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
