Brassica oleracea var. viridis

Brassica oleracea var. viridis, commonly known as curly kale, is a leafy green vegetable characterized by its tightly curled, dark green leaves.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant is typically biennial, though it is often grown as an annual in agriculture. Its leaves are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K, as well as dietary fiber.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
