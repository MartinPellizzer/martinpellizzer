Bauhinia rocheri

Bauhinia rocheri, commonly known as the rock bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often found growing on rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Bauhinia rocheri is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
