Banksia shuttleworthiana

Banksia shuttleworthiana, commonly known as the red banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are released after the follicles open. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Banksia shuttleworthiana is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for honeyeaters and other pollinators.
