Begonia capensis

Begonia capensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Begonia capensis is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
