Bryonia bonariensis

Bryonia bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine bitter cucumber, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to green, elongated fruits resembling cucumbers. The fruit is covered in a thick, warty skin and contains a toxic, bitter interior.

It is native to South America and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
