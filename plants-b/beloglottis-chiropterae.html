Beloglottis chiropterae

Beloglottis chiropterae, commonly known as the bat plant, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bat-like appearance, with dark green, velvety leaves that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Beloglottis chiropterae is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking foliage and ease of care in the right conditions.
