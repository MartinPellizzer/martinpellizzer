Bupleurum arborescens

Bupleurum arborescens, commonly known as the tree bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the spring and early summer. Its scientific name, *Bupleurum arborescens*, reflects its tree-like appearance due to its branching structure.

It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
