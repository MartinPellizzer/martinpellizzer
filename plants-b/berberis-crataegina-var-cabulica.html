Berberis crataegina var. cabulica

Berberis crataegina var. cabulica, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
