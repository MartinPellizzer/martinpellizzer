Bulbine mesembryanthoides

Bulbine mesembryanthoides, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
