Baccharis valdiviana

Baccharis valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and coastal areas. It produces small, woody fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Baccharis valdiviana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
