Bargemontia confinis

Bargemontia confinis, commonly known as the coastal mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky outcrops. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species suited to harsh maritime environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
