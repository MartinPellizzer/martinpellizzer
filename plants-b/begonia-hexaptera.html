Begonia hexaptera

Begonia hexaptera, commonly known as the six-winged begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and a deep, rich green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia hexaptera is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and ease of care in the right conditions.
