Betula japonica var. tauschii

Betula japonica var. tauschii, commonly known as the Japanese birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic bark and seasonal changes.
