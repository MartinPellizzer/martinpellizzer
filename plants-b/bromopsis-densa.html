Bromopsis densa

Bromopsis densa, commonly known as the dense feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Bromopsis densa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
