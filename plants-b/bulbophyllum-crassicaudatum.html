Bulbophyllum crassicaudatum

Bulbophyllum crassicaudatum, commonly known as the thick-tailed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its fleshy, thickened pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically produces dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and horticultural enthusiasts.
