Berberis willeana var. serrulata

Berberis willeana var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, spiny stems covered with small, yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. Its leaves are elliptical, with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
