Barnadesia laurifolia

Barnadesia laurifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red or orange berries. Its rapid growth rate and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its ability to outcompete native vegetation.
