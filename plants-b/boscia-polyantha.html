Boscia polyantha

Boscia polyantha, commonly known as the yellow boscia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Boscia polyantha is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
