Bulbophyllum vaughanii

Bulbophyllum vaughanii, commonly known as the Vaughan's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's unique morphology.

The flowers are often clustered and bloom during the wet season, showcasing the plant's adaptation to its tropical environment.
