Bonapartea filamentosa

Bonapartea filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf bonapartea, is a rare and unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
