Brassica sinapistrum var. orientalis

Brassica sinapistrum var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental horseradish, is a biennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its pungent, aromatic roots that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The roots are taproots that can grow up to 30 cm in length and are harvested for their spicy flavor.
