Bulbophyllum decarhopalon

Bulbophyllum decarhopalon, commonly known as the Orchid with a Twist, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often pendulous inflorescence and intricate floral structures that attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that aid in pollination.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens worldwide.
