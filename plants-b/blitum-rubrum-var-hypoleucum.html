Blitum rubrum var. hypoleucum

Blitum rubrum var. hypoleucum, commonly known as white blitum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green to white leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
