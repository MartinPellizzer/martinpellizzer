Berberis roemeriana

Berberis roemeriana, commonly known as the yellow barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
