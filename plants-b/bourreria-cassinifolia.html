Bourreria cassinifolia

Bourreria cassinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
