Batanthes scopulorum

Batanthes scopulorum, commonly known as the rock batanthes, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky crevices or sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies in arid regions.
