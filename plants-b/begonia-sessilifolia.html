Begonia sessilifolia

Begonia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
