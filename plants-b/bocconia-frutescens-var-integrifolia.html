Bocconia frutescens var. integrifolia

Bocconia frutescens var. integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf bocconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically broad and entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit.

Bocconia frutescens var. integrifolia prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
