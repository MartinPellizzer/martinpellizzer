Berberis × smithiana

Berberis × smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's barberry, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities, including its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.

Native to hybrid populations in the Pacific Northwest, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
