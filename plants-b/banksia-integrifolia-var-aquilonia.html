Banksia integrifolia var. aquilonia

Banksia integrifolia var. aquilonia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The flowers are typically yellow with a red or orange center, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This variant is part of the Proteaceae family and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing the plant's resilience in its native habitat.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
