Baptisia × serenae

Baptisia × serenae, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Baptisia, which includes several species of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that appear in early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Baptisia × serenae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
