Bunium elegans var. brevipes

Bunium elegans var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and a tall, slender stem that produces small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Bunium elegans var. brevipes make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
