Bolandia pedunculosa

Bolandia pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bolandia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
