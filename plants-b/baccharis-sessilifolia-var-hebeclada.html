Baccharis sessilifolia var. hebeclada

Baccharis sessilifolia var. hebeclada, commonly known as Hebeclada baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the late spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often used in native plant gardens and landscapes.
