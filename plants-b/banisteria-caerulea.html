Banisteria caerulea

Banisteria caerulea, commonly known as blue banister, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue-violet flowers, which are borne in clusters along its stems. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral displays.
