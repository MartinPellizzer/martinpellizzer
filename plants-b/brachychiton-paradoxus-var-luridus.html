Brachychiton paradoxus var. luridus

Brachychiton paradoxus var. luridus, commonly known as the lurid flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red floral bracts that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its seasonal display of vibrant flowers.
