Brachyactis latisquamata

Brachyactis latisquamata, commonly known as the broad-scale cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Brachyactis latisquamata is well-suited to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
