Brassica napus var. edulis

Brassica napus var. edulis, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are widely used for oil extraction and as a source of protein in animal feed. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its high yield and nutritional value.

Brassica napus var. edulis is also a significant crop in agricultural systems due to its adaptability and economic importance.
