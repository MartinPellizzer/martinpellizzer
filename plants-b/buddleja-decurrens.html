Buddleja decurrens

Buddleja decurrens, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Buddleja decurrens is also valued for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
