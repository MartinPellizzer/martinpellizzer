Bauhinia bahiensis

Bauhinia bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, flame-like pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, fern-like segments, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia bahiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
