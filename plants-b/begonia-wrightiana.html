Begonia wrightiana

Begonia wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to shades of red and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia wrightiana is popular among gardeners for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
