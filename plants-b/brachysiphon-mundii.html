Brachysiphon mundii

Brachysiphon mundii, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are modified into spines for protection. It is adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for xerophytic environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
