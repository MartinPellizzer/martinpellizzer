Bourreria divaricata

Bourreria divaricata, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its showy, bright orange or red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are surrounded by two rows of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
