Bulnesia arborea

Bulnesia arborea, commonly known as the desert ironwood, is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from great depths, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Its dense, dark wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, though it is rarely used due to the tree's slow growth and limited availability.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various desert wildlife species.
