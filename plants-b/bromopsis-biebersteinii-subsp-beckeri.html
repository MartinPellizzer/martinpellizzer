Bromopsis biebersteinii subsp. beckeri

Bromopsis biebersteinii subsp.

beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's bromus, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.
