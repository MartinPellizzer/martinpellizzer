Betula papyrifera var. subcordata

Betula papyrifera var. subcordata, commonly known as the paper birch subvariety with a subcordate leaf shape, is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval in shape, with a serrated margin and a subcordate base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
