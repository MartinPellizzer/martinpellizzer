Buddleja sancti-leopoldi

Buddleja sancti-leopoldi, commonly known as the Holy Leopold's bush.

It is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

Buddleja sancti-leopoldi prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
