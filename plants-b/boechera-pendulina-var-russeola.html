Boechera pendulina var. russeola

Boechera pendulina var. russeola, commonly known as the Russian pendulous rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with hairy, often blue-green foliage. The species produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
