Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp. maritimus

Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as sea club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flowering spikes that rise on slender stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically found in saline or brackish wetlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dark brown, cylindrical berries.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
