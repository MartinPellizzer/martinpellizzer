Bidens aurantiaca

Bidens aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Bidens aurantiaca is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
