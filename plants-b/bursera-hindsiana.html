Bursera hindsiana

Bursera hindsiana, commonly known as the Hinds' bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and simple, alternate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

Bursera hindsiana is often found in dry tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
