Barbacenia oxytepala

Barbacenia oxytepala, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Barbacenia oxytepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
