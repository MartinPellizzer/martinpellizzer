Buxus

Buxus, commonly known as boxwood, is a genus of evergreen shrubs belonging to the family Buxaceae.

These plants are characterized by their dense, compact growth habit and their ability to be shaped into intricate forms, making them popular in horticulture. The leaves are typically small, glossy, and dark green, often with a waxy texture. Buxus species are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they thrive in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

They are also valued for their resistance to pests and diseases, which contributes to their widespread use in landscaping.
