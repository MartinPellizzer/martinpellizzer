Begonia laxa

Begonia laxa, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, Begonia laxa is widely cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
