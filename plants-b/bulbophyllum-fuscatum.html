Bulbophyllum fuscatum

Bulbophyllum fuscatum, commonly known as the blackened orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients to help it survive in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum fuscatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
