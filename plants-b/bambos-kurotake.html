Bambos kurotake

Bambos kurotake, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast sharply with its green leaves. The plant grows in dense clusters and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its stems are hollow and segmented, typical of the grass family to which bamboo belongs.

Black bamboo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
