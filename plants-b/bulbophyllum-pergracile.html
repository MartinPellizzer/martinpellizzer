Bulbophyllum pergracile

Bulbophyllum pergracile, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a lithophytic orchid, meaning it grows on rocks rather than in soil, often in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rock surfaces.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
