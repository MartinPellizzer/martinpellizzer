Bragantia latifolia

Bragantia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bragantia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
