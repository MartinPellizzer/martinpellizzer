Bunchosia sphaerocarpa

Bunchosia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe fruit or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble cherries. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers during the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
