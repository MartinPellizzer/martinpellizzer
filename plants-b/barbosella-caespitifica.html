Barbosella caespitifica

Barbosella caespitifica, commonly known as the coastal barbosella, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is well adapted to withstand salty sea air and coastal winds.

Barbosella caespitifica is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
