Betonica officinalis subsp. peredae

Betonica officinalis subsp.

peredae, commonly known as the lesser betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and nervous system support.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
