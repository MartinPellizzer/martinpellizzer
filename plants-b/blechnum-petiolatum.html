Blechnum petiolatum

Blechnum petiolatum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
