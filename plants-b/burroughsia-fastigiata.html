Burroughsia fastigiata

Burroughsia fastigiata, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, with a prominent yellow stamen. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.
