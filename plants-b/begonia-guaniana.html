Begonia guaniana

Begonia guaniana, commonly known as the Guanica begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and blooms.
