Buddleja racemosa subsp. incana

Buddleja racemosa subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly buddleja, is a shrub native to parts of China and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are particularly attractive to butterflies and other pollinators.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
