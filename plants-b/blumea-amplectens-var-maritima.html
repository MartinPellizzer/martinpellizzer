Blumea amplectens var. maritima

Blumea amplectens var. maritima, commonly known as sea blumea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy or saline soils, often found in mangrove swamps and coastal dunes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
