Ballota hirsuta var. tibestica

Ballota hirsuta var. tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti ballota, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in harsh climates.
