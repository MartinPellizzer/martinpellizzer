Brassaia angiensis

Brassaia angiensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Brassaia angiensis is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
