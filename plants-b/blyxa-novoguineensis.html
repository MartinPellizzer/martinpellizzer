Blyxa novoguineensis

Blyxa novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments. Blyxa novoguineensis is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water.

Its presence supports a variety of aquatic life by providing habitat and food sources.
