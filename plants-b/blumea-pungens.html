Blumea pungens

Blumea pungens, commonly known as the pungent blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits.

Blumea pungens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
