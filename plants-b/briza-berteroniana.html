Briza berteroniana

Briza berteroniana, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear delicate, silver-white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with a soft, silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Briza berteroniana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
