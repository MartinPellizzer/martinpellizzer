Bulbophyllum coplandii

Bulbophyllum coplandii, commonly known as the Copland's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and delicate appearance.
