Bidens frondosa var. stenodonta

Bidens frondosa var. stenodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed or wetland environments.
