Begonia guaduensis var. andreana

Begonia guaduensis var. andreana, commonly known as the Guaduense Begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
