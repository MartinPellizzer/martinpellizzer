Bulbophyllum mindorense

Bulbophyllum mindorense, commonly known as the Mindanao orchid, is a rare and exquisite epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in a striking array of colors. The plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to retain water in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
