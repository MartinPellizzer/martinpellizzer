Begonia fusibulba

Begonia fusibulba, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
