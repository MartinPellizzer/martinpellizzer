Bauhinia goldmanii

Bauhinia goldmanii, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant yellow flowers.

Bauhinia goldmanii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
