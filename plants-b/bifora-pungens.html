Bifora pungens

Bifora pungens, commonly known as the prickly sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with sharp, spiny leaves that serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Bifora pungens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid gardens.
