Bomarea macranthera

Bomarea macranthera, commonly known as the giant bomarea, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, bulbous tubers underground, which serve as storage organs and help it survive in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Bomarea macranthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
