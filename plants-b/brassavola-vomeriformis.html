Brassavola vomeriformis

Brassavola vomeriformis, commonly known as the Vomerine Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, white to pale yellow petals and sepals. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture.

Brassavola vomeriformis is a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
