Bambusa nana var. alphonse-karrii

Bambusa nana var. alphonse-karrii, commonly known as the Alphonse karríi dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, dark green culms that grow to a height of about 2–3 meters, making it ideal for small spaces. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stems. It is well-suited for container gardening due to its slow growth and ability to thrive in partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
