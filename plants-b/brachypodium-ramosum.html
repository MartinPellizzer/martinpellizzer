Brachypodium ramosum

Brachypodium ramosum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Brachypodium ramosum is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its adaptability and rapid growth make it a common weed in disturbed soils.
