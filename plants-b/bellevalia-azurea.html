Bellevalia azurea

Bellevalia azurea, commonly known as the azure bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Bellevalia azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-shaped blooms.
