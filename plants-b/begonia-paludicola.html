Begonia paludicola

Begonia paludicola, commonly known as the marsh begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in marshy environments.

Begonia paludicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
