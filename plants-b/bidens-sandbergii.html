Bidens sandbergii

Bidens sandbergii, commonly known as sandberg's tickseed, is an annual flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in the Midwest region. Its yellow flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
