Bolax caespitosa

Bolax caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are upright with narrow, linear leaves.

Bolax caespitosa is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
