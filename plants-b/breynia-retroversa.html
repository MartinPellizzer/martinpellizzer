Breynia retroversa

Breynia retroversa, commonly known as the reverse-breynia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Breynia retroversa is known for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it adaptable to coastal environments.
