Bossiaea scortechinii

Bossiaea scortechinii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. It is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
