Briza erecta

Briza erecta, commonly known as the upright false oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically blue to purple in color. Briza erecta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and color.
