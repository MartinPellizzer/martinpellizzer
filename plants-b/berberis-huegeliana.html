Berberis huegeliana

Berberis huegeliana, commonly known as the Austrian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Berberis huegeliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
