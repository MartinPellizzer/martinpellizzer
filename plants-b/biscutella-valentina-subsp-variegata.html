Biscutella valentina subsp. variegata

Biscutella valentina subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a distinctive variegation pattern of white or yellow markings. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to bloom in late spring.
