Baccharis imbricata

Baccharis imbricata, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense thickets.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
