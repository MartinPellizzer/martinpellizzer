Baccharis berberifolia

Baccharis berberifolia, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis berberifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
