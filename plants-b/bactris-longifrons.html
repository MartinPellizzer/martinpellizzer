Bactris longifrons

Bactris longifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of elongated, yellowish fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris longifrons is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
