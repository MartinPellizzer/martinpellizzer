Bulbophyllum comatum var. inflatum

Bulbophyllum comatum var. inflatum, commonly known as the inflated bulbophyllum, is a fascinating orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
