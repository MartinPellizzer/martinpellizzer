Bidens micranthoides

Bidens micranthoides, commonly known as the small-flowered tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals and prominent white veins, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
