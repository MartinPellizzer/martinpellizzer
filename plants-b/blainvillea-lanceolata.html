Blainvillea lanceolata

Blainvillea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf blainvillea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It is often found in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in warm, humid environments.

Blainvillea lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
