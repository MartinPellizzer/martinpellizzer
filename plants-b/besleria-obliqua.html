Besleria obliqua

Besleria obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Besleria obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and warm climates.
