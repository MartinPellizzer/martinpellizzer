Bulbophyllum volkensii

Bulbophyllum volkensii, commonly known as the Volkens' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and typically thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a raceme. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

Bulbophyllum volkensii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivation.
