Bufonia calyculata

Bufonia calyculata, commonly known as the purple-flowered tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several small leaflets arranged along a central axis.

Bufonia calyculata is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
