Buddleja brevifolia

Buddleja brevifolia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
