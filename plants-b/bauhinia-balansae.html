Bauhinia balansae

Bauhinia balansae, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
