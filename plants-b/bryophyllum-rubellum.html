Bryophyllum rubellum

Bryophyllum rubellum, commonly known as the red-veined Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The leaves are typically green with distinctive red veins, and the plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in the spring.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
