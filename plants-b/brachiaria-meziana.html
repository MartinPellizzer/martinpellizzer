Brachiaria meziana

Brachiaria meziana, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or meadow grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often covered with fine hairs.

Brachiaria meziana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
