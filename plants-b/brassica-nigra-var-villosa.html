Brassica nigra var. villosa

Brassica nigra var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly black mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated, pod-like structures.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
