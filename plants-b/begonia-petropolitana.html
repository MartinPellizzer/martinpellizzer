Begonia petropolitana

Begonia petropolitana, commonly known as the Petropolitana begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
