Banisteria nigrescens

Banisteria nigrescens, commonly known as the black-barked bignon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Banisteria nigrescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and vigorous growth habit.
