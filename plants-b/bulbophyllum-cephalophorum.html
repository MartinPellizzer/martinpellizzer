Bulbophyllum cephalophorum

Bulbophyllum cephalophorum, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and its strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
