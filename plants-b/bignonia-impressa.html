Bignonia impressa

Bignonia impressa, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. Bignonia impressa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
