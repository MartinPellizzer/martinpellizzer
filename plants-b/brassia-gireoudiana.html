Brassia gireoudiana

Brassia gireoudiana, commonly known as the Gireoud's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

Brassia gireoudiana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
