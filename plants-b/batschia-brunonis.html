Batschia brunonis

Batschia brunonis, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Batschia brunonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
