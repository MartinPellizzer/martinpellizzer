Boscia albitrunca var. albitrunca

Boscia albitrunca var. albitrunca, commonly known as the white-trunk boscia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, flaky bark that peels away to reveal a lighter-colored inner layer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
