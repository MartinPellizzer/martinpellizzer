Brickellia floribunda

Brickellia floribunda, commonly known as the showy brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
