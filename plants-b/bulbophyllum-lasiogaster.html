Bulbophyllum lasiogaster

Bulbophyllum lasiogaster, commonly known as the hairy-stalked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy pseudobulbs and long, slender stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks, and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
