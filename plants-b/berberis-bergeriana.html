Berberis bergeriana

Berberis bergeriana, commonly known as the Tibetan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Berberis bergeriana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
