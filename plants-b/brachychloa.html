Brachychloa

Brachychloa, commonly known as dwarf hairgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically small, mat-forming species that thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. They have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. Brachychloa species are characterized by their dense, low-growing habit and are often used in ecological restoration projects.

Their ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms makes them ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
