Baobabus gregorii

Baobabus gregorii, commonly known as the Gregorii baobab, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The tree has thick, fibrous bark that provides insulation against extreme temperatures. Its leaves are palmate, with multiple lobes, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a large, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.
