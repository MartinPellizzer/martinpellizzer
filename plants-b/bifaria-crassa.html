Bifaria crassa

Bifaria crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf burrweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. Bifaria crassa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable species with potential uses in ecological restoration projects.
