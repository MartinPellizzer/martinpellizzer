Borreria leptoloba

Borreria leptoloba, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Borreria leptoloba is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
