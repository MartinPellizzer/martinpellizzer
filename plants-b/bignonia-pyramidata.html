Bignonia pyramidata

Bignonia pyramidata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in landscapes.
