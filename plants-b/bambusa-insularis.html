Bambusa insularis

Bambusa insularis, commonly known as the island bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has slender, hollow stems with a smooth, light green to yellowish-brown color. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Bambusa insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
