Bletia palmeri

Bletia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bletia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale yellow to greenish-white blooms with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Bletia palmeri is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
