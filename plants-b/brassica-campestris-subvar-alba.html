Brassica campestris subvar. alba

Brassica campestris subvar. alba, commonly known as white mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces long, slender pods containing round, white seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

White mustard is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
