Blechnum laevigatum

Blechnum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a smooth texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that are usually arranged in a dense, upright fashion.
