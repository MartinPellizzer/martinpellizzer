Bambusa inermis

Bambusa inermis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish-green culms that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

Bambusa inermis is relatively cold-hardy and can be grown in USDA zones 6 through 9.
