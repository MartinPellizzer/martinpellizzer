Banisteriopsis patula

Banisteriopsis patula, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

Banisteriopsis patula is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
