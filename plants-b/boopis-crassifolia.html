Boopis crassifolia

Boopis crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf boopis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Boopis crassifolia is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
