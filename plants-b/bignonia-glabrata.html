Bignonia glabrata

Bignonia glabrata, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant.
