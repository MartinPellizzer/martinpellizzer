Bellevalia feinbruniae

Bellevalia feinbruniae, commonly known as the Feinbrun's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
