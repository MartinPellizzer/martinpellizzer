Bulbophyllum dekockii

Bulbophyllum dekockii, commonly known as the Dekock's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in variable environmental conditions.

Bulbophyllum dekockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
