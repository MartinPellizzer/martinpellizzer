Bauhinia sprucei

Bauhinia sprucei, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Bauhinia sprucei typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
