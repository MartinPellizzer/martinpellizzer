Bromus porteri var. frondosus

Bromus porteri var. frondosus, commonly known as the fronded bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in dry to moist soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
