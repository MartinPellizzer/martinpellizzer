Bigelovia ligustrina

Bigelovia ligustrina, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are small, narrow, and have a leathery texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought-prone areas.
