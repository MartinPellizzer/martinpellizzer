Bomarea hirta

Bomarea hirta, commonly known as the hairy bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, often bi-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is known for its distinctive hairy foliage. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or purple, are often clustered in inflorescences at the base of the plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
