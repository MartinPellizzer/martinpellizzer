Berberis flavida

Berberis flavida, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, spiny branches and produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are edible but often tart. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and a distinctive yellowish-green underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall foliage and unique fruiting display.
