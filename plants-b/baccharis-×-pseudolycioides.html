Baccharis × pseudolycioides

Baccharis × pseudolycioides, commonly known as false lycia, is a hybrid shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It has a woody stem with dense, often branched growth and produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
