Bakeriella longistyla

Bakeriella longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled baker's orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb with slender, upright stems and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Bakeriella longistyla is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.
