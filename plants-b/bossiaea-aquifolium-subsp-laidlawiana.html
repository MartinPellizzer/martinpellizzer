Bossiaea aquifolium subsp. laidlawiana

Bossiaea aquifolium subsp.

laidlawiana, commonly known as the Laidlaw's hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive serrated margin.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
