Bactris gracilis

Bactris gracilis, commonly known as the slender palm or chicha palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
