Baccharis subcapitata

Baccharis subcapitata, commonly known as the Brazilian erythroxylum, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flower heads. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
