Bertholletia

Bertholletia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, woody fruits that contain multiple seeds enclosed in a hard, fibrous shell. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is well-adapted to the tropical rainforest environment, with a long lifespan and significant ecological importance.
