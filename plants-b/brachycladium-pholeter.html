Brachycladium pholeter

Brachycladium pholeter, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Brachycladium pholeter is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in degraded landscapes.
