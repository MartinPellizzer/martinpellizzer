Besleria arborea

Besleria arborea, commonly known as the tree gesneriad, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are borne on long, slender inflorescences. Its leaves are typically glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Besleria arborea is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining soil.
