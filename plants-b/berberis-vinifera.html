Berberis vinifera

Berberis vinifera, commonly known as the grape berberis, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
