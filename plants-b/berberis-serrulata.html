Berberis serrulata

Berberis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, orange, or yellow berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis serrulata is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with antimicrobial effects.
