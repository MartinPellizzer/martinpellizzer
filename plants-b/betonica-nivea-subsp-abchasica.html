Betonica nivea subsp. abchasica

Betonica nivea subsp.

abchasica, commonly known as the Caucasian betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Betonica nivea subsp.

abchasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
