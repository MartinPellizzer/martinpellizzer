Billbergia tinctoria

Billbergia tinctoria, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, with the inner leaves often displaying a striking red or orange hue. The plant produces a tall flower spike that bears colorful, showy bracts, which can range from pink to red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia tinctoria is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
