Bromus erectus var. racemifer

Bromus erectus var. racemifer, commonly known as the tufted brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bromus erectus var. racemifer plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native ecosystems.
