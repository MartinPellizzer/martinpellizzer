Buphthalmum spinosum

Buphthalmum spinosum, commonly known as the spiny ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Buphthalmum spinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
