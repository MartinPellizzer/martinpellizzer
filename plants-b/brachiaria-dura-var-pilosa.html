Brachiaria dura var. pilosa

Brachiaria dura var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy durra grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage in livestock grazing.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
