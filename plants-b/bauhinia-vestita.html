Bauhinia vestita

Bauhinia vestita, commonly known as the velvetwood or white bauhinia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that are often tinged with purple, adding ornamental value. Bauhinia vestita is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
