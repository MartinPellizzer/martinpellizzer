Bromus divaricatus var. lanuginosus

Bromus divaricatus var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces dense tufts of spikelets that are often tinged with reddish hues.

The hairy brome is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
