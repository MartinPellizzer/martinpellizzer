Banksia dallanneyi subsp. dallanneyi

Banksia dallanneyi subsp.

dallanneyi, commonly known as the Blackbutt Banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-west of Western Australia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking yellow to orange flowers that appear in cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for wildlife.

The subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and unique floral structure compared to other banksia species.
