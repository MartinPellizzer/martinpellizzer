Brassica repanda subsp. silenifolia

Brassica repanda subsp.

silenifolia, commonly known as the silenifolia mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust root system and a basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom from spring to summer. Its fruit is a slender siliqua containing several seeds.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
