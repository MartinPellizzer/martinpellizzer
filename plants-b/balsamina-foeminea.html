Balsamina foeminea

Balsamina foeminea, commonly known as the female balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with heart-shaped leaves and fleshy stems. Its flowers are often borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
