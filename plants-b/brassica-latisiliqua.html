Brassica latisiliqua

Brassica latisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions.
