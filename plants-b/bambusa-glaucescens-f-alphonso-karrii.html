Bambusa glaucescens f. alphonso-karrii

Bambusa glaucescens f. alphonso-karrii, commonly known as the Alphonso-Karrii bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a bluish-white or pale green sheath. The leaves are elongated and narrow, typically with a pointed tip, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences. This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for tropical and subtropical climates.
