Bredemeyera revoluta

Bredemeyera revoluta, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically tubular with five lobes. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Bredemeyera revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
