Brachiaria decaryana

Brachiaria decaryana, commonly known as finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Brachiaria decaryana is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage in some regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
