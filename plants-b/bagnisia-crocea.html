Bagnisia crocea

Bagnisia crocea, commonly known as the red-flowered bagnisia, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking crimson petals that contrast with its deep green foliage, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters and blooms during the spring and early summer months. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Bagnisia crocea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
