Bambusa fistulosa

Bambusa fistulosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features hollow, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and is characterized by its strong, lightweight culms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa fistulosa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in construction and paper manufacturing.
