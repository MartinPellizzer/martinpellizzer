Bossiaea rhombifolia subsp. concolor

Bossiaea rhombifolia subsp.

concolor, commonly known as the smooth bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
