Baimo

Baimo, commonly known as *Acorus calamus*, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface or grow submerged, and its rhizomes are often harvested for medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in a spike-like cluster. Baimo is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
