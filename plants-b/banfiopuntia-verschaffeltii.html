Banfiopuntia verschaffeltii

Banfiopuntia verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Schumann's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in arid conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
