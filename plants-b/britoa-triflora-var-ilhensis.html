Britoa triflora var. ilhensis

Britoa triflora var. ilhensis, commonly known as the Ilhensian Britoa, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
