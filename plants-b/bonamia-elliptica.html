Bonamia elliptica

Bonamia elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf bonamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
