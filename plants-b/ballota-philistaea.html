Ballota philistaea

Ballota philistaea, commonly known as the Jerusalem sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ballota philistaea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
