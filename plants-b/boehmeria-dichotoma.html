Boehmeria dichotoma

Boehmeria dichotoma, commonly known as Chinese grass or wild flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, hollow stems and alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is widely cultivated for its strong, fibrous stalks used in textile and paper production.

Boehmeria dichotoma thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
