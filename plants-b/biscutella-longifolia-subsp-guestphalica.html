Biscutella longifolia subsp. guestphalica

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

guestphalica, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky slopes and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine pubescence, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
