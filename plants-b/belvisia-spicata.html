Belvisia spicata

Belvisia spicata, commonly known as the spiky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Belvisia spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
