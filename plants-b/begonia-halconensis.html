Begonia halconensis

Begonia halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals, which are used to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow.

Begonia halconensis prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
