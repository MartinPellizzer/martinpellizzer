Brassica cheiranthos var. paucidentata

Brassica cheiranthos var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, and its narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf teeth, giving it a unique appearance compared to other members of the species.
