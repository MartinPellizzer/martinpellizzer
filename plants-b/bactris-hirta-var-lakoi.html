Bactris hirta var. lakoi

Bactris hirta var. lakoi, commonly known as the Lakoi palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its clustered growth habit and slender, smooth stems. The leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fibrous fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
