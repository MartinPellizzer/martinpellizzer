Brachypodium phoenicoides var. villiglume

Brachypodium phoenicoides var. villiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicle-like clusters, bearing small, two-lobed glumes.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
