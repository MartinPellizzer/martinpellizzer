Bauhinia horsfieldii

Bauhinia horsfieldii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its ornamental value is largely due to its vibrant floral display and unique leaf structure.

Bauhinia horsfieldii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
