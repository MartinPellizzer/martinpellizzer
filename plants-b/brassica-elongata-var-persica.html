Brassica elongata var. persica

Brassica elongata var. persica, commonly known as the Persian mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, cross-shaped flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are siliques, which contain several elongated, oval seeds.

This variety is cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
