Bulbophyllum sulcatum

Bulbophyllum sulcatum, commonly known as the ridged orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ridged pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
