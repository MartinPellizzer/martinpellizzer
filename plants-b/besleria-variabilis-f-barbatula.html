Besleria variabilis f. barbatula

Besleria variabilis f. barbatula, commonly known as the hairy besleria, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and vibrant floral displays.

Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a unique textured appearance. The flowers, which bloom in clusters, come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, adding striking visual interest.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in greenhouses and tropical gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
