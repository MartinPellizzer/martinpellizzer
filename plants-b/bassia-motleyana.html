Bassia motleyana

Bassia motleyana, commonly known as Motley's goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are typically prostrate or ascending, and it often forms low-growing mats.

It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a common sight in dry, sunny habitats.
