Bredia microphylla

Bredia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bredia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Bredia microphylla is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
