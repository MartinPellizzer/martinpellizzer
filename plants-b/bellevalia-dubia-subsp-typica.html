Bellevalia dubia subsp. typica

Bellevalia dubia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.
