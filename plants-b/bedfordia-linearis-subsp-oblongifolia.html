Bedfordia linearis subsp. oblongifolia

Bedfordia linearis subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedfordia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is a perennial herb with slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
