Bignonia tetraquetra

Bignonia tetraquetra, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

The flowers emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
