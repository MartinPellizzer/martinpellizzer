Bromopsis suksdorfii

Bromopsis suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bromopsis suksdorfii is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
