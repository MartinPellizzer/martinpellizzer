Bougainvillea rubicunda

Bougainvillea rubicunda, commonly known as the red paper flower, is a vibrant evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, colorful bracts that surround small, white flowers, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to flourish. Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The bracts, which are often mistaken for petals, come in shades of red, pink, and purple, depending on the variety.
