Begonia juliasangii

Begonia juliasangii, commonly known as the Julia Sangii begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among botanical enthusiasts and gardeners.
