Balanopsis

Balanopsis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in long, pendulous clusters, often resembling a shower of golden droplets. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Balanopsis species typically grow as small trees or shrubs, with compound leaves and pods that contain seeds.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
