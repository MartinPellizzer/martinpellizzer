Brickellia californica var. tenera

Brickellia californica var. tenera, commonly known as softleaf brickellia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
