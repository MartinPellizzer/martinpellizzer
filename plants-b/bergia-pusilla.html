Bergia pusilla

Bergia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bergia, is a small flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it is exposed to salt spray and strong winds. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats near the ground.

Bergia pusilla is well adapted to harsh maritime environments, showcasing resilience to coastal conditions.
