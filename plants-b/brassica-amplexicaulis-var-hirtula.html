Brassica amplexicaulis var. hirtula

Brassica amplexicaulis var. hirtula, commonly known as the hairy hearted cabbage, is a variety of the wild cabbage species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in horticultural cultivation for its unique foliage.
