Brachytropis microphylla

Brachytropis microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its conservation status, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
