Burlingtonia

Burlingtonia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Burlingtonia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
