Bomarea incana

Bomarea incana, commonly known as the woolly bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its leaves are often described as velvety to the touch due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Bomarea incana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and unique botanical features.
