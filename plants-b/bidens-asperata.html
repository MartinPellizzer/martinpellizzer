Bidens asperata

Bidens asperata, commonly known as rough ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
