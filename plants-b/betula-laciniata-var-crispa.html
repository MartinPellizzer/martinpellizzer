Betula laciniata var. crispa

Betula laciniata var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled birch, is a variant of the paper birch characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a crinkled or curled appearance, giving the plant its common name. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The bark of Betula laciniata var. crispa is white and peels in thin, papery layers, similar to other birch species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
