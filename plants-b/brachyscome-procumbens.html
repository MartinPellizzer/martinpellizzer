Brachyscome procumbens

Brachyscome procumbens, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a low-growing perennial native to Australia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Brachyscome procumbens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
