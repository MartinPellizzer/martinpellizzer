Baronia

Baronia, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

These plants are native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where they thrive in cool, moist environments. Baronia species are characterized by their striking blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

These plants are often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where they play a vital role in local ecosystems.
