Bauhinia brachycalyx

Bauhinia brachycalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia brachycalyx is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.
