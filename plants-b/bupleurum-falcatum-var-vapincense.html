Bupleurum falcatum var. vapincense

Bupleurum falcatum var. vapincense, commonly known as the fragrant cowparsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and flowers contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

The variety is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other Bupleurum species.
