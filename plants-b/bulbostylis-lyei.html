Bulbostylis lyei

Bulbostylis lyei, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity and waterlogged soil. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a tendency to droop under heavy rain.

Bulbostylis lyei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its native environment.
