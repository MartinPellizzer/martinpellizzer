Babiana mucronata subsp. mucronata

Babiana mucronata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods, and it is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The subspecies mucronata is distinguished by its sharp, pointed flower tips and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
