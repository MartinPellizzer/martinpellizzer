Beckwithia juniperina

Beckwithia juniperina, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against its rugged habitat. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, Beckwithia juniperina is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
