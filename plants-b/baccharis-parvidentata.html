Baccharis parvidentata

Baccharis parvidentata, commonly known as narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
