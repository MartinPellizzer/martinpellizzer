Butea listeri

Butea listeri, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive seed pods that split open when mature. Butea listeri thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
