Bulbostylis thouarsii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. The inflorescence consists of a dense spike of tiny, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.
