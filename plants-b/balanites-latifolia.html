Balanites latifolia

Balanites latifolia, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Balanites latifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
