Blumea atropurpurea

Blumea atropurpurea, commonly known as purple blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Blumea atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
