Badianifera

Badianifera, commonly known as star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which contains aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is known for its essential oils that contribute to its strong, licorice-like flavor. Badianifera thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.

Its leaves and fruits are rich in compounds such as shikimic acid, which has industrial and pharmaceutical applications.
