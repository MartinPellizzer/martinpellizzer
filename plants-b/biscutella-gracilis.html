Biscutella gracilis

Biscutella gracilis, commonly known as slender biscuitroot, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Biscutella gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
