Betula × purpusii var. typica

Betula × purpusii var. typica, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silver sheen beneath, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by oval, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval with serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

Betula × purpusii var. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
