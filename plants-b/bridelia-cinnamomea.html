Bridelia cinnamomea

Bridelia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible. It is valued for its aromatic bark and leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Bridelia cinnamomea is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural dye.
