Brazzeia soyauxii

Brazzeia soyauxii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and produces a single, tall flowering stem. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturalists alike.
