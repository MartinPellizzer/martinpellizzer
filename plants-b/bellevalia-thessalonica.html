Bellevalia thessalonica

Bellevalia thessalonica, commonly known as the Thessalonica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a compact rosette growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
