Blastotrophe

Blastotrophe, commonly known as the blastospore-producing structure in certain fungi, is a specialized cell that plays a key role in asexual reproduction.

It is typically formed during the process of budding in yeast-like fungi. The blastotrophe develops from a parent cell and eventually gives rise to a new individual. This structure is often found in dimorphic fungi that can switch between yeast and mold forms.

Its presence is crucial for the rapid propagation of the organism in suitable environments.
