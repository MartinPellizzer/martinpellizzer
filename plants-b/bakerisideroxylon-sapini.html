Bakerisideroxylon sapini

Bakerisideroxylon sapini, commonly known as the sapindus tree, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red, spiny fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The tree is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
