Begonia kinahimiae

Begonia kinahimiae, commonly known as the Kina Hill begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of vibrant red flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it prefers shady, moist environments.

Begonia kinahimiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
