Bulbophyllum antongilense

Bulbophyllum antongilense, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and striking floral structure. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Bulbophyllum antongilense is also noted for its ability to form pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.
