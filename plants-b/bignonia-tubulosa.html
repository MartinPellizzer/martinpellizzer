Bignonia tubulosa

Bignonia tubulosa, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bignonia tubulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
