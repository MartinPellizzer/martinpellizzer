Blitum cristatum

Blitum cristatum, commonly known as the starry knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-like white or pink flowers. The plant is often found in moist meadows, streambanks, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Blitum cristatum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
