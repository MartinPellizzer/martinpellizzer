Bulbostylis ramosissima

Bulbostylis ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bulbostylis ramosissima is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and forest understories.
