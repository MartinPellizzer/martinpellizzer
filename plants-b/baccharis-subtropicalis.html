Baccharis subtropicalis

Baccharis subtropicalis, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Baccharis subtropicalis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
