Bromus kalmii

Bromus kalmii, commonly known as Kalm's brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Bromus kalmii is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
