Beta monodiana

Beta monodiana, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple roots, which are rich in anthocyanins. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a reddish tint.

Beta monodiana is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal.
