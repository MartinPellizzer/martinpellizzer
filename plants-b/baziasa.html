Baziasa

Baziasa, commonly known as the wild garlic or wild onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Alliaceae family, which includes other members like garlic and chives. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Baziasa is characterized by its strong, pungent odor, similar to that of garlic, due to the presence of sulfur-containing compounds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
