Barberina tetrandra

Barberina tetrandra, commonly known as the four-threaded barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and turn a vibrant red in the fall.

Barberina tetrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
