Begonia congesta

Begonia congesta, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil under partial shade.

Begonia congesta is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
