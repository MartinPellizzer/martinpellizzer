Berrya tahitensis

Berrya tahitensis, commonly known as Tahitian berry or Tahiti berry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Berberidaceae.

It is native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor. Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
