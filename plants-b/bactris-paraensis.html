Bactris paraensis

Bactris paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet, lowland areas and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by both wildlife and humans. Its fibrous trunk is valued for its strength and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Bactris paraensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various species.
