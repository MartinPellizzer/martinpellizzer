Barleria prionitis subsp. prionitoides

Barleria prionitis subsp.

prionitoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and oppositely arranged.

The flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in dense spikes, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
