Bidens elegans

Bidens elegans, commonly known as the elegant tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive seed structure known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens elegans is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
