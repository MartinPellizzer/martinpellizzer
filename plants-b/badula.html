Badula

Badula, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant bears edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its roots and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties.

Badula is often found in humid, tropical climates and is known for its longevity and resilience.
