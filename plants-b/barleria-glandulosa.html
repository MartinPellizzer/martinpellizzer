Barleria glandulosa

Barleria glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and small, glandular structures on their surfaces. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

Barleria glandulosa is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
