Boehmeria holosericea var. izuosimensis

Boehmeria holosericea var. izuosimensis, commonly known as the Izu Islands nettle, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its soft, silky fibers that are often used in the production of textiles and paper. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, hair-like structures.

This variant is valued for its unique fiber quality and ecological significance in its native habitat.
