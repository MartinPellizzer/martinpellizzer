Berberis sublevis var. microcarpa

Berberis sublevis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers.

It is native to parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
