Begonia bangsamoro subsp. bagasa

Begonia bangsamoro subsp.

bagasa, commonly known as the Mindanao begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, thriving in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
