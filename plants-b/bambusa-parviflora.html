Bambusa parviflora

Bambusa parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, clump-forming stems that grow to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa parviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
