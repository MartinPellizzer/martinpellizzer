Buchnera nigrescens

Buchnera nigrescens, commonly known as the black-barked buchnera, is a unique plant species characterized by its dark, almost black bark that gives it its common name.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its small, leathery leaves are evergreen, and it produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in the spring.

Buchnera nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
