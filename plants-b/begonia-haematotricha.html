Begonia haematotricha

Begonia haematotricha, commonly known as the blood-red begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, reddish hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and typically white or pink, emerging from the leaf axils.

Begonia haematotricha is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
