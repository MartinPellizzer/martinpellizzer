Bignonia miranda

Bignonia miranda, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces elongated, seed-containing pods that mature in the fall. Bignonia miranda thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a climber or shrub.
