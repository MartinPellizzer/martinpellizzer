Blechnum occidentale var. solutum

Blechnum occidentale var. solutum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variety of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are bright yellow to golden in color, which contrast with the dark green veins. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
