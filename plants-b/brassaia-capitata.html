Brassaia capitata

Brassaia capitata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
