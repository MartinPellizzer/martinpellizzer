Bellis perennis var. integrifolia

Bellis perennis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the English daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically entire, giving it the species name *integrifolia*. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
