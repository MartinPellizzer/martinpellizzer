Bulbophyllum platyrrhachis

Bulbophyllum platyrrhachis, commonly known as the flat-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, flat, and often brightly colored pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
