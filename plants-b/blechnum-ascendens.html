Blechnum ascendens

Blechnum ascendens, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
