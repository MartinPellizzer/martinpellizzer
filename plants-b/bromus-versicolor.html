Bromus versicolor

Bromus versicolor, commonly known as the variable brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its variable leaf coloration, which can range from green to reddish or bronze, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often loosely arranged.

Bromus versicolor is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
