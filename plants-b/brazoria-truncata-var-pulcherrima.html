Brazoria truncata var. pulcherrima

Brazoria truncata var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
