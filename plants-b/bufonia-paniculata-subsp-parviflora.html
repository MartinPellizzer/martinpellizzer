Bufonia paniculata subsp. parviflora

Bufonia paniculata subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bufonia, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Bufonia paniculata subsp.

parviflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering structure.
