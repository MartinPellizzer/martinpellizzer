Boechera perennans subsp. fendleri

Boechera perennans subsp.

fendleri, commonly known as the desert rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves and flowering stems that bear small, white or yellowish flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
