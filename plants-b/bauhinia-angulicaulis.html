Bauhinia angulicaulis

Bauhinia angulicaulis, commonly known as the angular bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular, leaf-like pods that are typically green or brown in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Bauhinia angulicaulis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
