Biscutella intricata

Biscutella intricata, commonly known as the intricate biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow, are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Biscutella intricata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
