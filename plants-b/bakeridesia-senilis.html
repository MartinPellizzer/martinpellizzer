Bakeridesia senilis

Bakeridesia senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Bakeridesia senilis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
