Bactris pulchra

Bactris pulchra, commonly known as the purple-leafed palm, is a striking species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that display a vibrant purple hue on the underside. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into edible, sweet fruits. Bactris pulchra thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for both landscaping and botanical gardens.
