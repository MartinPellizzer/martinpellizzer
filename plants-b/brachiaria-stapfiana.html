Brachiaria stapfiana

Brachiaria stapfiana, commonly known as the Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Brachiaria stapfiana is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and pastures, and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for livestock and contributing to soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
