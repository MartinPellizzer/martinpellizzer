Bulbophyllum brevicolumna

Bulbophyllum brevicolumna, commonly known as the short-stemmed torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
