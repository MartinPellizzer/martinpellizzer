Bernardia myricifolia

Bernardia myricifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy texture. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Bernardia myricifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its eye-catching floral display.
