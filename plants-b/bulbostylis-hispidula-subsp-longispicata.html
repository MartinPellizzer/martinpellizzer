Bulbostylis hispidula subsp. longispicata

Bulbostylis hispidula subsp.

longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined bulbostylis, is a small, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of tiny, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color.

The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
