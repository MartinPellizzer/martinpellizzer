Bursaria lasiophylla var. atriplicina

Bursaria lasiophylla var. atriplicina, commonly known as the dark-leaved bursaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is characterized by its distinct dark foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
