Berberis jobii

Berberis jobii, commonly known as the Job's teat, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. Berberis jobii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or arid conditions.

It contains alkaloids, including berberine, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.
