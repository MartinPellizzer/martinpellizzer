Banisterioides

Banisterioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a woody base and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Banisterioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
