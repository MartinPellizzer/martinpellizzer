Bauhinia grazielae

Bauhinia grazielae, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bauhinia grazielae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
