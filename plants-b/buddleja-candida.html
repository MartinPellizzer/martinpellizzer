Buddleja candida

Buddleja candida, commonly known as the white butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja candida is known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
