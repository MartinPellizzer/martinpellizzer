Bambusa elevatissima

Bambusa elevatissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for ecological restoration and commercial applications.
