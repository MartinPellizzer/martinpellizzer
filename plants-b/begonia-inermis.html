Begonia inermis

Begonia inermis, commonly known as the silver velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to deep green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia inermis is widely grown as a decorative plant in gardens and homes.
