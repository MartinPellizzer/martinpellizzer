Bignonia caerulea

Bignonia caerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 feet in length, making it a vigorous climber. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
