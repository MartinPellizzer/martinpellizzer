Bartonia albicaulis

Bartonia albicaulis, commonly known as the whiteflower bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a notable species in botanical studies.
