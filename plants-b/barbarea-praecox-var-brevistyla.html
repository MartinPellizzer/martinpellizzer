Barbarea praecox var. brevistyla

Barbarea praecox var. brevistyla, commonly known as the early candytuft, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
