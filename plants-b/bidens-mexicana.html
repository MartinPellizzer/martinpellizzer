Bidens mexicana

Bidens mexicana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Bidens mexicana is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
