Begonia mariannensis

Begonia mariannensis, commonly known as the Marian Islands begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean, particularly the Mariana Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a dense, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Begonia mariannensis plays a significant role in the local flora of its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
