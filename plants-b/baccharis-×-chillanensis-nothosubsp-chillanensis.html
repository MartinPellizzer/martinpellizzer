Baccharis × chillanensis nothosubsp.

chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean burrobrush, is a hybrid shrub native to Chile and Argentina. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its morphology reflects a blend of its parent species, Baccharis linearis and Baccharis dentata.
