Bridelia cathartica var. lingelsheimii

Bridelia cathartica var. lingelsheimii, commonly known as the white bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories.
