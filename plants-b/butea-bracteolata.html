Butea bracteolata

Butea bracteolata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

The bark and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
