Bromus squarrosus subsp. commutatus

Bromus squarrosus subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose panicle, with lemma and palea surfaces that are often hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in prairies, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
