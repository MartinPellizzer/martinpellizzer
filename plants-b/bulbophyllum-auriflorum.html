Bulbophyllum auriflorum

Bulbophyllum auriflorum, commonly known as the golden-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
