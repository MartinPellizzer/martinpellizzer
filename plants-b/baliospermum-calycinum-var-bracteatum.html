Baliospermum calycinum var. bracteatum

Baliospermum calycinum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate blue morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various climates.
