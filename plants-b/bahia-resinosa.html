Bahia resinosa

Bahia resinosa, commonly known as resinous bahia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Bahia resinosa is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its resinous sap can be used in traditional remedies and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
