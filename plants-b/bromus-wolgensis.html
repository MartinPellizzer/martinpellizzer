Bromus wolgensis

Bromus wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Bromus wolgensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
