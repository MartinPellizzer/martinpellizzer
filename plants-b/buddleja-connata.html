Buddleja connata

Buddleja connata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre buddleia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to lilac. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. It blooms during the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Buddleja connata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
