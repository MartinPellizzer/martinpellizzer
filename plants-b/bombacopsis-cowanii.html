Bombacopsis cowanii

Bombacopsis cowanii, commonly known as the silky gum or silky ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy underside.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
