Bignonia exserta

Bignonia exserta, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly heart-shaped base.

Bignonia exserta thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
