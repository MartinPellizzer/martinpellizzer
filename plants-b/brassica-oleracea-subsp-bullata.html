Brassica oleracea subsp. bullata

Brassica oleracea subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the bulbous cabbage, is a perennial plant characterized by its swollen, bulb-like stems. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that form a compact rosette, often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
