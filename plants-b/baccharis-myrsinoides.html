Baccharis myrsinoides

Baccharis myrsinoides, commonly known as bluebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Baccharis myrsinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in drought conditions.
