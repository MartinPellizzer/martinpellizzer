Blechnum floresii

Blechnum floresii, commonly known as the Flores maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the island of Flores in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive pale underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum floresii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
