Balaniella ramosa

Balaniella ramosa, commonly known as the branched broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true roots and its reliance on vascular connections to its hosts. Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
