Barnadesia lehmannii var. villosa

Barnadesia lehmannii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy barnadesia, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in terminal spikes, and are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

The varietal name 'villosa' refers to the dense, hairy covering that covers much of the plant.
