Bromus alopecurus

Bromus alopecurus, commonly known as mousegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to form dense colonies.

Bromus alopecurus is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
