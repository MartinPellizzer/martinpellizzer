Bursera glabra

Bursera glabra, commonly known as the smooth bursera, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

Bursera glabra is well adapted to dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
