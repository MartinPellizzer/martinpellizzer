Berlandiera incisa

Berlandiera incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Berlandiera incisa is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
