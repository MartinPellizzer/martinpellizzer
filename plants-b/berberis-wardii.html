Berberis wardii

Berberis wardii, commonly known as the Ward's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, deciduous leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of climatic conditions.

Berberis wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
