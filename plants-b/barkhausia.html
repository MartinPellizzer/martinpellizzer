Barkhausia

Barkhausia, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plants are shrubs or small trees, often featuring compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. Barkhausia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to ecological benefits.

They are also used in traditional medicine and as sources of timber and fodder.
