Begonia umbellata

Begonia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia umbellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
