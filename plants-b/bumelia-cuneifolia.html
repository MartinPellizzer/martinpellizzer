Bumelia cuneifolia

Bumelia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

Bumelia cuneifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
