Bulbophyllum densiflorum

Bulbophyllum densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
