Blumea lyrata

Blumea lyrata, commonly known as the lyrate blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lyrate in shape, with a distinctive lobed structure, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Blumea lyrata is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and forest edges.
