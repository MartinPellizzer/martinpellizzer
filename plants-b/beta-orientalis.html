Beta orientalis

Beta orientalis, commonly known as the oriental beetroot, is a hardy perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, edible roots that are typically purple or red in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, green flowers in clusters. Beta orientalis is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is related to the common beetroot, Beta vulgaris.

Its cultivation requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
