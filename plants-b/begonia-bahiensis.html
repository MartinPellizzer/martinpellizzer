Begonia bahiensis

Begonia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia begonia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
