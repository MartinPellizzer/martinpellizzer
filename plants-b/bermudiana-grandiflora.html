Bermudiana grandiflora

Bermudiana grandiflora, commonly known as the Bermuda rose, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its association with Bermuda and its large, showy flowers.
