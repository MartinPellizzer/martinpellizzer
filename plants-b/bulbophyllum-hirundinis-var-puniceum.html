Bulbophyllum hirundinis var. puniceum

Bulbophyllum hirundinis var. puniceum, commonly known as the red-winged orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescences, which are often adorned with intricate, wing-like structures. The plant produces small, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers typically bloom during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to the forest canopy.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
