Boltonia indica var. collina

Boltonia indica var. collina, commonly known as the Eastern coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a prominent central cone, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Boltonia indica var. collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
