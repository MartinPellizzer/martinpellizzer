Blumea benthamiana var. minor

Blumea benthamiana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser blumea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs. This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
