Bromus arvensis var. japonicus

Bromus arvensis var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese barnyard grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

This species is considered a weed in many regions due to its competitive nature and adaptability.
