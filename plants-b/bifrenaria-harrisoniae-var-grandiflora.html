Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. grandiflora

Bifrenaria harrisoniae var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant red and orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.

It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for advanced orchid growers.
