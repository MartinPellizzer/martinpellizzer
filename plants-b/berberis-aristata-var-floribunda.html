Berberis aristata var. floribunda

Berberis aristata var. floribunda, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice in horticulture and traditional medicine.
