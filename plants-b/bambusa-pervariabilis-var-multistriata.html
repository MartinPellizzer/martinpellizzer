Bambusa pervariabilis var. multistriata

Bambusa pervariabilis var. multistriata, commonly known as the multistriga bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often exhibit a distinctive striped pattern on the culms. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
