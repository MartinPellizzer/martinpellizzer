Berberidopsis beckleri

Berberidopsis beckleri, commonly known as the Beckler's lily, is a rare and endangered plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada Mountains in California.

It is a member of the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers and evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its alpine habitat. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in late spring and early summer.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
