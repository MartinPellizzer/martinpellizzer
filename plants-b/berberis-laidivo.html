Berberis laidivo

Berberis laidivo, commonly known as the Laidivo barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its berries are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Berberis laidivo is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
