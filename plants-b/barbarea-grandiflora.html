Barbarea grandiflora

Barbarea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered winter cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lobed leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are borne on tall, branching stems and are visited by various pollinators.

Barbarea grandiflora is known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.
