Bulbostylis juncoides f. hirtella

Bulbostylis juncoides f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bulbostylis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. Its inflorescence is often densely packed, with the flowers being wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other forms of Bulbostylis.
