Bolax tridentata

Bolax tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along streambanks.

Bolax tridentata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
