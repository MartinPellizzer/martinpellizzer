Bauhinia abyssinica

Bauhinia abyssinica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, fern-like leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Bauhinia abyssinica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
