Balanocarpus anomalus

Balanocarpus anomalus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits.

The species is noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of lowland rainforest ecosystems.
