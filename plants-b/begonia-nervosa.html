Begonia nervosa

Begonia nervosa, commonly known as the veiny begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia nervosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both gardens and as a houseplant.
