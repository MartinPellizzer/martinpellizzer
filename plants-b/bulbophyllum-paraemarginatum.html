Bulbophyllum paraemarginatum

Bulbophyllum paraemarginatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
