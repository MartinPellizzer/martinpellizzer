Begonia larvata

Begonia larvata, commonly known as the larvate begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its growth habit is usually upright, with a compact form that makes it suitable for container gardening.

Begonia larvata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant foliage.
