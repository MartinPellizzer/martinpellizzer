Berberis keissleriana

Berberis keissleriana, commonly known as the Keissler's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
