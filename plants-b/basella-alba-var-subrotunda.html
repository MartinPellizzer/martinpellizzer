Basella alba var. subrotunda

Basella alba var. subrotunda, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round, red fruits. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
