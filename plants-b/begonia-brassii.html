Begonia brassii

Begonia brassii, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Begonia brassii is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
