Bestram

Bestram, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and can grow as a climbing vine or shrub, depending on the conditions. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bestram is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
