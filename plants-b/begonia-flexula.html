Begonia flexula

Begonia flexula, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker green and reddish veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, which adds visual interest to any garden setting.
