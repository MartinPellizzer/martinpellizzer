Berberis vulgaris f. leucocarpa

Berberis vulgaris f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow berries.

This shrub is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The leaves are typically dark green and holly-like, with spiny stems that provide structural interest. The white berries, which are non-toxic, attract birds and add ornamental value to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
