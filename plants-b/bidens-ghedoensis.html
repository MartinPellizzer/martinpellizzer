Bidens ghedoensis

Bidens ghedoensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent white markings. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant's resilience and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
