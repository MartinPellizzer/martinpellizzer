Banisteria acuminata

Banisteria acuminata, commonly known as the red batik plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Banisteria acuminata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
