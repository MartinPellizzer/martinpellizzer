Baccharis racemosa var. angolensis

Baccharis racemosa var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola baccharis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
