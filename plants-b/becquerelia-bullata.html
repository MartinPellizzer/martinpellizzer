Becquerelia bullata

Becquerelia bullata, commonly known as the bullate begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves with a unique bullate (blistered) texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouse displays.
