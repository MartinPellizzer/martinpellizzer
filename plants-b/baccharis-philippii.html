Baccharis philippii

Baccharis philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
