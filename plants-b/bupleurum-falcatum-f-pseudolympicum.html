Bupleurum falcatum f. pseudolympicum

Bupleurum falcatum f. pseudolympicum, commonly known as the Alpine bupleurum, is a variant of the common bupleurum found in mountainous regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
