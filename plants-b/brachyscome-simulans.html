Brachyscome simulans

Brachyscome simulans, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, featuring silver-gray foliage that reflects its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Brachyscome simulans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
