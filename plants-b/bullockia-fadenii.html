Bullockia fadenii

Bullockia fadenii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
