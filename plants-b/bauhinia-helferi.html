Bauhinia helferi

Bauhinia helferi, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, often followed by flat, papery pods. Bauhinia helferi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

It has cultural and medicinal significance in some African communities.
