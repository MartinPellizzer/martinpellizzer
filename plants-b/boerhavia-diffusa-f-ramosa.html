Boerhavia diffusa f. ramosa

Boerhavia diffusa f. ramosa, commonly known as spreading hogweed, is a variant of the common hogweed species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling growth habit and branched stems. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

This plant is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain environments.
