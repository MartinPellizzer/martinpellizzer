Bidaria cuspidata

Bidaria cuspidata, commonly known as the spiny bidaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows as a tall, perennial herb. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Bidaria cuspidata is often found in open areas such as savannas and along riverbanks.
