Bignonia cassinoides

Bignonia cassinoides, commonly known as the yellow cross vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and woodlands. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and small, oblong leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
