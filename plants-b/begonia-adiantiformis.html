Begonia adiantiformis

Begonia adiantiformis, commonly known as the water begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often described as resembling those of the maidenhair fern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
