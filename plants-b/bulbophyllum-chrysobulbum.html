Bulbophyllum chrysobulbum

Bulbophyllum chrysobulbum, commonly known as the golden bulb orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, colorful flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, making it an epiphytic species. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, tropical environments.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
