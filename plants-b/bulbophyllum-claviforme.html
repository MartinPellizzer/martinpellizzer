Bulbophyllum claviforme

Bulbophyllum claviforme, commonly known as the club-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive club-like shape that gives it its common name.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid environments.
