Berberis macrosepala var. sakdenensis

Berberis macrosepala var. sakdenensis, commonly known as the Sakden's barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
