Buchnera wildii

Buchnera wildii, commonly known as the wild wildflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit, often reaching only a few inches in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Buchnera wildii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
