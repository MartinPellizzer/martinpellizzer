Brazoria scutellarioides

Brazoria scutellarioides, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates with high salinity. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody at the base.

Brazoria scutellarioides is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
