Beaufortia microphylla

Beaufortia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf beaufortia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Beaufortia microphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in harsh environments.
