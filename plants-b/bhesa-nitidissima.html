Bhesa nitidissima

Bhesa nitidissima, commonly known as the glossy leaf bhesa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
