Berberis nevinii

Berberis nevinii, commonly known as the Nevin's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
