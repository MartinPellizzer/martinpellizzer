Brachiaria ambigens

Brachiaria ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria ambigens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
