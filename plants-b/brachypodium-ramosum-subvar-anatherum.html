Brachypodium ramosum subvar. anatherum

Brachypodium ramosum subvar. anatherum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
