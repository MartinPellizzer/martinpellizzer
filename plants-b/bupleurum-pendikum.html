Bupleurum pendikum

Bupleurum pendikum, commonly known as Turkish angelica, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The roots and rhizomes are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like essential oils and alkaloids.
