Blechnum cyatheoides

Blechnum cyatheoides, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a fibrous trunk and a crown of fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically pinnate, with a long, tapering shape that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

Blechnum cyatheoides plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
