Blepharis panduriformis

Blepharis panduriformis, commonly known as the long-beak grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it adaptable to arid environments.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
