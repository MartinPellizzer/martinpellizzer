Bactris praemorsa

Bactris praemorsa, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are widely used for thatching and weaving due to their durability and flexibility.

Bactris praemorsa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
