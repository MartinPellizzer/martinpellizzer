Baccharis eupatorioides

Baccharis eupatorioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in ecological studies.
