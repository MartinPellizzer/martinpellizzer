Blechnum cycadifolium

Blechnum cycadifolium, commonly known as the cycad-like fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often cycad-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually compound, with a feathery appearance, and they can reach considerable lengths.

This fern is native to New Zealand and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
