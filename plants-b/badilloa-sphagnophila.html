Badilloa sphagnophila

Badilloa sphagnophila, commonly known as the bog bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows, where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its root system is fibrous, helping it anchor in waterlogged soils.

Badilloa sphagnophila plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
