Berberis vernae

Berberis vernae, commonly known as the spring barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and versatile plant for garden use.
