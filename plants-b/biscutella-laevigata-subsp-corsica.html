Biscutella laevigata subsp. corsica

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its scientific name reflects its close relationship to the broader species Biscutella laevigata.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
