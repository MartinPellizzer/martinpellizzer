Bauhinia urbaniana

Bauhinia urbaniana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Bauhinia urbaniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
