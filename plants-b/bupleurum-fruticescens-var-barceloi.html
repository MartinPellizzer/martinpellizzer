Bupleurum fruticescens var. barceloi

Bupleurum fruticescens var. barceloi, commonly known as the Barcelona bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and typically have a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It is adapted to rocky and limestone soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
