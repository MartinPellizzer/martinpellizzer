Boronia quadrilata

Boronia quadrilata, commonly known as the four-angled boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture. It typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
