Begonia coelocentroides

Begonia coelocentroides, commonly known as the red-topped begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
