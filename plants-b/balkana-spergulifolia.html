Balkana spergulifolia

Balkana spergulifolia, commonly known as the Balkan sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. Balkana spergulifolia is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
