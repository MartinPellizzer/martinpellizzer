Brasilettia velutina

Brasilettia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Brasilettia velutina is also known for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
