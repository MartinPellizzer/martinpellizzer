Bromelia fastuosa

Bromelia fastuosa, commonly known as the showy bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can last for several months. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms and small aquatic life. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are often surrounded by showy bracts.

Bromelia fastuosa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
