Butneria fertilis

Butneria fertilis, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange petals, which resemble flames, and its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its blooms typically appear during the summer months, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Butneria fertilis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
