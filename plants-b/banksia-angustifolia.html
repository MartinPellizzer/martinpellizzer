Banksia angustifolia

Banksia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant yellow flowers. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

Banksia angustifolia plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
