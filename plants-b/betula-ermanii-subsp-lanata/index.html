Betula Ermanii Subsp. Lanata

Betula ermanii subsp. lanata is a unique variety of birch tree known for its distinctive hairy leaves and elegant appearance. Native to parts of East Asia, this subspecies thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage turns a striking golden yellow in the fall, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The tree's slow growth and compact form make it ideal for smaller landscapes. Despite its beauty, it requires careful attention to soil conditions and moisture levels to flourish.
