Boehmeria sieboldiana f. nakaiana

Boehmeria sieboldiana f. nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaian hemp plant, is a variant of the Boehmeria sieboldiana species, which belongs to the荨麻科 (Urticaceae) family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, leafy structure and is native to regions of East Asia, including Japan and parts of China. The plant is notable for its strong, durable fibers, which have been historically used in textile production. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy surface that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for both its industrial applications and ornamental value in horticulture.
