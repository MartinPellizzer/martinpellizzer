Baccharis angelica

Baccharis angelica, commonly known as angel's thorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with small, spiny leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Baccharis angelica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
