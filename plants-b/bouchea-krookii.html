Bouchea krookii

Bouchea krookii, commonly known as the Krook's bouchea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from red to orange, often with a striking contrast between the flower and the foliage. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a dramatic backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bouchea krookii is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
