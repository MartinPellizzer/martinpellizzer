Babiana namaquensis

Babiana namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Babiana namaquensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
