Bromus ciliatus f. denudatus

Bromus ciliatus f. denudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf brome, is a variant of the common brome grass.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf hairs, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form. This grass is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, awned florets.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
