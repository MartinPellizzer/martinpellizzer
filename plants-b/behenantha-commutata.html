Behenantha commutata

Behenantha commutata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or wild nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and stinging hairs on the stems and leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

The species is often found in woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
