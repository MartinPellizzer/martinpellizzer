Berinia foetida

Berinia foetida, commonly known as the stinking violets, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers. Berinia foetida typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
