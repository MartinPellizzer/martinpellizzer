Bacasia canescens

Bacasia canescens, commonly known as the hairy bacasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Bacasia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
