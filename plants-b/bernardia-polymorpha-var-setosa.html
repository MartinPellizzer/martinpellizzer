Bernardia polymorpha var. setosa

Bernardia polymorpha var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy Bernardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and distinctive hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
