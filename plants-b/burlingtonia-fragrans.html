Burlingtonia fragrans

Burlingtonia fragrans, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dry, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
