Bignonia aequinoctialis var. spectabilis

Bignonia aequinoctialis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, woody stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
