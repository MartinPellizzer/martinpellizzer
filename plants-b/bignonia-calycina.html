Bignonia calycina

Bignonia calycina, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture and emits a subtle, sweet fragrance. Bignonia calycina thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
