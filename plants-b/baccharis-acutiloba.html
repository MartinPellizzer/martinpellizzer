Baccharis acutiloba

Baccharis acutiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common component of many ecosystems in its native range.
