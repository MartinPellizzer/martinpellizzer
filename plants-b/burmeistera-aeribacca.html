Burmeistera aeribacca

Burmeistera aeribacca, commonly known as the skyflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Burmeistera aeribacca is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
