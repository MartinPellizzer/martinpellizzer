Bursera madrigalii

Bursera madrigalii, commonly known as the Madrigal Bursera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the dry forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
