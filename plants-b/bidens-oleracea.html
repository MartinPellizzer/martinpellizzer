Bidens oleracea

Bidens oleracea, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It features erect, hairy stems and produces small, yellow-orange flower heads with ray florets. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Bidens oleracea is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
