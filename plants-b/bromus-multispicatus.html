Bromus multispicatus

Bromus multispicatus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Bromus multispicatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
