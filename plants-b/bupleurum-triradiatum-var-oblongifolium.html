Bupleurum triradiatum var. oblongifolium

Bupleurum triradiatum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
