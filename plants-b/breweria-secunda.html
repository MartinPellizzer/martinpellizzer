Breweria secunda

Breweria secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf breweria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
