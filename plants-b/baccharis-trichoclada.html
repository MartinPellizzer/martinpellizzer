Baccharis trichoclada

Baccharis trichoclada, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis trichoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
