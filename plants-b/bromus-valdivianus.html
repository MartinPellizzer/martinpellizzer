Bromus valdivianus

Bromus valdivianus, commonly known as the Valdivian brome, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Bromus valdivianus is adapted to cool, wet climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
