Bursaria spinosa subsp. spinosa

Bursaria spinosa subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the spinifex burr bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local vegetation.
