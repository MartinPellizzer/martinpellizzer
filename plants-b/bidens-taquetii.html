Bidens taquetii

Bidens taquetii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Bidens taquetii is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is considered a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
