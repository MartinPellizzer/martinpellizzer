Bulbine

Bulbine, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, often yellow or orange flowers. Bulbine species are typically drought-tolerant, making them well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. They often grow in rocky or sandy soils and are popular in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, highlighting their ecological and cultural significance.
