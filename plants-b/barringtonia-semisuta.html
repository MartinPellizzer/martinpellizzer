Barringtonia semisuta

Barringtonia semisuta, commonly known as the lesser banyan, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Lecythidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, lowland forest areas.

The tree is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
