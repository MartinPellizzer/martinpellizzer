Blumea villosa

Blumea villosa, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy leaves that give it its common name. Its small yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea villosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
