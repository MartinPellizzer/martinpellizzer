Blumea lessingii

Blumea lessingii, commonly known as the lesser blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow with a central disk.

Blumea lessingii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
