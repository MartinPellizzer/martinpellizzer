Bryophyllum rolandi-bonapartei

Bryophyllum rolandi-bonapartei, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or circular in shape. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring and summer months. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
