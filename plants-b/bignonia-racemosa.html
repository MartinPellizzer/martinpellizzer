Bignonia racemosa

Bignonia racemosa, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along streambanks and in moist woodlands. It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other ornamental and woody plants.

The species is valued for its showy blooms and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
