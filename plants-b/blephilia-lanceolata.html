Blephilia lanceolata

Blephilia lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Blephilia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
