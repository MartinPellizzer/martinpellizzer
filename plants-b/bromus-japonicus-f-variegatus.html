Bromus japonicus f. variegatus

Bromus japonicus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated brome, is a variant of the Japanese brome grass.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes or spots. This plant is a perennial grass native to East Asia, particularly Japan. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
