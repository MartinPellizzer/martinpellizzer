Bupleurum tenuissimum subsp. columnae

Bupleurum tenuissimum subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
