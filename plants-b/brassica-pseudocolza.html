Brassica pseudocolza

Brassica pseudocolza, commonly known as false rapeseed, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright stem and basal leaves that are lobed or divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated pods that split open when mature.

Brassica pseudocolza is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.
