Brachystegia malengensis

Brachystegia malengensis, commonly known as the Malengeni teak, is a deciduous tree native to central Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pods containing flat, elongated seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hard timber, it is valued for both ecological and economic purposes in its native region.
