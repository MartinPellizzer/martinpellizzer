Berberis barilochensis

Berberis barilochensis, commonly known as the Bariloche barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with similar growing conditions.
