Bauhinia semibifida

Bauhinia semibifida, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Bauhinia semibifida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
