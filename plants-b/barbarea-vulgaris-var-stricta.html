Barbarea vulgaris var. stricta

Barbarea vulgaris var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf winter cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is notable for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
