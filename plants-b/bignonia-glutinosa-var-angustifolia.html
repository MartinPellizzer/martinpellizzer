Bignonia glutinosa var. angustifolia

Bignonia glutinosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can climb by twining around supports, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
