Begonia antongilensis var. cuneata

Begonia antongilensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
