Barbarea vulgaris proles arcuata

Barbarea vulgaris proles arcuata, commonly known as the curled leaf rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and distinctive, curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
