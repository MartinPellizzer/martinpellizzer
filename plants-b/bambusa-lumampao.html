Bambusa lumampao

Bambusa lumampao, commonly known as the bamboo of the Philippines, is a tall, slender grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, fibrous stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for making handicrafts.

Bambusa lumampao is well adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
