Bernardia polymorpha

Bernardia polymorpha, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive red or orange berries that persist through the winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.
