Bambusa amahussana

Bambusa amahussana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a tall and sturdy plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its strong and flexible stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
