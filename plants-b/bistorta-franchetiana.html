Bistorta franchetiana

Bistorta franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese bistort, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often medicinal properties. The plant features upright, reddish stems that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are often tinged with red or purple, especially along the veins.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.
