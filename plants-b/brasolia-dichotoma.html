Brasolia dichotoma

Brasolia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked leaf plant, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, Brasolia dichotoma is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
