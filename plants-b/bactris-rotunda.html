Bactris rotunda

Bactris rotunda, commonly known as the round-edged palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and distinctive, rounded leaf bases. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines. Its leaves are often used for thatching and weaving due to their fibrous texture.

Bactris rotunda is valued for both its ecological role and its utility in traditional practices.
