Bupleurum falcatum f. irregulare

Bupleurum falcatum f. irregulare, commonly known as the irregular leafed boldo, is a variant of the boldo plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves that give it its botanical name. The plant is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the base of the plant.
