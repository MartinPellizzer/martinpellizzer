Beta vulgaris var. ovaliformis

Beta vulgaris var. ovaliformis, commonly known as the beetroot or garden beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, cylindrical root that can vary in color from red to yellow or white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

The root is rich in nutrients, particularly nitrates and fiber, and is widely used in culinary and agricultural contexts.
