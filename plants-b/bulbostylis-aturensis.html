Bulbostylis aturensis

Bulbostylis aturensis, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

Bulbostylis aturensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
