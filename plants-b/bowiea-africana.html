Bowiea africana

Bowiea africana, commonly known as the African elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bowiea africana is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to survive in dry environments.
