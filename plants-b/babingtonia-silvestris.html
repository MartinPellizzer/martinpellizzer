Babingtonia silvestris

Babingtonia silvestris, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Babingtonia silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
