Butea crassifolia

Butea crassifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has a fibrous, woody stem with a distinctive reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Butea crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
