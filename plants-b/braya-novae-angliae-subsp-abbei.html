Braya novae-angliae subsp. abbei

Braya novae-angliae subsp.

abbei, commonly known as the northern rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically has a mat-forming habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as slopes and ledges.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
