Brassica tournefortii var. sisymbrioides

Brassica tournefortii var. sisymbrioides, commonly known as the desert mustangweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters tall, with hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by the development of elongated, slender seed pods.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
