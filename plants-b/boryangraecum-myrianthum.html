Boryangraecum myrianthum

Boryangraecum myrianthum, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are oppositely arranged. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Boryangraecum myrianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and coastal conditions.
