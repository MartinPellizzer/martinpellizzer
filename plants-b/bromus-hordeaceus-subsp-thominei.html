Bromus hordeaceus subsp. thominei

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

thominei, commonly known as the Thomine's brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
