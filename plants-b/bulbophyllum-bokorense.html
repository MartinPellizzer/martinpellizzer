Bulbophyllum bokorense

Bulbophyllum bokorense, commonly known as the Bokor orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and striking floral structure. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to yellow and pink. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
