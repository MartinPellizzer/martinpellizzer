Boesenbergia plicata var. lurida

Boesenbergia plicata var. lurida, commonly known as the purple ginger or wild ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Boesenbergia plicata var. lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
