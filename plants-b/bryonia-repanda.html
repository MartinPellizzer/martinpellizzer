Bryonia repanda

Bryonia repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf bryony, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a trailing or ascending stem with spiny tendrils, and its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface. Its yellowish flowers are followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
