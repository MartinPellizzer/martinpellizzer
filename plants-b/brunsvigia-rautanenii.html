Brunsvigia rautanenii

Brunsvigia rautanenii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, giving it its common name. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen and form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for Mediterranean climates and xeriscaping.
