Baccharis pentziifolia var. minor

Baccharis pentziifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser pentziifolia baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale yellow color that attracts various pollinators.

This species plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetland environments.
