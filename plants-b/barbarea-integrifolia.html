Barbarea integrifolia

Barbarea integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is an annual flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, rounded, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and can often be found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in cool climates.
