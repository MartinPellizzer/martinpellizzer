Brassica montana var. apenninica

Brassica montana var. apenninica, commonly known as the Apennine wild mustard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
