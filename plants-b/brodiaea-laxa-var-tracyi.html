Brodiaea laxa var. tracyi

Brodiaea laxa var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale lavender flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and ornamental value in gardens.
