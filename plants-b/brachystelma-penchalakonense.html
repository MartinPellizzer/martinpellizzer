Brachystelma penchalakonense

Brachystelma penchalakonense, commonly known as the Panchalakon rock orchid, is a small, epiphytic plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink blooms during the summer months, which are often clustered in a raceme. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, reflecting its drought-tolerant nature.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
