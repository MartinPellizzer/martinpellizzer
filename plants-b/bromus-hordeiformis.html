Bromus hordeiformis

Bromus hordeiformis, commonly known as the barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Bromus hordeiformis is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize open areas.
