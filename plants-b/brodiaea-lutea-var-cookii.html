Brodiaea lutea var. cookii

Brodiaea lutea var. cookii, commonly known as the Cook's yellow brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during periods of drought.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ecological importance in native plant communities.
