Benzoin obtusilobum

Benzoin obtusilobum, commonly known as the Madagascar benjoin, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by reddish-brown capsules containing aromatic resin. This resin, known as benjoin, is harvested for its use in perfumes and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest.
