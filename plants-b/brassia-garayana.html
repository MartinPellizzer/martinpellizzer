Brassia garayana

Brassia garayana, commonly known as the Garay's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant features long, slender leaves and blooms in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive purple or red lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassia garayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
