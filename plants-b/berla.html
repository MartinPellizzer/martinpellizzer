Berla

Berla, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is valued for its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly sought after in traditional crafts and furniture. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers. Berla thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique wood properties and ecological role in its native habitats.
