Bulbophyllum quadricaudatum

Bulbophyllum quadricaudatum, commonly known as the four-tailed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

It is also noted for its ability to produce multiple flower spikes from a single pseudobulb.
