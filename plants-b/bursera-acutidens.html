Bursera acutidens

Bursera acutidens, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown of thick, leathery leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Bursera acutidens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
