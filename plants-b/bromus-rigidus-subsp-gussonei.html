Bromus rigidus subsp. gussonei

Bromus rigidus subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower-bearing structures.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
