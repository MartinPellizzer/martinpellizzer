Bergia glomerata

Bergia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bergia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring. Bergia glomerata thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
