Bursa bursa-pastoris var. integrifolia

Bursa bursa-pastoris var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Greater Celandine, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
