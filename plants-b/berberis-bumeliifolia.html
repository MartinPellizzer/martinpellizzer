Berberis bumeliifolia

Berberis bumeliifolia, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, orange-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
