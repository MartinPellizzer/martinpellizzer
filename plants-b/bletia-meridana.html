Bletia meridana

Bletia meridana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its fragrant blooms.

Bletia meridana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
