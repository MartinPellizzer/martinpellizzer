Bahia schkuhrioides

Bahia schkuhrioides, commonly known as Brazilian grass or schkuhri grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Its adaptability and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity in landscaping.
