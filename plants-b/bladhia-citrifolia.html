Bladhia citrifolia

Bladhia citrifolia, commonly known as the citrus hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The citrus hibiscus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
