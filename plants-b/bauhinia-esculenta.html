Bauhinia esculenta

Bauhinia esculenta, commonly known as the edible bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant is notable for its edible pods, which contain seeds encased in a fibrous, gelatinous pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some cultures.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
