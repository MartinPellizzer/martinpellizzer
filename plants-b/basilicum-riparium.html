Basilicum riparium

Basilicum riparium, commonly known as water basil, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Basilicum riparium is valued for its adaptability to aquatic conditions and its potential medicinal properties.
