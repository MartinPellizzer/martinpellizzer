Banksia petraea

Banksia petraea, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Banksia petraea plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for a variety of native pollinators.
