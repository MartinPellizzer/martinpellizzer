Balsamina macrochila

Balsamina macrochila, commonly known as the giant balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive orange or yellow throat. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Balsamina macrochila is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, which includes several species with similar floral structures.
