Boesenbergia anomala

Boesenbergia anomala, commonly known as the yellow ginger or Malabar ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant produces narrow, sword-shaped leaves and bears small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
