Bidens dondiifolia

Bidens dondiifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
