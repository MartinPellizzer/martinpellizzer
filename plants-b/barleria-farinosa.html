Barleria farinosa

Barleria farinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sandbells, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Barleria farinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
