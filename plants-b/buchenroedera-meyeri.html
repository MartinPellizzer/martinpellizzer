Buchenroedera meyeri

Buchenroedera meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a unique morphology, with a slender, hairless stem and a single, showy inflorescence. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in shaded, moist environments with rich, organic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
