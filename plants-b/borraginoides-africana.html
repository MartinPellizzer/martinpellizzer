Borraginoides africana

Borraginoides africana, commonly known as African bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Borraginoides africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
