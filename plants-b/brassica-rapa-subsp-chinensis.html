Brassica rapa subsp. chinensis

Brassica rapa subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese mustard or napa cabbage, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, leafy stems that form a compact head, often used in Asian cuisine.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are edible and can be used raw or cooked, contributing to its culinary versatility.

It is cultivated for both its leaves and its seed pods, which are also used in various dishes.
