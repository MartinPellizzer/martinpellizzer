Bromus mucroglumis

Bromus mucroglumis, commonly known as the sharp-tipped brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets with awns, which are bristle-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture.

Bromus mucroglumis is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
