Bureava crotonoides

Bureava crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its bark is thick and corky, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Bureava crotonoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
