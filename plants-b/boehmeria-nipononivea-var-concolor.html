Boehmeria nipononivea var. concolor

Boehmeria nipononivea var. concolor, commonly known as the Japanese nettle or broadleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its strong fibers, which have been used historically in textile production.
