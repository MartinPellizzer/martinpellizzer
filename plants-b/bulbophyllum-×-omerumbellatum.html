Bulbophyllum × omerumbellatum

Bulbophyllum × omerumbellatum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a unique hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence is usually branched, with numerous small to medium-sized blooms that contribute to its ornamental value.

The hybrid was likely developed through crossbreeding between two parent species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Bulbophyllum genus.
