Beta vulgaris var. globosiflava

Beta vulgaris var. globosiflava, commonly known as the ornamental chard, is a cultivar of the beet plant characterized by its vibrant, showy leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is primarily grown for its attractive foliage rather than its roots. The leaves come in a variety of colors, including red, white, yellow, and purple, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is easy to grow from seeds and can be used in both garden beds and container plantings.
