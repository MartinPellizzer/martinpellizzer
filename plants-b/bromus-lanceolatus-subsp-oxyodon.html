Bromus lanceolatus subsp. oxyodon

Bromus lanceolatus subsp.

oxyodon, commonly known as the oxyodon brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike with short, dense spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
