Berberis × crenulata

Berberis × crenulata, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its attractive, spiny stems and compound leaves.

It features bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed in natural habitats.
