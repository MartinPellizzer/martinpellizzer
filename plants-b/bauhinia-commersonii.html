Bauhinia commersonii

Bauhinia commersonii, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Bauhinia commersonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
