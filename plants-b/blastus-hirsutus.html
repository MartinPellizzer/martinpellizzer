Blastus hirsutus

Blastus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy blastus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide protection against herbivores and environmental stress. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
