Bromus firmior var. leiorhachis

Bromus firmior var. leiorhachis, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
