Bauhinia divergens

Bauhinia divergens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Bauhinia divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant floral display.
