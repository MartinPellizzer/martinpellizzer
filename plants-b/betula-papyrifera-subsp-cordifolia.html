Betula papyrifera subsp. cordifolia

Betula papyrifera subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to North America. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and is known for its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas across the northern United States and Canada.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
