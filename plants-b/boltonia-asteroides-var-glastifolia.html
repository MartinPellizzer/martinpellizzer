Boltonia asteroides var. glastifolia

Boltonia asteroides var. glastifolia, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has tall, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
