Bignonia dasyonyx

Bignonia dasyonyx, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia dasyonyx is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 20 feet in length, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
