Brachylaena discolor var. mossambicensis

Brachylaena discolor var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid regions.
