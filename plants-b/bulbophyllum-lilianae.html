Bulbophyllum lilianae

Bulbophyllum lilianae, commonly known as the Lillian's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, often fragrant blooms that can range in color from white to yellow. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum lilianae is typically grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, requiring specific humidity and light conditions to thrive.
