Briza media subvar. glomerulosa

Briza media subvar. glomerulosa, commonly known as the tufted feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
