Bignonia amara

Bignonia amara, commonly known as the bitterbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Bignonia amara is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
