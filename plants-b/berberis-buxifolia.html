Berberis buxifolia

Berberis buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that resemble those of boxwood. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
