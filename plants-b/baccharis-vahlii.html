Baccharis vahlii

Baccharis vahlii, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis vahlii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
