Begonia diffusiflora

Begonia diffusiflora, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
