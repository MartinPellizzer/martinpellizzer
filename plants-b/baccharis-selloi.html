Baccharis selloi

Baccharis selloi, commonly known as the Sello's baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Baccharis selloi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
