Bromopsis pindica

Bromopsis pindica, commonly known as the Pindus grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Bromopsis pindica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
