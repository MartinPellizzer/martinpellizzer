Boronia ramosa var. anethifolia

Boronia ramosa var. anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle anise-like scent when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils across southeastern Australia.
