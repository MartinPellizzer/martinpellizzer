Bistorta hayachinensis

Bistorta hayachinensis, commonly known as Japanese bistort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and medicinal properties. The plant features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a prominent midrib and are covered in fine hairs.

Bistorta hayachinensis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
