Barbarea parviflora

Barbarea parviflora, commonly known as the lesser wintercress, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has pinnately divided leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Barbarea parviflora is known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
