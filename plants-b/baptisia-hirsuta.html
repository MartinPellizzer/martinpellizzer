Baptisia hirsuta

Baptisia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Baptisia hirsuta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
