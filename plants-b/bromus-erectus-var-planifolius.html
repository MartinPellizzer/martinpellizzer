Bromus erectus var. planifolius

Bromus erectus var. planifolius, commonly known as the flatleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned spikelets.

Bromus erectus var. planifolius is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both dry and moist conditions.
