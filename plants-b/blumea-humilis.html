Blumea humilis

Blumea humilis, commonly known as the lesser blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
