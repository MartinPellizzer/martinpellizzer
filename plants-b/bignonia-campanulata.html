Bignonia campanulata

Bignonia campanulata, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
