Berberis kangwuensis

Berberis kangwuensis, commonly known as the Kangwu barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
