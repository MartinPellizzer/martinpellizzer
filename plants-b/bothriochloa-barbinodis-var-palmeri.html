Bothriochloa barbinodis var. palmeri

Bothriochloa barbinodis var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bermudagrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
