Baissea calophylla

Baissea calophylla, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom profusely during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Baissea calophylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
