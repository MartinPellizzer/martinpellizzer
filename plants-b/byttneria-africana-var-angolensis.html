Byttneria africana var. angolensis

Byttneria africana var. angolensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and carving.
