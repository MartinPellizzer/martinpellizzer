Begonia cleopatrae

Begonia cleopatrae, commonly known as Cleopatra's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
