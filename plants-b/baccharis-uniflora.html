Baccharis uniflora

Baccharis uniflora, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a common species in xeriscaped gardens.
