Berberis xanthoclada

Berberis xanthoclada, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often sour. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various garden settings.
