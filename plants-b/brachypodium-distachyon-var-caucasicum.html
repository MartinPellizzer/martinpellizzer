Brachypodium distachyon var. caucasicum

Brachypodium distachyon var. caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian bristlegrass, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing two-flowered spikelets, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its genetic similarity to Brachypodium distachyon, it is also studied as a model organism in plant genetics and molecular biology research.
