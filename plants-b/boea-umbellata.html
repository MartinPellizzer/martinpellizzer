Boea umbellata

Boea umbellata, commonly known as the hanging lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Boea umbellata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or containers.
