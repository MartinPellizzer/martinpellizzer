Blumea integrifolia var. diffusa

Blumea integrifolia var. diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually entire, with a smooth margin, and the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in capitula.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
