Blepharis edulis var. gracilis

Blepharis edulis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained sandy soils. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
