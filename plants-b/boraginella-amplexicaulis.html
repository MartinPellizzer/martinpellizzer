Boraginella amplexicaulis

Boraginella amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bugloss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant has distinctive leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
