Bulbophyllum tridentatum

Bulbophyllum tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are typically dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This orchid is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
