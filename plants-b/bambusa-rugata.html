Bambusa rugata

Bambusa rugata, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms that are often covered with a fine, silky sheen. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a dark green color, providing a striking contrast to the silver stems. It is relatively cold-hardy compared to other bamboo species, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Bambusa rugata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and manageable growth habit.
