Brugmansia insignis

Brugmansia insignis, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant blossoms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Brugmansia insignis is a member of the Nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
