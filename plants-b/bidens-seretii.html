Bidens seretii

Bidens seretii, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in groups of three, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bidens seretii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
