Barbacenia chlorantha

Barbacenia chlorantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Barbacenia chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
