Begonia grandis

Begonia grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Begonia grandis is popular as a houseplant and in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
