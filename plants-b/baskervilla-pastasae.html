Baskervilla pastasae

Baskervilla pastasae, commonly known as the pasta vine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, edible berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its name is believed to be a nod to its culinary appeal, as the berries can be used in various food preparations.
