Benzoin obovatum

Benzoin obovatum, commonly known as the benzoin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves and can grow up to 20 meters tall. The plant produces a fragrant resin, known as benzoin, which is harvested from the bark and is used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish-brown trunk and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
