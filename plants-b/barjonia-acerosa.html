Barjonia acerosa

Barjonia acerosa, commonly known as the rigid barjonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Barjonia acerosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
