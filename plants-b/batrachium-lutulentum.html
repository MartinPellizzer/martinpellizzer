Batrachium lutulentum

Batrachium lutulentum, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments. Its roots system is adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in swampy areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
