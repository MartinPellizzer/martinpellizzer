Bridelia squamosa var. meeboldii

Bridelia squamosa var. meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in umbels, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
