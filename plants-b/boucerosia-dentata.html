Boucerosia dentata

Boucerosia dentata, commonly known as the toothed bouceros, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Boucerosia dentata is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.
