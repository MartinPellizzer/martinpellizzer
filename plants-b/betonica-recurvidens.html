Betonica recurvidens

Betonica recurvidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Betonica recurvidens is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

It has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
