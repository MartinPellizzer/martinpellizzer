Bactris acanthospatha

Bactris acanthospatha, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces yellow-orange inflorescences with both male and female flowers, and its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and use in traditional crafts, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
