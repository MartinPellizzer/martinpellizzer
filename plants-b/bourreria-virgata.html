Bourreria virgata

Bourreria virgata, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and hairy.

Bourreria virgata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
