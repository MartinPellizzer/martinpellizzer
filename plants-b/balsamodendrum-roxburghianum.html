Balsamodendrum roxburghianum

Balsamodendrum roxburghianum, commonly known as the Himalayan balsam, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its bark is aromatic and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Balsamodendrum roxburghianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
