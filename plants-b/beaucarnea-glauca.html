Beaucarnea glauca

Beaucarnea glauca, commonly known as the blue elephant tree, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted, and often gnarled trunk, which resembles the trunk of an elephant. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are thick, fleshy, and typically appear only after the plant has reached a certain maturity.

Beaucarnea glauca is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
