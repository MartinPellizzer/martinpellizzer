Breweria roxburghii

Breweria roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

Breweria roxburghii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
