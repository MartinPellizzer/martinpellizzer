Berberis jamesonii

Berberis jamesonii, commonly known as American barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it useful in landscaping and for erosion control.
