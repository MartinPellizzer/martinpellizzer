Benzoin setchuenense

Benzoin setchuenense, commonly known as the Sichuan benzoin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fragrant, resinous capsules that exude a balsamic scent.

The resin, known as benzoin, is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in incense and perfumes.
