Bignonia quadrilocularis

Bignonia quadrilocularis, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of yellow, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Bignonia quadrilocularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
