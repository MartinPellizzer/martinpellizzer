Bulbophyllum idenburgense

Bulbophyllum idenburgense, commonly known as the Idenburg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that attract pollinators.

The orchid's pseudobulbs serve as storage organs, helping it survive in its often harsh and variable environment.
