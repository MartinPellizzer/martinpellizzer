Brathys thuyoides

Brathys thuyoides, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, which helps the plant conserve water in its native arid environment.
