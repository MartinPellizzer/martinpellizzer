Brachystegia spiciformis var. latifoliolata

Brachystegia spiciformis var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in miombo woodlands, where it plays a significant ecological role.

This species is also valued for its timber and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
