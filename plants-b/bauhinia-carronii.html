Bauhinia carronii

Bauhinia carronii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bi-lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria also contributes to soil fertility.
