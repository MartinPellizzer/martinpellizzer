Bidens menziesii var. filiformis

Bidens menziesii var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender false beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairless stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its seeds are small and covered in bristly hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
