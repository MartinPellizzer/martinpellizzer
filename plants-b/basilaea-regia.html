Basilaea regia

Basilaea regia, commonly known as the royal basil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small purple or white flowers. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Basilaea regia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
