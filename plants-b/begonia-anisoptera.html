Begonia anisoptera

Begonia anisoptera, commonly known as the veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, veined leaves that often display a striking contrast between the upper and lower surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Begonia anisoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
