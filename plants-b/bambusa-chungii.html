Bambusa chungii

Bambusa chungii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands rather than spreading aggressively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa chungii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
