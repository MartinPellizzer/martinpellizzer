Bromus porphyranthos

Bromus porphyranthos, commonly known as the purple-flowered brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and purple-tinged inflorescences, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus porphyranthos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
