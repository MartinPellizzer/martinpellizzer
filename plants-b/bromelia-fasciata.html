Bromelia fasciata

Bromelia fasciata, commonly known as the spotted bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, elongated leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bromelia fasciata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
