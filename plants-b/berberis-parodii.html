Berberis parodii

Berberis parodii, commonly known as the Parodi barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
