Bergia arenarioides

Bergia arenarioides, commonly known as the sandbergia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Bergia arenarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
