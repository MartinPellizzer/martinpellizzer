Barkhausia taraxacifolia var. intybacea

Barkhausia taraxacifolia var. intybacea, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
