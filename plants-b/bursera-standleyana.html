Bursera standleyana

Bursera standleyana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often leathery in texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

Bursera standleyana is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine for its resinous sap.
