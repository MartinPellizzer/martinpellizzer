Bifora dicocca subsp. radians

Bifora dicocca subsp.

radians, commonly known as the radiating starflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal regions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
