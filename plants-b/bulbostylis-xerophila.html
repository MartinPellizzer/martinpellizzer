Bulbostylis xerophila

Bulbostylis xerophila, commonly known as the desert bulb grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous base, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base. It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
