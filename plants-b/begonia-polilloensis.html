Begonia polilloensis

Begonia polilloensis, commonly known as the Polillo begonia, is a striking flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the mountainous regions of the Polillo Islands. Its foliage is usually glossy and dark green, with leaves that can be heart-shaped or ovate in form.

Begonia polilloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
