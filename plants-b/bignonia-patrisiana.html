Bignonia patrisiana

Bignonia patrisiana, commonly known as the Florida trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

Bignonia patrisiana is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands.
