Balsamina fasciculata

Balsamina fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and branching.

Balsamina fasciculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
