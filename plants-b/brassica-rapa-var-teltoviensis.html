Brassica rapa var. teltoviensis

Brassica rapa var. teltoviensis, commonly known as the Teltovian mustard, is a wild Brassica species native to Central Asia.

It is a biennial or annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape depending on the growth conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
