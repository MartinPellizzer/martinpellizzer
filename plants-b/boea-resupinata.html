Boea resupinata

Boea resupinata, commonly known as the hanging lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has trailing stems that can be trained to grow along supports or allowed to cascade from containers. Its foliage is heart-shaped and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Boea resupinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
