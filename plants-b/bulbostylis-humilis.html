Bulbostylis humilis

Bulbostylis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
