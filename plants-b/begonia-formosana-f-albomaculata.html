Begonia formosana f. albomaculata

Begonia formosana f. albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted begonia, is a unique cultivar of the begonia genus native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a deep green base with distinct white or light green spots. The plant typically produces clusters of small, vibrant pink or red flowers during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
