Biglandularia

Biglandularia, commonly known as the hairy bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and inflated, pod-like fruits that resemble small bladders. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in regions of western North America. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces numerous seeds within its spongy pods.

Biglandularia is an annual plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
