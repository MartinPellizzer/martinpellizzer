Blumea axillaris

Blumea axillaris, commonly known as the axillary blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Blumea axillaris is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
