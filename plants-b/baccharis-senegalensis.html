Baccharis senegalensis

Baccharis senegalensis, commonly known as African feverfew, is a shrub native to Africa and parts of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant's adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
