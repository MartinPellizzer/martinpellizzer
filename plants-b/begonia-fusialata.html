Begonia fusialata

Begonia fusialata, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Begonia fusialata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and ease of care.
