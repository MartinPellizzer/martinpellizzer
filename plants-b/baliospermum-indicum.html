Baliospermum indicum

Baliospermum indicum, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsules that release small, hairy seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
