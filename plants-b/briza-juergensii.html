Briza juergensii

Briza juergensii, commonly known as the tufted feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an effective erosion control plant.

Briza juergensii is also valued in ornamental horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
