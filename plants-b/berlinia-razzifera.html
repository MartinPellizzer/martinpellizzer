Berlinia razzifera

Berlinia razzifera, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
