Blyxa griffithii

Blyxa griffithii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Blyxa griffithii plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
