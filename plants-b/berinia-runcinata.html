Berinia runcinata

Berinia runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Berinia runcinata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, making it a characteristic plant of Mediterranean scrublands.
