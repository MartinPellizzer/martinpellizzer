Bryanthus × erectus

Bryanthus × erectus, commonly known as the upright bryanthus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
