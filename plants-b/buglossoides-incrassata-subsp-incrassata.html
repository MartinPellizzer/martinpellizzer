Buglossoides incrassata subsp. incrassata

Buglossoides incrassata subsp.

incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
