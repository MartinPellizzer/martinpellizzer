Bulbophyllum planiaxe

Bulbophyllum planiaxe, commonly known as the flat-axile orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum planiaxe is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
