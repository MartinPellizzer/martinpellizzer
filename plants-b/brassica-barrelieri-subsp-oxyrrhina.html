Brassica barrelieri subsp. oxyrrhina

Brassica barrelieri subsp.

oxyrrhina, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has serrated leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
