Bambusa glaucescens var. annulata

Bambusa glaucescens var. annulata, commonly known as the bamboo with annular markings, is a striking ornamental bamboo species characterized by its distinctive blue-green culms with alternating light and dark bands.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are highly resistant to wind and drought. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green and provide a lush appearance throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique visual appeal and hardy nature make it a favored plant for both garden design and ecological applications.
