Beta sativa

Beta sativa, commonly known as beetroot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically red in color. The plant produces small, green, spade-shaped leaves that are edible. Its scientific name, Beta sativa, reflects its historical use in cultivation for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant is well-adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
