Buddleja heliophila var. adenophora

Buddleja heliophila var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花 Buddleja, is a perennial shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
