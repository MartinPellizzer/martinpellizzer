Bifrenaria aurea

Bifrenaria aurea, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, which is crucial for its survival.

Bifrenaria aurea is known for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
