Boronella verticillata

Boronella verticillata, commonly known as the clustered boronella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often form low, spreading mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in whorls around the stems.

Boronella verticillata is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
