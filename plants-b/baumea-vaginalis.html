Baumea vaginalis

Baumea vaginalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf baumea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories.

Baumea vaginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its delicate appearance.
