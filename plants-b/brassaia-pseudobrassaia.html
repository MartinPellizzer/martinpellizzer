Brassaia pseudobrassaia

Brassaia pseudobrassaia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a nuisance in certain regions.
