Brachanthemum krylovii

Brachanthemum krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's bush daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, adapted to arid conditions.

Brachanthemum krylovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
