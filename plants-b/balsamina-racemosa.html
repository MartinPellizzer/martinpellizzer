Balsamina racemosa

Balsamina racemosa, commonly known as the garden balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in racemes, which are clusters of flowers along a central stem.

Balsamina racemosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
