Butea merguensis

Butea merguensis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets.

Butea merguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
