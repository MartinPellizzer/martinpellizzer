Beloglottis boliviensis

Beloglottis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian spider orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This terrestrial herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. It features a rhizomatous stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant's unique morphology includes a fleshy, leaf-like structure called a "spathe" that encloses the inflorescence.

Beloglottis boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
