Buddleja aurantiacomaculata

Buddleja aurantiacomaculata, commonly known as the orange-spotted buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, clustered flowers that range from pale yellow to orange-red, with distinctive orange spots. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are medium green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Buddleja aurantiacomaculata is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and bees, contributing to pollinator habitats.
