Begonia sendangensis

Begonia sendangensis, commonly known as the Sendang begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers consist of five petals that are slightly curled at the edges.

This begonia is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
