Barleria bituminosa

Barleria bituminosa, commonly known as sticky barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with a sticky resinous substance.

Barleria bituminosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
