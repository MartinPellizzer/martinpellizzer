Begonia tricornis

Begonia tricornis, commonly known as the three-lobed begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
