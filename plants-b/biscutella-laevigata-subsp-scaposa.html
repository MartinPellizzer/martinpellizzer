Biscutella laevigata subsp. scaposa

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

scaposa, commonly known as the slender biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and are borne in clusters, characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
