Bouteloua racemosa

Bouteloua racemosa, commonly known as grama grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble a raceme. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and prairies. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and is often used in restoration projects.

Its flowers are typically pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
