Brahea

Brahea, commonly known as the blue agave, is a genus of flowering plants in the asparagus family.

These succulent plants are native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. They are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Brahea species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are adapted to arid environments.

They produce a tall flower stalk that bears small, white or yellow flowers.
