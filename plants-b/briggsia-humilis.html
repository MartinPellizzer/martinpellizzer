Briggsia humilis

Briggsia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique floral structure, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
