Bouea oppositifolia var. microphylla

Bouea oppositifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spiny fruits resembling small, green soursop. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, flavorful fruit.
