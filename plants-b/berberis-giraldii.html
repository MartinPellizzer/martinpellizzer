Berberis giraldii

Berberis giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or well-drained soils.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
