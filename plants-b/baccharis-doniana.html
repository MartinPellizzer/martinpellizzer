Baccharis doniana

Baccharis doniana, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating fever and inflammation.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
