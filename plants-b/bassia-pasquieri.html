Bassia pasquieri

Bassia pasquieri, commonly known as Pasquier's wormwood, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its unique morphology includes a sprawling growth habit and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Bassia pasquieri is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
