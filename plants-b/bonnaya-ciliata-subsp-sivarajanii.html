Bonnaya ciliata subsp. sivarajanii

Bonnaya ciliata subsp.

sivarajanii, commonly known as the Sivarajan's bonnaya, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
