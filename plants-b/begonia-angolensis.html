Begonia angolensis

Begonia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola begonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to dark purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Begonia angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
