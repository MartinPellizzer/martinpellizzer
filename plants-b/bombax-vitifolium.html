Bombax vitifolium

Bombax vitifolium, commonly known as the white silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
