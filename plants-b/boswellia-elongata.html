Boswellia elongata

Boswellia elongata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, fragrant resinous exudate. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by pale green or yellowish fruits containing three winged seeds. Its resin, known as frankincense, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and religious rituals.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
