Boesenbergia armeniaca

Boesenbergia armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often used in traditional medicine.

The rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are sometimes used in culinary applications.
