Begonia pulcherrima

Begonia pulcherrima, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and homes.
