Botryanthus commutatus var. lafarinae

Botryanthus commutatus var. lafarinae, commonly known as the Farina's bottlebrush, is a rare and unique plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bottle-shaped stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that resemble a bottlebrush, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
