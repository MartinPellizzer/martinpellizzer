Bromus intermedius var. hughii

Bromus intermedius var. hughii, commonly known as the Hugh's intermediate brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and glabrous, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
