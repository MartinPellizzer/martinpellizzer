Berberis praecipua var. major

Berberis praecipua var. major, commonly known as the large thorned barberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, spiny branches and small, rounded leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and erosion control in coastal regions.
