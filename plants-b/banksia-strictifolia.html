Banksia strictifolia

Banksia strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody flower spikes that produce abundant yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Banksia strictifolia is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for honeyeaters and other pollinators.
