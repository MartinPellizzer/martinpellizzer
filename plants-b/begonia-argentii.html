Begonia argentii

Begonia argentii, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
