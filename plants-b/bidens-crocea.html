Bidens crocea

Bidens crocea, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Bidens crocea is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
