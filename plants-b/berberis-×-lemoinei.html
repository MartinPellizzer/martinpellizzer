Berberis × lemoinei

Berberis × lemoinei, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, spiny stems and foliage that features a mix of green, gold, and white variegation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
