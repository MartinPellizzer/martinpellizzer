Bupleurum perfoliatum var. longifolium

Bupleurum perfoliatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive elongated shape. Small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.
