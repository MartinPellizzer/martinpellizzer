Brassica violacea

Brassica violacea, commonly known as the violet mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, purple or violet flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Brassica violacea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flower color and compact growth habit.
