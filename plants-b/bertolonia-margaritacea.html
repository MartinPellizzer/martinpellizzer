Bertolonia margaritacea

Bertolonia margaritacea, commonly known as the pearlflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble pearls, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Bertolonia margaritacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
