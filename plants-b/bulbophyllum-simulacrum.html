Bulbophyllum simulacrum

Bulbophyllum simulacrum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant produces striking, often colorful flowers that resemble other plants, a trait that gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small to medium-sized flowers arranged in a spike.

Bulbophyllum simulacrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral morphology.
