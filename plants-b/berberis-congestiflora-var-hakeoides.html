Berberis congestiflora var. hakeoides

Berberis congestiflora var. hakeoides, commonly known as the Hakea-like barberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its berries are red and edible, though they are often consumed by birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
