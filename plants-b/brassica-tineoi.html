Brassica tineoi

Brassica tineoi, commonly known as the Tineo's mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes. After flowering, it produces elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds.

Brassica tineoi is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
