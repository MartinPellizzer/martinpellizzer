Buceragenia glandulosa

Buceragenia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Buceragenia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
