Bulbophyllum wrightii

Bulbophyllum wrightii, commonly known as Wright's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers and is part of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, tropical environments.

The flowers of Bulbophyllum wrightii are usually small and clustered, displaying a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.
