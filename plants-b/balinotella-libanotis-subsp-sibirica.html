Balinotella libanotis subsp. sibirica

Balinotella libanotis subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, temperate climates.

The plant features slender, upright stems and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
