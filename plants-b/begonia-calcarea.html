Begonia calcarea

Begonia calcarea, commonly known as the limestone begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers partial shade.

Begonia calcarea is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
