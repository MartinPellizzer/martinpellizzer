Bignonia coito

Bignonia coito, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia coito is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
