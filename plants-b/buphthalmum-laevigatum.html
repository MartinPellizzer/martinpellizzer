Buphthalmum laevigatum

Buphthalmum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-eyed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy coating and a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming from late summer to autumn.
