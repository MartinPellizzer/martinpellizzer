Buddleja ledifolia

Buddleja ledifolia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buddleja ledifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
