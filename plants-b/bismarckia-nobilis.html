Bismarckia nobilis

Bismarckia nobilis, commonly known as the African giant lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem with prominent, waxy leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its botanical structure includes a bulbous base that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The flowers are arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster, making it a dramatic and eye-catching addition to gardens.
