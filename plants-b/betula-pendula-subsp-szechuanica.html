Betula pendula subsp. szechuanica

Betula pendula subsp.

szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan weeping birch, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its slender, pendulous branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance.

The bark is smooth and typically a pale grayish-white color, becoming fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
