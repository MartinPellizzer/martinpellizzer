Brachypodium pinnatum subsp. ligulatum

Brachypodium pinnatum subsp.

ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a degree of drought tolerance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, which are often tightly clustered.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological distinctiveness and ecological adaptability.
