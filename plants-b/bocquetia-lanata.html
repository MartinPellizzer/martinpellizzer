Bocquetia lanata

Bocquetia lanata, commonly known as the woolly bocquetia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Bocquetia lanata is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
