Blechnum organense

Blechnum organense, commonly known as the organensis holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, oval-shaped pinnae.

Blechnum organense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its lush, foliage.
