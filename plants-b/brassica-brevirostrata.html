Brassica brevirostrata

Brassica brevirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-beaked mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
