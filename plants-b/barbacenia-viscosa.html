Barbacenia viscosa

Barbacenia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Barbacenia viscosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
