Brassica chinensis var. dentata

Brassica chinensis var. dentata, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green stalks and clustered, tender leaves that are often eaten raw or cooked. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassica genus.
