Bulbophyllum bielecki

Bulbophyllum bielecki, commonly known as the Bielecki's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in conserving water.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
