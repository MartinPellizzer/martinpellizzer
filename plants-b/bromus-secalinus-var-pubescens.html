Bromus secalinus var. pubescens

Bromus secalinus var. pubescens, commonly known as pubescent barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often bracted.

This variant is often considered a weed in cultivated areas but is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
