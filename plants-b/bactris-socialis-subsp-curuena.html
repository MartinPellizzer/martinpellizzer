Bactris socialis subsp. curuena

Bactris socialis subsp.

curuena, commonly known as the Curuena palm, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits. Its botanical features include a fibrous, segmented stem and a robust root system adapted to wet, tropical environments.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential economic uses.
