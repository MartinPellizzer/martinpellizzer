Biasolettia bornmuelleri

Biasolettia bornmuelleri, commonly known as the African cycad, is a species of cycad native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ecological importance, it is often found in natural habitats and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
