Baeria chrysostoma var. gracilis

Baeria chrysostoma var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow bellflower, is a variant of the yellow bellflower species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
