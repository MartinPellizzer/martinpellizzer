Begonia alice-clarkiae

Begonia alice-clarkiae, commonly known as the Alice Clark's begonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the tropical rainforests of Ecuador.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and intricate patterns, often with a reddish or bronze hue on the underside. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
