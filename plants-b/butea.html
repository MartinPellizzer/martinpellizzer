Butea

Butea, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of South and Southeast Asia, including India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. Butea species are often found in dry, open habitats and are adapted to arid conditions.

The leaves are typically compound, and the plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous membrane.
