Bauhinia gracilipes

Bauhinia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia gracilipes is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
