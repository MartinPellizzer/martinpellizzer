Baconia corymbosa

Baconia corymbosa, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical corymbs, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance.

Baconia corymbosa is well-suited for gardens and containers, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
