Begonia semiparietalis

Begonia semiparietalis, commonly known as the semi-parietal begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves that are partially attached to the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia semiparietalis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and manageable growth habit.
