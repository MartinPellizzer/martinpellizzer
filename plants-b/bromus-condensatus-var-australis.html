Bromus condensatus var. australis

Bromus condensatus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dense brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces dense spikes of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
