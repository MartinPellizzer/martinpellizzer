Burtonia villosa

Burtonia villosa, commonly known as the hairy burtonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often pink or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Burtonia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
