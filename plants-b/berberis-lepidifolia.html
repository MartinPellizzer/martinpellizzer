Berberis lepidifolia

Berberis lepidifolia, commonly known as the prickly barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Berberis lepidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
