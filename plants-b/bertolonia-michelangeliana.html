Bertolonia michelangeliana

Bertolonia michelangeliana, commonly known as Michelangelo's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
