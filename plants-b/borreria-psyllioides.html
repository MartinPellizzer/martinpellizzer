Borreria psyllioides

Borreria psyllioides, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in arid and semi-arid regions.

It has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host plants.
