Basella

Basella, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, edible leaves and stems. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are used in various cuisines.

Basella is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with variegated leaves that add visual interest to gardens.
