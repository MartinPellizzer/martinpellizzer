Boea glabra

Boea glabra, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Boea glabra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
