Bambusa falcata

Bambusa falcata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or falcate bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The leaves are long, narrow, and often have a yellowish-green color, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in construction and crafts in its native regions.
