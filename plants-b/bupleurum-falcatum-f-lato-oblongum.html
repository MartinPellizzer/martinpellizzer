Bupleurum falcatum f. lato-oblongum

Bupleurum falcatum f. lato-oblongum, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a desirable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
