Bulbophyllum warmingianum

Bulbophyllum warmingianum, commonly known as the Warmington's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a lithophytic orchid, meaning it grows on rocks rather than in soil, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate patterns, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
