Bidens tripartita subsp. hirta

Bidens tripartita subsp.

hirta, commonly known as hirta false beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in swampy areas.

The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its fruit is a schizocarp composed of three parts, each containing a single seed.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family.
