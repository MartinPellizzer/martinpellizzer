Blumea sylvatica var. macrophylla

Blumea sylvatica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf blumea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
