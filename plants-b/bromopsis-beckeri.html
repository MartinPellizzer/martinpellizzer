Bromopsis beckeri

Bromopsis beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Bromopsis beckeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
