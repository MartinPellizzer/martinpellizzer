Buxus foliosa

Buxus foliosa, commonly known as the leafy boxwood, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. Buxus foliosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ability to be shaped into various forms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
