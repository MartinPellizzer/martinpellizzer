Brassica oleracea convar. capitata

Brassica oleracea convar. capitata, commonly known as cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that form a tight head. The plant produces a terminal flowering head composed of numerous small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and flowering heads, which vary in size and texture depending on the variety.

The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and is a staple in many cuisines worldwide.
