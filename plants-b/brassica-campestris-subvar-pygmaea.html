Brassica campestris subvar. pygmaea

Brassica campestris subvar. pygmaea, commonly known as field mustard, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and seed pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This subspecies is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
