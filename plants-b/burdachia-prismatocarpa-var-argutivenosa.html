Burdachia prismatocarpa var. argutivenosa

Burdachia prismatocarpa var. argutivenosa, commonly known as the prickly burdachia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures, aiding in water conservation.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
