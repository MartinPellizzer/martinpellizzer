Bromus krausei

Bromus krausei, commonly known as the Krause's brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is often found in areas with moderate to high sunlight exposure.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
