Brickellia venosa

Brickellia venosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, tubular shape.

Brickellia venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
