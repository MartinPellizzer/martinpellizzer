Brunfelsia bonodora

Brunfelsia bonodora, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Brunfelsia bonodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
