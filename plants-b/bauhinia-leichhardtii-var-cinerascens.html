Bauhinia leichhardtii var. cinerascens

Bauhinia leichhardtii var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The varietal form, cinerascens, is distinguished by its pale, ash-gray foliage.
