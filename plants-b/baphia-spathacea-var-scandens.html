Baphia spathacea var. scandens

Baphia spathacea var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing baphia, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
