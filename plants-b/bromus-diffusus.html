Bromus diffusus

Bromus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and somewhat hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike or multiple spikes, each bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Bromus diffusus is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
