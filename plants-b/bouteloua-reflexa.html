Bouteloua reflexa

Bouteloua reflexa, commonly known as gray beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to grayish seed heads that are distinctive in appearance. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Bouteloua reflexa is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
