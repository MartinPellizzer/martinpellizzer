Balantium culcita

Balantium culcita, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Balantium culcita is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
