Bromus erectus var. glaucus

Bromus erectus var. glaucus, commonly known as the bluegrass brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that mature into seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
