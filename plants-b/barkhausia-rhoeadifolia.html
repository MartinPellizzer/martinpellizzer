Barkhausia rhoeadifolia

Barkhausia rhoeadifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Barkhausia rhoeadifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
