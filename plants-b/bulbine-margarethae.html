Bulbine margarethae

Bulbine margarethae, commonly known as the margaret's bulbine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Bulbine, which is part of the family Xanthorrhoeaceae. The plant features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically thick and grass-like, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
