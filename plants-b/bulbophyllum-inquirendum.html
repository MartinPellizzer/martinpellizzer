Bulbophyllum inquirendum

Bulbophyllum inquirendum, commonly known as the seeking orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its bulbs, which are modified stems, store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
