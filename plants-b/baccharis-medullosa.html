Baccharis medullosa

Baccharis medullosa, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis medullosa is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
