Bryanthus intermedius

Bryanthus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate bryanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
