Bulbophyllum cambodianum

Bulbophyllum cambodianum, commonly known as the Cambodian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
