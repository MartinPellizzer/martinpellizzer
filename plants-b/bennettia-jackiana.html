Bennettia jackiana

Bennettia jackiana, commonly known as the Jack's bennettia, is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 15 meters in length, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species for studying tropical plant adaptations and biodiversity.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
