Berkheya speciosa subsp. ovata

Berkheya speciosa subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of the oxalis genus, hence its common name.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
