Boerhavia verticillata var. repanda

Boerhavia verticillata var. repanda, commonly known as redroot sandplum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
