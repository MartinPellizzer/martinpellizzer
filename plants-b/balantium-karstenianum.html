Balantium karstenianum

Balantium karstenianum, commonly known as the Karsten's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a dense, leafy crown and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar that attracts pollinators such as birds and insects. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that consume the berries, facilitating its propagation.

Balantium karstenianum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to biodiversity through its interactions with host trees and wildlife.
