Bletia villae

Bletia villae, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Bletia villae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
