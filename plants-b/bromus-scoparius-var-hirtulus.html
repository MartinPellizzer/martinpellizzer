Bromus scoparius var. hirtulus

Bromus scoparius var. hirtulus, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Bromus scoparius var. hirtulus is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
