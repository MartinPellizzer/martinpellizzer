Bouteloua curtipendula var. caespitosa

Bouteloua curtipendula var. caespitosa, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish to white seed heads that are highly visible during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
