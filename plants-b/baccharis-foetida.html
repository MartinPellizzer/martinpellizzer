Baccharis foetida

Baccharis foetida, commonly known as stink bush or sweet-smelling thorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thorny stems and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
