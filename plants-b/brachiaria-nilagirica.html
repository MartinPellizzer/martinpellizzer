Brachiaria nilagirica

Brachiaria nilagirica, commonly known as the Nilgiri grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria nilagirica is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
