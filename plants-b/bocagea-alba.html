Bocagea alba

Bocagea alba, commonly known as the white bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bocagea alba is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
