Bulbophyllum evansii

Bulbophyllum evansii, commonly known as the Evans' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bulbophyllum evansii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
