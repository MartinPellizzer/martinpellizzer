Baccharis grazielae

Baccharis grazielae, commonly known as the Argentine baccharis, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, white to yellowish seed heads.

Baccharis grazielae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
