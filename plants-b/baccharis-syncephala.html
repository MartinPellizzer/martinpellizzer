Baccharis syncephala

Baccharis syncephala, commonly known as whitebrush baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a woody stem with branching patterns that support its compact growth form.

Baccharis syncephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
