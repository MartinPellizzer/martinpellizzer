Bellis rotundifolia proles pumila

Bellis rotundifolia proles pumila, commonly known as the lesser oxeye daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow central disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has rounded, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
