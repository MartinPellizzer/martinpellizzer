Begonia coriacea

Begonia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental flowers, it is popular among plant enthusiasts and gardeners.
