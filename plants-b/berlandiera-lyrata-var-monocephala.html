Berlandiera lyrata var. monocephala

Berlandiera lyrata var. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has linear, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its lyrical flower shape and the single head of flowers characteristic of this variety.
