Bergenia delavayi

Bergenia delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and basal rosettes of thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Bergenia delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
