Brassica havardii

Brassica havardii, commonly known as the Havard's mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Brassica havardii is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
