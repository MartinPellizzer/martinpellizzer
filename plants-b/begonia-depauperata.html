Begonia depauperata

Begonia depauperata, commonly known as the ghost begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly veils, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle yet elegant touch to its appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden settings.
