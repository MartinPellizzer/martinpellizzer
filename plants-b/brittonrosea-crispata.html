Brittonrosea crispata

Brittonrosea crispata, commonly known as the brittlestem rose.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.
