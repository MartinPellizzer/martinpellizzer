Buxus sempervirens f. microrrhina

Buxus sempervirens f. microrrhina, commonly known as the miniature boxwood, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub prized for its dense, finely textured foliage.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a uniform dark green, providing a lush, ornamental appearance. This variety is particularly valued in horticulture for its use in topiary and formal garden designs due to its ability to maintain a neat, rounded shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.
