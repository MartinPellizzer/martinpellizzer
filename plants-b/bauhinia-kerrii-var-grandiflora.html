Bauhinia kerrii var. grandiflora

Bauhinia kerrii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a grayish appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
