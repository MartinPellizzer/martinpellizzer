Baccharis dunensis

Baccharis dunensis, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep roots that help it access underground water. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis dunensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
