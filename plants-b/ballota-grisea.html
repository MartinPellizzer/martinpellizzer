Ballota grisea

Ballota grisea, commonly known as gray ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Ballota grisea is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
