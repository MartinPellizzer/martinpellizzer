Brachypodium sylvaticum var. pilosum

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy wood rush, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinct hairy texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
