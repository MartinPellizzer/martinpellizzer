Briquetia sonorae

Briquetia sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran briquetia, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife species. Briquetia sonorae is well adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert flora and fauna.
