Brassica elongata var. pinnatifida

Brassica elongata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or long mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pinnately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive mustard scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing round, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
