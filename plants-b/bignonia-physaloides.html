Bignonia physaloides

Bignonia physaloides, commonly known as the crown vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia physaloides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
