Buddleja skutchii

Buddleja skutchii, commonly known as the Skutch's bushmaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Buddleja genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Buddleja skutchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
