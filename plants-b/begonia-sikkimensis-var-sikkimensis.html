Begonia sikkimensis var. sikkimensis

Begonia sikkimensis var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with a short, woody stem and produces showy bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
