Brassica subspinescens

Brassica subspinescens, commonly known as the spiny mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.

Brassica subspinescens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
