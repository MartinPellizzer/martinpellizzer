Bauhinia tenella

Bauhinia tenella, commonly known as the small-leaf bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its showy, pink or purple flowers bloom in clusters and are often followed by flat, papery pods.

Bauhinia tenella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
