Bromus thominei

Bromus thominei, commonly known as Thomine's brome, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile, where it grows in dry, open habitats. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces spikelets that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Bromus thominei is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability to arid environments.
