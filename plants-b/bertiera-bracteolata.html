Bertiera bracteolata

Bertiera bracteolata, commonly known as the red-flowered bertiera, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Bertiera bracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
