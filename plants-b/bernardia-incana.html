Bernardia incana

Bernardia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas.

Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
