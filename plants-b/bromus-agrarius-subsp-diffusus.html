Bromus agrarius subsp. diffusus

Bromus agrarius subsp.

diffusus, commonly known as the spreading annual brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become a weed in various ecosystems.
