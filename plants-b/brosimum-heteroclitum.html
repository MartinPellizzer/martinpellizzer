Brosimum heteroclitum

Brosimum heteroclitum, commonly known as the wild fig or red fig, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and unique, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
