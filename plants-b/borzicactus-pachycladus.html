Borzicactus pachycladus

Borzicactus pachycladus, commonly known as the woolly cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its shallow root system allows it to quickly absorb rainwater, making it well adapted to extreme drought conditions.

Despite its hardy nature, it requires well-draining soil and minimal watering to thrive.
