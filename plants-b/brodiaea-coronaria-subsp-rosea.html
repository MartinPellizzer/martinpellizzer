Brodiaea coronaria subsp. rosea

Brodiaea coronaria subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the roseate brodiaea, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of showy, rose-pink to lavender flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
