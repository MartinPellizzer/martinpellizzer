Blumea venkataramanii

Blumea venkataramanii, commonly known as the Indian blue mistflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Blumea venkataramanii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
