Blastus borneensis var. eberhardtii

Blastus borneensis var. eberhardtii, commonly known as the Bornean blastus, is a unique variant of the Blastus genus native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is usually glossy and dark green, with lance-shaped leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
