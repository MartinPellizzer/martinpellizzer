Bignonia elliptica

Bignonia elliptica, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and can grow as a vine or shrub, often reaching heights of several meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
