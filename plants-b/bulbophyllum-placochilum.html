Bulbophyllum placochilum

Bulbophyllum placochilum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or orange lip.

Bulbophyllum placochilum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
