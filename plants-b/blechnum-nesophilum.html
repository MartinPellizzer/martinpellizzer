Blechnum nesophilum

Blechnum nesophilum, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its fronds are leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori that house the spores.

Blechnum nesophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
