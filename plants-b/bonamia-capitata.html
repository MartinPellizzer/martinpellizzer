Bonamia capitata

Bonamia capitata, commonly known as the bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal areas.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
