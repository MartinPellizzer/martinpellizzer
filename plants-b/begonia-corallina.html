Begonia corallina

Begonia corallina, commonly known as coral begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-like inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia corallina is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
