Blumea wightiana var. cuneiformis

Blumea wightiana var. cuneiformis, commonly known as the cuneiform blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
