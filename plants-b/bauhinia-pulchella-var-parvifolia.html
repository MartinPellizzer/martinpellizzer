Bauhinia pulchella var. parvifolia

Bauhinia pulchella var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
