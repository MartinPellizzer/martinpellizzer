Bulbophyllum tumidum

Bulbophyllum tumidum, commonly known as the Fat Lip Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy pseudobulbs and hairy, leathery leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
