Blechnum discolor

Blechnum discolor, commonly known as the variable fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a variegated appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like structure.

Blechnum discolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
