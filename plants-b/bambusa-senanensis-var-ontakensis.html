Bambusa senanensis var. ontakensis

Bambusa senanensis var. ontakensis, commonly known as the Ontake bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, upright manner. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in construction and handicrafts.
