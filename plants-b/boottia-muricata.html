Boottia muricata

Boottia muricata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Boottia muricata plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
