Briza stricta

Briza stricta, commonly known as the rigid feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear feathery, silver-green inflorescences, which are distinctive and showy. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.

Briza stricta is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
