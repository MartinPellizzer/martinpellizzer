Bougainvillea praecox

Bougainvillea praecox, commonly known as the early flowering bougainvillea, is a vibrant, flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, colorful bracts that surround small, white flowers, often displaying shades of pink, red, or orange. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters tall, with woody stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Bougainvillea praecox is known for its long blooming season, which can last from late spring through early autumn.
