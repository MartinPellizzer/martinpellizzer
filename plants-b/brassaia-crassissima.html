Brassaia crassissima

Brassaia crassissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassaia crassissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
