Bernardia confertifolia

Bernardia confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered bernardia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Bernardia confertifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
