Berberis macrophylla

Berberis macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
