Bulbophyllum longidens

Bulbophyllum longidens, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spurred petals and dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
