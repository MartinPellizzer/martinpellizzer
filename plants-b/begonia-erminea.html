Begonia erminea

Begonia erminea, commonly known as the silver velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
