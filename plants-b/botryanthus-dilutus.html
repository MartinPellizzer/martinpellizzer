Botryanthus dilutus

Botryanthus dilutus, commonly known as the blue violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Botryanthus dilutus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
