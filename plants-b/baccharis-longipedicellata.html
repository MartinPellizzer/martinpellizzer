Baccharis longipedicellata

Baccharis longipedicellata, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis longipedicellata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
