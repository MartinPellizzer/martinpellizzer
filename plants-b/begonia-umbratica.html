Begonia umbratica

Begonia umbratica, commonly known as the shade begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to almost black hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, adding a subtle touch of color. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
