Barteria

Barteria, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Barteria longifolia, which is native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Barteria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
