Bassia thurstonii

Bassia thurstonii, commonly known as Thurston's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically hairy and adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

Bassia thurstonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
