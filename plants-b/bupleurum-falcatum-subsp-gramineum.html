Bupleurum falcatum subsp. gramineum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

gramineum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garden bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
