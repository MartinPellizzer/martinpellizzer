Buergeria obovata

Buergeria obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, obovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Buergeria obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
