Beadlea cearensis

Beadlea cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Beadlea cearensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
