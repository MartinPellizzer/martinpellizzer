Buddleja polystachya

Buddleja polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing long, panicle-like inflorescences.

Buddleja polystachya is known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
