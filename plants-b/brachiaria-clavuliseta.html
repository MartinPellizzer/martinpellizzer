Brachiaria clavuliseta

Brachiaria clavuliseta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria clavuliseta is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a common species in ecological restoration projects.
