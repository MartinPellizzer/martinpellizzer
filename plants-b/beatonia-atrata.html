Beatonia atrata

Beatonia atrata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

The botanical interest in Beatonia atrata lies in its rare coloration and ecological significance within its native habitat.
