Bambusa murielae

Bambusa murielae, commonly known as the Muriel's bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense clusters, forming a compact and upright growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically green, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a pointed tip and a slightly hairy underside.

Bambusa murielae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
