Bulbostylis brasiliensis

Bulbostylis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bulbostylis, is a small, grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact stature and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands, and is native to South America.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
