Buddleja forrestii

Buddleja forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pale lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja forrestii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
