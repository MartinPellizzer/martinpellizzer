Begonia griffithii

Begonia griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers partial shade.

Begonia griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
