Blumea riparia

Blumea riparia, commonly known as the river blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, along riverbanks, and in moist forests, showing a preference for humid environments. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate in shape, and often have a rough texture.

The yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.
