Blennodia procumbens

Blennodia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping blennosia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, Blennodia procumbens is often found in rocky or sandy habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil.
