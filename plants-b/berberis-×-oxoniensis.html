Berberis × oxoniensis

Berberis × oxoniensis, commonly known as the English barberry, is a hybrid shrub resulting from the cross between Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and ecologically valuable plant in temperate climates.
