Bourreria formosa

Bourreria formosa, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, bright pink to red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Bourreria formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
