Boissiera

Boissiera, commonly known as the "flowering broom," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, often yellow or white, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is native to various regions in the Mediterranean and parts of Asia. Boissiera species are often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
