Bauhinia cuyabensis f. vestita

Bauhinia cuyabensis f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a heart or a shield. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
