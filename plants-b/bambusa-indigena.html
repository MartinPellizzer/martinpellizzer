Bambusa indigena

Bambusa indigena, commonly known as the native bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Bambusa indigena is also valued for its sustainability and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
