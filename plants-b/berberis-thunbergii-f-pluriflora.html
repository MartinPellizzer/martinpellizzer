Berberis thunbergii f. pluriflora

Berberis thunbergii f. pluriflora, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a cultivar of the barberry plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered ornamental rather than cultivated for consumption. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
