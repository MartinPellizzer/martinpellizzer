Baccharoides guineensis var. cameroonica

Baccharoides guineensis var. cameroonica, commonly known as the Cameroon wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for reforestation purposes.
