Badiera divaricata

Badiera divaricata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
