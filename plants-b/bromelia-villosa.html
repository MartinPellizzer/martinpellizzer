Bromelia villosa

Bromelia villosa, commonly known as the woolly bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear colorful, showy flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and succulent, allowing it to store water, making it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.

Bromelia villosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
