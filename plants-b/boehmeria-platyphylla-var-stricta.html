Boehmeria platyphylla var. stricta

Boehmeria platyphylla var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flax, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its fibers are used in traditional crafts and have potential applications in textile production.
