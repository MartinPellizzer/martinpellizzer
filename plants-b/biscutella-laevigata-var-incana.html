Biscutella laevigata var. incana

Biscutella laevigata var. incana, commonly known as the hairy biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
