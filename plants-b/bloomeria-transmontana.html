Bloomeria transmontana

Bloomeria transmontana, commonly known as the mountain bloom, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and prefers well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Bloomeria transmontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
