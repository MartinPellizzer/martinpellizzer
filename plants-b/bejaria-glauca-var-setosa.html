Bejaria glauca var. setosa

Bejaria glauca var. setosa, commonly known as the woolly bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
