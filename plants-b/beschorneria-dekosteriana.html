Beschorneria dekosteriana

Beschorneria dekosteriana, commonly known as the Mexican bracted agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and dense rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are often adorned with white or pale blue markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This species is well-suited for arid climates and requires minimal water once established.
