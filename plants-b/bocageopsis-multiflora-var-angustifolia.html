Bocageopsis multiflora var. angustifolia

Bocageopsis multiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bocageopsis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and hairy foliage, which help it adapt to its native habitat.
