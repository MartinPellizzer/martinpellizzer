Berkheya carlinopsis subsp. sylvicola

Berkheya carlinopsis subsp.

sylvicola, commonly known as the forest berkheya, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
