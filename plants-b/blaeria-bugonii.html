Blaeria bugonii

Blaeria bugonii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Blaeria bugonii is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
