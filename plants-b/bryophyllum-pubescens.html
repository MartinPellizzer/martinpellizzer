Bryophyllum pubescens

Bryophyllum pubescens, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Bryophyllum pubescens is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
