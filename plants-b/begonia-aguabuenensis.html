Begonia aguabuenensis

Begonia aguabuenensis, commonly known as the Aguabuena begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
