Barleria purpureosepala

Barleria purpureosepala, commonly known as the purple-sepaled barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually pink to purple in color, with showy sepals that are often more prominent than the petals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
