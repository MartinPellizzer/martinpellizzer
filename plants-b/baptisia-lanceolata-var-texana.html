Baptisia lanceolata var. texana

Baptisia lanceolata var. texana, commonly known as Texas false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its showy, sparsely branched inflorescences of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a feathery appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
