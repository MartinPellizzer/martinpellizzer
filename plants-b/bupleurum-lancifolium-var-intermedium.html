Bupleurum lancifolium var. intermedium

Bupleurum lancifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate spearwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
