Buddleja henryi

Buddleja henryi, commonly known as Henry's bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flower spikes that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja henryi is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
