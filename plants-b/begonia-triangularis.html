Begonia triangularis

Begonia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, triangular-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with moist, humid conditions.
