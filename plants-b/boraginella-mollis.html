Boraginella mollis

Boraginella mollis, commonly known as the soft boraginella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Boraginella mollis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
