Bauhinia petersiana subsp. serpae

Bauhinia petersiana subsp.

serpae, commonly known as the serpentine bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, papilionaceous flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.

The subspecies is distinguished by its growth habit and specific habitat preferences compared to other members of the Bauhinia genus.
