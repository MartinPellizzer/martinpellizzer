Borinda boliana

Borinda boliana, commonly known as the Bolian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and often covered in a thin layer of white or grayish lichen. Borinda boliana thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
