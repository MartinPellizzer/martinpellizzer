Bryophyllum aliciae

Bryophyllum aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's mother's tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it can propagate easily through both seeds and leaf cuttings.

Bryophyllum aliciae is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appearance.
