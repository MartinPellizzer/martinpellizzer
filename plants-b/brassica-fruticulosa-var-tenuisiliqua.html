Brassica fruticulosa var. tenuisiliqua

Brassica fruticulosa var. tenuisiliqua, commonly known as the slender mustard bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its small, upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
