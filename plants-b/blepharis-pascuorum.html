Blepharis pascuorum

Blepharis pascuorum, commonly known as the common blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Blepharis pascuorum is known for its ability to bloom rapidly after rainfall, making it a characteristic plant of disturbed habitats.
