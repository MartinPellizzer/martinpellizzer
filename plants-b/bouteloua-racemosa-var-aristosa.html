Bouteloua racemosa var. aristosa

Bouteloua racemosa var. aristosa, commonly known as the silky blue grama, is a native grass species found in the central and southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This grass is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
