Bromelia sessiliflora

Bromelia sessiliflora, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, with vibrant red or orange bracts surrounding small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are capable of storing water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Bromelia sessiliflora is also noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
