Bauhinia sellowiana

Bauhinia sellowiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically velvety to the touch. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

Bauhinia sellowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
