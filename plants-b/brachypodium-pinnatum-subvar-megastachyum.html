Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. megastachyum

Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. megastachyum, commonly known as giant false brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
