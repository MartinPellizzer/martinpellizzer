Bauhinia moningerae

Bauhinia moningerae, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
