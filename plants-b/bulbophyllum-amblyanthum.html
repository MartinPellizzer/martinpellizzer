Bulbophyllum amblyanthum

Bulbophyllum amblyanthum, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid conditions.

Bulbophyllum amblyanthum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
