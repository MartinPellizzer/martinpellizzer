Boea verticillata

Boea verticillata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, round, bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Boea verticillata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
