Brickellia betonicifolia var. conduplicata

Brickellia betonicifolia var. conduplicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brickellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is typically gray-green, contributing to its ornamental appeal in native gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
