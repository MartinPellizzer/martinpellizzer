Byttneria scalpellata var. sessilis

Byttneria scalpellata var. sessilis, commonly known as the sessile byttneria, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually sessile, meaning they lack stalks. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
