Blepharocalyx canescens

Blepharocalyx canescens, commonly known as the white-flowered arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, white spadix surrounded by a purple spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouse cultivation.
