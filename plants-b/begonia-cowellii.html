Begonia cowellii

Begonia cowellii, commonly known as the Cowell's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses. It prefers cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade.

Begonia cowellii is known for its ability to produce numerous seedlings, making it a popular choice for propagation.
