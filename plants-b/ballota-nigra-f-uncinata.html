Ballota nigra f. uncinata

Ballota nigra f. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked ballota, is a hemicryptophytic plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its distinctive feature is the hooked or twisted style of its flowers, which is a characteristic of the f. uncinata form.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
