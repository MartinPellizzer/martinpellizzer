Bulbophyllum mutabile var. obesum

Bulbophyllum mutabile var. obesum, commonly known as the Fat Lip, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy pseudobulbs and showy, brightly colored flowers that often display a range of hues including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique growth form.
