Brassica cheiranthos var. montana

Brassica cheiranthos var. montana, commonly known as the mountain wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are elongated and often have a slight pubescence, while its flowers range in color from yellow to red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
