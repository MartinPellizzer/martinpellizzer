Buddleja mollis var. angustifolia

Buddleja mollis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf false candle bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
