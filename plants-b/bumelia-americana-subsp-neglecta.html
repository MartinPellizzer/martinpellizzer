Bumelia americana subsp. neglecta

Bumelia americana subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the slender bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.
