Brachychiton diversifolius var. caudatus

Brachychiton diversifolius var. caudatus, commonly known as the curly willow or curly sycamore, is a striking ornamental plant native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, cascading foliage that consists of long, narrow, curling leaves that give it a flowing, willow-like appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for both container and garden planting.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
