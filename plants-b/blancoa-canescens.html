Blancoa canescens

Blancoa canescens, commonly known as the white silky tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a hairy, silvery appearance due to the fine pubescence on its leaves and stems. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in shrublands and rocky areas.

Blancoa canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
