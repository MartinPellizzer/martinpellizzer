Bauhinia rotundata

Bauhinia rotundata, commonly known as the Orchid Tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
