Baccharis balnearia

Baccharis balnearia, commonly known as the beach baccharis, is a shrub native to coastal areas of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
