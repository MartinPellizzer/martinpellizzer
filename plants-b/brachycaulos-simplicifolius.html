Brachycaulos simplicifolius

Brachycaulos simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved short-stemmed plant, is a species characterized by its compact growth habit and simple, undivided leaves.

It typically features a short, sturdy stem with foliage arranged closely along its length. The leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a moderate to thick texture. This plant is often found in well-drained soils in temperate regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure makes it a subject of interest in studies related to plant morphology and adaptation.
