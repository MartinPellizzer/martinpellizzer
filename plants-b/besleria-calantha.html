Besleria calantha

Besleria calantha, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

Besleria calantha thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
