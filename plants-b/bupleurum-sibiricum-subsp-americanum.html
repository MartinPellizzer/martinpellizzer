Bupleurum sibiricum subsp. americanum

Bupleurum sibiricum subsp.

americanum, commonly known as American Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in loose racemes, blooming in early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and floral structure, making it identifiable within the genus Bupleurum.
