Berberis emeishanensis

Berberis emeishanensis, commonly known as the Emeishan barberry, is a shrub native to China, particularly found in the Sichuan province.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.
