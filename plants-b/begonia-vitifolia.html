Begonia vitifolia

Begonia vitifolia, commonly known as the leafy begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia vitifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
