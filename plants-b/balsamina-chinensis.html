Balsamina chinensis

Balsamina chinensis, commonly known as Chinese balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with succulent stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Balsamina chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
