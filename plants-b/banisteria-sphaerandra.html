Banisteria sphaerandra

Banisteria sphaerandra, commonly known as the globe arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a globe, hence its common name. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome and produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, surrounded by a spathe that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Banisteria sphaerandra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm environments.
