Bellis annua var. minuta

Bellis annua var. minuta, commonly known as the baby's breath daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, with foliage that is usually woolly and gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its delicate appearance and easy care make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
