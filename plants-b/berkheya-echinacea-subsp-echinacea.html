Berkheya echinacea subsp. echinacea

Berkheya echinacea subsp.

echinacea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor it in the soil. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
