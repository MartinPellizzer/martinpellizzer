Bolivaria robusta

Bolivaria robusta, commonly known as the Bolivian tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by its thick, rugged bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple berries. Bolivaria robusta is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
