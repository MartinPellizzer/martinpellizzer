Bauhinia fulva

Bauhinia fulva, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia fulva is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and its role in preventing soil erosion.
