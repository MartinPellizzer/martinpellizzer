Blumea subcapitata

Blumea subcapitata, commonly known as the yellow chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, daisy-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.
