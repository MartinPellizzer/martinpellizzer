Bromus arvensis var. racemosus

Bromus arvensis var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often found in open habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small spikelets that are often clustered in racemes.

This variety is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
