Begonia negrosensis

Begonia negrosensis, commonly known as the Negros begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green, white, and red tones, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
