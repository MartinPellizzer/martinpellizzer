Begonia fiebrigii

Begonia fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and red veins, often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia fiebrigii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
