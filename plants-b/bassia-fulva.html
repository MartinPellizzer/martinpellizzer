Bassia fulva

Bassia fulva, commonly known as Bluntleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with gray-green, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Bassia fulva is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and sandy soils.
