Bartsia curtiflora

Bartsia curtiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bartsia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.

Bartsia curtiflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
