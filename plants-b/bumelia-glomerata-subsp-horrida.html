Bumelia glomerata subsp. horrida

Bumelia glomerata subsp.

horrida, commonly known as rough bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its densely clustered, small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has rough, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in association with other understory plants.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
