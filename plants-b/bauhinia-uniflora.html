Bauhinia uniflora

Bauhinia uniflora, commonly known as the velvetwood or white-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Bauhinia uniflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in horticulture.
