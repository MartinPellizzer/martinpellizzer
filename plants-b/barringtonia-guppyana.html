Barringtonia guppyana

Barringtonia guppyana, commonly known as the Guppy's candle tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous white flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent, particularly at night. The plant produces a unique fruit that is covered in a thick, fibrous coating and contains a single large seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
