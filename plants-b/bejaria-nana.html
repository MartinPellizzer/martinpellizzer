Bejaria nana

Bejaria nana, commonly known as the dwarf bejaria, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Bejaria nana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its compact size and rapid growth make it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
