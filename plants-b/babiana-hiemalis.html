Babiana hiemalis

Babiana hiemalis, commonly known as the winter rainflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
