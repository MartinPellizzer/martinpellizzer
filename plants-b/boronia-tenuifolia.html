Boronia tenuifolia

Boronia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is often found in sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Boronia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
