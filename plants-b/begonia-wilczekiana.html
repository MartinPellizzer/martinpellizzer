Begonia wilczekiana

Begonia wilczekiana, commonly known as the Wilczek's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare tropical plants.
