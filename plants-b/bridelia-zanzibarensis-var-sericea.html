Bridelia zanzibarensis var. sericea

Bridelia zanzibarensis var. sericea, commonly known as the Zanzibar Bridelio, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

This species is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.
