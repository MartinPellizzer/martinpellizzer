Batanthes texana

Batanthes texana, commonly known as the Texas batanthes, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Batanthes texana is adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of local plant communities.
