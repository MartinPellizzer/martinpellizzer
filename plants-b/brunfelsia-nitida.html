Brunfelsia nitida

Brunfelsia nitida, commonly known as the evergreen nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and nocturnal fragrance.

Brunfelsia nitida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as moths and bats.
