Bromopsis paulsenii

Bromopsis paulsenii, commonly known as the Paulsen's bromus, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Bromopsis paulsenii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
