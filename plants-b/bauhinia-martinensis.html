Bauhinia martinensis

Bauhinia martinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves composed of three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds.

Bauhinia martinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
