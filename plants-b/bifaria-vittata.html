Bifaria vittata

Bifaria vittata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Bifaria vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
