Bromus vulgaris subsp. eximius

Bromus vulgaris subsp.

eximius, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
