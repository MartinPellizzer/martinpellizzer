Brazzeia scandens

Brazzeia scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a waxy texture.

Brazzeia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
