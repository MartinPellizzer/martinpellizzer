Bisnaga latispina subsp. spiralis

Bisnaga latispina subsp.

spiralis, commonly known as the spiral bisnaga, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, spiny leaves that are often ribbed and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiral-shaped fruits. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
