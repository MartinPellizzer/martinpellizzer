Balsamina suaveolens

Balsamina suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Balsamina suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
