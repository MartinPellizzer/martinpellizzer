Bathysa sylvestrae

Bathysa sylvestrae, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Bathysa sylvestrae is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
