Berberis vulgaris f. dulcis

Berberis vulgaris f. dulcis, commonly known as sweet barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical medicinal applications.
