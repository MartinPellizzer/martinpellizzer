Bulbine alata

Bulbine alata, commonly known as the arrowleaf bulbous plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
