Barbarea americana

Barbarea americana, commonly known as wild winter cress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Its leaves are lobed and often have a peppery taste, making it a plant of interest for both ecological and culinary studies.
