Bulbophyllum xanthochloron

Bulbophyllum xanthochloron, commonly known as the yellow-green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
