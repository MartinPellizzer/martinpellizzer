Biscutella variegata subsp. magacarpaea

Biscutella variegata subsp.

magacarpaea, commonly known as the variegated biscuitroot, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often variegated with white or yellow markings, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
