Ballota rotundifolia

Ballota rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It is typically found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Ballota rotundifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
