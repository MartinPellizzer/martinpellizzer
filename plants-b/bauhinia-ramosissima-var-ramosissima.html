Bauhinia ramosissima var. ramosissima

Bauhinia ramosissima var. ramosissima, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red or orange hues during the flowering season. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
