Brachiaria sadinii

Brachiaria sadinii, commonly known as white grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale green inflorescences. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a forage crop.

Brachiaria sadinii is valued for its adaptability and is used in pastureland management in several countries.
