Barkeria naevosa subsp. strophinx

Barkeria naevosa subsp.

strophinx, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Barkeria naevosa subsp.

strophinx is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
