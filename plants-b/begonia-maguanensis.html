Begonia maguanensis

Begonia maguanensis, commonly known as the Maguanensis begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and prefers shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers bloom during the rainy season.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
