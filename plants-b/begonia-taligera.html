Begonia taligera

Begonia taligera, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and bright yellow veins. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and blooming habits make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
