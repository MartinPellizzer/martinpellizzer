Bulbophyllum ridleyanum

Bulbophyllum ridleyanum, commonly known as the Ridley's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded conditions.

The flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.
