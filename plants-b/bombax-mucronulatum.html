Bombax mucronulatum

Bombax mucronulatum, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in many regions.
