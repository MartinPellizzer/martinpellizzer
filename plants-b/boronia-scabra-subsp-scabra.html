Boronia scabra subsp. scabra

Boronia scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with small, elliptical leaves covered in fine, rough hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
