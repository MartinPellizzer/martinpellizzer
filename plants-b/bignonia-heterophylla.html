Bignonia heterophylla

Bignonia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with varying leaf shapes, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Bignonia heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
