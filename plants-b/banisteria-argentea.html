Banisteria argentea

Banisteria argentea, commonly known as silver banister, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for use as a decorative vine in gardens and greenhouses.

Banisteria argentea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
