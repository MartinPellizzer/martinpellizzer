Bulbophyllum ambrosia subsp. ambrosia

Bulbophyllum ambrosia subsp.

ambrosia, commonly known as the Ambrosia orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flower structures.

The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insect wings.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation.
