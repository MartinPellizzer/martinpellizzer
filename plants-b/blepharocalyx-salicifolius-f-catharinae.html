Blepharocalyx salicifolius f. catharinae

Blepharocalyx salicifolius f. catharinae, commonly known as the Catharina's flame tree, is a rare and unique cultivar of the flame tree species.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, crimson-red bracts that resemble flames, which are prominent during the flowering season. The plant is a deciduous tree with a spreading canopy and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are similar in appearance to those of willow trees. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
