Benkara emanuelssoniana

Benkara emanuelssoniana, commonly known as the Ethiopian orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude, humid environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
