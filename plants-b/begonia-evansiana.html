Begonia evansiana

Begonia evansiana, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its foliage is often succulent, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Begonia evansiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in container gardens.
