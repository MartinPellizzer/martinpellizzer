Begonia mameiana

Begonia mameiana, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia mameiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
