Boykinia tellimoides var. watanabei

Boykinia tellimoides var. watanabei, commonly known as the Watanabei stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
