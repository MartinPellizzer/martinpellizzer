Bocquillonia lucidula

Bocquillonia lucidula, commonly known as the glossy leaf bocquillonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
