Bernardia allemii

Bernardia allemii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Bernardia allemii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
