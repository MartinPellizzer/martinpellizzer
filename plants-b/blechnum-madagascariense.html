Blechnum madagascariense

Blechnum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar maidenhair fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the species.

The leaves are usually bioluminescent, contributing to its unique appearance in its natural habitat.
