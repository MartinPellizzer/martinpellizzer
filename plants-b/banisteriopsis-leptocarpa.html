Banisteriopsis leptocarpa

Banisteriopsis leptocarpa, commonly known as the "chicory tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
