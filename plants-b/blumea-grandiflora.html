Blumea grandiflora

Blumea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disc of tubular florets. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Blumea grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
