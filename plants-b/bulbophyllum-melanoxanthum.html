Bulbophyllum melanoxanthum

Bulbophyllum melanoxanthum, commonly known as the black-yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with dark yellow petals and a contrasting lip. Its inflorescence is usually terminal, bearing several blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
