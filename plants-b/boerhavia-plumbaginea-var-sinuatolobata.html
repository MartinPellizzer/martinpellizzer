Boerhavia plumbaginea var. sinuatolobata

Boerhavia plumbaginea var. sinuatolobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed bluemink, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, sprawling growth habit and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lobed, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
