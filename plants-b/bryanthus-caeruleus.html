Bryanthus caeruleus

Bryanthus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue bryanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a dense cluster, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bryanthus caeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its color and resilience.
