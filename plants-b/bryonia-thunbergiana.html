Bryonia thunbergiana

Bryonia thunbergiana, commonly known as the Thunberg's bryony, is a perennial climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
