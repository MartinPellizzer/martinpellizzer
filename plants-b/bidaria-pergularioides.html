Bidaria pergularioides

Bidaria pergularioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bidaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or as a forage plant.
