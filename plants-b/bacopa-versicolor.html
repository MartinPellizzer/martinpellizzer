Bacopa versicolor

Bacopa versicolor, commonly known as the blue bacopa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bacopa versicolor is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticultural displays due to its attractive blooms.
