Buddleja gracilis

Buddleja gracilis, commonly known as the slender butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of fragrant, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Buddleja gracilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
