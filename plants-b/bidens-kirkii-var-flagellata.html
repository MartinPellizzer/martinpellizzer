Bidens kirkii var. flagellata

Bidens kirkii var. flagellata, commonly known as the whipweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features small, yellow flowers with ray florets and a distinctive, slender stem. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
