Blastus eberhardtii

Blastus eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. Native to wetlands and bogs in South America, it thrives in nutrient-poor environments. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Its unique adaptations for nutrient acquisition make it an interesting subject in the study of carnivorous plant biology.
