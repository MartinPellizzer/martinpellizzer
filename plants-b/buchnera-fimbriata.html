Buchnera fimbriata

Buchnera fimbriata, commonly known as the fringeleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Buchnera fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
