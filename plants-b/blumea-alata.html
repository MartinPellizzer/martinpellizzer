Blumea alata

Blumea alata, commonly known as the broadleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Blumea alata is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
