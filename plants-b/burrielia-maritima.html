Burrielia maritima

Burrielia maritima, commonly known as sea burrielia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a small shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found along the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Burrielia maritima is known for its salt tolerance, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
