Betula papyrifera f. elobata

Betula papyrifera f. elobata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and green beneath. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cold climates, reaching heights of up to 25 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

It produces small, cone-like fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
