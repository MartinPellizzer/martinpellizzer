Bellis microcephala

Bellis microcephala, commonly known as the common daisy, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features small, white petals surrounding a yellow central disk, forming daisy-like flowers. The plant has basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous flowering stems throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and gardens.

Bellis microcephala is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
