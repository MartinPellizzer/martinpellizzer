Boopis anthemoides f. patagonica

Boopis anthemoides f. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, linear leaves and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is of interest to botanists for its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
