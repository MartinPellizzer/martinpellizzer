Byrsonima crassa var. paraguariensis

Byrsonima crassa var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Guarana, is a species native to the Amazon basin and parts of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its most notable feature is the bright red, woody fruit that contains several elliptical seeds.

The seeds are rich in caffeine and are widely used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant in various beverages.
