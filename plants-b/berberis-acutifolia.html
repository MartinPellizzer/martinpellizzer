Berberis acutifolia

Berberis acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Berberis acutifolia is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
