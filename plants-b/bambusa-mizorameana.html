Bambusa mizorameana

Bambusa mizorameana, commonly known as the Mizoram bamboo, is a species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color, and its culms are strong and hollow, characteristic of the bamboo family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This bamboo is valued for its durability and is used in traditional construction and crafts in its native region.
