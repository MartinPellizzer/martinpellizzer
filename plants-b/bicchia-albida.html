Bicchia albida

Bicchia albida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Bicchia albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
