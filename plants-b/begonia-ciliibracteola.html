Begonia ciliibracteola

Begonia ciliibracteola, commonly known as the silky bract begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and delicate, fern-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
