Barleria tomentosa var. acuminata

Barleria tomentosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the woolly barleria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
