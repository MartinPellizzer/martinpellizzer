Buchnera orgyalis

Buchnera orgyalis, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter, a feature common among epiphytic orchids.

Buchnera orgyalis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
