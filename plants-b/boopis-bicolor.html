Boopis bicolor

Boopis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored boopis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, typically featuring a white base and a yellow or orange tip. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Boopis bicolor has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in wet, sandy soils near water bodies.
