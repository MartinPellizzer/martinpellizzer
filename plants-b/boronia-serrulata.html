Boronia serrulata

Boronia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its common name, while its flowers are often clustered in umbels.

Boronia serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
