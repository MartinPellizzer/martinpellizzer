Brassica chinensis f. hiroshimana

Brassica chinensis f. hiroshimana, commonly known as the Hiroshima mustard, is a cultivated variant of the Chinese mustard plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional Asian cuisine. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
