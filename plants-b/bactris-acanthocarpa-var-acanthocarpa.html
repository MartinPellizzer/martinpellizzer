Bactris acanthocarpa var. acanthocarpa

Bactris acanthocarpa var. acanthocarpa, commonly known as the red-scaled palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the fruits are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

This palm is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
