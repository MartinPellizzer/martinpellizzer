Baccharis linearifolia subsp. chilco

Baccharis linearifolia subsp.

chilco, commonly known as Chilean feverfew, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
