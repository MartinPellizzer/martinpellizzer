Begonia portoricensis

Begonia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican begonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for its survival.
