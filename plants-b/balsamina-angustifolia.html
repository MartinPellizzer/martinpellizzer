Balsamina angustifolia

Balsamina angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and delicate appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Balsamina angustifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in humid environments, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
