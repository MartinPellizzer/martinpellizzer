Bridelia penangiana

Bridelia penangiana, commonly known as the Penang bridelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, smooth seeds.

Bridelia penangiana is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental foliage and potential medicinal properties.
