Bellevalia macrobotrys var. minor

Bellevalia macrobotrys var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive, tubular corolla. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or rocky areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
