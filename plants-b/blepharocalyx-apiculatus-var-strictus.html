Blepharocalyx apiculatus var. strictus

Blepharocalyx apiculatus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
