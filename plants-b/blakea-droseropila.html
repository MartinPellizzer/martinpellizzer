Blakea droseropila

Blakea droseropila, commonly known as the velvetleaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Droseraceae and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that trap insects. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a dense layer of red or orange secretions.

Blakea droseropila is valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in nutrient-poor environments.
