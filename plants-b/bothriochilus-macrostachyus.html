Bothriochilus macrostachyus

Bothriochilus macrostachyus, commonly known as the large-stalked orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in yellow or greenish hues. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands or forest margins.

The orchid is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
