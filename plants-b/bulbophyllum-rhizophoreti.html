Bulbophyllum rhizophoreti

Bulbophyllum rhizophoreti, commonly known as the root-attached orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, where the plant attaches itself to the roots of other plants, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The species has elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a raceme, with a complex structure typical of the orchid family.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
