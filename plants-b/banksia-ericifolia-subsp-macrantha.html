Banksia ericifolia subsp. macrantha

Banksia ericifolia subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes.

The flowers are typically yellow to orange in color and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. This subspecies is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It plays an important role in providing nectar for native pollinators and birds.
