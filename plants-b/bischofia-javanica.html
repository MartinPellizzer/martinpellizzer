Bischofia javanica

Bischofia javanica, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescences. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, terminal panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Bischofia javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
