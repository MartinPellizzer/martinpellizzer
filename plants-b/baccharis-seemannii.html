Baccharis seemannii

Baccharis seemannii, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Baccharis seemannii is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
