Buchenavia tomentosa

Buchenavia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly senna, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets covered in a white, woolly pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Buchenavia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
