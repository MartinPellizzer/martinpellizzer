Babbagia dipterocarpa

Babbagia dipterocarpa, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
