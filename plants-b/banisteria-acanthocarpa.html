Banisteria acanthocarpa

Banisteria acanthocarpa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive, often hairy appearance.

Banisteria acanthocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
