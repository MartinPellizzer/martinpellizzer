Briza monspessulana

Briza monspessulana, commonly known as the silver grass or sweet grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, silver-blue foliage and fluffy, seed-bearing heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful appearance and ability to form clumps over time.

Briza monspessulana is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
