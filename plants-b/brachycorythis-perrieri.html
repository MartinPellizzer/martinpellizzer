Brachycorythis perrieri

Brachycorythis perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, which are typically male and female, with the female cones bearing a distinctive reddish coloration. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered critically endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a valuable subject for botanical research.
