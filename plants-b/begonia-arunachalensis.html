Begonia arunachalensis

Begonia arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal begonia, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in the state of Arunachal Pradesh in India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
