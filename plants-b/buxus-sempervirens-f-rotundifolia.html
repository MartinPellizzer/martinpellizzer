Buxus sempervirens f. rotundifolia

Buxus sempervirens f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, rounded foliage.

Its leaves are oval to round in shape, distinguishing it from the typical narrow, lance-shaped leaves of the standard boxwood. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for use as a foundation plant or topiary.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote dense growth.
