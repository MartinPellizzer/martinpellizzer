Begonia quetamensis

Begonia quetamensis, commonly known as the Quetam begonia, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of red, pink, or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
