Berberis thunbergii f. trispinosa

Berberis thunbergii f. trispinosa, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and vibrant foliage.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native plant species.
