Bifora radians

Bifora radians, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bifora radians is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
