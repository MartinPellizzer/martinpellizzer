Boerhavia erecta var. thornberi

Boerhavia erecta var. thornberi, commonly known as the red-rooted goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with upright, branching stems and small, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
