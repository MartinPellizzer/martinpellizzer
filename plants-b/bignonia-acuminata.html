Bignonia acuminata

Bignonia acuminata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange and yellow. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
