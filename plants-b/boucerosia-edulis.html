Boucerosia edulis

Boucerosia edulis, commonly known as the edible candelabra, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, upright stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Boucerosia edulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
