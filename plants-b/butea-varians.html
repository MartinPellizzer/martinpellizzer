Butea varians

Butea varians, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving the plant its dramatic appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry regions with well-drained soil.
