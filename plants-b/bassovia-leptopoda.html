Bassovia leptopoda

Bassovia leptopoda, commonly known as the slender-leaved bassovia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
