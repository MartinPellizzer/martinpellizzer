Brassavola reginae

Brassavola reginae, commonly known as the Queen of the Night Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often covered in a waxy coating.

Brassavola reginae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique nocturnal blooming behavior.
