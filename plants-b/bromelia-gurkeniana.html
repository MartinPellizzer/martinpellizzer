Bromelia gurkeniana

Bromelia gurkeniana, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically orange or red, emerging from the center of the leaf rosette.

Bromelia gurkeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
