Buchnera lippioides

Buchnera lippioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pinkish to white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Buchnera lippioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
