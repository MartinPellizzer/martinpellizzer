Bomarea dolichocarpa

Bomarea dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the cool, humid climate. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens.
