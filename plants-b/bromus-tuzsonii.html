Bromus tuzsonii

Bromus tuzsonii, commonly known as Tuzson's brome, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Bromus tuzsonii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
