Brachanthemum

Brachanthemum, commonly known as the common sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions across Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. They are characterized by their large, showy composite flowers that typically feature a yellow disk surrounded by ray florets. Brachanthemum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Many species within this genus are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
