Brunsvigia cooperi

Brunsvigia cooperi, commonly known as the cooper's bracts, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces showy, red-orange flowers with prominent bracts that resemble petals. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to store water in its bulb, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It blooms in winter, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.

The flowers are often used in cut flower arrangements due to their bold color and long-lasting nature.
