Bogenhardia nemoralis var. pilosula

Bogenhardia nemoralis var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces numerous thin, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
