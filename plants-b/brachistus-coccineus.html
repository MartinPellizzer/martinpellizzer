Brachistus coccineus

Brachistus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright red, bristle-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color.

The seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread in suitable habitats.
