Baccharis vernalis

Baccharis vernalis, commonly known as spring baccharis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Baccharis vernalis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
