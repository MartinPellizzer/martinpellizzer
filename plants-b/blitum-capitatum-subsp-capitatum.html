Blitum capitatum subsp. capitatum

Blitum capitatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the purple mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features hairy, basal leaves that are lance-shaped and have a distinctive purple underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, rounded heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
