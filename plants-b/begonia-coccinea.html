Begonia coccinea

Begonia coccinea, commonly known as the red begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically green with a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in horticultural diversity within the Begoniaceae family.
