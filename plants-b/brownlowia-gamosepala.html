Brownlowia gamosepala

Brownlowia gamosepala, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental value.

Brownlowia gamosepala thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.
