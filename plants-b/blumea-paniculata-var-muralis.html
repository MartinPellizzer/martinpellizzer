Blumea paniculata var. muralis

Blumea paniculata var. muralis, commonly known as the wall blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in paniculate inflorescences.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
