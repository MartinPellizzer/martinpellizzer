Blumea humifusa

Blumea humifusa, commonly known as the creeping Blumea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

Blumea humifusa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
