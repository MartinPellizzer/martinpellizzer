Bulbophyllum ximaense

Bulbophyllum ximaense, commonly known as the Ximaense orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
