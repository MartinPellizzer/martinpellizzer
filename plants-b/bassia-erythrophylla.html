Bassia erythrophylla

Bassia erythrophylla, commonly known as red-flowered goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
