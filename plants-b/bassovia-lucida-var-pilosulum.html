Bassovia lucida var. pilosulum

Bassovia lucida var. pilosulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both visual appeal and ecological benefits.
