Bignonia corchoroides

Bignonia corchoroides, commonly known as the creeping trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. Its stems are often prostrate or trailing, allowing it to spread along the ground.

Bignonia corchoroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
