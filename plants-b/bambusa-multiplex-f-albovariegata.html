Bambusa multiplex f. albovariegata

Bambusa multiplex f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves exhibit a variegated pattern of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast against the green stems. This bamboo species is native to Asia and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.

The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and is relatively low maintenance once established.
