Butia nehrlingiana

Butia nehrlingiana, commonly known as the golden pampas palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by ornamental fruits that turn red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a valuable addition to both tropical and subtropical landscapes.
