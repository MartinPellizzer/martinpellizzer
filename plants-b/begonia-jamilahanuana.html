Begonia jamilahanuana

Begonia jamilahanuana, commonly known as the Malay begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
