Burchardia umbellata var. typica

Burchardia umbellata var. typica, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental and ecological value.
