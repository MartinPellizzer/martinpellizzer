Baccharis saliens var. saliens

Baccharis saliens var. saliens, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
