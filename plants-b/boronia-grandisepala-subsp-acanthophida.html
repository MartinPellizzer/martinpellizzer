Boronia grandisepala subsp. acanthophida

Boronia grandisepala subsp.

acanthophida, commonly known as the mountain boronia, is a shrub native to the wetter regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
