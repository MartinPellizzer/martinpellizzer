Beaucarnea lindheimeriana

Beaucarnea lindheimeriana, commonly known as the Lindheimer's elephant tree, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, branching stems that resemble the trunk of an elephant, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically absent, with the stem serving as the primary photosynthetic structure.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for xeriscaping.
