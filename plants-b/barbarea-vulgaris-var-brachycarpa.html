Barbarea vulgaris var. brachycarpa

Barbarea vulgaris var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
