Bulbophyllum brachychilum

Bulbophyllum brachychilum, commonly known as the short-lipped bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
