Brassica mizuna

Brassica mizuna, commonly known as Japanese mustard spinach, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It features dark green, serrated leaves with a slightly peppery flavor, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to East Asia and is often grown for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in cool climates and is typically harvested before the flowers develop.

Brassica mizuna is rich in vitamins A and C, making it a nutritious addition to salads and stir-fries.
