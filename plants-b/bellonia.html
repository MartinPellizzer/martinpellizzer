Bellonia

Bellonia, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

These plants are native to Africa and are characterized by their bell-shaped, tubular flowers that often display vibrant colors such as blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Bellonia species are often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their striking floral displays.
