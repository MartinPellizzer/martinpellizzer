Bambusa mollis

Bambusa mollis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clumping form, making it suitable for small garden spaces. The plant features slender, green culms that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Bambusa mollis is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
