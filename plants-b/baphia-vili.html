Baphia vili

Baphia vili, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines. Baphia vili is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Its ornamental value, due to its attractive foliage, adds to its significance in horticulture.
