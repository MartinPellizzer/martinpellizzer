Bupleurum longifolium subvar. longiinvolucratum

Bupleurum longifolium subvar. longiinvolucratum, commonly known as the long-involucrate silkwormroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from a short, rhizomatous rootstock. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
