Bupleurum falcatum var. petiolare

Bupleurum falcatum var. petiolare, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that often bear leaves with long petioles, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
