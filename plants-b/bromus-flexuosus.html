Bromus flexuosus

Bromus flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow with a smooth surface. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Bromus flexuosus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common species in temperate regions.
