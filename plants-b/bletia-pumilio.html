Bletia pumilio

Bletia pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. This plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and cloud forests. Bletia pumilio has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
