Bidens cuspidata

Bidens cuspidata, commonly known as tickseed or tick trefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray flowers. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and equipped with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Bidens cuspidata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions of the United States.
