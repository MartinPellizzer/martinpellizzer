Begonia heringeri

Begonia heringeri, commonly known as the Heringer's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, which bloom in the late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
