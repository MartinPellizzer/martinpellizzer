Bulbophyllum porphyroglossum

Bulbophyllum porphyroglossum, commonly known as the purple-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers with a distinctive purple lip, which is a key feature in its common name. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
