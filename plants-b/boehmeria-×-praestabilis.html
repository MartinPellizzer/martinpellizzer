Boehmeria × praestabilis

Boehmeria × praestabilis, commonly known as Chinese grass or giant bamboo grass, is a hybrid plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and papermaking.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.
