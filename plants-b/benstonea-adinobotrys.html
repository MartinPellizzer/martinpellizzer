Benstonea adinobotrys

Benstonea adinobotrys, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Benstonea adinobotrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
