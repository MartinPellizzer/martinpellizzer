Begonia bosseri

Begonia bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Begonia bosseri is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in both indoor and outdoor settings.
