Begonia pseudodryadis

Begonia pseudodryadis, commonly known as the ghost begonia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique beauty and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
