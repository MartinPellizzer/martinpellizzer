Batrachium heterophyllum var. pseudolenormandii

Batrachium heterophyllum var. pseudolenormandii, commonly known as the pseudolenormandii variety of the genus Batrachium, is a member of the family Araliaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, typical of the Araliaceae family. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is native to parts of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
