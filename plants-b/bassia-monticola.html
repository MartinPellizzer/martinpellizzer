Bassia monticola

Bassia monticola, commonly known as mountain bassia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Bassia monticola is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
