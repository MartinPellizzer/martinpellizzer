Brachystephanus myrmecophilus var. amphipuberulus

Brachystephanus myrmecophilus var. amphipuberulus, commonly known as the ant-associated mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that forms a specialized relationship with ants.

It typically grows on the branches of host trees, drawing water and nutrients through its roots while also receiving protection from its ant symbionts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects rather than the ants. Its leaves are thin and scale-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its often-dry habitat.

This unique botanical adaptation allows it to thrive in specific ecological niches where ant activity is prevalent.
