Bromelia pauciflora

Bromelia pauciflora, commonly known as the spiny bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in a spike from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are typically thick and covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Bromelia pauciflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
