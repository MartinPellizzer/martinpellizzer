Brassica chinensis var. communis

Brassica chinensis var. communis, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stalks and tightly packed leaves, which are typically green or purple in color. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and sometimes the inner core, which are used in various Asian cuisines. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins A and C, and its ability to withstand mild frost.
