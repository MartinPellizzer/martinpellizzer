Bauhinia excelsa var. megalantha

Bauhinia excelsa var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which benefit soil fertility.
