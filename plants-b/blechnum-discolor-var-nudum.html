Blechnum discolor var. nudum

Blechnum discolor var. nudum, commonly known as the naked maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Blechnum discolor species.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically pale green to white in color, with a contrasting dark green margin. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread along the forest floor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
