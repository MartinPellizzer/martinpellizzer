Bicornella arabica

Bicornella arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by red berries. Its fruits contain seeds that are used to make coffee, though it is less commonly cultivated than Coffea arabica.

The plant has adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water.
