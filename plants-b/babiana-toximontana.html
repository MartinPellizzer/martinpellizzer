Babiana toximontana

Babiana toximontana, commonly known as the red-hot-plate, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that resemble a fiery plate, hence its common name. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Babiana toximontana is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
