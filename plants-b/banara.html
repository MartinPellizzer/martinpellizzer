Banara

Banara, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Sapotaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that turn yellow or red when ripe. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Banara species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and cultural significance.

They are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
