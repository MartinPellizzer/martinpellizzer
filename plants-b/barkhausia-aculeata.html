Barkhausia aculeata

Barkhausia aculeata, commonly known as the woolly barkhausia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

Barkhausia aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
