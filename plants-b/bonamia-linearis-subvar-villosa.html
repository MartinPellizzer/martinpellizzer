Bonamia linearis subvar. villosa

Bonamia linearis subvar. villosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf bonamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
