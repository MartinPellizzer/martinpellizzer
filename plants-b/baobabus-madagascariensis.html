Baobabus madagascariensis

Baobabus madagascariensis, commonly known as the traveler's tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The tree has broad, palmate leaves and produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a fibrous capsule that contains edible seeds.

The plant is well adapted to the tropical climate of Madagascar and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
