Belvisia mucronata

Belvisia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive narrow tip. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Belvisia mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
