Bursa rubella

Bursa rubella, commonly known as the red burr or red burweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and distinctive, spiny bracts that give it a bur-like appearance. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Bursa rubella is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
