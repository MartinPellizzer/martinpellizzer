Betonica jacquinii

Betonica jacquinii, commonly known as Jacob's tea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to violet flowers in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Betonica jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
