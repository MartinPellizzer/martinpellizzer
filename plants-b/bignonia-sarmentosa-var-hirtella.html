Bignonia sarmentosa var. hirtella

Bignonia sarmentosa var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy bignonia, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
