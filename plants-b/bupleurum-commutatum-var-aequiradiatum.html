Bupleurum commutatum var. aequiradiatum

Bupleurum commutatum var. aequiradiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and bear small, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and bloom in the spring.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
