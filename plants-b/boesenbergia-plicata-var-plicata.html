Boesenbergia plicata var. plicata

Boesenbergia plicata var. plicata, commonly known as the zingiberaceae or ginger plant, is a tropical herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
