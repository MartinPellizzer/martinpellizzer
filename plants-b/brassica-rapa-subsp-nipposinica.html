Brassica rapa subsp. nipposinica

Brassica rapa subsp.

nipposinica, commonly known as the Chinese kale or neep, is a leafy vegetable native to East Asia. It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

This subspecies belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.

The plant is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is valued in various Asian cuisines.
