Begonia maculata var. argentea

Begonia maculata var. argentea, commonly known as the silver-spotted begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy, silver-tinged appearance, featuring prominent dark green spots and margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in indoor gardening or as a decorative plant in shaded outdoor areas.
