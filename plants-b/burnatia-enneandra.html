Burnatia enneandra

Burnatia enneandra, commonly known as the nine-leaf burnatia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and its striking, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
