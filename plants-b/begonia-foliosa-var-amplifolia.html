Begonia foliosa var. amplifolia

Begonia foliosa var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
