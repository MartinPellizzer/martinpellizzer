Brachypodium pinnatum var. glaucum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the bluegrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets.

Brachypodium pinnatum var. glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
