Bixa purpurea

Bixa purpurea, commonly known as the bixin plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, waxy seeds that contain a natural orange-red pigment called bixin. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in food coloring and traditional medicine. Its leaves are also used in some cuisines, adding a slightly bitter flavor.

The plant's botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.
