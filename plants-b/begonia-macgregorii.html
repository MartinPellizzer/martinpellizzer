Begonia macgregorii

Begonia macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with reddish veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
