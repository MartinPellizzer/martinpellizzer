Betula platyphylloides

Betula platyphylloides, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, papery bark that peels in thin layers, giving it its common name. The tree has slender, pendulous branches and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Betula platyphylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
