Bromus brevis

Bromus brevis, commonly known as the shortgrass brome, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Bromus brevis is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
