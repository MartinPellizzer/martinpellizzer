Bupleurum giganteum

Bupleurum giganteum, commonly known as giant bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bupleurum giganteum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
