Berberis loudonii

Berberis loudonii, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping.

Berberis loudonii also has medicinal properties due to the presence of berberine in its roots and stems.
