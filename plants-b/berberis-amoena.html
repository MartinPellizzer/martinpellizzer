Berberis amoena

Berberis amoena, commonly known as the pink barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, bright pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Berberis amoena is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with antimicrobial effects.
