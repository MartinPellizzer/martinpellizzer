Bulbine erumpens

Bulbine erumpens, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
