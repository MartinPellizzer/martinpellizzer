Burmannia bicolor var. micrantha

Burmannia bicolor var. micrantha, commonly known as the pink burmannia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive waxy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
