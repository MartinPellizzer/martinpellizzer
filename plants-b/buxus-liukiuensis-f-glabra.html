Buxus liukiuensis f. glabra

Buxus liukiuensis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaf boxwood, is a rare and unique variant of the boxwood genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage, which distinguishes it from other members of the Buxus species. Native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
