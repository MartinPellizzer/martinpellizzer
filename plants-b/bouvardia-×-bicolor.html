Bouvardia × bicolor

Bouvardia × bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white bouvardia, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink, white, and red, which are particularly attractive in hanging baskets or containers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bouvardia × bicolor is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting flowers.
