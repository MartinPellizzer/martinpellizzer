Begonia porteri

Begonia porteri, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation. Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.

Begonia porteri is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it anchor itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
