Balanocarpus latifolia

Balanocarpus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and fragrant wood, which is valued for its aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various species and contributing to soil stability in forest ecosystems.
