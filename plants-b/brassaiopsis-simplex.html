Brassaiopsis simplex

Brassaiopsis simplex, commonly known as the dwarf fan palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath, and it produces clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
