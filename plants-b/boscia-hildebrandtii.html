Boscia hildebrandtii

Boscia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the red bosco, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish bark and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Boscia hildebrandtii plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
