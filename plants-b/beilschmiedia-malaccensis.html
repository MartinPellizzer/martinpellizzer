Beilschmiedia malaccensis

Beilschmiedia malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
