Bubbia calothyrsa

Bubbia calothyrsa, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Bubbia calothyrsa is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
