Bothriochloa parviflora f. violascens

Bothriochloa parviflora f. violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered bluestem, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, violet-colored inflorescences that distinguish it from other members of the genus. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant typically thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to the structural diversity of the vegetation.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its striking flower color, making it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
