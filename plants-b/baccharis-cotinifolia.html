Baccharis cotinifolia

Baccharis cotinifolia, commonly known as wild smokebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Baccharis cotinifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
