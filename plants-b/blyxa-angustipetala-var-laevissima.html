Blyxa angustipetala var. laevissima

Blyxa angustipetala var. laevissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are smooth and often slightly curved. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
