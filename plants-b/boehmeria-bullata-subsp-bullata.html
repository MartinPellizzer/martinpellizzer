Boehmeria bullata subsp. bullata

Boehmeria bullata subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hemp nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous stalks and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants.

Its fibrous properties have historically been utilized in traditional crafts and papermaking.
