Boehmeria platyphylla var. rugosissima

Boehmeria platyphylla var. rugosissima, commonly known as roughleaf mulberry, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
