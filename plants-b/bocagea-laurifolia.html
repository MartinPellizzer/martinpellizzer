Bocagea laurifolia

Bocagea laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
