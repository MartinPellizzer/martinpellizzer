Bilabrella kornasiorum

Bilabrella kornasiorum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, elongated capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
