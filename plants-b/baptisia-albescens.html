Baptisia albescens

Baptisia albescens, commonly known as white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. Baptisia albescens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
