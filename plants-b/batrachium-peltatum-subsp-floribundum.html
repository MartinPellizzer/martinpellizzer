Batrachium peltatum subsp. floribundum

Batrachium peltatum subsp.

floribundum, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in partial shade.
