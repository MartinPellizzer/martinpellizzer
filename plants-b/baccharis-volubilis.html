Baccharis volubilis

Baccharis volubilis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are produced in late summer to early fall.

Baccharis volubilis is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
