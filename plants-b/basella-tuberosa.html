Basella tuberosa

Basella tuberosa, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and edible, tuberous roots that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental foliage. Its leaves are commonly used in Asian cuisines, while the roots are valued for their starchy texture.

Basella tuberosa is also appreciated for its ability to grow in poor soils and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
