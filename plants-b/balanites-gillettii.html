Balanites gillettii

Balanites gillettii, commonly known as the desert ironwood, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense, spiny foliage, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, round fruits containing sweet, fleshy pulp. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-resistant.

Balanites gillettii plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
