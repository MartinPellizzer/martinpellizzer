Betula fontqueri

Betula fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontquerie birch, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by triangular, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have serrated edges.

Betula fontqueri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
