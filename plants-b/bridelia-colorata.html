Bridelia colorata

Bridelia colorata, commonly known as the red-flowered bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically red or orange, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds, contributing to its dispersal in natural habitats.

Bridelia colorata is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
