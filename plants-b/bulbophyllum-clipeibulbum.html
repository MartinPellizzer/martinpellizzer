Bulbophyllum clipeibulbum

Bulbophyllum clipeibulbum, commonly known as the crested bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom during the rainy season and attract various pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
