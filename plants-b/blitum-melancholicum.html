Blitum melancholicum

Blitum melancholicum, commonly known as the melancholy blite, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
