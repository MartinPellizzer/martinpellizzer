Babingtonia similis

Babingtonia similis, commonly known as the yellow-barked waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like leaves that are typically yellow or grey in color, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually red or orange in hue, often with a yellow center. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Babingtonia similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
