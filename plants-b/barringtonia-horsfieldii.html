Barringtonia horsfieldii

Barringtonia horsfieldii, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a distinctive, smooth, grayish bark and produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
