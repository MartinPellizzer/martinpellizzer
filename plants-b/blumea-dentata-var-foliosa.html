Blumea dentata var. foliosa

Blumea dentata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
