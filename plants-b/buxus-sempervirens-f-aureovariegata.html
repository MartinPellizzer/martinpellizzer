Buxus sempervirens f. aureovariegata

Buxus sempervirens f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, rounded foliage. The leaves are typically a combination of dark green and yellow or gold, creating an attractive variegated pattern. This variety is often used in formal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to maintain a compact shape.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hedges and topiary.
