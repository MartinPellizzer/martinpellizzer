Baccaurea capensis

Baccaurea capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive lantern-shaped calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small, yellow-orange berry. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
