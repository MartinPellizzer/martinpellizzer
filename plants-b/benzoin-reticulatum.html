Benzoin reticulatum

Benzoin reticulatum, commonly known as the fragrant resin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as benzoine, which is used in perfumes and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various insects and birds.
