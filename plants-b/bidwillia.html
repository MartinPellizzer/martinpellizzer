Bidwillia

Bidwillia, commonly known as the "Bottlebrush Bush," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, bright red or yellow flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Bidwillia species are native to Australia and are often found in coastal and sandy soils.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
