Butia yatay var. paraguayensis

Butia yatay var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a small, cold-hardy palm native to South America.

It features a slender, grayish trunk and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that develop into small, edible fruits. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various climates.
