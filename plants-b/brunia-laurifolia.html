Brunia laurifolia

Brunia laurifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically a silvery-gray or bluish-green, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
