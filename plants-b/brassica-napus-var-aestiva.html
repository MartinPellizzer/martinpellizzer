Brassica napus var. aestiva

Brassica napus var. aestiva, commonly known as oilseed rape, is a variant of the oilseed mustard plant.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flowers and is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce oil. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and has a rosette of basal leaves. Its rapid growth cycle and high yield make it a valuable crop in agriculture.

Additionally, it plays a role in agricultural biodiversity and can serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
