Bidens rosea var. aequisquama

Bidens rosea var. aequisquama, commonly known as the white beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant has hairy stems and leaves.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands.
