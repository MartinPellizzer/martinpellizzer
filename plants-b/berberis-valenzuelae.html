Berberis valenzuelae

Berberis valenzuelae, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
