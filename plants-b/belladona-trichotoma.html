Belladona trichotoma

Belladona trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy belladonna, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking purple to reddish-brown berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
