Brassica bracteolata

Brassica bracteolata, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glabrous leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Brassica bracteolata is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.
