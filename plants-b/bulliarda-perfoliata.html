Bulliarda perfoliata

Bulliarda perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate bulliarda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often described as having a "perfoliate" appearance, meaning they pass through the stems of adjacent leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bulliarda perfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
