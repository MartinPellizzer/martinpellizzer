Boebera porophyllum

Boebera porophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Boebera porophyllum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
