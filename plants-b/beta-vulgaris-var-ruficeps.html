Beta vulgaris var. ruficeps

Beta vulgaris var. ruficeps, commonly known as the red-rooted beet, is a cultivated variety of the beet plant.

It is characterized by its deep red taproot, which is often used as a root vegetable. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly grown for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
