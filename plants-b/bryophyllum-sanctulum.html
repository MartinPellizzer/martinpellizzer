Bryophyllum sanctulum

Bryophyllum sanctulum, commonly known as the sacred leaf plant, is a unique species belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves have the ability to propagate new plants through vegetative reproduction, making it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience.
