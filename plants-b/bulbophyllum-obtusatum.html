Bulbophyllum obtusatum

Bulbophyllum obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
