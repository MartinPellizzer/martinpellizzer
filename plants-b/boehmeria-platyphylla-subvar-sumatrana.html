Boehmeria platyphylla subvar. sumatrana

Boehmeria platyphylla subvar. sumatrana, commonly known as Sumatran nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its fibrous qualities.
