Boronia bipinnata var. typica

Boronia bipinnata var. typica, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and opposite, with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
