Begonia fernandoi-costae subsp. proxima

Begonia fernandoi-costae subsp.

proxima, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as under forest canopies. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green, white, and red tones, which adds to its ornamental value.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and ease of care.
