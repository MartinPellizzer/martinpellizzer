Beta vulgaris subsp. foliosa

Beta vulgaris subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the leaf beet or leaf chard, is a cultivated variant of the sugar beet. It is characterized by its large, edible leaves that are often used in cooking.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Beta genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.
