Ballota foetida var. glabrescens

Ballota foetida var. glabrescens, commonly known as the rough hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a woody, thorny structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
