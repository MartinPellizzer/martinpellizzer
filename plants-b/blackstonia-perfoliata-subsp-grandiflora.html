Blackstonia perfoliata subsp. grandiflora

Blackstonia perfoliata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Appalachian region of the United States. It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
