Bracea

Bracea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Bracea is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
