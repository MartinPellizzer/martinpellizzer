Brassica assyriaca

Brassica assyriaca, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica assyriaca is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
