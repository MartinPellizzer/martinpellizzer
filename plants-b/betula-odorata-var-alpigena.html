Betula odorata var. alpigena

Betula odorata var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine birch, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
