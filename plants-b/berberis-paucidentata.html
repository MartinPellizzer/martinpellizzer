Berberis paucidentata

Berberis paucidentata, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Berberidaceae family.
