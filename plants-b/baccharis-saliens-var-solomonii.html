Baccharis saliens var. solomonii

Baccharis saliens var. solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, narrow leaves, and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its resilience makes it a common species in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
