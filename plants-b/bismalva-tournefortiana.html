Bismalva tournefortiana

Bismalva tournefortiana, commonly known as the rock mallows, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems that are often covered in a thick, woolly layer of hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Bismalva tournefortiana is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
