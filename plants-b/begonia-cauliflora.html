Begonia cauliflora

Begonia cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing begonia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vining growth habit and ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with prominent, spurred petals. Its foliage is often heart-shaped and can be variegated, adding ornamental value.

Begonia cauliflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
