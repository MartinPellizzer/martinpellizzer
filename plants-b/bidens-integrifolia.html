Bidens integrifolia

Bidens integrifolia, commonly known as common beggarweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers with prominent white markings. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in certain regions.

Bidens integrifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
