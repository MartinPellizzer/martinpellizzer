Bignonia falcata

Bignonia falcata, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Bignonia falcata is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms and vigorous growth habit.
