Blechum pyramidatum

Blechum pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
