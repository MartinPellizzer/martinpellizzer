Bacopa procumbens var. tenuis

Bacopa procumbens var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
