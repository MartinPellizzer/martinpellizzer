Bursera infernidialis

Bursera infernidialis, commonly known as the infernal bursera, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, spiny fruits. Its bark and sap have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
