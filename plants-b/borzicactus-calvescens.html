Borzicactus calvescens

Borzicactus calvescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Borzicactus calvescens is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and extreme temperatures.
