Bellucia superba

Bellucia superba, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

Bellucia superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
