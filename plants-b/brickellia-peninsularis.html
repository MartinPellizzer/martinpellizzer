Brickellia peninsularis

Brickellia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Brickellia peninsularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
