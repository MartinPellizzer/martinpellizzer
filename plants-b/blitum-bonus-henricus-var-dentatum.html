Blitum bonus-henricus var. dentatum

Blitum bonus-henricus var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are finely toothed, giving it its scientific name, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
