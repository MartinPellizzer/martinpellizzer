Bulbophyllum peninsulare

Bulbophyllum peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces a single, showy inflorescence with numerous fragrant flowers. The plant typically has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Bulbophyllum peninsulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
