Boehmeria × tenuifolia var. nigricans

Boehmeria × tenuifolia var. nigricans, commonly known as black giant bamboo, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and are used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.
