Brassica amplexicaulis var. souliei

Brassica amplexicaulis var. souliei, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its rosette of large, fleshy, dark green leaves that are tightly clustered at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by round, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is native to China and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular vegetable in Asian cuisines.
