Bromus cavanillesii

Bromus cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and dry meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are usually tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, which is characteristic of many grass species.

Bromus cavanillesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
