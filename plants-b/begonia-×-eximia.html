Begonia × eximia

Begonia × eximia, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a hybrid plant originating from crosses between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia suaveolens.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, Begonia × eximia is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
