Bauhinia jermyana

Bauhinia jermyana, commonly known as the Jermy's bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pale pink to white, bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
