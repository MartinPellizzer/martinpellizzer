Bombax rawitscheri

Bombax rawitscheri, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the rainy season. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, cotton-like fiber. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the fiber obtained from its seeds, which has historical uses in textiles and medicine.
