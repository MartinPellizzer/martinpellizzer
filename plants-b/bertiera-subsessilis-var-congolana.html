Bertiera subsessilis var. congolana

Bertiera subsessilis var. congolana, commonly known as the Congo bertiera, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
