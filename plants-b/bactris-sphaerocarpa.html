Bactris sphaerocarpa

Bactris sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. The fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
