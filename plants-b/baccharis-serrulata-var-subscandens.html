Baccharis serrulata var. subscandens

Baccharis serrulata var. subscandens, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime hammocks. Its leaves are serrated and have a distinctive, pungent scent when crushed.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
