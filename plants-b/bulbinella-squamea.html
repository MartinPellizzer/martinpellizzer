Bulbinella squamea

Bulbinella squamea, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on leafless stems. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and coastal environments.

Bulbinella squamea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
