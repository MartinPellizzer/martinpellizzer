Blumea adenophora

Blumea adenophora, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Blumea adenophora is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
