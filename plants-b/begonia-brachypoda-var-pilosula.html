Begonia brachypoda var. pilosula

Begonia brachypoda var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dense covering of fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.
