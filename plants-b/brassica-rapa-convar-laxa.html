Brassica rapa convar. laxa

Brassica rapa convar. laxa, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and elongated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by spherical, hairy seed pods. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and fields.

Brassica rapa convar. laxa is an annual or biennial plant that plays a role in both agricultural ecosystems and natural habitats.
