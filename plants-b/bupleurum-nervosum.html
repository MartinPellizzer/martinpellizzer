Bupleurum nervosum

Bupleurum nervosum, commonly known as the veined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Bupleurum nervosum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
