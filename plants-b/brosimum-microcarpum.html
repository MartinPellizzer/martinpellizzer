Brosimum microcarpum

Brosimum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, globose fruits that are typically green when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high humidity and warm climates.

The tree is valued for its latex, which has potential uses in industrial applications.
