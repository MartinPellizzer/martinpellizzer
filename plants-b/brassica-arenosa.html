Brassica arenosa

Brassica arenosa, commonly known as desert mustard, is a hardy plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like broccoli and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers and has capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Brassica arenosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
