Bunium longilobum

Bunium longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's aromatic compounds contribute to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal uses.
