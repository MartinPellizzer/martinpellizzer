Bidens eatonii var. mutabilis

Bidens eatonii var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The species is known for its variability in flower color and morphology, which contributes to its common name.
