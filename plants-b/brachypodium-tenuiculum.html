Brachypodium tenuiculum

Brachypodium tenuiculum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a small, annual grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Botanically, it has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is widely used in ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
