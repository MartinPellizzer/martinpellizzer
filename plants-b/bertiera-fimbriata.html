Bertiera fimbriata

Bertiera fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bertiera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive fringed or toothed margin.

Bertiera fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
