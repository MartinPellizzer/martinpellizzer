Bromus haenkeanus

Bromus haenkeanus, commonly known as the Argentine grass, is a species of perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned spikelets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields, and is often found in temperate regions.

Bromus haenkeanus is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
