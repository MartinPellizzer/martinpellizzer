Berberis paraspecta

Berberis paraspecta, commonly known as the golden spurgebarberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Berberis paraspecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
