Bulbocodium grandiscapum

Bulbocodium grandiscapum, commonly known as the large-capped bulbous gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its showy flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats.
