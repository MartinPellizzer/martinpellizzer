Brassia allenii

Brassia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or red markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Brassia allenii prefers warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
