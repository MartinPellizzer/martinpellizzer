Berberis lycium

Berberis lycium, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.
