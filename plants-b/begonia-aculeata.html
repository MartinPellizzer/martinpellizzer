Begonia aculeata

Begonia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy, often spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is typically green with a velvety texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Begonia aculeata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
