Byrsocarpus orientalis var. pubescens

Byrsocarpus orientalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive success in dry conditions.

The species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
