Bromus indicus

Bromus indicus, commonly known as the Indian brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Bromus indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
