Boronia subulifolia

Boronia subulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is part of the citrus family, Rutaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Boronia subulifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
