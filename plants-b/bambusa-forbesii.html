Bambusa forbesii

Bambusa forbesii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing an attractive visual contrast. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

Bambusa forbesii is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
