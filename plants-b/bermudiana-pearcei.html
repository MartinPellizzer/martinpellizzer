Bermudiana pearcei

Bermudiana pearcei, commonly known as the Bermuda pear.

It is a flowering plant native to Bermuda, characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Bermudiana pearcei plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
