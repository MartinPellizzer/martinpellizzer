Berberis alba

Berberis alba, commonly known as the white barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Berberis alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
