Baptisia villosa

Baptisia villosa, commonly known as wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces long, cylindrical seedpods that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Baptisia villosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
