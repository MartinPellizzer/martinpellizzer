Buchnera grandiflora

Buchnera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered buchnera, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Buchnera grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
