Boscia stylosa

Boscia stylosa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Boscia stylosa plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
