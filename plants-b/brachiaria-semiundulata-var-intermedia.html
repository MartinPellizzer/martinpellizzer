Brachiaria semiundulata var. intermedia

Brachiaria semiundulata var. intermedia, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its spreading growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a significant species in ecological and agronomic contexts.
