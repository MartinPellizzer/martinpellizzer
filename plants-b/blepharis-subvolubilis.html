Blepharis subvolubilis

Blepharis subvolubilis, commonly known as the creeping blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Blepharis subvolubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
