Beringia

Beringia, commonly known as the Bering Land Bridge, was a vast expanse of land that connected Siberia to Alaska during the last Ice Age.

This region was characterized by its cold, arid climate and tundra vegetation, which supported a diverse range of plant life adapted to harsh conditions. The flora included grasses, sedges, and shrubs, which provided essential resources for both human and animal populations. Beringia's unique ecological conditions allowed for the migration of plant species between Asia and North America.

Today, remnants of this ancient landscape can still be found in the Arctic regions of Russia and Alaska.
