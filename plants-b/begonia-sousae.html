Begonia sousae

Begonia sousae, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and a unique, wavy margin. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding delicate beauty to its foliage. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for collectors and gardeners.
