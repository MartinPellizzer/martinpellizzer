Basselinia iterata

Basselinia iterata, commonly known as the slender rockheath, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. Its leaves and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against extreme temperatures and UV radiation.

Basselinia iterata is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, sandy areas.
