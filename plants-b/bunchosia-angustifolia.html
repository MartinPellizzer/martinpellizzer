Bunchosia angustifolia

Bunchosia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid regions.

Bunchosia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
