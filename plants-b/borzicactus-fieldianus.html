Borzicactus fieldianus

Borzicactus fieldianus, commonly known as the Field's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green and brown foliage. Its structure is adapted for minimal water loss, with a thick cuticle and reduced leaf surface.

Borzicactus fieldianus is often found in rocky soils and is a popular specimen for succulent enthusiasts.
