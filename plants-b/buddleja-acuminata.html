Buddleja acuminata

Buddleja acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buddleja, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Buddleja acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
