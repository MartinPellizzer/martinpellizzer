Breweria multicaulis

Breweria multicaulis, commonly known as the yellow centipede, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Breweria multicaulis is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
