Baccharis camporum f. parvifolia

Baccharis camporum f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils along the coastal regions of Florida and Georgia.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
