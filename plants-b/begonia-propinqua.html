Begonia propinqua

Begonia propinqua, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings due to its ornamental value.
