Baccharis conwayi

Baccharis conwayi, commonly known as Conway's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and desert scrub. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Baccharis conwayi plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
