Banisteria periplocifolia var. angustifolia

Banisteria periplocifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed banisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
