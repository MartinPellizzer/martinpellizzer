Bignonia bidwilliana

Bignonia bidwilliana, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant blossoms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or structures.
