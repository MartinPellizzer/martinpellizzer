Bomarea pseudopurpurea

Bomarea pseudopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bomarea pseudopurpurea is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
