Brosimum utile subsp. longifolium

Brosimum utile subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rubber tree, is a species native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its latex, similar to that of other Brosimum species, has been used historically for rubber production.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various tropical and subtropical climates.
