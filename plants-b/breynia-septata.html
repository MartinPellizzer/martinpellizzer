Breynia septata

Breynia septata, commonly known as the red leaf breynia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink leaves, which are often adorned with white or yellow veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Breynia septata is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
