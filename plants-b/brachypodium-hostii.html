Brachypodium hostii

Brachypodium hostii, commonly known as the Mexican grass or rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, dense foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble small, clustered ears. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically bluish-green in color and have a rough, textured surface.

Brachypodium hostii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
