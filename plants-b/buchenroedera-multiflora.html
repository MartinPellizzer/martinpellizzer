Buchenroedera multiflora

Buchenroedera multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered roseroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Buchenroedera multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
