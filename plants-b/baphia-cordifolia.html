Baphia cordifolia

Baphia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Baphia cordifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
