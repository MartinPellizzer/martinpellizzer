Baccharis litoralis

Baccharis litoralis, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Baccharis litoralis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
