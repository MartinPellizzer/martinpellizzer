Berlinia giorgii

Berlinia giorgii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
