Boscia longifolia

Boscia longifolia, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, peeling bark and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Boscia longifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
