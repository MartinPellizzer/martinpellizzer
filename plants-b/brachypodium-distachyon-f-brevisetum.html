Brachypodium distachyon f. brevisetum

Brachypodium distachyon f. brevisetum, commonly known as the short-setose grass, is a variant of the model plant Brachypodium distachyon.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more densely arranged inflorescence compared to the typical form. The plant exhibits a C4 photosynthetic pathway, making it efficient in hot and dry environments. Its genome has been extensively studied, contributing to research in grass genetics and bioenergy crops.

This form is often used in ecological and agricultural studies due to its adaptability and genetic tractability.
