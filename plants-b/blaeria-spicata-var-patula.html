Blaeria spicata var. patula

Blaeria spicata var. patula, commonly known as the Patula Blaeria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
