Bouvardia bicolor

Bouvardia bicolor, commonly known as the pink-flowered bouvardia, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Bouvardia bicolor is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
