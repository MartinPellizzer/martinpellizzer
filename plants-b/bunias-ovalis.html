Bunias ovalis

Bunias ovalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the development of long, slender pods containing seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
