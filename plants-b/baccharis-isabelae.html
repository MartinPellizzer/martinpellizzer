Baccharis isabelae

Baccharis isabelae, commonly known as Isabel's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Baccharis isabelae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
