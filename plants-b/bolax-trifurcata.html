Bolax trifurcata

Bolax trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and its stems are often hairy.

Bolax trifurcata is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
