Bulbophyllum tjadasmalangense

Bulbophyllum tjadasmalangense, commonly known as the Tjadasmalang orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
