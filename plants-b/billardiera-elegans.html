Billardiera elegans

Billardiera elegans, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to eastern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries. It typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as eucalypts, and relies on them for water and nutrients.

Its unique morphology and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
