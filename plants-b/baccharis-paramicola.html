Baccharis paramicola

Baccharis paramicola, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis paramicola is known for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
