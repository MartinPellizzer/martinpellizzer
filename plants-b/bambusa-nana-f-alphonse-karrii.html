Bambusa nana f. alphonse-karrii

Bambusa nana f. alphonse-karrii, commonly known as the Alphonse karrii dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green culms that grow to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it ideal for small spaces. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance.

Due to its slow growth and dense foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
