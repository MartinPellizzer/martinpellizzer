Bulbophyllum ericssonii

Bulbophyllum ericssonii, commonly known as the Ericsson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.
