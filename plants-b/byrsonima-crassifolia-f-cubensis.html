Byrsonima crassifolia f. cubensis

Byrsonima crassifolia f. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban mallow or cuban star-apple, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and agricultural studies.
