Bignonia tuberculata

Bignonia tuberculata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Bignonia tuberculata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
