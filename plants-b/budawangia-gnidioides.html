Budawangia gnidioides

Budawangia gnidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Proteaceae family.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
