Baccharis zamudiorum

Baccharis zamudiorum, commonly known as the Zamudio baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, thriving in arid to semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
