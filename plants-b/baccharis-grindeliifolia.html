Baccharis grindeliifolia

Baccharis grindeliifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a common species in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
