Betula platyphylla var. cuneifolia

Betula platyphylla var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are elongated and triangular, with a serrated margin, and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
