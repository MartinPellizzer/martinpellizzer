Bulbine circinata

Bulbine circinata, commonly known as the circular bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bulbine circinata is also known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
