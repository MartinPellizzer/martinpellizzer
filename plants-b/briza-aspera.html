Briza aspera

Briza aspera, commonly known as rough false oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, slightly rough leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in a loose panicle, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Briza aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
