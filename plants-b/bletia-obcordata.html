Bletia obcordata

Bletia obcordata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Bletia obcordata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
