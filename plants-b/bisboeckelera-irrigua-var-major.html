Bisboeckelera irrigua var. major

Bisboeckelera irrigua var. major, commonly known as the giant water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens and water features.
