Berrya hexaptera

Berrya hexaptera, commonly known as the African blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with six leaflets, giving it the species name "hexaptera.". The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Berrya hexaptera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edges.
