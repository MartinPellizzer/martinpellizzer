Blumea hieraciifolia var. macrostachya

Blumea hieraciifolia var. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
