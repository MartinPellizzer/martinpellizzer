Breynia vitis-idaea

Breynia vitis-idaea, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often thriving in well-drained soils.

Breynia vitis-idaea is also known for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and salt spray.
