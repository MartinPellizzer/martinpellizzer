Berberis vulgaris f. emarginata

Berberis vulgaris f. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate barberry, is a variant of the common barberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The emarginate barberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
