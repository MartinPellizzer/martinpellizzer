Bassia tetracuspis

Bassia tetracuspis, commonly known as the four-cusped borage, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms during the summer months.

Bassia tetracuspis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gravelly areas.
