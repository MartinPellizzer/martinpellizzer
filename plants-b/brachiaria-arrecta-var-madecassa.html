Brachiaria arrecta var. madecassa

Brachiaria arrecta var. madecassa, commonly known as madecassan bladegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, pastures, and disturbed areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a forage crop and a weed.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
