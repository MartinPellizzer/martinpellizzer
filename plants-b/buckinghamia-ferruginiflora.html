Buckinghamia ferruginiflora

Buckinghamia ferruginiflora, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its striking visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
