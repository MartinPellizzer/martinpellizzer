Breweria grandiflora

Breweria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered breweria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
