Breynia cernua var. genuina

Breynia cernua var. genuina, commonly known as the weeping breynia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy leaves that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are succulent, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
