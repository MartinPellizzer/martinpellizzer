Bulbophyllum hastiferum

Bulbophyllum hastiferum, commonly known as the arrowhead orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, often pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives on the forest floor. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Bulbophyllum hastiferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
