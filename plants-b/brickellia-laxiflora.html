Brickellia laxiflora

Brickellia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Brickellia laxiflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
