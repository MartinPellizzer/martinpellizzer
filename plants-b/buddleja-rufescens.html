Buddleja rufescens

Buddleja rufescens, commonly known as the redstem buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, woody stems that often turn reddish in color, especially in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in hue, blooming in summer. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

Buddleja rufescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
