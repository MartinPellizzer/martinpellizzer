Begonia germaineana

Begonia germaineana, commonly known as the Guiana Shield begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
