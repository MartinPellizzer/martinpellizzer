Brugmansia arbuscula

Brugmansia arbuscula, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, containing various alkaloids. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Brugmansia arbuscula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
