Bulbophyllum demissum

Bulbophyllum demissum, commonly known as the naked lady orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette, helping it store water in arid environments.

The orchid's flowers bloom in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, making it a visually striking addition to botanical collections.
