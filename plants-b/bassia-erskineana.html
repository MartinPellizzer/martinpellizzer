Bassia erskineana

Bassia erskineana, commonly known as the yellow goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.
