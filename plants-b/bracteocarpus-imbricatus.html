Bracteocarpus imbricatus

Bracteocarpus imbricatus, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, imbricate pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
