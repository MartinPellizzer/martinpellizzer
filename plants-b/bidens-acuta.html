Bidens acuta

Bidens acuta, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Bidens acuta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
