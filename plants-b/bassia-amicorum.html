Bassia amicorum

Bassia amicorum, commonly known as friendship grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Bassia amicorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
