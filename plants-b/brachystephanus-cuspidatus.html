Brachystephanus cuspidatus

Brachystephanus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stephanus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
