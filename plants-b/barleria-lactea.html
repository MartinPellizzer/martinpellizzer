Barleria lactea

Barleria lactea, commonly known as the millet grass or white barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Barleria lactea is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
