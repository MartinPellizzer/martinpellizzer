Blakea calycularis

Blakea calycularis, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
