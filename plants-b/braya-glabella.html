Braya glabella

Braya glabella, commonly known as the naked-bracted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Braya glabella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
