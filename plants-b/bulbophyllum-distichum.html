Bulbophyllum distichum

Bulbophyllum distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically features two rows of leaves, giving it its common name, and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
