Bidens grandiflora

Bidens grandiflora, commonly known as the greater beggarweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures and prominent veins. The plant produces yellow flowers with two ray florets and a distinctive central disk, blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
