Balanophora indica

Balanophora indica, commonly known as the Indian ghost flower, is a parasitic plant belonging to the family Balanophoraceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other plants. The plant has a fleshy, often yellowish or reddish stem with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are showy and usually white or pink, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Balanophora indica is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments.
