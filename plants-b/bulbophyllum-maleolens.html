Bulbophyllum maleolens

Bulbophyllum maleolens, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns resembling the moon's surface. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
