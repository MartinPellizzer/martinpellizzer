Bacopa dubia

Bacopa dubia, commonly known as water thread plant, is a semi-aquatic herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in shallow water or moist soil. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically rounded or ovate in shape. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Bacopa dubia is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in aquatic landscapes.
