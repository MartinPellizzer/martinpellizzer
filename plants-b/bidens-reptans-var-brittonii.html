Bidens reptans var. brittonii

Bidens reptans var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, sunny environments.
