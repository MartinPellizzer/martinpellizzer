Begonia macrotoma

Begonia macrotoma, commonly known as the giant leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
