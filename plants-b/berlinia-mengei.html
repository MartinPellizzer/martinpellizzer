Berlinia mengei

Berlinia mengei, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The tree is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
