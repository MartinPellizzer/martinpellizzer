Baccharis cochinchinensis

Baccharis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a lanceolate shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
