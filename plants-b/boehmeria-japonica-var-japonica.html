Boehmeria japonica var. japonica

Boehmeria japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese nettle or Japanese flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, hollow stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its strong, fibrous stalks, which have been used in textile production.
