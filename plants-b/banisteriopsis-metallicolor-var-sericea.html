Banisteriopsis metallicolor var. sericea

Banisteriopsis metallicolor var. sericea, commonly known as the silver-leaved balsam apple, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a velvety texture, and the stems are woody and climbing.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a decorative plant in warm climates.
