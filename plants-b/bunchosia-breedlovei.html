Bunchosia breedlovei

Bunchosia breedlovei, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Santalales order and is characterized by its lack of true leaves, relying instead on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. It typically grows on the branches of host trees such as species of the genus *Pseudolmedia*.

Due to its specialized parasitic lifestyle, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
