Bromus hordeaceus var. glabratus

Bromus hordeaceus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and has been introduced to other regions for agricultural purposes.
