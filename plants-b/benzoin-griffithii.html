Benzoin griffithii

Benzoin griffithii, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or wild benzoin, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The bark and resin of Benzoin griffithii contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.
