Baccharis neaei

Baccharis neaei, commonly known as the Neaei baccharis, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in riparian zones. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Baccharis neaei plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
