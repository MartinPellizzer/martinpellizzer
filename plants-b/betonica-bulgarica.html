Betonica bulgarica

Betonica bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian betony, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Betonica bulgarica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
