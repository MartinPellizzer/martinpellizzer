Brachycorythis ovata var. welwitschii

Brachycorythis ovata var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's cycad, is a unique species native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
