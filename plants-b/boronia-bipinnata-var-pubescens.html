Boronia bipinnata var. pubescens

Boronia bipinnata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rainforest habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a soft, hairy appearance.
