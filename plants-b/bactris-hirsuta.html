Bactris hirsuta

Bactris hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched inflorescences, which are followed by edible, oval-shaped fruits. Bactris hirsuta is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
