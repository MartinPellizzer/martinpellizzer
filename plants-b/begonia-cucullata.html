Begonia cucullata

Begonia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and glossy, with a variety of colors ranging from green to reddish hues.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, adding to its ornamental value.
