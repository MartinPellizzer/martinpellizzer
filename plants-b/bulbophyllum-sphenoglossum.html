Bulbophyllum sphenoglossum

Bulbophyllum sphenoglossum, commonly known as the "lily orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant, white or pink flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
