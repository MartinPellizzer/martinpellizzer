Brachypterum scandens

Brachypterum scandens, commonly known as the climbing brachypterum, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems that climb along supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Brachypterum scandens is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
