Bulbine namaensis

Bulbine namaensis, commonly known as the Namaqualand bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, basal leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the winter months, typically between June and August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Bulbine namaensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
