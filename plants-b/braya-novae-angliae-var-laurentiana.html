Braya novae-angliae var. laurentiana

Braya novae-angliae var. laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian braya, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

It is often found in areas with cool, humid climates and is adapted to thrive in partially shaded environments.
