Binghamia eriotricha

Binghamia eriotricha, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy soils and requires full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Binghamia eriotricha is also known for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
