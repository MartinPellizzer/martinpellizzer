Bignonia capensis

Bignonia capensis, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and pinnately veined, with a rough texture.

Bignonia capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
