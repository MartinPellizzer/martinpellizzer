Bauhinia vulpina

Bauhinia vulpina, commonly known as the flame tree or red bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, crimson or orange-red bracts that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia vulpina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
