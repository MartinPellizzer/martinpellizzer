Boehmeria densiflora

Boehmeria densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other members commonly referred to as nettles. The plant is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Boehmeria densiflora is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
