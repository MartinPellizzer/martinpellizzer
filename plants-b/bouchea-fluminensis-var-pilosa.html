Bouchea fluminensis var. pilosa

Bouchea fluminensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or pilosa bouchea, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is appreciated for its compact form and seasonal floral display.
