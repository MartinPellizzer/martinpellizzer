Batrachium nevense

Batrachium nevense, commonly known as the Neven's frog's bean, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies, particularly in humid forest ecosystems.
