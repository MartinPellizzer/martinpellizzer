Begonia bifolia

Begonia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pair of opposite leaves that are often large and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is thick and waxy, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Begonia bifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
