Brachythrix stolzii

Brachythrix stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, bristle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Brachythrix stolzii is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
