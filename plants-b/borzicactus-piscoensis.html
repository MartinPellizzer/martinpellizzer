Borzicactus piscoensis

Borzicactus piscoensis, commonly known as the Pisco cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Peruvian coastal region.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick stem to survive long dry periods.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable subject in botanical studies of coastal deserts.
