Betula brachylepis

Betula brachylepis, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a salmon-colored inner layer. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Betula brachylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
