Baccharis genistifolia

Baccharis genistifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
