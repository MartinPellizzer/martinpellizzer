Broussonetia papyrifera var. normalis

Broussonetia papyrifera var. normalis, commonly known as paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is fibrous and has been historically used for making paper and textiles.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
