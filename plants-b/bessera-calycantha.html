Bessera calycantha

Bessera calycantha, commonly known as the golden bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Bessera calycantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
