Bergia texana

Bergia texana, commonly known as the Texas bergia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bergia texana is often found in open habitats like grasslands and desert scrub.
