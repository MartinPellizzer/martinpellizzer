Bromus purgans var. latiglumis

Bromus purgans var. latiglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety latiglumis is distinguished by its broader glumes compared to the typical form of Bromus purgans.
