Byrsella belua

Byrsella belua, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close during the day. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as moths.

Botanically, Byrsella belua belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role.
