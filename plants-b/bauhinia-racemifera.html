Bauhinia racemifera

Bauhinia racemifera, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral clusters that appear in early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia racemifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
