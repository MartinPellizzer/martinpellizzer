Blitum chenopodioides

Blitum chenopodioides, commonly known as the common blitum or desert spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly by Native American communities. Its small, green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, triangular seeds.

Blitum chenopodioides is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
