Berberis chingshuiensis

Berberis chingshuiensis, commonly known as the Chingshui barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in Yunnan Province.

It typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil, often in forested or rocky habitats. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves, with small, yellowish-green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
