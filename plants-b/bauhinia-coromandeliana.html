Bauhinia coromandeliana

Bauhinia coromandeliana, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
