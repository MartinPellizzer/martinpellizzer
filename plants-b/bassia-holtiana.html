Bassia holtiana

Bassia holtiana, commonly known as the Holt's dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its adaptations to drought include thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water efficiently.

Bassia holtiana is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
