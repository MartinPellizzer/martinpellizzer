Brunsvigia comptonii

Brunsvigia comptonii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has a distinctive, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Brunsvigia comptonii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
