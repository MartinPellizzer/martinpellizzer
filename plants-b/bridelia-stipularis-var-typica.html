Bridelia stipularis var. typica

Bridelia stipularis var. typica, commonly known as the red-flowered bridelia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Bridelia stipularis var. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
