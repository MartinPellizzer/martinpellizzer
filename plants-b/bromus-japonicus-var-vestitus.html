Bromus japonicus var. vestitus

Bromus japonicus var. vestitus, commonly known as the Japanese veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
