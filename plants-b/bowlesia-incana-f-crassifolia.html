Bowlesia incana f. crassifolia

Bowlesia incana f. crassifolia, commonly known as the woolly bowlesia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
