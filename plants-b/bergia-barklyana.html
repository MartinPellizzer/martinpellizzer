Bergia barklyana

Bergia barklyana, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Bergia barklyana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
