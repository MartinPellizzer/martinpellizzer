Berberis weiningensis

Berberis weiningensis, commonly known as the Weining barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation.
