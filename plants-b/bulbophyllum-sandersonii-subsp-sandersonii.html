Bulbophyllum sandersonii subsp. sandersonii

Bulbophyllum sandersonii subsp.

sandersonii, commonly known as the Sanderson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The plant produces aerial roots and has linear, often hairy leaves. It blooms with small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
