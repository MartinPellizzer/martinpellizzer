Bulbophyllum farinulentum

Bulbophyllum farinulentum, commonly known as the farinulentum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The pseudobulbs of this orchid store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.
