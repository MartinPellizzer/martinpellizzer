Blumea duclouxii

Blumea duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer, and are visited by various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
