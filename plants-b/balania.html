Balania

Balania, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Balania species typically thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot. They are popular houseplants due to their compact size and ability to bloom indoors.

The genus includes several species, such as Balanitis zeylanica, which is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
