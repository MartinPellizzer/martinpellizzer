Begonia verdickii

Begonia verdickii, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining, rich soil.

Begonia verdickii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
