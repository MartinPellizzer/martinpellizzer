Betula

Betula, commonly known as birch, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs belonging to the family Betulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their smooth, white bark and triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Betula species are often found in temperate regions and are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. They produce small, catkin-like flowers and are important for their role in forest ecosystems.

The genus includes over 60 species, some of which are used in traditional medicine and as sources of paper pulp.
