Boronia rosmarinifolia var. albiflora

Boronia rosmarinifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It is often found in regions of New South Wales and Victoria, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and delicate blooms.
