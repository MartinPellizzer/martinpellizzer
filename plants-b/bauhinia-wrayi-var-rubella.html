Bauhinia wrayi var. rubella

Bauhinia wrayi var. rubella, commonly known as the red velvet bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers not only add visual appeal but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
