Brickellia betonicifolia var. typica

Brickellia betonicifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its aromatic properties. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a grayish-green color due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
