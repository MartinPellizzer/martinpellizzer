Blechnum auriculatum var. genuinum

Blechnum auriculatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a varietal form of the genus Blechnum native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often described as resembling the feathers of a bird. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
