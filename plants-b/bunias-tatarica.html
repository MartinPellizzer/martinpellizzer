Bunias tatarica

Bunias tatarica, commonly known as Siberian kale, is a hardy biennial plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces yellow flowers in the second year of growth, followed by small, round pods containing several seeds.

Bunias tatarica is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional cuisines.
