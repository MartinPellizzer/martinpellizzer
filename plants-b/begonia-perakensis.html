Begonia perakensis

Begonia perakensis, commonly known as the Perak begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
