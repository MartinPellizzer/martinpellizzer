Brassica nigra f. condensata

Brassica nigra f. condensata, commonly known as the compact black mustard, is a horticultural form of the black mustard plant.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered closely together, giving it a full, bushy appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mustard seeds.

This variety is well-suited for garden use due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
