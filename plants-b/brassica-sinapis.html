Brassica sinapis

Brassica sinapis, commonly known as mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stem and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its seeds, which are enclosed in siliques, are a key part of its reproductive cycle.

Brassica sinapis is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is widely cultivated for its seeds and leaves.
