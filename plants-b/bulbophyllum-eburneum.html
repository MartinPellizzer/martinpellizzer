Bulbophyllum eburneum

Bulbophyllum eburneum, commonly known as the ivory orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, pale green to ivory-colored flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the forest floor.

Bulbophyllum eburneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
