Bursera gummifera var. pubescens

Bursera gummifera var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, open areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
