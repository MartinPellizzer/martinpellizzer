Begonia harlingii

Begonia harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
