Berberis vulgaris var. turcomanica

Berberis vulgaris var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
