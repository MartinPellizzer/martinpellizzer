Bouteloua rigidiseta var. chihuahuana

Bouteloua rigidiseta var. chihuahuana, commonly known as Chihuahua threeawn, is a native grass species found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, bristly awns that give it its common name. This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its foliage is often used in traditional landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
