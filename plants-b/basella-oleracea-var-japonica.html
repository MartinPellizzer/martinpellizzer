Basella oleracea var. japonica

Basella oleracea var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese leaf spinach, is a leafy vegetable native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are tender and slightly succulent. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or edible plant. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are used in various Asian cuisines.

It is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and versatile species.
