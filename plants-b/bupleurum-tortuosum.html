Bupleurum tortuosum

Bupleurum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in a sprawling or ascending manner. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Bupleurum tortuosum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
