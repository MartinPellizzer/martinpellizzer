Boehmeria palmata

Boehmeria palmata, commonly known as the palmate nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length.

Boehmeria palmata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
