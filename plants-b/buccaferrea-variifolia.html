Buccaferrea variifolia

Buccaferrea variifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf buccaferrea, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in inflorescences, while its fruits are fleshy berries that attract various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
