Bactris hylophila var. macrocarpa

Bactris hylophila var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach impressive lengths. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are often consumed by both wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
