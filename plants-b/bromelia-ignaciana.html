Bromelia ignaciana

Bromelia ignaciana, commonly known as the red bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater, which serves as a microhabitat for various small organisms. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bromelia ignaciana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
