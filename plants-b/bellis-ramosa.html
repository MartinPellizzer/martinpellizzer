Bellis ramosa

Bellis ramosa, commonly known as the Shiny Woodsorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that gives them a shiny appearance. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and often have a white or pinkish center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
