Baccharis arbutifolia var. jamesonii

Baccharis arbutifolia var. jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's waxleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in panicles during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
