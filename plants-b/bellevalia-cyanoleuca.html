Bellevalia cyanoleuca

Bellevalia cyanoleuca, commonly known as the blue-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bellevalia cyanoleuca is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
