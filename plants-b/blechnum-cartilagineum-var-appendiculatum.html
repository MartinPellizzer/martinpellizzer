Blechnum cartilagineum var. appendiculatum

Blechnum cartilagineum var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, cartilaginous texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that give it a dense appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
