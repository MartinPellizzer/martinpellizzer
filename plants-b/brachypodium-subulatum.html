Brachypodium subulatum

Brachypodium subulatum, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and as a model organism in plant research.
