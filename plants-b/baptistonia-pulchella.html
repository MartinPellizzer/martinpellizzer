Baptistonia pulchella

Baptistonia pulchella, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in coastal or dune environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baptistonia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
