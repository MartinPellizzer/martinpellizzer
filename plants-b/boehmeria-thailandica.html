Boehmeria thailandica

Boehmeria thailandica, commonly known as Thai hemp or Thai flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant is known for its strong, durable fibers, which have been traditionally used for making textiles and paper. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant is valued for both its industrial and ecological significance.
