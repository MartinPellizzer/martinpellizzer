Bromopsis canadensis

Bromopsis canadensis, commonly known as Canada blue grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Bromopsis canadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
