Bursera macdougalii

Bursera macdougalii, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in limestone soils, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its small, white flowers are followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

The plant is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
