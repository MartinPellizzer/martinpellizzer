Bignonia obtusifolia

Bignonia obtusifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with rounded leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The fruit of the plant is a long, narrow pod that contains several seeds.
