Bactris turbinocarpa

Bactris turbinocarpa, commonly known as the turbinocarpus palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, spiky fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
