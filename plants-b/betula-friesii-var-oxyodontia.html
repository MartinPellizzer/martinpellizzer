Betula friesii var. oxyodontia

Betula friesii var. oxyodontia, commonly known as the white birch.

It is a variant of the white birch species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
