Berberis vulgaris var. enuclea

Berberis vulgaris var. enuclea, commonly known as the thorned barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, upright stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
