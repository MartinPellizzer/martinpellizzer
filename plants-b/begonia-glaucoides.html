Begonia glaucoides

Begonia glaucoides, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy, bluish sheen. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia glaucoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
