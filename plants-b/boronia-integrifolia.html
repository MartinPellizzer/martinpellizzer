Boronia integrifolia

Boronia integrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boronia integrifolia is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
