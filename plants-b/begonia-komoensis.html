Begonia komoensis

Begonia komoensis, commonly known as the Komo begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, often velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and horticultural collections.
