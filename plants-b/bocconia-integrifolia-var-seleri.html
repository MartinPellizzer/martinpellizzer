Bocconia integrifolia var. seleri

Bocconia integrifolia var. seleri, commonly known as the Seler's bocconia, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy surface and a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
