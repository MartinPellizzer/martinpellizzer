Brachychiton chrysocarpus

Brachychiton chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden fruit brachychiton, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow flowers during the wet season. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Brachychiton chrysocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
