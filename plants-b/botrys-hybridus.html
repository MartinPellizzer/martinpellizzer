Botrys hybridus

Botrys hybridus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer. Botrys hybridus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
