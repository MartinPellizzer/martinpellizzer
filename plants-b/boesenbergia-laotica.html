Boesenbergia laotica

Boesenbergia laotica, commonly known as the Laos ginger or Laotian yellow ginger, is a perennial herb belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine.

Boesenbergia laotica is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Laos, Thailand, and Vietnam.
