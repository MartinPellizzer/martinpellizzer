Barnebydendron

Barnebydendron, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a member of the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Barnebydendron is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
