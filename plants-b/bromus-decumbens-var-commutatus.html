Bromus decumbens var. commutatus

Bromus decumbens var. commutatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or smooth bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
