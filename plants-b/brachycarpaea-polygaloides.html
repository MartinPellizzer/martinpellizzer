Brachycarpaea polygaloides

Brachycarpaea polygaloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, contributing to its overall appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert environments.
