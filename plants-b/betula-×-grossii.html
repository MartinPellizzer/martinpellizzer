Betula × grossii

Betula × grossii, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a cross between the paper birch (Betula papyrifera) and the white birch (Betula pendula).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of lighter-colored tissue. The plant has slender, pendulous branches and produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

Betula × grossii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
