Blastus pulverulentus

Blastus pulverulentus, commonly known as the dusty bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, while its stems may emerge from the water's surface. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and relies on capturing prey to supplement its nutrient intake.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
