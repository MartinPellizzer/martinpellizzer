Betula pendula var. obscura

Betula pendula var. obscura, commonly known as the purple betula or the warty birch, is a variant of the silver birch characterized by its distinctive bark and foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and turn a striking yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and often has a warty texture, especially when young.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
