Begonia pedatifida

Begonia pedatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant and varied leaf patterns.

Begonia pedatifida prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to shaded gardens.
