Bupleurum latissimum

Bupleurum latissimum, commonly known as broadleaf angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Bupleurum latissimum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
