Buchosia aquatica

Buchosia aquatica, commonly known as the water buchus, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving waters, where it forms dense mats on the surface. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Buchosia aquatica is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

It plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
