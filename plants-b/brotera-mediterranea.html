Brotera mediterranea

Brotera mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean brotera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, demonstrating a degree of drought tolerance.
