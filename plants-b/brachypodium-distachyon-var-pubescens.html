Brachypodium distachyon var. pubescens

Brachypodium distachyon var. pubescens, commonly known as pubescent bristlegrass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which give it its common name. This variant is often used in botanical research due to its genetic similarity to Brachypodium distachyon, a model organism in plant biology. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a compact, tufted growth form.

Its small, two-flowered spikelets are arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
