Bombacopsis sepium

Bombacopsis sepium, commonly known as the Himalayan banyan, is a large, woody vine native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its extensive root system and aerial roots that form a dense network. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

It is often found in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
