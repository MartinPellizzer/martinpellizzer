Borago officinalis var. variegata

Borago officinalis var. variegata, commonly known as blueweed or variegated borage, is a cultivar of the borage plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in temperate climates, featuring hairy, blue-green leaves with white and yellow margins. The plant produces star-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its mild, peppery taste when the leaves are young.
