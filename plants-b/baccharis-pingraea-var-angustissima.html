Baccharis pingraea var. angustissima

Baccharis pingraea var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. It has a woody stem and produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
