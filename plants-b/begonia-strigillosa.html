Begonia strigillosa

Begonia strigillosa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a contrasting color on the reverse side. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
