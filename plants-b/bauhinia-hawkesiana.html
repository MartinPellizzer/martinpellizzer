Bauhinia hawkesiana

Bauhinia hawkesiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well adapted to dry conditions.
