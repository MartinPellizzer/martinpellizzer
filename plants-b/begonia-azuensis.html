Begonia azuensis

Begonia azuensis, commonly known as the Azuensis begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Begonia azuensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
