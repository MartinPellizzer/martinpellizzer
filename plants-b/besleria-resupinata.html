Besleria resupinata

Besleria resupinata, commonly known as the hanging begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often adorned with silver markings. Its inflorescences consist of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Besleria resupinata thrives in humid, well-drained conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
