Begonia pumila

Begonia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative yet low-maintenance plant.
