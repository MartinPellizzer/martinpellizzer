Blumea intermedia

Blumea intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Blumea intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
