Bellardiochloa violacea var. anthoxanthoides

Bellardiochloa violacea var. anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the yellow beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
