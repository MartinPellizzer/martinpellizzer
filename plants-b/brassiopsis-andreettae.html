Brassiopsis andreettae

Brassiopsis andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's brassia, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration that includes shades of purple and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
