Buddleja missionis

Buddleja missionis, commonly known as the Missionary buddleia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Buddleja missionis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
