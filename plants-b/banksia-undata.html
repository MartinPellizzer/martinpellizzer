Banksia undata

Banksia undata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cone-like inflorescences. Banksia undata is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in dry, sandy environments.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
