Balsamina javensis

Balsamina javensis, commonly known as the Java balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and often feature a distinctive spur at the base.

Balsamina javensis is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often found in gardens and greenhouses.
