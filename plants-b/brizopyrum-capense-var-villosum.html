Brizopyrum capense var. villosum

Brizopyrum capense var. villosum, commonly known as the silky broomrape, is a parasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
