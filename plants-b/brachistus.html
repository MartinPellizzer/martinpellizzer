Brachistus

Brachistus, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of toothed leaves and a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Brachistus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
