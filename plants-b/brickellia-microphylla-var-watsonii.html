Brickellia microphylla var. watsonii

Brickellia microphylla var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's brickellia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to coastal and inland regions. It is often found in open woodlands and prairies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
