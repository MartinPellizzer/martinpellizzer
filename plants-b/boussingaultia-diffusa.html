Boussingaultia diffusa

Boussingaultia diffusa, commonly known as the red-flowered rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

Boussingaultia diffusa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in arid climates.
