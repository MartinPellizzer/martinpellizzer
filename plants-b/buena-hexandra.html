Buena hexandra

Buena hexandra, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers have a distinctive tubular shape.

Buena hexandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
