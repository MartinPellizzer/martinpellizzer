Boerhavia eglandulosa

Boerhavia eglandulosa, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Boerhavia eglandulosa is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
