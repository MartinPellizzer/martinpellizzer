Bromus unioloides var. pubiflorus

Bromus unioloides var. pubiflorus, commonly known as the pubiflorous brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are covered in soft, pubescent hairs.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive flowering structure and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
