Brassica cheiranthos var. nevadensis

Brassica cheiranthos var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and has narrow, linear leaves.

The species is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
