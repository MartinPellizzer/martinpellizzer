Berberis xanthoxylon

Berberis xanthoxylon, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces small, yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
