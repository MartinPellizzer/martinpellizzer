Bactris speciosa

Bactris speciosa, commonly known as the pink pineapple, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, broad leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that resemble pineapples in appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Bactris speciosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential as a food source.
