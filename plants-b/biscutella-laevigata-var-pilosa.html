Biscutella laevigata var. pilosa

Biscutella laevigata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
