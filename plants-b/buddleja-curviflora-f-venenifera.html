Buddleja curviflora f. venenifera

Buddleja curviflora f. venenifera, commonly known as the toxic false butterfly bush, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is often overshadowed by its potential danger to livestock and pets.
