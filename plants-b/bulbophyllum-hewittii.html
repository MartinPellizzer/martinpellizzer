Bulbophyllum hewittii

Bulbophyllum hewittii, commonly known as the Hewitt's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its flowers typically feature a combination of green, yellow, and red hues, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Bulbophyllum hewittii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticultural gardens.
