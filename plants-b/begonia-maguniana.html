Begonia maguniana

Begonia maguniana, commonly known as the Magun's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
