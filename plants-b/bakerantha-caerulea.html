Bakerantha caerulea

Bakerantha caerulea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bakerantha caerulea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
