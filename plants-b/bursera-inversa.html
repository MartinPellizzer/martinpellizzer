Bursera inversa

Bursera inversa, commonly known as the Mexican lac tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain three seeds.

Bursera inversa is also valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
