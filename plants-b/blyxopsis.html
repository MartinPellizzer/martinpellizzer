Blyxopsis

Blyxopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have strap-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving them their common name. Blyxopsis species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and rocks rather than in soil.

These plants thrive in humid, shaded environments and are often found in cloud forests.
