Betula lutea var. alleghaniensis

Betula lutea var. alleghaniensis, commonly known as the Allegheny yellow birch, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, golden-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and has doubly serrated, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for furniture and cabinetry.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
