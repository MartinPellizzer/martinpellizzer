Begonia bimaensis

Begonia bimaensis, commonly known as the Bimaensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage and showy bracts. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with fleshy, smooth leaves that vary in shape and color. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by vibrant, leaf-like bracts that serve to attract pollinators.

Begonia bimaensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
