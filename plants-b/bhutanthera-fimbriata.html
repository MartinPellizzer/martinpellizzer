Bhutanthera fimbriata

Bhutanthera fimbriata, commonly known as the Bhutanese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral displays during the spring season. The tree has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces legume pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of Bhutan and India.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree in subtropical areas.
