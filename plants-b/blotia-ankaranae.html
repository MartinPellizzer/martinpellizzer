Blotia ankaranae

Blotia ankaranae, commonly known as the Ankarana orchid, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively in the Ankarana Reserve in Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant thrives in the humid, shaded environment of the rainforest, often growing on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its flowers are typically small, with vibrant colors that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from habitat destruction, Blotia ankaranae is considered critically endangered.
