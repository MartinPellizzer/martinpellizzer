Benzoin melissifolium

Benzoin melissifolium, commonly known as the honey-scented benzoin, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is often harvested for its use in perfumes and traditional medicine. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is valued not only for its fragrance but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
