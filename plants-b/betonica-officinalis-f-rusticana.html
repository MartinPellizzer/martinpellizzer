Betonica officinalis f. rusticana

Betonica officinalis f. rusticana, commonly known as the rustic betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Betonica officinalis f. rusticana has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
