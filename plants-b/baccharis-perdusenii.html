Baccharis perdusenii

Baccharis perdusenii, commonly known as the lost baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has small, narrow leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Baccharis perdusenii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.
