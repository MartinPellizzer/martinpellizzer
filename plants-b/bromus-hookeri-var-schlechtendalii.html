Bromus hookeri var. schlechtendalii

Bromus hookeri var. schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's brome, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bracts that are often hairy. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
