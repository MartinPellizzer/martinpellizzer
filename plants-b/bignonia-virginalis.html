Bignonia virginalis

Bignonia virginalis, commonly known as the yellow bellvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia virginalis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy blooms and vigorous growth.
