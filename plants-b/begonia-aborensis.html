Begonia aborensis

Begonia aborensis, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in forest understories. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a glossy surface, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Begonia aborensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
