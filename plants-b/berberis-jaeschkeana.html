Berberis jaeschkeana

Berberis jaeschkeana, commonly known as the Jaeschke barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, holly-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
