Beta cicla

Beta cicla, commonly known as beetroot or chard, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its roots are rich in nutrients, particularly nitrates and betalains, which give them their distinctive red or purple color.

Beta cicla is widely cultivated for both its edible leaves and roots, making it a versatile crop in many cuisines.
