Baccharis weirii

Baccharis weirii, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis weirii plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly as a food source for insects and birds.
