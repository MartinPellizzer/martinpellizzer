Balingayum decumbens

Balingayum decumbens, commonly known as the creeping balingayum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Balingayum decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
