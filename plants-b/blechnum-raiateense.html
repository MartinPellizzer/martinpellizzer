Blechnum raiateense

Blechnum raiateense, commonly known as the Raiatea fern, is a species of fern native to the Cook Islands.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that includes a narrow, elongated shape.

Blechnum raiateense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
