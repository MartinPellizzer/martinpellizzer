Bursera vazquezyanesii

Bursera vazquezyanesii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre Bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically grows in rocky soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in temperate and subtropical climates.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered, producing yellowish or greenish blooms.
