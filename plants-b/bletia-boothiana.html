Bletia boothiana

Bletia boothiana, commonly known as the white-faced bletia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish tinge. Bletia boothiana typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
