Berberis poluninii

Berberis poluninii, commonly known as the Polunin's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
