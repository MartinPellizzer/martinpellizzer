Begonia abbottii

Begonia abbottii, commonly known as the Abbott's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia abbottii is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and conservatories.
