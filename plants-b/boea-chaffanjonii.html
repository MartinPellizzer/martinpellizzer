Boea chaffanjonii

Boea chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon's ivy, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
