Bumelia obovata var. typica

Bumelia obovata var. typica, commonly known as the smooth bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical to oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in moist, sandy soils and is often found in forested areas.
