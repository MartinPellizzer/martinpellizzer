Brassica amblyorhyncha

Brassica amblyorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-nosed mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated, narrow pods. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, pinnately divided leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Brassica amblyorhyncha is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a minor weed in agricultural settings.
