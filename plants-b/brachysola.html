Brachysola

Brachysola, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and a short, upright stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. Brachysola is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
