Bursera paradoxa

Bursera paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Bursera, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds.

Bursera paradoxa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
