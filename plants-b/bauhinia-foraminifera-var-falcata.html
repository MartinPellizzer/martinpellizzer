Bauhinia foraminifera var. falcata

Bauhinia foraminifera var. falcata, commonly known as the fan-leaf bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the wet season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
