Bellevalia dubia var. boissieri

Bellevalia dubia var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
