Beaumontia longiflora

Beaumontia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered beaumontia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
