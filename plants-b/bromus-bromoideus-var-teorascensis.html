Bromus bromoideus var. teorascensis

Bromus bromoideus var. teorascensis, commonly known as the Teorascens brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, greenish-brown structures containing the flowers.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
