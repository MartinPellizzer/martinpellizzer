Bhesa sinica

Bhesa sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bhesa, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Bhesa sinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
