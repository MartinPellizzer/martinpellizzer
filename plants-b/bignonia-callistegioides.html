Bignonia callistegioides

Bignonia callistegioides, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
