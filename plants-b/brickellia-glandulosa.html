Brickellia glandulosa

Brickellia glandulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats.

Brickellia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
