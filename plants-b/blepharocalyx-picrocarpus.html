Blepharocalyx picrocarpus

Blepharocalyx picrocarpus, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent white veins and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The plant has a shallow root system and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.
