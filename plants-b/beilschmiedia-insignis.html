Beilschmiedia insignis

Beilschmiedia insignis, commonly known as the golden beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
