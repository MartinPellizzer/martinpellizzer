Berberis rubra

Berberis rubra, commonly known as red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis rubra is also known for its medicinal properties, as it contains alkaloids like berberine.
