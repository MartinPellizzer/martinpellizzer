Begonia bracteosa

Begonia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Begonia bracteosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
