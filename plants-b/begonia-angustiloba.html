Begonia angustiloba

Begonia angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
