Baccharis pulchella var. grisea

Baccharis pulchella var. grisea, commonly known as gray baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Gray baccharis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
