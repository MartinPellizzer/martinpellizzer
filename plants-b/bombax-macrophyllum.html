Bombax macrophyllum

Bombax macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant cotton tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that resemble cotton, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
