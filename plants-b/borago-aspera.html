Borago aspera

Borago aspera, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by its hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant features distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikes and bloom in early summer.

Borago aspera is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
