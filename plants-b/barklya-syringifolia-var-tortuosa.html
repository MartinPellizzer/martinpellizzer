Barklya syringifolia var. tortuosa

Barklya syringifolia var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted barklya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled bark that gives it its common name. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its flowers are usually pale yellow and appear in clusters, blooming in early summer.

The species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
