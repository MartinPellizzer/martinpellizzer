Bignonia nivea

Bignonia nivea, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Bignonia nivea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
