Billia

Billia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that have a silvery-white underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Billia thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
