Begonia mattampensis

Begonia mattampensis, commonly known as the Mattampady begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
