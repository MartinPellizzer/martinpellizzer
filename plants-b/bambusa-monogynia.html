Bambusa monogynia

Bambusa monogynia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a clumping growth habit. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Bambusa monogynia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
