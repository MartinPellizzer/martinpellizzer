Buddleja curviflora var. venenifera

Buddleja curviflora var. venenifera, commonly known as the poison bush, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has serrated leaves and produces small, greenish fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
