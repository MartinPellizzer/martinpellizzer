Berberis tinctoria

Berberis tinctoria, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for dyeing fabrics. Its roots contain berberine, a compound with medicinal properties.

It is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional herbal medicine.
