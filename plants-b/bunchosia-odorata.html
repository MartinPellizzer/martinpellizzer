Bunchosia odorata

Bunchosia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant bush or sweetbrush, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Bunchosia odorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
