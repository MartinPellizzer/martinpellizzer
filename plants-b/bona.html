Bona

Bona, commonly known as the Bona plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. Bona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its growth habits make it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
