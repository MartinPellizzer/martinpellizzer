Biarum tenuifolium var. latifolia

Biarum tenuifolium var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are broad and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
