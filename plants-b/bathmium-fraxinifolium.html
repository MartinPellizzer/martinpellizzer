Bathmium fraxinifolium

Bathmium fraxinifolium, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
