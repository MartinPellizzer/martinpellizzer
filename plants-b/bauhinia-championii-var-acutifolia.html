Bauhinia championii var. acutifolia

Bauhinia championii var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Acute-leaved Crown Flower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces showy, raceme-like inflorescences of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
