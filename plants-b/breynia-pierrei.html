Breynia pierrei

Breynia pierrei, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red to orange leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Breynia pierrei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
