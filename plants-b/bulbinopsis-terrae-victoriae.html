Bulbinopsis terrae-victoriae

Bulbinopsis terrae-victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria's bulbous orchid, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the summer months. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
