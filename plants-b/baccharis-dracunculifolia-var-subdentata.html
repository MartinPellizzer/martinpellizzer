Baccharis dracunculifolia var. subdentata

Baccharis dracunculifolia var. subdentata, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
