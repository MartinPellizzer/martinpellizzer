Bupleurum bicaule

Bupleurum bicaule, commonly known as the two-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a slender, branched stem that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Bupleurum bicaule is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments.
