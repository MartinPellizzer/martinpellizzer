Brayopsis colombiana subsp. colombiana

Brayopsis colombiana subsp.

colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian brayopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small fauna.
