Buprestis juncea

Buprestis juncea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed jewel beetle, is a species of beetle belonging to the family Buprestidae, known for their metallic green or blue coloration.

While not a plant, it is often associated with specific host plants such as willows and poplars, where its larvae develop. The beetle's life cycle involves a significant portion spent in the soil, feeding on the roots of these plants. Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, mature forest ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species in certain regions.
