Betula szaferi

Betula szaferi, commonly known as Szafer's birch, is a rare and ornamental tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, alders, and hazels. The plant is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pinkish or reddish inner layer, giving it a striking visual appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

Betula szaferi is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks due to its unique bark and delicate foliage.
