Berteroa incana var. prolifera

Berteroa incana var. prolifera, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a branching stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and also covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
