Bulbophyllum schwarzii

Bulbophyllum schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

It is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and unique morphology.
