Belvisia digitata

Belvisia digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by red or orange fruits. Belvisia digitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
