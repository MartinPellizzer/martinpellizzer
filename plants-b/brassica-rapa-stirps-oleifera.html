Brassica rapa stirps oleifera

Brassica rapa stirps oleifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds rich in oil. It is cultivated for its oilseed, which is used in cooking, biodiesel, and industrial applications.

Its botanical features make it a significant crop in agriculture and horticulture.
