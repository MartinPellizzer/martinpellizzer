Barbarea × rohlenae

Barbarea × rohlenae, commonly known as the hybrid winter cress, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Barbarea vulgaris and Barbarea nemorum, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
