Bauhinia apertilobata

Bauhinia apertilobata, commonly known as the yellow bauhinia, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Bauhinia apertilobata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
