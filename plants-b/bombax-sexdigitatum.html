Bombax sexdigitatum

Bombax sexdigitatum, commonly known as the white silk cotton tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. The tree has a broad, umbrella-shaped crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
