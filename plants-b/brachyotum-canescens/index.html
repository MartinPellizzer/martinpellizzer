Brachyotum Canescens

White cottonwood, scientifically known as Brachyotum canescens, is a fast-growing tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its soft, fibrous bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. This tree is commonly found in riparian zones and can thrive in sandy or clay soils with good drainage. Its lightweight, fluffy seeds are dispersed by the wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed areas quickly. Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, the white cottonwood plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
