Byrsonima bucidifolia

Byrsonima bucidifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or buri, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a straight, slender trunk and a rounded crown, making it a distinctive species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
