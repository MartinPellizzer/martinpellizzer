Boerhavia verticillata

Boerhavia verticillata, commonly known as redroot sandplume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices for various health benefits.
