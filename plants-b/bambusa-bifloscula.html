Bambusa bifloscula

Bambusa bifloscula, commonly known as the twin-flowered bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender bamboo that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in pairs, giving it its common name. Bambusa bifloscula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
