Bufonia caespitosa

Bufonia caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bufonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear clusters of tiny, inconspicuous blooms.

Bufonia caespitosa is noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in African herbal medicine.
