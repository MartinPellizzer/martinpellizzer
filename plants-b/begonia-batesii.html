Begonia batesii

Begonia batesii, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or white variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
