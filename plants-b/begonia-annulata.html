Begonia annulata

Begonia annulata, commonly known as the ringed begonia, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, rosette-shaped foliage with a distinctive ring-like pattern on the leaves. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in both horticultural and botanical settings.
