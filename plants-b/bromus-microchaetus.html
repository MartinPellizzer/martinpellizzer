Bromus microchaetus

Bromus microchaetus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Bromus microchaetus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
