Buddleja integrifolia

Buddleja integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja integrifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
