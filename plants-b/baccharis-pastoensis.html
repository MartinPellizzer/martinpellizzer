Baccharis pastoensis

Baccharis pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Baccharis pastoensis is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
