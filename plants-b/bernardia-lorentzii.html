Bernardia lorentzii

Bernardia lorentzii, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
