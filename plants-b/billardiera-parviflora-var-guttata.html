Billardiera parviflora var. guttata

Billardiera parviflora var. guttata, commonly known as the spotted flame vine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive yellow throat, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
