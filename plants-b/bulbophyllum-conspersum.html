Bulbophyllum conspersum

Bulbophyllum conspersum, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
