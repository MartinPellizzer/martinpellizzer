Brassica repanda var. blancoana

Brassica repanda var. blancoana, commonly known as the white-flowered mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape depending on the environment.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
