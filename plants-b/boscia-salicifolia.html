Boscia salicifolia

Boscia salicifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Boscia salicifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
