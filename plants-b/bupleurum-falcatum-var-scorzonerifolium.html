Bupleurum falcatum var. scorzonerifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the scorzonerifolium thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This variety is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
