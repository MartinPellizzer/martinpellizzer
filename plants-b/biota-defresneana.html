Biota defresneana

Biota defresneana, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Biota defresneana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects and animals.
