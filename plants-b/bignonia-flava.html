Bignonia flava

Bignonia flava, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces long, slender seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or in woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
