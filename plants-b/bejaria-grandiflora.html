Bejaria grandiflora

Bejaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bejaria, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Bejaria grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
