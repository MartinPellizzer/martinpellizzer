Boea swinhoei var. diffusior

Boea swinhoei var. diffusior, commonly known as the variegated ivy.

This plant is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The leaves are often heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is popular as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or trained on trellises due to its attractive foliage.
