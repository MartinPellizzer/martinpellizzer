Blechnum corcovadense

Blechnum corcovadense, commonly known as the coral fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern.

Blechnum corcovadense is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
