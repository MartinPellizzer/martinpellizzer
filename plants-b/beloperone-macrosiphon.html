Beloperone macrosiphon

Beloperone macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant siphon grass, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often coated in a fine layer of white hairs. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Beloperone macrosiphon is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, with roots that help stabilize the soil in marshy areas.
