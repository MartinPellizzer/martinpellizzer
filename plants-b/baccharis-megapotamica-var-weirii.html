Baccharis megapotamica var. weirii

Baccharis megapotamica var. weirii, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
