Bidens diversa subsp. diversa

Bidens diversa subsp.

diversa, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals and prominent yellow stamens. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

It is also known for its rapid growth and prolific seed production, which contributes to its invasive potential in some regions.
