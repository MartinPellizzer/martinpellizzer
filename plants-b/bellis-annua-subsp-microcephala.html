Bellis annua subsp. microcephala

Bellis annua subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as the common daisy, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, white flower heads with yellow central disks.

The plant is characterized by its short stature and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom from early spring to late summer, adding a delicate touch of white to gardens.
