Barleria arabica

Barleria arabica, commonly known as red sandalwood or red belleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
