Besleria dichrus

Besleria dichrus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Besleria dichrus is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
