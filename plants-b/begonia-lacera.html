Begonia lacera

Begonia lacera, commonly known as the trailing begonia, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, often bi-colored flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Begonia lacera is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
