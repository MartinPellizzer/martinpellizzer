Brosimum gaudichaudii f. macrophyllum

Brosimum gaudichaudii f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf balsa, is a variant of the balsa tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its lightweight, porous wood, which has been historically used for model building and other crafts. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
