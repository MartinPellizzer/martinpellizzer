Blechnum peruvianum

Blechnum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian holly fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
