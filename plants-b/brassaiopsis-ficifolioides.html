Brassaiopsis ficifolioides

Brassaiopsis ficifolioides, commonly known as the fig-leaf banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
