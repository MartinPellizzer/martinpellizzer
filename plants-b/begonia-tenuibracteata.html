Begonia tenuibracteata

Begonia tenuibracteata, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces unique, tubular bracts that enclose the actual flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia tenuibracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage and vibrant blooms.
