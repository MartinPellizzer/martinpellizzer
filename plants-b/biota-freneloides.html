Biota freneloides

Biota freneloides, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters.

Biota freneloides is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
