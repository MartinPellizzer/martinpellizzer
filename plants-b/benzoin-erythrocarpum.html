Benzoin erythrocarpum

Benzoin erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited benzoin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its sap, which is resinous and aromatic, is harvested for use in incense and perfumery.

The botanical features of benzoin erythrocarpum make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
