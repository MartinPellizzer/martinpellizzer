Betula humilis var. zabelii

Betula humilis var. zabelii, commonly known as the Zabel's birch, is a small, slow-growing deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright form and white, peeling bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal visual interest. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
