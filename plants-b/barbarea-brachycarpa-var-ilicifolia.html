Barbarea brachycarpa var. ilicifolia

Barbarea brachycarpa var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the creeping bittercress, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round seed pods. Its leaves and seeds contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and along woodland paths.
