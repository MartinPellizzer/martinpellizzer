Batrachium capillaceum

Batrachium capillaceum, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The species is notable for its unique morphology, with elongated leaves that resemble feathers, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
