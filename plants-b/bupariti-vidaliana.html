Bupariti vidaliana

Bupariti vidaliana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Bupariti vidaliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
