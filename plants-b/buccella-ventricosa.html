Buccella ventricosa

Buccella ventricosa, commonly known as the bladder bladder, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like seed pods that enclose the seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The botanical features of Buccella ventricosa make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
