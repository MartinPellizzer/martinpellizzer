Bergenia milesii

Bergenia milesii, commonly known as the Armenian bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a distinctive rosette pattern. Bergenia milesii is adapted to cold and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
