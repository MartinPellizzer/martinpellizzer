Blepharis integrifolia var. setosa

Blepharis integrifolia var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.
