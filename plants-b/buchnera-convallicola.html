Buchnera convallicola

Buchnera convallicola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Buchnera convallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with good drainage and full sun.
