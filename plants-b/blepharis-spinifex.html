Blepharis spinifex

Blepharis spinifex, commonly known as the spiked blepharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The species is part of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
