Bothriochloa ischaemum

Bothriochloa ischaemum, commonly known as wild millet or sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or pale brown. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
