Bulbophyllum longebracteatum

Bulbophyllum longebracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
