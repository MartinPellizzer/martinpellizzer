Blechnum lherminieri

Blechnum lherminieri, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the fronds.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori.
