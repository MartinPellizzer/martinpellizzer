Berberis grevilleana

Berberis grevilleana, commonly known as the Greville's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.
