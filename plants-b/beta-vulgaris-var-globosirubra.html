Beta vulgaris var. globosirubra

Beta vulgaris var. globosirubra, commonly known as the red beet or table beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy roots that are typically red in color, though they can also be yellow or white. The leaves are heart-shaped, smooth, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for its edible roots and leaves.

It is rich in nutrients such as iron, folate, and fiber, making it a valuable crop in agriculture.
