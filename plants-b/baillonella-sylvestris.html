Baillonella sylvestris

Baillonella sylvestris, commonly known as the wild quinine tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of quinine-like compounds in its bark.
