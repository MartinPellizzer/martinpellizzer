Bulbophyllum holttumii

Bulbophyllum holttumii, commonly known as the Holttum's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces compact, pseudobulb-like structures that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less-than-ideal conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green, with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum holttumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
