Boerhavia anisophylla var. paniculata

Boerhavia anisophylla var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, paniculate inflorescences. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
