Burtonia tenella

Burtonia tenella, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Burtonia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
