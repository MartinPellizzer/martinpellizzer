Bupleurum longifolium

Bupleurum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose, terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and often have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Bupleurum longifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
