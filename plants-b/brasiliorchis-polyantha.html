Brasiliorchis polyantha

Brasiliorchis polyantha, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its natural habitat.
