Barjonia cymosa

Barjonia cymosa, commonly known as the clustered barjonia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense cymes, which are clusters of flowers growing from a single point. Barjonia cymosa is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with succulent leaves that store water.

It is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
