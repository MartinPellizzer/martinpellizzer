Blossfeldia liliputana var. formosa

Blossfeldia liliputana var. formosa, commonly known as the miniature pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and clusters of delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is typically low-growing, reaching only a few centimeters in height, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant. Its succulent nature allows it to store water in its stems, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions.

The variety formosa is distinguished by its more vibrant floral display compared to the typical form of the species.
