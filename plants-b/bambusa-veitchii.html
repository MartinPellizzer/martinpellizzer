Bambusa veitchii

Bambusa veitchii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Veitch's bamboo, is a fast-growing, clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, with a smooth, greenish-brown stem covered in a whitish layer called the pith. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are followed by feathery, light green to brown bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

Bambusa veitchii is also valued for its strong and flexible stalks, which can be used in construction and crafts.
