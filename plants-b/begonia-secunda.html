Begonia secunda

Begonia secunda, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
