Bupleurum karglii var. longipetiolatum

Bupleurum karglii var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in alpine meadows.
