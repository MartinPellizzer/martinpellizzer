Bursera aloexylon

Bursera aloexylon, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
