Bulbostylis thouarsii

Bulbostylis thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
