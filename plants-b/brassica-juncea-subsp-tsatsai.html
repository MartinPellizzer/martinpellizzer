Brassica juncea subsp. tsatsai

Brassica juncea subsp.

tsatsai, commonly known as Chinese mustard or tsatsai, is a biennial or annual plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters and is known for its edible leaves and seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agriculture and traditional herbal medicine.
