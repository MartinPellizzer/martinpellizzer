Bryonia quinqueloba

Bryonia quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed bryony, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny, orange-red berries. Its leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five lobes, giving it its common name.

Bryonia quinqueloba is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
