Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. telonense

Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. telonense, commonly known as the Balkan cicely, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and its yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a taproot system and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
