Bejaranoa

Bejaranoa, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" plant, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of bright red flowers that resemble a lion's tail, giving it its common name. Native to Madagascar, Bejaranoa thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a woody stem that can grow quite tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
