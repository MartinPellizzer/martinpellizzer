Bulbophyllum steffensii

Bulbophyllum steffensii, commonly known as the Steffens' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. The plant produces pseudobulbs, which are thickened stems that store water and nutrients, aiding in survival during dry periods. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Bulbophyllum steffensii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
