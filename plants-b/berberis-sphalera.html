Berberis sphalera

Berberis sphalera, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and small, bright green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
