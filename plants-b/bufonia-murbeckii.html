Bufonia murbeckii

Bufonia murbeckii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bufonia murbeckii plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing nectar and habitat for various wildlife species.
