Bursera stenophylla

Bursera stenophylla, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
