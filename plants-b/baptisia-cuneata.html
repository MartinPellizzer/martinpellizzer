Baptisia cuneata

Baptisia cuneata, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Baptisia cuneata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardens and natural landscapes.
