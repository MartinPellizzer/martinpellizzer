Bambusa pauciflora

Bambusa pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, India, and Indonesia. This bamboo is characterized by its slender, erect stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa pauciflora is valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
