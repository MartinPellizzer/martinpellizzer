Bignonia multijuga

Bignonia multijuga, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia multijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
