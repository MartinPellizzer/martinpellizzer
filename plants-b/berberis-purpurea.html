Berberis purpurea

Berberis purpurea, commonly known as the purple barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or erosion control in suitable climates.
