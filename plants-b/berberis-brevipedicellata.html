Berberis brevipedicellata

Berberis brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions and has thorny stems that help protect it from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
