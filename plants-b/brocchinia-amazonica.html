Brocchinia amazonica

Brocchinia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian brocchinia, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, waxy, strap-like leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant is notable for its ability to store water in its leaf axils, which helps it survive in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a delicate, spiky inflorescence.

Brocchinia amazonica is often found growing on trees or rocks, showcasing its adaptability to diverse ecological niches.
