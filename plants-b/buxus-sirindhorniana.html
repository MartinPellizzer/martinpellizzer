Buxus sirindhorniana

Buxus sirindhorniana, commonly known as the Thai boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Thailand.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and small, dark green leaves that are often used in horticultural designs. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 meter and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

Buxus sirindhorniana is also noted for its resilience to urban conditions, enhancing its utility in landscaping.
