Baccharis regnellii

Baccharis regnellii, commonly known as Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis regnellii is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
