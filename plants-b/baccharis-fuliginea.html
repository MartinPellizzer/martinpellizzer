Baccharis fuliginea

Baccharis fuliginea, commonly known as black baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and clearings. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
