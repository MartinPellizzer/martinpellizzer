Begonia argentea guttata

Begonia argentea guttata, commonly known as the silver-streaked begonia, is a strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its uniquely veined leaves.

The foliage features a pale green to silvery-white coloration with prominent, dark green veins that create a striking contrast. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance. Native to tropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
