Bromus ciliatus var. latiglumis

Bromus ciliatus var. latiglumis, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

The seeds are small and have a distinctive hairy lemma, aiding in wind dispersal.
