Boehmeria nivea f. viridula

Boehmeria nivea f. viridula, commonly known as the green-flowered nettle, is a variant of Boehmeria nivea, a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers and serrated, hairy leaves. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
