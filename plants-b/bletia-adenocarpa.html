Bletia adenocarpa

Bletia adenocarpa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is characterized by its strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. Bletia adenocarpa is notable for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle scent, particularly when crushed.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
