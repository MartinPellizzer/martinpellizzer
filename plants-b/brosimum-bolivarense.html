Brosimum bolivarense

Brosimum bolivarense, commonly known as the Bolivian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant is notable for its ability to produce latex, which has been historically used for various industrial purposes. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
