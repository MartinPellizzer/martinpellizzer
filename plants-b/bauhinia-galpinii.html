Bauhinia galpinii

Bauhinia galpinii, commonly known as the Natal orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. Bauhinia galpinii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
