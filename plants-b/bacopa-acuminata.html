Bacopa acuminata

Bacopa acuminata, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found near water bodies.

Bacopa acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive benefits.
