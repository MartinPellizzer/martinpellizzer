Boronia ledifolia var. rosmarinifolia

Boronia ledifolia var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with a compact, bushy growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
