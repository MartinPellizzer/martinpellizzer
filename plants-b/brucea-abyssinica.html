Brucea abyssinica

Brucea abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian Brucea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Brucea abyssinica is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of malaria and other ailments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
