Berberis brandisiana

Berberis brandisiana, commonly known as the Brandis barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative shrub.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
