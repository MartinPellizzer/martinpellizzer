Blechnopsis javanica

Blechnopsis javanica, commonly known as the Javan holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually divided into narrow segments and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Blechnopsis javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
