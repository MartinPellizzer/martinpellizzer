Bromus commutatus subsp. commutatus

Bromus commutatus subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the common brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each containing several tiny flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and its role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
