Bucephalandra pygmaea

Bucephalandra pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf Bucephalandra, is a species of aquatic plant native to the slow-moving streams and rivers of Borneo.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant thrives in low to moderate light conditions and prefers soft, slightly acidic water with good water circulation. It is often used in nano aquariums due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

Bucephalandra pygmaea is a popular choice for aquascapers due to its ability to form dense, carpet-like growths in the foreground of an aquarium.
