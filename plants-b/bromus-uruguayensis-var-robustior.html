Bromus uruguayensis var. robustior

Bromus uruguayensis var. robustior, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and culms that are typically erect and somewhat stiff. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is often branched and contains small, spikelet-like flowers.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
