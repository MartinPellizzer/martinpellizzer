Baccharis patagonica subsp. palenae

Baccharis patagonica subsp.

palenae, commonly known as Patagonian baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in composite inflorescences.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
