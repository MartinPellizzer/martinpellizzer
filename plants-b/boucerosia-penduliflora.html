Boucerosia penduliflora

Boucerosia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, delicate white flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Boucerosia penduliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
