Barleria barbata

Barleria barbata, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Barleria barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
