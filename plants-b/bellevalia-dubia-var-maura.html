Bellevalia dubia var. maura

Bellevalia dubia var. maura, commonly known as the Maura bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a touch of elegance to its habitat.
