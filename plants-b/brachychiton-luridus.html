Brachychiton luridus

Brachychiton luridus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Brachychiton luridus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
