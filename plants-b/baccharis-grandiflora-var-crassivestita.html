Baccharis grandiflora var. crassivestita

Baccharis grandiflora var. crassivestita, commonly known as the broad-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in arid and semi-arid environments.
