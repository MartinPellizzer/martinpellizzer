Bambusa nagalandiana

Bambusa nagalandiana, commonly known as the Nagaland bamboo, is a species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, grass-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This bamboo is valued for its fast growth and is used in local construction and crafts.
