Bulbophyllum klossii

Bulbophyllum klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

The botanical features of Bulbophyllum klossii make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
