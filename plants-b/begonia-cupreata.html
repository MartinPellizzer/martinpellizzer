Begonia cupreata

Begonia cupreata, commonly known as the copperleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring leaves that range from reddish-purple to bronze hues. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Begonia cupreata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant coloration.
