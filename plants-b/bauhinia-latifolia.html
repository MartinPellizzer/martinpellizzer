Bauhinia latifolia

Bauhinia latifolia, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal.

Bauhinia latifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
