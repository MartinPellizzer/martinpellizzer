Bromus rigidus

Bromus rigidus, commonly known as rigid brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, wastelands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and sometimes pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Bromus rigidus is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
