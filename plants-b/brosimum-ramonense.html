Brosimum ramonense

Brosimum ramonense, commonly known as the ramon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, including parts of Mexico, Guatemala, and Costa Rica. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
