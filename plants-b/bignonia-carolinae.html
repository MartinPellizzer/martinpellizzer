Bignonia carolinae

Bignonia carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia carolinae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
