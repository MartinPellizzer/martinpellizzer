Bourreria pulchra

Bourreria pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tamarind, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Bourreria pulchra is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
