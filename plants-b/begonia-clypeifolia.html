Begonia clypeifolia

Begonia clypeifolia, commonly known as the shieldleaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners and collectors who appreciate its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
