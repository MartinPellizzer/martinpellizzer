Blitum bonus-henricus var. erosum

Blitum bonus-henricus var. erosum, commonly known as the Pacific coast speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal prairies and dunes.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the coastal maritime environment.
