Betula rhombibracteata

Betula rhombibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typically pollinated by wind. Its flowers appear in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits that disperse via wind.

Betula rhombibracteata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
