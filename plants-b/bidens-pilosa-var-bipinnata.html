Bidens pilosa var. bipinnata

Bidens pilosa var. bipinnata, commonly known as the twinleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers with two distinct petals. The leaves are usually pinnately divided, giving the plant its "bipinnata" designation. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
