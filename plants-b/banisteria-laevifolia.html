Banisteria laevifolia

Banisteria laevifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
