Bulbophyllum guttifilum

Bulbophyllum guttifilum, commonly known as the Orange-veined Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, brightly colored flowers with orange veins that contrast with its green petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
