Bufonia anatolica var. elatior

Bufonia anatolica var. elatior, commonly known as the greater red helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple or red markings. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are borne in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Bufonia anatolica var. elatior is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
