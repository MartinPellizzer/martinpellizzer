Baccaurea singaporica

Baccaurea singaporica, commonly known as the Singapore crabapple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
