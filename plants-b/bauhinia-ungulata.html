Bauhinia ungulata

Bauhinia ungulata, commonly known as the clawed bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven pointed lobes. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Bauhinia ungulata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
