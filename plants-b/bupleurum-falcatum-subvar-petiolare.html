Bupleurum falcatum subvar. petiolare

Bupleurum falcatum subvar. petiolare, commonly known as the narrow-leafed yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
