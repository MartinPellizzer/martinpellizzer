Balingayum

Balingayum, commonly known as the Philippine spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces a tall stem with narrow, strap-like leaves and blooms during the summer months. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Balingayum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a symbol of resilience in local culture.
