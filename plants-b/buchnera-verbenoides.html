Buchnera verbenoides

Buchnera verbenoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Buchnera verbenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
