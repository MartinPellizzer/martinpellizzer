Bauhinia corymbosa var. longipes

Bauhinia corymbosa var. longipes, commonly known as the longipes bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its traditional uses in folk medicine.
