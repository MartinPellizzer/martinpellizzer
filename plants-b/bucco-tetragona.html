Bucco tetragona

Bucco tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled bucco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, four-angled stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
