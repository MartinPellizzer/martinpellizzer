Bramia rotundifolia

Bramia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bramia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
