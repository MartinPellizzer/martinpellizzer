Barleria puccionii

Barleria puccionii, commonly known as the purple barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, with two prominent lobes at the front, and are borne in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive floral display.
