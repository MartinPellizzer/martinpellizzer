Blumea dasycoma

Blumea dasycoma, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and ovate, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Blumea dasycoma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
