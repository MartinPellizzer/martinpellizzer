Barbarea dentata

Barbarea dentata, commonly known as the toothed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Barbarea dentata is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
