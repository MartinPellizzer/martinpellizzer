Blepharis maderaspatensis var. abyssinica

Blepharis maderaspatensis var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian hairless bluemouth, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal irrigation, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
