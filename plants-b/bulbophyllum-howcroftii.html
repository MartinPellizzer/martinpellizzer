Bulbophyllum howcroftii

Bulbophyllum howcroftii, commonly known as the Howcroft's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients to help it survive in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum howcroftii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
