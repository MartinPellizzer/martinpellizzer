Brachylepis ramosissima

Brachylepis ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Brachylepis ramosissima is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
