Bulbophyllum luteolabium

Bulbophyllum luteolabium, commonly known as the yellow-labiated orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers feature a distinctive yellow labellum, which is a specialized structure that plays a role in pollination.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and scientific study.
