Bursera pannosa

Bursera pannosa, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the dry forests of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree has a dense, rounded crown with thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive fragrance when broken.

Bursera pannosa produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.
