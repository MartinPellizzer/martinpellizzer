Bidens sandvicensis var. ovatifolia

Bidens sandvicensis var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as rainforests and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
