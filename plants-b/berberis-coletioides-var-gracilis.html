Berberis coletioides var. gracilis

Berberis coletioides var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
