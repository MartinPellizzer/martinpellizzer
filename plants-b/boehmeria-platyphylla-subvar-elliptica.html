Boehmeria platyphylla subvar. elliptica

Boehmeria platyphylla subvar. elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemp plant, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its strong, fibrous stalks.

Its fibres are used in traditional textiles and papermaking, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
