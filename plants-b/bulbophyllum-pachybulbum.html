Bulbophyllum pachybulbum

Bulbophyllum pachybulbum, commonly known as the thick-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces striking, brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is a epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures rather than in soil.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
