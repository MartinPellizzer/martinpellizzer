Barkhausia leucocephala

Barkhausia leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial herb is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that feature a distinctive white or pale yellow head. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
