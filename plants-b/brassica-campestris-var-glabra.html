Brassica campestris var. glabra

Brassica campestris var. glabra, commonly known as field mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its flowers develop into pods containing several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an agricultural weed in many regions.
