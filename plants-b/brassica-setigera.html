Brassica setigera

Brassica setigera, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has small, edible leaves and is sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The plant also serves as a host for various pests and is important in ecological studies.
