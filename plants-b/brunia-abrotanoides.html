Brunia abrotanoides

Brunia abrotanoides, commonly known as the Cape mayweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Brunia abrotanoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy soils.
