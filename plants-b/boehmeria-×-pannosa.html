Boehmeria × pannosa

Boehmeria × pannosa, commonly known as Chinese nettle or broadleaf nettle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. Boehmeria × pannosa is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
