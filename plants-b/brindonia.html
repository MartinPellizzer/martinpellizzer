Brindonia

Brindonia, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has aerial roots that help it anchor in soft, waterlogged soil and facilitate gas exchange. Brindonia plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

Its unique botanical features make it an important component of mangrove forests.
