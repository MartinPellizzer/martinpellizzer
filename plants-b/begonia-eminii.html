Begonia eminii

Begonia eminii, commonly known as the Emir's begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, Begonia eminii is a favored plant among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
