Bucco obtusata

Bucco obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.
