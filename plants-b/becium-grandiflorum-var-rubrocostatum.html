Becium grandiflorum var. rubrocostatum

Becium grandiflorum var. rubrocostatum, commonly known as the red-costated becium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored leaf margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and private collections.
