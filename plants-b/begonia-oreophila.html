Begonia oreophila

Begonia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that range from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage is usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, clustered leaves.

Begonia oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
