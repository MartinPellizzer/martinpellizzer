Biscutella sempervirens subsp. gibraltarica

Biscutella sempervirens subsp.

gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to withstand drought and is characteristic of Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat's flora.
