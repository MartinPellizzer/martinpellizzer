Brunfelsia gracilis

Brunfelsia gracilis, commonly known as the slender bracken or slender Brunfelsia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Brunfelsia gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates.
