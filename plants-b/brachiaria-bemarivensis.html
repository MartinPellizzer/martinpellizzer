Brachiaria bemarivensis

Brachiaria bemarivensis, commonly known as the Trinidad grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean region.

It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in coastal areas and riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface.

Brachiaria bemarivensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
