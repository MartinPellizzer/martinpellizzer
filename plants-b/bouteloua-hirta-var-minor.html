Bouteloua hirta var. minor

Bouteloua hirta var. minor, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce light green to brown seeds. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in native plant landscaping and restoration projects.
