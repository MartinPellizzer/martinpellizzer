Barleria speciosa

Barleria speciosa, commonly known as the showy barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of bright pink or red tubular flowers, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Barleria speciosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
