Barnadesia polyacantha var. villosa

Barnadesia polyacantha var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy barnadesia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with serrated edges.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
