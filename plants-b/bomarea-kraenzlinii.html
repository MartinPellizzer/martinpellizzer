Bomarea kraenzlinii

Bomarea kraenzlinii, commonly known as the silverleaf bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its ornate, spirally arranged leaves that often display a metallic sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its tuberous roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Bomarea kraenzlinii is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and is often grown as a decorative plant in shaded gardens.
