Biscutella atropurpurea

Biscutella atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Biscutella atropurpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
