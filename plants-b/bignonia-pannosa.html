Bignonia pannosa

Bignonia pannosa, commonly known as the silky trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. These flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia pannosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
