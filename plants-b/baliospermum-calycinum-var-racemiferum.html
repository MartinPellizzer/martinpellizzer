Baliospermum calycinum var. racemiferum

Baliospermum calycinum var. racemiferum, commonly known as the racemiferum variant of the silky milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
