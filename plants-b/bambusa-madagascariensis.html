Bambusa madagascariensis

Bambusa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bambusa madagascariensis is valued for its use in construction, crafts, and as a source of sustainable materials.
