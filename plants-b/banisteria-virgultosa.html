Banisteria virgultosa

Banisteria virgultosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, twisted appearance, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
