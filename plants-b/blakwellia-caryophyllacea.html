Blakwellia caryophyllacea

Blakwellia caryophyllacea, commonly known as the pink rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Blakwellia caryophyllacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
