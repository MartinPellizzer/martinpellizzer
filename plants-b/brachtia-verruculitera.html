Brachtia verruculitera

Brachtia verruculitera, commonly known as the woolly brachtia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, with a thick, fleshy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Brachtia verruculitera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and compact size.
