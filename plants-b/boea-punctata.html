Boea punctata

Boea punctata, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often marked with distinctive spots or markings. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season.

This plant is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal.
