Bulbophyllum schinzianum

Bulbophyllum schinzianum, commonly known as the Schinz's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This epiphytic orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
