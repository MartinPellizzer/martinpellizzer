Bolax triloba

Bolax triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Bolax triloba thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
