Bulbostylis floccosa var. pumilio

Bulbostylis floccosa var. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf cotton sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its compact size and fluffy appearance, making it a unique addition to wetland ecosystems.
