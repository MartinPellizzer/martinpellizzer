Bromus lanceolatus subsp. biaristulatus

Bromus lanceolatus subsp.

biaristulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three small florets.

This subspecies is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
