Begonia linauensis

Begonia linauensis, commonly known as the Linau begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
