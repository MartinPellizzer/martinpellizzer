Boopis anthemoides

Boopis anthemoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Boopis anthemoides is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
