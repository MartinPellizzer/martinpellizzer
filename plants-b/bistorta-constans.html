Bistorta constans

Bistorta constans, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive purple tinge on the underside, and it produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer.

Bistorta constans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
