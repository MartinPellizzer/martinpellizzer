Bursera simplicifolia

Bursera simplicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bursera, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in dry, tropical, and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal and aromatic applications.

Bursera simplicifolia is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
