Blepharocalyx suaveolens var. cuneatus

Blepharocalyx suaveolens var. cuneatus, commonly known as the sweet-scented tree or the fragrant beauty tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like aroma. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and glossy, providing a lush green canopy. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its visual and olfactory characteristics.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
