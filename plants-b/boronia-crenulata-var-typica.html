Boronia crenulata var. typica

Boronia crenulata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It is often found in coastal and tableland regions of New South Wales and Victoria.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
