Bradburya virginiana f. pascuorum

Bradburya virginiana f. pascuorum, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white or silver hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
