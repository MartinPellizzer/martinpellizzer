Boraginella microcalyx

Boraginella microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered bugloss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Boraginella microcalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
