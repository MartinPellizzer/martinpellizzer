Brassica pekinensis var. laxa

Brassica pekinensis var. laxa, commonly known as Chinese kale or gai lan, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves that are loosely clustered around a thick, edible stalk. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild, slightly bitter flavor and is a common ingredient in Asian cuisines.
