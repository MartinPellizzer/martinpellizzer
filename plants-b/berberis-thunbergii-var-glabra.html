Berberis thunbergii var. glabra

Berberis thunbergii var. glabra, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, bright orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and hardy nature.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
