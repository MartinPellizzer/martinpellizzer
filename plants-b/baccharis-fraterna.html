Baccharis fraterna

Baccharis fraterna, commonly known as fraterna baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.

Baccharis fraterna is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
