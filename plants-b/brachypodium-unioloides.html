Brachypodium unioloides

Brachypodium unioloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib.

Brachypodium unioloides is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in grassland ecosystems.
