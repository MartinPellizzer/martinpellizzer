Berberis thunbergii var. pluriflora

Berberis thunbergii var. pluriflora, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.
