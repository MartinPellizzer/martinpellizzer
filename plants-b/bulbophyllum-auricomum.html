Bulbophyllum auricomum

Bulbophyllum auricomum, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, ear-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
