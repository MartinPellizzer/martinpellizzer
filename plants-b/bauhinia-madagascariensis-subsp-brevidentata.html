Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp. brevidentata

Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp.

brevidentata, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, orchid-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
