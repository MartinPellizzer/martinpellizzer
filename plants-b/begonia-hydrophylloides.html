Begonia hydrophylloides

Begonia hydrophylloides, commonly known as the water begonia, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is known for its ability to float on the surface. Its flowers are small and typically white or pink, appearing in clusters along the stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
