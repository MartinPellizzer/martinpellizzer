Bupleurum falcatum var. persicum

Bupleurum falcatum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on both sides. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
