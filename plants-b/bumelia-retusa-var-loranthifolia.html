Bumelia retusa var. loranthifolia

Bumelia retusa var. loranthifolia, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, red, inflated fruits called follicles. Its bark is smooth and gray, often marked with shallow fissures, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

This species is also a host plant for several insect species, contributing to local biodiversity.
