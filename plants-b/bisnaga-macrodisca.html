Bisnaga macrodisca

Bisnaga macrodisca, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Bisnaga macrodisca is notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well adapted to its challenging ecological niche.
