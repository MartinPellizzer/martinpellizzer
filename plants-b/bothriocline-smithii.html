Bothriocline smithii

Bothriocline smithii, commonly known as the Smith's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand dry conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like structures.

Bothriocline smithii is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
