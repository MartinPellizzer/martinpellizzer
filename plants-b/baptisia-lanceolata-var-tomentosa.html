Baptisia lanceolata var. tomentosa

Baptisia lanceolata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

The varietal name 'tomentosa' refers to the dense, woolly hairs that cover its stems and leaves.
