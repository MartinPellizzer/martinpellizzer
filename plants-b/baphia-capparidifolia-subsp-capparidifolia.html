Baphia capparidifolia subsp. capparidifolia

Baphia capparidifolia subsp.

capparidifolia, commonly known as the African cabbagewood, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
