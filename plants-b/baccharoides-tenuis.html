Baccharoides tenuis

Baccharoides tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
