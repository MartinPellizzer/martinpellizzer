Baccharis chubutensis

Baccharis chubutensis, commonly known as Chubut shrub, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Baccharis chubutensis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
