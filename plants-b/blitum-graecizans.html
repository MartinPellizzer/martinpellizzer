Blitum graecizans

Blitum graecizans, commonly known as the Greek cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence, typically blooming in early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Blitum graecizans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
