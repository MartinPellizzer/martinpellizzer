Betula × glandulosa

Betula × glandulosa, commonly known as the paper birch, is a hybrid species within the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in cold climates and is native to regions of North America and Asia. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in forest ecosystems.
