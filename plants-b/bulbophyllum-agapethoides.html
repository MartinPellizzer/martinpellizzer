Bulbophyllum agapethoides

Bulbophyllum agapethoides, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it requires well-drained soil and moderate light for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
