Byttneria subulifolia

Byttneria subulifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf byttneria, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
