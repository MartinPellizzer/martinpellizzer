Berberis gyalaica

Berberis gyalaica, commonly known as the Gyalaica barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart and acidic. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Berberis gyalaica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
