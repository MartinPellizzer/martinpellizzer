Baccharis flexuosiramosa

Baccharis flexuosiramosa, commonly known as the flexible baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its branching structure and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a silvery or grayish underside, while its flowers are small and clustered in heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
