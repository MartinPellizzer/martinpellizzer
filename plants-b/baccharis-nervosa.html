Baccharis nervosa

Baccharis nervosa, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Baccharis nervosa plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
