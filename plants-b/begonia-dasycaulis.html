Begonia dasycaulis

Begonia dasycaulis, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
