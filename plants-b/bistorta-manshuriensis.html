Bistorta manshuriensis

Bistorta manshuriensis, commonly known as the Far Eastern bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are broad, lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Bistorta manshuriensis produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late summer.

It is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
