Brachychiton guymeri

Brachychiton guymeri, commonly known as the Guymer's bottle tree, is a unique species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen trunk that stores water, a feature adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Brachychiton guymeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
