Blitum bonus-henricus var. alpinum

Blitum bonus-henricus var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine bittercress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the western United States.

It features small, dark green, toothed leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with four petals. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments at high elevations. It has a shallow root system and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in alpine ecosystems.
