Bauhinia purpurea var. corneri

Bauhinia purpurea var. corneri, commonly known as the purple bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
