Bulbostylis smithii

Bulbostylis smithii, commonly known as the Smith's bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
