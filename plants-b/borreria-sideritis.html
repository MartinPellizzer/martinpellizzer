Borreria sideritis

Borreria sideritis, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss, a feature common in xerophytic species.

Borreria sideritis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
