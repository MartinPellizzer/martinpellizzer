Brassica napus var. hiemalis

Brassica napus var. hiemalis, commonly known as winter rapeseed, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces large, thick pods containing numerous seeds, which are used for oil extraction and as a source of animal feed. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of wax, aiding in water retention.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its agricultural and ecological benefits.
