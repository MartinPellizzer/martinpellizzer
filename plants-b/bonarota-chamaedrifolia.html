Bonarota chamaedrifolia

Bonarota chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Bonarota chamaedrifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
