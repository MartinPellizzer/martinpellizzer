Blumea arenaria var. honigbergeri

Blumea arenaria var. honigbergeri, commonly known as the Honigberger's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, consist of both ray and disk florets, producing a delicate blue or purple coloration.
