Baccharis tricuneata var. paramorum

Baccharis tricuneata var. paramorum, commonly known as the Paramour baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
