Bulbophyllum betchei

Bulbophyllum betchei, commonly known as the Betche's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum betchei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
