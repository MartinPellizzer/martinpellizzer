Baphiopsis parviflora var. villosa

Baphiopsis parviflora var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy baphiopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "villosa.". It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
