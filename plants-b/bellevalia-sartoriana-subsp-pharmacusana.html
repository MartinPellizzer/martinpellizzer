Bellevalia sartoriana subsp. pharmacusana

Bellevalia sartoriana subsp.

pharmacusana, commonly known as the medicinal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, growing in open areas such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
