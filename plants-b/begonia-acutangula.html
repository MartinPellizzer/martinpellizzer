Begonia acutangula

Begonia acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-angled begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
