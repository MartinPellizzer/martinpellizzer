Bothriochloa gracilis

Bothriochloa gracilis, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that resemble panicles. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
