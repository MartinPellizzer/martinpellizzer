Baccharis lehmannii

Baccharis lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis lehmannii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
