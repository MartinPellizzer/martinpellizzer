Bothriocline nyiruensis

Bothriocline nyiruensis, commonly known as the Nyiru mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations above 2,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its environment.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow with a central disc, making it an attractive species for botanical study.
