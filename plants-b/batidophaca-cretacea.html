Batidophaca cretacea

Batidophaca cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous fern, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Cretaceous period.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble those of modern ferns. The plant's structure suggests it was adapted to moist, temperate environments, likely near water sources. Fossil records indicate that it played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.

Its botanical features provide valuable insights into the flora of ancient Earth.
