Broussonetia papyrifera f. lucida

Broussonetia papyrifera f. lucida, commonly known as the glossy paper mulberry, is a variant of the paper mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking visual contrast to its pale, papery bark. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are used in traditional paper-making, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various landscapes.
