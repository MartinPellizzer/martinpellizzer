Begonia coursii

Begonia coursii, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Begonia coursii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
