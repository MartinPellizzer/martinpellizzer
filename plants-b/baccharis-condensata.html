Baccharis condensata

Baccharis condensata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Baccharis condensata is drought-tolerant and is used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
