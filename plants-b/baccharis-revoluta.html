Baccharis revoluta

Baccharis revoluta, commonly known as Texas bluestem, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental use.

Baccharis revoluta also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
