Bupleurum denticulatum

Bupleurum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Bupleurum denticulatum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
