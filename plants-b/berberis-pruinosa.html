Berberis pruinosa

Berberis pruinosa, commonly known as the silvery barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens.

Berberis pruinosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
