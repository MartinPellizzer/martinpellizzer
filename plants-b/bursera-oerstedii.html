Bursera oerstedii

Bursera oerstedii, commonly known as Oersted's bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
