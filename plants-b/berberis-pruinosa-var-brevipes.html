Berberis pruinosa var. brevipes

Berberis pruinosa var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems covered in a whitish, waxy bloom that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Its foliage consists of small, oval leaves that are dark green and have a slight pubescence.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
