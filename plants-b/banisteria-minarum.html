Banisteria minarum

Banisteria minarum, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Banisteria minarum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
