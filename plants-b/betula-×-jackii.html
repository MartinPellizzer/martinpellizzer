Betula × jackii

Betula × jackii, commonly known as the hybrid birch, is a cross between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula pubescens (downy birch).

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its white, peeling bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and forest edges.

Betula × jackii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
