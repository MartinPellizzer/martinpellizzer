Bulbophyllum curtisii

Bulbophyllum curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a dense inflorescence. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum curtisii is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
