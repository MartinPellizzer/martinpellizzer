Betula ermanii f. costata

Betula ermanii f. costata, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch.

It is a cultivar of the paper birch species, characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub with finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal color changes.
