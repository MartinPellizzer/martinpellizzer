Bupleurum falcatum proles neglectum

Bupleurum falcatum proles neglectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
