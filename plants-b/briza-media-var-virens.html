Briza media var. virens

Briza media var. virens, commonly known as green foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with green or yellow, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
