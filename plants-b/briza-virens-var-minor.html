Briza virens var. minor

Briza virens var. minor, commonly known as the lesser basket sedge, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ecological restoration and landscape design.
