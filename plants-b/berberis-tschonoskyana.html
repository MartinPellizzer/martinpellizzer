Berberis tschonoskyana

Berberis tschonoskyana, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
