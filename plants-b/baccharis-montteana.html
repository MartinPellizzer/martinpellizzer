Baccharis montteana

Baccharis montteana, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Baccharis montteana is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
