Bomarea coccinea

Bomarea coccinea, commonly known as the red bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its showy inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
