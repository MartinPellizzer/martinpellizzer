Bulbophyllum verecundum

Bulbophyllum verecundum, commonly known as the shy orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The orchid's flowers are typically small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.
