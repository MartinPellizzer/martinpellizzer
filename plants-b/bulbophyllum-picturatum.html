Bulbophyllum picturatum

Bulbophyllum picturatum, commonly known as the Painted Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in a variety of hues, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and showy appearance.
