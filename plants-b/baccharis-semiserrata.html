Baccharis semiserrata

Baccharis semiserrata, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit. The plant features small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Baccharis semiserrata is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its drought tolerance.
