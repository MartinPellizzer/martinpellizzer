Baccharis racemosa var. eupatorioides

Baccharis racemosa var. eupatorioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-gray flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a woody stem, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant also serves as a valuable food source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
