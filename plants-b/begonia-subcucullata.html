Begonia subcucullata

Begonia subcucullata, commonly known as the hanging begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists alike.
