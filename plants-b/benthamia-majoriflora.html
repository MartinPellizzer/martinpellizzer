Benthamia majoriflora

Benthamia majoriflora, commonly known as the large-flowered benthamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the species is known for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh conditions.

Benthamia majoriflora is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
