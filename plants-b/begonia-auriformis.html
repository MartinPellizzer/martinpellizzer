Begonia auriformis

Begonia auriformis, commonly known as the auricle begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with wavy edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia auriformis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
