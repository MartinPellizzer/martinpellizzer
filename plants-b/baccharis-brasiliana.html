Baccharis brasiliana

Baccharis brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis brasiliana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
