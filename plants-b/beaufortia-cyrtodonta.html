Beaufortia cyrtodonta

Beaufortia cyrtodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beaufortia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to orange-red flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Beaufortia cyrtodonta is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of local flora in its native habitat.
