Bromus janczewskii

Bromus janczewskii, commonly known as the Janczewski's brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Bromus janczewskii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
