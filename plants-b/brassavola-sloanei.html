Brassavola sloanei

Brassavola sloanei, commonly known as the Sloane's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and often pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid conditions.

Brassavola sloanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
