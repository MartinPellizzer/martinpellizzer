Boronia pilosa var. lancifolia

Boronia pilosa var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its specific variety name.

It is often found in coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
