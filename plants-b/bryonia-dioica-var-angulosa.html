Bryonia dioica var. angulosa

Bryonia dioica var. angulosa, commonly known as the angular bryony, is a variant of the common bryony plant.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae, characterized by its angular stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

It is typically found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
