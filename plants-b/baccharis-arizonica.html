Baccharis arizonica

Baccharis arizonica, commonly known as Arizona baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils. It has small, narrow leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces berries that are an important food source for birds.

Baccharis arizonica is also used in traditional medicine by some Native American tribes.
