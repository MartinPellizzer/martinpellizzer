Begonia dielsiana

Begonia dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually red or orange, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Begonia dielsiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
