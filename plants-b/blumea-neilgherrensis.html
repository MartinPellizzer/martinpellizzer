Blumea neilgherrensis

Blumea neilgherrensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri Blumea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in capitula, which are surrounded by pale green to white bracts.

Blumea neilgherrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
