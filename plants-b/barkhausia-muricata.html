Barkhausia muricata

Barkhausia muricata, commonly known as the rough bark daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and Mediterranean climates.
