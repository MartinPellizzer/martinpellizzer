Bulbophyllum amplebracteatum subsp. carunculatum

Bulbophyllum amplebracteatum subsp.

carunculatum, commonly known as the horned bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
