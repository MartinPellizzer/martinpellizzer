Bulbophyllum bismarckense

Bulbophyllum bismarckense, commonly known as the Bismarck orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from yellow to orange.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
