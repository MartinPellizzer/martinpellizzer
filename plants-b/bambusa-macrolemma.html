Bambusa macrolemma

Bambusa macrolemma, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a smooth, light green surface. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a sustainable material source.

Bambusa macrolemma is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
