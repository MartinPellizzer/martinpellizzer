Buddleja wrightii

Buddleja wrightii, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Buddleja wrightii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a favored plant among gardeners and wildlife enthusiasts.
