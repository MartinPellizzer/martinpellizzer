Belamcanda chinensis var. taiwanensis

Belamcanda chinensis var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan yellow iris, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive purple-pink streaked pattern. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
