Blechnum australe

Blechnum australe, commonly known as the Australian holly fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, bushy growth habit. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance featuring elongated, pointed segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
