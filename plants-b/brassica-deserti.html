Brassica deserti

Brassica deserti, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers and has a low, spreading growth habit.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or barren areas.
