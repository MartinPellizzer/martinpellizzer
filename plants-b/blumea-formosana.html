Blumea formosana

Blumea formosana, commonly known as the Chinese blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Blumea formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
