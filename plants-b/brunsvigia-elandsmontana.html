Brunsvigia elandsmontana

Brunsvigia elandsmontana, commonly known as the Knysna glory bush, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in controlled environments.
