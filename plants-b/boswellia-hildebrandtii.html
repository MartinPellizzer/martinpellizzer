Boswellia hildebrandtii

Boswellia hildebrandtii, commonly known as frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Horn of Africa. The tree is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is tapped from its bark and used in incense and traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
