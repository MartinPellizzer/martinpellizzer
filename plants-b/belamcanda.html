Belamcanda

Belamcanda, commonly known as the "yellow iris," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking orange or yellow blooms in the summer. Belamcanda thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
