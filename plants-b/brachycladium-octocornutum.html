Brachycladium octocornutum

Brachycladium octocornutum, commonly known as the eight-horned grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
