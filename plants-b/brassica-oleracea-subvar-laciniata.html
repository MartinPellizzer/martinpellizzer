Brassica oleracea subvar. laciniata

Brassica oleracea subvar. laciniata, commonly known as ornamental kale, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage family.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and crinkled leaves that come in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is typically grown for its aesthetic appeal rather than for consumption. It thrives in cool climates and is often used in gardens for its visual interest.

The leaves are usually harvested before they become too tough, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
