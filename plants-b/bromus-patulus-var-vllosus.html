Bromus patulus var. vllosus

Bromus patulus var. vllosus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
