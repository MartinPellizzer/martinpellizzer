Barleria crotalaria

Barleria crotalaria, commonly known as the yellow flame or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features showy yellow flowers with red or purple veins, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Barleria crotalaria is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
