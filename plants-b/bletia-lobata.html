Bletia lobata

Bletia lobata, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and are pollinated by specific insects.

Bletia lobata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
