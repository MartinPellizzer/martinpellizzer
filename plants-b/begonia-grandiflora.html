Begonia grandiflora

Begonia grandiflora, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia grandiflora is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and vibrant blooms.
