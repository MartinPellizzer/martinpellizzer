Blechnum cunninghamii

Blechnum cunninghamii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have a silvery sheen due to the presence of trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its rhizome is robust, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.
