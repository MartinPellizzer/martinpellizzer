Berberis khorasanica

Berberis khorasanica, commonly known as Persian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The berries are also used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
