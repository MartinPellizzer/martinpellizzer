Betula pendula f. crispa

Betula pendula f. crispa, commonly known as the hairy birch, is a variant of the silver birch characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves.

This cultivar exhibits a unique foliage pattern that gives it a decorative appearance, making it popular in ornamental horticulture. The leaves are typically smaller and more textured compared to the standard silver birch, with a silvery-white underside. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate urban environments.
