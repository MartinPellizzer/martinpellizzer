Bulbophyllum javanicum var. sumatranum

Bulbophyllum javanicum var. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on tree branches or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
