Bobartia gladiata subsp. teres

Bobartia gladiata subsp.

teres, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bobartia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to tolerate shade, making it a valuable species in ecological studies.
