Bupleurum longicaule

Bupleurum longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. Bupleurum longicaule is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
