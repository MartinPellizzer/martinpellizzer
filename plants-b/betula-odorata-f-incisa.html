Betula odorata f. incisa

Betula odorata f. incisa, commonly known as the fragrant birch, is a botanical variant of the silver birch.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is pale and smooth, with a subtle fragrance when rubbed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
