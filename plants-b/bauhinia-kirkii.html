Bauhinia kirkii

Bauhinia kirkii, commonly known as the Natal orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often shed during dry periods, and its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple. Bauhinia kirkii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
