Baphia elegans var. vestita

Baphia elegans var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf texture and coloration. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a dense, velvety surface that gives the plant its common name.
