Brassica nigra var. abyssinica

Brassica nigra var. abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian black mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has dark green, lobed leaves. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The plant is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.
