Blainvillea dubia

Blainvillea dubia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features delicate, elongated leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Blainvillea dubia is considered vulnerable and requires careful preservation efforts.
