Besleria disgrega

Besleria disgrega, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
