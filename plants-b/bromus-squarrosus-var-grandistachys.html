Bromus squarrosus var. grandistachys

Bromus squarrosus var. grandistachys, commonly known as the tall curly bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often curled or twisted.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
