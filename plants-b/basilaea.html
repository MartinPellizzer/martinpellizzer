Basilaea

Basilaea, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like mint and lavender. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinct, spicy fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or light purple, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Basilaea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
