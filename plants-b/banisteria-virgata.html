Banisteria virgata

Banisteria virgata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often woody and thorny. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Banisteria virgata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
