Basanacantha spinosa f. puberula

Basanacantha spinosa f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent spiny hibiscus, is a variant of the Basanacantha spinosa species.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions, characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Its spiny foliage and ornamental flowers make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and landscapes.
