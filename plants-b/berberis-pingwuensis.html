Berberis pingwuensis

Berberis pingwuensis, commonly known as the Pingwu barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
