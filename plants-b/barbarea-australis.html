Barbarea australis

Barbarea australis, commonly known as the Australian rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Barbarea australis is adapted to arid conditions and is commonly used in native gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
