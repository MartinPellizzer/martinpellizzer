Bidens urbanii

Bidens urbanii, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Bidens urbanii is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
