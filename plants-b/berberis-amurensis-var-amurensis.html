Berberis amurensis var. amurensis

Berberis amurensis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and alternate, compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and as a hedging plant.
