Balizia elegans

Balizia elegans, commonly known as the golden bignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Balizia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
