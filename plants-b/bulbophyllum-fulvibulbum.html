Bulbophyllum fulvibulbum

Bulbophyllum fulvibulbum, commonly known as the golden bulb orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and leaf litter.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
