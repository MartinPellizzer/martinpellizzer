Bauhinia khasiana var. tomentella

Bauhinia khasiana var. tomentella, commonly known as the Kashmir bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while the leaves are trifoliate or bipinnate, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is also cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
