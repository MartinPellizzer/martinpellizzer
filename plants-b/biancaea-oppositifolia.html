Biancaea oppositifolia

Biancaea oppositifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
