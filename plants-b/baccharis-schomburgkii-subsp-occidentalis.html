Baccharis schomburgkii subsp. occidentalis

Baccharis schomburgkii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western sand verbena, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
