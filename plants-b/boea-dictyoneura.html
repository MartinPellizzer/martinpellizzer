Boea dictyoneura

Boea dictyoneura, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to climb or trail, adding visual interest to gardens and indoor spaces.
