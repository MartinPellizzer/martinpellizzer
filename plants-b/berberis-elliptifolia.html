Berberis elliptifolia

Berberis elliptifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis elliptifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional medicine.
