Bicornella aurantiaca

Bicornella aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bicornella aurantiaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
