Berberis amurensis var. japonica

Berberis amurensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
