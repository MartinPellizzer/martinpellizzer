Bejaria glauca var. glandulosa

Bejaria glauca var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
