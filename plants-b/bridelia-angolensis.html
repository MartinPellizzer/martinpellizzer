Bridelia angolensis

Bridelia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola bridelia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
