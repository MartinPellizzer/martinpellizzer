Barbarea × adulterina

Barbarea × adulterina, commonly known as the hybrid winter cress, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Barbarea vulgaris and Barbarea stricta.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features erect stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This species is known for its adaptability and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and North America.
