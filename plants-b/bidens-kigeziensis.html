Bidens kigeziensis

Bidens kigeziensis, commonly known as the Kigezi ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the montane regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks, and it produces small, ribbed fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Bidens kigeziensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
