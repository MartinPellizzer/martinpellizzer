Borassus sonneratii

Borassus sonneratii, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, pale green surface and produces large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are used for thatching and roofing, while the trunk is valued for its strength and durability.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
