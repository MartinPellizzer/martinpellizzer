Ballota frutescens var. velutina

Ballota frutescens var. velutina, commonly known as velvet ballota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name and contributes to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
