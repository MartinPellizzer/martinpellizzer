Berberis albicans

Berberis albicans, commonly known as the white barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Berberis albicans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
