Balbisia miniata var. anomala

Balbisia miniata var. anomala, commonly known as the dwarf flame bean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Balbisia miniata var. anomala is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
