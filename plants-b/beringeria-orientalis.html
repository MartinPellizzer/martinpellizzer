Beringeria orientalis

Beringeria orientalis, commonly known as the oriental beringia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short, stout stem and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant's leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Beringeria orientalis is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and high UV exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited genetic diversity.
