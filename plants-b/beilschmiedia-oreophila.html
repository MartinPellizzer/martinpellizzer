Beilschmiedia oreophila

Beilschmiedia oreophila, commonly known as the cloud forest beilschmiedia, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

This evergreen tree typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests at high elevations. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
