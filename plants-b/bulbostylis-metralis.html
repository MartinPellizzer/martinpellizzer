Bulbostylis metralis

Bulbostylis metralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbostylis, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically inconspicuous and lack showy petals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
