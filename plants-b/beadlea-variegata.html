Beadlea variegata

Beadlea variegata, commonly known as the variegated beadlea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
