Bulbophyllum argyropus

Bulbophyllum argyropus, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and covered in a silvery or pale waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.
