Begonia scutifolia

Begonia scutifolia, commonly known as the shield-leaf begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Begonia scutifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
