Bumelia octosepala

Bumelia octosepala, commonly known as the eight-sepaled bumelia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and alternate, simple, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are usually greenish-yellow in color.

Bumelia octosepala typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
