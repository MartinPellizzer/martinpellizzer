Boswellia obliqua

Boswellia obliqua, commonly known as frankincense tree, is a small deciduous tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is harvested for use in incense and traditional medicine. The tree has a gnarled, twisted trunk and small, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
