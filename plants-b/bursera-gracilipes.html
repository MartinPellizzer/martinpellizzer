Bursera gracilipes

Bursera gracilipes, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Bursera gracilipes is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
