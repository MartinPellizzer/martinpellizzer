Bubon globosum

Bubon globosum, commonly known as the globe bubble vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that turn bright red when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bubon globosum is often cultivated for its unique fruit structure and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
