Baccharis pycnocephala

Baccharis pycnocephala, commonly known as the thick-headed baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are often gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Baccharis pycnocephala is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
