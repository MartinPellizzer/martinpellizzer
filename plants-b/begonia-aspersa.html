Begonia aspersa

Begonia aspersa, commonly known as the moss begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Begonia aspersa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
