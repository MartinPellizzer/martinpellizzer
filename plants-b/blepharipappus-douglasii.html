Blepharipappus douglasii

Blepharipappus douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are silvery-gray, covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
