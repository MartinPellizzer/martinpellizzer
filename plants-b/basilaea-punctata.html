Basilaea punctata

Basilaea punctata, commonly known as the spotted basil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers surrounded by a ring of pale green to white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Basilaea punctata is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
