Brachystephanus coeruleus subsp. apiculatus

Brachystephanus coeruleus subsp.

apiculatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a robust, upright stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
