Berberis buceronis

Berberis buceronis, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
