Begonia acuminata

Begonia acuminata, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia acuminata is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
