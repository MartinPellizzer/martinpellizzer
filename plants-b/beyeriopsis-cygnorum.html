Beyeriopsis cygnorum

Beyeriopsis cygnorum, commonly known as the swan's feather plant, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with tightly packed, elongated leaves that resemble feathers, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in dense, elongated clusters at the tip of the stem.

Beyeriopsis cygnorum is valued in horticulture for its striking foliage and ability to add a tropical aesthetic to indoor or shaded garden spaces.
