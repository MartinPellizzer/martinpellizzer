Byrsonima coriacea f. eglandulosa

Byrsonima coriacea f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian plum or wild plum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
