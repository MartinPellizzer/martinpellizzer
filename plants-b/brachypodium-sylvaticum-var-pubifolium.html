Brachypodium sylvaticum var. pubifolium

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. pubifolium, commonly known as the pubescent wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name "pubifolium.". The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in ecological studies and as a model organism in plant genetics research.
