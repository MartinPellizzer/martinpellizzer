Briza media var. caucasica

Briza media var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that sway in the wind. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
