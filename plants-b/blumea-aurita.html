Blumea aurita

Blumea aurita, commonly known as the golden blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. Blumea aurita is often found in moist, forested areas and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
