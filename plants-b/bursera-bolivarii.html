Bursera bolivarii

Bursera bolivarii, commonly known as the Bolivian frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its bark exudes a fragrant sap, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
