Beta vulgaris var. praecox

Beta vulgaris var. praecox, commonly known as the sugar beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with elongated, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of wax. Its flowering occurs in the second year, producing small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The botanical features of Beta vulgaris var. praecox make it an important crop for sugar production and industrial use.
