Buddleja vetula var. angustata

Buddleja vetula var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf false candleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers in late summer, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
