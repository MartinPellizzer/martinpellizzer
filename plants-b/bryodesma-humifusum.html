Bryodesma humifusum

Bryodesma humifusum, commonly known as the mossy lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its greenish to yellowish thallus, which is often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with algae, which provide essential nutrients through photosynthesis. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for ecological health.
