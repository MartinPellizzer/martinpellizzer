Bladhia racemosa

Bladhia racemosa, commonly known as the pink root or Indian turnip, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
