Banksia prolata subsp. archeos

Banksia prolata subsp.

archeos, commonly known as the ancient banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical flower spikes that produce small, urn-shaped flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is notable for its historical significance and is considered a rare and endangered plant.
