Brassica cretica subsp. hilarionis

Brassica cretica subsp.

hilarionis, commonly known as the Cretan mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
