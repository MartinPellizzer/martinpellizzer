Bouvardia micrantha

Bouvardia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered bouvardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bouvardia micrantha is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with moderate light and water.
