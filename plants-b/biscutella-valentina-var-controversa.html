Biscutella valentina var. controversa

Biscutella valentina var. controversa, commonly known as the Valiant Biscuitflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with four petals arranged in a cross, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
