Besleria coccinea

Besleria coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vivid red blooms that appear in clusters. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves and produces pendulous flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
