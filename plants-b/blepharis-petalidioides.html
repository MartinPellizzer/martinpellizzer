Blepharis petalidioides

Blepharis petalidioides, commonly known as the silverleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces delicate, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
