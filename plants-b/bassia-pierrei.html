Bassia pierrei

Bassia pierrei, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep roots and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions.

Bassia pierrei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
