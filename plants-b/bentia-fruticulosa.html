Bentia fruticulosa

Bentia fruticulosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bentia fruticulosa is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
