Batatas paniculata

Batatas paniculata, commonly known as the yellow batata or yellow sweet potato, is a tropical vine belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces striking yellow-orange tuberous roots that are rich in starch and nutrients. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals and a prominent yellow or orange coloration.

Batatas paniculata is cultivated for its edible tubers and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
