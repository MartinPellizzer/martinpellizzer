Bladhia × montana

Bladhia × montana, commonly known as the hybrid sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
