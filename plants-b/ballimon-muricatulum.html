Ballimon muricatulum

Ballimon muricatulum, commonly known as the roughleaf ballimon, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Ballimon muricatulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
