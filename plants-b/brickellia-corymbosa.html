Brickellia corymbosa

Brickellia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia corymbosa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
