Bumelia obtusifolia subsp. excelsa

Bumelia obtusifolia subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican gum tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native habitats.
