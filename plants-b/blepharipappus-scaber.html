Blepharipappus scaber

Blepharipappus scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are rough to the touch, hence the species name "scaber," and it produces small yellow to pale yellow flowers.

The plant is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.
