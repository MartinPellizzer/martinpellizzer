Brachiaria ukambensis

Brachiaria ukambensis, commonly known as African grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often thriving in poor soil conditions. Brachiaria ukambensis is widely used for forage in livestock farming due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

It also plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and maintaining soil structure.
