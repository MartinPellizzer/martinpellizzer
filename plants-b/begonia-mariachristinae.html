Begonia mariachristinae

Begonia mariachristinae, commonly known as the Mariachristina begonia, is a rare and unique species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to dark purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
