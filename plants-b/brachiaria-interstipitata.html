Brachiaria interstipitata

Brachiaria interstipitata, commonly known as silky grass or Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, spreading growth habit. The plant typically thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas rapidly.

Brachiaria interstipitata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to prevent erosion and support biodiversity.
