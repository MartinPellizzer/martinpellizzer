Boerhavia linearifolia var. glabrata

Boerhavia linearifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
