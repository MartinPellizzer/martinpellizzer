Brachychiton trichosiphon

Brachychiton trichosiphon, commonly known as the velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers in late summer, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
