Buddleja melliodora

Buddleja melliodora, commonly known as the golden false baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Buddleja melliodora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
