Benthamiella pycnophylloides

Benthamiella pycnophylloides, commonly known as the dense-leaved benthamiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are densely packed, giving it a bushy appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow and are borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

It is generally hardy and can thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
