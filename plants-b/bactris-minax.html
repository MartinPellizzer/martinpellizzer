Bactris minax

Bactris minax, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and the fibers from its leaves, which are used in traditional crafts.
