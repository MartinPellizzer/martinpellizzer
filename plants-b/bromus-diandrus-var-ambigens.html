Bromus diandrus var. ambigens

Bromus diandrus var. ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is characterized by its morphological variability, which can make identification challenging.
