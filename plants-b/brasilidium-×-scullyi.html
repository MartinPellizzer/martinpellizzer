Brasilidium × scullyi

Brasilidium × scullyi, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Brasiliidium and another hibiscus species, showcasing traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
