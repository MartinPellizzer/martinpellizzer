Bulbophyllum sarcorhachis

Bulbophyllum sarcorhachis, commonly known as the horned lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks or branches.

The genus Bulbophyllum is known for its diverse morphology, and B. sarcorhachis is no exception, showcasing unique floral structures and ecological adaptations.
