Begonia ragozinii

Begonia ragozinii, commonly known as the Ragozin's begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display vibrant red and green variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom from summer to early autumn. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
