Baccharis loxensis

Baccharis loxensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
