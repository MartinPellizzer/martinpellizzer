Bromus erectus var. wildtii

Bromus erectus var. wildtii, commonly known as the wildtii brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, and it flowers in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
