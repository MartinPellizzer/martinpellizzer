Briggsia latisepala

Briggsia latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features showy sepals that are broader than the petals, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
