Brunia ciliata

Brunia ciliata, commonly known as the silky bushveld grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Brunia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
