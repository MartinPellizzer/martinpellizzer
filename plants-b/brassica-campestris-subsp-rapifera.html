Brassica campestris subsp. rapifera

Brassica campestris subsp.

rapifera, commonly known as field mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
