Basella vesicaria

Basella vesicaria, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Basella vesicaria is valued for its nutritional content and ability to grow in various soil conditions.
