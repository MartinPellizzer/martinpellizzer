Blyxa saivala

Blyxa saivala, commonly known as the Indian water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and rice fields. Blyxa saivala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its reproductive system involves the production of spores, which are dispersed by water currents.
