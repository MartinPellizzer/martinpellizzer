Berberis aristata var. micrantha

Berberis aristata var. micrantha, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, containing berberine, a compound with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
