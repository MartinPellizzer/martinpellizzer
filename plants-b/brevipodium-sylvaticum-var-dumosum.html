Brevipodium sylvaticum var. dumosum

Brevipodium sylvaticum var. dumosum, commonly known as the dense-footed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically clustered at the base of the plant.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
