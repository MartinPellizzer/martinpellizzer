Banara glauca

Banara glauca, commonly known as the blue banara, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, bluish-gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, blue-green berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Banara glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
