Baeckea grandis

Baeckea grandis, commonly known as the broad-leaved baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Baeckea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
