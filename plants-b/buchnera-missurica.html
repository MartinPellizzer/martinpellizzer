Buchnera missurica

Buchnera missurica, commonly known as the Missouri bugweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the central United States.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Buchnera missurica is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and some edible plants.
