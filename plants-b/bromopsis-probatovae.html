Bromopsis probatovae

Bromopsis probatovae, commonly known as the Probatovae bromus, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Bromopsis probatovae is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments with low temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
