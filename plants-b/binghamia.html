Binghamia

Binghamia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale purple to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds. Binghamia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is known for its unusual and fleeting blooming period.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
