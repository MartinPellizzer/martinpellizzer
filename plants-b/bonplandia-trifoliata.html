Bonplandia trifoliata

Bonplandia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, three-lobed leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
