Begonia cuneatifolia

Begonia cuneatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
