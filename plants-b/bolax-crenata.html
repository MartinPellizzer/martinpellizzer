Bolax crenata

Bolax crenata, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Bolax crenata thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
