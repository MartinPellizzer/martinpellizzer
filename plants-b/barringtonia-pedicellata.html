Barringtonia pedicellata

Barringtonia pedicellata, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The fruit is a round, segmented structure that turns from green to red when mature, containing edible arils surrounding the seeds. The plant has a fibrous, aromatic bark and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
