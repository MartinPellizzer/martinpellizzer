Brassia boliviensis

Brassia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted to its humid, tropical environment.

Brassia boliviensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
