Brachiaria lachnantha

Brachiaria lachnantha, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

Brachiaria lachnantha is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
