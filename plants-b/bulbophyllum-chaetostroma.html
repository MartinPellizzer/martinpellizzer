Bulbophyllum chaetostroma

Bulbophyllum chaetostroma, commonly known as the hairy-stalked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy stems and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
