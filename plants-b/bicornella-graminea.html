Bicornella graminea

Bicornella graminea, commonly known as the two-horned grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. Bicornella graminea is often found in moist, temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its environment, making it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
