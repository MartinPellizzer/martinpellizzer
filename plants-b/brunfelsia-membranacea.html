Brunfelsia membranacea

Brunfelsia membranacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in regions with suitable growing conditions.
