Brassica muralis

Brassica muralis, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or wall crevices, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Brassica muralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a companion plant for other flowers.
