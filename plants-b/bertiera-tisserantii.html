Bertiera tisserantii

Bertiera tisserantii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Bertiera tisserantii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
