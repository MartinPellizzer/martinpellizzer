Bonatea foliosa var. pauciflora

Bonatea foliosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bonatea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.
