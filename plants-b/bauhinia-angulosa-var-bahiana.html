Bauhinia angulosa var. bahiana

Bauhinia angulosa var. bahiana, commonly known as the Bahia bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
