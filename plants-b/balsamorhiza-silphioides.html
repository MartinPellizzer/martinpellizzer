Balsamorhiza silphioides

Balsamorhiza silphioides, commonly known as the golden balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Balsamorhiza silphioides is also known for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous peoples.
