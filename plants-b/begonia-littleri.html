Begonia littleri

Begonia littleri, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a small, flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of pink or white, adding ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized garden settings.
