Bulbophyllum quadrans

Bulbophyllum quadrans, commonly known as the four-angled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, quadrangular pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
