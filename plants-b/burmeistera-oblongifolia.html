Burmeistera oblongifolia

Burmeistera oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle purple or greenish tinge. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Burmeistera oblongifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often used in shade gardens or as a bedding plant.
