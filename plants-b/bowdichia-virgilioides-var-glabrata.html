Bowdichia virgilioides var. glabrata

Bowdichia virgilioides var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bowdichia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
