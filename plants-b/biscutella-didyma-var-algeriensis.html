Biscutella didyma var. algeriensis

Biscutella didyma var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian biscuit plant, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is often found in regions with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure.
