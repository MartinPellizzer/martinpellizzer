Brachychiton tridentatus

Brachychiton tridentatus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters during the wet season. The tree has thick, flaky bark and large, palmate leaves that are lobed and trifoliate. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Brachychiton tridentatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
