Benkara scandens

Benkara scandens, commonly known as the climbing benkara, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth habit and flexible stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Benkara scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
