Bignonia elongata

Bignonia elongata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that can grow up to 15 feet in length, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Bignonia elongata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
