Bryonia cretica subsp. marmorata

Bryonia cretica subsp.

marmorata, commonly known as the marble bryony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Bryonia.
