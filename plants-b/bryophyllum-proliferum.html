Bryophyllum proliferum

Bryophyllum proliferum, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and summer seasons. One of its most notable features is its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which can develop into new plants rapidly.

This adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
