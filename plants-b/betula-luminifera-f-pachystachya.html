Betula luminifera f. pachystachya

Betula luminifera f. pachystachya, commonly known as the glowing birch variant, is a unique form of the luminifera species, characterized by its striking bioluminescent properties.

This botanical variant exhibits elongated, starchy stamens that emit a soft, blue-green glow under certain environmental conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest ecosystems. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

The bioluminescence is believed to be a result of specialized microbial symbiosis within its vascular system.
