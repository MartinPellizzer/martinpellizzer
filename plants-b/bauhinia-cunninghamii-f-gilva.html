Bauhinia cunninghamii f. gilva

Bauhinia cunninghamii f. gilva, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.
