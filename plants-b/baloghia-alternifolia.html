Baloghia alternifolia

Baloghia alternifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are alternate in arrangement and often have a striking red or orange coloration. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically red or yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant appearance.

Baloghia alternifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
