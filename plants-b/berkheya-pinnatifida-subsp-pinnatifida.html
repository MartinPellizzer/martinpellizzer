Berkheya pinnatifida subsp. pinnatifida

Berkheya pinnatifida subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September.

The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
