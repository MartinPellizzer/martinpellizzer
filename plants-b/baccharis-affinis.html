Baccharis affinis

Baccharis affinis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Baccharis affinis is also known for its resilience to drought and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
