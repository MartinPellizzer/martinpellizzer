Brassica pekinensis var. cephalata

Brassica pekinensis var. cephalata, commonly known as the Chinese kale or gai lan, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, thick, and stalked leaves that are often curled or crinkled. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in cool climates and is commonly cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves.

This variety is valued for its mild, slightly sweet flavor and is a staple in many Asian cuisines.
