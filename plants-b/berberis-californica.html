Berberis californica

Berberis californica, commonly known as California barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features erect, spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically bright green in spring, turning yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
