Berlinia acuminata var. pubescens

Berlinia acuminata var. pubescens, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
