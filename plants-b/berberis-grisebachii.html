Berberis grisebachii

Berberis grisebachii, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins, typical of the Berberis genus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

The species is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
