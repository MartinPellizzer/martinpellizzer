Banisteria ovata

Banisteria ovata, commonly known as the red-flowered banisteria, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Banisteria ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
