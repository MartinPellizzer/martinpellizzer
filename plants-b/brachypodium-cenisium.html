Brachypodium cenisium

Brachypodium cenisium, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers and produces small, dry seeds.

Brachypodium cenisium is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
