Blumea zollingeriana

Blumea zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in a flat, disc-like structure, often surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
