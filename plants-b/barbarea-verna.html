Barbarea verna

Barbarea verna, commonly known as spring glory-of-the-snow, is an annual or biennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Barbarea verna is known for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
