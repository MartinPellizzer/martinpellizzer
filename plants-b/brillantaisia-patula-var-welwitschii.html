Brillantaisia patula var. welwitschii

Brillantaisia patula var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschii bristlebush, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

This species is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a resilient plant in semi-desert ecosystems.
