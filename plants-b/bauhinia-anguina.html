Bauhinia anguina

Bauhinia anguina, commonly known as the snakebush, is a small deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a snake's head, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Bauhinia anguina is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
