Berberis asperma

Berberis asperma, commonly known as rough barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are also used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.
