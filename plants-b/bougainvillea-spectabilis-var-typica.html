Bougainvillea spectabilis var. typica

Bougainvillea spectabilis var. typica, commonly known as the common bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled true flowers inside the bracts. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Bougainvillea spectabilis var. typica is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
