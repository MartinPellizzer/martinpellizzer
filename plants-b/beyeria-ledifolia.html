Beyeria ledifolia

Beyeria ledifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits.

Beyeria ledifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
