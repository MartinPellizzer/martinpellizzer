Begonia depressinerva

Begonia depressinerva, commonly known as the depressed begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
