Betchea aglaiiformis

Betchea aglaiiformis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Betchea aglaiiformis is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
