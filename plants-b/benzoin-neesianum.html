Benzoin neesianum

Benzoin neesianum, commonly known as the Malayan benzoin, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fragrant, resinous exudates known as benzoin.

These resins have been used historically in incense, perfumes, and traditional medicine.
