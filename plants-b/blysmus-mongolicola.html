Blysmus mongolicola

Blysmus mongolicola, commonly known as the Mongolian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Blysmus mongolicola is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil, making it a resilient species in arid and semi-arid regions.
