Botryanthus commutatus

Botryanthus commutatus, commonly known as the commutated botryanthes, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
