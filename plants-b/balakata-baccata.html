Balakata baccata

Balakata baccata, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully mature.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
