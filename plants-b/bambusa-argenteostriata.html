Bambusa argenteostriata

Bambusa argenteostriata, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms with distinctive silver-white stripes running vertically along the stems. The plant typically grows in a clumping form, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green and contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa argenteostriata is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to provide privacy or screening in garden settings.
