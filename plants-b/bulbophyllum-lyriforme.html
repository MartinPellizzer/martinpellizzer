Bulbophyllum lyriforme

Bulbophyllum lyriforme, commonly known as the lyre-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, lyre-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are often yellow or orange with intricate patterns. It grows as an epiphyte, typically on trees in humid tropical environments.

The leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention and photosynthesis.
