Bambusa sesquiflora

Bambusa sesquiflora, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast with its green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its culms are strong and hollow, making it a popular choice for construction and crafts.

Bambusa sesquiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
