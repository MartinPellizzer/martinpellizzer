Baccharis pflanzii

Baccharis pflanzii, commonly known as the Argentine baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, while its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters.

Baccharis pflanzii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
