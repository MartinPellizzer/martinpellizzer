Bomarea patinii subsp. umbellata

Bomarea patinii subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or orange.

The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
