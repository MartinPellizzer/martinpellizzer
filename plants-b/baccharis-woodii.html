Baccharis woodii

Baccharis woodii, commonly known as the desert baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Baccharis woodii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
