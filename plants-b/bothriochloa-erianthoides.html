Bothriochloa erianthoides

Bothriochloa erianthoides, commonly known as silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
