Brassica oleracea var. major

Brassica oleracea var. major, commonly known as cauliflower, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its dense, white curd composed of tightly packed flower buds, which are harvested before they fully open. The plant is a biennial, though it is typically grown as an annual in agriculture. It thrives in cool climates and requires a long growing season to develop properly.

Its botanical features include large, waxy leaves and a short, sturdy stem.
