Bouvardia ternifolia

Bouvardia ternifolia, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bouvardia ternifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
