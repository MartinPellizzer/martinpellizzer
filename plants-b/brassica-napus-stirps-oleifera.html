Brassica napus stirps oleifera

Brassica napus stirps oleifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its oil-rich seeds. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a stem that branches near the base.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are the primary source of vegetable oil used in cooking and industrial applications.
