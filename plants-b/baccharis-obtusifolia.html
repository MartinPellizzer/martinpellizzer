Baccharis obtusifolia

Baccharis obtusifolia, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Baccharis obtusifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
