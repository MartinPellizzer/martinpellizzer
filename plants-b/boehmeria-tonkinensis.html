Boehmeria tonkinensis

Boehmeria tonkinensis, commonly known as Chinese hemp or ramie, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits. Its fibers are highly valued for their strength and are used in the production of textiles and paper.

Boehmeria tonkinensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
