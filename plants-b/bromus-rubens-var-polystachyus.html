Bromus rubens var. polystachyus

Bromus rubens var. polystachyus, commonly known as the red brome, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-colored inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.
