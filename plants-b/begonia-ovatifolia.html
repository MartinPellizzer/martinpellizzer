Begonia ovatifolia

Begonia ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in garden landscapes.
