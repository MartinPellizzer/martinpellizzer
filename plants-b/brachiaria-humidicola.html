Brachiaria humidicola

Brachiaria humidicola, commonly known as watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with small, spike-like inflorescences.

Brachiaria humidicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
