Broussonetia integrifolia

Broussonetia integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually entire or slightly lobed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
