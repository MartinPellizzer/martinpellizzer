Bulbophyllum matitanense subsp. rostratum

Bulbophyllum matitanense subsp.

rostratum, commonly known as the Rosy Lip Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, showy flowers with a distinctive rosy lip.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in the horticultural trade.
