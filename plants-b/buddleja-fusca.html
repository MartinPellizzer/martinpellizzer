Buddleja fusca

Buddleja fusca, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, grayish-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Buddleja fusca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its role in attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both garden borders and container planting.
