Blepharis katangensis

Blepharis katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a warm climate.
