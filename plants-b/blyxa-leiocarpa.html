Blyxa leiocarpa

Blyxa leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and slightly waxy in texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in spiky inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

Blyxa leiocarpa plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
