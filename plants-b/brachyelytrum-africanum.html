Brachyelytrum africanum

Brachyelytrum africanum, commonly known as the African short-winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
