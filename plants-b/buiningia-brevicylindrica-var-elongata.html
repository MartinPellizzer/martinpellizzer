Buiningia brevicylindrica var. elongata

Buiningia brevicylindrica var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bamboo orchid, is a unique variant of the bamboo orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its bamboo-like appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to tropical climates.
