Berkheya speciosa subsp. speciosa

Berkheya speciosa subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a showy display during the summer months.

This subspecies is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
