Baccharis tricuneata var. magellanica

Baccharis tricuneata var. magellanica, commonly known as Magellanic baccharis, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, follicle-like fruits that disperse seeds via wind.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
