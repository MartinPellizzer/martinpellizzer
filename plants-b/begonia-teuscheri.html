Begonia teuscheri

Begonia teuscheri, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
