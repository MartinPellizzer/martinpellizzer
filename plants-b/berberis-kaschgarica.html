Berberis kaschgarica

Berberis kaschgarica, commonly known as the Karakoram barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
