Bryonia stipulacea

Bryonia stipulacea, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, followed by edible tubers that are rich in inulin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Despite its name, it is not related to the artichoke but is often cultivated for its nutritious and versatile tubers.
