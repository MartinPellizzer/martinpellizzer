Begonia phamiana

Begonia phamiana, commonly known as the Phamiana begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia phamiana prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
