Bulbophyllum absconditum

Bulbophyllum absconditum, commonly known as the hidden orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique and often striking floral structures, which are adapted for specific pollination strategies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its bulbs, which are essential for its survival, store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
