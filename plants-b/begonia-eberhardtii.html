Begonia eberhardtii

Begonia eberhardtii, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its glossy green background. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in shaded garden settings.
