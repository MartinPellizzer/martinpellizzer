Berberis setigrifolia

Berberis setigrifolia, commonly known as the Sierra foothill barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Berberis setigrifolia is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
