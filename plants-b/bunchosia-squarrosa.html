Bunchosia squarrosa

Bunchosia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for coastal gardens.

Bunchosia squarrosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
