Bacopa scabra

Bacopa scabra, commonly known as Indian Pennywort, is a small herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Bacopa scabra is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
