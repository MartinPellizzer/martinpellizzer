Becium monocotyloides

Becium monocotyloides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits containing a milky sap. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also notable for its chemical composition, which includes compounds used in traditional medicine.
