Bidens pilosa f. decumbens

Bidens pilosa f. decumbens, commonly known as hairy beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
