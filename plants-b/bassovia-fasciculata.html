Bassovia fasciculata

Bassovia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in dense clusters along the stem, giving it its common name.

Bassovia fasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
