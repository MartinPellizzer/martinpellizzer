Becium ctenodon

Becium ctenodon, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
