Benzoin macrophyllum

Benzoin macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved benzoin, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and fragrant resinous sap, which is often harvested for its aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family, also known as the frankincense and myrrh family. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender stem with a crown of broad, glossy leaves.

The resin produced by Benzoin macrophyllum has been used in traditional medicine and as a perfume ingredient for centuries.
