Brassaia heterotricha

Brassaia heterotricha, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
