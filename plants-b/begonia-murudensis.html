Begonia murudensis

Begonia murudensis, commonly known as the Murudensis begonia, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Begonia murudensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
