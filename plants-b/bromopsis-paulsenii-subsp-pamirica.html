Bromopsis paulsenii subsp. pamirica

Bromopsis paulsenii subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches. Bromopsis paulsenii subsp.

pamirica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and its potential use in restoration projects.
