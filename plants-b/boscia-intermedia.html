Boscia intermedia

Boscia intermedia, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are typically sour in taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Boscia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
