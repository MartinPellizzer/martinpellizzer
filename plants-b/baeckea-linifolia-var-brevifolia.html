Baeckea linifolia var. brevifolia

Baeckea linifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baeckea, is a compact shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry woodlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
