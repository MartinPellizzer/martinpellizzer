Bunochilus melagrammus

Bunochilus melagrammus, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
