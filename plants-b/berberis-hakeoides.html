Berberis hakeoides

Berberis hakeoides, commonly known as the Hakea barbed spurge, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its potential invasiveness in certain regions.
