Boelia sphaerocarpa

Boelia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, glandular hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spherical berries that turn red as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
