Brachypodium × ambrosii

Brachypodium × ambrosii, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, feathery inflorescence that resembles a beard. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Brachypodium × ambrosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
