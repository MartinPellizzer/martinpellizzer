Brachyspatha consimilis

Brachyspatha consimilis, commonly known as the similar-leaved fern, is a species of fern characterized by its compact, upright growth habit.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Brachyspatha consimilis is a relatively rare species, often found in specific geographic regions with particular microclimatic conditions.
