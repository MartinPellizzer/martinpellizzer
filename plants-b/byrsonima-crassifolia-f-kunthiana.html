Byrsonima crassifolia f. kunthiana

Byrsonima crassifolia f. kunthiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or kinkajou tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
