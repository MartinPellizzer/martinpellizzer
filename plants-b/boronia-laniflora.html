Boronia laniflora

Boronia laniflora, commonly known as the woolly boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

Boronia laniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
