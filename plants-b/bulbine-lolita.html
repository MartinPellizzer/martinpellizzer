Bulbine lolita

Bulbine lolita, commonly known as the dwarf bulbine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and rosette-forming leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
