Begonia altimontana

Begonia altimontana, commonly known as the Andean begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features upright stems with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of light.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
