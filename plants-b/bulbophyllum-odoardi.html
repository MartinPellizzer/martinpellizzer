Bulbophyllum odoardi

Bulbophyllum odoardi, commonly known as the Odoardi's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a lithophyte, meaning it typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
