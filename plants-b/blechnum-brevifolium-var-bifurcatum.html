Blechnum brevifolium var. bifurcatum

Blechnum brevifolium var. bifurcatum, commonly known as the bifurcated shield fern, is a varietal form of the shield fern genus Blechnum.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that split into two branches, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are leathery and have a serrated edge, which helps in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
