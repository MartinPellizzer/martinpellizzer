Brassica rapa f. depressa

Brassica rapa f. depressa, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are followed by spherical, pale green pods containing several seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

This variety is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
