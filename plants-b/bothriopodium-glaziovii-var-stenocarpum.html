Bothriopodium glaziovii var. stenocarpum

Bothriopodium glaziovii var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often densely arranged, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
