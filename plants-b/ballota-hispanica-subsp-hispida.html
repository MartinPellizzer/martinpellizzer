Ballota hispanica subsp. hispida

Ballota hispanica subsp.

hispida, commonly known as Spanish ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Ballota hispanica subsp.

hispida is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
