Boscia caffra

Boscia caffra, commonly known as the sour fig or wild quince, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Boscia caffra is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
