Bombacopsis retusa

Bombacopsis retusa, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
