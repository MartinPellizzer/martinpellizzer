Berberis vulgaris subsp. aetnensis

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna barberry, is a shrub native to the volcanic regions of Sicily. It features spiny, thorned stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
