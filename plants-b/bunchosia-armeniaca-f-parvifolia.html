Bunchosia armeniaca f. parvifolia

Bunchosia armeniaca f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
