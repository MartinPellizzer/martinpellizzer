Bulbophyllum pileolatum

Bulbophyllum pileolatum, commonly known as the pineapple orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
