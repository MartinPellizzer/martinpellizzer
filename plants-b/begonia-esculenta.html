Begonia esculenta

Begonia esculenta, commonly known as the tuberous begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique tuberous structure allows it to store water and nutrients, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
