Baccharis albida

Baccharis albida, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-white, fuzzy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Baccharis albida is often found in dry forests and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
