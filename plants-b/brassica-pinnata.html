Brassica pinnata

Brassica pinnata, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found growing in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in fields.

Brassica pinnata is also known for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
