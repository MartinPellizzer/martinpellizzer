Begonia triginticollium

Begonia triginticollium, commonly known as the thirty-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fleshy leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
