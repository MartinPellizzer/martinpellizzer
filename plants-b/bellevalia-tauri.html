Bellevalia tauri

Bellevalia tauri, commonly known as the Taurian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellevalia tauri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
