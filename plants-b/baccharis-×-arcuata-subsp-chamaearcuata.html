Baccharis × arcuata subsp. chamaearcuata

Baccharis × arcuata subsp.

chamaearcuata, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing form and silver-gray foliage, which reflects its adaptation to saline and well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, white flowers in panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and ability to tolerate coastal conditions make it a valuable plant for stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

It is also used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
