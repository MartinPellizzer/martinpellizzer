Brassica napus convar. oleifera

Brassica napus convar. oleifera, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual or biennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are rich in oil, making it a valuable crop for oil production. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both agricultural and industrial purposes.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a robust root system that aids in nutrient absorption.
