Bromus hordeaceus var. palustris

Bromus hordeaceus var. palustris, commonly known as the marsh barley, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils and is often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
