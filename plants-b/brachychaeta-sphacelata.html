Brachychaeta sphacelata

Brachychaeta sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
