Boehmeria pavonii var. diversifolia

Boehmeria pavonii var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that often exhibit a striking variegation of green and white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
