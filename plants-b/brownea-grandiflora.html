Brownea grandiflora

Brownea grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.
