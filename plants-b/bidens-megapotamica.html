Bidens megapotamica

Bidens megapotamica, commonly known as the yellow beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals and bristly, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
