Brassica recurva

Brassica recurva, commonly known as the curled leaf mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica recurva is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
