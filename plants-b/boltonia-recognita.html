Boltonia recognita

Boltonia recognita, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with large, showy white to pinkish-white ray flowers surrounding a yellow disk. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Boltonia recognita thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.
