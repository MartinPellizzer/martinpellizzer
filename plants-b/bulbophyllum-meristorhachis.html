Bulbophyllum meristorhachis

Bulbophyllum meristorhachis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks or branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
