Bellis arabica

Bellis arabica, commonly known as the Persian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance and long-lasting nature.
