Berberis julianae var. patungensis

Berberis julianae var. patungensis, commonly known as the Patungensis barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, bright green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
