Barbarea heterophylla

Barbarea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable yellowcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided, and its stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Barbarea heterophylla is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
