Banara guianensis

Banara guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese banara, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

Banara guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
