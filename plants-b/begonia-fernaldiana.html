Begonia fernaldiana

Begonia fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant typically blooms with small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare tropical plants.
