Baccharis anomala var. albipappa

Baccharis anomala var. albipappa, commonly known as white-pearled baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
