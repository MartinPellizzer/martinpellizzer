Bulbophyllum ichthyostomum

Bulbophyllum ichthyostomum, commonly known as the fish-mouth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and the flowers are borne in clusters.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
