Begonia cavum

Begonia cavum, commonly known as the hollow begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hollow leaves that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, Begonia cavum is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
