Bouea diversifolia

Bouea diversifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid environments and indirect sunlight. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Bouea diversifolia is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it an ideal plant for beginners.
