Bignonia moringifolia

Bignonia moringifolia, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Bignonia moringifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
