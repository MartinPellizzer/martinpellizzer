Boykinia jamesii

Boykinia jamesii, commonly known as the James's rockfoam, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold, dry conditions and is often found in meadows and on slopes.

Boykinia jamesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
