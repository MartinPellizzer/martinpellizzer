Bambusa daporijoeana

Bambusa daporijoeana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a sustainable building material.
