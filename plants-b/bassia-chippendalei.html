Bassia chippendalei

Bassia chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale's bassia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its vibrant flowers, often in shades of red or orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
