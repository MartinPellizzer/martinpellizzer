Besleria glabra

Besleria glabra, commonly known as the naked stem gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
