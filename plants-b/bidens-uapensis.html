Bidens uapensis

Bidens uapensis, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bidens uapensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed or wet environments.
