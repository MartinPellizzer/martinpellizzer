Bulbophyllum stramineum

Bulbophyllum stramineum, commonly known as the yellow bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure, which features a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the orchid family's diversity.
