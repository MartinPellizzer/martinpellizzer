Brachanthemum gobicum

Brachanthemum gobicum, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with small, hairy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces yellow to brown flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects in dry landscapes.
