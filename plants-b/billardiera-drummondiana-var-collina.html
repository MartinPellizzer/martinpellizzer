Billardiera drummondiana var. collina

Billardiera drummondiana var. collina, commonly known as the coastal billardiera, is a climbing shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features slender, twining stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dunes.
