Bumelia retusa subsp. typica

Bumelia retusa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the white gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.
