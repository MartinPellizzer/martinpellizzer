Bromus mollis var. effusus

Bromus mollis var. effusus, commonly known as the soft brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus mollis var. effusus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
