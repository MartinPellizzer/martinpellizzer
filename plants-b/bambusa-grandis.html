Bambusa grandis

Bambusa grandis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features hollow, jointed stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern. Its rhizome system allows for rapid propagation and dense clumping growth.

Bambusa grandis is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction, crafts, and as a sustainable resource.
