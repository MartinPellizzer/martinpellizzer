Bigelovia porulosa

Bigelovia porulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf bigelovia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Bigelovia porulosa is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
