Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp. meridionalis

Bauhinia madagascariensis subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern bauhinia, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces showy, papilionaceous flowers in shades of pink or white. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographical distribution and specific morphological traits compared to other members of the species.
