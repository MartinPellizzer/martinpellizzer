Batrachium hederaceum var. omoiophyllum

Batrachium hederaceum var. omoiophyllum, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
