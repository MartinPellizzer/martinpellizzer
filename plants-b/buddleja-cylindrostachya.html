Buddleja cylindrostachya

Buddleja cylindrostachya, commonly known as the African butterfly bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, hairy stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Buddleja cylindrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
