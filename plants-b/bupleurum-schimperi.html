Bupleurum schimperi

Bupleurum schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian barberry, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Bupleurum schimperi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at elevations above 2,000 meters.
