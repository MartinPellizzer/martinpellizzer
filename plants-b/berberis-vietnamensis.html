Berberis vietnamensis

Berberis vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
