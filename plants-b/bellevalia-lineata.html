Bellevalia lineata

Bellevalia lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant a grass-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
