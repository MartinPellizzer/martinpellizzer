Bactris oligocarpa var. brachycaulis

Bactris oligocarpa var. brachycaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed palm, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, short trunk and broad, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
