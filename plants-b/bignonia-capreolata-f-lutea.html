Bignonia capreolata f. lutea

Bignonia capreolata f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. Its stems are often woody and can have a reddish or brownish hue, especially in the bark.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
