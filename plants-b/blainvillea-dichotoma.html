Blainvillea dichotoma

Blainvillea dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous blainvillea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Blainvillea dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
