Boronia multicaulis

Boronia multicaulis, commonly known as the coastal boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Boronia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
