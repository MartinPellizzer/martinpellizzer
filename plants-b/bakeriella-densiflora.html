Bakeriella densiflora

Bakeriella densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower bakeriella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Bakeriella densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
