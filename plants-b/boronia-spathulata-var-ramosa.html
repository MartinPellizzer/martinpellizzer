Boronia spathulata var. ramosa

Boronia spathulata var. ramosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
