Bulbostylis subsphaerocephala

Bulbostylis subsphaerocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
