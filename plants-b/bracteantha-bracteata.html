Bracteantha bracteata

Bracteantha bracteata, commonly known as the golden trumpet or bracteantha, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

Bracteantha bracteata is typically grown as a tropical plant, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
