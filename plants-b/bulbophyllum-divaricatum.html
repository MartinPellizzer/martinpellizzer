Bulbophyllum divaricatum

Bulbophyllum divaricatum, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
