Baccharis decussata subsp. jelskii

Baccharis decussata subsp.

jelskii, commonly known as the Andean shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
