Baphia pyrifolia

Baphia pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Baphia pyrifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its use in traditional medicine.
