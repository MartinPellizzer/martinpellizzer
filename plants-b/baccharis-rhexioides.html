Baccharis rhexioides

Baccharis rhexioides, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry forests and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis rhexioides plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
