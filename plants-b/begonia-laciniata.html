Begonia laciniata

Begonia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like leaves that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant typically produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia laciniata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
