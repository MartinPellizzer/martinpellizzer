Bromus sibiricus

Bromus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Bromus sibiricus is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
