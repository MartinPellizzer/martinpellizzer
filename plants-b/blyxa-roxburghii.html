Blyxa roxburghii

Blyxa roxburghii, commonly known as the narrowleaf water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating in water. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that help it thrive in slow-moving or still water bodies. Blyxa roxburghii is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects.

It reproduces through spores and is an important species for aquatic ecosystems.
