Bulbophyllum vinculibulbum

Bulbophyllum vinculibulbum, commonly known as the vine-like bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often pendulous inflorescences and brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
