Bambusa sinthana

Bambusa sinthana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. This bamboo is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bright green color that contrasts beautifully with the golden stems.
