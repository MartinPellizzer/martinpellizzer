Bassia sieversiana

Bassia sieversiana, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Bassia sieversiana is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
