Begonia subrotunda

Begonia subrotunda, commonly known as the rounded begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia subrotunda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
