Betula verrucosa var. elegans-laciniata

Betula verrucosa var. elegans-laciniata, commonly known as the silky birch or paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are finely serrated and alternate along the branches, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is also cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
