Bulbophyllum capilligerum

Bulbophyllum capilligerum, commonly known as the hairy-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a greenish tint. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
