Blechnum spicant f. serratum

Blechnum spicant f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated holly fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance and a robust, upright growth habit. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its foliage is valued for ornamental purposes in horticulture due to its attractive, textured leaves.
