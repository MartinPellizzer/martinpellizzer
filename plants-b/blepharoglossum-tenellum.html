Blepharoglossum tenellum

Blepharoglossum tenellum, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of its leaves.

Blepharoglossum tenellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
