Battandiera tubiformis

Battandiera tubiformis, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Battandiera tubiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
