Becium grandiflorum var. obovatum

Becium grandiflorum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved becium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves that are often pubescent, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in terminal inflorescences, and bloom during the warmer months. This variant is closely related to other species in the genus Becium, sharing similar morphological traits.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
