Brachyglottis repanda

Brachyglottis repanda, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
