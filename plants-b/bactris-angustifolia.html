Bactris angustifolia

Bactris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnate leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which develop into edible berries. Bactris angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
