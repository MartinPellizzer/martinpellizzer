Brittonrosea

Brittonrosea, commonly known as the Britton's rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses, apples, and cherries. The plant features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Brittonrosea typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
