Baccharis longipes var. angustissima

Baccharis longipes var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, and is adapted to saturated soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the fall, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
