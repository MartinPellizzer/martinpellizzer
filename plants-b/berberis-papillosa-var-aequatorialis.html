Berberis papillosa var. aequatorialis

Berberis papillosa var. aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal color.
