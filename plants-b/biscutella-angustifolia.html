Biscutella angustifolia

Biscutella angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Biscutella angustifolia is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
