Bulbophyllum bolivianum

Bulbophyllum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and yellow. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
