Brassica oleracea var. alboglabra

Brassica oleracea var. alboglabra, commonly known as the Chinese cabbage, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stalks and tightly wrapped leaves that form a compact, cabbage-like head. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is closely related to other cruciferous vegetables such as broccoli and cauliflower. Its leaves are typically pale green to white, and it is cultivated for its edible stems and leaves.

The plant is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
