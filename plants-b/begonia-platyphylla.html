Begonia platyphylla

Begonia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom on upright stems. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
