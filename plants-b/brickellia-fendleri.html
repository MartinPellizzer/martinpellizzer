Brickellia fendleri

Brickellia fendleri, commonly known as desert brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Brickellia fendleri is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
