Brunia passerinoides

Brunia passerinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf brunia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
