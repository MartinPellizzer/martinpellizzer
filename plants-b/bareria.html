Bareria

Bareria, commonly known as the "false mallow," is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a yellow center. Bareria is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
