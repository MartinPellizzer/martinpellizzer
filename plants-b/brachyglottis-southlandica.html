Brachyglottis southlandica

Brachyglottis southlandica, commonly known as the Southland daisy, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
