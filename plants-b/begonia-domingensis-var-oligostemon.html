Begonia domingensis var. oligostemon

Begonia domingensis var. oligostemon, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent silver veins.

Native to the humid forests of Central and South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, while its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate moderate light conditions, making it adaptable to various growing settings.
