Blumea conyzoides

Blumea conyzoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Blumea conyzoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
