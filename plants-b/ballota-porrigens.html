Ballota porrigens

Ballota porrigens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Ballota porrigens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in Mediterranean climates.

It is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
