Brachyloma strictum

Brachyloma strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallaby bush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or orange.

Brachyloma strictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
