Brachyactis angusta

Brachyactis angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf brachyactis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
