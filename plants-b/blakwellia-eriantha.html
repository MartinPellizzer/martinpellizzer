Blakwellia eriantha

Blakwellia eriantha, commonly known as the silky sandalwood, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its aromatic foliage and unique morphology make it a notable species in its ecological community.

Blakwellia eriantha is also valued for its potential in horticulture and conservation due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
