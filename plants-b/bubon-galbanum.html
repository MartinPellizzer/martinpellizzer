Bubon galbanum

Bubon galbanum, commonly known as the galbanum plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic and often pungent scent.

It is native to regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, resinous fragrance that has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a taproot system, which help it survive in harsh conditions.

The resinous sap, known as galbanum, is harvested for its therapeutic and aromatic properties.
