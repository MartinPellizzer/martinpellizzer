Brachanthemum pulvinatum

Brachanthemum pulvinatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a central stem that bears the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Brachanthemum pulvinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
