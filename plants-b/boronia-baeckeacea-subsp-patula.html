Boronia baeckeacea subsp. patula

Boronia baeckeacea subsp.

patula, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies patula is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and growth habits compared to other subspecies of Boronia baeckeacea.
