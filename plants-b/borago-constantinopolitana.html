Borago constantinopolitana

Borago constantinopolitana, commonly known as Turkish heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of violet-blue flowers resembling those of the common heliotrope. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.

Borago constantinopolitana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
