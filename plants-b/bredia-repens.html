Bredia repens

Bredia repens, commonly known as the creeping bredia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender stems. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually oval in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Bredia repens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
