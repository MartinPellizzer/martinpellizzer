Berberis xinganensis

Berberis xinganensis, commonly known as the Xing'an Mountain barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation projects.
