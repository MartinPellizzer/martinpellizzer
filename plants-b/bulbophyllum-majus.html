Bulbophyllum majus

Bulbophyllum majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.
