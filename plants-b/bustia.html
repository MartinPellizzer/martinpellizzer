Bustia

Bustia, commonly known as the common bustard, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody shrubs or small trees, often with compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is native to various regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. It typically thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climates.

The fruits are legumes that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by animals or through wind.
