Bunchosia armeniaca

Bunchosia armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian bunchosia, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Bunchosia armeniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in its native habitat.
