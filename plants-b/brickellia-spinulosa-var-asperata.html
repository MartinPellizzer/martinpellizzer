Brickellia spinulosa var. asperata

Brickellia spinulosa var. asperata, commonly known as roughleaf brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and are visited by various pollinators.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
