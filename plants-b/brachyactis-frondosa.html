Brachyactis frondosa

Brachyactis frondosa, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
