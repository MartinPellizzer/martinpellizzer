Barbacenia hildebrandtii

Barbacenia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's barbacenia, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a climbing plant with vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. Barbacenia hildebrandtii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it a valuable species in both conservation and horticultural contexts.
