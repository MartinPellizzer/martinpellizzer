Bumelia lycioides var. ellipsoidalis

Bumelia lycioides var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the elliptic bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
