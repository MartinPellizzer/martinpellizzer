Brassica napus var. sarcorhiza

Brassica napus var. sarcorhiza, commonly known as the wild radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by long, cylindrical pods containing round, edible seeds. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its rapid growth and potential as a weed in agricultural settings.
