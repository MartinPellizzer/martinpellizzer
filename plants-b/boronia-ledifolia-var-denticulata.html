Boronia ledifolia var. denticulata

Boronia ledifolia var. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
