Belvisia hymenolepioides

Belvisia hymenolepioides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Belvisia hymenolepioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
