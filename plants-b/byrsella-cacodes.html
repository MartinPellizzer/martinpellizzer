Byrsella cacodes

Byrsella cacodes, commonly known as the poison gooseberry, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are highly toxic due to the presence of alkaloids. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and dark green color.

The plant thrives in acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
