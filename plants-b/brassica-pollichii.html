Brassica pollichii

Brassica pollichii, commonly known as the Pollich's mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Brassica pollichii is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.
