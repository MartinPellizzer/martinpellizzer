Berberis dawoensis

Berberis dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian barberry, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Berberis dawoensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
