Begonia vinagrera var. vinagrera

Begonia vinagrera var. vinagrera, commonly known as the vinegar begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements have made it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
