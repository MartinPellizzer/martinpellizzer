Bupleurum gerardi subvar. jacquinianum

Bupleurum gerardi subvar. jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's boldroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
