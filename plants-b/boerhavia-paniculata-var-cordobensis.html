Boerhavia paniculata var. cordobensis

Boerhavia paniculata var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
