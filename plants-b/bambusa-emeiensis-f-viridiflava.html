Bambusa emeiensis f. viridiflava

Bambusa emeiensis f. viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered bamboo, is a variant of the emei bamboo species characterized by its unique foliage and floral characteristics.

It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant green leaves and striking yellow-green inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo with slender, hollow stems that are often used in landscaping and traditional crafts. Its foliage is dense and provides a lush, green appearance throughout the growing season.

The flowering process, while rare, adds a distinctive visual element to the plant's overall aesthetic.
