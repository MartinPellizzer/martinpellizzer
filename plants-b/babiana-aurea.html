Babiana aurea

Babiana aurea, commonly known as the golden babiana, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spurred petals that are often tinged with red or orange. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Babiana aurea is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
