Bauhinia faberi var. megaphylla

Bauhinia faberi var. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright orange to red flowers during the summer months. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
