Bidens coreopsidis var. procumbens

Bidens coreopsidis var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in clusters of three, typical of the Bidens genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
