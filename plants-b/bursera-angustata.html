Bursera angustata

Bursera angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and fragrant, resinous sap that is used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
