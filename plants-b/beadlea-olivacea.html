Beadlea olivacea

Beadlea olivacea, commonly known as the olive tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree with dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when immature and turn black when ripe. Beadlea olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is also known for its historical and cultural significance in ancient civilizations.
