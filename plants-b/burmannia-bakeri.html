Burmannia bakeri

Burmannia bakeri, commonly known as the red lady's slipper, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the family Burmanniaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically bright red or orange, often with a distinctive pouch-like structure. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique floral morphology is adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
