Bancalus gracilis

Bancalus gracilis, commonly known as the slender water beetle, is a species of aquatic beetle belonging to the family Dytiscidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender body and typically metallic green or blue coloration. This beetle is often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its larvae, known as water beetles, are aquatic and play a role in the food web by feeding on small invertebrates.

The adult beetles are predatory, contributing to the control of insect populations in their ecosystems.
