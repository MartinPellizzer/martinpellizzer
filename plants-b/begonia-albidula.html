Begonia albidula

Begonia albidula, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers with prominent yellow veins. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Begonia albidula prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
