Bumelia laurifolia

Bumelia laurifolia, commonly known as sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, spherical seed capsules. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Bumelia laurifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
