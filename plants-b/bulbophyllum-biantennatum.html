Bulbophyllum biantennatum

Bulbophyllum biantennatum, commonly known as the two-antenna orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and long, slender leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip, which is a hallmark of the genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
