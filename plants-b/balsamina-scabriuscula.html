Balsamina scabriuscula

Balsamina scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy, often rough-textured leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
