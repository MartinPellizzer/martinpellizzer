Barleria villosa

Barleria villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Barleria villosa is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
