Banksia petiolaris

Banksia petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Banksia petiolaris is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is an important component of the local flora and plays a role in supporting native wildlife.
