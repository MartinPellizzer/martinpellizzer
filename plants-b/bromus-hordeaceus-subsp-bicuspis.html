Bromus hordeaceus subsp. bicuspis

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

bicuspis, commonly known as the two-cusped brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, awned florets. Its seeds are typically small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its spread across open habitats.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
