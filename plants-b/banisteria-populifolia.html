Banisteria populifolia

Banisteria populifolia, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a cascading, shower-like appearance. The plant has palmate leaves that are broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Banisteria populifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
