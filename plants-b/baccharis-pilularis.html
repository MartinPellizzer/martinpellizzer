Baccharis pilularis

Baccharis pilularis, commonly known as coast currant or silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species for Mediterranean climates. Its aromatic leaves and showy flower clusters make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.

Baccharis pilularis also serves as a food source for various insects and birds.
