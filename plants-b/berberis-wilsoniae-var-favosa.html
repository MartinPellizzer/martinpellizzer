Berberis wilsoniae var. favosa

Berberis wilsoniae var. favosa, commonly known as Wilson's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
