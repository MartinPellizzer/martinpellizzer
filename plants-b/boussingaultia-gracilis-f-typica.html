Boussingaultia gracilis f. typica

Boussingaultia gracilis f. typica, commonly known as the slender Boussingault's grass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelets with two to three bractlets. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

Boussingaultia gracilis f. typica is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
