Barkhausia taraxacifolia

Barkhausia taraxacifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed and resemble dandelion leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
