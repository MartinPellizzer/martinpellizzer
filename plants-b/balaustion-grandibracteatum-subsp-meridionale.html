Balaustion grandibracteatum subsp. meridionale

Balaustion grandibracteatum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as the Mediterranean balaustion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
