Becium grandiflorum var. cordatum

Becium grandiflorum var. cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf becium, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
