Bignonia pallida

Bignonia pallida, commonly known as the pale bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Bignonia pallida is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
