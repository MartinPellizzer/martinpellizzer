Brachypodium pinnatum var. gracile

Brachypodium pinnatum var. gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachypodium pinnatum var. gracile is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.
