Beloglottis bifida

Beloglottis bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves that split into numerous narrow segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understory habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clusters.

Beloglottis bifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, indirect light conditions.
