Brasiliorchis picta

Brasiliorchis picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display intricate patterns of red, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense, humid understory of the forest. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its moist, shaded habitat.

The species is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a symbol of the biodiversity of the Brazilian rainforest.
