Bomarea squamulosa

Bomarea squamulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The flowers, which are often blue or purple, are borne on slender stems and attract various pollinators.
