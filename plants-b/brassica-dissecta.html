Brassica dissecta

Brassica dissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
