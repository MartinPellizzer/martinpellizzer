Bambusa fruticosa

Bambusa fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming thickets or shrub-like structures. The plant has stiff, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it thrive in shaded environments.

Bambusa fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various soil types.
