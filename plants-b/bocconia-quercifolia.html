Bocconia quercifolia

Bocconia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved bocconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Bocconia quercifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
