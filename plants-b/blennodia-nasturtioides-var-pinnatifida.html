Blennodia nasturtioides var. pinnatifida

Blennodia nasturtioides var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it a common species in wetlands and stream edges.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful aquatic plant and a potential weed in certain habitats.
