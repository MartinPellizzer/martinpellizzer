Bletia crispina

Bletia crispina, commonly known as the crinkled bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. Bletia crispina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and subtle floral display make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
