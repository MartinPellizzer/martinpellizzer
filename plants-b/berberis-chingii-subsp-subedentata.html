Berberis chingii subsp. subedentata

Berberis chingii subsp.

subedentata, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
