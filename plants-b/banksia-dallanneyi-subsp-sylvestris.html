Banksia dallanneyi subsp. sylvestris

Banksia dallanneyi subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the serrated banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a common sight in heathlands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
