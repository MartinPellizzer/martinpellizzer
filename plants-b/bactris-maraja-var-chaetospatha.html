Bactris maraja var. chaetospatha

Bactris maraja var. chaetospatha, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible, oval fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the fruits are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
