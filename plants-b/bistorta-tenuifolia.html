Bistorta tenuifolia

Bistorta tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bistort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features upright, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lanceolate with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Bistorta tenuifolia produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
