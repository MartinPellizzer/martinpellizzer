Bulbophyllum davidii

Bulbophyllum davidii, commonly known as the David's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors.

Bulbophyllum davidii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
