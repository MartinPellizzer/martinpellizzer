Barkhausia chondrilloides

Barkhausia chondrilloides, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
