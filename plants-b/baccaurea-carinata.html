Baccaurea carinata

Baccaurea carinata, commonly known as the spiny gooseberry, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, lowland areas.
