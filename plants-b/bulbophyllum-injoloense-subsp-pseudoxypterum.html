Bulbophyllum injoloense subsp. pseudoxypterum

Bulbophyllum injoloense subsp.

pseudoxypterum, commonly known as the Injolo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure.

The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
