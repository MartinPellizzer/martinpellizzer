Bargemontia deserticola

Bargemontia deserticola, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Bargemontia deserticola plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in erosion-prone areas.
