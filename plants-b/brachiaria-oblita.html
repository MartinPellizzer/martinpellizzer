Brachiaria oblita

Brachiaria oblita, commonly known as Brazilian sprangletop, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is typically found in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Brachiaria oblita is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
