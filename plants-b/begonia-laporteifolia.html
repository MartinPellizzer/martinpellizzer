Begonia laporteifolia

Begonia laporteifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
