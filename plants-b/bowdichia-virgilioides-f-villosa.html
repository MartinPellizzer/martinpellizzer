Bowdichia virgilioides f. villosa

Bowdichia virgilioides f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bowdichia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The hairy bowdichia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
