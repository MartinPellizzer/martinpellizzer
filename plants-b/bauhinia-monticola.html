Bauhinia monticola

Bauhinia monticola, commonly known as the mountain bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves composed of three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Bauhinia monticola is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical gardens.
