Berberis yaanica

Berberis yaanica, commonly known as the Yaanica barberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
