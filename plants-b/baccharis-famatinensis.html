Baccharis famatinensis

Baccharis famatinensis, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Baccharis famatinensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
