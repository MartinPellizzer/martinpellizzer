Briza purpurascens

Briza purpurascens, commonly known as purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a soft, ornamental touch to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
