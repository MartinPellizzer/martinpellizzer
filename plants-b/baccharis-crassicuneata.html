Baccharis crassicuneata

Baccharis crassicuneata, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has rough, often hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

Baccharis crassicuneata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
