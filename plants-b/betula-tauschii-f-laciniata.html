Betula tauschii f. laciniata

Betula tauschii f. laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf birch, is a variant of the Siberian birch.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and pale, often peeling in thin layers.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
