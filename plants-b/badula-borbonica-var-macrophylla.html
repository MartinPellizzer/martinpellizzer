Badula borbonica var. macrophylla

Badula borbonica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaved badula, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other economically important species such as the cashew and mango. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
