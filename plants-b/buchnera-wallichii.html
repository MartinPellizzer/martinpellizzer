Buchnera wallichii

Buchnera wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Buchnera wallichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
