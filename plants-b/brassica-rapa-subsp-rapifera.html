Brassica rapa subsp. rapifera

Brassica rapa subsp.

rapifera, commonly known as turnip, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy root that is typically white or yellow in color.

The plant has alternate, rough, and hairy leaves that are lobed or toothed. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The turnip is cultivated for its edible root and leaves, and it is adapted to temperate climates.
