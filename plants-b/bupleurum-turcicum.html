Bupleurum turcicum

Bupleurum turcicum, commonly known as Turkish bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
