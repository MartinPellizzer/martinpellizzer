Bothriochloa imperatoides

Bothriochloa imperatoides, commonly known as imperial grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
