Bupleurum multinerve var. minus

Bupleurum multinerve var. minus, commonly known as lesser multifurrowed bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that have prominent veins. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
