Bowlesia incana f. tenera

Bowlesia incana f. tenera, commonly known as the woolly bowlesia, is a unique and rare plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
