Bidens elliptica var. integra

Bidens elliptica var. integra, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides. Its leaves are elliptic in shape with entire margins, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
