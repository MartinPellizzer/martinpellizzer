Bulbophyllum hampelianum

Bulbophyllum hampelianum, commonly known as the Hampel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
