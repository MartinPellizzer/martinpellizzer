Betonica fusca

Betonica fusca, commonly known as the lesser betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Betonica fusca is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
