Bistorta ludlowii

Bistorta ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. The plant features upright, slender stems that are covered in dense, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. Bistorta ludlowii produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
