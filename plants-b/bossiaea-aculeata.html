Bossiaea aculeata

Bossiaea aculeata, commonly known as the prickly bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, aculeate (spiny) leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Bossiaea aculeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
