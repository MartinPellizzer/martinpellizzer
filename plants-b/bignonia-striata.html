Bignonia striata

Bignonia striata, commonly known as crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and a woody vine structure that allows it to climb supports. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia striata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
