Burkea caperangau

Burkea caperangau, commonly known as the African burkea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its thick, often spiny stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Burkea caperangau is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
