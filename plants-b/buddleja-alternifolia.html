Buddleja alternifolia

Buddleja alternifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are alternate in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Buddleja alternifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
