Brassica apula

Brassica apula, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Brassica apula is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies in its native region.
