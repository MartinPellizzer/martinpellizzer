Begonia lehmannii

Begonia lehmannii, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are often hairy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic plants.
