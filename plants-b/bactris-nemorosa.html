Bactris nemorosa

Bactris nemorosa, commonly known as the forest palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Peru.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small, round berries. Bactris nemorosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
