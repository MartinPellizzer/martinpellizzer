Bauhinia pubescens

Bauhinia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetwood or orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its bark is smooth and pale, often with a faintly flaky appearance.

Bauhinia pubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
