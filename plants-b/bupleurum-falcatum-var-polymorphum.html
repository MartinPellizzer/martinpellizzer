Bupleurum falcatum var. polymorphum

Bupleurum falcatum var. polymorphum, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
