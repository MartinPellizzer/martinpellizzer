Braya purpurascens var. thorild-wulffii

Braya purpurascens var. thorild-wulffii, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to cold climates. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
