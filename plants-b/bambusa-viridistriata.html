Bambusa viridistriata

Bambusa viridistriata, commonly known as the green-striated bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking green and yellow striped culms. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its culms are hollow and jointed, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Bambusa viridistriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
