Bauhinia forficata var. latifolia

Bauhinia forficata var. latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble the spread-out feathers of a peacock. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
