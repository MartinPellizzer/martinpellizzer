Bambusa striatomaculata

Bambusa striatomaculata, commonly known as the bamboo stripe and spots bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking variegated foliage with green stripes and spots. The plant typically grows as a clumping bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its shoots are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The bamboo's rapid growth rate and adaptability make it a favored plant for landscaping and sustainable agriculture.
