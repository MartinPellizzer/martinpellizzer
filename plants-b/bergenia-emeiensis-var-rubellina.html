Bergenia emeiensis var. rubellina

Bergenia emeiensis var. rubellina, commonly known as the Emei Mountain bergenia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and dense rosettes of leathery, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
