Bauhinia laui

Bauhinia laui, commonly known as the velvetwood or blue bauhinia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often blue-tinged leaves that are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Bauhinia laui thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests or along riverbanks.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
