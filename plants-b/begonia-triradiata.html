Begonia triradiata

Begonia triradiata, commonly known as the three-rayed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that split into three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red flowers with three petals, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia triradiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
