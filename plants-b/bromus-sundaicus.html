Bromus sundaicus

Bromus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda brome, is a grass species native to the island of Java in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a distinct blue-green coloration.

Bromus sundaicus is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas.
