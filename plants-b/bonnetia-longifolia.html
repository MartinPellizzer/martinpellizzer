Bonnetia longifolia

Bonnetia longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, making it visually distinctive.

Bonnetia longifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
