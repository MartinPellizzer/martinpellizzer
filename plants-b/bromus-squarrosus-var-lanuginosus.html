Bromus squarrosus var. lanuginosus

Bromus squarrosus var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, disturbed soils, and open woodlands.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
