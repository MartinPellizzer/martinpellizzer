Bulbophyllum cryptomeriicola

Bulbophyllum cryptomeriicola, commonly known as the Cryptomeria orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, featuring a blend of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage structures to help it survive in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
