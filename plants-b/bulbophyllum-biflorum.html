Bulbophyllum biflorum

Bulbophyllum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.
