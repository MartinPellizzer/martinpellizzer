Barnardia japonica f. albiflora

Barnardia japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with some varieties displaying pale pink or lavender hues. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of elegance to the landscape.
