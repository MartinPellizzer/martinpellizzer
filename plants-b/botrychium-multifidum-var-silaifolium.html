Botrychium multifidum var. silaifolium

Botrychium multifidum var. silaifolium, commonly known as the Silaifolium moonwort, is a fern species characterized by its unique morphology and ecological niche.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a complex, branched structure. Its leaves are typically bi- or trifoliate, with a distinctive, delicate appearance.

This variety is relatively rare and is often found in specific geographic regions, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
