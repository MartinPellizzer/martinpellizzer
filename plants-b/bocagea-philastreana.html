Bocagea philastreana

Bocagea philastreana, commonly known as the Florida bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscapes.

Bocagea philastreana is also noted for its ability to withstand salt spray, making it suitable for maritime environments.
