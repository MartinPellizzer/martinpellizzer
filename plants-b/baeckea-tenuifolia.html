Baeckea tenuifolia

Baeckea tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Baeckea tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
