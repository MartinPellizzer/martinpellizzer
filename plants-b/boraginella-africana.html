Boraginella africana

Boraginella africana, commonly known as the African boraginella, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has simple, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which includes other plants with similar medicinal and ornamental uses.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
