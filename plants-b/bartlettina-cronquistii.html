Bartlettina cronquistii

Bartlettina cronquistii, commonly known as the Cronquist's barberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Bartlettina cronquistii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
