Berberis koreana var. ellipsoidea

Berberis koreana var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the Korean barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
