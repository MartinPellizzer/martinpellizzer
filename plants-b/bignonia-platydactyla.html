Bignonia platydactyla

Bignonia platydactyla, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and ability to climb or trail over structures.
