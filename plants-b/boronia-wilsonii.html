Boronia wilsonii

Boronia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Boronia wilsonii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
