Bootia ocymoides

Bootia ocymoides, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions by efficiently absorbing and storing water. Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
