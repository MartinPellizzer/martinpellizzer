Bogoria gracile

Bogoria gracile, commonly known as the slender bog orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils, often found in swampy areas along rivers and lakes. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to its specialized habitat, Bogoria gracile is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
