Brachionidium machupicchuense

Brachionidium machupicchuense, commonly known as the Machu Picchu waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the high Andes region of Peru.

It is characterized by its spirally arranged, ribbon-like leaves that resemble the structure of a waterwheel, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in mountain streams and lakes. Its leaves are typically green to bluish-green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Brachionidium machupicchuense plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
