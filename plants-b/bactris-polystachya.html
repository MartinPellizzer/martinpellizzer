Bactris polystachya

Bactris polystachya, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. Bactris polystachya is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for local communities.
