Bromus tectorum var. nudus

Bromus tectorum var. nudus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and dry slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
