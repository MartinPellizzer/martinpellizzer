Berberis breviracema

Berberis breviracema, commonly known as the short-stalked barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
