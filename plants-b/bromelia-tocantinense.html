Bromelia tocantinense

Bromelia tocantinense, commonly known as the Tocantins bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many tropical plants known for their vibrant foliage and unique floral structures. The plant features a rosette of waxy, sword-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in a tall inflorescence and are pollinated by insects and birds.

Bromelia tocantinense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its natural habitat.
