Burnatia alismatoides var. elliptica

Burnatia alismatoides var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features elongated, elliptical leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted for aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Alismataceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in shallow water.

It plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
