Balsamina mutila

Balsamina mutila, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often with a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their symmetry and vibrant coloration.

Balsamina mutila is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its beauty and ease of care.
