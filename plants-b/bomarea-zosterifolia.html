Bomarea zosterifolia

Bomarea zosterifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its ornamental, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate flowers, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
