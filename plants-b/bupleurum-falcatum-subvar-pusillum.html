Bupleurum falcatum subvar. pusillum

Bupleurum falcatum subvar. pusillum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, somewhat hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive, slightly curved shape.

This species is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
