Bulbine semibarbata

Bulbine semibarbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbine, is a perennial succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant blooms with clusters of yellow flowers that are typically tubular in shape, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in many regions.
