Bursa origo

Bursa origo, commonly known as the "origin burr," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a dry, spiny capsule that can adhere to clothing and animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal.

Bursa origo is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
