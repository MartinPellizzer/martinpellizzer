Begonia botryoides

Begonia botryoides, commonly known as the marsh begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage.
