Banksia argentea

Banksia argentea, commonly known as the silver banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Banksia argentea plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
