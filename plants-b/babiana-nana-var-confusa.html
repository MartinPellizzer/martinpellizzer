Babiana nana var. confusa

Babiana nana var. confusa, commonly known as the dwarf babiana, is a small, perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and colorful, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal in gardens.
