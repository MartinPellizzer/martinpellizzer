Bambusa brassii

Bambusa brassii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of clumping bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-green culms that stand out against the surrounding foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, adding to its ornamental appeal. Bambusa brassii thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
