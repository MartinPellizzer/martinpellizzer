Bauhinia cumingiana

Bauhinia cumingiana, commonly known as the Chinese bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The plant produces flat, papery pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Bauhinia cumingiana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a landscape plant in warm regions.
