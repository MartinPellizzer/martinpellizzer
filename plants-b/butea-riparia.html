Butea riparia

Butea riparia, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its woody, branching structure. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

Butea riparia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
