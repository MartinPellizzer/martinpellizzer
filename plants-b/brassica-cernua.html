Brassica cernua

Brassica cernua, commonly known as the downy mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and often covered in a white waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are small and contained within pods that split open when mature.

Brassica cernua is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and meadows.
