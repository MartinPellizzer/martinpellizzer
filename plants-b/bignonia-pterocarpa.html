Bignonia pterocarpa

Bignonia pterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
