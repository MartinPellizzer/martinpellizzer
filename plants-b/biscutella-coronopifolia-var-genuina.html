Biscutella coronopifolia var. genuina

Biscutella coronopifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
