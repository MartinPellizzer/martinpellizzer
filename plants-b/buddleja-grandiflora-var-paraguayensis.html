Buddleja grandiflora var. paraguayensis

Buddleja grandiflora var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay butterfly bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It features slender, woody stems and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
