Baccaurea reticulata

Baccaurea reticulata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a thick-skinned fruit that contains a juicy, segmented pulp. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
