Bupleurum falcatum var. diversifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variable cowplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are typically lanceolate and have a distinctive, wavy margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
