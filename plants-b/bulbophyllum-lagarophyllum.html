Bulbophyllum lagarophyllum

Bulbophyllum lagarophyllum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient water retention.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
