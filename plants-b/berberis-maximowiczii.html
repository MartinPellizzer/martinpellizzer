Berberis maximowiczii

Berberis maximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different climates.
