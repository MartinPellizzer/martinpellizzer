Bougainvillea racemosa

Bougainvillea racemosa, commonly known as the "paper flower," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The actual flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense inflorescences called racemes. This plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is known for its ability to grow on trellises or as a shrub.
