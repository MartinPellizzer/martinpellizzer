Bromus commutatus subsp. decipiens

Bromus commutatus subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the mimic bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct flowering period in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
