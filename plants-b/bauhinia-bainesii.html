Bauhinia bainesii

Bauhinia bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' burrbean, is a deciduous leguminous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a hand. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that enclose the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Bauhinia bainesii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
