Baptistonia × amicta

Baptistonia × amicta, commonly known as the hybrid blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a cross between Baptistonia uniflora and Baptistonia leptophylla, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species is notable for its dense, silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Baptistonia × amicta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
