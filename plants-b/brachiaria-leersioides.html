Brachiaria leersioides

Brachiaria leersioides, commonly known as partridge grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Brachiaria leersioides is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage production.
