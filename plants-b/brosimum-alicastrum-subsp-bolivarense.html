Brosimum alicastrum subsp. bolivarense

Brosimum alicastrum subsp.

bolivarense, commonly known as the "chachaco" or "chachacomo," is a member of the Moraceae family, native to the Amazon Basin. It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its latex, similar to that of the rubber tree, has been historically used for commercial purposes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographic distribution and specific morphological traits compared to other subspecies of Brosimum.
