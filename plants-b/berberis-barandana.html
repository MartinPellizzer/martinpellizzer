Berberis barandana

Berberis barandana, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
