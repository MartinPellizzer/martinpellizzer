Brassica amplexicaulis var. maroccana

Brassica amplexicaulis var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan heartleaf mustard, is a variant of the heartleaf mustard species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional purposes.
