Blephariglottis × osceola

Blephariglottis × osceola, commonly known as the Osceola bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid climates.
