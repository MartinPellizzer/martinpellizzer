Brassica oleracea subvar. compressa

Brassica oleracea subvar. compressa, commonly known as curly kale, is a leafy vegetable characterized by its dense, curled leaves that are typically dark green.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small garden spaces and diverse growing conditions.

The leaves are often used in salads, soups, and cooked dishes, contributing a slightly bitter, earthy flavor.
