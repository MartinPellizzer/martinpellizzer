Becium empetroides

Becium empetroides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Becium empetroides is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
