Brassica oleracea f. rubra

Brassica oleracea f. rubra, commonly known as red cabbage, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species characterized by its dense, compact heads of deep red or purple leaves.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, with a vibrant coloration due to high levels of anthocyanins. It is cultivated for its edible leaves and is a popular vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins and minerals.
