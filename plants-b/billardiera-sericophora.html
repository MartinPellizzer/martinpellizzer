Billardiera sericophora

Billardiera sericophora, commonly known as the silky billardiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Billardiera sericophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
