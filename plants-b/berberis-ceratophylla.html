Berberis ceratophylla

Berberis ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply spined leaves and bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its autumn foliage, which turns a vibrant red or orange.
