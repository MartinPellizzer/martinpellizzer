Blechnum reedei

Blechnum reedei, commonly known as the Reed's harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, lance-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found clinging to tree trunks or growing in shaded, moist environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and the plant is noted for its slow growth and ornamental value in humid, shaded gardens.

Blechnum reedei is valued for its unique foliage and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
