Baudinia

Baudinia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stems. Baudinia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
