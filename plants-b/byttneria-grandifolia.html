Byttneria grandifolia

Byttneria grandifolia, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red, resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor, and it is often used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
