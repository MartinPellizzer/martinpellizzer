Berberis kumaonensis

Berberis kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
