Berteroa peruviana

Berteroa peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian burr or wild lettuce, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its latex contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
