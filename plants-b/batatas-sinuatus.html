Batatas sinuatus

Batatas sinuatus, commonly known as the warty sweet potato, is a tropical vine belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, warty tuberous roots that are typically orange or reddish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and vitamin A, making them a valuable food source in many regions.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
