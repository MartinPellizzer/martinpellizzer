Botrychium rutifolium

Botrychium rutifolium, commonly known as the common moonwort, is a fern that belongs to the Botrychiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are covered in small, upward-pointing spore cases called sorus. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short, creeping rhizome and is often found in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

Botrychium rutifolium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
