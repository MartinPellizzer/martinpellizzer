Berberis racemosa

Berberis racemosa, commonly known as the red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant bears red, orange, or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval and have a leathery texture.

Berberis racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
