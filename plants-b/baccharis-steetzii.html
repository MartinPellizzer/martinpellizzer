Baccharis steetzii

Baccharis steetzii, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry to mesic habitats, including woodlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate, often gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are small and clustered.

Baccharis steetzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
