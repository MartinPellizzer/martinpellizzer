Begonia satrapis

Begonia satrapis, commonly known as the Satrapis begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often have a distinctive reddish or bronze hue. The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, are borne on erect spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
