Blechnum orientale var. cristatum

Blechnum orientale var. cristatum, commonly known as the cristate harts-tongue fern, is a variant of the harts-tongue fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, crinkled fronds that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are leathery and dark green, with a crinkled margin that adds to its visual appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
