Bothriospermum chinense

Bothriospermum chinense, commonly known as Chinese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
