Bolivicactus aureicentrus

Bolivicactus aureicentrus, commonly known as the golden-centred bolivian cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the arid environment.

Bolivicactus aureicentrus is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
