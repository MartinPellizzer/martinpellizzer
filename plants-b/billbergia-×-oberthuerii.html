Billbergia × oberthuerii

Billbergia × oberthuerii, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia recurvata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that contrast with its green foliage. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
