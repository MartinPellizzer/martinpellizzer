Bipinnula canisii

Bipinnula canisii, commonly known as the dog's tooth fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

Its fronds are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two levels of pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia along the margins of the pinnae.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
