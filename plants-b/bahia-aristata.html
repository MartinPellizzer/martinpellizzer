Bahia aristata

Bahia aristata, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Bahia aristata is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
