Bombax religiosum

Bombax religiosum, commonly known as the sacred cotton tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink or red. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like fibers that are used in traditional crafts and textiles. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in various regions.
