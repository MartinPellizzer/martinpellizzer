Brachychiton paradoxus var. bidwillii

Brachychiton paradoxus var. bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's flame tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a deciduous tree that produces large, showy orange-red flowers during the wet season. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
