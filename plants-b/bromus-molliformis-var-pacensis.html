Bromus molliformis var. pacensis

Bromus molliformis var. pacensis, commonly known as the Pacific bromegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering season that occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
