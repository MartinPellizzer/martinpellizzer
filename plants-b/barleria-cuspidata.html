Barleria cuspidata

Barleria cuspidata, commonly known as the needle-leaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pointed leaves and clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria cuspidata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
