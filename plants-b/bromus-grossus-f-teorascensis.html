Bromus grossus f. teorascensis

Bromus grossus f. teorascensis, commonly known as the Teorascens brome, is a botanical variant of the common brome grass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in parts of Italy and Greece. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowering spikes are usually compact and bear small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.
