Bromus grossus var. arduennensis

Bromus grossus var. arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennian rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
