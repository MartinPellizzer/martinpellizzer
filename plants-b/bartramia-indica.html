Bartramia indica

Bartramia indica, commonly known as the Indian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant grows in wet, marshy habitats and has a short rhizome with a single leaf. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns.

Bartramia indica is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth.
