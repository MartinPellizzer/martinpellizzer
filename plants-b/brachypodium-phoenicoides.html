Brachypodium phoenicoides

Brachypodium phoenicoides, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelike flowers that are typically purplish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Brachypodium phoenicoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
