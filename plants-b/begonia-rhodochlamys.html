Begonia rhodochlamys

Begonia rhodochlamys, commonly known as the flame begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or trailing plant, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
