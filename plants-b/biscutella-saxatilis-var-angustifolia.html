Biscutella saxatilis var. angustifolia

Biscutella saxatilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a hairy surface, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
