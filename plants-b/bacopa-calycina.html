Bacopa calycina

Bacopa calycina, commonly known as the small-leaf water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, small ovate leaves, and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.

Additionally, it is valued for its ability to improve water quality by filtering pollutants.
