Berberis magnifolia

Berberis magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a serrated edge.

Berberis magnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
