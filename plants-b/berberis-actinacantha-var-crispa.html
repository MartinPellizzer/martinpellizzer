Berberis actinacantha var. crispa

Berberis actinacantha var. crispa, commonly known as the curled barbed wire bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive curled or twisted appearance, which gives the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardiness.
