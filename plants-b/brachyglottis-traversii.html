Brachyglottis traversii

Brachyglottis traversii, commonly known as the Stewart Island bristlebush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
