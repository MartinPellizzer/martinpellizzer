Bahiella blanchetii

Bahiella blanchetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bahiagrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green flowers in clusters. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
