Begonia glabricaulis

Begonia glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
