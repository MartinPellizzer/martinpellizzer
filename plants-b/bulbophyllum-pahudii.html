Bulbophyllum pahudii

Bulbophyllum pahudii, commonly known as the Pahudii orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small, showy flowers that bloom in clusters.

The botanical features of Bulbophyllum pahudii make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
