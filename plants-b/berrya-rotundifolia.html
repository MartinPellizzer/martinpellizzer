Berrya rotundifolia

Berrya rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf berry or wild strawberry, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are round or heart-shaped, and its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Berrya rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
