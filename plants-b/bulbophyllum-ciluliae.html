Bulbophyllum ciluliae

Bulbophyllum ciluliae, commonly known as the Ciluliae orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
