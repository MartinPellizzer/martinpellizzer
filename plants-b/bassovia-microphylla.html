Bassovia microphylla

Bassovia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
