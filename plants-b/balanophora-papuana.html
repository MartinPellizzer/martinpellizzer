Balanophora papuana

Balanophora papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ghost flower, is an epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Papua.

It belongs to the family Balanophoraceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, relying instead on parasitic relationships with other plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often hidden within the host plant's foliage. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the host, and it has a unique reproductive system involving mycorrhizal fungi.

Balanophora papuana is a rare and elusive species, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
