Bulbophyllum rheophyton

Bulbophyllum rheophyton, commonly known as the stream orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, linear leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
