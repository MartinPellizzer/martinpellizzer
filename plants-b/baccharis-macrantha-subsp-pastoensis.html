Baccharis macrantha subsp. pastoensis

Baccharis macrantha subsp.

pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often used in local landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
