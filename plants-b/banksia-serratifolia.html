Banksia serratifolia

Banksia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and it has narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges. Banksia serratifolia is adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
