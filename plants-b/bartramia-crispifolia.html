Bartramia crispifolia

Bartramia crispifolia, commonly known as the slender false orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are often clustered in loose inflorescences, and the species is known for its subtle fragrance.

Bartramia crispifolia is a relatively rare plant, with limited geographic distribution and specific ecological requirements.
