Boesenbergia callophylia

Boesenbergia callophylia, commonly known as the purple ginger or Malabar ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture with a distinct aroma. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
