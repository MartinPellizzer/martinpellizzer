Brachychloa fragilis

Brachychloa fragilis, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a small annual grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, which aids in its colonization of disturbed areas.

Due to its fragile nature, it is often found in ephemeral habitats where it completes its life cycle quickly.
