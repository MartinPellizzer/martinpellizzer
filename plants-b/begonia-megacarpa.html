Begonia megacarpa

Begonia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its most distinctive feature is its enormous, fleshy, red fruits that can reach up to 10 cm in diameter.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit structure.
