Berginia hintonii

Berginia hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's bergenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically thick and fleshy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. Berginia hintonii thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
