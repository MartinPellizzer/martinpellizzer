Brickellia secundiflora var. nepetifolia

Brickellia secundiflora var. nepetifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences, and the species is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in xeric habitats.
