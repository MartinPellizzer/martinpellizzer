Bulbophyllum quasimodo

Bulbophyllum quasimodo, commonly known as the Quasimodo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often pendulous flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pink and purple. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Bulbophyllum quasimodo is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
