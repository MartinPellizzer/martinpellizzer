Begonia scabridoidea

Begonia scabridoidea, commonly known as the rough-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Begonia scabridoidea is a perennial species that thrives in partial to full shade.
