Boehmeria tiliifolia

Boehmeria tiliifolia, commonly known as Chinese grass or silky senna, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, fibrous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its strong, fibrous stalks, which have been used in traditional crafts and papermaking.
