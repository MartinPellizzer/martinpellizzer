Betula microphylla var. coreana

Betula microphylla var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean birch, is a deciduous tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its bark is smooth and pale, often with a slight reddish tinge.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
