Bignonia articulata

Bignonia articulata, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Bignonia articulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
