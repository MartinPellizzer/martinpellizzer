Brassavola cuspidata

Brassavola cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near forest floors or along streams. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Brassavola cuspidata is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
