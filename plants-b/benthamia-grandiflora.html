Benthamia grandiflora

Benthamia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central and western Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust, branching stem that often grows up to 50 cm in height. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Benthamia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
