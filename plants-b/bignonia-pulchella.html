Bignonia pulchella

Bignonia pulchella, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia pulchella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
