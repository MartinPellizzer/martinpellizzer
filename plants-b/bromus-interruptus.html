Bromus interruptus

Bromus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with a fibrous root system, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Bromus interruptus is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
