Begonia melinauensis

Begonia melinauensis, commonly known as the Melinau begonia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy texture, which help it retain moisture in the humid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
