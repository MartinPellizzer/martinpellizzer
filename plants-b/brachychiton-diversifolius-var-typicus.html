Brachychiton diversifolius var. typicus

Brachychiton diversifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal floral display.
