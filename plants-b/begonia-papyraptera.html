Begonia papyraptera

Begonia papyraptera, commonly known as the paper-winged begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
