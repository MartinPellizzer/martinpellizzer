Bistorta attenuata

Bistorta attenuata, commonly known as redtop bistort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish hue, especially when grown in full sun.

Bistorta attenuata is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
