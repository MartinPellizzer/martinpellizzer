Bombax insigne

Bombax insigne, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release cotton-like fibers, which are used in various traditional and industrial applications. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a broad, spreading canopy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often planted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
