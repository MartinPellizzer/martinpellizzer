Buddleja brachystachya

Buddleja brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Buddleja brachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
