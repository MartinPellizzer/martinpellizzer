Belamcanda villosa

Belamcanda villosa, commonly known as the hairy daylily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Belamcanda villosa is known for its short blooming period in late summer, producing clusters of fragrant flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
