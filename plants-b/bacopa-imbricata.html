Bacopa imbricata

Bacopa imbricata, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Bacopa imbricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive and calming effects.
