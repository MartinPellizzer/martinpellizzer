Bartonia pentandra

Bartonia pentandra, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Bartonia pentandra plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
