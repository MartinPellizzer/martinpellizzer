Barbarea vulgaris f. plena

Barbarea vulgaris f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered winter cress, is a cultivated variant of the winter cress species.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the winter months. The plant is a biennial or short-lived perennial, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or as a winter-flowering ground cover.
