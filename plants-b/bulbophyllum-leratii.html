Bulbophyllum leratii

Bulbophyllum leratii, commonly known as the Lemon-scented Pterostigma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered with a waxy cuticle, aiding in moisture retention.

Bulbophyllum leratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
