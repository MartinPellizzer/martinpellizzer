Bulbophyllum forbesii

Bulbophyllum forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
