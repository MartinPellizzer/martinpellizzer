Baccharis pteronioides

Baccharis pteronioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Baccharis pteronioides is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is commonly used in xeriscaping and wildlife habitats.
