Blepharocalyx tweediei f. angustus

Blepharocalyx tweediei f. angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame tree, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
