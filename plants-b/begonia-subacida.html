Begonia subacida

Begonia subacida, commonly known as the sour begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia subacida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
