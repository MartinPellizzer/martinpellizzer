Brassavola perrinii

Brassavola perrinii, commonly known as the Perrin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a short rhizome with a dense cluster of fleshy, green leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Brassavola perrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
