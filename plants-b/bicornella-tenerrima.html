Bicornella tenerrima

Bicornella tenerrima, commonly known as the slender horned orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
