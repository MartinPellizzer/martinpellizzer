Boquila trifoliolata var. discolor

Boquila trifoliolata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated boquila, is a climbing plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow hues. The plant has small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a specimen plant in botanical collections.
