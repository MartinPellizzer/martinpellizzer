Brickellia spinulosa var. typica

Brickellia spinulosa var. typica, commonly known as the rough brickellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
