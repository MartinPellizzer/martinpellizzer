Bluffia eckloniana

Bluffia eckloniana, commonly known as the bluebell of the Cape, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Bluffia eckloniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
