Begonia aurantiiflora

Begonia aurantiiflora, commonly known as the orange-flowered begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
