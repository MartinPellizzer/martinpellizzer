Bauhinia sericella

Bauhinia sericella, commonly known as the silk tree or velvet bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia sericella is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
