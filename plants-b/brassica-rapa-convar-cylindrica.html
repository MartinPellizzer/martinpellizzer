Brassica rapa convar. cylindrica

Brassica rapa convar. cylindrica, commonly known as the turnip, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, globe-shaped root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant is cultivated for its edible root and is also used as a forage crop in agriculture.
