Brassica racemiflora

Brassica racemiflora, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which is a type of inflorescence. Its leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape depending on the variety.

Brassica racemiflora is often found in moist environments and can be cultivated as a vegetable or used in traditional medicine.
