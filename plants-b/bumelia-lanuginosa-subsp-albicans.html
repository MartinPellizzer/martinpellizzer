Bumelia lanuginosa subsp. albicans

Bumelia lanuginosa subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white-hairy bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its branches and stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by oval, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
