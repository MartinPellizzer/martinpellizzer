Bulbophyllum klabatense

Bulbophyllum klabatense, commonly known as the Klabatense orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. It typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with periods of drought.

Its unique morphology and floral display make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
