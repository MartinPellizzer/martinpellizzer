Bigelowia nudata

Bigelowia nudata, commonly known as the naked bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has no leaves along its flowering stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in dry, sunny conditions.

Bigelowia nudata is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for native pollinators.
