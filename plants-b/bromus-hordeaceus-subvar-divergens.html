Bromus hordeaceus subvar. divergens

Bromus hordeaceus subvar. divergens, commonly known as the divergent brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, tufted culms with dense clusters of spikelets at the nodes. Its leaves are generally narrow and have a bluish-green sheen.

The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated raceme bearing small, spikelet-based flowers.
