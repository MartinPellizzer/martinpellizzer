Biscutella rotgesii

Biscutella rotgesii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces delicate blue or violet flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its unique structure, with the flowers forming a bell-like shape on slender stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Biscutella rotgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
