Brassica gemmifera

Brassica gemmifera, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by siliqua fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Brassica gemmifera is an annual plant that has been used traditionally in agriculture and herbal medicine.
