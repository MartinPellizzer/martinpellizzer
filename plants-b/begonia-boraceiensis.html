Begonia boraceiensis

Begonia boraceiensis, commonly known as the Boraceiense Begonia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
