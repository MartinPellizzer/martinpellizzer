Byrsonima sericea var. eglandulosa

Byrsonima sericea var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the silky false mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silver-green, hair-covered leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
