Brachyotum callosum

Brachyotum callosum, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact, woody structure and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, thick, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Brachyotum callosum is well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
