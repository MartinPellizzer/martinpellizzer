Baldingera glandulosa

Baldingera glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular baldingera, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
