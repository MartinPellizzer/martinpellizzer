Berberis temolaica

Berberis temolaica, commonly known as the Temolaic barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
