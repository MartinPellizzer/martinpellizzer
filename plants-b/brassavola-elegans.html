Brassavola elegans

Brassavola elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands.

Brassavola elegans is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific habitat conditions for successful growth.
