Beaufortia empetrifolia

Beaufortia empetrifolia, commonly known as the rough beaufortia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the autumn season. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a rough or hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Beaufortia empetrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
