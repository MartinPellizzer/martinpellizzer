Begonia thaipingensis

Begonia thaipingensis, commonly known as the Taiwan begonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.
