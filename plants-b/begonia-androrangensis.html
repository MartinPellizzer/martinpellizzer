Begonia androrangensis

Begonia androrangensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with intricate patterns, and the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
