Bursa × gracilis

Bursa × gracilis, commonly known as the slender bursa, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Bursa falcatum and Bursa pendula.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, elongated bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, which are typically pink or red in color. Bursa × gracilis is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
