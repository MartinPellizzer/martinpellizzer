Barbarea macrophylla

Barbarea macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces rounded, green pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is a hardy perennial that can spread aggressively in suitable conditions.
