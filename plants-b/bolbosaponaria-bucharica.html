Bolbosaponaria bucharica

Bolbosaponaria bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian bulbous saponaria, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears pink or purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The genus Bolbosaponaria is known for its saponin-containing compounds, which can have medicinal properties.
