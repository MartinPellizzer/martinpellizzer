Balsamorrhiza rosea

Balsamorrhiza rosea, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy pink to purple flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, fleshy root that is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Balsamorrhiza rosea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
