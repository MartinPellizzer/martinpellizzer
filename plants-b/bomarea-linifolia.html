Bomarea linifolia

Bomarea linifolia, commonly known as the silky bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, often hairy surface, and its stems are typically slender and climbing. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
