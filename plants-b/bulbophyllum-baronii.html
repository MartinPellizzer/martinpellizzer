Bulbophyllum baronii

Bulbophyllum baronii, commonly known as the Baron's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer, featuring a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Bulbophyllum baronii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
