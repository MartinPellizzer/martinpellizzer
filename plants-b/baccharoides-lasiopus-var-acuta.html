Baccharoides lasiopus var. acuta

Baccharoides lasiopus var. acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf baccharoides, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and grayish-white underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, which are usually pink or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological botany.
