Banksia aemula

Banksia aemula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking yellow flowers that appear in dense cylindrical spikes. Banksia aemula is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators.
