Brachiaria adspersa

Brachiaria adspersa, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria adspersa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
