Bauhinia ornata var. burmanica

Bauhinia ornata var. burmanica, commonly known as Burmese Bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, bi-lobed leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in its native regions.
