Barleria trispinosa subsp. glandulosissima

Barleria trispinosa subsp.

glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a desirable choice for rock gardens and container planting.
