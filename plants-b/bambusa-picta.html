Bambusa picta

Bambusa picta, commonly known as the painted bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it highly sought after in horticulture. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo with slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive coloration that changes with the seasons.

Bambusa picta thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
