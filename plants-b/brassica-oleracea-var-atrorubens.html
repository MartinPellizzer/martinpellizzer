Brassica oleracea var. atrorubens

Brassica oleracea var. atrorubens, commonly known as red cabbage, is a cultivar of the cabbage species characterized by its deep crimson to purple-colored leaves.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is closely related to other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant typically grows as a biennial, though it is often cultivated as an annual in temperate climates. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a slightly sour taste due to the presence of glucosinolates.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins C and K, as well as its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
