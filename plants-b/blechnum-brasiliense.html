Blechnum brasiliense

Blechnum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian harts tongue fern, is a tropical fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the fronds are sometimes used as ornamental plants.

The species exhibits a unique reproductive strategy with sporangia arranged in clusters on the undersides of its leaves.
