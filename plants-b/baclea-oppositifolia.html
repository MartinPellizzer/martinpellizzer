Baclea oppositifolia

Baclea oppositifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild cotton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Baclea oppositifolia is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
