Bernoullia nutans

Bernoullia nutans, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive, bell-like blooms.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
