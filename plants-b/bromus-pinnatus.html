Bromus pinnatus

Bromus pinnatus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with brown or purple.

Bromus pinnatus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
