Bourreria succulenta f. umbrosa

Bourreria succulenta f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded bourreria, is a rare and specialized succulent plant native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a typical adaptation to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are often a deep green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
