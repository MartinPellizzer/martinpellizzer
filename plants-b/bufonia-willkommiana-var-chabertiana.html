Bufonia willkommiana var. chabertiana

Bufonia willkommiana var. chabertiana, commonly known as the Chabert's devil's backbone, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
