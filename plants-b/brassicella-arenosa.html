Brassicella arenosa

Brassicella arenosa, commonly known as the sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Brassicella arenosa is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.
