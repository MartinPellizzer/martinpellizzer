Beta patellaris f. campanulata

Beta patellaris f. campanulata, commonly known as the campanulata form of the sea beet, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped leaves.

This form is a subspecies of Beta patellaris, which is native to the coastal regions of southern Europe. The leaves are typically more elongated and curled compared to the standard form, giving the plant a unique appearance. It thrives in saline and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions.

The plant is often found growing in association with other halophytic species, contributing to the biodiversity of maritime ecosystems.
