Botryanthus lelievrei var. pulchellus

Botryanthus lelievrei var. pulchellus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The variety pulchellus is noted for its compact growth habit and vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
