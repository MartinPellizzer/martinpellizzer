Bignonia tecomiflora

Bignonia tecomiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia tecomiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used as a decorative vine in gardens or as a support plant for other structures.
