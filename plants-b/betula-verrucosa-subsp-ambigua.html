Betula verrucosa subsp. ambigua

Betula verrucosa subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the Alaska paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellow or cream-colored inner layer.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal forests and tundra regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental bark and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
