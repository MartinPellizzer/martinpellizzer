Boerhavia transvaalensis

Boerhavia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
