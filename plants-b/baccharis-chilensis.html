Baccharis chilensis

Baccharis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean shrub or *Baccharis* shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its resilience to harsh environmental conditions makes it a common component of coastal vegetation.
