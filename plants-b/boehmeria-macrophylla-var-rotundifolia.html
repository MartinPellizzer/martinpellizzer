Boehmeria macrophylla var. rotundifolia

Boehmeria macrophylla var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems are used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fiber.
