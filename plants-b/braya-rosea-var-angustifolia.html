Braya rosea var. angustifolia

Braya rosea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are linear and usually less than 1 inch in length, while its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.
