Boscia albitrunca var. macrophylla

Boscia albitrunca var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale grey bark and large, rounded leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
