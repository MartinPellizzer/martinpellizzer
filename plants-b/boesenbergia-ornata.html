Boesenbergia ornata

Boesenbergia ornata, commonly known as the golden ginger or orange ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple base. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Boesenbergia ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in horticulture for its resilience and adaptability.
