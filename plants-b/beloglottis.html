Beloglottis

Beloglottis, commonly known as the forest bell or the silver bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where they thrive in humid, shaded environments. Beloglottis species are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature typical of many bromeliads. The flowers, which are often colorful and showy, are borne on short, erect inflorescences.

These plants are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to adapt to indoor conditions with proper care.
