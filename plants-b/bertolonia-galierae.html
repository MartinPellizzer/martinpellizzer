Bertolonia galierae

Bertolonia galierae, commonly known as the Galierae daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Bertolonia galierae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
