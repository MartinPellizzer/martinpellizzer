Baccharis poeppigiana subsp. ocellata

Baccharis poeppigiana subsp.

ocellata, commonly known as the "Mexican baccharis," is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescences consist of small yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
