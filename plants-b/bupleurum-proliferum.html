Bupleurum proliferum

Bupleurum proliferum, commonly known as the creeping barbed wire plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with multiple stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly across the ground. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
