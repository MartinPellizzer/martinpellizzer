Berardia affinis

Berardia affinis, commonly known as the common berberis, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a leathery texture, while its stems are woody and covered in thorns.

Berardia affinis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
