Brachycladium destitutum

Brachycladium destitutum, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

Due to its low profile and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
