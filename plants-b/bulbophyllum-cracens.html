Bulbophyllum cracens

Bulbophyllum cracens, commonly known as the Cracens orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Bulbophyllum cracens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
