Berberis × helenae

Berberis × helenae, commonly known as the Helen's barberry, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from several Berberis species.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
