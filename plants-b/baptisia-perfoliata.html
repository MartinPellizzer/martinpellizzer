Baptisia perfoliata

Baptisia perfoliata, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters. The plant features palmate leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. Baptisia perfoliata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
