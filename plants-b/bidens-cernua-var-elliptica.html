Bidens cernua var. elliptica

Bidens cernua var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are followed by three-lobed, bristly seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in various habitats.
