Begonia incarnata var. gracilis

Begonia incarnata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, shade-tolerant plant.
