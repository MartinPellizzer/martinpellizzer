Bauhinia stipularis var. brachystylus

Bauhinia stipularis var. brachystylus, commonly known as the dwarf bauhinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
