Begonia dentatobracteata

Begonia dentatobracteata, commonly known as the serrated bract begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are often hairy and have serrated edges, which give the plant its scientific name. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant's unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical gardens.
