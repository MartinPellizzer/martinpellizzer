Bupleurum falcatum var. verum

Bupleurum falcatum var. verum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, somewhat hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
