Balsamina lacca

Balsamina lacca, commonly known as the lacca balsam.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive yellow or green center. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
