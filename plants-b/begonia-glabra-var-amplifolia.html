Begonia glabra var. amplifolia

Begonia glabra var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually red or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
