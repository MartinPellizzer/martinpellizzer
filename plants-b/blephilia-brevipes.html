Blephilia brevipes

Blephilia brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in early spring, typically blooming before the leaves fully emerge. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Blephilia brevipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
