Bromus villosus var. minor

Bromus villosus var. minor, commonly known as the hairy brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open fields, meadows, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has slender, hair-covered stems and produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
