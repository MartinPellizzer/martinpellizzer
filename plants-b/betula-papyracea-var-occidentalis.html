Betula papyracea var. occidentalis

Betula papyracea var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
