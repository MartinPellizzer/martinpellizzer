Banksia tenuis var. reptans

Banksia tenuis var. reptans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and unique, woody cone-like flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathland and scrub environments. It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native wildlife, including honeyeaters and bees.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking floral display.
