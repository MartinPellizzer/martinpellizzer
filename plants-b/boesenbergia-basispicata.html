Boesenbergia basispicata

Boesenbergia basispicata, commonly known as the golden ginger or yellow ginger, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

Boesenbergia basispicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
