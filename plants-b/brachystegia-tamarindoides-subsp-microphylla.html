Brachystegia tamarindoides subsp. microphylla

Brachystegia tamarindoides subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in miombo woodlands and is adapted to seasonal climates with distinct wet and dry periods.
