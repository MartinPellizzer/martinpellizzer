Boronia coerulescens subsp. subspicata

Boronia coerulescens subsp.

subspicata, commonly known as the blue boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, aromatic leaves and is often found in coastal regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
