Bellardia

Bellardia, commonly known as the bellardia grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Australia and South America. The plants typically have slender, tufted grass-like foliage with small, inconspicuous flowers. Bellardia species are often found in sandy or rocky soils, growing in coastal or inland areas.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping due to their hardy nature.
