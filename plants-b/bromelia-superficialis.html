Bromelia superficialis

Bromelia superficialis, commonly known as the slender bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Bromelia superficialis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
