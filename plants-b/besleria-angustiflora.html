Besleria angustiflora

Besleria angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved besleria, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, often giving rise to colorful, showy bracts.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm, humid climates.
