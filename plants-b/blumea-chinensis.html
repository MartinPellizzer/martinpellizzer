Blumea chinensis

Blumea chinensis, commonly known as Chinese blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Blumea chinensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
