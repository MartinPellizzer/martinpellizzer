Brossaea nitida

Brossaea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf brossaea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in its arid environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers, while its drought tolerance makes it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.

Brossaea nitida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
