Bauhinia siamensis

Bauhinia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Bauhinia siamensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
