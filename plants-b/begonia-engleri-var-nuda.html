Begonia engleri var. nuda

Begonia engleri var. nuda, commonly known as the naked begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
