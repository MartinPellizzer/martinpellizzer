Bambusa paniculata

Bambusa paniculata, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often have a silvery-white sheath at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, narrow fruits. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa paniculata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
