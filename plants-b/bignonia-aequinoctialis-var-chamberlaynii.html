Bignonia aequinoctialis var. chamberlaynii

Bignonia aequinoctialis var. chamberlaynii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, serpentine seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or trellises.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, flowering vine.
