Berberis amurensis var. quelpaertensis

Berberis amurensis var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright orange to red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden settings.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
