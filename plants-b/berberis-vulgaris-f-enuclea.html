Berberis vulgaris f. enuclea

Berberis vulgaris f. enuclea, commonly known as the naked-stem barberry, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its reduced stem structure.

It features bright green, leathery leaves that are often arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. It is native to parts of Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
