Besleria amabilis

Besleria amabilis, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
