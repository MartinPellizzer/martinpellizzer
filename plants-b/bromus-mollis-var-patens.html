Bromus mollis var. patens

Bromus mollis var. patens, commonly known as the spreading brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, slightly hairy blades. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and grasslands.

Bromus mollis var. patens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
