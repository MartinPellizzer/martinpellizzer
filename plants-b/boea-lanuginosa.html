Boea lanuginosa

Boea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a climbing vine.

Boea lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
