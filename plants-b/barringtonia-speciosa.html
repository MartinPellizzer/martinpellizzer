Barringtonia speciosa

Barringtonia speciosa, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a distinctive, asymmetrical fruit that splits open to release its seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture.

Barringtonia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
