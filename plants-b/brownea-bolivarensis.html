Brownea bolivarensis

Brownea bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody structure with smooth, gray bark.

Brownea bolivarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its striking floral display.
