Begonia vuijckii

Begonia vuijckii, commonly known as the Vuijck's begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
