Banksia serratuloides subsp. serratuloides

Banksia serratuloides subsp.

serratuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant yellow to orange flowers.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a serrated or toothed margin, giving the plant its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
