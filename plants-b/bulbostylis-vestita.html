Bulbostylis vestita

Bulbostylis vestita, commonly known as the naked-styled bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a single bract, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
