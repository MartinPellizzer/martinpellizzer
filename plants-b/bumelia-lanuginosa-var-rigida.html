Bumelia lanuginosa var. rigida

Bumelia lanuginosa var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid woolybumelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
