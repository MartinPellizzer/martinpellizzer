Bignonia sieberi

Bignonia sieberi, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to South America.

It features long, slender stems that can climb up to 10 meters, supported by tendrils that grasp onto structures. The plant produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated edge.

Bignonia sieberi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
