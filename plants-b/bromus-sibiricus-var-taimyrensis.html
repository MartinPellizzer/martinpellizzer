Bromus sibiricus var. taimyrensis

Bromus sibiricus var. taimyrensis, commonly known as Siberian grass or Taimyrensk grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as tundra and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
