Brassica rapa subvar. viridis

Brassica rapa subvar. viridis, commonly known as garden mustard, is a leafy vegetable characterized by its rapid growth and high nutritional value.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant produces small yellow flowers and has a distinct peppery flavor due to glucosinolates. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its tender leaves and seed pods.

This variety is valued for its versatility in cooking and its ability to grow quickly in various soil conditions.
