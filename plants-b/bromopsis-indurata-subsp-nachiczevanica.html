Bromopsis indurata subsp. nachiczevanica

Bromopsis indurata subsp.

nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Nachiczevan bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is part of the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
