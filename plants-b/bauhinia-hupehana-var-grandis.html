Bauhinia hupehana var. grandis

Bauhinia hupehana var. grandis, commonly known as the Chinese honeytree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces flat, papery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and ecological purposes.
