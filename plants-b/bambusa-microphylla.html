Bambusa microphylla

Bambusa microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often green or purplish stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a few centimeters in length. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Bambusa microphylla is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
