Bauhinia wrayi var. blumeana

Bauhinia wrayi var. blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's Bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
