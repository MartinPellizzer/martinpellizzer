Bignonia acapulcensis

Bignonia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant yellow or orange hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

Bignonia acapulcensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
