Bauhinia macrostachya

Bauhinia macrostachya, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods and are dispersed by wind.

Bauhinia macrostachya is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
