Brickellia adenocarpa var. typica

Brickellia adenocarpa var. typica, commonly known as the smooth brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
