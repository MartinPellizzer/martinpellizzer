Blutaparon vermiculare var. aggregatum

Blutaparon vermiculare var. aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered bluetail fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and its distinctive, finely divided, bluish-green fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on high humidity and well-drained, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
