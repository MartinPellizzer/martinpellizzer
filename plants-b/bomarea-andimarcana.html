Bomarea andimarcana

Bomarea andimarcana, commonly known as the Andean bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features elongated, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with prominent stamens and a spurred ovary. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Bomarea andimarcana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
