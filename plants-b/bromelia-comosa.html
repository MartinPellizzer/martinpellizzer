Bromelia comosa

Bromelia comosa, commonly known as the woolly bromeliad, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and can grow up to several feet in length, creating a lush, water-holding structure.

Bromelia comosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
