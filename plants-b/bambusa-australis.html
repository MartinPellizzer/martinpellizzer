Bambusa australis

Bambusa australis, commonly known as the Australian bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to eastern Australia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or yellow-green in color, with a smooth, fibrous surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by rod-like fruits that contain minute seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural form.

Bambusa australis is also valued for its sustainable use in construction and crafts due to its strong, lightweight bamboo poles.
