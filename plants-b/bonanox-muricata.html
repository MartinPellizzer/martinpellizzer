Bonanox muricata

Bonanox muricata, commonly known as the spiked nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, known for its toxic properties.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features dark green, lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to red or black as they mature.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should not be ingested.
