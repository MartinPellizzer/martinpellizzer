Bulbine tetraphylla

Bulbine tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, fleshy stem and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Bulbine tetraphylla is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
