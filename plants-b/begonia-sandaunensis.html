Begonia sandaunensis

Begonia sandaunensis, commonly known as the Sandaun begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and red, which makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its adaptability to different soil types but thrives best in well-draining, nutrient-rich soils.
