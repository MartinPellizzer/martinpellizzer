Bromelia rohaniana

Bromelia rohaniana, commonly known as the red bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its unique leaf structure creates a natural reservoir that collects rainwater, supporting a micro-ecosystem.

Bromelia rohaniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
