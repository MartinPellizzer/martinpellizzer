Bauhinia acida

Bauhinia acida, commonly known as sour bauhinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines. Bauhinia acida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
