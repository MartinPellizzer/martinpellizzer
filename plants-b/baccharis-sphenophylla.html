Baccharis sphenophylla

Baccharis sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
