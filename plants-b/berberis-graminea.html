Berberis graminea

Berberis graminea, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis graminea is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts of the plant containing alkaloids used in traditional medicine.
