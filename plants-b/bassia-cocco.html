Bassia cocco

Bassia cocco, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

Bassia cocco plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
