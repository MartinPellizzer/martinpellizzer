Bulbophyllum contortisepalum

Bulbophyllum contortisepalum, commonly known as the twisted-sepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
