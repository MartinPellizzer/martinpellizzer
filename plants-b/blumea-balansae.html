Blumea balansae

Blumea balansae, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a cluster that resembles a dandelion, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

Blumea balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
