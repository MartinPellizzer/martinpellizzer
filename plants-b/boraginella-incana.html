Boraginella incana

Boraginella incana, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Boraginaceae family.
