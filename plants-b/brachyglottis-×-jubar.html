Brachyglottis × jubar

Brachyglottis × jubar, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a hybrid species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used for traditional weaving and basket-making by Māori communities.

Brachyglottis × jubar is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native habitats.
