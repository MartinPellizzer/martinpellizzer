Blechnum chilense

Blechnum chilense, commonly known as the Chile fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in temperate forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with long, pinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Blechnum chilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
