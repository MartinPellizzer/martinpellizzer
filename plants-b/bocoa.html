Bocoa

Bocoa, commonly known as the "coffee bean tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which also includes coffee and gardenias. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

Bocoa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
