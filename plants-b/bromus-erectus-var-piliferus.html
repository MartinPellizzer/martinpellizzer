Bromus erectus var. piliferus

Bromus erectus var. piliferus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often hairy, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
