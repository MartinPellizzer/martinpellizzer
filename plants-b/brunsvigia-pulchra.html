Brunsvigia pulchra

Brunsvigia pulchra, commonly known as the "Rainbow Flower," is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors, from red to orange and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often following a period of drought. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
