Bauhinia ruiziana

Bauhinia ruiziana, commonly known as the velvetwood or pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia ruiziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
