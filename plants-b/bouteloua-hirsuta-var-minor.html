Bouteloua hirsuta var. minor

Bouteloua hirsuta var. minor, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.

The plant is drought-tolerant and often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
