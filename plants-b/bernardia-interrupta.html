Bernardia interrupta

Bernardia interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted berberis, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
