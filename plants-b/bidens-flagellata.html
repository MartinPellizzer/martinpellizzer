Bidens flagellata

Bidens flagellata, commonly known as the tickseed or horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with ray florets that resemble daisies. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Bidens flagellata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
