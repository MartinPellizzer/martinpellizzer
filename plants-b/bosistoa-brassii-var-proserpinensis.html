Bosistoa brassii var. proserpinensis

Bosistoa brassii var. proserpinensis, commonly known as the Queensland hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to northeastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and riparian habitats. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
