Butea oblongifolia

Butea oblongifolia, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral display, which appears in early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with elliptical to oblong leaflets. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
