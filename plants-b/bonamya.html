Bonamya

Bonamya, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that resemble silver coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange-red fruit, which contains a single seed. Bonamya is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
