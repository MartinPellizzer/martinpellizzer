Bacopa bacopoides

Bacopa bacopoides, commonly known as the lesser water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in late summer.

Bacopa bacopoides is known for its ability to thrive in moist to submerged conditions, making it a useful plant for wetland restoration projects.
