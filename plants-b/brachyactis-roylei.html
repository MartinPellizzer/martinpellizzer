Brachyactis roylei

Brachyactis roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky and alpine areas. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that helps it withstand cold temperatures.

Brachyactis roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
