Begonia heineri

Begonia heineri, commonly known as the Heiner's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and attractive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia heineri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
