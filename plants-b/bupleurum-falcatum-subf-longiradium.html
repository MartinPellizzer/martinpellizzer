Bupleurum falcatum subf. longiradium

Bupleurum falcatum subf.

longiradium, commonly known as the long-leaf bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, often branched stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels.

The subspecies is characterized by its elongated leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
