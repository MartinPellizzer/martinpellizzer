Bidens amplectens

Bidens amplectens, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that often have a rough texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers with two white petals, which are typically clustered in groups of two. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, a structure that aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens amplectens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
