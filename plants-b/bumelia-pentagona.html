Bumelia pentagona

Bumelia pentagona, commonly known as the five-angle bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with five distinct angles, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is often fissured and can be used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples.

Bumelia pentagona typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
