Betonica officinalis var. incarnata

Betonica officinalis var. incarnata, commonly known as red betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Betonica officinalis var. incarnata has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
