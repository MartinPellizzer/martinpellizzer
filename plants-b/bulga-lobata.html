Bulga lobata

Bulga lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Bulga lobata is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
