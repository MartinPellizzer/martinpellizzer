Bulbophyllum namoronae

Bulbophyllum namoronae, commonly known as the Namoronae orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers typically feature vibrant colors and intricate patterns, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
