Bambusa lixin

Bambusa lixin, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that stand out against its green foliage. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its fast growth rate and strong, hollow stems make it valuable for construction and handicrafts.

Additionally, Bambusa lixin plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
