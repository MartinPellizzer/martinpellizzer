Brownlowia rubra

Brownlowia rubra, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and arranged oppositely on the stems. Its flowers are fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Brownlowia rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
