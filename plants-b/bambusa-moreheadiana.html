Bambusa moreheadiana

Bambusa moreheadiana, commonly known as the Morehead bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and seeds, typical of the grass family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
