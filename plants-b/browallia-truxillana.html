Browallia truxillana

Browallia truxillana, commonly known as the Trinidad browallia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, with vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
