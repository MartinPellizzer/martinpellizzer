Betula platyphylla var. mandshurica

Betula platyphylla var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree has slender, pendulous branches and produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring. Its leaves are triangular with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in cool climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
