Benzoin umbellatum var. latifolium

Benzoin umbellatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf benzoin, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that is often harvested for its aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and resin have been used in traditional medicine and incense-making for centuries.
