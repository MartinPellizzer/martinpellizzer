Bupleurum polyclonum

Bupleurum polyclonum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it is often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges.

Bupleurum polyclonum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
