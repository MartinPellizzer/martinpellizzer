Brickellia squarrosa var. oligadenia

Brickellia squarrosa var. oligadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
