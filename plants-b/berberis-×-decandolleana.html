Berberis × decandolleana

Berberis × decandolleana, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its spiny stems and dense foliage.

It is a hybrid between Berberis julianae and Berberis darwinii, resulting in a plant with enhanced ornamental features. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, while the flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
