Bulbophyllum odoratum

Bulbophyllum odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in clusters and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.
