Boltonia latisquama var. microcephala

Boltonia latisquama var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Boltonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
