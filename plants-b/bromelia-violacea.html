Bromelia violacea

Bromelia violacea, commonly known as the violet bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environments.

Bromelia violacea is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and long-lasting blooms.
