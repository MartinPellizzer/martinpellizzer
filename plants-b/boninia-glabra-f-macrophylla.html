Boninia glabra f. macrophylla

Boninia glabra f. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed snowbell, is a cultivar of the snowbell plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
