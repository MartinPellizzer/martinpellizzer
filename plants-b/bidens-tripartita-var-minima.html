Bidens tripartita var. minima

Bidens tripartita var. minima, commonly known as the smallleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early fall, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
