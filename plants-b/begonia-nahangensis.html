Begonia nahangensis

Begonia nahangensis, commonly known as the hanging begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated as a decorative hanging plant. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia nahangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardening.
