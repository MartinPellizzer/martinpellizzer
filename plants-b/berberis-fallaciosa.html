Berberis fallaciosa

Berberis fallaciosa, commonly known as the Sierra Madre barberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense canopy that offers habitat for wildlife.

Berberis fallaciosa is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
