Bulbophyllum trachyanthum

Bulbophyllum trachyanthum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive violet or purple hue.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
