Beschorneria pubescens

Beschorneria pubescens, commonly known as the Mexican bristle fern, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Agavaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow quite large, reaching up to several feet in length. This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
