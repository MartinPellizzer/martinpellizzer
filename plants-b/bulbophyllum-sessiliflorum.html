Bulbophyllum sessiliflorum

Bulbophyllum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
