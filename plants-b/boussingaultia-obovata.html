Boussingaultia obovata

Boussingaultia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen, succulent leaves that are broad and obovate in shape. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
