Bilabrella kyimbilae

Bilabrella kyimbilae, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features a single, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and are known for their fragrant, bell-like shape.

Due to habitat loss, it is classified as vulnerable, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
