Bigelowia nauseosa var. latisquamea

Bigelowia nauseosa var. latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, woolly foliage and showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
