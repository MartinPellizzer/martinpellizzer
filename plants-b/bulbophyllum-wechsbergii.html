Bulbophyllum wechsbergii

Bulbophyllum wechsbergii, commonly known as the Wecsberg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The flowers, which can be yellow or green, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
