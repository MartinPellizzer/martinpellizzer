Brassica rapa subvar. punicea

Brassica rapa subvar. punicea, commonly known as Red Chinese cabbage, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tightly packed, reddish-purple leaves that form a compact head. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is closely related to other members of the mustard family. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.

The coloration of the leaves is due to high levels of anthocyanins, which also contribute to its ornamental value.
