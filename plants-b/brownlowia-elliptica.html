Brownlowia elliptica

Brownlowia elliptica, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has palmate leaves that are broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brownlowia elliptica is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
