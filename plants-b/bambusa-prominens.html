Bambusa prominens

Bambusa prominens, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered in a silvery-white sheath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow inflorescences that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

Bambusa prominens is also valued for its lightweight and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
