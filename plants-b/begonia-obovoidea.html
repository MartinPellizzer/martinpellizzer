Begonia obovoidea

Begonia obovoidea, commonly known as the round-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically round or oval in shape, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its unique foliage and modest blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
