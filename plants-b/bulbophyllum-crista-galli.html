Bulbophyllum crista-galli

Bulbophyllum crista-galli, commonly known as the Crested Vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers, which are often brightly colored and showy. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and can have a distinctive crest-like pattern on the petals.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
