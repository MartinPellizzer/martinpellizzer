Brissonia coronillifolia

Brissonia coronillifolia, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Brissonia coronillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
