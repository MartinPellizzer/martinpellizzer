Bernardia caperoniifolia

Bernardia caperoniifolia, commonly known as the leafy bernardia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
