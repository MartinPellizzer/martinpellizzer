Begonia pohliana

Begonia pohliana, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
