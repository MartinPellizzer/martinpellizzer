Bulbine graminea

Bulbine graminea, commonly known as the grassland bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, arranged in a branched inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
