Brachypodium distachyon var. subtile

Brachypodium distachyon var. subtile, commonly known as the slender brachypodium, is a grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces two-flowered spikes, giving it its scientific name "distachyon," which refers to the paired flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its genetic simplicity and rapid growth, it is widely used in plant biology research as a model organism.
