Bothriochloa hassleri

Bothriochloa hassleri, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, pale green or brown spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Bothriochloa hassleri is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
