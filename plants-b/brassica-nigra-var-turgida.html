Brassica nigra var. turgida

Brassica nigra var. turgida, commonly known as the inflated or swollen mustard, is a variant of the black mustard plant.

It is characterized by its large, inflated pods that can reach several inches in length. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its botanical features include serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
