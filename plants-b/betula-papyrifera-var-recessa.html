Betula papyrifera var. recessa

Betula papyrifera var. recessa, commonly known as the paper birch variant, is a subspecies of the paper birch tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used by Indigenous peoples for various practical and cultural purposes.
