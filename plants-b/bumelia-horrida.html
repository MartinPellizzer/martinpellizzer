Bumelia horrida

Bumelia horrida, commonly known as the roughbumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Bumelia horrida is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
