Borago crassifolia

Borago crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, thick, and slightly hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Borago crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
