Brassica oleracea var. albiflora

Brassica oleracea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white cabbage, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, white or pale green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is a variant of the broader Brassica oleracea species, which includes other vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It is typically grown for its edible leaves and is cultivated in temperate climates.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their delicate appearance.
