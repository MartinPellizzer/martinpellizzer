Brittonamra caribaea var. mollis

Brittonamra caribaea var. mollis, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
