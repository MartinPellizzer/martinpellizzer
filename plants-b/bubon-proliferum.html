Bubon proliferum

Bubon proliferum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Bubon proliferum is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
