Basella madagascariensis

Basella madagascariensis, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and edible, fleshy stems that are often used in cooking. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is a member of the Basellaceae family and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental gardening and agricultural purposes.
