Ballota vellerea

Ballota vellerea, commonly known as the velvet ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal and inland areas. Its hairy, velvety stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ballota vellerea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
