Berlinia acuminata var. bruneelii

Berlinia acuminata var. bruneelii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

However, due to overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species in some regions.
