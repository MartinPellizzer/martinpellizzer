Boehmeria comosa var. neglecta

Boehmeria comosa var. neglecta, commonly known as the silky fiber plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is cultivated for its fibrous stems, which are used in traditional textiles and papermaking. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and industrial contexts.
