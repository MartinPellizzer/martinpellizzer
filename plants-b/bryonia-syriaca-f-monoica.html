Bryonia syriaca f. monoica

Bryonia syriaca f. monoica, commonly known as the Syrian fig-weed, is a variant of the common bryony plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Syria and Turkey. The plant features tendrils that help it climb and attach to supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, followed by green, fleshy fruits that mature to a red or orange color.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
