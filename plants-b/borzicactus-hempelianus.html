Borzicactus hempelianus

Borzicactus hempelianus, commonly known as the Mexican cactus or the Humpback cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive hump-like growth on its body. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in the spring.

Borzicactus hempelianus is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
