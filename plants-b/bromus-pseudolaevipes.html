Bromus pseudolaevipes

Bromus pseudolaevipes, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually smooth and narrow, with a prominent midrib.

Bromus pseudolaevipes is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
