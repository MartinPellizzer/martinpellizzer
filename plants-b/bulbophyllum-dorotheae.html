Bulbophyllum dorotheae

Bulbophyllum dorotheae, commonly known as the Dorothea's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and often colorful floral structures. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its flowers typically feature a combination of sepals, petals, and a labellum, which are arranged in a striking, symmetrical pattern.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
