Bargemontia aplocaryoides

Bargemontia aplocaryoides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
