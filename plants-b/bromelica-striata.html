Bromelica striata

Bromelica striata, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a fragrant, edible fruit that is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Bromelica striata is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal.
