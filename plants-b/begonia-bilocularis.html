Begonia bilocularis

Begonia bilocularis, commonly known as the two-lobed begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive two-lobed shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Begonia bilocularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
