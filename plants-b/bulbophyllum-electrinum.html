Bulbophyllum electrinum

Bulbophyllum electrinum, commonly known as the electric orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often colorful inflorescence and intricate floral structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinct labellum that aids in pollination.

Bulbophyllum electrinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
