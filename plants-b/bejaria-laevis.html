Bejaria laevis

Bejaria laevis, commonly known as the smooth bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Bejaria laevis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
