Brassica repanda subsp. repanda

Brassica repanda subsp.

repanda, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
