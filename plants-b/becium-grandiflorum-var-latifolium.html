Becium grandiflorum var. latifolium

Becium grandiflorum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf becium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
