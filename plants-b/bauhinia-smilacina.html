Bauhinia smilacina

Bauhinia smilacina, commonly known as the climbing bauhinia, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Indonesia. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a common sight in tropical forests. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The plant also has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil, contributing to its ecological significance.
