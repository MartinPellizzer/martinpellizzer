Boronia ledifolia var. repanda

Boronia ledifolia var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved boronia, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornate floral display.

The varietal form 'repanda' is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.
