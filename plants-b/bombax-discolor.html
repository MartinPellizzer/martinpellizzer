Bombax discolor

Bombax discolor, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The tree produces a capsule fruit that contains silky, cotton-like fibers, which are used in various industrial applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
