Babingtonia minutifolia

Babingtonia minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf babingtonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, compact foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
