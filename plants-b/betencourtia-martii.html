Betencourtia martii

Betencourtia martii, commonly known as the Guajillo pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Betencourtia martii is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional Mexican cuisine, particularly in the preparation of sauces and seasonings.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
