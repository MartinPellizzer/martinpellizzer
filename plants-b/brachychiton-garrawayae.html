Brachychiton garrawayae

Brachychiton garrawayae, commonly known as the Garraway's flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a rough, bark-like texture and produces woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
