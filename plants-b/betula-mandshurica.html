Betula mandshurica

Betula mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Betula mandshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
