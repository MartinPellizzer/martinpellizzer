Bromopsis ircutensis

Bromopsis ircutensis, commonly known as the Iberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces delicate, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Bromopsis ircutensis is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.

It plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
