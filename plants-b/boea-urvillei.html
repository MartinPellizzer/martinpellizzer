Boea urvillei

Boea urvillei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Boea urvillei thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
