Boscia patens

Boscia patens, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, papery bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Boscia patens is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
