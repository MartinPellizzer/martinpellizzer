Bromus incanus

Bromus incanus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Bromus incanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
