Brassica saxatilis subsp. africana

Brassica saxatilis subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its characteristic cruciform flowers and mustard oils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh conditions.
