Botrychium nipponicum

Botrychium nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese moonwort, is a fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, upright fertile frond with spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually small and delicate, with a distinctive, often curled appearance.

Botrychium nipponicum plays a role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
