Bergenia × beesiana

Bergenia × beesiana, commonly known as the hybrid bergenia, is a perennial plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and clusters of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense, upright clusters. Bergenia × beesiana is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia and is well adapted to cold climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
