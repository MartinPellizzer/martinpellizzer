Berberis finetii

Berberis finetii, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bright yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local communities.
