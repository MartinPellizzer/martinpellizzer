Bonafousia pendula

Bonafousia pendula, commonly known as the weeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pendulous, thorned branches that bear bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns reddish in the fall.

Bonafousia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
