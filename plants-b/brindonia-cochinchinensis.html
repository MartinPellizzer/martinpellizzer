Brindonia cochinchinensis

Brindonia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese redwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
