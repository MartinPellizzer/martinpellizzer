Barringtonia flagellata

Barringtonia flagellata, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescences that resemble candles, hence its common name. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its fruit is a fibrous, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
