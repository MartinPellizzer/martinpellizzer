Bromus unioloides var. major

Bromus unioloides var. major, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
