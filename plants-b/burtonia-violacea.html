Burtonia violacea

Burtonia violacea, commonly known as the violet flame or violet orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet and pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, fragrant blossoms.

Burtonia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
