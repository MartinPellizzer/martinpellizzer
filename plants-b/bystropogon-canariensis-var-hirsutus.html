Bystropogon canariensis var. hirsutus

Bystropogon canariensis var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
