Baccharis barragensis

Baccharis barragensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Baccharis barragensis is known for its resilience to drought and its role in local ecosystems.
