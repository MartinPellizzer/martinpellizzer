Baccharis verbenifolia

Baccharis verbenifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a common component of arid landscape designs.
