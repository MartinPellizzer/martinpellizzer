Berkheya eryngiifolia

Berkheya eryngiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf Berkheya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish foliage and spherical, spiky flower heads. The plant has narrow, silvery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators.

Berkheya eryngiifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
