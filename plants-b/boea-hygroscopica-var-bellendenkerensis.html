Boea hygroscopica var. bellendenkerensis

Boea hygroscopica var. bellendenkerensis, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and hanging baskets.
