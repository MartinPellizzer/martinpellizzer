Belonophora gilletii

Belonophora gilletii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Belonophora gilletii is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
