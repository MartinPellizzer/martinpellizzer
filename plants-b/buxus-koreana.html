Buxus koreana

Buxus koreana, commonly known as the Korean boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Korea.

It features dense, dark green foliage that remains consistent throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. It produces tiny, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Buxus koreana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, preferring a cool, humid climate.
