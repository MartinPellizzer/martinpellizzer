Biscutella apula subsp. lyrata

Biscutella apula subsp.

lyrata, commonly known as the lyrated biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils.

The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fleshy and tuberous.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
