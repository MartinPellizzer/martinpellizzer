Baccharis vernicosa

Baccharis vernicosa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Baccharis vernicosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
