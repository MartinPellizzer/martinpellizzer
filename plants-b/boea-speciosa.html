Boea speciosa

Boea speciosa, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, fuzzy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Boea speciosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
