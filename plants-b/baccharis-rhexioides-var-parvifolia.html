Baccharis rhexioides var. parvifolia

Baccharis rhexioides var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, and is adapted to saturated soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
