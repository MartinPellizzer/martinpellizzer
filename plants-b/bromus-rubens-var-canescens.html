Bromus rubens var. canescens

Bromus rubens var. canescens, commonly known as the silver brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. It produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and its inflorescence is often tinged with reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
