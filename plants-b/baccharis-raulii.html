Baccharis raulii

Baccharis raulii, commonly known as Raul's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Baccharis raulii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soils.
