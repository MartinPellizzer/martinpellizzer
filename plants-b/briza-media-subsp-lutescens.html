Briza media subsp. lutescens

Briza media subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that range in color from green to yellowish.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
