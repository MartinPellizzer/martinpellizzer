Baccharis hemiprionodes

Baccharis hemiprionodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds, due to its nectar-rich flowers and habitat structure.
