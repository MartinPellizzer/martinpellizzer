Borinda angustissima

Borinda angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf borinda, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Borinda angustissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
