Bunium cataonicum

Bunium cataonicum, commonly known as the Crimean fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that emit a mild anise-like fragrance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Bunium cataonicum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
