Botryanthus vulgaris

Botryanthus vulgaris, commonly known as the common wax flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, waxy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Botryanthus vulgaris is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
