Banisteria comans var. latifolia

Banisteria comans var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved banisteria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
