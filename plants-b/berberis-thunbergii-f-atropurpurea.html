Berberis thunbergii f. atropurpurea

Berberis thunbergii f. atropurpurea, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

Its leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a distinctive dark purple to red hue, especially on the underside, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to Japan and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often restricted in certain areas where it can outcompete native vegetation.
