Bupleurum exaltatum var. multicaule

Bupleurum exaltatum var. multicaule, commonly known as the tall and branched Chinese angelica, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust root system and multiple stems that are often branched. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its botanical significance lies in its use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
