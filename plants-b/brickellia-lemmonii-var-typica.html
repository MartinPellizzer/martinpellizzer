Brickellia lemmonii var. typica

Brickellia lemmonii var. typica, commonly known as the desert brickellia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for xeriscaping.
