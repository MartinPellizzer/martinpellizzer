Barbosella rhynchantha

Barbosella rhynchantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. Barbosella rhynchantha thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
