Bactris sobralensis

Bactris sobralensis, commonly known as the Sobral palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits resembling small cherries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching material.

Bactris sobralensis is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
