Bauhinia rubescens

Bauhinia rubescens, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Bauhinia rubescens is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
