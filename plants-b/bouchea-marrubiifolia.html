Bouchea marrubiifolia

Bouchea marrubiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Bouchea marrubiifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
