Begonia hirtella

Begonia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Begonia hirtella is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
