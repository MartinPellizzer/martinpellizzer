Backhousia australis

Backhousia australis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved backhousia, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or dry forest areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

The leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports local wildlife.
