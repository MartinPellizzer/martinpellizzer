Baccharis subopposita var. affinis

Baccharis subopposita var. affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a hardy species in xerophytic landscapes.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
