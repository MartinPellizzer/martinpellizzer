Buddleja pterocaulis

Buddleja pterocaulis, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces long, cylindrical clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to yellow in color. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja pterocaulis is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
