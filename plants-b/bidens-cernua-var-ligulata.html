Bidens cernua var. ligulata

Bidens cernua var. ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
