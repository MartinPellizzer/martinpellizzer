Bothriochloa saccharoides var. torreyana

Bothriochloa saccharoides var. torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's sugargrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spikelets that are typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
