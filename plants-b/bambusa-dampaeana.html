Bambusa dampaeana

Bambusa dampaeana, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a compact, clump-forming bamboo that typically grows to a height of less than two meters. The plant features slender, green culms with a smooth, often slightly hairy surface. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa dampaeana is well-suited for small garden spaces and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
