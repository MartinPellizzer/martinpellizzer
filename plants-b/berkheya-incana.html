Berkheya incana

Berkheya incana, commonly known as the silverleaf berkenia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
