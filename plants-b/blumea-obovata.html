Blumea obovata

Blumea obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Blumea obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
