Bernardia flexuosa

Bernardia flexuosa, commonly known as the red-flowered bernardia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with branching patterns that support its dense foliage and showy blooms.
