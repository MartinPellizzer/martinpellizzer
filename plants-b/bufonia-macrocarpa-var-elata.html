Bufonia macrocarpa var. elata

Bufonia macrocarpa var. elata, commonly known as the slender sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a clustered, spiky structure that contains small, edible seeds.
