Bambusa nigra f. lutea

Bambusa nigra f. lutea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species characterized by its striking yellow to golden-green culms.

This ornamental bamboo is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage is typically a deep green, contrasting beautifully with the vibrant yellow stems. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.

The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
