Bulbine dubia

Bulbine dubia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, Bulbine dubia is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
