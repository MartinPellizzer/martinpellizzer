Brachyscome exilis var. scabrida

Brachyscome exilis var. scabrida, commonly known as the scabrous wallaby tail, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Australia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the spring and summer. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
