Buddleja cuspidata

Buddleja cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, pale purple to white inflorescences that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Buddleja cuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
