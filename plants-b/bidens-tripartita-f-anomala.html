Bidens tripartita f. anomala

Bidens tripartita f. anomala, commonly known as the three-parted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with two petals, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
