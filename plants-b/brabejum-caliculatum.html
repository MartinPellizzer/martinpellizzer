Brabejum caliculatum

Brabejum caliculatum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, red fruits that are edible for certain animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brabejum caliculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native environment.
