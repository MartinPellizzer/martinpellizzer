Backhousia leptopetala

Backhousia leptopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved backhousia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and emit a fragrant scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in suitable conditions.

The backhousia genus is known for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
