Bassia malabarica

Bassia malabarica, commonly known as Malabar groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster, attracting various pollinators.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
