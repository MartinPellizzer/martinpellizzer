Byronia anomala

Byronia anomala, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Byronia anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
