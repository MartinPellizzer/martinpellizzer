Berberis gilgiana

Berberis gilgiana, commonly known as the Gilgian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rocky gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to horticultural landscapes.
