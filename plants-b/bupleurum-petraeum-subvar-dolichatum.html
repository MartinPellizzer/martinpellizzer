Bupleurum petraeum subvar. dolichatum

Bupleurum petraeum subvar. dolichatum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
