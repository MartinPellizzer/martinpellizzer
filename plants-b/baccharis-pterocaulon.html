Baccharis pterocaulon

Baccharis pterocaulon, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters.

Baccharis pterocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
