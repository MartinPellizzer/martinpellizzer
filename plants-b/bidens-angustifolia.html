Bidens angustifolia

Bidens angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has slender, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Bidens angustifolia is also known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
