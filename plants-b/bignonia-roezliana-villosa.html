Bignonia roezliana villosa

Bignonia roezliana villosa, commonly known as the hairy bignonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody vines and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features palmate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bignonia roezliana villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
