Bombacopsis subandina

Bombacopsis subandina, commonly known as the Andean balsa, is a fast-growing tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its lightweight, buoyant seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. Its bark is fibrous and has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential for sustainable forestry.
