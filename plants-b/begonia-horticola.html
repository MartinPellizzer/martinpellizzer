Begonia horticola

Begonia horticola, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a trailing or upright shrub, making it popular for ornamental use in gardens and as a houseplant. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its botanical features make it a favored choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.
