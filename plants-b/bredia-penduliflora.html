Bredia penduliflora

Bredia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Bredia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
