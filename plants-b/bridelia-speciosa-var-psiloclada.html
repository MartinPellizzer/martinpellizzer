Bridelia speciosa var. psiloclada

Bridelia speciosa var. psiloclada, commonly known as the purple-flowered bridelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a mildly aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and blooms.
