Baccharis rufescens var. alpestris

Baccharis rufescens var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
