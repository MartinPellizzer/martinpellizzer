Bidens cicutifolia

Bidens cicutifolia, commonly known as the cut-leaved tickseed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small yellow flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive serrated edge, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It produces tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
