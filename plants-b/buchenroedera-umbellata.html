Buchenroedera umbellata

Buchenroedera umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Buchenroedera umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
