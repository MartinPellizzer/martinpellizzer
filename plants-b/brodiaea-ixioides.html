Brodiaea ixioides

Brodiaea ixioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It produces slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Brodiaea ixioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
