Bystropogon graveolens

Bystropogon graveolens, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in dense spikes. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, sweet-smelling roots, which have been used in traditional medicine. Bystropogon graveolens thrives in waterlogged soils and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and historical medicinal applications.
