Bredemeyera altissima

Bredemeyera altissima, commonly known as the giant bog orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the wetlands of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting a dense cluster of large, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reflect excess light in its boggy habitat. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic environments and requires consistent moisture, making it a specialist of wetland ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many regions, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
