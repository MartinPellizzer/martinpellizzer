Bromus hordeaceus var. ovalis

Bromus hordeaceus var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the apex of each stem, which is typically pale green to brown in color. This variety is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
