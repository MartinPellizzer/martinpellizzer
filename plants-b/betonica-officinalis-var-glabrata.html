Betonica officinalis var. glabrata

Betonica officinalis var. glabrata, commonly known as lesser betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Betonica officinalis var. glabrata has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
