Buddleja variabilis var. veitchiana

Buddleja variabilis var. veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, pale lavender to white flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
