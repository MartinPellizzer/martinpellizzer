Bolivicereus rufus

Bolivicereus rufus, commonly known as the red-barked cactus, is a species native to Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown skin and sparse, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically at the ends of its stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Bolivicereus rufus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
