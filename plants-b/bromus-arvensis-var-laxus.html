Bromus arvensis var. laxus

Bromus arvensis var. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
