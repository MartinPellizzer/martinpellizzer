Bonaveria securigera

Bonaveria securigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bonaveria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in spikes during the summer months. Bonaveria securigera is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
