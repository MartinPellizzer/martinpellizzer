Buddleja subcapitata

Buddleja subcapitata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

Buddleja subcapitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
