Balanophora spicata

Balanophora spicata, commonly known as the spicate balanophora, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the family Balanophoraceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of other plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale in color. Its morphology is highly reduced, with no true leaves or stems, and it reproduces through a complex system of pollination involving specific insect vectors.

Balanophora spicata is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments.
