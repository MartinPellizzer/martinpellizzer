Bauhinia depauperata

Bauhinia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often heart-shaped or kidney-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in clusters. Bauhinia depauperata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
