Bogenhardia tiubae f. intermedia

Bogenhardia tiubae f. intermedia, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in water gardens and bog landscapes.
