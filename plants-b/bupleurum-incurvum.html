Bupleurum incurvum

Bupleurum incurvum, commonly known as the curly valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered umbels. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Bupleurum incurvum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
