Borago hortensis

Borago hortensis, commonly known as blueweed or borage, is an herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and large, star-shaped, bright blue or violet flowers. The leaves are broadly ovate with a fuzzy texture and a slightly peppery taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a companion plant.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of edible flowers.
