Bassovia lucida var. rufescens

Bassovia lucida var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-tinged sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species characterized by its distinctive reddish hues in its foliage and bracts.

It is native to the arid regions of central and southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flower heads are typically yellow with a prominent central disk. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
