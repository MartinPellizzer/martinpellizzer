Berlinia giorgii var. pubescens

Berlinia giorgii var. pubescens, commonly known as the African bristle-leaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are woody pods containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
