Baccharis tandilensis

Baccharis tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil barbaren, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in heads, which are visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Baccharis tandilensis is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
