Boesenbergia bruneiana

Boesenbergia bruneiana, commonly known as the Bornean ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal uses.
