Barbarea arcuata

Barbarea arcuata, commonly known as the curled dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and near water sources.

Barbarea arcuata is also known for its ability to spread through seeds and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
