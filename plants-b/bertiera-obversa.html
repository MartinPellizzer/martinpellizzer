Bertiera obversa

Bertiera obversa, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Bertiera obversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
