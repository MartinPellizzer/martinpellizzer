Bieneria densipapillosa

Bieneria densipapillosa, commonly known as the dense-papillate bieneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, papillate (bumpy) surface on its leaves, which give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
