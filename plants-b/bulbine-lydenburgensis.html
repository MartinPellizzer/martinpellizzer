Bulbine lydenburgensis

Bulbine lydenburgensis, commonly known as Lydenburg bulbine, is a perennial succulent native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has fleshy, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
