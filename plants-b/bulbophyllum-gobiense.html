Bulbophyllum gobiense

Bulbophyllum gobiense, commonly known as the Gobi orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and distinctive, often intricate floral structures. The plant possesses specialized adaptations to survive in extreme desert conditions, including thick, succulent-like leaves and a reduced number of leaves to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically pale in color, with a delicate appearance that contrasts with its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
