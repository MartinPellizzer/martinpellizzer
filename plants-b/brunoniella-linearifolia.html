Brunoniella linearifolia

Brunoniella linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved brunoniella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length, giving it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along streamlines or in rocky crevices.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
