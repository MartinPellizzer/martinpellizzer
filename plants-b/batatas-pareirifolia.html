Batatas pareirifolia

Batatas pareirifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible tubers resembling potatoes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
