Begonia robinsonii

Begonia robinsonii, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in humid, shady environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia robinsonii is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
