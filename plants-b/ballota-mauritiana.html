Ballota mauritiana

Ballota mauritiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance for salt and drought.

Ballota mauritiana is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
