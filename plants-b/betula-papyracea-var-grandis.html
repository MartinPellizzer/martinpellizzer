Betula papyracea var. grandis

Betula papyracea var. grandis, commonly known as the giant paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips, giving it a striking appearance. The tree typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
