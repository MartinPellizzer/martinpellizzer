Besleria quitensis

Besleria quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a strong, spicy aroma. Its flowers, which can be red, orange, or yellow, bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value.
