Berberis vulgaris

Berberis vulgaris, commonly known as barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features thorny stems and alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. Its roots and stems contain berberine, a compound with medicinal properties.

Barberry is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
