Borreria suffruticosa

Borreria suffruticosa, commonly known as the redroot shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish tinge, which contributes to its common name.

Borreria suffruticosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
