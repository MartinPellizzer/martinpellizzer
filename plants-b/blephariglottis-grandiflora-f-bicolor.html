Blephariglottis grandiflora f. bicolor

Blephariglottis grandiflora f. bicolor, commonly known as the variegated grandiflora bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique floral display and foliage make it a standout addition to both container gardens and landscapes.
