Brownanthus fraternus

Brownanthus fraternus, commonly known as the brother's flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in dense, spherical clusters. Native to the southeastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in its natural environment.
