Betula lenta var. populifolia

Betula lenta var. populifolia, commonly known as the sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow-green catkins and is known for its high-quality timber used in furniture and musical instruments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Betula lenta var. populifolia plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
