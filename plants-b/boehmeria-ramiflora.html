Boehmeria ramiflora

Boehmeria ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflora nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Boehmeria ramiflora is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and papermaking.
