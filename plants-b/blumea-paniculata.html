Blumea paniculata

Blumea paniculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Blumea paniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
