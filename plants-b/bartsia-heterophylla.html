Bartsia heterophylla

Bartsia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bartsia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the growth conditions. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have five petals with a distinct yellowish throat.

Bartsia heterophylla is notable for its adaptability and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
