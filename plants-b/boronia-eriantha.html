Boronia eriantha

Boronia eriantha, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rocky areas.

Boronia eriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
