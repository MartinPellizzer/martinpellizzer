Bassia johnsonii

Bassia johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's bladderwrack, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, typically in desert or semi-desert habitats.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it an important component of its ecosystem.
