Brassica deflexa var. tigridis

Brassica deflexa var. tigridis, commonly known as the tigridian cabbage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed and slightly hairy. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and compact growth habit.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural research due to its genetic diversity.
