Bunium microcarpum subsp. microcarpum

Bunium microcarpum subsp.

microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically significant in traditional herbal practices.
