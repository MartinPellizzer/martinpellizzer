Bombax mediterraneum

Bombax mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in textile production.
