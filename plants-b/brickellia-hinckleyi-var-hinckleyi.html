Brickellia hinckleyi var. hinckleyi

Brickellia hinckleyi var. hinckleyi, commonly known as Hinckley's brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in terminal heads, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Brickellia, valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
