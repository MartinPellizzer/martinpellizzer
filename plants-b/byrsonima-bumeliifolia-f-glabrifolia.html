Byrsonima bumeliifolia f. glabrifolia

Byrsonima bumeliifolia f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved soursop, is a variant of the soursop species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a flowering pattern that occurs throughout the growing season.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
