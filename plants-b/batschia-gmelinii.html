Batschia gmelinii

Batschia gmelinii, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Batschia gmelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
