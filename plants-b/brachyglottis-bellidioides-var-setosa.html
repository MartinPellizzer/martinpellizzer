Brachyglottis bellidioides var. setosa

Brachyglottis bellidioides var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy bristlebush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with many branches, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
