Buxus sinica var. sinica

Buxus sinica var. sinica, commonly known as Chinese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ability to be shaped into formal topiaries and its tolerance to pruning.

Buxus sinica var. sinica prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
