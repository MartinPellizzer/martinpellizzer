Bulbostylis bozumensis

Bulbostylis bozumensis, commonly known as the Zambian bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tussock-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
