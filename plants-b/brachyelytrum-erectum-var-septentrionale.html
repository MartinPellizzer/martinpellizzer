Brachyelytrum erectum var. septentrionale

Brachyelytrum erectum var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern short-winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows, often forming dense stands.

Due to its ecological importance, it is an indicator species for wetland health and biodiversity.
