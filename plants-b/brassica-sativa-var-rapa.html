Brassica sativa var. rapa

Brassica sativa var. rapa, commonly known as turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, globe-shaped root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are dark green and edible, often used in cooking.

This variety is cultivated for both its nutritious root and leafy greens, making it a versatile crop in various cuisines.
