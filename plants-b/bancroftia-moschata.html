Bancroftia moschata

Bancroftia moschata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small seeds in a fluffy, white pappus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation.
