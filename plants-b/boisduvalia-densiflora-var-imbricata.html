Boisduvalia densiflora var. imbricata

Boisduvalia densiflora var. imbricata, commonly known as the dense-flowered boisduvalia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
