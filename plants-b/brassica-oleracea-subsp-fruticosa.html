Brassica oleracea subsp. fruticosa

Brassica oleracea subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables such as broccoli and cauliflower. It has a taproot system and can grow up to 1 meter in height, often forming dense clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its historical use in traditional medicine.
