Begonia pilderifolia

Begonia pilderifolia, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured surface, adding visual interest to any garden or indoor setting. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the summer months.

This plant prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded borders and container gardening.
