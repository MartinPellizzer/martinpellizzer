Bauhinia purpusii

Bauhinia purpusii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
