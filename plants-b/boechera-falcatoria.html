Boechera falcatoria

Boechera falcatoria, commonly known as the hooked rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in crevices and on slopes where it can receive adequate sunlight.
