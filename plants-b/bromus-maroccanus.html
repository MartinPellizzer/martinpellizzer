Bromus maroccanus

Bromus maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan brome, is a grass species native to North Africa, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with each spikelet containing several small, awned florets. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Bromus maroccanus is often found in Mediterranean climates and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
