Bactris rhaphidacantha

Bactris rhaphidacantha, commonly known as the arrowleaf palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by triangular, edible fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands and riverine forests.
