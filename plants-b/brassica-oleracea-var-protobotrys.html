Brassica oleracea var. protobotrys

Brassica oleracea var. protobotrys, commonly known as the cauliflower.

This variety is characterized by its compact, dense inflorescence of undeveloped flower buds, which are typically white or green in color. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species. It is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The botanical structure of the plant includes thick, fleshy stems and leaves that provide support for the tightly packed flower heads.
