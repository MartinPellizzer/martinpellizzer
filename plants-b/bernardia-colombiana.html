Bernardia colombiana

Bernardia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots are known for their aromatic properties and have been traditionally used in local herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at higher altitudes.
