Begonia leytensis

Begonia leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
