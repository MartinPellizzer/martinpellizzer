Bufonia tenuifolia subsp. battandieriana

Bufonia tenuifolia subsp.

battandieriana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bufonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and slightly different growth form compared to the main species.
