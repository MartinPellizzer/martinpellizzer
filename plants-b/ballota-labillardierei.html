Ballota labillardierei

Ballota labillardierei, commonly known as the goldenball bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Ballota labillardierei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
