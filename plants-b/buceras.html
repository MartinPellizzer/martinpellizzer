Buceras

Buceras, commonly known as the wax tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plants typically have a shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. Buceras species are native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.
