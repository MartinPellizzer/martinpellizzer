Brassica saxatilis var. maritima

Brassica saxatilis var. maritima, commonly known as sea kale, is a hardy coastal plant adapted to saline and wind-swept environments.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and mustard-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a slightly bitter taste, and it produces small, yellow flowers in early summer.

Sea kale is often used in local cuisines and has historical significance in coastal communities.
