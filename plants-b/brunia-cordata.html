Brunia cordata

Brunia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf brunia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, Brunia cordata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
