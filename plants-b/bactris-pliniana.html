Bactris pliniana

Bactris pliniana, commonly known as the Plinian palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small, round dates. Bactris pliniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
