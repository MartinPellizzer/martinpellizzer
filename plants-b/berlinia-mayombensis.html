Berlinia mayombensis

Berlinia mayombensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
