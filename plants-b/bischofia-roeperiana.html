Bischofia roeperiana

Bischofia roeperiana, commonly known as the red torchtree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that appear in dense clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
