Blechnum pallidum

Blechnum pallidum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a shimmering appearance. Native to New Zealand, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The fronds are typically erect and can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Blechnum pallidum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and small animals.
