Biserrula epiglottis

Biserrula epiglottis, commonly known as the coastal biserrula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, globular heads. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

Biserrula epiglottis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
