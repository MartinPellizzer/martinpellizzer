Babiana rigidifolia

Babiana rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid stalked babiana, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features tall, stiff stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It typically blooms in late summer and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Babiana rigidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
