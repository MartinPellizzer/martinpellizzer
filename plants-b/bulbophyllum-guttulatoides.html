Bulbophyllum guttulatoides

Bulbophyllum guttulatoides, commonly known as the spotted lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with spots or stripes.

The plant's morphology includes a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.
