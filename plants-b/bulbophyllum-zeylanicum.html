Bulbophyllum zeylanicum

Bulbophyllum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blossoms with three sepals and three petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
