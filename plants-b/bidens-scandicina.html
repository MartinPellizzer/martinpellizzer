Bidens scandicina

Bidens scandicina, commonly known as the climbing ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, often sprawling growth habit and its ability to climb using its hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Bidens scandicina is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
