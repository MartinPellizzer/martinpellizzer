Buchnerodendron bussei

Buchnerodendron bussei, commonly known as the West African sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its fragrant, white flowers are followed by small, round fruits containing winged seeds.

Buchnerodendron bussei is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
