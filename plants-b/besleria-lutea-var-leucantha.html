Besleria lutea var. leucantha

Besleria lutea var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered besleria, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental, strap-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its beauty and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
