Begonia vinkii

Begonia vinkii, commonly known as the Vink's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a deep green color and often have intricate patterns or contrasting veins. The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, are borne on short, hairy stalks.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
