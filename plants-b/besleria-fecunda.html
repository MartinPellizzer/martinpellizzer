Besleria fecunda

Besleria fecunda, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
