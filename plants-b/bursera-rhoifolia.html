Bursera rhoifolia

Bursera rhoifolia, commonly known as the Mexican wild fig or red cedar, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Bursera rhoifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
