Bursera frenningiae

Bursera frenningiae, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits that release seeds when mature.

Bursera frenningiae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its aromatic resins used in traditional medicine and incense.
