Banksia sessilis var. sessilis

Banksia sessilis var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climate conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native habitats.
