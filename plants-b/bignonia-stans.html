Bignonia stans

Bignonia stans, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Bignonia stans is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.
