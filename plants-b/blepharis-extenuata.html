Blepharis extenuata

Blepharis extenuata, commonly known as the slender blepharis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with small leaves and a deep root system to conserve water.

It blooms during the rainy season, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and birds.
