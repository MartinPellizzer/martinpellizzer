Bromus secalinus subvar. villosus

Bromus secalinus subvar. villosus, commonly known as the hairy crested grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Bromus secalinus subvar. villosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
