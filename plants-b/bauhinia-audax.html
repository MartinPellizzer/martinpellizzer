Bauhinia audax

Bauhinia audax, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and consist of numerous small leaflets. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Bauhinia audax thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
