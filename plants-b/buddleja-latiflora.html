Buddleja latiflora

Buddleja latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features showy, clustered spikes of pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja latiflora is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
