Brunsvigia radulosa

Brunsvigia radulosa, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces vibrant, red-orange flowers that resemble fireworks, hence its common name. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
