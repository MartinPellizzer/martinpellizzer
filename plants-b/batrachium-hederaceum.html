Batrachium hederaceum

Batrachium hederaceum, commonly known as the common greenbriar, is a perennial climbing vine native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle plants, and is characterized by its stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it both a useful plant for erosion control and a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
