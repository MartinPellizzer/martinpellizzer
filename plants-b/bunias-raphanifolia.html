Bunias raphanifolia

Bunias raphanifolia, commonly known as hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Bunias raphanifolia is an annual herb that thrives in moist, well-drained environments.
