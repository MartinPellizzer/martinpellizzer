Berberis caesia

Berberis caesia, commonly known as the blue barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking blue-green color in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
