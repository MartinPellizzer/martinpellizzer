Begonia pringlei

Begonia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from white to pink, with some varieties displaying intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
