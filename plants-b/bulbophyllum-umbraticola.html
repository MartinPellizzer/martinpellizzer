Bulbophyllum umbraticola

Bulbophyllum umbraticola, commonly known as the shade orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are adapted to low-light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
