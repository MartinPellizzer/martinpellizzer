Bromus trisetoides

Bromus trisetoides, commonly known as the three-bristle brome, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture.

Bromus trisetoides is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
