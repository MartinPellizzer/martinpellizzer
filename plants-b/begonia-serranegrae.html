Begonia serranegrae

Begonia serranegrae, commonly known as the black serrated begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety foliage and vibrant red flowers. The leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and they often have a glossy texture. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is popular among collectors for its unique appearance.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
