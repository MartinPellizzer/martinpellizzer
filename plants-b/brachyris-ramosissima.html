Brachyris ramosissima

Brachyris ramosissima, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize soil in sandy or disturbed areas.

Brachyris ramosissima is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
