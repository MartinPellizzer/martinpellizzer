Bambusa gigantea

Bambusa gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to southern China.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green in color, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale, whitish culms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

Bambusa gigantea is also valued for its strong, lightweight material, which is used in construction and crafts.
