Begonia gueritziana

Begonia gueritziana, commonly known as the Gueritz's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green and red hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
