Blumea bovina

Blumea bovina, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Blumea bovina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
