Bonatea speciosa var. antennifera

Bonatea speciosa var. antennifera, commonly known as the featherleaf bonatea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
