Bulbophyllum grotianum

Bulbophyllum grotianum, commonly known as the Grotian's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
