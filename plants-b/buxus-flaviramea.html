Buxus flaviramea

Buxus flaviramea, commonly known as the yellow-veined boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Buxaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically South Africa, where it grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, glossy leaves that have prominent yellow veins. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries.

Buxus flaviramea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
