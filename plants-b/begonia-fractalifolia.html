Begonia fractalifolia

Begonia fractalifolia, commonly known as the fractal-leaved begonia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate leaf patterns that resemble fractal designs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually dark green with contrasting veins, and it produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months.

Begonia fractalifolia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
