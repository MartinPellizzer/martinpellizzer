Beadlea oligantha

Beadlea oligantha, commonly known as the slender beadleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bead-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Beadlea oligantha is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
