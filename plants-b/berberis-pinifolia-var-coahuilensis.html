Berberis pinifolia var. coahuilensis

Berberis pinifolia var. coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuilensis barberry, is a shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, reddish-brown stems and small, needle-like leaves that give it a pine-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration in desert regions.
