Blechnum feei

Blechnum feei, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy scales.

Blechnum feei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
