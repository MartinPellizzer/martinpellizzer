Berberis glauca

Berberis glauca, commonly known as the blueberry barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright blue berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and its stems are armed with sharp spines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The botanical characteristics of Berberis glauca make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
