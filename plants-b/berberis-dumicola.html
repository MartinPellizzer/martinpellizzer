Berberis dumicola

Berberis dumicola, commonly known as the desert barberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant features small, thorned stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are small, oval, and often have a reddish or bronze hue.

The fruit is a red, berry-like structure that contains several seeds and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
