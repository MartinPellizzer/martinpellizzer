Bulbophyllum angustifolium

Bulbophyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
