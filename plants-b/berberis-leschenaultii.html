Berberis leschenaultii

Berberis leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
