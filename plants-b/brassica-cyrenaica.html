Brassica cyrenaica

Brassica cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. It is often found growing in arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

Brassica cyrenaica is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
