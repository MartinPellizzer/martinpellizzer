Betula fauriei

Betula fauriei, commonly known as the Japanese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula fauriei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
