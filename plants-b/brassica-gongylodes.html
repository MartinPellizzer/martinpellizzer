Brassica gongylodes

Brassica gongylodes, commonly known as Chinese mustard or bird's wing, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, edible pods that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are also edible and can be used in various culinary applications.

Brassica gongylodes is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli.
