Biophytum agasthyamalayanum

Biophytum agasthyamalayanum, commonly known as the Himalayan false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in rocky or clayey soils.

Biophytum agasthyamalayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
