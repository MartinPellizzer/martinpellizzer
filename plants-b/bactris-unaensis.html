Bactris unaensis

Bactris unaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to the northeastern region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible, fleshy fruits. Bactris unaensis is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
