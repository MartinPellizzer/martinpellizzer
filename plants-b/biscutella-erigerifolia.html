Biscutella erigerifolia

Biscutella erigerifolia, commonly known as the mountain biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its alpine habitat. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Biscutella erigerifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
