Barleriopsis glandulosa var. brevifolia

Barleriopsis glandulosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological significance, it is an important component of native vegetation in arid and semi-arid regions.
