Borassus aethiopum var. bagamojense

Borassus aethiopum var. bagamojense, commonly known as the East African fan palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a straight, unbranched trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are composed of numerous narrow, pointed segments. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy or coastal soils.

This species is often used for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some African communities.
