Brownea grandiceps

Brownea grandiceps, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in warm climates.
