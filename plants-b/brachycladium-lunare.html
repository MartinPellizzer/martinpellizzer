Brachycladium lunare

Brachycladium lunare, commonly known as the moon grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, especially under moonlight. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Brachycladium lunare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
