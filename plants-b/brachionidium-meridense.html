Brachionidium meridense

Brachionidium meridense, commonly known as the Mediterranean brachionidium, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Brachionidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, filamentous structure and is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant exhibits a simple morphology with a central axis and lateral branches, which help it to anchor in substrates. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
