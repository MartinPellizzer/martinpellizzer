Bromus secalinus var. integer

Bromus secalinus var. integer, commonly known as the common brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, often with a hairy texture.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
