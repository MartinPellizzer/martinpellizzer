Baccharis lymanii

Baccharis lymanii, commonly known as Lyman's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. It has a woody stem with many branches and is known for its aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed.

Baccharis lymanii is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
