Blechnum fluminense

Blechnum fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian holly fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Blechnum fluminense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
