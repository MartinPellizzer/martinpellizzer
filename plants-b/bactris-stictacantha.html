Bactris stictacantha

Bactris stictacantha, commonly known as the red-veined palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnately compound leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatch, while the fruit is edible and consumed by local populations.

This palm is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
