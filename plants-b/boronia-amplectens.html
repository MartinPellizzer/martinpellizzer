Boronia amplectens

Boronia amplectens, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

Boronia amplectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
