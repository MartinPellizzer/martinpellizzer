Bauhinia picta

Bauhinia picta, commonly known as the painted bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns of white, pink, or purple. The plant produces showy, papilionaceous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Bauhinia picta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
