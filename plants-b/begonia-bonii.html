Begonia bonii

Begonia bonii, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia bonii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
