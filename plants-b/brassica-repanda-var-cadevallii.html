Brassica repanda var. cadevallii

Brassica repanda var. cadevallii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small, orange lantern. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals and has a biennial life cycle, typically growing in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fruit is edible, often used in culinary applications, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Brassica repanda var. cadevallii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
