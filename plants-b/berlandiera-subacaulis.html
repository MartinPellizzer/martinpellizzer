Berlandiera subacaulis

Berlandiera subacaulis, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with hairy, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Berlandiera subacaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
