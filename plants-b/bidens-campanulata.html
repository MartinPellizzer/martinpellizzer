Bidens campanulata

Bidens campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

Bidens campanulata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
