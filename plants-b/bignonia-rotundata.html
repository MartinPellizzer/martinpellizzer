Bignonia rotundata

Bignonia rotundata, commonly known as the scarlet trumpet creeper, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding birds.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
