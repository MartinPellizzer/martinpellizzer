Bowdichia brevipes

Bowdichia brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf senna, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds with a hard, dark coat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest habitats.

Bowdichia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
