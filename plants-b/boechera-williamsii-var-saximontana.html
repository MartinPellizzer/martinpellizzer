Boechera williamsii var. saximontana

Boechera williamsii var. saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra Mountains rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
