Becium knyanum

Becium knyanum, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often evergreen flowers. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive, spiky appearance.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
