Bomarea glaucescens var. puberula

Bomarea glaucescens var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
