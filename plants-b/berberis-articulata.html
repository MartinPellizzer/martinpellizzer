Berberis articulata

Berberis articulata, commonly known as the jointed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, which are often segmented, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Berberis articulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
