Berberis impedita

Berberis impedita, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
