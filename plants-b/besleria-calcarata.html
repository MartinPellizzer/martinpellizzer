Besleria calcarata

Besleria calcarata, commonly known as the coral plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
