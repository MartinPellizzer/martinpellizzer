Barbarea vulgaris var. arcuata

Barbarea vulgaris var. arcuata, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, segmented seed pods. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
