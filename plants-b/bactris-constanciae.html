Bactris constanciae

Bactris constanciae, commonly known as the Constantia palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
