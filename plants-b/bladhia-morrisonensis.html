Bladhia morrisonensis

Bladhia morrisonensis, commonly known as the Morrison's bladhia, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, followed by bright red or orange fruits that attract various bird species. Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical collections.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
