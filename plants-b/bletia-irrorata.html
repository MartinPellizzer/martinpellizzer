Bletia irrorata

Bletia irrorata, commonly known as the silver bletia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and understoreys. The plant features a pseudobulb-like structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a waxy, glossy appearance.

Bletia irrorata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
