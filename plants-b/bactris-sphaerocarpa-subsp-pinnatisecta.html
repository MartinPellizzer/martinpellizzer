Bactris sphaerocarpa subsp. pinnatisecta

Bactris sphaerocarpa subsp.

pinnatisecta, commonly known as the pinnatisected palm, is a species native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional agriculture.
