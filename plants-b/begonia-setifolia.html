Begonia setifolia

Begonia setifolia, commonly known as the silky begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a silvery sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia setifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
