Benzoin formosanum

Benzoin formosanum, commonly known as Formosan benzoin, is a species of the genus Styrax in the family Styracaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan, characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its bark and resin have been traditionally used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
