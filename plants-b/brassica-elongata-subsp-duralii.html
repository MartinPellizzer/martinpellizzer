Brassica elongata subsp. duralii

Brassica elongata subsp.

duralii, commonly known as the Duralii mustard, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a biennial or annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical siliqua containing several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats.
