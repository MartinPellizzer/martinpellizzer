Borbonia crenata

Borbonia crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Borbonia crenata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.
