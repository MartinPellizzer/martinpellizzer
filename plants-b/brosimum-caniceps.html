Brosimum caniceps

Brosimum caniceps, commonly known as the Cuban trumpet tree, is a large, fast-growing tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark and long, pendant-like leaves that hang in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its roots have traditional medicinal uses, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Brosimum caniceps plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and sustenance to various species.
