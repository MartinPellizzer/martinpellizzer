Bromus boraei

Bromus boraei, commonly known as the Arctic brome, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray flowers. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Bromus boraei plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
