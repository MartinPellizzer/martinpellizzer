Bulbophyllum gibsonii

Bulbophyllum gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences and is typically epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its flowers are usually fragrant and can bloom multiple times a year under optimal conditions.

Bulbophyllum gibsonii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
