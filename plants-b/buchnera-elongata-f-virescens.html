Buchnera elongata f. virescens

Buchnera elongata f. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf buchnera, is a variant of the species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is dense and can form a rosette structure, which helps in water retention.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
