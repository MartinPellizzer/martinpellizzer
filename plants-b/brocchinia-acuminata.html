Brocchinia acuminata

Brocchinia acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Brocchinia, is a species of bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment.

Brocchinia acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
