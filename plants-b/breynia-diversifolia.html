Breynia diversifolia

Breynia diversifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. Breynia diversifolia produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
