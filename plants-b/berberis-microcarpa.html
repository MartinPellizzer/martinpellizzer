Berberis microcarpa

Berberis microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
