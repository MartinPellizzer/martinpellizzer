Biscutella longifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes during the spring, which are followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and limestone areas.
