Benzoin prattii

Benzoin prattii, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or Prat's benzoin, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudate. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a dense, rounded canopy with leathery, elliptical leaves. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits containing winged seeds.

The resin of Benzoin prattii is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in incense and perfumes.
