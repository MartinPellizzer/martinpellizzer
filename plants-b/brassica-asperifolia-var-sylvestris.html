Brassica asperifolia var. sylvestris

Brassica asperifolia var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. This variety is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
