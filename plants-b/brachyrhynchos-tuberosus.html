Brachyrhynchos tuberosus

Brachyrhynchos tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous brachyrhynchos, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which serve as storage organs for nutrients and water. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

Brachyrhynchos tuberosus is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
