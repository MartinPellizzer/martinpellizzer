Bupleurum falcatum var. marginatum

Bupleurum falcatum var. marginatum, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are elongated and often have a distinctive fringed or toothed edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
