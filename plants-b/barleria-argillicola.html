Barleria argillicola

Barleria argillicola, commonly known as the clay-loving barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in well-drained, sandy or clay soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or red in color, arranged in clusters.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
