Bulbophyllum papulilabium

Bulbophyllum papulilabium, commonly known as the papillate-labiated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy labellum that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
