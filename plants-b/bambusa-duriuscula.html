Bambusa duriuscula

Bambusa duriuscula, commonly known as the hard-leaved bamboo, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, stiff leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant forms clumps of tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, often reddish-brown surface.

Bambusa duriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
