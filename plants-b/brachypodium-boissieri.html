Brachypodium boissieri

Brachypodium boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's shortgrass, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
