Beluttakaka grandieriana

Beluttakaka grandieriana, commonly known as the Grandierian flameleaf, is a tropical plant native to the dense rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, flame-colored leaves that vary in hue from deep red to orange. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil.

Beluttakaka grandieriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
