Bidens ekmanii var. paucidentata

Bidens ekmanii var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
