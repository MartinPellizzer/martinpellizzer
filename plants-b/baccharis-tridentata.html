Baccharis tridentata

Baccharis tridentata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions and is drought-tolerant.

Baccharis tridentata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
