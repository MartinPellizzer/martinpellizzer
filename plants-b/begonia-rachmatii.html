Begonia rachmatii

Begonia rachmatii, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

Begonia rachmatii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
