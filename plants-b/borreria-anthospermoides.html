Borreria anthospermoides

Borreria anthospermoides, commonly known as the scarlet cholla, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and succulent-like leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Borreria anthospermoides plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
