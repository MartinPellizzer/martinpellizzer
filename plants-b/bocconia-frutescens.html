Bocconia frutescens

Bocconia frutescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf bocconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique leaf structure and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Bocconia frutescens is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
