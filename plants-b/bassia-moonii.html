Bassia moonii

Bassia moonii, commonly known as moon's bassia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
