Bumelia ambigua

Bumelia ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark that peels in thin layers. Bumelia ambigua is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in bottomlands.

It is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
