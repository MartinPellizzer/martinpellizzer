Brachystegia tamarindoides subsp. torrei

Brachystegia tamarindoides subsp.

torrei, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and elliptical leaves.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its durable, heavy timber.
