Benstonea copelandii

Benstonea copelandii, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from golden yellow to orange-red. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. The leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environments.

Benstonea copelandii blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.
