Barbarea minor var. libanotica

Barbarea minor var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Barbarea minor var. libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
