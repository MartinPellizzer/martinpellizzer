Bromus purpurascens

Bromus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple grass or purple brome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence is a panicle, composed of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The seeds are small and dispersed by wind, allowing the plant to colonize new areas effectively.
