Begonia kemumuensis

Begonia kemumuensis, commonly known as the Kemumu begonia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
