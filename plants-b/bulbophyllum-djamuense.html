Bulbophyllum djamuense

Bulbophyllum djamuense, commonly known as the Djamuense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its humid, shaded environment.

The flowers are usually small and colorful, contributing to its ornamental value.
