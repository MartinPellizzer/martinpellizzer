Brachypodium sylvaticum var. luzoniense

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. luzoniense, commonly known as Luzon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
