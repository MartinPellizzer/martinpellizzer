Biscutella laevigata subsp. gracilis

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically tubular and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender stem and smaller leaves compared to other subspecies of Biscutella laevigata.
