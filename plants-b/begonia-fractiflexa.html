Begonia fractiflexa

Begonia fractiflexa, commonly known as the drooping begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a tendency to curl when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and adaptability to various growing conditions.
