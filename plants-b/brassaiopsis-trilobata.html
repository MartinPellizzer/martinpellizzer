Brassaiopsis trilobata

Brassaiopsis trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fiddlehead-like young fronds that unfurl as they mature. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
