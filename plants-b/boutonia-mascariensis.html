Boutonia mascariensis

Boutonia mascariensis, commonly known as the Mascarene bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Boutonia mascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
