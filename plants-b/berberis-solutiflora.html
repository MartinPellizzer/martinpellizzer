Berberis solutiflora

Berberis solutiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces clusters of bright red, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Berberis solutiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
