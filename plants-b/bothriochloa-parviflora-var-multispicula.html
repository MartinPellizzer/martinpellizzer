Bothriochloa parviflora var. multispicula

Bothriochloa parviflora var. multispicula, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable environments.
