Bridgesia incisifolia

Bridgesia incisifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bridgesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with five petals, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
