Boerhavia diffusa var. obtusifolia

Boerhavia diffusa var. obtusifolia, commonly known as spreading horsebean, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that can spread up to several meters, often forming dense mats. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces, and the plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and waste places.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
