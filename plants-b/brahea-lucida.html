Brahea lucida

Brahea lucida, commonly known as the silver crown cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

This plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stem covered in dense, white to silver spines. Its foliage consists of small, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically present during the growing season. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green appearance.

Brahea lucida is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
