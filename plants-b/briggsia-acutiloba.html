Briggsia acutiloba

Briggsia acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Briggsia acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
