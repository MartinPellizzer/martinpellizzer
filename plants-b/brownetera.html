Brownetera

Brownetera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Brownetera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
