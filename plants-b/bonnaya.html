Bonnaya

Bonnaya, commonly known as the "crown of the forest," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Bonnaya thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
