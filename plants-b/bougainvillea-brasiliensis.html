Bougainvillea brasiliensis

Bougainvillea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian paperflower, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, known for its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters tall, with woody stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, surrounded by the colorful bracts that serve as its primary ornamental feature.

Bougainvillea brasiliensis thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
