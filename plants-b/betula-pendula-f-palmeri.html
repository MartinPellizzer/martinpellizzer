Betula pendula f. palmeri

Betula pendula f. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's weeping birch, is a unique form of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and has papery, yellowish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and a pale green underside.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
