Begonia duclouxii

Begonia duclouxii, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a striking red or purple hue. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
