Bromus madritensis var. purpureus

Bromus madritensis var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Bromus madritensis var. purpureus is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
