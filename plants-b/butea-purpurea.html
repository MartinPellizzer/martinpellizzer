Butea purpurea

Butea purpurea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has slender, branching stems and palmately compound leaves that are alternate and often shed during dry periods. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Butea purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
