Bolivaria

Bolivaria, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Peru. Bolivaria species are characterized by their intricate, often colorful flowers and their ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the rich biodiversity of the Andes.
