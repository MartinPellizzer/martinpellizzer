Bactris pulchella

Bactris pulchella, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which eventually develop into edible, orange-colored fruits. Bactris pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fruit in some regions.
