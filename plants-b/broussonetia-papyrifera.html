Broussonetia papyrifera

Broussonetia papyrifera, commonly known as paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous bark, which has been traditionally used for making paper and textiles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
