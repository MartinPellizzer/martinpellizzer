Bulbophyllum acutilobum

Bulbophyllum acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
