Binghamia australis

Binghamia australis, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stem and dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Binghamia australis is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
