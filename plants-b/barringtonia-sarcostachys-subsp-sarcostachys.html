Barringtonia sarcostachys subsp. sarcostachys

Barringtonia sarcostachys subsp.

sarcostachys, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The plant produces a fruit with a thick, aromatic rind and a segmented, juicy interior. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses.
