Baccharis meridionalis

Baccharis meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
