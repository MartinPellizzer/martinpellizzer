Bumelia reclinata

Bumelia reclinata, commonly known as the drooping bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.
