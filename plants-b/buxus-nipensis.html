Buxus nipensis

Buxus nipensis, commonly known as Japanese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in a rounded or cylindrical shape, making it popular for topiary and ornamental landscaping. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Buxus nipensis is valued for its durability and ability to withstand pruning, which allows for intricate shaping.
