Begonia nebulosa

Begonia nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a distinctive mottled or cloud-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It prefers cool, humid environments and is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia nebulosa is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
