Bidens warszewicziana var. simplicifolia

Bidens warszewicziana var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
