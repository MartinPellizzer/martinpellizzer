Babiana mucronata

Babiana mucronata, commonly known as the spear lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of flowers on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Babiana mucronata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
