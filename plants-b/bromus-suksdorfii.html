Bromus suksdorfii

Bromus suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Bromus suksdorfii is adapted to cold climates and is a member of the Poaceae family.
