Begonia laciniata subsp. nepalensis

Begonia laciniata subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayan region. It features striking, deeply lobed leaves that are often a vibrant green with reddish veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
