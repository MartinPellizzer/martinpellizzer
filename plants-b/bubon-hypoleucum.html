Bubon hypoleucum

Bubon hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white or pale blue, with five lobes and a distinctive throat. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bubon hypoleucum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
