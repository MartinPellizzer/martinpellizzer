Bumelia strigosa

Bumelia strigosa, commonly known as the rough bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many plants with milky sap. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Bumelia strigosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to several meters in height.
