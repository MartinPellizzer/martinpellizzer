Bupleurum gerardi subsp. filicaule

Bupleurum gerardi subsp.

filicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features slender, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale yellow.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
