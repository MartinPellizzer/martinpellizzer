Bambusa nigrociliata

Bambusa nigrociliata, commonly known as the black-haired bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender culms that are often adorned with black or dark brown cilia along the nodes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bambusa nigrociliata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
