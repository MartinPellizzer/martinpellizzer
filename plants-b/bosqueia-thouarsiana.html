Bosqueia thouarsiana

Bosqueia thouarsiana, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Bosqueia thouarsiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
