Blechnum seminudum

Blechnum seminudum, commonly known as the naked fern, is a species of fern native to various parts of the world, including Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with small, scale-like structures along the veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum seminudum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
