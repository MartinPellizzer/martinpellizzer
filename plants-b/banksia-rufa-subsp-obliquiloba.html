Banksia rufa subsp. obliquiloba

Banksia rufa subsp.

obliquiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Western Australia. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hairs.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous follicles, each holding a single seed. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal flowering.
