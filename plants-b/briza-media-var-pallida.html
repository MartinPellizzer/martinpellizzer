Briza media var. pallida

Briza media var. pallida, commonly known as the silver false oat or pallid oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silvery sheen that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in landscaping for its delicate, airy look and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
