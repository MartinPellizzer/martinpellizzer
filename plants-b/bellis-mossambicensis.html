Bellis mossambicensis

Bellis mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rosette growth habit. It produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, creating a daisy-like appearance.

Bellis mossambicensis is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
