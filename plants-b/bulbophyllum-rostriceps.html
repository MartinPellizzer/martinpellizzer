Bulbophyllum rostriceps

Bulbophyllum rostriceps, commonly known as the beak-faced orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike that resembles a beak, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Bulbophyllum rostriceps is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
