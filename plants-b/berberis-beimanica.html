Berberis beimanica

Berberis beimanica, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Berberis beimanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
