Buxus suffruticosa

Buxus suffruticosa, commonly known as common boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically 1 to 2 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary.

Its compact growth habit and foliage make it a versatile plant in both ornamental and landscape settings.
