Bramia

Bramia, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a genus of flowering plants native to wetland areas in North America.

It typically features showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in marshy or boggy environments. Bramia species are known for their ability to thrive in saturated soils and their role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
