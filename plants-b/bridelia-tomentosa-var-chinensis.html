Bridelia tomentosa var. chinensis

Bridelia tomentosa var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese bridelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a pubescent surface, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
