Berberis russellii

Berberis russellii, commonly known as Russell's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Russell's barberry is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
