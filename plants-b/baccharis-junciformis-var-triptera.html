Baccharis junciformis var. triptera

Baccharis junciformis var. triptera, commonly known as the three-branched baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wiry, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
