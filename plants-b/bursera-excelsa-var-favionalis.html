Bursera excelsa var. favionalis

Bursera excelsa var. favionalis, commonly known as the favion tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are fragrant and yellowish-green, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
