Bauhinia decumbens

Bauhinia decumbens, commonly known as the "peacock flower," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, papery leaves that resemble the wings of a peacock. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia decumbens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
