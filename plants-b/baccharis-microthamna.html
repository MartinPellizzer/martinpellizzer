Baccharis microthamna

Baccharis microthamna, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Baccharis microthamna is drought-tolerant and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
