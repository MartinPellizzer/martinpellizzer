Berberis diaphana

Berberis diaphana, commonly known as the silver barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
