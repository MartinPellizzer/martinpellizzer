Bauhinia deserti

Bauhinia deserti, commonly known as the desert bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often reduced to small, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. Bauhinia deserti is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots and a slow growth rate to conserve water.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
