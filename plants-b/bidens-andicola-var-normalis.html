Bidens andicola var. normalis

Bidens andicola var. normalis, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the cooler months, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
