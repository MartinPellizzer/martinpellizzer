Brachiaria bovonei

Brachiaria bovonei, commonly known as the Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Brachiaria bovonei is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage agriculture due to its nutritional value for livestock.
