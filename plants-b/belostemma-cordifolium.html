Belostemma cordifolium

Belostemma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple or violet throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Belostemma cordifolium is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
