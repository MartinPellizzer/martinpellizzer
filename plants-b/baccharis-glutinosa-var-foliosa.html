Baccharis glutinosa var. foliosa

Baccharis glutinosa var. foliosa, commonly known as sticky baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
