Biota orientalis pendula-variegata

Biota orientalis pendula-variegata, commonly known as the hanging variegated biota, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit and striking variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor landscaping.
