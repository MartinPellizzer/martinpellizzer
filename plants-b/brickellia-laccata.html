Brickellia laccata

Brickellia laccata, commonly known as the scarlet brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color. It produces showy yellow flowers with orange centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia laccata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
