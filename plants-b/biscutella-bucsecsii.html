Biscutella bucsecsii

Biscutella bucsecsii, commonly known as the Transcaucasian biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

Biscutella bucsecsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
