Babiana obtusifolia

Babiana obtusifolia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces offsets that allow it to propagate easily. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Babiana obtusifolia is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in Mediterranean climates.
