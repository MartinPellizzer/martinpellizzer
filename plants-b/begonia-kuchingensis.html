Begonia kuchingensis

Begonia kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching begonia, is a flowering plant native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rich green color and may have a slight reddish or purple tinge.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, are often borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
