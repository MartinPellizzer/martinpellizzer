Bulbophyllum caputgnomonis

Bulbophyllum caputgnomonis, commonly known as the crown-headed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a lithophytic epiphyte, typically growing on rocks or trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers, with long, narrow leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its most distinctive characteristic is its tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
