Bulbophyllum longispicatum

Bulbophyllum longispicatum, commonly known as the long-spined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant's pseudobulbs are covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots, which help it survive in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
