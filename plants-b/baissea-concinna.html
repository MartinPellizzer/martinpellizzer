Baissea concinna

Baissea concinna, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Baissea concinna plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
