Barbarea brachycarpa subsp. anfractuosa

Barbarea brachycarpa subsp.

anfractuosa, commonly known as the wrinkled rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its deeply lobed, wrinkled leaves and compact growth habit. It typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This plant is often found in alpine meadows and on rocky slopes at high elevations.
