Brassica rapa convar. cephalata

Brassica rapa convar. cephalata, commonly known as the field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and basal leaves that are elongated and lobed. The plant produces small, yellow, cross-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a long, slender siliqua that contains several seeds.

This species is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
