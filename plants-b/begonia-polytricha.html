Begonia polytricha

Begonia polytricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green foliage that gives it its common name. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Begonia polytricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
