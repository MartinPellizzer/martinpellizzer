Begonia cremnophila

Begonia cremnophila, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Vietnam and Laos.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.

Begonia cremnophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
