Badusa occidentalis

Badusa occidentalis, commonly known as the western badusa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
