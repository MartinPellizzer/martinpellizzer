Biscutella laciniata

Biscutella laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Biscutella laciniata is also known for its edible seeds, which have a nutty flavor and were historically used in traditional cuisines.
