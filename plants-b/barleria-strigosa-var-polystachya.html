Barleria strigosa var. polystachya

Barleria strigosa var. polystachya, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
