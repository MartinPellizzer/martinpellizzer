Bomarea hispida

Bomarea hispida, commonly known as the hairy bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features tuberous roots and possesses hairy foliage, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
