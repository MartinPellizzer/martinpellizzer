Bupleurum semidiaphanum

Bupleurum semidiaphanum, commonly known as the semi-transparent bugloss, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bupleurum semidiaphanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
