Boea havilandii

Boea havilandii, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a climbing, evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, waxy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a versatile and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
