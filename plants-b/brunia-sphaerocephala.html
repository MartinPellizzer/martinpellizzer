Brunia sphaerocephala

Brunia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate ornamental value to the garden.
