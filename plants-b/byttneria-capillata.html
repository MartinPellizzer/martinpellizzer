Byttneria capillata

Byttneria capillata, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
