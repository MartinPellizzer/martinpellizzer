Bupleurum falcatum f. scorzonerifolium

Bupleurum falcatum f. scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the scorzonera-leaved bellflower, is a distinctive variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of the scorzonera genus, giving it its unique appearance. The plant typically produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in herbal medicine.
