Beyeria cinerea

Beyeria cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Beyeria cinerea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
