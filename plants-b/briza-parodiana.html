Briza parodiana

Briza parodiana, commonly known as the Parodiana grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Briza parodiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
