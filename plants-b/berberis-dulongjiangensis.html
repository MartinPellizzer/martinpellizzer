Berberis dulongjiangensis

Berberis dulongjiangensis, commonly known as the Dulongjiang barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
