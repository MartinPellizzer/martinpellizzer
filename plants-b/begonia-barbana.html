Begonia barbana

Begonia barbana, commonly known as the Barbana begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
