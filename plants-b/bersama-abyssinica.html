Bersama abyssinica

Bersama abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing three seeds.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, Bersama abyssinica is now considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
