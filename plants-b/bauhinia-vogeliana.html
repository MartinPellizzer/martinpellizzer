Bauhinia vogeliana

Bauhinia vogeliana, commonly known as the velvetwood or flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in bright red or orange hues, often appearing in late summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia vogeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
