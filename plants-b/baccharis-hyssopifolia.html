Baccharis hyssopifolia

Baccharis hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue curl, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
