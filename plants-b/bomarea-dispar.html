Bomarea dispar

Bomarea dispar, commonly known as the white bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features a tuberous root system and possesses arrow-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are often clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and is appreciated for its unique floral display.
