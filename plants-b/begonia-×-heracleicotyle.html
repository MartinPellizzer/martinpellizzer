Begonia × heracleicotyle

Begonia × heracleicotyle, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Begoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble flowers, which are used to attract pollinators. The plant typically has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and unique inflorescences.
