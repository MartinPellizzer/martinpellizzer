Brachionidium muscosum

Brachionidium muscosum, commonly known as the mossy brachionidium, is a type of aquatic plant belonging to the order Brachionidiales.

It is characterized by its small, filamentous growth and is often found in freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant typically forms dense mats on the surface of water, contributing to the ecological structure of these habitats. It plays a role in water filtration and provides a substrate for other microorganisms.

Due to its simple morphology, it is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality and environmental changes.
