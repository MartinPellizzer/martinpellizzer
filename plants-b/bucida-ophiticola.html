Bucida ophiticola

Bucida ophiticola, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is often found in hammocks and along the edges of wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
