Begonia setifera

Begonia setifera, commonly known as the threadleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in partial to full shade. It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

Begonia setifera is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds due to its delicate appearance and adaptability.
