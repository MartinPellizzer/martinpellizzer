Bulbophyllum mekongense

Bulbophyllum mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, adapted to its humid, tropical habitat.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, are often clustered and display intricate patterns.
