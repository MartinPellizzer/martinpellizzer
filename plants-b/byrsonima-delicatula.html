Byrsonima delicatula

Byrsonima delicatula, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces small, bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
