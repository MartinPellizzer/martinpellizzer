Banara guianensis var. isadena

Banara guianensis var. isadena, commonly known as the Guianan banara, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
