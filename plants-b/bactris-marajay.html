Bactris marajay

Bactris marajay, commonly known as the Marajay palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts by local communities. Its fruit, which is edible, contains a sweet, starchy pulp surrounded by a hard shell.

The plant is valued for both its ecological role and its economic importance in sustainable harvesting practices.
