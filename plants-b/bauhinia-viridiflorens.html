Bauhinia viridiflorens

Bauhinia viridiflorens, commonly known as the green-flowered bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and distinctive, showy greenish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, flat pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
