Bignonia cinerea

Bignonia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and long, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

Bignonia cinerea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy blooms and adaptability.
