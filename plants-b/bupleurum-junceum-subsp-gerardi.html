Bupleurum junceum subsp. gerardi

Bupleurum junceum subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as Gerard's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
