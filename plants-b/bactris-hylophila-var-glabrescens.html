Bactris hylophila var. glabrescens

Bactris hylophila var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed palm, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of palm heart, though its conservation status remains uncertain due to habitat loss.
