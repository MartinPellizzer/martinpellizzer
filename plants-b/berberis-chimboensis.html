Berberis chimboensis

Berberis chimboensis, commonly known as Chimbo barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

Berberis chimboensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
