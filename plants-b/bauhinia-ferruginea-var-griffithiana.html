Bauhinia ferruginea var. griffithiana

Bauhinia ferruginea var. griffithiana, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in a rusty or reddish-brown pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pink or red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
