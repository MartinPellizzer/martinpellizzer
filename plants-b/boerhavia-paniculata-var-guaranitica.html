Boerhavia paniculata var. guaranitica

Boerhavia paniculata var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features slender, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and arranged in panicle-like clusters, which attract various pollinators.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
