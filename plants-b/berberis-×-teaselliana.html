Berberis × teaselliana

Berberis × teaselliana, commonly known as the evergreen barberry, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from its parent species, Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and hardy nature make it suitable for a variety of climates and garden settings.
