Biscutella lusitanica var. macrocarpa

Biscutella lusitanica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in racemes. Its fruits are distinctive, being elongated pods with prominent, ribbed structures.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to rocky and arid environments.
