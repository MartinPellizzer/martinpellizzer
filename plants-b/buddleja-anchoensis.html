Buddleja anchoensis

Buddleja anchoensis, commonly known as the Anchoensis Buddleia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun. Its foliage is typically gray-green, providing an attractive texture against the floral displays.

Buddleja anchoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and resilience.
