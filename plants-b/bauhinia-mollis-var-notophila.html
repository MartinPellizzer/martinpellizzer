Bauhinia mollis var. notophila

Bauhinia mollis var. notophila, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are compound and divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bauhinia mollis var. notophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
