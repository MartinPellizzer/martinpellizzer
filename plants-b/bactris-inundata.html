Bactris inundata

Bactris inundata, commonly known as the water palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, adapting to waterlogged soils through its fibrous root system. Its fruits are small, clustered berries that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species.

Bactris inundata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for numerous animals.
