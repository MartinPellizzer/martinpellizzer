Biscutella columnae var. depressa

Biscutella columnae var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its prostrate stems. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
