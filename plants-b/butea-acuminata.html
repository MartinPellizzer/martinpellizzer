Butea acuminata

Butea acuminata, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are odd-pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and dye production.
