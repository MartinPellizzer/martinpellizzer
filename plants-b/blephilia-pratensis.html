Blephilia pratensis

Blephilia pratensis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is noted for its clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Blephilia pratensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and woodland edges.
