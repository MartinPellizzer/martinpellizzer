Bifrenaria tyrianthina

Bifrenaria tyrianthina, commonly known as the Tyrian star, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Bifrenaria tyrianthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
