Blepharocalyx strictus

Blepharocalyx strictus, commonly known as the wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The wood of Blepharocalyx strictus is valued for its durability and is used in traditional craftsmanship and construction.
