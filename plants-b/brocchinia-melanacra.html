Brocchinia melanacra

Brocchinia melanacra, commonly known as the black-stemmed brocchinia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green, ribbed leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps collect rainwater. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
