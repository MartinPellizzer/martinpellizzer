Benthamantha trifoliolata

Benthamantha trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved benthamantha, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central axis. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
