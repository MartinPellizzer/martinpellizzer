Balsamina latifolia

Balsamina latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Balsamina latifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
