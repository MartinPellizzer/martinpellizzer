Borinda emaculata

Borinda emaculata, commonly known as the slender borinda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Borinda emaculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
