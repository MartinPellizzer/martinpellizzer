Bulbophyllum reclusum

Bulbophyllum reclusum, commonly known as the Reclus' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum reclusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
