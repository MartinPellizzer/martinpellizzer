Barleria paludosa

Barleria paludosa, commonly known as swamp barleria, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months.

Its root system is fibrous, contributing to its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
