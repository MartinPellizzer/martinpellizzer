Beata ekmanii

Beata ekmanii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the subtropical regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, aiding in water retention.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Beata ekmanii is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
