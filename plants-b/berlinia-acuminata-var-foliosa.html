Berlinia acuminata var. foliosa

Berlinia acuminata var. foliosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
