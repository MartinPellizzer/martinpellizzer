Baccharis assuensis

Baccharis assuensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis assuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
