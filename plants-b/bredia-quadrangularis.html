Bredia quadrangularis

Bredia quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Bredia quadrangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
