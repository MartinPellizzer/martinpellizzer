Bulbine bachmannii

Bulbine bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's bulbous root, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
