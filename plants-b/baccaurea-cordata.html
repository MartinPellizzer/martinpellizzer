Baccaurea cordata

Baccaurea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf baccaurea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale yellow, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round, edible berry with a sweet, acidic flavor, making it a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans.
