Bromus oostachys

Bromus oostachys, commonly known as the eastern brome, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a prominent midvein.

Bromus oostachys plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
