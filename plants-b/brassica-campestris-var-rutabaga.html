Brassica campestris var. rutabaga

Brassica campestris var. rutabaga, commonly known as rutabaga, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen root that is typically purple or yellow in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the second year of growth, which are arranged in racemes. Rutabaga is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its edible root.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a root vegetable in temperate regions.
