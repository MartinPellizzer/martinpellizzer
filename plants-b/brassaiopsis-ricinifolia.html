Brassaiopsis ricinifolia

Brassaiopsis ricinifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brassaiopsis ricinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
