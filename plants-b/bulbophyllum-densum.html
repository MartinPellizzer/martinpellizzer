Bulbophyllum densum

Bulbophyllum densum, commonly known as the dense-lipped orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often green or reddish-brown pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and fragrance.
