Banksia laevigata subsp. laevigata

Banksia laevigata subsp.

laevigata, commonly known as the smooth banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically golden-yellow in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
