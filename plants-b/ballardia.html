Ballardia

Ballardia, commonly known as the goldenrod or false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, yellow or orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ballardia species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They have a basal rosette of leaves and produce seeds in small, fluffy clusters.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
