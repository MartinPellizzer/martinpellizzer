Bridelia fruticosa

Bridelia fruticosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bridelia fruticosa is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
