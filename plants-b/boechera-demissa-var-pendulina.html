Boechera demissa var. pendulina

Boechera demissa var. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Boechera demissa var. pendulina is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
