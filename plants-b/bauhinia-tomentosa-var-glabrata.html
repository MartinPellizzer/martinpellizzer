Bauhinia tomentosa var. glabrata

Bauhinia tomentosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind or velvet tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
