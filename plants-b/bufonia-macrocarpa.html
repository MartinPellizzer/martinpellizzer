Bufonia macrocarpa

Bufonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces edible fruits that are rich in starch and are often used as a food source. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The wood of Bufonia macrocarpa is also valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
