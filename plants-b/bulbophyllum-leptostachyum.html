Bulbophyllum leptostachyum

Bulbophyllum leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom sequentially from the base of the pseudobulb. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

This epiphytic orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
