Banisteria metallicolor var. subsalicina

Banisteria metallicolor var. subsalicina, commonly known as the silverleaf banisteria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by decorative, winged fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded or semi-shaded garden settings.
