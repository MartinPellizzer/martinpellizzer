Baccharis orientalis

Baccharis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

Baccharis orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
