Bubbia parviflora

Bubbia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered burr daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers surrounded by pale green bracts, and it has a compact, shrub-like growth habit. Bubbia parviflora is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
