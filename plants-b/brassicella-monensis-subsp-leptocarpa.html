Brassicella monensis subsp. leptocarpa

Brassicella monensis subsp.

leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wallaby bush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
