Boronia denticulata

Boronia denticulata, commonly known as the rough-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often serrated, giving it the common name "rough-leaved.".

Boronia denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
