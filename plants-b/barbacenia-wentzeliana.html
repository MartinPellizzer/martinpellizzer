Barbacenia wentzeliana

Barbacenia wentzeliana, commonly known as the Zambian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a bell-shaped form and a distinctive, spurred lower lip. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Barbacenia wentzeliana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
