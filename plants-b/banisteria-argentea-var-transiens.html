Banisteria argentea var. transiens

Banisteria argentea var. transiens, commonly known as the silver banister, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
