Bunium alpinum subsp. alpinum

Bunium alpinum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent odor.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
