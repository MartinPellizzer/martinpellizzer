Bambusa villosula

Bambusa villosula, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Bambusa villosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
