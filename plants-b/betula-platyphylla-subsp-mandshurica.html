Betula platyphylla subsp. mandshurica

Betula platyphylla subsp.

mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of yellow and brown beneath.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
