Boldus chilensis

Boldus chilensis, commonly known as *boldo*, is a shrub native to southern Chile and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils and compounds such as boldine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Boldus chilensis thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.
