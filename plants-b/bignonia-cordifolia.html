Bignonia cordifolia

Bignonia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Bignonia cordifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
