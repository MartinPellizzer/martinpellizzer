Begonia trujillensis

Begonia trujillensis, commonly known as the Trujillo begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep red or purple, which stand out against its delicate, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia trujillensis is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
