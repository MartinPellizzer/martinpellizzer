Bromus adoensis

Bromus adoensis, commonly known as the Ado grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit, which helps it compete effectively in its native habitat.

Bromus adoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range and is of interest for ecological and botanical studies.
