Bambusa ventricosa

Bambusa ventricosa, commonly known as the cannon bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hollow stems that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant has a clumping growth habit, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Bambusa ventricosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
