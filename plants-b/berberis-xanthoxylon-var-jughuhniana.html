Berberis xanthoxylon var. jughuhniana

Berberis xanthoxylon var. jughuhniana, commonly known as the Jughuhnian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, glossy evergreen leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
