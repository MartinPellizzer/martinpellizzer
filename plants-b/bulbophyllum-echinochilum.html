Bulbophyllum echinochilum

Bulbophyllum echinochilum, commonly known as the spiny-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces strikingly colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, and the plant is known for its long-lasting blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
