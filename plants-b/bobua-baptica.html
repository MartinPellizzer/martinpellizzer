Bobua baptica

Bobua baptica, commonly known as the sacred water plant, is a rare aquatic species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the muddy substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable subject in both botanical and ecological studies.
