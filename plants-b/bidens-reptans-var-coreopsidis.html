Bidens reptans var. coreopsidis

Bidens reptans var. coreopsidis, commonly known as the yellow beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, trailing stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
