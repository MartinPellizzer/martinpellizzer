Brachiaria chusqueoides

Brachiaria chusqueoides, commonly known as horse grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Brachiaria chusqueoides is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and as a forage plant for livestock.
