Ballota hirsuta subsp. gracilis

Ballota hirsuta subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairy ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies gracilis is distinguished by its more slender stems and smaller leaves compared to the typical form of B. hirsuta.
