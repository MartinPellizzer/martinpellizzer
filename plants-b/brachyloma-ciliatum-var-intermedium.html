Brachyloma ciliatum var. intermedium

Brachyloma ciliatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the spring season. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with fine cilia along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, sunny environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
