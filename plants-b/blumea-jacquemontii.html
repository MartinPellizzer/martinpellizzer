Blumea jacquemontii

Blumea jacquemontii, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Blumea jacquemontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
