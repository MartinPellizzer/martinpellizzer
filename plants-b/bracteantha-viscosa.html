Bracteantha viscosa

Bracteantha viscosa, commonly known as the sticky bracteantha, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
