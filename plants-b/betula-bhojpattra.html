Betula bhojpattra

Betula bhojpattra, commonly known as the sacred birch of India, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and slender, pendulous branches. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and is often found in moist, temperate forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
