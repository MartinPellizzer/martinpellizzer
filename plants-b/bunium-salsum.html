Bunium salsum

Bunium salsum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray leaves. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is often found growing in coastal areas and saline soils. Its structure includes a central stem with branching flower heads, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Bunium salsum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
