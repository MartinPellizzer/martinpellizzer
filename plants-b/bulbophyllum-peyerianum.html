Bulbophyllum peyerianum

Bulbophyllum peyerianum, commonly known as the Peyer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with intricate patterns, and it produces numerous small, fragrant blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
