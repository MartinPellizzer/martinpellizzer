Boesenbergia plicata

Boesenbergia plicata, commonly known as the ginger lily or flame ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stem. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Boesenbergia plicata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
