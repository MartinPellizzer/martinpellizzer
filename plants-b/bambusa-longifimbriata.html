Bambusa longifimbriata

Bambusa longifimbriata, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with elongated, feathery bracts that give it its distinctive appearance.

Bambusa longifimbriata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant growth habit and visual appeal.
