Boscia rautanenii

Boscia rautanenii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears reddish or greyish in color. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, orange fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Boscia rautanenii is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
