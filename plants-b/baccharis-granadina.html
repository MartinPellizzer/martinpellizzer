Baccharis granadina

Baccharis granadina, commonly known as granadina or black baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often thorny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis granadina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
