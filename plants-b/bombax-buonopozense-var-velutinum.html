Bombax buonopozense var. velutinum

Bombax buonopozense var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fiber and timber.
