Bromus biebersteinii

Bromus biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Bromus biebersteinii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
