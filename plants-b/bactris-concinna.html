Bactris concinna

Bactris concinna, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown leaves and tall, slender trunk. The plant typically grows in wet tropical forests and is often found near rivers or in swampy areas. Its fruit, which is edible, contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp.

Bactris concinna is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
