Begonia erectotricha

Begonia erectotricha, commonly known as the erect-hairy begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and may have a slight serration along the edges.

Begonia erectotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
