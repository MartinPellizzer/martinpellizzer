Biovularia minima

Biovularia minima, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, delicate flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle scent.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare and endangered species.
