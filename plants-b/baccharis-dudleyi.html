Baccharis dudleyi

Baccharis dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators.
