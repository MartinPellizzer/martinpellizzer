Boronia warrumbunglensis

Boronia warrumbunglensis, commonly known as the Warrumbungles boronia, is a flowering plant native to the Warrumbungle region of New South Wales, Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
