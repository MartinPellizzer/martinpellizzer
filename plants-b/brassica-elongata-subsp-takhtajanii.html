Brassica elongata subsp. takhtajanii

Brassica elongata subsp.

takhtajanii, commonly known as the Tashkent mustard, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Brassicaceae diversity in arid regions.
