Bombax larutense

Bombax larutense, commonly known as the Malayan silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus plants. The tree is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a cotton-like capsule that releases fluffy seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
