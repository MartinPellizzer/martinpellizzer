Bulbophyllum inconspicuum

Bulbophyllum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
