Brassica saxatilis subsp. humilis

Brassica saxatilis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or on slopes.

Its foliage consists of lobed, serrated leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
