Bellevalia douinii

Bellevalia douinii, commonly known as the Douin's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Bellevalia douinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
