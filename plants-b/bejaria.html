Bejaria

Bejaria, commonly known as the Indian cress or watercress, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. Bejaria species are typically annual or biennial herbs that thrive in moist environments, often found near streams or in wetlands. The plant has small, bright green leaves and produces small white or yellow flowers.

It is valued for its peppery flavor and is often used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
