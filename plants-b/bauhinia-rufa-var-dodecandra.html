Bauhinia rufa var. dodecandra

Bauhinia rufa var. dodecandra, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, bi-lobed leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bauhinia rufa var. dodecandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
