Bistorta subauriculata

Bistorta subauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often swollen nodes. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Bistorta subauriculata is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
