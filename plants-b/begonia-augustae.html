Begonia augustae

Begonia augustae, commonly known as the August Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Begonia augustae is also valued for its ability to produce numerous offspring through rhizomes, enhancing its propagation and survival in natural habitats.
