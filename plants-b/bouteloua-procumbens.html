Bouteloua procumbens

Bouteloua procumbens, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a low-growing grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance, especially during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and blue-green in color, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
