Bambusa multiplex var. incana

Bambusa multiplex var. incana, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grayish-green culms that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant form and adaptability.

Its growth habit makes it an excellent choice for creating privacy screens or adding texture to garden designs.
