Borreria savannarum

Borreria savannarum, commonly known as the redroot false flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments, often found in savannas and coastal areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are thick and fibrous.

Borreria savannarum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
