Betula pubescens var. murithii

Betula pubescens var. murithii, commonly known as the Murith birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. This species thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
