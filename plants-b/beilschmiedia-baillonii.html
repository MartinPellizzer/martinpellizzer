Beilschmiedia baillonii

Beilschmiedia baillonii, commonly known as the Baillon's beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
