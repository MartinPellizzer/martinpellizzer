Boehmeria sieboldiana var. scabra

Boehmeria sieboldiana var. scabra, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Japanese nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other members known for their stinging hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems have been historically used in papermaking and textile production.
