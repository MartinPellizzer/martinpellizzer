Bowlesia sodiroana

Bowlesia sodiroana, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in yellowish spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
