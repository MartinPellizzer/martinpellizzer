Bupleurum scabrum

Bupleurum scabrum, commonly known as rough burnet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a fibrous root system and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

Bupleurum scabrum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
