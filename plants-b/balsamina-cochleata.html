Balsamina cochleata

Balsamina cochleata, commonly known as the spiral balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble a coiled shell, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, and are borne on short, erect stems.

Balsamina cochleata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
