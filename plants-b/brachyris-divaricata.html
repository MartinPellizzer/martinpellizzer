Brachyris divaricata

Brachyris divaricata, commonly known as the variable brachyris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in clusters.

Brachyris divaricata is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
