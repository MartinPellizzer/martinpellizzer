Brosimum mello-barretoi

Brosimum mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its latex-producing capabilities, which have been historically used in the rubber industry. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
