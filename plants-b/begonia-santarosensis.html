Begonia santarosensis

Begonia santarosensis, commonly known as the Santa Rosa begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and a wide range of colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
