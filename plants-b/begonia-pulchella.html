Begonia pulchella

Begonia pulchella, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming during the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
