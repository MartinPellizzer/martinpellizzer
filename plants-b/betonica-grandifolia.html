Betonica grandifolia

Betonica grandifolia, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Betonica grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
