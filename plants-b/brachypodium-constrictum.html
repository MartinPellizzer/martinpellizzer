Brachypodium constrictum

Brachypodium constrictum, commonly known as narrowleaf basketgrass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachypodium constrictum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
