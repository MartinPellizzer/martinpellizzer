Brassica christobalis

Brassica christobalis, commonly known as the Christobal's cabbage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stem and large, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract a variety of pollinators. It is native to South America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it has been cultivated in some regions for both garden display and culinary use.
