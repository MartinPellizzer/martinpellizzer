Bulbine hookeri

Bulbine hookeri, commonly known as the silver leaf bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating, which helps to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Bulbine hookeri prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
