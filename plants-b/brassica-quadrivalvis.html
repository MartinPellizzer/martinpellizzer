Brassica quadrivalvis

Brassica quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a dry, four-valved pod, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Brassica quadrivalvis is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
