Bromus rechingeri subsp. afghanicus

Bromus rechingeri subsp.

afghanicus, commonly known as Afghan brome, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike-like cluster of spikelets.

It is characterized by its small, awned lemmas and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
