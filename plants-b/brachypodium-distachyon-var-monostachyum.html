Brachypodium distachyon var. monostachyum

Brachypodium distachyon var. monostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are typically arranged in a single, dense spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic simplicity and potential use in ecological and agricultural research.
