Biscutella laevigata var. cardonica

Biscutella laevigata var. cardonica, commonly known as the Cardoon Biscuitflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
