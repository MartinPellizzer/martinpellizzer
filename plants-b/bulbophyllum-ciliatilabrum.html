Bulbophyllum ciliatilabrum

Bulbophyllum ciliatilabrum, commonly known as the feather-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure with elongated, ciliate labellum that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinct three-lobed lip.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
