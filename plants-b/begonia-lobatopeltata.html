Begonia lobatopeltata

Begonia lobatopeltata, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage, which makes it a desirable addition to gardens and greenhouses.
