Bulbophyllum seidenfadenii

Bulbophyllum seidenfadenii, commonly known as the Seidenfaden's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers that are often brightly colored. Its pseudobulbs serve as storage organs, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with periods of drought.

This orchid is appreciated by horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
