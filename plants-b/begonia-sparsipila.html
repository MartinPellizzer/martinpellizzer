Begonia sparsipila

Begonia sparsipila, commonly known as the sparse-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ovate leaves that are spaced apart along the stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia sparsipila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
