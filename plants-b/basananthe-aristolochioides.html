Basananthe aristolochioides

Basananthe aristolochioides, commonly known as the Malabar arrowhead, is a tropical climbing plant native to India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that resemble pipes or horns. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
