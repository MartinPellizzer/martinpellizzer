Belvala parviflora

Belvala parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered belvala, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Belvala parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
