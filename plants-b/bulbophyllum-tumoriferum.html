Bulbophyllum tumoriferum

Bulbophyllum tumoriferum, commonly known as the tumor orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy pseudobulbs that are often swollen at the base. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically during the rainy season. Its unique morphology, including the tumor-like structures on its stems, makes it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful attention to watering and light conditions for optimal growth.
