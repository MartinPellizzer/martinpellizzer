Batanthes collina

Batanthes collina, commonly known as the mountain batanthes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Batanthes collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
