Bombax mexicanum

Bombax mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces capsule fruits that contain fibrous seeds surrounded by a cotton-like material. Its bark is smooth and grey, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
