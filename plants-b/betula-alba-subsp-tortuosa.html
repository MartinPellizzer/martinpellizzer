Betula alba subsp. tortuosa

Betula alba subsp.

tortuosa, commonly known as the wavy-bark birch, is a shrub-like species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often peeling bark that exhibits a mottled appearance.

The leaves are triangular with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest edges or wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
