Barbarea vulgaris var. rivularis

Barbarea vulgaris var. rivularis, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients and have a peppery flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, nitrogen-rich soils along streams, rivers, and ditches. It produces yellow flowers with four petals, which are followed by long, slender seed pods.

Watercress is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
