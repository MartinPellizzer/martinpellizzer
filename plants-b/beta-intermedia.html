Beta intermedia

Beta intermedia, commonly known as the sea beet, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and vibrant red stems. The plant typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in cooking, while the roots can be processed into a root vegetable similar to spinach.

Beta intermedia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
