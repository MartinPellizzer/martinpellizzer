Balsamina carnea

Balsamina carnea, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.

Balsamina carnea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
