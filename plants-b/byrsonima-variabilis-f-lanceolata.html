Byrsonima variabilis f. lanceolata

Byrsonima variabilis f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf physalis, is a variant of the physalis genus native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant orange berries. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is known for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops in a distinctive inflated calyx.

The species is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
