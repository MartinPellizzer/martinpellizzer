Bromus secalinus var. elongatus

Bromus secalinus var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of study in ecological and agricultural research.
