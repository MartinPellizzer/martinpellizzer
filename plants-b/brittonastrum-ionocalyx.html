Brittonastrum ionocalyx

Brittonastrum ionocalyx, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its fibrous root system allows it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Brittonastrum ionocalyx plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
