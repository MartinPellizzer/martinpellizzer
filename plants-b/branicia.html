Branicia

Branicia, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. Branicia typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant produces small, toxic berries that are often used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to its potential hazards.
