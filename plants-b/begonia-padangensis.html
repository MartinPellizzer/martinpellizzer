Begonia padangensis

Begonia padangensis, commonly known as the Padang begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to deep red. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
