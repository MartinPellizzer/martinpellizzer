Betula × seideliana

Betula × seideliana, commonly known as the white poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and heart-shaped with a serrated margin.

Betula × seideliana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.
