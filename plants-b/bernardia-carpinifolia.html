Bernardia carpinifolia

Bernardia carpinifolia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking yellow or orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Bernardia carpinifolia make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
