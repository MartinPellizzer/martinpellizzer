Bulbophyllum caecilii

Bulbophyllum caecilii, commonly known as the Celestial Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures that resemble celestial bodies, hence its common name. The plant produces intricate, pseudobulb-like structures that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters, showcasing a wide range of hues from white to deep red.

Bulbophyllum caecilii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of dense forests.
