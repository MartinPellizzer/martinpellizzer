Bucida molinetii

Bucida molinetii, commonly known as the Brazilian beach bean, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray, making it a common species along shorelines.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
