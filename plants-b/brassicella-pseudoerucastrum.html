Brassicella pseudoerucastrum

Brassicella pseudoerucastrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has slender, branched stems and linear, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Brassicella pseudoerucastrum is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for certain insects.
