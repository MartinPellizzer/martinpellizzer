Bulbophyllum consimile

Bulbophyllum consimile, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often pale green or white leaves that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses.
