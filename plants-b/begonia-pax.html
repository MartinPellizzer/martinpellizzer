Begonia pax

Begonia pax, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia pax is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
