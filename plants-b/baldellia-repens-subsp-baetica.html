Baldellia repens subsp. baetica

Baldellia repens subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican marsh orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its preference for calcareous substrates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its morphological features and geographic distribution compared to other subspecies of Baldellia repens.
