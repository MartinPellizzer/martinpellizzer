Bonplandia geminiflora

Bonplandia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that bloom in pairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Bonplandia geminiflora is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
