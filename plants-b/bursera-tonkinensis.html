Bursera tonkinensis

Bursera tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin frankincense or Vietnamese frankincense, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, aromatic capsules that contain the resinous sap used in traditional medicine. This species is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used in incense and medicinal preparations.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
