Bromus lanceolatus var. oxyphloeus

Bromus lanceolatus var. oxyphloeus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence is a panicle composed of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
