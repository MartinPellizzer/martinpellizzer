Baltimora

Baltimora, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Baltimora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners.
