Biermannia calcarata

Biermannia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred orchid-like flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

Biermannia calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
