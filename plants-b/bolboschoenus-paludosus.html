Bolboschoenus paludosus

Bolboschoenus paludosus, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Bolboschoenus paludosus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
