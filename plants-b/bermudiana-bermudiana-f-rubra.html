Bermudiana bermudiana f. rubra

Bermudiana bermudiana f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered Bermuda grass, is a variant of the species native to the Bermuda Islands.

It is characterized by its distinct reddish hue in the flower heads, which sets it apart from the typical green or golden varieties. This form thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and landscaping purposes.

The plant also plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
