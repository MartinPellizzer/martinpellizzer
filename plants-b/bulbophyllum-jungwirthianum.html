Bulbophyllum jungwirthianum

Bulbophyllum jungwirthianum, commonly known as the Jungwirth's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, usually featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry periods.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
