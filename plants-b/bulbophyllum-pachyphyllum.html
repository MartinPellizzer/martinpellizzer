Bulbophyllum pachyphyllum

Bulbophyllum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the fat lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
