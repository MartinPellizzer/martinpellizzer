Bambusa maculata

Bambusa maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink hues. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, often bluish-gray appearance.

Bambusa maculata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
