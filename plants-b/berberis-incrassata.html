Berberis incrassata

Berberis incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Berberis incrassata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
