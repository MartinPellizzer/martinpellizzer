Bouchea integrifolia

Bouchea integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-orange ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a slightly woolly texture.

Bouchea integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
