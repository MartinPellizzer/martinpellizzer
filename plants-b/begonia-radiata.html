Begonia radiata

Begonia radiata, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia radiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
