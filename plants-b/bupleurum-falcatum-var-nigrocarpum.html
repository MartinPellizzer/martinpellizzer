Bupleurum falcatum var. nigrocarpum

Bupleurum falcatum var. nigrocarpum, commonly known as black-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that turns black when mature, giving the variety its common name.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
