Brachycladium nummularium

Brachycladium nummularium, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
