Brassica campestris var. biennis

Brassica campestris var. biennis, commonly known as oilseed rape, is an annual plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flowers and thick, fibrous stems. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and lobed, while its roots develop extensive taproots.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce oil and animal feed.
