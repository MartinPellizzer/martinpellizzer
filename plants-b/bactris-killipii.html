Bactris killipii

Bactris killipii, commonly known as the Killip's palm, is a species of palm native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are round and fibrous. Bactris killipii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
