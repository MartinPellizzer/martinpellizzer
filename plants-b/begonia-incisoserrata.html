Begonia incisoserrata

Begonia incisoserrata, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
