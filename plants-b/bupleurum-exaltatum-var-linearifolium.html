Bupleurum exaltatum var. linearifolium

Bupleurum exaltatum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
