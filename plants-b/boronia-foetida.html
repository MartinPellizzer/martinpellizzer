Boronia foetida

Boronia foetida, commonly known as the stinking boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, often strongly scented flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in various regions of southern Australia. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Boronia foetida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
