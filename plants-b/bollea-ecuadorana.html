Bollea ecuadorana

Bollea ecuadorana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.
