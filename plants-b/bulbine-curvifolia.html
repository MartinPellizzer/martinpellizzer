Bulbine curvifolia

Bulbine curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive zigzag pattern along their edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick leaves.

Bulbine curvifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
