Bigelowia howardii var. attenuata

Bigelowia howardii var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender bigelowia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its slender stems and narrow leaves are adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
