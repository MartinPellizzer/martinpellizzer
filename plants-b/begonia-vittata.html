Begonia vittata

Begonia vittata, commonly known as the zigzag begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that create a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, which adds visual interest to both indoor and outdoor settings.
