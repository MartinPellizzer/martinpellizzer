Berberis × anniae

Berberis × anniae, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. The plant is native to the Himalayas and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often restricted in certain areas.
