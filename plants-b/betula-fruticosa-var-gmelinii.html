Betula fruticosa var. gmelinii

Betula fruticosa var. gmelinii, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a shrub-like species native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellowish-green bark. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in tundra and rocky soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is an important component of polar ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in cold, nutrient-poor conditions.
