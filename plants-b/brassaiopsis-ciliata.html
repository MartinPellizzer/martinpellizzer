Brassaiopsis ciliata

Brassaiopsis ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color. Brassaiopsis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures for its purported health benefits.
