Bulbophyllum gomphreniflorum

Bulbophyllum gomphreniflorum, commonly known as the Gomphrena-flowered Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles the flower of the Gomphrena species, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in a spadix-like structure, and come in various colors such as white, pink, and red.

Bulbophyllum gomphreniflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
