Bambusa tootsik

Bambusa tootsik, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass family plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, often green or yellowish-green culms that rarely exceed a meter in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Bambusa tootsik is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens and indoor settings.
