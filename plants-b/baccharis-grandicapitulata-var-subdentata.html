Baccharis grandicapitulata var. subdentata

Baccharis grandicapitulata var. subdentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
