Begonia natunaensis subsp. natunaensis

Begonia natunaensis subsp.

natunaensis, commonly known as the Natuna Begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate veining patterns.

The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
