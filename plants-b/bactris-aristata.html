Bactris aristata

Bactris aristata, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that develop into small, edible berries. Bactris aristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices.

Its resilience to drought and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a hardy and adaptable species.
