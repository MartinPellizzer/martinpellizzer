Besleria mucronata

Besleria mucronata, commonly known as the small-flowered gesneriad, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, brightly colored blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
