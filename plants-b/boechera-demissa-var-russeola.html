Boechera demissa var. russeola

Boechera demissa var. russeola, commonly known as the rusty rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and mustard-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive rust-colored hue, especially along the edges.
