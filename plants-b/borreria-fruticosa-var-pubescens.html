Borreria fruticosa var. pubescens

Borreria fruticosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy borreria, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features hairy or pubescent foliage, which gives it its varietal name. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Borreria fruticosa var. pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
