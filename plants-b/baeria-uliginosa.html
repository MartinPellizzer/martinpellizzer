Baeria uliginosa

Baeria uliginosa, commonly known as the bog moss, is a species of liverwort that thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wetlands.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, closely packed leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes. Baeria uliginosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it a key component of peat-forming ecosystems.
