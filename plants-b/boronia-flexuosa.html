Boronia flexuosa

Boronia flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, often waxy leaves that have a distinctive wavy edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

Boronia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
