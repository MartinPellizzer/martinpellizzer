Begonia vestita

Begonia vestita, commonly known as the evergreen begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Begonia vestita is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
