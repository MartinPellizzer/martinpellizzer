Bassia iranica

Bassia iranica, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry climates, with deep roots that help it access underground water. It often grows in rocky soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
