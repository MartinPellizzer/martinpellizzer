Barleria pubiflora

Barleria pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Barleria pubiflora is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
