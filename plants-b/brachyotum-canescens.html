Brachyotum canescens

Brachyotum canescens, commonly known as the woolly brachyotum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in dry, hot environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
