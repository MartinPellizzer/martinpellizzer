Begonia beccarii

Begonia beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green color with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
