Bulbophyllum araiophyllum

Bulbophyllum araiophyllum, commonly known as the Araiophyllum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically small, fragrant, and arranged in a raceme, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
