Boscia zimmereri

Boscia zimmereri, commonly known as the yellow bosco, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spherical, red or orange fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Boscia zimmereri is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
