Barleria ilicifolia

Barleria ilicifolia, commonly known as red-hot cat's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, red bracts that resemble a cat's tail, which surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria ilicifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
