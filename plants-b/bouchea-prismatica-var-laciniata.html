Bouchea prismatica var. laciniata

Bouchea prismatica var. laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf bouchea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are finely serrated, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a standout addition to any botanical collection.
