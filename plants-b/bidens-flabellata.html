Bidens flabellata

Bidens flabellata, commonly known as the fanleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a distinctive fan-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Bidens flabellata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
