Bidens trelawniensis

Bidens trelawniensis, commonly known as the Trelawniense tickseed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
