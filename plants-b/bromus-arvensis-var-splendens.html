Bromus arvensis var. splendens

Bromus arvensis var. splendens, commonly known as the showy brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-brown flowers in early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its visual appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
