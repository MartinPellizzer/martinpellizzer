Baccharis simplex

Baccharis simplex, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall.

Baccharis simplex is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
