Barringtonia racemosa var. subcuneata

Barringtonia racemosa var. subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree has broad, glossy leaves and produces fibrous, coconut-like fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
