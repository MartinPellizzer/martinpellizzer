Bupleurum longifolium f. normale

Bupleurum longifolium f. normale, commonly known as the common valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that branch near the base and produce small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
