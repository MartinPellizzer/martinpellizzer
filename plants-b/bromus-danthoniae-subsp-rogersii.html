Bromus danthoniae subsp. rogersii

Bromus danthoniae subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and dry grasslands.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of dryland ecosystems.
