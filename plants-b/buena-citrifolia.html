Buena citrifolia

Buena citrifolia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, thornless trunk and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces a fragrant, fleshy fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in desserts and beverages. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
