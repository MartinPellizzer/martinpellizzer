Bondtia adenotricha

Bondtia adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and slender, woody stems that allow it to climb and trail along supports. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
