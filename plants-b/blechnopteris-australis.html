Blechnopteris australis

Blechnopteris australis, commonly known as the Australian holly fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its fronds are usually divided into lance-shaped segments, and the plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Blechnopteris australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
