Bulbophyllum paramjitii

Bulbophyllum paramjitii, commonly known as the Paramjit's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and fleshy, adapted to store water.

The flowers, which can be white or pale yellow, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
