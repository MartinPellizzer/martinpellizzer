Belladonna pudica

Belladonna pudica, commonly known as the Indian belladonna or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it produces berries that contain toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
