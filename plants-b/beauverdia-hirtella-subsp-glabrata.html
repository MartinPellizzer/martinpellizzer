Beauverdia hirtella subsp. glabrata

Beauverdia hirtella subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a stabilizing plant in degraded soils.
