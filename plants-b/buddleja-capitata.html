Buddleja capitata

Buddleja capitata, commonly known as the summer lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods that split open to release seeds with silky hairs. Buddleja capitata is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
