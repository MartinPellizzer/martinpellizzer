Begonia pseudisoptera

Begonia pseudisoptera, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf patterns and vibrant floral display.
