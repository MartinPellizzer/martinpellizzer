Baccharis mutisiana

Baccharis mutisiana, commonly known as Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The genus Baccharis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in some species.
