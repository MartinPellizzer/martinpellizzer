Bromus reimannii

Bromus reimannii, commonly known as the Reimann's brome, is a grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Bromus reimannii is often found in areas with moderate to high sunlight exposure.
