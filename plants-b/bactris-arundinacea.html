Bactris arundinacea

Bactris arundinacea, commonly known as the palm heart or areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its fibrous trunk is valued for its use in construction and handicrafts.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
