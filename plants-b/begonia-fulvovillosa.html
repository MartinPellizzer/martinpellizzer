Begonia fulvovillosa

Begonia fulvovillosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Begonia fulvovillosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value.
