Bupleurum longicaule var. strictum

Bupleurum longicaule var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and adaptability to well-drained soils.
