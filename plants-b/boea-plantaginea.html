Boea plantaginea

Boea plantaginea, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its spreading nature.

Boea plantaginea is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
