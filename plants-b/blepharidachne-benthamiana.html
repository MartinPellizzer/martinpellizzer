Blepharidachne benthamiana

Blepharidachne benthamiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
