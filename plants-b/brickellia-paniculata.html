Brickellia paniculata

Brickellia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellowish-white flower clusters in the form of panicles. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
