Banksia kippistiana var. kippistiana

Banksia kippistiana var. kippistiana, commonly known as the Kippist's banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
