Baptisia albiflora

Baptisia albiflora, commonly known as white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has palmately divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for prairies and gardens.

Baptisia albiflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
