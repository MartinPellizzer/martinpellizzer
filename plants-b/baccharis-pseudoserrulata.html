Baccharis pseudoserrulata

Baccharis pseudoserrulata, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
