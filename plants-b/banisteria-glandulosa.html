Banisteria glandulosa

Banisteria glandulosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

Banisteria glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
