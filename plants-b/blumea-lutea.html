Blumea lutea

Blumea lutea, commonly known as the yellow blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The yellow flowers bloom in clusters, producing small, inconspicuous florets that attract various pollinators.
