Bassia mirandae

Bassia mirandae, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems. Its scientific name reflects its rare and delicate nature, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
