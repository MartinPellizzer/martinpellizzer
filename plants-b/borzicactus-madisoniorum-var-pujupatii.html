Borzicactus madisoniorum var. pujupatii

Borzicactus madisoniorum var. pujupatii, commonly known as the Madison's borzicactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Texas.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation status.
