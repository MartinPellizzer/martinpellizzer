Bulbophyllum pulchellum

Bulbophyllum pulchellum, commonly known as the beauty orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
