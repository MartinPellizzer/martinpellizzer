Beesha travancorica

Beesha travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore bee plant, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles a cluster of bees, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forested regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Beesha travancorica plays a vital role in local ecosystems by providing nectar to pollinators like bees and butterflies.
