Brizopyrum capense

Brizopyrum capense, commonly known as the Cape hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

Brizopyrum capense plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small coastal organisms.
