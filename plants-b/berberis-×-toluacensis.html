Berberis × toluacensis

Berberis × toluacensis, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries, which attract birds and provide seasonal interest.
