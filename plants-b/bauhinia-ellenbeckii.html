Bauhinia ellenbeckii

Bauhinia ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Bauhinia ellenbeckii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
