Barkhausia amygdalina

Barkhausia amygdalina, commonly known as the African cherry or wild almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels off in thin strips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, almond-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
