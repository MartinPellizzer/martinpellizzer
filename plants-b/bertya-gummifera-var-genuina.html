Bertya gummifera var. genuina

Bertya gummifera var. genuina, commonly known as the gum tree of the genus Bertya, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and yellowish or orange flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its bark, which is one of its distinguishing features. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, contributing to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
