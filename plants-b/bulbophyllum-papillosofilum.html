Bulbophyllum papillosofilum

Bulbophyllum papillosofilum, commonly known as the feathered lip orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, feather-like lips that distinguish it from other orchid species. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
