Blumea fruticulosa

Blumea fruticulosa, commonly known as the fragrant blumea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, elliptic leaves that are often aromatic. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in heads, and the plant produces small, smooth, oval fruits.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
