Brossaea triquetra

Brossaea triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb with a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which are arranged in a cross shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
