Biscutella didyma var. didyma

Biscutella didyma var. didyma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a rare and specialized species within its native habitat.
