Berberis podophylla

Berberis podophylla, commonly known as the desert barberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its berries are edible but often have a bitter taste, and the plant contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Berberis podophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
