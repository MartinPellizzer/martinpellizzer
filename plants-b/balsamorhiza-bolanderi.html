Balsamorhiza bolanderi

Balsamorhiza bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has deeply lobed leaves and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats. It is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.

Balsamorhiza bolanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
