Bulbophyllum dischorense

Bulbophyllum dischorense, commonly known as the orchid with the unique yellow and green markings, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with green or purple markings, often resembling a spider's web. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
