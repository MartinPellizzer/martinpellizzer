Bulbophyllum semipellucidum

Bulbophyllum semipellucidum, commonly known as the translucent orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often translucent or semi-transparent pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive pattern of markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
