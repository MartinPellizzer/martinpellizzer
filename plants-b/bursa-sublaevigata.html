Bursa sublaevigata

Bursa sublaevigata, commonly known as the smooth burr vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and the production of small, spiny fruits that resemble burrs. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are woody and often twine around supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
