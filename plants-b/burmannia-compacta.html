Burmannia compacta

Burmannia compacta, commonly known as the dwarf burmannia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that are often clustered together in a spadix-like structure. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention.

Burmannia compacta is noted for its rapid growth and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
