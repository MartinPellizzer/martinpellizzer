Bencomia brachystachya

Bencomia brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and white, fleshy berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. It plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by influencing tree growth and biodiversity.

However, it can also cause damage to its host trees, leading to reduced growth and, in severe cases, tree mortality.
