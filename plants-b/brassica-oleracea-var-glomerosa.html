Brassica oleracea var. glomerosa

Brassica oleracea var. glomerosa, commonly known as the cauliflower.

This variety is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence of undeveloped flower buds, which are tightly clustered and typically white or green in color. It is a cool-season crop that thrives in temperate climates and requires a long growing season to develop properly. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing genetic relationships with other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
