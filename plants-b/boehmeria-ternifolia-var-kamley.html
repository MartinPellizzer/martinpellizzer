Boehmeria ternifolia var. kamley

Boehmeria ternifolia var. kamley, commonly known as Kamley's hemp, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its slender, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibers are valued for their strength and have been traditionally used in textile production.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
