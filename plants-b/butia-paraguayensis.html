Butia paraguayensis

Butia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay date palm, is a striking palm native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is a medium-sized palm, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and a crown of long, arching leaves. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-green, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Butia paraguayensis produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are rich in nutrients.

It is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
