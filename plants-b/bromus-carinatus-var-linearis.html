Bromus carinatus var. linearis

Bromus carinatus var. linearis, commonly known as the linear brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact growth habit.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous awned florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil.

This variety is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in preventing erosion.
