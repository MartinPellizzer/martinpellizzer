Boerhavia viscosa subsp. apiculata

Boerhavia viscosa subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the hairy bluish morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, often bluish or purple flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the genus Boerhavia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
