Bignonia hirta

Bignonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy bignonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Bignonia hirta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
