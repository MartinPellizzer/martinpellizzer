Bocconia cordata var. thunbergii

Bocconia cordata var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg's bocconia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
