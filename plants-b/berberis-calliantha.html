Berberis calliantha

Berberis calliantha, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features thick, spiny stems and alternate, glossy green leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are edible but have a sharp, bitter taste, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and thorny structure.
