Brongniartikentia vaginata

Brongniartikentia vaginata, commonly known as the Cape mountain zamioculcas, is a unique cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stiff, unbranched trunk and dense, feathery foliage composed of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a branched inflorescence. Its botanical features reflect adaptations to its arid and rocky habitat, with a slow growth rate and high drought tolerance.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and evolutionary significance within the cycad family.
