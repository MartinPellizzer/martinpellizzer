Buchnera fulgens

Buchnera fulgens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange petals that resemble small bells, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Buchnera fulgens is a perennial herb with a compact, bushy growth habit.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
