Berberis kawakamii var. formosana

Berberis kawakamii var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan barberry, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
