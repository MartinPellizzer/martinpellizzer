Bulbophyllum pachyglossum

Bulbophyllum pachyglossum, commonly known as the long-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated lip that is often brightly colored, which plays a role in attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a complex structure that facilitates specialized pollination.

The species is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
