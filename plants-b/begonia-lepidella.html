Begonia lepidella

Begonia lepidella, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
