Bignonia ghorta

Bignonia ghorta, commonly known as the yellow trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
