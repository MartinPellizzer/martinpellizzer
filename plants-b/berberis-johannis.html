Berberis johannis

Berberis johannis, commonly known as the Johann's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis johannis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some parts containing alkaloids used in traditional medicine.
