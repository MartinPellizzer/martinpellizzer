Bulbophyllum josephi

Bulbophyllum josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as a compact, compacted plant with a pseudobulb structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often in clusters. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Bulbophyllum josephi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
