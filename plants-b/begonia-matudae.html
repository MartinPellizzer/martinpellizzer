Begonia matudae

Begonia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, and its heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes.
