Byrsonima variabilis var. rotundata

Byrsonima variabilis var. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved cacao tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its elliptical to round leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe.

This variety is closely related to the cacao tree (Theobroma cacao) and shares similar ecological and botanical features.
