Begonia estrellensis

Begonia estrellensis, commonly known as the Estrellensis Begonia, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or forested areas, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in local ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
