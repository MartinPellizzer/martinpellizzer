Brassica asperifolia

Brassica asperifolia, commonly known as the rough cabbage, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and compact, club-shaped inflorescence. It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a dense cluster.

Brassica asperifolia is often grown for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
