Banksia trifontinalis

Banksia trifontinalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow to orange flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its unique inflorescence consists of multiple flowers clustered in a dense, cylindrical shape.

Banksia trifontinalis is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
