Brasiliorchis barbosae

Brasiliorchis barbosae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, grass-like appearance, characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces its blooms during the rainy season, typically between October and December. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
