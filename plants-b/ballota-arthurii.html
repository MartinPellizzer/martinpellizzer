Ballota arthurii

Ballota arthurii, commonly known as Arthur's ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Ballota arthurii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
