Benoicanthus

Benoicanthus, commonly known as the golden flame or firebush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Benoicanthus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its striking appearance and adaptability have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
