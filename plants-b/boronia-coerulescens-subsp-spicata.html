Boronia coerulescens subsp. spicata

Boronia coerulescens subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spicate boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathland and open forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, spike-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
