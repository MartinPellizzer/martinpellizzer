Bartonia virginica var. sabulonensis

Bartonia virginica var. sabulonensis, commonly known as the sandplain bartonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal plain habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
