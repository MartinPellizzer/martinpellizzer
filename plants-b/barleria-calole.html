Barleria calole

Barleria calole, commonly known as the narrowleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Barleria calole is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
