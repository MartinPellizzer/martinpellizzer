Bunchosia cauliflora

Bunchosia cauliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences, which give it the name "cauliflora," meaning "leafy flower.". The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
