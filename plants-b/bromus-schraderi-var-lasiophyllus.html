Bromus schraderi var. lasiophyllus

Bromus schraderi var. lasiophyllus, commonly known as the sandberg bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green blades that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with lemmas that are usually hairy and have a distinct blue-gray hue. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
