Berchemia congesta

Berchemia congesta, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Berchemia congesta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and unique flowering habit.
