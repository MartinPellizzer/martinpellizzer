Blechnum penna-marina var. bolivianum

Blechnum penna-marina var. bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern native to South America.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help in anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
