Bistania

Bistania, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, open habitats. Bistania has hairy stems and produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes.

It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
