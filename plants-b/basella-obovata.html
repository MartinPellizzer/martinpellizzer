Basella obovata

Basella obovata, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often eaten as a leafy vegetable. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Basella obovata is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
