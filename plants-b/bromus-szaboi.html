Bromus szaboi

Bromus szaboi, commonly known as the Hungarian brome, is a grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Bromus szaboi is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
