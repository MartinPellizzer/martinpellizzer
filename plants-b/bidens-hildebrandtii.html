Bidens hildebrandtii

Bidens hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Mexican tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with prominent black markings, which resemble ticks, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bidens hildebrandtii is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
