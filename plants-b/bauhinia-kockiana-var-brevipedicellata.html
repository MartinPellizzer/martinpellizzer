Bauhinia kockiana var. brevipedicellata

Bauhinia kockiana var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the small-leaved bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
