Bursera rubra

Bursera rubra, commonly known as the red bark tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Bursera rubra is also valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
