Bromus koeieanus

Bromus koeieanus, commonly known as Koeie's brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, sometimes hairy stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically two-ranked, with lemmas that are often hairy and pointed. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Bromus koeieanus is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed environments.
