Banksia squarrosa subsp. argillacea

Banksia squarrosa subsp.

argillacea, commonly known as the red banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright red or orange in color.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
