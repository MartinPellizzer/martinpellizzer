Baccharis aretioides

Baccharis aretioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a woody stem and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Baccharis aretioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
