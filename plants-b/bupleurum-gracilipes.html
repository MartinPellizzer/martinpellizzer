Bupleurum gracilipes

Bupleurum gracilipes, commonly known as slender valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white to pale blue, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Bupleurum gracilipes is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
