Baliospermum meeboldii

Baliospermum meeboldii, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a branching stem covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Baliospermum meeboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
