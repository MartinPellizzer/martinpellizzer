Baccharis salicifolia var. mocoafluminis

Baccharis salicifolia var. mocoafluminis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is often found in wetland areas and riparian zones, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
