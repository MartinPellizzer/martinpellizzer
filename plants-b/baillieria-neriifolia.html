Baillieria neriifolia

Baillieria neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed baillieria, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Baillieria neriifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
