Blumea ramosii

Blumea ramosii, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are hairy and aromatic, while its yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Blumea ramosii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
