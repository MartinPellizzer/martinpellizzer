Biscutella laevigata subvar. ajmasiana

Biscutella laevigata subvar. ajmasiana, commonly known as the Ajmasian biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
