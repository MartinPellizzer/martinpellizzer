Boehmeria tricuspis f. viridipes

Boehmeria tricuspis f. viridipes, commonly known as the green-veined nettle, is a variant of the species Boehmeria tricuspis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its finely serrated leaves and distinctive net-like venation. The plant belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes true nettles, and is known for its stinging hairs. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The green-veined nettle thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
