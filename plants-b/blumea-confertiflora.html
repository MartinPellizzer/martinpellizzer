Blumea confertiflora

Blumea confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Blumea confertiflora is often found in moist forest areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
