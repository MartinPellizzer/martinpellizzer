Berberis × emarginata

Berberis × emarginata, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that often display striking variegation of yellow and green. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardiness and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and natural habitats.
