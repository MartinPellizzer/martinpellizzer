Barbarea minor var. anfractuosa

Barbarea minor var. anfractuosa, commonly known as the zigzag rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
