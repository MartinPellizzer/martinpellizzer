Bridelia retusa var. squamosa

Bridelia retusa var. squamosa, commonly known as the rough bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, scaly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
