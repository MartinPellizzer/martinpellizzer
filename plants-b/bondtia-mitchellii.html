Bondtia mitchellii

Bondtia mitchellii, commonly known as the Mitchell's bondtia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Bondtia mitchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
