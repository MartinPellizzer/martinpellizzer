Baccharis macraei var. lucida

Baccharis macraei var. lucida, commonly known as the shining baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Its small yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems along the Atlantic coast.
