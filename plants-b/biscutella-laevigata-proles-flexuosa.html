Biscutella laevigata proles flexuosa

Biscutella laevigata proles flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
