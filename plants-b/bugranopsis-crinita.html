Bugranopsis crinita

Bugranopsis crinita, commonly known as the hairy bugranopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Bugranopsis crinita is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.
