Begonia androturba

Begonia androturba, commonly known as the Andean begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically features succulent leaves with a textured surface, and its flowers are usually small and clustered. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
