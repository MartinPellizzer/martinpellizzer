Berrya africana

Berrya africana, commonly known as African blackberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly aromatic scent.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of food for various wildlife species.
