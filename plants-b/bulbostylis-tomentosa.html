Bulbostylis tomentosa

Bulbostylis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
