Bauhinia suaveolens var. loretana

Bauhinia suaveolens var. loretana, commonly known as the Loretana Bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often fragrant and have a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
