Bignonia leucopogona

Bignonia leucopogona, commonly known as the white-flowered crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive floral display.
