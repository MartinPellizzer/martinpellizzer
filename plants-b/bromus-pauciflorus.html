Bromus pauciflorus

Bromus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bromus pauciflorus is primarily a flowering plant that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.
