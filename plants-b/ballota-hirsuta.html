Ballota hirsuta

Ballota hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by pods containing several seeds. Ballota hirsuta is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
