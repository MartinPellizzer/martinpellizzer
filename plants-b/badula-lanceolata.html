Badula lanceolata

Badula lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf badula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically white or pale green.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
