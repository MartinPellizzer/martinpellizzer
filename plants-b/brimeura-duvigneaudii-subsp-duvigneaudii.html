Brimeura duvigneaudii subsp. duvigneaudii

Brimeura duvigneaudii subsp.

duvigneaudii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brime, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
