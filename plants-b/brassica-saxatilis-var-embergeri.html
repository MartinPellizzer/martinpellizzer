Brassica saxatilis var. embergeri

Brassica saxatilis var. embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous environments.

Its botanical features include pinnately lobed leaves and a short, stout stem that supports its floral display.
