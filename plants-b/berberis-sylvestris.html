Berberis sylvestris

Berberis sylvestris, commonly known as the European barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp taste. It is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Berberis sylvestris is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
