Byttneria corylifolia

Byttneria corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It has palmately compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a decorative plant.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
