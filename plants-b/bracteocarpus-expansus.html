Bracteocarpus expansus

Bracteocarpus expansus, commonly known as the broad-leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water in its native arid environment.

Bracteocarpus expansus is considered a living fossil, with a lineage dating back to the Mesozoic era.
