Bromus vestitus var. minor

Bromus vestitus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has slender, leafy stems with a distinctive velvety texture on the lower parts. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
