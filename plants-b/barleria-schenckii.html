Barleria schenckii

Barleria schenckii, commonly known as the African ironvine, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
