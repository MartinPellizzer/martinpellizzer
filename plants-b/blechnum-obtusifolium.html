Blechnum obtusifolium

Blechnum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in humid conditions.

The fronds are evergreen, providing a consistent ornamental appearance throughout the year.
