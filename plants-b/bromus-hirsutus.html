Bromus hirsutus

Bromus hirsutus, commonly known as hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and waste grounds. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus hirsutus produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
