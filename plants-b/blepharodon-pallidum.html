Blepharodon pallidum

Blepharodon pallidum, commonly known as the pale leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The pale leaf orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
