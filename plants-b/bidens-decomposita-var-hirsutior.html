Bidens decomposita var. hirsutior

Bidens decomposita var. hirsutior, commonly known as hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.
