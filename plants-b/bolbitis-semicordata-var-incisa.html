Bolbitis semicordata var. incisa

Bolbitis semicordata var. incisa, commonly known as the serrated hart's tongue fern, is a variant of the hart's tongue fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like fronds that are deeply serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps over time.

The fronds are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in the garden.
