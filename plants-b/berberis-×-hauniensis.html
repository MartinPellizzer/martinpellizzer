Berberis × hauniensis

Berberis × hauniensis, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
