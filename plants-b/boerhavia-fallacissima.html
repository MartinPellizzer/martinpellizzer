Boerhavia fallacissima

Boerhavia fallacissima, commonly known as the false goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.
