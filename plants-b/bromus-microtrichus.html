Bromus microtrichus

Bromus microtrichus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hairless leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Bromus microtrichus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
