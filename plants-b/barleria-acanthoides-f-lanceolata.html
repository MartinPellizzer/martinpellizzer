Barleria acanthoides f. lanceolata

Barleria acanthoides f. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry and coastal environments. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
