Benkara pandacaki

Benkara pandacaki, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has hollow, jointed stems and is often used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color that adds aesthetic value to landscapes.

Benkara pandacaki plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
