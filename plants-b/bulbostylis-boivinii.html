Bulbostylis boivinii

Bulbostylis boivinii, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce tiny grains that are adapted for wind pollination.

Bulbostylis boivinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
