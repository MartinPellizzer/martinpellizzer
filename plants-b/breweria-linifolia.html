Breweria linifolia

Breweria linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf breweria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
