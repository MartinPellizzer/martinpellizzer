Besleria guadalupensis

Besleria guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
