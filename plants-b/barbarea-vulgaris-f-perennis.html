Barbarea vulgaris f. perennis

Barbarea vulgaris f. perennis, commonly known as the perennial winter cress, is a variant of the common winter cress.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its spread.

This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
