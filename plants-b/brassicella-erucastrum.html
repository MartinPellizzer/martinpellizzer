Brassicella erucastrum

Brassicella erucastrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Brassicella erucastrum is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various insects.
