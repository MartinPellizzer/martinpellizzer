Burttianthus longipedunculatus

Burttianthus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled burttianthus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.
