Boehmeria celtidifolia

Boehmeria celtidifolia, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Japanese nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and small, greenish-white flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its fibrous stems are also valued for their potential in textile and paper production.
