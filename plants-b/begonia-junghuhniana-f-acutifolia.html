Begonia junghuhniana f. acutifolia

Begonia junghuhniana f. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved begonia, is a hybrid form of the begonia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
