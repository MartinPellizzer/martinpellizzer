Baeckea umbellata

Baeckea umbellata, commonly known as the white-leaf baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with four petals, which bloom in spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded environments.

Baeckea umbellata is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of native Australian landscapes.
