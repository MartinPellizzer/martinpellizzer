Brassica nigra f. dentifera

Brassica nigra f. dentifera, commonly known as the serrated black mustard, is a variant of the black mustard plant characterized by its serrated leaves.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

The seeds are small, dark, and often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
