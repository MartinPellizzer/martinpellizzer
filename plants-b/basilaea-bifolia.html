Basilaea bifolia

Basilaea bifolia, commonly known as twinleaf basil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

Basilaea bifolia is valued for its fragrant leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
