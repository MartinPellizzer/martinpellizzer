Berberis jujuyensis

Berberis jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
