Bornoa

Bornoa, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Bornoa species are typically small, herbaceous plants that thrive in moist, well-drained soil. They are popular as houseplants due to their ease of care and ornamental value.

The flowers often bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
