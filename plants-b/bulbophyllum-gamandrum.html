Bulbophyllum gamandrum

Bulbophyllum gamandrum, commonly known as the Gamandrum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in fluctuating conditions.

The flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
