Byrsonima ligustrifolia f. megaphylla

Byrsonima ligustrifolia f. megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leafed surinam cherry, is a variant of the surinam cherry plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
