Bulbostylis barbata subsp. barbata

Bulbostylis barbata subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense mats in shallow water or moist soil. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive hairy appearance, which aids in its identification and adaptation to its environment.
