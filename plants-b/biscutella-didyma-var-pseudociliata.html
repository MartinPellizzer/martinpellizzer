Biscutella didyma var. pseudociliata

Biscutella didyma var. pseudociliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens.
