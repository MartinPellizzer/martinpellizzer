Blechnum × rodriguezii

Blechnum × rodriguezii, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Blechnum capense and Blechnum penna-marina.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its elegant appearance.
