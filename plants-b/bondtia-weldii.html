Bondtia weldii

Bondtia weldii, commonly known as the African firecracker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble fireworks, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, contrasting with its showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Bondtia weldii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
