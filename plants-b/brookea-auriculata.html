Brookea auriculata

Brookea auriculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are large, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a dense, rounded canopy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant floral display.
