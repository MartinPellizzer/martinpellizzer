Boutonia pomifera

Boutonia pomifera, commonly known as the pineapple flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a pineapple. The plant produces large, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Boutonia pomifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
