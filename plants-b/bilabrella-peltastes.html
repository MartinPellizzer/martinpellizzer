Bilabrella peltastes

Bilabrella peltastes, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
