Bredemeyera integerrima

Bredemeyera integerrima, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in clusters of bright orange to red tubercles. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

Bredemeyera integerrima is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
