Betula alnus var. glutinosa

Betula alnus var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky birch, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its sticky, resinous sap.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, temperate regions, often near streams or in wetlands. The leaves are oval with a doubly serrated margin and turn yellow in the fall. The bark is smooth and white, with some peeling to reveal a lighter inner layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
