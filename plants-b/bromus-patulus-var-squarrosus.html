Bromus patulus var. squarrosus

Bromus patulus var. squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, with bract-like glumes and small, two-lobed lemmas. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
