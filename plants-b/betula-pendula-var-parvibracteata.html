Betula pendula var. parvibracteata

Betula pendula var. parvibracteata, commonly known as the weeping birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive, elegant appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval in shape, turning yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and white, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual appeal.
