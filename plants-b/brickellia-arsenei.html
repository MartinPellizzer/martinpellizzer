Brickellia arsenei

Brickellia arsenei, commonly known as the Arizona brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
