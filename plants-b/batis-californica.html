Batis californica

Batis californica, commonly known as California batis, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It is often found in salt marshes and tidal zones, demonstrating its tolerance to brackish conditions.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for coastal ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
