Bignonia pajanelia

Bignonia pajanelia, commonly known as the Pajanelia crossvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and cascading clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Bignonia pajanelia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
