Begonia bella

Begonia bella, commonly known as the beautiful begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, brightly colored flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in partial shade with well-draining soil. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens or indoor spaces.

Begonia bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
