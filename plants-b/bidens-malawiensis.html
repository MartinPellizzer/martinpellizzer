Bidens malawiensis

Bidens malawiensis, commonly known as the Malawi ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Bidens malawiensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
