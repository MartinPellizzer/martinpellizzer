Bauhinia thonningii

Bauhinia thonningii, commonly known as the African satinflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting central stripe. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Bauhinia thonningii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
