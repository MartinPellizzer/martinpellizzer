Bassia pauciflora

Bassia pauciflora, commonly known as sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Bassia pauciflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
