Beta diffusa

Beta diffusa, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, branching stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with distinctive yellowish-orange sap. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Beta diffusa is known for its strong, pungent odor and has been historically used for its purported healing properties.
