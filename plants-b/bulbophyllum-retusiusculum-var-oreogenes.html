Bulbophyllum retusiusculum var. oreogenes

Bulbophyllum retusiusculum var. oreogenes, commonly known as the Oregon orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually dark green and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native, often humid and shaded habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
