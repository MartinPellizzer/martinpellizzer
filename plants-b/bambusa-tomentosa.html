Bambusa tomentosa

Bambusa tomentosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces long, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread rapidly, requiring regular maintenance to prevent overgrowth.

Bambusa tomentosa is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
