Bumelia angustifolia

Bumelia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Bumelia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and birds.
