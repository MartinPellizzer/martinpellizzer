Bignonia pratensis

Bignonia pratensis, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
