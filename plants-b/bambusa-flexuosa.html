Bambusa flexuosa

Bambusa flexuosa, commonly known as the weeping bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping culms that create a cascading effect, making it popular in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with slender, green shoots that turn yellow as they mature. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa flexuosa is often cultivated for its visual interest and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
