Boehmeria incana

Boehmeria incana, commonly known as Chinese hackberry, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its fibers used in traditional crafts.
