Bunium alpinum subsp. montanum

Bunium alpinum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

Its subspecies montanum is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the main species.
