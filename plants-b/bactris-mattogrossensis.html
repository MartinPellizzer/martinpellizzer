Bactris mattogrossensis

Bactris mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, unbranched trunk that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that can span several meters in diameter, providing shade in its natural habitat. Its fruit is a small, yellow-orange drupe that is edible and often consumed by local communities.

This species plays an important ecological role in the rainforest by supporting various wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
