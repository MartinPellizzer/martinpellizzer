Bryonia cretica var. elwellia

Bryonia cretica var. elwellia, commonly known as Elwell's cretic bryony, is a variant of the genus Bryonia, which belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae.

This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces yellow, cucurbit-like flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a spiny, orange-colored capsule. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
