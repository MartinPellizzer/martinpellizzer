Bathmium trifoliatum

Bathmium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved bathmium, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
