Barringtonia ashtonii

Barringtonia ashtonii, commonly known as the African lantern tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom at night. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, with a rough texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
