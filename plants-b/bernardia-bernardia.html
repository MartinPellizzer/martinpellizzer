Bernardia bernardia

Bernardia bernardia, commonly known as the red cedar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Costa Rica. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its durable wood and ornamental qualities.
