Berula erecta f. incisa

Berula erecta f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
