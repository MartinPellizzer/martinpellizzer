Beguea vulgaris

Beguea vulgaris, commonly known as the common begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to gardens and green spaces.

Beguea vulgaris thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
