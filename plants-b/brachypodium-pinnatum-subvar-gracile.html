Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. gracile

Brachypodium pinnatum subvar. gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
