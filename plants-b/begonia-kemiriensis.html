Begonia kemiriensis

Begonia kemiriensis, commonly known as the Malabar begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Begonia kemiriensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.
