Brunfelsia cabiesesiana

Brunfelsia cabiesesiana, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
