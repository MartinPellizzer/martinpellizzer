Besleria trichiata

Besleria trichiata, commonly known as the hairy besleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family. The plant is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant orchid flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
