Bulbophyllum marcidum

Bulbophyllum marcidum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a sweet scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum marcidum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
