Boottia thorelii

Boottia thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelli's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Boottia thorelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
