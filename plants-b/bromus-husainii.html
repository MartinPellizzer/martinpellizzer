Bromus husainii

Bromus husainii, commonly known as the Husain's brome, is a species of grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it flowers during the summer months.

Bromus husainii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
