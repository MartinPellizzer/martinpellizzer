Boesenbergia longiflora

Boesenbergia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its slender, rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, spicy aroma.

Boesenbergia longiflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
