Bolbosaponaria kafirniganica

Bolbosaponaria kafirniganica, commonly known as the Kafirniganica bulbous saponaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the warmer months. Its roots contain saponins, which are natural detergents used in traditional medicine.

Bolbosaponaria kafirniganica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
