Brassica elongata var. scabra

Brassica elongata var. scabra, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, elongated leaves.

It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found growing in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
