Bignonia pentandra

Bignonia pentandra, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Bignonia pentandra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
