Baccharis gaudichaudiana

Baccharis gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the coastal baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands. Its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season, attracting various pollinators.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
