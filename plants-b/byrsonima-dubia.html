Byrsonima dubia

Byrsonima dubia, commonly known as wild cashew, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
