Barjonia chlorifolia

Barjonia chlorifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved barjonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters. Barjonia chlorifolia is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
