Brachycladium ariasianum

Brachycladium ariasianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, pale blue flowers.

Brachycladium ariasianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
