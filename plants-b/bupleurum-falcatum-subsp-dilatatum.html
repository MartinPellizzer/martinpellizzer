Bupleurum falcatum subsp. dilatatum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf goat's-beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
