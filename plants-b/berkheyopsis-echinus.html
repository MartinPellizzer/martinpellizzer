Berkheyopsis echinus

Berkheyopsis echinus, commonly known as the spiny rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spiny, glaucous leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in dry and sunny conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Berkheyopsis echinus is a hardy, low-maintenance plant often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
