Biarum crispulum

Biarum crispulum, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly wrinkled texture, giving it its common name.

Biarum crispulum is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
