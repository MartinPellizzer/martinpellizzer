Brahea conzattii

Brahea conzattii, commonly known as the Conzattii palm, is a rare and endangered species native to the Sonoran Desert in Arizona and northern Mexico.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has a smooth, gray trunk with a distinctive crown of feathery, blue-green fronds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
