Briza media var. clusii

Briza media var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusius sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, water-tolerant plant.
