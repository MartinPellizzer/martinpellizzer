Bunium elegans var. pachypus

Bunium elegans var. pachypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its essential oils, rich in anethole, make it valuable in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
