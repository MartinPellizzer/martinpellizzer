Breynia carnosa

Breynia carnosa, commonly known as the "crimson flame" or "fire plant," is a succulent shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings, and it produces vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions. Breynia carnosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
