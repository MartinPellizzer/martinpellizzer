Brassica arvensis var. orientalis

Brassica arvensis var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
