Bupleurum aureum subsp. aureum

Bupleurum aureum subsp.

aureum, commonly known as golden bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
