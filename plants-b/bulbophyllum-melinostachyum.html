Bulbophyllum melinostachyum

Bulbophyllum melinostachyum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
