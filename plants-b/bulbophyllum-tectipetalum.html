Bulbophyllum tectipetalum

Bulbophyllum tectipetalum, commonly known as the hooded lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like petals that give it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
