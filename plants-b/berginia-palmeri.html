Berginia palmeri

Berginia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bergenia, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically thick and fleshy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. Berginia palmeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant gardens.
