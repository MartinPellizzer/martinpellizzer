Baccharis candolleana

Baccharis candolleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in the spring and summer months.

Baccharis candolleana is often found in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
