Bidens pilosa f. bimucronata

Bidens pilosa f. bimucronata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
