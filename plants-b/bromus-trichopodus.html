Bromus trichopodus

Bromus trichopodus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, two-rowed spikelets.

Bromus trichopodus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
