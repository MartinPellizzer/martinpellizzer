Biscutella coronopifolia proles granitica

Biscutella coronopifolia proles granitica, commonly known as the granitic biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suited to extreme climates.
