Bennettia fulva

Bennettia fulva, commonly known as the golden bennettia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in panicle-like clusters. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense rainforests.

Bennettia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
