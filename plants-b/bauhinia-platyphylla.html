Bauhinia platyphylla

Bauhinia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features distinctive bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, arranged in racemes, and bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
