Bupleurum fruticescens subsp. spinosum

Bupleurum fruticescens subsp.

spinosum, commonly known as the spiny bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its woody, sprawling stems that are often covered in spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. Bupleurum fruticescens subsp.

spinosum is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
