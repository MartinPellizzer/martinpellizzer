Begonia panamensis

Begonia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Begonia panamensis prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
