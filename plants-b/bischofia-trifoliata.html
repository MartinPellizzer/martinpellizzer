Bischofia trifoliata

Bischofia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Bischofia trifoliata is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
