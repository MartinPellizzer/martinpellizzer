Bougainvillea glabra var. alba

Bougainvillea glabra var. alba, commonly known as white paperflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white bracts that surround the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber with thick, woody stems and can grow in a variety of soil types. It thrives in full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
