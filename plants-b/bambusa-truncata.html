Bambusa truncata

Bambusa truncata, commonly known as the chuntung bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green culms that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Bambusa truncata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
