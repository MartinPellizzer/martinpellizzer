Begonia ornithocarpa

Begonia ornithocarpa, commonly known as the bird's beak begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often twisted flower buds that resemble a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its foliage is typically dark green with a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia ornithocarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in tropical gardens.
