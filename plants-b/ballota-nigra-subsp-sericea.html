Ballota nigra subsp. sericea

Ballota nigra subsp.

sericea, commonly known as velvet ballota, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
