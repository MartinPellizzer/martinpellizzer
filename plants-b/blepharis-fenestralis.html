Blepharis fenestralis

Blepharis fenestralis, commonly known as the windowleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky areas.

Blepharis fenestralis is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
