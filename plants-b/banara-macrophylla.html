Banara macrophylla

Banara macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The plant has showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night and closing during the day. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Banara macrophylla plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
