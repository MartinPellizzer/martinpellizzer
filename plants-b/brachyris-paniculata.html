Brachyris paniculata

Brachyris paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Brachyris paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
