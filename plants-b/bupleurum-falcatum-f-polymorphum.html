Bupleurum falcatum f. polymorphum

Bupleurum falcatum f. polymorphum, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is noted for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, making it a distinctive and interesting botanical specimen.
