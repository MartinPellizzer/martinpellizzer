Buchnera palustris

Buchnera palustris, commonly known as the marshbuchnera, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating on the water surface or just above it.

Buchnera palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
