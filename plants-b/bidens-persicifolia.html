Bidens persicifolia

Bidens persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf false fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
