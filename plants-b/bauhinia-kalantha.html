Bauhinia kalantha

Bauhinia kalantha, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. Bauhinia kalantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodland or along forest edges.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
