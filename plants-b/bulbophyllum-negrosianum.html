Bulbophyllum negrosianum

Bulbophyllum negrosianum, commonly known as the Negros spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base and are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
