Bacopa ranaria

Bacopa ranaria, commonly known as aquatic bacopa, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. The plant has delicate, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or oval in shape. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with four petals and are borne in clusters.

Bacopa ranaria is known for its adaptability to aquatic environments and is sometimes used in ornamental water gardens.
