Boebera fastigiata

Boebera fastigiata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a tall, narrow growth habit with a dense canopy of dark green leaves. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

Boebera fastigiata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.
