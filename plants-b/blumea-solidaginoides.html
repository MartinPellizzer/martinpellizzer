Blumea solidaginoides

Blumea solidaginoides, commonly known as goldenbuttons, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is often found in open areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branched.

Blumea solidaginoides is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
