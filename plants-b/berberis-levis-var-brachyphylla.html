Berberis levis var. brachyphylla

Berberis levis var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically no longer than 2 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
