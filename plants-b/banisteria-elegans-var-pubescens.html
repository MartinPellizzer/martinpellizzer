Banisteria elegans var. pubescens

Banisteria elegans var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety stems covered in short, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its unique appearance.
