Bothriochloa venusta

Bothriochloa venusta, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and dark green, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Bothriochloa venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
