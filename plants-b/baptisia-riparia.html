Baptisia riparia

Baptisia riparia, commonly known as the swamp false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Baptisia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
