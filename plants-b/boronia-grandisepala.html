Boronia grandisepala

Boronia grandisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Boronia grandisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
