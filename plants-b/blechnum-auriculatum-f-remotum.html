Blechnum auriculatum f. remotum

Blechnum auriculatum f. remotum, commonly known as the remote ear-lobed harts-tongue, is a fern species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often recurved fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with serrated margins that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This fern is valued for its unique foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
