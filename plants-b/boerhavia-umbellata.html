Boerhavia umbellata

Boerhavia umbellata, commonly known as the redroot sandplum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with low moisture availability.

Boerhavia umbellata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
