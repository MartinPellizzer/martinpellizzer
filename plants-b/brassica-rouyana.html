Brassica rouyana

Brassica rouyana, commonly known as Chinese kale or gai lan, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, curly leaves and thick, stalk-like stems. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. Brassica rouyana is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as dietary fiber.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown in temperate regions for both culinary and nutritional purposes.
