Bambusa arundinacea

Bambusa arundinacea, commonly known as golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has a dense, clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This bamboo is also valued for its strong, flexible culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
