Brassia keiliana

Brassia keiliana, commonly known as the Keil's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are aerial, attaching to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat.

Brassia keiliana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
