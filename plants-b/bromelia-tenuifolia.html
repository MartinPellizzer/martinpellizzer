Bromelia tenuifolia

Bromelia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many ornamental and epiphytic plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form a rosette, often with a vibrant green or blueish hue. Its flowers, which are typically red or orange, emerge from the center of the leaf rosette and are surrounded by showy bracts.

Bromelia tenuifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
