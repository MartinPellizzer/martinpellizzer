Begonia walpersii

Begonia walpersii, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia walpersii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and foliage texture.
