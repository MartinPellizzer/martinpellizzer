Binghamia pseudomelanostele

Binghamia pseudomelanostele, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small, evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.
