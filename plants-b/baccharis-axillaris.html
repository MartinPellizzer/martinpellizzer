Baccharis axillaris

Baccharis axillaris, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis axillaris is known for its drought tolerance and is used in some regions for erosion control.
