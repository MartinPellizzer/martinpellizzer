Bromus tectorum f. nudus

Bromus tectorum f. nudus, commonly known as the naked cheatgrass, is a variant of cheatgrass that lacks the typical awns found in the typical form.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe but has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. This plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its rapid growth and high seed production, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
