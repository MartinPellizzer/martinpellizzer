Bridelia retusa var. glabra

Bridelia retusa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
