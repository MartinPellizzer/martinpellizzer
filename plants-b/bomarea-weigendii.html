Bomarea weigendii

Bomarea weigendii, commonly known as the silverleaf bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue leaves and delicate, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
