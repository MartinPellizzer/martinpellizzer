Briza capensis

Briza capensis, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry, and feathery appearance, with slender leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a landscaping plant in various regions.
