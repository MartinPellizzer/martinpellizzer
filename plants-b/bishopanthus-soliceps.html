Bishopanthus soliceps

Bishopanthus soliceps, commonly known as the desert bishopweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Bishopanthus soliceps is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
