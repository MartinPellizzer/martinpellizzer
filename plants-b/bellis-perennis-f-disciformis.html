Bellis perennis f. disciformis

Bellis perennis f. disciformis, commonly known as the "Daisy with a disc," is a horticultural form of the common daisy.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive white petals surrounding a yellow central disk. The plant features a basal rosette of silvery-green leaves that are spoon-shaped and slightly hairy. It produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a charming touch to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in well-drained soil.
