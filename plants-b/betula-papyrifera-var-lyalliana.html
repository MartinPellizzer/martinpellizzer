Betula papyrifera var. lyalliana

Betula papyrifera var. lyalliana, commonly known as the Lyall's paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This tree is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, with serrated edges.

It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
