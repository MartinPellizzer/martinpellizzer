Braya alpina subsp. americana

Braya alpina subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American rockcress, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often found in mountainous regions of the western United States and Canada.
