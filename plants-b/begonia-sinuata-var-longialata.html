Begonia sinuata var. longialata

Begonia sinuata var. longialata, commonly known as the long-leaf begonia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture and a distinctive sinuate margin. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
