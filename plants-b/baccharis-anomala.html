Baccharis anomala

Baccharis anomala, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability to arid environments make it a valuable plant in native landscaping.
