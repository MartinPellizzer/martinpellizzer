Bassia pilosa

Bassia pilosa, commonly known as Russian spinach, is an annual herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.
