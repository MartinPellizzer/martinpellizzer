Bromus muralis var. madritensis

Bromus muralis var. madritensis, commonly known as the Madrid wall grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky or disturbed soils, often found growing on walls and in cracks in urban environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
