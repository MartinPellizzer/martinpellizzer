Berkheya hispida

Berkheya hispida, commonly known as the woolly Berkheya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
