Bucklandia populifolia

Bucklandia populifolia, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

Bucklandia populifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
