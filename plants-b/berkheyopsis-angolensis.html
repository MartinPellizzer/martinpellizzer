Berkheyopsis angolensis

Berkheyopsis angolensis, commonly known as the Angola thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Berkheyopsis angolensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
