Bupleurum andronakii

Bupleurum andronakii, commonly known as the Greek hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Bupleurum andronakii is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
