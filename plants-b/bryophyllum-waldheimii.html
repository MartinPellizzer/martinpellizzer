Bryophyllum waldheimii

Bryophyllum waldheimii, commonly known as the Waldheim's leaf plant, is a species of succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the spring or summer months. Native to southern Africa, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
