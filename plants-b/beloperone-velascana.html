Beloperone velascana

Beloperone velascana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.
