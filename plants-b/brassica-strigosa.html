Brassica strigosa

Brassica strigosa, commonly known as rough mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing a high amount of glucosinolates.

Brassica strigosa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
