Biserrula pelecinus var. brevipes

Biserrula pelecinus var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed biserrula, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value and ecological benefits.
