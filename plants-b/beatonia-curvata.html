Beatonia curvata

Beatonia curvata, commonly known as the curly beard plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, curly, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a compact cluster, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Beatonia curvata is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
