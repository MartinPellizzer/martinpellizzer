Bumelia retusa subsp. neglecta

Bumelia retusa subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the Florida bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, greenish-black fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a somewhat waxy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in wetland ecosystems.
