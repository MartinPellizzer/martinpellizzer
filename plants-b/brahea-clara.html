Brahea clara

Brahea clara, commonly known as the desert palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Brahea clara is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
