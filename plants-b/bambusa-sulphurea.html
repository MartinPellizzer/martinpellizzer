Bambusa sulphurea

Bambusa sulphurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to gold-colored culms, which stand out against its green leaves. This bamboo grows in clusters and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it a popular choice for landscaping.

Additionally, Bambusa sulphurea is valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
