Bulbophyllum ponapense

Bulbophyllum ponapense, commonly known as the Ponape Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, leafy growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
