Blumea paniculata var. gracilis

Blumea paniculata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
