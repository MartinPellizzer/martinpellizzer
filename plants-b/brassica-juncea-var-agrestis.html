Brassica juncea var. agrestis

Brassica juncea var. agrestis, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has serrated leaves and produces a strong, pungent odor when crushed.

This variant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
