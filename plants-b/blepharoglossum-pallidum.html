Blepharoglossum pallidum

Blepharoglossum pallidum, commonly known as the pale mossy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Blepharoglossum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
