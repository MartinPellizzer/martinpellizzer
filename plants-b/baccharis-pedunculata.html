Baccharis pedunculata

Baccharis pedunculata, commonly known as rough baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
