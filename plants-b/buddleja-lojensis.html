Buddleja lojensis

Buddleja lojensis, commonly known as the Loja butterfly bush, is a species native to Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually pink to purple in color, attracting a variety of pollinators.

Buddleja lojensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
