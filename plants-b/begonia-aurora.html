Begonia aurora

Begonia aurora, commonly known as the Aurora begonia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as a trailing or upright shrub, with succulent-like stems that store water. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia aurora thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.
