Bidens carpodonta

Bidens carpodonta, commonly known as the common beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers with distinctive white markings. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
