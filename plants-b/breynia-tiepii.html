Breynia tiepii

Breynia tiepii, commonly known as the yellow breynia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and coastal areas.

Breynia tiepii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
