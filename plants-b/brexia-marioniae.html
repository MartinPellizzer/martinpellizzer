Brexia marioniae

Brexia marioniae, commonly known as the Marion's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, almost ethereal appearance that mimics the appearance of a ghostly figure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Brexia marioniae is critically endangered and is protected in many regions.
