Bonjeanea microphylla

Bonjeanea microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bonjeanea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Bonjeanea microphylla is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.
