Bothriochloa springfieldii

Bothriochloa springfieldii, commonly known as Springfield grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
