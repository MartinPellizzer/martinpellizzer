Barleria prionitis subsp. appressa

Barleria prionitis subsp.

appressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to spread through rhizomes.
