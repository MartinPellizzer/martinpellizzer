Bowlesia flabilis

Bowlesia flabilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Bowlesia flabilis is adapted to high-altitude environments and prefers well-drained, rocky soils.
