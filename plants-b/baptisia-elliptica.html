Baptisia elliptica

Baptisia elliptica, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Baptisia elliptica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
