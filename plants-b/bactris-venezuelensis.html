Bactris venezuelensis

Bactris venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Venezuela and adjacent regions.

It is a medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are fibrous, with a sweet, edible pulp. Bactris venezuelensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid lowland areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture and conservation efforts.
