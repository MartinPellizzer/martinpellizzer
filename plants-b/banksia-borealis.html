Banksia borealis

Banksia borealis, commonly known as the southern banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia borealis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing nectar for a variety of pollinators.
