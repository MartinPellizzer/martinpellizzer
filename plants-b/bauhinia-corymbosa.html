Bauhinia corymbosa

Bauhinia corymbosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from orange to red in color. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia corymbosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
