Berberis vulgaris f. aureomarginata

Berberis vulgaris f. aureomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin barberry, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny branches and vibrant foliage.

Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with a striking golden-yellow margin that contrasts beautifully with the dark green central portion. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used ornamentally. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a hedge due to its hardy nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
