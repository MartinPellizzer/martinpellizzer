Bowdichia virgilioides var. pubescens

Bowdichia virgilioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bowdichia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its hairy texture and seasonal foliage make it a distinctive plant in its native habitat.
