Biscutella variegata var. megacarpaea

Biscutella variegata var. megacarpaea, commonly known as the variegated biscuit flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
