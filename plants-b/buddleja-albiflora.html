Buddleja albiflora

Buddleja albiflora, commonly known as the white buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, woody stems that often branch out to form a bushy structure. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja albiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
