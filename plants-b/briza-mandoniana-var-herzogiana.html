Briza mandoniana var. herzogiana

Briza mandoniana var. herzogiana, commonly known as the Herzog's quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
