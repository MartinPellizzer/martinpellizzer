Bonplandia angostura

Bonplandia angostura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, Bonplandia angostura is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
