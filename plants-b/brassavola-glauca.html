Brassavola glauca

Brassavola glauca, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Brassavola glauca is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
