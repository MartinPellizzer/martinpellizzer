Bignonia splendor-sylvae

Bignonia splendor-sylvae, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting stems that can climb up to 30 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
