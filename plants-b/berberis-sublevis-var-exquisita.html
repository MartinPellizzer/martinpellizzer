Berberis sublevis var. exquisita

Berberis sublevis var. exquisita, commonly known as the velvet barberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow, appearing in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various climate conditions.
