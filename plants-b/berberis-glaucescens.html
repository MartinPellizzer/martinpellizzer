Berberis glaucescens

Berberis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.

Berberis glaucescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
