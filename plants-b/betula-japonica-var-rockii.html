Betula japonica var. rockii

Betula japonica var. rockii, commonly known as the Japanese birch or Rockii birch, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, cone-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and white, often peeling in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
