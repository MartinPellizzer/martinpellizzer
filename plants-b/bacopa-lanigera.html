Bacopa lanigera

Bacopa lanigera, commonly known as the velvet bacopa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and easy maintenance.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
