Bellia temulenta

Bellia temulenta, commonly known as the wild bellflower, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Bellia temulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
