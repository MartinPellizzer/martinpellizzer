Byrsonima vacciniifolia

Byrsonima vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries resembling cherries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
