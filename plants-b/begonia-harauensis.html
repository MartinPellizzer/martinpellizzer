Begonia harauensis

Begonia harauensis, commonly known as the Harau begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
