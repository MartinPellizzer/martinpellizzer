Barkhausia erucifolia

Barkhausia erucifolia, commonly known as the African horned vulture plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
