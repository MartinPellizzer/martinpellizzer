Blackstonia perfoliata subsp. acuminata

Blackstonia perfoliata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blackstonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often have a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are often hollow and can be found growing through the leaves, a feature known as perfoliate.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
