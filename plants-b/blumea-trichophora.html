Blumea trichophora

Blumea trichophora, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Blumea trichophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
