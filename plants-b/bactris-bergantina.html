Bactris bergantina

Bactris bergantina, commonly known as the Bergantina palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of the coconut.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable use, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for local economic purposes.
