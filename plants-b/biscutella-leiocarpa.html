Biscutella leiocarpa

Biscutella leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Biscutella leiocarpa is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
