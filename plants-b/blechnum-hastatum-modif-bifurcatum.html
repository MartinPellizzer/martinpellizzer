Blechnum hastatum modif. bifurcatum

Blechnum hastatum modif.

bifurcatum, commonly known as the bifurcated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern genus characterized by its unique frond structure. It features lanceolate, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive bifurcated or forked appearance at the tips, giving it a striking visual appeal.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, wiry rhizomes that spread horizontally along the soil surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its intricate foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
