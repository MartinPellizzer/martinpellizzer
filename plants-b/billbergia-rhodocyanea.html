Billbergia rhodocyanea

Billbergia rhodocyanea, commonly known as the red-tipped billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that features red bracts with yellow-orange flowers. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
