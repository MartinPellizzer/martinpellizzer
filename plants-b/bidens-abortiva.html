Bidens abortiva

Bidens abortiva, commonly known as common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed achene that is often surrounded by bracts.

Bidens abortiva is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
