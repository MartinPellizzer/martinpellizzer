Begonia nicolai-hallei

Begonia nicolai-hallei, commonly known as the Hallei begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouse cultivation.
