Boechera pulchra var. pallens

Boechera pulchra var. pallens, commonly known as the pale stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry, well-drained soils.
