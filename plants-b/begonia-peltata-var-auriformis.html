Begonia peltata var. auriformis

Begonia peltata var. auriformis, commonly known as the golden leaf begonia, is a strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive golden or variegated pattern.

Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its foliage is particularly notable for its vibrant coloration, which can range from yellow to cream and green.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
