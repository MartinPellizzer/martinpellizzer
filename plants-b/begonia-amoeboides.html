Begonia amoeboides

Begonia amoeboides, commonly known as the amoeboid begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves that resemble the shape of an amoeba, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of pink or white, adding ornamental value.

Due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet low-maintenance plant.
