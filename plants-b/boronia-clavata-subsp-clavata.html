Boronia clavata subsp. clavata

Boronia clavata subsp.

clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
