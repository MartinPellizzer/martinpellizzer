Bomarea simplex var. furcata

Bomarea simplex var. furcata, commonly known as the forked bomarea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, often bi-colored flowers that range from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a velvety texture, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy blooms.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
