Blitum tartaricum

Blitum tartaricum, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of western North America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Blitum tartaricum is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
