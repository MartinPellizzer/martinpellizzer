Bursa cochlearina

Bursa cochlearina, commonly known as the cochlearia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Bursa cochlearina is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
