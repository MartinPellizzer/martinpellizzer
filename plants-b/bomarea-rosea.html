Bomarea rosea

Bomarea rosea, commonly known as the pink bomarea, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant pink to lavender-colored flowers. The plant features slender, tubular leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
