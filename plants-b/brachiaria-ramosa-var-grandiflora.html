Brachiaria ramosa var. grandiflora

Brachiaria ramosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
