Boopis filifolia

Boopis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf boopis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and thread-like, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences.

Boopis filifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
