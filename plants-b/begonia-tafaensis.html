Begonia tafaensis

Begonia tafaensis, commonly known as the Tafa begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
