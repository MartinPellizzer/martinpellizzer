Bidens pueblensis

Bidens pueblensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers produce small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Bidens pueblensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
