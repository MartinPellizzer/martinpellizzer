Brassica argyi

Brassica argyi, commonly known as the Chinese yellow mustard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also edible and have a slightly bitter taste.

Brassica argyi is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
