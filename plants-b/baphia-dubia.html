Baphia dubia

Baphia dubia, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. Baphia dubia is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments within dense forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native habitat.
