Bulbophyllum sambiranense

Bulbophyllum sambiranense, commonly known as the Sambiranense orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its floral structure features intricate sepals and petals, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
