Begonia casiguranensis

Begonia casiguranensis, commonly known as the Casiguran begonia, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and display a range of colors, from deep green to reddish hues.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or red and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
