Behuria corymbosa

Behuria corymbosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral displays that appear in spring, covering the entire plant in a vibrant show. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. Its flowers are clustered in corymbs, which are flat-topped or rounded inflorescences.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
