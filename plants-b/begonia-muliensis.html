Begonia muliensis

Begonia muliensis, commonly known as the Muli begonia, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and attractive foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to shaded environments.
