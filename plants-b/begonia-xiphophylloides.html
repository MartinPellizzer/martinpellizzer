Begonia xiphophylloides

Begonia xiphophylloides, commonly known as the sword-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, fuchsia or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
