Begonia rambutan

Begonia rambutan, commonly known as the "hairy begonia," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small tomatoes. Its foliage and fruit are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.
