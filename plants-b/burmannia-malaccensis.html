Burmannia malaccensis

Burmannia malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca burmannia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its unique, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on short, erect stems. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a source of nectar.

Burmannia malaccensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
