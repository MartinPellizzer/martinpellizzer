Balsamorhiza serrata

Balsamorhiza serrata, commonly known as the mountain balsam or yellow balsam, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and high elevations.
