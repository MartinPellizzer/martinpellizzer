Bactris socialis

Bactris socialis, commonly known as the red rata or red bamboo palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Bactris socialis thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to adapt to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
