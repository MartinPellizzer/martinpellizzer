Baileya multiradiata var. pleniradiata

Baileya multiradiata var. pleniradiata, commonly known as the "Mammoth Desert Sun" or "Desert Sunflower," is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to desert gardens and a valuable plant for supporting pollinators.
