Brassica oleracea subsp. pourretii

Brassica oleracea subsp.

pourretii, commonly known as the Mediterranean cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves that are thick and succulent.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It is adapted to dry climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has historical significance in traditional Mediterranean agriculture.
