Bromus sylvaticus

Bromus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wood brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant has slender, often reddish stems and produces spikelets that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus sylvaticus is valued for its role in ecological systems, providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in various habitats.
