Boophone amaryllidea

Boophone amaryllidea, commonly known as the African rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a central bulb, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Boophone amaryllidea is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental value.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice among gardeners interested in exotic flora.
