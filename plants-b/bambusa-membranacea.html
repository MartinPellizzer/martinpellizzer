Bambusa membranacea

Bambusa membranacea, commonly known as the parchment bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered with a thin, papery sheath. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a light green color.

Bambusa membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
