Berberis cuneata

Berberis cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorned stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis cuneata is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to many pests and diseases.
