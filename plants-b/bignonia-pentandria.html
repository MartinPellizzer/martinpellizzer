Bignonia pentandria

Bignonia pentandria, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in moist habitats such as riverbanks and woodlands.

Bignonia pentandria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
