Begonia princeae f. rhodesiana

Begonia princeae f. rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodesian begonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-purple underside, which adds to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
