Begonia robusta var. rubra

Begonia robusta var. rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often deep green with a reddish tinge along the veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms, which add a splash of color to any space.
