Bilderdykia aubertii

Bilderdykia aubertii, commonly known as the African snowbells, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-blue, hairy leaves that are succulent and adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
