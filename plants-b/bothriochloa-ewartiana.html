Bothriochloa ewartiana

Bothriochloa ewartiana, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Bothriochloa ewartiana is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
