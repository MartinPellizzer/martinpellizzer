Begonia trisulcata

Begonia trisulcata, commonly known as the three-ridged begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that have three distinct ridges running from the base to the tip. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia trisulcata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
