Bulbophyllum fordii

Bulbophyllum fordii, commonly known as the Ford's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow, and are adorned with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum fordii is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique botanical features.
