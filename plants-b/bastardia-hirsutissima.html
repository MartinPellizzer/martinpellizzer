Bastardia hirsutissima

Bastardia hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Bastardia hirsutissima is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
