Blumea racemosa

Blumea racemosa, commonly known as the featherleaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has narrow, feather-like leaves that give it its common name, and it is often found in moist, forested areas. Blumea racemosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its ability to thrive in humid environments makes it a notable species in tropical flora.
