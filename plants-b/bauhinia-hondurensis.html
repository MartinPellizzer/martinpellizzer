Bauhinia hondurensis

Bauhinia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Bauhinia hondurensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
