Buena triflora

Buena triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered beauty, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Buena triflora thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight for optimal growth.
