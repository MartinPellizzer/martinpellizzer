Bejaria glauca var. tomentella

Bejaria glauca var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
