Basella oleracea var. cordifolia

Basella oleracea var. cordifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical leafy vegetable native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are tender and edible, often used in soups and stir-fries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of iron and vitamin C.
