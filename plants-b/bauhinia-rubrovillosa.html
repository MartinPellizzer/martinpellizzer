Bauhinia rubrovillosa

Bauhinia rubrovillosa, commonly known as the red-veined bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with red veins. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia rubrovillosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
