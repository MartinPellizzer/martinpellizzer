Baccharis phylicifolia

Baccharis phylicifolia, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.

Baccharis phylicifolia is drought-tolerant and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples.
