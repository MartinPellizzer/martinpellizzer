Bulbophyllum adenophorum

Bulbophyllum adenophorum, commonly known as the hairy-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers with long, hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with contrasting purple or red markings.

Bulbophyllum adenophorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
