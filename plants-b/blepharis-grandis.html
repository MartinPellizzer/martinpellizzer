Blepharis grandis

Blepharis grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Blepharis grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a drought-tolerant plant.
