Buchnera phoenicea

Buchnera phoenicea, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark, which becomes more prominent as the tree matures. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Buchnera phoenicea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and seasonal floral display.
