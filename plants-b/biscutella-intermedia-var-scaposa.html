Biscutella intermedia var. scaposa

Biscutella intermedia var. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering stems are usually elongated and unbranched. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
