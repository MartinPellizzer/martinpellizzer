Berberis huanucensis

Berberis huanucensis, commonly known as the Huancabamba barberry, is a shrub native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
