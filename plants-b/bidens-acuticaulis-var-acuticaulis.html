Bidens acuticaulis var. acuticaulis

Bidens acuticaulis var. acuticaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems and small, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets and a distinctive disk of tubular flowers, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
