Brunfelsia calycina var. eximia

Brunfelsia calycina var. eximia, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and glossy, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to warm, humid climates.
