Bothriochloa saccharoides var. parvispicula

Bothriochloa saccharoides var. parvispicula, commonly known as dwarf sugar grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is of interest for its potential use in sustainable land management and as a forage plant.
