Bignonia fockeana

Bignonia fockeana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, orange-yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, due to its invasive potential, it is often restricted in certain areas.
