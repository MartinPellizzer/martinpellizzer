Biscutella tumidula

Biscutella tumidula, commonly known as the inflated biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.
