Brunsvigia rosea var. pallida

Brunsvigia rosea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale firecracker lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with clusters of pale pink to white, trumpet-shaped flowers that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, showy inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often overlooked due to the plant's dramatic floral display.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
