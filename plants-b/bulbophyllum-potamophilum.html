Bulbophyllum potamophilum

Bulbophyllum potamophilum, commonly known as the river stream orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to yellow, with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
