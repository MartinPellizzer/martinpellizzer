Boesenbergia kenali

Boesenbergia kenali, commonly known as the Kenali ginger or yellow ginger, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the plant is valued for its aromatic properties.

Boesenbergia kenali is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking floral display.
