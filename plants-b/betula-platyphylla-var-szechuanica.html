Betula platyphylla var. szechuanica

Betula platyphylla var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan birch, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, pendulous branches. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
