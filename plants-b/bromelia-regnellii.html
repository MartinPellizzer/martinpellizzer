Bromelia regnellii

Bromelia regnellii, commonly known as the red bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small animals. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Bromelia regnellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
