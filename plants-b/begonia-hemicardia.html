Begonia hemicardia

Begonia hemicardia, commonly known as the heartleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
