Baccharis chilquilla

Baccharis chilquilla, commonly known as Chilean feverfew, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
