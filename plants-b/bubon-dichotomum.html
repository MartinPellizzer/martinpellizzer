Bubon dichotomum

Bubon dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-branched stem and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has small, narrow leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Bubon dichotomum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
