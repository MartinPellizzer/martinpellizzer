Betula papyrifera var. montanensis

Betula papyrifera var. montanensis, commonly known as the mountain paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pinkish inner layer, which gives it its common name. The tree typically grows in cold, high-altitude environments, often in coniferous forests or open areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are triangular with doubly serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is adapted to harsh climates and plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
