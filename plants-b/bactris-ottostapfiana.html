Bactris ottostapfiana

Bactris ottostapfiana, commonly known as the red-fruit palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
