Baccharis monantha

Baccharis monantha, commonly known as the Brazilian erythroxylum, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
