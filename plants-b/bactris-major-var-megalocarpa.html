Bactris major var. megalocarpa

Bactris major var. megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and horticulture.
