Bassia prostrata subsp. grisea

Bassia prostrata subsp.

grisea, commonly known as gray sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish or whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
