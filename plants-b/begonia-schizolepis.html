Begonia schizolepis

Begonia schizolepis, commonly known as the Split-leaf Begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, coming in shades of red, pink, or white.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
