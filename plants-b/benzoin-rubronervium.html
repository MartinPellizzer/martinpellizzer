Benzoin rubronervium

Benzoin rubronervium, commonly known as the red-veined benzoin, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on its broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its sap, which is viscous and aromatic, is harvested for use in perfumes and traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Benzoin rubronervium make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
