Bulbophyllum leptocaulon

Bulbophyllum leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its roots are typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants rather than in soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
