Baliospermum calycinum var. calycinum

Baliospermum calycinum var. calycinum, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces a milky sap that is a distinctive feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
