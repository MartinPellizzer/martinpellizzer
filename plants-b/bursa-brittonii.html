Bursa brittonii

Bursa brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's burr, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose its small, yellowish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Bursa brittonii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
