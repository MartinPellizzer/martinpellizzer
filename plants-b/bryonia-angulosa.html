Bryonia angulosa

Bryonia angulosa, commonly known as the angular bryony, is a perennial climbing plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and squashes, and is characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. However, due to its toxic nature, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
