Bombax longipedicellatum

Bombax longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed cotton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
