Bondtia forrestii

Bondtia forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's bondtia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a complex inflorescence with numerous fragrant, lip-shaped petals that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Bondtia forrestii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
