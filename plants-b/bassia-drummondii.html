Bassia drummondii

Bassia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-covered stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry climates and recover quickly after droughts.
