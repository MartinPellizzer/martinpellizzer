Briza triloba f. violacea

Briza triloba f. violacea, commonly known as the violet false oatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, arching stems and small, violet-blue florets that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for wetland or garden environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue, adding visual interest to the landscape.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
