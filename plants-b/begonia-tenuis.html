Begonia tenuis

Begonia tenuis, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
