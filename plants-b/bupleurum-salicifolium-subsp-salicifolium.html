Bupleurum salicifolium subsp. salicifolium

Bupleurum salicifolium subsp.

salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
