Baikiaea zenkeri

Baikiaea zenkeri, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber, which is used in furniture and construction.

Baikiaea zenkeri is also important in traditional medicine and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
