Bactris oraria

Bactris oraria, commonly known as the purple-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow flowers, which are followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Bactris oraria is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
