Blechnum vulcanicum var. feani

Blechnum vulcanicum var. feani, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Blechnum genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant is a fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
