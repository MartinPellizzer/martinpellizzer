Beureria

Beureria, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique genus of flowering plants native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in swampy and marshy environments, often forming dense mats on the water's surface. Beureria species are typically herbaceous and have small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its specialized habitat, it plays an important role in the ecological balance of its wetland ecosystems.
