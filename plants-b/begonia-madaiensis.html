Begonia madaiensis

Begonia madaiensis, commonly known as the Madai begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms, which make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
