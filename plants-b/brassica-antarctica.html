Brassica antarctica

Brassica antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic mustard, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Antarctica and subantarctic islands.

It is a hardy species that thrives in extreme environments, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compact, evergreen foliage. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known crops like cabbage and mustard. It has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological role is significant in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
