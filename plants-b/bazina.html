Bazina

Bazina, commonly known as wild carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and an umbel of small white flowers. The plant produces a taproot that is often harvested for its aromatic, edible qualities. Bazina thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields and along roadsides.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
