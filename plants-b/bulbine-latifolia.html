Bulbine latifolia

Bulbine latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
