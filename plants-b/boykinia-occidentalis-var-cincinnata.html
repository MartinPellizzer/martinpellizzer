Boykinia occidentalis var. cincinnata

Boykinia occidentalis var. cincinnata, commonly known as the western rockfoam, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
