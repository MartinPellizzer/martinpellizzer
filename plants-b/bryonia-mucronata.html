Bryonia mucronata

Bryonia mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bryony, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with hairy stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
