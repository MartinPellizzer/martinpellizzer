Blechnum spinulosum

Blechnum spinulosum, commonly known as the spinulose holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Pacific, including Hawaii, New Zealand, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, spinulose (spiny) hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky areas, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and the plant reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Blechnum spinulosum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
