Brunfelsia Hydrangeiformis

Brunfelsia hydrangeiformis is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is known for its large, showy flowers that resemble hydrangeas, hence its common name. This species belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, though it is not toxic. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts. Due to habitat loss, it faces threats to its survival in the wild.
