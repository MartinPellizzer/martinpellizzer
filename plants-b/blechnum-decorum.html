Blechnum decorum

Blechnum decorum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are composed of small, narrow pinnae that are arranged in a zigzag pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Blechnum decorum is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
