Bulbophyllum bisepalum

Bulbophyllum bisepalum, commonly known as the two-sepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive inflorescence with small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Bulbophyllum bisepalum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
