Bacopa hirsuta

Bacopa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy water bead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in wetland gardens.

Bacopa hirsuta is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.
