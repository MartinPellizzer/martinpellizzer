Bromopsis lacmonica

Bromopsis lacmonica, commonly known as the lacmonica grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. This perennial herb is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically greenish or pale in color.

Bromopsis lacmonica is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
