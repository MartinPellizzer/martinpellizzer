Bupleurum linearifolium var. bracteatum

Bupleurum linearifolium var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate silvery bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate, often silvery-haired leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
