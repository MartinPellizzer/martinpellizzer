Begonia johnstonii f. pilosa

Begonia johnstonii f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage.

Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and come in shades of red or orange, adding visual interest.

This variant is prized for its unique foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
