Berberis caroliniana

Berberis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitat.
