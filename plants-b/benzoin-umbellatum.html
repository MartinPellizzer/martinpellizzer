Benzoin umbellatum

Benzoin umbellatum, commonly known as the gum benjoin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is harvested for its medicinal and perfumery uses. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, giving it its scientific name.

The resin, known as benjoin, has a sweet, balsamic scent and is used in traditional medicine and incense.
