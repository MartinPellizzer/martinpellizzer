Basanacantha spinosa f. grandiflora

Basanacantha spinosa f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered spiny milkweed, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae). Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
