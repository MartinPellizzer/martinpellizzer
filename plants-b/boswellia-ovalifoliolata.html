Boswellia ovalifoliolata

Boswellia ovalifoliolata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a species of the genus Boswellia native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces aromatic resin, which is harvested for use in incense, medicine, and traditional remedies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky soils and coastal regions.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
