Biota stricta

Biota stricta, commonly known as the rigid biota, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, stiff stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
