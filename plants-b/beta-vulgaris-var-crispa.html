Beta vulgaris var. crispa

Beta vulgaris var. crispa, commonly known as the curly leaf beetroot, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its crinkled, wavy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Europe. It is typically grown for its ornamental value rather than its edible roots.

The leaves are often used in gardens for their visual appeal and can be harvested for salads or cooking.
