Brasilaelia purpurata

Brasilaelia purpurata, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes due to its bold, showy display.
