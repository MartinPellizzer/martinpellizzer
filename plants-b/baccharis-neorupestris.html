Baccharis neorupestris

Baccharis neorupestris, commonly known as the rock baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and resilience make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
