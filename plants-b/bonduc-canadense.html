Bonduc canadense

Bonduc canadense, commonly known as Canadian bonduc, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits called drupes, which are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
