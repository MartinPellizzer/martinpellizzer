Badianifera griffithii

Badianifera griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or saffron tree, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical classification places it within the family Rutaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like citrus.
