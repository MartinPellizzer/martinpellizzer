Bumelia lacuum

Bumelia lacuum, commonly known as the swamp bumelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slight pubescence.

Bumelia lacuum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
