Bipinnula montana

Bipinnula montana, commonly known as the mountain bipinnula, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, yellowish to white flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Bipinnula montana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
