Brexiella cymosa

Brexiella cymosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Brexiella cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
