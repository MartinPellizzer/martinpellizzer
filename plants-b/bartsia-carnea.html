Bartsia carnea

Bartsia carnea, commonly known as red bartsia, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Bartsia carnea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
