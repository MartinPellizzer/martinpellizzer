Boottia brachyphylla

Boottia brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved boottia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
