Bergenia gorbunowii

Bergenia gorbunowii, commonly known as the Siberian begonia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and rosettes of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Bergenia gorbunowii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
