Balsamina hortensis

Balsamina hortensis, commonly known as the garden balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Balsamina hortensis is cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
