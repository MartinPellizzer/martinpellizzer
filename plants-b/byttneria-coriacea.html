Byttneria coriacea

Byttneria coriacea, commonly known as African satinwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
