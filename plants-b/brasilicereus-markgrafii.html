Brasilicereus markgrafii

Brasilicereus markgrafii, commonly known as the Markgraf's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
