Bombax buonopozense subsp. buonopozense

Bombax buonopozense subsp.

buonopozense, commonly known as the Puerto Rican silk cotton tree, is a tropical tree native to the island of Puerto Rico. It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule.

The plant has a tall, straight trunk with smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves that are alternate on the branches. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
