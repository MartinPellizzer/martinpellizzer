Baumea iridifolia subsp. laevinux

Baumea iridifolia subsp.

laevinux, commonly known as the silver tufted grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand. It features dense, tufted clusters of narrow, silver-blue leaves that form a compact rosette.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in coastal landscapes.
