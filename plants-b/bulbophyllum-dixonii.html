Bulbophyllum dixonii

Bulbophyllum dixonii, commonly known as the Dixon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
