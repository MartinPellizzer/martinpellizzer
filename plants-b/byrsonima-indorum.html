Byrsonima indorum

Byrsonima indorum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits that resemble small cherries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
