Baccharis mattogrossensis

Baccharis mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
