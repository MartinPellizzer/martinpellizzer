Bocoa ratteri

Bocoa ratteri, commonly known as the golden bocoa, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and velvety green foliage. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Bocoa ratteri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
