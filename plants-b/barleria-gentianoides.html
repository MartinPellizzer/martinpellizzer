Barleria gentianoides

Barleria gentianoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
