Barleria comorensis

Barleria comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian barleria, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Barleria comorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
