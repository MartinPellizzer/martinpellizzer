Blechum anisophyllum

Blechum anisophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed blechum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, blooming in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.
