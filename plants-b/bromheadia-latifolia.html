Bromheadia latifolia

Bromheadia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bromhead, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Bromheadia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruit structure.
