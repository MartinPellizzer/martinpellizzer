Berberis pindilicensis

Berberis pindilicensis, commonly known as the Pindilensis barberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
