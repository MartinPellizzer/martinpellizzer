Barringtonia augusta

Barringtonia augusta, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-like flowers that hang from the branches, which are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces large, fibrous fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Barringtonia augusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
