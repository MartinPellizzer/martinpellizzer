Blumea sonchifolia

Blumea sonchifolia, commonly known as the common blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by pale blue or white bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
