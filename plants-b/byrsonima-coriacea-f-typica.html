Byrsonima coriacea f. typica

Byrsonima coriacea f. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian plum or jambul, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon region.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, purple or black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and local ecosystems.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
