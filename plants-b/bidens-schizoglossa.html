Bidens schizoglossa

Bidens schizoglossa, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals and prominent white veins, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. Its fruit is a cluster of tiny, dry seeds enclosed in a pithy, spiny structure.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
