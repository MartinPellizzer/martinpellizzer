Betula × koehnei

Betula × koehnei, commonly known as the white poplar hybrid, is a deciduous tree that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Betula pendula and Betula platyphylla.

It is known for its smooth, white bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Betula × koehnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
