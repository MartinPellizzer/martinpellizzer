Bauhinia schlechtendaliana

Bauhinia schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its woody pods contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
