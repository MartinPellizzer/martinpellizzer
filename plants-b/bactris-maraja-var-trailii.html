Bactris maraja var. trailii

Bactris maraja var. trailii, commonly known as the Marajoa palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for cultivation in warm climates.
