Begonia argyrocoelis

Begonia argyrocoelis, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with distinctive silver veins that contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation or shaded garden settings.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
