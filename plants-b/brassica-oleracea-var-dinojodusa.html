Brassica oleracea var. dinojodusa

Brassica oleracea var. dinojodusa, commonly known as the Dinojodusa cabbage, is a rare and genetically modified variant of the cabbage species.

It exhibits unique leaf morphology, with a deep green, waxy surface and a compact, spherical shape. The plant is characterized by its high concentration of glucosinolates, which contribute to its distinct flavor and potential health benefits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its genetic modifications, it is primarily cultivated for experimental agricultural research.
