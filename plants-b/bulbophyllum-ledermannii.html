Bulbophyllum ledermannii

Bulbophyllum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with fleshy, often dark-colored pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

Bulbophyllum ledermannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
