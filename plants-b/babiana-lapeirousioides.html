Babiana lapeirousioides

Babiana lapeirousioides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It produces tall, slender stems adorned with showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Babiana lapeirousioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
