Belangera speciosa

Belangera speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Belangera speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
