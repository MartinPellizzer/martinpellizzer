Bursera laxiflora subsp. filicifolia

Bursera laxiflora subsp.

filicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into narrow, feather-like segments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

This species is valued for its aromatic resins and its role in traditional medicine and incense production.
