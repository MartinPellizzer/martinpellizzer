Brassica rutabaga

Brassica rutabaga, commonly known as the rutabaga, is a root vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family, which also includes cabbage and broccoli.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-to-orange colored root, which is typically harvested when it reaches a diameter of about 5 to 7 centimeters. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Rutabagas are adapted to cooler climates and thrive in well-drained soils.

They are rich in nutrients such as vitamin C, fiber, and potassium, making them a valuable addition to a healthy diet.
