Bathiorhamnus louvelii var. reticulatus

Bathiorhamnus louvelii var. reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate bathiorhamnus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaf venation pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive reticulate network. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a yellowish-green color, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and morphological uniqueness.
