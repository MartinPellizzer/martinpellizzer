Bidens leptomeria

Bidens leptomeria, commonly known as the slender false beggar's ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces tiny, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bidens leptomeria is adapted to thrive in sunny, dry environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
