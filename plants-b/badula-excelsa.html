Badula excelsa

Badula excelsa, commonly known as the tall fern tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it ecologically significant.

The plant's unique structure and growth habit make it a notable species in botanical studies.
