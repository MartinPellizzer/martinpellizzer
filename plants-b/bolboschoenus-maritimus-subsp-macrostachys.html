Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp. macrostachys

Bolboschoenus maritimus subsp.

macrostachys, commonly known as salt marsh bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are robust and help it thrive in saline and wetland environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
