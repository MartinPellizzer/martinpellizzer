Bursera verapacensis

Bursera verapacensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing resinous sap. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, followed by woody, fragrant fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
