Blumea purpurea

Blumea purpurea, commonly known as purple blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple bracts that give it its common name.

Blumea purpurea is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
