Bidens meruensis

Bidens meruensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its yellow flowers are composed of ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens meruensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
