Byrsonima verbascifolia var. denudata

Byrsonima verbascifolia var. denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf mallow, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
