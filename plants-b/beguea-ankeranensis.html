Beguea ankeranensis

Beguea ankeranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ash tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits containing a single seed. Native to Madagascar, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
