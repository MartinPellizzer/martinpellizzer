Berberis coriaria

Berberis coriaria, commonly known as the European barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
