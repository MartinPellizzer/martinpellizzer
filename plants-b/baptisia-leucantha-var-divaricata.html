Baptisia leucantha var. divaricata

Baptisia leucantha var. divaricata, commonly known as the white false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower spikes that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound leaves with slender, elongated leaflets, and its root system is extensive, making it adaptable to various soil types. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant once established, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
