Boehmeria grandifolia

Boehmeria grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface that can cause skin irritation upon contact. Boehmeria grandifolia typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and for making textiles in some cultures.
