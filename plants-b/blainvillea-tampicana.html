Blainvillea tampicana

Blainvillea tampicana, commonly known as the Tampico Blainvillea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

Blainvillea tampicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
