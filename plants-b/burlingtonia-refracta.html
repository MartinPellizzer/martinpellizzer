Burlingtonia refracta

Burlingtonia refracta, commonly known as the glassy leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that often appear translucent or glassy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for greenhouse and indoor gardening.
