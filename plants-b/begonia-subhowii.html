Begonia subhowii

Begonia subhowii, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
