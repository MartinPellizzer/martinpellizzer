Begonia qingchengshanensis

Begonia qingchengshanensis, commonly known as the Qingchengshan begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to Sichuan Province in China.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a vibrant, showy inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within mountainous regions, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
