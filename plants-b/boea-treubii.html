Boea treubii

Boea treubii, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually a striking silver-gray color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Boea treubii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor growing conditions.
