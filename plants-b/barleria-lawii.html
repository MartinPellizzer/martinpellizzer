Barleria lawii

Barleria lawii, commonly known as the velvetleaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Barleria lawii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
