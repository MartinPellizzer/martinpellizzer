Barbarea sibirica

Barbarea sibirica, commonly known as Siberian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped pods containing seeds.

Barbarea sibirica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
