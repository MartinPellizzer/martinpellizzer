Bupleurum jumceum subsp. gerardii

Bupleurum jumceum subsp.

gerardii, commonly known as Gerard's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
