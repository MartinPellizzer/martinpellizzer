Begonia keeana

Begonia keeana, commonly known as the Hawaiian begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow centers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Begonia keeana is considered a rare and endangered species.
