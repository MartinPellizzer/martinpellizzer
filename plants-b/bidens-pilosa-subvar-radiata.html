Bidens pilosa subvar. radiata

Bidens pilosa subvar. radiata, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of leaflets that are often hairy. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive bristly seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
