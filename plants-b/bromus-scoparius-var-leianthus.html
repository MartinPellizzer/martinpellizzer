Bromus scoparius var. leianthus

Bromus scoparius var. leianthus, commonly known as the Hawaiian brome, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy blades that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to reddish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
