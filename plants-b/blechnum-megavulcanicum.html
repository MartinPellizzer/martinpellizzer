Blechnum megavulcanicum

Blechnum megavulcanicum, commonly known as the giant fern of the Andes, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a feature typical of ferns.

This species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native range.
