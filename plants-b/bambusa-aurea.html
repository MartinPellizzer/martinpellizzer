Bambusa aurea

Bambusa aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a striking species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-green culms, which add a unique visual appeal to gardens and landscapes. This bamboo is a clumping type, forming dense clusters that provide structure and privacy. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Bambusa aurea is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
