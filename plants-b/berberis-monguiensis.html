Berberis monguiensis

Berberis monguiensis, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and its historical use in traditional medicine.
