Burneya gaudichaudii

Burneya gaudichaudii, commonly known as the New Zealand burnet, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features a rosette of fleshy, reddish-pink leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Burneya gaudichaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
