Brachypodium phoenicoides var. reynieri

Brachypodium phoenicoides var. reynieri, commonly known as Reynier's false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and genetic studies.
