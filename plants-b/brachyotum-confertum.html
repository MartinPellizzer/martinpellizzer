Brachyotum confertum

Brachyotum confertum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Brachyotum confertum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
