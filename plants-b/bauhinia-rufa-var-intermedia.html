Bauhinia rufa var. intermedia

Bauhinia rufa var. intermedia, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into small, feathery segments. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, and it produces woody pods containing flat, flat, smooth seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically in traditional crafts and construction.
