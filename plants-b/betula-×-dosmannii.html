Betula × dosmannii

Betula × dosmannii, commonly known as the silver birch hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is a cross between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula platyphylla (Manchurian birch), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The tree has smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and it produces small, winged seeds in catkins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Betula × dosmannii is also valued for its ability to withstand urban conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
