Bassia aellenii

Bassia aellenii, commonly known as the Aellen's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Bassia aellenii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
