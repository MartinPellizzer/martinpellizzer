Begonia dusenii

Begonia dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green leaves that often have a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
