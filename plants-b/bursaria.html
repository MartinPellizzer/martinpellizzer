Bursaria

Bursaria, commonly known as the "burr bush," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and the presence of small, spiny fruits that resemble burrs, which aid in seed dispersal. These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological roles in supporting local wildlife.
