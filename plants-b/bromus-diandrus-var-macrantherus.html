Bromus diandrus var. macrantherus

Bromus diandrus var. macrantherus, commonly known as the broad-leaved brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites. The plant has broad, flat leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
