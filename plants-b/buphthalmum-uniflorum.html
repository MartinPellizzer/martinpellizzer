Buphthalmum uniflorum

Buphthalmum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered ox-eye daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces a single large, showy yellow flower surrounded by white ray florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
