Bactris oligocarpa

Bactris oligocarpa, commonly known as the red raphia, is a palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or processed into beverages, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and for its fibrous materials.
