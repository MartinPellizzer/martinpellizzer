Baccharis subopposita

Baccharis subopposita, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
