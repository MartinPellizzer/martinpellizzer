Berlinia verdickii

Berlinia verdickii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Berlinia verdickii is adapted to dry climates and plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
