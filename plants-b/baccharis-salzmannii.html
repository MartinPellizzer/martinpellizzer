Baccharis salzmannii

Baccharis salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Baccharis salzmannii is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
