Brachyglottis bellidioides var. orbiculata

Brachyglottis bellidioides var. orbiculata, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
