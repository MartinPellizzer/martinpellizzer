Baccharis burchellii

Baccharis burchellii, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
