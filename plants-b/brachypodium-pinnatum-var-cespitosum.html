Brachypodium pinnatum var. cespitosum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. cespitosum, commonly known as tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
