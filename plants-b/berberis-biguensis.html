Berberis biguensis

Berberis biguensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It typically grows in humid, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. The plant features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal color.
