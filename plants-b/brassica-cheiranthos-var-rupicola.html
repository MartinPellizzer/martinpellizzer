Brassica cheiranthos var. rupicola

Brassica cheiranthos var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained environments. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to colonize harsh, alpine habitats.
