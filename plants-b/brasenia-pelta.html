Brasenia pelta

Brasenia pelta, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture and a distinctive white or pinkish bloom. The plant's flowers are typically showy, with a central white or pinkish color surrounded by green sepals. Brasenia pelta thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, requiring full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
