Ballota integrifolia

Ballota integrifolia, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Ballota integrifolia is also known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
