Byrsonima sericea var. typica

Byrsonima sericea var. typica, commonly known as silky soursop, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant bears fruits that are typically round or oval, with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a white, fleshy pulp that is edible and has a mild, sweet taste.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in local cuisines.
