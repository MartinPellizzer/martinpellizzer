Bignonia lindleyi

Bignonia lindleyi, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a vigorous climber with pinnately compound leaves and produces long, slender seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Bignonia lindleyi make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
