Borzicactus hempelianus var. spinosissimus

Borzicactus hempelianus var. spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny borzicactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny tubercles, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
