Breynia fruticosa

Breynia fruticosa, commonly known as the fire tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, often reddish or orange-colored leaves that can store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Breynia fruticosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its ornamental appeal.
