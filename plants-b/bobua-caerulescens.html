Bobua caerulescens

Bobua caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered bobua, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
