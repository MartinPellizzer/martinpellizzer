Bilderdykia convolvulus

Bilderdykia convolvulus, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming a weed in agricultural and garden settings.

Due to its vigorous growth and persistent nature, it can be challenging to control once established.
