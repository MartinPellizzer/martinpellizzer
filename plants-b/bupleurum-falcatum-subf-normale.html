Bupleurum falcatum subf. normale

Bupleurum falcatum subf.

normale, commonly known as the common valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is commonly found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
