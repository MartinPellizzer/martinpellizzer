Buddleja davidii var. magnifera

Buddleja davidii var. magnifera, commonly known as the magnificent butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting numerous pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
