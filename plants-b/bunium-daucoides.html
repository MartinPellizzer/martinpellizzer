Bunium daucoides

Bunium daucoides, commonly known as wild celery or goat's beard, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant contains essential oils and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
