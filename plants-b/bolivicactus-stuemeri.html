Bolivicactus stuemeri

Bolivicactus stuemeri, commonly known as the Stuemer's bolivian cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Bolivicactus stuemeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
