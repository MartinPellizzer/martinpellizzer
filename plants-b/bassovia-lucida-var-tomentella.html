Bassovia lucida var. tomentella

Bassovia lucida var. tomentella, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white or pale throat. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in a dense, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its "tomentella" variety name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
