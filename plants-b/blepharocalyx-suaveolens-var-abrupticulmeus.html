Blepharocalyx suaveolens var. abrupticulmeus

Blepharocalyx suaveolens var. abrupticulmeus, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, with a distinctive, lantern-like shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
