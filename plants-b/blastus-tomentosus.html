Blastus tomentosus

Blastus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blastus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
