Bromus setifolius var. brevifolius

Bromus setifolius var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush communities. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is an important component of native plant communities in its range.
