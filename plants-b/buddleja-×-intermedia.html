Buddleja × intermedia

Buddleja × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid weeping buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja × intermedia is often used in landscaping for its striking visual impact and relatively low maintenance requirements.
