Baterium

Baterium, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant is native to various regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Baterium species are often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

They are generally herbaceous, with some species being perennial and others annual.
