Brassicella erucastrum var. recurvata

Brassicella erucastrum var. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is native to parts of North America and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in pollination and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
