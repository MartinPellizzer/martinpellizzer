Bupleurum cappadocicum

Bupleurum cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in late spring to early summer.
