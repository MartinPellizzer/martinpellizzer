Bromelia karatas var. caulescens

Bromelia karatas var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender pineapple bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

This bromeliad is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ornamental appeal.
