Bursera multijuga

Bursera multijuga, commonly known as the Mexican birch, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that resembles the bark of birch trees, and its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Bursera multijuga is often found in dry forests and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
