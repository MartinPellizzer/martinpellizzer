Bidens sandvicensis var. caduca

Bidens sandvicensis var. caduca, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a pithy, three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils.
