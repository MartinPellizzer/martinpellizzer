Begonia tjiasmantoi

Begonia tjiasmantoi, commonly known as the Tjiasmantoi begonia, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
