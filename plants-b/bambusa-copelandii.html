Bambusa copelandii

Bambusa copelandii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its visual texture, while its culms are strong and flexible.

Bambusa copelandii is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a sustainable building material.
