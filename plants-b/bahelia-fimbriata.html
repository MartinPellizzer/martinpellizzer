Bahelia fimbriata

Bahelia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Bahelia fimbriata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
