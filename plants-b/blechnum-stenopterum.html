Blechnum stenopterum

Blechnum stenopterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds that are often divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.
