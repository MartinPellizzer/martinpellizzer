Bursera xochipalensis

Bursera xochipalensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic bark and is valued for its ornamental and possibly medicinal properties.
