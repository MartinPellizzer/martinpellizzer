Bouchea sessilifolia

Bouchea sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
