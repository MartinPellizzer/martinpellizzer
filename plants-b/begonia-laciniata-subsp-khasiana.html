Begonia laciniata subsp. khasiana

Begonia laciniata subsp.

khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern regions of India. It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that are often a deep green with contrasting veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden beds.
