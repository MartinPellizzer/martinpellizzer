Betonica orientalis

Betonica orientalis, commonly known as the greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Betonica orientalis has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.

Its flowers are also popular in herbal teas and floral arrangements.
