Bernardia axillaris var. trichoclada

Bernardia axillaris var. trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant has hairy foliage, which is a distinguishing feature of its varietal name "trichoclada.". It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
