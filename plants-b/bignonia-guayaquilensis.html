Bignonia guayaquilensis

Bignonia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces long, trailing vines with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Bignonia guayaquilensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
