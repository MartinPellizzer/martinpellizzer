Boehmeria polystachya

Boehmeria polystachya, commonly known as Chinese hemp or giant bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on long, branching inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Boehmeria polystachya is valued for its strong fibers, which have been used traditionally in papermaking and textiles.
