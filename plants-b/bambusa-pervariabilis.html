Bambusa pervariabilis

Bambusa pervariabilis, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, with a characteristic green to yellowish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly serrated. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Bambusa pervariabilis is also valued for its sustainable use in construction and papermaking.
