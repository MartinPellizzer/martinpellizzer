Breynia subterblanca

Breynia subterblanca, commonly known as the silverleaf breynia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Breynia subterblanca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
