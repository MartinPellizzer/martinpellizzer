Bulbophyllum comorianum

Bulbophyllum comorianum, commonly known as the Comorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns and are pollinated by insects.
