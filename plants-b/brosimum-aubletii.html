Brosimum aubletii

Brosimum aubletii, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a large tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk that contains a milky latex sap, which is used in the production of natural rubber. The tree has large, palmate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its branches. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and has been cultivated for its economic value.
