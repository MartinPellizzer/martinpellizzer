Brachypodium caespitosum var. collinum

Brachypodium caespitosum var. collinum, commonly known as red brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
