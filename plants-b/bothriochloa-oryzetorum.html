Bothriochloa oryzetorum

Bothriochloa oryzetorum, commonly known as ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas. Its seeds are used as a food source for livestock and wildlife.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
