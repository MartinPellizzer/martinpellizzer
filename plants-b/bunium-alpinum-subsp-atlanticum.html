Bunium alpinum subsp. atlanticum

Bunium alpinum subsp.

atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic fennel, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast. It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its seeds are small and contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
