Berberis vulgaris var. emarginata

Berberis vulgaris var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, often bearing small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically have a tart flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with medicinal properties, though it is not commonly used for culinary purposes.
