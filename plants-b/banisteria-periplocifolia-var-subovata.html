Banisteria periplocifolia var. subovata

Banisteria periplocifolia var. subovata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed banisteria, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its distinctive clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
