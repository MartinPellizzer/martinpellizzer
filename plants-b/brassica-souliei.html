Brassica souliei

Brassica souliei, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or mountain mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be either basal or alternate, depending on the growth stage.

Brassica souliei is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.
