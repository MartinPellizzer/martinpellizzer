Babiana lilacina

Babiana lilacina, commonly known as the lilac babiana, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs that store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Babiana lilacina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
