Bambusa teres

Bambusa teres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth, grass-like texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
