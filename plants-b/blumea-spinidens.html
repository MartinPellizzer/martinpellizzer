Blumea spinidens

Blumea spinidens, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Blumea spinidens is often found in moist, tropical environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
