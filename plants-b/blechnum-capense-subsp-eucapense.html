Blechnum capense subsp. eucapense

Blechnum capense subsp.

eucapense, commonly known as the Cape shield fern, is a fern species native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated edge.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
