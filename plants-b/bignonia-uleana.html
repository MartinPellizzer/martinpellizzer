Bignonia uleana

Bignonia uleana, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
