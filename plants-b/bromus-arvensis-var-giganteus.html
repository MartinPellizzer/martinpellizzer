Bromus arvensis var. giganteus

Bromus arvensis var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant downy brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spiky seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its invasive spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can outcompete native vegetation and is considered a weed in many regions.
