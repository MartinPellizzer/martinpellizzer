Burmeistera pastoralis var. glabrifolia

Burmeistera pastoralis var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved burmeistera, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy layer. It produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
