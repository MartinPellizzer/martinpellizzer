Batatas edulis var. erythrorrhiza

Batatas edulis var. erythrorrhiza, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a tuberous root crop native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish skin and flesh, which contains high levels of antioxidants and dietary fiber. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and broad, while the stems are typically hairy and upright.

This variety is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
