Bromopsis himalaica

Bromopsis himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small, clustered in panicles.

Bromopsis himalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
