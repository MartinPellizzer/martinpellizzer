Bulbophyllum simii

Bulbophyllum simii, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters and can vary in color from yellow to red, depending on the specific variety.

Bulbophyllum simii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
