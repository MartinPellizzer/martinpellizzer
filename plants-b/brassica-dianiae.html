Brassica dianiae

Brassica dianiae, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and edible flower buds that resemble small cabbage florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in cool climates. It is cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves and stems, which are used in various Asian cuisines.

Brassica dianiae is relatively easy to grow and can be harvested within 50–70 days after sowing.
