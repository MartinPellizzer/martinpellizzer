Blumea floresiana

Blumea floresiana, commonly known as the Flores chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Flores.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and resemble those of chrysanthemums, often appearing in clusters.

Blumea floresiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
