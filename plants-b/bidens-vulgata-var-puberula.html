Bidens vulgata var. puberula

Bidens vulgata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy beggar's tick, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It features alternate, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets and a disc of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its seeds are equipped with barbed awns that attach to animals or clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.
