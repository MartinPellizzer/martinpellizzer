Brassica pseudoerucastrum

Brassica pseudoerucastrum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by slender, elongated pods containing seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions, it can become invasive in some regions.
