Besleria daui

Besleria daui, commonly known as the white spider orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy white flowers with intricate patterns, often resembling spiders or butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
