Bambusa dissimulator var. hispida

Bambusa dissimulator var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that grow in dense clusters and its finely textured, narrow leaves. The plant features a unique hairy texture on its young shoots, which gives it its common name. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

This bamboo is relatively low maintenance and suitable for small garden spaces or container planting.
