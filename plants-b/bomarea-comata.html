Bomarea comata

Bomarea comata, commonly known as the silky bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, tubular flowers with hairy bracts, which give it its common name. It is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures.

Bomarea comata is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
