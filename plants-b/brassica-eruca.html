Brassica eruca

Brassica eruca, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica eruca is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
