Bauhinia pinnata

Bauhinia pinnata, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often feather-like in appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. Bauhinia pinnata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It also has cultural and medicinal significance in various traditional practices.
