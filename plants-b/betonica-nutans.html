Betonica nutans

Betonica nutans, commonly known as the nodding betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces blue or violet flowers that bloom from June to August, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Betonica nutans is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
