Braya purpurascens

Braya purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Braya purpurascens is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
