Batatas betacea

Batatas betacea, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a tuberous root plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-colored flesh and skin. The plant produces edible roots that are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and various nutrients such as iron and vitamin C. Batatas betacea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
