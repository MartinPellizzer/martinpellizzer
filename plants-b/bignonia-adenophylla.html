Bignonia adenophylla

Bignonia adenophylla, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
