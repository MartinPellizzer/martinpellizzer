Baccharis brachylaenoides

Baccharis brachylaenoides, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
