Benzoin cuspidatum

Benzoin cuspidatum, commonly known as the sacred sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by dark, round berries.

The resin of Benzoin cuspidatum has been historically valued for its medicinal properties and its role in religious ceremonies.
