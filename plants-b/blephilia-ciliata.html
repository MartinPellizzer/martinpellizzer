Blephilia ciliata

Blephilia ciliata, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Blephilia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
