Brassiopsis glumacea

Brassiopsis glumacea, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Brassiopsis glumacea is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
