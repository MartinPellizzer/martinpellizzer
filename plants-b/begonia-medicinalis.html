Begonia medicinalis

Begonia medicinalis, commonly known as the medicinal begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
