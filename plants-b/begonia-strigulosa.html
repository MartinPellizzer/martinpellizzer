Begonia strigulosa

Begonia strigulosa, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia strigulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
