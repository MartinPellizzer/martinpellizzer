Barkhausia hackelii

Barkhausia hackelii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a woody stem covered in thick, peeling bark, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Barkhausia hackelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in rock gardens and as a hedge plant.
