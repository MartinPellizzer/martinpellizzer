Bulbostylis rigida

Bulbostylis rigida, commonly known as the rigid bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
