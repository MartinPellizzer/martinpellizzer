Balsamorhiza hookeri var. hirsuta

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bitterroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its scientific name reflects its classification within the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
