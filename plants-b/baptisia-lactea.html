Baptisia lactea

Baptisia lactea, commonly known as white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces long, cylindrical seedpods that split open when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Baptisia lactea is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
