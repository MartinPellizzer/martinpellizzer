Begonia tafiensis

Begonia tafiensis, commonly known as the Tafiensis begonia, is a species native to the humid forests of West Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers shaded, moist environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and white, adding visual interest to gardens.

Begonia tafiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
