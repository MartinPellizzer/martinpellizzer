Brassaia capitellata

Brassaia capitellata, commonly known as the Cape rose or the white-flowered bracken.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
