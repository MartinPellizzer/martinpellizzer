Boea alata

Boea alata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Boea alata is also known for its ability to climb and can be used in vertical gardening.
