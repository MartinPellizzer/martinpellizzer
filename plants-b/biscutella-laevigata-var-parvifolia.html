Biscutella laevigata var. parvifolia

Biscutella laevigata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and arranged in clusters, characteristic of the family.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
