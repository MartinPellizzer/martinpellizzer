Bastardia macrophylla

Bastardia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed bastardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Bastardia macrophylla is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
