Biarum homaid

Biarum homaid, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Biarum homaid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
