Barbarea brachycarpa subsp. minor

Barbarea brachycarpa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf winter cress, is a low-growing annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, round seed pods that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.

This species is also known for its peppery flavor, making it a potential edible plant in some regions.
