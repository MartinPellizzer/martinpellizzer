Berberis sonnei

Berberis sonnei, commonly known as the Sonne's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, spiny leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis sonnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
