Brassica setulosa

Brassica setulosa, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Brassica setulosa is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
