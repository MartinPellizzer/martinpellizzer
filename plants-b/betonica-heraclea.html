Betonica heraclea

Betonica heraclea, commonly known as hedge hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Betonica heraclea is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
