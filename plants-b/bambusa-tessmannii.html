Bambusa tessmannii

Bambusa tessmannii, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast with its green leaves. The plant grows in dense stands and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It has a rapid growth rate and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

Bambusa tessmannii is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms used in construction and crafts.
