Boronia ternata var. promiscua

Boronia ternata var. promiscua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
