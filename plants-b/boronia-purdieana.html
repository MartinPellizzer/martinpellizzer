Boronia purdieana

Boronia purdieana, commonly known as the Pink Boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Boronia purdieana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
