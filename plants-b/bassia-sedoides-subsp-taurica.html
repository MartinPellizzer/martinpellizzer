Bassia sedoides subsp. taurica

Bassia sedoides subsp.

taurica, commonly known as Tauric wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to arid conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
