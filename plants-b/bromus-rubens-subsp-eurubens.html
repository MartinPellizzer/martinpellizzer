Bromus rubens subsp. eurubens

Bromus rubens subsp.

eurubens, commonly known as red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, while the flowering structures are usually pink or reddish-brown.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
