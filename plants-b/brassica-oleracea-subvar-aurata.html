Brassica oleracea subvar. aurata

Brassica oleracea subvar. aurata, commonly known as Brussels sprouts, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, leafy rosette and clusters of small, cabbage-like buds along the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year, which are followed by round, green pods containing seeds. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassica genus, including cabbage and kale.
