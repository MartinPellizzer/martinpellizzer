Bupleurum fruticosum

Bupleurum fruticosum, commonly known as wild celery or sea holly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along coastal areas.

Bupleurum fruticosum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
