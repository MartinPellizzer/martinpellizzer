Boronia clavata subsp. grandis

Boronia clavata subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and a branching structure that supports its flowering display. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
