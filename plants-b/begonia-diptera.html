Begonia diptera

Begonia diptera, commonly known as the winged begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped foliage. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of red or pink.

Begonia diptera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
