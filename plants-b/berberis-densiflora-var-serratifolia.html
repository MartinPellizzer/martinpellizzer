Berberis densiflora var. serratifolia

Berberis densiflora var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge in arid climates.
