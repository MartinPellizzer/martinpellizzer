Bromus unioloides var. coloratus

Bromus unioloides var. coloratus, commonly known as the colored brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and landscape design.
