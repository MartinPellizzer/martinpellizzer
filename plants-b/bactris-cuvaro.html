Bactris cuvaro

Bactris cuvaro, commonly known as the Cuvaro palm, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Bactris cuvaro plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
