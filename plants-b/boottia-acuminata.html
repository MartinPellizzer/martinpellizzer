Boottia acuminata

Boottia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late summer. Boottia acuminata is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
