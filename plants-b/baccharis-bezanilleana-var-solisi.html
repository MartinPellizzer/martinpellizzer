Baccharis bezanilleana var. solisi

Baccharis bezanilleana var. solisi, commonly known as the Solisi baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
