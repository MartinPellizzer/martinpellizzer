Batatas jalapa

Batatas jalapa, commonly known as Mexican sweet potato, is a tuberous root vegetable native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-purple skin and creamy, sweet flesh. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with heart-shaped leaves and trailing vines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Batatas jalapa is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamin A and dietary fiber.
