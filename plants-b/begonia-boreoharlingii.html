Begonia boreoharlingii

Begonia boreoharlingii, commonly known as the Bornean begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage is often variegated, displaying shades of green, white, and red, which enhance its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
