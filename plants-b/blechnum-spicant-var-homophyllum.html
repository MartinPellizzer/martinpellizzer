Blechnum spicant var. homophyllum

Blechnum spicant var. homophyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a variant of the common sword fern.

It is a perennial plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a fern with a central stem and radiating leaflets. The plant exhibits a unique morphological feature where the fronds are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of its fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.
