Baccharis venulosa var. occigranatensis

Baccharis venulosa var. occigranatensis, commonly known as the smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, sometimes flaky bark, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy underside. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
