Begonia consobrina

Begonia consobrina, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Begonia consobrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
