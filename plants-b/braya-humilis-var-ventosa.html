Braya humilis var. ventosa

Braya humilis var. ventosa, commonly known as the ventose bladderwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and tiny, inconspicuous flowers that often appear in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
