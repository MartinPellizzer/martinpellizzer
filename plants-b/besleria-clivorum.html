Besleria clivorum

Besleria clivorum, commonly known as the cliff orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes or cliffs, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
