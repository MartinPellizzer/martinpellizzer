Betonica purpurea

Betonica purpurea, commonly known as purple betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Purple betony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
