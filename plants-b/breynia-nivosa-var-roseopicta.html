Breynia nivosa var. roseopicta

Breynia nivosa var. roseopicta, commonly known as the pink-breynia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pink-tinged leaves that resemble a mosaic of colors. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
