Brassica oleracea subvar. incana

Brassica oleracea subvar. incana, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by round, green or purple pods containing seeds. It is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.

This subspecies is considered an ancestor of several cultivated Brassica vegetables, including cabbage and broccoli.
