Baccharis × caramavidensis

Baccharis × caramavidensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a hybrid shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence, while its inflorescences consist of dense clusters of small flowers.

Baccharis × caramavidensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
