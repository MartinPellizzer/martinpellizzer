Bromus maximus var. macrantherus

Bromus maximus var. macrantherus, commonly known as the giant brome, is a tall grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth and long, narrow leaves that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets at the tips of its stalks, which are often tinged with a reddish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain regions.
