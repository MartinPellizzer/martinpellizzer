Baccharis Caespitosa Subsp. Procumbens

Baccharis caespitosa subsp. procumbens is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of western North America. It is known for its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife. Its small, silver-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers make it a distinctive and hardy species. Often used in xeriscaping, it is a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
