Bourreria cestroides

Bourreria cestroides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
