Bambusa riparia

Bambusa riparia, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast with its green leaves. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological purposes.

The plant is also valued for its strong and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
