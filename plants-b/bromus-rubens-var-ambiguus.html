Bromus rubens var. ambiguus

Bromus rubens var. ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
