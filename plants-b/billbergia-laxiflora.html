Billbergia laxiflora

Billbergia laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered billbergia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia laxiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
