Ballota nigra var. foetida

Ballota nigra var. foetida, commonly known as the stinking broom.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It produces a distinctive, often unpleasant odor, which is why it is sometimes referred to by its common name. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and rocky environments.
