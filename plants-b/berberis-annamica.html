Berberis annamica

Berberis annamica, commonly known as the Annam prickly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Berberis annamica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
