Baikiaea robynsii

Baikiaea robynsii, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to termites. The tree has a spreading canopy with elliptical, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in panicles, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Baikiaea robynsii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
