Bermudiana bermudiana f. graminoides

Bermudiana bermudiana f. graminoides, commonly known as the grassy form of the Bermuda grass, is a variant of the species characterized by its more herbaceous growth habit.

It exhibits a fine, dense turf structure with narrow, grass-like leaves, making it suitable for lawn and erosion control applications. The plant typically grows in warm climates and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is a clump-forming perennial that spreads through stolons, forming a dense mat over time.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a subtropical to temperate climate.
