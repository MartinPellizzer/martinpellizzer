Byttneria laevigata

Byttneria laevigata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in horticulture.
