Baccharis juncea

Baccharis juncea, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soils. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture.

Baccharis juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
