Banisteria apiculata

Banisteria apiculata, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. Its stems are woody and often have a climbing habit, allowing it to grow along trees or structures.

Banisteria apiculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
