Bassovia stellata

Bassovia stellata, commonly known as the star-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and bell-like, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, Bassovia stellata is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
