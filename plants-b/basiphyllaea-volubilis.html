Basiphyllaea volubilis

Basiphyllaea volubilis, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Basiphyllaea volubilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
