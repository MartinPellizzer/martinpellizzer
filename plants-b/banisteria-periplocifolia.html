Banisteria periplocifolia

Banisteria periplocifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, elliptical fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

Banisteria periplocifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
