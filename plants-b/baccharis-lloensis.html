Baccharis lloensis

Baccharis lloensis, commonly known as the Lloensis baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Baccharis lloensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
