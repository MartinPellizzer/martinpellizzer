Begonia salicifolia

Begonia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
