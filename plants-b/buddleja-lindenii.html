Buddleja lindenii

Buddleja lindenii, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically pink or purple in color and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
