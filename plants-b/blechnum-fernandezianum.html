Blechnum fernandezianum

Blechnum fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandezia fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that are often used in horticulture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The fern's adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
