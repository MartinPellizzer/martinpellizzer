Baccharis schultzii

Baccharis schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, often thriving in coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flower heads make it an attractive addition to gardens, while its adaptability to various soil types enhances its ecological value.
