Blyttia arabica

Blyttia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian blyttia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Blyttia arabica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
