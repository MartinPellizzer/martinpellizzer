Bryonia quinquefolia

Bryonia quinquefolia, commonly known as poisonwood, is a toxic plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often divided into five parts, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are highly poisonous to humans and animals. It thrives in woodland habitats across eastern North America.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
