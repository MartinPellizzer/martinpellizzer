Bassia oblongifolia

Bassia oblongifolia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Bassia oblongifolia is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
