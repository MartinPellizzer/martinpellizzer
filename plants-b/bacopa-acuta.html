Bacopa acuta

Bacopa acuta, commonly known as water thread, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow waters and has slender, ribbon-like leaves that help it float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It is often used in water gardens and as a decorative element in ponds.

Bacopa acuta is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
