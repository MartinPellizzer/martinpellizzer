Bulbophyllum khasyanum

Bulbophyllum khasyanum, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
