Baikiaea eminii

Baikiaea eminii, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to pests. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
