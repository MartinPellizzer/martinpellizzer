Begonia colliculata

Begonia colliculata, commonly known as the peacock begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage with intricate patterns resembling peacock feathers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia colliculata is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
