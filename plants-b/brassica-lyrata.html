Brassica lyrata

Brassica lyrata, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Brassica lyrata thrives in alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.
