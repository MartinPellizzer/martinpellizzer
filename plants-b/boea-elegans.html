Boea elegans

Boea elegans, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pink, which bloom in the summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine.

Boea elegans thrives in humid, shady environments and is popular as a ornamental plant.
