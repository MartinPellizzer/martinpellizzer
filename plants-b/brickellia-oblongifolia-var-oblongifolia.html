Brickellia oblongifolia var. oblongifolia

Brickellia oblongifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age, and its foliage is often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
