Bulbophyllum clarkeanum

Bulbophyllum clarkeanum, commonly known as the Clarke's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The orchid's intricate floral structure and vibrant hues make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
