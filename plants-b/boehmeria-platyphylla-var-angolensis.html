Boehmeria platyphylla var. angolensis

Boehmeria platyphylla var. angolensis, commonly known as African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fibers of its stems are used in traditional crafts and have potential for natural fiber applications.
