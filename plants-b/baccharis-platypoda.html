Baccharis platypoda

Baccharis platypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Baccharis platypoda plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
