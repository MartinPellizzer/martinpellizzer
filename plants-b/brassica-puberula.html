Brassica puberula

Brassica puberula, commonly known as the woolly cabbage, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. Brassica puberula typically grows in moist, temperate climates and can be found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
