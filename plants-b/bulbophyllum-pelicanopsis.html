Bulbophyllum pelicanopsis

Bulbophyllum pelicanopsis, commonly known as the Pelican's Mask Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that resemble a pelican's beak, hence its common name. It typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid tropical environments.

The orchid's botanical features include long, slender pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves.
