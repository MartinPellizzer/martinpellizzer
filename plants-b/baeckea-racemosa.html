Baeckea racemosa

Baeckea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, leathery leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsules as its fruit type.

Baeckea racemosa is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
