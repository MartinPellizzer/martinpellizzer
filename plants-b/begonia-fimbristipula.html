Begonia fimbristipula

Begonia fimbristipula, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Begonia fimbristipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in horticulture for its unique foliage.
