Brachychiton diversifolius subsp. orientalis

Brachychiton diversifolius subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, papery, flame-like flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and vary in shape, often being elliptical or ovate. The tree produces distinctive, round, woody fruits that split open to release its seeds.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
