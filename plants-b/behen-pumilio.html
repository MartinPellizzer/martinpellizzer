Behen pumilio

Behen pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf Indian bean, is a small leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical name reflects its historical association with traditional medicinal practices in South Asia.
