Bellis perennis var. microcephala

Bellis perennis var. microcephala, commonly known as the Shiny Shieldflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring. The plant has a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
