Bauhinia marowijnensis

Bauhinia marowijnensis, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences. The plant produces papery, triangular leaves that turn yellow before falling off during the dry season. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
