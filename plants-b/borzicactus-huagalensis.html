Borzicactus huagalensis

Borzicactus huagalensis, commonly known as the Huagalensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem with a ribbed structure that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its adaptations to harsh environments include spines for protection and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Borzicactus huagalensis is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to desert climates.
