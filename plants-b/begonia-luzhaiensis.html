Begonia luzhaiensis

Begonia luzhaiensis, commonly known as the Luzhai begonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that often display intricate patterns and a striking red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
