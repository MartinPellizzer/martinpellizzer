Barrettia umbrosa

Barrettia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Barrettia umbrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant collections.
