Broussonetia greveana

Broussonetia greveana, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Broussonetia greveana thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
