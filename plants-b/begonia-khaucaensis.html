Begonia khaucaensis

Begonia khaucaensis, commonly known as the Khauca begonia, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, Begonia khaucaensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
