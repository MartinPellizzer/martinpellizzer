Berberis hookeri subsp. hookeri

Berberis hookeri subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
