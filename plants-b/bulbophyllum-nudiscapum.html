Bulbophyllum nudiscapum

Bulbophyllum nudiscapum, commonly known as the naked-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence and lack of prominent floral bracts.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
