Brevipodium flexum

Brevipodium flexum, commonly known as the flexible dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its habitat.

Brevipodium flexum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
