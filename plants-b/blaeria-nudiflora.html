Blaeria nudiflora

Blaeria nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower blazer, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive lack of leaves, with its flowers emerging directly from the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.

Blaeria nudiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique appearance.
