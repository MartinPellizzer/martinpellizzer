Bonaga columnae

Bonaga columnae, commonly known as the columnar bonaga, is a unique species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation for minimizing water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant's ability to store water in its thick stems makes it well-suited for survival in dry climates.
