Begonia tindan

Begonia tindan, commonly known as the Tindan begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
