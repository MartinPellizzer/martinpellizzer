Bomarea gloriosa

Bomarea gloriosa, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features showy, pendulous flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Bomarea gloriosa is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in shaded, humid environments.
