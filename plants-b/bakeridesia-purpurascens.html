Bakeridesia purpurascens

Bakeridesia purpurascens, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vivid, veined leaves and showy flowers.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to deep red, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.

Bakeridesia purpurascens is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
