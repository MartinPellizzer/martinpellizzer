Bupleurum fruticosum var. insulare

Bupleurum fruticosum var. insulare, commonly known as the island bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, branched stems with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
