Bomarea praeusta

Bomarea praeusta, commonly known as the red-flowered bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that are often accompanied by a showy spathe. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its unique floral structure and vivid coloration make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservatories.

Due to habitat loss, Bomarea praeusta is considered endangered in its native range, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
