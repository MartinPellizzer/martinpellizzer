Bupleurum hazaricum

Bupleurum hazaricum, commonly known as the Hazarica bitterroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is noted for its distinctive yellowish-green flowers and its role in local ecosystems.
