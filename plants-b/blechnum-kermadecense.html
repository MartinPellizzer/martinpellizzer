Blechnum kermadecense

Blechnum kermadecense, commonly known as the Kermadec maidenhair fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is a member of the Blechnaceae family, characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with upright, slender stems that produce small, lance-shaped leaves. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
