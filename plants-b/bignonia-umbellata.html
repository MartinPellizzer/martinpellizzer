Bignonia umbellata

Bignonia umbellata, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, yellow, funnel-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
