Balsamiflua bonnetiana

Balsamiflua bonnetiana, commonly known as the Bonnet's balsam, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that release a resinous scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems and evergreen foliage make it a distinctive member of its ecosystem.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
