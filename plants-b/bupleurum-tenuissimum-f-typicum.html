Bupleurum tenuissimum f. typicum

Bupleurum tenuissimum f. typicum, commonly known as narrowleaf cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically divided into three parts, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
