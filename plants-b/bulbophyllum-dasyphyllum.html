Bulbophyllum dasyphyllum

Bulbophyllum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the spiky orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
