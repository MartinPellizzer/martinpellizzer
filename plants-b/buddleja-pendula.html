Buddleja pendula

Buddleja pendula, commonly known as weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces tubular, pink to purple flowers that attract butterflies and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja pendula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
