Brassavola stricta

Brassavola stricta, commonly known as the rigid lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its long, slender inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in pine and hardwood forests. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its fragrant, vanilla-like scent.

Brassavola stricta is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-drained soil and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
