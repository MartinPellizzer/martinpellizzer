Bulbophyllum depressum

Bulbophyllum depressum, commonly known as the depressed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often reddish roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is known for its ability to store water in its pseudobulbs, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
