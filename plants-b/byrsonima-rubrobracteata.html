Byrsonima rubrobracteata

Byrsonima rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red-bract byrsonima, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include distinctive red bracts that surround the flowers, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary growth areas.
