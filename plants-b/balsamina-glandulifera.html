Balsamina glandulifera

Balsamina glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically red or purple with yellow markings, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Balsamina glandulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
