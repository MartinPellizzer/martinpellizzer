Berberis kawakamii

Berberis kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami barberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
