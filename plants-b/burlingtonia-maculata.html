Burlingtonia maculata

Burlingtonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
