Bauhinia crudiantha

Bauhinia crudiantha, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bean-like pods and palmate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Bauhinia crudiantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
