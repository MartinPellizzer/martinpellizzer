Balsamocitrus paniculata

Balsamocitrus paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like fruits. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.
