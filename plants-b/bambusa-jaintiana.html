Bambusa jaintiana

Bambusa jaintiana, commonly known as the Jaintia bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a smooth, light green to yellowish-green exterior. The leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curved, contributing to the plant's graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in construction and handicrafts in its native regions.
