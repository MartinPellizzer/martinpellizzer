Bulbophyllum amanicum

Bulbophyllum amanicum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, golden-yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
