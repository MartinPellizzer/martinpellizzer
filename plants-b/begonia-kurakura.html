Begonia kurakura

Begonia kurakura, commonly known as the Kurakura begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purplish hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
