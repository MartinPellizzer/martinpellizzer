Berberis petiolaris

Berberis petiolaris, commonly known as the creeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

Berberis petiolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
