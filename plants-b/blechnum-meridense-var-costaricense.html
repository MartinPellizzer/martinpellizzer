Blechnum meridense var. costaricense

Blechnum meridense var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican holly fern, is a species of fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, serrated margin.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage.
