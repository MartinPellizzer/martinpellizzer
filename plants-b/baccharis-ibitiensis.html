Baccharis ibitiensis

Baccharis ibitiensis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forest understories.

Baccharis ibitiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
