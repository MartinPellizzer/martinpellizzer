Baccharis opuntioides

Baccharis opuntioides, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless, fleshy stems and clustered, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Baccharis opuntioides is also known for its medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
