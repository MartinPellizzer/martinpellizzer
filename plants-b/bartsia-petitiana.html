Bartsia petitiana

Bartsia petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf bartsia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often in areas with rich, well-drained soils. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Bartsia petitiana is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
