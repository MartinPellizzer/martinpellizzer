Brachypodium obtusifolium

Brachypodium obtusifolium, commonly known as bluntleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and broad, blunt-tipped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Brachypodium obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting insect and bird life.
