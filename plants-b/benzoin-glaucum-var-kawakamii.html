Benzoin glaucum var. kawakamii

Benzoin glaucum var. kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakamii benzoin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that secrete a resinous substance, which is the source of its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within subtropical forests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in inflorescences, while its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its unique fragrance and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
