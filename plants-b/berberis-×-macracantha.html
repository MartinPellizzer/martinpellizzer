Berberis × macracantha

Berberis × macracantha, commonly known as the variegated barberry, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a range of botanical features.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that often display variegation. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are usually dark green with white or yellow margins, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
