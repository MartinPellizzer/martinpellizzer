Benthamantha fruticosa

Benthamantha fruticosa, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub-like species with small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent characteristics to store water. It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Benthamantha fruticosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
