Barkhausia apargioides

Barkhausia apargioides, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a single, showy orchid flower that typically blooms in spring. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, often fragrant flower that can be yellow, orange, or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in both gardens and conservation areas.
