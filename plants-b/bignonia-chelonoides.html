Bignonia chelonoides

Bignonia chelonoides, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia chelonoides is also known for its ability to climb using its twining stems, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
