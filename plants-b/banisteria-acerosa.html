Banisteria acerosa

Banisteria acerosa, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Banisteria acerosa is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
