Baptisia alba var. alba

Baptisia alba var. alba, commonly known as white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and often have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
