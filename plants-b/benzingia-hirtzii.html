Benzingia hirtzii

Benzingia hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy benzingia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as sandy soils and pine flatwoods. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white in color and bloom in the spring.

Benzingia hirtzii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
