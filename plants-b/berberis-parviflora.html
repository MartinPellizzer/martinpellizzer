Berberis parviflora

Berberis parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a dark green upper surface and a pale underside, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Berberis parviflora is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and harsh climates.
