Bupleurum brevicaule

Bupleurum brevicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Bupleurum brevicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
