Berberis densifolia

Berberis densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, often with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
