Blysmus rufus

Blysmus rufus, commonly known as the red hair grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and reddish-brown inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Blysmus rufus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
