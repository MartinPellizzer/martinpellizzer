Bacopa parviflora

Bacopa parviflora, commonly known as the lesser water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage.

Bacopa parviflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive and calming effects.
