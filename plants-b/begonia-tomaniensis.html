Begonia tomaniensis

Begonia tomaniensis, commonly known as the Tomanensis begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green color with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
