Bursera denticulata

Bursera denticulata, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing resin. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves with finely toothed edges. It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Bursera denticulata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
