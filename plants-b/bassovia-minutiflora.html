Bassovia minutiflora

Bassovia minutiflora, commonly known as the tiny-flower bassovia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
