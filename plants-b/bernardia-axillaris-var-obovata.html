Bernardia axillaris var. obovata

Bernardia axillaris var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with obovate-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
