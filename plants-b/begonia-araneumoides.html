Begonia araneumoides

Begonia araneumoides, commonly known as the spider begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pink flowers clustered on long, thin stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia araneumoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique and intricate floral display.
