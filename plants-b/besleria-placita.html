Besleria placita

Besleria placita, commonly known as the white-faced gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Besleria placita requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
