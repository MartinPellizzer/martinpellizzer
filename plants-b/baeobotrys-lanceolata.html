Baeobotrys lanceolata

Baeobotrys lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate baeobotrys, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baeobotrys lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
