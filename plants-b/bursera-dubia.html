Bursera dubia

Bursera dubia, commonly known as the Mexican balsam or wild myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Bursera dubia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
