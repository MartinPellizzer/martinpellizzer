Botrys pagana

Botrys pagana, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms in early summer and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Botrys pagana is also known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
