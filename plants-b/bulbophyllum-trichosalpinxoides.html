Bulbophyllum trichosalpinxoides

Bulbophyllum trichosalpinxoides, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, dense rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting it to its tropical environment. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern of lines and dots. It is an epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
