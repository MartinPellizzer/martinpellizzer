Borassodendron machadonis

Borassodendron machadonis, commonly known as the Malagasy fan-palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible fruits that are used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the flora of Madagascar.
