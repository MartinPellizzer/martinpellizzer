Brachyscome melanocarpa subsp. melanocarpa

Brachyscome melanocarpa subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-veined bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with dark veins.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces small, hairy leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
