Begonia tripicoensis

Begonia tripicoensis, commonly known as the three-lobed begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive three lobes, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
