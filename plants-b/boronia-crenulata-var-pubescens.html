Boronia crenulata var. pubescens

Boronia crenulata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in well-drained environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
