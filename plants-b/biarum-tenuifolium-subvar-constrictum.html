Biarum tenuifolium subvar. constrictum

Biarum tenuifolium subvar. constrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
