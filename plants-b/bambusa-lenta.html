Bambusa lenta

Bambusa lenta, commonly known as the slow-growing bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, typically ranging from 20 to 40 centimeters in length. Its culms are strong and fibrous, making it suitable for construction and crafts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
