Bellevalia zygomorpha

Bellevalia zygomorpha, commonly known as the zigzag bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellevalia zygomorpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
