Bambusa ragamowskii

Bambusa ragamowskii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and has a dense, upright growth habit. Its leaves are narrow, long, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa ragamowskii is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic value and hardy nature.
