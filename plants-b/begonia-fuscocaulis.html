Begonia fuscocaulis

Begonia fuscocaulis, commonly known as the dark-stemmed begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia fuscocaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
