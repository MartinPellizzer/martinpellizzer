Burmeistera valdiviana

Burmeistera valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often along stream banks or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Burmeistera valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
