Bupleurum falcatum var. pusillum

Bupleurum falcatum var. pusillum, commonly known as the small-leafed goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from 30 to 90 cm in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and floral characteristics.
