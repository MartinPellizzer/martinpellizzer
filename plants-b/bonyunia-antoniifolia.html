Bonyunia antoniifolia

Bonyunia antoniifolia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance during the flowering season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
