Bernardia intermedia

Bernardia intermedia, commonly known as the red-flowered bernardia, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. The plant is characterized by its clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
