Bambusa angustifolia

Bambusa angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and crafts.
