Brasolia semperflorens

Brasolia semperflorens, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, colorful florets that resemble tiny daisies. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its flowers are known to remain intact for extended periods, giving it its common name.
