Bacopa aquatica var. multiflora

Bacopa aquatica var. multiflora, commonly known as water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves and produces clusters of tiny blue or violet flowers. It is often used in ornamental gardening and has traditional medicinal uses.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in water make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
