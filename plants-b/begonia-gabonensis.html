Begonia gabonensis

Begonia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon begonia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a deep, rich green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It prefers shady, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among collectors and botanists.
