Bulbophyllum lasiopetalum

Bulbophyllum lasiopetalum, commonly known as the hairy-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy petals and a unique floral structure typical of the orchid family. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
