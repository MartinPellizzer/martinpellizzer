Balsamorhiza incana

Balsamorhiza incana, commonly known as the woolly goldenbush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow composite flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Balsamorhiza incana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
