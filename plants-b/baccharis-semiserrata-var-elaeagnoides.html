Baccharis semiserrata var. elaeagnoides

Baccharis semiserrata var. elaeagnoides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas. It has a woody stem with rough, fissured bark and produces small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
