Bambusa laxa

Bambusa laxa, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing spikes. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan shape, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bambusa laxa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant growth habit and air-purifying qualities.
