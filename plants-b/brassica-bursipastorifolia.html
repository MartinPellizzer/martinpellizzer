Brassica bursipastorifolia

Brassica bursipastorifolia, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica bursipastorifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
