Bambusa venezuelae

Bambusa venezuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to South America.

It features slender, green culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, upright growth habit. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Bambusa venezuelae is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
