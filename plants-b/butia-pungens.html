Butia pungens

Butia pungens, commonly known as the jelly palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small, round dates. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Butia pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
