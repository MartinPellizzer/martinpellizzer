Baccharis dependens

Baccharis dependens, commonly known as the hanging baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its drooping branches and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Baccharis dependens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
