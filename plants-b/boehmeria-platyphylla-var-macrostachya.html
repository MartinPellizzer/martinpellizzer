Boehmeria platyphylla var. macrostachya

Boehmeria platyphylla var. macrostachya, commonly known as giant mulberry, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and papermaking.
