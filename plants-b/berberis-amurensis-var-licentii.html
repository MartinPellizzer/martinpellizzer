Berberis amurensis var. licentii

Berberis amurensis var. licentii, commonly known as the Amur barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
