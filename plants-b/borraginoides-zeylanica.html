Borraginoides zeylanica

Borraginoides zeylanica, commonly known as Indian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Borraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
