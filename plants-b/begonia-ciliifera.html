Begonia ciliifera

Begonia ciliifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its attractive foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
