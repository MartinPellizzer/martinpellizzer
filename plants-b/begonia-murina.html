Begonia murina

Begonia murina, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. Begonia murina thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural collections.
