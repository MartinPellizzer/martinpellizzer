Boesenbergia reticosa

Boesenbergia reticosa, commonly known as the fingerroot or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomes that are often harvested for their aromatic and medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive reticulate pattern on the underside.
