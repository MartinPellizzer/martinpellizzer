Boea philippensis

Boea philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Boea philippensis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.
