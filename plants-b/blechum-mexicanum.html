Blechum mexicanum

Blechum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican blechnum, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, serrated margin.
