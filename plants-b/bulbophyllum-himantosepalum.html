Bulbophyllum himantosepalum

Bulbophyllum himantosepalum, commonly known as the "Pineapple Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are fleshy and strap-like, typically green with a waxy coating.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
