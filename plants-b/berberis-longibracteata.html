Berberis longibracteata

Berberis longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
