Beta sylvestris

Beta sylvestris, commonly known as sea beet, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or coastal environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its salt-tolerant properties. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

Beta sylvestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
