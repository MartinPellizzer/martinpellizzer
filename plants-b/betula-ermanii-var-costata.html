Betula ermanii var. costata

Betula ermanii var. costata, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark that reveals a pinkish underlayer, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has slender, pendulous branches and produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
