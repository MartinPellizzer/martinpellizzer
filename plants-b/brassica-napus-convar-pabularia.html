Brassica napus convar. pabularia

Brassica napus convar. pabularia, commonly known as wild oilseed rape, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
