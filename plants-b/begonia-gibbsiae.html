Begonia gibbsiae

Begonia gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and bright red veins, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
