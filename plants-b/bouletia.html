Bouletia

Bouletia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of a single species, Bouletia erythrophloia, which is native to Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in dry tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
