Bignonia crispa

Bignonia crispa, commonly known as the creeping yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Bignonia crispa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
