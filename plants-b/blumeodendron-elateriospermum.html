Blumeodendron elateriospermum

Blumeodendron elateriospermum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, typically blooming in red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to grow quickly and provide shade in tropical landscapes.
