Bumelia microphylla

Bumelia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruits are dry pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Bumelia microphylla is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
