Burmannia tenella

Burmannia tenella, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Burmannia tenella is notable for its unique appearance and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
