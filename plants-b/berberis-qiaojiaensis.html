Berberis qiaojiaensis

Berberis qiaojiaensis, commonly known as the Qiaojia barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but typically consumed in small quantities due to their tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Berberis qiaojiaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
