Begonia brachyptera

Begonia brachyptera, commonly known as the short-winged begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers typically bloom in the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Begonia brachyptera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
