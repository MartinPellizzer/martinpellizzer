Ballota rubra

Ballota rubra, commonly known as the red ballota, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their antispasmodic properties. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ballota rubra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
