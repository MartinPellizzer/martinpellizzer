Basella alba var. cordifolia

Basella alba var. cordifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are thick and fleshy, and its edible, mild-tasting stems and leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in culinary applications. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by round, red berries containing seeds.

Basella alba var. cordifolia is a hardy, fast-growing plant that is well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
