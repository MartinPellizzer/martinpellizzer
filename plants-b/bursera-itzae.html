Bursera itzae

Bursera itzae, commonly known as the Itza bursera, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Mexico and northern Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant typically grows in tropical dry forests and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in local communities for various purposes.
