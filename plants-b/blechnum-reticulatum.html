Blechnum reticulatum

Blechnum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like fronds that are deeply divided and have a net-like vein pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Blechnum reticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
