Bromus squarrosus var. cyrii

Bromus squarrosus var. cyrii, commonly known as the Sierra bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and the leaves are narrow with a bluish-gray sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
