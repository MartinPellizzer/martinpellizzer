Blumea salviifolia

Blumea salviifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Blumea salviifolia is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display.
