Boronia tenuis

Boronia tenuis, commonly known as the slender boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the spring. Boronia tenuis is often found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
