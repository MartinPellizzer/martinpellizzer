Begonia amparoae

Begonia amparoae, commonly known as the Amparo begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after plant among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
