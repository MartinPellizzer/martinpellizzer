Buddleja hypsophila

Buddleja hypsophila, commonly known as the mountain buddleia, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Buddleja hypsophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
