Bouteloua hirta

Bouteloua hirta, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
