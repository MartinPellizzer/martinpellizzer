Bactris concinna subsp. depauperata

Bactris concinna subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the slender palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 8 meters.

The plant has a slender trunk and is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in warmer regions.
