Brachypodium distachyon var. elongatum

Brachypodium distachyon var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces dense spikes of small, two-chambered flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its genetic simplicity and rapid growth, it is widely used in plant research as a model organism.
