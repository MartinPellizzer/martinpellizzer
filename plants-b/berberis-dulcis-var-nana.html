Berberis dulcis var. nana

Berberis dulcis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf barberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, spiny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
