Biarum longifolium

Biarum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are elongated and lanceolate, often with a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
