Baccharis brachylaenoides var. parvifolia

Baccharis brachylaenoides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
