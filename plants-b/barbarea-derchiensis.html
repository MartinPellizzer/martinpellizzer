Barbarea derchiensis

Barbarea derchiensis, commonly known as the Tibetan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are often yellow or white.
