Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. simaczevae

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

simaczevae, commonly known as the Simaczev's bromus, is a grass species native to specific regions of Southeast Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
