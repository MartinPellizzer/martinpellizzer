Berberis rufescens

Berberis rufescens, commonly known as the red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn bright red in the fall.

Berberis rufescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
