Butea frondosa

Butea frondosa, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving the plant its vivid common name. The leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnately compound, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as forests and scrublands.
