Barleria parvispina var. angustifolia

Barleria parvispina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, angular stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
