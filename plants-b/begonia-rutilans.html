Begonia rutilans

Begonia rutilans, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia rutilans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
