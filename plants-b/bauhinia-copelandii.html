Bauhinia copelandii

Bauhinia copelandii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia copelandii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
