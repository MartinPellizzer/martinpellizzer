Brachylaena merana

Brachylaena merana, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
