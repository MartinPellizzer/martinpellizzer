Bistorta suffulta subsp. pergracilis

Bistorta suffulta subsp.

pergracilis, commonly known as the slender bistort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its stems are upright and often covered in fine hairs, while the leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
