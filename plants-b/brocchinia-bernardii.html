Brocchinia bernardii

Brocchinia bernardii, commonly known as the glassy pitcher plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and its distinctive, waxy, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are covered in a transparent, waxy coating that gives it its common name and helps in water retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
