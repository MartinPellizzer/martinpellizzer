Baccharis myrtilloides

Baccharis myrtilloides, commonly known as blue baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-gray foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Baccharis myrtilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
