Bomarea engleriana

Bomarea engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's bomarea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy inflorescences that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bomarea engleriana is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
