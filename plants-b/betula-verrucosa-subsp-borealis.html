Betula verrucosa subsp. borealis

Betula verrucosa subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the Arctic birch, is a shrub-like species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and smooth, gray bark.

The plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and coastal areas. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and have a serrated edge. Betula verrucosa subsp.

borealis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
