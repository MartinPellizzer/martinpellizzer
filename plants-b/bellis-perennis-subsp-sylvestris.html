Bellis perennis subsp. sylvestris

Bellis perennis subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and shaded areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disc. It is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
