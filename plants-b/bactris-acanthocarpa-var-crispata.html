Bactris acanthocarpa var. crispata

Bactris acanthocarpa var. crispata, commonly known as the hairy palm or crinkled fruit palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into edible, spiny fruits. Its unique foliage and fruit structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.

The varietal name "crispata" refers to the crinkled appearance of its leaves, a distinctive feature of this species.
