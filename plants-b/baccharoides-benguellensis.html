Baccharoides benguellensis

Baccharoides benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguella bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, often clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
