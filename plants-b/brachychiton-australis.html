Brachychiton australis

Brachychiton australis, commonly known as the Queensland bottle tree, is a unique and striking plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The tree has palm-like leaves and produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
