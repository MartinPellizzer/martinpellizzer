Baccharis vacciniifolia

Baccharis vacciniifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, waxy leaves that are often gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the fall and winter months. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild winters.
