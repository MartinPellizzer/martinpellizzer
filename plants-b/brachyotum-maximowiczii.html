Brachyotum maximowiczii

Brachyotum maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
