Betula alba f. laciniata

Betula alba f. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated birch, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves.

This ornamental tree is native to northern Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin, giving it a unique appearance compared to the standard white birch. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, and its bark is smooth and pale, often with a white or silver hue.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
