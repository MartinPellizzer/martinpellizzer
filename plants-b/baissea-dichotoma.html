Baissea dichotoma

Baissea dichotoma, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring slender, branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, daisy-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Baissea dichotoma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
