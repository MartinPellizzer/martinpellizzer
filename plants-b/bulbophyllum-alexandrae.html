Bulbophyllum alexandrae

Bulbophyllum alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
