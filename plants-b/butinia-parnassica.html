Butinia parnassica

Butinia parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, sturdy stem.

Butinia parnassica is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in meadows and open slopes.
