Blitum virgatum var. minus

Blitum virgatum var. minus, commonly known as dwarf white clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a common sight in grassy areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a sprawling growth habit.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
