Betula verrucosa var. vulgaris

Betula verrucosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
