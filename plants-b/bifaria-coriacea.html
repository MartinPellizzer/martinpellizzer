Bifaria coriacea

Bifaria coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Bifaria coriacea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
