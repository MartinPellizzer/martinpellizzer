Bromus dudleyi

Bromus dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's brome, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces spikelets that are usually clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a bluish-green color.

Bromus dudleyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
