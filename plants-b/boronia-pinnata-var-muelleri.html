Boronia pinnata var. muelleri

Boronia pinnata var. muelleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
