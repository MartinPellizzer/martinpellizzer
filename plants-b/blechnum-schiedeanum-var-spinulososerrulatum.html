Blechnum schiedeanum var. spinulososerrulatum

Blechnum schiedeanum var. spinulososerrulatum, commonly known as the spinulose serrulate maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Blechnaceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive spinulose and serrulate margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce upright, delicate fronds that are often used in horticultural settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
