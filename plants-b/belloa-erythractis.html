Belloa erythractis

Belloa erythractis, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Belloa erythractis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
