Begonia antsiranensis

Begonia antsiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can exhibit a range of colors, from green to red or purple.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
