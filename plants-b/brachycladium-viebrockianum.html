Brachycladium viebrockianum

Brachycladium viebrockianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf star grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
