Bromus moellendorffianus

Bromus moellendorffianus, commonly known as the red brome, is a grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish-colored stems. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Bromus moellendorffianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
