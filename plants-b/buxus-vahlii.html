Buxus vahlii

Buxus vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Buxaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are thick and glossy, providing a lush appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buxus vahlii is also valued for its ability to tolerate pruning, making it suitable for shaping into hedges or topiaries.
