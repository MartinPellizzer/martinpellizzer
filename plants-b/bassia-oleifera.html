Bassia oleifera

Bassia oleifera, commonly known as the Russian spinach or winter cress, is an annual herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive peppery aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped, edible seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its high nutrient content, it is used as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.
