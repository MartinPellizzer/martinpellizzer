Blechnum australe f. triloba

Blechnum australe f. triloba, commonly known as the variable shield fern, is a fern species native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a broad, flat structure that provides a dense, lush appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The plant's morphology reflects its adaptation to humid, tropical climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
