Begonia popenoei

Begonia popenoei, commonly known as the Popenoe's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts around the world.
