Blechnum hastatum f. bifida

Blechnum hastatum f. bifida, commonly known as the bifid maidenhair fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that often split into two distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clusters of small, spore-producing structures called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after specimen in horticulture and botanical collections.
