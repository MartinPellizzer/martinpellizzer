Bouchea longiflora

Bouchea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered bouchea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Bouchea longiflora is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
