Batrachium paucistamineum

Batrachium paucistamineum, commonly known as the small-stemmed frog's mouth, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with smooth surfaces. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in cymes. Its fruits are follicles that release tiny, winged seeds for wind dispersal.

Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
