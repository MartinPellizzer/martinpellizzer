Bulbophyllum hapalanthos

Bulbophyllum hapalanthos, commonly known as the Hapalanthos orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often arranged in a spike. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
