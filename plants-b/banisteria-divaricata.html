Banisteria divaricata

Banisteria divaricata, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small showers, hence its common name. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria divaricata is also known for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
