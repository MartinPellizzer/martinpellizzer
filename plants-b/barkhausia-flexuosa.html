Barkhausia flexuosa

Barkhausia flexuosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Barkhausia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
