Butea loureiroi

Butea loureiroi, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
