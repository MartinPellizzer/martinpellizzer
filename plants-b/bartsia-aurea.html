Bartsia aurea

Bartsia aurea, commonly known as golden bartsia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the moist woodlands of eastern North America.

It features delicate, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Bartsia aurea is known for its ornamental value and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
