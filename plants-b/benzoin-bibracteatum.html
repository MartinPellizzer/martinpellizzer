Benzoin bibracteatum

Benzoin bibracteatum, commonly known as the sacred benjamin, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic resin, which is tapped from the bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its resin, known as benjoin, has a strong, balsamic scent and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species is also notable for its unique morphology and is considered a key component of Madagascar's endemic flora.
