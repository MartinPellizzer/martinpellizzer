Bambusa vulgaris f. vittata

Bambusa vulgaris f. vittata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variegated form of the common bamboo species.

It features striking yellow and green striped foliage, which makes it highly ornamental. The plant is a clumping bamboo, growing in dense clusters rather than spreading aggressively. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This bamboo is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a lush, tropical look.
