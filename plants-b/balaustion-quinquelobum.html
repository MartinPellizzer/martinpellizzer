Balaustion quinquelobum

Balaustion quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed balaustion, is a rare and lesser-known plant species with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its five lobes on the leaf margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
