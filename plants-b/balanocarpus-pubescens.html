Balanocarpus pubescens

Balanocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.
