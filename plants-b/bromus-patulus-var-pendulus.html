Bromus patulus var. pendulus

Bromus patulus var. pendulus, commonly known as the weeping brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and is adapted to a variety of soil conditions.
