Baccharis perulata

Baccharis perulata, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, rough-textured leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Baccharis perulata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
