Bumelia celastrina var. angustifolia

Bumelia celastrina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
