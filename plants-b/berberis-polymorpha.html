Berberis polymorpha

Berberis polymorpha, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and winter interest.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native plant species.
