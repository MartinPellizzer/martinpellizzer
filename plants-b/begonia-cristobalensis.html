Begonia cristobalensis

Begonia cristobalensis, commonly known as the cristobalensis begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
