Bambusa flagellifera

Bambusa flagellifera, commonly known as the long-leaf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with a pale green color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Bambusa flagellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in construction and handicrafts.
