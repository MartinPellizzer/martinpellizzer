Bredemeyera ericina

Bredemeyera ericina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bredemeyera ericina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
