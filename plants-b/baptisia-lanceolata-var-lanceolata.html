Baptisia lanceolata var. lanceolata

Baptisia lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
