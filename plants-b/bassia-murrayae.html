Bassia murrayae

Bassia murrayae, commonly known as Murray's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found growing on rocky soils or in disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dryland regions.
