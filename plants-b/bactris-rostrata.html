Bactris rostrata

Bactris rostrata, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple leaf bases, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and has a slender, upright trunk. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Bactris rostrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
