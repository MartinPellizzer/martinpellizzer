Banksia rosserae

Banksia rosserae, commonly known as the Geraldton banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color, and its distinctive woody cone-like fruit. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various native fauna.

Banksia rosserae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
