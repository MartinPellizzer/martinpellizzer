Bomarea acutifolia

Bomarea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble umbels. Bomarea acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
