Bandeiraea simplicifolia

Bandeiraea simplicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that contain a single large seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The plant is valued for its edible seeds and its role in traditional medicine.
