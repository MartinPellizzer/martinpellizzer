Bactris insignis

Bactris insignis, commonly known as the pink pineapple, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen palm characterized by its short trunk and dense rosette of sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces an edible fruit that resembles a pineapple in appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
