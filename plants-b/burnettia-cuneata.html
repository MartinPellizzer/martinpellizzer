Burnettia cuneata

Burnettia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burnettia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
