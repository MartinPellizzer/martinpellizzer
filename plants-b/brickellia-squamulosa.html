Brickellia squamulosa

Brickellia squamulosa, commonly known as silverleaf brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
