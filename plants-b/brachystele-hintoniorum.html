Brachystele hintoniorum

Brachystele hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's stunted moss, is a rare and diminutive moss species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its extremely short, compact growth form, with tightly packed, leaf-like structures that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on the forest floor or on the bases of trees. Its small size and specialized habitat make it a challenging subject for study and conservation.

Due to habitat destruction, Brachystele hintoniorum is considered endangered, highlighting the need for further research and protection efforts.
