Bryophyllum pseudocampanulatum

Bryophyllum pseudocampanulatum, commonly known as the Cape flower or African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Bryophyllum pseudocampanulatum can be propagated through leaf cuttings, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
