Ballota hispanica var. hispida

Ballota hispanica var. hispida, commonly known as Spanish ballota, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves with a distinctive glandular texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds.

Ballota hispanica var. hispida is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
