Bignonia aesculus

Bignonia aesculus, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
