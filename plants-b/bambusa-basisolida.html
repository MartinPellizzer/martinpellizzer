Bambusa basisolida

Bambusa basisolida, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it a popular ornamental species. Its culms are strong and hollow, typical of many bamboo species.

Bambusa basisolida thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
