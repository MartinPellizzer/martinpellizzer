Bassia salsuginosa

Bassia salsuginosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Bassia salsuginosa is an important plant for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
