Brickellia tenera

Brickellia tenera, commonly known as the silky brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.

Brickellia tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
