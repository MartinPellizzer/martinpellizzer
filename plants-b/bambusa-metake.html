Bambusa metake

Bambusa metake, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green color. It is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands through rhizome growth.

Bambusa metake is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
