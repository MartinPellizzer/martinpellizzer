Biscutella didyma var. macrocarpa

Biscutella didyma var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
