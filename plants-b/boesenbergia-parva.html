Boesenbergia parva

Boesenbergia parva, commonly known as the small ginger or dwarf ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spindle-shaped, yellow fruits. Boesenbergia parva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Zingiberaceae family.
