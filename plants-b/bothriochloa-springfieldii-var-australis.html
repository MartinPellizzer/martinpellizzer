Bothriochloa springfieldii var. australis

Bothriochloa springfieldii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-lying turf. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which produce numerous tiny grains.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in native landscapes.
