Banksia tridentata

Banksia tridentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a striking Australian shrub or small tree belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the south-western region of Western Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight zigzag pattern along their edges.

Banksia tridentata plays an important ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
