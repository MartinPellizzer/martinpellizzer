Bromus arvensis var. inermis

Bromus arvensis var. inermis, commonly known as the slender annual brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

The species is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
