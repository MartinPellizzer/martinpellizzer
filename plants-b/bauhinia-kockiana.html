Bauhinia kockiana

Bauhinia kockiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral clusters that appear during the dry season, making it a visually distinctive plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feathery leaflets, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
