Bauhinia monandra

Bauhinia monandra, commonly known as the velvetwood or blue-flowered bauhinia, is a tropical legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Bauhinia monandra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
