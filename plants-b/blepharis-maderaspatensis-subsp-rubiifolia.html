Blepharis maderaspatensis subsp. rubiifolia

Blepharis maderaspatensis subsp.

rubiifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers.

The subspecies rubiifolia is distinguished by its reddish-pink flowers and hairy foliage. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
