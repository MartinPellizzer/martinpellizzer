Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. arctica

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Arctic and alpine regions. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments, often found in tundra and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic flora and fauna.
