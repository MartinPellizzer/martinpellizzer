Begonia maculata var. wightii

Begonia maculata var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a unique, almost velvety texture. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This variety is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid environments, making it a versatile addition to greenhouses or shaded outdoor spaces.
