Betula baranovii

Betula baranovii, commonly known as the Baranov birch, is a species of birch native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern China.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that becomes more pronounced with age, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, deciduous leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

Betula baranovii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
