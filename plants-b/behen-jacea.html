Behen jacea

Behen jacea, commonly known as Indian mustard or wild radish, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
