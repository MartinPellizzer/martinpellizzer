Bauhinia pauciflora

Bauhinia pauciflora, commonly known as the lace flower or white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia pauciflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
