Bursera nesopola

Bursera nesopola, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a leathery texture.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are yellow or white, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.
