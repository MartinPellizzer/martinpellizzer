Berberis vulgaris var. cretica

Berberis vulgaris var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, bright green leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The Cretan barberry is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
