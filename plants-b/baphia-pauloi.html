Baphia pauloi

Baphia pauloi, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Baphia pauloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
