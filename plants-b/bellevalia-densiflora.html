Bellevalia densiflora

Bellevalia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Bellevalia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
