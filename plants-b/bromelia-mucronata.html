Bromelia mucronata

Bromelia mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful foliage. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and provides a habitat for small organisms. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that attract pollinators.

Bromelia mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
