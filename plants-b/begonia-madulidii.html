Begonia madulidii

Begonia madulidii, commonly known as the Madulidii begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, often velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
