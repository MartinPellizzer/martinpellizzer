Blandfordia uniflora

Blandfordia uniflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and a single, showy flower that blooms in late winter to early spring. The flower features a distinctive red or pink spathe with a white or pink spatula, creating a bell-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the Christmas season in some regions.
