Bocconia oblanceolata

Bocconia oblanceolata, commonly known as the red bocconia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, velvety bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Bocconia oblanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
