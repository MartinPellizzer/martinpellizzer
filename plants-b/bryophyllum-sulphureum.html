Bryophyllum sulphureum

Bryophyllum sulphureum, commonly known as the golden leaf plant, is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters during the summer months. Native to southern Africa, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
