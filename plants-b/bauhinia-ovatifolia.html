Bauhinia ovatifolia

Bauhinia ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf bauhinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven oval-shaped lobes. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in racemes, often appearing in spring.

Bauhinia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
