Bauhinia teysmanniana

Bauhinia teysmanniana, commonly known as the Java orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that resemble orchids, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is smooth with a light grayish color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia teysmanniana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
