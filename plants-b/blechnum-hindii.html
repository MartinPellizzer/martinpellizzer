Blechnum hindii

Blechnum hindii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum hindii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
