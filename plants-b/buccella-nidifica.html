Buccella nidifica

Buccella nidifica, commonly known as the "nesting plant," is a unique species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with thin, succulent stems that can root at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground.

Despite its delicate appearance, Buccella nidifica is well adapted to thrive in rocky and arid environments.
