Bomarea nervosa

Bomarea nervosa, commonly known as the pink bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, often fragrant blooms. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Bomarea nervosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
