Bignonia jasminifolia

Bignonia jasminifolia, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia jasminifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
