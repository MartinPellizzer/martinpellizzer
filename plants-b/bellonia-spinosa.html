Bellonia spinosa

Bellonia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny bellonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
