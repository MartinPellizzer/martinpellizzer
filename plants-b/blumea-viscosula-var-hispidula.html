Blumea viscosula var. hispidula

Blumea viscosula var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy Blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a viscid or sticky texture.

The flowers are arranged in corymbs and are often visited by insects, contributing to its ecological importance in pollination.
