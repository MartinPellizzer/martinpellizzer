Boronia tetragona

Boronia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring slender, often angular stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture with a distinct four-angled cross-section.

Boronia tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
