Bulbophyllum oblongum

Bulbophyllum oblongum, commonly known as the oblong orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and strap-shaped, while its flowers are usually yellow or green with intricate patterns.

Bulbophyllum oblongum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
