Bachmannia

Bachmannia, commonly known as the grass or the genus of flowering plants, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The genus includes several species that are native to various regions of South America. Bachmannia plants typically thrive in temperate and subtropical climates.

They are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
