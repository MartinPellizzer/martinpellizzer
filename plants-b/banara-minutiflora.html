Banara minutiflora

Banara minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered banara, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Banara minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
