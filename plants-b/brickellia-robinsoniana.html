Brickellia robinsoniana

Brickellia robinsoniana, commonly known as the scarlet brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright orange to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Brickellia robinsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
