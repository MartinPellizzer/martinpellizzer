Baccharis cinnamomifolia

Baccharis cinnamomifolia, commonly known as cinnamon leaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong cinnamon-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and savannas, and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
