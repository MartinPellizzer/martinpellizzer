Begonia matangensis

Begonia matangensis, commonly known as the Matangensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is sometimes grown in controlled environments.
