Begonia felis

Begonia felis, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purplish hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
