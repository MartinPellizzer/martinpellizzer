Berberis sinensis f. canadensis

Berberis sinensis f. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian barberry, is a cultivar of the barberry plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, making it a plant of both aesthetic and ecological interest.
