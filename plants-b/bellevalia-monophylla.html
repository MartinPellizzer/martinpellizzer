Bellevalia monophylla

Bellevalia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique single leaf and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
