Brachypodium pinnatum var. villosissimum

Brachypodium pinnatum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged. This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
