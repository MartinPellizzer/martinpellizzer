Butia punctata

Butia punctata, commonly known as the punctate palm, is a striking palm native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, round fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Butia punctata is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
