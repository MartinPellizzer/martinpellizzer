Butomopsis latifolia

Butomopsis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and typically have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Butomopsis latifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
