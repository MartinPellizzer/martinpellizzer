Berberis emilii

Berberis emilii, commonly known as the Emili's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
