Binaria coronata

Binaria coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Binaria coronata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
