Brachycladium ciliare

Brachycladium ciliare, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy margins, which give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with bracts that are often hairy and elongated.

Brachycladium ciliare is typically found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
