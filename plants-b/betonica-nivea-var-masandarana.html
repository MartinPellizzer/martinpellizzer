Betonica nivea var. masandarana

Betonica nivea var. masandarana, commonly known as the white betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Betonica nivea var. masandarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
