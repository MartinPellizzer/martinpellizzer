Blechnum aequabile

Blechnum aequabile, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a species of fern found in various parts of the world, including Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically evergreen, with a leathery texture and a dark green color, often featuring a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
