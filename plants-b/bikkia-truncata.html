Bikkia truncata

Bikkia truncata, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive truncate (notched) base, giving it its scientific name.

Bikkia truncata is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.
