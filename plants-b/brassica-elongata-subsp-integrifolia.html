Brassica elongata subsp. integrifolia

Brassica elongata subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth cabbage or smooth wallaby cabbage, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
