Baccharis venulosoides

Baccharis venulosoides, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Baccharis venulosoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
