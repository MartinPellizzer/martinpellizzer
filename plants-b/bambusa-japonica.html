Bambusa japonica

Bambusa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, green culms that grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Bambusa japonica is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
