Begonia × caulohybrida

Begonia × caulohybrida, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia evansiorum.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, often vibrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening and as a bedding plant. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
