Baccharis subpingraea f. crassifolia

Baccharis subpingraea f. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
