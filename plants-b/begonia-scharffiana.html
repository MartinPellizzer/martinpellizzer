Begonia scharffiana

Begonia scharffiana, commonly known as the Scharff's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
