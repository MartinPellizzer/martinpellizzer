Beesha baccifera

Beesha baccifera, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, fibrous pulp that has been used for food and traditional medicine.

Beesha baccifera plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
