Berberis vulgaris subsp. spryginii

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

spryginii, commonly known as the Caucasian barberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but tart. It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
