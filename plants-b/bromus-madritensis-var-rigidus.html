Bromus madritensis var. rigidus

Bromus madritensis var. rigidus, commonly known as rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces dense spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
