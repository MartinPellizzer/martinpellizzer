Begonia rubrovenia var. meisneri

Begonia rubrovenia var. meisneri, commonly known as the Meisner's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface with a slightly hairy texture. This variety thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its colorful foliage and unique floral display.
