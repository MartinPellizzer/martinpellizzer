Baccharoides calvoana

Baccharoides calvoana, commonly known as the Calvo's baccharoides, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing structural support in coastal wind conditions.

Baccharoides calvoana plays a role in stabilizing dune systems and supporting local biodiversity.
