Benincasa

Benincasa, commonly known as the winter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins.

It is a trailing vine that produces large, green, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it has a pale green, waxy skin with a sweet, slightly musky flavor. Benincasa is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for growing in warm climates with ample sunlight.
