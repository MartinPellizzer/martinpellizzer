Brownseya serpentina

Brownseya serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's tail fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading fronds that resemble the shape of a serpent, giving it its common name. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can grow extensively, allowing it to spread along forest floors or rocky surfaces.

The plant's foliage is evergreen, providing a lush green appearance throughout the year.
