Bupleurum graminifolium var. lonchophorum

Bupleurum graminifolium var. lonchophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cicely, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic, with a distinctive, slightly bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
