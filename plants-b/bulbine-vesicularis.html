Bulbine vesicularis

Bulbine vesicularis, commonly known as the bladder bulb, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bulbine vesicularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
