Bernardia brandegeei

Bernardia brandegeei, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
