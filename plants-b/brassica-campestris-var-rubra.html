Brassica campestris var. rubra

Brassica campestris var. rubra, commonly known as red mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a peppery aroma. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for use in cooking or as a medicinal herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
