Bulbophyllum exasperatum

Bulbophyllum exasperatum, commonly known as the angry orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers that are usually yellow or orange with purple markings. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
