Bauhinia kockiana var. scarlatina

Bauhinia kockiana var. scarlatina, commonly known as the scarlet bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, scarlet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments, and are typically green with a smooth texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
