Beta macrocarpa

Beta macrocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are tender and edible. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to orange-red berries. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious leaves.

Beta macrocarpa is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
