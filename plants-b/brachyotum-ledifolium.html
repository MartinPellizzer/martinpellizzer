Brachyotum ledifolium

Brachyotum ledifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, followed by the formation of seed heads that resemble small dandelion puffs.

Brachyotum ledifolium is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
