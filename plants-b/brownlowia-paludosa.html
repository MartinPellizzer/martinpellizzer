Brownlowia paludosa

Brownlowia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy habitats, thriving in moist and nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Brownlowia paludosa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
