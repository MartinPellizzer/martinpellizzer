Bryonopsis laciniosa var. erythrocarpa

Bryonopsis laciniosa var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited bryonopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
