Baccharis beckii

Baccharis beckii, commonly known as the yellow baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Baccharis beckii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
