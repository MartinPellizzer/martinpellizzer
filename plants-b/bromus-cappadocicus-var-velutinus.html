Bromus cappadocicus var. velutinus

Bromus cappadocicus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that give the plant its velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a loose panicle.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
