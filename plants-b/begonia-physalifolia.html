Begonia physalifolia

Begonia physalifolia, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
