Bulbophyllum emiliorum

Bulbophyllum emiliorum, commonly known as the Emilie's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, green, and white. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
