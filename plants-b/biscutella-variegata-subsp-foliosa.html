Biscutella variegata subsp. foliosa

Biscutella variegata subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the variegated biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its unique leaf patterns and delicate flowers make it a distinctive addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
