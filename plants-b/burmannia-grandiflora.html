Burmannia grandiflora

Burmannia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant burmannia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vivid red or orange hues. The plant has a short, thick stem with broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and greenhouses.
