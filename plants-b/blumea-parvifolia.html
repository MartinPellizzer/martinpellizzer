Blumea parvifolia

Blumea parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
