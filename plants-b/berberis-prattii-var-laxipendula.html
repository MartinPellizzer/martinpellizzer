Berberis prattii var. laxipendula

Berberis prattii var. laxipendula, commonly known as the laxipendulous barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
