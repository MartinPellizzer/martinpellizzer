Berberis beijingensis

Berberis beijingensis, commonly known as the Beijing barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
