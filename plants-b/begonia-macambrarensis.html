Begonia macambrarensis

Begonia macambrarensis, commonly known as the Macambarensis Begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
