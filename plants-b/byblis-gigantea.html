Byblis gigantea

Byblis gigantea, commonly known as the giant mistletoe, is a carnivorous plant native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red, sticky hairs that secrete a digestive fluid to trap small insects. The plant grows as a terrestrial herb, often forming dense mats on the forest floor. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance and predatory behavior make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
