Brickellia chenopodina

Brickellia chenopodina, commonly known as desert brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Brickellia chenopodina is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
