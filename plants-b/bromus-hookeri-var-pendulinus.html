Bromus hookeri var. pendulinus

Bromus hookeri var. pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the flowering stems are usually erect and branched.

Bromus hookeri var. pendulinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rugged environments.
