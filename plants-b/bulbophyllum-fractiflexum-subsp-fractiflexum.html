Bulbophyllum fractiflexum subsp. fractiflexum

Bulbophyllum fractiflexum subsp.

fractiflexum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and dark green, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with a distinctive, sweet scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
