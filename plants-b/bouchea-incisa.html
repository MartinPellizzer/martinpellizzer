Bouchea incisa

Bouchea incisa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bouchea incisa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
