Berberis hancockiana

Berberis hancockiana, commonly known as the Hancock's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage, which turns a striking orange or red.
