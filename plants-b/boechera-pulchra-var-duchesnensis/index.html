Boechera Pulchra Var. Duchesnensis

Duchesne's rockcress, scientifically known as Boechera pulchra var. duchesnensis, is a rare and beautiful flowering plant native to the western United States. This member of the Brassicaceae family is characterized by its delicate, blue-green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern. The plant's unique appearance and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
