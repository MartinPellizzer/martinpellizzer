Brassica sativa subsp. napus

Brassica sativa subsp.

napus, commonly known as rapeseed, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae). It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces pods containing round, yellow or brown seeds that are used for oil extraction and as animal feed. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its high oil content. Brassica sativa subsp.

napus is also used in the production of biodiesel and has been bred for various agricultural purposes.
