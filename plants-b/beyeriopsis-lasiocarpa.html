Beyeriopsis lasiocarpa

Beyeriopsis lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bark tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bark that becomes woody and cracked with age. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Beyeriopsis lasiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
