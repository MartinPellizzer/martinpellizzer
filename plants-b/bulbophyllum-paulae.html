Bulbophyllum paulae

Bulbophyllum paulae, commonly known as the Pauline's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and vibrant hues, which range from yellow to orange and red.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
