Blumea pubiflora

Blumea pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often ovate to elliptic in shape. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Blumea pubiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
