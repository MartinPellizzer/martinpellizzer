Balsamea welwitschii

Balsamea welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a woody trunk that can reach several meters in height and is covered with a thick, protective layer of bark. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and trunk.

Balsamea welwitschii is also known for its slow growth and longevity, with some specimens living over 2,000 years.
