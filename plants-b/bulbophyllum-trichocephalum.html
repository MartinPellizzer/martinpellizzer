Bulbophyllum trichocephalum

Bulbophyllum trichocephalum, commonly known as the hairy-headed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive hairy appearance on its floral bracts. It has a complex root system that helps it absorb moisture and nutrients from its substrate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
