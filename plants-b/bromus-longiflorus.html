Bromus longiflorus

Bromus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or pink. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Bromus longiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
