Brachiaria megastachya

Brachiaria megastachya, commonly known as giant beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
