Browallia americana f. alba

Browallia americana f. alba, commonly known as the white browallia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its white petals are often adorned with a contrasting purple or violet base, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

The foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
