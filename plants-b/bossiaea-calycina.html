Bossiaea calycina

Bossiaea calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the autumn season. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

Bossiaea calycina is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
