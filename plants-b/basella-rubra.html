Basella rubra

Basella rubra, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often red-veined and edible. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by round, orange fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious leaves.

Basella rubra is a hardy, fast-growing plant that is well-suited for both ornamental and culinary use.
