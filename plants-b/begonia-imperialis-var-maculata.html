Begonia imperialis var. maculata

Begonia imperialis var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted imperial begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
