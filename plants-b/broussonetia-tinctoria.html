Broussonetia tinctoria

Broussonetia tinctoria, commonly known as Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. The species is also valued for its hard wood and has historical uses in dyeing and tanning.
