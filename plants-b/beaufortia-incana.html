Beaufortia incana

Beaufortia incana, commonly known as the grey beaufortia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Beaufortia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
