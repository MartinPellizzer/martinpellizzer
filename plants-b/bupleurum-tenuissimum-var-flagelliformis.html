Bupleurum tenuissimum var. flagelliformis

Bupleurum tenuissimum var. flagelliformis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display.
