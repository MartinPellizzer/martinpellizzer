Bignonia albiflora

Bignonia albiflora, commonly known as the white trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, white, trumpet-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
