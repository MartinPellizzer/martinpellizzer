Berberis xanthoxylon var. sumatranica

Berberis xanthoxylon var. sumatranica, commonly known as the Sumatran barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
