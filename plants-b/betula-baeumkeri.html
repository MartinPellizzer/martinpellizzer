Betula baeumkeri

Betula baeumkeri, commonly known as the Baeumker's birch, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Betulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a subtle, pale yellowish hue.

Betula baeumkeri is typically found in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
