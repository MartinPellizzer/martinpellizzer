Biscutella laxa var. bastetana

Biscutella laxa var. bastetana, commonly known as the Balkan biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
