Botryopanax dichrostachyus

Botryopanax dichrostachyus, commonly known as the red-flowered banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a fibrous pulp. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The tree's extensive root system and ability to grow in various soil types make it adaptable to different environments.
