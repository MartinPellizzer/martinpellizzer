Betonica monieri

Betonica monieri, commonly known as lesser betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

Betonica monieri is known for its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
