Brosimum acutifolium subsp. acutifolium

Brosimum acutifolium subsp.

acutifolium, commonly known as the Guayacan tree, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Central and South America. It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a pointed tip, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small figs.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in tropical dry forests and savannas.
