Ballota nigra var. variegata

Ballota nigra var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated black thorn, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
