Bombax buonopozense var. cristata

Bombax buonopozense var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate ceiba, is a striking tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has a distinctive, buttressed trunk and can grow quite tall, often reaching heights of over 30 meters. Its leaves are large, palmate, and provide dense shade, while its fibrous bark has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting a variety of wildlife in its native habitats.
