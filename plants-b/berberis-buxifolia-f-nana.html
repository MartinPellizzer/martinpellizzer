Berberis buxifolia f. nana

Berberis buxifolia f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf boxleaf barberry, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture, resembling the foliage of boxwood. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and compact form, it is ideal for rock gardens, borders, or as a ground cover.
