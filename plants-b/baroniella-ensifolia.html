Baroniella ensifolia

Baroniella ensifolia, commonly known as the sour sop or graviola, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny, green berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp with a sour-sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
