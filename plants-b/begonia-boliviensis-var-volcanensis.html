Begonia boliviensis var. volcanensis

Begonia boliviensis var. volcanensis, commonly known as the Volcanic Begonia, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a range of colors from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and requires high levels of sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized plant species.
