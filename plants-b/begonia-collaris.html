Begonia collaris

Begonia collaris, commonly known as the collared begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with a distinct collar-like pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
