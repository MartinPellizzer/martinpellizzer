Bromus porteri var. lanatipes

Bromus porteri var. lanatipes, commonly known as the hairy bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Bromus porteri var. lanatipes is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
