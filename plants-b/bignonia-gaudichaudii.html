Bignonia gaudichaudii

Bignonia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces long, pendulous racemes of flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Bignonia gaudichaudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
