Berberis dentata

Berberis dentata, commonly known as the toothed barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and persistent berries.
