Bebbia atriplicifolia

Bebbia atriplicifolia, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Bebbia atriplicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
