Brickellia wrightii var. tenera

Brickellia wrightii var. tenera, commonly known as the woolly brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
