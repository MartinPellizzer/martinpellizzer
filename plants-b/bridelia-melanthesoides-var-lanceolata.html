Bridelia melanthesoides var. lanceolata

Bridelia melanthesoides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
