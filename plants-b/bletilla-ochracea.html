Bletilla ochracea

Bletilla ochracea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found in forested areas with moist, shaded conditions. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bletilla ochracea is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
