Butea harmandii

Butea harmandii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red floral display, which appears in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and its flowers are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. The leaves are bipinnately compound, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
