Blepharacanthus procumbens

Blepharacanthus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping blepharacanthus, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
