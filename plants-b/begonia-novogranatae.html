Begonia novogranatae

Begonia novogranatae, commonly known as the Colombian begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to deep red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Begonia novogranatae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
