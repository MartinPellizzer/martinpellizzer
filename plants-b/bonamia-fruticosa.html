Bonamia fruticosa

Bonamia fruticosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, sprawling growth habits. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bonamia fruticosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland areas.
