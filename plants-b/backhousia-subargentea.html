Backhousia subargentea

Backhousia subargentea, commonly known as silverbacked backhousia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sunny climates.

Backhousia subargentea is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
