Baeria microglossa

Baeria microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baeria, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in compact, spherical clusters called umbels.

Baeria microglossa is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
