Berchemia discolor

Berchemia discolor, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Berchemia discolor is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
