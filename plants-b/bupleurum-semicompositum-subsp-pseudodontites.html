Bupleurum semicompositum subsp. pseudodontites

Bupleurum semicompositum subsp.

pseudodontites, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
