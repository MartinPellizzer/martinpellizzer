Bulbophyllum mulderae

Bulbophyllum mulderae, commonly known as the Mulder's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the dry season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Bulbophyllum mulderae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
