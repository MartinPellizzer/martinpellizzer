Bulbostylis pedunculosa

Bulbostylis pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its delicate structure and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
