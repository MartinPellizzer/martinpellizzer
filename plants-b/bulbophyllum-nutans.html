Bulbophyllum nutans

Bulbophyllum nutans, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, downward-facing flowers that resemble small, nodding bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a delicate, fragrant bloom.

Bulbophyllum nutans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its visual beauty.
