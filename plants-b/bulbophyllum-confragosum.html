Bulbophyllum confragosum

Bulbophyllum confragosum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement along the stem. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive scent.
