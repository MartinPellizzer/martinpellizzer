Brassavola filifolia

Brassavola filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Brassavola filifolia is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
