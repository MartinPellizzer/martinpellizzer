Blumea crepidifolia

Blumea crepidifolia, commonly known as the creeping blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

Blumea crepidifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
