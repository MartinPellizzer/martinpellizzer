Brathys

Brathys, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that resemble small flames, hence its common name. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid environment. Brathys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is believed to have spiritual significance in some cultures.

Its botanical classification places it within the family Acanthaceae, where it shares characteristics with other flowering plants in the region.
