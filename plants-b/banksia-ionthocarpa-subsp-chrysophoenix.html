Banksia ionthocarpa subsp. chrysophoenix

Banksia ionthocarpa subsp.

chrysophoenix, commonly known as the golden banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
