Bermudiana campestris

Bermudiana campestris, commonly known as the Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of Bermuda.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a common weed in lawns and agricultural fields. Its rhizomes and stolons allow it to spread aggressively, forming dense mats that can outcompete other vegetation.

Due to its resilience, Bermudiana campestris is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
