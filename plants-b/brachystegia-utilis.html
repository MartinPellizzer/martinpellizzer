Brachystegia utilis

Brachystegia utilis, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often trifoliate, with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.

This species is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
