Bellia hirsuta

Bellia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

Bellia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
