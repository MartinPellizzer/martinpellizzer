Baccharis alata

Baccharis alata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite and often have a velvety texture.

Baccharis alata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
