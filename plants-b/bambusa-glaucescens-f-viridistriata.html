Bambusa glaucescens f. viridistriata

Bambusa glaucescens f. viridistriata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

This plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a striking combination of green and white or silver markings along the midribs. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, upright clusters, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The culms are smooth and have a pale greenish hue, while the leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
