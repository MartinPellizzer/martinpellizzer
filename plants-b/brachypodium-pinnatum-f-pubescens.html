Brachypodium pinnatum f. pubescens

Brachypodium pinnatum f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent little grass, is a variant of the species Brachypodium pinnatum, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

This grass is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture that distinguishes it from other similar species. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
