Bonania nipensis

Bonania nipensis, commonly known as the Nipentian fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that unfurl from a creeping rhizome. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are located on the underside of its leaves, aiding in its reproductive cycle.

Due to habitat loss, Bonania nipensis is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
