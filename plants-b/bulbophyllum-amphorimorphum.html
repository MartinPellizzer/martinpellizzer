Bulbophyllum amphorimorphum

Bulbophyllum amphorimorphum, commonly known as the bottle-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unusual appearance.
