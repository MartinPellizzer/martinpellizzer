Bolanthus fruticulosus

Bolanthus fruticulosus, commonly known as the thorny flax or desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and succulent leaves. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.

Bolanthus fruticulosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
