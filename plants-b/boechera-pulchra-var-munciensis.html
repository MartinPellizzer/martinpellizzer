Boechera pulchra var. munciensis

Boechera pulchra var. munciensis, commonly known as the Muncie rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States, specifically found in Texas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are basal and linear, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.
