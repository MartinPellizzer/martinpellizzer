Baccharis mocoafluminis

Baccharis mocoafluminis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and riparian zones, adapting well to moist and humid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in autumn.

Baccharis mocoafluminis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
