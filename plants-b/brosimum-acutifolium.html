Brosimum acutifolium

Brosimum acutifolium, commonly known as the ceiba tree or ceiba, is a large, deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a distinctive crown of spreading branches. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a pointed tip, contributing to its scientific name.

The ceiba tree plays an important role in the ecosystems of the rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
