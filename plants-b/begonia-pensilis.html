Begonia pensilis

Begonia pensilis, commonly known as the hanging begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia pensilis is popular among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
