Bursera cinerea

Bursera cinerea, commonly known as white cedar or ghost tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bursera cinerea is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
