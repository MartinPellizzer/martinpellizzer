Bupleurum oppositifolium

Bupleurum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Bupleurum oppositifolium make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
