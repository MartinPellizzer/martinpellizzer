Barbarea vulgaris f. stolonifera

Barbarea vulgaris f. stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping winter cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in salads. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
