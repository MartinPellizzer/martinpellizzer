Bignonia punicoides

Bignonia punicoides, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, pendulous racemes of flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Bignonia punicoides prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.
