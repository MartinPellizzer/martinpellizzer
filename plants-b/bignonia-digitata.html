Bignonia digitata

Bignonia digitata, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along stream banks and in moist woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Bignonia digitata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
