Betula pubescens var. fragrans

Betula pubescens var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and small, fragrant catkins that release a sweet scent. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and turn yellow in autumn. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and has been used in traditional medicine.
