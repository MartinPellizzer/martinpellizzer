Berlinia angolensis var. subcordata

Berlinia angolensis var. subcordata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
