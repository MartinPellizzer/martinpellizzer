Bulbophyllum louisiadum

Bulbophyllum louisiadum, commonly known as the Louisiad Islands orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It is typically found growing on rock faces or in shaded forest areas, where it relies on specific symbiotic fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
