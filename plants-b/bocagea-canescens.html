Bocagea canescens

Bocagea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bocagea canescens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
