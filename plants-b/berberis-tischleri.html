Berberis tischleri

Berberis tischleri, commonly known as the Tischler's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
