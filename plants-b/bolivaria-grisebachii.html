Bolivaria grisebachii

Bolivaria grisebachii, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred shape.

Bolivaria grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
