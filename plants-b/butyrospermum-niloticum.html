Butyrospermum niloticum

Butyrospermum niloticum, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical fruits containing a single seed. The seed kernel is rich in fats and is the source of shea butter, widely used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
