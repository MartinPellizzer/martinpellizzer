Bradburya sagittata

Bradburya sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Bradburya sagittata is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
