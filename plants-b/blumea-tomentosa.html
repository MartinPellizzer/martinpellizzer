Blumea tomentosa

Blumea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its soft, velvety leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Blumea tomentosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
