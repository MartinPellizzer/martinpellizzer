Bahia confertiflora

Bahia confertiflora, commonly known as Brazilian grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control.

Its ability to form a thick sod makes it an effective ground cover in warm climates.
