Buxus microphylla var. sinica

Buxus microphylla var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 feet, making it ideal for topiary and hedging. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
