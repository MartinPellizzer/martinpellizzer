Bossiaea halophila

Bossiaea halophila, commonly known as the saltbush bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline or waterlogged soils, showcasing its halophytic nature. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid or coastal environments.

Bossiaea halophila plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
