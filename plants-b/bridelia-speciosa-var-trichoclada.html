Bridelia speciosa var. trichoclada

Bridelia speciosa var. trichoclada, commonly known as the velvetleaf bridelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, smooth seeds.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
