Baccharis anomala subsp. ramboana

Baccharis anomala subsp.

ramboana, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
