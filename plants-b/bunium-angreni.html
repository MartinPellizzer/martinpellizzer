Bunium angreni

Bunium angreni, commonly known as the Angreni fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems and finely divided leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
