Begonia trispathulata

Begonia trispathulata, commonly known as the three-lobed begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in both indoor and outdoor settings.
