Begonia spicata

Begonia spicata, commonly known as the spiculate begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
