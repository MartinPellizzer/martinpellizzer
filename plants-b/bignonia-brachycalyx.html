Bignonia brachycalyx

Bignonia brachycalyx, commonly known as the small-calyx crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its calyces are short and tubular, giving it its scientific name.

Bignonia brachycalyx is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial to full sunlight.
