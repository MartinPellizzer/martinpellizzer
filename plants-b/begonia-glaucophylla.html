Begonia glaucophylla

Begonia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia glaucophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
