Bletia sanguinea

Bletia sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Bletia sanguinea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
