Baccharis glomerata

Baccharis glomerata, commonly known as Spanish bayonet, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
