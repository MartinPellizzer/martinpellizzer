Bergenia coreana

Bergenia coreana, commonly known as Korean rock白菜, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Bergenia coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
