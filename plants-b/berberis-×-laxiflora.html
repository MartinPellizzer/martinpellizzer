Berberis × laxiflora

Berberis × laxiflora, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features spiny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in various climates.

However, it has become an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
