Berberis julianae

Berberis julianae, commonly known as Julian's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis julianae is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
