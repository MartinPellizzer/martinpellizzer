Bauhinia ligulata

Bauhinia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Bauhinia ligulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic features in gardens.
