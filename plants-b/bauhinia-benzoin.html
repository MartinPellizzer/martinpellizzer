Bauhinia benzoin

Bauhinia benzoin, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, papilionaceous flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
