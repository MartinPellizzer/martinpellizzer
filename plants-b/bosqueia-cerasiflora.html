Bosqueia cerasiflora

Bosqueia cerasiflora, commonly known as the sweet-pea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in fynbos vegetation. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh coastal conditions.

Bosqueia cerasiflora is also noted for its resilience to drought and salt spray.
