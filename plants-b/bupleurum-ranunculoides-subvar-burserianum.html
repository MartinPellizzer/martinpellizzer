Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. burserianum

Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. burserianum, commonly known as the Burserianum buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
