Bambusa viridis

Bambusa viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Bambusa viridis is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
