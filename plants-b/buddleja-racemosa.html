Buddleja racemosa

Buddleja racemosa, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces long, racemose inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, pale blue to purple flowers. Buddleja racemosa is notable for its ability to attract a wide variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
