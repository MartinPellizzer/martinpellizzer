Brosimum alicastrum subsp. alicastrum

Brosimum alicastrum subsp.

alicastrum, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates, making it a vital food source.

The plant belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing trees like the mulberry. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
