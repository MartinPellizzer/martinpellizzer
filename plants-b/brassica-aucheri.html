Brassica aucheri

Brassica aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Persian shallot, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, black seeds. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a mild garlic-like flavor.
