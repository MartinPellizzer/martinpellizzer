Bulbophyllum levatii subsp. levatii

Bulbophyllum levatii subsp.

levatii, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often golden-yellow inflorescences.

The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many epiphytic orchids. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
