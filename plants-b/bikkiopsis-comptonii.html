Bikkiopsis comptonii

Bikkiopsis comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing some drought tolerance.

Bikkiopsis comptonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
