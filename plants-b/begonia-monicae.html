Begonia monicae

Begonia monicae, commonly known as the Monica's begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
