Barleria mirabilis

Barleria mirabilis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It blooms throughout the year, especially in warmer months, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Barleria mirabilis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
