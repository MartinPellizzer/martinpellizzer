Borrichia frutescens

Borrichia frutescens, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. It has a short lifespan and is an important part of coastal ecosystems.

Borrichia frutescens is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind, making it well adapted to harsh maritime environments.
