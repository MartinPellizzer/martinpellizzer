Braya humilis var. novae-angliae

Braya humilis var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a compact rosette of linear, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
