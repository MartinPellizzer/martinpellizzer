Biauricula spathulata

Biauricula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed spathulate plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are broad and spatulate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The botanical interest of Biauricula spathulata lies in its unique leaf shape and its ecological role in forest understories.
