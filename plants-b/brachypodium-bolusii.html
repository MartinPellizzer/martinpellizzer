Brachypodium bolusii

Brachypodium bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Brachypodium bolusii is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
