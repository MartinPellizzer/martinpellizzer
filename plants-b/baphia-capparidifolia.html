Baphia capparidifolia

Baphia capparidifolia, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, green, edible fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically followed by elongated, ridged fruits with a bitter taste. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Baphia capparidifolia is often grown for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and has medicinal properties.
