Bionia tomentosa

Bionia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bionia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features hairy, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Bionia tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
