Baccharis penningtonii

Baccharis penningtonii, commonly known as Pennington's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

Baccharis penningtonii plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing wetland environments.
