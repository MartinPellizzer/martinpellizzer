Begonia caudilimba

Begonia caudilimba, commonly known as the trailing begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic value.
