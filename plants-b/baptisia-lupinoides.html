Baptisia lupinoides

Baptisia lupinoides, commonly known as the indigo bluestem, is a North American perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Baptisia lupinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
