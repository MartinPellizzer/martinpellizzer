Berchemia longipes

Berchemia longipes, commonly known as the longipes bushveld vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Berchemia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
