Bauhinia ramirezii

Bauhinia ramirezii, commonly known as the Ramirez's bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that resemble the shape of a fern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Bauhinia ramirezii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
