Bulbophyllum purpureum

Bulbophyllum purpureum, commonly known as the purple lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic orchid features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. It produces striking, fragrant inflorescences with numerous purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and humidity.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
