Bambusa senanensis

Bambusa senanensis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it suitable for landscaping and erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its strong, lightweight shoots, which can be used in construction and crafts.
