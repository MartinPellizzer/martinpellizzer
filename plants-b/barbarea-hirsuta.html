Barbarea hirsuta

Barbarea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Barbarea hirsuta is a perennial plant that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
