Bassia iranica var. gossypina

Bassia iranica var. gossypina, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
