Bulbophyllum napellii

Bulbophyllum napellii, commonly known as the Napo Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
