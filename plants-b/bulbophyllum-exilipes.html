Bulbophyllum exilipes

Bulbophyllum exilipes, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
