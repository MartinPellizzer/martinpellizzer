Begonia paleacea

Begonia paleacea, commonly known as the paleaceae begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that often resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pink.

Begonia paleacea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
