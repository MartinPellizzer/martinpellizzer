Baccharis divaricata

Baccharis divaricata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas.

Baccharis divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
