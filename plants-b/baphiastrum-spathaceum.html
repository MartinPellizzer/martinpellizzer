Baphiastrum spathaceum

Baphiastrum spathaceum, commonly known as the purple flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of bright purple to red bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by large, fleshy, colorful bracts, which serve to attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Baphiastrum spathaceum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and unique foliage.
