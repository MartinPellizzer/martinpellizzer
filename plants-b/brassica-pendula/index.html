Brassica Pendula

Brassica pendula is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is known for its delicate, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. This species belongs to the mustard family, which includes many edible plants. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
