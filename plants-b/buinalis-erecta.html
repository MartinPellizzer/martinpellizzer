Buinalis erecta

Buinalis erecta, commonly known as the upright buinalis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes well-known genera such as Amaryllis and Hippeastrum. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, providing a lush foliage base before the flowering period.

Buinalis erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its striking appearance.
