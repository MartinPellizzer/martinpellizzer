Bassovia setosa

Bassovia setosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Bassovia setosa is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
