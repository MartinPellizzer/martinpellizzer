Batidaea strigosa subsp. acalyphacea

Batidaea strigosa subsp.

acalyphacea, commonly known as the woolly batidaea, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Acanthaceae family.
