Bauhinia amatlana

Bauhinia amatlana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia amatlana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
