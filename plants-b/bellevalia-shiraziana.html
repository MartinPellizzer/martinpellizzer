Bellevalia shiraziana

Bellevalia shiraziana, commonly known as the Shiraz bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
