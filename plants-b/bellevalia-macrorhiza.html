Bellevalia macrorhiza

Bellevalia macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a fibrous root system and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Bellevalia macrorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
