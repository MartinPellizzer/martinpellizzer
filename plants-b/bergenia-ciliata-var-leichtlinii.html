Bergenia ciliata var. leichtlinii

Bergenia ciliata var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the creeping bergenia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, sturdy stalks. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Bergenia ciliata var. leichtlinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in colder climates.
