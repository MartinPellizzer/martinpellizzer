Brassica chinensis f. piaoh-tsai

Brassica chinensis f. piaoh-tsai, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or Chinese cabbage, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, leafy stems and tightly wrapped leaves that form a compact head, similar to other members of the Brassica genus. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild, slightly peppery flavor and is commonly used in Asian cuisines.
