Bulbophyllum algidum

Bulbophyllum algidum, commonly known as the cold orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
