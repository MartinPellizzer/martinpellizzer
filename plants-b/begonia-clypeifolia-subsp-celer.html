Begonia clypeifolia subsp. celer

Begonia clypeifolia subsp.

celer, commonly known as the fast-growing begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its rapid growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
