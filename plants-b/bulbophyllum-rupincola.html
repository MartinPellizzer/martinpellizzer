Bulbophyllum rupincola

Bulbophyllum rupincola, commonly known as the Rupincola orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid environments. The plant features clustered pseudobulbs and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
