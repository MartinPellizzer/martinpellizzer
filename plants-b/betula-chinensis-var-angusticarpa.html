Betula chinensis var. angusticarpa

Betula chinensis var. angusticarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated cones and smooth, white bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, turning yellow in autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
