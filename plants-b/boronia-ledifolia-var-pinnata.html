Boronia ledifolia var. pinnata

Boronia ledifolia var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
