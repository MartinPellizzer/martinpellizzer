Botryanthus lelievrei

Botryanthus lelievrei, commonly known as the Lelievre's bottlebrush, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bottle-shaped stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are small, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Botryanthus lelievrei is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
