Beyeria ledifolia var. angustifolia

Beyeria ledifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf beyeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is appreciated for both its foliage and floral display.
