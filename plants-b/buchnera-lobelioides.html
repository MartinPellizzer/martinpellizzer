Buchnera lobelioides

Buchnera lobelioides, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, alternate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

Buchnera lobelioides is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
