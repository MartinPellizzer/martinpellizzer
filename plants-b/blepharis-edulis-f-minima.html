Blepharis edulis f. minima

Blepharis edulis f. minima, commonly known as the dwarf glassworts, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in dunes or salt marshes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
