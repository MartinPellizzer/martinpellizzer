Buddleja shaanxiensis

Buddleja shaanxiensis, commonly known as the Shaanxi buddleja, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Buddleja shaanxiensis is noted for its adaptability and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
