Baeckea clavifolia

Baeckea clavifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baeckea, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Baeckea clavifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
