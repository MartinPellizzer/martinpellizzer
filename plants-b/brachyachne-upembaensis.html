Brachyachne upembaensis

Brachyachne upembaensis, commonly known as the Upemba grass, is a species of grass native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy or wetland areas. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
