Bactris fusca

Bactris fusca, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellow to orange in color. Bactris fusca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
