Boscia rotundifolia

Boscia rotundifolia, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, pear-like flavor. The plant has pinnate leaves with rounded lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

Boscia rotundifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
