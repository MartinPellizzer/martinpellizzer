Bromus silvaticus

Bromus silvaticus, commonly known as the wood brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Bromus silvaticus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
