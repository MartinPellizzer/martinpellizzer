Boechera sparsiflora var. subvillosa

Boechera sparsiflora var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
