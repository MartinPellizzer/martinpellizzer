Barleria pulchella

Barleria pulchella, commonly known as the red barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Barleria pulchella is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
