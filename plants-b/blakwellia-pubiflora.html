Blakwellia pubiflora

Blakwellia pubiflora, commonly known as the woolly blakwellia, is a flowering plant native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
