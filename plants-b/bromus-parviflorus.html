Bromus parviflorus

Bromus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower brome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a grass species that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinct sheath at the base.

Bromus parviflorus is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
