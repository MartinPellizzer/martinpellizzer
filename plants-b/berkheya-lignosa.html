Berkheya lignosa

Berkheya lignosa, commonly known as the hardy yellow dandelion, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
