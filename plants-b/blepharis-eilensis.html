Blepharis eilensis

Blepharis eilensis, commonly known as the Eil's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a beardtongue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
