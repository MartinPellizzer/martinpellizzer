Bromus patagonicus

Bromus patagonicus, commonly known as Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish glumes. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Bromus patagonicus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
