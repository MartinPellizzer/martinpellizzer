Bauhinia integerrima

Bauhinia integerrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. Its bark is smooth and grey, with a tendency to flake off in small pieces.

Bauhinia integerrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
