Begonia humericola

Begonia humericola, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
