Baccharis vincifolia

Baccharis vincifolia, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
