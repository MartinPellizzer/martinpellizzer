Bomarea spectabilis

Bomarea spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds.
