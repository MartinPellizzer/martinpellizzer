Begonia langsonensis

Begonia langsonensis, commonly known as the Langson begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and elliptic or ovate leaves with prominent veins.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
