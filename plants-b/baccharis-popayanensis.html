Baccharis popayanensis

Baccharis popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky slopes, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
