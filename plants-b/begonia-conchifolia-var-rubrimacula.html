Begonia conchifolia var. rubrimacula

Begonia conchifolia var. rubrimacula, commonly known as the red-spotted begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or maroon blotch on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
