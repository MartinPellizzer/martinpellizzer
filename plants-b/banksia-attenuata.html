Banksia attenuata

Banksia attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking yellow floral spikes that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are an important food source for native bees and birds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
