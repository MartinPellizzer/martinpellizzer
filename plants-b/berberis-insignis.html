Berberis insignis

Berberis insignis, commonly known as the Andean barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Berberis insignis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
