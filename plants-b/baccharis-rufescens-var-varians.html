Baccharis rufescens var. varians

Baccharis rufescens var. varians, commonly known as the variable baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are often branched and woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
