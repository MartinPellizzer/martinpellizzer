Berberis monyulensis

Berberis monyulensis, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
