Beyeria similis

Beyeria similis, commonly known as the dwarf African violet, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, velvety, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under proper care. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Beyeria similis is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
