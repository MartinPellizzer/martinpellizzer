Bactris negrensis var. carolensis

Bactris negrensis var. carolensis, commonly known as the Carolinian palm, is a small, fan-shaped palm native to the Caribbean islands.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant is adapted to coastal and lowland habitats, thriving in sandy soils and humid climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
