Bambusa capitata

Bambusa capitata, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clumping form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the top of the stalks. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Bambusa capitata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to thrive in various soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
