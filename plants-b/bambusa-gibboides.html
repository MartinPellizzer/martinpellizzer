Bambusa gibboides

Bambusa gibboides, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often curved culms. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, making it suitable for small spaces or container gardening. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa gibboides is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscape design and as a decorative plant.
