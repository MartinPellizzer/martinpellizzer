Benzoin bifarium

Benzoin bifarium, commonly known as the Borneo benzoin, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has smooth, greyish bark with a distinctive fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree produces a gum resin that is harvested for its aromatic properties and medicinal applications.
