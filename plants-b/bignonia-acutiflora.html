Bignonia acutiflora

Bignonia acutiflora, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking orange to red tubular flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for warm climates.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
