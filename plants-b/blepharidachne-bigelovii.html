Blepharidachne bigelovii

Blepharidachne bigelovii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is characterized by its fine, grass-like appearance and is often found in disturbed soils.
