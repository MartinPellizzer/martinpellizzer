Bertolonia × mirandae

Bertolonia × mirandae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Bertolonia species native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Bertolonia genus.
