Blaeria fastigiata

Blaeria fastigiata, commonly known as the upright blaeria, is a unique species belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
