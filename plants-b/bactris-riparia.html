Bactris riparia

Bactris riparia, commonly known as the red-brown palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, red-fleshed fruits. Bactris riparia is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its unique botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in which it is found.
