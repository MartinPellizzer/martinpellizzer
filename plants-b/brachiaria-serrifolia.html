Brachiaria serrifolia

Brachiaria serrifolia, commonly known as serrated brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, serrated leaf blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Brachiaria serrifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
