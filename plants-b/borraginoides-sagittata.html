Borraginoides sagittata

Borraginoides sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf borage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Borraginoides sagittata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
