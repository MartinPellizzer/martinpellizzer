Blumea gariepina

Blumea gariepina, commonly known as the Cape Blumea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, rounded fruits that are dispersed by wind.
