Bothriochloa saccharoides

Bothriochloa saccharoides, commonly known as sugar grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, often forming dense clumps with a fibrous root system. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and useful in certain agricultural contexts.
