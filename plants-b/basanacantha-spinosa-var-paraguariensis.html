Basanacantha spinosa var. paraguariensis

Basanacantha spinosa var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan thornvetch, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
