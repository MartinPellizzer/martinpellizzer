Bromelia paniculigera

Bromelia paniculigera, commonly known as the panicled bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears numerous small, showy flowers. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central water-holding structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bromelia paniculigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
