Barkeria skinneri var. superbum

Barkeria skinneri var. superbum, commonly known as the giant Barkeria, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
