Bulbophyllum cordemoyi

Bulbophyllum cordemoyi, commonly known as the Cordemoy's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation.
