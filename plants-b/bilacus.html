Bilacus

Bilacus, commonly known as the bilacus plant, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
