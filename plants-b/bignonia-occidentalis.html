Bignonia occidentalis

Bignonia occidentalis, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia occidentalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
