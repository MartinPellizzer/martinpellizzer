Begonia lanuzaensis

Begonia lanuzaensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Begonia lanuzaensis prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
