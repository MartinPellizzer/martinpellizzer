Buddleja asiatica var. sundaica

Buddleja asiatica var. sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces long, cylindrical clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
