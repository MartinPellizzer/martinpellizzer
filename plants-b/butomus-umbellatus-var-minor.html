Butomus umbellatus var. minor

Butomus umbellatus var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and jointed stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
