Botryanthus vulgaris var. strangwaysii

Botryanthus vulgaris var. strangwaysii, commonly known as the Strangways' rose.

This variety is a hybrid within the genus Botryanthus, characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescence that resembles a cluster of grapes. The plant typically features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal in horticultural settings.
