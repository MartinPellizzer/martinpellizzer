Brassica juncea var. multiceps

Brassica juncea var. multiceps, commonly known as Chinese kale or gai lan, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, thick, and tender stalks with tightly clustered leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. Its botanical features include small, yellow flowers that form in racemes, and it produces round, smooth pods containing seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal uses.
