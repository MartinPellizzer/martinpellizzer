Begonia bullatifolia

Begonia bullatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
