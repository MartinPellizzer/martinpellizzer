Brugmansia × knightii

Brugmansia × knightii, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can reach heights of up to 15 meters when mature. Its flowers are typically white, yellow, or orange, and they emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening.

Brugmansia × knightii is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
