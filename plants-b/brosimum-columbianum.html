Brosimum columbianum

Brosimum columbianum, commonly known as the Colombian balsa, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its wood is lightweight and historically valued for use in model building and insulation.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.
