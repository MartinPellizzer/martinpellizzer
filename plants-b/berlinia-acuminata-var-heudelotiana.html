Berlinia acuminata var. heudelotiana

Berlinia acuminata var. heudelotiana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in moist lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
