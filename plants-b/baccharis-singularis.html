Baccharis singularis

Baccharis singularis, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with grey-green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baccharis singularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
