Brassica cheiranthos var. major

Brassica cheiranthos var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered wallflower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers, which are typically four-petaled and arranged in racemes. The plant has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
