Besleria acutifolia

Besleria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved besleria, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped, acuminate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are showy, with tubular corollas that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
