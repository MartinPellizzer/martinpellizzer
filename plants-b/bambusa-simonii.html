Bambusa simonii

Bambusa simonii, commonly known as the Simon's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, providing a lush appearance, while its culms are strong and durable.

Bambusa simonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and practical uses in traditional and modern applications.
