Brownea rosa-de-monte

Brownea rosa-de-monte, commonly known as the rose-of-the-mountains, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences consisting of multiple pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers have five petals, with a distinctive white or pink center, and are known for their long-lasting display.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, exotic appearance.
