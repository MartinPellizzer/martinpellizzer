Bigelowia coronopifolia

Bigelowia coronopifolia, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
