Begonia semperflorens

Begonia semperflorens, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. This perennial plant is often grown as an annual in temperate climates due to its preference for warm temperatures. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

The plant's compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a favored ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
