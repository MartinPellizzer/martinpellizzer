Berberis discolor

Berberis discolor, commonly known as the red barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the autumn, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green leaves in the summer. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Berberis discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also notable for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively low maintenance.
