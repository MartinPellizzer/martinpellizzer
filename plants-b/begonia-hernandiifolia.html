Begonia hernandiifolia

Begonia hernandiifolia, commonly known as the silver velvet begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, silver-green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners who appreciate its unique foliage.
