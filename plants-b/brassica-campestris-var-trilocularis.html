Brassica campestris var. trilocularis

Brassica campestris var. trilocularis, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and fields.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seed dispersal and vegetative propagation.
