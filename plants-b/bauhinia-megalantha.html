Bauhinia megalantha

Bauhinia megalantha, commonly known as the giant bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically divided into three to five lobes. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color, blooming in clusters. Bauhinia megalantha is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
