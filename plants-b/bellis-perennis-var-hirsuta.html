Bellis perennis var. hirsuta

Bellis perennis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lawn daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, often appearing in lawns and meadows. The variety 'hirsuta' is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it a unique texture compared to other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a hardy and resilient plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
