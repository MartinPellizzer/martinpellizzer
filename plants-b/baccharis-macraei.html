Baccharis macraei

Baccharis macraei, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
