Bulbostylis leiolepis

Bulbostylis leiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Bulbostylis leiolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
