Barbarea perennis

Barbarea perennis, commonly known as perennial winter cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lobed or heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It is known for its ability to self-seed and spread readily, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.

Barbarea perennis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
