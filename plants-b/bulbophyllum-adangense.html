Bulbophyllum adangense

Bulbophyllum adangense, commonly known as the Adangense orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.
