Barleria holubii subsp. holubii

Barleria holubii subsp.

holubii, commonly known as the Holub's barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
