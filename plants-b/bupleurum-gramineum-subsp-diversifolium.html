Bupleurum gramineum subsp. diversifolium

Bupleurum gramineum subsp.

diversifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf bupleurum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lanceolate to ovate leaves that vary in shape and size.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
