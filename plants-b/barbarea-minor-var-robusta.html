Barbarea minor var. robusta

Barbarea minor var. robusta, commonly known as the rock cress or winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, bright green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Barbarea minor var. robusta is valued for its hardiness and ability to colonize challenging habitats.
