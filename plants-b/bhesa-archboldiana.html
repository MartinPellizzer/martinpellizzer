Bhesa archboldiana

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and possesses small, elongated leaves with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are pollinated by specific insect species. Its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant resilience.
