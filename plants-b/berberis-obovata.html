Berberis obovata

Berberis obovata, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental value and is commonly used in landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
