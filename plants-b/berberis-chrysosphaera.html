Berberis chrysosphaera

Berberis chrysosphaera, commonly known as the golden globe barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow foliage, which becomes especially striking in the autumn. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

However, it is important to note that it can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
