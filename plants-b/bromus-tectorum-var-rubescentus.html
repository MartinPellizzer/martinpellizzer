Bromus tectorum var. rubescentus

Bromus tectorum var. rubescentus, commonly known as the red-stemmed cheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is highly invasive in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a significant role in shaping ecosystems due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
