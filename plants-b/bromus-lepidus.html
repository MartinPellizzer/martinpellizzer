Bromus lepidus

Bromus lepidus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
