Bambusa helferi

Bambusa helferi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow coloration. The plant grows as a tall, clump-forming bamboo with slender, hollow stems that are often segmented. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Bambusa helferi is valued for ornamental purposes in gardens and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
