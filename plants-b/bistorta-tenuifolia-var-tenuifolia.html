Bistorta tenuifolia var. tenuifolia

Bistorta tenuifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Bistorta tenuifolia var. tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
