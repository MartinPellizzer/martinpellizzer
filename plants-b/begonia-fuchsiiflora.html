Begonia fuchsiiflora

Begonia fuchsiiflora, commonly known as the Fuchsian begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Begonia fuchsiiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and indoor cultivation.
