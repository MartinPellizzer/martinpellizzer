Bermudiana patagonica

Bermudiana patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid regions.
