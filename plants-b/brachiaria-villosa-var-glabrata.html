Brachiaria villosa var. glabrata

Brachiaria villosa var. glabrata, commonly known as hairy bladegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as pastures, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its rhizomatous and stolonal growth allows it to form extensive mats, making it a competitive weed in agricultural settings.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged, contributing to its reproductive success in various environments.
