Bukiniczia cabulica

Bukiniczia cabulica, commonly known as the Afghan daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Bukiniczia cabulica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
