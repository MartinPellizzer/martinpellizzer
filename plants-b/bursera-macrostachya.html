Bursera macrostachya

Bursera macrostachya, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.

The tree is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
