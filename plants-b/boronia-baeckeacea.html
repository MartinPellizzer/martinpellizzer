Boronia baeckeacea

Boronia baeckeacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
