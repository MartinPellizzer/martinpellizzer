Bakeropteris triphylla

Bakeropteris triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf water fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the order Osmundales.

It is characterized by its distinctive triphylly structure, where each frond is divided into three leaflets. This plant thrived during the Carboniferous period, contributing to the formation of coal deposits. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

The fossilized remains of Bakeropteris triphylla provide valuable insights into ancient plant life and paleoenvironments.
