Bertya cunninghamii subsp. rupicola

Bertya cunninghamii subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rough bertya, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. Bertya cunninghamii subsp.

rupicola is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
