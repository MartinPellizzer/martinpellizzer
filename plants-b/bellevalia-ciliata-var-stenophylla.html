Bellevalia ciliata var. stenophylla

Bellevalia ciliata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
