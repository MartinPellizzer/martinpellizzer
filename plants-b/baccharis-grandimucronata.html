Baccharis grandimucronata

Baccharis grandimucronata, commonly known as the white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and often grayish.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
