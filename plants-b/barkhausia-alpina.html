Barkhausia alpina

Barkhausia alpina, commonly known as the alpine barkhausia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish. Its botanical features make it a hardy species adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Barkhausia alpina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
