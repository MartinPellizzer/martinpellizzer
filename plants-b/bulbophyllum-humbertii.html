Bulbophyllum humbertii

Bulbophyllum humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, with a distinctive lip structure. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
