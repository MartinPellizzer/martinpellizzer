Berberis hallii

Berberis hallii, commonly known as the Hall's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
