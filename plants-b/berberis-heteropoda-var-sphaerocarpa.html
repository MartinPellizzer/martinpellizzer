Berberis heteropoda var. sphaerocarpa

Berberis heteropoda var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found at high altitudes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
