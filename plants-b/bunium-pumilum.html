Bunium pumilum

Bunium pumilum, commonly known as dwarf fennel, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and it produces seeds that are finely divided and wind-dispersed.

Bunium pumilum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
