Buxus baracoensis

Buxus baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoen boxwood, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Buxaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, making it a valuable ornamental plant in horticulture.

Buxus baracoensis is noted for its slow growth rate and ability to form thick, rounded shapes when pruned.
