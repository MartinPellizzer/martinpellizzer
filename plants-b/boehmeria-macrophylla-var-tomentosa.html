Boehmeria macrophylla var. tomentosa

Boehmeria macrophylla var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The botanical features of this species make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
