Bauhinia kockiana var. sericeinervia

Bauhinia kockiana var. sericeinervia, commonly known as the silky-veined bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves with silky, fine hairs along the veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
