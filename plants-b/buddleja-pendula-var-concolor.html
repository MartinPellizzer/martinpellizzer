Buddleja pendula var. concolor

Buddleja pendula var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features long, drooping branches that bear clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
