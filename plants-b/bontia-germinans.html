Bontia germinans

Bontia germinans, commonly known as the Florida germander, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or disturbed habitats.

Bontia germinans is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
