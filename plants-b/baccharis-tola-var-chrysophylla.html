Baccharis tola var. chrysophylla

Baccharis tola var. chrysophylla, commonly known as goldenleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish to golden flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
