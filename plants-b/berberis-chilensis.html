Berberis chilensis

Berberis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
