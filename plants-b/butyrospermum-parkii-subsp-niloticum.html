Butyrospermum parkii subsp. niloticum

Butyrospermum parkii subsp.

niloticum, commonly known as shea tree, is a species of the Sapotaceae family native to Africa. It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and produces a valuable seed kernel used in cosmetics and food products.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a dense canopy, making it an important component of savanna ecosystems. Its fruit, which is a yellowish drupe, contains the oil-rich seeds that are harvested for their economic and nutritional value.

The plant is well adapted to seasonal climates and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.
