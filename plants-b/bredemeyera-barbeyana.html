Bredemeyera barbeyana

Bredemeyera barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's kalahari, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Bredemeyera barbeyana is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
