Boopis gracilis var. lazulina

Boopis gracilis var. lazulina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
