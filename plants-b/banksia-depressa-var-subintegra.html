Banksia depressa var. subintegra

Banksia depressa var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in coastal and inland regions.
