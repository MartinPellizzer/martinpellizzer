Brickellia californica var. normalis

Brickellia californica var. normalis, commonly known as the California brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as chaparral and coastal sage scrub.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
