Butea ridleyi

Butea ridleyi, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

The bark and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
