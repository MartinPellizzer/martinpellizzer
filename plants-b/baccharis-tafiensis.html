Baccharis tafiensis

Baccharis tafiensis, commonly known as Tafiense Baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract various pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
