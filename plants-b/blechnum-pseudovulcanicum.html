Blechnum pseudovulcanicum

Blechnum pseudovulcanicum, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the rhizomes are thick and fibrous.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
