Blitum spathulatum

Blitum spathulatum, commonly known as broadleaf filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to spoon-shaped, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
