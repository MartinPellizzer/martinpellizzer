Biophytum juninense

Biophytum juninense, commonly known as the Andean flame plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
