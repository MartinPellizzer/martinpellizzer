Boronia clavata

Boronia clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils.

Boronia clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
