Bulbophyllum hirsutum

Bulbophyllum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, often reddish-brown stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum hirsutum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique appearance.
