Bambusa multiplex var. nana

Bambusa multiplex var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact variety of bamboo characterized by its short, sturdy stalks and dense foliage.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it ideal for small spaces or container gardening. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a vibrant green color that adds aesthetic value to gardens.

This variety is also valued for its resilience and ability to form dense clumps, providing both ornamental and functional benefits in landscaping.
