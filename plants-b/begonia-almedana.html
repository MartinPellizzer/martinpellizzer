Begonia almedana

Begonia almedana, commonly known as the Almedana begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green color with contrasting veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
