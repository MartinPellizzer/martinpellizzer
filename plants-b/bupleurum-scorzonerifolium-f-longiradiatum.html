Bupleurum scorzonerifolium f. longiradiatum

Bupleurum scorzonerifolium f. longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-rayed garden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
