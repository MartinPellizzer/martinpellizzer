Bignonia inaequalis

Bignonia inaequalis, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinct aroma.

Bignonia inaequalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
