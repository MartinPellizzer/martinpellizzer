Blumea runcinata

Blumea runcinata, commonly known as the broad-leafed blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Blumea runcinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
