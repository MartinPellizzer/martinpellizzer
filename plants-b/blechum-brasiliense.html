Blechum brasiliense

Blechum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian blechum, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding pool. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
