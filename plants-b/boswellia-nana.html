Boswellia nana

Boswellia nana, commonly known as dwarf frankincense, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is harvested for use in incense and traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, leathery, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Boswellia nana is a member of the Burseraceae family, which includes other frankincense-producing trees.
