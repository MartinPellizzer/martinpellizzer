Bigelovia elata

Bigelovia elata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, white seed heads that disperse easily. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
