Begonia baliensis

Begonia baliensis, commonly known as the Bali begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Indonesian island of Bali.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct reddish underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Begonia baliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
