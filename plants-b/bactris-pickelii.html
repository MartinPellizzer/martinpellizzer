Bactris pickelii

Bactris pickelii, commonly known as the Pickel's palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
