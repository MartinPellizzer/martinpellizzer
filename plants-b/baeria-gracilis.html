Baeria gracilis

Baeria gracilis, commonly known as the slender baeria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
