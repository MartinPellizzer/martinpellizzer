Balsamorhiza hirsuta var. neglecta

Balsamorhiza hirsuta var. neglecta, commonly known as the desert balsam or western balsam, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush habitats. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
