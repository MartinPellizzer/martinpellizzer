Bulbophyllum unigibbum

Bulbophyllum unigibbum, commonly known as the single-lobed orchid, is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, elongated lip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water and nutrients, adapting it to its natural habitat.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
