Berberis hookeri var. microcarpa

Berberis hookeri var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its berries, which are edible, add a decorative touch in the fall.
