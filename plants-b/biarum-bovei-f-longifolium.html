Biarum bovei f. longifolium

Biarum bovei f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a botanical variant of the species Biarum bovei.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical form. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white, with a distinctive three-lobed throat. It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
