Bambusa oldhamii

Bambusa oldhamii, commonly known as the oldham bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a smooth, light green surface. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the nodes. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.

Bambusa oldhamii is also appreciated for its resilience to environmental stress and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
