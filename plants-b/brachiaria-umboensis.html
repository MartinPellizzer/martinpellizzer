Brachiaria umboensis

Brachiaria umboensis, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.

Brachiaria umboensis is known for its high biomass production and is used in forage and erosion control.
