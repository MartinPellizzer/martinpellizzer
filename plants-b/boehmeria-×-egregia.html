Boehmeria × egregia

Boehmeria × egregia, commonly known as the Chinese hemp or giant arrowroot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the荨麻科 (Urticaceae) family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are fibrous, making it valuable for industrial and textile purposes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
