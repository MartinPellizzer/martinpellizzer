Bifrenaria grandis

Bifrenaria grandis, commonly known as the giant bifrenaria, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This climbing plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, showcasing a vibrant green color with a slightly waxy texture. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that transition into bright red berries, attracting various bird species. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor collections.
