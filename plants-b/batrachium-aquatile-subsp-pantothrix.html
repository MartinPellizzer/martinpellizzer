Batrachium aquatile subsp. pantothrix

Batrachium aquatile subsp.

pantothrix, commonly known as the aquatic frog orchid, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific wetland habitats in South America. It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated, strap-like leaves and a distinctive inflorescence with showy, frog-like flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.

Due to habitat loss and degradation, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
