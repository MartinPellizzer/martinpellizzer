Biancaea

Biancaea, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They are characterized by their compound leaves and showy, often white or pale pink flowers. Biancaea species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Some species are also cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive blooms.
