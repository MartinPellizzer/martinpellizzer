Boehmeria grandis

Boehmeria grandis, commonly known as Chinese hemp or giant hemp, is a flowering plant in the nettle family, Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its strong, fibrous stalks. Boehmeria grandis is valued for its fibers, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or semi-woody structure depending on the environmental conditions.
