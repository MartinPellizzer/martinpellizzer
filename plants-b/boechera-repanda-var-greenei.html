Boechera repanda var. greenei

Boechera repanda var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
