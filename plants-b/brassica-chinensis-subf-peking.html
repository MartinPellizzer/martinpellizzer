Brassica chinensis subf. peking

Brassica chinensis subf.

peking, commonly known as Chinese kale or kai lan, is a leafy vegetable native to East Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, tender leaves and thick, edible stalks.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 40-60 cm and thrives in temperate climates. It is cultivated for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins A and C, as well as dietary fiber.

This variety is well-suited for both home gardens and commercial farming due to its relatively easy cultivation and adaptability.
