Beilschmiedia filicifolia

Beilschmiedia filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that resemble ferns, hence its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-colored berries.

Beilschmiedia filicifolia is often found in shaded understory habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
