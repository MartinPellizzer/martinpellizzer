Berberis conferta var. typica

Berberis conferta var. typica, commonly known as the dense barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, spiny foliage with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
