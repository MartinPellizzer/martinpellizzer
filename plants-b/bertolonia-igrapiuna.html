Bertolonia igrapiuna

Bertolonia igrapiuna, commonly known as the pink ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink or almost white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
