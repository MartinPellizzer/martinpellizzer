Boscia albitrunca var. parvifolia

Boscia albitrunca var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-trunked boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale, fissured bark and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance and succulence.

The species is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
