Berberis nemorosa

Berberis nemorosa, commonly known as shrub barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a waxy coating, contributing to its ornamental value.

Berberis nemorosa is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
