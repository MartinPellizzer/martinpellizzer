Benthamia japonica var. minor

Benthamia japonica var. minor, commonly known as the Japanese benthamia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in the spring, are usually pale blue or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
