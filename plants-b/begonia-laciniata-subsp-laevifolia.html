Begonia laciniata subsp. laevifolia

Begonia laciniata subsp.

laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and moderate care requirements.
