Bihai reticulata

Bihai reticulata, commonly known as the giant daylily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Bihai reticulata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Its brief blooming period, typically in summer, adds a dramatic flair to landscapes.
