Badiera virgata var. scabridula

Badiera virgata var. scabridula, commonly known as the roughleaf badiera, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a rough, scabrous texture, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Its unique foliage makes it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
