Belmontia cordata var. intermedia

Belmontia cordata var. intermedia, commonly known as the pink river hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
