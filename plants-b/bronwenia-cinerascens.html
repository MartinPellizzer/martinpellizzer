Bronwenia cinerascens

Bronwenia cinerascens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Bronwenia cinerascens is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
