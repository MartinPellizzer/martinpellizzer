Baphia marceliana

Baphia marceliana, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

Baphia marceliana is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
