Bauhinia acuruana var. nitida

Bauhinia acuruana var. nitida, commonly known as the velvetwood or silky bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
