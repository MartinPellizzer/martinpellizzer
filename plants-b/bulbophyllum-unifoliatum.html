Bulbophyllum unifoliatum

Bulbophyllum unifoliatum, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, single leaf that often has a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
