Bambos

Bambos, commonly known as the bamboo plant, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and rapid growth rate. Bambos can grow in a variety of climates and soils, making it highly adaptable. The plant is valued for its strength and flexibility, which make it useful in construction and crafts.

Its rhizomes also play a role in soil stabilization and erosion control.
