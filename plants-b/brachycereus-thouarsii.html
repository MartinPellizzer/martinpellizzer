Brachycereus thouarsii

Brachycereus thouarsii, commonly known as the Saint Helena cactus, is a unique species native to the island of Saint Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a small, spiny cactus with a compact, globular shape and is adapted to the island's arid climate. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in the nutrient-poor, rocky soils of its native habitat.

Brachycereus thouarsii is considered a rare and endemic species, making it a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
