Baccharis grossedentata

Baccharis grossedentata, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a common sight in disturbed areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
