Barbarea praecox

Barbarea praecox, commonly known as early winter cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, yellow pods that contain the seeds. Its leaves are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Barbarea praecox is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.
