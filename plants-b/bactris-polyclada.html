Bactris polyclada

Bactris polyclada, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the plant plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.

Bactris polyclada is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
