Boophane

Boophane, commonly known as the "dwarf dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their small, dandelion-like yellow flowers and basal rosettes of leaves. Boophane species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. They have a short stem and produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
