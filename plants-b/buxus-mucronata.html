Buxus mucronata

Buxus mucronata, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green color, with a leathery texture. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in horticulture for its dense growth habit and ability to form compact hedges.

Buxus mucronata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
