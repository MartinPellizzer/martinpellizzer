Barleria argentea var. petiolosa

Barleria argentea var. petiolosa, commonly known as silverleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is an erect herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
