Bambusa purpurascens

Bambusa purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking purple-colored culms, which become more intense as the plant matures. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
