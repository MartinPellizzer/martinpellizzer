Bromopsis taimyrensis

Bromopsis taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyren sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as tundra and wet meadows. Bromopsis taimyrensis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various arctic fauna.

Its low-growing form and cold tolerance make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
