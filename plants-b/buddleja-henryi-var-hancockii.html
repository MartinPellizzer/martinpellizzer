Buddleja henryi var. hancockii

Buddleja henryi var. hancockii, commonly known as Henry's Buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, pale purple to lavender flowers in late summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and they turn yellow in autumn. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

It is also valued for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
