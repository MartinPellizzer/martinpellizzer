Brachypodium barbinode

Brachypodium barbinode, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Brachypodium barbinode is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
