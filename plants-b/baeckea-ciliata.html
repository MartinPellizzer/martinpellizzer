Baeckea ciliata

Baeckea ciliata, commonly known as the silky baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are often visited by insects such as bees and flies. Baeckea ciliata is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
