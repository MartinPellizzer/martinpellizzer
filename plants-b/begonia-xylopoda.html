Begonia xylopoda

Begonia xylopoda, commonly known as the wood begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Begonia xylopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
