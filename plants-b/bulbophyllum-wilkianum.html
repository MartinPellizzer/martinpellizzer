Bulbophyllum wilkianum

Bulbophyllum wilkianum, commonly known as the Wilkian's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum wilkianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
