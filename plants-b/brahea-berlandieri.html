Brahea berlandieri

Brahea berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a striking evergreen palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of blue-gray, fan-shaped leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that attract various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and as a landscape specimen.
