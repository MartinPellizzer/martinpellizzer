Barleria micans

Barleria micans, commonly known as the narrowleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Barleria micans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
