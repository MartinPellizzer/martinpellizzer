Betonica officinalis subsp. alpigena

Betonica officinalis subsp.

alpigena, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and open woodlands.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Boraginaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
