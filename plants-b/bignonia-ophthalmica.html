Bignonia ophthalmica

Bignonia ophthalmica, commonly known as the eye-flower or golden cross vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.
