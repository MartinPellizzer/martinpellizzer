Banisteria terminalis

Banisteria terminalis, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small showers of color. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Banisteria terminalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
