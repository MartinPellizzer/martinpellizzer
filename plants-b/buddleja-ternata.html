Buddleja ternata

Buddleja ternata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buddleja ternata is also valued for its adaptability and ability to grow in a range of environmental conditions.
