Bulbophyllum orbiculare subsp. cassideum

Bulbophyllum orbiculare subsp.

cassideum, commonly known as the Cassideum Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and are covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, bloom in summer and are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for their unique appearance.
