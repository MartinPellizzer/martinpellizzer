Boehmeria stenostachya

Boehmeria stenostachya, commonly known as Chinese hemp or giant nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on long, slender stalks. Its fibrous stems are valued for their strong, durable fibers used in traditional textiles and papermaking.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
