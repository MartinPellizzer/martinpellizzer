Butea braamiana

Butea braamiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red floral display during the flowering season, which occurs in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Butea braamiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.
