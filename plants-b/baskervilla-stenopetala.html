Baskervilla stenopetala

Baskervilla stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled baskervilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.
