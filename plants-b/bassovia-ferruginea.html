Bassovia ferruginea

Bassovia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with rust-colored markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
