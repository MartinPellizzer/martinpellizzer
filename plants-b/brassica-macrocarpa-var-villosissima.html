Brassica macrocarpa var. villosissima

Brassica macrocarpa var. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly cabbage, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both decorative and culinary purposes.
