Brassica Campestris Var. Napus

Brassica campestris var. napus is a versatile and economically significant plant known for its oil-rich seeds. This variety is commonly referred to as rapeseed and is widely cultivated across temperate regions worldwide. It plays a crucial role in agriculture as a source of vegetable oil and animal feed. The plant is also valued for its ability to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. Its importance extends beyond food production to include biofuel and industrial applications.
