Bidens cosmoides

Bidens cosmoides, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its seeds are surrounded by bracts that aid in wind dispersal.

Bidens cosmoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
