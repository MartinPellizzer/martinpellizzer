Banksia sphaerocarpa var. pumilio

Banksia sphaerocarpa var. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf banksia, is a compact shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that are typically yellow to orange in color, surrounded by leaf-like bracts. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
