Buphthalmum procumbens

Buphthalmum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sunflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, spreading stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Buphthalmum procumbens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
