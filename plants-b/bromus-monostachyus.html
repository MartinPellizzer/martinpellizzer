Bromus monostachyus

Bromus monostachyus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Bromus monostachyus is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
