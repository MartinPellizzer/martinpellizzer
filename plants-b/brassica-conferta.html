Brassica conferta

Brassica conferta, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear.

Brassica conferta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
