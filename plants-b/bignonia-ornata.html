Bignonia ornata

Bignonia ornata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

Bignonia ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
