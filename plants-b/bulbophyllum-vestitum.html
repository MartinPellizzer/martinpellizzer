Bulbophyllum vestitum

Bulbophyllum vestitum, commonly known as the dressed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted for efficient water retention.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
