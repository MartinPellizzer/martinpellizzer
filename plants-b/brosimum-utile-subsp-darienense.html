Brosimum utile subsp. darienense

Brosimum utile subsp.

darienense, commonly known as the Darien beautyleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
