Beta cicla variegata

Beta cicla variegata, commonly known as the variegated beetroot, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, white, and red. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other leafy vegetables like spinach. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their distinct flavor and visual appeal.

The variegation in its foliage is due to genetic mutations that affect chlorophyll production.
