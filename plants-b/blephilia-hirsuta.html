Blephilia hirsuta

Blephilia hirsuta, commonly known as wooly betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
