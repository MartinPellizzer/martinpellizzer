Bidens leucanthema var. humilis

Bidens leucanthema var. humilis, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender stems and small, yellow flowers with two white rays, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has hairy leaves and produces numerous seeds that are often attached to hairy structures, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
