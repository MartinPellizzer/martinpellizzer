Begonia burmensis

Begonia burmensis, commonly known as the Burmese begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and floral display.
