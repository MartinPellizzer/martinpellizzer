Buxus cucullata

Buxus cucullata, commonly known as the hooded boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive hood-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in compact clusters. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to be shaped into formal topiaries.

Buxus cucullata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden borders and foundation plantings.
