Brassica rapa subsp. pekinensis

Brassica rapa subsp.

pekinensis, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its thick, leafy stems and compact rosette of leaves, which are typically green or purple in color.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and flowers, which are used in various traditional Chinese dishes.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
