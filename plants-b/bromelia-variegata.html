Bromelia variegata

Bromelia variegata, commonly known as the variegated bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with distinct variegation patterns. The plant typically features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can range in color from green to red, yellow, or white. It produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that can bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Bromelia variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
