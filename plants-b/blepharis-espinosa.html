Blepharis espinosa

Blepharis espinosa, commonly known as the spiked glassworts, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

Blepharis espinosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
