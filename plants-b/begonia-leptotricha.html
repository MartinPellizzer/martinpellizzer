Begonia leptotricha

Begonia leptotricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia leptotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
