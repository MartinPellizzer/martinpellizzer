Baccharis xylophylla

Baccharis xylophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
