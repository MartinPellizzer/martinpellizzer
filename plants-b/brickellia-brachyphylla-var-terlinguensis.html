Brickellia brachyphylla var. terlinguensis

Brickellia brachyphylla var. terlinguensis, commonly known as the Terlinguilla brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
