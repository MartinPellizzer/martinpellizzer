Banksia pinnatisecta

Banksia pinnatisecta, commonly known as the serrated banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stems and its distinctive, cone-like inflorescences that contain numerous yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in heathlands and dry forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
