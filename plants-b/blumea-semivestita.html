Blumea semivestita

Blumea semivestita, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, often blooming in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
