Bromus mollis var. leiostachys

Bromus mollis var. leiostachys, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
