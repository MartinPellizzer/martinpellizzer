Begonia iridifolia

Begonia iridifolia, commonly known as the rainbow begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from purple to pink and white, often resembling petals. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Begonia iridifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
