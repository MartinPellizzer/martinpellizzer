Begonia rhopalocarpa

Begonia rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture, making it well-suited for arid or rocky environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.
