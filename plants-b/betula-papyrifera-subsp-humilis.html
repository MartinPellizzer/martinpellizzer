Betula papyrifera subsp. humilis

Betula papyrifera subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the eastern paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it its common name.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, oval leaves are alternate and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
