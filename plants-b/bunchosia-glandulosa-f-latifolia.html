Bunchosia glandulosa f. latifolia

Bunchosia glandulosa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bunchosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
