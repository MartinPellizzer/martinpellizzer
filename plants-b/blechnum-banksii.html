Blechnum banksii

Blechnum banksii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and the fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern.

Blechnum banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
