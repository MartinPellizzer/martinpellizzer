Brachystelma galpinii

Brachystelma galpinii, commonly known as the Cape karoo orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with a single, short stem and a cluster of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
