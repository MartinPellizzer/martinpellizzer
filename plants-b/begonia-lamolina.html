Begonia lamolina

Begonia lamolina, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Begonia lamolina thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
