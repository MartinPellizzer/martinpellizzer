Borago cordifolia

Borago cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
