Begonia sinensis var. haemaloneura

Begonia sinensis var. haemaloneura, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with distinctive red veins that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden beds.

Its unique leaf patterns and vibrant blooms make it a desirable addition to both tropical and temperate landscapes.
