Bulbophyllum nazrii

Bulbophyllum nazrii, commonly known as the Nazri's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
