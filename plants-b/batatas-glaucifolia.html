Batatas glaucifolia

Batatas glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leaf arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and yellowish-green spathe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf known as a spathe. Batatas glaucifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
