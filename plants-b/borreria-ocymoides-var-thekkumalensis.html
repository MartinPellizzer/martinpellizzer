Borreria ocymoides var. thekkumalensis

Borreria ocymoides var. thekkumalensis, commonly known as the Kerala borreria, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's flora and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
