Bambusa dissimulator var. albinodis

Bambusa dissimulator var. albinodis, commonly known as the white-stemmed bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its distinctive light-colored, almost white, culms.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions of China and India. The plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense clusters with slender, hollow stems that are often used in ornamental landscaping. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green, contrasting with the pale stems.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for both decorative and practical purposes in gardens and as a sustainable building material.
