Bathysa mendoncae

Bathysa mendoncae, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for bird dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyons.

Bathysa mendoncae is valued for its drought tolerance and historical use by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
