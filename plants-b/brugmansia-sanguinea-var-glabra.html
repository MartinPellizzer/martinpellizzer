Brugmansia sanguinea var. glabra

Brugmansia sanguinea var. glabra, commonly known as the naked brugmansia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, tubular flowers that are typically white to yellow in color. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
