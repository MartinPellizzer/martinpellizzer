Brassica cheiranthos var. genuina

Brassica cheiranthos var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the flowers can range in color from yellow to orange.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant.
