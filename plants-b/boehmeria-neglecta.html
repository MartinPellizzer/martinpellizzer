Boehmeria neglecta

Boehmeria neglecta, commonly known as the common nettle or Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Boehmeria neglecta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
