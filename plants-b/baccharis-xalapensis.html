Baccharis xalapensis

Baccharis xalapensis, commonly known as Mexican thimbleweed, is a shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Baccharis xalapensis is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
