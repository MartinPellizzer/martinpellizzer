Bocoa guianensis

Bocoa guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan cocoa, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
