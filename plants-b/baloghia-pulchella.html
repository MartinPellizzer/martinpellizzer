Baloghia pulchella

Baloghia pulchella, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Baloghia pulchella is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
