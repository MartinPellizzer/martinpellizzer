Begonia wakefieldii

Begonia wakefieldii, commonly known as the Wakefield's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
