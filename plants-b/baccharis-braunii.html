Baccharis braunii

Baccharis braunii, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands. It has a woody stem with grayish bark and produces small, berry-like fruits.

Baccharis braunii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
