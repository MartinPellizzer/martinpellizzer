Brassica oleracea var. jodogongyla

Brassica oleracea var. jodogongyla, commonly known as the Chinese broccoli, is a cultivated variety of the cabbage species.

It is characterized by its thick, edible stalks and clusters of small, green leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in cool climates.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for both culinary and nutritional purposes.
