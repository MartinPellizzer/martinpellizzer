Bauhinia bassacensis var. nicobarica

Bauhinia bassacensis var. nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, papilionaceous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near riverbanks or in forested areas. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
