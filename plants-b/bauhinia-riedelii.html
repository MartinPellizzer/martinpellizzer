Bauhinia riedelii

Bauhinia riedelii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Bauhinia riedelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
