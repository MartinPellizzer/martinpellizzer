Buphthalmum equinum

Buphthalmum equinum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a robust, branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
