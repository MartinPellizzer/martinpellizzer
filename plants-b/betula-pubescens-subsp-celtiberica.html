Betula pubescens subsp. celtiberica

Betula pubescens subsp.

celtiberica, commonly known as the Iberian birch, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and slender, often pendulous branches.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a pale underside. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
