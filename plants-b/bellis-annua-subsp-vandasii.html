Bellis annua subsp. vandasii

Bellis annua subsp.

vandasii, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets.

The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and its preference for partial shade.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
