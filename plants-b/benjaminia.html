Benjaminia

Benjaminia, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, most notably Benjaminia ferruginea, which is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance. Benjaminia plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

They play an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
