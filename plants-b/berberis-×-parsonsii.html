Berberis × parsonsii

Berberis × parsonsii, commonly known as the Parson's barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in hedges and as a decorative element in gardens.
