Bellevalia stepporum var. edumea

Bellevalia stepporum var. edumea, commonly known as the steppe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, featuring basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth and ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
