Bellevalia kurdistanica

Bellevalia kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow linear leaves, and a short stem that bears the floral inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
