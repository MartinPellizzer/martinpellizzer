Berberis wilsoniae

Berberis wilsoniae, commonly known as Wilson's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southwestern China.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and structural interest.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
