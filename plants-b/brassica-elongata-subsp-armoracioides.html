Brassica elongata subsp. armoracioides

Brassica elongata subsp.

armoracioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a slender, elongated silique containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
