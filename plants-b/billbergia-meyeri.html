Billbergia meyeri

Billbergia meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette of strap-shaped leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia meyeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
