Bartsia remota

Bartsia remota, commonly known as the remote bartsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in racemes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Bartsia remota is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
