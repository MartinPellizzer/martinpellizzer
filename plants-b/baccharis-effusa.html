Baccharis effusa

Baccharis effusa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open areas such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis effusa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
