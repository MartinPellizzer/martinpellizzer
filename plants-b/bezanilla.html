Bezanilla

Bezanilla, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the water crowfoot and the buttercup. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed. Bezanilla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
