Beyeria viscosa var. genuina

Beyeria viscosa var. genuina, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, thick leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in various colors. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among both amateur and professional gardeners.
