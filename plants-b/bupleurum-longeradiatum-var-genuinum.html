Bupleurum longeradiatum var. genuinum

Bupleurum longeradiatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the true longroot valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous linear to lanceolate leaflets.

The roots are fibrous and have a distinctive aromatic scent, making it valuable in traditional medicine.
