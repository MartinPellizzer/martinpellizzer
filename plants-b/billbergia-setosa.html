Billbergia setosa

Billbergia setosa, commonly known as the slender billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Billbergia setosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its adaptability to various light conditions and minimal water requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
