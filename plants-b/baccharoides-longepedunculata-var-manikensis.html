Baccharoides longepedunculata var. manikensis

Baccharoides longepedunculata var. manikensis, commonly known as the Manikensis baccharoides, is a shrub native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and elongated, branching stems that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
