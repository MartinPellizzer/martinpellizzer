Batatas abyssinica

Batatas abyssinica, commonly known as the Ethiopian sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its edible, sweet, and starchy roots. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the vines can spread vigorously.

Batatas abyssinica is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
