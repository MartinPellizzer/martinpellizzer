Briza caroliniana

Briza caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina grass or blue grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Briza caroliniana is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
