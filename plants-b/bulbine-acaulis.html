Bulbine acaulis

Bulbine acaulis, commonly known as the rock bulbous, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, basal leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
