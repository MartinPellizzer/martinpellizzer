Brassica repanda var. montsicciana

Brassica repanda var. montsicciana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, yellow flowers and has narrow, linear leaves.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential for genetic diversity.
