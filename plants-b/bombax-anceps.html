Bombax anceps

Bombax anceps, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a three-lobed capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
