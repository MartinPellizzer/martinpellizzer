Buckleya lanceolata

Buckleya lanceolata, commonly known as the slender buckleya, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Nyssaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. Buckleya lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
