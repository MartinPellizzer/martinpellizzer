Bupleurum Triradiatum Subsp. Arcticum

Bupleurum triradiatum subsp. arcticum is a rare and hardy plant native to the Arctic regions of northern Europe and Asia. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to survive in extreme conditions. This subspecies is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pale blue or white flowers. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for local pollinators and supporting biodiversity. Due to its limited distribution and habitat sensitivity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
