Banisteria latifolia

Banisteria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf banisteria, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
