Bromus mollis var. multiflorus

Bromus mollis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the multiblade brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
