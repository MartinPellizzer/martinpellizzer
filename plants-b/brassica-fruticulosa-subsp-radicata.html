Brassica fruticulosa subsp. radicata

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

radicata, commonly known as the clustered mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and serrated, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
