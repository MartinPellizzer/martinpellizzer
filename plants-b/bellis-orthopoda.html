Bellis orthopoda

Bellis orthopoda, commonly known as the upright daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white flowers with yellow centers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in grassy areas and woodland edges. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Bellis orthopoda is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
