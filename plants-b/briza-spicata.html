Briza spicata

Briza spicata, commonly known as the spike grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Briza spicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
