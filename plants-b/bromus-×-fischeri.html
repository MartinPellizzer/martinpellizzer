Bromus × fischeri

Bromus × fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's brome, is a hybrid grass species that occurs naturally in regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of the Poaceae family. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. Bromus × fischeri is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and prairies.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits genetic variability and can be challenging to distinguish from its parent species.
