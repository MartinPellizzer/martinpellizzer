Besleria leucocarpa

Besleria leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited besleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, and its fruit is a fleshy, yellow to orange berry that contains several seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the understory vegetation.
