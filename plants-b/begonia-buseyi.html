Begonia buseyi

Begonia buseyi, commonly known as the Buse's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually small, tubular, and come in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Begonia buseyi prefers shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
