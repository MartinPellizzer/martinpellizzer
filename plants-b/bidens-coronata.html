Bidens coronata

Bidens coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Bidens coronata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
