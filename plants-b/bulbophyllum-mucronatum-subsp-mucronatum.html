Bulbophyllum mucronatum subsp. mucronatum

Bulbophyllum mucronatum subsp.

mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-tipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand and Indonesia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.
