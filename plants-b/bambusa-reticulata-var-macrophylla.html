Bambusa reticulata var. macrophylla

Bambusa reticulata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features sturdy, hollow culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are long, narrow, and dark green, often with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is widely cultivated for landscaping, erosion control, and even as a source of sustainable materials.
