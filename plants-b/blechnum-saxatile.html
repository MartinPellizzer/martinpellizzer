Blechnum saxatile

Blechnum saxatile, commonly known as the rock holly, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Blechnum saxatile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
