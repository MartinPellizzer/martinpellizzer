Baccharis oxyphylla

Baccharis oxyphylla, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a distinctive silver-woolly underside.

It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
