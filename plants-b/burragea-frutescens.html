Burragea frutescens

Burragea frutescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrager, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
