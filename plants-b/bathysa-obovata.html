Bathysa obovata

Bathysa obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its small, clustered purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Bathysa obovata is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
