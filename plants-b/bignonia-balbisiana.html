Bignonia balbisiana

Bignonia balbisiana, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia balbisiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
