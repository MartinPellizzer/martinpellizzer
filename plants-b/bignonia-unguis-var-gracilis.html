Bignonia unguis var. gracilis

Bignonia unguis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender glory vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.
