Bignonia viminalis

Bignonia viminalis, commonly known as sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that range from green to purple. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage.

Bignonia viminalis is also valued for its edible tubers, which are similar to sweet potatoes in texture and flavor.
