Burdachia prismatocarpa var. sphaerocarpa

Burdachia prismatocarpa var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe burdachia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical fruits that are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
