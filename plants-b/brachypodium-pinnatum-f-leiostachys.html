Brachypodium pinnatum f. leiostachys

Brachypodium pinnatum f. leiostachys, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stem and reduced leaf auricles.

It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
