Baccharis lychnophora

Baccharis lychnophora, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, though it can be invasive in some areas.
