Bambusa guadua

Bambusa guadua, commonly known as the Guadua bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight blue-green hue.

Bambusa guadua is highly valued for its strength and sustainability, making it a popular choice for construction and handicrafts.
