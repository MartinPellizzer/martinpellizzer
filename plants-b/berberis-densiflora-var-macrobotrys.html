Berberis densiflora var. macrobotrys

Berberis densiflora var. macrobotrys, commonly known as the large-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
