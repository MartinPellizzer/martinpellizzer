Brachypodium ramosum f. capillaceum

Brachypodium ramosum f. capillaceum, commonly known as the slender bristlegrass, is a variant of the species Brachypodium ramosum.

It is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, hair-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
