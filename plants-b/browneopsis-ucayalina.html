Browneopsis ucayalina

Browneopsis ucayalina, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on tree roots.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
