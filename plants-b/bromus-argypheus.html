Bromus argypheus

Bromus argypheus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Bromus argypheus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
