Biscutella erucifolia

Biscutella erucifolia, commonly known as the woolly biscutella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants.

Biscutella erucifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
