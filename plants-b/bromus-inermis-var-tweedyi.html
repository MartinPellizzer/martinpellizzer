Bromus inermis var. tweedyi

Bromus inermis var. tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
