Brassica peregrina

Brassica peregrina, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its seeds are small and capsule-like.

Brassica peregrina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
