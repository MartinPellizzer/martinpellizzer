Bermudiana luzula

Bermudiana luzula, commonly known as the Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to the subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit, which allows it to form dense mats. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including drought and partial shade. Its rhizomatous and stolonal growth system enables it to spread rapidly and establish itself in various habitats.

Bermudiana luzula is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
