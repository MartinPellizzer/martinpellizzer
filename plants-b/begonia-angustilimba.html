Begonia angustilimba

Begonia angustilimba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
