Balsamina noli-tangere

Balsamina noli-tangere, commonly known as the "touch-me-not" balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Balsamina noli-tangere is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
