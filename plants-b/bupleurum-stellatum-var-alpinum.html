Bupleurum stellatum var. alpinum

Bupleurum stellatum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
