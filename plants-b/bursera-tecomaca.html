Bursera tecomaca

Bursera tecomaca, commonly known as Mexican frankincense or tecomate, is a small, evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant produces a fragrant, sap-like substance that can be tapped from its bark, similar to frankincense. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Bursera tecomaca thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and shrublands.
