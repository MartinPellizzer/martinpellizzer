Bidens bicolor

Bidens bicolor, commonly known as the common beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and white flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Bidens bicolor is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
