Brunfelsia hopeana var. pubescens

Brunfelsia hopeana var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet flower or purple morning glory, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep purple to pale blue, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
