Begonia gracilior

Begonia gracilior, commonly known as the slender begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Begonia gracilior prefers shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
