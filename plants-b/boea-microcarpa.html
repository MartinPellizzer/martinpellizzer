Boea microcarpa

Boea microcarpa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
