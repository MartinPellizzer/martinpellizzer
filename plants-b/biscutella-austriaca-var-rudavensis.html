Biscutella austriaca var. rudavensis

Biscutella austriaca var. rudavensis, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
