Barringtonia moluccana

Barringtonia moluccana, commonly known as the Whitebeard tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped white flowers that hang from the branches, emitting a strong fragrance at night. The tree produces large, fibrous fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp. Its leaves are large, simple, and opposite, with a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
