Boronia caerulata

Boronia caerulata, commonly known as the blue boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a contrasting texture to the vibrant flowers.

Boronia caerulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
