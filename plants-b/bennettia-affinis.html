Bennettia affinis

Bennettia affinis, commonly known as the mountain bennettia, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bennettiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, woody structure and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.
