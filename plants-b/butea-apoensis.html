Butea apoensis

Butea apoensis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
