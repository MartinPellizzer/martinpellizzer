Betula verrucosa subsp. oycowiensis

Betula verrucosa subsp.

oycowiensis, commonly known as the Ocyowian birch, is a rare subspecies native to specific regions of eastern North America. It is characterized by its smooth, often white-barked trunk and slender, spreading branches.

The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in riparian zones or near wetlands.

This subspecies plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
