Banksia incana var. brachyphylla

Banksia incana var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that typically appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
