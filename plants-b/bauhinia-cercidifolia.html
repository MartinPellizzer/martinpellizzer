Bauhinia cercidifolia

Bauhinia cercidifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces flat, winged pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and seasonal display make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological landscapes.
