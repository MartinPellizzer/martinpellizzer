Brassica oleracea var. chamaegongyla

Brassica oleracea var. chamaegongyla, commonly known as the tree kale, is a perennial plant characterized by its woody stems and broad, dark green leaves.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators, and its leaves are often used in culinary applications.

It is valued for its nutritional content and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
