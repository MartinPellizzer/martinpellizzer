Bulbophyllum medioximum

Bulbophyllum medioximum, commonly known as the Medioximum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that are often fragrant, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
