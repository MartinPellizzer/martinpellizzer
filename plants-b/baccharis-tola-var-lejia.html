Baccharis tola var. lejia

Baccharis tola var. lejia, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
