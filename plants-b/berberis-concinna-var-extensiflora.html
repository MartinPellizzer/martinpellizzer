Berberis concinna var. extensiflora

Berberis concinna var. extensiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its bright green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.
