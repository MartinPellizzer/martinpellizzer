Berberis daurica

Berberis daurica, commonly known as Daurian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Berberis daurica is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
