Beta vulgaris convar. crassa

Beta vulgaris convar. crassa, commonly known as莙菜 or chicory, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in salads and as a bitter herb. The plant produces blue or purple flowers, and its root, known as the "chicory root," is commonly used in coffee substitutes and as a vegetable. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes other familiar plants like daisies and sunflowers.
