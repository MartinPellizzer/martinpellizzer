Bouchea agrestis

Bouchea agrestis, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Bouchea agrestis is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.
