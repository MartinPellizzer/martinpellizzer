Blumea arfakiana

Blumea arfakiana, commonly known as the Arfakian blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in blue or white hues, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Blumea arfakiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
