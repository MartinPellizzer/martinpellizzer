Berberis rawatii

Berberis rawatii, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small evergreen leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Berberis rawatii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
