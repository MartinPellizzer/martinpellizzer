Brunfelsia ramosissima var. pauciflora

Brunfelsia ramosissima var. pauciflora, commonly known as the pink angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with numerous branches and elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bats and moths, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
