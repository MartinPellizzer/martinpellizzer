Broussonetia × hanjiana

Broussonetia × hanjiana, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its fruit is a small, fibrous capsule that contains seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
