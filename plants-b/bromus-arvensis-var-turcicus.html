Bromus arvensis var. turcicus

Bromus arvensis var. turcicus, commonly known as Turkish quackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
