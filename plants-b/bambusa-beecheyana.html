Bambusa beecheyana

Bambusa beecheyana, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often covered in a fine, silky sheen. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Bambusa beecheyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
