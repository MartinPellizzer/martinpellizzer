Baccharis sagittalis

Baccharis sagittalis, commonly known as arrowleaf baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and arrow-shaped, which gives it its common name.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
