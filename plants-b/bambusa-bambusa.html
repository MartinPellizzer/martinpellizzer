Bambusa bambusa

Bambusa bambusa, commonly known as the common bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Bambusa bambusa is highly valued for its strong and flexible culms, which are used in construction, furniture, and papermaking.

It also plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
