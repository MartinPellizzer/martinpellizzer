Bastardia viscosa f. subtriloba

Bastardia viscosa f. subtriloba, commonly known as the sticky bastardia, is a variant of the annual flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a resilient species in harsh environments.
