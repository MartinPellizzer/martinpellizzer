Bauhinia porphyrotricha var. killipiana

Bauhinia porphyrotricha var. killipiana, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
