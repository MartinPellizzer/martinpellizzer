Billardiera coriacea

Billardiera coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved billardiera, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features rough, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

Billardiera coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its salt tolerance.
