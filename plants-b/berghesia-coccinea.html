Berghesia coccinea

Berghesia coccinea, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues.

The botanical features of Berghesia coccinea make it a visually captivating and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
