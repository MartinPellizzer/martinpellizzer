Betula transsilvanica

Betula transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian birch, is a deciduous tree native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its white bark and slender, weeping branches. The tree produces catkins and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The Transylvanian birch is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
