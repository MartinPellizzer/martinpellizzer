Boehmeria nivea var. reticulata

Boehmeria nivea var. reticulata, commonly known as Chinese nettle or reticulated flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous stalks, which are used in traditional textile production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique reticulate venation pattern gives it its specific variety name.
