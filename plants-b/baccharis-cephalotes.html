Baccharis cephalotes

Baccharis cephalotes, commonly known as the white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Baccharis cephalotes is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
