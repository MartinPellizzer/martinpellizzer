Baccharis gibertii

Baccharis gibertii, commonly known as the mountain baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Baccharis gibertii is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
