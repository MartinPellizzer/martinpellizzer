Bridelia ovata var. exaltata

Bridelia ovata var. exaltata, commonly known as the tall bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
