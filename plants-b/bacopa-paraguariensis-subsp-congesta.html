Bacopa paraguariensis subsp. congesta

Bacopa paraguariensis subsp.

congesta, commonly known as Brazilian water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
