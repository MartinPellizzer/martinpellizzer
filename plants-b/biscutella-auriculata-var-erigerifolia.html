Biscutella auriculata var. erigerifolia

Biscutella auriculata var. erigerifolia, commonly known as the alpine biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, demonstrating a preference for well-drained environments. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees.

The species is noted for its frost-resistant nature and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.
