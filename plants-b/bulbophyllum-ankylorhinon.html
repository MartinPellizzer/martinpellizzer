Bulbophyllum ankylorhinon

Bulbophyllum ankylorhinon, commonly known as the horned bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive horn-like appearance on the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
