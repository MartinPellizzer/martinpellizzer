Bromus japonicus subsp. phrygius

Bromus japonicus subsp.

phrygius, commonly known as the Phrygian brome, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spike with small, two-lobed glumes and spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in ecological succession.
