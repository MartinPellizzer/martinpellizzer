Batrachium peltatum var. quinquelobum

Batrachium peltatum var. quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped lobes, and the variety quinquelobum is distinguished by its more deeply divided, five-lobed leaf structure. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
