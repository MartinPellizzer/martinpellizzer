Beauverdia recurvifolia

Beauverdia recurvifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white, waxy flowers that resemble small balls, which bloom in winter. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
