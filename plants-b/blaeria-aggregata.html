Blaeria aggregata

Blaeria aggregata, commonly known as the clustered bladdernut, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, oval leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant's fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Blaeria aggregata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
