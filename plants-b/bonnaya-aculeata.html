Bonnaya aculeata

Bonnaya aculeata, commonly known as the spiny bonnaya, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, spiny leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
