Brachypodium megastachyum

Brachypodium megastachyum, commonly known as giant false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
