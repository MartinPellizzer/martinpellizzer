Betula papyrifera subsp. commutata

Betula papyrifera subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are triangular with serrated edges, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
