Byrsonima rhombifolia

Byrsonima rhombifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
