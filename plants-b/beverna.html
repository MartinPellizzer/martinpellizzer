Beverna

Beverna, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments. Beverna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Its berries are toxic to humans and livestock, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
