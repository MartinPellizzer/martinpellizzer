Berberis taronensis

Berberis taronensis, commonly known as the Taronian barberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, holly-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Berberis taronensis is valued for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
