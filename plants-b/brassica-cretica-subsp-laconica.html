Brassica cretica subsp. laconica

Brassica cretica subsp.

laconica, commonly known as the Laconian mustard, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing plant with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open fields.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in parts of Greece.
