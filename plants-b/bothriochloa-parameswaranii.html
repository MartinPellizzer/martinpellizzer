Bothriochloa parameswaranii

Bothriochloa parameswaranii, commonly known as the Parameswaranii grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is adapted to thrive in dry, tropical, and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native vegetation in its native habitat.
