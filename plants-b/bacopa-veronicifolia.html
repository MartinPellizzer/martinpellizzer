Bacopa veronicifolia

Bacopa veronicifolia, commonly known as the Indian water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Bacopa veronicifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
