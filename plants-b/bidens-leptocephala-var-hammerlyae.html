Bidens leptocephala var. hammerlyae

Bidens leptocephala var. hammerlyae, commonly known as the narrow-headed beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that are often attached to the hairs of animals or clothing, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its adaptability and resilience in tropical and subtropical environments.
