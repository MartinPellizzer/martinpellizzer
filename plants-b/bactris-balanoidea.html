Bactris balanoidea

Bactris balanoidea, commonly known as the red rata or red palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered, edible fruits that are orange to red in color. The fruit's pulp is sweet and juicy, making it a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
