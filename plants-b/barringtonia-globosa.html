Barringtonia globosa

Barringtonia globosa, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The plant has palmate leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. Its fruit is an important food source for various birds and bats.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in coastal areas.
