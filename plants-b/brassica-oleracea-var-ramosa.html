Brassica oleracea var. ramosa

Brassica oleracea var. ramosa, commonly known as kohlrabi, is a leafy vegetable with a swollen stem that is typically eaten as a vegetable.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant has a compact rosette of dark green, edible leaves surrounding a bulbous, pale green stem. Kohlrabi is known for its mild, sweet flavor and crisp texture.

It is cultivated for its edible stem and leaves, and it thrives in cool climates.
