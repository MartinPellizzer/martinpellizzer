Berberis sumatrensis

Berberis sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
