Baccharis × fraudulenta

Baccharis × fraudulenta, commonly known as false musk thorn, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Baccharis × fraudulenta is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
