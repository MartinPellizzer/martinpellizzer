Bromus orientalis

Bromus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental brome, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus orientalis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
