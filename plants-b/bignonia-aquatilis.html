Bignonia aquatilis

Bignonia aquatilis, commonly known as the waterbinder, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found near water sources.

Bignonia aquatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
