Berula erecta f. dentata

Berula erecta f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed water parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.
