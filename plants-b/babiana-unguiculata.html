Babiana unguiculata

Babiana unguiculata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stems that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, often forming a basal rosette.

Babiana unguiculata is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
