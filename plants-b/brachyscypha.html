Brachyscypha

Brachyscypha, commonly known as the small moss, is a genus of mosses belonging to the family Brachyscyphaceae.

These plants are characterized by their compact, cushion-like growth form and are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Brachyscypha species often have small, densely packed leaves that are usually less than 1 mm in length. They play an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in their ecosystems.

Due to their small size and specialized habitat requirements, they are often overlooked but are vital components of microhabitats in forests and other damp areas.
