Bignonia dichotoma

Bignonia dichotoma, commonly known as the evergreen morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.

Bignonia dichotoma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
