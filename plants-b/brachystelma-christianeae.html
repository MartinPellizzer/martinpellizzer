Brachystelma christianeae

Brachystelma christianeae, commonly known as the Karoo star orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, usually between September and November. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
