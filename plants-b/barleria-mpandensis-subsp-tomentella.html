Barleria mpandensis subsp. tomentella

Barleria mpandensis subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the velvet barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
