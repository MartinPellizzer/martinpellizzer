Barleria vincifolia

Barleria vincifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red barleria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and emit a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Barleria vincifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
