Briquetina mollis

Briquetina mollis, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a hairy stem, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

Briquetina mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
