Benzoin argenteum

Benzoin argenteum, commonly known as silver benzoin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous substance called benzoin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
