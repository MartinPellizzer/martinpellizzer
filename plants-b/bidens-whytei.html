Bidens whytei

Bidens whytei, commonly known as the yellow beggarweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Bidens whytei is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
