Batatas multiflora

Batatas multiflora, commonly known as the forest yam, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rainforests, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
