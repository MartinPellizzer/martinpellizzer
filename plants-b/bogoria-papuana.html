Bogoria papuana

Bogoria papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bog orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highland regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a specialized labellum. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments, typically found in bogs and swampy areas. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense clumps with a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Bogoria papuana is considered a rare and endangered species.
