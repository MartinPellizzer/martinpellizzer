Beta maritima subsp. danica

Beta maritima subsp.

danica, commonly known as sea beet, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in saline environments.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
