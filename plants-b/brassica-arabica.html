Brassica arabica

Brassica arabica, commonly known as the Abyssinian mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical characteristics of Brassica arabica make it well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments.
