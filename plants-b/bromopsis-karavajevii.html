Bromopsis karavajevii

Bromopsis karavajevii, commonly known as the Karavajev's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Bromopsis karavajevii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
