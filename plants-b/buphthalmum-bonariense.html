Buphthalmum bonariense

Buphthalmum bonariense, commonly known as the South African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Buphthalmum bonariense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
