Biscutella austriaca

Biscutella austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched. Biscutella austriaca thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
