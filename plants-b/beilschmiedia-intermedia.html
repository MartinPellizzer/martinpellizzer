Beilschmiedia intermedia

Beilschmiedia intermedia, commonly known as the red-berried beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
