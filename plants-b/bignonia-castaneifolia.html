Bignonia castaneifolia

Bignonia castaneifolia, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia castaneifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and along roadsides.
