Begonia ferox

Begonia ferox, commonly known as the wild begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from dark green to almost black in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
