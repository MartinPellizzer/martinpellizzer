Bambusa bambos f. gigantea

Bambusa bambos f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its rhizomes are robust and help in the propagation of the plant through underground growth.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
