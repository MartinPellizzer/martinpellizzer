Batatas bonariensis

Batatas bonariensis, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a tuberous root vegetable native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple skin and pale yellow to white flesh. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is closely related to other sweet potato varieties. Its tubers are rich in starch, dietary fiber, and various nutrients such as vitamin C and potassium.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
