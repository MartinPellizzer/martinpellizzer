Bromus scoparius var. confertus

Bromus scoparius var. confertus, commonly known as the dense curly bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, curly inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
