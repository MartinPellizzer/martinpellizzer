Bulbophyllum brevidenticulatum

Bulbophyllum brevidenticulatum, commonly known as the short-toothed bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic orchid produces compact, hairy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species. The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in controlled growing conditions.
