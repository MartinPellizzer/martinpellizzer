Brosimum echinocarpum

Brosimum echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiny acacia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
