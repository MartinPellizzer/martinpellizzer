Baccharis macrodonta

Baccharis macrodonta, commonly known as the Mexican heather or silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of small, narrow leaves arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Baccharis macrodonta is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
