Betula austrosinensis

Betula austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, triangular leaves. The tree produces catkins that are either male or female, typical of the birch genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
