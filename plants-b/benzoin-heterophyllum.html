Benzoin heterophyllum

Benzoin heterophyllum, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudate, which is harvested for its use in perfumes and traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a fragrant scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive balsamic fragrance that contributes to its commercial value.
