Bomarea velascoana

Bomarea velascoana, commonly known as the velvet bomarea, is a striking climbing plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has thick, tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

Bomarea velascoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
