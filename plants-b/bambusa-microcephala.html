Bambusa microcephala

Bambusa microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact and hardy species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, stout culms that rarely exceed 2 meters in height, making it ideal for small garden spaces. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are densely packed, contributing to its lush appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Bambusa microcephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to create a dense, privacy-screening effect in landscaping.
