Beta incarnata

Beta incarnata, commonly known as pink flowering begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Beta incarnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
