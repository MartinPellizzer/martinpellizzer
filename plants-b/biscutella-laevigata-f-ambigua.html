Biscutella laevigata f. ambigua

Biscutella laevigata f. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The f. ambigua form is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form.
