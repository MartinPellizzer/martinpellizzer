Bambusa duquilioi

Bambusa duquilioi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo is a clumping species, forming dense clusters rather than spreading aggressively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
