Balanophora cryptocaudex

Balanophora cryptocaudex, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a heterotrophic plant that relies on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, and it has a distinctive root system that allows it to attach to trees.

Due to its specialized habitat and conservation status, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
