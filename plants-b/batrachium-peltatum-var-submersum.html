Batrachium peltatum var. submersum

Batrachium peltatum var. submersum, commonly known as the submerged water clover, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific freshwater habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for life in submerged conditions. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, are usually submerged or just at the water's surface.
