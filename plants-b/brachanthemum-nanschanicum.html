Brachanthemum nanschanicum

Brachanthemum nanschanicum, commonly known as the Karakorum daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Brachanthemum nanschanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
