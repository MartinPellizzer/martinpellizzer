Betonica nivea

Betonica nivea, commonly known as white betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. Betonica nivea is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
