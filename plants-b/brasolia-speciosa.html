Brasolia speciosa

Brasolia speciosa, commonly known as the showy brasolia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals. Brasolia speciosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
