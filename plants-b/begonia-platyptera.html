Begonia platyptera

Begonia platyptera, commonly known as the shieldleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
