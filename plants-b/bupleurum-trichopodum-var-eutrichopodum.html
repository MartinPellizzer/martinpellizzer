Bupleurum trichopodum var. eutrichopodum

Bupleurum trichopodum var. eutrichopodum, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
