Biota orientalis var. aurea

Biota orientalis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden biota, is a variant of the Biota orientalis species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its glossy, golden-yellow leaves that provide striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The golden biota thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
