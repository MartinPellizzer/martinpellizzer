Brassica oleracea var. sylvestris

Brassica oleracea var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild cabbage, is a wild relative of cultivated cabbage species.

It is characterized by its robust, leafy structure and is native to Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates. Its leaves are often rough and have a strong, mustard-like odor.

This variety is important in plant breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and potential for improving crop resilience.
