Barbarea vicina

Barbarea vicina, commonly known as the lesser winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, lobed leaves.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
