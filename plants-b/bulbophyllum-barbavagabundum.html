Bulbophyllum barbavagabundum

Bulbophyllum barbavagabundum, commonly known as the bearded wanderer, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in tropical climates. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss.
