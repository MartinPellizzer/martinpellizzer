Begonia dietrichiana

Begonia dietrichiana, commonly known as the dwarf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden spaces.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia dietrichiana is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
