Bacopa limosa

Bacopa limosa, commonly known as water bead or aquatic bacopa, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and its ability to thrive in wet or aquatic environments. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Bacopa limosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
