Barbarea longisiliqua

Barbarea longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-stalked winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by heart-shaped, lobed pods. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive siliqua (a type of pod) that is longer than the leaves themselves.

Barbarea longisiliqua is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
