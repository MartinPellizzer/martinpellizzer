Brachyglottis rangiora atropurpurea

Brachyglottis rangiora atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple brachyglottis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

This species is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to New Zealand's climate and ecosystems.
