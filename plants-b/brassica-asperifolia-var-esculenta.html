Brassica asperifolia var. esculenta

Brassica asperifolia var. esculenta, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
