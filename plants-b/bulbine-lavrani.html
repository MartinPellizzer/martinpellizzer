Bulbine lavrani

Bulbine lavrani, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bulbine lavrani is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
