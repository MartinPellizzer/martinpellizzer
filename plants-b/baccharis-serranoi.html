Baccharis serranoi

Baccharis serranoi, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the fall. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Baccharis serranoi plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
