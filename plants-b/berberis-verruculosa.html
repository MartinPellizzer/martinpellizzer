Berberis verruculosa

Berberis verruculosa, commonly known as the roughleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Berberis verruculosa is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
