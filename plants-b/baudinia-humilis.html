Baudinia humilis

Baudinia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf baudinia, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Baudinia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
