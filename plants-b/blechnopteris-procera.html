Blechnopteris procera

Blechnopteris procera, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. Its fronds are deeply divided, with a distinctive pinnate structure that aids in water movement and light absorption.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in moist habitats.
