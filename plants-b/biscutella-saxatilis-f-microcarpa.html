Biscutella saxatilis f. microcarpa

Biscutella saxatilis f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
