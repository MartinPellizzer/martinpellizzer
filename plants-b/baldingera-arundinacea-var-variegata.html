Baldingera arundinacea var. variegata

Baldingera arundinacea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated reed, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in riparian zones.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.
