Bakeridesia gloriosa

Bakeridesia gloriosa, commonly known as the splendid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a vibrant combination of colors, including shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Bakeridesia gloriosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
