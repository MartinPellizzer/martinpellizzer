Brachyrhynchos eupatorioides

Brachyrhynchos eupatorioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and a valuable resource for pollinators.
