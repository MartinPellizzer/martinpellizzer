Baccharis alamanii

Baccharis alamanii, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Additionally, it provides habitat and nectar for various pollinators and wildlife.
