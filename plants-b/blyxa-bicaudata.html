Blyxa bicaudata

Blyxa bicaudata, commonly known as the two-tailed water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has slender, branching stems that often trail along the water's surface or submerge in shallow waters. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it its distinctive appearance. Blyxa bicaudata thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

It is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its attractive, flowing form.
