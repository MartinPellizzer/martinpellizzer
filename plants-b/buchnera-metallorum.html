Buchnera metallorum

Buchnera metallorum, commonly known as the metallophile bacterium, is a species of endosymbiotic bacterium found in certain insect species.

It is notable for its ability to synthesize essential amino acids that its host insect cannot produce on its own. This symbiotic relationship is crucial for the insect's survival, particularly in nutrient-poor environments. Buchnera metallorum is often found in the hemolymph of its host, where it resides within specialized cells called bacteriocytes.

Its presence is a key factor in the evolutionary adaptation of its host to specific ecological niches.
