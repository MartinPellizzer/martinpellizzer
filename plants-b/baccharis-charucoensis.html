Baccharis charucoensis

Baccharis charucoensis, commonly known as the Charuco burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with small leaves that reduce water loss. It often grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, demonstrating resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its flowering period typically occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators.
