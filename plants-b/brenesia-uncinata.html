Brenesia uncinata

Brenesia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hooked tip, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
