Balaka microcarpa var. longicuspis

Balaka microcarpa var. longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined balaka, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated spines that adorn its stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.
