Begonia matarombeoensis

Begonia matarombeoensis, commonly known as the Matarombeo begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Begonia matarombeoensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments.
