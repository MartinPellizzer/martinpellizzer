Blaeria johnstonii subsp. keniensis

Blaeria johnstonii subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the family Spermatophyta and is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

The plant typically features submerged leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It is adapted to still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its presence indicates good water quality, making it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
