Bambusa nutans

Bambusa nutans, commonly known as the nodding bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, gracefully drooping culms that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical, three-angled seed clusters. Bambusa nutans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant form and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
