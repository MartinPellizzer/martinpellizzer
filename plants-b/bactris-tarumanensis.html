Bactris tarumanensis

Bactris tarumanensis, commonly known as the Taruma palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon basin, particularly in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, oval fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
