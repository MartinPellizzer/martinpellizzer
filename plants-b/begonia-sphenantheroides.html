Begonia sphenantheroides

Begonia sphenantheroides, commonly known as the narrow-veined begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with intricate veining patterns and clusters of small, fleshy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green and silver tones, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both gardens and as a houseplant.
