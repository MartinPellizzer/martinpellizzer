Blumea senecioides

Blumea senecioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, often camphor-like scent.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are pale blue to white and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
