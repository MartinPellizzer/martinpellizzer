Bucetum pratense

Bucetum pratense, commonly known as the meadow sweet or sweet flag, is a wetland plant species characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is known for its aromatic properties. Bucetum pratense plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
