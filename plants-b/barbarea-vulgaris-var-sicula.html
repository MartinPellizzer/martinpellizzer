Barbarea vulgaris var. sicula

Barbarea vulgaris var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in rocky or coastal areas. It has lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Sicilian winter cress is often used in local cuisine and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
