Bactria lazkovii

Bactria lazkovii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Bactria lazkovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
