Bulbophyllum cheiri subsp. cheiri

Bulbophyllum cheiri subsp.

cheiri, commonly known as the cheiri orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
