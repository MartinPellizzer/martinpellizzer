Bignonia platyphylla

Bignonia platyphylla, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia platyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
