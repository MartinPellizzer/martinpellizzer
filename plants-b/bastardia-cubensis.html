Bastardia cubensis

Bastardia cubensis, commonly known as Cuban bastardia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is adapted to coastal and saline environments. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Bastardia cubensis is notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
