Baccharis quadrangularis

Baccharis quadrangularis, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a quadrangular cross-section, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and savannas.

Baccharis quadrangularis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
