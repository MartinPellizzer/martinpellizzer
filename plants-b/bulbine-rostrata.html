Bulbine rostrata

Bulbine rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick, cylindrical stem with dense clusters of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring or early summer, which are arranged in a loose inflorescence. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical collections.
