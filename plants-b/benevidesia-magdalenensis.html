Benevidesia magdalenensis

Benevidesia magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalenese orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the Magdalena River Valley in Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blossoms that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near water sources, and thrives in nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected by local conservation efforts.
