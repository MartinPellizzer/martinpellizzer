Banisteria arborescens

Banisteria arborescens, commonly known as the red-bark tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, glossy green leaves. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries.

Banisteria arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
