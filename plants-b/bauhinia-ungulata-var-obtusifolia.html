Bauhinia ungulata var. obtusifolia

Bauhinia ungulata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are typically rounded and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This variety is closely related to other bauhinia species and shares similar growth habits and ecological preferences.
