Barbarea intermedia proles gautieri

Barbarea intermedia proles gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, temperate environments. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
