Begonia hasskarlii

Begonia hasskarlii, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
