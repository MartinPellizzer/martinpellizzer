Bursera trimera

Bursera trimera, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Bursera trimera is known for its aromatic resins, which have been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.
