Bambusa vulgaris var. aureovariegata

Bambusa vulgaris var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking yellow and green variegated foliage.

It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense, upright culms with a smooth, light green surface. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a lush, textured landscape.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
