Braemia vittata

Braemia vittata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with white markings, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Braemia vittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
