Brargentina bornmuelleri

Brargentina bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Argentine flame tree, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, providing a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding a dramatic visual impact. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its fast growth rate and adaptability, it is also used in reforestation projects in its native range.
