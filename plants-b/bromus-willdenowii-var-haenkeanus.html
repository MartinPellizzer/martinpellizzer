Bromus willdenowii var. haenkeanus

Bromus willdenowii var. haenkeanus, commonly known as the Haenke's brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in various habitats.
