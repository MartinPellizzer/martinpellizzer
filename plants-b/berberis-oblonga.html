Berberis oblonga

Berberis oblonga, commonly known as the longleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems that bear alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and persistent berries, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
