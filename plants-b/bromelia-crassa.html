Bromelia crassa

Bromelia crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its thick, broad, and often spiky leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. It produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange.

Bromelia crassa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments.
