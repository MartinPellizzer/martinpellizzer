Buxanthus hildebrandtii

Buxanthus hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Buxanthus hildebrandtii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
