Bambusa nigropunctata

Bambusa nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender culms that are often dotted with black specks, giving it its common name. The plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value.

This bamboo is often cultivated for landscaping and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
