Brassica perviridis

Brassica perviridis, commonly known as the green cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, leafy head that is typically green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are edible and often used in various culinary traditions.

Brassica perviridis is also valued for its nutritional content and agricultural importance.
