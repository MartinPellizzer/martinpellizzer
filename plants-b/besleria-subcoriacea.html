Besleria subcoriacea

Besleria subcoriacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
