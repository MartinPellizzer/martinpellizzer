Barcena

Barcena, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its bright orange to red floral bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Barcena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
