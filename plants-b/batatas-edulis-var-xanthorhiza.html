Batatas edulis var. xanthorhiza

Batatas edulis var. xanthorhiza, commonly known as yellow sweet potato, is a tropical tuberous root crop native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its deep orange to yellow flesh, which is rich in beta-carotene and other nutrients. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its vines spread horizontally along the ground. The tuberous roots develop underground, often forming clusters, and are harvested when mature for culinary use.

This variety is valued for its nutritional profile and adaptability to various soil conditions.
