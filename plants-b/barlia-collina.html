Barlia collina

Barlia collina, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and along woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Barlia collina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in wildflower gardens.
