Briza maxima var. pubescens

Briza maxima var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that sway in the wind. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.
