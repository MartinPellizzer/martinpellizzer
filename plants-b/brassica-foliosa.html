Brassica foliosa

Brassica foliosa, commonly known as the leafy cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are edible and often used in traditional cuisines.

Brassica foliosa is native to temperate regions and thrives in cool climates.
