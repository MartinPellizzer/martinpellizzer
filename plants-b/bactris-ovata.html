Bactris ovata

Bactris ovata, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Bactris ovata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
