Braya supina

Braya supina, commonly known as the prostrate rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Braya supina is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
