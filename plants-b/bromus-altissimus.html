Bromus altissimus

Bromus altissimus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Bromus altissimus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife.
