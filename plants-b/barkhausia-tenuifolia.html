Barkhausia tenuifolia

Barkhausia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barkhausia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in width. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Barkhausia tenuifolia is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
