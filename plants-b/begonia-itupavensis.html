Begonia itupavensis

Begonia itupavensis, commonly known as the Itupavensian begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and silver hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
