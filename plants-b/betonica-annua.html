Betonica annua

Betonica annua, commonly known as lesser betony, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Betonica annua is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
