Bulbophyllum janus

Bulbophyllum janus, commonly known as the Janus orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble a split face, giving it its Latin name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a compact growth habit with a short rhizome. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, round pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum janus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
