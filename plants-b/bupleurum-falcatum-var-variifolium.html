Bupleurum falcatum var. variifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. variifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and vary in size and shape, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
