Bucephalophora aculeata subsp. aegaea

Bucephalophora aculeata subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean aculeate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
