Berberis japonica var. trifurca

Berberis japonica var. trifurca, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
