Buceras platycarpos

Buceras platycarpos, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its fragrant wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional medicine and incense.
