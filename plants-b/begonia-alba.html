Begonia alba

Begonia alba, commonly known as the white begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It thrives in partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Begonia alba is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and add aesthetic value to gardens and homes.
