Baphia letestui

Baphia letestui, commonly known as the African baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in forested or disturbed habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
