Begonia tlapensis

Begonia tlapensis, commonly known as the Mexican begonia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia tlapensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
