Bidens superba var. angustifolia

Bidens superba var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and yellow flowers with two protruding rays. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread readily.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various regions.
