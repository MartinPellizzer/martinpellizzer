Begonia sarcophylla

Begonia sarcophylla, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
