Bambusa alemtemshii

Bambusa alemtemshii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and dense, bushy growth habit. This bamboo is known for its rapid growth rate, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control. The plant's leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa alemtemshii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
