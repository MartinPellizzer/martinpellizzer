Brachychiton obtusilobus

Brachychiton obtusilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with rounded lobes, and its most striking characteristic is its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The flowers are trumpet-shaped and are borne in clusters, making it a visually appealing species for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
