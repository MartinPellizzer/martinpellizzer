Begonia × versaliensis

Begonia × versaliensis, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia × tuberhybrida.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, orange, and white. The plant typically has thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with contrasting patterns. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia × versaliensis is widely cultivated in gardens and as indoor plants.
