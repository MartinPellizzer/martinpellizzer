Buddleja variabilis var. wilsonii

Buddleja variabilis var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, pale purple to white flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in gardens.
