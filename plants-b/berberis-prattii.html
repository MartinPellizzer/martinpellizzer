Berberis prattii

Berberis prattii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It has thorny stems and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Berberis prattii is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with antimicrobial effects.
