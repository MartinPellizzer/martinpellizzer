Bromus secalinus f. velutinus

Bromus secalinus f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet brome, is a cool-season grass that exhibits a distinctive velvety texture on its leaves.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world. Its leaves are narrow and have a soft, hairy surface, contributing to its unique appearance.

This grass is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
