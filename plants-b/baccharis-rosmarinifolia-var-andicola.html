Baccharis rosmarinifolia var. andicola

Baccharis rosmarinifolia var. andicola, commonly known as Andean baccharis, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves with a silvery underside, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to harsh climates with low precipitation and strong sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
