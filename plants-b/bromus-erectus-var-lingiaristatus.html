Bromus erectus var. lingiaristatus

Bromus erectus var. lingiaristatus, commonly known as the Lingiari bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a low, dense growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate salt and drought, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
