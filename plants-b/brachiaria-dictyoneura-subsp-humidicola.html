Brachiaria dictyoneura subsp. humidicola

Brachiaria dictyoneura subsp.

humidicola, commonly known as humidicola grass, is a tropical perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant typically thrives in wetland and marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to high moisture conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often concealed by the foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop in humid climates.
