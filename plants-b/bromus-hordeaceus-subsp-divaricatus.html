Bromus hordeaceus subsp. divaricatus

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

divaricatus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in areas with moderate moisture levels.
