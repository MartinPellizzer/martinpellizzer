Ballota nigra var. subvillosa

Ballota nigra var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky ballota, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a similar silky pubescence.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
