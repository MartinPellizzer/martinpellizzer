Brassavola cucullata var. elegans

Brassavola cucullata var. elegans, commonly known as the Elegant Lady's Slipper, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure that attracts pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
