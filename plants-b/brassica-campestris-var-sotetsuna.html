Brassica campestris var. sotetsuna

Brassica campestris var. sotetsuna, commonly known as Japanese mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round pods containing numerous seeds, which are used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a cover crop or for its edible parts.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
