Bulbophyllum chrysanthum

Bulbophyllum chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
