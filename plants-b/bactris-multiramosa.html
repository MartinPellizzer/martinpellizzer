Bactris multiramosa

Bactris multiramosa, commonly known as the multibranch palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. This palm is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits.

Bactris multiramosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
