Baptisia australis var. minor

Baptisia australis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native gardens and restoration projects.

Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems.
