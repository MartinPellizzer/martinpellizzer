Bromus mollis var. glabriusculus

Bromus mollis var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

The plant plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
