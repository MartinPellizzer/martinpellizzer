Borreria vulpina

Borreria vulpina, commonly known as redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Borreria vulpina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
