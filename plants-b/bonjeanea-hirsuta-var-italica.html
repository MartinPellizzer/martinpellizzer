Bonjeanea hirsuta var. italica

Bonjeanea hirsuta var. italica, commonly known as the Italian hirsute bonjeanea, is a variant of the bonjeanea plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
