Bidens chiapensis

Bidens chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Bidens chiapensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
