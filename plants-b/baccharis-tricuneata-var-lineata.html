Baccharis tricuneata var. lineata

Baccharis tricuneata var. lineata, commonly known as the lineated baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
