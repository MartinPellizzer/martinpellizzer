Balsamea pedunculata

Balsamea pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Balsamea pedunculata is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
