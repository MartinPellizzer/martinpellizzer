Brunfelsia multiflora

Brunfelsia multiflora, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces showy, showy, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Brunfelsia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
