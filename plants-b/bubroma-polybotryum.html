Bubroma polybotryum

Bubroma polybotryum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming at night. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally beneath the water.

Bubroma polybotryum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
