Baccharoides hookeri

Baccharoides hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months.

Baccharoides hookeri is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
