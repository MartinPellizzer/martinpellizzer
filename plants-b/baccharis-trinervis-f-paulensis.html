Baccharis trinervis f. paulensis

Baccharis trinervis f. paulensis, commonly known as the Pauline baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its species name "trinervis.". It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in ornamental landscaping.
