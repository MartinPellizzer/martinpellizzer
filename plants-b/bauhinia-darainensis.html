Bauhinia darainensis

Bauhinia darainensis, commonly known as the Darain's bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, a common feature among legumes.

Bauhinia darainensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
