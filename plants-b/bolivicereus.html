Bolivicereus

Bolivicereus, commonly known as the Bolivian cactus, is a genus of cacti native to South America, particularly Bolivia and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its columnar stems, which are often ribbed and covered in spines. The flowers of Bolivicereus are typically vibrant, ranging from yellow to red, and bloom during the warmer months. These plants are adapted to arid environments, storing water in their thick stems to survive in drought conditions.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
