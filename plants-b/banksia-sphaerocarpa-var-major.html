Banksia sphaerocarpa var. major

Banksia sphaerocarpa var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, with periods of drought and seasonal rainfall.

Its flowers attract a variety of nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
