Barleria buddleioides

Barleria buddleioides, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting, showy inflorescences and drought tolerance.

Barleria buddleioides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
