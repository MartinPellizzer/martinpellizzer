Brachypodium diaphanum

Brachypodium diaphanum, commonly known as the transparent grass, is a small, annual grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often translucent or semi-transparent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
