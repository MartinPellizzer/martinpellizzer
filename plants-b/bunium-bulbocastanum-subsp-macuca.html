Bunium bulbocastanum subsp. macuca

Bunium bulbocastanum subsp.

macuca, commonly known as the Macuca fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces a bulbous root system, which is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Apiaceae family.
