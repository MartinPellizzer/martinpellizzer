Begonia dasypoda

Begonia dasypoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or white, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
