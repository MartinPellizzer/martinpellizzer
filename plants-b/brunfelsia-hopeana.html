Brunfelsia hopeana

Brunfelsia hopeana, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Brunfelsia hopeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
