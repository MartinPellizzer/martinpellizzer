Bernardia cinerea

Bernardia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Bernardia cinerea is considered a rare and endangered species.
