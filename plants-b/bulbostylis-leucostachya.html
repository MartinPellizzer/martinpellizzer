Bulbostylis leucostachya

Bulbostylis leucostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed bulbostylis, is a species of grass found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and a tufted inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster. It is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies.

Bulbostylis leucostachya plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
