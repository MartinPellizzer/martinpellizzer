Bridelia stipularis subsp. philippinensis

Bridelia stipularis subsp.

philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine bridelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
