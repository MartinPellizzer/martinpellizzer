Bupleurum falcatum f. elatum

Bupleurum falcatum f. elatum, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in open, disturbed habitats.
