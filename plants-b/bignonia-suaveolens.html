Bignonia suaveolens

Bignonia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and striking, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces long, slender, green tendrils that help it climb and spread along structures. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Bignonia suaveolens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
