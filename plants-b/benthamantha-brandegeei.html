Benthamantha brandegeei

Benthamantha brandegeei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces silky, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a fluffy, three-lobed capsule. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

Benthamantha brandegeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
