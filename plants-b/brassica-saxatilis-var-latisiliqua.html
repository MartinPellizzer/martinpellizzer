Brassica saxatilis var. latisiliqua

Brassica saxatilis var. latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

It is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
