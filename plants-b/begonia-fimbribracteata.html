Begonia fimbribracteata

Begonia fimbribracteata, commonly known as the fringed-bract begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive patterns and margins, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
