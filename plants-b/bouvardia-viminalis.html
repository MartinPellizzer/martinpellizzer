Bouvardia viminalis

Bouvardia viminalis, commonly known as the trailing bouvardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Bouvardia viminalis is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in containers due to its elegant appearance.
