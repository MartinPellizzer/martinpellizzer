Botrychium matricariifolium subsp. patagonicum

Botrychium matricariifolium subsp.

patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian moonwort, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae. It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that resemble those of a fern, though it lacks true leaves.

The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a distinctive, helmet-like shape at the tip of the frond. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in temperate regions of South America.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
