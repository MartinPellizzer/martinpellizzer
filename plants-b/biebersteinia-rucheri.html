Biebersteinia rucheri

Biebersteinia rucheri, commonly known as the St. Helena biebersteinia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's unique microclimate. Due to its restricted habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
