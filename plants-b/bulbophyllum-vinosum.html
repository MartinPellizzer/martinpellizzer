Bulbophyllum vinosum

Bulbophyllum vinosum, commonly known as the wine-pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to pink petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in a dense inflorescence.

Bulbophyllum vinosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
