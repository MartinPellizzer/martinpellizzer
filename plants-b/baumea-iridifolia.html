Baumea iridifolia

Baumea iridifolia, commonly known as the silver grass or silverleaf grass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, silver-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Baumea iridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
