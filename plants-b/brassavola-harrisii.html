Brassavola harrisii

Brassavola harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassavola harrisii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
