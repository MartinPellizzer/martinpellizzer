Bulbinella graminifolia

Bulbinella graminifolia, commonly known as the grassland lily, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It produces small, delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought.
