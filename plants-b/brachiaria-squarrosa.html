Brachiaria squarrosa

Brachiaria squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in wet or poorly drained environments.

Brachiaria squarrosa is also recognized for its potential use in forage and erosion control in agricultural settings.
