Bombax chastifolium

Bombax chastifolium, commonly known as the silky ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed trunk and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The silky ceiba plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
