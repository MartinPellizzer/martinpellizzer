Brachiaria subrostrata

Brachiaria subrostrata, commonly known as *grass of paraguay*, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both florets and sterile lemmas. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachiaria subrostrata is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value and adaptability.
