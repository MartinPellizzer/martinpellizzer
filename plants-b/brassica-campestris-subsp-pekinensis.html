Brassica campestris subsp. pekinensis

Brassica campestris subsp.

pekinensis, commonly known as Chinese kale or neep, is a cultivated variety of the mustard family. It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and sturdy stalks, which are often used in Asian cuisine. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is a popular vegetable in China and other parts of East Asia.
