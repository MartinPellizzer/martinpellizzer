Boehmeria platyphylla var. nigeriana

Boehmeria platyphylla var. nigeriana, commonly known as African nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, stinging hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued for its strong, durable fibers used in traditional textiles and papermaking.
