Begonia parvipeltata var. bahiensis

Begonia parvipeltata var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian begonia, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
