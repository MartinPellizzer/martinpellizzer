Bupleurum intermedium var. genuinum

Bupleurum intermedium var. genuinum, commonly known as the European flathead, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
