Brachycladium pilosellum

Brachycladium pilosellum, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluemink, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue-green flowers, which are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens for ornamental purposes.
