Bridelia tomentosa var. rhamnoides

Bridelia tomentosa var. rhamnoides, commonly known as the silky bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a pubescent surface, and it produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and visual appeal.
