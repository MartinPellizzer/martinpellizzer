Bromus madritensis var. polystachyus

Bromus madritensis var. polystachyus, commonly known as the Spanish brome, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.
