Benthamia capitata

Benthamia capitata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows from a short, tuberous root system and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually oblanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in dense, spherical clusters.

Benthamia capitata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
