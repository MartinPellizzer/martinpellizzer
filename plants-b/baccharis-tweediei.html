Baccharis tweediei

Baccharis tweediei, commonly known as smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important species in wetland restoration projects.

Additionally, it serves as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.
