Berberis × rehderiana

Berberis × rehderiana, commonly known as the Oregon grape.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow berries. The plant belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.
