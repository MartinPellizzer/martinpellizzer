Bauhinia buscalionii

Bauhinia buscalionii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in vibrant orange or red hues, typically during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are followed by flat, papery pods that contain several seeds.

Bauhinia buscalionii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
