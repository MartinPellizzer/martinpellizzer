Begonia leprosa

Begonia leprosa, commonly known as the moss begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that resembles moss, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
