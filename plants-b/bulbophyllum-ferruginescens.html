Bulbophyllum ferruginescens

Bulbophyllum ferruginescens, commonly known as the rust-colored orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a reddish tinge. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, adapting to its humid, shaded habitats.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
