Berberis trifurca

Berberis trifurca, commonly known as the three-branched barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often divided into three parts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that mature in the fall. Berberis trifurca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy plant in various garden settings.
