Balsamita vulgaris

Balsamita vulgaris, commonly known as common balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

Balsamita vulgaris is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
