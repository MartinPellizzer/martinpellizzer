Bromus villosus var. rigidus

Bromus villosus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid hairy bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which helps stabilize soil in various environments. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil erosion control.
