Bermudiana humilis

Bermudiana humilis, commonly known as the Bermuda grass.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass native to the subtropical regions of the Caribbean and parts of the southeastern United States. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought and heat.

Its botanical features make it a common species in lawns and pastures.
