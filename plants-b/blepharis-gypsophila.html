Blepharis gypsophila

Blepharis gypsophila, commonly known as the gypsum blepharis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often on gypsum outcrops, which contribute to its specific ecological niche. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species, often found in isolated populations.
