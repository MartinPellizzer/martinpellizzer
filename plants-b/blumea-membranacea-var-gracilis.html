Blumea membranacea var. gracilis

Blumea membranacea var. gracilis, commonly known as slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
