Badula leandriana

Badula leandriana, commonly known as the Cape foveolata, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive central boss. The plant has a woody stem and produces compound leaves with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
