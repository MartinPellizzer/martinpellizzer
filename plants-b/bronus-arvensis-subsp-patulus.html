Bronus arvensis subsp. patulus

Bronus arvensis subsp.

patulus, commonly known as the slender field gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
