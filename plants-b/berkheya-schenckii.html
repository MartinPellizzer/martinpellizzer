Berkheya schenckii

Berkheya schenckii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a South African flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange ray florets and is native to the fynbos biome. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. It has a compact, mat-like growth habit and produces small, tubular disk flowers.

Berkheya schenckii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
