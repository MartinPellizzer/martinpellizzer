Brachistus riparius

Brachistus riparius, commonly known as the slender waterwheel plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are adapted for water retention, and its root system helps stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Brachistus riparius plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
