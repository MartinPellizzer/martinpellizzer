Brachycladium ursulum

Brachycladium ursulum, commonly known as the bear's tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachycladium ursulum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems, supporting various insect and bird populations.
