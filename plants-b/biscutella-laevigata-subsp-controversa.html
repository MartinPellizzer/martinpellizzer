Biscutella laevigata subsp. controversa

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

controversa, commonly known as the smooth biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
