Bergeronia

Bergeronia, commonly known as the glasshouse plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. Bergeronia species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and ease of care.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and require consistently moist, well-draining soil.
