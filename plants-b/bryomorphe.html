Bryomorphe

Bryomorphe, commonly known as the moss-like plant, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the division Bryophyta.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called rhizoids and gametophytes. Bryomorphe reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia. These plants play a crucial role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in their ecosystems.

Their simple structure makes them valuable indicators of environmental conditions.
