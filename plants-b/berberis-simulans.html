Berberis simulans

Berberis simulans, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall color and historical use in traditional medicine.
