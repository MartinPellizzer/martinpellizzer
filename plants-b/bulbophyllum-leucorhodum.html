Bulbophyllum leucorhodum

Bulbophyllum leucorhodum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with numerous white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
