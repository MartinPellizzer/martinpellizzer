Baccharis radin

Baccharis radin, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baccharis radin is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
