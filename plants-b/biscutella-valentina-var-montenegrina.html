Biscutella valentina var. montenegrina

Biscutella valentina var. montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
