Begonia rhyacophila

Begonia rhyacophila, commonly known as the water begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia rhyacophila is popular in hanging baskets and as a ground cover in shaded gardens.
