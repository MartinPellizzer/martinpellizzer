Bombax encephalartos

Bombax encephalartos, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are typically yellow and green, respectively. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its slow growth and susceptibility to environmental stress, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
