Bolelia ornatissima

Bolelia ornatissima, commonly known as the ornate bolelia, is a unique flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble intricate lace patterns, which are adapted for attracting specific pollinators. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its humid, high-altitude habitat. Bolelia ornatissima thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing along forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
