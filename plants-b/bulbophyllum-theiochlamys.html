Bulbophyllum theiochlamys

Bulbophyllum theiochlamys, commonly known as the dark-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and inflorescence. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive dark lip that gives it its common name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
