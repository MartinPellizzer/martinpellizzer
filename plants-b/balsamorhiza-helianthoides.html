Balsamorhiza helianthoides

Balsamorhiza helianthoides, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Balsamorhiza helianthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
