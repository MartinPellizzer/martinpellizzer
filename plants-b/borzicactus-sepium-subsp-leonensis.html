Borzicactus sepium subsp. leonensis

Borzicactus sepium subsp.

leonensis, commonly known as the Leonensis Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations include thick, water-storing stems and reduced leaves, which help it survive in harsh, dry environments.

It is often found growing in rocky soils and is well-suited to regions with low rainfall.
