Boehmeria amarantus

Boehmeria amarantus, commonly known as red bamboo or red silk cotton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and are used in traditional crafts and textiles.

Boehmeria amarantus is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
