Beta vulgaris var. perennis

Beta vulgaris var. perennis, commonly known as sugar beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its roots, which are used in the production of sugar and other industrial products.
