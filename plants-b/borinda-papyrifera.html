Borinda papyrifera

Borinda papyrifera, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Borinda papyrifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique bark features.
