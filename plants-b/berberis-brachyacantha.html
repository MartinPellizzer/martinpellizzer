Berberis brachyacantha

Berberis brachyacantha, commonly known as the narrow-fruited barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp taste.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
