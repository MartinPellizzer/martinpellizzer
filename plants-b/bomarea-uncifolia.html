Bomarea uncifolia

Bomarea uncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with prominent veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
