Bulbophyllum gracile

Bulbophyllum gracile, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Bulbophyllum gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
