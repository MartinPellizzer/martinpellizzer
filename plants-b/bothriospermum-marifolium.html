Bothriospermum marifolium

Bothriospermum marifolium, commonly known as the marigold tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, bark-like surface and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
