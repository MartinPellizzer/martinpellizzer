Bacopa montevidensis

Bacopa montevidensis, commonly known as water bead or Java moss, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth pattern and tiny, rounded leaves that resemble beads. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life. It reproduces through vegetative propagation, spreading rapidly by attaching to surfaces in its habitat.

Bacopa montevidensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
