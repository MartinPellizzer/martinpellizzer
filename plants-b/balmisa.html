Balmisa

Balmisa, commonly known as the common balm, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Balmisa is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and mild antiseptic qualities.
