Bunias erucago

Bunias erucago, commonly known as field mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and pinnately divided, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces long, slender pods containing seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
