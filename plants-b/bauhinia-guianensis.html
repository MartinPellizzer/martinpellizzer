Bauhinia guianensis

Bauhinia guianensis, commonly known as the "Cape Flats bauhinia," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bauhinia guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
