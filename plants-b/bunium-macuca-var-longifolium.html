Bunium macuca var. longifolium

Bunium macuca var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar herbs like parsley and dill. Its long, slender leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The seeds are also harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes.
