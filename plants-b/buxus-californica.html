Buxus californica

Buxus californica, commonly known as the California boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in compact clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

Due to its slow growth and compact form, it is often used in formal garden designs.
