Bursera bonairensis

Bursera bonairensis, commonly known as the Bonaire bursera, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Bonaire in the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds.

Bursera bonairensis is adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a resilient component of Bonaire's unique flora.
