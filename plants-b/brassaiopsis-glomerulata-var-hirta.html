Brassaiopsis glomerulata var. hirta

Brassaiopsis glomerulata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy clustered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
