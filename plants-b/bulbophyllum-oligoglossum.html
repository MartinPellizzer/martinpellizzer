Bulbophyllum oligoglossum

Bulbophyllum oligoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
