Benjaminia glabra

Benjaminia glabra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed benjaminia, is a rare and woody shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the autumn season, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures make it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
