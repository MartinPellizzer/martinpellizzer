Bupleurum longeradiatum var. sachalinense

Bupleurum longeradiatum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and northern Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.
