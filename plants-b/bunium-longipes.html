Bunium longipes

Bunium longipes, commonly known as the longipes fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine for their digestive properties.

The plant's structure includes a taproot and hollow stems, typical of many members of its family.
