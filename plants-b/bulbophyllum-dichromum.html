Bulbophyllum dichromum

Bulbophyllum dichromum, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers with a distinctive two-tone pattern on the petals and sepals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
