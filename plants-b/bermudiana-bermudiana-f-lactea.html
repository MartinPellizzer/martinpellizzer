Bermudiana bermudiana f. lactea

Bermudiana bermudiana f. lactea, commonly known as the white-veined Bermuda grass, is a variant of the Bermuda grass species characterized by its distinctive white veins on the leaves.

This ornamental grass is native to the subtropical regions of the Caribbean and parts of the southern United States. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates. The plant's foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a dense growth habit that provides good ground cover.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored option for erosion control and decorative use in gardens.
