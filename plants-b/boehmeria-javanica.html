Boehmeria javanica

Boehmeria javanica, commonly known as Chinese hemp or Bambu kertas, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its fibrous, woody stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and are used in making paper, textiles, and other industrial products.
