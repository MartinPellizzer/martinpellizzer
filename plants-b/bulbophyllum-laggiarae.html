Bulbophyllum laggiarae

Bulbophyllum laggiarae, commonly known as the Laggiara's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, showy inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry periods.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and research.
