Bignonia sorbifolia

Bignonia sorbifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia sorbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
