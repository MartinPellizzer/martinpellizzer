Bambusa bifolia

Bambusa bifolia, commonly known as the split bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that often split into two distinct parts, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Bambusa bifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
