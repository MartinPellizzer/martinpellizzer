Berberis affinis

Berberis affinis, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Berberis affinis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
