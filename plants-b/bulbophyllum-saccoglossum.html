Bulbophyllum saccoglossum

Bulbophyllum saccoglossum, commonly known as the sac-like tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or green with purple markings, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture. It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
