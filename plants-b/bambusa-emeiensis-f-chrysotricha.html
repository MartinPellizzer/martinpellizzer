Bambusa emeiensis f. chrysotricha

Bambusa emeiensis f. chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired bamboo, is a variant of the Emei bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden to yellowish-brown culm sheaths that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to China and thrives in subtropical climates with high humidity. It has a robust growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are typically narrow and green, complementing the colorful sheaths.
