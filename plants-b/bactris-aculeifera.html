Bactris aculeifera

Bactris aculeifera, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and a tall, slender trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small pineapples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
