Bulbophyllum lehmannianum

Bulbophyllum lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lemon Balm Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a yellowish-green color.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
