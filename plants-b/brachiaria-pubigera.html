Brachiaria pubigera

Brachiaria pubigera, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Brachiaria pubigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
