Bulbophyllum artvogelii

Bulbophyllum artvogelii, commonly known as the Artvogel's orchid, is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain water in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
