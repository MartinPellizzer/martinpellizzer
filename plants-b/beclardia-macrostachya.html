Beclardia macrostachya

Beclardia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant beclardia, is a striking member of the Poaceae family, native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stature, with slender, grass-like blades that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, panicle-like inflorescences that are adorned with small, spikelet-based flowers, often displaying a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Beclardia macrostachya is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
