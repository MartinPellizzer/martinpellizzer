Bignonia arthrerion

Bignonia arthrerion, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces long, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
