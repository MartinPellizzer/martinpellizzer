Bencomia maderensis

Bencomia maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Bencomia maderensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
