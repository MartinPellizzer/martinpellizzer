Bromus squarrosus var. villosus

Bromus squarrosus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

Bromus squarrosus var. villosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
