Begonia acclivis

Begonia acclivis, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate blooms and foliage.
