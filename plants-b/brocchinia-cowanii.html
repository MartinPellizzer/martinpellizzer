Brocchinia cowanii

Brocchinia cowanii, commonly known as the Cowan's brocchinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Colombia and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne on a central stem. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, tropical environment.

Brocchinia cowanii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
