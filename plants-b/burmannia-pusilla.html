Burmannia pusilla

Burmannia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf burmannia, is a small herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its short, upright stem and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its delicate nature, it is rarely seen outside its natural habitat.
