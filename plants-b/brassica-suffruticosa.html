Brassica suffruticosa

Brassica suffruticosa, commonly known as the shrub mustard, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, compact growth habit, often forming shrub-like structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Brassica suffruticosa is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
