Bidens setigeroides

Bidens setigeroides, commonly known as the horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its rough, bristly stems and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is considered a weed in many regions.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
