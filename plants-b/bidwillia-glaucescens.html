Bidwillia glaucescens

Bidwillia glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf bidwillia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Bidwillia glaucescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
