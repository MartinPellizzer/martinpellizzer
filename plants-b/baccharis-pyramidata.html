Baccharis pyramidata

Baccharis pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis pyramidata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
