Bursera vejar-vazquezii

Bursera vejar-vazquezii, commonly known as the Vejar-Vazquez Bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical dry forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by triangular, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
