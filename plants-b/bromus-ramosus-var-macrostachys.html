Bromus ramosus var. macrostachys

Bromus ramosus var. macrostachys, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features tufted culms with ascending to erect growth, and its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
