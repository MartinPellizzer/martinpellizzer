Borzicactus sepium subsp. sepium

Borzicactus sepium subsp.

sepium, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, shrub-like structure.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment. Borzicactus sepium subsp.

sepium is well adapted to extreme drought conditions and thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils.
