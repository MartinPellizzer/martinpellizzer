Bupleurum longicaule var. ramosum

Bupleurum longicaule var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and serrated, arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
