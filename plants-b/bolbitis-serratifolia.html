Bolbitis serratifolia

Bolbitis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Africa, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

The plant's spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
