Barberina crenata

Barberina crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can reach several feet in height.

Barberina crenata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
