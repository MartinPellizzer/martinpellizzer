Begonia descoleana

Begonia descoleana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
