Basella oleracea var. nigra

Basella oleracea var. nigra, commonly known as black nightshade or black basella, is a tropical and subtropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black leaves, which are thick and succulent, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, spinach-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and regular watering.

Basella oleracea var. nigra is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
