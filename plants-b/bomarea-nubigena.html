Bomarea nubigena

Bomarea nubigena, commonly known as the cloud-bomarea, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant is notable for its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests where humidity is high.

Due to its habitat specificity, Bomarea nubigena is considered a rare and endangered species.
