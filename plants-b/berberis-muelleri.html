Berberis muelleri

Berberis muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
