Barleriola saturejoides subsp. hirsuta

Barleriola saturejoides subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy barleriola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

The plant has slender, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, opposite leaves are usually lanceolate and also bear a hairy texture.

The flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and are typically pink to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.
