Berberis vulgaris var. nigra

Berberis vulgaris var. nigra, commonly known as black berberis, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
