Banisteria simulans

Banisteria simulans, commonly known as the red-eyed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Banisteria simulans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
