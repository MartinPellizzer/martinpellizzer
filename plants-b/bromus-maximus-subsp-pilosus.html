Bromus maximus subsp. pilosus

Bromus maximus subsp.

pilosus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually gray-green in color and have a rough texture.

It is often found in areas with low precipitation and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
