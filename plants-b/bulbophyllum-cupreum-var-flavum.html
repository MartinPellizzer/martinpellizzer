Bulbophyllum cupreum var. flavum

Bulbophyllum cupreum var. flavum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often yellow or golden-colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
