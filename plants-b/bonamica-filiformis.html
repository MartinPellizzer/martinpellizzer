Bonamica filiformis

Bonamica filiformis, commonly known as the slender bonamica, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its flowering period typically occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in specialized botanical gardens and conservation areas.
