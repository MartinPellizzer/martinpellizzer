Bombax globosum

Bombax globosum, commonly known as the cotton tree or kapok tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive central column. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a fibrous pod, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several broad, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in producing natural fibers.
