Blephilia hirsuta f. albiflora

Blephilia hirsuta f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
