Brachiaria soluta

Brachiaria soluta, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and long, slender leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria soluta is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as forage for livestock.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
