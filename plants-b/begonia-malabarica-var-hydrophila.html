Begonia malabarica var. hydrophila

Begonia malabarica var. hydrophila, commonly known as the water begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to purplish hues and vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil to flourish. Its unique ability to retain water in its leaves makes it well-suited for aquatic or boggy habitats.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
