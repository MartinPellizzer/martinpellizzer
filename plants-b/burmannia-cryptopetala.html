Burmannia cryptopetala

Burmannia cryptopetala, commonly known as the underground burmannia, is a unique tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive underground stem and lack of above-ground foliage, which allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath the soil surface. Its reproductive strategy involves attracting specific insect pollinators through scent rather than visual cues.

Due to its rare and specialized growth habits, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical research.
