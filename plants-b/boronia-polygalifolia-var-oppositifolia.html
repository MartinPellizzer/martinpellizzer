Boronia polygalifolia var. oppositifolia

Boronia polygalifolia var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, giving it the specific epithet "oppositifolia.".

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
