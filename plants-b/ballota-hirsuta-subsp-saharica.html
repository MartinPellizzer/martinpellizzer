Ballota hirsuta subsp. saharica

Ballota hirsuta subsp.

saharica, commonly known as the Saharan ballota, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping. Ballota hirsuta subsp.

saharica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing desert soils.
