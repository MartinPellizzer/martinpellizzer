Bidens barrancae

Bidens barrancae, commonly known as the Barranca ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in yellow to orange hues.

Bidens barrancae is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
