Barleria megalosiphon

Barleria megalosiphon, commonly known as the large-lipped barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value in native habitats.
