Berberis tomentosa

Berberis tomentosa, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense, sprawling colonies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and covered in a dense white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

Berberis tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
