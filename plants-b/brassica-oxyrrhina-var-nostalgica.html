Brassica oxyrrhina var. nostalgica

Brassica oxyrrhina var. nostalgica, commonly known as the nostalgic mustard, is a rare and unique variant of the wild mustard plant.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are small and contain high levels of glucosinolates, which are natural compounds with potential health benefits.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
