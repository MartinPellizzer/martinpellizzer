Baphicacanthus multibracteolata

Baphicacanthus multibracteolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowroot or the elephant's ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible tubers.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens.
