Begonia glechomifolia

Begonia glechomifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
