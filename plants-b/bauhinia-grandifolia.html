Bauhinia grandifolia

Bauhinia grandifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia grandifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
