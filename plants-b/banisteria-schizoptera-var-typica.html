Banisteria schizoptera var. typica

Banisteria schizoptera var. typica, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance in native habitats.
