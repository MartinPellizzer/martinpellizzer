Begonia fuchsioides

Begonia fuchsioides, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that are typically borne on upright stems. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its health.

Begonia fuchsioides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
