Bignonia cinnamomea

Bignonia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking orange-red flowers resembling those of the trumpet vine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, reddish hairs. Bignonia cinnamomea thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a climbing plant or trained against a trellis.
