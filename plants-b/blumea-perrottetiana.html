Blumea perrottetiana

Blumea perrottetiana, commonly known as the broadleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can reach up to 20 centimeters in length.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.
