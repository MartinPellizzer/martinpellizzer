Baeckea schollerifolia

Baeckea schollerifolia, commonly known as the Scholler's baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs during the spring and summer months.

Baeckea schollerifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
