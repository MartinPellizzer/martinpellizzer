Bipinnula biplumata

Bipinnula biplumata, commonly known as the double-plumed fern, is a distinctive fern species characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds.

Its leaves are composed of multiple pairs of pinnae, each further divided into smaller segments called pinnules, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The fronds are usually upright and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a graceful, arching form.

It is native to regions in Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticultural settings.
