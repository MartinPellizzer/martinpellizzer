Bauhinia kockiana var. beccarii

Bauhinia kockiana var. beccarii, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the woods, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often shed seasonally, and its pods are elongated, containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
