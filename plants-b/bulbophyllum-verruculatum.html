Bulbophyllum verruculatum

Bulbophyllum verruculatum, commonly known as the疣状万代兰, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
