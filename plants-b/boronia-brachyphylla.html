Boronia brachyphylla

Boronia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Boronia brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
