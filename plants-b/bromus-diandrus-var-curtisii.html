Bromus diandrus var. curtisii

Bromus diandrus var. curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is typically erect and glabrous. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
