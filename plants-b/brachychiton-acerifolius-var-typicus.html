Brachychiton acerifolius var. typicus

Brachychiton acerifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The tree has large, palmate leaves that are typically lobed and resemble maple leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
