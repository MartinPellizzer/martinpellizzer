Blechnum japanense

Blechnum japanense, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually evergreen and have a leathery texture.

Blechnum japanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
