Begonia sogerensis

Begonia sogerensis, commonly known as the Soger's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of small, brightly colored blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
