Berberis pectinata

Berberis pectinata, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, red, edible berries. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Berberis pectinata is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
