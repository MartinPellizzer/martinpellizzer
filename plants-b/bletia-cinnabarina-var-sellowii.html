Bletia cinnabarina var. sellowii

Bletia cinnabarina var. sellowii, commonly known as the red-tipped bletia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a glossy texture, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
