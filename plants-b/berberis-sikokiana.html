Berberis sikokiana

Berberis sikokiana, commonly known as the Sikokian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
