Barberina ramentacea

Barberina ramentacea, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Barberina ramentacea is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation.
