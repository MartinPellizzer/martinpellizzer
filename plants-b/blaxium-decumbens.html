Blaxium decumbens

Blaxium decumbens, commonly known as the creeping blaxium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Blaxium decumbens is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
