Bassia crassifolia

Bassia crassifolia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Bassia crassifolia is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
