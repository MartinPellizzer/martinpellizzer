Begonia piresiana

Begonia piresiana, commonly known as the Pires' begonia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which come in shades of red and pink, bloom in spring and summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Begonia piresiana prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
