Brassica nigra var. incana

Brassica nigra var. incana, commonly known as hoary mustard, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

Hoary mustard is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
