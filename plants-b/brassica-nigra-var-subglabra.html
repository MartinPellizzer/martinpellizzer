Brassica nigra var. subglabra

Brassica nigra var. subglabra, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by triangular, dark-colored pods containing several seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a significant species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
