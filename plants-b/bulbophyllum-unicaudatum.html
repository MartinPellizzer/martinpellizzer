Bulbophyllum unicaudatum

Bulbophyllum unicaudatum, commonly known as the single-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
