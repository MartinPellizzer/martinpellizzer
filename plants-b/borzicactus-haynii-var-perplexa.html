Borzicactus haynii var. perplexa

Borzicactus haynii var. perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing borzicactus, is a rare and unusual cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its textured foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
