Bryonia flexuosa

Bryonia flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping bryony, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, often found in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow, cucumber-like flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are lobed and can be mistaken for those of other nightshade plants.

Bryonia flexuosa is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.
