Brachystegia obliqua

Brachystegia obliqua, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It is often found in miombo woodlands and plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in reforestation efforts in degraded areas.
