Bifrenaria bella

Bifrenaria bella, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Bifrenaria bella is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
