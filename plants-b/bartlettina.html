Bartlettina

Bartlettina, commonly known as the "mountain daisy," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plants typically grow in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. They are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white.

Bartlettina species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
