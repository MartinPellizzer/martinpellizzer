Buddleja cooperi

Buddleja cooperi, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its ability to attract a wide variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Buddleja cooperi thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower colors, which range from white to pink and purple.
