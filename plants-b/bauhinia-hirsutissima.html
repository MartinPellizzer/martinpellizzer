Bauhinia hirsutissima

Bauhinia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bauhinia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a hairy texture, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, and they appear in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
