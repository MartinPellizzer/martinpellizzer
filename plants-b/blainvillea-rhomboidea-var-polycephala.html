Blainvillea rhomboidea var. polycephala

Blainvillea rhomboidea var. polycephala, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that form a compact, bushy appearance. The plant produces yellowish to orange spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in small, rounded heads. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a leathery texture, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
