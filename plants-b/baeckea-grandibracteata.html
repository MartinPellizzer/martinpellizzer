Baeckea grandibracteata

Baeckea grandibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Baeckea grandibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
