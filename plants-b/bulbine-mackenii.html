Bulbine mackenii

Bulbine mackenii, commonly known as the Natal bulbous plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to drought, with a short growing season and a reliance on seasonal rainfall.
