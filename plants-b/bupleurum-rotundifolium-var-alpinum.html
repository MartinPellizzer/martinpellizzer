Bupleurum rotundifolium var. alpinum

Bupleurum rotundifolium var. alpinum, commonly known as Alpine Bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often forming dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are rounded and lobed, with a distinctive wavy margin, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

This variant is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting a compact growth form to withstand harsh weather conditions.
