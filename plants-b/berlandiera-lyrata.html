Berlandiera lyrata

Berlandiera lyrata, commonly known as the hummingbird trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branching.

The species is valued in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
