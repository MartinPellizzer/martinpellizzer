Brassica oleracea var. procera

Brassica oleracea var. procera, commonly known as the tree kale, is a cultivated variety of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that are often used as a vegetable. The plant produces large, dark green leaves that are edible and have a slightly bitter taste. It is typically grown in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and versatility in culinary use.
