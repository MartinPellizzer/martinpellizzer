Brugmansia chlorantha

Brugmansia chlorantha, commonly known as the white angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters tall, with thick, corky bark and opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Brugmansia chlorantha is noted for its ornamental value but is also toxic, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
