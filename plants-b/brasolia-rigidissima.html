Brasolia rigidissima

Brasolia rigidissima, commonly known as the rigid flamebush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its foliage is typically narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Brasolia rigidissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a drought-tolerant landscape plant.
