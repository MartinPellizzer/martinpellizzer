Barleria bechuanensis var. espinulosa

Barleria bechuanensis var. espinulosa, commonly known as the spiked barleria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
