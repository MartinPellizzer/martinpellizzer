Baccharis daphnoides subsp. schomburgkii

Baccharis daphnoides subsp.

schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
