Brassica nigra

Brassica nigra, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing dark, elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Brassica nigra is valued for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
