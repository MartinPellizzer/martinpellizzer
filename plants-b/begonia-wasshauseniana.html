Begonia wasshauseniana

Begonia wasshauseniana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique botanical features.
