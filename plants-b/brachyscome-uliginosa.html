Brachyscome uliginosa

Brachyscome uliginosa, commonly known as the bog bristlebur, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in swampy areas along the eastern coast of Australia. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Brachyscome uliginosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in waterlogged conditions.
