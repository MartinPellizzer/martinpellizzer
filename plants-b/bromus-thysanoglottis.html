Bromus thysanoglottis

Bromus thysanoglottis, commonly known as the silky brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are covered in soft, silky hairs.

Bromus thysanoglottis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
