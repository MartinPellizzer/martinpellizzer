Barleria heterotricha subsp. heterotricha

Barleria heterotricha subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, narrow leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
