Bridelia griffithii var. penangiana

Bridelia griffithii var. penangiana, commonly known as Penang bridelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
