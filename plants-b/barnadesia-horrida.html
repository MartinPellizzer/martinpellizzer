Barnadesia horrida

Barnadesia horrida, commonly known as the rough barnadesia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Barnadesia horrida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
