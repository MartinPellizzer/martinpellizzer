Bursera discolor

Bursera discolor, commonly known as the white balsa or white bursera, is a small, evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
