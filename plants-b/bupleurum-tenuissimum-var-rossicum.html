Bupleurum tenuissimum var. rossicum

Bupleurum tenuissimum var. rossicum, commonly known as the Ross's slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
