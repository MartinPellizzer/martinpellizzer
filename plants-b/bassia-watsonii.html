Bassia watsonii

Bassia watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's wormwood, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
