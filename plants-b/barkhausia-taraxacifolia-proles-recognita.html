Barkhausia taraxacifolia proles recognita

Barkhausia taraxacifolia proles recognita, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds resembling those of a dandelion. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, unbranched stem.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and unique floral display.
