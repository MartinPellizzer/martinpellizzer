Bothriochloa bladhii subsp. glabra

Bothriochloa bladhii subsp.

glabra, commonly known as smooth bladh's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or very softly hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
