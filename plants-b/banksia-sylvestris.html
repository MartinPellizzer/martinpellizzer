Banksia sylvestris

Banksia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. Banksia sylvestris thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
