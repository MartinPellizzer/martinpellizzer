Burtonia gompholobioides

Burtonia gompholobioides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Burtonia gompholobioides is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
