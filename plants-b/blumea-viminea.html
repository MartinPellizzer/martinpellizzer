Blumea viminea

Blumea viminea, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces elliptical, woody fruits.

Blumea viminea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
