Beta vulgaris var. mediasiatica

Beta vulgaris var. mediasiatica, commonly known as the leaf beet or leaf beetroot, is a variant of the beet plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, edible leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are rich in nutrients such as iron and calcium, making it a valuable vegetable.

The plant's botanical structure includes a taproot system and a rosette of leaves that form the edible portion.
