Bambos tabacaria

Bambos tabacaria, commonly known as the tobacco bamboo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a historical association with tobacco due to its name.
