Bulbophyllum subapproximatum

Bulbophyllum subapproximatum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped inflorescence and its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid produces small, intricate flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
