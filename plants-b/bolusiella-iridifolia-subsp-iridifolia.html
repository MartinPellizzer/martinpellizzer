Bolusiella iridifolia subsp. iridifolia

Bolusiella iridifolia subsp.

iridifolia, commonly known as the bluebell of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters along the stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
