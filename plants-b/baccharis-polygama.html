Baccharis polygama

Baccharis polygama, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Baccharis polygama is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous peoples.
