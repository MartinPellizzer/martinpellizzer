Banksia rufa subsp. flavescens

Banksia rufa subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical inflorescences, which are typically yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating, and the plant is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Banksia rufa subsp.

flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
