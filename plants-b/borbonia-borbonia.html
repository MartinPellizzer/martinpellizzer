Borbonia borbonia

Borbonia borbonia, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

Borbonia borbonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
