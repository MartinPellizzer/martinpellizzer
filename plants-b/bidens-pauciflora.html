Bidens pauciflora

Bidens pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers with white ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Bidens pauciflora is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
