Bursera gummifera var. glabrata

Bursera gummifera var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bursera, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The tree is also known for its ability to exude a resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
