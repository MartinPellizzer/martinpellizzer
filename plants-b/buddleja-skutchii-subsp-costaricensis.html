Buddleja skutchii subsp. costaricensis

Buddleja skutchii subsp.

costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican weeping buddleia, is a shrub native to Central America. It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral displays. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
