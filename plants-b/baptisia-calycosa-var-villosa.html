Baptisia calycosa var. villosa

Baptisia calycosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its varietal name "villosa.". It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
