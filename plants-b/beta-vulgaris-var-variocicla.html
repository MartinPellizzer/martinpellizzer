Beta vulgaris var. variocicla

Beta vulgaris var. variocicla, commonly known as the rainbow chard, is a cultivar of the beetroot plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful stalks.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to the sugar beet and regular beetroot. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can range in color from deep red to purple, yellow, and white. Its stems are edible and often used in cooking, while the leaves are also consumed as a leafy green vegetable.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in both culinary and ornamental settings.
