Betela

Betela, commonly known as betel nut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Areca family and is cultivated for its large, fibrous seeds. The plant produces a palm-like structure with elongated leaves and clusters of yellowish flowers. Its fruit, which contains the edible seed, is widely used in traditional practices and rituals.

The botanical features of Betela make it an important species in both agricultural and cultural contexts.
