Baccharis tola subsp. altiplanicola

Baccharis tola subsp.

altiplanicola, commonly known as the Andean baccharis, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in the Andes Mountains. It has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to the harsh conditions of the altiplano, including strong sunlight and low oxygen levels.
