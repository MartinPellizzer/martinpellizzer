Bulbostylis truncata

Bulbostylis truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
