Bridelia griffithii var. glabra

Bridelia griffithii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bridelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
