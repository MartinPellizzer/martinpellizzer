Bolanthus ortegioides

Bolanthus ortegioides, commonly known as the Ortegui's burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces small, spiky fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Bolanthus ortegioides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.
