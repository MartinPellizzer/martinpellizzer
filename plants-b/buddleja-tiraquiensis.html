Buddleja tiraquiensis

Buddleja tiraquiensis, commonly known as the Tiraqui bush, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja tiraquiensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
