Begonia × albopicta

Begonia × albopicta, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a variety of colorful foliage and flowers.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that come in shades of green, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in clusters. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Begonia × albopicta is popular as a houseplant and for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
