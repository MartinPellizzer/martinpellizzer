Blumea cabulica

Blumea cabulica, commonly known as the Indian blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Blumea cabulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
