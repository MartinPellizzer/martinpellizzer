Beckmannia borealis

Beckmannia borealis, commonly known as the Arctic grass, is a low-growing perennial grass found in arctic and alpine regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize the soil in harsh environments.

Beckmannia borealis plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various arctic organisms.
