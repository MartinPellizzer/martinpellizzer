Blaeria sphagnicola

Blaeria sphagnicola, commonly known as the bog blazer, is a rare and unique moss species found in acidic, nutrient-poor environments.

It thrives in peat bogs and other wetland habitats where it forms dense, cushion-like growths. The plant has a dark green to blackish appearance, with a distinct, velvety texture that helps it retain moisture. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically developing on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Blaeria sphagnicola is considered an indicator species for undisturbed, pristine wetlands.
