Bassia paradoxa var. latifolia

Bassia paradoxa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert flora and ecological adaptations.
