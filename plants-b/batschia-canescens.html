Batschia canescens

Batschia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a distinctive, hollow stem that supports its flowering head. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Batschia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
