Berberis vulgaris subsp. seroi

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

seroi, commonly known as the Seroy barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including regions of China, India, and Nepal. It typically grows in forested areas and along mountain slopes, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is characterized by its spiny stems, compound leaves, and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is often consumed by birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
