Biscutella caroli-pauana

Biscutella caroli-pauana, commonly known as the Brazilian biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with four petals.

Biscutella caroli-pauana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
