Bidens asymmetrica

Bidens asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands, ditches, and roadside areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with white markings.

Bidens asymmetrica is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
