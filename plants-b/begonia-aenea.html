Begonia aenea

Begonia aenea, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a metallic sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, compact foliage that is both visually appealing and relatively low-maintenance.
