Brachycladium persimile

Brachycladium persimile, commonly known as the slender grass or slender bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Brachycladium persimile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
