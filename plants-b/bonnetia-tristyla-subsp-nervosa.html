Bonnetia tristyla subsp. nervosa

Bonnetia tristyla subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves with distinct venation.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
