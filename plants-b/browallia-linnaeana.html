Browallia linnaeana

Browallia linnaeana, commonly known as the common browallia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in shades of blue, violet, and white, often with contrasting yellow or orange markings.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
