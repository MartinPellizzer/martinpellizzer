Bidens pilosa f. odorata

Bidens pilosa f. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant horseweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant has small, serrated leaves and produces seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The fragrant odor associated with this plant is a distinguishing feature that contributes to its ecological and ornamental value.
