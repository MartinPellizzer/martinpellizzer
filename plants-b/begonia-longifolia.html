Begonia longifolia

Begonia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
