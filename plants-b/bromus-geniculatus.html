Bromus geniculatus

Bromus geniculatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or nodding brome, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often nodding inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus geniculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
