Baccharis czermakii var. corymbosa

Baccharis czermakii var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Chilean waxtree, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are often borne in showy corymbs. It is well adapted to coastal and arid environments, showing drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild winters.
