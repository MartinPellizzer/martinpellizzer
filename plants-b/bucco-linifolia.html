Bucco linifolia

Bucco linifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf bucco, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
