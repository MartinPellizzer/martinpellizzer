Blechnum keysseri

Blechnum keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a dense, lush appearance.

Blechnum keysseri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
