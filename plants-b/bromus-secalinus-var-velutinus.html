Bromus secalinus var. velutinus

Bromus secalinus var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often produce panicles of spikelets. The plant has soft, hairy leaves and bracts, giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Velvet foxtail is an important forage plant but can become invasive in certain environments.
