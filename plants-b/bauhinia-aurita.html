Bauhinia aurita

Bauhinia aurita, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellowish or reddish hue. Bauhinia aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
