Bambusa glaucescens f. kimmei-suhou

Bambusa glaucescens f. kimmei-suhou, commonly known as the Kimmei-Suhou bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue sheaths and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique coloration and graceful appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and bonsai cultivation.
