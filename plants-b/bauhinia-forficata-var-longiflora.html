Bauhinia forficata var. longiflora

Bauhinia forficata var. longiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, butterfly-like leaves that are bipinnate. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
