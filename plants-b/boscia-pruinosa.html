Boscia pruinosa

Boscia pruinosa, commonly known as the white boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, slightly hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Boscia pruinosa is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in dry regions.

Its ecological role includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
