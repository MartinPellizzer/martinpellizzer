Babiana falcata

Babiana falcata, commonly known as the spear lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Babiana falcata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
