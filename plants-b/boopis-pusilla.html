Boopis pusilla

Boopis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf boopis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in greenish-yellow or white, and bloom in the spring.

Boopis pusilla is characterized by its compact size and delicate appearance, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
