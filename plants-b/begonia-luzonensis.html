Begonia luzonensis

Begonia luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
