Brassica hybrida

Brassica hybrida, commonly known as the oilseed rape, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces pods containing round, oily seeds that are harvested for their high oil content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for agricultural and industrial purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in both food and biofuel production.
