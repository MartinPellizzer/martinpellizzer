Benzoin glaucescens

Benzoin glaucescens, commonly known as the white benzoin, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fragrant, resinous capsules.

The resin, known as benzoin, is harvested from the tree and is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
