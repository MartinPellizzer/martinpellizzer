Baccharis vauthieri

Baccharis vauthieri, commonly known as the golden baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and yellowish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Baccharis vauthieri is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and visual appeal.
