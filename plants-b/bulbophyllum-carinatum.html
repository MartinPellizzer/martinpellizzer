Bulbophyllum carinatum

Bulbophyllum carinatum, commonly known as the ridged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in forested areas with high humidity. The plant features a short rhizome with a cluster of fleshy, bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they are arranged in a raceme.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
