Bidens tenuissima

Bidens tenuissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its small, yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often considered a weed but has some traditional medicinal uses.
