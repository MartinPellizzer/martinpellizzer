Begonia labordei

Begonia labordei, commonly known as the laborde's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, colorful foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish or variegated patterns. The flowers, which bloom in spring and summer, are usually red or orange and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
