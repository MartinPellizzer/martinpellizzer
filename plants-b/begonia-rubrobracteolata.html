Begonia rubrobracteolata

Begonia rubrobracteolata, commonly known as the red bract begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose the small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with succulent stems and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters at the base of the plant, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
