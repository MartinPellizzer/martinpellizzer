Baptisia australis var. australis

Baptisia australis var. australis, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring tall, upright stems covered in blue to purple flower clusters. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

Baptisia australis var. australis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant for improving soil fertility.
