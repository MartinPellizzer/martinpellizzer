Bignonia staminea

Bignonia staminea, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are followed by long, narrow pods that contain several seeds.

Bignonia staminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
