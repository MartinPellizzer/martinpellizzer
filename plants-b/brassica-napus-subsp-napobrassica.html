Brassica napus subsp. napobrassica

Brassica napus subsp.

napobrassica, commonly known as field mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. It produces small, yellow, cross-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in racemes.

The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a smooth surface, and are often used as a source of oil.

This subspecies is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a weed and an agricultural crop.
