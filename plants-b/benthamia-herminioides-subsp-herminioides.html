Benthamia herminioides subsp. herminioides

Benthamia herminioides subsp.

herminioides, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in extreme desert conditions.
