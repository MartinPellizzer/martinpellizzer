Buphthalmum coriaceum

Buphthalmum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough buphthalmum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features hairy, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its scientific name reflects its tough, leathery foliage, which is a key botanical characteristic.
