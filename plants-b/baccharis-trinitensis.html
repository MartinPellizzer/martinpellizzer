Baccharis trinitensis

Baccharis trinitensis, commonly known as Trinidad baccharis, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Baccharis trinitensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
