Brasiliopuntia argentina

Brasiliopuntia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

Brasiliopuntia argentina is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
