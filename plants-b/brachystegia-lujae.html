Brachystegia lujae

Brachystegia lujae, commonly known as the Lujas' brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the miombo woodlands of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Brachystegia lujae is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in local crafts and construction.
