Berlinia acuminata

Berlinia acuminata, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

It is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
