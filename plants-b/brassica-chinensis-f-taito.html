Brassica chinensis f. taito

Brassica chinensis f. taito, commonly known as the Taito Chinese cabbage, is a cultivated variant of the Chinese cabbage species.

It is characterized by its compact, heady growth habit and thick, succulent leaves. The plant is typically harvested before the head fully matures, resulting in a tender and crisp texture. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown in temperate regions for its nutritional value and culinary versatility.

This variety is valued for its ability to withstand mild frost, making it a popular choice for fall and winter gardening.
