Bouteloua oligostachya var. ramosa

Bouteloua oligostachya var. ramosa, commonly known as the ramose bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, branching stems that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color, adding visual interest during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
