Brachyscome perpusilla var. tenella

Brachyscome perpusilla var. tenella, commonly known as the small globemallow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It features a dense mat of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
