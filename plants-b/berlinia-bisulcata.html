Berlinia bisulcata

Berlinia bisulcata, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its deep root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.
