Begonia californica

Begonia californica, commonly known as the California begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
