Bulbophyllum xanthobulbum

Bulbophyllum xanthobulbum, commonly known as the yellow bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to survive in humid, shaded environments.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.
