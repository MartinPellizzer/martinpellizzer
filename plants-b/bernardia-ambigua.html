Bernardia ambigua

Bernardia ambigua, commonly known as the false holly, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Bernardia ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
