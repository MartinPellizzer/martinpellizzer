Biermannia lohokii

Biermannia lohokii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the tropical rainforests of its native habitat. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Biermannia lohokii is also noted for its unique morphology, with intricate petal structures that aid in attracting pollinators.
