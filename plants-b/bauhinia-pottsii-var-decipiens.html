Bauhinia pottsii var. decipiens

Bauhinia pottsii var. decipiens, commonly known as the "false flame tree," is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
