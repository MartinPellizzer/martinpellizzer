Baccharis magellanica var. viscosissima

Baccharis magellanica var. viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky baccharis, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that produces a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually yellow or greenish, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
