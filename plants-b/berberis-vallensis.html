Berberis vallensis

Berberis vallensis, commonly known as the valley barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Berberis vallensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
