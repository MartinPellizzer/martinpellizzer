Borya cassinoides

Borya cassinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf borya, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dissemination.

Borya cassinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
