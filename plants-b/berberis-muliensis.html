Berberis muliensis

Berberis muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
