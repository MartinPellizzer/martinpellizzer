Botryolotus griffithii

Botryolotus griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible and contain a single seed. Botryolotus griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in regions where it is found.
