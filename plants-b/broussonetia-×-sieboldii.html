Broussonetia × sieboldii

Broussonetia × sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a hybrid species within the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its fibrous bark and leaves have been used historically in traditional crafts and medicine.
