Bauhinia faberi var. microphylla

Bauhinia faberi var. microphylla, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree or silk-flower bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, fern-like leaves that are small and delicate. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
