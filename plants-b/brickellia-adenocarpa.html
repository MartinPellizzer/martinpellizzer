Brickellia adenocarpa

Brickellia adenocarpa, commonly known as brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glandular-hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Brickellia adenocarpa is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
