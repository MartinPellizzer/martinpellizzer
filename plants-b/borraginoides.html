Borraginoides

Borraginoides, commonly known as the "buttercup family" or "burrweed," is a genus within the Boraginaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their hairy stems and often showy, blue or purple flowers. They typically grow in temperate regions and are found in various habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly woolly texture.

Borraginoides species are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
