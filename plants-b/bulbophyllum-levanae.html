Bulbophyllum levanae

Bulbophyllum levanae, commonly known as the Vanuatu orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the island of Vanuatu in the South Pacific.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its tropical habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
