Bulbophyllum williamsii

Bulbophyllum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
