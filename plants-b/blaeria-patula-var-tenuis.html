Blaeria patula var. tenuis

Blaeria patula var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender blaeria, is a herbaceous perennial native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
