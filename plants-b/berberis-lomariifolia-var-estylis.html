Berberis lomariifolia var. estylis

Berberis lomariifolia var. estylis, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
