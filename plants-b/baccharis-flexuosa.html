Baccharis flexuosa

Baccharis flexuosa, commonly known as brittle bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Baccharis flexuosa is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife in wetland ecosystems.
