Byrsonima perseifolia

Byrsonima perseifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its fruit, known as a "brazil nut," is enclosed in a hard shell and contains several seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
