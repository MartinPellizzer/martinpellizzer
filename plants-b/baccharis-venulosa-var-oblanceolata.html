Baccharis venulosa var. oblanceolata

Baccharis venulosa var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of swamps, thriving in moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
