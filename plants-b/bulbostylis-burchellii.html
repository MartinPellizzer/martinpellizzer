Bulbostylis burchellii

Bulbostylis burchellii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.

Bulbostylis burchellii plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
