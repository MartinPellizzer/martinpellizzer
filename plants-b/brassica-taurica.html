Brassica taurica

Brassica taurica, commonly known as the Taurian mustard, is a wild species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are small and contained within siliques, which are characteristic fruit structures of the mustard family.

Brassica taurica is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
