Blumea decurrens

Blumea decurrens, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, while its leaves are usually hairy and aromatic.

Blumea decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
