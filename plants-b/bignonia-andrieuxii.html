Bignonia andrieuxii

Bignonia andrieuxii, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces long, slender seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its invasive potential in some areas, it is often cultivated with caution.
