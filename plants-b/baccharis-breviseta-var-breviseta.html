Baccharis breviseta var. breviseta

Baccharis breviseta var. breviseta, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as plains, rocky hillsides, and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the fall. It is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.

The species is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a component in wildlife habitats.
