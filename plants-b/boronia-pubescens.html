Boronia pubescens

Boronia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
