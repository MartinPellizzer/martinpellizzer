Bonannia graeca

Bonannia graeca, commonly known as the Greek bonannia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Bonannia graeca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
