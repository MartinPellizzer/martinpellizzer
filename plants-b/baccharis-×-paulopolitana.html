Baccharis × paulopolitana

Baccharis × paulopolitana, commonly known as the Paulopolis shrub, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the fall, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Baccharis × paulopolitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
