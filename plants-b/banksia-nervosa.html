Banksia nervosa

Banksia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Banksia nervosa plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for a variety of pollinators.
