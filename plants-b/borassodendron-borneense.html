Borassodendron borneense

Borassodendron borneense, commonly known as the Bornean areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It features a single, unbranched trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a smooth, fibrous bark. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that form a dense crown, providing shade and habitat for various wildlife. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
