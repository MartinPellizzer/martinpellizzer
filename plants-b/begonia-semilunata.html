Begonia semilunata

Begonia semilunata, commonly known as the moonlight begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia semilunata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
