Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. ircutensis

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

ircutensis, commonly known as the Alaskan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as tundra and wet meadows.
