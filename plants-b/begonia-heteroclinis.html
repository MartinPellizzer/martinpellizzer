Begonia heteroclinis

Begonia heteroclinis, commonly known as the silver-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with silvery veins and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value, while its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a favored choice for indoor and garden settings.
