Bungea vesiculifera

Bungea vesiculifera, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Bungea vesiculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
