Bursera queretarensis

Bursera queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and leathery, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

Bursera queretarensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
