Blepharis cristata

Blepharis cristata, commonly known as the crested bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, blue-violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique, crested appearance is due to the arrangement of its leaves, which form a rosette-like structure.
