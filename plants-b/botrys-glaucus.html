Botrys glaucus

Botrys glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous cluster flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Botrys glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
