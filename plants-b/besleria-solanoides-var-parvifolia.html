Besleria solanoides var. parvifolia

Besleria solanoides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved besleria, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
