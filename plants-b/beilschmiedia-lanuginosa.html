Beilschmiedia lanuginosa

Beilschmiedia lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
