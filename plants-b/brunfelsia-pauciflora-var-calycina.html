Brunfelsia pauciflora var. calycina

Brunfelsia pauciflora var. calycina, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening at night. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the seeds. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
