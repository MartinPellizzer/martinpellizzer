Blainvillea latifolia

Blainvillea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf blainvillea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Blainvillea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
