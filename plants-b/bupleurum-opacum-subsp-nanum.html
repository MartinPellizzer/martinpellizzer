Bupleurum opacum subsp. nanum

Bupleurum opacum subsp.

nanum, commonly known as dwarf Chinese angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 cm, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white or pale yellow flowers appear in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
