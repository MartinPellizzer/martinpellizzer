Bulbophyllum stictanthum

Bulbophyllum stictanthum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
