Buda marina var. minor

Buda marina var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, adapted to thrive in salty, windy environments. It typically grows in shallow waters or on rocky shores, demonstrating its halophytic nature.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy, salt-tolerant qualities.
