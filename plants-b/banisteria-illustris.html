Banisteria illustris

Banisteria illustris, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria illustris is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
