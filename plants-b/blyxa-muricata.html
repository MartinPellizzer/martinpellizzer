Blyxa muricata

Blyxa muricata, commonly known as the spiny water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, sharp spines, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes form dense mats, helping to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.

Blyxa muricata is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
