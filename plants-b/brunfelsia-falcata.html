Brunfelsia falcata

Brunfelsia falcata, commonly known as the wax flower or Brazilian jasmine, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Brunfelsia falcata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
