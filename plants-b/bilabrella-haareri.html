Bilabrella haareri

Bilabrella haareri, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bilabrella haareri is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
