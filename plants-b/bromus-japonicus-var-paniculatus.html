Bromus japonicus var. paniculatus

Bromus japonicus var. paniculatus, commonly known as Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening and can become invasive in certain environments.
