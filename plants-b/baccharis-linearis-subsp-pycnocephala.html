Baccharis linearis subsp. pycnocephala

Baccharis linearis subsp.

pycnocephala, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
