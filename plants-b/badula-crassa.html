Badula crassa

Badula crassa, commonly known as the rough badula, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Badula crassa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its resilience to drought makes it an important component of its ecosystem.
