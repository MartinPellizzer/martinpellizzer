Berchemia arisanensis

Berchemia arisanensis, commonly known as the mountain yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Berchemia arisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
