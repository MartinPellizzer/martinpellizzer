Bistorta glastifolia

Bistorta glastifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tint. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

Bistorta glastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
