Begonia tovarensis var. palustris

Begonia tovarensis var. palustris, commonly known as the marsh begonia, is a species native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in peat bogs or swampy areas. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine, velvety hair, which helps retain moisture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
