Bellevalia leucophaea

Bellevalia leucophaea, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically white or pale blue in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellevalia leucophaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
