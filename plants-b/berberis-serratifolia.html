Berberis serratifolia

Berberis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
