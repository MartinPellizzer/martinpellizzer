Batidaea vulgaris

Batidaea vulgaris, commonly known as the common batidaea, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in rich, organic soils.

Batidaea vulgaris is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported tonic properties.
