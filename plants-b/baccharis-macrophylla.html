Baccharis macrophylla

Baccharis macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense panicles, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and disturbed soils, and is often found near water sources.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
