Baccharis glutinosa

Baccharis glutinosa, commonly known as sticky baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its sticky, resinous leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and grasslands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
