Bignonia tweedieana

Bignonia tweedieana, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Bignonia tweedieana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
