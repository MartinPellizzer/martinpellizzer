Begonia sinuata var. malaccensis

Begonia sinuata var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
