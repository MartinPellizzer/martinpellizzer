Brahea sarukhanii

Brahea sarukhanii, commonly known as the Santa Rosa palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, particularly in the Sonoran Desert. This palm is characterized by its slender trunk and feathery, bright green fronds that grow in a compact, rounded crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and conservation efforts.
