Betula cajanderi subsp. minutifolia

Betula cajanderi subsp.

minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf paper birch, is a small, deciduous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender trunk and papery, white bark that peels in thin layers.

The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
