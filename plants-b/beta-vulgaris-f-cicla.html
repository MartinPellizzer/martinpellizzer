Beta vulgaris f. cicla

Beta vulgaris f. cicla, commonly known as莙菜 or Chinese spinach, is a leafy vegetable characterized by its broad, tender leaves that are typically harvested before flowering.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C, iron, and calcium. The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, and it can be grown in both temperate and subtropical regions.

Its growth cycle is relatively short, making it a popular choice for seasonal farming and home gardens.
