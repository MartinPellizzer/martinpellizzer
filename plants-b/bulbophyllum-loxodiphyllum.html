Bulbophyllum loxodiphyllum

Bulbophyllum loxodiphyllum, commonly known as the "Lizard's Tail" orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish with a distinctive, spurred lip that resembles a lizard's tail, giving it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
