Bupleurum erubescens

Bupleurum erubescens, commonly known as red valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Bupleurum erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
