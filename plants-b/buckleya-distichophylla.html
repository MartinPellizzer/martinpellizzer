Buckleya distichophylla

Buckleya distichophylla, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with two opposite leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, bean-like pods that contain seeds. Buckleya distichophylla thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
