Bromus simplex var. commutatus

Bromus simplex var. commutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and soil stabilization capabilities.
