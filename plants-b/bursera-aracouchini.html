Bursera aracouchini

Bursera aracouchini, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.

Bursera aracouchini is often found in dry tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
