Brassaia eriocephala

Brassaia eriocephala, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Brassaia eriocephala is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salty conditions, which contributes to its adaptability in various environments.
