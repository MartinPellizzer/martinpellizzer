Bothriochloa haenkei

Bothriochloa haenkei, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive blue-gray sheen, and its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of spikelets. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Bothriochloa haenkei is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
