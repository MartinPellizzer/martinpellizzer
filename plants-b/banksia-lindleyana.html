Banksia lindleyana

Banksia lindleyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a striking Australian plant with distinctive, cone-like inflorescences.

It is native to the south-western region of Western Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal areas. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important species in the local flora.

Banksia lindleyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
