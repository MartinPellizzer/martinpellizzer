Blechnum ekmanii

Blechnum ekmanii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are released during the spring and summer months.

Blechnum ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
