Begonia foveolata

Begonia foveolata, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. Begonia foveolata thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
