Brickellia oblongifolia subsp. typica

Brickellia oblongifolia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to local biodiversity.
