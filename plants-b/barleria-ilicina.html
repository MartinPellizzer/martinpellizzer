Barleria ilicina

Barleria ilicina, commonly known as redflower barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
