Briza media f. pilosa

Briza media f. pilosa, commonly known as silky false oatgrass, is a perennial grass that features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
