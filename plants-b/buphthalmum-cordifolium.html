Buphthalmum cordifolium

Buphthalmum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.

Buphthalmum cordifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
