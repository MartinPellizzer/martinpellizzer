Boronia polygalifolia var. pubescens

Boronia polygalifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has hairy foliage, which is a key distinguishing feature of this variety. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Boronia polygalifolia var. pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
