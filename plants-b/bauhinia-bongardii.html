Bauhinia bongardii

Bauhinia bongardii, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Bauhinia bongardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
