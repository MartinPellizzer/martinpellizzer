Blumea longipes

Blumea longipes, commonly known as the longipes blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
