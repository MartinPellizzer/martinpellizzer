Barathranthus productus

Barathranthus productus, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the cooler months. It grows in a trailing or shrub-like form, often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
