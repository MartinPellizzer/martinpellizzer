Bonnetia ptariensis

Bonnetia ptariensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bonnetia ptariensis thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
