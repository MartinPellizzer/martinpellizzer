Bactris glaucescens

Bactris glaucescens, commonly known as the greenheart palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clustered, spiky fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its hard, dense wood is highly valued for construction and furniture due to its durability and resistance to decay.
