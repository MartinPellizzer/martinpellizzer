Borzicactus fossulatus

Borzicactus fossulatus, commonly known as the fissured barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its spines are modified leaves, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Borzicactus fossulatus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
