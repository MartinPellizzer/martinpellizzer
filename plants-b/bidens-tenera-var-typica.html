Bidens tenera var. typica

Bidens tenera var. typica, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features small, yellow flowers with ray florets and a distinctive, prickly seed structure. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
