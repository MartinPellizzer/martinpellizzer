Bulbophyllum longibrachiatum

Bulbophyllum longibrachiatum, commonly known as the long-armed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence and showy, fragrant flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
