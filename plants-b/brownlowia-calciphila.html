Brownlowia calciphila

Brownlowia calciphila, commonly known as the limestone hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, which are rich in calcium, making it well adapted to limestone environments. Its leaves are usually green and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Brownlowia calciphila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
