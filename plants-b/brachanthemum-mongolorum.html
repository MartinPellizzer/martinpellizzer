Brachanthemum mongolorum

Brachanthemum mongolorum, commonly known as the Mongolian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Brachanthemum mongolorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
