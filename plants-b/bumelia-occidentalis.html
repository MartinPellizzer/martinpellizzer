Bumelia occidentalis

Bumelia occidentalis, commonly known as western bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
