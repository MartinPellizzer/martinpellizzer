Bowdichia major var. fruticosa

Bowdichia major var. fruticosa, commonly known as the red cedar or Mexican cedar, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
