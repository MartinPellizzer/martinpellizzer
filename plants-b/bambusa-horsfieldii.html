Bambusa horsfieldii

Bambusa horsfieldii, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that often have a silvery or grayish sheen. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green color, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa horsfieldii is valued for its fast growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
