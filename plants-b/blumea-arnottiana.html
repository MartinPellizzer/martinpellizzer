Blumea arnottiana

Blumea arnottiana, commonly known as the golden heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Blumea arnottiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
