Brassica campestris subsp. annua

Brassica campestris subsp.

annua, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect stems, alternate leaves, and bright yellow flowers that form clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Field mustard is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
