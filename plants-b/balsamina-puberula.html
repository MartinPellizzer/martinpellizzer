Balsamina puberula

Balsamina puberula, commonly known as the velvet flower or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Balsamina puberula is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
