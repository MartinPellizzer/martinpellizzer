Brickellia rosmarinifolia subsp. chlorolepis

Brickellia rosmarinifolia subsp.

chlorolepis, commonly known as the green-leafed brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
