Brachiaria perrieri

Brachiaria perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its low-growing, tufted structure and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Brachiaria perrieri is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
