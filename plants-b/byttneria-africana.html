Byttneria africana

Byttneria africana, commonly known as African tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its shade-providing canopy.
