Brachyglottis repanda var. arborescens

Brachyglottis repanda var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a shrub-like plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and inland areas, often in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
