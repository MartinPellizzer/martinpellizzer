Bromus patulus var. luxurians

Bromus patulus var. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
