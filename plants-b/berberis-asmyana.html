Berberis asmyana

Berberis asmyana, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Pakistan, and Nepal.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. The plant features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that mature in late summer.

Berberis asmyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
