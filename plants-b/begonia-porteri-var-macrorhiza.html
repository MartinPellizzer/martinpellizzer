Begonia porteri var. macrorhiza

Begonia porteri var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

Native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America, it thrives in warm, moist environments with indirect light. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense clumps. Its foliage is often variegated, with shades of green, silver, and pink, adding visual interest to its overall appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture but requires careful attention to watering and humidity levels for optimal growth.
