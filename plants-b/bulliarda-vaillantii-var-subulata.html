Bulliarda vaillantii var. subulata

Bulliarda vaillantii var. subulata, commonly known as the slender bulliarda, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
