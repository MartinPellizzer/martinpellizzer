Begonia cucullifolia

Begonia cucullifolia, commonly known as the hooded begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like bracts that enclose the small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and distinctive appearance.
