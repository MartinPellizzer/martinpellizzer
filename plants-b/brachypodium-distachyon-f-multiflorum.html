Brachypodium distachyon f. multiflorum

Brachypodium distachyon f. multiflorum, commonly known as the multiflorous brachypodium, is a variant of the grass species Brachypodium distachyon.

It is characterized by its dense inflorescence, which consists of multiple spikelets arranged closely along the stem. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Poaceae family, including parallel venation in its leaves and a hollow, jointed stem.

Its genetic simplicity and rapid growth make it a valuable model organism for studying grass biology and plant genetics.
