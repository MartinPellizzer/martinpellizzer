Boottia obovata

Boottia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate boottia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Boottia obovata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
