Blackburnia monadelpha

Blackburnia monadelpha, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Blackburnia monadelpha is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
