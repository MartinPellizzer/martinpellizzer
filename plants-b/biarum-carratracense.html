Biarum carratracense

Biarum carratracense, commonly known as the Carratracense bellflower, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.
