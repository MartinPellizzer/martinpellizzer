Brachyscome diversifolia var. diversifolia

Brachyscome diversifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the "Blue Mist" or "Bluebell Bush," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy inflorescences that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
