Blechnum simillimum f. binerve

Blechnum simillimum f. binerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Blechnum simillimum species.

It is a fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae, characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-veined, which distinguishes it from the typical form of the species.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
