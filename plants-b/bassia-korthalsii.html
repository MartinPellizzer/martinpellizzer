Bassia korthalsii

Bassia korthalsii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to thrive in sandy soils, making it resilient to drought.

Overall, Bassia korthalsii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
