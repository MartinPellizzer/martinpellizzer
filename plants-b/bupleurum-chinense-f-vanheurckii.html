Bupleurum chinense f. vanheurckii

Bupleurum chinense f. vanheurckii, commonly known as the Vanheurck's Chinese skullcap, is a rare and cultivated variant of the Bupleurum genus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale blue. The plant possesses pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
