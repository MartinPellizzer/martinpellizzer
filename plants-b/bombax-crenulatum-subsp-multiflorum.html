Bombax crenulatum subsp. multiflorum

Bombax crenulatum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain fibrous, silky seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
