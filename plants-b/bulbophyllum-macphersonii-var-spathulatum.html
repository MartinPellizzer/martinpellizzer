Bulbophyllum macphersonii var. spathulatum

Bulbophyllum macphersonii var. spathulatum, commonly known as the Spatulate Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, spatulate-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
