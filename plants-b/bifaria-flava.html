Bifaria flava

Bifaria flava, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Bifaria flava thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides in its native habitat.
