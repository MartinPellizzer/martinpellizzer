Bursera delpechiana

Bursera delpechiana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, three-lobed fruits.

The tree is known for its resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
