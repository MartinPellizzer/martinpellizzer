Brassavola fragrans

Brassavola fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

Brassavola fragrans is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
