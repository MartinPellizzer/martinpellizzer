Blastania lucorum

Blastania lucorum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically used in herbal medicine and as a companion plant in gardens.
