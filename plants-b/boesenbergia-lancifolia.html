Boesenbergia lancifolia

Boesenbergia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. Its rhizomes are utilized in both culinary and medicinal applications due to their pungent flavor and potential health benefits.

Boesenbergia lancifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
