Banisteria metallicolor var. falcata

Banisteria metallicolor var. falcata, commonly known as the silverleaf banisteria, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, metallic sheen on its leaves, which can range from silver to pale green. The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape, often with a waxy texture. This plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
