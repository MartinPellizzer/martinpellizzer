Brassica fruticulosa var. leucantha

Brassica fruticulosa var. leucantha, commonly known as the white flowering wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Brassica fruticulosa var. leucantha is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
