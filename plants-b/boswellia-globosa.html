Boswellia globosa

Boswellia globosa, commonly known as frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, rounded crown of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces aromatic resin, known as frankincense, which is harvested from its branches and used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soils.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also valued for its cultural and economic significance.
