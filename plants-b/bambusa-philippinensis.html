Bambusa philippinensis

Bambusa philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, forming dense stands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Bambusa philippinensis is valued for its strong, durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
