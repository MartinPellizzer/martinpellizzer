Baccharis rivularis

Baccharis rivularis, commonly known as water baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Baccharis rivularis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
