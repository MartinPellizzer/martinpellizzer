Buchnera pinnatifida

Buchnera pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buchnera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Buchnera pinnatifida is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments.
