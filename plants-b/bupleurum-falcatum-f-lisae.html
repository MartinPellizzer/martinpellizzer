Bupleurum falcatum f. lisae

Bupleurum falcatum f. lisae, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the Bupleurum genus.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
