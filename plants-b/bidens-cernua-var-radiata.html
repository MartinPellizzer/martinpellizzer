Bidens cernua var. radiata

Bidens cernua var. radiata, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
