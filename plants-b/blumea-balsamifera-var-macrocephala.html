Blumea balsamifera var. macrocephala

Blumea balsamifera var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic leaves and produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical significance includes its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
