Brassaiopsis speciosa var. serrata

Brassaiopsis speciosa var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of purple to violet berries that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
