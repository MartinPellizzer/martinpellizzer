Betonica hylebium

Betonica hylebium, commonly known as the greater betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Betonica hylebium has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
