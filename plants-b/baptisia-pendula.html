Baptisia pendula

Baptisia pendula, commonly known as the hanging false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often droop, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with three to seven leaflets that are lance-shaped and hairy.

Baptisia pendula is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
