Banisteriopsis arborea

Banisteriopsis arborea, commonly known as the "bark apple" or "gumbo lima," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and the fruit is a round, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
