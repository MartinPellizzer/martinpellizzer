Beringeria rugosa

Beringeria rugosa, commonly known as the rugose beringia, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the Arctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its unique, rugged foliage and distinctive, waxy leaves that help it survive in harsh, cold climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. Beringeria rugosa is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
