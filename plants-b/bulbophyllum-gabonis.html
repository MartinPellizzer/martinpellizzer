Bulbophyllum gabonis

Bulbophyllum gabonis, commonly known as the Gabon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
