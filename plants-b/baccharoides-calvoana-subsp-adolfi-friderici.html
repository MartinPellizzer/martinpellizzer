Baccharoides calvoana subsp. adolfi-friderici

Baccharoides calvoana subsp.

adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolpho-Friedrich baccharoides, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of the understory in humid tropical environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
