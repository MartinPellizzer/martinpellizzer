Burlingtonia caloplectron

Burlingtonia caloplectron, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
