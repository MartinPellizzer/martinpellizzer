Blastus apricus

Blastus apricus, commonly known as the apricot blastus, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, apricot-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Its fruits, though not commonly consumed by humans, are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Blastus apricus is considered a rare and endangered species.
