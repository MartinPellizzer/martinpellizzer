Blaeria glabra

Blaeria glabra, commonly known as the smooth blaeria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a drought-resistant plant.
