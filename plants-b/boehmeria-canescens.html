Boehmeria canescens

Boehmeria canescens, commonly known as Chinese hemp or white flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer. Its fibers are valued for their strength and are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.

Boehmeria canescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
