Bulbophyllum reticulatum

Bulbophyllum reticulatum, commonly known as the netted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth during dry periods. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
