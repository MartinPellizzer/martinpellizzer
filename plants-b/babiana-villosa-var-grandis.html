Babiana villosa var. grandis

Babiana villosa var. grandis, commonly known as the giant adder's tongue, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

This perennial herb features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of orange and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
