Borbonia angustifolia

Borbonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf borbonia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
