Buchnera tenuifolia

Buchnera tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf buchnera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Buchnera tenuifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in restoration projects.
