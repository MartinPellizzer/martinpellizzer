Bilderdykia ciliinodis var. laevigata

Bilderdykia ciliinodis var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ciliate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, glabrous (smooth) leaves that are often covered in fine, ciliate (fringed) margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a globular inflorescence, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
