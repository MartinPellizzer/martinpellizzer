Bactris maguirei

Bactris maguirei, commonly known as the Guajillo palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and Hispaniola. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
