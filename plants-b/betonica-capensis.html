Betonica capensis

Betonica capensis, commonly known as the Cape betony, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Betonica capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
