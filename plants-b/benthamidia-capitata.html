Benthamidia capitata

Benthamidia capitata, commonly known as the Cape mountain cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
