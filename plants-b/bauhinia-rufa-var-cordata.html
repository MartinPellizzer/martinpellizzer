Bauhinia rufa var. cordata

Bauhinia rufa var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and moderate care requirements, it is often cultivated in warm climates for both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
