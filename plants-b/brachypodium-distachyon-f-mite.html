Brachypodium distachyon f. mite

Brachypodium distachyon f. mite, commonly known as the mite form of brachypodium, is a variant of the grass species Brachypodium distachyon.

This form is characterized by the presence of mites, which are microscopic arachnids that often inhabit the plant's tissues. The botanical features of this form include its compact growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology compared to the standard variety. It is typically found in moist, temperate environments where humidity supports mite infestation.

The presence of mites can influence the plant's physiology and may affect its ecological interactions and agricultural use.
