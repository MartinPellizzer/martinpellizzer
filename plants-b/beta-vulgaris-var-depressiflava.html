Beta vulgaris var. depressiflava

Beta vulgaris var. depressiflava, commonly known as the beetroot or chard, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, lobed leaves. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its edible roots and leaves. Its leaves are often used in cooking, while the roots are valued for their high nutrient content.

The variety 'depressiflava' is distinguished by its deep red or purple coloration in the roots and leaves.
