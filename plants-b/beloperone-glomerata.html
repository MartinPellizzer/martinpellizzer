Beloperone glomerata

Beloperone glomerata, commonly known as the clustered beloperone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Beloperone glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
