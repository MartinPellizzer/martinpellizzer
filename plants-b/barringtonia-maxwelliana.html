Barringtonia maxwelliana

Barringtonia maxwelliana, commonly known as the Maxwell's candle tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-like inflorescences that hang from the branches, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by nocturnal insects and bats. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
