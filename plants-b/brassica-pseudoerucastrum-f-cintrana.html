Brassica pseudoerucastrum f. cintrana

Brassica pseudoerucastrum f. cintrana, commonly known as the curled cabbage, is a cultivar of the Brassica genus characterized by its tightly curled leaves.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is typically biennial, producing rosettes of fleshy, green leaves in its first year. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in certain regions of Europe.
