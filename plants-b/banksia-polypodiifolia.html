Banksia polypodiifolia

Banksia polypodiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that produce abundant yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

Banksia polypodiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
