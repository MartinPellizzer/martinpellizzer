Bromus sterilis var. fasciculatus

Bromus sterilis var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is characterized by its clustered growth habit.

This variety is often found in disturbed or degraded environments, where it can form dense stands.
