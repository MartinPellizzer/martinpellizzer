Boehmeria laevis

Boehmeria laevis, commonly known as silky ginseng, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle species, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Boehmeria laevis is also valued for its strong, durable fibers, which have been used in textile production.
