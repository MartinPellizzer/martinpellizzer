Begonia saxicola

Begonia saxicola, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Southeast Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often with intricate patterns. It is well adapted to grow in crevices and on rocky surfaces, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
