Berberis viridiflora

Berberis viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
