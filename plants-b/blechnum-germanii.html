Blechnum germanii

Blechnum germanii, commonly known as the German maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
