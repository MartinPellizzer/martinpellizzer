Brandtia holcoides

Brandtia holcoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, puffball-like seed heads that release wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
