Buphthalmum leucoifolium

Buphthalmum leucoifolium, commonly known as the white-leafed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant has distinctive white, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Buphthalmum leucoifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
