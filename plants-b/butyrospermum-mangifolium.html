Butyrospermum mangifolium

Butyrospermum mangifolium, commonly known as shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It is valued for its large, edible fruits that contain a high amount of oils in its seeds. The botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a deep root system.

Its fruit is crucial for both local communities and the production of shea butter.
