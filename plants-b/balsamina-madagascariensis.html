Balsamina madagascariensis

Balsamina madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar balsam, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with fleshy, alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
