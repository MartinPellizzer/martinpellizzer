Berberis boissieri

Berberis boissieri, commonly known as the Algerian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
