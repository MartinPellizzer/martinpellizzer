Brachyloma pilosum

Brachyloma pilosum, commonly known as the hairy brachyloma, is a small, perennial herb native to the southeastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant features wiry, upright stems covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in spring.

Brachyloma pilosum is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species.
