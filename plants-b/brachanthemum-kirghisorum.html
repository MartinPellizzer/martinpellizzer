Brachanthemum kirghisorum

Brachanthemum kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz daisy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Brachanthemum kirghisorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
