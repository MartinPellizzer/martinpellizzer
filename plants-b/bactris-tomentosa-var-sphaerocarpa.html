Bactris tomentosa var. sphaerocarpa

Bactris tomentosa var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red raphia, is a palm species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences and distinctive reddish-brown fruiting structures. The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or processed into beverages. Its leaves are large and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
