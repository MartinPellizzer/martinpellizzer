Baptisia × stipulacea

Baptisia × stipulacea, commonly known as false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seedpods that split open to release its seeds.

Baptisia × stipulacea is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
