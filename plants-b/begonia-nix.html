Begonia nix

Begonia nix, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorch.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
