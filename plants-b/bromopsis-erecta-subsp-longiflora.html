Bromopsis erecta subsp. longiflora

Bromopsis erecta subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with elongated, pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
