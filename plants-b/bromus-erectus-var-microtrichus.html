Bromus erectus var. microtrichus

Bromus erectus var. microtrichus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, linear leaves.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are covered with short, fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is often found in regions with moderate rainfall and well-drained soils.
