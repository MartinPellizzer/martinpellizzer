Brachiaria remota

Brachiaria remota, commonly known as the slender blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Brachiaria remota thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
