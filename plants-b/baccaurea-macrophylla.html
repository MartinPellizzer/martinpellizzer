Baccaurea macrophylla

Baccaurea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant hogplum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
