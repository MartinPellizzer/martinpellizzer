Belairia angustifolia

Belairia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf belairia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the spring and summer seasons. Belairia angustifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora.
