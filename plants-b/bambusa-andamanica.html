Bambusa andamanica

Bambusa andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to the Andaman Islands.

It features slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa andamanica is often used in landscaping and for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.
