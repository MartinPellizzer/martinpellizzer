Brittonrosea multicostata

Brittonrosea multicostata, commonly known as the multibract rosebay, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
