Bidens bidentoides var. mariana

Bidens bidentoides var. mariana, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two distinct lobes, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.
