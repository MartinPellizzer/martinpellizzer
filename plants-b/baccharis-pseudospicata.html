Baccharis pseudospicata

Baccharis pseudospicata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and arid regions.

Baccharis pseudospicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
