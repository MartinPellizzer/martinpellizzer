Beta vulgaris var. phoenicopleura

Beta vulgaris var. phoenicopleura, commonly known as the red beet or chard, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant also produces edible leaves that are often used in cooking. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its nutritional content and culinary versatility.
