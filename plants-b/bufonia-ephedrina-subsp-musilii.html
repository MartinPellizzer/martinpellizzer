Bufonia ephedrina subsp. musilii

Bufonia ephedrina subsp.

musilii, commonly known as the Mexican ephedra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ephedraceae. It is characterized by its branched stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine.
