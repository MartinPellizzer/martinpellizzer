Begonia brevirimosa

Begonia brevirimosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
