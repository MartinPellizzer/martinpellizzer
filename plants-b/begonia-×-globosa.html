Begonia × globosa

Begonia × globosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
