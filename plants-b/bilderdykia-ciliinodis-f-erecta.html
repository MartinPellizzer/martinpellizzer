Bilderdykia ciliinodis f. erecta

Bilderdykia ciliinodis f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the silky fringed bellflower, is a variant of a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, distinguishing it from the more sprawling form of the species. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
