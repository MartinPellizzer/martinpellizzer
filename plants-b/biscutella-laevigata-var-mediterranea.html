Biscutella laevigata var. mediterranea

Biscutella laevigata var. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean biscuitweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly areas, making it a characteristic component of Mediterranean flora.
