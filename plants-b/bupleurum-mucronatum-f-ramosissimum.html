Bupleurum mucronatum f. ramosissimum

Bupleurum mucronatum f. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and produces erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in umbel-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
