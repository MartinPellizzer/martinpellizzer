Bombax sclerophyllum

Bombax sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
