Beschorneria roseana

Beschorneria roseana, commonly known as the Mexican torch plant, is a striking evergreen perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of small, bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
