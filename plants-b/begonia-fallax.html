Begonia fallax

Begonia fallax, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Begonia fallax is a popular choice among gardeners.
