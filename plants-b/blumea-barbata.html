Blumea barbata

Blumea barbata, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
