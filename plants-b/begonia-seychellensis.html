Begonia seychellensis

Begonia seychellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in coastal forest areas. Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
