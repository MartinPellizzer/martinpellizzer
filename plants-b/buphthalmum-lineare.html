Buphthalmum lineare

Buphthalmum lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Buphthalmum lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
