Bromopsis korotkiji

Bromopsis korotkiji, commonly known as the Korotkij's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in short, silky hairs.

Bromopsis korotkiji is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
