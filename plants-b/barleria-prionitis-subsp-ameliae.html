Barleria prionitis subsp. ameliae

Barleria prionitis subsp.

ameliae, commonly known as Amelia's barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
