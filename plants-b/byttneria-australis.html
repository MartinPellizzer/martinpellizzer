Byttneria australis

Byttneria australis, commonly known as the Australian silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are soft and hairy beneath. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
