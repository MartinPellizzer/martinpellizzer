Betonica sabauda

Betonica sabauda, commonly known as the sabauda betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Betonica sabauda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
