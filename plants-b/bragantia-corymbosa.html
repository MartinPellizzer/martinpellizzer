Bragantia corymbosa

Bragantia corymbosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
