Boninofatsia

Boninofatsia, commonly known as the Bonin fat tree, is a unique plant native to the Bonin Islands in Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often swollen stems that resemble fat deposits, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, helping it retain moisture in its arid environment.

Boninofatsia is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
