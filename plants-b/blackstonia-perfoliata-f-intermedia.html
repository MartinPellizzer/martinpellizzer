Blackstonia perfoliata f. intermedia

Blackstonia perfoliata f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate blackstonia, is a variant of the blackstonia plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture and a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
