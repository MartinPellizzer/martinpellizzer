Begonia lossiae

Begonia lossiae, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
