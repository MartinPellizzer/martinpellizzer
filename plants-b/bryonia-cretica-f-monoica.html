Bryonia cretica f. monoica

Bryonia cretica f. monoica, commonly known as the Cretan bryony, is a variant of the genus Bryonia native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
