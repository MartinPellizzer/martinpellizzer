Babiana plicata var. maculata

Babiana plicata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted babiana, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal gardens due to its drought tolerance.
