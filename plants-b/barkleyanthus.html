Barkleyanthus

Barkleyanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a short, woody stem with smooth, pale bark that gives it a spectral appearance. Barkleyanthus blooms only once in its lifetime, producing a single, delicate flower that lasts for a few days.

Due to its extreme rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered one of the most elusive plants in the botanical world.
