Boswellia carteri var. subintegra

Boswellia carteri var. subintegra, commonly known as African frankincense, is a small, deciduous tree native to the dry regions of East Africa.

It produces a resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic and medicinal properties. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. Its botanical characteristics include smooth, greyish bark and small, leathery leaves.

The resin, known as frankincense, is used in traditional medicine and religious ceremonies.
