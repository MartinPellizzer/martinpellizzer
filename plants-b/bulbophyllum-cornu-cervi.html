Bulbophyllum cornu-cervi

Bulbophyllum cornu-cervi, commonly known as the horned deer orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a thick, fleshy rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces striking flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red veins, resembling the antlers of a deer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Bulbophyllum cornu-cervi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
