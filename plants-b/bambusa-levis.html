Bambusa levis

Bambusa levis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered with a silvery-white sheath, giving it its common name. The plant grows in clusters and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green color, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa levis is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is frequently used in landscaping and bamboo crafts.
