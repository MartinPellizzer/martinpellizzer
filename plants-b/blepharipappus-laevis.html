Blepharipappus laevis

Blepharipappus laevis, commonly known as the silky pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. Its most notable botanical feature is the silky, hair-like pappus that surrounds its seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
