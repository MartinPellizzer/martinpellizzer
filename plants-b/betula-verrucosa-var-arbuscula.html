Betula verrucosa var. arbuscula

Betula verrucosa var. arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small deciduous shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in taiga and tundra ecosystems.

Betula verrucosa var. arbuscula is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in cold climates.
