Bouteloua barbata var. rothrockii

Bouteloua barbata var. rothrockii, commonly known as Rothrock's threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and spikelets that are often adorned with bristly hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in late spring.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soils in native plant communities.
