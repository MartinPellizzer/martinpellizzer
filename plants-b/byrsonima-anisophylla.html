Byrsonima anisophylla

Byrsonima anisophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
