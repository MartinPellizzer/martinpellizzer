Buddleja hypoleuca

Buddleja hypoleuca, commonly known as the white flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The flowers are tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
