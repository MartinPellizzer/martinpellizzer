Buchnera usafuensis

Buchnera usafuensis, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf bamboo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. Buchnera usafuensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional Japanese gardens for its aesthetic and cultural significance.
