Bupleurum divaricatum f. elegans

Bupleurum divaricatum f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant variegated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
