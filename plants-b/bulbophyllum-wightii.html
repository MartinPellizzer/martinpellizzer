Bulbophyllum wightii

Bulbophyllum wightii, commonly known as the Wight's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients, and produces multiple lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in various colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Bulbophyllum wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
