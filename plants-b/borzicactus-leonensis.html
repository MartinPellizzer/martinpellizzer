Borzicactus leonensis

Borzicactus leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
