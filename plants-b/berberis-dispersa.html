Berberis dispersa

Berberis dispersa, commonly known as the scattered barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Berberis dispersa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
