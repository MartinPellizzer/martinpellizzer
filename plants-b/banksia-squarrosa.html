Banksia squarrosa

Banksia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white or grey hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Banksia squarrosa is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for a variety of pollinators.
