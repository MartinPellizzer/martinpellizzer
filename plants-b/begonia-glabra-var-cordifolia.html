Begonia glabra var. cordifolia

Begonia glabra var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
