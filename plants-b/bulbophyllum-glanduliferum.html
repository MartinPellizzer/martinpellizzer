Bulbophyllum glanduliferum

Bulbophyllum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered with a dense layer of fine, glandular hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
