Begonia rubricaulis

Begonia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
