Bactris glaucescens var. melanacantha

Bactris glaucescens var. melanacantha, commonly known as the black thorn palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family native to South America.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its dark, almost black, spiny leaves and a slender, unbranched trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
