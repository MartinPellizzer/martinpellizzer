Bredemeyera altissima var. mollis

Bredemeyera altissima var. mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
