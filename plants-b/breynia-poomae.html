Breynia poomae

Breynia poomae, commonly known as the "blue ghost plant," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue leaves that shimmer under sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Breynia poomae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
