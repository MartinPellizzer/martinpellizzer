Bassovia anonacea

Bassovia anonacea, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that resembles a soursop, containing a white, fleshy pulp with a pleasant aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
