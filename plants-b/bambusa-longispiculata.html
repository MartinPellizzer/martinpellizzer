Bambusa longispiculata

Bambusa longispiculata, commonly known as the long-bristle bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
