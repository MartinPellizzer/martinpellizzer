Brachiaria ambigua

Brachiaria ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Brachiaria ambigua is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
