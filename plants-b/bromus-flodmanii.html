Bromus flodmanii

Bromus flodmanii, commonly known as Flodman's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Bromus flodmanii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
