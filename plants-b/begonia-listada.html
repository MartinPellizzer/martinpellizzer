Begonia listada

Begonia listada, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature a mix of green, white, and red hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia listada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
