Biscutella pseudolyrata

Biscutella pseudolyrata, commonly known as the false lyreleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often lyre-shaped, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Biscutella pseudolyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
