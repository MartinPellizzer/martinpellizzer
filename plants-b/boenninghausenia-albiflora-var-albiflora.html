Boenninghausenia albiflora var. albiflora

Boenninghausenia albiflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered boenninghausenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
