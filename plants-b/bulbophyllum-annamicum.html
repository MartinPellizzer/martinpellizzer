Bulbophyllum annamicum

Bulbophyllum annamicum, commonly known as the Annamite orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a few lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or orange.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
