Bignonia filiformis

Bignonia filiformis, commonly known as the slender trumpetvine, is a climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
