Batatas loureiroi

Batatas loureiroi, commonly known as the São Tomé batata, is a tuberous plant native to the island of São Tomé in the Gulf of Guinea.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, starchy roots that are often used as a food source. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually white or purple and are borne on slender stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
