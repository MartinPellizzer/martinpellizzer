Berberis sikkimensis var. glabramea

Berberis sikkimensis var. glabramea, commonly known as the Sikkim barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving it its specific variety name.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
