Bupleurum falcatum var. ramosissimum

Bupleurum falcatum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
