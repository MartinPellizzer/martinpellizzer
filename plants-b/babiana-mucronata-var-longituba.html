Babiana mucronata var. longituba

Babiana mucronata var. longituba, commonly known as the long-tubed babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange throat. The plant produces a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in dry, rocky environments. Its bulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
