Buxus arborescens

Buxus arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a compact, rounded growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically 1 to 2 centimeters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

Buxus arborescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to be shaped into various forms.
