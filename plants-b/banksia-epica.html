Banksia epica

Banksia epica, commonly known as the giant banksia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that can reach up to 2 meters in height, which are composed of numerous yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia epica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
