Betula microlepis

Betula microlepis, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf birch, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
