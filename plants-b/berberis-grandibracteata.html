Berberis grandibracteata

Berberis grandibracteata, commonly known as the giant barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it a popular choice in horticulture.
