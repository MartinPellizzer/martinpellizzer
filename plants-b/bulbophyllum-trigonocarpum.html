Bulbophyllum trigonocarpum

Bulbophyllum trigonocarpum, commonly known as the triangular fruit orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique triangular-shaped seed pods, which are a distinguishing feature of the plant. The orchid typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

The plant thrives in humid environments with high levels of light and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
