Berlandiera basalaris

Berlandiera basalaris, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Berlandiera basalaris is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
