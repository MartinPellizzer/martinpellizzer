Byttneria wingfieldii

Byttneria wingfieldii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
