Begonia fritz-muelleri

Begonia fritz-muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners who grow it as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
