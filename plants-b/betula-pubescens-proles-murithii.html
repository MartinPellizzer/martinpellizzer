Betula pubescens proles murithii

Betula pubescens proles murithii, commonly known as the Murithii birch, is a subspecies native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its white to yellowish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are important for pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The Murithii birch is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
