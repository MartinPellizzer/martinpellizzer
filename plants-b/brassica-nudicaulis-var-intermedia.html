Brassica nudicaulis var. intermedia

Brassica nudicaulis var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are lobed or divided. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
