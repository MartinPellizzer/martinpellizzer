Bidens foliosa

Bidens foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
