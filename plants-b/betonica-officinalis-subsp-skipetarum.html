Betonica officinalis subsp. skipetarum

Betonica officinalis subsp.

skipetarum, commonly known as the Balkan betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces blue to purple flowers in spikes during late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Boraginaceae family, sharing similar morphological traits.
