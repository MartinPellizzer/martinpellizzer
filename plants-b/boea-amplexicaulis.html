Boea amplexicaulis

Boea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging ivy or ivy vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that wrap around stems and branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
