Bambusa tenuis

Bambusa tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the nodes. It is a fast-growing plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Bambusa tenuis is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
