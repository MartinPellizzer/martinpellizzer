Bulbophyllum pipio

Bulbophyllum pipio, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture.

The species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
