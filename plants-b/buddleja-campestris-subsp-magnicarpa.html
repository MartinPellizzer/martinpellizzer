Buddleja campestris subsp. magnicarpa

Buddleja campestris subsp.

magnicarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its greyish-green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to lilac flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces large, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
