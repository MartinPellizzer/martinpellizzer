Bigelowia albida

Bigelowia albida, commonly known as the white bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bigelowia albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
