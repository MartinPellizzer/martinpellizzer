Brindonia oxycarpa

Brindonia oxycarpa, commonly known as the sour fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits that are sour in taste.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
