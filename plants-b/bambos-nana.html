Bambos nana

Bambos nana, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, hollow stems. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Bambos nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
