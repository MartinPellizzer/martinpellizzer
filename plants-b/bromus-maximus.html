Bromus maximus

Bromus maximus, commonly known as the great brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Bromus maximus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
