Berlinia acuminata var. congolensis

Berlinia acuminata var. congolensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's hard, heavy wood is highly valued for its durability and strength, making it useful for construction and tool-making. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant also plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
