Begonia tenella

Begonia tenella, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive silvery or white underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a prized addition to gardens and collections.
