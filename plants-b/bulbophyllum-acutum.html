Bulbophyllum acutum

Bulbophyllum acutum, commonly known as the sharp-angled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and its long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white or grayish pubescence. The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
