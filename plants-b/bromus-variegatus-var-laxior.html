Bromus variegatus var. laxior

Bromus variegatus var. laxior, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, wiry stems and variable leaf morphology.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and dunes, often in temperate climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish glumes, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is a perennial species that can form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in certain environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in regions with moderate precipitation and well-drained soils.
