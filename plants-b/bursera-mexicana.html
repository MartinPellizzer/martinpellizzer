Bursera mexicana

Bursera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican frankincense or wild teak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
