Buddleja misera

Buddleja misera, commonly known as the lesser butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Buddleja misera is relatively low maintenance, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
