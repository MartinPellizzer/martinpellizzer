Berberis vulgaris var. ilicifolia

Berberis vulgaris var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits in the fall, which are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to the presence of berberine, a compound with antimicrobial properties.
