Bryonia laciniosa

Bryonia laciniosa, commonly known as the Indian figleaf gourd, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
