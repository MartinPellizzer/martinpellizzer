Bulbostylis geniculatus

Bulbostylis geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and water filtration.
