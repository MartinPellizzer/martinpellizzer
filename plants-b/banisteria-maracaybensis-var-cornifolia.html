Banisteria maracaybensis var. cornifolia

Banisteria maracaybensis var. cornifolia, commonly known as the horned banisteria, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like appendages on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that are often fragrant. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
