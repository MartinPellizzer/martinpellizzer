Brachybotrys

Brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Viscaraceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of coniferous trees, such as pines, firs, and spruces. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants. Brachybotrys lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system.

Its life cycle involves both a seed stage and a vegetative stage, with the latter often forming dense clusters on the host tree.
