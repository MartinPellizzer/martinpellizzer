Bulbostylis hispidula subsp. filiformis

Bulbostylis hispidula subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender bulbostylis, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its small, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often overlooked but plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
