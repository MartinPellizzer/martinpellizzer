Begonia alpina

Begonia alpina, commonly known as the alpine begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
