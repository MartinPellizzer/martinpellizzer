Biscutella mediterranea var. picroides

Biscutella mediterranea var. picroides, commonly known as the Mediterranean biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry grasslands.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
