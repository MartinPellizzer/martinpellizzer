Begonia subelliptica

Begonia subelliptica, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
