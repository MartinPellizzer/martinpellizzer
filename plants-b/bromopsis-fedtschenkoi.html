Bromopsis fedtschenkoi

Bromopsis fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Bromopsis fedtschenkoi is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
