Bromelia fosteriana

Bromelia fosteriana, commonly known as the Foster's bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers, often in red, orange, or yellow hues. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Bromelia fosteriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.
