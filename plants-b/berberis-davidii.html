Berberis davidii

Berberis davidii, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Berberis davidii is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile plant in horticulture.
