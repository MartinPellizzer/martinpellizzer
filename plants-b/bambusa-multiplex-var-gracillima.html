Bambusa multiplex var. gracillima

Bambusa multiplex var. gracillima, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to China.

It features slender, gracefully arching culms that are often covered in a silvery-white sheath, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow panicles of flowers, which are typically visible in the spring. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant look.

This bamboo is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
