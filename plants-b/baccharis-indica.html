Baccharis indica

Baccharis indica, commonly known as the Indian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Baccharis indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
