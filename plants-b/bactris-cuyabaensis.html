Bactris cuyabaensis

Bactris cuyabaensis, commonly known as the Cuyaba palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small, elongated berries. Bactris cuyabaensis is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for its fruit in traditional practices.
