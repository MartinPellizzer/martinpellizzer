Bromus variegatus var. leiantha

Bromus variegatus var. leiantha, commonly known as the variegated brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, which often display shades of green and white. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
