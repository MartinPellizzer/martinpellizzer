Bambusa longiflora

Bambusa longiflora, commonly known as the longiflorous bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Bambusa longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional construction and crafts.
