Bignonia cararensis

Bignonia cararensis, commonly known as the Carare trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking orange to red tubular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Bignonia cararensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
