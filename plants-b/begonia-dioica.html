Begonia dioica

Begonia dioica, commonly known as the arrowleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Begonia dioica thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
