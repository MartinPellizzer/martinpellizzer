Berberis trichohaematoides

Berberis trichohaematoides, commonly known as the red-berried barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
