Barleria cristata var. lactea

Barleria cristata var. lactea, commonly known as the milk barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
