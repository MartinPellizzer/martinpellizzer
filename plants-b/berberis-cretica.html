Berberis cretica

Berberis cretica, commonly known as creeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or low-growing thickets. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Berberis cretica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
