Bipinnula gibertii

Bipinnula gibertii, commonly known as the giant red fern, is a unique fern species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant features a bipinnate structure, with multiple layers of pinnae that give it a feathery appearance. Its leaves are typically bright red or orange when young, gradually turning green as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking visual appeal.
