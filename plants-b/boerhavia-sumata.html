Boerhavia sumata

Boerhavia sumata, commonly known as redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a fibrous root system and succulent, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are often arranged in dense inflorescences.

Boerhavia sumata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
