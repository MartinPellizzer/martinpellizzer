Blechnum occidentale

Blechnum occidentale, commonly known as the Western maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the western regions of the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Blechnum occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
