Bletia coccinea

Bletia coccinea, commonly known as the red lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red and orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and pine flatwoods. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bletia coccinea is a perennial herb that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
