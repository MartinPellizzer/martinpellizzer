Buckleya joan var. tanigawaensis

Buckleya joan var. tanigawaensis, commonly known as the Japanese buckleya, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically white to pale pink, with showy, fluffy petals that attract pollinators. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental display and relatively low maintenance requirements.
