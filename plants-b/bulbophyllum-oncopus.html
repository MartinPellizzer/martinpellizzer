Bulbophyllum oncopus

Bulbophyllum oncopus, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The plant's unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
