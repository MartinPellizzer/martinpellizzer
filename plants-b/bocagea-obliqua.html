Bocagea obliqua

Bocagea obliqua, commonly known as the creeping bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that features small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

Bocagea obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
