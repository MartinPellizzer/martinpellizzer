Bellis magellanica

Bellis magellanica, commonly known as the Antarctic buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is often found in alpine and subantarctic environments. The plant has a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in poor soil conditions.

Bellis magellanica is an important species in its ecosystem, providing food for local wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
