Begonia trichochila

Begonia trichochila, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia trichochila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
