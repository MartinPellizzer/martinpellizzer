Bellardiochloa violacea subsp. aetnensis

Bellardiochloa violacea subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Etna violetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and features dense, violet-colored inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
