Barkhausia roylei

Barkhausia roylei, commonly known as the Kashmiri bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in forested areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
