Banksia aurantia

Banksia aurantia, commonly known as the golden banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright yellow or golden in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. Banksia aurantia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

It is an important component of the local flora and plays a role in supporting native wildlife.
