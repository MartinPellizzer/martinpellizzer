Bignonia laserpitiifolia

Bignonia laserpitiifolia, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia laserpitiifolia is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.
