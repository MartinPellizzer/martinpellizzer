Barkhausia amplexifolia

Barkhausia amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, clasping leaves that wrap around the stem. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
