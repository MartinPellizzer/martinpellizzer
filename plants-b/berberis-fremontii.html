Berberis fremontii

Berberis fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.
