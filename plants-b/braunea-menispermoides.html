Braunea menispermoides

Braunea menispermoides, commonly known as the yellow braunea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in panicles and bloom during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Braunea menispermoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
