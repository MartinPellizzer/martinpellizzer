Bignonia jasminoides alba

Bignonia jasminoides alba, commonly known as the white trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
