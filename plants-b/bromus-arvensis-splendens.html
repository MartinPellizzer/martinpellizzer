Bromus arvensis splendens

Bromus arvensis splendens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silver-blue blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
