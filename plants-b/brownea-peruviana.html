Brownea peruviana

Brownea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces woody, branching stems and has large, pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Brownea peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
