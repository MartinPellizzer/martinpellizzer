Baccharis trinervis var. debilis

Baccharis trinervis var. debilis, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.
