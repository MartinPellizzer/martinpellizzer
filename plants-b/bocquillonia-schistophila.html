Bocquillonia schistophila

Bocquillonia schistophila, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in crevices and access moisture from deep within the soil.

Bocquillonia schistophila is often found in rocky outcrops and is a common sight in the Succulent Karoo biome.
