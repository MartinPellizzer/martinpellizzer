Bauhinia mucora

Bauhinia mucora, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Bauhinia mucora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
