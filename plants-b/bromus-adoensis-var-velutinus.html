Bromus adoensis var. velutinus

Bromus adoensis var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Bromus adoensis var. velutinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
