Bellevalia zoharyi var. pyricarpa

Bellevalia zoharyi var. pyricarpa, commonly known as the fireberry bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique color and drought tolerance.
