Bulbophyllum solheidii

Bulbophyllum solheidii, commonly known as the Solheid's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, with distinctive spurred petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with limited rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
