Bupleurum hamiltonii var. peucefulcrans

Bupleurum hamiltonii var. peucefulcrans, commonly known as the Hamilton's bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
