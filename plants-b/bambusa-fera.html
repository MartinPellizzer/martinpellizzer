Bambusa fera

Bambusa fera, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure with a smooth, greenish-brown culm surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sustainable use in construction and crafts.

Bambusa fera is also valued for its ability to regenerate quickly, contributing to its ecological and economic importance.
