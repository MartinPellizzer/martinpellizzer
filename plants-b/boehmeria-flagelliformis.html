Boehmeria flagelliformis

Boehmeria flagelliformis, commonly known as Chinese hair grass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, hair-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its fibrous stems are used in traditional papermaking and textile production.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
