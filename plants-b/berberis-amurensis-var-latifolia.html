Berberis amurensis var. latifolia

Berberis amurensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Amur barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, which are dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest, making it popular in gardens and landscapes.
