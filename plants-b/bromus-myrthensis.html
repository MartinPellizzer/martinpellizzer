Bromus myrthensis

Bromus myrthensis, commonly known as the Myrthens grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike or panicle, bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus myrthensis is known for its ecological adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
