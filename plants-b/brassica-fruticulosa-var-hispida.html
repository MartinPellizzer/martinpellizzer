Brassica fruticulosa var. hispida

Brassica fruticulosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid mustard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, bushy growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its capsule fruits contain numerous small, rounded seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
