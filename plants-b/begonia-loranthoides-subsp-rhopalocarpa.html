Begonia loranthoides subsp. rhopalocarpa

Begonia loranthoides subsp.

rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. It is a perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant.
