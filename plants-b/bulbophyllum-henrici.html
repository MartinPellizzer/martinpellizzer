Bulbophyllum henrici

Bulbophyllum henrici, commonly known as the Henry's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Bulbophyllum henrici is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
