Bulbophyllum lobbii subsp. lobbii

Bulbophyllum lobbii subsp.

lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
