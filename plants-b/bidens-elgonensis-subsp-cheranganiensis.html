Bidens elgonensis subsp. cheranganiensis

Bidens elgonensis subsp.

cheranganiensis, commonly known as the Cherangani ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Kenya and Uganda. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, with both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
