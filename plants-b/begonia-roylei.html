Begonia roylei

Begonia roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Begonia roylei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
