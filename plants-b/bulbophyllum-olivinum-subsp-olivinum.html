Bulbophyllum olivinum subsp. olivinum

Bulbophyllum olivinum subsp.

olivinum, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.
