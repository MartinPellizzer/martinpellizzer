Bulbostylis argenteobrunea

Bulbostylis argenteobrunea, commonly known as silver-brown bulbostylis, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces tiny, pale brown flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes.

Its silver-gray appearance is due to the presence of fine, waxy hairs on its foliage.
