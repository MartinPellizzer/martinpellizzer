Basella excavata

Basella excavata, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Basellaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often eaten as a leafy vegetable. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value.

Basella excavata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
