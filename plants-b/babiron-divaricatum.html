Babiron divaricatum

Babiron divaricatum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a striking purple underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

The plant is known for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the year under proper care.
