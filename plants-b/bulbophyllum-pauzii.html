Bulbophyllum pauzii

Bulbophyllum pauzii, commonly known as the Pauzii's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage structures adapted to its natural habitat. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
