Begonia macvaughii

Begonia macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers are borne on long, slender stalks.

Begonia macvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
