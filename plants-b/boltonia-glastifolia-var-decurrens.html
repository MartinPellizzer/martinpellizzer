Boltonia glastifolia var. decurrens

Boltonia glastifolia var. decurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf Boltonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink composite flowers in late summer, arranged in spherical or conical clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often found in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial to full sun.
