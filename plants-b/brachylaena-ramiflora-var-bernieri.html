Brachylaena ramiflora var. bernieri

Brachylaena ramiflora var. bernieri, commonly known as the red-hot-plate tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, fiery red inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grey-green, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects due to its nectar-rich blooms.
