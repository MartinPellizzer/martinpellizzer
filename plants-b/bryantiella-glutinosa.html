Bryantiella glutinosa

Bryantiella glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky bryantiella, is a unique member of the bryophyte group, specifically a liverwort.

It is characterized by its small, flattened thallus that secretes a viscous, sticky substance, which aids in water retention and may deter herbivores. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, produce spores that are dispersed by wind.

Bryantiella glutinosa plays a minor but ecologically significant role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
