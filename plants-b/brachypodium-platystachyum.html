Brachypodium platystachyum

Brachypodium platystachyum, commonly known as the broad-leaf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
