Bambusa nana f. albovariegata

Bambusa nana f. albovariegata, commonly known as the variegated dwarf bamboo, is a compact, ornamental bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking variegated foliage with white and yellow markings on its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant grows in a clumping form, reaching a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for small garden spaces. Its slow growth rate and cold hardiness make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The variegation enhances its visual appeal, adding a decorative touch to landscapes or container gardens.
