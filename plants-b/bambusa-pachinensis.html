Bambusa pachinensis

Bambusa pachinensis, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking purple-colored stems and leaves. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a tall and sturdy ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface that contrasts beautifully with the purple stems.

Bambusa pachinensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
