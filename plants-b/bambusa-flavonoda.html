Bambusa flavonoda

Bambusa flavonoda, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-green coloration, which is more pronounced in its younger shoots. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to several meters in height, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Bambusa flavonoda is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
