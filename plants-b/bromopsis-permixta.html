Bromopsis permixta

Bromopsis permixta, commonly known as the mixed grass bromeweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Bromopsis permixta is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for grazing animals and supporting local insect populations.
