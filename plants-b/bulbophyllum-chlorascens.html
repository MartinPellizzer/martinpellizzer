Bulbophyllum chlorascens

Bulbophyllum chlorascens, commonly known as the green-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers with intricate veining. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

Bulbophyllum chlorascens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
