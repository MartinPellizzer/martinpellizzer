Barnardia japonica var. major

Barnardia japonica var. major, commonly known as Japanese barnardia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and somewhat woody.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
