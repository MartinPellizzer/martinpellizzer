Bulbophyllum mascarenense

Bulbophyllum mascarenense, commonly known as the Mascarene Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically has fleshy, pseudobulb-like structures that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
