Barombia

Barombia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and broad, waxy leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Barombia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
