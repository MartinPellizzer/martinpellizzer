Bulbophyllum xylophyllum

Bulbophyllum xylophyllum, commonly known as the wood-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, woody rhizome and strap-like leaves that resemble wood in texture. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
