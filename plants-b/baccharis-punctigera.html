Baccharis punctigera

Baccharis punctigera, commonly known as rough baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis punctigera is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
