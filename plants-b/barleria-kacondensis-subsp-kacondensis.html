Barleria kacondensis subsp. kacondensis

Barleria kacondensis subsp.

kacondensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
