Bidens adhaerescens

Bidens adhaerescens, commonly known as the clinging ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers with ray florets. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The species is characterized by its ability to adhere to surfaces, contributing to its common name.
