Begonia tuberculosa

Begonia tuberculosa, commonly known as the warty begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty tubercles on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
