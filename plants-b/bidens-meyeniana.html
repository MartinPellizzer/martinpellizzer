Bidens meyeniana

Bidens meyeniana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Bidens meyeniana is often found in disturbed habitats, including roadsides and dry fields, and is known for its hardy nature.
