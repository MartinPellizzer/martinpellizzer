Boerhavia anisophylla var. micrantha

Boerhavia anisophylla var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-leaved blusher, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are typically small, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
