Brassica repanda

Brassica repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soils. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Brassica repanda make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
