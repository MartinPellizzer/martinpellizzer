Buddleja monocephala

Buddleja monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its unique growth habit, with a single main stem that bears multiple flowering branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Buddleja monocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
