Byrsella hylodes

Byrsella hylodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
