Bambusa widjajana

Bambusa widjajana, commonly known as the Java bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to form dense clumps over time.

Bambusa widjajana is well-suited for ornamental gardening and has a strong structural integrity, making it useful in landscaping and construction.
