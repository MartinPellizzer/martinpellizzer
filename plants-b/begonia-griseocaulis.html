Begonia griseocaulis

Begonia griseocaulis, commonly known as the silver-stem begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.

Begonia griseocaulis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
