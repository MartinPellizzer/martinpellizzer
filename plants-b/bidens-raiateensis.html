Bidens raiateensis

Bidens raiateensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in coastal and dry habitats, often found in sandy soils.

Bidens raiateensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
