Brassicella valentina subsp. transtagana

Brassicella valentina subsp.

transtagana, commonly known as the Trans-Tigana mustard, is a small annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

The seeds are small and capsule-like, typical of the mustard family, and are dispersed by wind.
