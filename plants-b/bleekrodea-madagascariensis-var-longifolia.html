Bleekrodea madagascariensis var. longifolia

Bleekrodea madagascariensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Its varietal name, *longifolia*, refers to its long, slender leaves.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and unique growth habit.
