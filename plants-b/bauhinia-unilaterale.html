Bauhinia unilaterale

Bauhinia unilaterale, commonly known as the one-sided bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is recognized for its unique, asymmetrical leaves that give it its common name. Bauhinia unilaterale produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers, often blooming in the spring.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
