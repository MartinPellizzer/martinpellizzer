Blechnum christii

Blechnum christii, commonly known as the Christ's harts-tongue fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically fan-shaped. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Blechnum christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
