Bridelia micrantha var. genuina

Bridelia micrantha var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bridelia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
