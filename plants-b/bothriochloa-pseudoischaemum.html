Bothriochloa pseudoischaemum

Bothriochloa pseudoischaemum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects or as a forage plant for livestock.
