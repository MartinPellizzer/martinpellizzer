Boehmeria virgata

Boehmeria virgata, commonly known as Chinese nettle or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its fibers are used in traditional crafts and textiles due to their strength and durability.

Boehmeria virgata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
