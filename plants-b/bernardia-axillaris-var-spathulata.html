Bernardia axillaris var. spathulata

Bernardia axillaris var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bernardia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a dense network of veins. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
