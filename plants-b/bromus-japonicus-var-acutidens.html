Bromus japonicus var. acutidens

Bromus japonicus var. acutidens, commonly known as the Japanese brome, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
