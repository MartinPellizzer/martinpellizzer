Blumea myriocephala

Blumea myriocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed chamaedorea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a distinct yellow-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of small yellow disc flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Blumea myriocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
