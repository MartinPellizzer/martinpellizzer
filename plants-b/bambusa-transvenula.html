Bambusa transvenula

Bambusa transvenula, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This clumping bamboo grows in dense stands and prefers well-drained soils. It is a fast-growing plant, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
