Balsamita grandiflora

Balsamita grandiflora, commonly known as Mexican balsam or grandiflora verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and long blooming season make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.

Balsamita grandiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
