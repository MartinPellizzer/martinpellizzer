Blumea sericans

Blumea sericans, commonly known as the broadleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent.

Blumea sericans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
