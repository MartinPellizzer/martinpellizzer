Baptistonia nitida

Baptistonia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Baptistonia nitida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
