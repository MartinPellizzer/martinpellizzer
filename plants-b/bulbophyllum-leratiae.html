Bulbophyllum leratiae

Bulbophyllum leratiae, commonly known as the Lerat's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
