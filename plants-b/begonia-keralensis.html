Begonia keralensis

Begonia keralensis, commonly known as the Kerala begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, glossy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth. Its botanical features include a short stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive calyx that persists after flowering.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
