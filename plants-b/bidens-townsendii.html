Bidens townsendii

Bidens townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
