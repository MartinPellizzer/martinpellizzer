Brahea psilocalyx

Brahea psilocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue agave, is a species of agave native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen perennial that features a rosette of thick, narrow, bluish-gray leaves. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its leaves are lined with sharp spines and are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
