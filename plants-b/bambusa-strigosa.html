Bambusa strigosa

Bambusa strigosa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-green color and a rough, textured surface. Bambusa strigosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense clumps.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
