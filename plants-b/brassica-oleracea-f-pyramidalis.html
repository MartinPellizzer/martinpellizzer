Brassica oleracea f. pyramidalis

Brassica oleracea f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the curly kale or tree kale, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features dark green, curled leaves that form a conical shape, giving it a tree-like appearance. This plant is typically grown as an ornamental or for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often used in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
