Bryonia syriaca var. marmorata

Bryonia syriaca var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled bryony, is a variant of the bryony plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive marbled patterns on its leaves and stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
