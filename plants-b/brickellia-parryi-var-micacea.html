Brickellia parryi var. micacea

Brickellia parryi var. micacea, commonly known as the silverleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as plains and canyons. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
