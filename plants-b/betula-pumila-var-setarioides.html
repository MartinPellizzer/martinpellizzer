Betula pumila var. setarioides

Betula pumila var. setarioides, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and other moist environments.

Betula pumila var. setarioides is a hardy species that plays an important ecological role in its habitat.
