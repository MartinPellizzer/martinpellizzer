Bredemeyera laurifolia var. parvifolia

Bredemeyera laurifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are smaller and more densely packed compared to the typical variety. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice for hedges and container planting.
