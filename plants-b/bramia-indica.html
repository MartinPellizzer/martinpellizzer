Bramia indica

Bramia indica, commonly known as the Indian arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

Bramia indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
