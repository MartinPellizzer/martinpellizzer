Boronia anemonifolia subsp. variabilis

Boronia anemonifolia subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sclerophyllous vegetation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
