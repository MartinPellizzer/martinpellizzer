Bombax heptaphyllum

Bombax heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaf ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink or red. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a smooth, pale bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are palmate, typically consisting of seven leaflets, giving it its common name.

The tree plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
