Biscutella laevigata var. coronopifolia

Biscutella laevigata var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and unique foliage.
