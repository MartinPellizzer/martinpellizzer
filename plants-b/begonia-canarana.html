Begonia canarana

Begonia canarana, commonly known as the Canary begonia, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Begonia canarana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
