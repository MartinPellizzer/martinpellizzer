Bellevalia zoharyi subsp. pyricarpa

Bellevalia zoharyi subsp.

pyricarpa, commonly known as the fireberry bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a red or orange throat. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger and more robust flowers compared to other members of the species.
