Baccharis medullosa f. gracilis

Baccharis medullosa f. gracilis, commonly known as slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
