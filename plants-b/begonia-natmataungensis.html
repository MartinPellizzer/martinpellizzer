Begonia natmataungensis

Begonia natmataungensis, commonly known as the Myanmar begonia, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical regions of Myanmar.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Begonia natmataungensis is considered a conservation concern.
