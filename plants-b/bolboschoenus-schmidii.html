Bolboschoenus schmidii

Bolboschoenus schmidii, commonly known as the Schmid's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil.

Bolboschoenus schmidii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
