Boninia grisea var. crassifolia

Boninia grisea var. crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf boninia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
