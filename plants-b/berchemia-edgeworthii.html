Berchemia edgeworthii

Berchemia edgeworthii, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is noted for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Berchemia edgeworthii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
