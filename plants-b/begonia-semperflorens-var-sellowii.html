Begonia semperflorens var. sellowii

Begonia semperflorens var. sellowii, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a compact, trailing plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.

Its ability to produce foliage and flowers simultaneously adds to its ornamental value.
