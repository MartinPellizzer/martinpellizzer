Boehmeria australis subsp. dealbata

Boehmeria australis subsp.

dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging nettle species.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.
