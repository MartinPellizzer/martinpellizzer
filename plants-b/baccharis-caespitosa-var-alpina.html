Baccharis caespitosa var. alpina

Baccharis caespitosa var. alpina, commonly known as Alpine Baccharis, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features dense, compact growth with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions, tolerating cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
