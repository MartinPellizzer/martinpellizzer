Brachistus rhomboideus

Brachistus rhomboideus, commonly known as the diamondleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Brachistus rhomboideus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
