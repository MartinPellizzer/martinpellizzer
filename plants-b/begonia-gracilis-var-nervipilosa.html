Begonia gracilis var. nervipilosa

Begonia gracilis var. nervipilosa, commonly known as the slender-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinct venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
