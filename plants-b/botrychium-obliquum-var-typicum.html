Botrychium obliquum var. typicum

Botrychium obliquum var. typicum, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include a single, erect sporangium at the tip of each frond, which produces spores for propagation.

The species is notable for its primitive morphology and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
