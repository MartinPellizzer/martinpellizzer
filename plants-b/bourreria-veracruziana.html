Bourreria veracruziana

Bourreria veracruziana, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical climates.

Bourreria veracruziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
