Biscutella lyrata subsp. laxiflora

Biscutella lyrata subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and often edible or economically important species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
