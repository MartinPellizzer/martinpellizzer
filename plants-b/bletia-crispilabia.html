Bletia crispilabia

Bletia crispilabia, commonly known as the crinkled bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often crinkled or folded, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bletia crispilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
