Bromopsis alexeenkoi

Bromopsis alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Alexandrov's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Bromopsis alexeenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
