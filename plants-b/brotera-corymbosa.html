Brotera corymbosa

Brotera corymbosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscapes.
