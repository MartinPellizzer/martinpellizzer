Bauhinia runyonii

Bauhinia runyonii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia runyonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
