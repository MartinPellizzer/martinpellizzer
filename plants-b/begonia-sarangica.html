Begonia sarangica

Begonia sarangica, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Begonia sarangica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
