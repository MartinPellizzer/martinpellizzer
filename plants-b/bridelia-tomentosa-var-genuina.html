Bridelia tomentosa var. genuina

Bridelia tomentosa var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
