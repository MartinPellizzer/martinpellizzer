Besleria aggregata var. semiannularis

Besleria aggregata var. semiannularis, commonly known as the semiannular begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by its colorful foliage. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and as a decorative plant in greenhouses.
