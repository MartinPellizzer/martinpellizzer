Blepharipappus gaillardioides

Blepharipappus gaillardioides, commonly known as the Gaillardioides aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is generally gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
