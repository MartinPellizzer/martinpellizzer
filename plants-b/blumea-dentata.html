Blumea dentata

Blumea dentata, commonly known as the toothed blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, elliptic leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Blumea dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
