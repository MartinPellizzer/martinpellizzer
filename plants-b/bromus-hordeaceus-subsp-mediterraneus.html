Bromus hordeaceus subsp. mediterraneus

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in dry, temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
