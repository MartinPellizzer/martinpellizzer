Berberis × declinata var. oxyphylla

Berberis × declinata var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Oregon grape.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and dark blue berries. The plant has holly-like leaves with spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners in regions with mild climates.
