Baccharis hyemalis

Baccharis hyemalis, commonly known as winter baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and silvery-gray foliage that provides winter interest. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Baccharis hyemalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
