Baccharis lancifolia

Baccharis lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

Baccharis lancifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
