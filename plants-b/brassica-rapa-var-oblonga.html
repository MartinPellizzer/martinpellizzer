Brassica rapa var. oblonga

Brassica rapa var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese mustard or field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape, and bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both its edible leaves and seeds.

Brassica rapa var. oblonga is also known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to cooler temperatures.
