Bondtia angustifolia

Bondtia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bondtia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Bondtia angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
