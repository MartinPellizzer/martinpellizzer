Berberis aggregata var. prattii

Berberis aggregata var. prattii, commonly known as the Pratts' barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
