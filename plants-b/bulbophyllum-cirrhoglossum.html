Bulbophyllum cirrhoglossum

Bulbophyllum cirrhoglossum, commonly known as the curly-tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and long, thin leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive, curling lip that resembles a tongue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum cirrhoglossum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
