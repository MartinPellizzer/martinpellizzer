Brassica retrorsa

Brassica retrorsa, commonly known as the oriental mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known crops like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Brassica retrorsa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
