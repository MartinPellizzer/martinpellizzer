Bignonia rusbyi

Bignonia rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its cascading clusters of orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces long, slender, woody stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Bignonia rusbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
