Blyxa somae

Blyxa somae, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediment and improve water quality.

Blyxa somae is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter pollutants and provide habitat for aquatic life.
