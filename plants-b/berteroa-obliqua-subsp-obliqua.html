Berteroa obliqua subsp. obliqua

Berteroa obliqua subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the Mexican horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
