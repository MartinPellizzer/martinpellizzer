Blechnum montbrisonis

Blechnum montbrisonis, commonly known as the Montbrison fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
