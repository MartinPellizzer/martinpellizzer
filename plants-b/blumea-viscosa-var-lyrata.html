Blumea viscosa var. lyrata

Blumea viscosa var. lyrata, commonly known as the broad-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with yellow central disks, and it produces small, spherical fruits called cypsela.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
