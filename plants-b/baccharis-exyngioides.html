Baccharis exyngioides

Baccharis exyngioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis exyngioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
