Boronia latipinna

Boronia latipinna, commonly known as the broad-leaf boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and it is often found in coastal heathlands and dry forests.

Boronia latipinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
