Bromopsis gentryi

Bromopsis gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's bromes, is a genus of grasses native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Bromopsis gentryi is often used in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
