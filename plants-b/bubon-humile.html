Bubon humile

Bubon humile, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Bubon humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
