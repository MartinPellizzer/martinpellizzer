Borassus

Borassus, commonly known as the areca palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the palm family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. Borassus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and for the use of their fibrous materials in making ropes and mats.

Additionally, some species produce edible fruits that are consumed in various cultures.
