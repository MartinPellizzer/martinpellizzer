Buphthalmum arabicum

Buphthalmum arabicum, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant produces small, oval-shaped tubers underground, which are edible and have a nutty flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Buphthalmum arabicum is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
