Bulbophyllum ebulbum

Bulbophyllum ebulbum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous golden-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can vary in color from deep green to reddish hues depending on light exposure.

Bulbophyllum ebulbum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native environment.
