Bactris major var. infesta

Bactris major var. infesta, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. This variant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach impressive sizes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
