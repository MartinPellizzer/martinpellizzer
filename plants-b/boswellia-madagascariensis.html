Boswellia madagascariensis

Boswellia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family native to the island of Madagascar.

This tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and produces a resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic properties. The plant thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The resin, known as olibanum, has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic qualities.
