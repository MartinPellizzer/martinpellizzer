Byttneria scabra var. serrata

Byttneria scabra var. serrata, commonly known as the rough bark hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or red, often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
