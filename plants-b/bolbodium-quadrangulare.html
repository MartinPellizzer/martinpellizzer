Bolbodium quadrangulare

Bolbodium quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-sided bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sided, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Bolbodium quadrangulare is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
