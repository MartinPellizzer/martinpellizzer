Baldwinia peltata

Baldwinia peltata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed baldwinia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Baldwinia peltata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
