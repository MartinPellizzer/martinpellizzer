Bergenia crassifolia

Bergenia crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bergenia, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in cold, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in early spring, which are borne on tall, sturdy stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats, making it a hardy ground cover.

Bergenia crassifolia is well adapted to harsh climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in colder regions.
