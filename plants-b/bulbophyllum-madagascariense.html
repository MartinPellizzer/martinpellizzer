Bulbophyllum madagascariense

Bulbophyllum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascan orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Madagascar.

It is a lithophytic plant, meaning it grows on rocks, often in shaded and humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellow with contrasting purple or red markings. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its often arid habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
