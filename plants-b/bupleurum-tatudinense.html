Bupleurum tatudinense

Bupleurum tatudinense, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes with cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive central yellow spot.

Bupleurum tatudinense is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
