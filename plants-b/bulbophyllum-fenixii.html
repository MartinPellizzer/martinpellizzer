Bulbophyllum fenixii

Bulbophyllum fenixii, commonly known as the Phoenix orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with numerous fragrant flowers. The plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
