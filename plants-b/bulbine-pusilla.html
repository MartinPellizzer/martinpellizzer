Bulbine pusilla

Bulbine pusilla, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbine, is a small, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with minimal care.
