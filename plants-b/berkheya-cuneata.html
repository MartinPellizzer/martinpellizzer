Berkheya cuneata

Berkheya cuneata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, parachute-like seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in gardens.
