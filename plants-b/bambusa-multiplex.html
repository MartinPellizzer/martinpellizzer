Bambusa multiplex

Bambusa multiplex, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a smooth, green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength.

Bambusa multiplex is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a sustainable source of bamboo for various applications.
