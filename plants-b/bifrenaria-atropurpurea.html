Bifrenaria atropurpurea

Bifrenaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to violet, often with a distinctive two-lobed shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

Bifrenaria atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
