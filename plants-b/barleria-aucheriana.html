Barleria aucheriana

Barleria aucheriana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
