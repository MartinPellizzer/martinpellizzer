Bactris chaetospatha

Bactris chaetospatha, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often armed with spines along the margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are typically consumed by various wildlife species. Bactris chaetospatha thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as both a food source and a habitat for numerous organisms.
