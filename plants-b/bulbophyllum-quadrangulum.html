Bulbophyllum quadrangulum

Bulbophyllum quadrangulum, commonly known as the quadrangular orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, quadrangular-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive color gradient. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
