Bothriocline misera

Bothriocline misera, commonly known as the small-flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
