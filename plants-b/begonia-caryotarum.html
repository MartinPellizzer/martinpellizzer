Begonia caryotarum

Begonia caryotarum, commonly known as the purple-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant leaves and delicate blooms, which make it a sought-after addition to gardens and collections.
