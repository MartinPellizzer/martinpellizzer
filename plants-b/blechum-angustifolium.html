Blechum angustifolium

Blechum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blechnum, is a fern species native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The fronds are often adorned with small, tooth-like serrations along the margins, and they may have a bluish-green hue when young. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitats.
