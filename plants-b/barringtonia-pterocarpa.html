Barringtonia pterocarpa

Barringtonia pterocarpa, commonly known as the mangrove banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water, aiding in its propagation in coastal areas. It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in tropical botany.
