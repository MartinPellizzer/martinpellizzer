Begonia burabod

Begonia burabod, commonly known as the Burabod begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
