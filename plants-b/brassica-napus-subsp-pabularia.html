Brassica napus subsp. pabularia

Brassica napus subsp.

pabularia, commonly known as wild radish, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are pubescent and have a distinct mustard-like odor when crushed.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.
