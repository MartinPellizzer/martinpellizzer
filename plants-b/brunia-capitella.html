Brunia capitella

Brunia capitella, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Brunia capitella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
